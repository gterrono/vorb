    FOOD: 4/5 (5/5 for the cheese, 3/5 for entrees)
    I'm a huge fan of this restaurant, not in particular of its entrees but rather the amazing cheese boards! As a party of two, we ordered two cheese boards - World Travel & The Master Collection. World Travel was to die for! It's great that the cheeses come with a blend of lighter ones and stronger ones. I was a huge fan of the stronger cheeses, and my date liked the lighter ones, so it worked out pretty darn well. The Master Collections was still great, but definitely not as amazing as World Travel. Nonetheless, my favorite cheese of the night was the Midnight Moon.

    AMBIANCE: 5/5
    The inside had great decor; outside looked even better, but it's definitely meant for lunch under the daylight instead. The restaurant had a high ceiling which made the small restaurant feel much more spacious.

    SERVICE: 5/5
    Our waitress "cheesemonger (?)" was helpful and knowledgeable. She knew the cheeses inside-out, and apparently had to go through some intense cheese camp before she could get hired! Intense stuff...

    All in all, $25 for a cheese board that lets you travel the world and another $25 that lets you eat even more awesome cheese is pretty darn worth it! Definitely will be coming back for more, or at least to shop at its cheese shop next doors!

    Talula's Garden was the most incredible dining experience I have ever had anywhere, let alone in Philadelphia. The entire meal was blow-you-away good.

    My girlfriend and I started with the Autumn Harvest Cheese plate, which was outstanding. The notables were the Cypress Grove Truffle Tremor (Goat, CA), Cabot Clothbound Cheddar (Cow, VT) and the Valdeon Blue (Cow/ Goat, Spain). We then moved on the appetizers, where she had the Braised Rabbit and Fazzoletti Pasta and I went with the Torchon Of Hudson Valley Foie Gras - both outstanding in their own, unique way. My foie gras was paired with pears, which gave it a much different (read: lighter) taste that I was expecting.

    For entrees, I had the Local Brook Trout and Smokey Bacon, with crisp Brussels sprouts, parsnip cream, lemon, cipollini onion, and bacon vinaigrette. Simply outstanding. Honestly, there are not enough adjectives to describe how well-done the dish was - fish was cooked to perfection with crispy skin, the Brussels sprouts were done just right. My girlfriend went with the Muscovy Duck Breast and Crisp Confit,  with cabbage and duck fat fried spaetzle choucroute, turnips, pear, cashews, and duck jus. This was a super interesting mix (duck and pear and turnips!) that worked really well. I'd also be remiss if I didn't mention the desserts - Melting Dark Chocolate "S'mores" & Smoked Ice Cream and "Sweet Autumn...Ice Cream, Sorbet, and Warm Cookies" - both really, really good.

    The thing that Talula's does better than almost anyone is make you step ever so slightly outside of your comfort zone. Before my meal, I though I would HATE Brussels sprouts with trout, but they complimented the the fish so well. My girlfriend thought that she hated blue cheese, but the Valdeon Blue on the cheese plate changed her mind. Although not inexpensive, I don't think Talula's Garden is overly expensive for the quality of the food, the innovative cooking and great location, either. I can't wait to go back.

    Easily one of my Philly favorites.

    Effortless and casual is the vibe at Talula's.

    Whether we are there in the Summer for effervescent cocktails and salmon salad in the stunning outdoor garden, or osso bucco and patè indoors in the rustic dining room, sheer perfection is easily found here in many forms.

    I'm not sure why, but the restaurant's moniker always screams 'healthy dining' to me. Like I'm about to dig into platter of sprouts and kale chips instead of intense cheese platters and duck confit. Garden visions aside, Talula's is exactly the kind of chef-driven, ingredient-forward menu that I love to indulge in. Refined dishes that shine with a restrained simplicity are the stars here.

    You must begin with a Talula's cheese board here first and foremost. With something like over 50 selections of cheese on hand, the offerings are impressive for any dairy enthusiast. The Cheesemonger's Secret Surprise Stash (for $20) is always an adventurous time and a great primer for your main course.

    Entrèes like the "Seafood Cassoulet" with poached Monkfish and seafood sausage as well as the pan-roasted chicken with fluffy swiss chard dumplings are hearty, flavorful, tasty, perfection. Although, you really can't go wrong with just about anything. Flawless execution has always been evident dining here.

    So, grab a cocktail at the bar (may I recommend The Northerner, a boozy cider on ice?) and settle in for what is sure to be a memorable meal. Talula's Garden is 100% Feast.

    Josh, the manager came by to check on us when he noticed our appetizers weren't being eaten and thoughtfully interpreted my partner's non-verbals as not being too happy.

    We discussed with him the physical arrangements of the table and settled in for another awkward dinner since we couldn't speak to each other.

    Moments later, Josh came to our table, collected our drinks, and moved us.

    Such a simple thing, but so considerate! We were moved to a nice table that was turned the short way so we were close and in the transition between two sections so we both felt we could see what was happening around us.

    I can't adequately describe how much this improved our experience. Thoughtfully doing the right thing, with no fanfare. If only customer service like this existed everywhere, but since it doesn't it makes Talula's stand out even more.

    Thank you to Josh and Tatiana, our server, for not only providing a delightful dinner for us, but acting to save us from what might have been a disappointing experience.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
