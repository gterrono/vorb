    5/31/2011

    My friend Cambria had been talking up Banana Leaf for weeks, so we finally went out for dinner.  First off, the menu is huge.  It will take at least a few visits to order everything that sounds appetizing.  

    We started with the roti, which seems to be their most popular appetizer.  It was a flaky, slightly crispy fried dough served with a curry dipping sauce.  It was pretty good, but I prefer naan.  The entrees are pretty large, so I think this dish is miss-able.  

    For the main, I ordered crispy fried squid.  I liked this dish, but I wish it would have had some king of sauce served with it.  Further, all of the squid pieces were from the mantle (or the head) and there weren't any tentacles, which I think are the best part.  I asked for it extra spicy and they obliged with adding a healthy serving a thai chilis.  I wouldn't order this again, but it wasn't bad.  

    Cambria ordered a curry chicken dish, and overall it had good flavor.  Nothing exceptional, the meat and spices were simply good.  Both of our dishes were served with coconut rice, but I couldn't taste the difference from normal white rice.  The fried rices dishes, however, are great.  We ordered chicken fried rice, and I surmise that the pork fried rice is also tasty.  

    The dining room is large, but there isn't much to remark about in the way of service or atmosphere.  It's like a lot of other Chinatown eateries, and is also similarly priced.  It's also a BYO, which is a great feature.

    Bottom Line: The extensive menu offers a lot of good choices, and this is a good representation of Malay food.  Still, the area does offer a lot of good options, and Banana Leaf isn't my first Chinatown option.  Good, not great.

    Listed in The Illadelph (and burbs), Y U SO FAR

    I've been here several times and have really enjoyed the food and the experience, but after my birthday party here a few days ago I'm definitely giving Banana Leaf a 5-star review. I made a reservation on Wednesday for a 12-person table for Friday evening, and it was not an issue.

    When I chose Banana Leaf I wanted a good, affordable restaurant with choices for all palates. We were all either students or recent grads with minimal income streams, so the price point had to be low. At the end of the meal everyone commented on how cheap it was for the quality of the food, so I guess I chose well.

    We started off with some appetizers - the ever popular roti canai and some roti telur to experiment a bit. The roti canai portion was pretty small, so a big party might want to order a few. The roti telur (roti with eggs and onions inside, served with curry sauce) was amazing and reminded me a bit of the Taiwanese breakfast food danbing.

    We all ordered our own entrees because everyone had different degrees of spice tolerance, experience with Malaysian food, and tolerance of meat. The char kway teow (rice noodles stir-fried with seafood, eggs, dark soy sauce, shrimp paste, and other things) was delicious and full of complex flavors. The Hokkien char mee (similar but with yellow noodles) was also very good. I've had the nasi lemak (coconut rice with small bits of yummy things all around) before, and my friend said it was superb. Even the pad Thai was reportedly very good!

    We decided to get dessert elsewhere this time, but I've had the ABC and it is excellent. My friends surprised me by asking the waiters bring me a mooncake topped with happy birthday candles (my friends brought both with them). They happily obliged to this strange request and we had a jolly good time.

    Service was great all around--they worked like ninjas, refilling our glasses of water all the time without us noticing. They were a bit annoyed that only half of our party had arrived half an hour after the time our reservation was for, but you can't really blame them for that.

    Summary: excellent food, great prices (cheaper than Penang, in any case), good for groups large and small.
