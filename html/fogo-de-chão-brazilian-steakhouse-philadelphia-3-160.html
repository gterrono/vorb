        Having been here twice and having had enough time to digest the sheer amount of overeating that tends to be associated with this place, I have to say I'm pretty meh about it.

        In all honesty, the price tag is the killer. You're paying for an unlimited supply of premium meat. It's done well, some might argue not well enough, but it's good. There's really no price tag on being able to eat your body weight in bacon-wrapped filet mignon. Fogo caps it at a mere $49.50 per person for dinner. (Frankly ends up being around $65+ or so after tax, gratuity and beverages/dessert.) That's a priceless notion.

        Like others have said, it's the theatrics and the limitless meat at a steep price tag.

        Pro tip: If you're going to go here, please make a half-assed effort in even trying to come close to pronouncing it right. The last word is not a homonym for "CHOW" for bloody beef's sake. (fo-go dèe shoun)

        If you're really interested in good Brazilian without such a hefty price tag, venture into the Northeast and check out the places in Castor Gardens near Bustleton and the Boulevard. Pros: Usually buffet-style pay-per-pound. Cons: It's in Northeast Philly.

        If you haven't been to Fogo de Chao or any other Brazilian steakhouses, you should go for the experience. It's worth noting that you get the exact same thing during lunch as you're getting during dinner but it's much cheaper to go for lunch.

        Upon entry, your waiter will explain to you how this works. Each patron get a round coaster card which on one side it's green which means you welcome the opportunity of getting cuts of meat from various floating gauchos carrying imposing chunk of meat, the other side of the card is in red, in which you're resting and content for now. After the explanation, you'd be directed to the central salad bar which contains various exotic vegetables, cheeses, cured meat and salmon. Each patron seems to be required to get something from the salad bar before returning to his/her table to start the meat fest.

        Each table is issued a few side dishes which include a plate of fried polenta, a plate of mash potatoes and a plate of fried plantains. The best basket contains some soft spongy cheese bread poppers that's fragrant with grease. The cuts of meat carrying in spears by balloon pants wearing gauchos include filet mignon, sirloin, rib eye, sausages, lamb chops, leg of lamb, chicken wrapped in bacon, pork loin, pork ribs, beef ribs and maybe a few others. My favorite is definitely the beef ribs called Costela which is in the fattier side. The problem with that is they don't seem to bring that one around often and usually have to be asked before they bring them around.

        Most of their meat is heavily salted at the point where you can't really eat that much of it. The seasoning is the regular salt, pepper and at most garlic, so all the cuts of meat taste pretty much the same. With your cards in green, the gauchos came in drones like hornets. Since the cheaper cuts of meat get passed around a few times, you are tempted to refuse their approach if you've already tasted what they are carrying but you'd feel uneasy for leaving the green side up. Since the taste is just so-so, you can be dazzled by the format and the environment if you haven't been but at most visit it twice and it's quite enough, especially at that price range. Better yet, come here once, go to Chima and Picanha and compare for yourself. No matter what you do the desserts are not really worth it, but then you'd probably be too full to have some anyway.

        It's a 3.5 star experience for me.

        I am not often thoroughly impressed by restaurants. I can't think of a single negative for this one.

        The service was friendly and attentive. From the waiters to the carvers to the hostess, they all get an A+.

        The ambiance ... very classy and stylish  without being overly so. Customers were dressed in business attire, casual attire and even a few quite formal (I think those folks were going to the theater).  

        Now the good stuff ... THE FOOD !

        I went with my brother for dinner. OMG!  Everything was stupendously delicious! My brother and I were moaning with pleasure (wash your dirty minds with soap ... you know what I mean! - - the "yummy moan" ... we've all done it at some point, unless you've had the misfortune of eating crap your entire life).

        Anyway, the carved meat was delicious. We were served about 7 different meats - every single one was juicy and tasty. I particularly liked the little baby lamb chops. The cheese rolls (pão de queijo) were heavenly. I could load up with those alone!  They were warm and crispy on the outside but moist and chewy on the inside. They're not overly cheesy. You just get a hint of cheese. They are little pieces of perfection. Have you ever had mochi before? Well, for those who have, the inside texture of the bread reminded me of mochi.  Then there's the salad bar. Very nice. The selection included salad ingredients, roasted veggies, prepared salads like Waldorf and potato,  smoked salmon, olives, etc. They serve three starches delivered to the tables ... We had caramelized bananas  (OMG!), fried polenta (OMG!), and mashed potatoes (did I mention OMG?).

        After dinner, we had dessert (which was probably not a good idea because I almost had to be rolled out of the restaurant). My bro had the chocolate mousse cake (chocolately and moussey without being overly sweet) and I had the creme brûlée.  Wow!  Both awesome. And I had some coffee ... Give me a break!  The coffee was heavenly :)

        Now for the pricing ... If you're going to have the carved meat, then you pay $50, which includes the salad bar and dessert and the starches. You can opt to just have the salad bar for $30, but why do that!  Drinks are separately priced.

        From the moment you walk in this Brazilian steakhouse until the moment you are rolled out, you are treated to an elegant dining experience that has no equivalent in the all-you-can-eat world.

        Fogo cuts no corners, from its classic decor, to the salad bar filled with expensive cheeses, to the consummate, sword-bearing professionals who serve you an endless barrage of meats.

        I'm convinced they have to have a slaughterhouse on the premises just to keep up with the demand. I'm also convinced that one trip here would ruin the song "Old MacDonald Had a Farm" for my daughter.

        I agree with Michael M (and the others), who said that you get more bang for your buck at lunch, but when you remember that this is a steakhouse, the price doesn't seem all that extravagant for dinner either.

        About every 5 years I have the urge to stuff my guts with salty dead carcass, only to regret it each and every time as I lay in bed while all my blood rushes to my stomach in an attempt to digest the 10 pounds of flesh.

        So I enter Fogo de Chao pounding my chest repeating the saying - give the woman meat!

        I immediately want to hide my face as I see how bright the restaurant is, and wonder how I'm going to inhale all this flesh without looking like I'm famished.

        The waitress begins by saying "Weeeez are an authentic Brazilian restaurant and weeez are well known for our meats, so what would ewes like to drink."  Um, ok lady, eyes would like...

        Anyhow, an all you can eat salad bar is offered prior to the meat, but who wants to fill up on salad when carcass is about to arrive all hot and juicy at your table.

        The little paper coaster suggesting 'yes bring me meat', or' stop before I explode', immediately grabs the gauchos' attention and the skewers are staring at you in the face as soon as you flip the coaster to green.

        It was like a whirlwind.  As soon as one gaucho left, another would be right behind him shoving the next skewer in your face so you can smell the delicious aroma.

        The meat is out of this world...perfectly cooked, perfectly seasoned, and at the right temperature when it hits your plate.

        My favorite was the lamb.  I must have eaten an entire skewer!  The pork I politely passed on, coz as those who know me, know that pork tastes like bum.

        The sides they provide with the meal were just as amazing as the meat itself.  Grilled polenta, mashed potatoes and plantains - all 3 were delicious.

        The entire meal is $49.50 which is worth it if you can eat pounds of flesh in one sitting.  It's not worth it if your date is shy, eats like a pigeon, or turns her nose up at all the meat parading around the floor.

        My only discrepancy is that they should turn the lights down a little as it felt like I was in a huge buffet in Vegas.  I thought to myself that maybe I was about to die since an all you can eat Vegas buffet is what I'd choose as my last meal on earth.

        On the flip side, if the lights were dim, people would probably curl up on the floor and go into a food coma.

        The salad bar was great, lots of different choices and great quality.  Now lets talk meat - PHENOMENAL.  The filet mignon, the garlic sirloin, the everything.  If I can recommend one thing it would be to try the sausage.  OMG - If I didn't fill up on everything else I would eat a plate of those.  

        I have experienced the one in Philly and am looking forward to experiencing the one in Denver.  I was so excited to hear there was one in Denver.

        Make sure to starve yourself for days before going so you can get your moneys worth.  I don't consider this place expensive, because lets make sure to mention that $50 per person is not that much especially for the quality and amount of food you can have.  At a nice restaurant that is not Prix Fixed you could spend double that per person.  So let's be honest and mentions that this place is a great deal and should be experienced by all.  

        I do recommend reservations for prompt seating and recommend not filling up on the salad bar and eating some meat first.  

        Add this place to your Christmas List and maybe Santa will bring you large skewers of meat...LOL
