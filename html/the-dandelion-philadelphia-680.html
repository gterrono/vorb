    Listed in Philadelphia

    Always love the Dandelion - European pub feel with contemporary small plates. Stephen Starr rarely misses the mark. This is the only Starr restaurant with a TV at the bar. Story goes, foreign visitors couldn't take missing soccer and pushed Starr to give in. So for those soccer fans out there, it's on 24/7 (no sound).

    This place is known for their great whiskey and gin selection. Bartenders are paper experts, for the most part. So if your new to the scene or a veteran check this place out for the best of the best.  Chef Robert Aikens has infused his English style into every dish on the menu. There isn't a thing you won't love love love! Try the Beef Tartar or Liver mouse, to die for! Also, great entrees and pub fare - Deviled Eggs yum yum.

    The bar is beautifully designed, old world hard woods, ornate, and comfortable. For the first few years there was no foot bar, but they gave in and now you can rest your feet and drink happy.

    Check this place out in your next walk to the park. You won't be disappointed!

    Happy eating Philly!

    There is a well curated set of taps, and the staff was knowledgeable about the drafts. Well, done, guys. The cocktail list is worth a look. I tied the Basil Punnet. It was a refreshing, mildly fruity pink concoction. Very nice.

    My brother ordered the Lamb Shepherds pie and really enjoyed it. A small note, the "pie" is a small portion, but rich and filling. The baked trout was quite good. The fish preparation was simple and straightforward, well executed and let the fish shine through.

    For desserts, the Chai rice pudding is a small pot of creamy goodness. It is served with a citrus gellato that is quite extraordinary in flavor and texture.

    One drawback, the bar on the first floor gets loud on popular nights.

    My husband and I went here for dinner last night.  We've been wanting to try it for a while and I'd say many of the reviews here are on point in a number of ways.  The atmosphere is relaxed and low-key, the service is excellent, and the decor is charming.  What put this experience over the top, though, is the following.  

    The house-made ricotta.  It is probably one of the best things I've ever eaten.  Anywhere.  Ever.  It's served with crispy, toasted pieces of sourdough.  It spreads like butter.  It's light.  It's fluffy.  It's absolute heaven.  It is what drove me to write this review, in all honesty, and the sole reason I will come back to The Dandelion.  If this place ever closes down, I might cry because I'll never get to taste the ricotta again.  Get it and thank me later.

    We also had the deviled eggs as an appetizer and they were just ok.  Nothing to write home about, in my opinion.  They're very mustard-y, so if you're not a mustard fan, skip them.

    For entrees, my husband had the rabbit pie and I had the renowned fish and chips.  If I could do it all over again, I'd order the rabbit pie instead.  The puffed pastry was light and crispy, the filling was creamy and flavorful, and captured the pure essence of comfort food.  The fish and chips were good, but sadly, I couldn't reciprocate the other reviewers' sentiments that they were the best I've had.  The fish has a thick-battered crust and tasted fresh, but like a lot of white fish, was sort of flavorless.  It is served in a single filet as well, and I would have preferred several chicken-finger-esque pieces for hand-dipping in the malt and tartar sauce.  Instead, you have to eat it with a fork and knife which felt a little bizarre.  The flavors and textures were nice once you started dipping into the various provided condiments, but it was really nothing spectacular.  The fries were good, and they were served in a metal cup, so they stayed hot through the entire meal which was really nice, but I'm a little confused about the rave reviews they're getting on here.  They were crunchy and tasted like, well, fries.  But I've had fries like these before, and I personally prefer thinner cut fries I've had elsewhere.  It probably sounds now like I didn't enjoy the meal at all, which isn't true - It was good.  But that's it.  Maybe it's just me, but for an almost $20 price point for french fries and a piece of fried fish, I expected to be blown away.  

    We were sufficiently stuffed after dinner, so we skipped dessert, but we did indulge in a few cocktails.  I had a cosmopolitan, and the hubs had a Scotch Honeysuckle off of their cocktail menu, and they were pretty fantastic.  The Honeysuckle smelled like a rose, which was intriguing.  Coming from someone who HATES scotch, whiskey, or anything of the sort, I had three sips of this because of the gloriously sweet and floral aftertaste.  Highly recommend ordering one of these if you're a scotch fan.  

    Next time we go, we'll probably try a couple of different entrees, but if all else fails, I will ALWAYS go back for the ricotta. Speaking of which, I'm going to go indulge in the portion I intentionally left over and had our waiter wrap up for me with some extra bread ... Officially obsessed.

Specialties

London's culinary revolution comes to Philadelphia with this eclectic English pub. Cozy rooms and intimate bars create a storied, Old World aura, while updated seasonal takes on British food make up the menu. In keeping with tradition, cask and tap craft beers are always rotating, a proper afternoon tea service has a sweet and savory menu, and classic beef family-style roasts are served on Sundays.

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
