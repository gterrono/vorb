    My experience at Audrey Claire was surprisingly underwhelming.  Perhaps I've been spoiled by the plethora of fresh and novel ingredients used in Californian "new American" cooking at gourmet-fast-food-style eateries (such as restaurants at the Ferry Building in San Francisco or in the Mission District), but aside from the intimate, "quaint" ambiance, I didn't find much that I was impressed with.  The latter also backfired somewhat because the wooden flooring amplified the chatter and the tables were too close together.  I found myself having to lean in and yell a little so that my friend could hear me in our conversation.  My less than effusive review may also be attributed to the fact that my friend and I came here during restaurant week.  Naturally, since these places get more customers during this promotional week, they have to prepare a 3-course menu that isn't too difficult to prepare or else the chefs would be really backed up in the kitchen.  

    I ordered the following items:
    Appetizer: Tiger shrimps with balsamic vinaigrette dressing and sweet potatoes
    Main Course:  Pork chops with maple flavoring and bacon bits.  Sweet potatoes on the side.
    Dessert: Warm chocolate cake

    I unfortunately didn't have the foresight to bring my own wine or beer.  It's also inconvenient to bring wine because you can't get alcohol at the grocery store in Pennsylvania (ugh).

    The pork chops were only moderately infused with maple syrup flavor and the marination was insufficient.  For such a thick slice of meat one should infuse it longer (overnight) for optimal flavor.  Salt was also lacking.  Perhaps the chefs should have soaked the pork chop overnight in brine and then transferred the pork to its sweet, maple marinade.  I had to add some salt on top.

    The ahi tuna my friend ordered was rare in the middle (though the server did ask her how she wanted it and basically made her get it "raw") and crusty on the outside.  I would have preferred it if the middle were better cooked and not as cold as it was.  I like my food to be hot.

    The desserts didn't disappoint as much as the main course did.  The warm chocolate cake I got was rich and came with a side of whipped cream that was only moderately sweetened (or maybe it was just whipped cream without any added sugar).  My friend got the vanilla creme brulee which had a nice crusty sugar topping, though it was a bit too crunchy for my tastes.  It was a good size, though, so I won't gripe too much about that.

    All in all, I didn't have a terrible experience, but I was disappointed given how enthusiastic others were about Audrey Claire.

    I probably won't go back unless there's another restaurant week in the near future.

    Another caveat: this place is CASH ONLY.  There's an ATM that only charges $1.00 for fees across the street at a beer/snack shop (you can't miss it).

    Service: A-
    Taste: B

    I came here last week for the BF's birthday after he pestered me for months that we had to eat here.  Luckily, his birthday was during the week, so I was able to make a reservation for two at this charming Rittenhouse Square BYO.  As we sat down, we looked at the menu and couldn't decide what to order because everything looked and sounded delicious.  But we ended up splitting an antipasti platter, fried halloumi cheese, and two entrees.

    The antipasti platter was really good, but the standout favorite was the artichoke hearts.  So delicious and filling, and honestly much better than most of the other items on the plate (olives, beets, tomatoes and mozzarella, baby asparagus).  The fried halloumi cheese with the apricot jam and candied dates was delicious though, and the combination of salty and sweet was perfect.  The cheese wasn't too greasy, and there were enough of the accompanying elements to be shared equally between the two of us.

    For the main dish, I ordered the Barramundi special (sort of like a Chilean Sea Bass, came with English Pea Risotto and Baby Asparagus), while he ordered the Rack of Lamb.  My fish tasted excellent, a soft and tender filet with a crispy crust and the side dishes were savory as well.  HIs rack of lamb was also terrific, especially when paired with the slightly tangy yogurt sauce on the side.

    When I casually mentioned to the waiter that it was his birthday, he brought out not one, but TWO complimentary desserts: a flourless chocolate torte and a creme brûlée.  The chocolate torte was very dense and rich, and accentuated by a creamy chocolate center which paired well with the whipped cream and berries that were also on the plate.  The creme brûlée was one of the best versions of the dish I've had to date: a hard sugary crust with the soft creamy custard underneath.

    With exceptional service and strict attention to detail regarding the food, Audrey Claire will definitely not disappoint your foodie friends or that special someone you're looking to impress.  My only two complaints: I only wish they had real wine glasses instead of small tumblers for the BYO, and the inside area where we sat was particularly noisy (probably would have been better off outside if we wanted to have an intimate conversation).  Overall, I would definitely return to try more menu items that I didn't get a chance to have the first time.
