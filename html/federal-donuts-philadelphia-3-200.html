    Listed in Philadelphia Restaurants

    Mmm mmm mm.

    Growing up in the South, I'm familiar with fried chicken, but not with donuts. But, come to think of it, it does make sense. There's chicken and biscuits, chicken and waffles, so why not chicken and donuts?

    The fried chicken here was crispy and juicy at the same time. Seasoning comes in dry (Shabazzi, buttermilk ranch, ballpark bbq) and glazed (honey ginger and chili garlic). I tried their house special dry seasoning - the Shabazzi. It's a blend of herbs, and a good choice if you're looking for something savory. However, I think it was a bit too dry. I'll have to try one of the glazed choices next time!

    For the donuts, there are two kinds "Fancy" and "Hot fresh". The "Fancy" donuts are glazed and come in a variety of daily flavors. I've tried the cookies and cream, the sea salt and chocolate and the watermelon - all bring the same sugary satisfaction! The "Hot fresh" donuts are made to order and sprinkled with flavored sugar (non-glaze). I tried the strawberry lavender, but think I personally prefer the "Fancy" donuts.

    Tips: Each order of chicken comes with a plain donut (which I forgot to leave room for!) Come early for popular "Fancy" donut flavors and wings as they tend to run out! There are a few (though small and awkward) single person seats inside, but this is more of a to-go place.

    Considering how great they can be individually, I'm not sure why chicken and waffles doesn't blow me away.  Perhaps I just haven't found a place that can do both well, but maybe my taste buds just prefer them separated (BTW, that Lay's chips version is kind of disgusting...).  Fried chicken and donuts though?  Now THAT is a combination I COULD get on board with!

    At a glance, Federal Donut's menu looks rather modest (fried chicken and donuts), but it actually has a bit of variety - roughly ten flavors of donuts and six flavors of chicken that can be served naked, glazed, or seasoned.  Since we were also grabbibg take out from Shake Shack nearby, my friends and I opted just to split some wings to bring back (you can also get whole and half chicken orders).

    -- SHABAZZI WINGS...$9 for a half dozen / $17 for a dozen
    (yelp.com/biz_photos/fede…)
    The cashier recommended this one and I'm so glad she did.  Of the two we tried, this one was by far better and it wasn't even close.  The skin was crispy, the meat was juicy, and the seasoning added a great complimentary flavor.  So good!

    -- HONEY GINGER WINGS...$9 for a half dozen / $17 for a dozen
    (yelp.com/biz_photos/fede…)
    My first bite was fine, but it went downhill after that.  The meat was also pretty juicy, but the glaze made the skin a bit soggy and was overpoweringly sweet.  

    Now, why was "could" in caps earlier?  Well, that's where this review takes a sad, sad turn.  Even though each order of chicken came with a honey donut, and despite the fact I also ordered a Boston Crème donut ($2) specifically for my train ride from Philly to NY, I didn't actually try any of the donuts (too full initially and then I forgot to take it with me later on).  Fail, I know, especially considering I still took a picture of it too (yelp.com/biz_photos/fede…).

    Since I can't say how the donuts are, my three stars are based purely on the chicken - four for the Shabazzi and two for the Honey Ginger.  However, I will gladly come back to get more of the Shabazzi chicken, to finally try the donuts, and to potentially up this to four stars.  Potentially.

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge, Fried Chicken Fried Hard, Mmm..., Epic Philadelphia Food Tour

    This place needs to expand nationwide already. Since my visit to Philly in 2012, they have now expanded to this new location, which is just as great as the original.

    Just a day earlier, I was at the original location at around 2 PM, and they had already sold out of 4 of the 6 fancy donut flavors. After trying the other 2, I remembered how great this place was, and knew I had to try those other 4. A quick Yelp search revealed this other location, so I decided to try out this one.

    Larger than the original, this one has a more narrow, longer setup, with a few more seats, though it is still pretty small. As expected, it was packed when I arrived at a little before 11 AM.

    Since we had a long day of eating ahead of us, I just got 3 of the 4 remaining fancy donuts ($2 each). And of course one of the hot, fresh donuts ($1.25 each). And of course some of the excellent fried chicken ($9 for 6 wings).

    We got the Sea Salt & Chocolate, Sticky Bun, and Cookies & Cream, and all were incredible. The Cookies & Cream was definitely the best, tasting just like an Oreo mixed with a donut. The super salty and deliciously sweet Sea Salt & Chocolate was close behind. For the fresh hot donut, we tried the Vanilla Lavender: so pillowy soft, piping hot, with just the right touch of sweetness, these are a must try.

    Lastly, don't skip the fried chicken here either. With a ton of different kinds of dry coatings and sauces, there are so many different flavors to try. This time, we tried a glazed one, the Honey Ginger, and it was just as good as the dry seasoned one we had the day before.

    I'll be back next time I'm in Philly to polish off the entire new menu of flavors and to get some more of that excellent chicken.

There is just no escaping the fried chicken and donuts that this place is pumping out. I must admit that I was turned off about the idea of fried chicken AND donuts. Like who eats that, together? Well let me tell you. I DO! And so should you!

I had no clue what that meant because I was a rookie but by looking at the menu I figured it out. The menu also had fried chicken and I totally disregarded the crazy notion of even attempting to try it. I went with the only 3 they had to sample and share with the mister. From the business

Cake donuts in interesting flavors and Korean-style, twice-fried chicken sold by the half and whole bird. House-roasted coffee available hot or cold-brewed and iced. That's basically it.

Learn more about Federal Donuts , Opens a popup

Specialties

Cake donuts in interesting flavors and Korean-style, twice-fried chicken sold by the half and whole bird.  House-roasted coffee available hot or cold-brewed and iced. That's basically it.

History

Established in 2012.

Chef Michael Solomonov and partner Steve Cook of Zahav and Percy Street Barbecue got a wild idea to pair exotic donuts and super-crispy fried chicken. With Bodhi Coffee owners' Bob Logue and Tom Henneman's coffee expertise and professional bigmouth Felicia D'Ambrosio, the team opened the first FedNuts at 1219 S. Second St. in Pennsport, South Philly in 2011.  The Sansom Street shop followed one year later in October 2012.  We have since been cranking out fancy donuts, melt in your mouth hot fresh donuts, and truly badass chicken.  Follow us on Twitter www.Twitter.com/federaldonuts for status updates and fun contests.
