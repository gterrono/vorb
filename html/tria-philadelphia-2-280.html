    My girlfriend and I came here to relax with a little beer and wine.  We were seated immediately, and it was a very good experience except for a fruit fly issue. We'll reconcile that later.

    Let me be brief. Dog Fish Head 120 Minute IPA **ON DRAFT** $5 a glass.  That's 18%ABV for $5.  That's amazing.  It's beer with the alcohol content of wine that costs less than any other wine Tria serves.  I had two of them, and that's all they allow.

    This is where Tria shined.  My girlfriend ordered a white wine that was "mellow" and did not have much flavor as it did a decent after-taste. She wanted a crisp, up-front wine with plenty of acidity.  Our server (so sorry I forget her name) made it clear: "I don't want you to drink something you don't enjoy. Please, let me get you another one that you like.  Would you like to taste a couple?"  My girlfriend happily settled on a Macabeo wine from Madrid that was full of floral, honey notes and plenty of crisp flavor.  

    The only issue I have is that there was an overabundance of fruit flies.  Now, it's said, you can catch more flies with honey than vinegar, but it's not the case for fruit flies.  So, a place with wine is a perfect place for them. It was hard to drink the wine with flies landing on our glasses and flying at our faces.  Clean it up, Tria.

    Bottom line: If I want great wine (or beer) and I want to be treated decently, I'll go to Tria.  Now, I'll have to go back for the food...mmmm.

    I cannot believe I haven't found time to rate this on here!  

    Both this location and the Rittenhouse are fantastic, both have the same cool vibe, but this one feels more spacious.  

    Everyone that works there seems to be not only incredibly knowledgeable, but darn good looking too! haha!  

    I always like to describe the experience to people as you can go in and not know squat about beer/wine/cheese, and know exactly what you will like immediately.  Well, after reading through the amazingly detailed and well-thought out menu pages!  Each of the fermentables section is set up so you can say, hey I really don't like stinky/sharp cheese and really want something very light but yummy, and you will find it.  There are also some funny descriptions that make you want to try something just by how it was written!  If you aren't in the mood to search out your choices, the staff will help you pick the perfect ones, and any needed pairings for them.  

    If you want a nibble, there are a ton of amazing choices besides the kickass cheeses, you really can't go wrong here.  If you need a bit more substance, the sandwiches are all incredible!  It's the only place that I actually will seek out a tuna sandwich!  

    They have killer fermentation school classes -if you see one you are interested in, book it immediately, as the classes are small and fill up very fast.  The Sunday School specials always have some killer goodies too.  Make sure to get on the email list so you will know what is coming up with classes/events/special selections, etc.

    This was one of the better places I ate at while in Philadelphia.  It is small wine bar on Spruce and 12th.

    I started with the bruschetta ($4.50) with robiola due latti with saba-roasted strawberries.  The robiola is similar to a brie type cheese and the strawberries were roasted place on top of the cheese.  It was yummy, except the strawberries were a little too sweet or maybe a nuttier/saltier cheese would be a better pair.

    The best thing I ate was the duck salad ($10) with seared duck, poached cherries, pistachios, grana podano, spinach and a citrus vinaigrette.  The duck was perfectly medium rare and just melted in my mouth.  It is nicely paired with the cherries and spinach.  The pistachios give it a nice crunchy texture.  Lastly the dressing is nice and tangy.  It was so delicious, I want to go back again.

    I fondues with a nutella panini ($4) for dessert.  While anything with nutella is yummy, I would pass on this next time. Mostly because you can easily make this just as well at home.  Plus I was already full off of the bruschetta and salad.

    I sat at the bar and although I quit drinking, I know the people working here know their stuff.  They were discussing the different wines and pairings. I was impressed.  Service was also stellar!  The waitress was very friendly, food came quickly, she always checked in with me, filled my water expeditiously and when I left she even said "it was nice to meet you".  I haven't had a server say this before but it has a nice ring to me as in they are interested in meeting their customers rather than an in and out establishment.

    Overall, excellent value to dollar.  Yummy food, reasonable prices and great service. Can't beat that!

    This was really good! I'm not a sweets person and I really liked this. The chocolate cake was very dense and fudge-like with a really nice coffee aftertaste. I would definitely recommend this.

    Warm Poached Black Mission Figs with Gorgonzola and Prosciutto di Parma ($6)
    This was very underwhelming. I would skip this.

    Ambiance: Very good place to go on a date. It is slightly dark, not too loud, trendy, and has a large beer and wine selection. The space is intimate without being formal or romantic (I think this is because Tria has a long and thin dining room).

    I'd give the appetizers 3 stars but the ambiance, attentive service, and extensive beer/wine menu bring the overall review to 4 stars.

    Tria stands out because it's affordable, serious, and aggressive. It's really a place that will give you some of the best food for the price, a ridiculous cheese list, and a consolidated bottle and taplist that rivals any in town.

    Beer is more than a drink at this place, it's an experience. And it allows beer and wine to coexist, as if the two were meant to be served next to each other. Tria (while it may contribute to the winofication of beer), is evangelical in its approach to beer. It doesn't make you feel like an idiot, it's not pretentious, but it's always quite elegant and proper. Tria invites you to its bar to sit, laugh, and enjoy. The music playing is always great, and the service fascinating--even when it gets packed at Sunday School. Tria makes you feel special, while making you realize that the beer you are drinking and the cheese you are chewing are just as special.

    This is a place that's great for an after-dinner drink, a first date, or just a bargain. Don't even bother researching the taplist, just expect to be impressed. A great selection of beers from all over the world is always present--and it gives homage to local beers as well, often pouring Dogfish Head and Stoudt's, too. This place gets beer. It's one of my favorite places to drink and eat. I'm sure you've been there already, and if you haven't, I'll take you there pronto.

    Tria is a school. It's about education. It teaches its clients that understanding what you are consuming--not just from the standpoint of sustainability--is important. Asking questions about your beer. Smelling it. Sipping it. Looking at it. Lifting it up real high because it's just fun to do that. It let's you be a child, and treats you fully and completely like an adult. There are no wrong answers here. Come here, enjoy yourself, and even if you don't want to be schooled, order something safe. And if you read up, you might meet a brewer, a representative, or just be in good company on your visit there, too. A highlight of the city, one that is not easy to replicate, either. It offers a product and a service that no one else comes close to.

    Hats off to Tria, for being fun, serious, and committed.
