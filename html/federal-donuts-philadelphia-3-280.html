    First time visit to Federal Donuts.  As a regular traveller to Philly had Fednuts on my "To Do" list for a while - but it was on this rainy Saturday in March that I finally got there.

    Great donuts - bought a "sampler" one of each of the day's Fancy list - and didn't break into them until returning to North Jersey about six hours later.  They were great - and stayed soft and moist through the following morning - which is not always the case with the donuts offered by others.

    My favorite of the 6 was a chocolate covered strawberry concoction - not sure if that's what they call it - but it was perfectly balanced not too sweet - with the sweet "bright" berry flavor contrasted by the dark chocolate "over-coat".  Delicious.

    Will be back - (still haven't tried the chicken) :-)!

    So I was visiting Philly and heard through some locals that Federal Donuts were a MUST TRY.

    The next day, my friends and I decided to come here for breakfast. Chicken wings & donuts for breakfast? Don't mind if I do!

    I had the strawberry lavender donut which was made to order. It came out warm and gooey, perfect when it had just snowed outside. The flavors blended so well together, I was in donut heaven.

    I also ordered the za'atar wings. I was so impressed with these wings... Maybe because I'm from the west coast and their chicken wing game is just so off...  Anyway, these wings were ginormous. Back home, I'm used to one of the sections of the wings to be considered one piece, but here, all 3 sections is considered one piece! So the 6 piece wings I ordered was basically equivalent to 12 pieces back home. I felt like I got a really good deal. The wings were perfectly crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside, just the way I like it! To top it all off, the order of wings came with a lemon donut.

    Next time I'm in town, this will be my first stop. I wish they delivered to the west coast!

    The donuts here are very good and have unique flavor combinations.  (I didn't try the chicken, but it smelled great.)  They're a little expensive at $1.24-$2 per donut, though.  (At least for those of us for whom one or two good donuts is barely a snack.)  These are relatively light and airy cake donuts . . . _very_ sweet, relatively light and airy cake donuts.  I tried Chocolate Sea Salt, Marshmallow2 [my favorite -- soooo good!], and the hot donuts, Strawberry-Lavender (also excellent) and Vanilla Spice (which I thought was too salty).  The fresh, hot donuts made to order is a neat idea; think of the cinnamon-sugar donuts made fresh in front of you at carnivals and fairs, but here they have a few flavor options.

    Federal Donuts is a small place, with very limited seating.  On a bad weather day with a crowd, you're probably better off not getting the made-to-order donuts as they'll be too hot to eat immediately but will cool down by the time you find someplace else to stop and eat them.  I thought the small area of somewhat school desk-type seating along one wall was pretty neat.  The staff is friendly and patient.

From the business

Cake donuts in interesting flavors and Korean-style, twice-fried chicken sold by the half and whole bird. House-roasted coffee available hot or cold-brewed and iced. That's basically it.

Specialties

Cake donuts in interesting flavors and Korean-style, twice-fried chicken sold by the half and whole bird.  House-roasted coffee available hot or cold-brewed and iced. That's basically it.

History

Established in 2012.

Chef Michael Solomonov and partner Steve Cook of Zahav and Percy Street Barbecue got a wild idea to pair exotic donuts and super-crispy fried chicken. With Bodhi Coffee owners' Bob Logue and Tom Henneman's coffee expertise and professional bigmouth Felicia D'Ambrosio, the team opened the first FedNuts at 1219 S. Second St. in Pennsport, South Philly in 2011.  The Sansom Street shop followed one year later in October 2012.  We have since been cranking out fancy donuts, melt in your mouth hot fresh donuts, and truly badass chicken.  Follow us on Twitter www.Twitter.com/federaldonuts for status updates and fun contests.
