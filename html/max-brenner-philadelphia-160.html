        Considering Mr Brenner is a chocolatier, the food (and selection) were surprisingly good with a touch of modern American style. I was not expecting a huge selection of fodder, and the select few on the menu is adequate fare for the casual city diner like myself. Besides, we came for the chocolat.

        For starters, our group shared a pizza, wings and the macaroni and cheese.  

        The macaroni and cheese- perfect pasta. Just al dente, and the cheese filled the cavities of the pasta nicely. There was a hint of ham in the cheesy goodness, and the mozarella in the cheese sauce held the pasta together just enough to play nicely on my fork. There was also the presence of diced tomatoes, just enough to provide a small contrast between the cheese and pasta. Panko breadcrumbs adorned the whole presentation, and the parsley on top just added enough color to the otherwise bland whitish yellow landscape.  Very good Mac and cheese, it  seemed like something concocted out of my own kitchen, especially the panko essence. Nicely done.

        I had a piece of the chicken and it was doused in a savory citrus glaze. The chicken was succulent and flavored with just right amount of acid, sweet and salty, and had the right appeal to my taste buds to beg for another sliver of it.

        The pizza we shared was good but not great. Thin crust with just the right amount of toppings- fresh herbs, cheese and some meat slathered with a bit of sauce.  I wouldn't specifically come out here for pizza, save those hard earned coins for other items on the menu.

        Then our food came-

        I had the corn croquettes while the other half had the fish tacos.  I snuck a piece of Fish taco and it was very good!  The fish was fresh, but what implored me to write this part of the review was the side sauce (looked like a thick dipping sauce actually, but was meant to go on the tacos).  Not only was it hearty, it was also spicy.  Spicy hot good.  I haven't been to a modern contemporary restaurant that offers real heat in a loooong time.  'Twas a sauce to remember.  Back to my corn croquettes- they were creamy in the middle and fried till crispy.  I couldn't figure out if I should pop the whole thing or cut them in half.  For the sake of present company, I decided to take the gentleman route and cut it in half.  I really wanted to stuff the whole thing since it looked so good.  The creaminess of the croquette reminded me of sweet grits, but it had chunks of corn in it.  It almost reminded me of an uncooked hush puppy, but this was better than that.  Not a mega fan, but fan enough to wanna eat them as an appetizer only, perhaps that's what I ordered, HA!

        DESSERT-  this is what one should come here for.  Between the 4 of us, we ordered the dessert for 2 for a little over twenty four bucks.  It was enough for 4 especially after appetizer, food and drinks.  The presentation was playful enough to continue our conversations and still be interactive.  There was a mini chocolate fondue (chocolate of choice) and a mini flame grill with marshmallows, fresh strawberries and bananas on the side.  The rest of the platter presented a sweet chocolate spring roll, brownie chunks, some kind of nutty brittle, a waffle topped with something good I couldn't figure out (lol) and topped with Ice cream.  Then there was this little cute bowl with something that looked like chocolate covered rice krispies.  oh and last but not least, a dark chocolate sauce in a little chemistry flask looking thingy.  DESSERT COMA is all I could say.  It was a lot of dessert for 2 people.  I said it at the table and I'm gonna say it again.  Diabetics stay away.

        Service was great, Dom was attentive to everyone's needs and was very prompt with all the little things we needed such as cream for my coffee, water, and extra napkins to my messy friend HA!

        The only thing that I questioned were the selection of "plates" or serving platters which consisted of wooden boards.  They were awkward at times, maybe that's something that the establishment could look at, perhaps make them more table friendly as the tables weren't that large to begin with.

        Would go again!
