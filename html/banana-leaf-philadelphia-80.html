This restaurant also looks a bit more spacious than Penang, although that may be a trick of interior decor. Now if only they'd come out to the suburbs, so I wouldn't have to pay $2/hour for street parking.

    With so many overwhelming positive reviews here I can only arrive at one conclusion, our experience at Banana Leaf was skewed because of the food choices that our dinner companions made.

    We went here for dinner with 2 other couples and 3 of the 4 people that comprised said couples were first generation Malaysian Americans. They graciously offered to do most of the ordering for our table and so we had little idea what we were getting. They were even ordering in Malay when talking to the waitresses.

    We got a bunch of different appetizers and dishes to share. I am guessing that the bulk of the dishes that were ordered were hard-core Malaysian dishes, i.e. they do not taste like what you'd expect from a typical Asian dish, because I haven't yet met an Asian food style that I didn't like (Korean, Chinese, Japanese, Vietnamese, etc.), but I enjoyed very few of the dishes that we ordered. Most had a very strange or off taste and/or smell. An example, one of the appetizers that was ordered is called Rojak. On the description for this dish it states "Please ask server for advice before you order!" and there's good reason. This is a fruit salad from hell that has chunks of fruit, vegetables and octopus in it served with a shrimp paste sauce. The smell and taste of this dish is roughly the equivalent of rotting fish stored in an outhouse in August. I kid you not. We could smell the plate long before the waitress even got near our table and my wife and I were like oh no, please tell us that the sewer just backed up in the bathroom and that's not our dish we're smelling! It was. I ate some of it just to see if I could. It was nauseating. I realize that it is obviously an aquired taste as the Malaysian people at our table devoured it.

    Now I am a VERY adventurous eater and don't turn my nose up at things very often but even my wife was shocked at how few of the dishes I enjoyed and didn't have to force down. There were about 7 or 8 dishes between appetizers, entrees and sides that we ordered and only 2 that my wife actually enjoyed eating and like 1 additional dish for me. The rest were just....UGH!

    I think I would like to try Banana Leaf again some time but next time I will definitely be doing the ordering for myself.

    Visited Banana Leaf for the first time of Saturday night. I usually go to Penang, but my friend described Banana Leaf as a BYO version of Penang, so we went.

The decibel level of the back room, which included no less than 6 parties of 8 or more people, was deafening. The place was packed - but with such a large dining area, you won't have to wait long for a table. They just won't seat you without your entire party.  

    Service was lacking. After we were seated, we got water right away, but waited a while to order. Our Roti Telur never came and several tables that were seated well after us, got their entrees well before us. No one came by our table for at least 20 minutes.

    I ordered the Thai Basil Noodle and my friend ordered the Mee Siam and I am completely blanking on what our third ordered, but we had made serious dents in our plates - mostly because we were starving - but the food was satisfying, and moderately priced.

    I'd go back - especially as it's a BYO - with the hope that the service issue was a one-time thing, but I would stay away from going on a weekend night, it was just too crowded and too noisy.

    The decor left me confused. There's what appears to be a... boat dock in the middle of the restaurant. One wall has some flowers and leaves or something, another wall has what I think were sugar canes, or bamboos or... something. And then there was a little vineyard sort of doohickie in the corner through which the employees on break were totally spying on us. Confusing (albeit totally interesting) decor aside, the food was pretty good. The restaurant recommendation came from fellow Yelpers when I mentioned that I was looking for a Thai restaurant near Center City. I don't know how a Malaysian restaurant counts as Thai, but I wasn't going to complain because I was looking for an excuse to try Banana Leaf anyway.

    The food:
    - Banana Leaf Satay Chicken: chicken was uber-bland, but the peanut sauce was pretty good.
    - Indian Mee Goreng: Indian style stir-fried noodles with various ingredients thrown in. I'm assuming the "Indian" part of the dish was the curry flavor; where it lacked in creativity, it made up in deliciousness.
    - Sizzling Beef with Black Pepper: meh. The title says it all. Not sure why I expected it to be anything other than beef that was sizzling... with pepper on it.
    - Shrimp fried rice: standard fare that was done well.
    - Malaysian Iced Tea: "Does it taste sort of like Thai iced tea?" "Oh yeah, like Thai iced tea." Lies. But still pretty good.
    - Fried ice cream: is a misnomer; it was fried batter with the most miniscule amount of ice cream. And the awkwardly placed fried banana pieces with the giant battered balls just made me kind of uncomfortable because I was sitting next to my girlfriend across from her parents.
    - There was something else but I can't remember it now.

    I'm a huge (HUGE) fan of Penang, and I think if I had gotten what I normally get there at Banana Leaf, I would have been equally impressed. But for now, I liked it but didn't love it. Will definitely be back to try some of my favorite dishes though; really hoping to bump the rating up to a four or five because from what I've heard, Banana Leaf doesn't have stupid limits on how much you have to spend to use your credit card like Penang.
