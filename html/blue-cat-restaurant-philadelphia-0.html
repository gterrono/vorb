    This place is def good ... liked the vibe as soon as I walked in and the service followed suit with a friendly 'tude.  And then there was the food ....     Blue Cat brings something to Fairmount...and Philadelphia for that matter...that I haven't experienced.  Casual dining, that's legitimately casual, with really good unique food.  

    When leaving the house, I was already challenged.  What IS casual?  We're going out to dinner...we're going to drink wine which in my mind is romantic and fancy...so shouldn't I dress up?  But when you look into Blue Cat, dressing up seems silly.  The waitstaff wears jeans and t-shirts, the lighting is bright, and the floors and decor are all within a light color scheme...there's no romantic footsie in the dark going on here.  It was awkward when I tried.

    The food however, is not casual.  It's delicious, visually appealing, and priced appropriately.  We started with guacamole, I had the shrimp and chorizo skewers, boo had cod fish tacos, and we got a side of plaintains.  The shrimp (appetizer) was big enough for my meal.  It came with 3 jumbo shrimp, 3 slices of chorizo, a purple cabbage concoction and this really fresh cucumber watermelon salad.  The boo's fish tacos were massive even though there were only two of them.  He forked his way through the battered cod, avacado, and salsa verde.  The food is very fresh and almost guilt free.  I think the guacamole could have had a kick added in, and the accompanied chips are more like potato chips, but I believe they're homemade.

    My only gripe is that there's no background music in the place.  So the couple of times people dropped their plates, bottles, glasses, babies it got all hush which is probably so embarrassing for that baby.  And the pauses between the bickering with my boo were quite apparent, and I had nothing I could belt out uncontrollably without being noticed while I ate my guac.  This is prob something they're working on though as it's brand spankin new.

    Excited to know that a new BYOB restaurant is opening up in our neighborhood (I first thought it was a pet store from its name), I've been waiting to try it.

    One friday night after work and not feeling up to cooking, my boyfriend and I decided to give the new place a try. I walked in without a reservation to see if the wait would be long. The hostess was very accommodating in seating me right away even though they have a large party coming in soon and my bf wasn't there yet (none of this 'we won't seat you until your full party arrives' nonsense). I was already happy with the service.

    Onto the food--we had the Papas Rellenas,  potato croquettes filled with braised chicken and peas, as appetizer. It was delicious and flavorful, accompanied by a refreshing watermelon salad.

    For the main course, I had the Oxtail Stew, and my bf had the Arroz Con Pollo & Chorizo, both were well-portioned (actually, generous for the price) and YUMMY. I could not even finish my entree though I had room for dessert.

    I read somewhere that the pastry chef used to work at Le Bec Fin, so it was a must for me to try their sweets. We had the bread pudding, which was moist, decadent, but not overly sweet. Though now that I've read other reviews, I've got to go back to try the banana cake.

    The atmosphere is friendly, casual, and modern. Friendly staff, friendly pricing, and very good food! A must try!

Specialties

Casual indie BYOB specializing in Latin-inspired dishes from Cuba, Puerto Rico, Mexico, Chile and beyond.  Chef purchases daily from local markets, bringing fresh preparation and ingredients to each dish.  Save room for house-made desserts.  Call ahead to request freshly squeezed juice mixes for margaritas, mojitos, daiquiris, sours and more!

History

Established in 2012.

Husband and wife veterans of the food industry bet the farm and opened shop in a sunny new space on the developing fringe of the neighborhood where they live.

Meet the Business Owner

Guy S. Business Owner

Following his training at the CIA, Chef-owner Guy Shapiro began his career in hotels working with classically trained European chefs.  His love of food and travel led him beyond Philadelphia to positions in New Orleans, Montauk, Vail, Memphis, France, Greece and Russia.  His love for Latin flavors has been cultivated by his many Latin co-workers over the years, as well has his own exceptional palette.
