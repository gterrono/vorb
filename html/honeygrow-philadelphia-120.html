        This restaurant is too small and too busy during peak lunch hours for me to return again, most likely.

        I perceive three things that contribute to their success.1) It is their location and 2) knowing their clientele, which also bleeds over to point #1. Basically, center city is bustling with businesses which means a lot of both younger and older health conscious working professionals which means they will most likely seek out a salad or two at some point during their lunch week. And, what do you know? Boom. Honey grow is right there - at the prime spot of CC. Third factor, they have done a well with their branding. They stress that their ingredients are from local grown items, and that they only carry healthy foods - foods that nourish you and help you grow stronger, healthier etc. Their workers are all fit-looking, clean cut, young, energetic.

        Anyway, all these factors contribute to their success which, also is the cause of my low rating.

        A) The place is way too small as it draws a huge crowd of hungry people.
        B) Because they are scurrying to meet the high order demands, their quality of food isn't consistent. The stir fry I got (the coconut rice noodles) was really bland and unimpressive.
        C) There is a lot of people working behind the counter, and there is a lot of people waiting all throughout the restaurant. It's too chaotic...

        Just be warned that these are the things you'll face if you go during peak lunchtime. Could be a much better experience if you went during some other time.

        Listed in $10 Paleo Meals - Center City

        There's a healthy eating trend percolating in Philly, and these guys are on the front lines.

        In a nutshell:  Quick real food, ordered via a touch screen (lots of customization), for approx 10 bucks.  Super casual.  Good choice for Gluten Free / Paleo folks or peeps looking for low carb options.  Nice.

        The Food:  Two options:  Salad or Stir Fry

        You order and wait for your food to be called.  Self serve, no tip, keeps it cheap.

        Salad:  I dig the cob.
        Pro Tip:  You can ask for beef.  Beef is my preferred meat source, but it doesn't show up on the menu when ordering a salad.  Ask and you shall receive.  They'll probably hate me for writing this -- (assuming anyone reads it...)

        Stir Fry:  I always do custom on this and just add just about every vegetable and I dig the Thai coconut milk sauce.  They couldn't close the lid on my stir fry last night and had to use two containers to make it fit.  They apologized;  I was happy.  If you are Gluten Free -- they have rice noodles.  If you are trying to lean out, get romaine lettuce instead.

        Other Important Info:  I asked what kind of oil they use for frying and they told me Canola.  Could be better, but could be worse.  At least it's not soybean!  I'd love to see them have the option to fry in coconut oil.

        Oh and they have complimentary water cups by the soda machine.  The soda machine also has a "soda" lever (similar to the water lever -- a couple to the left).  Chill out with some sparkling water while you wait for your food.

        In theory a fully automated ordering/payment set up is genius, however this place doesn't pull it off in practice.  Honeygrow is a honey bar...I have no idea what the Hell that is, and upon leaving I'm still not sure.  They have touch screens where you order and pay if you're paying with a credit card.  I guess they do salads, stir fry, smoothies, and whatever a honey bar is.  Clicking on the stir fry button (and by clicking I mean mashing my hand against the screen until it decided I was in fact trying to interact with it) gave me two options, specials and make your own.  I wouldn't have hated to have a board with the specials on it, so I knew what I was getting into before getting to a terminal, but I guess you really only need that help once.  That being said, they did have a huge list of where all their ingredients came from, so if you're anal-retentive about being a locavore then this is your place.  Anyway, I went with the spicy garlic option which comes with all the choices already made, which is good because there are like a 1000 choices of things to put in your stir fry.  The one I choose had chicken, broccoli, peppers, onion, chicken, and pineapple.  I also ordered a kale smoothie.

        The kale smoothie was nothing to write home about...probably since I used to make them at home all the time and this tasted exactly how I used to make them.  At six bucks I was hoping for something larger, even accounting for the premium of me not having to buy the whole bunch of kale, etc.  If you've never had a kale smoothie, it is nothing like a thick, cold strawberry banana smoothie, this more of a thin-ish green sludge slightly reminiscent of ecto-cooler.

        The two stars is really for the stir fry. At $9 I was hoping for considerably better than I would order at a more traditional stir fry place or Chinese restaurant.  Yes, somehow this place is healthy since they have pictures of grass and such around the store, but that doesn't excuse lacking any depth of flavor.  The sauce/flavor really didn't penetrate any of the ingredients so the food mostly comes off bland and just sat on top of the food, which is a shame since thick sauces and face-melting flavor from the sauce is what stir fry should be about.  Perhaps they didn't add enough, but the volume of food isn't that large, so I'd be surprised if they couldn't spare it. Also it lacked salt which is always a pain.

Just walk by Honeygrow and smell the heavenly food being prepared as its ordered - then come back and tell me you didn't feel compelled to at least stop in and check the place out!

        I've been to Honeygrow many times and I absolutely love it! The concept and the ordering system is very simple too. When you come in, use one of the kiosks to select a suggested stir-fry or salad, or better yet, you can create your own. The price is quite reasonable for the amount of food you're getting and the list of ingredients are endless. There is a board on the wall that lets you know exactly where your food is coming from. Another strong point is that they cater to EVERYONE, gluten free? Great! Vegan? No problem! When your finished at the kiosk (you can also pay there if you're using a card), just print your ticket and listen for your number. That being said, it can become a bit loud during peak hours, I've found it easier to wait closest to the station where my food is being made (toward the entrance for salads/smoothies and near the register for stir fry and other beverages-there are signs hanging from the ceiling to direct you).

        Other things to consider:

        1) If you're in a hurry for lunch I highly suggest calling your order in ahead, especially if your limited on time. You'll skip the line and your food will be ready when you get there.

        2) The employees and customer service are absolutely spectacular. They're always friendly and engaging (I'm always greeted with a smile). They did make a mistake on my stir fry once but when I brought it back they quickly corrected my order.

        3) TRY THE HONEYBAR!! This is my absolute favorite thing there and it's a healthy alternative for dessert or breakfast.

        4) your food comes in a stir fry box or a salad bowl, I've noticed that this issue is a toss up on the review boards but personally I like them. It gives me the opportunity to pack my lunch up and head out if need be.
