    Always in search of the perfect slice of pizza, I am a man that has no limits on how many places I will try if it is accompanied by a good review. And, I often take advantage when I'm in an area I don't travel to often. Now, this is a very important precursor, I am a New York pizza snob who moved to Philly just over two years ago. I've been to some of the best places in New York.

    On this particular day, the wife and I traveled to the Old City part of Center City. We were heading over to Ritz East to catch a flick, the one where Joaquin Phoenix has a romance with a computer operating system. Yeah, that happened.

    Anyway, we needed to squeeze in a quick bite to eat and I took to Yelp, like I so often do, to see what pizza places were nearby. And there it was, Gianfranco.

    Gianfranco reminded me of many pizza joints in Brooklyn that I ate at while growing up. It's a small space. You have the side where the pizza is made and displayed with an old school mechanical cash register. There's a thin aisle, and very little seating, all high tables and stools, seating for two only, since a third would block the aisle.

    I was not here for the atmosphere. I was not here for the spacious seating. I was here for the pizza. It certainly looked the part. I got a couple of pepperoni slices. They were pipping hot after a nice little reheating.

    Once it hit my mouth, I felt joy overcome me. It was not New York top ten worthy, but it was a darn good slice of pizza. It was better than New York average. it was a tasty piece of dough, sauce, and cheese. And, the pepperoni was the real deal, not some store package over-sized type. It was the goods.

    If this were New York, I might give it 4 stars. But this is Philly. You don't come here expecting your pizza to be as good as New York pizza, certainly not top ten New York pizza. So, since this is as good as New York pizza, because not all New York pizza is top ten New York pizza, it gets 5 stars. Gobble gobble!
