    Only had drinks here, but atmosphere was very cool. There is a mix of indoor seating & outdoor seating with more rooms than I could count - also with upstairs and downstairs seating. There is also a pretty cool outside deck area with seating, which was where I was. Beer menu was good, but again, I didn't have food. Their menu is listed on chalkboards throughout the restaurant, which is interesting...except if the menu is located right above tables with people sitting at them. It kind of made looking for a drink to order a little strange while hovering over a table with people.

    Only challenge I had was parking, but I think that had more to do with me going there at 8pm on a Thurs night, and not being overly familiar with N. Liberties.

    All in all, service was friendly and quick, and the prices, while not exactly cheap, were pretty standard for what you'd see in a city in a place that isn't a corner dive-bar. I'd definitely recommend stopping in. If you want to go to another spot after, there are tons of bars and restaurants within a few square blocks, so the location is pretty good too.

Specialties

Standard Tap features locally-brewed draft beer.  Our food menu both complements the beer and expands the notion of what tavern food can be.  We use only the best available produce, poultry, meats and seafood -- much of it from area farms and local vendors - to create fresh, seasonal dishes.

Our full menu is available from 4 pm to 1 am, seven days a week.  Brunch is served Sat. & Sun. from 11 am to 3 pm.

Wine and liquor also available.

History

Established in 1999.

When Standard Tap opened its doors in December 1999, our goal was simple:  to create a neighborhood tavern that featured great beer and wholesome food.  With that in mind, we offer a draft-only beer selection that highlights the vast array of styles crafted by breweries located in Pennsylvania, New Jersey and Delaware.  Our food menu both complements the beer and expands the notion of what pub food can be.  We use only the best available produce, poultry, meats and seafood -- much of it from area farms and local vendors - to create fresh, seasonal dishes.
