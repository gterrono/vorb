    After selling photos at First Friday I showed up at Growler's to meet friends around 10:30 and was surprised many people were inside!  The bar was full, but not unbearably crowded.  It only took a few minutes to get drinks at the main bar, getting drinks at the second bar (full bar minus beer taps) was mostly effortless for the whole night.  Luckily, friends had a table outside.  Unluckily, the table had to be move inside at 11:30.  This gave us the opportunity to meet Jay, the owner, he was very friendly and explained the migration from outside to inside had to take place because of Pennsylvania's funny liquor laws.  Inside, the living room (behind the bar) was open and comfortable; we even managed to get a seat on the sofa after not too long.  The leather sofa and chairs faced a fireplace with a TV on the mantle which will no doubt show football all winter.  For a soft opening the food we ordered came very quickly, the prices seemed very fair, and I am definitely a fan that will be back often.  Maybe even tonight?

    I've been watching this place come together during my walks home, anxiously awaiting its opening, which finally came this past Friday. Today my lovely lady friend and I made our way in for the first time.

    They're in the midst of what our server called their "soft opening," but we still found plenty to sample from their menu: pork cracklins (hot pork rinds with a delicious cheesy sauce), truffle fries (with a delicious shaved pecorino cheese on top), wings (the "hot" were disappointingly lacking in flavor, and only six to an $11 order), and "crispy bacon" (a plate full of bacon, with a dipping sauce containing - yes - more bacon).

    The full menu will be available two days from now, with burgers, steaks, and so forth. For now, their beer menu is more than adequate; their space is gorgeous and features some of the best downtempo music I've ever heard in a bar at 7 pm; and we loved the staff. Our server was super friendly and pleasantly touchy with my lady friend - she wants a massage the next time we go in.

    Four stars, on the way to five!

    My friend and I had a great time at Growlers. Their beer list consists of varying craft beers, most of which we tried and confirmed to be delicious (and very reasonably priced)! Several beers on tap were not exclusive to Growlers, but good beers nonetheless, e.g. Yards IPA, Hop Devil, Yuengling.

    Growlers occupies the space where "The Little Bar" used to be, but they have expanded and added a dining room. We didn't eat dinner, but we snacked on some truffle fries and they were definitely among the best I've ever had. My five-star rating can be most attributed to the bartenders' attentiveness and positivity. Shannon is probably the happiest and friendliest bartender I have ever encountered. Unsatisfied with the small serving of truffle fries the kitchen gave us, she comped us another full order before even bringing them out. We had fries for days! Recongizing how awesome this move was, she celebrated with an unforgettable shimmy back down the bar. She also made a great beer recommendation that I will certainly try again: Lost Abbey Avante Garde.

    Shannon's partner behind the bar, Steve, was also a pleasant addition to our evening. "Go with the gusto and order Flying Fish Exit 4", he said. Another excellent recommendation. When my friend ordered a beer that had just been 86'ed, Steve recommended the Brooklyn Sorachi Ace, a beer unlike any other in the world. Though he won't order it again, now my friend can say he's tried a beer that tasted like apricots, Banana Boat suntan lotion, and -- as the brewery refers to it -- "Earth." A unique experience, to be sure.

    I will definitely be coming back to Growlers for the beers, the service, the truffle fries and of course... the gusto.

Specialties

Growlers is a bar, kitchen and living room in the Bella Vista section of Philadelphia.

We're a beer-centric neighborhood pub offering great food and a relaxing environment, open Monday thru Saturday from 5pm to 2am, Sundays from 11am to 2am. Our kitchen is open daily until 1am.

We have a rotating draft list of craft beer that is available by the pint or in growlers. We also offer beer in bottles and cans, and have a serious selection of spirits. Build your own 6 packs and growlers togo are available until 2am daily.

Our living room features a wood burning fireplace, and we have a game room downstairs for darts, as well as a large selection of board games to keep you occupied. Wednesday nights feature Free For All Comedy sessions, an open mic night showcasing local talent.

Come join us!

History

Established in 2012.

Growlers was born from a collaboration between two already well-established restaurateurs in the city. Partners Jason Evenchik (Vintage, Time, Garage, & Bar) and Jay Willard, a man with a long history of experience at Starr Restaurant establishments in his back pocket (including The Dandelion, El Vez, Jones, Frankford Hall, and Parc), took over the former Vesuvio to launch their newest venture.
