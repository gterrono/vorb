    My friend Dan and I were in the neighborhood yesterday afternoon to visit the Mutter Museum and it was hot as hell (102 degrees) and we were starving and all we knew was we wanted Mexican. Fired up the good ol' Yelp app for iPhone and in a flash we were walking through the front door of El Rey. Greeted by the nice host in some sweet flannel we were seated in the back immediately. A very kind and funny waitress greeted us (I wish I got her name!) and explained the menus (there were several) and specials, letting us know that their frozen drink that day was a pineapple margarita. Dan and I looked at each other and immediately took her up on the offer, as well as two glasses of ice water.
    We start looking over the menus and we are clearly overwhelmed when she comes back with our drinks, so she makes a couple suggestions. I finally decide on the chicken tostadas ($7) and Dan orders the lamb tacos ($9) and rice and beans ($Idon'tremember...heh). She walks away and we sip our drinks. HOLYMOTHEROFWOWWWWW!! That was a good fucking margarita. REALLY good. Served in a nice understated highball glass.
    Now, Dan is the kind of person who is always cracking jokes and will literally talk to anyone, even complete strangers. Very loud, funny, boisterous. I am [slightly] more reserved, and before I know what was happening, he's YELLING ACROSS THE RESTAURANT to the bartender about how good our drinks were, and the staff was all totally cool with it and before long we were all engaging with each other and cracking jokes. It was really fun.
    Before long our waitress brought our food out and my mouth was hanging open. Before me were three beautiful chicken tostadas oh a bed of shredded lettuce and onions. The chicken was melt-in-your mouth shredded tinga-style topped with cotija cheese. I immediately dug in and was quite pleased with my decision to order this. The chicken was very tender and well-flavored and the tortillas maintained their crispiness even under all that lovely, sauce-y chicken. Perfect.
    Dan's three tacos looked amazing, albeit a little on the small side (c'mon Steven Starr!) and were served with a lime wedge. His bowls of rice and beans were heartily portioned and looked delicious as well. For the next twenty minutes we were silent as we ate in happiness. I could only manage one and a half of my tostadas, so I gave Dan the other one (he's a big dude and wasn't full enough with his own order and I'm a nice gal!) He echoed my sentiments of how good the dish was. I finished my margarita and Dan ordered another one, guava on the rocks, and let me taste it (hey, I had to drive...) and it was equally as good as the frozen pineapple. We cut up with the staff a while longer paid the bill ($51 bucks without the tip...ouch!) and were on our way, full and happy, out into Sahara-like concrete jungle to figure out where we left the car.
    Interesting happy hour specials and a secret bar in the back! We'll be back, and you kids better be ready!

    I had the most amazing salad here! Let me tell you about it because you must order the extraordinary concoction of the CRAB, AVOCADO, and BACON salad. Yeah, you read that right. Those three ingredients are meshed with some grapefruit for a little zing, the necessary mixed greens, and a savory chipotle dressing. The bacon was crunchy, the crab was plentiful, and the avocado was perfect. I expected to come to this restaurant for guacamole, tacos, or even a burrito. Nope, this salad caught my eye, and I am so glad that I ordered it. Well worth the $12. I'm thinking that the next time I'm there, I'll just have to order two!

    However, my real reason in coming to El Rey was for the margaritas. I've tried the classic and the mango before, so I tried the tamarind. It had a nice sour kick. I don't fancy salt on the rim, or anything too sweet so this flavor fit the bill.

    Service was pretty attentive and friendly. At 10pm on a Saturday night, most of the dinner crowd was just finishing up, and most tables were there for apps and drinks.

    I'm a fan of this place, and hope to make it to happy hour very soon! $1 tacos and $4 margaritas between 5-6:30 PM. Yes, please!

    We arrived in Philly late in the morning and stumbled upon this place while looking for a place to have an early lunch. It's great to stumble upon something that is actually worth reviewing!
    First of all, you can't help but notice El Rey's decor. Lots of places have nods to retro style these days, but El Rey goes full-on 1970s retro. It doesn't look like it has been there forever, though, but rather like it was obviously designed with a 70s aesthetic. It's obviously in a space that was originally a diner, and I found myself wondering how much of the style was done out of necessity (because, say, it was going to be too costly to replace the dark wood wall and booth paneling) and how much of it was a from-scratch choice. Regardless, it was definitely one of the more visually interesting restaurants I've dined at in a while. (Bonus: my baby was so fascinated by all the cool light fixtures that he was content to look at those and chill out and let his dad and I actually enjoy our meal!)
    Since it was 11:30 AM on a Saturday, we were basically the only table in the restaurant, so I can't really speak to what it's like when there's a crowd. However, our service was efficient and friendly, and it looks like the sort of place that probably has a pretty good bar scene in the evenings.
    As for the food, yum! We ordered off the brunch menu. I had the chilaquiles. I love chilaquiles, and these didn't disappoint. My husband had the chalupas al pastor and those were really good, too. The only part of the meal that wasn't a hit was the "salsa," which wasn't complimentary (c'mon, what kind of Mexican restaurant doesn't offer complimentary chips and salsa?) and was actually more like pico de gallo than what I consider salsa. The price was fair, too: two generous meals (I could have taken my leftovers home and made a second meal out of them had we not been traveling), chips and salsa, and two sodas for less than $30 before tip. Sounds like they have a good happy hour special, too: dollar tacos and $4 margaritas.
    All in all, a really good find!
    P.S.-I sort of doubt this place is kid friendly in the evenings since it looks like it's wanting to be hip and trendy, but for lunch it was definitely kid-friendly. They had a high chair and were very accommodating about storing our stroller up front near the hostess stand.

    On some level, I want to dislike this place more than I do.  It kinda pisses me off that this place has so many reviews and there are so many fantastic restaurants around the city that get so much less attention, but setting aside my skepticism for our society's focus on fame and famous chefs, I think this is a great restaurant that I love on many levels.  As much as I want to dislike this place and the other restaurants run by Mr. Starr, I think they actually are pretty great places that I like going to.  This one is my favorites.

    The happy hour specials here are great...$1 tacos, small but gourmet, and $2 tecate beers (recently they had $3 Yuengling drafts, which I was a big fan of) are enough to keep me coming in the door.  But other stuff here is really good.  Even the basic stuff: chips, salsa, guacamole, are top-notch.  The chips are thick, dark and toasty, and not at all greasy...the salsa is very fresh and light, and is so tasty that I often eat/drink the rest of it after the chips are used up.  I usually am here for happy hour but I've tried a few other items off the menu, and everything here is consistently good.

    I love the ambiance in here.  It's hard to describe; I checked every box except "Touristy" because I believe it's a little bit of all those things.  Aspects of it are slightly upscale but it feels casual and informal overall...but very well put together.  The walls are partly plastered with really amusing posters of Mexican movies and other things...I wonder where they came up with some of it.

    I really like the wait staff here.  They're all really nice, and they've been pretty attentive too.  I also like that their uniform is plaid shirts of different colors.  I love the different plaids that people sport in here.

    The only thing that I don't like about this restaurant is the pickled onions that they occasionally (inconsistently) use as a garnish on tacos and a few other random items.  They taste like mouthwash, not like food...it's some odd combination of herbs in it that just tastes like an antiseptic smell, not a food smell, and I find it off-putting.  I'd rather they not get rid of these pickled onions, but change the formulation of them somehow.  Pickling is really great...I think they could do a better job of coming up with a mix of spices for these pickled onions that would capture the best aspects of these onions while making them more palatable.

    I'm not the only one who has been weirded out by the pickled onions though.  Others in my party have complained to the table about them too.  I usually eat them, but I wish they'd change the spices on them.

    Oh, and I HATE this place's website.  It's flash-only and I hate flash-only websites.  Don't visit it.  Just go to the restaurant for yourself.
