    Listed in Culinary Colonialism, Nobody Likes You, Mom, I forgot my lunch!

    ... wow. Just, wow. This review is going to be useless to you. I read the reviews before I went, too. "Big sandwiches," I read, "I get it."

    You do not get it. You do not understand. You are going to walk in and be served a sandwich the size of your head. This sandwich will look at you and dare you to eat it. "Just try to figure out where to put your mouth," it will mock you, "Do you think you have a detachable jaw !? You cannot best me."

    This is a serious, serious deli. I came at an off time so the place was empty - my server was incredibly friendly and attentive, but I get the sense that they'll treat you like family even if every table at the restaurant is full. You're started off with a bowl of free pickles, which were dee-lish. I ordered the Lee Avenue sandwich - turkey, pastrami, swiss, cole slaw, and Russian dressing. It was a delicious monstrosity. I ended up redistributing about 1/4 of the meat, cheese and other fillings to make a manageable-sized sandwich. It was incredibly flavorful - great bread, wonderful-quality meat, perfectly balanced flavors. And the best part is that I got to walk home with 3/4 of that "sandwich," which I proceeded to share with my entire extended family. Nope, not kidding. My grandmother, 3 cousins, aunt, mom, stepdad, and 3 sisters all had sizeable servings of said sandwich. You can feed a family with these things.

    These sandwiches don't come cheap - even just ordering a sandwich, you're going to be looking at a $20 check. It's worth it. Remember, you're feeding your whole family (... or yourself for the next 3-4 meals), you got free pickles, and you'll get a cookie at the end of your meal so you go home with a sweet taste in your mouth. Mm hmm.

    True, if you eat here more than once a month, the blood in your arteries will be replaced by cholesterol instead.

    Bottom line: Go for the full fat corned beef if you're getting corned beef. Come hungry.

        When we entered this establishment, we saw a bemused couple seated in front of a mound of corn beef hash the size of a small infant, about twelve inches long and four inches high.. We commented to them that it was enough to feed a family of four but they disagreed and showed us that it was more than enough for a family of eight.
         My wife and I agreed to share a single smoked fish sandwich, by ordering an extra portion of pumpernickle. I ordered my favorite, jumbo whitefish on rye with cream cheese tomato and onion. My cousin remarked, only half in jest" Remember to ask them to remove the tail".
         What came to the table was a fresh four inch slice of jumbo white fish, freshly cut of the fish both sides, untouched by human hands. Having worked in a number of Delis I proceeded to filet the fish myself , carefully cutting along the backbone inside and then removing the spine and ribs on one side and picking out the spineless ribs on the other and then pulling the skin away from the two fresh moist filets.
        The rye and pumpernickel was sliced much too thick for our tastes. About a half inch thick. I then smeared the cream cheese on the bread, added the fish and the tomato and onion, sliced the two sandwiches in half and presented one on my wife's  plate. The resulting sandwiches I had created were delightful, made from wholesome ingredients. But I wondered why I was paying the Deli for making my own sandwiches.
