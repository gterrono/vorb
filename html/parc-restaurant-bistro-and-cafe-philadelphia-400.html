    The food actually matches the decor!  We had an al fresco late lunch which easily accommodated five guests plus a 50lb friendly mutt.  Great setting on Rittenhouse which combines the sport of people watching with the charm of Center City.  Steven Starr rarely misses a beat, and Parc is no exception.  The interior is cavernous, lively, and inviting with almost-too-cool-for-school service to match.  Steak frites was wonderfully tender and juicy (and also very large) and two bottles of perfect rose pleased the whole table.

    Parc stays true to the Bistro tradition with excellent French staples and an ambiance that invites hours boistrous conversation, but it's just not Philly enough to have 5 stars.  Still a great addition to the fantastic Rittenhouse stable of restaurants.

    A glass of pinot grigio+abundant bread basket+onion soup gratinee+warm shrimp salad (with lemon beurre blanc, mixed greens, and avocado)+a window table overlooking Rittenhouse Square=food coma.

    The wine was perfect, with just enough crispness and bite to cut the indulgence of my food.

    The bread basket was ginormous for one person, with two hearty slices of cranberry walnut bread, a crusty baguette with at least four servings, two hunks of sourdough, and a little pot of perfectly salted butter.  The cranberry walnut bread and the baguette were good, but would have been downright delicious if they had been served warm rather than room temperature.

    The onion soup gratinee was tasty, but not worth the eleven bucks for a medium sized bowl.  The cheese was also a bit gummy rather than stringy and succulent.

    The warm shrimp salad was out of this world good.  Four fat shrimp cooked perfectly and tossed in a warm butter sauce accompanied a mountain of perfectly dressed greens and roasted tomatoes, a slice of parmegiano, and seasoned slices of avocado.  It's not often I pay $16 for a salad, but this salad is certainly worth it, and one I'm already strategizing to find a way to eat it again before I leave Philadelphia.

    The setting -from location to decor- really do make you feel like you're in Paris.  It's not often that I can have $50 dinners, but Parc made this a memorable one and an experience I would repeat if back in the area.

Specialties

This stylish addition to Rittenhouse Square pays tribute to French cafe culture, serving breakfast, lunch and dinner seven days a week. Ideal for a quick bite or a leisurely meal, the menu is filled with classics: onion soup, escargots, steak frites and towering plateaus de fruits de mer. The baguettes are made in-house and  the wine list is expansive. Sip a Kir Royale at the zinc bar or enjoy a cafe au lait while basking in the sun.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
