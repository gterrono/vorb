    For food alone, I would give Jones a solid 3, leaning towards a 3.5 due to tots that don't quit. The extra star, though, comes from the atmosphere and our server. He was funny, charming, and attentive without being invasive or irritating. Three of our party of four ordered huevos rancheros, and one enjoyed a breakfast sandwich (which included tots). We also enjoyed two side orders of tots for the table and a shareable mini-loaf of monkey bread. As far as beverages go, I erred on the side of sobriety, but my buddies enjoyed strawberry bellinis, watermelon sangria, and a Bloody Mary garnished with everything from olives to asparagus, tomatoes to bacon, tots and pickles. It was truly a sight to behold.

    The huevos were decent, but I think that my eggs were a little underdone. I appreciate a little dippy as much as the next gal, but when the whites are borderline transparent, some of the novelty is lost. The bean blend was flavorful, but I didn't really like the fact that they were pulverized and no longer had any resemblance to actual beans. Also, the only hot sauces they have are sriracha and tabasco. Give me Cholula, Valentina, or Franks.... or give me nothing. I opted for a side of chopped jalapenos for a little heat. Meh. The tater tots were delicious, but that's kind of hard to screw up. The portions are reasonable, and the price isn't outrageous.

    The vibe is killer, it's very throwbacky, shag carpet and all. Think "modern" but interpreted in the 1960s. It was comfortable booths, and I almost expected the servers to be rocking aprons and rollerskates (I clearly don't know the 1960s very well.) Bottomless Bellinis for $21, for when you're feeling boozy, and adult (read liquor-filled) milkshakes to boot. I'll be back, Jones, armed with a bigger appetite next time.

    Oh Jonesy, I really wanted you to win me over on the first date but sadly I don't there is a future for us. I love the concept and theme of Jones being a causal but classy retro diner serving everyone's favorite comfort food. But at the end of the day I can go home for all these dishes and Momma just makes them better. The ambience and décor here is spot on perfection for a casual evening or a funky drunk brunch.

    The menu has all your typical favorites like meatloaf, chicken pot pie, brisket, and mac n' cheese. If you look at the menu you will notice the standout plates that customers rave about: Buf-faux-lo tofu wings, Baked Mac N' Cheese, Jones Market Salad, Meatloaf, Chicken Pot Pie, Pierogies, Fried Chicken and Waffles or Cheese "Hold the Beef Burger", etc. These are not to be missed, however it seems like everything else on the menu is very average and lacks any unique spark in its taste or execution. I would love to see some guacamole on those chicken nachos or even a fun twist on the meatloaf with Kobe beef or truffled mushrooms. I am all for classics and comfort food, but when I dine out I want something memorable and mouthwatering that I couldn't easily replicate in my kitchen. Even though the Pierogies here are popular, they really aren't anything you couldn't find in your local grocery store.

    Overall the menu at Jones might be more suited for brunch, but overall I think this is a restaurant I could see myself passing over in the future. If you like to play it safe and stick to traditional meals than the diner style menu here maybe just what you've been looking for. Personally I want a dining experience worth remembering and a restaurant to frequent that is worth going out of my way for.

We walked in and were immediately taken by the restaurant's 50s style decor, despite the fact that an absent-minded mother allowed her children to aimlessly weave in, out and into me and my group of friends (not the fault of Jones but perhaps the first sign of danger within the restaurant). We were seated on the upper deck of the restaurant, which seemed to me like a cozy and private elevated area (loved the shag carpet), but later proved to be the land of forgotten patrons. As for the food, it was simply eh, which is incredibly disappointing when the banana maple walnut pancakes are advertised as though you'll never taste anything better. I would love to try Jones again for another meal, in part because I'm enthralled with the fact that it looks like a set of Mad Men, but I think I'd rather stick with diner food, which is a no doubt safer (and cheaper) bet. Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
