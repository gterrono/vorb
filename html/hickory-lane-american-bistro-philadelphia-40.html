    I don't give one-star reviews often, but I don't even know where to begin with this one.  Since moving to the area, we've been looking forward to trying Hickory Lane, and a new Saturday Happy Hour (4:30-6:30) seemed like the perfect opportunity.

    We wound up sitting outside, and I must say, this restaurant is in a perfect location.  The ambiance and setting is wonderful.  So to start things off, our server comes over with a pitcher of water, looks at it and says, "Look at that, a bug got in there from the time I walked out the door over to your table."  We laughed, but then she looked at us forever in awkward silence like she wanted us not to worry about it and pour the drinks.  Finally she says she'll go get a new one.  She comes back, pours the water without saying a word, and I ask her about the bacon braised brussel sprouts....her reply:  "they're just brussel sprouts cooked in bacon," and she walks away!

    The "select" beers at happy hour prices are a joke:  cans of Tecate, Yuengling, and Sly Fox Pilsner.  We opted to try the different house infused punches instead, which was also a bad idea.  They were incredibly weak, all ice, and not that tasty.  

    Just when we thought it couldn't get any worse, our $10 order of calamari with spicy ginger aoli came out.  There were literally no more than 12-15 small rings of calamari served with watery dipping sauce.  They were over battered with cornmeal, and were just horrible...possibly the worst I've ever had.

    Needless to say, we may have caught them on a bad evening, but I'm not willing to give this one a second shot, particularly with the overpriced menu.  Fare, Urban Saloon, Rembrandt's, and even London are far better places to start a Saturday night.

    Although I never wrote a review after my first experience here six months ago, my more recent dinner was so incredibly different and better that I felt compelled to comment.

    For Round 1, I ordered the standard burger, medium-rare. The accompanying fries and aioli were very good and my girlfriend enjoyed her mushroom bisque. The burger however, was nowhere close to medium-rare and gave me concerns about the safety of eating such a bloody thing. I persisted in light of this feeling, but to little satisfaction. The bun was already a bit soggy, the flavor of the burger seemed muted somehow. It wasn't a terrible experience, but with so many great food options in the city, my girlfriend and I didn't feel compelled to try again.

    Fast forward to this weekend. I'd been getting an itch to explore Fairmount food, and Hickory Lane jumped back into my head for whatever reason. I checked out the brunch menu and it was worded so nicely that I figured, "What the hell, I'll give this place another shot".

    VERY happy that I did. The key to these burgers is essentially to order up by one level when conveying how you want it cooked. You want it Medium-Rare? Ask for Medium, etc. Knowing this, I asked for medium and the burger came out a little on the low end of what I'd consider medium-rare (subjective, of course). This ended up being perfect. Again, the fries were great, and the burger was delicious. I believe the bun was challah, which the chef crisped perfectly.

    The decor of the restaurant is very relaxing in the afternoon and it has a wonderful view of the street and Penitentiary. Recommend.

    During a recent visit to Philadelphia, my friend suggested we go to Hickory Lane, and I'm so glad we did.  I'm told that they've been rated the "best burger in Philadelphia", but if that's all you're going for, you could be doing yourself a disservice.  The rest of their menu is quite good.  

    My friend, her fiance and I started with several shared appetizers, including the cheese plate, the wild mushroom bruschetta, and the watermelon panzanella salad.  The cheese plate was very nice - relatively well-curated with all of the delicious accompaniments you'd want, including fig spread, etc.  The watermelon panzanella salad was very good, although the watermelon wasn't quite sweet enough to balance the acid in the dish.  The real star was the bruschetta.  Delicious, sauteed mushrooms along with creamy chevre on crusty bread.  It was our favorite of the three.  

    My friend's fiance had the burger, which he really enjoyed.  My friend had the boulliabaise, which she did not finish.  The mussels overpowered the dish, though she did enjoy the striped bass it contained.  I had the sea scallops with pork belly and charred corn snow pea succotash.  It was an exceptional dish.  Each element complemented the other, the flavors melded perfectly.   The fatty, salty crisped pork belly paired with the sweet, tender scallops was delicious.  

    For dessert we split the s'mores lava cake.  The flavor was good, but the cake was a bit overdone.  Nothing lava-y about it.  

    I have to comment that I'm a bit amused about the complaints of stingy bread.  I don't see any problem with being served a single piece of bread;  that's not what I'm there for, really.  If you want all you can eat bread, go to Olive Garden.

    As a resident of Francisville I've had the chance to try this restaurant 4 times.  The menu is straight forward - not much to look at - but the execution is on point.  Every dish exceeded my expectations - especially the entrees.  

    The best part about this restaurant isn't the food, though.  The beer list, outdoor seating, and service are exceptional.  Whoever was in charge of the beer selection deserves a high-five and raise....but let me explain.  Now-a-days restaurants/bars seem to think that more selections = good selections. Wrong.  There is no reason to have 7 different chocolate stouts on your menu in the middle of July.  Conversely, I could careless about your refreshing list of fruit flavored whit beers in February.  They keep it simple, offering beers of eat type, local and not so local, with appropriate alcohol contents (no one likes beer with 14% ABV, it tastes like sh*t).  I'm a beer snob and this list is my favorite in the city (for sitting and drinking, not trying some weird beer the sake of it).

    The wait staff is knowledgeable and professional, I will leave it at that. No need to elaborate - they met my expectations each time i went.  

    Lastly, if i can recommend some dishes:  the roast chicken is beyond what you would expect from a token chicken dish, the hanger steak is seasoned perfectly without using too much salt (which wouldn't work well with the salted frittes), and the burger lives up to its reputation.  It might not be the best in the city, but it is definitely best burger north of market st.  No joke.

Specialties

Hickory Lane American Bistro's cuisine is founded in exceptional ingredients prepared with expert techniques by its chef, presenting a dining experience and atmosphere that is highly energized.  Hickory Lane offers dinner and brunch in a casually elegant space in the Art Museum/Fairmount neighborhood of Philadelphia, right next to the Eastern State Penitentiary.

Ideal for a quick bite, leisurely meal or an exceptional memory,  Hickory Lane's menu will change constantly to capture seasonal and exceptional ingredients.

Great food deserves great drink and Hickory Lane offers both with a short but well rounded wine list that is available by the glass, carafe or bottle. The beer list hovers around 15 selections from around the world with a focus on local craft beers. Spirits are the heart and soul of the bar at Hickory Lane. The spirits are selected for their wonderfully different qualities and their applications make fun and soulful interpretations of classic pre-prohibition cocktails.

History

Established in 2011.

Hickory Lane American Bistro's cuisine is founded in exceptional ingredients prepared with expert techniques by its Executive Chef, Albert Alvarado, formally of Green Eggs . Hickory Lane offers nightly dinner and weekend brunch in a casually elegant space in the Art Museum/Fairmount neighborhood of Philadelphia. Ideal for a quick bite, leisurely meal or an exceptional memory Hickory Lane's menu will change constantly to capture seasonal and exceptional ingredients.

Great food deserves great drink and now Hickory Lane offers both with a short but well rounded wine list that is available by the glass, carafe or bottle. The beer list always hovers around 15 selections with a focus on local craft beers. Spirits are the heart and soul of the bar at Hickory Lane. The spirits are selected for their wonderfully different qualities and their applications for making fun and soulful interpretations of classic pre-prohibition era cocktails like Martinis, Manhattans, Sazeracs and punches.
