    Mixto specializes in Latin American, Cuban, and Caribbean cuisine.  The name is ironic, as in the Tequila world, "Mixto" refers to a Tequila only partially made with Blue Agave, with other sugars making up the balance...essentially a lower-grade Tequila than 100% Agave.  

    Despite the name, Mixto is a nice place with two levels of seating indoors, and a little outdoor seating when the weather cooperates.  The atmosphere runs from casual to mildly upscale, and you could feel comfortable both in a sport jacket and dockers as well as shorts and a t-shirt.  Dark wood dominates both levels of the dining room, and there are also bars downstairs and upstairs.  

    The service is generally good, and the food is above average; there are a wide selection of cured meats, empanadas, ceviches, salads, and entrees.  The menu has a good range of proteins, so whether you're in the mood for pork, beef, poultry, seafood, or vegetarian options, you'll likely find something you'll enjoy.  The chicken soup is a standout, as is the bisque they sometimes have.  

    A nice place for happy hour, group dining, or a Friday night date, it's worth a try.

    I went here for Saturday brunch (my favorite meal). Locations was really nice and the decor was great. I was seated quickly and the server came right over. I ordered a cappuccino (a hold over obsession/habit from my Milan trip) and decided to look over the menu.

    Mixto is a pan-Latin restaurant so all of their meals are appropriately inspired. What I found cool is they basically listed brunches on a country basis. I found this pretty simple to pick from, but you can get basically whatever else you want. My pick was the Costa Rica plate, which included fried eggs, black beans and rice, avocado, plantains and grilled cheese.

    Meal came quickly, exactly as ordered. The rice was great and moist. The cheese had a slight smokey flavor that I liked a lot. Eggs and bacon were cooked as I wanted them. The overall meal was great, but slightly salty. I liked that, but anyone who has an issue with too much sodium might want to pick something else or choose this with caution.

    I'll 100% come back here again as it was one of the most reasonably priced and delicious brunch places I have gone to. I plan on stopping by for dinner in the next couple weeks to check out their entrees and appetizers. I imagine they will be equally as delicious.

    Mixto is a solid option for Latin American cuisine. My party began with peach sangria, not too sweet - really a good option for splitting the interests of ladies and gentlemen. The others then ordered an array of appetizers: guacamole with plantain chips, maduros (sweet plantains), and Puerto Rican style mofongo. For my main dish I ordered the Paella Vegeteriana.     I've been on the search for a well-made mojito for a while in Center City Philadelphia.  This is not the place, but they do make some large, sweet drinks with a fair amount of booze if that's what you're looking for.

    My husband and I went to Mixto early on a Saturday evening.  The downstairs bar was full, but there was plenty of room at the upstairs one.  After about fifteen minutes, the place became packed, so I guess we'd just missed the rush.  Make sure you go early, I would say before 7:00.  After that, if you don't have a reservation for a table, you'll find it hard to find a seat at one of the bars.

    The bartender was friendly and happy to answer my questions.  I ordered the tropical mojito.  They come in regular and large.  I figured I was going to have a couple of drinks anyway, and it's cheaper to get the large ($14) if you're going that route instead of two smalls ($9).  My drink was sweet and fruity, and not like a mojito at all.  The mint hadn't been muddled, but was just floating in there.  I prefer more sour, less sweet, and a lot of muddling.  It was a cocktail, but not a proper mojito.  I still enjoyed it, though it didn't satisfy my mojito craving.  My husband got a caipirinha and that was a little less sweet than mine.  

    Next I ordered a margarita because they had interesting flavors and I was curious about the coconut one.  Again, it was way sweeter than I like my margarita, though the size was huge and the coconut flavor was pleasant.  Also, they certainly didn't skimp on the booze on that one.  

    I wanted a snack and plantains are one of my favorite foods ever, so of course I had to order them when I saw them on the menu.  The tostones bruschetta is fried plantains topped with tomatoes, basil, and mozzarella cheese.  It was okay, but really small for the price. It was $9 and only contained three plantains with too much cheese. The plantain appetizer is much better at Bahama Breeze, so this was quite disappointing.  

    Overall this place is just okay.  I would come back here if a group of people I was with wanted to, but if I had my pick, there are better places in the area for both food and drinks.
