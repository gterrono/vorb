    One of the best Italian joints in Philly that I've been to so far - the only reason it lacks the full five stars is because the service was a bit mediocre. Despite being pretty busy, it is a pretty small space, especially with the open kitchen right by the dining area, so the server could have been way more attentive (once passing right by us when we were motioning him over). However, the food was pretty fantastic.

    We went a little over a week ago for the first day of restaurant week. The menu selections were pretty solid: for appetizer we tried the bruschetta trio (one with roasted peppers, one with butternut squash and the last with pears and prosciutto-the BEST pairing of salty and sweet) and the fontina stuffed meatballs (the grilled garlic bread topping was a delicious touch as you could soak up the last bits of tomato sauce after you finish the meatballs). For entrees, we went with the short rib ragu (tender, fall-apart meat on a bed of an abundance of ricotta gnocchi) and pan-seared diver scallops - perfect sear on the scallops and complemented with a rich mushroom and pea risotto. We capped the meal with the homemade pistachio gelato-pretty thick for gelato, it tasted more like ice cream but generously sprinkled with crunchy chunks of real pistachios- and the cheesecake covered with raspberry (I think?) jam (the jam made it a little too sweet but still overall very tasty).

    The ambiance was nice-very cozy and slightly dim lighting but the noise level was a little too loud (couldn't hear across the small table/had to shout a bit at times). The BYOB was probably the best component but we weren't smart enough to pick up a bottle before liquor stores closed (at 5 PM on Sundays!! oops) so we were literally the only ones without a bottle of wine... Otherwise, it was funny watching people confusedly trying to figure out how to eat the whole grilled artichoke appetizer and would definitely go back to try other items on the bigger tasting menu.

    I went here last week with my fiance and another couple. I have been meaning to try it for a while. We went on a Saturday during restaurant week, and it was nice that Mercato honored the Restaurant Week deal and we were able to get an app, entree, and dessert for $35 - definitely worth it! I got the bruschetta appetizer, which came with three different types of bruschetta. It was very tasty. The star of the meal though was my entree, which was the special of the night - a crispy skinned striped bass. The flavors used to cook the fish (capers, olives, olive oil) were absolutely delicious. The fish was cooked to perfection. I had a nice berry sorbet to finish. Everyone else's food was also delicious. We all went home happy and full.

    The only downside to Mercato is it's size. It is definitely "cozy" and if you don't like eating right next to the people next to you, then this is not the place for you. There is not a single table in this restaurant that is even remotely private. Mercato is also BYOB, which the other reviewers have mentioned. I'd say this would also be a good place to try in the summer or if the weather is warm enough to wait outside because there is absolutely no waiting room and I felt bad for any of the diners who had tables near the door because they had people with big bulky coats basically on top of them while they were trying to eat.

    All in all though, one of Philadelphia's best italian BYOBs!

    First, the negatives.  I can see where the room could get pretty noisy, and a number of the reviews below refer to an uncomfortable noise level.  Since it was an early summer weeknight evening, the windows were open, and the restaurant wasn't too crowded, noise wasn't and issue.  The tables inside are really close together, which made the room feel a bit cramped, as well.  There are also quite a number of tables outside, which I think would be an ideal seating location on a summer evening, to avoid the possibility of noise in the dining room.

    But the worst negative of the meal was the indifferent attitude of our server, who never broke a smile or made us feel welcome. She was very business like but there was an edge about her manner that made us feel uncomfortable almost as if we had offended her in some way. I noticed that she acted the same way with other patrons, so I don't think it was anything we did.  Perhaps she just doesn't really like her job, or people in general, or maybe feels that serving others is beneath her.  Whatever the issue, it really kept the whole dining experience from the level of satisfaction it should have.

    On to the positives. I totally loved that the crusty italian bread which was served with a compound butter flavored with herbs and chopped olives. That butter was addictively creamy, delicious and savory. My salad, a mixture of watercress, pistachio nuts and house cured bacon, was a real taste treat as well. Plus, you didn't have to look for the bacon, which came in several thick strips and made the salad actually quite filling as a first course, and could be enjoyed as a shared item if you are light eater.

    For the mains, I would recommend the pasta dishes which were creative, reasonably priced, and came in very filling portions. Unlike many of the "small plates" restaurants that seem so trendy today, Mercato served a real main dish portion and I appreciated that!

    Overall, I would say that I enjoyed the food so much that I look forward to going back to Mercato, despite issues with the cramped room and service.

    So many restaurants these days provide "tapas", i.e., tiny portions where everyone looks to others for the last serving. This place is large portions of solidly good, interestingly presented, well-sauced memorable creations, including the daily fish offering. No "molecular" cuisine; no "way too many ingredients to really work" dishes. Just a few well-chosen ingredients in each course that harmonize into a satisfying whole dish.

    As these things go, the prix fixe menu is a real deal, too. Even the made-on-premises gelatos were memorable.

    This is the "whole deal": good and inspired from start to finish. Great pasta dishes. Interesting appetizers. Satisfying mains with nice ingredients shining through.

    Pleasant though a little loose service, but, it's the food that I am really looking for to please at such places, so....I can deal with it. It would be nice if the wineglasses they offer at this BYOB were as good as the wines the cuisine warrants; they were pretty bad, and no stems, just small glasses. And, the place is seemingly otherwise "no frills".
