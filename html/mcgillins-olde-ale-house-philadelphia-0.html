    It's cold outside, and there is no better place to warm yourself than McGillins.  Philadelphia's oldest bar is one of my personal favorites in the city.  The decor is all dark, stained wood and the walls and ceiling are always seasonally decorated.  There is a great, real, working fire place which makes McGillins an awesome place to day drink during a snow storm (they also have beer specials for the first snow storm over 2 inches).  With 22 beers on tap and $5 pitcher specials almost every night of the week, McGillins is both affordable and popular for everyone.

I understand they want to go home after a night of work, but I have been tipping all night so don' t make me feel like you can't wait for me to leave. And finally, the last time I went to Mcgillins it was "dude-rific" with probably only a handful of the female species...not a big deal, just worth noting.

    Listed in The Places I Go Out

    McGillin's is a staple night spot, especially on Wednesday's for me.  The place has been open for 147 years, so they've had time to learn how to be one of the best pubs out there.

    As previously mentioned, McGillin's still has its old, rustic charm.  Virtually everything in the bar is old, lacquered wood that holds the historic feel.  The ceilings are low, the lighting dim, and the real wood fireplace raging in the winter.

    Located in the newly named Midtown Village (shut up, it's the Gayborhood), McGillin's is surrounded by great shopping, eating and drinking.  It's easily accessible from just about anywhere, and I've never had an issue with finding a place to park within 4 blocks.

    McGillin's most redeeming quality is its nightly specials.  There's one for every night of the week, save Sunday.  The special is pretty fantastic every night, but as I mentioned, my favorite night is Wednesday.

    In fact, my friends and I were there last night partaking in every aspect of the special:
    $5 Pitchers: Bud Light, Pabst Blue Ribbon -- I had myself a lovely pitcher of PBR.
    $2 Pint - Featured Micro Brew -- A friend sampled this and wasn't disappointed.
    $1 for two tacos -- OHMUHGAWD four delicious tacos for $2.
    Live Karaoke @ 9 pm -- I sang both "Hey Jealousy" - Gin Blossoms and "Your Love" - The Outfield.

    Basically, what I'm saying is that I have a crush on McGillin's.

    PS - There's a parking garage with green awnings around the corner from Macy's on 13th and Chestnut.  I'd be wary of parking there, because last night we went to get my friend's car at 1am, and the place was totally locked up.  Not cool!

Specialties

Philadelphia's Oldest Pub, featuring 29 beers on draft focusing on local, fresh micro brews.  A pub menu loaded with homemade items, everything from our famous Nachos and French Onion Soup to the Irish Mixed Grill and Cape May Stew.

McGillin's is considered the BEST place for karaoke in the city - Sundays and Wednesdays are hopping with DJ Joe at the mic.

History

Established in 1860.

Philly's Oldest Pub!

Meet the Business Owner

Chris M. Business Owner

Christopher Mullins, Jr. runs McGillin's Olde Ale House with his parents, Mary Ellen & Chris Mullins, Sr. They purchased it from Mary Ellen's father & uncle (Christopher's grandfather & great-uncle). They're only the second family to run the historic tavern in its 150+ year history. McGillin's is the oldest continuously operating tavern in Philadelphia and one of the oldest in the country. Christopher believes that beer is the eternal equalizer - friends have been made, marriages begun, wars ended. If we can ignite camaraderie and friendship than we have done our job!
