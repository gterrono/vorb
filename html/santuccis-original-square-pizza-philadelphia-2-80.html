    I'm a purist.  I like to open a pizza box and see the oozy, cheesy goodness that awaits me, so I don't eat the pizza at Santucci's (please don't throw things at me for saying this).  

    And yes, despite the above statement, I'm still giving Santucci's a 4 star review.  We ordered from here recently and I got the pepperoni stromboli (I'm a sucker for a stromboli) and wings.  They were both excellent.  The stromboli tasted like they took garlic bread and rolled pepperoni and cheese inside of that and baked it.  They served it with a very generous side of sauce for dipping.  I ate the entire thing and I'm not ashamed to say it.  The wings were crispy, even after delivery, and covered in sauce which looked like it had blue cheese mixed into it, a very nice little surprise.  

    They have a large variety of items on their menu and I know that I'll always find something delicious to eat instead of pizza.

    New Jersey native with an affinity for all things New York, especially food (keyword - pizza). When I first heard of this rectangular (or square, whichever you prefer) pizza, my skepticism meter went off the charts. "Yeah right, box-shaped pizza that's not a Sicilian pie?"

    I couldn't have been more wrong.

    Since then (which was years ago), Santucci's has been my favorite place in the city for takeout/delivery pizza, and I was like a fat kid in a candy store when I heard this location was opening on Christian. Great sauce, crispy yet doughy crust and snappy delivery. Wings ain't bad either, but the pizza is my main obsession here.

    I recently dined in with my fiancee for the first time and found the experience overwhelmingly satisfying, despite choosing a crowded Friday night. We still snagged a cozy table without a wait, popped a couple nice bottles of craft beer and enjoyed a white pie with spinach and mushrooms.

    The prices are a little high (think $20ish for a large with one topping delivered, including tip), but the fact that I continue to pay them speaks volumes.

    Protips - cash only, BYOB, save two slices for lunch the next day.
