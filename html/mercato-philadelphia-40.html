    One of the best meals I've had in a long long time - mercato sits on a quite corner, very discreet, but full of great food, good drink, and overall great atmosphere.

    Even though they don't take reservations, I found that leaving your phone number was just as easy and efficient - just leave it with the hostess (who is super nice by the way), and walk about four blocks to a wine shop and buy a few bottles of wine to bring for dinner. Like people have said, when its BYO, you'll always be a winner.

    The restaurant itself is so discreet we almost missed it. The setting is small, full of bustling people, conversation a plenty, and a pretty open kitchen. The menu is full of delicious italian fare - and you won't be disappointed.

    We had the beef bolognese and the diver sea scallops with risotto, coupled with a side order of the brussel sprouts with smoked bacon. The brussel sprouts were unfortunately too smokey and too vinegary for our taste - and even though this is a negative for me, the fact that our server noticed it and without even asking, he took it off the bill (we were fine with paying for it regardless), which is a great touch of service.

    The diver sea scallops were perfectly seared, moist in the center, seasoned well with a little bit of kick, and the risotto was perfect and with fresh vegetables.

    The highlight of my night though, was the beef bolognese. Creamy sauce with delightful tender juicy beef pieces, mixed with greens into homemade thick and melt in your mouth pasta, topped with shaved cheese.....just phenomenal. The sauce was not too salty, not too heavy...just perfect. I found myself looking forward to each subsequent bite. The beef itself felt like they slow cooked it until it was fork tender melt in your mouth fall off the bone beef. And the pasta....obvious quality and hand made goodness, with the right amount of satisfaction without weighing you down.

    The best part? The price you pay for this quality is perfectly decent (about $20-$25 for each dish).

    You CANNOT go wrong with Mercato. Just come here and be ready to taste some of the best Philly-style Italian food you'll ever have. Olive Garden, eat your heart out.

    I love Italian places like this. Little one-room spaces with wood floors, lots of tables, lots of people and an open kitchen. Only thing it was missing was a chalkboard menu but naturally, I was pretty pleased when I stepped in here on a bustling Monday night (every table was taken).

    If the olive oil menu didn't already, nice silverware and glassware lets you know that Mercato is more ambitious than your typical Italian BYO. The menu represents a good range, from typical antipasti to a half-dozen pastas, to fish, veal and meats.

    To start, we split the pumpkin fettucine. Cooked in brown sage butter with a liberal helping of thin, crispy pancetta sprinkled in, this was one of the richer -- and most delicious -- pasta dishes I can remember. The noodles were paper-thin and al dente, although they strangely clumped up in the middle of the bowl, almost like a Jewish noodle kugel. No matter.

    Mains were arctic char, served on a bed of risotto with two large shrimp and some asparagus, and a cioppino. The arctic char was pretty good, as was the risotto, although I like the latter creamier and silkier than the way Mercato makes theirs. The cioppino was excellent (although having never had cioppino before, I have no standard for comparison), with a nice little spicy kick to the broth and all the seafood cooked perfectly.

    Dessert was a tiramisu, which I surmised was store-bought. Still it was pretty good, though I can't say the same for the double espresso I had, which was the typical burnt, no-crema type that too many restaurants serve.

    For $120 after tax and tip, we got a lot of great-tasting food (the portions are very big here) in a great atmosphere. Can't do much better than that. This place deserves 4.5 stars, but I can't bring myself to give it 5.
