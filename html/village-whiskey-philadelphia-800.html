    Be prepared to wait. This place is tiny (four or five tables, plus the bar), but totally worth it! We had a couple cocktails while waiting for our table (on a Monday night), and they were delicious. I especially recommend the Ginger Rogers. Amazing. I don't normally go for fruity, but this was light and balanced and not too sweet. I would have had them all night if they hadn't run out of some key ingredient after my first one.

    Once we were seated at our table, we went heavy on the appetizers: the house-made cheese puffs (gougères), pickled truffled cauliflower, pickled seasonal veggies (pumpkin, kale, and onion), and duck fat French fries with cheese sauce. Good grief. I'm not very picky when it comes to pickles--soak something in vinegar and I want to eat it, but these were ridiculous. We refrained from drinking the leftover pickling liquid when they were gone.  If you are going to order one thing here, make it the truffled cauliflower...but get two of them. The cheese puffs didn't do much for me, but the fries were delicious--thick, crisp on the outside, tender on the inside, and so savory.

    By the time my Thanksgiving burger came (the seasonal special with Brussels sprout/bacon slaw, a stuffing bun, and cranberry sauce), I was pretty well full. Then I tasted the burger. And came impressively close to finishing it. Definitely an all-time top 10 burger...maybe even top five.

    I'd love to come back and dive into their impressive whiskey/Scotch menu...next time.

    Last night, I finally had the chance to dine at Village Whiskey.  It was a Wednesday night around 7pm.  The young hostess had told me that there would be a 45 minute wait for our table of 2 since it was their dinner rush hour.  We were welcomed to dine at the bar, so we decided to venture on in to try our luck.  The room had a beautiful high ceiling, but width wise, it was pretty tight.  Booths to the left and the bar to the right.  

    As we got to the end of the bar, we discovered that there was only one vacant bar stool.  My boyfriend and I looked at each other and debated on whether or not to stay but since this place had so much hype, we decided to go for a walk to kill time.  Just as we were about to turn and leave, one of the servers asked if we wanted to purchase some bar food or drinks while we waited for our table.  He tried to convince us that he would be gladly to move our food and tab to the table as soon as it was open.  

    Neither one of us really wanted to fill up on bar food when we came for dinner, so we politely declined and said we'll be back.  I could tell the server didn't like that answer as he turned and walked away.

    Getting to the point:
    The wait was a little shy of 45 minutes.  36 minutes to be exact since they sent me a text once I placed my name down and I received the 2nd text 36 minutes later.  So, they did keep their word with getting me a table within that time frame.

    The menu was incredibly small.  Only 3 types of burgers and a few other things on the side.  My boyfriend and I ordered the Village Burger since nothing else seemed to catch our attention.

    Both burgers were..."okay".  Nothing truly out of this world that would make my feet kick back and forth like a little kid with a sundae.  Speaking of sundae, my boyfriend ordered the S'mores Milkshake and THAT made my feet kick!  The presentation itself caused the couple across from us to stop mid conversation and stare at the sweet tall glass of deliciousness until their eyes met mine with a bit of envy.

    My boyfriend allowed me to try his milkshake, and yes, I *HAD* to order my own.

    Back to the meal at hand, we also had the Duck Fat Fries with Fox sauce and that was also "okay".  

    For the money that was spent at Village Whiskey, I would've been much more content with getting a burger across the street at the Shake Shack.  Don't get me wrong, the food was good for just about anyone, but "okay" for my standards.  I expected more than an average tasting burger for $12.  Would I come back?  Probably not.  

    However, I would definitely recommend this place as a first date because of its tight space, simple meal, and AWESOME S'mores milkshake.  From the looks of the drink menu, I think it would definitely be fit for a social lubricant on anyone's first date.

Specialties

A ground-to-order Angus Beef Burger, a Veggie Burger, a Bison Burger or an Vienna All-Beef Hot Dog with all the trimmings are each served alongside duck fat French fries and Sly Fox cheddar sauce.  Burgers are topped with Village Whiskey's house-made thousand-island dressing, Bibb lettuce, beefsteak tomato and a potato-beer bun, with optional toppings including bacon, caramelized onion, truffles, artichokes and a selection of cheeses.

As one of the most comprehensive collections of its namesake spirit available in Philadelphia, Village Whiskey features a veritable library of 80 to 100 varieties of whiskey, bourbon, rye and scotch from Scotland, Canada, Ireland, United States and even a few Japanese options.

History

Established in 2009.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
