    If you go on the weekends, you might experience up to an hour and a half wait. Hopefully it's not in the rain... like mine was. There's little shelter outside and no waiting room inside besides a small bench to the side.

    That being said, it was worth the soaking wet socks and shirt. Hell, Honey's would have been worth getting a cold from exposure to the elements.

    In the past, I only went to Honey's for their dessert. Their warm Jewish apple cake served with a side of vanilla ice cream and caramel drizzle is to die far. My mouth is watering just thinking about it.

    After seeing their menu for brunch and the list of specials for the week I decided to visit one Saturday with my boyfriend. When we finally were seated at the counter, I was disappointed to discover there was no homemade grapefruit juice but the orange juice was an excellent substitution.

    I ordered the chicken and waffles while my boyfriend ordered his staple breakfast burrito. Upon first glance I was super confused. No syrup, a roasted chicken leg over cornbread waffles with a combination of avocado and tomato salsa on top? I had a feeling I was in for disappointment. Apprehensively, I took the first bite of chicken and waffle.

    Holy crap. All I could wonder was how the hell they did this!

    The chicken was the juiciest most succulent meat I've ever tasted. The dark meat was roasted to perfection and literally was falling off the bone. The cornbread waffle was flaky and full of flavor reminiscent of my Southern Grammy's cornbread muffins. The avocado tomato paste was surprisingly a perfect complement to the spice rub on the chicken leg and paired with the waffle perfectly. Syrup would have been an abomination to that meal.

    I begrudgingly shared my dish with my boyfriend who quickly lamented not ordering the same meal. I'm the queen of taking home leftovers but not on that day. I would have licked the damn plate if I wasn't in public.

    I left Honey's full and extremely excited to go back and try the next dish on the menu.

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge - NYC Edition

    On one of my few trips to Philly, I decided that I really wanted to get some solid breakfast/lunch food. What better way to find some good food, then to search for it on Yelp?! Anyhow, with some searching and filtering, I ended up deciding on Honey's Sit-N-Eat. This spot is located on the north end of Philly, so with any part of downtown, you'll have to find street parking. Come here around that 11-11:30 timeframe, and you shouldn't have too much trouble finding a spot. Plus, you'll skip the eventual line that will form as it gets closer to noon-time.

    For breakfast, I decided to go with the 3-egg omelet. I loaded this bad boy up with: ham, bell peppers, and bacon. On the side, it was complemented with some home fries and a corn muffin. At $10, this is quite the deal. For an omelet, this had some real fresh tasting ingredients. The eggs tasted like real eggs, which is always a plus, and the bacon/ham actually meshed well with the bell peppers. The coffee here isn't bad either. If I can remember correctly, they serve up La Colombe coffee here at Honey's, so at least you know you're getting real coffee instead of colored water.

    If you're coming here for lunch, they Honey's has tons of sandwiches and platters such as country fried steak for you to munch on. For the ones with a sweet tooth, they have a bunch of desserts like challah, that are made in house.

    All this combined together, makes it a hipster's dream. And yes, there are tons of hipsters that come here.

    Before coming here, make sure you have some cash because they do not take credit cards. This place is sure worth the trip though. The food here is solid, the service is pretty good, and it's at a very reasonable price. No wonder there's always a line out the door.
