    When I moved to center city from Pennsport, I had the unfortunate task of finding a closer replacement for my favorite sandwich in the entire world -- John's Roast Pork (mushroom steak with onions and sharp).  What an impossible task!  But I kept my chin up and did the best I could.

    Eventually I came to Sonny's for a cheesesteak.  I thought I had a winner, and I in fact returned multiple times.  There's decent but unbecoming indoor seating with TV's and alright music playing.  Or you can even sit outside in the summer.

    But it was not to last.  Sonny's just didn't stick.

    Why?  Well, the steak is fine enough... but I found its quality to be inconsistent.  Depending on who was manning the grill -- be it either the white person propped up front or one of the Spanish workers lurking behind -- the taste and construction of the sandwich fluctuated because you never could tell who would be cooking that day.  One day it would be exquisite; another day it was kind of lackluster.  This time would have an abundance of meat; the next time the sandwich would be skimpy.

    The fries, however, are delicious.  I do suggest them.

    Another thing that got kind of annoying after awhile was the inundation of tourists.  Every time I was there... the place would be packed with tourists.  Sonny's must be in every travel guide available.  Or, they must tip out every hotel front desk in Old City.  Seriously...

    Plus -- their witty but ironic and curt "South Philly" slogans are gimmicky at best.  And the counter workers have their own spiel down designed to make tourists giggle which includes terms like "authentic original Philly cheesesteak experience."  

    But what really turned me off is you have to pay something like $0.75 to $1.00 for a refill of your soda.  Come on, guys...

    I would certainly go back... but you've yet to convert me, Sonny's.

    TIP:
    Go during midday and get the lunch special, which includes a steak, fries, and soda for around $10.  It's their best deal.

    Listed in East of Broad, Let's Get Large, Yelp Approved!, Cheap Lunch, I love meat

    Ok, here goes. My favorite cheesesteak in Philly? Yeah. Sonny's.

    Contrary to popular (tourist) belief, a typical Philadelphian's breakfast, lunch, and dinner is -not- comprised of a cheesesteak. If it were, however, I suppose Sonny's is where I'd go. Not Pat's, not Geno's, not Jim's, but Sonny's.

    While the aformentioned hot spots cater to visitors, Sonny's feels a bit more local and genuine. There's no colloquially forced ordering procedure ("wit," "witout," "whiz" - I'm pretty sure Pat's/Geno's/Jim's does that to suggest local flavor) and they offer more sophisticated toppings. After all, a cheesesteak is just like any other sandwich, one that's not limited to just onions and cheese.

    So if you're in the neighborhood, hopping off your Olde City carriage ride or walking out of the Constitution Center, Sonny's is a sure bet. You won't find a line trailing around the corner and you won't find a bunch of tourists reciting the proper way to order their cheesesteak in line. No pretension, no bull, just good meat and cheese sandwiches.

    Holla.

    Sonny's is in a huge tourist trap section of the city, but we send the tourists down to 9th and Passyunk so it's easy for East Market Street workers like me to make a quick stop for lunch.  Here is my concise argument for why Sonny's is better than Pat's or Geno's or most other spots.  

    1.) The roll is great, soft but just crusty enough, and smaller than the others.
    2.) Just so we're clear, I like my cheesesteak with whiz and fried onions.... Pat's and Geno's are assembly lines, that crank hundred and even thousands of cheesesteaks a day,  they slop the whiz on and slop inconsistent amounts of meat, sometimes this works out in your favor, and sometimes its a huge mess.
    3.) Lines, ALWAYS a line down there, Sonny's, I can walk to from work and have a minimal wait.
    4.) Whenever I got to Pat's I feel like I need like 20 showers to get rid of the grease, Sonny's, not as messy, or greasy....
    5.) Because it's less messy, it's less of a guilt trip, and as a 28 year old woman with hopes for keeping my figure while I can, it allows me to keep a cheesesteak on my semi-regular lunch rotation without tremendous amounts of guilt and extra trips to the gym.
    6.) Climate Controlled (indoors) I mean no one feels like a cheesesteak on a 100 degree day and I've also been after a birds game in 0 degree weather, neither are awesome. Sonny's is inside!
    7.) Sonny's is cheaper, it's like a $7 cheesesteak vs a $9-11 cheesesteak (i can't even remember what it is at this point)

    Please do not get me wrong, I LOVE Pats and will eat most any cheesesteak...and the whole experience is really the best part of it, But if you eat cheesesteaks with any consistency and want to avoid the crowds and excess mess, Sonny's is the real deal.

    That is all!

History

Established in 2000.

Sonny's Famous Steaks was created to bring the authentic Philadelphia Cheesesteak experience to the heart of Old City.  Using Fresh-Sliced Domestic Ribeye and Locally-Baked Italian Long Rolls, Sonny's Famous Steaks brings you the finest quality Cheesesteak Philly has to offer, without the hassle of long outdoor lines and surly, intimidating staff.
