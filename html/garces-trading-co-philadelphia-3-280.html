    This is one of my favorite places to have a business lunch - the food is always amazing, the pairings unique and wonderful and the service is awesome.  Sometimes I order from the menu and others we do the chef's selection - and never disappointed.  The sandwiches are fresh, on unbelievably good bread . . . the Cubano Clasico is a great take on the traditional Cuban sandwich.  The Funghi Pizza is a wonderful blend of mushrooms and truffles, on a thin, crispy crust.  As I said, the service is awesome - it's like watching a well-oiled machine at work.

    The one thing that is keeping me from going to the 5th star is our Valentine's night at the restaurant with their two tasting menus.  There were two choices - the $45 or $65 tasting menu.  We chose the $65 menu.  The complimentary oysters at the start may have actually been the taste bright spot on the menu - and they weren't even on the menu!  Everything else was just in our opinion not up to the usual Garces standards.  The chicken dish was bland, with so little flavor or personality.  It was up to the standards of MY kitchen, but not up to the standards of what I've come to expect from Garces.  The lamb pasta dish was flavorful, but lacked much indication of the star ingredient - lamb.  The Funghi Pizza, long one of my favorites, was way too charred on the bottom, giving the pizza an unneeded bitterness that didn't compliment it at all.  The flank steak was really plain-jane . . . and surprisingly a little on the tough side.  The dessert - a neopolitan-style ice cream with a layer of lime, dark chocolate and caramel ice creams was tasty, the combination of the flavors reminded us of why we like Chef Garces - and this was a nice close to the evening.

    A quick word about the service last night - David, our waiter, was at the top of his game.  Friendly, helpful, and - in the style of the perfect server - always there and never there - in other words, he was always there when needed, everything was done in a timely and coordinated way, but he was never intrusive in any way.  Outstanding service - probably the best of all my visits to Garces restaurants.

    Maybe re-think the tasting menus next year for Valentine's Day - this one missed the mark for us.  But, still - it was a nice dining experience and we continue to be big fans of Chef Garces!

    So far this is the 3rd Jose Garces restaurant I've tried and although it wasn't my favorite out of the three (amada and tinto create some steep competition for any restaurant) it was a solid meal. In regards to the service, the hostess who greeted us was very sweet and efficient, seating us quickly. However the waitress left a little more to be desired. She was nice enough, but very slow when it came to taking our orders (it was an early lunch and there were only a few tables occupied) and could have been better versed on the menu, as she had a difficult time explaining what things were when we had questions.

    My mom and I did the prix fixe lunch menu for $25. We got the margherita pizza and the panini di pollo, which were both really delicious. For our sides i had to try the duck fat fries (fabulously crispy and seasoned nicely) and the gin bacon (because I didn't know what else to get as a side and the waitress recommended it). I know a lot of people rave about pork belly and how it's to die for, but I think when it comes to bacon, it should be crispy. I don't like a lot of gristle. The gin bacon side was 95% white fatty meat and 5% what I consider the good part of the bacon. I wasn't a fan.

    The deserts, which are also included in the $25  set menu were the best part of the meal. I chose the chocolat, which is a soft chocolate shell with pretzels, peanut butter, and more chocolate. If you are a huge fan of chocolate and peanut butter (come on who doesn't love reeses peanut butter cups?) with a little crunch, this wqwqdesert is calling your name. My mom ordered the lemon tart which was equally delicious to the chocolat, but in its own way. Think lemon meringue on top of a crispy lemon cookie.

    One little weird thing was that I though the prix fixe menu included coffee (I'm pretty sure the menu says it on it), but our waitress charged us for it. I was kind of confused on whether or not it was included and didn't have the menu in front of me, so I didn't say anything.

    Overall, Garces Trading company is a nice place for a more upscale luncheon close to the theatre district. Also, I've heard their little wine store is reasonably priced, so I'll have to come back in at least to check that out.

    Our server opened our bottle of red and then pointed us towards the olive oil and balsamic "bar" where we could fill tiny cups and bring them back to our table to accompany the delicious sourdough we were already munching on.  Each oil had a description of its origin, when and where it was pressed, and the different notes you should taste.  My favorite was the California olive oil with strong notes of pepper at the end.

    Our second course, not on the menu, was a cold, picked trout escabeche with a hot, roasted red pepper soup poured on top, for presentation's sake and to preserve the coldness of the escabeche for the diner so that it would contrast appropriately with the warmth and slight spice of the soup.  This dish was also a big success.

    Somewhere in there, we also received [my favorite] the funghi pizza which is more of a flatbread.  How could maitakes, black truffle, and royal trumpets, ever disappoint when baked over taleggio cheese? - and I was finally able to identify the mystery topping not on the menu: lemon peel, practically invisible but you can smell and taste it.

    For the main course, we were given two entrees - arguably the juiciest, sliced pork I have ever consumed over diced celery root, served in its own juices and a goat bolognese.  The bits of goat were incredibly tender; the pasta clearly homemade and just so savory and comforting .  Both dishes were out of this world.  I was much less a fan of the sides - roasted brussel sprouts which needed more jazzing up despite the presence of bacon in the dish and small, round potatoes with a tangy red sauce we didn't realize was there until we reached the bottom of the bowl (oops).

    All in all, this experience was exceptional - spot on food that surprised and delighted and impeccable, friendly service.  Make a reservation via OpenTable to get some points and fill your belly with Garces goodness.

Specialties

Garces Trading Company is a full-service bar and restaurant focused on Iron Chef Jose Garces' own favorite dishes from his travels to cafes throughout Western Europe, with a focus on Italy, Spain and France.  Our thoughtful menu of cafe staples is served throughout the day, and our bar offers a wealth of cocktails, wines and beers that pair beautifully with our menu.  Our on-site PLCB Wine Boutique, the first of its kind, makes a great resource either before sitting down to dinner (corkage fees apply) or to complement one of our decadent gift baskets, available in our newly redesigned retail section.

History

Established in 2010.

Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a refined, Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, a contemporary American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago, IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale, AZ and Palm Springs, CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the landmark Revel Resort. Today, in addition to our much-lauded restaurants around the country, Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
