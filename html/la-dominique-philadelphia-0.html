    Listed in My Neighborhood, Food trucks

    I have not eaten here often, because the lines usually scare me away, but I really should do it more often. La Dominique, a crepe lunch truck run by Zbigniew Chojnacki, is frankly delicious. And its proprietor, who is also an artist, makes the crepes into little works of art themselves. The first time I had ever ordered anything here, I was having a bad day, and the apple cinnamon crepe on the menu (which changes seasonally--I didn't notice this one today) seemed to fit the bill to cheer anyone up.

    Watching him make the crepes is almost as fun as eating them. Every step has its due: he slices apples to order, arranges them in an attractive fan shape on the warm, buttery crepe, which is then laced with swirls of caramel sauce, pralines, whipped crime, more pralines... suffice to say this was a rather divine experience.

    There are also savory crepes on the menu, with a daily-changing special. For example, today's was a veal stew. There is a smoked salmon breakfast crepe that I am dying to try one day, as well as a coconut chicken veggie crepe, served with steamed rice. Today, I ordered the pepperoni pizza veggie crepe (which may be slightly blasphemous to both pizza and crepes, but was reassuringly tasty, with pepperoni, melted mozzarella, and copious amounts of thinly sliced onions). There are a number of different combinations to choose from, all of them wrapped in that light, airy, signature crepe. I also noticed the girl in line ahead of me order some pierogies which looked incredibly tasty, served with onions and dollops of sour cream--I might have to make another trip back for that.

    The only downside that I can think of is that it's very slow on school days at lunch time, and the lines can be intimidatingly long, but it's always worth the wait.

    Listed in Philly Favorites

    I'd like to think I know a thing or two about crepes. I've tried them in the crepe motherland (France) and am always interested in trying new crepe places because let's face it, they're one of the most delicious things you can eat on this planet, if done right.

    Houston Hall has passable crepes, but given the choice in University City, food trucks are where it's at for crepes. Who would have guessed? A few of my coworkers are obsessed with the Crepewalk crepe truck on spruce st near 36th (yelp.com/biz/crepes-and-…) and I dig their blackened chicken crepe as well as the grilled chicken crepe there. But one day a third coworker of mine claimed that there was yet another, better crepe truck. Enter the contender, La Dominique.

    With two crepe carts vying for "best crepe around", we all knew there was only one way to settle this, and that was a crepe off. One wednesday lunch, 6 crepes were purchased... 3 Mexican crepes from La Dominique and 3 blackened chicken crepes from Crepewalk. Since my coworkers were all somewhat biased to favor their favorite crepe cart, I came in to serve as the most impartial of the crepe testers (best job ever).

    Although I hesitated to really choose one crepe definitively over another (they both really are very good!), clearly my stomach and my actions spoke louder, since I only took bites of the blackened chicken, saving most of my appetite for the edible work of art that is the Mexican crepe. Somewhat sweet but savory, like a taco in a crepe with fresh veggies and herbs on top. It was pretty divine. Maybe it was just the novelty of this Mexican crepe compared to the blackened chicken that I had had before that made it so delicious... but I don't think that was the only reason why I liked it so much. It's just a damn good crepe! It's only too bad that it's almost double the distance from my workplace compared to the other carts, and lines can get pretty insane unless you bypass prime lunch times and go early or late. All I can say is, if you want an awesome crepe... check out this cart, get the Mexican crepe and watch the artist chef in action. You won't be disappointed!

    Tip - this is NOT your normal food truck!

    Ambiance:  So no shock here, this is a food truck and thus there is little to no ambiance.  It is a quaint little spot with no other trucks around it.  The owner has proudly posted an article about him and his truck on the front window.  Surprisingly quiet block.

    Service:  So you queue up in line and wait for him to finish making the prior order and take your order (one at a time!)  So stand around or lean on the railing and be prepared to let newcomers know you haven't ordered and they have to fall in line.  Waiting around isn't a problem as long as you have the time and weather conditions permit.  It is accurate to say that the owner is precise, almost methodical, with his technique.  DO NOT COME HERE IF YOU ARE IN A RUSH.  Because 1 - he will not rush for you and 2 - you want him to take the time on your meal because it will be all the better for it!  A quiet mannered man who runs the cart with a strong sense of rules.

    Food:  We tried the breakfast crepe and the tiramisu crepe.  After taking my first bite I thought to myself, "seriously?"  I could not believe that a $6 crepe would have such quality ingredients and well execution.  I almost felt bad that he spent so much time and care making the perfect crepe for little old me.  The quality of ingredients is great, the care put into construction is great, the composition of flavors is great.  This isn't some slap dash crepe, this has been carefully thought out to maximize your eating pleasure.  That being said, it is still a crepe, so it is accessible to a wide spectrum of eaters.

    Overall:  I would happily come again because I know it will be delicious.

    We had heard of this food truck years ago, and when we finally moved to Philadelphia recently, went looking for it during a walk around University City.  But I was looking in the wrong area - around 38th-40th.  I didn't find his truck, and thought "ah well, maybe he went back to sculpting full-time".  

    So imagine my delight when my husband and I randomly passed his truck on the street, during another U. City stroll more east of where we'd looked before, on the Drexel campus (side note- my husband spent a year as a Drexel student a long time ago, and was looking for a particular block that no longer exists, as in it's been covered over by buildings.)  

    We ordered a veggie crepe and a pear crepe.  Veggie crepe - really a lot of food for $6.  And a nice diversity of vegetables - zucchini, broccoli, spinach, red peppers.  It was tasty comfort food, to me.  It tasted a lot like when my husband and I buy a bunch of vegetables from a farmer's market and stir-fry it all together to put over rice, for a healthy, quick dinner.  I think we both agreed that it wasn't mind-blowing, but very tasty and satisfying.

    The pear crepe, however, was nothing short of mind-blowing.  My husband thought it was a tad sweet overall, but I didn't agree, though I could see how it might be a litlte hard to finish if you were eating it on your own.  All of the ingredients just worked together so well.  The pear provided a tartness and acidity that counteracted the sweetness of the chocolate and the cream.  The almonds provided texture that counteracted the softness of the other ingredients.  And he doesn't skimp on ingredients - everything was very fresh, and enjoyable, taken individually.  

    The crepes themselves are expertly prepared.  I foresee myself wandering down to the Drexel campus more often, to try the other ones.
