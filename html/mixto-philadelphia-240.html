    This is my go-to restaurant when friends or family from out of town come to visit and don't want something too flashy or crowded. It's perfectly nestled in the lower end of the Gayborhood on a nice quiet corner . With it's multi-level layout and open windows in the front (where a small bird will occasionally skitter across the floor during brunch), you feel like you're on vacation every time you sit down.

    Upon walking in, the host/hostess is always pleasant and accommodating in terms of seating preference. Not soon after, without fail, our water glasses are filled and our server appears and pleasantly takes a drink preference. The food is out-of-this-world and there's something for everyone on the menu. The guacamole is a go-to when picking appetizers, and the Pollo Empanizado will always leave me feeling satisfied.

    The drinks are excellent, and the margaritas in particular are huge. I've definitely gotten unintentionally tipsy a few times over an otherwise harmless dinner.

    The place has some strange quirks to it, though. On certain nights it just seems oddly empty. Other nights, packed to the brim. And occasionally the server is a bit scatter-brained (on two occasions I received a mojito instead of a margarita...not that I complained). However, they're always wonderfully attentive and were very nice when a friend and I expressed concern after leaving the middle of a torrential downpour... "Take all the time you need!", they exclaimed.

    Definitely consider this place one of my top 5.

    How I do love me some Mixto.

    Whether its dinner and drinks (dear god, MOHITOS!) or a lazy Sunday Brunch (....still mohitos!) Mixto is a wonderful play to enjoy a variety of Latin American foods for a reasonable price.

    Mixto is nestled into the corner of 11th & Pine. The neighborhood is great for a walk before or after your meal with plenty of other businesses/bars to enjoy.

    In the spring/fall when the full length windows are opened on the first floor at Mixto, it is a paradise for brunchgoers. Upstairs in the eveningtime the lighting and general atmosphere sets an intimate mood even if you've got a dozen people at your table.

    For brunch the Gallo Pinto is heaven.  The Columbian Fritatta is great too.
    Lomito Pico de Gallo at dinnertime is glorious.
    The best mohitos by far are the mango or strawberry flavored ones ;-)

    Happy hour is 5:00-7:00 M-F - and Mohitos are just $5.00, plus $2.00 off cocktails.

    I was here for dinner over the Christmas holiday. My friends had been here before but were silent on how good the food is. They wanted me to experience it for myself and they didn't steer me wrong. I am now a Mixto fan!!

    We shared appetizers - trio of ceviche (halibut, shrimp and clams), calamari and guacamole. The calamari was not greasy and lightly seasoned. The ceviche was to die for, particularly the halibut.

    For dinner I had the scallops with fingerling potatoes and fresh vegetables. The scallops were lightly dusted with parmesan cheese. It was one of the best scallop dishes I've had. One friend had tuna and I don't remember the other dish but we were all members of the "clean plate club."

    And because it was the holidays we decided to splurge and have dessert - tres leches, apple pie and cheesecake. The tres leches was a little too sweet for me and I have had better in Miami but it wasn't bad. The apple pie was simple and delicious.

    We sat upstairs and there's a huge big screen tv behind the bar; could be distracting if you're on a date but not all tables have a clear view of it. Be sure to try the mojito.

    Finally, let's be clear on price. The bill for 3 without tip was $156. We each had appetizers, entrees, desserts and one cocktail. There was one coffee and one hot tea. Obviously, you can get out cheaper and I don't think the entree prices were outrageous. Portion sizes are generous.

    It's a great place for happy hour, with friends, or a romantic date and I'd highly recommend it!

    I went here yesterday for a casual happy hour and was very satisfied. We ordered chicken empandads, all their skewers, and an order of calamari along with some drinks.

    Cuban food is typically pretty greasy, and this place is no exception (however, it is not overly greasy).

    The calamari was VERY good. Very soft and tender. The skewers were the happy hour special for $2 a skew. If you are hungry, these are not for you. They come with two small pieces of meat/fish with peppers and onions. They were small but delicious. All  the meats were tender and juicy. The chicken empandas were great too. The salsa they came with had the perfect kick to it that made me order a second.

    The bread served is amazing. If they don't serve it to you, ASK FOR IT. It is pounded with butter, but so good.

    Lastly, order the guava mojito. Any cuban loves guava. I've never had a guava mojito, but did at mixto, and was surprised on how good it was.

    This is not a place I would go out of my way to go to, but definitely a place I wouldn't mind going to again.
