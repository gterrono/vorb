    3.5 stars..
    Checked Philly out 2 weekends ago and having received a recommendation, went to Jim's Steaks instead of the usual stop @ Pat's.  While both places aren't exactly sparkling and pretty, there's a certain atmosphere a real Philly cheesesteak shop has to deliver.  Jim's offers you the same cheeses as Pat's and Geno's does but I believe their menu is a tad more extensive, for instance, if you don't want steak, they have egg sandwiches.

    I ordered the cheesesteak with provolone and onions... not my normal cheese wiz drenched steak sandwich.  Jim's is definitely generous with portions and a good amount of meat and a must for me, soft bread.  The steak is tender and thin, you'll see this by the time you get to the ordering "glass".  However, I won't go the provolone route again.. it didn't melt like cheez wiz at all.  Overall, next time it will be @ Jim's with a heavy order of Cheez Wiz!

    Finally, you best know what you want to eat by the time you get to the glass or you'll experience some of that good ol' South Philly 'tude!

    as a native of Philly people are always asking me where to go to get a good cheesesteak.

    before i can answer that...a little lesson:

    1. it's a "cheesesteak" or "steak"  not a cheese steak, Philly steak and cheese, steak and cheese sandwich etc.  get it straight!  wikipedia will account for these other names for outsiders, but their really just catering to the simpletons.  en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ch…

    2. if you're outside a 50 mile or so radius of Philadelphia, it's not really a cheesesteak.  it's a fact.  it's just the way it is.  cope.  of course certain exceptions do exist but YOU are not qualified to make that assessment.

    3. cheez whiz is a very appropriate way to account for the "cheese" part of the steak.  when ordering one's steak with cheez whiz, simply asking for "whiz" will suffice.

    4.  it's not a steak if it doesn't have onions.  to order your steak with onions, one need only ask for a steak "with."  "with' is the universally understood request for onions on your steak in Philly.  

    5. a steak with marinara sauce on it is referred to as a 'pizza steak."  that's the way i lime em!

    6. the secret to a great cheesesteak, which often goes unnoticed by those enjoying them is the bread.  most self-respecting establishments will use Amoroso's rolls.  they are the unsung heroes of the cheesesteak and a long-time Philly favorite.  amorosobaking.com

    let's review.  if i were to order a steak while back home in Philly, i'd say "gimme a pizza steak - whiz - with!"  

    as for Jim's, they make the best steak in downtown Philadelphia.  forget Pat's and Geno's.  they make good steaks as well, but i truly believe their reputation isn't fully deserved.  they're the places that tourists flock too.  if you're going to stand in line (and you most certainly will!) for my money, you can't beat Jim's.  and don't be afraid of that line!  it may snake it's way out the door and around the corner but the very most you'll ever wait will be an hour and you'll find out at the end of it that it was well worth it.

    Now, my favorite thing about Jim's is that they chop their meat. I like it diced up because it helps in the mastication process. However, it's important to note that chopped meat can mean dry meat. So I suggest going just before or after a busy period. (Those being lunch / dinner)

    When I order I get Whiz & American. I do this because of Sandwich Science. You see, the Whiz acts as a sort of lubricant, and is quickly soaked into the fresh Italian roll. If meat were put directly on that the oil from the meat would too soak into the sandwich, making it dry out faster. The American Cheese acts as a sort of barrier which prevents the two savories from mixing, while acting as a glue to keep the meat from falling out. Finally, I get it topped with onions and ketchup. Mmm.

    Note: Don't go there if you have anything important to do afterwords. You will reak of onions!

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
