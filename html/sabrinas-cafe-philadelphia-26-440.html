    I took my mom and dad here the Saturday before father's day. I had never been here before, but it was highly recommended by the few people I know who have lived in Philadelphia most of their lives. We were seated outside, and it was beautifully sunny and cool. The waitress was quick and friendly, and a lady named Danielle came to chat with us after she heard it was our first time.

    It was neat that "first-timers" are recognized... The fact that they have so many returning customers shows that they are a welcomed neighborhood gem. When the food came out, the portions were absurd. In a good way. My mom and dad, who are picky as all hell, LOVED our experience there.

    I got the coconut shrimp. The waitress asked me if I wanted 3 or 5. I picked 3, thinking to myself how few 3 was.
    THEY WERE MASSIVE. And delicious! My boyfriend got the "facehugger" burger (the promethues-style brunch specials were pretty adorable), my dad chose the hamburger on the menu, and my mom the Mediterranean wrap. All huge, tasty choices.

    I returned for a late-lunch for my one year anniversary with my boyfriend and got the fish tacos. Absolutely amazing.

    I am only doing 4 instead of 5 for a few reasons:

    The outside seating is oddly uncomfortable. My dad is a big, beefy guy, and I don't think he liked it. The sidewalks are also really slanted and roughed up, so the chairs/tables are too. But the beauty of the neighborhood is too good to pass up.

    The coconut shrimp made me feel a little sick later, probably from the grease from the fryer. I am sensitive to heavy oil like that. As amazing as it was, I don't think I'll be ordering it again.

    The server on our second visit was semi-unattentive. Nice guy, though, and otherwise he was great.

    After quick review, I was completely into the idea of trying everything on the menu at Sabrina's. A friend and I ordered two dishes from the Brunch menu: chocolate chip pancakes with shortbread cookie crumbles and raspberry-chocolate chip sauce, and the stuffed French toast (filling of cream cheese, Reese's cups, grape jelly).

    The pancakes not only lacked chocolate chips, but were also extremely dry - something I'd compare to leather. The shortbread crumbles would have been a nice touch if they hadn't been stale. The french toast was equally as dry as it's pancake cousin. The filling tasted purely of cream cheese, and the only element of sweetness was contributed by the grape jelly which did not compliment the dish whatsoever.

    After tasting each dish I covered each with the two sauces which had come with the breakfast. Neither sauce did anything to enhance the dishes.

    I was thoroughly disappointed with my first Sabrina's experience, mainly because I was blown away by the eclectic menu and I expected much more. I wouldn't recommend this restaurant to anyone, however I am giving Sabrina's the benefit of the doubt by assuming my experience was just a total fluke (maybe from the high dining volume that particular morning). I would give Sabrina's one more try, because I don't want to prematurely  accept that such a cool spot could consistently put out less than mediocre food.
