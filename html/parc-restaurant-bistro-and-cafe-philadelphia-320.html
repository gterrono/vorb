    The seafood cocktails - shrimp, crab (2 legs for $18 - ouch) and lobster all were effortless to eat.  ($16 for 4 shrimp - eeek!)  My friend and I were still hungry so she got the French onion soup (and I helped!) which quickly killed our appetites.  (Heavy - but sweet and not to salty and delicious.)

    Profiteroles for dessert were very good but not orgasmic.  (And I had been eye-ing them all evening.)

    So would I return?  Hell, yes!  But will I run right back?  Heck, no!

    Listed in Best Places to Eat - Philly, Philadelphia Date Night, Brunchadelphia

    Parc is Stephen Starr's take on a classic French Bistro.  Overlooking Rittenhouse Square, Parc creates the perfect setting for an otherwise run-of-the-mill upscale dinner.  With an expansive dining room, tiled floors, and typical Parisienne interiors, it's no wonder that this Franco-American eatery is consistently packed every night.  But aside from some menu highlights, expect to shell out big bucks for otherwise ordinary french cuisine.

    Like any of Mr. Starr's establishments, the food is terrific.  The bread alone is to die for.  I could likely eat two baskets of it before the first course and not look back.  The last time I went I had the Coq au Vin with his Food Network famous mashed potoatoes which were delicious, creamy, and perfect.  But while the food is good and there are no complaints, there is nothing that truly stands out about Parc that would set it apart or make me want to come back again and again.  With all of the work that goes into the theming, ambiance, decor, and cuisine, Parc just lacks that certain (wait for it) je ne sais quoi (bingo).

    When the weather is nice, definitely sit outside on the sidewalk overlooking the park; not just for the great views and the people watching, but because it is incredibly noisy inside and impossible to hear anyone, let alone your dining companion(s).  While Parc succeeds as a very good french restaurant, it lacks a certain special something that would make it great, and Starr's food alone doesn't propel it into the upper echelon of great Philadelphia restaurants.

    People who adore Philly, please don't hate me for what I'm about to say.  If ever I had a reason to return, it wouldn't be for the Art Museum or Boathouse Row, lit by lights on the waterfront at night.  It wouldn't be for the noisy public transportation system or the honking drivers that seem to be everywhere, day or night.  It would be for PARC, which is like a little spot of French heaven in Rittenhouse Square.  Now, for non-Philadelphians, it's important to know that Rittenhouse has an urbane ambiance.  The best places to shop and dine exist in this little city square.  

    Parc is in the heart of that, with wicker furniture littering the sidewalk during the warm weather.  I've visited Parc both in the summer and winter, but in the summertime, it is just like a Parisian cafe, complete with cosy tables and interesting sights.  I can understand how some people might not like how closely the tables are put together.  It is very European, and Americans aren't used to sitting so close to one another as they eat.  Another couple had to squeeze in next to us, and it almost felt like they were a part of our dinner party.

    On to the good stuff: drinks!  Parc offers a wide range of hand-crafted cocktails, most of them with unique twists on a familiar theme, like a cucumber cooler.  They even offer sodas like Orangina for that continental flair.   Their wine list is extensive, and on my numerous visits, I've found the staff to be very helpful in making recommendations.  

    The menu is written in an old-fashioned font and is simple.  Rather than florid descriptions, Parc gives you the title of the dish and relies on your trust to sway you in ordering.  The first time I went, I had the Steak Frites in the bar area.  The bar-tenders were gracious and quick.  My drink stayed full and strong for the duration of my visit.  The restaurant was full, but the low-lit atmosphere was nice.  I found myself wishing I'd come with a date because of the romance the lighting inspired.

    On my second visit, I sat outside and the server was equally attentive.  That time I started with Shrimp Cocktail.  The Shrimp were exceptionally fresh and sweet, letting me know that Parc cares about absolute freshness when selecting its ingredients.  My friend had half a dozen Littleneck Clams, which were arrayed on chipped ice with seaweed.  I sampled some, and they were equally fresh and quite light on the palate.  I followed the Shrimp Cocktail with the Beet Salad, which featured the hearty taste of the beet balanced out with a light vinegar dressing.  

    Every time I've walked by Parc on a warm weekend morning, it's been filled with brunchers.  I'm dying to try it, to really get a handle on the whole experience.  Although I no longer live in Philadelphia, I'd love to return, if only to see if my fantasies about the Parc brunch live up to my expectations.

Specialties

This stylish addition to Rittenhouse Square pays tribute to French cafe culture, serving breakfast, lunch and dinner seven days a week. Ideal for a quick bite or a leisurely meal, the menu is filled with classics: onion soup, escargots, steak frites and towering plateaus de fruits de mer. The baguettes are made in-house and  the wine list is expansive. Sip a Kir Royale at the zinc bar or enjoy a cafe au lait while basking in the sun.
