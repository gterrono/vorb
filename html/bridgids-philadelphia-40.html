    Listed in Yard's: Cheaper than the brewery

    Almost 3 years had lapsed between meals at Bridgid's.  I remember really liking this spot, but honestly, my standards have gone up since my last visit here.  I was under the impression that it was a Belgain joint, but they only had one Belgain on tap.  The beer list here is solid, but not stellar.  They're all $5 or $6.

    As far as a bar goes, they do offer two unique things.  First, although small, the U shaped bar is great.  Many bars could take cues on how to effectively use a small space.  Secondly, the gravity tap is great.  When I visited with Dan they had Yard's ESA on.  The gravity tap comes from the uninsulated attic.  The winter air chills the beer and the gravity forces out through the tap.  The result is a beer that has the mouth feel of a beer engine and the temperature of a keg.  It made the ESA even better.

    I ordered the burger, and this came stuffed with bacon, bleu cheese and onions.  It sounded good, but in reality, its a disaster.  The burger came out pretty well done, which it needed to be to hold all of the ingredients together.  The flavors tripping over each other, and the distribution of the filling was all wrong; they all were concentrated at the center and the edges of the burger were woefully plain.  they do serve the most perfectly crispy fries.  They're truly excellent.

    Bottom Line: This spot didn't live up to my memory.  That said, I still think it's one of, if not the, best in Fairmount.  I like the gravity tap, but if you're going to dine here I recommend it for dinner rather than lunch.  I still am planning on going back in the near future.

    There is something to be said about being the first one. Does that make you the best one? Well, I guess if you continually reinvent and replant yourself, it very well may. Though new contender from Tom Peters, Belgian Cafe, is intentionally placed in Fairmount to compete with the reigning champ. In the final round, the faithful, consistent, and pioneer bar known as Bridgid's delivers the knock-out blow. Indeed, it is the best place in Fairmount to go for a drink. It may even be the best in the city to do so.

    This is the bar that made some call Philadelphia, Brussels, U.S.A. It's a classic Belgian bar with a great sensibility for excellent drinks (from other countries, too), as well as great cuisine. We're not just talking about the definitive Belgian bar in Philadelphia, we're talking about the definitive gastropub. Bridgid's is a place that makes people realize that beer is the working-man's drink; to get a similar experience a French bistro with equivalent quality wine, you'd pay double the price you might at Bridgid's. Bridgid's isn't desperately affordable, but it is still an incredible value. It continually pours some of the best beers in the world; while continuing to respect the culture of beer, hosting great events. It's conscious of itself and the neighborhood that surrounds it, and continues to be committed to playing an important role within that neighborhood.

    Oh yeah, and it has an very cool horseshoe shaped bar, with cask options, a gravity tap, and a dozen lines. It also has a great bottlelist. It has delightful bartenders, great patrons, and a good atmosphere. It feels so much different than those bars directly on Fairmount Avenue. Tucked away in the middle of a residential neighborhood, it feels organic in a fashion that so many others don't.

    Bridgid's is a testament that tradition, consistency, and frontierism are more important than being bigger and better. Sure, Monk's and Eulogy have their bragging rights because of their volume and capacity. But on a Friday evening, those spots will be packed--Bridgid's won't be dead, but it'll feel movable. It won't be overwhelming. And for serving some of the best beverages in the world, Bridgid's just doesn't act that way. It's humble. Bridgid's is the genius you sat next to in Geometry class that never told you he aced the tests you failed.

    This is a bar that shows that beer culture in Philadelphia doesn't have to be pretentious, expensive, or anything close to the nose-in-the-air nature of wine culture. It shows that beer is as much about fun, community, and company as it is about taste. Bridgid's is a bar that personifies Philadelphia. A bar that is sure of itself, and never flaunts itself. And even though it's the original Belgian bar, this bar continues to set standards for quality, service, and excellence that other bars only try to achieve. Though it pours world-renowned beers, it has a strong presence that local is better and pays homage the local breweries.

    Every other bar is just trying to be more like this one. Even if they don't know it, yet. The perfect bar? Not far from it.
