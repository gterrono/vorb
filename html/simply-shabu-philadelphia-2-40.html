    I am a firm believer that a city with long winter must have shabu.  Philadelphia is in luck because someone thought the same and brought forth Simply Shabu!  Simply Shabu is tucked at the edge of Chinatown.  A small and casual do-it-yourself type of cuisine.  It specializes in Japanese hotpot, which demands fresh and simple ingredients.  I would recommend making reservations for your party ahead of time because it seems like many tables were reserved when I got there.  My friends and I sat on the counter where each of us have our own shabu pot.  The menu was easy to navigate, where you can order combos, a la carte, and more.  I opted for the seafood combo, with a customized vegetable bowl and udon.  The vegetable bowl comes with a HUGE selection of vegetable.  I have never seen so many variety at a shabu spot.  They have Taiwanese cabbage, napa, japanese squash, taro, corn, enoki, mushroom, tofu, egg, broccoli, spinach, and more.  The seafood platter "appears" small (shrimp, cod fish, salmon, scallop, clams, and fishballs) but there isn't anything small about it.  The scallops were big and there were a good amount slices of fish.  And I would recommend getting a la carte with your shabu - always!  I usually get 2-3 sides, but I have since cut back.  At simply shabu,  I ordered the fish ball with roe.  It can do no wrong with the sweet roe inside.

    Simply Shabu features a "sauce bar" where you get to make your own sauce.  Rule of thumb, you must get soy sauce and the BBQ sauce.  I tend to put everything except hot pepper in mine, especially cilantro, scallion, garlic.  They even have chili oil and sesame sauce.  I love that they have grounded radish!  Honestly never encountered that at a shabu place.

    In the future, I hope simply shabu would offer smoothies.  As of now, it is BYOB..and it has been across the board for majority of the philly restaurants I've been to.  So that might be why.

    Hallelujah! Decent shabu shabu comes to Philly!

    We moved from Boston 6 years ago, where there are not one, not two, but THREE great shabu shabu restaurants, and hand to heart we have actually gone there (5 hours each way!) just to eat at one of them. THAT'S how much we love shabu shabu. When we lived there we would go at least once a week and when friends visited that's where we'd take them. Since moving to Philly we've chased a few rumored phantoms and tried the good faith effort at Fuji Mountain, and we've tried five of the six shabu venues in Manhattan. Each gets one or a few things right, but none of them has come close to being as good as Shabu Zen in Boston. But hallelujah -- Simply Shabu is great, as good as the Boston venues with a few tiny flaws.

    Shabu shabu ("swish swish") is Japanese hot pot cooking, sort of like fondue, but with broth. Simply Shabu is a clean, modern white long room with two- and four-top tables and a long U-shaped counter. Each table or counter spot has a heating element built into the surface, upon which will rest your pot of broth. Like the best places, Simply Shabu has pots that can be divided just in case you want to try two of the three broth options: plain, spicy or vegetable. Then you choose what you want to cook. Very thin slices of meat cook surprisingly quickly in the hot broth. You can get beef (very good!), organic chicken, pork, lamb, cod, salmon, shrimp, crab, scallops or  clams. You can pick a protein, which then comes with a vegetable medley and white rice or udon noodles, but we always opt for the a la carte menu where, a bit like raw tapas, you mix and match proteins and vegetables to put together your own meal. This is lots of fun when you have a big group and want to try a lot of items. There is an excellent selection, including 3 kinds of dumplings, 5 kinds of mushrooms, 2 kinds of corn, squash, cabbage, spinach, broccoli, taro and more, each for a few dollars a plate.

    The elements that make a great shabu shabu place, in my opinion, are: a variety of broths, a wide a la carte selection, good pots and utensils, and the SAUCE. No place outside Boston we've been to has the right sauce. But Simply Shabu really shines here: there is a sauce bar with more than 10 items; you mix your own sauce, in which you dip the items you've just swished through your broth. It's the suggested savory sauce that really makes the food sing -- a mix of soy sauce and BBQ sauce, with whatever else you fancy. We go for garlic, scallions and chili oil. Yum. That BBQ sauce (which can be found in the Asian grocery stores of the Italian Market area) is so unique! You will crave it in your sleep.

    So grab some friends, pick your broth, proteins and vegs, mix your sauce, and prepare to swish yourself into culinary nirvana. I've never met anyone who didn't have fun and enjoy good shabu shabu.

    We are so lucky that someone has FINALLY brought this to Philadelphia. Between Arch and Race, and 10th and 11th, the friendly staff will guide you through this delightful dinner. Please go! And go again! We were already planning our next visit before we'd even paid the (eminently reasonable) bill.

    This is a BYOB, with canned beverages available for a cheery dollar.

    Positives: very good broths, a great variety of proteins and vegetables, terrific pots and utensils, very nice staff, good prices ($20-25 per person), and amazing sauce

    Negatives: no liquor license, no desserts, no smoothies, udon noodles should be parboiled

    Currently open for dinner and Saturday lunch. I wish these guys the best of luck.

    First time trying this place out and it was great! When you go to chinatown you imagine some less than clean venue with some greasy food slapped down on your table with some careless waiter who just takes your order hastily. However Simply shabu is a nice clean break away from the mundane dining of the Philly Chinatown. If you havn't had Shabu before then you must try it! Slightly different dining experience where you cook your own food right in front of you using the tasty broth they make.

    Customer Service: The young waiter/waitress will answer any questions you have on the wide variety of food on the menu with a smile. Always prompt to answer your requests even when they are scurrying by.

    Food: Got the Lamb Combo! High quality meat with great meat to fat ratio freshly sliced and brought to you upon ordering. Comes with a big boat load of fresh veggies, none of that tarnished or day old veggies either. Fresh food, clean eats, not greasy. Add an assortment of condiments from the bar to dip your food in, (bbq, soy sauce, dicon, scallions etc.etc.)

    Decor: The venue itself is aesthetically pleasing to the eye with very bright lighting and great seating at the bar if your alone. Bonus: Very clean and bright bathroom, no weird smells.

    Definitely a stop in go to spot for an out of the box dining experience, go with friends to have a meal and relax with the BYOB policy! No complaints really. Give it a shot!
