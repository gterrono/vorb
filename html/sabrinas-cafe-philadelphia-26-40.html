    Listed in This is why you're fat, Hangover haunts

    If I had a list of all my favorite brunch places in the city then Sabrina's would definitely be in my top 3. I was first turned onto this place by my lovely roommate when I first moved to the city during one of our many bonding moments and to be honest, that was probably the nicest thing she could've done. Brunch is my favorite meal of the day and not only do I now have a place to recommend to others, I also get to eat there with them. It's a win-win situation!

    Sabrina's features a huge and extensive brunch and lunch menu, complete with weekly additions to the menu. It can be overwhelming but that just means more visits back to try them all! My personal favorites include the barking chihuahua and my own personal variation of the bella vista omlette (in case you're curious it contains goat cheese, mushrooms, spinach, and egg whites). I've never ventured over here for dinner but the prices seem moderate, especially for the quality of food.

    The only negative thing about Sabrina's is that they are ALWAYS busy, especially on the weekends. If you attempt a meal on a Saturday and/or Sunday then be prepared to get there early or experience a wait. Trust me, the food will be worth it but I loathe waiting in lines so there is no way I'd consider arriving there after 10:30am. Because it's so busy I've found that the service  can be compromised because it begins to get slow. Now, being the restaurant industry I can commiserate and it doesn't bother me as much BUT I do know that sometimes other people aren't as forgiving and that's why I'm giving this place 4-stars.

    Sorry about that, Sabrina's. I hope you know that you'll always be 5-stars in my heart!

    First off - 5 stars for the food. 5 stars really. I had a Southwestern Corned Beef Hash with black bean, green/red peppers, asparagus, chorizo and everything else under the sun and it was totally sublime. yes, sublime. Really...one of the best breakfasts I've had in a long time...and I don't even like breakfast! All the ingredients were fresh, seemingly straight from the street markets in the surrounding neighborhood.

    What I really loved here was the inventiveness of the food. Sabrina's takes old favorites, and throws about 20 unexpected and unrelated fresh ingredients in with it, and out comes something new. Sweet.

    Sabrina's is located in a old Philadelphian house in South Philly near both old and new immigrant neighborhoods. It some sense it sticks out with the lines of posh yuppies waiting on the sidewalk there. This Old House (get it) is just an awesome setting for a weekend brunch. It almost feels like you're having brunch in a tv-stereotype home and dad is flipping pancakes back in the kitchen. It's a sunny, bright, interior.

    The one drawback is, with any small-sized, quality joint, the enormous popularity and subsequent wait to be seated. We called ahead to put our name on th list about 40 minutes before we should up. And then...we waiting another hour and half. So, 2 hours, 10 minutes total. That's a wee bit much. It is far too tempting when hungry to go the street markets and shops down the street and get a cheesesteak, corn on the cob, tamale, or anything else, while waiting. But that struggle against temptation might be part of the experience after all. It's worth it.

    I used to walk up to Sabrina's all the time when I lived in Pennsport. Now that I'm in Midwest Philly, and don't own a car anymore, it's a rare occasion that I get down for brunch. Friends in town from Baltimore brought out their old station wagon last weekend, so we decided to take advantage.

    I've never minded the wait at Sabrina's because it gives me time to shop in the Italian Market. There are fewer stores and vendors on Sunday, but I was still able to pick up fresh pasta and a hard goat cheese before we got seated.

    Sadly, the "All-American"  breakfast that 3 out of 4 of my party ordered was all sold out (I guess that's what you get for going to brunch at 1pm). Everyone was please with what we finally ordered anyway. The polenta fries, in particular, were a big hit, the french toast was fluffy, and the hollendaise sauce had just enough lemon. One companion thought that the blue cheese, while tasty, overpowered the rest of his blue cheese and bacon frittata, and he also expressed some distress that it contained no potatoes, but overall we left stuffed and happy, and with mild food comas.

    Tips: if you're going to get two glasses of orange juice, you might as well get a pitcher and share with everyone. Any more than 4 people in your party is likely to make your wait a little too long - save the big groups for less popular places!

    After reading my fellow yelpers split reviews, I decided to try Sabrina's with out-of-town guests. While the majority of menu consists of traditional fare, I'd like to give them 3-Stars for Creativity, mostly for their trademark "stuffed french toasts".  

    Portions are HUGE, so we ordered a couple of plates to share.  We still had leftovers, which held out pretty well for reheating later in the evening.I tried the Huevos Rancheros and some of the vanilla/banana cream filled french toasts.  The Huevos were decent and the french toasts were too sweet and gooey for me (you do not need any syrup).

    We thought late Sunday afternoon (2:30 pm) would mean avoiding the brunch crowd, but still waited 40-45 min to be seated.  This, after we observed the "call ahead" policy (you can call and put your name on the list, but not considered a reservation).  So be sure to arrive with a high level of patience and a granola bar to pass the time. Oh, bring an umbrella if its stormy---we waited outside under the rain for lack of waiting area in the restaurant.

    Other menu options---egg whites, omelettes, pancakes, toasts, bagels, salmon/lox, fruits; lunch fare includes salads, sandwiches, etc.  and some daily specials.  

    Service: Waitress sat us, took our orders and served us. Although we were a party of 5, we weren't complicated or high maintenance. She seemed aggravated when we asked for ketchup/tabasco/extra cup of tea...not sure if she was having an off-day or suffers from poor customer orientation.  

    Bottom line: I want to be excited for it and say the experience was fantastic, but I'm afraid Sabrina is a "one trick pony" with its unique take on french toasts.
