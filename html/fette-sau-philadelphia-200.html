    I've wanted to try this Brooklyn, NY spinoff for a longtime.  After a couple of failed attempts, I finally made it here for dinner.  The restaurant was beyond crowded when we arrived.  At first, it's rather confusing where to place your order.  There were so many people that I could not tell where the line started.  People were either standing around drinking from mason jars or sitting down eating. They do not have a host to greet you.  You are on your own.  Ordering is cafeteria style, the meat is sold by the weight.  The sides come in two sizes.  While waiting in line, they started to erase items off the menu chalkboard which meant they were sold out of a particular item.  I was getting nervous that my turn would come and they would be like sorry we are sold out.  I've waited in line for so long that I almost ordered everything on the menu.  

    When it comes to barbecue, people have their specific preference.  Fette Sau plays it safe.  The smoke on the meats is subtle to almost non-existent.  None of the meats comes pre-sauced.  The ribs were excellent.  There was a nice amount of hickory smoke and I enjoyed the dry rub seasoning.  The brisket was tender and juicy.  The half of chicken had an exquisite char and was moist.  The pulled pork was a little disappointing.  It was dry and did not have any smoked flavor.  The bake beans were so-so.  Now the mac-n-cheese was freaking good!  I could taste the Parmesan cheese.  The mushrooms were also very good.  All of the meats were perfectly barbecued -- my compliments to the pit master.

    If you come on Saturday, be prepared to wait.  Some items did not seem to have the level of smoke I like for barbecue and I didn't care for any of the sauces.  With that said, the barbecue is good, but it is not cheep.

    Really good BBQ. I am not a big meat-lover, so it takes some pretty good BBQ to make me happy, and Fette Sau doesn't disappoint.

    The chicken was surprisingly good (I expected the chicken to be dry and flavorless)--the sauce on the skin was deeply caramelized and the meat was clearly brined beforehand, so even the breast was juicy and flavorful. I ate about half a chicken on my own. The pork belly was also melt-in-your-mouth, and the pulled pork and brisket were also tasty. The mushroom side was wonderful--a pleasant surprise from a BBQ place (the tangy broccoli and dijon potato salad were also reasonable).

    The drink selection was good as well--I enjoyed the cider I had--it was just a touch sweet and very crisp and refreshing.

    Rounding out the meal for dessert was key lime pie (no complaints) and bacon walnut brownie (pretty good--not moist/fudgy enough for me personally...and the bacon was subtle).

    Pretty amazing BBQ, I tried the pulled pork, the beef ribs, and the chicken.  All were excellent.  Cafeteria style, but pretty trendy yet comfortable atmosphere.  The staff was helpful (especially the meat handlers checking how many people we were buying for because no one ever knows what they are doing when buying bbq by the pound).  I also liked that there was a staff member trying to find tables for people waiting in line, it made the line much easier to handle.  

    MEAT:
    Pulled pork was smokey, had a nice texture, and tasted great.  The bottom of our pile of meat was even better, as the juices soaked into the bottom layer of pork.  One of the best pulled porks I've had.

    Beef ribs are not usually my cup o' tea (I usually go for pork) but their beef ribs looked too good to pass up.  I was surprised to say that this was my favorite cut of meat for the meal.  Fatty, well seasoned, soft cuts of meat that easily separates from the bone (not fall off the bone, but we were left with completely clean bones at the end of our meal).  

    I often pass of chicken at BBQ places.  It is too easily dried out, and why eat chicken when you can eat pork?  That being said, the chicken was very good.  Moist, juicy meat.  While I would still recommend the ribs or pulled pork over the chicken, this was pretty great in it's own right.

    SAUCE:
    Hot, sweet, and vinegar are your options.  The hot isn't very hot and the sweet has more of a taste to it.  I enjoyed mixing the hot and sweet together.

    SIDES:
    I tried most of the sides, but I was disappointed that they were out of the cole slaw by the time that I arrived.

    The mushrooms were very good: I would recommend trying them even if you don't like mushrooms.  They are small mushrooms with a meaty, smokey taste to them that works perfectly.  

    The half sour pickles were very good, and mixed well with the meat.  The potato salad was pretty good, it had a mustard vinegar flavor to it.  I don't like sauerkraut, but this was wasn't bad because it wasn't that sour (my girlfriend is a sauerkraut buff and it seemed like she could have used more sour).  The broccoli could have used more seasoning for it to be considered a "salad," I like unseasoned broccoli, but that doesn't really make a salad.  The mac and cheese was fine, but nothing really special there.

    DRINKS:
    Interesting beers on tap, and an extensive whiskey list.  A few inventive cocktails, but nothing that really caught my attention.  I enjoyed drinking a glass of bourbon and a glass of rye with my meat, I'd recommend something similar.

History

Established in 2012.

Fette Sau (German for 'fat pig') is a barbecue restaurant specializing in dry-rubbed meats, sourced from
small, local farms and smoked in-house. The menu changes daily, with a rotating selection of six meats
available for purchase by the pound. The sides are kept simple and include burnt end baked beans, broccoli
salad, pickles, sauerkraut, house-made coleslaw and potato chips. The drink list offers nine beers on
tap, plus a cider, and an extensive list of North American bourbon and whiskeys. Picnic tables are the
primary form of seating, giving the restaurant a communal atmosphere. The space has an industrial feel,
with it's reclaimed wood, cement floor and exposed beam ceiling.

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country.
