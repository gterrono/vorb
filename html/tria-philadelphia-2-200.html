    I moved to an apartment very close to Tria this past weekend and took my two movers/best friends here for lunch. Not only was it super convenient but it was also really really good.

    My girlfriend and I have been coming to Sunday School almost every Sunday for over two years now. We usually went to the center city location, but after moving, we have started coming to Washington Square.

    Tria is fantastic, and in some ways, this location is the best. The Sunday School choices are carefully picked, and in most cases go together nicely. The half-price beer, wine, and cheese are perfect for a date - I usually drink a beer, while my girlfriend prefers wine. We split the cheese.

    Other notable menu items are the beet salad - almonds, beets, and onions go together perfectly and the salad is never drenched in dressing. Any of the small dishes are also fantastic, particularly the ham/figs. Portions are very price-appropriate, I have never felt robbed after leaving.

    The other great thing about the Washington Square location is the environment. Located on a quiet block on Spruce Street, it is easy to sit down outside with a book and spend 2-3 hours over a cold beer and cheese. The staff is friendly and the place rarely gets loud.

    Looking for a good place to take a casual weekend date? Want to spend a lazy Sunday afternoon reading the newspaper? Go to Tria, now.

    I live in the vicinity, so Tria is basically my old standby.  I have been to the Rittenhouse Tria location a few times as well, but this one is slightly larger as well as more outdoor seating options.  I absolutely love this place - I'm a sucker for a well crafted wine and beer list, including some options that you would never have anywhere else.  The waiters and bar staff are incredibly knowledgeable about the menu and beverage selections as well.  
    The cheese menu - please, don't even get me started.  I love cheese so much, and they always have a great selection and variety.  I'm not particularly a fan of blue cheeses, but I decided to try the Roquefort they had and loved it.  I also appreciate the portions they give as well - not too much but not too little as well.  I have to been to a some more "upscale" restaurants in the city and feel like a lot of places skimp in the cheese portions, especially if you have multiple people.
    General food menu is great - love the salads (duck salad and goat cheese/walnut/dried cranberry salad are my faves), bruschettas (obsessed with the goat cheese/pesto), and small app plates (Gorgonzola stuffed figs, white bean spread, olives are great).  The only thing I haven't been overly impressed with are the sandwiches; I've had a few in the past that I wasn't impressed with.
    Overall, Tria is a fantastic place to go for a casual meal or drink.  I also adore the Sunday School menu that offers one half price beer, wine, and cheese, which is a great deal.

Specialties

Welcome to Tria, Philadelphia's wine, cheese and beer cafe. We serve real wines, cheeses and beers crafted by passionate people. We relentlessly seek out the best products both locally and from around the world. We dig flavor. Our ever-changing fermentation menus are complemented by a cafe menu served from opening to late night. Don't know the difference between Cabernet and Carmignano? Don't worry. Our friendly, knowledgeable staff will help guide you through a memorable wine, cheese and beer experience.
