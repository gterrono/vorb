        This is one of my favorite restaurants in the city. I go here about once every two weeks and always order the same 2 things (-which I'll get to in a moment.)

        What spurred me to write this review is that I went to Shaio Lan Kung last night- alone. I've never gone to a restaurant by myself before but after a grueling weekend at work and with a pocket full of cash and a good book, I decided that a quiet evening gorging myself at my favorite hole-in-the-wall (and it is a hole-in-the-wall... no one goes there for the atmosphere) was just what the doctor ordered. However, I was afraid that I'd be hurried, or ignored, or have to deal with an annoyed waiter (less food= less tips) but was both relieved and surprised when my service was on-point as always (though a little forgetful, which I find charming. "Oh! Your tea! I'll get it right now!" *scurry in the back, run to my table tea in hand looking slightly but endearingly embarrassed*) I read my book for a long while and slowly munched my food while my water magically refilled itself every time I turned the page (Shiao Lan Kung- the enchanted eatery of China Town?) and the food was, as it always is, orgasmically delicious.

        As a vegan my options are somewhat limited in a place like SLK, but it doesn't matter because they have two of the most delicious vegan meals in the entire city (rendering other options superfluous.) This is what I get (and my friends get, and their friends get, and so on, and so on...)

        SALT BAKED TOFU ($7.95)
        EGGPLANT WITH GARLIC SAUCE IN A HOT POT ($8.95)

        This is food enough for two people (though I ate it all myself last night and waddled home with unbuttoned jeans.) The tofu is not baked at all, but rather fried (or enchanted to appear fried) and comes out sauceless, topped with chopped scallions and hot peppers, and outrageously delicious. I don't even like tofu, but this is undeniably amazing. (My meat eating friends even order it!) Salty, crisp, yummy.

        The eggplant hot pot is of another world. It comes out spitting and sputtering in a thick garlic sauce with wood ear mushrooms, scallions, and thick pieces of superbly cooked eggplant. When my Dad visited Philly we got this dish 3 times in 2 days. The portion is huge, the price is right, and thus far I've never got that slimy-coated-greasy-stomach that some Chinese joints leave you with.

        Also, as for the reviewer who said they never see Asian people at SLK, they must have gone on backwards day. It's usually filled with non-english speaking Asians when I am there (or non Asians like myself that know good food when they find it), in fact I was first introduced to this restaurant by a Chinese friend who ordered for me in Cantonese so I wouldn't know what I was getting (...so I couldn't go without him.)

        This is a great place for vegans and vegetarians to go with omnivores, or go with each other, or, as I found out last night, to go alone. I highly recommend you witness the magic for yourself.

        I've been here a couple of times, most recently with my girlfriend last night for dinner. She's a huge fan of the place - says it's the best Chinese food in Philly Chinatown, and having been there a couple of times I'm inclined to agree it's definitely one of the best. It's not the biggest restaurant in town, so you may have to share one of the bigger tables with other people or wait for a spot to open up if it's crowded. Luckily, I've gotten a seat right away, and the waiters are very good at bringing out the food promptly as it's ready and making sure you have tea and water handy. I'm a fan of that, because a good number of Chinese restaurants I go don't bother with the water unless requested.

        The sauteed clams in black bean sauce are very, very delicious. The sauce adds a very nice marinade to the clams, and it works well to eat them with rice. Last night, for some reason, they didn't do it in black bean sauce and instead altered the dish, unrequested, by adding very ground-up pork. It was okay, but the sauce really brings out the flavor in the clams. The other problem was that some of the shells chipped, and I ended up getting chunks of the shell in with the pork. The fish maw soup is also really good; it's a light way to start off a meal without filling you up. The dumplings are decent, if not memorable, but their lobster is extremely delicious. It's the first time, surprisingly enough, that I've gotten lobster to eat as a standalone entree, and was it good. It was very fresh and succulent, and my only complaint is that we didn't get a bigger lobster!

        All in all, this is a nice little spot to grab a meal, and as noted the service is pretty good compared to other Chinese restaurants I've been to in the past. The clams are a must-try, and they seem to do seafood in general quite well.

        So I went here with a group of friends for a meetup dinner.  The place is a byo which is always nice and the prices are very reasonable.  The decor inside is not much to speak of.  It pretty much looks like any other run of the mill chinese restaurant I've ever been to.  One cool thing about our table was that it had a lazy susan in the middle of it.  Gotta love that, ya know, because it spins.

        So onto the food.  We were a relatively large group so we ordered quite and array of items.  This place really excels in the food department.  The salt baked squid is phenomenal.  It's like there is salt running through the batter but it's not overwhelming.  The sliced chiles on the plate as well just make it that much more awesome to eat.

        The hot pots that we got were very tasty and definitely enough food for one person.  The service was very prompt and they were extremely nice and accommodating to our fairly large group.  I ordered the sizzling wor ba which I expected to come out with huge fan fair.  It was tasty but I was a bit underwhelmed by the dish.  Still, there is enough good about this place that I won't dwell on my tasty but not stellar entree.

        Shiao Lan Kung - why don't you open until 3 p.m.?  I would give anything for a salt-baked squid… Shiao Lan Kung - why don't you open until 3 p.m.?  I would give anything for a salt-baked squid lunch special.

        SLK is the kind of restaurant where no matter what you order, you know it's going to be good.  You could close your eyes and plunk you finger down anywhere on the menu, and I guarantee you'd be happy with your meal.

        That said, the cream of the crop would have to be anything "salt-baked."  Whether it's squid, softshell crab, or a seafood melange, I don't know what they put in that batter but I want more of it.  It's like tempura, but more subtle and less greasy.  I also couldn't get enough of the side dishes - Choy Sum in Oyster Sauce and Spicy Eggplant in Garlic Sauce.  I brought the leftover eggplant in for lunch the next day, and it was even better cold.

        This place is incredibly cheap, and it's BYOB.  We had 9 people and ordered over 13 dishes...the grand total?  A whopping $25 per person INCLUDING tip.

        So yea, it's a little cramped in there, but I'd happily eat on top of another person for a plate of their salt-baked squid.  It's also open until 3 a.m. on Fridays and Saturdays - what a great late-night alternative to Little Pete's!         Read more
