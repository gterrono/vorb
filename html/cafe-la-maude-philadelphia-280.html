    Have been hearing about this place for a WHILE as an up-and-coming brunch spot in Philly, so I'm glad I finally had the chance to make the trek over.

    The menu is just stunning and perfect. So many options, and everything sounds absolutely drool-worthy. +1. The waitress made it clear that almost all options could be customized (can take out ingredients, swap ingredients, make things Vegetarian, etc.) which I truly loved.

    Finally ended up settling on the: Parisian Zá (vegetarian version), open-face Mediterranean burrito, and Caprese benedict. All three dishes were just STELLAR. A+.

    Definitely a bit on the pricier side, and the portion sizes are a bit small. I also felt as though there was an element of salt lacking in all of the dishes.

    That being said, though, I would definitely be back in a heartbeat. There are at LEAST 5-6 other things I want to try on the menu, and now that I know that quality is definitely executed and that the descriptions on the menu aren't just fluff covering up lackluster flavors, I will definitely be back soon!

    I plan my busy schedule around how often I can visit Cafe La Maude and I appreciate their morning hours (6:00 AM!)--and there are always early dawn regulars who beat me to the door!  I love sitting by the open bay windows and the place feels like a charming European cafe.  Their French-Lebanese menu is phenomenal--traditional and hearty dishes with exotic and accented flavors from half a world away.  I can't name a favorite breakfast order because I have truly enjoyed them all (but oh my God, the breakfast tacos).  Their sweet potatoe home fries (crisp, soft, savory) play the role of sidekick to many of the omelets and breakfast mains, but they are so divine, they should be offered as just a sole entree.  And the plates will fill you up until the late afternoon, but it's not because they serve oversized portions--each meal is a strong balance of delicious flavors with simple, filling nutritious foods.  This ain't no greasy eggs-and-bacon diner--the kitchen sends out each meal with a petit salad and a side of fresh fruit.  Finally, I recently learned that my usual sweet waitress, Nathalie, is actually the owner and head chef.  Just like her cafe, she exudes genuine charm, humility, and you always feel so welcome and content after a fine meal.

Specialties

With the authentic look and feel of a true European café, this hidden gem in Philadelphia's Northern Liberties neighborhood serves up some of the most delicious food, coffee and pastries anywhere in the city. Café La Maude's specially brewed La Colombe coffee is known for its strong taste that's never bitter.  The French-Lebanese menu combines the exotic flavors of the Middle East with a distinctly Continental French accent and includes traditional favorites such as Brioche French Toast, Nicoise Salad, Taboule and Baklava along with more unique items such as the Grilled Haloumi, a baguette sandwich with olives, roasted tomatoes, mesculun greens and a basil-arugula aioli.  House specialties include the Café La Maude Salad of  arugula topped with warm seasoned chickpeas and fava beans.  Another popular dish is the traditional lebanese recipe of foul moudamas of marinated fava beans served with fresh tomatoes, chopped parsley and pita served with scrambled eggs. For meat lovers the cafe's marinated chicken sandwich and kebbe is not to be missed. Whether it's breakfast, lunch or brunch that brings you to Café La Maude, it will feel like Paris and taste like Beirut.
