    Everything about Federal Donuts is genius. Donuts? Fried Chicken? Coffee? Count me in.

    Seriously though, as someone who considers himself as a "clean eater," Fed Nuts is my favorite place when I decide I want my post-workout carbs to come from a hot, greasy donut. The hot fresh are always awesome, and they always have quite a few tantalizing "fancy" donuts. Yes, they are more expensive than say, Dunkin, but if you're honestly trying to compare the two, just stop it.

    The fried chicken options, whether dry or glazed, are always delicious as well. Shabazzi, Federal BBQ and glazed onion something-or-other have all been tasty.

    As far as coffee goes, the hot seems pretty standard, but the cold brew is my FAVORITE COLD BREW IN THE FUCKING CITY. And if you go to the location in Pennsport, you get to check out the crazy tubed contraption the makes it. I don't know what it is, but if God is real, it's that thing.

    Listed in Yelp 100 Challenge!, strolling through philly, bomb donuts everywhere

    I asked a bunch of people what is a must in Philly and they all told me that I must visit Federal Donuts, which is this gourmet/hipster donut shop that sells donuts and fried chicken, and DAMN it did not disappoint.

    Inside has a very simple but pretty cool decor.  Not much seating but they do have these table/chairs that make you feel like you are in school and oddly perfect for eating donuts.

    For the donuts, they have two categories: fancy and hot fresh.  Fancy donuts cost a bit more and exactly like the name implies, fancier than your average donuts.  It is displayed right next to the cashier and when I went, they only had two different ones left.  Got the Blueberry Muffin, which is a donut covered with blueberry glaze, then sprinkled with blueberry muffin crumbs!  The Strawberry Lemonade was the other and definitely my favorite.  Lemonade glaze with strawberry icing is amazing.  When I took that first bite, my brain was so confused because it really did taste like a strawberry lemonade, but I was eating a donut.

    I also got the last of the Strawberry Lemonade so if you want the fancy donuts, go early, or else you'd be like the 4 people that walked in after me hoping for some gourmet donuts!

    Besides donuts, also got a 1/2 order of the fried chicken.  I'm actually not too sure which seasoning I got... since I ordered the Shabazzi but may have gotten the honey ginger.  In any case, ridiculously good fried chicken.  It's done like the Korean-style fried chicken, so a nice crunch outside and moist inside. They also give you a small side of Japanese cucumber pickles to help cut through the fat.

    With every order of the fried chicken, you get a honey donut!  It's part of the hot fresh, so they fry it on the spot.  Nothing better than fresh donuts.

    Some fried chicken and three donuts later, I walked out a really happy dude.  Dammit.  LA needs a place that sells donuts and fried chicken!

    Third star is for the donuts. The donuts are delicious. My husband and I each got a 1/2 chicken which came with a donut. I asked for a cinnamon+sugar donut thinking they wanted to know which kind I wanted with my 1/2 chicken. Apparently, you get a honey donut with the chicken, no substitutes, so I was charged $2 extra for the donut. They made it fresh, as in they made it to order, and it was piping hot when they served it to me. That was worth $2, but I'd rather do a dozen next time, and get a better deal. (That is, a dozen to take home, not eat on the spot.)  

    The chicken is decent but not the best I've had. (Crown Chicken in the Norristown ghetto is better.) My Federal chicken was pretty dry and the meat-to-fried-skin ratio was not as even as I'd prefer (more meat than skin, which may please some, but when I want a junk food fix, I want my fried battered skin). There were 3 pcs white meat and a drumstick in my basket. Personally, I'd have preferred more dark meat to help combat the dryness.

    What else? Well, I was a little turned off by the atmosphere. Their mix of Mowtown music was playing, which I love, but somehow the atmosphere at Federal is contrived and ingenuine enough to make the Mowtown feel canned and cheesy to me. I didn't want to get into it because it felt like something they slapped together to try to add "soul" to the place, rather than earn soul through the blood, sweat and tears it takes to earn soul. The design of the restaurant felt like it was imitating a southern diner from the mid-century era.  I expected the Nashville Sit-In Lunch Counter boys to take a seat beside me, and then for the trendsters around me to ask for their autographs in that voyeuristic way unique to trust fund brats who also think soul can be purchased by rubbing up against the sweat of someone who put his neck on the line to earn that soul. Anyway, it felt a little gimmicky that way. The T-shirts for sale said something like "'Hipstery' raves the New York Times." And that said it all to me. Why? Why put that on a T-shirt? If someone called my restaurant "hipstery" I would lie in bed for a week crying, not print it on a t-shirt.  Well, to each his own. I'll go back for donuts, and thank you for that, Federal, but I'll take 'em to go; the atmosphere and clientele is something I'd like to avoid.

    We got there at the end of the day and they were cleaning up as we ate. Lights and music got turned off, etc. and it felt a little rude. They stay open until they run out of food, I get it, but we spent over $20 for take-out for 2 people... could they spend the dime to leave the lights on long enough for us to get out the door?

    My friends and I preordered a dozen donuts (2 per flavor) and boxes of chicken for July 4th. Yep, we preordered to avoid the risk of anything being sold out. That would be too shameful.

    In short: best donuts I've ever had that I could not decide which flavor was my favorite as I ate my second.. fourth... and sixth donut.

    Donuts:
    I tried (forgive me if I get the exact names wrong):
    -Cookies & Cream
    -Peanut Butter
    -White Peach
    -Banana Cream
    -Apple Crumb(?)
    -Strawberry
    ALL of them were so delicious. The donuts are a perfect size where they're not too small nor grossly large. The frosting on top was sticky, soft, and sweet- not the kind that tastes like day-old hardened sugar at Dunkin' Donuts. The dough was so soft that the ensemble just kind of melted in your mouth.

    The flavors were very distinct and stayed true to the description and name. The only one I wasn't really feeling was the peanut butter one because it was a bit salty and overwhelmingly strong.

    Chicken:
    We ordered the garlic and chili chicken breasts & wings. It wasn't that spicy thankfully. There dressing on the skin was flavorful and there was tons of meat on the chicken. Definitely a great complement to the donuts.

    Location:
    It was really small inside and kind of gave a hole in the wall feeling. Doesn't really matter though because you most likely won't be eating in there anyway.

    Seems like most people took off stars because they sell out quickly. Well, luckily for me, I got the whole spectrum because of the special pre-order. Trust me, this place deserves more than just 4 stars. Visit if you're in Philly. :)

Specialties

Cake donuts in interesting flavors and Korean-style, twice-fried chicken sold by the half and whole bird.  House-roasted coffee available hot or cold-brewed and iced. That's basically it.

History

Established in 2012.

Chef Michael Solomonov and partner Steve Cook of Zahav and Percy Street Barbecue got a wild idea to pair exotic donuts and super-crispy fried chicken. With Bodhi Coffee owners' Bob Logue and Tom Henneman's coffee expertise and professional bigmouth Felicia D'Ambrosio, the team opened the first FedNuts at 1219 S. Second St. in Pennsport, South Philly in 2011.  The Sansom Street shop followed one year later in October 2012.  We have since been cranking out fancy donuts, melt in your mouth hot fresh donuts, and truly badass chicken.  Follow us on Twitter www.Twitter.com/federaldonuts for status updates and fun contests.
