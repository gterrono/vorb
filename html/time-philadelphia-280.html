        Service was good but their food was just ok.  

        We had arrived during their happy hour.  They have a HH bar menu that included about 8 items.  I went with a burger that included fries.  The burger had house made bacon, jack cheese and caramelized onions.  It was eh.  I don't know if it was the bun or the burger but it was just dry.  Fries were just ok as well but nothing spectacular.  

        They did have nice house musicians that played at a low level.  It made for great dinner music.  It was a nice compliment to the cozy ambiance the restaurant was going for.  

        I didn't have any complaints about the service.  He was also the bartender and did a great job balancing both areas.

        I wouldn't rule it out to visit again.  I would just try something else on the menu.

        Go early (before 8 pm) on a weekday and enjoy jazz and the brilliant, French-inspired, chef's tasting menu! For a recent romantic evening, we enjoyed an amuse bouche of fried oyster on the half shell with peach jam and jalapeño, followed by stuffed eggs with Spanish anchovies and roe, beef tartare with frozen rape, roasted duck breast with chanterelles, rabbit sage sausage and squid with roasted cauliflower and potato puree, and butternut squash ice cream with parsnip chips dusted with dark cocoa. Each dish was a carefully prepared palette of tastes and textures, beautifully presented. The chef is a genius! Add very attentive service on a somewhat slow evening and a jazz duo for an exceptional mid-week date!

        Around nine, the louder band began to set up, and the crowd came in. I'm guessing at that point the tasty looking bar food might become a better option. We left for a walk to digest and marvel over our meal!
