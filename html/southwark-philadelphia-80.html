        It's nice to be just off South Street. We went to Southwark for drinks and dinner. It was very quiet at 6 pm when we arrived. The bar is beautiful, a perfect place to hang out, a long bar with stools and a number of small high top tables. The drinks are professionally made with a focus on American whiskeys and rye but fully stocked with all manner of libations. The bartenders are smart and attentive.

        Dinner was good, we had fluke and pork belly. Everything was delicious.  My only complaint, the toasted bread with the cheese plate and meal tasted like gas, common when the bread is done over a gas grill. The waiter didn't seem to agree with me on this point, but he did bring me some plain bread.

        Although we sat inside, there is a nice outdoor seating area as well.

        Very good food and drinks. Highly recommended.

        Five star service - my boyfriend and I walked in to this cozy, warm space and started scanning the full bar for a spot to sit while waiting for our two other friends en route. Within moments, the bartender leaned over to a patron and before we knew it everyone was shifting down to make room for us. We later learned that this is unspoken bar code here (we moved twice to accomodate newcomers, and gladly so to pay the kindness back.)

        Five stars for the cocktails/mixologists magic:

        The bartenders were Kip and Ryan. Kip mixed my first cocktail, the Aviation, and it was simply delicious. Lemon forward with a light violette aftertaste on my tongue.  Ryan actually reached out to shake our hands and introduce himself.  A very nice touch. Ryan mixed my second cocktail, sticking to gin again with the Corpse Reviver #2. It was equally smooth yet complex, a yummy anise touch from the lillet. I went back to the Aviation for my third and final and Ryan did an equally great job with it.

        Five star food - we ordered the goat cheese to snack on and it was decadent. Not your average tangy goat cheese, it was milder and creamier with buttery parmesean crumbles on top.  Yum.

        We also ordered the sausage special, which was a glorified spin on pork and beans. While salty, the dish was flavorful, tangy and hearty.

        These small plates were memorable enough that we look forward to returning for a full meal.

        Really low key, warm inviting place to come in as a couple, a few good friends or even solo, to enjoy a good meal, a superior cocktail and the company of an unpretentious and friendly staff.

      Specialties

      At Southwark we source our  ingredients from local farmers and co-ops in order to sustain and support local farms and economies.  We have built up our relationships with over 12 family farms to ensure that our produce, dairy and meats are at their peek of freshness and flavor when harvested.  This results in a truly unique menu that is one of the freshest and most dynamic in the Philadelphia region.  We strive to set the standard in the farm to plate slow food movement that is sweeping the region and nation.

      A truly unique experience awaits you at our bar when you saddle up and enjoy cocktails the old fashioned way.  We have amassed one of the largest gin and rye collections of any bar in the nation enabling us to recreate some of the world's great classic cocktails.  Don't look for any new fangled creations here; what you will get is some of the best drinks from Philadelphia's premier bartenders who will guide you through our choices to find what suits you best.
