    I went to Jose Pistolas earlier tonight. It was a but small and on the crowded side, but a good experience.

    When we realized it would be an hour wait for a party of six, we made ourselves comfortable at the bar.

    I started off the one IPA they had on tap. It was decent, but most IPAs are decent to me.

    The first food item was their pickle chips. they were very tasty, but certainly a sharing type of dish. one can only have so many pickles. It's hard to go wrong when battering a frying just about anything.

    My buddy ordered some chips and salsa. The chips were fresh, and cooked well, but a bit on the salty side. The salsa that accompanied it was okay.

    For my entree I had the grouper tacos. They were pretty good. I also had a side of rice and beans, which was very tasty.

    All in all, it was a good experience, but more in the slightly above average ball park.

    David Byrne once said, This Must Be The Place.... maybe he was talking about an acid trip, maybe he was talking about the beach... maybe both. Who knows?? He probably wasn't talking about Jose Pistolas, but the sentiment is no less appropriate for this Tex-Mex oasis amongst the high-falutin' snobbery and low brow dives in it's vacinity. The pure irony of it being directly across from a Buca DiBeppos (arguably the most disgusting "Italian" food ever shit from the dense shadow of the Olive Garden) is enough to make me shoot my 8.5% Belgian import out of my nose and across the bar.

    Casey and Joe have outdone themselves with this haven for beer lovers and foodies alike. Once the shoddy and unpredictable Copa 2 (one wasn't enough?!), Jose Pistolas is consistently serving delicious treats inspired by both sides of our southern border along side a rich array of beers from around the world, rivaling even that of (dare I say it?) Monks' selection. The draughts are always a delightful surprise, the menu specials always mouth-watering; even the menu descriptions of the food and drink are hilarious and fitting of the laid back, freindly atmosphere. The bartenders are welcoming and attentive and the music is always good, or at least either memorable or nostalgic.

    Try the guac and chips, the carnitas burrito or the fish tacos; they never disappoint. The pickled onions are addictive! Grab a beer; they're pretty much all good.
