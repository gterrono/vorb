        Listed in Patronizing Patron

        Ever since I heard about a Chutney company opening near my neighborhood, I had to revisit stalking 101, as I scouted the area each day waiting in eager anticipation for the opening of this place. Well finally yesterday was the fateful day when they opened the doors to the public.

        A bunch of us might be accustomed to a different variety of the indian fare with the likes of curries and naans, this time around the adventure is headed to the  peninsular regions of south india. Albeit south indian cuisine hasn't achieved mainstream aura in Philadelphia , this certainly marks a step in the right direction.

        A chutney can be refered to as any sweet, tangy or sour accompaniment to the main dishes. The traditional ways to make chutneys is to crush into a paste a bunch of ingredients using a contraption similar to a mortar and pestle, and diluted using either water, lime vinegar , tamarind or coconut paste. There is no limitation to what can be transformed into a chutney and with imagination running amok there are endless possibilities. And that's what I expect from the phila chutney company to surprise me each and every visit.

        I tried all the wet chutneys, and other than the traditional coconut that is a staple south indian chutney, I highly recommend the mango and the tomato chutneys. The coconut chutney needed a little more kick as I felt it was a bit mellow for my palate. Nonetheless the ingredients were very fresh and flavorful.

        Coming to my next favourite item on the menu - the Dosas. A distant cousin to the french crepes and Ethiopian injera, are primarily made of a mixture of rice and lentils and the batter is left to ferment overnight, and spread thinly over a hot pan glazed with either butter or traditional ghee. With a different permutations to the ingredients and the way of spreading across the pan, a few varieties like Rava ( semolina ) and Uttapams  can be made.  They can be just had as is or even stuffed with things that you like to savor.

        Though back home I'd devour a whole army of plain dosas, doing the same at a restaurant is not a financially sound proposition so go with some filling inside the dosas to satiate the stomach. The common fillings are the spiced mashed potatoes masala, cheese, chillies, Spinach, onions etc. We ordered the paneer dosa, a masala dosa and a deluxe masala. The latter ones stole the show as the masala was evenly spiced and with no clunky pieces of uncooked potatoes made it a stellar choice. The deluxe had the cheese and spinach in it as was certainly my favourite, though the fried paneer and the caramelized onions were a close second. The batter is perfectly portioned, which helps the dosa to be nice and crispy  to bring out a plethora of flavors to the forefront.

        Albeit they are still fairing in uncharted waters, they were welcoming of any suggestions to make improvements and make the experience better. There are still a bunch of kinks that need to be ironed out, but there is great promise about this place. They go to great distances to cater to ur specific tastes if u asked.

        In conclusion, My cousin said my face was glowing when I ordered my dosa and the pretty much sums up my visit here, and am certainly looking forward to more such outings.

        Several fast food concepts have opened up in the Rittenhouse area within the last year, finally one of them gets it right.

        Philly Chutney Co offers inexpensive unique meals made with fresh ingredients.  The hands-on owners are friendly and eager to receive feedback.  I've visited twice during their soft opening.  

        I'm a vegetarian, so typically I scan a restaurant's menu searching for the veggie friendly items and pick between a few options.  Really, being a vegetarian makes things sooo much easier.  But here comes Philly Chutney Co intent on broadening my horizons with a whole restaurant full of meat free options to choose from.  So how the hell do I tackle this menu?  I'm going to treat this as a single elimination tournament - a "favorite item" bracket of sorts.

        First up is the #11 dosa (thin crepe-style) with veggie chicken, spinach and onions paired with the mango chutney that I devoured in house.  I will compare this to the #13 uttapa (same batter, thicker pancake consistency) with house made paneer, onions, spinach & jack paired with the curry chutney that I took to go on my second visit.  Hands down the dosa was more fun to eat - it was light, crispy and enormous.   The mango chutney started out sweet and ended with mild heat.  The uttapa (which held up well in transit) was tasty too, paired with the cheese & sweet onions it was heavy and rich.  

        The winner?  #11 dosa all the way!  Love the soy protein, non-greasy vegetables & sweet/spicy chutney.  It was filling without weighing me down.

        Next on my list?  The masala deluxe dosa - hopefully I can sub paneer for American cheese with the cilantro chutney.

        Need more reason to go?  This Wednesday (9/22) they're offering 30% off!

        I ordered:
        - Dosa with veggie chicken, spinach and roasted onion
        - Idli
        - Sambar
        - Curry Chutney
        - Mango Chutney
        - Masala Chai

        If you'll recall, a dosa is a rice-flour crepe, and an idli is a savory rice-flour cake. Sambar is a stew/gravy made from lentils, tomatoes, tamarind, hot pepper, and various spices and vegetables. An excellent accompaniment to any Indian meal, sambar can be poured on dosa, idli, rice, or just about anything.

        The spinach, onion, and veggie chicken did a really nice job of adding body to a dosa as a meal. I split an order of idli with my coworker. Both the dosa and idli dipped in mango chutney, curry chutney, or sambar were, savory, spicy, and quite tasty. The sambar is solid, especially at the price point of 93 cents. Its kick leaves you with a gentle spicy glow.

        The masala chai was as expected.

        So go grab a dosa, idli, or samosa and drop an extra buck on the sambar. You'll be glad you did.

        Listed in Indian Cuisine, Center City Lunch Destinations- The best and worst...

        I love Indian food (though I've never had the South Indian variety), so I was really looking forward to giving Philadelphia Chutney Company a try.  The restaurant is located in the space where the Remedy Tea Bar used to be. They've done a really good job of transforming the space - it has a bright cheerful blue, orange, and green interior, with a decent number of tables and ample counter space to sit and eat.  

        I stopped in for a quick lunch and was greeted by the man at the counter, who was very friendly, answered my questions about the menu, and helped me select an option.  It was my first time eating a dosa and I was looking for something traditional and spicy, so I went with the Chef's Special Dosa and the cranberry chutney (i asked for something similar to tamarind).  My dosa was ready in about 5 minutes...it was thin and flaky with a potato, onion, and tomato filing.  The potatoes appeared to have been sautéed in turmeric along with other spices which not only added some heat to the dish (good), but also unfortunately made the dosa way too salty (not good).  I'm also not really sure how this was the 'special' dosa, since it didn't seem to be all that different from the 'The Classic Masala Dosa' (the filling inside mine was mostly all potato).  

        Since I wasn't all that crazy about the dosa, I decided to come back later in the week to try something different.  I decided on the #13 Smoked Paneer,Spinach,Jack Cheese & Balsamic Roasted Onions, and ordered it as an Uttapa (rice pancake), along with a spicy curry chutney (more like a sauce).  I'll also note that this time I was told that any of the menu options #1-#15 are available as either dosa or uttapa.  The Uttapa was similar to a personal pizza- only indian style.  It was a bit bland for the first few bites- the curry chutney added a bit more flavor.  The paneer was good, as were the balsamic roasted onions.  I would have also liked to see a bit more spinach on the uttapa.  However, the one part of this dish that I had a real issue with was the Jack Cheese.  It seemed really out of place alongside the paneer, and the flavor combination was a bit strange as well...

        The Philadelphia Chutney Company serves American Indian Fusion cuisine, and for sure is a welcome new lunch option in center city.  I'll return again for sure - I just hope that in the future they focus a bit more on the unique South Indian aspect of their cuisine and a bit less on trying to please the stereotypical American palate.

      Specialties

      At the Chutney Co., we have a fun, Indian-fusion concept that we're taking to locations all around the city. Our Southern Indian fusion menu (which you'll see on our cool chalkboards in our bright blue, orange and green stores) is lighter and healthier than the heavy food most people associate with Indian cuisine. And on top of that, our food here is organically sourced, health-conscious, low fat, natural and 100% vegetarian.

      History

      Established in 2010.

      In visionary cities like London and Paris, you can find a fast-food Indian boite bustling on every corner. But in the States, our take-outs options are restricted to only Mexican, Chinese, pizza or burger joints. The Philadelphia Chutney Company is looking to change that and bring Philly up to speed.

      Meet the Business Owner

      Nirav M. Business Owner

      This project of passion was fueled by Nirav Mehta, a former technology entrepreneur/attorney with dreams of the dot-com era to create something special, and Baldev Singh, a former attorney whose chef dreams led him to open Aman's restaurants and eventually become the boisterous "cooking guy" behind the scenes at Chutney.
