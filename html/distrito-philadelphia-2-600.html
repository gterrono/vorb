        We went in around 1PM on Saturday. Distrito has a brunch menu on the weekend, which was fine for our group. The choices were interesting. Quick disclaimer: I am from the southwest, so I was expecting interesting choices. I selected the $21 brunch meal (first and second course) with Salsa and Guacamole, and Steak & Eggs, along with a beer. Friend 1 had the brunch meal with the tortilla soup and fish tacos. Friend 2 had the Dulce de Leche french toast.

        The salsa was more like a pico de gallo, which seemed like it had been in a too cold refrigerator, or sat overnight (the tomatoes had puckering, the taste was a bit off). The guacamole was nice, not too overworked, with cilantro and some crunch of onion. The tortilla chips were tasty but cold and oily. The tortilla soup was very tasty, a nice assortment of smokey peppers without too much punch on the spice; just enough to make your nose start to run. One odd thing I noticed about our appetizers was that they were larger than our main dishes.

        Our dishes were pleasantly presented. I ordered my steak medium rare and my eggs over medium (I find that if I ask for over easy, I end up with runny whites, yuck!). Unfortunately the skirt steak was very well done and the eggs were overdone (no runny yolk). The taste was fine, unfortunately the texture killed it for me. The Mahi Mahi tacos were fried, which disappointed the friend who ordered them, but she seemed to enjoy them. The Dulche de Leche french toast was very tasty. The fresh whipped cream was a perfect topping over the pain perdu, along with the caramel drizzle. My only complaint was that the toast didn't seem to be saturated with the custard mixture enough, which didn't fit what my vision of a good pain perdu. My friend, on the other hand, enjoys the less saturated pain perdu, so she was satisfied.

        Waitstaff was attentive, but not overly so. We did have a manager-type who hovered at one point. I didn't comment on my meal to him because he seemed a little too aggressive with the rest of the staff and I didn't want a line cook getting yelled at. I would have preferred a critique to a reaming, so I kept quiet when asked. The only disappointment regarding service came at the end of service where we waited a bit too long for the waiter to pick up our payment to process our credit cards.

        The only environmental oddity was the playing of AC/DC's Big Balls, which just doesn't seem appropriate at any time (it is on my playlist, but I wouldn't play it at a dinner party).

        I give my  visit three stars, because my full meal was disappointing for me based on the price point and the reputation of Garces establishments. I am willing to give it another try at a later date.

        I've been to Distrito twice now for Sunday brunch. I think it's a solid choice for Mexican brunch, but nothing to really rave about.

        The Sunday brunch is $21 for two courses, but I always opt for the a la carte option (I personally think $21 is a bit of a rip-off for tiny brunch portions).

        Today's brunch consisted of: an order of the salsa/guacamole appetizer plate, the huevos rancheros, and the chocolate torte.

        Salsa/Guacamole appetizer plate: This was a solid appetizer dish, consisting of a bowl of tortilla chips, a small serving plate of guacamole and fresh tomato salsa. Everything tasted fresh, and the guacamole didn't disappoint. The ratio of the guacamole to chips was a bit off-- I would have loved some more guacamole. Nonetheless, I still enjoyed this dish (though it definitely didn't blow me away). Extremely overpriced, at about $10 (and it costed more than our main course dish which was the Huevos Rancheros)

        Huevos Rancheros: This is the reason why I wanted to come here in the first place; I am obsessed with the huevos rancheros brunch dish, and so I was so excited to see that Distrito had it. The presentation of the dish was excellent-- garnished, and in a beautiful skillet serving dish. I enjoyed the flavors of this dish, but I do believe it was a tad bit spicy.       Specialties

      Distrito is the new restaurant concept by Jose Garces, the chef behind the acclaimed Philadelphia restaurants Amada and Tinto. Distrito is a high-energy restaurant inspired by the spirited culture and cuisine of Mexico City. The Distrito menu features Chef Garces' fresh interpretations of classic Mexican fare and beverages include over 65 tequilas and margaritas made with fresh seasonal fruits. A striking decor includes a hot pink and bright green color scheme, a wall constructed from over 600 colorful, campy masks of lucha libre, or "free fight" professional wrestlers, and a booth crafted from a Volkswagen Beetle, a typical Mexico City taxi cab.

      History

      Established in 2008.

      Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

      Meet the Business Owner

      Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

      Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
