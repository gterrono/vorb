    There were some really stand out dishes here, but others just missed the mark.

    The restaurant is cozy and romantic ,despite it's vulnerable location on Broad. I love how the kitchen is open so that you can see how Chef Sbraga (who is on the line all six nights of the week the place is open!) and his team working their magic.

    To start, you get these amazing truffle and Gruyere popovers. They're salty, crunchy, chewy, and a real appetite-whetter.

    The eggplant, pepper, and goat cheese tartine is fantastic. It's beautiful on the plate, and delicious in the mouth. It's the perfect ratio of crisp-tender vegetables and creamy goat cheese.

    The beet salad with shaved gorgonzola and nifty soil creation was playful and delicious. Gorgonzola usually overpowers these sorts of salads, but the blue cheese flavor was just right here.

    The risotto is a must-order. Although I prefer my rice a little more al dente, the flavor is earthy and creamy, with just a hint of truffle.

    The black cod was so-so. The fish was cooked nicely, but the beans it rested on seemed out-of-place, and the kimchi was muddled and over dressed with sesame oil.

    The gnocchi with mint pesto and pickled blueberries was another looker. The gnocchi were light and pillowy, and all of the components created a nice earthy and natural flavor profile. I think  it could've used just a hint of acid to brighten it up though.

    I got the hot chocolate, which is more like a molten cake, with Grand Marnier ice cream. The orange flavor was subtle so the deep, dark chocolate flavor could really stand out and the ice cream was smooth and creamy.

    Our waiter was really nice and was excited that we were excited about the dishes and asked lots of questions. As Top Chef fans, we went up to thank Kevin Sbraga for a great meal, and despite the craziness in the kitchen, he was super-friendly.

    The majority of the food was really good, but I can't say that I am pressed to venture back here, hence the 3 stars. That said, I think this place will only get better over time as Chef Sbraga gets comfortable in his new role.

    YUM!!!  Kevin Sbraga lives up to the hype!  We came here for my hubby's birthday and weren't disappointed.  We opted to go for the 4-course tasting menu.  Since we split everything we ended up getting to taste a total of 8 dishes, instead of opting for the 6-course chef's tasting menu which would require us both to get the same 6 courses.

    Listed in Hey Ms. Fancy Pants, Philly Staycation

    Months overdue, here I am finally getting around to writing my review of Sbraga. A shame because I wanted to be one of the first to alert the masses on what a wonderful addition this restaurant is to the city, now I'm just another opinionated diner amongst a flurry of other well versed yelp constituents. Pity party aside, I bring you my fantastic fall night experience of which my company of the evening Vinny P. and Christine B. have already done a stand up job in describing.

    The Offering: Let it be known that the four course tasting menu is the norm at $45/pp with an optional wine pairing addition should you choose. There are also options to indulge in an 8 or 10 course meal, who can eat that much I don't know, available upon request. The full menu is offered a la carte if you can manage to find a space at the bar, however seating is limited and given that it's still difficult months after opening to grab a regular reservation you may want to consider camping out for a coveted seat come dinnertime.

    Space and Service: Give yourself time. Enjoy your experience, don't rush it, take it in. The space is warm and inviting, a modern rustic with reclaimed wood paneling and an open air kitchen which offers patrons the opportunity to see the chef and staff hard at work preparing the camera-ready dishes brought to your table. Though there are not many negatives in the reviews, the few seem to be about service being slow. There is a difference between creating an enjoyable dining experience for patrons by not rushing through four courses and slipups in communication between waitstaff and kitchenstaff. Our table of four found ourselves in a predicament where we thought that we had allowed ourselves enough time (two hours) to enjoy dinner and get to a show at the Merriam Theater. Because of a miscommunication, but not by any means the fault of our server, we were left incredibly tight for time. After bringing to our servers attention the fact that we had a schedule to keep (something I typically hate doing because why are we any more special than the rest of the diners?), we were met by the manager who handled the situation with style and grace in a manner that I have never seen offered, but one that turned a would-be rushed and unenjoyable situation into a positive experience. To say that service and customer care is lacking would be inaccurate, however in reading that months later there are still some timing issues from order placing to plate presentation find myself hoping that these can be ironed out.

    Food: I mulled over the menu for weeks leading up to our night out, sad I know, but don't pretend like you've never done the same thing in anticipation of a great meal. When push came to shove I went with a four course selection of Kevin Sbragas take on classic comfort foods, if we're going casual upscale I'm going straight up American comfort. My first course of sweetbreads was the most unconventional of the meal theme, but to keep somewhat consistent would have meant going with a caesar salad and that just seemed too safe. Of the sweatbreads I have had, sadly these just were not my favorite, somewhat heavy on breading with redemption found in the tonnato and capers which still only partially filled the textural sadness I was feeling. My second course was the fish and chips, not heavy by any means but crisp and piping hot adorned with a cumin salt and sliced jalapeno while set over lightly fried potatoes and a curry remoulade, Indian flare to a classic this could have easily been my favorite course if it weren't for the third. Let's just pause for a second so that you can understand my disgust of the word "meatloaf". Break it down, it's a whole loaf of meat, there's really nothing classy about it. If you backtrack to how mom made it and take out the breadcrumbs, bind it instead with veal mousse like Sbraga, and lather it with a salty but sweet bacon marmalade you're well on your way to gluttony. For dessert I rounded things out with the caramel apple mille-feuille. Though light and sweet with crisp apples and flaky puff pastry, this was sort of a bland ending to an otherwise quality meal. This is not to say that it was not full of flavor or presented beautifully because both are true, I just don't think it packed the wow factor of the previous courses.

    I don't always care for prix fixe menus because you're almost forced to order items you normally wouldn't care to order. I am not always an appetizer person and almost never an orderer of dessert, I could be perfectly happy with a salad and an entree and calling it a night. I am however happy that my dining companions and I agreed to make the reservation because without them I would  be sadly sitting here reading the reviews instead of writing my own. If you are looking for a solid meal before a night at the theater, a special occasion, or just looking to splurge, this is one reservation you need not hesitate in making.

    Birthday dinner review!

    Kevin Sbraga has been one of the very few Top Chef finalists to win it all that I've actually cheered on to do so! So as the restaurant opened, the solid reviews kept pouring in, and Adam Erace (citypaper.net/food/2011-…) and Brian Freedman (philadelphiaweekly.com/f…) painted pictures of culinary excellence with their articles; I knew that I was destined to use my birthday dinner as the chance to finally savor it for myself. And, with what another kitchen wizard who needs little introduction on here dished out for me last year coming in first (yelp.com/biz/sycamore-la…), This might just be the 2nd best birthday meal that I've ever had!

    When you're eating at Top Chef winners restaurant you expect to have an incredible experience that leads to incredible food. But what I liked about Sbraga was the fact that you got one of those without it being cocky in the process. Throughout the evening you could catch glimpses of Kevin holding it down in the kitchen with his crew, or checking in on his front of house. And through those glimpses you could definitely see someone who truly loved what he was doing, and didn't put himself above anyone else on his team because he won a TV Show. And that projected onto a team who delivered some of the best service possible!

    Whether you go with the Prix Fixe 4 course for $45, or the special Chef's Tasting meal that features whatever Sbraga prepares on the spot, you're treated to a real celebration of culinary perfection. Even more so if you go with the beverage pairings, although the Sbraga Punch and Coffee Stout from Williamsburg Alewerks suited me perfectly.

    The Foie Gras Soup was table poured, and its rose petal relish accompaniment featured just a hint of pumpernickel. It was so delicious that I was tempted to lap it out of the bowl! Ever since reading Freedman's article I've been dreaming about the duo of perfectly seared scallop and pork belly. And the beets, cream fraiche, and beet jus that they shared its plate with made it a perfect ballet between sweet and savory. For the main course I just had to get the combo of lamb chop and belly; and it was perfectly aligned with Asian pears and steel cut oats. While ordering an artisanal cheese plate is a dessert tradition that I normally do when its offered, The hot chocolate and Grand Mariner concoction proved to be a decadent and satisfying treat that was too good to pass up!

    So in the end I walked away satisfied after a perfect meal. And while its still too early to call, Sbraga might just be leaving its mark as best new restaurant of the year!

    I came here with the BF last night for Valentine's Day.  I wish I could give this 3.5 stars, but I'm rounding up to 4 because given all the food we had, it was definitely worth the hype and the wait.  Despite some kinks in the service and dishes, Sbraga delivered an exceptional dining experience that's best saved for a special occasion.

    We had a 8:45 reservation, but waited roughly 20 minutes for a table (as did many of the other couples that came).  When we finally sat down, we looked at the menu and sat through our waiter's 3 minute long spiel describing each item in detail along with the paired wines.  We ultimately decided to do the prix fixe with wine pairings because, you know, it WAS Valentine's Day.

    I started with the Foie Gras soup which was hands down my favorite dish of the night.  A rich creamy soup that was warm, inviting, and exceptionally savory.  His eggplant, goat cheese, and tomato terrine was ok, if not a little cold.  For the second course, I had the risotto which was pretty good, but not as good as his black cod with apple and kimchi which was cooked so perfectly and tasted incredible.  As for the main course, I had the house specialty: a meatloaf with potato puree topped with tomato-bacon marmalade.  It was very good, considering that it's...meatloaf.  However, as the dish wore on, I found the seasoning to become overpowering and a bit much.  His lamb chop and belly was much better in my opinion.  And for dessert, we shared the mascarpone with chocolate and coffee granita (excellent) and the "banana passion" (not quite as excellent).

    On the whole, the food was great and the service was good.  Sbraga still has a ways to go before it can join the venerable ranks of Philly's tried and true fine dining experiences (Morimoto, Amada, Chifa, etc...), but it's definitely better than some of the other local places on its level (Buddakan).  And of course, Top Chef fans should definitely dine here if the occasion calls for it.
