    It truly breaks my heart to write this since Mercato was my favorite Philly restaurant from 2007-2010, but since Chef Mackenzie Hilton left town, things just haven't been the same.  

    The short rib ragu, once one of my all-time favorite dishes, has become somewhat of a muddled mess.  On my most recent trip, the ragu sauce was really greasy, the locatelli was clumped on top, and the carrots (which are a newer addition, don't really add much).  Additionally, I think Mercato is either starting to skimp on the quality of their ingredients or on the attention they pay to them.  Since Mackenzie left, the short ribs are noticeably fattier (and not in a good way).  The appetizers are less inspired (the market salad with black kale was nothing special) and the new food additions are incredibly bland (lamb pappardelle, I'm looking at you).

    To top it off, prices have risen and the wait staff (at least on our most recent visit) was kind of drippy.  I really hope Mercato is able to find itself again, but it will likely need to turn to its past to move forward.

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge, Philadelphia Center City Restaurant Week

    This has to be one of the best deals for restaurant week!  It's one of the few places where the menu selections are the same as they would be on a regular night.  I had a great experience, but there are a few things you should know:  

    The WEIRD:  

            It was a bit of a pain to get a reservation here for restaurant week, but in the end it was well worth it.  I called about a month before, but the person who answered the phone said they were in the middle of dinner service so they'd have to call me back the next day.  I thought it was a little strange because it was almost 10 PM on a weekday, but whatever. I guess this place is really popular. I didn't receive a call the next day, but did about three days later.  There weren't that many times left, and I wanted to go on Sunday, so I settled for 5:30.  The early time ended out working out well though.  I had to leave a credit card number in case I didn't show, so they can charge you $50 and then apparently they send two $25 gift certificates in the mail if you don't show.  Okay, fine I get it.  Restaurant week is busy, and you want to make sure your reservations show.  They only weird thing is that they can't be bothered to take a credit card normally as they are cash only, but are happy to charge you if you don't show.  

             So, I showed up at about 5:20 alone because my husband had to go back to the car we parked five minutes away to get the wine we'd left there as this place is BYOB.  The restaurant was over half full already, and I think they just opened at 5:00.  I told the host about my reservation and he seated me immediately and brought me some bread and delicious olive butter. Olives are my favorite food, and who doesn't love butter?  So, we were off to a good start.  The server then told me he couldn't find my name on the list.  I was a little panicked because I'd left a credit card number and everything.  Oh, well, he didn't seem too worried about it.  I had my husband ask about it at the end of the meal though because I am a little worried that since they couldn't find it, they'll find it later and mark me as a no show and charge my card.  The server said don't worry about it though because they would call before they charged the card and it probably was a mistake with overbooking.  I'll update if there is a mistake and  I get charged, but hopefully I won't.
         

    The EXCELLENT:  

         Everything was delicious!  From the bread and olive butter to start to the final course.  They immediately brought us an ice bucket for our wine and removed the cork.  I started with the whole grilled artichoke.  The citrus aoli they serve with it really makes the dish.  It was also nice and peppery and had little crystals of sea salt on the outside.  It's not something I'd recommend ordering on a first date because you don't look too elegant eating it, but it was really delicious.  My husband enjoyed his market salad with bacon vinaigrette.  

        I thought was going to order the pyramid pasta going in, but after finding out that the whole menu was up for grabs during restaurant week, including the daily specials, I had a change of heart.  I ordered the fish special which was mahi mahi with a caper sauce with olives, zucchini, tomatoes, fingerling potatoes, and a garnish of lump crab meat.  The sauce was tarty and savory, but not to heavy.  The olives were good quality Mediterranean olives that they had removed the pits from.  It may sound like a no-brainer, but plenty of places throw these in a dish with pits and all making it hard to eat with your food.  The  vegetables were cooked perfectly.  The sauce was so good, I wish they'd brought me a spoon to make sure I got every last drop of it.  My husband had the short rib gnocchi, which he said was rich and enjoyable.  

        Dessert was the perfect finish to a pleasant evening.  The mascarpone on the tiramisu was very light, airy and tasted like it had been freshly whipped.  The lady fingers were soaked in something boozy and espresso, or maybe coffee liquor?  I'm not sure, but it was much stronger than what I'm used to, but I really liked it.  I tasted my husband's cheesecake out of curiosity.  I usually hate cheesecake, but this was light, airy, and delicious!

        The place was packed to the gills, and is tiny, but the service was great and all the courses were spaced out perfectly!  I normally hate it when tables are placed that closely together, but everything was so good that I didn't care and the coziness of this restaurant is part of its charm.  Even with all the weirdness with the reservation, everything worked out well in the end, and it was a very pleasant experience.  I'm giving them five stars because even with the mix up with my reservation, they seated me immediately and made everything right, and the food and service was excellent.   I can't wait to go back and try some of the fresh made pasta!

    This is my favorite restaurant in Philly, hands down.  I've been just about everywhere, and while there are other places that might be better for special occasions, in terms of food quality, price, and atmosphere, Mercato can't be beat for a regular dining spot.

    The restaurant itself is cozy and romantic.  The floors are hardwood, with exposed brick on the walls.  The kitchen is open and right in the back of the dining area.  The space is really tiny, and the tables are very close together, which may seem uncomfortable at first, but it really creates a lovely atmosphere.  In the warmer months, there are outdoor tables that you can sit at as well.  It's a BYOB, so you save some money by bringing your own bottle (or two!).

    The service here is flawless, and the host or hostess will send a text when your table is ready, so you can grab a drink at one of the other bars nearby, or take a stroll through the beautiful, quiet surrounding neighborhoods.  A few things you should know beforehand: (1) Mercato doesn't take reservations, so there is often a wait.  Sometimes it can be pretty long if you are trying to get a table at prime dinner time.  (2) The place is tiny.  There is barely any room.  When it is full, it gets really loud.  There will also not be much space between you and other parties.  If you have a big party, it can take a really long time to get a table because you will need to wait for several parties to get up before they can join the tables. (3) It's cash only, and not exactly cheap, so fill up your wallet beforehand.  If you do forget to bring cash, there is a convenience store a short walk away that has an ATM you can use.  

    The food is where Mercato excels.  I highly recommend the meatballs for an appetizer.  There are really flavorful, and the sauce is one of the best I've had on anything.  The meat and cheese trays are also really delicious, and everything is high-quality.  I brought my father here once, and both of us tried salads and were delicious.  

    For an entree, I always get the bolognese.  Every time I come here, I say I'm going to get something different, but then I see it and I end up ordering the same thing.  I can't say enough good things about the bolognese.  The dish combines pasta with chunks of veal and pork in a cheesy, cream sauce.  The meat is extremely (and I mean EXTREMELY) flavorful, and the noodles are perfect to hold the sauce.  
    At first the portion looks a bit small, but it's really more than enough.  They have a side that combines broccoli rabe with sausage and meatballs and is also one of my favorites.

    Finally, the service is where Mercato really shines and has won me over for life.  Earlier this winter, we decided to go on a cold evening.  We got there and were told it would be a twenty minute wait.  We waited outside under the heat lamps for almost an hour.  At first, we were frustrated about being told such an incorrect estimate, but when we were finally seated, they apologized profusely, and brought out a free meat and cheese tray with other goodies.  When our meatballs were brought out a bit too rare, they cooked the originals more and brought us out an additional order as well.  Overall, I recommend Mercato to absolutely anyone.

    WHOA WHOA - hold the phone... why have i lived in Philly all my life and not been here before??!??!?!! This place is magnificent!!  

    The place is super tiny, cozy, romantic and not 'too serious'.  The staff was wonderful, all taking part in greeting- clearing tables- pouring water, serving bread (the bread- omg we'll get to that later) etc.  All dressed in identical red shirts, the handful of staff combined with the tiny restaurant, and bright view of the kitchen (largely placed right behind the tables) helped give this Italian joint just the right "Italian mothers kitchen" feel.  

    The inside was small, like you might need to wear spanx to get into your table small- but it made for an intimate dining experience.  It was pretty well lit, you didn't need a flash light to read the menu and we got a seat right next to the window.  The lanterns hung by twine, the slow moving ceiling fans, and thick cutting-board like tables were a nice touch.  

    Since we came here for restaurant week- my boyfriend and I each got to pick an appetizer, an entree, and a dessert.  I got the antipasti, he got the charred caesar salad ( romaine hearts, toasted crostini, creamy Parmesan caesar dressing, grilled anchovies, parmesan cheese)  Had I not been wearing a fancy shirt and heels, I would have jumped over the table and devoured his salad.  Phenomenal. Yes I realize I am typing phenomenal for a caesar salad but it was amazing.  The stalk of romaine was charred and served with creamy warm dressing. Perfect.

    Oh the bread I mentioned earlier?? Warm and toasty- served with infused garlic and olive butter. melt.in.your.mouth.

    For entrees- He got the Short Rib Ragu served with ricotta gnocchi, broccoli rabe, shaved locatelli, truffle oil)- a nice portion.  The short rib was flavorful and tender. Another A+ dish. I ordered the Rigatoni Bolognese. Richly flavored pork and sausage, veal in sauteed sauce of olive oil, fresh thyme, garlic and shaved parmesan.  Not your usual bolognese sauce- but veryyy flavorful.     Over all-this place gets 5 stars.  Even though the restaurant could potentially get obnoxiously loud because of its small size and large groups that don't like to use their 'inside voices'-  the amazing food and the BYO factor made the noise an afterthought.

    Dear Marilyn Hagerty,

    I loved your earnest and glowing review of the Olive Garden in the Grand Forks Herald last year.  I am impressed that with your words you were able to capture the attention of Anthony Bourdain and have inspired so many people to re-examine American culture through food (whether it's our own self-criticism about bi-coastal elitism, the over-thinking of degustation, or rethinking restaurant chains in general).  I do believe, though, that if you came to Philly and tried Mercato, you would have a lot to say.

    First, I think you would fall in love with the charm of the area around 12th and Spruce.  I imagine that you would come by around opening time for the restaurant to avoid the later crowds and wait times.  You would enjoy walking down Spruce Street, admiring the brownstones and older architecture, and up the steps to the restaurant and finding it to be a single room with an open kitchen where you can watch the chefs firing up dishes.  You may be surprised to find the host or hostess keeping track of seating via an iPad but then immediately look around to the simple yet elegant details in the restaurant's decor, complete with carvings on the ceiling tiles.  Yes, the ceiling tiles will make the Tuscan arches of the Olive Garden fade away like a bad dream.

    You may be a little disappointed that the bread at Mercato does not quite have the same allure as the hot, processed white oil-covered breadsticks one gets used to at the Olive Garden, but you may be pleased by the herb butter and your ability to bring your own favorite wine here.  No, the waitstaff at Mercato do not wear the crisp black trousers and yellow dish towels you so love at the OG, but you would quickly become impressed at their knowledge of all the ingredients that go into the specials of the day.  

    Now on to the food.  The few black olives offered with the unlimited salad bowl at your Grand Forks eatery would pale in comparison to the flavorful savory broth of the mussels you can start with here; no canned garnishes are needed to add flavor.  Although you found the chicken alfredo ($10.95) at OG to be warm and comforting on a cold winter day, you would find the short rib ragu to be the definition of comfort food ($13-14 for fresh ingredients for a half-portion that is more than enough for one person) with well made gnocchi and broccoli rabe to accompany the tender meat that soaks up the broth.  I would not judge you if you ended up licking the bowl clean and gobbling up one of the succulent seafood pyramids from your dinner companion's pyramid pasta plate.

    After the ragu, you would thank yourself for wearing pants with an elastic waistband in anticipation for splitting the salmon dish, which again is so beautifully cooked, sitting on expertly prepared risotto and kale, all enhanced with the acidity of the lemon butter sauce.  At this point, you may pass out from a food (or wine) coma, but I am sure your dinner companion would inform you that the tiramisu was quite good.

    Ah, Marilyn, how you would love this place.  It may inspire you to move away from Grand Forks, North Dakota and come live / eat / critique in Philly.  One can only hope and dream.

    Much love from one of your biggest fans,
    Jen

    I loved that Mercato has an extensive specials list. In addition, the place is BYOB (also, remember: cash only). I also loved the decor in Mercato-- the butcher's block tables made it all very rustic and the candlelight made it romantic. In addition, it was bright enough to see the menu properly. However, and this was a big downside, the amount of tables that were crammed into the restaurant made it almost impossible to get up from the tables without invading another table's space. Seriously, like half a foot between tables. VERY intimate.

    My boyfriend and I opted for the four course tasting menu, priced at $48. It seemed pricey, but given the fact that the limitations are almost nonexistent (surcharges on only a few items) and that the appetizers are high-priced, we thought it would be worth it. The amount of food we received was absolutely on par with the price. Full-sized courses, except for the pasta course, which was a half plate (thank God, or I would have burst).

    However, with the amount of money that we paid and the apparent authenticity of the menu, I was disappointed in the quality of the dishes we were served. My first course was a pork ravioli (a nightly special), which came out fried, which was surprising. In addition, we weren't informed that the tasting menu offered two ravioli instead of three, which confused us at first. The ravioli weren't anything to rave about, but the dipping sauce/aioli that arrived with it was heavenly. I couldn't get enough of the tangy, fresh taste. One of the highest points of the meal, in my opinion. My boyfriend's octopus salad was simple (not in itself a bad thing) and the octopus was well-prepared, but it erred on the side of simply bland instead of simply elegant. My rigatoni bolognese was tasty and light, but the sauce just was not able to hold its own against the heavy meats, which led to an off-balance dish. The short rib ragu that the boyfriend chose had tasty little gnocchi, but was mostly short rib, which made me wonder why it was listed as a pasta course. We both chose fish for our entrees: he got the scallops and I got the snapper, thinking we needed something light. The fish was well-cooked and the pancetta was yummy, but the onions and mushrooms seemed charred and this reflected poorly in their flavor. Finally, my dessert was the molten chocolate cake, while my boyfriend got the cheese platter. The cake was my second high point of the night-- moist chocolate cake and deliciously decadent chocolate in the middle was a great way to end my not entirely impressed evening. I would go back for the cake, but I would probably skip the rest of the meal.

    After looking at all of the stellar reviews on Yelp and taking a quick look at the menu online, I was excited and ready to have an authentic Italian dining experience. But the quality of the dishes overall was just not up to par for such an expensive place. I have high standards for Italian food, and I did not feel that Mercato lived up to my expectations.
