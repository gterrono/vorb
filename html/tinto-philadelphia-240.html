    I like tapas, but have never been truly blown away by any place I've tried in New York. Not like I was at Tinto in Philly. I came here over Memorial Day weekend on the recommendation of a friend of a friend and was really taken with the quality and attention to detail showcased in everything we tried here:

    - Sopa de castaño (truffled chestnut soup, duck &
    mushroom hash, fried quail egg), $12
    - Duck (duck confit, serrano ham,
    black cherry, la peral spread), $10
    - Scottish salmon (black trumpet escabeche, herbed crème fraîche), $12
    - Chuleta (berkshire pork loin, salbitxada, green onions, pork jus), $18
    - Revuelto de bacalao (soft scrambled egg, black cod, goat cheese, spinach) $13

    The space was beautiful, and although we were told there was a thirty minute wait for two we were seated in about ten minutes while enjoying a glass of wine at the bar without having someone's elbow or purse shoved in our faces every few minutes. There were plenty of people enjoying dinner, but not so much that you couldn't hear yourself talk. A most refreshing change from a typical New York experience at a comparable place on a Saturday night.

    Yeah, so Tinto is delicious, what's new?

    Is weird that a man renowned for his meat served the best bialy I've ever had? It comes with the cured salmon platter, and is really an everything bialy. It's moist and warm and has that sort of doughy fresh-baked bread appeal except in bialy form...and what's even more strange is that it doesn't border on bagel territory. Definitely a bialy. Definitely an everything bialy. Definitely delicious.

    (I ate it plain after eating the rest of the Cured Salmon Platter similarly...because everything was just so darn tasty that I couldn't risk mixing flavors and screwing up food chemistry.)

    The grilled asparagus is served with hollandaise. It's the paragon of asparagus flavor with just the most miniscule of hints of the olive oil it was sauteed in. I dropped a precious spear on the ground, and if my dining partner hadn't witnessed that crime, I would have probably picked it up, wiped it off, and ate it just the same. Yeah. It's that good.

    Brunch at Tinto is a 3 course affair to which you must certainly partake...a first and second course, a side dish, and a dessert? There's no better way to start a day than with Jose Garces' ingenuity.

    Oh, and get the maple grits. Maple syrup on grits more buttery than Amish pretzels served in a mini cast iron skillet is sure to make your mouth happy (and your belly equally fat...but when filled with such love, who really cares?).

    This place is probably my favorite restaurant in Philly!  Amada was one of the first Philadelphia restaurants that I really loved, but I'm definitely prepared to say that Tinto is even better.  While dinner here is definitely not cheap, I can't help but feeling that it was worth every penny each time I get up from my meal.

    Overall, the atmosphere is great.  They have a few high-top tables in their semi-bar area, so if you aren't into that make sure to reserve a table or booth in their dining room or downstairs.  They pack the two-person tables in a little close (so you can't help but hear the conversations of the couples next to you), but once the food arrives you will be oblivious to everything else but the amazing dinner you about to enjoy.

    As far as specific recommendations for dishes, the Chestnut Soup is quite possibly the best single dish I have ever had...EVER!  The only thing that comes close for me is Kobe beef (that costs 6X as much), and I'm not even sure if that can trump it.  If it were socially acceptable, I would just ask them to bring me out a mixing bowl of that soup, and I would devour the entire thing.  I'm also a big fan of all of their flat breads, as well as their short rib sandwich.  Pretty much everything on the menu is great, so you really can't go wrong.

    This place is great, and is definitely worth a try if you haven't gone already.  A bit to pricy to eat there every week, but definitely merits a trip on special evenings.

Tinto features a mouth-watering selection of "pinxtos" (pronounced PEEN-choes), the Basque equivalent of tapas.  Along with pinxtos, Tinto offers a tempting array of charcuterie, cheeses, mariscos (shellfish), brochettes and bocadillos (sandwiches).  

Some of the inspired dishes include:  Duck Confit Montadito, with wild black cherries and bleu de Basque;  Gambas Brochettes, shrimp with grape tomato and sherry espelette chili sauce;  Roasted Rack of Lamb Au Jus, with truffled artichoke puree and candied lemon;  Marmitako de Ventresca, tuna toro, parsley oil, migas and shaved mojama;  Txanguro Montadito, Dungeness crab, avocado and espelette;  and Mousse de Queso, with goat cheese, calabaza, orange, olive oil, caramel and lavender tuille.

History

Established in 2007.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
