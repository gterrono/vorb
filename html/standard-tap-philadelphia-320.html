    Listed in Brunch: My favorite meal of the week

    We've been here for drinks and for brunch, and despite the mixed reviews and staunch opinions over the burger, we have only had great experiences here.  We've always sat downstairs at the bar and received friendly service.  The bloody marys here are top notch - definitely homemade, not a mix out of a bottle - spicy and addictive.  I always feel like I have to try something great off the beer list as well...and as I'm writing this, it is now becoming obvious how quickly trips to the Standard Tap turn into boozy Sunday Fundays.  For brunch, make sure you have something that comes with or has a biscuit (or two) in it - golden buttery goodness, and the jam is fresh, flavor-packed and house made as well.  It can feel a little chaotic here when it gets busy, and we saw some fellow bar patrons' food orders get messed up (by the bartender and by the kitchen), but everything we ordered was spot on and hearty enough to soak up the beers and bloodys.

    Philly Brews! Standard Tap has one of the best selections of Philly(and area) beers in  the area. Kenzinger is standard (clever) with many other rotating beers on tap (double clever).     Listed in Drinking in Philly

    Standard Tap was one of the first places Tom brought me to introduce me to Philly, and it remains a favorite place of ours. My impression is that Standard Tap has been doing what I would call "the right thing" - rotating local craft beers, and a food menu that changes daily based on what is in season and locally available - long before anyone started calling it a "gastropub" - and they continue to do it unpretentiously and without calling attention to themselves as if they were anything more than a regular corner bar. I appreciate that. Know what else I appreciate? No TV! I started keeping a list of bars in Philly with no TVs and the list is surprisingly short. Anyway, more often than not we sit at the bar, but I've had a good experience in the dining room as well. Recently we ordered some antelope skewers, and were pleased that they also came with a delicious side of greens. One can almost feel healthy ordering bar food here! I am quite taken with their fried jalapeños lately, which may not be as healthy but come with this lovely cornbread breading that makes it feel not at all greasy. Naturally, the beer selection is always a treat. If we lived in the neighborhood, we'd be here all the time. Still, it's an easy walk from the Spring Garden stop on the MFL, so we do try to make it here when we can. I hope it sticks around a long, long time, as a stellar example of a damn good bar that does things right.

    I'm sorry.  I just can't get over the fact that they a) don't publish their menu on their website b) don't have printed menus to read at your table and c) the menu does not have any descriptions!

    My husband and I headed there to try the famous burger, but I'd be very reluctant to go again because I'd have no idea what kinds of food they serve or the price range of the menu options.  And I certainly wouldn't suggest it as a group dinner location for the same reasons.  But I digress.  Back to our burger experience.

    We headed up around 2 pm on a Saturday and pretty much had the place to ourselves.  Only one other table was occupied, as well as a few patrons at the bar.  I ordered the PBC Cider and was a little disappointed with it.  It was like a slightly alcohol-y apple juice.  Not a reflection on Standard Tap, but figured I'd share.  

    Both of us got the burger- one with fries and one we substitued the fries with a salad ($3 upcharge, FYI).  I really enjoyed my burger, but I didn't feel like it was the best one I've eaten in the city.  I really liked the roll and the fact that they use monterrey jack cheese.  The "caramelized" onions were not caramelized at all, but were still tasty.  The meat itself was good, but nothing out of the ordinary.  I thought the bun to meat ratio was spot on.

    Service was good- probably they need to hire good people to field all the menu questions!  The waitress explained what was on the burger when we ordered them, which was helpful.

    Would I come back?  Eh, I feel like there are better places to get a burger in the city (and places that are closer to my home in S Philly).  If I had a friend that was dying to check out their burger, I would certainly head up there with them.  But I wouldn't go out of my way to come back.

Specialties

Standard Tap features locally-brewed draft beer.  Our food menu both complements the beer and expands the notion of what tavern food can be.  We use only the best available produce, poultry, meats and seafood -- much of it from area farms and local vendors - to create fresh, seasonal dishes.

Our full menu is available from 4 pm to 1 am, seven days a week.  Brunch is served Sat. & Sun. from 11 am to 3 pm.

Wine and liquor also available.

History

Established in 1999.

When Standard Tap opened its doors in December 1999, our goal was simple:  to create a neighborhood tavern that featured great beer and wholesome food.  With that in mind, we offer a draft-only beer selection that highlights the vast array of styles crafted by breweries located in Pennsylvania, New Jersey and Delaware.  Our food menu both complements the beer and expands the notion of what pub food can be.  We use only the best available produce, poultry, meats and seafood -- much of it from area farms and local vendors - to create fresh, seasonal dishes.
