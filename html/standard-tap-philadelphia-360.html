    Listed in Beer me up, Scotty, Little candle on the table

    Standard Tap is another place brought down for me by high expectations and lots of hype. Was it good? Yeah, it was. Was it the best gastropub ever? Probably not.

    The place itself is kind of tree house-like with lots of rooms and stairways and dark corners. There was even a little phone booth-type enclave (sans the photo booth) on the second floor, which is where we ate. Overall the ambiance is pretty cool and I don't mind the chalkboard menu thing except that it kept me from brainstorming what to eat before I got there.

    Their beer list didn't have much that I don't see at other places around town. We started with the octopus, and it was certainly the standout of the meal. This was my first time having grilled octopus and it was killer--crispy on the outside and incredibly juicy on the inside. The downside was the very prominent vinegar-y taste which almost overpowered it, but the bed of arugula, cauliflower, potatoes, etc. was an upside and it didn't feel like the filler they put with octopus at a lot of other places to fill the plate. Well worth $14.

    For entrees we both split the burger with fries (yum, prepared nicely with sauteed mushrooms and fresh cut fries and a nice bun) and the pulled pork sandwich. The pulled pork sandwich taught me a very valuable Philadelphia pulled pork lesson--when a menu says "pulled pork sandwich" it does NOT mean BBQ pulled pork. In the midwest and the south, a pulled pork sandwich is dry pork with BBQ sauce. In Philadelphia, it means pulled pork, in pork-y juices that sog up the bun, probably with sharp provolone. This is my fault and if you're looking for a Philly pork sandwich this is a good one, but since I was expecting barbeque it just wasn't my favorite part of the meal. Also, it came with house made chips instead of fries which were less good than the fries.

    Anyway, solidly okay. I'd definitely go back. That octopus is wow.

    It's pretty amazing that I've lived in Philly for this long without coming to Standard Tap until this past weekend, but it eventually happened during a late March weekend while starting a NoLibs/Center City bar crawl.  The much celebrated gastropub, which is well known for its food as well as its expansive selection of local and regional craft beers, is a solid joint.  Good food, good drinks, and an unpretentious atmosphere make Standard Tap pleasant and enjoyable.

    Forgoing menus, Standard Tap posts all of its food and drink upon chalkboards that dot the dining room.  The menu features an array of bar snacks, a limited number of sandwiches, and some larger plates.  I decided to opt for the Chicken (pot) Pie, a pretty generously sized puff pastry filled with creamy chicken and vegetables atop a crisp salad.  It was so hot, I had to wait just a little bit for the pie itself to cool down, but the ingredients inside were buttery and savory (and the chicken was pretty moist, not just disguised by the gravy surrounding it).  I wasn't a fan, however, of the awkward mouthfeel created by the raw carrots combined with the soft cooked vegetables inside the pie, but aside from that, this dish was executed quite nicely.  My friend's burger, however, was much better.  The patty was juicy and extremely flavorful.  The fries were also crispy and delicious.

    Our server was nice, although it did take a bit of time for the food to come out from the kitchen.  However, the quality of the food definitely made up for the longer than usual wait.  I also noticed the second floor outdoor patio, which I could only imagine would make a terrific spot for spring and summer daydrinking.  Overall, I was very pleased with my first (of hopefully more than one) experience here and I can't wait to return for brunch, dinner, or just to hang out with friends.

    Yikes, this feels somehow blasphemous to write a 2* review of a much-vaunted institution, but alas, that was the impression it left on me.

    We came by for a couple of drinks and apps over the weekend, and it turned out to be their "big wood" beer week. I was excited to hear that they had a number of oak-aged beers on tap, and available in flights. I ordered one of the flights, and it's definitely my fault for not noticing sooner, as it was right there on the board, but I found it was $15. OK, let me qualify this: it had a beer from Manayunk, one from Sly Fox (suburbs), one from Weyerbacher, and another one--all local brews. These are are not fancy belgians or even particularly special. They are decent beers. Not $15 worth of beer.

    We also ordered the meat and cheese plate, which came with a couple of pieces of toasted bread (perhaps ciabatta), a couple of fanned pieces of hot capicola, three little piece of cheese, and mustard. This was $13. No fig jam, honey, etc. That also seems pretty pricey.

    Service was fine.

    So perhaps the lesson here is that this place is good for stopping in and ordered a beer from their nice draft list, but to be careful with the prices for anything else. At $45 for a flight, a beer, and a meat/cheese plate, it's definitely at the higher end of the price range--and there are lots of better places within a short walk from here.

    Sorry again for my lack of reverence to a storied institution, but I have to call 'em like I see 'em.

Specialties

Standard Tap features locally-brewed draft beer.  Our food menu both complements the beer and expands the notion of what tavern food can be.  We use only the best available produce, poultry, meats and seafood -- much of it from area farms and local vendors - to create fresh, seasonal dishes.

Our full menu is available from 4 pm to 1 am, seven days a week.  Brunch is served Sat. & Sun. from 11 am to 3 pm.

Wine and liquor also available.

History

Established in 1999.

When Standard Tap opened its doors in December 1999, our goal was simple:  to create a neighborhood tavern that featured great beer and wholesome food.  With that in mind, we offer a draft-only beer selection that highlights the vast array of styles crafted by breweries located in Pennsylvania, New Jersey and Delaware.  Our food menu both complements the beer and expands the notion of what pub food can be.  We use only the best available produce, poultry, meats and seafood -- much of it from area farms and local vendors - to create fresh, seasonal dishes.
