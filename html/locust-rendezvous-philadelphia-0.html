    The Good:

    The Vous has some pretty awesome drink specials. Each week they have a different draft beer for $3, a bottled beer for $2, and a shot for $2. and it's good stuff  too. I was enjoying some Blue Moon drafts in all it's orange filled splendor. The Vous also has a pretty good late night menu.     The Bad:

    There's not too much bad by my book, but I love me some dive bars. and this bar hit that mark. I did get to sample the worst of it's jukebox and I'll get to that later. they also had some pretty horrible Budweiser memorabilia that I wasn't big on. I do think that the only truly bad thing for it though is just the fact that Good Dog and Mcglinchy's are the better dive spots to go to in that area.

    The Ugly:

    The fact that there was a group of older men serenading the entire bar with old man rock. We're talking the some pretty horrible musical choices sang very out of tune. And it wasn't even a karaoke night! scary. I also got to find out what burnt Smarties smelled like courtesy of Chrissy R.'s randomly creative pyromania .

    Overall:

    while the ugly moments of the Vous are better left avoided, it does make for a very worthwhile nightly Rendevous.

    Broke? Only have $20 left in your wallet? Fear not, LRV is here to assist you in your time of need.

    Every week, LRV has different specials - a $2 beer, a $3 beer, and a $3 shot - good all day, every day. Sometimes the beer is some weird name you've never heard of, sometimes it's lame like PBR, but a lot of times it's awesome (or just very drinkable). The shot ranges from bubble gum flavored vodka to Jameson. If by chance, the special vodka is banana, I strongly advise against it.

    In addition to the specials, LRV has pitcher specials after 8 p.m., great for sharing (or not). Their regular drink prices aren't bad either.

    If you're hungry, they have food too. Nothing special, just sandwiches, lots of fried options, apps, nachos, and some plate specials. The sandwiches are pretty decent and the wings are similar to Moriarty's in size and almost in quality.

    The crowd here can be very diverse. On any given night, there can be a mix of hipsters, good ol' working class people, punks, young lawyers, sports guys, regular people, old people, trashy people, you name it, all just hanging out enjoying a drink.

    LRV also has a decent jukebox and their restroom is slightly better than other dive bars.

History

Established in 1989.

Locust Rendezvous was established in 1989.  Surviving in an everchanging neighborhood, we have withstood the test of time......Many establishments have come and gone, not sure what they wanted to be when they grew up.  Fortunately, we learned early on that we were just a good bar with great food.  Nothing more, nothing less. Our goal as always, has been to make all our customers feel welcome.   You will find when you come in regularly that there are many familiar faces.  Our customers come from far and wide, remembering the great service, comfortable atmosphere and reasonable prices from their first venture thru our front door.  Repeat business is our mainstay, and all new are welcome.  
Stop in, you'll be pleasantly surprised how nice it is to sit at 'the Vous' and feel like your back in your own neighborhood.....
