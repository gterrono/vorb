    Despite the barrage of restaurant week hate on yelp, I'm still a fan. My strategy is to pick nice, fancy (read: expensive) restaurants that I wouldn't ordinarily go to. This time around, that was Davio's, a place I hadn't actually noticed before but seemed both pricey and delicious.

    When you arrive at Davio's, you have to take an elevator to the 2nd floor, which makes for an immediately exclusive feel.  The lobby/bar area is small, and dark, which adds to the intimacy, though I thought it was a bit bizarre there was a TV hanging over the bar. We made our way to the hostess, and were seated immediately, actually a few minutes before our 8pm reservation.  The restaurant was about 3/4 full, but for some reason seemed very loud to me - I suppose voices echo through the large interior room.

    From the moment we sat down the service was top-notch. We actually had two waiters, which made the service seamless (never were our water glasses low or our dirty plates resting in front of us). The bread and spreads brought out before our meal were excellent - was hard to stop myself from eating all of it.  I had the tomato with buffalo mozzarella as a starter - disappointingly tiny, but my boyfriend had the calamari, which was a generous portion and very, very good. He also aded the cheesesteak spring rolls ($1 off during restaurant week), and raved about them.

    We both ordered the scallops for dinner, which were cooked to perfection, though we only got 3 each (sort of a small portion - but it is restaurant week). It was served over asparagus risotto, which honestly wasn't worth eating. Neither of us adore asparagus anyway, but the risotto seemed dry and bland.

    Now, people often say that restaurant week essentially amounts to free dessert - and ya know what? I love it. Dessert at Davio's was a trio (no choices and you get to try everything - even better!) that included lemon meringue pie, peach panna cotta, and a to-die-for chocolate and pretzel concoction.  We left happy!

Specialties

The concept for Davio's Northern Italian Steakhouse took shape in our Philadelphia, PA location. Situated on the second floor in the historic Provident Bank Building just off fashionable Rittenhouse Row,

Davio's Philadelphia is a jewel in the Philadelphia restaurant world,
winning accolade after accolade, year after year. Our dramatic dining room is the perfect place for business breakfasts, lunches and dinners or special occasions. And our lounge has become a favorite of the weekday wind-down-after-work set.

History

Established in 1985.

In 1985, at the young and ambitious age of 24, Steve DiFillippo purchased a locally-owned family restaurant called Davio's on Newbury Street in Boston's trendy Back Bay. As Boston's culinary tastes evolved, he transformed the location into a destination. He retooled the menu, the space, the wine list. The new Davio's soon garnered rave reviews.

The idea behind Davio's Northern Italian Steakhouse was simple, regional Italian foods with a focus on the grill.
Everything made by hand from the best ingredients. Serve everything from aged steaks to simple-yet-unique pasta creations. Mirror this philosophy in the kitchen with our expert and attentive staff in the dining room, seeing to your every need and taking pride in your enjoyment of the meal. Steve has since opened locations in Foxborough, MA (Patriot Place), Philadelphia, and a new location in Atlanta.

Meet the Business Owner

Steve D. Business Owner

"After 25 years I feel that I am just getting started." says Davio's CEO, Steve DiFillippo. At the age of 24, Steve purchased an established restaurant called Davio's in Boston's Back Bay. And this was the first in a succession of popular restaurants. Steve is involved in the community, serving on several non-profit boards.

In 2008, he was inducted into the Massachusetts Restaurant Hall of Fame.
Steve lives in Wenham, MA, with his wife Pamela and their four children. He runs 4-5 miles a day, "so I can wrongfully delude myself that I can eat anything I want," he says.
