    First to Review

    Vic falls into the "Whole Meal for Under $15" category, and the whole restaurant is the size of a shoe box, but don't let that stop you.

    Their sushi is phenomenal. If you're going for your run of the mill maki (california roll, eel roll, avocado roll, etc) you can indulge in the two or three roll special. Where else can you get a whole bunch of sushi for $7 or $9, respectively? They also offer a 39 piece "party tray" for $30 that I've been itching to order.

    Their special rolls are pretty exciting as well, I recommend you splurge at least once on one of these!

    Overall, Vic is becoming a favorite cheap date. I often call in takeout and eat in the park (their proximity to Rittenhouse is ridiculously convenient) or take advantage of their delivery service--Yes, it's true. They DELIVER!

    The hours for Vic have changed- open until 9pm Mon-Wed, 10pm Thu-Sat. and still closed on Sundays.

    The nigiri sport the perfect smear of wasabi between the fish and the rice. The tuna dumpling appetizer is a pair of delicious slightly larger than a golf ball sized tuna purses enveloping a mix of avocado, spicy sauce and crab stick. The nigiri combo is $16 for 12 pieces +miso. The naruto roll consists of tuna, salmon, and spicy sauce wrapped in cucumber instead of seaweed- Vic cuts a sheet of cucumber by spiraling in from the outside. Although there isn't a lot of variety or adventure in the varieties of fish available, the ridiculous bargain prices, consistently fresh fish and Vic and his wife being awesome people make it a perfect neighborhood joint for frequent casual sushi.  Love it.

    If you request delivery at prime night time, please be patient. Vic is the only one making sushi and there's only one delivery guy. Actually, as of October there is a second guy helping with sushi and the bar has 7 chairs- the last one feels like you're out in no-man's land as you're sitting in front of the cash register rather than the sushi bar, but at least you get to sit and watch the magic. The menu has also grown to include a new page of special rolls- some wrapped in soy paper.

    I just want to start off by saying that I am OBSESSED with this place.

    Vic's is the type of sushi bar where you walk in and immediately feel comfortable. Vic and his wife are always there and greet you with a smile. It's very cozy, mostly because it consists of a 5-seat sushi bar. Most people choose to get take-out or delivery (I know, it's heaven), but I like to go for the atmosphere. I've always enjoyed watching people make my sushi, so it's nice to get to see that up close and personal.

    The sushi itself is amazing, and it's by far the best I've had. All of their ingredients are fresh and high quality. The best part is that he offers sushi specials for the more popular/typical maki (spicy tuna, boston, philadelphia, california, etc): You can get 2 rolls for $6.95 and 3 for $9.95. Don't let that deter you from trying his house special rolls, though. They are very creative, absolutely delicious, and average-priced. Make it a point to try some of his appetizers too. On my most recent visit I shared the seafood salad (I forget the exact name unfortunately) and, of course, it was great.

    Vic's has become a staple in my diet and I'd recommend this place over any other sushi restaurant in the city (and believe me, I've been to my fair share!)

    No question the best sushi in Philly.

    If the large portions, fresh fish, and creative rolls weren't enough, Vic makes you feel like a regular right from the start. The small bar seating means that every dish is prepared and handed to you by the chef, and the quality and exacting technique never wavers.

    There's a good amount of creativity on the menu. From mango and eel rolls, to an extensive use of soy paper as roll wrappers, the food is delicious and original. I suggest any of the specialty rolls which offer heaping portions that are just comfortable enough to fit in your mouth and savor. My favorite is the volcano Roll, which is a spicy tuna roll--with an exceptionally super delicious preparation and mixture of the key ingredients--topped with spicy mayo and a dash of Sriracha Chinese hot sauce. This variation of flavors, and the extra kick, puts this as one of my all-time favorite individual entrees.

    Of course sushi is really the rice component of the meal, and this is where Vic shines. The rice is always perfectly cooked and never mushy, with a slightly above room temperature mouth feel. This makes the rolls soft but not pulpy.

    As a side note, Sagami is often listed as the best sushi in the area. It doesn't hold a candle to Vic. Honestly. Seriously. No lies.

    Let me preface this review by saying that I've been eating sushi since I was about 4 years old.  Probably not healthy, but I can say that I have a good 17 years of solid sushi eating under my belt.  Plus, I come from a family of sushi-mongers.  

    Anyways, I think everything's been summed up by other reviews.  I just thought I'd add some tips.

    1.  To avoid the crowds, go during the week - early (around 5pm) - or stop by for lunch.
    2.  Try something you've never tried - I recommend the TUNA DUMPLINGS.  It's not what you'd expect.
    3.  If you frequent the place, ask Vic to make you something he thinks you'd like.  I did and the rolls were so good he decided to put it on the menu - Princess Roll and a soon to come mango roll.
