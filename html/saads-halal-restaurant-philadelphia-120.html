    I've been wanting to try this place for a while and I am SO GLAD I did!

    I ordered a falafel sandwich with hummus as well as a fish platter with tabouli and potato salad and a sweet potato pie. Definitely not a glutton I've barely made a dent in this food having had dinner last night and lunch this morning. There's just so much food!

    Falafel definitely tasted better the next day with ample hot sauce. It reminded me of the very first falafel I've had a tiny lil place in Wilm. Del called Olive Tree (I was in high school). This was a taste I've been chasing forever with my own cooking and trying various other falafels but none has come close to this. I refuse to go anywhere else now.

    The fish platter was lukewarm by the time I got it home but still delicious. It had about 3+ cups yellow rice with sauteed onions, at least 2 cups of tabouli and potato salad eachpacked in there. There were like 3.5-4 filets of fried fish that barely smelled fishy (meaning it was definitely fresh!) There's also this AMAZING garlicky mayo-type sauce that I've been rationing like nobody's business because it is so good with the rice and fish. Fish was well cooked but on its own wouldn't blow my mind. rice is definitely flavorful and tender. tabouli was a tad underseasoned for my liking but ample amount made up for this. potato salad was above average. oh and the pie was pretty good too.

    The only reason i took off a star for this amazing place is you can't pay with a card unless it's over $30 worth of food purchased. Usually its $20 and I understand but this was annoying since i had to hike to an atm (to avoid additional fees).

    I feel a little weird writing a review for Saad's because I've been eating here ever since it opened and I consider Saad a friend. But good food is good food and he still makes some of the best Middle Eastern food in Philly. And at this point, Saad's is a landmark. A must stop for any foodie.

    Saad's makes the best falafel in the city, hands down. It's big and messy, and loaded with tons of good stuff. For a vegan version, I get the falafel with hummus and tabouli. Otherwise, I like the falafel with feta. Also great are his huge Greek salads and Veg platter. Order an extra pita and you'll have enough food for at least two meals. On the non veg side, the lamb shwarma, kofta kabob, and chicken cheesesteaks are amazing. Also, the half chicken is another 2-mealer when you add extra pita. I know people that swear by his wings and his fish steaks (cheesesteak made with fried fish), which are increasingly hard to find these days.

    Saad's is cash only under $30, but there's an ATM inside that's only a dollar. Fridays are insanely busy, so be prepared to wait for your food and a table. You don't have to speak english to order; Saad is multi-lingual. He also has a no cell phone rule that he takes very seriously, so don't be that person.

    So, hubs and I recently watched the Avengers and had a good laugh at the scene at the end of the film, where the heroes are all seated around a table at a Shwarma place. Leaving the theatre, I remarked how I have never had a shwarma, and hubs tells me he had those when he was once stationed in Saudi and thought they were good, but he hasn't had one since. So we decide a good idea is to locate a place in Philly that has them and have a go at some shwarma. That's how we found Saad's.

    We both entered a nice but small place that had a fast food kind of vibe to it, and walked up to a counter with a nice friendly girl waiting to take our order. Both of us got the lamb shwarma platter (he with macaroni salad & potato salad, I with Baba Ganoush & Tabouli on the side) and sat down at one of the tables to enjoy our meal. Right off the bat we were enjoying the good, tender, tasty lamb with pita. I had only ever tried lamb once before, but didn't like the taste of it. I suppose the place I had tried it at didn't season it well, so I was hesitant about trying lamb again. After the first bites of this lamb however, I was really glad I decided to give lamb another go. The taste and seasoning were very good, and I had no trouble eating it up.

    Usually my hubs eats more food off his platter than I do, and I usually end up taking leftovers home in a box, where he rarely takes anything home because he's already gobbled it all down at the restaurant. This platter had such ample servings for a good price, that even he ended up with quite a bit to take home as well. We bought a couple pieces of baklava to take home with us for later dessert. They were sweet and yummy.

    We will definitely be back in the future for more from Saad's. We also eyed the Marakeesh place right across the street, which we have also added to our to do list. ;)
