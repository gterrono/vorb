    I've been to Alla Spina more times than I can count, including visits for brunch, dinner and happy hour, and I've always had a good experience here.

    My fav time to visit is for happy hour. They take a couple of dollars of all of their draft beers, which shifts the price range to about $3-$10. The selection is great and contains several Italian craft beers that are difficult to get in this area. There is also a small happy hour menu with items like their soft pretzels with beer cheese ($3), mortadella hotdog ($6), pig-head bruschetta ($5), etc. My fav is the pretzels. It sounds so simple, but the spicy beer cheese sauce is amazing. The bartenders are all very nice too, so that makes for an even better experience.

    For brunch, I had the deconstructed salmon and cream cheese bagel, which is presented more like a salad with smoked salmon on top, with a creamy dressing and an everything bagel on the side. It was pretty good. I also got a half dozen of the oysters, which were not as good as those at Oyster House or Route 6, but still good.

    All in all, I highly recommend visiting. Pretty sure you can't go wrong with the food or the beer here.

    Awesome beer menu + Vetri's delicious cuisine. Count me in! Absolutely loved my visit to Alla Spina, the beer menu isn't enormous but it's an expertly picked combination of some fantastic American craft brews that you've liked heard of and some European beers you're not going to find on tap anywhere else. The food was excellent and I especially enjoyed the kale salad and the gnocchi pasta. Deliciousness didn't stop at dinner as the soft serve ice cream was so stellar. Usually I avoid soft serve ice cream on a restaurant dinner menu as I just don't see how it can be worth the price. The texture and flavor here at Alla Spina was really awesome (grape swirl, fantastic. It's a little on the expensive side but I can tell from the beer side of the menu at least that the price is correlated with acquisition of product. The domestic craft beers all coming in a little lower than their counterparts from across the Atlantic.
