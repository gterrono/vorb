    The Sidecar is the place to go for great bar food, and an excellent selection of reasonably priced craft brews.  I wouldn't give such a good rating if I hadn't had multiple great dining/drinking experiences there.  

    Their carnitas are delicious and filling, packed full of pork and flavor, and they're just an appetizer!  

    The mussels are banging, steamed with bits of bacon and plenty of savory herbal goodness.  If you order the mussels you'll want to ask for extra bread because the sauce is so good you'll just want to soak it all up.

    The burgers are more than decent, and they offer an interesting deepfried egg burger, if you're feeling like a little breakfast reprise.  

    Can't forget to mention $4 Truffle Mashed Potatoes, I am hesitant to say just how good a deal this is, both because I would hate for them to raise the price and because I don't want you to go and eat all of them before I do.

    Now last thing I have to say, the service is great!  Just chat up your waiter or waitress a bit and they'll do their best to hook you up with anything you ask for.

    Tons of Fun!  Amazing Food! Great Drinks!

    I like the vibe of this place. The staff is chill, but not too chill that they forget to pay attention to you.

    And the food is good. I'm not a huge meat-eater, but after tasting the pretzel chicken fingers, I was already thinking about how to recreate them at home. The fried poached egg on their signature egg burger is something special. So special, I might consider ordering the egg burger without the actually beef patty. Don't judge.

    For non-meat eaters, the black bean burger won't disappoint, but it also won't be something you crave. It's spicy and has just enough texture to keep it interesting, but it could use a little more cheese and a not-so-toasty bun. I know most people will order fries with their burger, but they actually do a well-dressed side salad.

    Sidecar could probably afford to offer just okay food and drinks -- after all, it doesn't have much competition in its immediate vicinity.  But it doesn't slack off.  The food here is consistently delicious, whether it's the veggie burger, the chicken quesadilla, nachos, sandwiches, pizza, brunch stuff, or whatever else I've sampled here.  The side salads that come with some items have colorful mixed greens -- not sad and pale industrial iceberg lettuce chunks.  The beer selection is full of interesting and changing micro-brew offerings.  Many are hits.  Some are misses.  All are fun to try.  The place is usually packed and when the weather is nice, the outdoor seating area is also packed.  Prices are reasonable.  The staff is professional.  The decor says serious gastropub and it means what it says.  Oh, and it's close to home, so, yeah, I'll be back.

    In a word, Side Car is simply inconsistent.  While they do have some solid options on the menu (I recommend the carnitas in particular), others, like the burgers, are passable but nothing to get excited about.  I've never really understood the hype about this place, although I will give them credit for being one of the first  restaurants to move in south of South.

    If you are looking for a spot that is often very crowded and noisy with decent bar food, than this could be the place for you.  But if you want to have a conversation with your friends and enjoy some serious culinary skills, there are other spots in the Grad Hospital neighborhood that are probably a better option at a similar price point.

    During the Summer, the outdoor seating is a great reprieve from the roar inside, and I believe they are working on expanding to the second floor, which will hopefully provide more room and a somewhat less raucous experience.

    The service is also varies across a wide spectrum, with a couple wait staff who are borderline rude and very slow to serve you, while others are very friendly and attentive.

    To elaborate:…     Summary: damn good food, damn good beer selection, and breakfast burritos from heaven.

    To elaborate: This is another neighborhood place that I love, love, love. So far we've been twice for brunch and once for dinner, and it's one of my favorite brunch places. We always end up getting their bread basket, which changes all the time; the first time it was zucchini bread, the second time it was pumpkin chocolate chip bread, and each time it was delicious. It gets packed for brunch, so the key is in the timing, getting there right when they open at 10:30, or after noon when it starts thinning out. Their regular menu is great, but we almost always end up ordering from the specials board. The one time we went for dinner we got an amazing stew of white beans, escarole, sausage, and mussels as well as a tasty mushroom pizza from the specials board. The beer selection is crazy good and changes frequently. I personally would recommend just drinking beer here, but maybe that's just me; the coffee's not so good, and the liquor selection is meh. Thanks to their taunting Twitter & Facebook updates, Sidecar never strays too far from my radar; on an almost daily basis I'm like, ooo, look what they have at Sidecar today, we've got to go soon.

    Now, their breakfast burritos deserve their own paragraph. Granted, I've only tried one, the Holy Chorizo, but it is so ethereal that is deserves the Holy in its name. Ever since I moved here 3 months ago, I've been on a mostly disappointing quest for breakfast burritos. I'm from Southern California, where I ate a breakfast burrito almost every day and my local taco stand a few blocks from my apartment had 10 different kinds of breakfast burritos. Even the cafeteria at my former workplace had good breakfast burritos. It's just one of those things I crave constantly. And to me, it's street food, something you get from a stand or a walk-up window or a truck, not something you have to sit down at a restaurant and eat with a knife and fork. Unfortunately, Philly food trucks have not caught onto this as far as I can tell, so I'm stuck with the knife and fork variety. That said, Sidecar is the only place I've found so far with a worthy breakfast burrito: spicy, with the perfect proportion of ingredients, and not at all bland. I LOVE IT SO MUCH.

    Anyway, thanks for existing, Sidecar, I'll see you soon!     Read more
