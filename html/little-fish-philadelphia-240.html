    In an of itself, Little Fish might be a slightly better than average BYOB seafood restaurant.  Based on the other Yelp reviews I read prior to my visit, Little Fish was a total disappointment.

    Yes, this little restaurant is a BYO with extremely fresh seafood.  The quality is top notch, that is clear.  It alone carries this rating.  Beyond that, it offers little.  The ambiance is nothing to get excited over - its tiny, gets hot quickly, and the only intimacy you'll get is with the table next to you.  Service is friendly, but extremely slow.  By the time we left, the place was packed, but since it didn't start that way, I don't know what caused  the hold up.  Finally, the portions could be described as "cute" but aren't balanced with the prices charged (even given the quality).

    I could be wrong... the other reviews paint an awesome picture which might have raised my expectations.  I also didn't take advantage of the BYO side, going instead with a coworker from out of town empty handed.  Still, I'm not sure that enough to leave me to wonder if I just visited on a off-night...

    Been to little fish for the tasting menu twice and I am only giving it a 4 because my first experience was mind blowing and I had been dreaming of returning ever since. But this second visit around was lack luster (3ish stars)
    The Sunday tasting menu is a great deal (5 courses for $33+tax). It's really fun since you get whatever you are served and the pacing is fine tuned.
    My first tasting menu visit was a year ago. I can remember that I had this raw salmon soup with pistachios (still dreaming of it) and some gorgeous salad my boyfriend remembers well. Every dish the first visit was astonishingly creative and exciting.
    This second visit was good, but didn't wow me. The first plate was smoked sturgeon in sort of like a sushi roll laid flat. It was okay.
    The chimichurri sauce that comes with the bread is excellent! I could drown in it.
    Scallop and scrimp salad was the best! Fresh, perfectly cooked scallops that melted in my mouth. Sauce was a tad salty.
    Third plate was my least favorite. Some thick, fishy raw albacore tuna with peppers, bacon, cucumbers, and etc. I love bacon, but I'm not sure where it fit into this dish. Fish was...not my favorite.
    Fourth dish was the mackerel with radishes, grapefruit, squid ink foam and etc. which was cooked well and very good overall. This was the only warm dish, except it wasn't warm and it was sort of cold.
    Lastly, the olive oil pound cake with pineapple soaked raisins and candied peanuts. It was okay. I'm not crazy about raisins or pound cake so it wasn't my fav, but it was interesting.
    Overall, the tasting menu is something you should definitely try! But I do not think I will be dreaming of my return quite as much this time.

    I came here on a Tuesday night for a belated birthday dinner with my sister. Little Fish was highly recommended by a few co-workers. I was able to reserve a table for two on OpenTable for 7:00 pm. Unfortunately, due to bad weather, my sister and I arrived about 10 minutes late for our reservation.     When we arrived, we were greeted by a friendly server, who would be the only server on staff. The restaurant is small, cozy, and intimate but the bright white walls and minimalist style make the space feel bigger than it really is. We sat across from the open kitchen and were handed two small hand-written menus. My sister and I decided to split two small plates and one large plate as per our server's recommendation.

    Our server was kind and attentive without being too overbearing. I appreciated that she brought over a few extra pieces of sliced bread over to my sister, who is a notoriously picky eater. I guess the server noticed that she hadn't eaten much throughout the two small plate courses. It was a small detail that I appreciated very much. My sister is also underaged (and I celebrated a little too heavily the night before) so we passed on bringing wine but were offered Pellegrino and bottled soft drinks along with our still water. We ordered a bottle of Pellegrino and two Diet Cokes and read over the small handwritten menu. The menu is divided by a choice of East or West Coast oysters, small plates, and large plates.

    I first started with one West Coast oyster. It was a great start to the meal: refreshing, salty and acidic enough to whet my appetite. The server quickly cleared this plate away and a few minutes later, brought our first small plate: halibut, served sashimi style, in thin, long slices. The fish was drizzled with coconut milk, mango puree, and lime. The chef garnished this first small plate with fresh coconut cubes, crushed peanuts, and hand-torn Thai basil and mint. The plate itself looked a little flat; white fish, white coconut, white sauce, but thankfully the flavor was balanced and light. My brave little sister ate one piece and shuddered. I devoured the plate and thought it was delicious but the plate was too warm for such a delicate starter.

    Next small plate: lobster with za'atar and lamb. The lobster was nicely cooked, perhaps a little too chewy for my sister. The lamb won us both over, I admittedly was skeptical about how the lamb would turn out but it was beautifully cooked. This plate was adorned with black oil-cured olives, roasted new potatoes, and baby watercress. The lobster and lamb small plate was certainly more powerful in flavor than the first plate but overall, I enjoyed the first plate much more.

    Finally, we received our large plate: black cod. It was served atop green lentils, carrot foam, and avocado puree. The cod was perfectly cooked, lightly poached in miso, gently cooked and flaked easily when touched by a fork. The lentils were nicely cooked, a little too mushy for my preference; I like my lentils to have a slight al dente bite. The carrot foam was nicely seasoned and delicious but it was definitely not visually appealing. It was runny and when combined with the thick, but sadly, bland avocado puree, the whole plate became a saucy mess. Despite the lack of visual presentation, this entrée was a great way to finish my birthday meal. The server kindly re-mised us after every course and topped off both our soft drinks and Pellegrino when they were running low in our glasses.

    We passed on dessert at the restaurant but left happy and full. I was very proud of my sister for trying lots of new foods. Her excitement and enthusiasm to try new ingredients coupled with the fresh seafood, balanced seasonings, and kind service made it a great birthday dinner.

    Little things to note from my experience: we sat with our finished plates and silverware for a little too long between courses, especially compared to the other guests around us. The restaurant was about 80% full and there was only one server but I noticed that perhaps the older-looking guests were looked after a little more attentively. Admittedly, I look fairly young and my sister is only seventeen and we did not drink, but despite this, service was still sharp.  On the kitchen/food side, the food could have been presented with a little more finesse. I'm not expecting anything fussy or pretentious but perhaps just an overall cleaner presentation.

    I'd come here again, definitely and would gladly recommend it to anyone who is looking for a great seafood meal to share with a small group or another guest. I definitely want to try and stop by for their Sunday prix fixe menu soon!

    The next course was disappointing.  It was a small piece of swordfish over a bed of potato hash.  I had it without the bacon, so maybe it was better with that if you like pork, but I felt that it was boring and didn't really have interesting flavors going on.  My husband had it with the bacon and wasn't impressed either.  

    The main course was amazing.  It was striped bass over a bed of black lentils with a spring onion puree.  The puree was so fresh tasting and savory. I scraped every last drop of that sauce off my plate with my fork.  I think I could have drank it if they served it up in a bowl.  The lentils were tender, but not too soft, and the fish was fresh and delicious.  

    The desserts at Little Fish usually don't impress me as much as the savory dishes do, but this was a different case on that evening.  We were served an olive oil cake with a pistachio sauce.  Wow!  The cake with light and fluffy like angel food and the sauce was creamy and full of pistachio goodness. If you ever see that on the menu there, get it!
