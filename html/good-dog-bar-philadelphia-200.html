    The food is always Good Dog's saving grace. The sweet potato fries are amazing. The Good Dog burger is fantastic. And my recent favorite is the beet and goat cheese salad.     They always have an excellent beer selection and the music never fails to be awesome. Overall I really want to love Good Dog and give it 5 stars, but I just can't until I have a meal or a drink where I don't have to wave down the server or bartender.

    Old reliable. The Good Dog.

    There is a lot to love about the Good Dog. Go to other reviews to hear about the great beer, the great environment and cool menu. You wont be disappointed. However, to me, the Good Dog will always represent one thing: The Burger Renaissance.  

    The Good Dog is the place I first understood what a burger could be. That it could truly ascend beyond its modest ingredients. That it could be more than simply the sum of its part. Yes, it was The Good Dog which taught me that there are no rules with a burger, that it could be bigger, better and indeed bolder than the boring burgers I grew up with in Jersey.

    The Good Dog was my go-to bar after I turned 21. Friday nite, I would get all done up with my roomies, call a cab and head there. At first, i thought it was just a nice spot with good beers. Only after a number of trips did I decide to even look at the menu. Up and down, I peered the interesting items. I decided to go with my favorite comfort food, the classic burger.

    It seems quaint to say it now that gourmet burgers dominate literally every menu in the city, but wow, this burger truly changed the way I thought of it. The bitter cheese, the hearty meat, the sweet onions. It was so simple, yet so perfect. Biting into the medium rare meat, the juice literally drips down the jaw. Un-effing-real.

    Now, maybe this is hyperbole, or maybe I am simply a glutton far too obsessed with food, but I can tell you kind reader- without a hint of inauthenticity-   that enjoying this burger completely changed my outlook not just on burgers but in fact food in general. To that point I cooked easy meals and thought of food as fuel.

    A lot of people say "Eat to live, dont live to eat." I propose that those people have never eaten a Good Dog burger.
