    I wanted to love this restaurant. And based on the food I should have. However, an hour and fifteen minute wait with a reservation and unhelpful/unapologetic management ruined our experience.
        We went on a Saturday night (group of 4 girls, 2 of whom were on their first trip to Philly) with an 8:45 reservation. We arrived on time and were told there was a 30 minute wait even with our reservation. So we were pointed towards the bar and ordered some drinks. After 45 minutes of waiting, I went over to talk to the hostess. She explained that tables were not leaving when they expected and there were still 6 groups ahead of us. She offered to send the manager over to us and said she would try and comp us something. No one ever came to talk to us. After an hour of waiting I asked to speak to the manager again...no one came over yet again.
        Finally after 1 hour and 15 minutes, we were seated. After the 3rd attempt to speak with a manger, she came to our table. She did not seem concerned that we had waited so long and frankly gave us a bit of an attitude. We got a generic "sorry for the wait" and 2 orders of free dumplings out of the whole ordeal. Not what I would expect from an establishment that made its customers wait over an hour for a reservation.
       Our waitress and hostess were very friendly and apologetic but could not make up for the long wait and mistreatment by management. The food was delicious, especially the edamame dumplings, pork buns, and korean bbq beef. I would love to return for the food, but I'm not sure I would risk another very long wait and inconsiderate service. A shame really, because those pork buns were the best I've ever had...

    Listed in My Bucket List (Pt. II), The Few, The Proud... (US Edition), The Phew, The Proud..., Don't Die Before You Try..., Celebri-chefs

    I just had my first (of many) Sampan experiences tonight, and this review might be a little inflated because I'm still on a food high, but I'm gonna write it anyway.

    First off, we got here for their amazing Happy Hour (4-7 every day) and had some cocktails. The cocktail was their special for tonight, pear juice with bacardi limon, sour mix and some grenadine. I gave this a try and immediately decided I needed to stick with that because their special was indeed very special.

    It was cool to enjoy cocktails while watching Michael Schulson and the staff in the kitchen prepping for the dinner rush.

    The food was all amazing. Very inspired. We sat at the kitchen bar, so we got to see a very busy kitchen making some very delicious-looking dishes. Everything we had was fantastic. I had Brussels sprouts for the second time in my life (and in the past two weeks, for that matter) and they were definitely one of the most delicious things I can remember having.

    I had a Kobe beef slider which was fantastic. I had mine rare (the way to go) while the server neglected to ask my fiance how she wanted hers cooked which resulted in a medium burger that she did not enjoy. Aside from that snafu and disappearing at times, our waitress was friendly, knowledgeable and very helpful

    I also had the Philly Cheesesteak- fantastic.

    The only thing I had the whole night (there was a lot of trying of other people's dishes going on) that I wasn't really blown away by was the duck.  For a place with such unique dishes, their duck entree (one of the pricier options) was a bit bland.

    Watching the kitchen, I found so many things I want to try, though and will definitely be back here soon. If, for nothing else, their amazing happy hour!

    This past week, a girlfriend and I went to Sampan to check it out and partake in Yelp Cocktail Week (note to Sampan, in case you care - great move participating in the yelp event, otherwise I most likely would not have eaten at your restaurant tonight. And now, because of it, I will probably return. So way to go for successful "advertising"!). Overall, I had an enjoyable dinner for a good price in a fun place.

    Why only 3 stars? For one, my friend and I were seated at a table RIGHT next to one of the only other two-tops in the entire section of the restaurant. We could have at least had one table between us, even that would have been better. To be fair, either of us could have said something, but really it shouldn't have ever been at issue. Secondly, the wait staff was friendly but slightly arrogant, if that's the right word. I was trying to ask questions about the menu and was consistently given unhelpful response. Example: "how much tuna do I get with the order?" "3 crackers." Now, the tuna was fantastic, but the crackers were not your average crackers, so that answer did not in any way describe how much I was actually getting. I know it sounds like I'm nitpicking here, but stuff like that really does bother me. I also thought the waiter was a tad condescending when he ask if I wanted "another YELP cocktail." Yes, I would like another, and no, I don't care that you're annoyed that I'm getting it for half off. That's the point.

    Now for the good - the food was all very tasty, the presentation of everything was awesome and, for the most part, the portion sizes were fair. The half-priced cocktail - Junmai Fizz, I think it was called - was delicious, and a steal at $4 each. For two girls with average appetites we were able to eat just the right amount, have a little left over, and spend under $35 each. If I were to go with someone who was hungrier or ate more (aka a boy), I think it would be fairly expensive to fill up, but for us it was perfect.

    I am definitely interested in going back, most likely for happy hour. I would recommend Sampan, just can't quite give it 4 stars yet.

    Sunday dim sum brunch was fun, mid-scale casual.  At $6 a dish, I'm not sure why this garners $$$ instead of $$.  Four of us spent $100 including a round of cocktails.

    The Bloody Mary's were ample and unusual.  They were supposedly made with wasabi, but seemed like regular horseradish to me.  Rim was not only salted, but included wasabi powder and seeds.

    The Bao was good, but the corned beef dim-sum stole the show.  The description seemed like an unappetizing fusion, but how wrong I was.  The soup dumplings were a novel concept, but execution was lacking -- all the liquid had seeped out of the dumplings and the filling lacked the complexity of flavors described in the menu.

    Services was fast, attentive and friendly, but not overbearing.  I was surprised the restaurant was pretty empty when we arrived at 12:30, but the dim sum brunch is a relatively new addition.  I expect it will become more crowded as word spreads.

    I had hoped to get authentic dim sum on this trip to Philly, and expected to underwhelmed by this compromise with my spouse who wanted to avoid a loud, brightly lit traditional Chinese restaurant.  But I ended up being pleasantly surprised and will definitely visit again!
