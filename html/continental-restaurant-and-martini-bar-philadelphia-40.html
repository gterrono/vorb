    Awesome. Really, seriously awesome. Continental is a Jetsons-style futuristic 50's Diner-type martini bar. Yeah.

    Screwing up this kind of theme and making it look tacky and cheesy is an extremely easy thing to do. Making it look "fun" and "different" while not tacky and kitsch has only been accomplished once in my experience - at The Standard Hotel in Los Angeles. But, Continental pulls it off perfectly. You sit under giant olives with huge toothpicks tying them to the ceiling (see picture). But it doesn't startle you. Awesome.

    There is a front-bar next the dining area and back-bar for darker, more intimate times (supposedly). My friend who recommended this place says they pour a mean martini and I don't doubt it, but I didn't have one myself.

    We stumbled in here about 1am on an average Saturday night in March. It was cold out and we were hungry. Contintental hit everything spot on. In sum, we ordered the following dishes (in this actual order):

    Irish Coffee
    Dessert
    Edamame
    Mexican Corn on the Cob
    Dessert
    A Mojito Martini

    The waitress was not phased at all and happily obliged. Everything was good. Everything. The dessert is of special note. They come out with a large display holding about 30 small glasses of various desserts. You order one of the display and they simply hand you that glass and a spoon. Doesn't matter if its a mango dessert or a chocolate mousse. It's awesome, it's small in portion (good after a meal), and its cheap ($4 or so).

    Overall, a really pleasant surprise and good conclusion to a night out.

    It's overrated for sure, but I do enjoy people-watching from the street-side seating. The view isn't the most thrilling (you'll see mostly oncoming traffic, and get to inhale their vapors) but if the weather is good enough, you're golden. If you sit inside (this venue can handle a decent-sized party), check out the nifty olive lights, and bask in the diner-like decor. (Note their menu isn't diner-like at all, unfortunately.) Specialties

This stainless steel 60's-style diner offers an exciting menu of global cuisine, including crab pad thai, cheesesteak eggrolls and lobster mashed potatoes. Famed for its sensational cocktails, the extensive bar features a wide variety of libations and over 15 types of martinis.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
