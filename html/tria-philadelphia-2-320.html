    Tria Café is a wine and cheese bar (seems to be all the rage in Philadelphia).  They also serve craft beers.  We read about it as we were planning our trip to Philadelphia for the holidays.  My wife and I visited the Washington Square West location and were pleasantly surprised and educated.  They had a nice selection of cheeses and not one but two hostesses were courteous and explained the types of cheeses and what went well with them.  Although, we did not choose the wine, they were helpful in selecting beers that would complement the cheese we chose.

    Listed in Philadelphia, je t'aime (Or best of the best), Beer me up, Scotty, Little candle on the table, Happy Hour in Philly

    Tria has been way at the top (okay okay, THE top) of my list of places to try in Philly, but it occupies a weird niche that I didn't know where to place in my lunch or dinner routine. I never wanted to have a dinner of cheese and light bites and could never find the time for Sunday school.

    Last night we stopped in for drinks/cheese before dinner at Varga bar, which is a perfect geographical match since they're only blocks away on Spruce. I got the Allagash White (props though for having Tripel Karmeliet, one of my favorite beers ever) and the boyfriend got the Bell's Two Hearted Ale. We went with the tete de moine cheese based on our server's recommendation, and it came beautifully shaved into a flower formation with spicy honey and sliced baguette. The cheese itself was on the bland side, but with the presentation and accompaniments it was perfect with my beer.

    I expected the quality of food and drink, so the service is what really blew me away. We had three people at our table (team service, I'm guessing?) making suggestions, clearing crumbs, and bringing more bread before we even asked for it. After we asked for the check they cleared our plates and brought us glasses of ice water to finish off, which is usually when a server ignores you for greener pastures. The best part was that even with this excellent, attentive service the atmosphere was such that we never felt rushed or bothered. This place is surprisingly not pretentious. You will not feel less cool than other patrons.

    I can't wait to be back for happy hour: five items for $5 each 5-7pm Monday-Thursday.

    Listed in A+ Dinners

    I see that it's been some time since I've raved about Tria. It's still perfect! I go here about twice as often as I go to every other bar in Philadelphia combined. Here are some things that make Tria the best:

    -There isn't a bad item on the menu. Every once in awhile I'll order something that isn't to my taste, but you could randomize the menu via computer, order whatever drinks, cheeses and snacks come up first, and be completely assured that you will have a high-quality, lovely meal.

    -The staff is so great: knowledgeable and nice and willing to listen to your preferences and suggest just exactly the right thing. Sometimes they suggest just exactly the right thing with nary a clue from you at all; it's kind of uncanny.

    -Deals! On weekdays at happy hour time, they offer a selection of menu items (usually a red wine, a white, a cheese and two different beers) for $5 each. All day on Sunday, there's a wine, a beer and a cheese for half price. I trust Tria a lot, so I usually just get whatever's on special without thinking twice - you can try all kinds of delicious, well-chosen stuff for a pittance.

    -The rewards card. On the plus side, you get gift cards after you spend a certain amount, as well as prizes for trying x different wines, beers and cheeses. The minus is that every time you use the card, you can see a lifetime running total of how much you've spent at Tria, and the number can be downright staggering. Don't you think, though, that drinking and eating really well is easily as responsible a way to spend hard-earned cash as, say, paying down student loans? Yeah, me too.

    Tria's top-shelf menu would be enough to bring me there frequently at full price, but when you factor in the top-shelf service and discounts, it's hard to justify going anyplace else.

    I have been walking past Tria for years but never thought that I'd want to go inside.

    I don't drink. You could be giving me the best wine/beer in the world and I wouldn't know how to appreciate it. I also hate patroning establishments who give me small plates of food and charge me a bundle for them. Compounding with my mild claustrophobia and the place is tight especially on a winter's night when everyone has their bulky coats on, it was difficult for me. It's not until my friends told me that the place is legit that I've thought about checking them out and I have to say I truly liked it.

    Again, the place is small and there's not a lot of space for you to mosey about, but the waitresses' attentiveness more than make up for it. From the menu, you'll see that some items are quite similar to others, given their small kitchen you'd understand that they can't have really elaborated dishes, but what they do, they do them well. My favorites include their poached figs with gorgonzola and prosciutto, pistachio ricotta and lavendar honey bruschetta and truffled mushroom fontina bruschetta.

    I love, love the categorizing of their wine, cheese and beer menus. Instead of the same old boring categories like red and white, there are more detail descriptions. For cheese, there are racy, stinky and approachable. For beer, there are invigorating, friendly, and profound. I would recommend people to get a cheese plate. I got a plate of 3 cheeses once and they all come with their own little condiment like a fig paste, mustard or toasted almonds that makes the experience so much better. One of the cheeses I had was cut into the shape of a flower, it was stunning and delicious.

    If you come to Tria, do try to come Sunday night to try their Sunday school special. For a little more than half the usual price, you'll get to try a new cheese, a new wine or a new beer. It is indeed an education. I'm looking forward to the warmer weather, when people are wearing less clothes it may seems less stuffy inside.

    Go try it.

    I went here to meet someone for drinks, and was worried at first because I've heard how crowded it can get, but was delightfully surprised when we were able to get a table immediately.
    Though absent of a host stand, there was a very friendly, attentive host to greet us as soon as we entered. He definitely made it seem as if I was another regular customer rather than a first time visitor. Having a great deal of experience in the restaurant industry I know how difficult this can be especially during a hectic Friday service.
    The tables were close together, but alas it is Center City and space is a premium, and there was certainly enough room to get up, stretch out a bit, and not knock someone else's glass over. The décor was modern, but modern in a sense that Tria still made itself out to be very warm and inviting, though clean and sleek. The place was darkly lit and light was provided at the table by candlelight, always a nice touch.
    I wish I was here to eat dinner or for snacks, because the food I saw looked delicious, and very fresh. Judging by the open kitchen this seems like a place that prides itself in the food it prepares. The items on the menu, especially the cheese list, makes me regret not ordering something, but I most certainly will upon my next visit.
    The beer and wine list was extensive, and uniquely described. Instead of describing their sections by location, or by type, they used adjectives such as "invigorating" or "bubbly" and they were, surprisingly, very accurate, and the menus being on clipboards was a nice touch indeed.
    The staff was friendly and knowledgeable, and very quick especially for a Friday and how crowded the restaurant was. They also seemed to take pride in that knowledge, which is always beneficial in my opinion.
    My only two complaints, and they're minor as I'm giving this place a 5 star review, are that the prices are slightly steep as they aren't a liquor dispensing establishment meaning they do not have to pay for the infamously expensive Philadelphia liquor license, and they should pass that onto their customers, and the other is that they do not take reservations.
    I definitely did enjoy my experience here, however, and look forward to returning.

Specialties

Welcome to Tria, Philadelphia's wine, cheese and beer cafe. We serve real wines, cheeses and beers crafted by passionate people. Our ever-changing fermentation menus are complemented by a cafe menu served from opening to late night. Don't know the difference between Cabernet and Carmignano? Don't worry. Our friendly, knowledgeable staff will help guide you through a memorable wine, cheese and beer experience.
