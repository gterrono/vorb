    Listed in I Love Gastropubs

    You can't get a better selection of Belgian beers anywhere in the city.  Tap list is a little static, but if you've got a good server to help you navigate - go bottle.  Prices are very fair, and the beer, well, it's exceptional.

    Unfortunately, the food is so-so - fries being the exception (they are great).

    It gets nuts in here on the weekends, but it's sort of fun when it's packed!  There's always lots of stereotypical Olde City folks to poke fun at :)  If possible, sit upstairs.  It's much cozier, and although the coffin table (you'll know it when you see it) is awkwardly low, I find it makes for an "unconventional" conversation.

    I recommend: pre-dinner beers and fries or post-dinner beers and beers.

    Went in here for lunch on a Saturday.  It was kind of empty when I arrived, but started to fill up by the time I left.  It's somewhat of an institution I guess, and judging from the number of reviews, it is very popular.

    The bartender was kind of sassy and knew some of the other customers by name.  He was helpful enough when I ordered from their gigantic list of Belgian beers, but he wasn't real warm and friendly.  No big deal, but so many bartenders are warmer.

    I ordered the mussels mariniere, you gotta do that in a Belgian beer bar!  Unfortunately 2 of them hadn't opened, and 2 of them had shards of shell in them.  I actually bit down on a shard, and that was not good.  The rest of the mussels were a tad "firm", not delicate at all.  The twice-cooked fries were delightful but the dipping sauces were bland.  One tasted like someone ground some pepper into some mayo and the other tasted like watery French dressing.

    The ambience is nice, the place reminds you of a cool neighborhood bar, but my experience was not pleasant at all, I won't be back.

    Boyfriend and I had a wonderful dinner date last night at Eulogy before our movie. We both enjoy beer and heard they had great food so we were excited to test it out.

    We arrived a little before 6:30 so it wasn't crowded yet. It's a little hard to find the door - they have weird curtains hanging to keep the cold out, we weren't sure it was open but we kind of pushed right in! The seating for food is upstairs - we went right up and waited for a waitress to follow us up so she could seat us. It was nice and quiet, just dark enough to be cozy and very intimate. My boyfriend ordered off of the beer list and I had the Eulogy Busty Blonde, which I really enjoyed. I ordered the hanger steak sandwich for dinner and he had a burger, and we both loved our food. I was JUST full enough without being too stuffed. My only complaint about the food was that the bread they served my sandwich on was hard - not too hard to eat, but just a little too hard to keep the toppings in! It just made it a little messy to eat, which would have been embarrassing if I was on a first date or something. We had a while to kill before the movie and the waitress did not try to rush us at all, even though more people kept coming up to be seated and they were running out of tables. She was very nice and just the right amount of attentive without being too overbearing.

    The only thing that is really keeping me from giving Eulogy 5 stars is the set up upstairs... it was really nice and intimate at first, but some louder guys were eventually seated next to us and at times I felt like we were all at dinner together. It was hard not to be distracted by their conversation. I know the area is small but it was a noticeable distraction during dinner.

    Otherwise the food was excellent, the beer selection was awesome and I am really excited to come back and hang out at the bar sometime soon!

Specialties

This is Beer Heaven, give a "high praise" Eulogy for your favorite beer after you consume it.  We also prepare our food in the authentic Flemish Belgian Style as opposed to other Belgian restaurants who choose the French Walloon Belgian style.  This means our fries are thick enough to truly hold a double fry technique, our fries go "crunch, goo" because they are the right thickness, the wimpy skinny fries elsewhere go "goo goo goo" because they are too skinny and the double fry technique does not work.  Also our mussels are larger than the PEIs other restaurants use, this is because our mussels are the authentic North Sea size like in Belgium.  The more meat the more flavor the mussel can hold in our ala minute pan technique (all ingredients added fresh, never ever ever any pour over sauce on mussels).  Also our handburgers are award winning based on seven secret spices and our fish and chips and other entrees are as good as the beers ! Make a reservation at www.eulogybar.com

History

Established in 2002.

My family is from Brugge, Belgium, I am passionate about Belgian beer, food and heritage, this adds an authenticity to the business.  I got the building in 2002 and built the restaurant myself using antique stain glass behind the bar from an old Philadelphia church and a chandelier from an old church as well.  The bar was constructed in Fishtown in Philadelphia by a local carpenter using Honduras Mahogany I imported and the design of the bar was based on a blueprint I drew.  
We take our beer very very very serious, our beer starts in a beer cooler and is then delivered via dual gas blenders using co2 and nitrogen blends for the proper carbonation technique.  Most of our draft beer never travels more than six feet but along that journey there are glycol filled lines keeping the beer at 40 ish degrees until it hits your glass.  We have about 200 different glasses we keep in stock because most of the taste for beer is accomplished through your smelling it, yes your nose!
