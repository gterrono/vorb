        Listed in 2013:  LA's Yelp 100 Challenge

        After going to two different bars on Memorial Night that happened to be closed for the holiday, my husband and I ran into this one walking around aimlessly for a place that looked cool to grab some drinks at.  The decor inside definitely makes you take a few seconds to soak in the mysteriousness of what you just walked into.  There's a huge chandelier type thing of various pastel lights hanging from the ceiling.  The booths are very modern and stylish on the first floor.  Then you look up and see swings hanging from the ceiling that serve as dining chairs on the second floor.  We definitely had to sit up there for the experience.

        The menu looked interesting, but we weren't hungry for food.  More so thirsty for drinks.  Their martinis are inspired by candy and are DELICIOUS!  This is the place for you if you like fruity cocktails.  They have "dessert tapas".  We ended up ordering 3 different desserts:

        Banana dumplings:  good, but nothing unique

        Smores bar:  Graham cracker crust, topped with what tasted like chocolate pudding but with a thicker texture, and finished off with a dollop of marshmallow frosting.  This was surprising delicious.  I expected the chocolate to be overpowering and fudge like, but it was very smooth.  Highly recommend.

        Pretzels & Beer:  This took the concept of salted caramel outside the box.  3 small soft pretzel bites sprinkled with sea salt and accompanied by chocolate guiness beer ice cream.  My husband and I are determined to find this chocolate guiness beer ice cream somewhere...and if not sold, we're going to find a way to make it.  It's that good!  I highly recommend you get one of the chocolate martinis to accompany this dessert.  We paired it with the Midtown Mocha and OMG...I was in heaven...almost orgasmic for me!

        And there's also a roof top bar area with a fire pit.  Walk all the way to the back of the restaurant and the elevator will take you up there.  Cool place!

        My experience with Steven Starr restaurants has been that you pay more for the ambience than the food. In this case, however, I'd say the food measures up to the ambience.

        A friend and I made our way to the rooftop bar after work. We ordered tapas and cocktails, and each was delicious. Continental gets creative with their mixology, and comes up with some really delicious cocktails in the process. My favorite by far is the Perfect Pear: grey goose la poire, st germain, lemon, and soda. I'm also a fan of the champagne o-rama: champagne, grey goose, served in a flute rimmed with grenadine sugar.

        The chicken tikka masala is delicious. Not too spicy, and comes with basmati rice dotted with raisins and nuts. We also tried the ahi tuna tartare, which comes with sesame chips - this is probably my favorite tapas dish. Our other two orders were the soy-glazed pork belly and rock shrimp tacos. I wasn't that big a fan of the pork belly - or maybe it was because by the time that was served, we were too full to enjoy the dish.

        Overall, the experience is worth the price. If you elect to sit inside, there are these basket chairs hanging from the ceiling on the second floor that make for a unique dining experience.

        I always forget about this place, I think because I haven't figured out what it is - restaurant or lounge, rooftop or recessed seating, tapas or entrees, global or local kitsch, mod or modern, inviting or exclusive?  I suppose it's a little bit of all of that, and if you can look past it trying to be something different for everyone, you'll have a nice time and walk away satisfied, and full.

        My most recent brush with this restaurant/lounge enigma was Tuesday night.  I was down in the dumps and hanging out with a good friend, who suggested we find somewhere for tapas.  One quick yelp search later, we found ourselves here.  It was very empty at 5:30p - too early for dinner and no happy hour offerings - but we didn't mind the mellow vibe.  We were seated upstairs.  So many different furniture themes happening in this joint, it's almost overwhelming; but if this is your first visit, it keeps it interesting.

        We ordered our drinks - rose for me, IPA for my bud - and then our food.  The menu is pretty eclectic, and the server did a good job of giving a quick overview.  Lots of it sounded great, but we settled on the following:
        1. Cheesesteak egg rolls.  I don't seek out cheesesteaks because that's like law-of-attracting a heart attack on yourself, but these bites are just the right amount of meat and cheese without making you feel like a porker.  The egg roll shell was perfect.
        2. Tuna tartare.  Complete with airy, crunchy, sesame chip-like things.  I almost never seek out tartare because it's raw and scary and like inviting a giant mean bacteria with hair and teeth to set up camp in your warm and comforting intestines, but this pile of raw tuna was delicious and had absolutely no side effects, other than making me sad when it was gone.
        3. Mushroom flatbread.  Who doesn't like bread?  No one.  Even you gluten-frees still like it, even if you don't eat it.  And yes, I know a lot of people don't like mushrooms, but those people are dumb.  This flatbread was savory and amazing - a little oily from the cheese, but nothing a blot or two couldn't take care of.  Fungus rules.
        4. Lettuce wraps.  My only other lettuce wrap experience has been at that big chain Chinese restaurant that rhymes with B.Heff. Trang's, and I always walk away from that so thirsty that I could feasibly drain the Schuylkill.  The lettuce wraps here are far superior and not one bit thirst-inducing.  (My insides are thankful, as is that dirty excuse for a river.)  Great lime and lemongrass-y flavors, and the meat tasted fresh - maybe because they leave it pretty neutral and let you determine how much sauce to douse on it.

        You'd have to be a pretty bad orderer to leave here dissatisfied with the food.  It's not Courdon Bleu-worthy, but it's great for what it is - small tastes from many cuisines.
