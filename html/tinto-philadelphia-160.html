    I would give the food 5 stars, but the service was pretty disappointing. It was a Sunday evening, during Restaurant Week and we got there at 5:45, had reservations. I can understand the Restaurant Week must suck for restaurants sometimes- you get people who wouldn't normally go there who are just looking for a deal, who don't order drinks, who don't tip well... But if they aren't going to do a good job, why sign on to do a Restaurant Week menu?

    So, first of all, they tried to put us in the very dark, very secluded basement even though it was 5:45 on a Sunday. When I pointed out all the empty tables in the dining room, they put us at a tiny bar table in the lounge area. Alright, I guess that's a little better.  Two women don't usually get great treatment in restaurants, we should be used to it. The next problem was that all of the food came out at once. The food was great, and it would have been really nice to take a little more time with it before the next thing showed up. Of course all the plates didn't fit on the little bar table, probably because that kind of table is better suited for happy hour drinks and one or two tapas than for a full dinner, and because all of those dishes should not come out at once.

    Maybe it was because it was Sunday and they didn't have the best people working in the kitchen, but it should have been paced better. The waiters seemed irritated with us for not cleaning our plates in the allotted time. I actually had to ask one of the waiters to wait until we were done with the plates on our teeny tiny table when he brought out the next thing. "Yeah, but it's gonna get cold" he said, like it was my fault they cooked it way too soon. I pointed out that it could get cold if he waited to bring it out, or get overcooked under the lamp, or it could get cold on our table, or the other things we were eating could get cold. Unless we ate as fast as we possibly could, there really wasn't a way to avoid this.

    I know pacing is really hard, but maybe prioritize with things that suffer the most from being cold/overcooked? I wish the sea bass had come out first, and the duck confit had been under the lamp instead of the other way around. Wouldn't you rather have a light pan-seared piece of fish with salsa verde *before* a rich duck confit with cherries, anyway?

    But yeah, they food was great. I haven't been to Amada in a while but I think Tinto was better. The green beans were outstanding, and the asparagus were perfectly cooked. I really appreciate when restaurants cook vegetables carefully and thoughtfully. The arugula salad with figs and goat cheese was also wonderful. I hope to come back soon and sit at a real table for a more leisurely paced meal.

Specialties

Tinto features a mouth-watering selection of "pinxtos" (pronounced PEEN-choes), the Basque equivalent of tapas.  Along with pinxtos, Tinto offers a tempting array of charcuterie, cheeses, mariscos (shellfish), brochettes and bocadillos (sandwiches).  

Some of the inspired dishes include:  Duck Confit Montadito, with wild black cherries and bleu de Basque;  Gambas Brochettes, shrimp with grape tomato and sherry espelette chili sauce;  Roasted Rack of Lamb Au Jus, with truffled artichoke puree and candied lemon;  Marmitako de Ventresca, tuna toro, parsley oil, migas and shaved mojama;  Txanguro Montadito, Dungeness crab, avocado and espelette;  and Mousse de Queso, with goat cheese, calabaza, orange, olive oil, caramel and lavender tuille.

History

Established in 2007.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
