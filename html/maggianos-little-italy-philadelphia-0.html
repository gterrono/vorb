    If you are against chain restaurants and Starbucks, yes you obviously shouldn't come here. If you are a tourist, you should proably try something you have in your own city.

    Having said that, let's rate Maggiano's for what it is: a national franchaise.

    I am an avid fan of authentic Italian food, so naturally I know what to expect when I make reservations at Maggiano's, and for what it is, it is five stars.

    The food is is really great (not like Hosterio, though :)). Sometimes when I have a craving I will just come in and order the fried zucchini appetizer (coated with Parm and bread crumbs and you dip them in a lemon ranch-like sauce). I could eat those all day.

    Their  Rigatoni D (like chicken marsala but mixed with pasta) is incredible and their lemon chicken is one of their best, too.

    Believe it or not, their Chopped Salad is something you should be eating right now (avocado and some type of creamy dressing).

    The food is not terrible and is definitely on par with Italian Bistro (instead of the horror that is Olive Garden), but I have a difficult time ranking better than average Italian places all that high.  Seriously?  How can you screw up your standard Italian food?  As a dago, I'm just sayin'...the food of my people is not that terribly difficult to make.  I can crank out chicken marsala while drunk, stoned, blinded, hearing impaired and with two broken kneecaps, let alone a few pounds of pasta and marinara.

    With that being said, Maggiano's gets more stars for their staff than their food.  These people are on the freakin' ball.  Over winter break, a friend and I organized a group dinner for the girls in one of our student groups.  Our server kicked ass with all of  our difficult requests (we had meat eaters, vegetarians and a vegan), and she even had the cooks custom make an appetizer for me.  On top of it, she charged me the kids' price (because they don't have a lot of vegan options there) and personally made me a chocolate martini, since there were no vegan desserts.  

    Basically: the worst service I have gotten there is still 10x better than most restaurants in the city.

    A++++

    It's good for groups who like to pick at one another's dishes, and their cocktails are usually good, with the exception of one time I was there.  I got a watermelon (?) faux-tini after two very stressful days.  It was okay (tasted like it was made with watered down Absolut), but once my fork hit my mouth, the drink tasted really off.  My friends agreed with me on this.  I'm not sure if the bartender was new or having a bad day, but I've had plenty of excellent cocktails there to make up for it.  (The above mentioned chocolate-tini was delicious.  Just enough sweet, just enough booze and just heavy enough to feel like I wasn't getting shafted as the rest of our party inhaled their desserts.)

    If you're looking for a fairly inexpensive dinner and want a lot of variety, I'd recommend it.  Even though there are certainly better Italian restaurants in the city, it's certainly not the worst.
