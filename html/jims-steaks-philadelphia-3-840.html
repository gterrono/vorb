I waited at least an hour in line. Now I would be okay if the wait was actually due to the amount of customers. But what I don't get is that they have about 5 workers, 1 for taking orders, 1 for taking money, 1 for wrapping up the food, 1 for putting the food into a bag, and only 1 for cooking.         Fuck Pat's & Geno's.

        This is the place I've gone to for years because simply I like their steaks better.  It's not as greasy as the other two and while I also do love Ishkibbibles across the street, this place is still one of my favorite Philly institutions.

        I go pretty simple - nothing fancy - steak with whiz and onions.  Sometimes I'll sub whiz with provolone, but either way it's pretty straightforward.  The lines here can get long (though it doesn't seem as long as it used to be), you'll smell like fucking onions while you wait on line and you'll probably wolf down your sandwich in 15 minutes or less - but it's so worth the wait.  The hoagies here are also good but I really pretty much get the steaks here 90% of the time.  

        Only slight gripe is sometimes the meat here is a bit dry.  Doesn't happen often but has happened a couple of times before.  Otherwise, it's good, it's filling and it's one of the reasons why I love coming to Philly.

      Specialties

      Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

      History

      Established in 1976.

      This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

      In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
