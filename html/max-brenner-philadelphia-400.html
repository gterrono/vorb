    I agonized over whether to rate Max Brenner's 4 or 5 stars. I finally went with five stars because of my own personal bias: I LOVE chocolate. One of the best features of the restaurant, for me, is the little chocolate store up front. The things that would have knocked this down to 4 stars if I didn't love chocolate so damn much are as follows:
    1) The food isn't any more spectacular than most other restaurants in Philadelphia. That isn't to say that the food isn't good. It's great food. But with so many restaurants with great food in Philadelphia, I start to take that for granted.
    2) Someone should tell Mr. Brenner that hazelnut flavoring does not need to go into every chocolate thing he creates.
    3) The price. A bit high for what they are giving you. Making it prohibitive for people like me. I will only go to Max Brenner's when my partner's parents are in town and would like to treat us to dinner.
    4) Quite a few of their "tables" are absurdly small and they have no shame about seating a party of 4 at them. And the food does not come on tiny plates. Most of it comes on ridiculously shaped plates that take up a ridiculous amount of room (but it's very artsy.) Be prepared to throw some elbows and work on your reflexes so you can catch anything that might fall.
    5) Every time I have been there, the service has been very slow and not friendly enough to make up for the slowness. This is a popular place that is almost never not busy. They should know by now to hire either more staff or higher quality staff to deal with their popularity.

    But those things do not change the fact that this is the best place for chocolate and I am smitten by it.

    OK, OK, let's start with the *food*-food: Besides the tea, I can't name a single thing we had that night that didn't have chocolate.

From the specials menu, the chicken and goat cheese salad was a perfect portion, quite fresh, with slightly charred chicken. The salad was piled on top of a waffle that we didn't end up eating that night, but it was still crispy on the outside and fluffy on the inside--think Eggo waffles, *ELEVATED*.

    Oh, god, let's talk about dessert. The dessert menu is like a bible. Seriously, get your reading glasses on and be prepared for a tough decision on desserts (unless you/your party is down for multiple desserts--in which case, I recommend going BUCKWILD).

    Eventually, we went for the European classic fondue. For any of the fondues, you can choose among dark, milk, and white chocolate--but you should make sure to specify what kind of chocolate you want with your server (Urban S'mores being a good example of this). Our European classic fondue came with strawberries, bananas, brownies, marshmallows, these crazy Max Brenner cookies, two types of chocolate fondue dips, and butterscotch caramel dip. Everything was delicious, but in the end, the chocolate overcame us (as we saw with many other parties around us, so don't feel bad!). Oh yeah, the infusion teas come with a three-inch biscotti, so don't think you're ordering JUST tea.

    Despite coming here on a crowded Saturday night, service was enthusiastic, helpful, and some of the most professional I've experienced. Service was extremely friendly, attentive, and the food was still warm when it arrived at the table.

    4 stars, straight up.
