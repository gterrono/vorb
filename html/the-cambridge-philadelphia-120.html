    It you love beer, a small intimate setting, and good food, this is the place to go.  The Cambridge has an awesome beer selection if you are a beer snob, and only drink good beer.  They have a large variety, which includes cream stouts, wheats, IPAs, and lagers.  In addition to good beer, the food is delicious also.  The menu is small but they do have rotating specials, so if you live in the area, there is always something new. The restaurant itself is small, but the wait is worth every minute.  You can grab a drink at the bar while you wait.  The last time I was there, it was Saturday at 7:30 and we only had a 20 minute wait for a table for two.  It is definitely a better place for a small group.  If you are a large group, I would either get their early or late.  In nice weather, all the windows are open so you get the nice spring breeze.

    This place has potential: well executed gastropub food at reasonable prices (e.g. burger and fries for $12, steak with mash, sautéed greens and 'shrooms for $18), craft beer, decent service (if often slow).
    But (and these are big buts, if I may say so) the food portions are tiny if you get anything but the nachos and burgers. I'd be willing to pay 50% more to get entrées 50% larger, because right now I am always hungry at the end. Also, I don't know about you, but I enjoy a bit of variety in my food menu, whereas I value predictability in my drink list. If I know my favorite beer is Allagash white, I should be able to get it every time I come. Right now, it's always the same food menu, with always the same 'daily' specials for weeks. But the beer selection changes every single time. Whenever we find a beer we like, we know it won't be available the next time. Unless you like absolutely any beer (so you don't mind changing it up every time) I really don't see that as an appealing feature.
    So we used to like it here, but we are getting tired of it fast.

    In my line of living I generally encounter 3 types of brunches. There's the occasional Sunday Funday brunch that leads to an all day rager. These are most common during the football season and right when Spring starts to appear. Then there's the more common Saturday night continuation brunch where you wake up rearing to go, but after a bloody or two the night catches up to you and you want to go home and take a nap. Finally there's the dignified brunch, typically with parents or bosses on business trips where a bloody or mamosa may be acceptable but why risk it, you're really just there to eat.

    Since moving into the neighborhood in April I've been to the Cambridge for brunches 1 & 3. The first was in preparation for the US vs Portugal game. It was me, boo, and our friend. Boo was there for a brunch #2 but the friend and I were totally in it for a #1. I got the roast beef sandwich and the girls got omelets. My roast beef was perfect and just the right amount of food. I got a bloody to start which was nice and spicy and then I switched to the beer list. You can tell they put a lot of thought into it, there were a lot of rare and unique drafts which I thought was cool albeit a bit expensive. Afterwards I grabbed a few six packs (1 Dales, 1 Laganitas IPA, 1 Prima Pils) to hold us over till the game started. It was very convenient.

    My second brunch there was of the 3rd variety. Boo's parents were up for the weekend and they wanted to take us out for brunch. We decided on Cambridge bc it was such a hit the last time. This time however there would be no broing out. While that made the experience different it was no less enjoyable. I went for the Cambridge Omelet which has broccoli rabe, peppers, sharp prove, and then I added chorizo. It was amazing. Again, big enough to satisfy me without feeling stuffed. I also got to try the home fries which the girls were yammering about the first time. The yammering was legit, they were crispy and delicious.

    Both times our waitress was Susan, she was really great, one of the best waitresses I think I've ever had actually. They also have an outdoor courtyard that looks awesome. I'm really looking forward to coming back here at other times besides brunch, especially during hockey season.

    Big ups to The Cambridge!!

    I came here last night with my girlfriend for a long-overdue catch up session over dinner and drinks. We were seated at a booth near the bar, and while it did get noisy we were seated catty-corner to eachother instead of across a table so it was still easy to hold a conversation.

    We ordered the nachos, house grind burger, and warm vegetable salad, all to share. The nachos were excellent, only Cantina does them better but these were really solid. It was also a nice large portion. The warm vegetable salad was also very good - perfectly dressed, excellent combination of flavours, and I really loved the bleu cheese. I only wished it had included more vegetables (it was mostly arugula) and that the lentils had been cooked a little more. The burger was pretty much burger perfection. We ordered it medium rare and it was cooked exactly to the correct temperature, the patty was thick and very well-seasoned, the toppings were all fresh and in the proper proportion, and the brioche bun held the package together beautifully. It didn't even need any condiments, that how great all the flavours were. The fries that came with it were also excellent.

    My only gripe was inconsistency in the cocktails. She had a Dark and Stormy to start and I went with the Southside, which we both got for our second round as well. My first one had a little too much simple syrup but the second one was perfect. Hers, however, was seriously lacking in flavour and seemed like it was mixed by a different bartender. It was sort of odd, I tasted both and you could hardly tell they were the same drink.

    Service was good. The server seemed like she was new but she was thorough, friendly, and did an excellent job. Total tab for an app, burger, salad, and 4 drinks was $85 after tax. The cocktails are a bit pricey and for the prices they should have been mixed better. I will be back again soon for that burger. It haunted my dreams...
