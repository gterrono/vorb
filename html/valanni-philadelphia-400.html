    Who says a guys night out can't involve a classy dinner lo-cal? VP, BP, and I decided some on and off weekend rain wasn't going to get in the way of a good dinner.  Valanni was our landing spot.

    Starting with drinks, I highly recommend the LOADED GUN.  I made a good sell as BP followed suit. I mean muddled Brandy Soaked Cherries, seriously?!  We also used of their check-in offer (free red sangria w/2 tapas).  While no loaded gun, the sangria was enjoyable as well.

    Rather than each picking an entree and being jealous of others once they arrived, we split several Tapas and a SEAFOOD PAELLA.  We had the BUFFALO CHICKEN CROQUETTES, PORK BELLY SLIDER CUBANS, GRILLED MERGUEZ, SPICY PULLED CHICKEN EMPANADAS, and LAMB LOLLIPOPS.  

    If you've seen any of my other reviews, you'll note I have trouble picking a favorite.  Valanni's offerings fared no different.  The lollipops came closest to a favorite - and I hate to admit it as VP picked them out, followed by the Merguez.  Both took full advantage of the lamb, making it front and center.  The empanadas and croquettes were surprisingly tasty and also a bit filling.  While the sliders were a bit of a let down, it may have more to do with high expectations (and some tapas competition).  You get two, which also made them different for our group of three to share.

    The Paella is worth speaking about separately.  Food service was prompt at Valanni, but we were given a bit of a breather between tapas and paella.  Once it arrived, it was clear that time was put to good use in its preparation.  Scallops, Clams, Mussels, Calamari, Shrimp, and Lobster in portions the 3 of us had no problems splitting and a flavor that had me finishing every bit of rice on my plate (note: I am not a big rice fan).

    Overall, Valanni killed it and I recommend you go there for dinner.  Like this week!

    Valanni, I'm so sorry I haven't reviewed you earlier. I hope this review makes up for my tardiness.

    I met up with a friend of mine for a Saturday brunch around 1 PM (05/10), and since the weather was warm but unpredictable (showers were expected at some point that day), the host seated us indoors, but facing a large open window to the street. Our server, Jason, was a friendly, mild-mannered guy who knew the menu well and made some recommendations on drinks (I had the April in Paris and I was very pleased). My friend ordered the Ruby Slipper and loved it.

    For our actual meals, I ordered the butter poached lobster Eggs Benedict, which came served with roasted potatoes on the side. I had this before on my first trip to Valanni back in February 2013, and it was just as good, if not better...although I had to leave the potatoes behind, because I was too full to touch them. My friend ordered the caramel macchiato (sic?) stuffed French toast. I sampled a bit and I was blown away by the sweet texture of the caramel and the warm, moist consistency of the bread. Excellently done.

    Jason gave us plenty of time to finish our meals (extra points to him for recognizing my slow eating habits), and the music was good throughout the meal (60s and 70s Motown hits), so we found ourselves singing along as we ate.

    The next time I come for brunch, I will order that scrumptious French toast.

History

Established in 2000.

Valanni restaurant opened its doors in September of 2000, introducing "Medi-Latin" cuisine to Philadelphia. Situated right off of the "Avenue of the Arts," Valanni has become a pre-theater ritual and place to meet for a night-cap for many who live in and around the neighborhood. The bar at Valanni keeps up with the creative cuisine by offering an assortment of sangria, mojitos and other specialty drinks. The kitchen is open until 1 AM daily, serving Tapas and a Mediterranean inspired lounge menu.
