        I really enjoyed my brunch at Cafe La Maude this morning.  I don't go out for brunch too often because I hate waiting in those long lines.  When my husband and I arrived at Cafe La Maude, it was packed, and there wasn't an empty table.  People were waiting both inside and outside.  We were told it would be about a thirty minute wait, which isn't too bad for prime-time brunch hour.  Everything looked and smelled so good.  We got lucky and only waited about fifteen minutes for a table.  

        There were so many delicious sounding entrees on both the board on the wall and on the little menus that were attached to clipboards.  I was having a hard time making up my mind.  I decided on the haloumi press, a side of fried potatoes with saffron aoli, and strawberry lemonade.  You order at the counter, then are seated and the food gets brought to your table.  There are little water cups and cream and sugar for your coffee at the little cupboard to the side of the bar.  

        Our food came out with only a ten minute wait or so.  The haloumi press was very flavorful due to the arugula pesto, salty and tangy green olives, and the rich haloumi cheese.  It was stuffed with leafy greens and put on crusty French bread.  The potatoes were crispy and the saffron aioli was amazing!  I could have eaten it alone.  The lemonade was sweet but not too sweet with lots of strawberry flavor.  My husband enjoyed his Croque Madame.  

        The service was prompt and friendly, and the atmosphere is cozy and welcoming.  There is outdoor seating as well, which would be nice if the weather is warm.  Cafe La Maude is an excellent choice for vegetarians, as there are many non-meat options on the menu. I can't wait to try more things here!

        FYI, you have to ask to put the tip on the credit card before they run it.  Otherwise, you will need to tip in cash.

        In a neighborhood known for "hipster" and "alternative", this little French cafe stands out as a breath of fresh air.  A Parisian influenced cafe with a bench and high-top tables along the wall facing the counter/coffee bar, the decor is simple with comfortable seating.

        The large chalkboard menu behind the counter is ambitious and a bit overwhelming.  There seems to be no obvious choice as the savory egg dishes are fighting against the sweet french toast...the only logical option is to share with a friend (or just order one of each and skip lunch!).  Coffee and variants (cap/frap/mocha/espresso) are all served up better than most barista's can accomplish!

        Unique breakfast sandwiches and egg dishes, all with a french twist.  l'oeuf au jambon gruyere is my staple, order it on a croissant.

        The staff is friendly and except for the occasional coffee refill, gives you all the time in the world to just hang out and get some work done, such a better scene than your typical velvet sofa'd, oversized mug douchery coffeeshop.  Treat yourself to a relaxing brunch done right.

        French-Lebanese fusion cafe mere blocks from apartment? How have I lived life thus far without coming here?

        Let me first say that the service is some of the best that I've experienced anywhere. The owners  are super personable and welcome you like an old friend of the family. They take the time to visit each table to see how you are doing and even ask your name hoping to remember it for the next time you visit.

        My experience here was phenomenal. The atmosphere is cozy, vibrant, and relaxed. You order at the counter and then are seated to await your meal. We went on a Sunday afternoon and arrived to no wait. We ordered off of their weekend brunch menu. My choice: The N-Liberties (whole grain buns topped with homemade hummus, jalapeno, chorizo, tomatoes, avocado and 2 fried eggs. Comes with a petit salad and fried cauliflower). It was exceptionally executed. Matt ordered the Twisty Reuben (Pastrami, turkey, avocado and sauerkraut served on raisin bread. Comes with hand cut fries with some badass saffron aoli and some bangin' homemade coleslaw). He was also psyched about his choice. The ingredients sometimes don't sound like they have any business hanging out together, but they work their magic and everything comes together in perfect flavor, texture, temperature harmony!

        I noticed some delicious looking baked goods on the counter, both pastries and breads. Also, they have fresh juices and tasty coffee.

        The portions are generous and the prices are right! I think the most expensive thing on their regular menu was $11.50. Make your way to Cafe La Maude for a wonderful breakfast/brunch/lunch experience. Just be aware that the cafe probably only seats about 20 people so I wouldn't recommend coming here with a large party. They also have 2 small tables outside that will be lovely to occupy when the weather warms up a bit! It is safe to say I will soon be a regular here!

        Christina sold me on how great the place was when she raved about how great management was and how she loved working there.

        The actual space doesn't afford the restaurant the luxury of having servers, but for the prices, I don't prefer to order at the counter. That's probably my only complaint. My three star review is close to four, as well. One more visit and it'll get there, I bet.

        Israel to Starbucks:

        F*#K YOU STARBUCKS!

        If you're Italian, Israeli, Greek, Lebanese, Mediterranean...You know what I'm talking about.  Starbucks didn't fail in my homeland because of the crappy coffee, it failed because the "Coffee as Drug" business model doesn't work with the Israeli culture.  As type A and accident prone as we are on the roads (I saw a family of four swerve into oncoming traffic repeatedly to save a few minutes on descent to the red sea), we can SIT and have a coffee-for HOURS.

        Coffee shops where I come from have slammin' food, Mediterranean salads, fresh eggs, 5 O'Clock shadow owners who alternatively extol and curse the cavalcade of pretty young things who staff the front of the house.  You sit back, wash your eyes (our expression for looking at pretty girls), talk to a friend you haven't seen in a while, have a coffee...and a salad...and a coconut brownie...and another coffee.  It's epicurean, cheap, and the fabric of the culture.  It's not a wham bam caffeine ma'am pre-made sandwich experience.  It's the journey man, the journey.

        But America-I'm sorry, you just don't get it.  Everybody WORKS in your coffee shops.  My favorite center city coffee shops are guilty of this too.  La Colombe is full of Rittenhustlers, college interviewees, med students, and frenetic artists, and I feel like 5 start ups are launched a week at Elixr's warm industrial confines.  I just want, just once, to see a girl hanging back, devil may care style, while watching the last 3 episodes of shark tank on her electronic device of choice (If this is you-marry me).

        But La Maude gets it man, here's what they do so right:

        NO WAIT: I took my mom here for mothers day.  Guess how long we had to wait for a table outside?  We didn't.

        GREAT DRINKS: I like their latte over La Colombe's...And that's not something I say about lattes.  Surprisingly, I don't order it that much, because the other options, iced white tea, fresh juice, lemonade etc. are more enticing post workout on a weekend.  

        GREAT MEDITERRANEAN FOOD: Homemade hummus, Zatar, Avocado salads, Fou Moudamas, this is what I grew up on.  But there are no weaknesses in their menu, there's nary a bad order to be found.

        CASUAL AUTHENTICITY: In the middle of hipster kingdom, a movement characterized by a quixotic quest for authenticity, is a place that is...Shockingly authentic.  Marble counters, beautiful chairs, idiosyncratic coffee cups, and none of the forced "old world" charm, just simple, elegant, rumpled, lived in authenticity, so the aesthetics in the audience can breathe a sigh of pleasure and relief.

        So grab a coffee, or a melon mint juice, or an iced white tea, and relax on a calm Sunday morning while watching the brunch crowd lumber into Honey's, and pray they don't read this review.

        Say hi to Gabby ;)

      Specialties

      With the authentic look and feel of a true European café, this hidden gem in Philadelphia's Northern Liberties neighborhood serves up some of the most delicious food, coffee and pastries anywhere in the city. Café La Maude's specially brewed La Colombe coffee is known for its strong taste that's never bitter.  The French-Lebanese menu combines the exotic flavors of the Middle East with a distinctly Continental French accent and includes traditional favorites such as Brioche French Toast, Nicoise Salad, Taboule and Baklava along with more unique items such as the Grilled Haloumi, a baguette sandwich with olives, roasted tomatoes, mesculun greens and a basil-arugula aioli.  House specialties include the Café La Maude Salad of  arugula topped with warm seasoned chickpeas and fava beans.  Another popular dish is the traditional lebanese recipe of foul moudamas of marinated fava beans served with fresh tomatoes, chopped parsley and pita served with scrambled eggs. For meat lovers the cafe's marinated chicken sandwich and kebbe is not to be missed. Whether it's breakfast, lunch or brunch that brings you to Café La Maude, it will feel like Paris and taste like Beirut.
