    Hubby and I stopped at this place last week, since we both love good Turkish  and Middle Eastern food. Being stationed in Turkey and Saudi years back before we met, he introduced me to this fantastic fare we have been enjoying around the Philly area ever since. Though our favorite place for Turkish is a little out of the way across the bridge in NJ, we like to try a new place around Philly here and there, just to shake things up a bit.

    We stopped in the place, and the very first good impression was how friendly and full of good humor our host was. We all joked about the downpour we had just come out of, and hubs and I were practically soaked. We were promptly seated, and then greeted by our nice and friendly waitress. She gave us quick drinks and took our orders, which didn't take too long to arrive.

    We enjoyed our apps, though I have to honestly say, the falafel was a bit too dark... looked a little over fried to me. I'm starting to wonder if I have to try making falafel myself at home to get a nice crispy golden brown falafel to enjoy, instead of over crispy, really dark brown falafel. On the other hand, the sigara borek was very nice and enjoyable. The pita was also good.

    My main dish was the ocra w/veggies dish with two types of rice served on the side. Hubs had his usual favorite turkish treat, the doner kebab. Both were very good. The yogurt drink I ordered was smooth and yummy as well.

    We were also helped during our service by a young man who was also very attentive and friendly. Refills were dispensed in a quick and timely manner. Also, the restroom was very clean and beautifully decorated.

    We had a lovely time, and would definitely give this place another try in the future.

    Leziz Turkish Cuisine used to be S&H Kebab House.  Same owner, same menu, but the name better… Leziz Turkish Cuisine used to be S&H Kebab House.  Same owner, same menu, but the name better represents the restaurant now, since they have more than just kebabs.  It's BYOB.  The name change must have worked because I didn't order any kebabs while I was there last!

    The Pan-fried Veal Liver was surprisingly light and tasty.  I expected it to be heavy but I was wrong.  Good for sharing, since you get quite a bit of liver.

    Turkish Dumplings were yummy.  These are small flavourful dumplings filled with lamb, topped with creamy yogurt sauce.  The skin is similar to that of Chinese (water) dumplings, but they're much smaller, about tortellini-sized.  The dumplings are great for sharing -- if I was eating alone, I wouldn't want to order just a plate of these (since I like more variety) but during the meal, I kept getting more dumplings because they're just tasty and creamy little bursts of lamb.

    Whole Grilled Branzino Fish was good and light. Served with some kind of rice -- I'm not sure how it was seasoned but it was tasty.

    I'd been here before and had a Combination Kebab plate.  Favourites were the (Lamb) Adana and Chicken Adana kebabs, which are both chopped meat, seasoned with various spices, herbs, etc.  Very tasty.  The Kofte (Turkish Meatball) was also flavourful, but I think I prefer the Adana (both).  The grilled chunks of chicken and lamb kebab were all right, but not as flavourful or tender as Adana.  I wasn't a fan of the Doner.

    Great service, very friendly, pleasant & helpful.  Definitely plan to return.     Read more

Because I have no basis of comparison, I gave Leziz four stars, however, with better education (and probably a much bigger waistline), I will revisit Yelp when I do my due diligence and rate again.

    I dined with my best friend; his cousin is Turkish, so he was able to help me decide what to order. I chose the Iskender Kebab, sliced lamb with tomato paste and yogurt. It was absolutely delicious. The lamb was tender, juicy, and cooked to perfection. I almost licked the plate clean. I liked it even more when I went on the website and realized that all lamb served at this establishment is organic. I am always more apt to support restaurants that make a point to source more natural meat. Overall, the Iskender Kebab was a great introduction to Turkish food, and I can't wait to try more.

    For dessert, the manager was nice enough to bring us a dessert tasting platter. It included baklava, kazandibi, and firin sutlac. Amazing. My boyfriend is Lebanese, so I eat baklava regularly; the baklava here was less sweet than that of which I'm familiar, and I enjoyed it much more than usual. The kazandibi and the firin sutlac were fantastic. They were light enough to serve as the perfect finish to a filling meal, and neither were overly sweet. It was the ideal ending to a wonderful meal.

    Notably, the service at Leziz was amazing. We were greeted the moment we walked through the door, and immediately seated. The waiter was doting and incredibly helpful. He asked us if we needed any clarification about anything on the menu, and was quick to answer our questions. He was not at all imposing; he waited until our conversation lulled to approach the table. Great service is incredibly important to me, and here, they have it down to a science.

    Leziz is a really cozy spot, and I'd definitely recommend it to even those who aren't too familiar with Turkish fare.

History

Established in 2009.

This is family owned restaurant, specializes in Turkish, Middle Eastern and Mediterrenaen cuisine. It also features live entertainment with belly dancing every Friday and Saturday.

The story of S& H Kebab House begins many moons ago in New Jersey, as these stories always do. The Garden State is like fate's corner bar; it carefully orchestrates serendipitous encounters from frumpy Turnpike rest stops, rigorously landscaped Wisteria Lanes and many, many Cheesecake Factories. That's how Sal Kucuk met Huseyin Yuksel 13 years ago. Fate, you could say.

The kebabs, made with chicken or leg of lamb ground in-house, skewered on metal spits and cooked over open charcoals that imbue each stick with a smoky, almost sensual undertone. Turkish pepper paste stokes a fire in the long, sausage-shaped Adana kebabs, while the classic, familiar shish kebabs bring cubed lamb and chicken that had been marinating for days in yogurt, onion juice and tomato paste.

Meet the Manager

Huseyin Y. Manager

Head chef, Huseyin Yuksel, has brought the intricate layers and flavors of traditional Turkish cuisine to South Street.  Born in Samsun, Turkey, Chef Yuksel lives the food that he prepares, and cooks as much by instinct as he does by recipes.  Having run kitchens in New Jersey, New York and, most recently, at Anchorage, Alaska's Istanbul Cafe, he brings years of experience with him as he sets out to make S&H Kebab House (Huseyin is the 'H') a Philadelphia culinary staple for years to come.
