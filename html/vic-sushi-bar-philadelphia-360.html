    Vic where do I begin...

    When I worked in town I had lunch at Vic and ordered the three roll special. While a great price, I found the rolls to be teeny and lackluster.

    My next foray into Vic was once I moved nearby and they finally delivered to me. Granted, I live around the block, but from the time I called to place the order to the time they were in my lobby with my sushi was a total of 8 minutes. Which is awesome. I had a green salad, the holiday roll and a spicy tuna roll. As far as the salad goes, the ginger dressing is bomb. The holiday roll was tasty, but I realized I'm not a fan of raw scallops. Spicy tuna was standard. Vic will be my go-to once a week sushi delivery spot.

    I went for dinner last night  for the first time with my boyfriend. When we got there we had to wait about 20 minutes because the place is tiny, but thankfully got a good seat at the bar. The decor is not my favorite, but the sushi wins me over. We shared an order of shumai to start, which were excellent. I got the sansom roll, which I had had before, and my boyfriend got the lucky roll. The sansom is one of my favorite rolls of all time, perfect blend of sweet and salty. The lucky wasn't as tasty in comparison, but still great.

    Overall fresh, delicious sushi. Be prepared to wait to sit but not for delivery.

    5 stars for great sushi in the city! Our go to sushi takeout (because the shop is very small). BYOB and very cozy atmosphere (if you don't mind siting on top of each other) A lot of tasty specialty roll combos and fish is always fresh. Call in and pickup orders within 20 mins. Delivery is a bit longer when they are busy anywhere from 20-45 mins.

    Order: Our favorites...Tuna dumplings (filled with an avocado mixture), Samson roll, Tiger roll, Naruto roll, Spicy tuna roll, 3 sushi maki rolls for $10.95 (can't beat that deal). Their fish is always fresh and rolls are not overloaded with rice, its the perfect combo of protein to starch. They are the firsts one in the city that introduced tuna dumplings (a must!), which is bigeye tuna pounded thin wrapped around mashed avocado, spicy mayo, tempura and imitation crab mixture and topped with green tobiko. You really can't go wrong with any of the sushi roll selections.

    Service: The cashier and sushi chef (owners) are very friendly. Orders are taken very quickly on the phone and wait times given are pretty accurate. When they are not busy, delivery is usually 20 min to west part of the city.    

    Overall: Best sushi in the city! We will be craving their sushi if we ever move out of the city.

    Small, cute sushi bar right at 20th and Samson. There are about 10 counter seats, and one table- so get there early or else there will be a wait!

    We arrived around 5:45pm on a Friday and snagged two counter seats in the corner. We both went for the "choose 3 8-piece rolls" ($10.95). I ordered the fried sweet potato, shrimp tempura, and Philadelphia rolls (I actually don't like fish, I know, weird). My friend also ordered the shrimp tempura roll, but also a few rolls with fish in them. The waitress collecting orders was very efficient, but it was a little awkward to yell our order across the counter to her. It's a BYOB, so we brought in some beer to enjoy with our rolls. Halfway through the meal, I wanted some water, but couldn't catch anyone's eye to order it.     When our orders were ready, the chef handed it right over the counter to us, so the wait was not very long!  The fish was wonderfully fresh and the rolls were well balanced -- there wasn't too much rice and none of the flavors engulfed the others.  Surprisingly, despite both having large appetites (especially for sushi!) both my friend and I were pleasantly filled with just one roll.

    Some may see the informal, be-your-own-waiter aspect of Vic's as a downside, but when you want really good food without all the awkward ordering and waiting and frills of a typical sit-down restaurant, this place is really great!
