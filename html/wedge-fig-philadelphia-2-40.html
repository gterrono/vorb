    My husband & I literally collapsed into this, warm, welcoming cafe after a grueling two days at The Flower Show and The Art Museum. I found the menu creative w/o being bizarre or over the top but I was exhausted from having over done it on our weekend in Philly and asked our charming waitress to help me. I told her that I wanted something "easy to eat." I have NEVER done or said anything like this in my entire 61 yrs of life!

    She was wonderful and helped me customize a dish that was exactly what I needed and wanted - it was delicious and as was my husband's dish. The unexpected delight of their pot of French Press coffee was rich and wonderful, as was the amazing dark, salted chocolate, caramel  brownie that another friendly staff member recommended.

    The owner passed by our table and we engaged in an interesting conversation about the restaurant business and cheese, of course. He then helped me select 4 fabulous cheeses for purchase from his unique and outstanding selection. I have to say that I am not one who usually seeks or welcomes help when it comes to food but these individuals had a way of offering advice that was unobtrusive and unassuming - unique!

    A terrific place and as another reviewer said, I wish that we lived near this place!

    If you suffer from the crippling affliction known as Veganism, I'm going to let you in on a little secret: the cure is cheese.

    And where can you acquire this delicious, heavenly substance, you ask? Why, Wedge & Fig is your place.

    The cheese, arriving clearly labelled on a slate with fruit-based accoutrements and some crusty bread with the right amount of bite, is generous. Three plentiful helpings of cheese will set you back $18, which is fair. The three-cheese plate of the week is a nice $15. We tried a 7-year aged cheddar, the manchego, and fromage de Affinois at the (excellent) recommendation of our server. This last cheese was fantastic... creamy and Brie-like, but without the bitter taste and the oft-unpalatable rind. The others were good as well, the aged cheddar robust and rich in cheddary flavor. If only they made Cheez-its with this kind of cheddar. The service was extremely friendly, accommodating, and helpful, and the outdoor garden patio in the back is an adorable urban haven.

    The brunch options were great, and all were between $8-11. We tried the nova lox, which came on pumpernickel bread spread with a delicious rich cream cheese and the usual accompaniments over a generous helping of dressed greens. We also tried the eggs Nola, which were perfectly poached and perched atop grilled cornbread and some deliciously spicy ham (and I don't even typically like ham). We were over-ambitious and also ordered a bacon lattice mac and cheese pie, which thankfully the chef forgot about so we got it packaged up to go. Dinner is now waiting in the fridge, and I'm salivating at the thought of it.

    Wedge & Fig offers delicious food in generous portions at reasonable prices in a friendly, cute environment (really, the outdoor seating is key). Also, who doesn't love cheese (unless you're vegan, in which case I strongly urge you to reconsider)? For these reasons, I hereby proclaim this my new favorite brunch place in Philly.

    I have been wanting to try this place since it opened but never seem to find myself in Old City. I finally made it there this past Sunday afternoon for brunch, armed with a bottle of wine and a Daily Candy voucher that included a three-cheese plate and two entrees for $27 (supposedly a $54 value).

    We sat outside, which we both loved since the weather was absolutely beautiful this weekend. Once seated, we found the service to be erratic and slow. Small glasses and a jug of room temperature water arrived, then we waited.. and waited.. and waited for menus. The table next to us was seated about five minutes after we arrived, and they were already placing their orders by the time we got our menus. When we got our menus I asked for wine glasses, which didn't come for another 5-10 minutes.

    We ordered beemster, ewephoria (my new favorite cheese!!), and a Brie for our cheese plate, with two condiments -- candied walnuts and fig cake. We weren't crazy about these and hardly touched them (although my sister did end up eating the candied walnuts alone later) but the cheese was great and the man who served it to us extremely knowledgeable, at least to our novice palates.

    For brunch I got the Shackamaxon, which was a Brie and turkey panini with mango, arugula, and mustard. My sister got the Cobb salad, as recommended by the waitress. The food was just.. meh. It wasn't anything spectacular and hardly memorable. My panini, especially the turkey, tasted dry and really needed something to pick it up. My sister's Cobb didn't even come with the bacon, as promised on the menu, though the presentation was pretty.

Factoring in tax, tip, and the price I paid for the wine, this was a pricey little meal that neither of us loved, nor will we even remember in the next few months (except the ewephoria, which I have already bought a wedge of at Di Bruno Brothers).

    Listed in Eat & Drink Here in Philadelphia!, BYOB

    My experience at Wedge + Fig was one of the most enjoyable I've had in Philadelphia.

    I went to Wedge + Fig with two close friends as a pseudo graduation send-off Saturday brunch, on Penn's graduation weekend. Naturally, it was packed with students and parents, but the kind waitress took down our number and suggested we go admire the beauty of Olde City for 20 minutes. We did, and promptly 20 minutes later, we were called back and seated in the beautiful backyard garden.

    My friends and I are cheese novices, so upon sitting down, the waiter offered us some excellent cheese suggestions based on what we'd liked in our cheese past. (Get the Ewephoria cheese! It's incredible!) After we had devoured our very unique cheese plate (which was cutely accompanied by fig cake, bread, and quince paste,) our waiter made spot-on recommendations. My friends got a brunchy egg-based dish, and a quinoa salad, respectively, and were satisfied with their dishes. But being at a cheese restaurant, I just HAD to go for the grilled cheese bar! Based on the ingredients I wanted in the sandwich (which were numerous and very unique, in some cases,) my server made cheese and bread recommendations. I ended up getting gouda, tomato, and avocado on pumpernickel, which was EXCELLENT, despite how strange it might seem. The bread was perfectly pressed and the cheese was so gooey and melty, but not excessively so. Ah, it was just perfect.

    The ambiance of the outdoor garden was beautiful, with its flourishing greenery, cobblestone, and peeking sunlight. The staff was very friendly as well, though we ended up waiting for a while in between courses. I think that was the fault of coming on graduation weekend, though, and it allowed me to spend more time with my friends, so I'm not complaining.

    Overall, Wedge + Fig is one of the cutest BYOs in the city. I highly recommend it--but if you go, you've got to get something with cheese in it!

Specialties

Old City's Cheese Bistro, featuring dinner, lunch and weekend brunch.  Sample our chicken pot pie, mac & cheese and scrumptious salads in the evening.  For lunch, try  our unique sandwiches, salads and soups (take out available for most items).  Omelettes, strata, cream-cheese-stuffed French toast are some brunch favorites.  Dine under the stars in our secluded garden.  Bring a bottle of wine or ale and we will pair it with a selection of cheeses.  The perfect romantic evening!

History

Established in 2006.

We rescued Old City Cheese Shop just before it closed.  We gave it some badly needed upgrades, changed the menu, re-named the store to wedge+fig.  And we stocked the cheese case with cheese!

Meet the Business Owner

Kirk N. Business Owner

Cheese lover from an early age.  Lived on a sailboat for 15 years.  Used to play guitar.  Now I own a restaurant.
