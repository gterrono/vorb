    FEAR!! That's exactly what I felt before walking into Saad's. My friends and I all promised to turn off our cell phones, we decided we would follow the rules. My friend eats Halal only, and went to Philly for cheesesteaks, so we had to be in our best behavior to make sure he was able to eat one too. I really felt like I was in a classroom and scared of my teacher!

    As you have noticed...no cell phones people. There were 14 signs (we counted), and when someone got on their phone, the whole restaurant gasped! There's also a sign the second you walk in the door of Dont's - No credit, No cell phone (yes Saad, we get it), No Pets, No Alcohol (duh), No Smoking, No Soliciting, No Unruly Customers (we were trying to maintain). Ok...now time for the food...

    As stated, we went to Philly strictly for cheesesteaks and I heard this was the only place that had Halal cheesesteaks. It was actually really really good, I thought it was delish! But alas, this is not a real cheesesteak my friends, this is a Middle Eastern version. So if you eat Halal and are looking for authentic cheesesteak, this is not the place.

    I'm sure the other food was great, I believe in Saad. With all those rules, he has too be good. And what we did have was great...so I give it 3 stars and fear Saad knows I secretely had my blackberry on.

    Saad's Halal was a bit overrated for me.  I know that "authentic" Middle Eastern food is actually very light in flavor.  I suppose I had hoped for more of a Pakistani flavor (I prefer Makkah Market down the street).  I ordered the lamb shawarma platter and a beef shish kabob sandwich.  The sandwich came with a mayonnaise sauce that appeared to be tartar sauce.  The sandwich was decent, but I felt both were a tad overpriced for what they were.

    The lamb came with a bed of rice and 2 sides (I chose hummus and tabouleh) and a side of the same mayonnaise that was on the beef sandwich.  The hummus had very little salt but was very creamy, probably because it was more tahini than chick pea.  The tabouleh was decent, but fairly average.  The shawarma itself was pretty dry with not much flavor and the rice  to be boxed yellow rice.  I found that hot sauce was needed, but instead of a Middle Eastern friendly sauce, they only offered Sririacha.  I love Sriracha but I found this to be a terribly inappropriate application; I would've preferred a more vinegary hot sauce.  The lamb was a very generous serving, but I feel like they could have done more with the flavor.

    Oh and there are WAY TOO many no cell phone signs, so don't even try it.

    I'm always pretty wary of places that serve predominantly one ethnic cuisine yet dabble in other types of food, cuz they usually aren't that good. Like the Japanese restaurants that sell Bulgogi and Mapo tofu or the Chinese takeout that sells tacos. yes. tacos.

    So I walked into Saad's for the first time and noticed the middle eastern decor, presumably arabic singing/chanting playing over the speakers, and then the menu. As I glanced from left to right I noticed the dishes getting more Americanized. Cheesesteak, fries, onion rings wtf. Saad's turning into Roy Rogers.

    I decided to order the chicken maroosh, a lot of yelpers, recommended it, so I'd thought it I give it a shot. Waited a few minutes got my sandwich and with one bite, all the skepticism that I had was wiped clean. I haven't had a sandwich this awesome in a while. The bread was toasted to a perfect crunch, the chicken was seasoned well, garlic sauce was super creamy, and the tomatoes added a nice guilt free touch.

    I look forward to trying a Saad's cheesesteak in the future.

    1/2 star off for "feeling" like I'm getting death stares for breaking out my iPhone to tweet, 10-15 signs about no cell phones is a lil overkill.
