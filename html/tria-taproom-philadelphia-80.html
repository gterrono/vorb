        This place looks deceptively small however when you walk in, the restaurant goes way back into the building. I like the flexibility of this place. In the afternoon it can be casual, you can have your flip flops on and sit with your dog outside on the sidewalk and in the evening, you can dress up for a nice dinner with romantic mood lighting.

        Went here for dinner before a boat party with some friends and my husband was able to have his coat checked it, they moved us immediately when there was a better seating arrangement available, the service was polite and patient. I received samples of beers that I was hesitant about.

        The food was appealing to the eyes and satisfactory to the taste buds. The prices were comfortable for our wallets and the environment was really relaxing but with an air of sophistication. It was a pleasant time for dinner but I perhaps would be more inclined to the do the lunch, outside seating with Milo my Fido next time.

        Listed in Top Shelf Liquor, Delicious Dinners

        My stomach is getting rumbly just sitting here thinking about the last time I went to Tria Taproom. The vibe in TT was "sophisticated hipster" and the place was packed and buzzing loudly with conversation when we arrived around seven. We managed to squeeze in at the bar, thanks to the advice of a friendly host. I went with a fellow beer nerd and we were in fits about the sheer amount of taps available. We eagerly poured over the iPad with the draft list (hah, see what I did there?) and made our first choices.

        I went with a draft of Saison di Pipaix, which was a farmhouse ale that was very heavy on the barnyard/funky aroma side. I felt that this and my second draft (a Left Hand Nitro Milk Stout) were both served far too cold for me to enjoy. I had to let both sit out for a few minutes before really being able to taste them.

        I don't know how much Tria can do about serving temperatures (as I expect all of the kegs are lined up next to each other behind the taps), but a warmer pour would have been much appreciated.

        Finally, the food. In a word: delicious. I had an heirloom tomato and cucumber salad that was fantastic in its simplicity. Also ordered their beer burger, which I excitedly gobbled down as well (beer pickles, beer-naise sauce, and Chimay cheese? Holy moley). You could tell the patty was hand-packed only a few moments before and it was perfectly cooked to order. The crispy fried onions on the side were a perfect addition. I thought I was over gourmet/artisanal burgers, but boy was I wrong.

        As I stared at the last sliver of the deliiiicious three-cheese flatbread still on my plate, I probably appeared to be in agony.

        Our server came over to ask if I wanted a box or if I was done with my plate.
        "No, no. I am DETERMINED(!) to finish!!!! this piece!" I replied.
        "....Okay then." (I'm sure she thought I was nutty. I would have.)

        And there it sat, in front of me, for quite some time, as I continued to coax myself to finish it. There was barely anything left. Definitely not enough to take home in a box. But to leave it? That would be truly insane. No. I would finish it.

        And I did. ...Eventually.

        All the while, this somewhat ridiculous scene of culinary dedication was accompanied by a Belgian ale, selected on one of Tria's fancy iPads, and the loud atmosphere of a weeknight at the taproom.

        I love this Rittenhouse gem. It's so sophisticated and fun. Following their Instagram makes me want to visit them every week. Cauliflower mac and cheese? Beer burger? Want and want.
