    I walked here on a recent rainy evening for a quick dinner on my own, and given the rain, a nice plate of hot chicken sounded like it might just hit the spot. I chose the leg & thigh combination with a side order of mac & cheese and a green salad. I don't know much about the economics of running a small restaurant, but they do nickle-and-dime you a little here, charging for extras like sauce - I mean, come on, Sriracha is a RIGHT NOT A PRIVILEGE by gum!

    Anyway, grumbling aside, I did have a nice meal sitting at the window, watching the rain tail off. The mac underwhelmed, tasting like it'd been sitting out all day, which it probably had been, and the bread crumb topping had gone from crunchy to soggy. But the meat was real good, fork-tender with a crispy skin, salted and seasoned just the way I wanted it, and let's face it, it makes more sense in the end to rate a chicken restaurant on the chicken.

    Quick, tasty food, decent prices - makes for an excellent neighborhood restaurant, though I probably wouldn't go out of my way for it. I'll be back.

    The chicken at Rotisseur is everything you could hope for in a rotisserie chicken - and considering that the chicken is the metaphorical bread and butter of this establishment, I'd say this place is a success. The chicken is very tender all the way through (even the breast meat!) and the skin is really crisp, with a hint of char.

    The sides are also great. I've tried most of them and none of them have disappointed me. The mac and cheese and brussels sprouts are my favorites. I talked to the chef one night and he was pretty awesome. It's great when you find one of these hole-in-the-wall establishments run by someone who used to work in a good restaurant. This is one of the few places I feel like going back to regularly and one of the few places the quality of whose food I consider nearly impossible to replicate at home.

    First customers of the day.  We had chosen this place based on the part of town we were going to hang out in for the afternoon . . . looked promising, based on the reviews!

    First of all, my partner is Vietnamese - and we love Bahn Mi.  We also understand that, outside of an actual Vietnamese-owned/run place, it's probably going to be Americanized - that's fine, always up for a new flavor for an old favorite.

    First of all - the bread wasn't the greatest . . . as others have mentioned.  A little too heavy.  I got the Bo Ssam Sandwich . . . which wasn't bad.  A good portion of pork, but the big complaint I'd have is it was just too salty (and, as I've said before, I'm Southern - and it takes a lot of salt for me to call something salty).  Could have been great, but was only okay.  My partner got the BBQ Chicken Sandwich . . . which was just totally saturated with a very sour barbecue sauce.  This could have been sold as chicken, pork, maybe even beef - because there was so much sauce in this that it really didn't matter what the protein was . . . My partner thought it was the better of the two sandwiches, I honestly thought it was pretty horrible.  We had swapped half of our sandwiches.  We ended up swapping back.

    The Mac N' Cheese was tasty, but could have been warmer.  There was really only a hint of warmth in it.

    A friend who was with us got the chicken - I didn't try it, but it looked good - and she said it was tasty.  I did try one of the corn muffins - which was pretty good!

    The service wasn't bad, wasn't anything special . . . it was more like, "oh God, it's Sunday morning and I'm still hung over and I'd really rather be home - but, I'll try to be nice" . . . - there was certainly nothing inviting about the service.

    I'll be honest.  It's not on my "go back and try again" list . . . it just doesn't seem worth the trouble.  

    The idea behind this place is solid!  But, to work, it really has to be exceptional.  It's just not.  It's just okay.
