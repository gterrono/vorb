    Great service, high prices, just okay food. We were both very hungry and looking for a delicious dinner date. We ordered from the "small" items menu, which is an interpretation of a tapas menu.

    Five dishes in all, and they all came out quickly. Edamame (salt was dumped on top, not sprinkled over all the green morsels), ahi tuna tar tare (awesome, needed one more crisp to eat it with - only comes with three), Korean pork tacos (comes with a kick, reminiscent of the tacos at El Vez, go figure), grilled octopus with spinach and pita (kind of dry and not very flavorful)... and there was one more... what was it, what was it??? Oh yes, the forgettable scallops. I've made better scallops at home, and paid a lot less.

    Dessert ended up being the "oreo" mint chocolate chip ice cream sandwich. Two tiny bite-sized sandwiches come on a plate decorated in chocolate syrup. Oreos taste better than the "oreo" sandwich cookies here, and overall it left me wanting more ice cream. Oh well.

    Eh, for a good dinner I'd rather go to Marathon - and for the area of this Continental (Old City), I'd rather hit up Cuba Libre, Triumph,  or Farmicia. Do yourself a favor and follow my lead, you'll be glad you did.

    Before you criticize my review for being unfair, I'm admitting I've only ever been here for brunch. I know it's a martini bar restaurant, and it's probably not fair to review its brunch experience. But, since it offers Sunday brunch, it is fair. So here goes:

    I'm usually a Stephen Starr fan. Buddakhan and Morimoto are two of our favorite Philly restaurants. Parc has an excellent lunch (and location to boot).  But Continental is a Starr aberration. It's just... bad. It has all the trappings of a Starr restaurant: retro and hip decor, trendy clientele, but the brunch food is boring and predictable. I've been here twice for Sunday brunch with my mother, husband and four kids. So we are a large party to please. But really, the kids just like french toast or pancakes, or anything they can dump syrup on, so brunch is a pretty easy meal to order.  

    The brunch menu reads like that of just about any diner, and we ordered pretty typical brunch fare. My kids ordered the brioche French toast (served with caramel bananas), I had the nova on a bagel, my husband had oatmeal with raisins and granola, and my mother had the Big Bang (pancakes, bacon, sausage, eggs, home fries and toast).

    The food presentation was boring, and the food was no better than IHOP's. Come to think of it, IHOP's pancakes are actually much better than the flapjacks that came with the Big Bang. The brioche French toast was a real disappointment. There was nothing original or flavorful about it. The best French toast with caramelized bananas we've ever had was at BrickTops in Naples, FL; Starr should just throw out his recipe and beg them to use theirs.

    I'm sure Continental is a fun place to meet friends for drinks, have a night out or stop in after a play (it's near the Arden Theatre), but save your money and go elsewhere for brunch.

    Maybe I'm a sucker for quirky drinks, but Continental is definitely not a 3-star place.

    And so what if the hummus tastes like hummus and isn't loaded with butter? It's still good, creamy hummus. It's got a slight olivey flavor and the pita bread is warm and fluffy. There are crisp slices of cucumber too if you're going the low-carb route or just need something green and crunchy to sandwich between two pita slices spread with hummus.

    And who cares if the Arugula Salad is huge? It's delicious and the balsamic dressing isn't soaked into every piece of green, so you actually do get to experience Arugula complimented by balsamic vinaigrette not soaked in it. There probably could have been more goat cheese...after all, every mountain needs an equitable amount of snow.

    And those shoestring fries are really amazing. They're intertwined within one another - almost like a crispy, golden, warm woven sculpture lightly drizzled with mustard sauce. These are amazingly delicious, and any table would be woe to miss out on their greatness.

    And so what if I paid $10 for a really great martini? Where else can I get St. Germain with Smirnoff and sparkling wine...with nerds at the bottom? This Sourpuss was probably the best martini I've had, and being given the shaker was just plain nice. So really, it's $10 for two full martinis. Yeah...get over it.

    ...and don't you know that "you're so cool" if you eat at a Steven Starr Restaurant?

    I love the Continental, always have, but the waiters should be a little more pro-active in stopping patrons dining outside from smoking.

    Early on a recent Friday evening, a patron sat at the sidewalk table next to us just as our appetizers arrived, and he was conspicuously holding an unwrapped cigar in his hand. As soon as he sat down he lit the cigar and the smoke began wafting over our table a few feet away. I watched the waiter go to his table with a menu, come back for orders, and back again with drinks without saying anything to him (the customer wasn't hiding the cigar). Finally when I was able to nab our waiter, I let him know that the cigar was a problem, cigar smoke was drifting over our table and ruining our meal, and asked if he could do something. He apparently didn't want to confront the patron, went inside, and after several minutes the manager went to the patron's table and explained to the patron that smoking wasn't allowed. All together we sat for a good 15-20 minutes smelling cigar smoke.

    (And of course now the smoker knew it was us that said something, causing some un-needed tension, instead of the restaurant simply enforcing the law.)

    Maybe this is a common problem at outdoor tables in Philadelphia? We were walking back to our car along Market St. past the sidewalk tables of other restaurants, and I saw at least one other table with someone smoking a cigarette.

    When we sat outside we expected to hear the noise of the busses along 2nd and Market, and even maybe smell an occasional exhaust fume. But we also went with the expectation that smoking isn't allowed at Philadelphia restaurants. The Continental needs to set a precedent and give your waiters solid instruction for dealing instantly with customers that try to smoke at the expense of other diners enjoyment.

Specialties

This stainless steel 60's-style diner offers an exciting menu of global cuisine, including crab pad thai, cheesesteak eggrolls and lobster mashed potatoes. Famed for its sensational cocktails, the extensive bar features a wide variety of libations and over 15 types of martinis.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
