    I happened by Manakeesh one afternoon and was struck by the building facade.  Upon looking inside, I made a note to comeback with at least one friend in tow.  It didn't take much convincing to find a good friend to see what the bakery had to offer in terms of Lebanese food.

    If that isn't enough to make you want to head for 44th, the nice, friendly, and caring staff coupled with the available wi-fi will surely make it more difficult to resist.  You won't be sorry for seeking out the Manakeesh Bakery.  In fact, it's so good, I have a new favorite place to go in the mornings.

    Manakeesh's interpretation of a schwarma is a pretty bad.  The "marinated sirloin strips" was actually more like coarsely ground beef.  It had a most bizarre texture.  There was also a problem with the flavor - there was none.  I don't think they use any salt in their cooking.  Very bland.  No soulfulness.  No deep flavors I've come to expect from true middle eastern eateries.  

    The "garnish" of the schwarma were some bitter white onions and some slimey tomatoes that looked like they had been cut last weekend.  I guess the parsley was a nice touch, but it was too little too late.  

    As for the flatbread, there was no life to it.  I had the sense that no cook there had given the dough the love it so badly needs.  Also, the dough was still a little raw on the inside.  

    I could really only stomach about half a sandwich.  Every bite just got progressively worse.  I was waiting for that bite where everything made sense.  

    I had made the mistake of ordering the "cheesecake" while I ordered the sandwich, so that's how I was gonna finish the meal.  This "cheesecake" was unlike any cheesecake I've ever had before.  The texture was that of a six month old tasty cake.  WAY too sweet.  Not soft, rich and luscious how I know my cheesecakes to be.  Maybe this is their interpretation of cheesecake, but I would grade it as a total fail.

Specialties

History

Established in 2011.

Owner Wissam Chatila's dreams of having Lebanon's famous flatbread sandwiches close to home have been realized in the form of Manakeesh, a Lebanese cafe and bakery, serving up the famous sandwiches after which it is named in over 30 varieties.

In addition to the manakeesh, expert Chef Wissam Zayat whips up the best Middle Eastern sweets in the area, including award winning baklava.

Manakeesh is open for breakfast, lunch, dinner and dessert!

Meet the Manager

Abd G. Manager

A self described 'people person', Abd brings his upbeat attitude and energy to Manakeesh, where he can be seen greeting customers and making sure their experience is quite memorable.
