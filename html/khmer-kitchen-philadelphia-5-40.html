    Listed in I tried the 2013 Yelp 100 Challenge and failed...

    I will let the other Yelp reviews steer you to the best of the menu. They are all right, and you should order all of those things.

    My advice isn't the what, but the how:

    Go as a party of six and order a lot of food. Khmer Kitchen serves family style, meaning one dish comes out at a time and for the table to share. Six dishes and three appetizers came out to $20 a person (including tax and a generous tip), and for two and a half hours we grazed through a meal I will remember when that day comes that I am sipping my dinner through a straw.

    Go early or on a weeknight. The restaurant is tiny and fills up fast.

    Have a backup plan for take-out in the event that you can't get a table. I have the luxury of being within walking distance, so that may not be fair of me to say, but a backup plan means you can't feel bad about a ridiculous wait for a table. You won't get the experience of a multi-course feast, but that's what you get for going on a Saturday.

    Thank everybody who works there. Seriously, they appreciate hearing it and they deserve it. They work hard and put out dishes full of complex, surprising flavors. Look around you and you will see a room full of patrons whose eyes are rolling back into their heads with every bite.

    Finally, don't forget to bring your favorite beer or wine, and cash because they do not accept credit cards.

    Listed in Yelp's 100 Review Challenge, The Great Philly Food Experience

    When you're having a reunion with friends, you can't go wrong with Cambodian food .... at least that's what I decided last night and I was right - it was awesome.  Cambodian food is an extremely tasty cuisine that has influences from neighboring countries like Thailand and Vietnam but has its own distinctive style.  And Khmer Kitchen is the best and most authentic Cambodian restaurant in Philadelphia. This is not the easiest restaurant to get to as it is nestled deep in South Philadelphia but it is totally worth seeking out.

    My favorite dish here is the Prah-hok kahteeh pork dish prepared spicy.  When you add rice to this and the accompanying vegetables, you have quite a treat (think chile con carne but better).  The way the plate is set up here looks so pretty that you almost hate to eat it.  All the famous Cambodian sour soups like the iconic Salaw machu kre'ung are also hearty and individually prepared.  The papaya salad is awesome and just looks and tastes totally healthy.  And I love that they are able to obtain and use some real Cambodian vegetables like Cambodian eggplant or cabbage. The food is prepared to order so it's not coming out fast from the kitchen.  Mom and Grandmom and Auntie are all in the back putting each dish together so be prepared to wait.  Just bring along some good wine or beer with you and anticipate what is to come.

    Last evening, Tony was our server (Cambodian name Chomnan).  He is awesome.  He is so cool and personable and knows everything about Cambodian food and what he doesn't know he'll ask his Mom or Grandmom in the kitchen.  His smile and easy-going manner adds to the ambience of this awesome Cambodian restaurant.  Don't miss it ... it's as good as advertised.

They didn't have a big enough table for 5, so we had to squeeze into a wonky, slanted cramped 4-seater, but we managed.

    Everything is served family style, so for 5 people, we did not have to order much:

    Grilled Beef Stick - these were  among the best skewers I've
    had anywhere. They had a sweet red crisp coating that reminded me of Pok Pok (Portland) wings.

    Prah-Hok Kahteeh (caramelized ground pork coconut milk) - although I enjoyed this, and it was highly recommended, I was a bit less impressed. I was confused by the presentation of sliced raw vegetable dippers it was served with (especially the raw eggplant), because it went much better with rice.

    Sach-Khoe Ang' (grilled beef served with a grilled tomato sauce) - This was steak cooked to your desired doneness, and strip-cut, so you can  turn it into a lettuce wrap

    Cha Kre'ung (stir fried lemongrass beef) - This was the all-around favorite of the table. I've had my share of lemongrass beef at Vietnamese restaurants, but this was different, with perfectly crisp fried beef pieces, and bright yellow sautéed vegetables in a rich brown sauce. Really, really good.

    I've never had Cambodian food before, and this was the best introduction I could ask for. It's also BYO, which I think is a huge plus. We were all full at the end, and it only cost us around $10 a person. I will definitely be back to try the rest.

Specialties

History

Established in 2012.

We wanted and notice that there were no actual true Cambodian restaurant. A Cambodian restaurant that has no Chinese or Vietnamese influence. Not only that, when we think about Asian foods, automatic ally Pho, or Lo-mien come to mind, with shrimp fried rice. So the family agreed lets do something to get true Cambodian cuisine out there and show that our Cambodian culture is very unique, and intriguing. What better way to show case that but through a universal language of food. A language that that can be spoken in with out words but understood by every one

Meet the Business Owner

Sopheak N. Business Owner

First generation American Cambodian. Loves food, loves to cook, and is blessed with wife, a sister, and a mother that can cook unbelievably great. Main intention is to revive a slowly forgotten tradition, culture, and cuisine of Cambodia. Re-introducing the Khmer heritage to the younger (Americanized) Cambodian generation.
Ultimate goal is to distinguish Cambodian Cuisine from the style of plating, to the time of preparation, ingredient, and layering of flavor from your main stream Asian food.
