    Wanted to try another cheesesteak while we were in town for a Phillies game so we stopped at Tony Luke's beforehand.

    My husband and I ordered a cheesesteak with provolone and an order of sweet potato fries. My aunt and her fiancé got the regular fries for everyone to share. They prefer the cheesesteak with American over provolone.

    The cheesesteaks were smaller than the ones I've had a Nick, Geno's or Jim's. As far as I remember, the price was on par ($8.95 for the cheesesteak). The cheesesteak at Tony Luke's was definitely far less greasy which I was a fan of. Obviously it's not healthy per say but there was absolutely no grease that dropped off the cheesesteak. I would recommend you get your own cheesesteak instead of sharing since they are normal sized sandwiches. For a girl, you'll likely have 1/4 or so leftover but my husband polished his whole steak off easily.

    I liked the waffle-style sweet potato fries, however I would order them either without the caramel or have it on the side. They came with cinnamon, powdered sugar, and caramel so the bottom fries were a little soggy. The normal fries were good as well, I think one portion of fries is plenty for two people to share.

    The seating area at Tony Luke's was also slightly above the other cheesesteak joints in my opinion as was the customer service. The employees were more courteous and understanding if you had questions.

    This is a solid place, especially when you get that hankering for a classic Philadelphia cheesesteak or as they refer to them an "old style sandwich".  An easy to get to and very popular Philadelphia eatery.

    Tony L has a wider variety of sandwich offerings than some of his local South Philly competitors.  Other options include roast pork, chicken cutlets and meatballs to name a few.  You can also get broccoli rabe on many of them, which is a Philly favorite.

    Their Customer service can still be improved (see previous review).  I still do not get a warm fuzzy feeling from the woman that usually takes orders and runs the register.  She has been their front line face for some time now, but I am not going to let that deter me from properly acknowledging a Philly staple.  However, a star has been detracted.

    Funny occurrence/poor marketing: I did one time receive a "special offer" for LA Fitness in my bag that accompanied my cheesesteak and fries.  Is that the message you want to send your customers as they are about to enjoy one of your calorie filled offerings?  I can only assume that they were receiving some sort of compensation in exchange for the ad placement.  Very poor cross marketing attempt.

    Interesting tidbits: Tony has been featured on the Food Network, most notably on Throw Down with Bobby Flay.  He is also an aspiring actor and has landed some acting roles.  A copy of his movie The Nail, is also available for purchase at this location (have not seen it).

    This is one of the more easier places to get to as well as park at.  Heck, park right in the middle of the street under the overpass.  This usually impresses and maybe even more so slightly frightens my out of town guests.  Great spot for before and after the game as well...or take it with you.  Also great, as it makes for an easy pit stop before taking an out of town guest to the airport.

    All in all, a Philadelphia landmark.  Great sandwiches that will not disappoint.  They also make their own bread here so you know it is fresh.  I usually go whiz with!

    Enjoy!

    I am a little scared to write this review... I've learned people are seriously passionate about their cheesesteaks!  My inbox blew up with hate mail when I said I liked Geno's over Pat's... but I am a rebel, so I am writing anyway.

    Tony Luke's has been featured on the Food Network on a few different occasions, specifically "Throw Down with Bobby Flay" and I have to ask myself... was this really a case of being the "best"... or was this a case of "Hey Bobby.. Old friend, will you feature us on your show so we can be famous?"  Harsh I know. Don't get me wrong, the sandwich wasn't bad but in my very humbled (yet stridently public) opinion, Tony Luke's is a far cry from the best.

    We got there on a Sunday afternoon, the line was about 20 people deep but still maintained inside the building, by the time we made it to the counter, the line extended well out the building and down Oregon Ave.  The 'oh so pleasant' lady at the counter took our complicated order of 'two regulars wiz wit" and two birch beers, she asked my name and literally shoved our change in the chest of my friend.  Look, it's Philly, niceties are scarce, I get it... but seriously, take it down a notch chicka!  Another of my friends made the mistake of asking if the breakfast menu was served all day... she shot that down with a harsh "no!" even before he finished his sentence.  As obnoxious as she was, she did provide me with the highlight of my Tony Luke's experience.  When she asked my name for my order... I went out on a limb and said "Jodi"... when we got the receipt... it was written out "Joday", which in her strong Philly accent is EXACTLY how she would have pronounced it.  

    Now... as far as the subs go, the bread was eh.  I am a fan of Geno's bread so perhaps I am being overly biased, but it was on the far end of the dry side.  The most important part of a cheesesteak, is the steak, and I remember saying flat out while eating that it tasted just like grocery store steak-ums. The most apparent difference in TL's meat versus their competitors is that they serve it in slices as opposed to the usual "chopped", also, the onions and meat are not cooked together, they are totally separate. The onions were amazing, probably much to do with them being cooked in steak-um fat - none the less they were cooked to perfection and added the much needed flavor and moisture I craved.  I opt for wiz on my subs... it's one of the very few places that cheese wiz is appropriate.  I tend to like my wiz ON my steak and onions and not on the bread - but that is an anti-soggy bread thing.  My most favorite part of the whole sandwich was the hot sauce they have at the condiment bar, along with the pickles and peppers - this added a lot of flavor!!

    I wouldn't turn my nose up at TL's when I am in Philly... even this is better than the sad sad sad imitations we have in Northern Virginia... but if I had my choice I'd rather go to Geno's... Jim's... or Pat's!
