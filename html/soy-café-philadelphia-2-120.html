        After moving into the neighborhood, I was excited to learn there was a vegan restaurant right around the corner.
        Enter Soy Cafe, it has a very hippy, earthy, chill vibe. It bills itself as vegan, though it is not really a vegan restaurant, they display the items that are not vegan as so on the menu, which is nice.
        They only have one work station so I am sure some cross contamination goes on here, but hey.
        I don't like their baked goods, they are hard to describe: soft but crumbly and oily, but dry, and flavorless.
        The vegan cheesesteak wrap that was recommended by my friend was very squishy and watery, again, not a lot of flavor.
        I have been to soy cafe 4 times and have went away disappointed every time.
        The smoothies all have honey in them, I ordered one without the honey, it was sour and flavorless. I know, I know, I changed the menu item. But some people just don't do honey.
        The last time, the babe and I went in for dinner. I got a bagel sandwich with spicy hummus (was really good) and some veggies. My girl got a sandwich, I forget which one, but about halfway through there is a hair on the plate that matches the cooks. We didn't say anything because everyone is really nice there. But that ruined it for me, the food was bad, pastries bad, hair in food bad.
        I probably will be back to soy, to stop in for a coffee or tea, but will not be dining here anymore. This is not a 4+ star restaurant by any means, keep it real.

        This is my favorite food-related place of any kind in Philadelphia.

        It occurred to me recently that I have probably been to the Soy Cafe close to 300 times in the last 8 years and I have never had a bad experience.  Not a single one.  That's pretty amazing.

        My current favorite menu item is the Spicy Seafood Ramen.  I like all of their sushi-inspired wraps (Philadelphia, Spicy Tuna, etc.) and all of their salads.  They use the freshest, highest-quality fruit and vegetable ingredients, which combined with the creative dressings is what makes the food so great.

        The smoothies are great.  The tea is very good.  The coffee is very good.

        Fake meat is not one of my favorite things, but I have had the vegan sausage wrap and the vegan cheese steak wrap and they were both good, in my inexpert opinion.

        The staff is great.  I have noticed that they have one of the lowest staff turnover rates in the food business and that must speak to something.  They are always friendly and inviting.

        I like the decor.  They rotate their art every few months, maybe more sometimes.

        The outside area is small but very nice.

        Once in a while when they turn the blender on its pretty loud for 15-20 seconds.  That's literally the only bad thing I could say about this place.

        I can't say enough about Soy Cafe.  This place really just does have a special place in my heart.… I can't say enough about Soy Cafe.  This place really just does have a special place in my heart.  And not just because of the food, but also the kind people who work there who clearly put a lot of effort into preparing fresh, awesome food and drinks.

        Their smoothies were my first love.  The Berry Berry is my favorite.  There is just something about Soy Cafe's homemade soy milk in the smoothies that makes them about 100 times better than anything I can make in my kitchen.  The Funky Monkey is so much better than such a basic peanut butter and banana smoothie usually would be. I also highly recommend the Hot Apple Cinnamon Soy on a cold day!  Yum!

        Their food is also amazing.  My absolute favorite is the soy wrap (with soy cheese!).  The wasabi sauce in it is not too spicy -- the perfect level of heat. My second favorite is the grilled eggplant, either on black Russian toast or in a wrap.  Their sandwiches - to me - are the perfect size either for lunch or dinner, and are filling without making you feel stuffed.  Oh and can't forget their homemade soy dips!!!  They always have samples out and must be tried!!!  

        And no review of Soy Cafe would be complete without mentioning their baked goods.  Vegan chocolate chip cookies are AMAZING and you would never know they are vegan.  Also they have muffins and cupcakes.  

        There really is nothing to not like about Soy Cafe.  Everything is made fresh while you wait.  The food is overall very healthy and made with whole, unprocessed foods.  Staff is awesome and patient.  Only teensy tiny downside is that this isn't a place where you can order a sandwich and have it ready to take out in five minutes.  But that is because of the quality of the food!  (Hint: just order ahead if you're in a rush!)       Specialties

      -Homemade soy milk which goes into all of our delicious smoothies.
      -Soy specialty drinks: such as green tea soy steamer, black sesame soy, soy chai, fresh apple-cinnamon soy, soy hot cocoa, and more...
      -Homemade vegan salad dressings from whole fruits and veggies: apple-ginger dressing and maple-mustard dressing.
      -Gourmet meal-sized salads served with our homemade apple-ginger dressing.
      -Organic & Fair Trade coffee, espresso & blended coffee drinks.
      -Delicious and hearty sandwiches/wraps made with freshest ingredients, and unique homemade condiments(all vegan) such as sun-dried tomato pesto, soy mayo, wasabi-hummos, spicy hummos, & ginger hummos.  Puts a new spin on traditional sandwich affairs!
      -Yummy vegan and non-vegan baked goods.
      -Made-to-order soups: mushroom miso, spicy veggie dumpling soup, egg-dropped miso, buckwheat noodle soup, udon noodle soup, & seafood ramen soup.

      History

      Established in 2004.

      Soy Cafe got it's start in a much smaller space on the 800 block of North Second Street in Northern Liberties.  We moved into our new home on the 600 block of North Second Street in 2007.
      The concept of Soy Cafe is to combine a relax comfy environment along with a healthy tasty menu for patrons to enjoy and just to take a break from the busy world.  
      In order to create the all-around homey feel, not only did we put a lot of thoughts into our decor but our food & drinks are always prepare with a lot of care & love.  Healthy comfort-food is our passion.
