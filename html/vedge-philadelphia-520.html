        8/6/2014

        Every vegan, vegetarian and occasional omnivore I know loves this place. I don't get it.

        Service is very good: Water refilled, food delivered in a timely fashion and cleared promptly. My only quibble was the one person who attended our table who obviously felt that everyone in the restaurant wasn't nearly vegan enough - he appeared to be the 'super vegan' possibly because he resembled Nosferatu. Parenthetically - what would a vegan vampire eat?

        Then there is the ambience issue: Vedge is LOUD. Completely caps lock loud. When Chef Blanc ran the space as Deux Cheminees, it was possible to have dinner and a conversation without having the person across the table either shout or repeat almost everything. A few sound absorbing decorative pieces and turning off or lowering the volume of the music would make the noise level tolerable.

        Then there is the food: My opinion, as a cook and diner, is that it is over rated. Vedge has a case of 'The Emperor's New Clothes'.

        Not that the presentation or execution of the dishes isn't of a very high quality: They are, but unfortunately verging on precious, reminiscent of the early excesses of cuisine minceur. And, the prices of everything are seriously out of line. Four of us spent $200 plus 20% tip (and if the restaurant wants to be in the vanguard they could get rid of the tipping - but that's a discussion for another time), for not enough food and over priced drinks. Yes, I get that Center City rents are high, yes, I get that a liquor license is expensive, yes, I understand all the costs associated with running a restaurant. That being said, I was still hungry after three courses. I'm not looking for super sized portions by any means, but the feeling of having eaten a complete meal.

        And another quibble - the over use of soy products. Processed, processed, processed. Sure they have a non soy menu, and that's a good thing, but the whole soy thing just doesn't make sense.

        I get what the folks at Vedge are trying to do - show that vegetarian and vegan cuisine can compare and compete with non vegetarian food. And it's an admirable effort as we could all do with eating less meat and less fats. But, if I want vegetarian I'll head to an Indian or Chinese or Thai restaurant.

        Oh, and sorry, but the desserts, apart from the sorbets were, like most vegan desserts that attempt to mimic classic dishes, dry and not super tasty. Beautiful, but not worth the time. Yes, Vedge wants to cater to a vegan clientele, but you know what? Vegetarians eat butter and cream and a little of both would not go amiss, particularly in the desserts. Please don't call something 'cheesecake' when there is no cheese involved.

        Her experience:

        Last night my partner and I went to a long awaited dinner a Vedge. Our experience at Vedge was very mixed. The interior of the restaurant was beautiful but the formality of the atmosphere felt a bit forced and the staff was welcoming but cool.  

        Our first dish, the chilled cucumber soup, was outstanding. It was extraordinarily well executed. It was a distillation of all the best tandoori flavors with a perfect balance of flavor and a nice texture. Our two main dishes were also very good. The fresh peas and cauliflower crepe was Incredibly rich, buttery and delicious. The flavor of the fresh peas was fantastic. It was a very high quality example of the type of food Vedge is trying to create. The palm leaf heart tangine was also lovely. There was a nice crunch added to the couscous, the palm heart had an almost scallop-like texture and flavor. The sauce was delicious but could have been slightly more abundant. We were less impressed by the zucchini dan dan noodles which were a bit too spicy, to the detriment of the other flavors in the dish.

        Unfortunately our dessert, the Yuzu cornbread, was awful, like something the chef did not taste before it left the kitchen. The miso frosting, which sounded intriguing was way too salty and abundant, the cornbread had a terrible dry texture and was sour. We left almost all of the dessert on the plate but were not asked by the waiter if it was ok.    

        His experience:

        This place was really hit or miss. Several of the dishes were great, but I don't think I'll be back again.

        The wood paneled room was incredibly beautiful, perhaps the most beautiful interior of a restaurant I've been in recently. No acoustic tiles on the ceiling meant it was quite loud. The music was soft, but added to a loud environment.

        Our waitress was... awkward. She was disturbingly smiley, and mumbled at an inaudible level so that we nearly had to ask her to repeat herself every time. Also, she seemed to have no knowledge of the menu other than to tell us to order more stuff. OK. We ordered drinks and 5 dishes. My porter was excellent, my gf's sangria was watery. The chilled cucumber soup and Zucchini Dan Dan noodles came first. I highly recommend the cucumber soup. The distillation of Tandoori flavors was superb and well paired. No complaints about the Dan Dan noodles, they were quite tasty too.

        A rather large gnat landed in my beer, and I'd kill two more before leaving. Ok. Next came the fresh peas and cauliflower buckwheat crepe and hearts of palm tagine. I very much recommend these dishes too, the distillation of flavors in both were excellent. The peas were particularly outstanding in very rich, buttery flavored broth. The heart of palm had a taste and texture not unlike scallops. Very good.

        At this point we both agreed the chefs at Vedge had a very high ability for distilling down key flavors (the kind that are understated in many other foods). So in spite of the uncertainty we had regarding the pairing of flavors in Yuzu cornbread, we ordered anyway, excited to see how they would translate miso flavor into icing. It was one of the worst items I've ever had at a restaurant. It tasted like someone had emptied a shaker of salt onto pillsbury frosting, mixed in pineapple and placed the concoction on top of very dry cornbread. What a way to end a meal!!

        We were anxious to leave, so when the check came I took it up to the front to pay. I find restaurants normally don't want to give the impression of pushing you out the door, so often they're not super fast to pick up my card. When I'm in a hurry and need to be someplace I'll often walk the bill up to pay, I think this is pretty normal. The host at the front informed me my waitress needed to take care of that, and that I needed to sit back down. Wow. I wanted to scream: get me out of here.

        So would I recommend Vedge or ever go back? Probably not. Most of the food was quite good, and the things that detracted from our evening were mostly minor. But there were a lot of them. It was also pricey. I expect that in a top restaurant, but this is not one. How do I know? No one ever came to our table to ask how our meal was. There are many great restaurants in Philadelphia, I'm not sure why I would go back here?

        Leaving Vedge I felt much the same as I did at the end of the workday, tired, stressed and a bit (V)edgy.

        Food

        -As previously alluded, there are no animal products at all used in Vedge's kitchen- no meat, no cream, no cheese, no butter and definitely no bacon. Everything you see is made of vegetables. As off-putting as that may sound to some of you, let me assure you from one carnivore to another- there is nothing to be unsatisfied about. Every taste, texture and aroma will prove itself satisfying, and you'll inevitably leave feeling satiated and content.
        -Landau has been lauded (hehe- see what I did there with those similar words??) as one of the city's top chefs, but I will venture to say he's in North America's top. There are not that many chefs I know who can become so comfortable with the biochemical properties of different vegetables that they can find ways to make them taste like dairy and meat.
        -The menu is divided into share-plates For the Table (3 for $9), the Vedge Bar (cold items), Hot Plates (hot items), The Dirt List (sides) and Desserts.

        For the Table (3 for $9)
        U-Peel Lupini Beans with Piri Piri & Fried Garlic; Curry Pickled Cauliflower; Chili Roasted Almonds

        A fun choose-your-own-adventure dish, all of which were tasty and refreshing. We happened to actually pick three options with a solid level of heat, but they were so addictively tasty, that didn't stop me. I particularly enjoyed the pickled cauliflower, which had a perfect balance of acid, sweet and heat.

        Chilled Corn Soup ($9)
        Cubanelle Pepper, Yuzu, Cilantro Crumbs

        One of my favourite dishes of the night. I loved the sweetness of the smooth thin corn puree with the supple pops of delicate kernels strewn throughout and the refreshing crispy crumbs on top.

        Salt Roasted Gold Beets ($10)
        Avocado, Smoked Tofu, Rye, Capers, Creamy Cucumber

        A tasty take on lox, cream cheese and dark rye bread. I loved the natural sweetness of the beets against the creamy layer of tofu and avocado, as well as the satisfying bite of the briny capers.

        Fresh Peas & Cauliflower ($14)
        Buckwheat Crepe, Beech Mushrooms, Preserved Lemon

        Another favourite dish for me that I would absolutely love to try to replicate. The nutty chewy buckwheat crepe was packed with a creamy cauliflower puree reminiscent of ricotta cheese. With such a lusciously decadent mouthful, the crepe paired beautifully with the sweet fresh peas and earthy mushrooms.

        Roasted Maitake & Trumpet Mushrooms ($15)
        Celery Root Fritter, Smoked Leek Remoulade

        As expected from such hearty shrooms, the maitakes and trumpet were satisfyingly meaty, and totally addictive paired with the sweet celeriac fritter and tangy remoulade. A stellar vegetarian dish.

        Grilled Seitan ($16)
        Swiss Chard, Whipped Tahini, Pickled Turnips, Za'atar

        I am no expert when it comes to vegan cuisine, so I haven't had a whole lot of really excellent seitan dishes in my day. But man, if this was what it always tasted like, I would totally hit this up more often. The protein was smoky and tender with a texture nearly identical to succulent shawarma chicken, absolutely brilliant with the Middle Eastern spices and nutty cream.

        Brussels Sprouts ($8)
        Shaved & Grilled, Smoked Mustard

        I will never turn down a tasty Brussels sprouts dish, and this baby did not disappoint. I loved the sweet smoky leaves with the tangy zippy bite of the smoked mustard cream, and really enjoyed the tender delicate texture.

        Saffron Cheesecake ($9)
        Basil Gel, Rhubarb Ice Cream, Black Olive-Pistachio Crumble

        The servers told us that aside from Chef Landau, no one in the restaurant is aware of the recipe for the cheesecake base, and I'll tell you, it really stumped me. It was creamy, tangy and blessedly dense with an aromatic perfume from the saffron. I was definitely skeptical about the vegan ice "cream", but Vedge nailed it with the luscious creamy texture of the Rhubarb frozen treat. And to help cut some of the richness and sweetness going on, the slightly savoury bite of the olive crumble balanced everything out.

        Cherry Jelly Donut ($8)
        Horseradish Cream, Zhoug, Turkish Iced Coffee

        Another stand-out dessert with an interesting Middle-Eastern flavour profile and a satisfying balance of savoury and sweet. The donuts reminded me of some of the "cronuts" I've had, in that they were wickedly crispy on the outside and meltingly moist within. I loved the mild heat of the zhoug and the horseradish spicy-sweet cream with the tangy bite of the cherries on the plate. The unsweetened coffee was also a nice touch as it added a mildly bitter hit to take the edge off the sweets. After these two sweet picks, I would definitely return to Vedge to sample all of the desserts.

        Closing Remarks

        -For 4 cocktails, 1 glass of Tokaji, and 9 dishes, the bill came to about $240 including tax and tip. Sure, there were no pricy oysters, foie gras, or kobe beef on my plate, but was it worth it? Holy, yes. The food and cocktails were satisfying and memorable, the service was professional and sweet, and the space is beautiful for a casual meal or a special date.

        I went with my best friend from Seattle.  She is vegetarian, I used to be vegan for 7 years but am now a carnivore.

        OVERVIEW

        The problem with vedge is the vegetables seem like an after thought.  Vedge tries to be so creative with flavors that they entirely miss the point that vegetables themselves are so damn flavorful they need less done to them than most any other kind of food.   Rarely have I been to a restaurant where the food is inedible, but we left much of the food on our plates and high tailed it around the corner for a budino dessert from Barbuzzo to get the nasty taste of Vedge out of our mouths.  

        THE DETAILS

        * Service
        Stiff, awkward, utterly impersonal

        * Menu
        Late August should be a produce wet dream for a vegetarian chef; this menu read like the dead of winter.  Nothing sounded enticing to us.  We ultimately ordered three hot dishes because nothing at all appealed to us on the rest of the menu.  

        *  Food
        (1) Cauliflower and peas dish had eight hard starchy peas on it.  If you cannot find enough seasonal peas, nix the item from the menu.  The crepe had a good flavor but the cauliflower puree inside had no taste - it was like dull baby food. (2) How in god's name do you make a flavorless tagine?  Honestly, the closest flavor I could come up with was the time my brother made me taste my ear wax in third grade.  (3) Smoked mushrooms did have a good flavor but the serving size was so scant it was ridiculous.  My friend disliked the remoulade, I thought it was edible.  (4) We ordered a ginger brew that tasted like clove water.  Perhaps they didn't stir it?  We sent it back it was so bad.

        * Atmosphere
        The one thing I love about this restaurant is they have done a fantastic job with the interior decor.  They have preserved an incredible building and let the bones of the architecture speak for itself.  It is simple and stunning.  

        SUMMARY

        We sat wondering during our entire dinner why people are so captivated with this restaurant.  Perhaps it is the novelty of a non-meat menu?  Perhaps most of the people eating there grew up on Applebee's and this is far out?  Maybe all the carnivores want to pat themselves on the back for being open minded enough?  I would not go back to eat here even if I were given a gift certificate.

We got Caramel apple cheesecake for dessert, which was really good, and a great combination of flavors. I never thought they could make non-dairy cheesecake that tastes better than the real thing.

        This is a must visit restaurant for anyone from Philly. Would recommend it to outsiders, but it's usually really difficult to get reservations. Still worth it, if you are willing to sit at the bar and have food. The service at this place is exceptional. Hands-down!
