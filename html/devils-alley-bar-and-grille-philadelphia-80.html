    I was real close to giving this place 4 stars but I stuck with 3. Overall, the food was decent, they have an alright selection of drafts, and the service was good. We came in without a reservation (tried to go to Monks and there was like an 80 minute wait), and sat right now. It was like 9pm on a Saturday night, so that was really cool. The place isn't crowded with a bunch of loud obnoxious people, and it's classy without being to sophisticated, kind of. Hard to explain unless you actually experience it.

    Start by talking about the service. Most likely the busiest night of the week, right in the middle of prime time, and we were seated right away, got out drinks right away, and food always came out with 10 minutes of ordering it. That's top notch. It's really too bad that the service was the only thing that was really great.

    All of our food was good, but nothing was GREAT. I got the catfish sandwich platter. It was pretty delicious but like, it just was really simple. Seems like the dish wasn't seasoned at all, and they counted on the sauce on the side to give it any flavor whatsoever. My friend ordered the strip steak. Now, that was the one dish that looked great all around, but he also paid like $20 for it, while we each paid like $10. My girlfriend ordered a hamburger platter. Now when I say simple, this was simple. Yeah they used good quality beef but, that's ALL it tasted like, just plain beef. They really don't season their food here at all from what I've seen. My friends girlfriend ordered the calamari salad. That once again, looked good, but so plain. I had a calamari salad at Buddakan once that was the best thing I ever have tasted with the most tasty dressing ever, this if I can remember correctly, came with a little cup of dressing on the side and nothing on it at all.

    I understand the food is moderately priced but at least have a little more kick to it. Now to the beer. Decent selection, kind of pricey but I could deal. Some good lagers and ales, pretty powerful fall in the range of like $6 or $7. You really only need a couple beers though they have some stronger ones. We had a couple real hoppy beers, and the girls got more seasonal ones. I believe they got the pumpkin ale and then tried a citrus-y type beer, both of which were pretty decent. Their cocktails seemed too pricey so we didn't order any.

    One thing that saved this place was their dessert. It was cheap, and HUGE. We got the peanut butter and chocolate bread pudding. Oh my god it was delicious and we couldn't even finish it between 4 people.

    As you can see the place seems good all around, but once you go there and sit down and have a meal, order a couple beers each, grab some dessert, and then get the bill for like $30 a person, all of sudden you find yourself questioning, "Was it really worth it?" I would say Happy Hour would be a absolutely great deal for this place though, but I haven't tried it.

    Overall I'd say the pluses outweigh the minuses. Would give it another shot, you should too.

    The Devil's Alley provides a consistently good spot to stop for beers (or cocktails) and some excellent bar food at any time from brunch to late night.  The decor of "bringing the outside in" with exposed walls, high ceilings and two-story plants with Christmas lights in them provides a relaxed setting of being at a great outdoor barbecue.  

    In dozens of visits, I have never had a bad meal.   The barbecue ribs and chicken REALLY stand out, and the price of their barbecue meals with a choice of two of their awesome sides are a great deal.  They make tasty fries, have a delicious chili featured daily, top their mac and cheese with tomato jam . . .  I could go on and on.  They have a great brunch menu too!

    The Devil's Alley loses one star for having uncomfortable chairs (seriously, take a scarf to fold and sit on) and an odd bathroom set-up with just a single stall for the entire upstairs bar area, and a weird communal sink for the downstairs bathroom.  Sometimes the service can be a little slow, but the only times I experienced this have been when they were quite busy and everyone was clearly doing the best they could.
