    A charming BYO with creative New American dishes in the heart of Center City, Matyson hits all the bases. Great food, intimate ambiance, attentive service. My dining partner and I were happily satisfied with all our dishes. The appetizer of grilled seabass over braised greens and carmelized salsify was a great starter to the meal. Light, flavorful, and interesting, it was hard not to eat it all too quickly. Next the foie gras stuff quail, cooked tender with a sweet balsamic sauce drizzled on top. While the foie gras flavor was hidden, the quail was cooked to perfection and was well complimented by the sauce. The pork chop, a favorite of our waiter, lightly charred on top, could have been more flavorful and moist, but the spaetzle below was divine. Flavorful and just chewy enough to entice, it was the highlight of the dish. Lastly the dessert was a treat. While not mindblowing, the chocolate espresso decedance was exactly that: decandent. Rich and dense, with the slight hint of bitter espresso, served slighlty chilled, the waiter accomodated our request to add their housemade peanutbutter ice cream on the side. The lemon-lime souffle torte was just the right about of sweet and tart, topped with a chilled blackberry sorbet that cut right through the tartness of the dessert. While non were as impressive as the appetizers, the desserts did their job well as the end to the meal, leaving us full and happy, and ready for another trip back.

    I've been getting in the habit of Yelping places, like Matyson, that I used to keep for myself; I guess I'm getting better at sharing in my old age.

    Matyson often has a table when other trendier places are full-up and pumping out the decibels and "you should've made a reservation" attitude.

    Their Monday through Thursday five-course tasting menu is a treat at $45.  You'll easily pay double for comparable food elsewhere in the city.  It's a great place to take out of town guests (including your parents), or to celebrate when you have an appropriate occasion (like tonight!).  The service is perfect.

    They're BYOB, but there's no need to make a one-off trip to the store before you arrive if you don't have anything laying on the shelf at home; there's a Premium liquor store less than a block away (down on Chesnut and to the right).

    Tonight's "Let's Get Nutty" themed tasting menu included:

    - Roasted Chestnut Soup: Foie Gras croutons, pickled wild mushrooms (Mmm... so good.  The chestnuts were roasted with duck fat giving them a decadent silkiness once they were pureed.  Additionally, there were actual chunks of foie gras in the soup.  Unforgettable.)

    - Lemongrass Poached Lobster: Peanut sambal, quick kimchi, mint & cilantro (Included a really generous half of a lobster tail which was amazingly flavored by the lemongrass.)

    - Seared Jumbo Scallop: Almonds, chickpeas & chorizo, smoked paprika-almond broth (The perfectly prepared, tender scallops were slightly overpowered by the spicy chorizo, so we just picked out the chorizo.)

    - Pistachio Crusted Lamb Loin: Toasted quinoa, currants, black olive tapenade (A perfect culmination of a wonderful meal.)

    - Dulce de Leche Napoleon: Pecan caramel sauce (Not subtle in the least, but I'll take four out of five odds any day...)

    Not bad for $45, eh?

    Listed in Itching to get your BYOB on?

    I had heard the hype about Philly's awesome BYOB restaurants.  After dining at Matyson, I now understand what everyone was talking about.  Consider me a believer!

    My gf and I had dinner with a couple friends at Matyson last Saturday night.  First off, the place is on the small side so definitely try to make reservations as early as possible if you want to eat at a "normal" hour.  We weren't in a rush so we made 9 pm reservations, though we lucked out by getting seated at around 8:40 pm.

    Onto the food.  To be honest, I was more impressed with the appetizers than the entrees, though that is not to say that the entrees weren't good.  I started with the Smoked Veal Sweetbreads while my gf got the Bacon Wrapped Dates.  Her dates were very tasty -- nice contrast between the sweetness of the dates with the saltiness of the bacon.  But as good as her dates were, they were nothing compared to my sweetbreads.  All I have to say is: holy crap, I never thought thymus glands could taste so good!  The sweetbreads were lightly breaded and fried, providing for a nice crunchy outside and rich creamy inside.  The sweetbreads comes with a fried poached egg, some greens and potatoes and a truffle vinaigrette drizzled all over.  The entire thing was simply divine.  After taking my first bite, I immediately regretted sharing a couple pieces with the other folks at my table.  Ok, just kidding.  Sort of.

    After being blown away by my appetizer, I was a little disappointed by my entree, the Pistachio Crusted Lamb Loin.  To be fair, the lamb was actually quite good and very well prepared.  It just didn't quite compare to the awesomeness of the sweetbreads.  My gf liked her Horseradish & Potato Crusted Halibut, which she found to be very moist and flaky.

    Our server was pretty good, though he did seem a bit spazzy.  He seemed to have difficulty maintaining a conversation with us while pouring our wine; he basically had to really concentrate on the wine, which meant he couldn't make eye contact with us while he talked.  No biggie -- nothing got spilled and he still managed to answer our questions.  The ambiance at Matyson is pleasant and very conversation friendly and the crowd is a bit on the older side (I know old is all relative, but we are in our late 20's/early 30's and we were probably the youngest people in the restaurant).  

    So definitely go check out Matyson if you haven't had the chance.  And make sure you order yourself the sweetbreads -- you won't regret it!

Specialties

One feature that sets us apart from the pack is our weekly tasting menu that showcases whatever is fresh and in season. The themes can range from lobster to vanilla. Five courses for $45 Monday through Thursday. Check the menu page for this weeks tasting menu.

History

Established in 2003.

Matyson opened its doors in the fall of 2003  and became an immediate success under the leadership of Matt & Sonjia Spector. Matt & Sonjia installed the framework for what Matyson has become today. After four great years the couple left to pursue another restaurant in the Napa Valley named Jole, but they left the restaurant in very capable hands. Younger cousin, Ben Puchowitz, whom had been working at Matyson as a line cook since its opening, assumed the role of Executive Chef, and his father, Butch Puchowitz became full owner. Ben has instilled his own personality into the food, without changing the original concepts. The staff of Matyson has continued to win awards and high acclaim for their refined cuisine and cozy atmosphere.
