    The Farm and Fisherman
    1120 Pine St, Philadelphia, PA 19107 (267) 687-1555
    Tuesdays - Sunday: 5:00-10:00pm
    BYOB thefarmandfishermanbyob.…

    The farm and fisherman is a small 30 seat restaurant owned by husband-wife team Josh & Colleen Lawler. Its premise as the name implies is a focus on sustainable, locally-sourced organic ingredients in the nearby area to go from "farm" to "tabletop". The menu is another highlight which fluctuates quickly due to availability of foods and the changes in seasons. One will never tire of dining here as the restaurant consistently puts out fresh takes on seasonal dishes. The restaurant is unimposing and does not have an exterior sign save the simple fishes logo above the door. It could easily be mistaken for a residential house and in my opinion is one of the better kept secrets of Philadelphia.

    The interior of the restaurant: done in a contemporary elegant style with gorgeous chandeliers, white table clothes and a pretty decent crowd. I was impressed that in such a small space, they were able to fit so many people and not make it seem very cramped.

    One caveat I must warn about is the seats abutting the wall (one of which I happened to be seated in) unfortunately have inter-spaced air vents for the air conditioning and I was sitting directly on top of one of them. It made for a chilly dinner until I was able to switch seats with my dining companion.

    My food choices:
    Frosted green doctor tomato gazpacho. Summer squash, canteloupe, cucumbers, tomato cloud. Lovely lemon verbana cream and cucumbers gave this soup a most refreshing summer feel.

    Summer vegetable salad: green beans, tarragon, shaved roots, tomatoes. The tarragon are the thin green herbs seen on the plate which added nice flavor to the dish. All the vegetables were so fresh it was ever apparent these were straight from the farm to the table.

    Grilled rosa bianca eggplant. almond puree, currants, burrata, roasted tomatoes. Rosa bianca is spanish for white rose, and represents the specific type of eggplant used which is generally a little lighter and sweeter than most varieties. The almond puree was tasty and the burrata was the perfect cheese choice. Burrata is a fresh Italian cheese made from mozzarella and cream.

    Gemmeli pasta: Walnut basil pesto, heirloom tomatoes, ricotta. This was not bad, but personally, I thought the eggplant much better. The pesto and pasta mixed with the creamy ricotta cheese.

    New England Pollack. Baby green beans, ruby streaks, soffritto, white garlic. Soffritto is carrots, onions and celery with some garlic that can be used to make base sauces in Italian cuisine. It is basically finely chopped vegetables. Ruby Streaks will change your mind about mustards with its tempting beauty and mildly pungent flavor that's deliciously fresh in salads.

    Natural Beef Ribeye. NJ brown rice, saffron, heirloom sweet peppers. Had wonderful taste to it. The au jus was very good as well. Excellent choice to the meal.

    Dessert consisted of coffee and the ginger panna cotta. Summer berries, oats, almonds. Fantastic. Loved the addition of the black berries. Classic carrot cake. Walnuts, cream cheese, peach butter. Very good as well. I can't decide if the carrot cake or the panna cotta was better. Both great finishes to excellent meals.

    Of Note: The Farm and Fisherman is a BYOB. Restroom areas were clean and maintained.

The ever-changing menu always features fresh seasonal vegetables and well-prepared and flavorful proteins.  Every seafood dish I have tried here has been excellent.  The chef avoids doctoring up the food with sauces or reductions for the sake of including such things.  I welcome any plate where the several ingredients comprising the dish are complementary and inventive flavor combinations.  This is a definite must-try for your next date night, however, just remember there are limited seats so you should make reservations in advance.

    9/29/2014

    I have nothing but fantastic things to say about the Farm and Fisherman, as it's one of the best restaurants we have dined at in Philadelphia. I was drawn to it because its owner/chef used to work at Blue Hill at Stone Barns (the "better" sister of Blue Hill New York), which was one of my favorite restaurants. Farm and Fisherman is a restaurant falling in the farm-to-table category but they're not pretentious about it whatsoever. Instead, they let the food do the talking and it really does show. We went to Farm and Fisherman for a special occasion during the week. I notified the staff beforehand that we were celebrating and they offered to compile a tasting menu to our liking, which was an extremely surprising and nice gesture. They offered to create either a 4 or 5 course menu, and we elected to go with the 5 course. The meal had a mixture of items that were both on and off the menu, and we were truly blown away. Course 1 was their beet steak, which is apparently a staple on the menu. It is perfectly combined with greek yogurt, balsamic vinegar and drizzled with honey, resulting in a wonderful sweet and sour flavor profile that compliments the beet steak perfectly. Course 2 was a small filet of bass over a smoked corn, pepper and bacon semi-chowder. The fish was crispy on the outside and the smoked corn enhanced the flavor of the dish. I call it a semi-chowder because it's not very creamy and is in between a chowder and a stew. It also had quinoa mixed in, giving it a lovely texture that unified all of the ingredients. Course 3 was what appears to be a combination of the Elmer Auction Farm Egg and the Grilled Heirloom Eggplant dishes. Our dish consisted of the grilled eggplant over beans with a poached egg on top. Everything blended so well, and the beans were a smoky, tangy flavor. The sauce it was prepared in also contained chopped black truffle, which quietly made the dish. Course 4 was ribeye tips, which were savory and perfectly cooked. They were served with a pistachio pesto and roasted potatoes, all topped with a light dusting of homemade candied pistachio brittle. Course 5 was dessert, where we had panna cotta and an amazing portion of filo dough "crackers" layered with chocolate mousse and drizzled with caramel.  The panna cota was good but the filo dough dish was amazing, and is quite similar to Italian Millefoglie, except with chocolate mousse instead of cream. Throughout the meal, we shared a nice bottle of red wine that we brought from home, since Farm and Fisherman is a BYOB. The waitstaff were all kind and helpful, and were happy to discuss the contents of each dish and how everything was prepared (we were delighted to hear that several dishes even contained ingredients straight from the chef's garden). Every dish was so well prepared and had a fantastic combination of flavors--the ingredients combined together to make the particular dish, rather than single flavors standing out and/or overpowering all else. We can't wait to go back!
