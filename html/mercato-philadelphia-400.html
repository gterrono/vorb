    Completely fabulous. I went with four other people, while visiting friends in Philly, and the outdoor seating was charming, the service was pretty good (our waiter spilled a bunch of the prosecco we brought so that was kind of "meh", but other than that, he was great) and the food was AMAZING.

    We did not get the tasting menu, but we did make sure to get a different dish for each person, in order to sample. My favorite was (conveniently) my own dish. I had the special, which was a proscsiutto wrapped some sort of meaty white fish...I want to say sole...(this was a week ago...) with crab meat flaking around it and covered in beurre blanc sauce. I think my mouth just started to water while typing this...

    There were also scallops, pyramid pasta, squid ink pasta, and the ragu. All perfectly excellent. The dessert left a wee bit to be desired. (Gelatto in an espresso cup is not, like, the most exciting thing in the world.) But I'm still giving five stars for such a nice evening.

    The outdoor summer ambiance was totally charming. We arrived at 8pm, just enough time to enjoy the setting sun and then have dessert by candlelight.

    If you're in Philly and you want to enjoy a special occasion without going the far end of the high dining spectrum, I definitely recommend this place.

    This place is THE ONE.  How did it only get two bells?  I've been here with friends but recently took my family here, so we were able to try a large portion of the menu and enjoy BYOB.

    The "make your own cheese / charcuterie plate" is a sizeable appetizer.  We tried the taleggio, truffle camembert, and soft blue cheese - it's rare that I like anything better than cheese with truffles in it, but the taleggio stole the show.  We also tried the proscuitto and the chorizo - very flavorful.  In addition to this appetizer, we also tried the mussles/clams and the meatballs.  ORDER THE MEATBALLS.  Hello-stuffed-with-fontina... these were my favorite item of the night.  I may get this as my dinner next time I come here as it's a pretty hefty portion - my dad was not complaining.

    For our meals, we tried the pyramid pasta (a perennial favorite, you must try it once), the rigatoni amatriciana, the raviolo, and the veal milanese.  The raviolo was a singular, large, stuffed ravioli that took up nearly half the plate in a delicious corn / garlic white sauce - I will be ordering this all to myself eventually.

    The wait staff was super accommodating - when we asked for bread to enjoy with the cheese and the clams/mussels sauce, they did not bring out a measly three pieces - they brought us an overflowing plate of bread. AMEN.  And when my mother wanted a different starch with her veal, they whipped up some delicious fingerling potatoes.

    All in all - a great BYOB for those who want something a little more gourmet than the traditional Philly Italian spots.

    3 letters. 1 word. WOW.
    Mercato is exquisite. As we all know, I am a stickler for great Italian food, and super picky as well after studying abroad in Rome- nothing ever comes close.
    Mercato was perfection from the decor to the food.
    We came here on Tuesday night and reserved a table for 6 for my roommate's birthday. Everyone brought a bottle of wine (no corkage fees WAHOO!) and we made our experience last over 3 hours.
    We started with the meatballs as an app- three perfectly portioned meatballs filled with fontina topped with their red sauce. Granted, the best red sauce I have come across in the city was not found here, the dish was still wonderfully executed. I then ordered the summer corn risotto as a main. I thought $19 was steep in price for a risotto dish with no meat, but it was so filling, I had to take half of it home- $19 seemed reasonable at the end of the evening. The risotto was the best risotto I have had since moving back from Italy. Charred sweet Jersey corn, truffle, micro arugula and maitake mushrooms and peccorino romano cheese. PERFECTION.
    My roommate had the raviolo- one huge ravioli stuffed with ricotta and an over easy egg. The egg made the dish even more savory and the charred corn nestled in the romano cream sauce added richness. Both dishes were fantastic.
    The service was mediocre. The restaurant is so small so there is no excuse to have to flag down the staff to get another bottle of wine open or to order.
    I would definitely come back here- the food was too good.
