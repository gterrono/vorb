    Listed in Italian Market/ South Philly

    My boyfriend and I are huge fans of the Cantina. The waitstaff is always great, the service is fast and friendly and they have great outdoor seating when the weather warms up. It has a great atmosphere and makes for a great date night!

    They give you chips and salsa to start out which is always nice. Their salsa verde is my favorite. Flavorful and not overly spicy. The food is some of the best Mexican we've had. The nachos, burritos, quesadillas and fajitas never disappoint. The meat is very flavorful and the portions are huge! They also have a lot of vegetarian and vegan options. You can't go here without having a margarita! They are pricy, but very strong and very delicious. A pitcher of frozen could definitely get a buzz on for 3-4 people. I believe they have happy hour specials, but we are never there early enough to take advantage of them.

    There are a few downsides though. This place gets PACKED at night, more so in warm weather, so expect a wait, especially if you want to sit outside. Also, the prices are very high. My boyfriend and I split a frozen margarita pitcher, an appetizer and an order of fajitas and our bill was about $60. I don't mind paying that for good food, but this is not the place to go for a cheap night out. A lot of the add-ons are pricey too. If you want guac, cheese, etc. added to your platter, expect to pay a little more.

    It's a great place to go with a group of friends. Get some nachos, some pitchers and enjoy!

    Their drink selection is pretty impressive. Would recommend the Sly Fox IPA. They also are known for their margaritas. I didn't have one, but my friend did and liked the peach variety.

    They lead off with home made tortilla chips with salsa picante and salsa verde. Both dips were nice but the picante could use a bit of heat IMO.  I had their empanadas, chicken quesadillas, and a mexican-type shrimp scampi (no pasta) but I forgot the name.     Whenever I visit a new Mexican joint I order a house margarita.  This one was served in a heavy glass cup and was perfect for $4.50.  Of course I was sipping on it while munching on the complimentary chips with two salsas.

    As I perused the menu I spotted chorizo tacos. Chorizo is one of my favorite foods.  It's made in several countries with different spices and even different meats but the Mexican version is my favorite.  The guy that invented it should be made a saint.  That way I could wear a cool little medal around my neck.  I can see in now; Miguel XXX, the Patron Saint of Chorizo.  Anyway the chorizo tacos arrived.  Three warm doubled soft tortillas stuffed with meat, onion, and cilantro.  I could have added extra toppings for 50 cents or so each.  They were spicy and near perfect.  Easily some of the best tacos I have ever consumed.

    So I had great tacos and a strong margarita in a great atmosphere.  Oh, the bartender was just fine.  I visit here again in a heartbeat!
