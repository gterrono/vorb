    Locked away from the busy streets is a secret alleyway that is cleverly designed as a garden with delicious fine dining. It is perfect for a glass of wine, cocktail or cheese plate. The prices are a splurge but try the 'beekeeper' with its honey lemonade, dark rum and ginger. It isn't exactly cutting edge but the 'beekeeper' went well with my meal and hey, if I didn't have to make it, even better.

    On the other hand, my boyfriend thought the 'gardener' - a fragrant mint mojito was appealing but thought the rum was missing or it just tasted like the key ingredient was gone. On 2nd rounds, I stuck with my 'beekeeper' and the boyfriend tried plan B with the 'farmer' which was a step up in quenching the drink he wanted. Still, beekeeper was the winner in our little drink contest. As a side note--if you re-read that last paragraph I said it sounds like a naughty affair at the local farm. I dig it.

    Talula's has a large selection of small cheese plates from $10 - 18. We decided on  the one called Not Your Granny's and it had 6 cow and goat cheeses made from local farms. Each had a smooth and creamy taste. We both had our favorites but they weren't the same cheeses. I've had better compliments with cheese boards at wine bars but I liked the toasted flatbread when compared to a fresh bread. We did have to ask for extra but there wasn't a problem when we requested a refill. Overall, Granny's was a 4 star cheese board.

    Our entrees were splurge #2 (unless you're bloody rich and do this all the time of course! Lucky!). The boyfriend had a duck breast, foie gras, confit leg parcel and beet greens with smoked beets for 28.00. I had the golden potato gnocchi, exotic mushrooms and egg in a raisin puree and mushroom jus for 25.00. Gnocchi is generally simple to make in most fine dining places. I'm torn with two thoughts: I think it was cooked the best at Talulas, but still more could have been done with the rest of it. The sauce was good and there isn't anything that I could complain about but fantastic food is fantastic food regardless of the price tag. I wasn't wowed and when you splurge you expect MORE. My boyfriend still had a dish that was also best described as generic in the world of fine dining. We liked our meals but we've had better.  

    Looking back I think the beekeeper and the cheese plate with the outdoor garden seating is what would bring me back to Talula's Garden.

    Listed in My Love Affair With Starr

    My birthday this year fell on a Wednesday, which is probably the worst day to have that happen. It sucked more because my bf had to leave that morning for a conference in Boston, but his wonderful sister was so sweet and thoughtful she told me that she was going to take me out to dinner at a restaurant of my choice. I had noted that Talula's Garden had opened recently, and after our experience at Talula's Table I was eager to check out her Philly venture with Starr.

    The garden entrance is fantastic - they struck a nice balance between chic and rustic, and the cute water fountain on one wall adds a nice ambiance to the vine covered area. It was so nice outside that we had to dine there - the views we got of the interior dining areas were great, much more subtle than Starr's other restaurants but with the same level of quality. Not sure how the noise level is while dining inside, but it didn't seem as bad as at his other ventures. I'd recommend eating outside though, if possible. You have to take advantage of outdoor-dining weather while we still can!

    But more importantly, on to the food. I opted to begin my celebration with a lovely glass of Prosecco, and the warm bread with apricot butter they give each table. Choosing dishes was impossible, and thank goodness she likes to share dishes too because then I would've been reduced to tears. Everything sounds delicious! And even better - it is!! We started with the chilled golden beet soup with sliced baby beets and buttermilk foam, which was light and sweet and perfect for people who love beets. The soft shell crab with spicy fennel pickle and lemon mayo was fantastic as well, the meat was so juicy and I loved that the crab wasn't drenched in batter; it made it a much lighter tasting dish than the usual soft shells you find.

    We then shared the caramelized sea scallops on kohlrabi puree with pine nuts, lemon, lovage and watercress. The scallops were super tender and succulent (although I'm going to complain about their size; they're not dry scallops, and if I can get myself some big ass scallops at Reading Terminal I don't understand why nice restaurants don't do the same), and the kohlrabi puree added to their buttery flavor nicely. We had initially ordered the crab and lobster salad, but our lovely waiter accidentally entered in the spring salad and apologized for his error. We really didn't mind because by the time we realized it, we had consumed the whole plate of local feta, almonds, almond milk dressing, and croutons.

    ...and we can't forget about dessert. It's my birthday, I wasn't getting a cake, so instead we shared two desserts. Their little sweet doughnuts, made with ricotta, were fantastic and still quite hot when they reached our table! Served with strawberry-lavender sauce and homemade nutella, it was hard not to lick the plate clean of those sauces. The rhubarb gateau (olive oil shortbread, rhubarb, citrus chibouste, rhubarb sorbet) was another winner. Every component of that dish was delicious, and combined it was even better! I should probably mention that the plating here is top notch, in particular with those desserts. Mmmm, I can still taste them. Service here is also great, our waiter always checked on us, water was refilled before glasses were empty, every server was knowledgeable about the dishes, and even one of the hosts came over to make sure we were enjoying ourselves. Needless to say, I'm checking my calendar now to go back with my bf - those tempura fried stuffed squash blossoms are calling me...as is everything else on that menu...

    I don't even know how many stars to give this place because as I left, I was a mixed bag of emotions. My belly satisfied, but my wallet was so, so empty.

The fish was flaky and seared nicely, the foam (which I was totally skeptical of) was flavorful, and the artichokes with oxtail were melt-in-your-mouth good. The dish was light, fresh and summery, but still flavorful. I tried some of the lamb and beef dishes, both of which were tender and moist. And finally, the service- our waitress was really nice, helpful with our dinner choices, and volunteered to split our bill 4 ways according to what we ordered- I think she could tell that we were all struggling with the math...

    The not-so-good things: My drink was so-so. I had heard great things about them and was a sucker for the mojito made with garden mint. Not only was the drink not minty, but I couldn't taste any alcohol in it. The brioche rolls with ramp butter were buttery but still dry and stale tasting. How does that happen when they have so much butter in them? I had high hopes for them considering they looked so cute on the plate. The cheese selections were extensive and the little homemade crisps that came with them were good, but the portions were miniscule for what they charged. The accompaniments seemed to be an after-thought. I also think they should serve a card with the cheese names and flavor profiles on them. And you know how I said that the food was good? Well, it should be for what they charged. I'm all for proper sized portions and not the gigantic ones that you see in a lot of places, but these were small. Especially for the price. If they left the portion size the same and just knocked $5-10 off each dish, I may consider going back there. After all, most of the food was really well-prepared. But with those prices, it's probably too expensive for a repeat visit for a special occasion. And that's too bad, because I love their local emphasis and their flavor profiles.

    An epic Saturday night disaster.   Giving Talula's 2 stars instead of zero because after the fiasco, management did everything they could to make it up to us.

    Had a 9:15 reservation for 2 people -- our anniversary celebration.  Were told they were running 15 minutes behind.  No problem.  At 9:45, after no communication, we inquired again.  Then were advised we had 1 group in front of us.  The hostess was neither apologetic nor kind about the situation.   A manager intervened very shortly thereafter, apologized profusely and offered a free cheese plate.

    We were finally seated at 10 p.m., in the bar area.  Not what we had in mind for our anniversary.  If we had wanted to eat at a bar, we would have gone to Pub and Kitchen or GoodDog.  Given the late hour, we took the table.

    Our knowledgeable waiter took drink orders, delivered beverages and then disappeared.  We had to ask someone else to seek him out.  There may have been confusion about our order because of the complimentary cheese plate on our table.  We ordered at 10:40 p.m.  

    The cheese plate selected by the manager was fantastic, special and arguably the best dish of the evening.

    We had the salad of raw tuna, which was fabulous, and the oxtail tortelloni.  The broth for the tortelloni was overly salted, to the point of being nearly inedible.

    For main courses, we had the bluefish (very good) and the cod.  The cod, like the tortelloni, was extremely salty.  The main course arrived at 11:30.  At that point, it was way too late to argue about it or substitute another dish.

    When our waiter inquired about the uneaten cod, we explained the issue.  To the restaurant's credit, at the end of our meal, the manager came by and told us our dinner was on the house and would not let us pay for any of it.  She encouraged us to come back another time and suggested trying on a less busy night.  

    We genuinely appreciated that management took responsibility for what appears to be an unusual crater in Talula's food and service.  With that said, it was a truly disappointing evening.  I much prefer to write more positive reviews.
