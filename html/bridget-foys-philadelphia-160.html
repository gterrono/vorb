    I made a reservation for Saturday afternoon lunch with two friends. I had been wanting to visit Bridget Foy's for a while and I was not disappointed! A few small hiccups and oddities, but this place is a solid four stars.

    Service: The waitress/bartender came over right away and asked if I wanted to order something so that it would come out right after the rest of my party arrived. Drinks took about 30 seconds to arrive after being ordered (no lie). Additionally, she was able to provide us with tons of guidance about the best dishes to order.

    Food: We were very hungry and we left completely stuffed from the amount of food we ordered. We got the mushroom flatbread and the BBQ pulled pork spring rolls. The spring rolls were very tasty and the shells were flaky and crisp. The sauce was killer, but overall I didn't notice too much BBQ flavor. The flatbread was heavenly, if a little oily from the mushrooms/artichokes. The ingredients also included mozzarella and arugula and the whole tasted fresh and light. We all shared an order of the mac-and-cheese, which had potato chips crumbled on top and four cheeses. HOLY GOD, ORDER THIS. I would have been happier with a thicker crust on top (the chips were more of a dusting), but seriously, this is probably the best mac-and-cheese that I've had in Philadelphia. My friend got the short rib tacos, the other friend got the standard burger, and I got the crispy chicken sandwich. Everyone guarded their food jealously, so I can only comment on mine: although (again), the aioli was fantastic and fresh, it really just seemed like dressed up Chick-Fil-A. Additionally, the fries were a little limp and soggy-- those definitely could be done better. Finally, even though we were really too full to order it, we got dessert: chocolate torte (yay for Yelp check-in rewards). It was so wonderfully dense and chocolatey (almost fudgey) that I couldn't even deal. Delicious. It also had strawberry preserves and thick whipped cream. I had to wash it down with a glass of water; thank goodness, it was being shared.

    Drinks: I'm feeling that $8 is a reasonable price for a specialty cocktail, and Foy's orange take on the Bellini was delicious. The menu specifically said blood orange, but my garnish was a regular orange, so that was a little odd.

    Yum. Yum. Yum. Definitely order the mac-and-cheese and the chocolate torte. You will not be disappointed.

    I have been to Bridget Foy's twice now, but my experience is unique for two reasons.

    1. On both occasions we were renting the upstairs for private events.
    2. Both times were for breakfast.

    I would say that if you are looking to plan a breakfast or brunch event, this place is pretty perfect.  The ambiance is nice but not over the top, and a group of 15 - 40ish can feel comfortable in one of the two event space.

    I am a frequent organizer of mid-sized group events (25-30 people) and I have experienced the good, the bad, and the ugly of planning private parties.

    Good:
    - The breakfast options are great.  I especially enjoyed the baked french toast and the salmon quiche.  In my opinion buffet style is just easier, but they do offer table service as well.
    - The servers are always very sweet, but since I have private events we have a dedicated person for our room.
    - No hassle.  This is a big one.  You pick out what you want from the menu, pay the deposit with a card, and pay the balance the day of the event.  Bridget's lays the linens, provides flowers, and had a chord for me to use to connect my laptop to the TV (we played Family Feud).  There are no room fees as long as you spend their stated minimum.
    - Easy parking!  What?  Early morning parking in the area is a breeze (unlike evening parking).  At 10 a.m. none of our guests had a problem at all.

    The Bad:
    - No real issues, except that maybe the flowers could have been on the table earlier.  I think the girl may have just forgotten, but it definitely did not dampen our experience.  We will definitely be back.

History

Established in 1978.

Bridget Foy's restaurant has been a Philadelphia staple since 1978. The business started as East Philly Café.  Owner John Foy, met his wife at the restaurant, they had a daughter named Bridget and changed to name to Bridget Foy's.  Bridget is all grown up and is now running the business!
