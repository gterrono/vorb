    My wife and I had our anniversary dinner here at 8:30pm on an October Friday.  We made reservations though Open table so we were able to walk right in an sit down.  Like everyone says the place is tiny.  We had a table for two in the rear middle of the room but did not feel too squashed and soon forgot about the other tables around us.  It was noisy but was more energetic to me than overly annoying.

    We elected to get two small plates and a main course and skipped desert.  The portions were perfect and the waitress made helpful suggestions as to what main dishes to order with our small plates so we would not get overly stuffed and she was spot on.  We had the foie gras and a spicy beat salad that were both delicious and complimented each other well.  We then had a carrot soup that was a special and  the kale salad.  I usually do not get too excited about salads but both the beat and the kale salad were some of the most unique and flavorful salads I have had.  We then had the sea bass and diver scallops for our entrees.  Both cooked perfectly and were not overly filling.  The pork dish sounded amazing but the waitress indicated it was a larger portion and may be too filling with our two starters.

    We both loved our meals and would come back again.  The wait staff was pleasant and attentive and we never felt rushed.  The waitress also was very forthcoming with her favorites and did not steer us wrong.  The fact it is a BYOB made this an affordable meal for the quality of the food.

    This is a great place for date night or catching up with a friend. It's a pretty small place (like all good BYOs in Center City), so I wouldn't recommend it for a large group. I'd also recommend calling way in advance for a reservation.

    In terms of food, everything is pretty good. They menu, while a little small, is impressively varied for its size and has a good mix of traditional favorites and contemporary twists. I'd tell you what I had (the watermelon gazpacho was a highlight) but the menu will probably have already changed by the time you get there. While it's not the farm-to-table setup like Talula's Garden, it seems to be seasonally adjusted to accommodate changes in the weather and flavors.

    The best part of the evening was definitely the service, though. Often times you can get pretty snooty service in a place like this, but everyone was very nice all evening. They were particularly attentive about making sure our wine was at the right temperature, our food was right, and everything was going well. I'd definitely go back!

    What a find!! Went to Matyson for our pre-pre-New Years Eve dinner on 12/30. When I made the reservation through Opentable (which was SUPER easy btw), they informed me that there was a $35 tasting menu that night, but didn't tell us what was on it, and I forgot to ask. I figured, based on previous reviews, that no matter what it ended up being, it would be good. So, I didn't ask and expected to be surprised.

    Also, $35 seemed pretty cheap for a four course meal, so we were psyched!

    When we arrived at Matyson, we received the tasting menu which was titled "Beef Sundays." What a relief! Since Mr. is a bit of a picky eater, I was nervous about there being something he liked. Sure enough, everything was a hit!

    We started off with the Mushroom Barley soup, with smoked oyster mushrooms and kale, which was nice and warm and a great way to start the meal, having just come in from the freezing cold.

    Next up was quite possibly our favorite, the Ancho Chile Braised short rib on a goat cheese arepa with salsa verde. This was a pulled beef appetizer on a goat cheese flavored soft cracker/pita/bread. On the side was a spicy salsa verde which was deliciously spicy.

    Then we had the Black Angus Hanger Steak Frites with truffle fries. This was also very tasty and just the right amount, served with a side of salad greens and the amazing truffle fries.

    Finally we had the Meyer Lemon cheese cake which was decadent and deliciously topped with raspberry whipped cream.

    All in all, the meal was delicious, servers were attentive and happy to answer questions and fill our water glasses. Decor wasn't so impressive, and it was a little cold right by the door, but I'm willing to overlook those things since the food makes up for it.

Specialties

One feature that sets us apart from the pack is our weekly tasting menu that showcases whatever is fresh and in season. The themes can range from lobster to vanilla. Five courses for $45 Monday through Thursday. Check the menu page for this weeks tasting menu.

History

Established in 2003.

Matyson opened its doors in the fall of 2003  and became an immediate success under the leadership of Matt & Sonjia Spector. Matt & Sonjia installed the framework for what Matyson has become today. After four great years the couple left to pursue another restaurant in the Napa Valley named Jole, but they left the restaurant in very capable hands. Younger cousin, Ben Puchowitz, whom had been working at Matyson as a line cook since its opening, assumed the role of Executive Chef, and his father, Butch Puchowitz became full owner. Ben has instilled his own personality into the food, without changing the original concepts. The staff of Matyson has continued to win awards and high acclaim for their refined cuisine and cozy atmosphere.
