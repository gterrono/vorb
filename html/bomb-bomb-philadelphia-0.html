    i heard the bbq at Bomb Bomb was ... the bomb, so I had to go check it out. Plus, I recently watched the movie "10th and Wolf" and was very confused by the depiction of that area. Is this some sort of magical South Philly with doubly wide streets and trees everywhere? No, its still the Philly I know and love (can't fool me hollywood!) Actually, I was kind of expecting something like Ron's or Phoebe's or maybe even Dwight's, but instead I found a small italian restaurant with a great local South Philly vibe to it. The waitress called us "hon" and was South Philly through and through. But when i saw the menu I thought "Italian food? I'm here for ribs!" Sure enough, the only piece of bbq on Bomb Bomb BBQ's menu is a baby back rib platter. I quickly ordered a platter and it came piping hot with a giant side of fries that weren't that bad. The ribs have the look of ribs from Chilli's, but man they are quite tasty. Mostly sweet, a small amount of tangy smokiness. But more than anything, TENDER! You could literally suck the meat off the bone here. I ate the whole entire rack while enjoying a couple drinks. Street parking was easy on a weeknight in the summer.

    I have only had the ribs, but if you like BBQ this might be the best place to get some decent ribs in all of South Philly. Highly recommended, I just wish they had more on the menu than baby backs. I mean, if these are this good, can a man get some brisket?!?

    Bomb Bomb is a friendly, cozy place with great food and usually very good service.

    The food has never disappointed, but the menu is a bit of a wonderful mish mash. The Italian entrees are good. The Sammiches are good. The barbecue is good. Whatever's on special is usually very very good. We were recently there for "Calamari week."

    This place is around the corner from my house, and I am sooooo glad! No, it doesn't have the best food in the known universe. But, it has a unique South Philly flavor that makes you love it. From the friendly bar to the great waitstaff, you are made to feel like you're visiting old friends.

    I have to say, as a Southerner transplanted to the NE . . . I'm pretty picky about ribs and barbecue and such - but, the ribs here are really good! The barbecue chicken can be a little overcooked, but the ribs are succulent, full of flavor and just the right amount of caramelization. Lovely!

    The Italian dishes - well, I'm not a huge Italian food fan - I can take it or leave it. But, the crab cakes here are good (can be a little salty, but I can deal with salt), the calamari appetizer is excellent, with a syrupy sweet sauce that pairs perfectly. You get a lot of food, great attentive service and the added bonus of having the retired owner (his son now runs the place, I understand) holding court at the front table in the bar - greeting customers, chatting about the bars rich history (yes, it WAS bombed twice - hence Bomb Bomb) and making you glad you came. He greets the regulars like old friends (and the entire staff does this, too) - and is just the added touch to make this little South Philly gem a real treasure for the neighborhood.

    There's definitely a special feel to this place.  Some won't get it.  But if you love South Philly like I do, you will get it.  

    I made a reservation for my Mom's birthday this past Friday.  She's been wanting to try this place for quite some time.  When we got there, they didn't quite have our table ready.  But the bartender made it up to us by comping a round of drinks.  Nice touch.  

    When we did get the table, I was surprised to see just one waitress working the room.  I'd say there were 20 people dining in a room that doesn't hold much more.  The bartender would help run some dishes out.  They seem to make a good team.  I can see why other reviewers might knock the place for this.  i will say that in our experience, we didn't experience any overly long waits for food once we sat down.  

    Our table loved the bread and oil, which we were told had a touch of Locatelli cheese.  That would make my grandmother happy who swears by the stuff if she had been with us.  We also enjoyed the Caprese Salad.  I'm not much for raw tomatoes in general, but these combined with the fresh mozzarella were working for me.  

    Of course, after reading reviews, I knew I had to try the barbecue.  Mom and I went for the Chicken and Ribs combo with a side of pasta, while my wife went for the gnocchi with a blush sauce.  

    The ribs... yes, I can see why they would win some awards!  They came off the bone easily, but didn't fall off.  Falling off is a sign of overcooked ribs.  These were cooked just the right amount.  These ribs needed the sauce to be good.  I could tell that much.  But the sauce is a winner, and I finished my half rack happily.  Mom loved them too.  

    But I really have to say that my wife's dish was probably the hit of the night.  She couldn't get enough of the sauce.  In fact, she's dreaming of the leftovers as I write this.  My taste of the dish confirmed it.  That''s an addictive dish.  Be sure to ask for the blush sauce.  

    So what kept this from being 5 stars?  Well, the chicken was a bit overcooked and the pasta sauce was pretty basic.  Also, my sauce as well as my wife's blush sauce all had the faintest hint of a crab taste.  Considering they do a heavy seafood business, it's possible that the sauces pick up a touch of crab flavor through some kind of kitchen osmosis.  Now, if you have any issue with crabs, this might be a concern to you.  For me, it was not bad by any means.  But not 5 star good for what I had.  The ribs were 5 star good, and I decided next time I would just order a full rack instead.  

    Desserts were incredible.  The cannoli cake was truly special, and kudos to the waitress who by now was far less busy, and came over to sing Happy Birthday to Mom and make it a little more special.  The cake had a wonderful buttery taste to it.  

    My wife said it best.  She felt like we were eating at someone's family home.  I will certainly recommend this place to others.

Specialties

For the past 20 years, Bomb Bomb has prided itself in Award Winning BBQ Baby Back Ribs, Juicy and Plump Mussels, as well as your favorite Italian Specialities.

Our unique, slow cooked ribs are fall-off-the-bone tender and smothered in our homemade original or spicy BBQ sauce. With two NBC 10 Golden Spoon Awards and a Gary Maddox BBQ Challenge Award, our ribs continue to be a staple of our quality.

Not in the mood for BBQ? Try one of countless seafood platters or Italian specialities. Our famous mussel recipe has only been perfected since Frank Sr took over the tavern in 1951, and with the additions of some of your favorite Italian specialities such as chicken parm and pasta dishes, Bomb Bomb is sure to satisfy any craving you might have!

History

Established in 1951.

The question arises at least 15 times a day; "Why would you guys name your restaurant Bomb Bomb?"

The answer is really quite simply... when you place was bombed twice in the 1930's because of a business feud, the nickname spreads quickly in South Philly!

For our full history and orignal articles follow the link to our History Page

www.bombbomb-restaurant.com/History.html

Meet the Business Owner

Frankie B. Business Owner

As the son of Frank Sr, Frankie has worked at the tavern since 1974 and officially took over the family business in 1992 with his wife Debbie. A hardworking couple, they have transform this bar into a full restaurant with a diverse and exciting menu.

Come in during the day or on a Saturday night and you'll see the couple behind the bar making sure your experience is a memorable one!
