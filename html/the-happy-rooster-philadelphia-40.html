        We came here on a whim for lunch today because the Oyster House down the street was supposedly having special lobster rolls for $15 but they ran out after less than an hour so we missed it.  By seemingly happy coincidence, the Happy Rooster was just steps away and also advertised a lobster roll for about the same price as one normally runs at Oyster House ($23 at Happy Rooster vs $26).

        My dining companions had their hopes way up for a summery lobster roll so they decided to order some here even though it 's a pretty steep price for a lunch sandwich.  I settled on the veggie burger for lack of other options not containing cheese as an integral ingredient or too pricey to justify for a weekday lunch.  It of course arrived with the cheese I had asked them to omit, so they whisked it away and brought out a new one very quickly - my guess is they scraped off the caramelized onions and cheese and just added new onions.  No biggie, I'm not a vegan just a lactard.  It was edible, but not exactly yummy.  The outside of the patty had a nice crust but inside was very mushy but the flavor was tolerable.  The brioche bun is was served on was texturally appropriate but the whole combination felt like a mouthful of bready mush.  The fries were OK, definitely frozen.

        But oh, that lobster roll made me so sad.  I'm from Maine, born and raised, and let me tell you I've had a whole lot of lobster rolls in my day.  Up there you can get them at roadside shacks for $10 and they put this one to shame.  The lobster chunks, while plentiful enough to sort of justify the price tag, were much too large.  There was way too much mayo and not enough salt.  I like my lobster rolls made with a dash of celery salt which would have helped overcome the overabundance of mayo.  Save yourself the disappointment and $23, don't bother with this item.

        In fact, probably don't bother with this place at all.  The menu was limited, lacked imaginative or appealing options, and was surprisingly expensive for the dive-bar atmosphere.  If you really must try for yourself, please watch your knees when sliding into a booth- I have a knot the size of an egg on my knee where I bashed it on the table support that takes up entirely too much real estate under the table.

        We decided to forgo the insane crowds of Sips-happy folks at Zavino, Nodding Head, Oyster House, Ladder 15...and finally stopped into the Happy Rooster. Definitely an institution in my book - my grandfather spent many a happy hour here when he worked at Liberty Place and I have been a couple of times during some booze-filled evenings. As I understand it, it's changed hands a few times recently.

        The space is tiny but cozy. We had great service as well. I am surprised about the lack of drafts and the beer list wasn't exactly cheap (most were $7-8) or inspired. I stuck with a glass of house white (also not inexpensive at $8). A cocktail with Stoli was $9 (and tiny).

        Complimentary bread was pretty standard. I know that everything is NOT going to be JG Domestic's warm parker house rolls.

        The chilled red pepper and tomato soup was absolutely delicious. Seriously, I wanted to order another bowl to take home. Great flavor and a kick!

        Beer batter fried pickles. Oh my word. These are bread and butter pickles and frying them was seriously inspired. I love the slices, not the spears, and these are tasty.

        This is probably one of the rare times in which I've ordered a burger medium rare and it IS medium rare. It's a big burger - topped with bacon-onion jam and cheddar. I wish the cheddar was a thick slice. It looked like it was sprinkled, which was...odd. Bacon onion jam was pretty baller though.

        I tend to forget this place is here, which is sad. I need to come back more often!

        From what I understand, this place is an institution.  At one point in time, this was THE (emphasis on the word THE) place to go to.  And it was said it's because people on the street can't see in through the windows, so you had your privacy when eating/drinking/schmoozing.

        That being said, I will admit, for the first time in a public forum, that being a resident of the Philadelphia-area for almost 10 years, I have never had a Philly cheesesteak.  Never been to Pat's, never been to Geno's, never been anywhere else to try this Philadelphia staple food.  I've never had an interest.  Oh, I know all about them, but a cheesesteak doesn't sound too appetizing to me.  That was until I saw the CHICKEN cheesesteak on the menu here at the Happy Rooster.

        Okay, okay you can stop booing me now for turning my back on the steak cheesesteak, not everyone is going to like everything, and I know I've never even TRIED one before, but the meat looks gross.  That's just me and my opinion and I'm sticking to it.  This may change at some point in time, but for now, no thanks.

        Getting back to my chicken cheesesteak lunch.  The chicken was soft, not overcooked, the cheese was melty, gooey good. But what did it for me were the onions.  This place knows how to caramelize an onion.  They weren't burnt, they weren't too soft, they were perfect.  And there weren't too many of them on the sandwich to overwhelm the other flavors.

        The presentation was nice, the fries were crunchy and it made for a good lunch.

        It was kitschy and whimsical and a place that I would stop by again and try something else off of their menu.  Maybe if they added a beef cheesesteak...

        It's a tiny old place, and right after work on a Tuesday the bar was mostly full, but we had no trouble getting one of the handful of tables.  It happened to be Mardi Gras so they had some weird cocktail specials - I tried one and it was awesome.  They gave us some bread & butter to curb our hunger until the real food arrived - which took a bit longer than I thought it should have, but at least we had booze and bread.

        You can guess what we started with.  Unfortunately, in the couple of months that had passed, a new chef had taken over and changed the recipe slightly, so while the calamari was still very good, it wasn't exactly what my friend was expecting.  I thought the calamari inside had the perfect consistency - not chewy, not squishy.  It was a little heavy on the breading, but I can deal with that.  The sauce, which I can only liken to the sweet chili sauce from Pei Wei, was an unusual but really great choice.  I guess they used to toss the calamari in this sauce - which supposedly makes it to die for.  The waitress actually asked us how we felt about the calamari, and when my friend started to explain how it used to be better, the waitress boisterously agreed with her and said she was going to make sure the chef knew that customers missed the old recipe.

        We also split the burger.  It was very good, perfectly cooked (medium = pink), and very tasty, with awesome fries.  The best part was that they brought it all out on two plates - already split for us!  So nice of them to go that extra little step, and it made for a far prettier presentation than what would have happened had we tried to cut it with our butter knives.

      Specialties

      Conveniently located within walking distance of Rittenhouse Square, The Happy Rooster is a Philadelphia icon that has been serving delicious food for over four decades. With the exception of a whimsical rooster-themed stained glass window, the exterior of this corner bar is unassuming. Once inside however, quiet music and low lights create a relaxing atmosphere to enjoy drinks or a fabulous meal.

      We offer a great menu of lunch and dinner favorites, including Grilled Chicken Risotto and Lobster Mac 'n Cheese. Our most popular dish, the Lobster Roll, is hailed as the best lobster roll in Philadelphia. Spend an evening eating and drinking in our outdoor dining area, or enjoy our live entertainment such as jazz shows and karaoke nights. We also offer private dining, so consider us for your next office party, birthday or special event. Come see why The Happy Rooster has been a favorite amongst locals and visitors alike since 196

      History

      Established in 1968.

      For over 45 years, The Happy Rooster has been a staple in the Philadelphia area for delicious food and a warm and inviting atmosphere. We take pride in providing a sanctuary in the middle of the busy city where you can take a moment to relax and enjoy a great meal. Our friendly staff are always excited to help you out, and appreciate the loyal following we have gained over the years.

      Meet the Business Owner

      Debbie Reid J. Business Owner

      Debbie and the rest of the team at The Happy Rooster are proud of the great reputation the restaurant has earned over the years, and look forward to welcoming back regulars and meeting new faces for years to come. We proudly serve fresh, tasty food made with great ingredients and served in a friendly, inviting atmosphere. Whether you are a Philadelphia local or you are visiting for the first time, we look forward to serving you.
