    Yes it's good. Yes everyone knows that. I'm just here for the fancy vs hot fresh donuts vote.

    I'd been here a few times before I even tried a hot & fresh. It's just hard for me to look at something enticing and not buy it, especially if it's food. So for the first few times I came by, I would only get the fancy ones. They were right there and they looked so good!

    My favorite is probably a toss up between the sea salt chocolate and crumberry. Because the dough is so cake-like, I really need a non-sweet note to bump it up. I love the sea salt chocolate because I love chocolate and nearly anything sweet & savory. The crumberry was unexpectedly tart, which I found delightful.

    Now then, given that the other fancy ones didn't really impress (cookies and cream is a menace on foods and drinks, much like an invasive parasite), and they are a denser dough and cold lot, the hot & fresh is actually what I'll go for in the future. Unless they change the dough in the fancy ones (is it because it has toppings that they need a cake-y dough to hold it up??), and start serving it made to order (yes I WOULD stand outside for 20 minutes while one is being prepped!).

    In any case, hot & fresh has the leg up despite being burn your mouth hot when you receive it. The dough is fluffy and light as a proper donut should be, and the flavored, scented sugars crystals are blissful. I'm factoring in nostalgia here as well, as I grew up with sugar sprinkled donuts and not glazed ones.

    I've tried the buttermilk ranch dry chicken and the sweet chili glazed chicken. Surprisingly I liked the sweet chili more. Usually I am wary of things fried to a crispy exterior and then doused in sauce. To me, sogginess is one of the most unpleasant textures for any food. But the sweet chili chicken was still crispy and the sauce had a fantastic balance of heat and sweet. The buttermilk ranch, on the other hand, was really salty. I don't know if they can cut the ranch powder with something or devise a way to distribute it more evenly and sparingly, but the bites where the powder puddled were unpleasant.

    Workers are generally nice, and there's a really cute girl with glasses and short hair too.

    I'm from San Francisco. I was in Philly visiting my best friend. We both love donuts. He tells me Federal is the place to go. Neither one of us as been.

    Two words. Overpromised. Underdelivered.

    I. We arrived at 12:15 PM. Of the 10 donuts on their menu, 7 were sold out. Which is fine if we came in an hour before closing. But this location, according to the sign in the window, was open until 7 PM.

    Let me get this straight. Federal Donuts was planning on being open for another 7 hours with 70% of their menu unavailable? Either that's some business model I'm not familiar with or these folks need a lesson in planning and customer service. At the very least, put a damn "Sold Out" sticker up on the sign. Or remove the menu featuring the sold out donut flavors. Advertising what you don't have is at best, dumb and at worst, kinda rude. Would you go back to a donut shop where the first thing you saw was a menu of delicious donut flavors and the second was a chirpy employee telling you they are all gone? I'd start to think these folks didn't care much about my experience as a customer.

    II. I ordered one vanilla spice and one strawberry. And was then told to have a seat and wait. Already a dozen people were waiting for their orders. Fine. It it's coming fresh out of the fryer, worth the wait.

    It's not worth the wait. Of the three available selections on the "Hot Fresh" menu, I ordered two of them. And what I got was a warm ring of dough with artificially flavored sugar sprinkled on it.

    I blame myself. For my money "Vanilla Spice" means at the very least vanilla frosting, or vanilla flavored dough. it does not mean a plain donut, however hot and fresh you stuck some vanilla flavored chemical sweetener on.  

    And ditto for the strawberry. If I say "Would you like a strawberry flavored fresh donut?" do you think "hooray, a donut with strawberry frosting or a filled with strawberry jam" or do you think "hooray, a plain hot donut dragged through pink barely flavored saccharine?"

    A fresh donut is an empty promise when this is what you've been waiting for. And it was your only choice of orders in the first place.

    An A + effort for a C + result. Not impressed.

    I try to drop by any Federal Donuts location whenever I remember that I'm around one.  Yeah, dropping in for a donut (or two or twelve) isn't exactly a adrenaline pumping experience but it's one of those simple little pleasures that makes the day a bit better.

    Generally, their fresh, hot donuts are my go-to. They're perfect for someone who just wants a good pastry. The sugar coating isn't at all overwhelming though the specific flavors like vanilla spice or strawberry lavender never tasted all that distinct to me. Just seemed like sugar with different colors.

    Prices are reasonable, especially for the Rittenhouse Square area. $1.25 for plain donuts; $2.25 for fancy.

    The staff is friendly and the establishment is clean. These are things I think we take for granted but they make or break a lot of places for me!

    My relatively small gripes:

    -The seating is a bit cramped given the small size of the store. It's not ideal for more than two or three people, but not a huge factor for me.

    -The availability of fancy donuts can be a bit spotty. I prefer the plain donuts so I don't have a big problem with the random availability of fancy, but it's something to keep in mind!

    Make your workmates (or any mates) happy by bringing them a box of "Hot and Fresh" Federal Donuts.  Federal Donuts are available in two main varieties:  Fancy, which are glazed in a variety of unusual and delicious flavors (e.g., Chocolate salted caramel) or Hot and Fresh.   The  appeal of the Hot and Fresh variety is that they are made to order before your very eyes.   They are then rolled in your choice of sugar mixtures.  My fav, which had cardamom, has rolled off the menu, so I content myself with the cinnamon variety.  So delicious are these donuts that even folks on low carb diets abandon their restrictions to indulge.  Voluntarily and enthusiastically.   With an accompanying cup of coffee from La Colombe (Federal's coffee is good but a little harsh at the back of the palate) you will experience a bit of heaven.  

    I did not grow up with donuts and have generally found them to be heartburn carriers.   Not these!  Federal's Hot and Fresh (and Cronuts from a little bakery in Feasterville) are the only ones that agree with me.   Thanks to the NYT for shining a light on this gem and to Michael Solomonov for giving Center City the gift of one of his donut shops.

From the business

Specialties

Cake donuts in interesting flavors and Korean-style, twice-fried chicken sold by the half and whole bird.  House-roasted coffee available hot or cold-brewed and iced. That's basically it.

History

Established in 2012.

Chef Michael Solomonov and partner Steve Cook of Zahav and Percy Street Barbecue got a wild idea to pair exotic donuts and super-crispy fried chicken. With Bodhi Coffee owners' Bob Logue and Tom Henneman's coffee expertise and professional bigmouth Felicia D'Ambrosio, the team opened the first FedNuts at 1219 S. Second St. in Pennsport, South Philly in 2011.  The Sansom Street shop followed one year later in October 2012.  We have since been cranking out fancy donuts, melt in your mouth hot fresh donuts, and truly badass chicken.  Follow us on Twitter www.Twitter.com/federaldonuts for status updates and fun contests.
