    I hate long lines. I hate cafeteria seating. I hate soggy sandwhich rolls. But you can't deny it: a DiNic's sandwich might be the greatest food in Philadelphia, period. Well worth the wait and price for just a taste of these kings of sandwiches. Some tips: 1) the line never dies, so if you see a seat open at the counter, pass on it; it can get uncomfortable with 500 people breathing down your neck. 2) if you want brisket, go early; that sells out first. 3) provolone is your best option for cheese, no matter what sandwhich you get. 4) be prepared to stand and wait for a seat in the communal area; might be better to grab your sandwich and take it to the marriot lobby across the street (though you lose the atmosphere and energy of the Market). DiNic's is rightfully called the best. Don't pass it up!

    And another thing that you already know about the looong and winding liiiine thaa-aaa-aaat leads to your pork: During peak lunchtimes and especially over the weekend, that queue is ridic.

    So, unless I'm at the RTM with a visiting friend or fam who inevitably have DiNic's famed roast pork on their list of sandwiches to try in Philly, the sandwich capital of the country, I tend to seek out these sandwiches on the off-peak hours...but then you're taking chances too...cause so many times, after the lunch rush, they'll be outta meatballs. See what I'm sayin?

    These are the chances a non-line-waiter makes. Then, if I'm striking out, I can act like the most so spoiled individual, grumble grumbling, "What,  now I gotta eat a tasty roast pork sandwich instead?" So spoiled.

    Anyway, my most ideal situation is this: DiNic's is fully stocked with meatballs, I'm there at the Reading Terminal Market with a pal who's down to split, and we go halfsie-hoosies. Half a peppery roast pork, sharp provolone, and broccoli rabe and half a delicious meatball parm. Try the meatballs! That's the ticket.
