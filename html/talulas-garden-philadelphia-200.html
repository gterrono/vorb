    The garden atmosphere and interior surroundings are very nice and a step up from the former Washington Square, the service seemed very good and our server knew the menu and the wine list well.

    We came here because we wanted to sit outdoors, when we made the reservation earlier that day, we were told that outdoor seating was on a first come, first serve basis - however when we couldn't sit outside and saw that many tables were open and "reserved" we were very disappointed.    Once indoors, the neighboring table was covered with small talk by the server and served complimentary cheese, brioche and other tasty looking stuff.   It became very clear that if you're a regular here, you'll have a different and better experience than the less frequent guest.   Hey no biggie, frequent flyers deserve and extra perk or two, let's talk about the food:

    I ordered the menu's hanger steak, and Ms. Fly had the Tilefish (fresh but a little bland).    The hanger steak was sliced and served in a delicious jus; it was slightly underdone (requested medium) and knowing what happens to hanger steak when you overcook it, the chef probably wanted to play it safe - still it was underdone.  The heirloom tomatoes were covered with a vinegary drizzle that killed the tomatoes flavor and the few roasted potatoes were mushy.   The portion size was just right for a 5-year old, so we ordered desert.

    The cookie plate was four cookies, cold & nothing spectacular other than the caramel which tasted fresh and home made.

    The wine list was OK, we found several nice choices and thought the prices were OK also.

    Overall, I wasn't wowed.   I guess I set the culinary expectations too high, and  to top it off, Talula's prices are higher than I expected also.

    A second meal at Talula's Garden was enjoyable, though I still believe four stars is appropriate. While some items soar, others leave me wanting. We saw a two top get turned away for not having reservations on a notoriously slow night. So people are clearly loving the place.

    As was the case last time, the restaurant's decor was warm and comfortable, and service was perfect. We started with a fall cheese plate, which was really nicely done. The mushroom soup looked beautiful, but the creamy broth lacked the rich flavor I expected.

    My skirt steak entree was very good, with vinegary cipollini onions, broccoli and crispy potatoes. One thing I will say about Talula's is that the portions are generous. I couldn't finish my entree.

    For dessert, we shared the fig pudding, which is topped with butterscotch sauce and served with goat cheese ice cream. It's not the prettiest dessert, but on first taste I was absolutely hooked. One of the better desserts I've had in a while.

But… Walking through the garden on a hot and humid Friday night, I was relieved to be sitting inside. But only because of the weather. The garden itself is a wonderland of flora, lights and the sounds of trickling water. Truly magical.

    Inside, the decor is a little more country farmhouse chic, and while the decorations can be a bit overdone in some areas, the overall feeling is very comfortable. We started with the seasonal sangria, served with fresh blueberries, green apple and basil. It was a refreshing choice for the day and I stuck with it on a second round.

    First up was "Not Your Granny's" cheese plate, consisting of six cheeses ranging from soft and slightly funky to a bold blue. Each came served along an appropriate accompaniment, and a ramekin with the most delicious flatbreads.

    Next we shared the peekytoe crab and lobster salad. I wasn't sure what to make of this, because every bite was different and I alternated between liking it a lot and not being so sure. I do have to admit, the substance covering the salad was a bit off-putting and I later noticed a neighboring diner picking around it as well.

    Fortunately, my main course of seared scallops with pine nuts and watercress picked things up again. The scallops were cooked perfectly. I was also so happy we ordered the delicious chard and braised leek gratinee at the last minute - it went perfectly with our dishes, and provided just enough food for a filling (but not too filling) dinner. Art had the wild striped bass with red quinoa, which he absolutely loved.

    For dessert, French press coffee and the gooseberry almond crumble, served with black pepper meringue and heavenly lemon ice cream that I would personally like to order by the vat.     Read more

    This is becoming one of my favorite places for date night in Philly.  It has a beautiful  outdoor patio garden with white lights, and flowers everywhere, and the warm and inviting atmosphere extends into the inside dining area as well. The entire place has a very causal, but romantic feel.

    My date and I arrived early, so we grabbed a drink at the bar.  The bar area is a little small for the size of the restaurant, so we had to stand to enjoy our drinks.  They have a cute specialty cocktail list, with cocktails names such as "The Lover", and "The Haymaker".  The drinks were well crafted and fit into the theme of the restaurant.

    For appetizers my date got the fois gras, and I got the pear salad.  As he is not normally a fan of pate style fois gras, he was a little hesitant to order it, but he was pleasantly surprised when the accompanying flavors, and it pushed the dish over his usual threshold.  I enjoyed my orchard pear salad - it was a sizable salad - however some of the pears weren't completely ripe.

    For the main dishes, my date got the duck breast, and I got the braised beef, and a side of brussel sprouts. The duck breast was perfectly cooked, and the crispy confit made for a nice paring with the breast.  My braised beef melted in my mouth, and I wish I could have taken a huge bowl of the jus home to enjoy as a bowl of soup.  I wasn't too impressed with the brussel sprouts - the alpine cheese was a bit too much, and made the dish a bit heavier than I wanted it.

    For dessert we got a cheese plate.  The cheesemonger/waitress was super knowledgeable and gave us a nice overview of each of the cheeses, along with a brief history of some of them.  The cheeses were perfectly paired with the suggested ice wine.

    I can't wait to come back here on a nice spring day, and enjoy some wine and cheese in the outdoor garden.  This place is a delight!

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
