    Listed in Happy Places in Philly

    Make a reservation, and make it NOW!  This is the restaurant for students at the Art Institute Culinary Program.  Sadly, few people know about it.  For just $30, you'll get a six-course meal that is simply fabulous.

    The room is small (and, when we went, only half full).  You'll get a large window into the kitchen to see the students in action.  They pull out all the stops when it comes to food and service.  Highlights were the trois potages (three tastes of soup, served cleverly in an espresso cup, a cordial glass, and a shot glass), each served with a delicious accompaniment.  The chicken soup had a delicious parmesan crisp, the curried lentil soup a tiny samosa, and the New England clam chowder a bitty clam fritter.  The lentil soup was a favorite.  The 'creative contorni'  wins points for best use of asparagus; the asparagus fettucine was wonderful.  I also loved the rich ice creams for dessert, peanut butter swirl and chocolate silk.  In between were many other great dishes from Dr. Pepper baby back ribs to sweetbreads.

    The students make as many dishes as possible for the experience.  We didn't realize that we'd be served every item on the menu.  No decisions necessary!  Fear not.  The servings are small enough to let you enjoy each course, but there's enough to leave you stuffed by the end of the evening.

    If you aren't an adventuresome eater, this is not for you.  You don't get to order.  But trust the chefs and try everything.  You'll be glad you did.  And check out the online menu in advance.  This place is BYOB, so you'll need to choose your own wine pairings.  

    Now stop reading and make your reservations!

    I'm still giving Petit Passion 5 stars.  Is it Le Bec Fin?  No, but I think based on merit, and the fact that it's a learning laboratory, it's a damned good deal and really nifty place to eat.  

    I went for lunch today with fellow Yelper Karen F.  I had checked out the menu on line, and everything look appealing.  Also made a reservation.  What a lovely meal!  I had:

    Fried Chesapeake Bay Oysters, Spicy Remoulade, Pommes Frittes, Vegetable Slaw:  3 small, crisply fried oysters.  Yummy.  Pomme frittte were thin thin thin, and a bit on the oily side, however the vinegar from the vegatable slaw balanced it perfectly.

    Lentil Soup, with buttered crouton, Herb OIl & Lardoons of Bacon:  just the thing for a chilly, blustery day!  Plenty of diced carrots & onions in the soup, with exceptionally crisp shards of bacon.  A big bowl would have made a very nice meal in itself.

    Biscuit Sliders (Barbeque Pork, Fried Chicken, Beef Burger):  all on home-made biscuits.  The pork was disappointing, a lttle dry, although flavorful.  The chicken was a very moist, perfectly cooked chunk of chicken breast.  The beef burger was outstanding, moist & flavorful.  The real hit was the hoppin' john accompaniment.  This was not mentioned on the menu, but certainly got my attention.  The student who prepared it was allowed to do a riff on the standard, and added pecans and a bit of cayenne.  If she ever gets in a place of her own, I'd put this one on the menu!

    Dessert was a pecan pie, with little fluffs of chocolate/peanut butter mousse on the side.  OK, but not wowser.  The crust on the pie, however, was top notch.

    Service was excellent.  This session will end October 30th, so is you like the current menu, ya better hustle.  The next session, with a new menu, will begin the week after.

    Just remember that reservations are preferred.  The $15 prix fixe includes tax, but not a gratuity, and is cash only.

    PLEASE give Petit Passion a try!  You will be supporting The Art Institute of Philadelphia's culinary program, giving students valuable practice, AND treating yourself to a wonderful experience!!

    For those of you who are not quite so adventurous (see Cherie C.'s review on dinner at Petite… For those of you who are not quite so adventurous (see Cherie C.'s review on dinner at Petite Passion), but want to support a great learning experience, try Petite Passion for lunch!

    There are 2 seatings, 11:30 and 12:00, with reservations recommended.  $15 Prix Fixe for 4 courses, however (unlike dinner) you have choices within those courses -- usually 3-4 choices for each course (app, soup, entree & dessert).  None of the courses are overwhelming in size, however you will be pleasantly full when you leave; it is not the place to go if you want a light lunch.  The set menu runs for number of weeks, and is posted on the website when the restaurant is in session.  You can go on line to check it out before you go.  I've discovered that some sessions have more appealing menus (to me)  than others, but there are almost always choices that I'd like.  The next session starts Thursday, October 9th, 2008.   www.petite-passion.tk

    Try it.  You may need to be a little patient with your server -- remember, they are learning how to do this -- and you may even hear some stern "teaching" going on in the kitchen -- but that's part of the adventure.  Because it is a a school, and the various jobs within the restaurant are part of the students' learning experience, a tip is not "required";  you can leave one if you think it is appropriate.  It's also cash only.  Believe me, I think this place is an absolute treasure!  The students come from all walks of life, the food is amazing, and it is a wonderful experience.     OK - Now they get the fifth star.  If you read Pat D.'s review - you'll hear all about the yummy treats she orded when we had lunch here today.

    I had the greek salad - and they were kind enough to "hold" the feta cheese for me.  The greens were crisp and 3 types of olives were used (2 green and 1 black variety) along with batons of red pepper and home-made dolmades.  The scrumptios  - and simple - lemon and olive oil vinaigrette made it SING.  Presented on a white rippled plate - very clever.  The plate allowed the dressing to pool away from the crisp greens and thus I could dress the salad as I wished.  YUM-MY!

    The I had the Asian Dumpling soup.  The single dumpling had a shrimp-based filling and was scrumptious, as were the veggies in the soup.  The broth was a bit salty for my liking - but I am a no-salt added kinda gal.

    Next was the star of the show - and what I was WAITING for - Chicken & Waffles.  It was accompanied by tender-crisp southern greens (with a healthy shot of garlic) and thin-ly julienne deep-fried sweet potato strings.  Damn, what a great waffle!  The pulled chick on top was tender and delicious - but my one complaint was the copious amount of bland sauce.  It nade it a bit too "a la king" -ish for me.

    Dessert for me was a simple - yet perfect fruit salad of watermelon (sweet and seedless) bites, blackberries,and a single slice of orange.

    Service was good - I felt so bad for the guy in crutches who had broken his femur.  What a trooper.  

    I will be back - hopefully SOON. (And maybe even with Pat D!)

    This restaurant has a lot going for it, although extensive hours are not among them. Come during the seating times (1130, 1145, 1200, 530, 600) and be the test audience for students at the Art Institute's culinary school.

    It succeeds at it's goal - to be a 4-star dining experience with admittedly student staff. It's clear that they've been trained and are striving for that level of service and quality, though as students there are still a few rough edges you wouldn't see at a really fancy place. Give them another few months and they'll be great. And that's exactly why instead of paying $40 for lunch, you only pay a $15 prix fixe for a 4-course meal, including drinks!

    While most dishes were good, the strong standouts for us were the spicy scallop amuse bouche and the excellent soups - potato-leek, shrimp wonton, and lentil. The chocolate truffle in the final course far outpaced all the other desserts.
