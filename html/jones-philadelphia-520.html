    it seems like jones is always hoppin', no matter the hour. i was there for an off hour late lunch, and it was still full!

    my husband had a cold at the time and wanted whiskey, which i'm typically not too keen on. they managed to find a whiskey we both loved, with a mellow touch of maple.

    we were most impressed by the award-winning nachos. they were perfect. the kitchen takes the extra time to make sure every freshly-made chip has a little bit of everything and no chip ends up naked and alone. that would be so sad.

    i was very pleasantly surprised by both the tofu wings and the fish sticks. the fish sticks are a standout, made with thick pieces of haddock and just enough batter.

    the tofu wings don't try to pretend to be chicken. the texture is definitely tofu and it's important to eat them right away when they're hot. they're very tasty and tangy with a good spice level, but if they get cold, well, you have cold tofu.

    the sesame seared tuna was very good, and came with a wasabi smiley face. cute touch! we split a classic black and white milkshake for dessert which was great - i definitely want to return to try some of their more creative adult milkshakes as well.

    even with the packed house, the service is excellent. everyone is friendly, just the right amount of attentive, and gladly willing to explain all of the elements of each dish.

    the manager, stephen, stopped by our table, and everyone else's, to chat and check on everything which was remarkable considering how full his hands must have been!  he even helped a nearby table of out-of-towners with a list of things to see and do while in town. it's clear the staff here goes the extra mile, and then some, for their customers.

    I've been going to Jones for years.  I always think of it as a winter/ cold weather restaurant  Probably because it's cozy inside and I always happen to sit near the fire place.  

    I would rate the mac and cheese as one of the best in the city, if not the best.  It's listed as an appetizer, but really, if you're not sharing it, you should get it as your entree, because after eating that, you are probably not going to be able to eat anything else.  There is so much cheese in it.  I've heard from former employees that they use a blend of at least 4 different types of cheese.  The noodles are elbows and perfectly cooked and there is a crunchy layer of bread crumbs on top.  There also seems to be spices added to the cheese sauce, since each bite is super flavorful.  If you can't tell, I have thought about this mac and cheese in detail.

    Another menu highlight is the pierogis.  Another cold weather hearty favorite.  Also listed as an appetizer, and is appetizer-sized.  These are perfect.  They are pan sautéed (not deep -fried, which I don't like) and they are served with a side of sautéed onions and sour cream.  They might also be my favorite pirogues in the city.

    One star taken off for the size of the drinks for the price.  If I am paying $9-10 for a glass of wine, I don't want it served in one of those small glasses.  Don't be stingy Steven.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
