        If you live in any major American city, there's likely a pizza place around the corner from you. So the frequency with which you go to any particular place may just reflect how convenient it is to go there. Here's my formula for rating a pizza place in any major city:  (number of places that are closer to you than the pizza place in question) X (number of times you go the pizza place in question).

        There are roughly 50 pizza places that are closer to me than Nomad, and I've been there 6 or 7 times in the last few months. The highly overrated Lozarros and Randazzo's are right around the corner me. Despite being fairly lazy, I will pass on ALL 50 of these places, going out of my way just to get a Nomad pizza. This is about as good as thin-crust pizza gets. The crust is chewy but not too tough, with a little blackened crispiness to it.

        And the ambiance here is great, too. You can sit downstairs for a nice-night-out vibe (I've never done this, but it looks nice) or you can head upstairs, sit at the bar, or sit at a table and take in a movie on the big screen. The times I've been there, they've shown old Chaplin or Buster Keaton movies while playing some good tunes. Good beer selection, too.

        This is one of my favorite places to eat in Philly. No matter how far you need to go, get there and give it a try!

        Came here on an exceptional date last night. As I didn't select the restaurant, I had no idea of the magnitude that my socks were about to get blown clear off.

        We ordered an appetizer special (fresh mozzarella on a bed of arugula salad, accompanied with prosciutto and figs,) the Margherita di Bufala, and the Truffle Pecorino. The entire meal was like getting slapped in the face with decadent flavors.

        The Margherita di Bufala was easily the best margherita that I've had in 5ever. Both of our pizzas had an exceptional crust: crispy while still slightly chewy. It was the optimal handle for a serving of fresh mozzarella, tangy sauce, and fresh basil.

        The real superstar, however, was the Truffle Pecorino pizza. If I was convicted of murder and sentenced to death, a slice of this concoction would be a serious contender for my last meal. Our pizza was brought out with a sunny side up egg perched proudly in the middle. Our server, Pete, proceeded to break the yolk and swirl around the golden goodness with such finesse that my eyes actually began to water as much as my mouth. Each bite of the pizza was a symphony on my tongue, the sharp saltiness balanced by the creamy cheese and egg. For a minute, I forgot about my date, completely. In that moment is was just me and ol' Truffle Pecorino.

        In conclusion, go here. Eat the Truffle Pecorino. Save me a piece as a thanks for the recommendation. Seriously.

        I love this place. The food is great, and I think reasonably priced considering the menu curation and atmosphere they provide. In terms of the typical eating experience I don't think I can add much to most of the reviews here, but I've come here with a large group several times, and I'll focus on that here.

        This is a great place to bring a large group. If you plan ahead you can have a long table reserved and set upstairs, which gives you privacy, whether you want raucous large group conversations, or space for many smaller conversations. The awesome variety of pizza and the way it is served allows people to order what they like, but also to share a few pieces with others, while trying out a few other flavors from those around them. A lot of the appetizers are also very good for sharing with the group. What makes the great food, atmosphere, and service even better is that you get it all for a great price. They are also very good at

        This is probably once of the best places for groups I've ever gone to, in terms of the value you get for the price. To give a couple examples, we've hosted groups of admitted doctoral students and visiting scholars for a small conference (a very international crowd), and this allowed us to provide a great taste of the interesting food Philly has to offer, in an interesting and intimate environment allowing for large and small conversations around a big table, within a very reasonable budget. I can't think of any place else in the city where you could spend less and get anywhere near the experience, or get more without spending a lot more.

        The location usually means a lot of people will be walking, but at least coming from the Rittenhouse/University City area it's a very pleasant group walk - I mention that primarily because it highlights the type of group I think this is good for. If you have money to burn it might not be what you're looking for, but I've brought groups here even when I had the budget to spend at least 50% more (of other people's money).
