    Valanni is my kind of place.

    50% bar, 50% restaurant, but 1000% fabulosity, Valanni is a biz that I would find myself at *very* often if I lived in Philly.  The quality of the drinks and food along with the chic and swanky decor draw me in and and refuse to let me go.

    My most recent jaunt to Valanni was for a quick and boozy dinner.  Even at 5:30pm on a Sunday, the place was poppin'.  After weaving through the crowd, two of us got cozy at the bar.  (And you *will* be cozy -- the mirrored walls give the illusion of Valanni being spacious, but it's really not.)

    First thing I noticed was that the cocktail list seems to have expanded from the usual froofy martinis and skinny diet drinks that Valanni would serve in years past.  Now there are tempting craft cocktails of all kinds.  I was in a ginger mood, so I opted for the Moscow Mule -- and the bartender didn't flinch when I decided to be high-maintenance and ask for it to be modified to a gin mule.  It tasted fabulous...and so did the second one.

    The food is excellent too.  In the past, I nibbled on Valanni's various small plates and apps, but this time I decided to have a proper dinner and ordered their veggie paella.  And it was absolutely fantastic!  Not only were the warm veggies and rice the perfect comfort food on a cold and rainy night, but the portion was immense.  There was easily enough food for 2 (maybe 3) on that plate.  All for a reasonable price too.

    So, the drinks and food are amazing.  But what really makes Valanni click is its personality.  Something about the stylish interior makes all of Valanni's bar-flies a lot more outgoing than normal.  New to town and looking to meet people?  Feeling shy? Need someone to coax you out of your shell?  Then Valanni is the place to go!

    Well done, Valanni... I'll be back real soon.  :-)

    So when I think lounge, I think of a beautiful, modern bar, with a fantastic wine list and an array of sexy cocktails, served out with tempting munchies by some super cool bartenders. Basically, Valanni is my idea of the ideal lounge.

    The venue itself is beautiful, with sleek white booths,  wavy white walls, a glass rack over the central bar that looks like a chandelier, and purple backlighting. It's modern but still cozy, classy but not pretentious.

    I've come here several times, from Industry parties on a Monday, to Happy Hour with the girls, to dinner with the boy. I would actually go to this place even more if I didn't have to worry about the HORRIBLE parking situation in the Gayborhood (but I'm in the minority of drivers, as many of the people that come here either cab it or live in the surrounding blocks, so my complaint is irrelevant).

    My last trip, yesterday, was to grab a cocktail with my girl around 8pm. I arrived to find a steady bar, not packed but certainly busy enough with scattered couples and groups throughout. I grabbed an end seat next to my friend and proceeded to order a Boys Gone Wild. Now I'm not a sweets fanatic, especially with cocktails. But I figured I'd start out with a fancy martini for some girl talk then make it over to my standard Ketel and club. I ended up sticking with the Boys Gone Wild all night. It was a perfectly balanced blueberry Stoli cocktail with strawberries, white cran, sparkling wine, a sugar balsamic rim. It wasn't particularly strong as after 4 of them and two shots of Jameson, we were solidly buzzed but not really drunk (all good things).

    We ordered some mussels in a red chili sauce, buffalo chicken croquettes, grilled caesar, and grilled octopus. The mussels were good and not as spicy as we thought they'd be. We ordered more bread to soak up some of the yummy sauce. The buffalo croquettes (also not-so-spicy) had good, creamy flavor. The grilled octopus was a little dry but the flavor was solid, not my favorite dish of the night but still good. The grilled romaine caesar was fantastic, with a chipotle dressing and large charred bacon bits. I'll be back for that! We also made friends with some randoms and tried some of their cheese plate that was very good.

    All in all, it turned into a fun night of debauchery with us making friends with not only the other patrons but the bartenders (who are HELLA CUTE, and super fun), by sharing Nutella with everyone (long story, someone at the bar never had it so we decided to pop their Nutella cherry by bringing some over from the deli across the street....not really kosher for a nice lounge but it turned into a fun experience for all involved, resulting in rounds of shots and new friends).

    Anyway, from flavorful food, to fantastic staff, sexy cocktails to suave atmosphere, Valanni is an excellent choice for dinner or drinks any night of the week!! Forever and always a favorite of mine =)

    I kinda love this place.  The atmosphere, the menu, and the clientele are all an interesting, eclectic mix... many styles coming together in a way that just works.  Valanni is authentically chic and decidedly hip - not in a phony way like a lot of other places.

    I can't get too far into this review without mentioning the lobster Mac and cheese.  I love eating out and I love food, and in all my travels this is still one of my favorite restaurant meals of all time.  The lobster actually takes too much of the credit in this dish, because it's really the delicious crab meat and combination of creamy cheeses that steals the show.  You haven't lived until you've tried this.

    For me personally this is a five star restaurant, but only because I've been coming here long enough to know exactly what to order.  Truth be told, there are some real duds on this menu, and it was only through trial and error that I figured out how to maximize my Valanni experience.  

    Despite the fact that 80% of the menu consists of tapas and appetizers, the real highlights in my opinion are the entrees.  The pepita-crusted tuna, the scallops, and the kobe beef burger are all top notch.  Resist the urge to go nuts with flat breads, cheeses and tapas and stick with some select choices from the 'Taste' menu, paired with one of the excellent full sized plates.  

    The bar, restaurant and lounge are all great options, depending on mood and time of night.  Cocktails are usually expertly prepared, and paired with an appetizer or snack makes this a perfect pre or post dinner destination as well.  

    A shorter, more focused menu would really launch Valanni into the upper tier of Philly restaurants, but even as it stands Valanni is more than deserving of regular visits.

History

Established in 2000.

Valanni restaurant opened its doors in September of 2000, introducing "Medi-Latin" cuisine to Philadelphia. Situated right off of the "Avenue of the Arts," Valanni has become a pre-theater ritual and place to meet for a night-cap for many who live in and around the neighborhood. The bar at Valanni keeps up with the creative cuisine by offering an assortment of sangria, mojitos and other specialty drinks. The kitchen is open until 1 AM daily, serving Tapas and a Mediterranean inspired lounge menu.
