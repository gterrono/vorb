    I forget how much I used to like Audrey Claire until I went back recently for the first time in a while.  Sometimes BYOB can be great, and sometimes I find it annoying...but when you can agree on a bottle of something to share...it's fantastic!

    Their menu is always consistently good and ever-changing, but not to the point where it limits the offerings.  There is always a nice selection of specials in addition to a nice-sized menu for a BYOB.  I've had all types of dishes here, and it's always consistently great food.  On my recent visit, I had shared the portobello mushrooms as an appetizer (though an old favorite used to be the trio of spreads, hummus/baba/tzaziki), and then had a delicious sea bass (I think?) with a shallot crust (think there were some capers up in there too).  It was outstanding - never had a "breaded" fish like that before with no breading (yay for gluten free and lots and lots of flavor).  Entrees usually come with some nice sides, and the haricot verts (yeahhhh fancy string beans) and mashed potatoes were great. Tried the homemade rice pudding for dessert and it did not disappoint though I was super-full at that point in the evening and probably didn't need a dessert.

    Be warned - Audrey Claire is NOISY.  I don't usually mind if I'm kicking back with great food and a big ole bottle of wine.  My buzz usually takes care of the noise. :)  I like going on the later side so you don't have to wait as long and hopefully the crowd has thinned out by then.  Also be warned that in the summer on a hot night, that place gets heated by the kitchen and there is no chance you're leaving sweat-free.  

    Then again, I'll sweat it out for a great meal, which they always deliver.

    I've been to Audrey Claire twice now. Once, on a packed Saturday night with my girlfriends on my… I've been to Audrey Claire twice now. Once, on a packed Saturday night with my girlfriends on my 23rd birthday, and again last night on a quiet Wednesday with my parents. The food was above average both times.

    During my first adventure, I shared the gorgonzola and fig flatbread with three others. It was truly to die for-- the pungent cheese and the sweet fig complemented each other so nicely. However at $16, it was a little pricey for an appetizer. The flatbread was followed by the sole with crispy shallots. I had actually never had sole before, and it was, well, a gentle fish. The crispy shallots were lovely, and the side dish of fresh crisp green beans was a nice change from some restaurants' over-seasoned over-cooked veggies. The fish was $20.

    Last night I shared the arugula salad with my parents to start. The salad was PERFECT. Piled high on the plate, it split nicely three ways. It was arugula tossed with french lentils, strawberries, blueberries, figs, red onion (which I carefully moved to the side-- that stuff stays on your breath FOREVER) and nicely sized dollop of warm goat cheese. I could have easily eaten the whole thing for an entree.

    Following the salad I had my "new thing," the seared ahi tuna (can you tell I'm just starting to really get into fish?). It was a little less flavorful than the sole. It was "crusted" with potato and had a slight mustard glaze drizzle. I could have used more of the drizzle, but overall it was tasty. Plus the portion of tuna was huge for $19. The only thing I wasn't crazy about was the Israeli cous cous that comes with nearly every dish. It's lacking something.

    Go to Audrey Claire on a weeknight when it's not so crowded. You can relax, enjoy your wine, and really be able to enjoy your time there.

    Side note: Our waitress looked like she might have been the chef. She was wearing an apron from the hip down and a once-white wifebeater ( I could see her bra through it, and when she poured us water her armpit was right in my face), which to be honest I found unappetizing and unprofessional.
