    Bridgid's was known as a good neighborhood bar with a better-than-average beer selection and more-than-decent comfort food. Now, with Chef Peter MacAndrews getting involved with his old friend, the longtime owner, totally redoing the menu... and his sous chef from his highly-regarded Modo Mio in the kitchen as head chef ... this is a very different level of dining. My son and I had the Skate special and it was awesome. I tasted over a dozen different items and can't even pick a favorite as they were all so good.It,  It is  also reasonably priced.  And casual.  Jeans were in vogue just like when it was nothing but a good local watering hole.  Kudos to the new team!!!!  I will be back again and again.

    Oh my god the new menu...

    I used to come here fairly frequently about 3 years ago, mostly for the excellent beer list.  A friend of mine told me this past Friday night that the menu had been overhauled and was pretty tasty.  I promptly looked up the menu on my phone (ah technology) and was quite intrigued by what I saw.  I am a big fan of Peter McAndrews and his fleet of Italian-inspired restaurants, so was excited to try the new menu.

    I was not disappointed.  

    Point one: the service was GREAT.  The staff at Brigid's has always been friendly and fun, but they have taken everything up a notch.  Our server was EXTREMELY knowledgeable, both about the beer menu and the food.  She was also very personable, as was the rest of the staff.  Everyone seemed as excited about the food as we were, if not more so.  As an aside, I really don't understand the people that are on here bitching and moaning about how the server isn't doting on them enough.  Maybe try being polite to your server and/or bartender and they will give you a little more attention.  Don't be such a-holes.  

    Point two:  the food.  Good lord, the food.  I had the Polpettone (a succulent, garlicky meatball served with pesto and fondue) to start and my boyfriend had the melt-in-your-mouth Polpo (grilled octopus with a red onion mostarda).  As one can likely infer from my choice of adjectives, they were both pretty incredible (and that's where my creative adjective choice ends...).  My main course was the Gnocchi (very tender, but not in a fall-apart-on-your-fork kind of way -- I hate that... I like my gnocchi to have a little heft) in spicy lamb ragu.  It was VERY rich, and I should have gotten the small portion (did I not mention that you can get a small or large pasta?  You can.), but hey... now I have delicious lunch for tomorrow!  My boyfriend got the duck confit over mixed green salad with dried blueberries and roasted baby potatoes.  This was not some stupid pseudo-confit that was basically deep fried duck leg, but REAL confit that had been sauteed.  Awesomesauce.  We finished up with the surprisingly light and mildly flavored cheesecake with dried blueberry compote, and I rolled myself home to put on some sweatpants because I felt like I was about to have a food baby.

    Point three: the beer.  Still an amazing beer list, still a pretty great draft selection... I couldn't be happier.  

    I loved this place before, and I am happy to say that I love it again.  I will definitely be back.  A lot.
