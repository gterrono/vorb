    When an area is as convoluted in Burger offerings as Rittenhouse Square is (See - yelp.com/biz/hipcityveg-…) you have to compare it to what other spots within its surroundings have to offer; especially when its backed by the celebrity chef that's the equivalent of Danny Meyer. Normally that's a bad thing; but when one burger spot down the way offers varieties like Cajun and duck fat fries, the local vegetarian joint offers up freshly cut and seasoned sweet potato fries, and even the Five Guys a few blocks away offers fresh cut fries by the bag load; its a JOKE! The 50/50 blend of iced tea and lemonade i ordered was also bland in taste and completely failed to taste like either, thusly even giving a bottle of Nantucket Nectar's Half and Half more of an edge.

    Overall when I'm looking for a burger in the area I think I'm still going with 500 Degrees (or HipCityVeg for their vegan awesomeness!). But the good burger and fact that I need to try the milkshakes and custards that I've been hearing awesome things about does merit another visit.

    Shake Shack, by virtue of its location and fare, could be one of my favorite spots in Philly. As it is now, it will be a place to go for a milkshake or concrete, but not for dinner. I ordered a bacon cheeseburger and was sorely disappointed to find that I had spent $7 on the same size patty and the same quality bacon served on the Jr. Bacon Cheeseburger at Wendy's. The fact is, if you're going to charge that kind of money for a burger at a self-titled "fast food" restaurant, it better be a large patty and cooked bacon. Down the street at 500 degrees, I hear they serve large, mouthwatering burgers for around the same price.

    I agree with the other reviews regarding the desserts. The milkshake was delightful, and I will certainly return for a concrete or two (don't look at me that way!). In all seriousness, it's a cool spot right near Rittenhouse with a really easygoing feng shui. All the same, I don't think I'll be going back for an overpriced burger any time soon. I would recommend taking friends along for a shake, or maybe for a large order of fries. I saw several people with bottles of wine, so it may be a BYOB (though maybe they were just taking matters into their own hands).

    In the meantime, if Shake Shack decides to increase their burger size, someone hit me up because I'll go back in a heartbeat.
