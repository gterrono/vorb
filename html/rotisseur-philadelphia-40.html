        Update: New tofu bahn mi!  I walked home from work on Saturday and was surprised to see them open and because there was a sign on the sidewalk about tofu bahn mi, I had to check it out as part of my vegetarian-in-residence duties.

        Rotisseur is a fancy word for "rotisserie"  and rotisserie is a fancy word for… Rotisseur is a fancy word for "rotisserie"  and rotisserie is a fancy word for "rolled-my-chicken-'­round-while-I-baked-it" and all of that can be put into short hand at this new, sweet home spot on 21st by just calling it "yum".  

        That is what I said when I ate the rich mac n' cheese and the kale chips (frizzled kale with sea salt --- get some!) and the watermelon and orzo salad and the house-made pickled veggies.  Yum.  

        I do not eat chicken but my friend's boyfriend ate his to the bare bone and said it was juicy. Yum.

        The menu is roasted chicken (pieces, 1/4, 1/2, whole) and what ever sides they feel like having.  Tonight it was kale chips, mac n' cheese, pickled cabbage, little corn muffins, potato salad, roasted potatoes and watermelon orzo salad.  They told me that the mac n' cheese will be a staple but the other sides will rotate weekly/ seasonally.  My small sides were $2.78 each.  I think the chicken dinner plate was something like $6 - $8 and was chicken, two sides and a corn muffin.

        I asked if they were going for soul food and the guys told me that they were going for "Tastes really good with roasted chicken".  Also they knew which of their sides had meat (potatoes) which made this veggie girl really happy.

        I rate them 4 stars as they are still working out some kinks with timing and this was their day 3 "soft opening" (I had no idea that was a thing) so no desserts (lunch coming next week apparently).  Plus one more star for really being something unique to a neighborhood full of pizza, burritos, so-so Asian food and really good, but really expensive, sit-down restaurants.

        Rotisseur is a just what my neighborhood of west Rittenhouse needs, an inexpensive, home-style, meat n' veg dinner/ takeout spot.  A few places to sit and eat casually (plastic forks, plates) but super clean and cheerful inside.   Best of all, the food was really fresh, they were making new batches of everything right up front as they were selling out.  All of the ingredients were obvious in what you were eating.

        So: inexpensive + fresh + home style + roasted chicken + yummy sides + veg friendly + clean = go there!         Read more

        Vegans and Vegetarians avert your eyes.

        I have a dark meat secret: I love crispy chicken skin. I usually don't really care about the whole bird meat, but give me the delicious crispy candy skin that covers it and I'll be a happy girl.  The caramelized and tasty texture of chicken skin can really only be achieved when one roasts chicken perfectly and achieves maximum crispy perfection.  The last time I can remember having chicken this good was at the once dearly departed and much loved Noble (and a close second would be Thomas Keller's Roast Chicken, but I digress).

        Rotisseur does chicken skin right by me.  It is dark, crisp, super delicious and they include it as a main ingredient in the sandwiches!  Hallelujah!  Along with four types of sandwiches (Italian, Banh mi and BBQ), they also offer meals with a variety of freshly-made sides, along with half and whole Rotisserie chickens to go.  Portions are conservative, but they fill you up.  Aside from the chicken skin, the chicken itself is right on point: juicy and well-seasoned.  Bread is fresh, not too dense, ingredients and sides don't disappoint either.

        I also appreciate that they use chickens that are local, cage-antibiotic-hormone free.
        Must be another reason why that chicken skin is so damn yummy.

        7/25/2012 We're very sorry that you were so disappointed. We aim to please, which is why we give what we think… We're very sorry that you were so disappointed. We aim to please, which is why we give what we think are generous sides for the price, as compared to similar prepared food establishments. Our small sides are 8 oz and our large sides are 16 oz. So if you had two small sides and a large side, you had a total of 32 oz of prepared from scratch food. That's an entire quart worth of food. Fourteen dollars for a quart of delicious house made goodness seems like a pretty good deal to us.

        As for the local, organic claim. We never made it and we never will. We are local when we can be, which is spring thru fall, and organic if we can be, but we refuse to lie like so many other places and tell you that we are organic when we are not. It's a simple matter of integrity. If we were to go all organic, the sides would have to be twice the price, and as you well know, price is a sticking point for so many customers. Beware of the place that claims organic and has low prices, it's not possible.

        Vegetarians take note: all of our delicious sides are vegetarian except for the potatoes, as they are cooked in chicken fat and then roasted. We also have a veggie banh mi with tofu that we press, marinate and roast in house before adding pickled red cabbage, cilantro, togarashi mayo, lime juice, cucumbers and sesame seeds. It is a fantastic sandwich. As an avowed meat eater, I eat it myself for lunch quite often, just because it's so damn good. And it's only $6.50.
