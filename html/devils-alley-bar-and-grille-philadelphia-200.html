    Unexpectedly unique & tasty brunch option in Philly... I especially liked the Eggs Benedict Trio.

    I've been to Devil's Alley a number of times, but mostly for drinks or a late night burger... but their Sunday brunch was a positive surprise.  Not as crowded as their Friday/Saturday night bar scene, friendly service, and a Southern-inspired brunch menu with Shrimp n Grits (with an egg on top).  

    The brunch drinks were pretty good too--the Bloody Mary comes with a grilled shrimp on it. Its pretty spicy, but not too strong in terms of alcohol.  The raspberry mojito is another great option, also not too strong but very flavorful.

    I'm definitely adding this to the [short] list of good brunch options in Philly.  Its at least on par with Sabrina's, Beau Monde and Continental.

    Is it fair to review a place based on Hurricane Irene night? Probably made little difference, except that Devil's Alley was a lot less crowded than it would have been on a normal Saturday night.

    Upstairs bar area is nice. Sadly, they were out of ginger beer so the Dark and Stormy was not an option. No worries, because they actually do a nice mojito. At least the first one ... the second ones went back because they were too sweet (bartender switch), but that was corrected. They've got a solid beer selection, too.  

    The fare was pretty good. Sam's BBQ ribs were better than the dry rub, the mac n cheese was a highlight, and cornbread muffins weren't bad. I wouldn't rush back here for the ribs; they just seemed to fit the bill.

    Service was fine overall, and our server was friendly and helpful. The owner also owns Sam's Grill in Wynnewood (which explained why the ribs tasted familiar) and Smokin' Bettys in town.

    Not quite a four, but a strong three. I'll try it again, maybe go with a beer and burger next time. And no hurricane.

    During my pre-Yelp days, I'd had a fairly bad experience at Devil's Alley -- bad service, solidly charred BBQ ribs, and grungy atmosphere.  It was enough to make me balk at my friend's bday invite last week when it was time for celebratory drinks.  But friendships prevail, and thankfully I left with a much better experience than the first time.

    First of all, let's talk about a restaurant that lets you book reservations for 10 people on a Friday night -- practically impossible in Center City, but DA said yes without hesitation.  Then we managed to score a fantastic waiter who was both patient and friendly as he rattled off the happy hour and food specials several times as the friends trickled in.  Somehow, DA also kindly ignored the faux pas of us bringin' in a bday cake from outside and served us slices of said cake on their plateware that they'd cut from the back of the kitchen.  Guys, you totally rock.

    Throughout the night, we were speculating as to whether the place was built between two existing buildings, given the interior graffiti decorating the interesting bathroom layout downstairs.  For some reason, the vibe is more win than creepy, and it works for DA when it comes to giving it an edgy atmosphere.

    Girlfriends raved about their pulled pork sandwiches, while I thoroughly enjoyed my mahi-mahi taco special and side of freshly battered onion rings (guilt be damned!).  Though my friend's Zephyr drink was more cucumber juice than anything, my delicious Dark and Stormy cocktail (ginger beer, rum and lime juice) was lightning enough to make my evening.  Whoo-ee to that!
