    I am a huge fan of Sabrina's cafe.  The atmosphere is inviting; the indie waiters/waitresses are curtious and attentive; the coffee is good (La Combe), strong and bottomless; and the food is simply amazing. I have been here a few times for dinner, but generally go for brunch.  Here are a few of my favourites:
    - Eggs benny (the hollandaise sauce is excellent and the spinach/ avocado/ tomato is a nice mix);
    - Huevos rancheros best I have had in the city (I think its the chirizo sausage);
    - Blue cheese, mushroom and spinach omelette (nuff said)
    - Parmesan cheese fries (well made pommes frites and fresh cheese)
    - Sweet potato fries (I recommend getting 1/2 sweet and 1/2 parmesan)
    - French toast (thick Texas toast bookends a sweet cream cheese filling usually topped with a healthy portion of fresh berries... for sweet toothes only)

    A few other notes:
    - Call ahead (especially if your party is bigger than 2)
    - They recently expanded the waiting area to another building inside
    - They give you free coffee while you wait

    Happy Eating!

    Anyway, I'd recommend going..  Just be sure to call ahead though.  The restaurant itself is small with no waiting area, so the check-in waiting area is actually the storefront next door.  You can have some coffee in that room while waiting, too.

    Let's just get down to it: the menu and actual food isn't as good as Honey's, but the service is awesome. I've had this one server there who genuinely perks up my day. Also note: I've gone alone before and was treated very respectfully. There are magazines in a rack at the very very back of the restaurant. They are also very happy to accomodate our tot-sized friends, despite the limited space.

    Anyway, the food IS good. I say this mostly relying on feedback from friends who've gone, as I get the same thing everytime:

    Fresh-squeezed orange juice and the lox and onion omelette, to which I ask cream cheese be added. This comes with home fries and bread (I substitute the cream cheese for the bread at no charge, cuz I can't eat bread). HUGE portions. Yummy yum yum; the closest to a bagel and lox I get. Now, I think maybe my definition differs from most people, but the "home fries" are actually what I call roasted potatoes. They are not the mashed down, somewhat crispy potatos things that some might call "hash". They are good, but they aren't what I really want.

    The jalepeno polenta fries are also gluten free; they weren't my favorite--I was expecting french fry looking things, but they were shapped more like sticks of butter and came with a spicy marinara dipping sauce. I've seen friends thoroughly enjoy the pancakes (with blueberries added). The decaf coffee is disgusting, but they have Frank's hot sauce which is, to me, an obvious brunch necessity.

    This Christian Street location is more crowded and busier than the other location, but I like the overall decor so much better. It has several smaller rooms and is more home-y. Plus, it's 2 blocks from my house.
