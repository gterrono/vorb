    Listed in Bang for your buck!

    Many times, I'll have the intention to eat elsewhere.... but somehow I always manage to end up here.  For one thing, I'd say it's definitely the best bang for your buck, also it's byob restaurant on the more affordable end.  I tend to take my out of towners here too, and they always leave happy.  My very impatient brother(especially when it comes to food) will not usually mind waiting 30 mins. on the weekend for their brunch because he likes it so much.  I think the call ahead for the weekend brunch gets too confusing though.  I've called, been told the wait was 20 minutes, put my name on the list, show up 20 minutes later and then be told that my wait starts when I get there or something like that?  Huh?

    I love the strange, yet appealing and usually oh so delicious concoctions they put together.  Definitely an awesome place for brunch, or if you have the munchies.  Rarely have my meals been disappointing. I recall one time my veggie lasagna being on the luke warm side, but it wasn't enough to keep me from going back or even complaining.  If you're there for supper, definetly hit up the giant bowl of mussels in the red sauce.  A really good value.

    It'd be nice if they kept shut the main door, especially in the winter or you'll be eating with your coat on if you sit by it.  I think they could use new floors and curtains as well.  I've been able to tell that they've let the decor go a bit the last few years.  Also, again regarding the wait list, I understand it makes sense to seat the parties according to the seats available at the table, but not when you've been waiting 35 minutes with one other person and the party of 4 who's been waiting 5 minutes gets seated ahead of you.  That's not exactly first come-first serve.  Sometimes you'll be sitting by the door, or by the kitchen, or by the loud fan in the summer.  But you'll leave with a full belly and then some!

    I so wish they had the grandmoms apple pie more frequently.  They never seem to have it anymore.

    When me and my sweetie can't make it to Honey's Sit-'N-Eat, we stroll a couple blocks over to Sabrina's Cafe, home of my second fave breakfast in Philly.  Like Honey's, Sabrina's often attracts a bit of a crowd outside, so it is sort of best to call ahead and put your name on the list.  The couple of times I did this, it didn't really make much of a difference the first time, but the second time we went in and were seated immediately.

    The brunch is very tasty here.  I enjoy the buttermilk pancakes with strawberries with a side of scrapple and syrup.  The eggs are damn fine as well, and the polenta fries are unique and probably my gal's fave thing on their menu.  I like the parmesan fries myself.  But what I really love most is the chicken salad samitch on whole grain toast from LeBus!  Holy God Almighty, it's heavenly.  Try it with a cup of the homemade chicken noodle soup and a splash of RC Cola and you have yourself a tasty treat.

    The staff is always super friendly, especially the owner/manager (I'm assuming) who knows patrons by their faces and strikes up a nice little conversation with them, making sure everything is to their liking.  There's always some funky goodness being played on the sound system ranging from Devo to classic soul tunes to everything in between.

    The front dining room can be a bit noisy and crowded at times, and I much prefer sitting in the secondary dining area (the one you can look in through the window on the street) as it's a little more sane. Still, the food is really good no matter where you're seated.  Never had the dinner here, but the daily entrees as listed on the chalk boards seem very eclectic and range in price from $12 to $17 from what I've usually seen.

In the hour-and-a-half that I was there, I was offered at least six refills (which explains my current jittery state).

    There were probably at least ten other tables there, but my food came out pretty quickly. I know I have yet to experience the insanity that is Sabrina's on weekends, but I have to say that the service has been phenomenal both times I've been here (Hunter and I came for dinner last week and we both loved it). Today, my frittata was divine. I substituted bell peppers for tomato, and they gave me yellow, red, and green. The turkey bacon was perfectly cooked and the provolone was melted across the whole thing. I have to say, I was pretty intimidated by the size of it (the plate also included two huge pieces of multigrain toast and perfectly cooked home fries), and for good reason: I could only finish half of everything. Smaller portions, please!

    After I enjoyed all I could eat of my meal and drank enough coffee to kill a baby, a nice couple sat down at the table next to mine and we engaged in a little conversation. There isn't much that accents a nearly perfect meal quite like a little dessert conversation. I haven't had such an enjoyable solo breakfast experience in a while. I'll be back for brunch!
