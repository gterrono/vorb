    Tinto's ! Where to begin? We found ourselves in the city on the spur of the moment. Thanks to Yelp we made reservations for our second night in the city. We arrived during Restaurant Week much to our delight! The night of our reservations we arrived on time and we were seated promptly. The space is tiny and our spot next to a wall was a bit cramped. The ambience and decor were modern and humming with energy.
    My friend and I chose the chef's tasting menu with the wine pairing. The arugula salad was tasty and unique with fried goat cheese. I am a vegetarian so I chose the cheese plate and the green beans. Both were delightful. The green beans were dressed in a light olive oil and tossed with spiced almonds. The Spanish wines, excellent! My friend enjoyed the pork belly and the chicken skewers. I did taste the figs (even though they are wrapped in bacon) and they were done to perfection. Dessert was to die for and I am not a dessert person. I loved the chocolate ganache with the passion fruit sherbet. My friend had the toasted brioche with caramel mousse. We loved both.
    Now on to the service. While efficient, they were not very personable. The bus boys cleared without asking if we were finished. Several dishes left before we were done. I realize it was restaurant week but we visited other establishments during our stay and no one else made us feel so rushed. I would have lingered and had another cocktail but I did not feel welcome to do so.
    Overall a good experience but not where I would go for a quiet relaxed evening.

    The last time I was at Tinto, the waiter coughed on my food.  I was less than pleased.  But, I had recently heard favorable things about Tinto's brunch from a friend, and thought it would be a great place to celebrate a great achievement - the completion of the Philadelphia Half-Marathon by a close friend.  

    I started off with the Jamon Serrano (cured Spanish ham with egg salad and pickled guindilla).  This had a nice balance of flavors and was a good start to brunch.  Next came the Huevos Benedictos (poached egg, brioche toast, jamon serrano, truffle hollandaise).  The poached egg (on both of our plates) was a bit more cooked than either of us would have liked, but it didn't ruin the dish.  As soon as the plate was brought to the table, I could smell the truffle hollandaise so get your nose ready!

    Our sides included the piperade (served with more jamon serrano) and maple grits.  The former was good, while the latter should have been on the dessert menu.  After 1-2 bites, the maple grits became too sweet to eat, especially when we were still eating our savory dishes.

    For dessert, both the Torrijas (roasted brioche, caramel mousse, creme fraiche sherbert, orange) and Brulee of Grapefruit (with creme fraiche gelato) were good.  Unlike the desserts at most restaurants, these two had just the right amount of sweetness such that we didn't feel like we would fall into a sugar coma afterwards.

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge, Tasting Menu, Pork Belly Diet

    To celebrate our second anniversary, the GF and I decided to enjoy the tasting menu at Tinto, Iron Chef Jose Garces' second location. Opened in 2005, Garces' intimate restaurant focuses on Spanish cuisine, offering pintxos ("peen-choes"), which is the coastal Basque region's equivalent of tapas. Thus, there are obvious Spanish and French influences at play here.

    Tinto exudes a warm atmosphere and is modeled on a wine cellar. The walls are lined with empty wine bottles and the entirety of the restaurant is dimly lit with candles and low lighting.

    There is a separate bar area which provides seating for up to 12 guests and during the warmer months, al fresco dining offers the opportunity for guests to enjoy the sights and sounds of  Rittenhouse Square.

    While we initially were planning on partaking in Chef Garces' tasting menu ($55 pp), we were offered an off-menu deluxe tasting ($75 pp). While the number of courses (thankfully!) did not differ between the two, the deluxe tasting offered Jamon Iberico and the dry-aged sirloin. Natch, we opted for the latter.

    Note: I've listed the a la carte pricing as well for any future diners that may want to try specific dishes at Tinto, rather than indulge in a tasting. If you'd like descriptions of the dishes - feel free to check out my blog.

    Deluxe Tasting Menu ($75 pp)

    Knowing that it was a special night, the staff at Tinto brought us complimentary Cava (Trepat, Castell Roig, Brut Rosat, Penedès, NV), essentially Spanish champagne. This was definitely a nice touch.

    Mahon Cheese Crisp (amuse bouche)

    Jamon Iberico de Belotta (Market Price)

    Olives ($5)
    Mixed Olives, Mahon, Saucisson Sec

    Cheese Mixto ($15)
    P'tit Basque, Blue de Basque, Queso Leonora, Acacia Honey, Bee Pollen, Espellette, Quice, Apple

    Revuelto de Bacalao ($13)
    Soft scrambled egg, black cod, goat cheese, spinach

    Gambas Brochette ($12)
    Shrimp, chorizo, lemon grape tomato, espelette chile

    Pork Belly Montaditos ($8)
    Berkshire pork belly, honey lacquer, shaved apples

    Sangria Blanca ($9 glass / $32 pitcher)
    White wine, absolut peach, fruit

    Mairritze ($13)
    Cachaca, muddled mint, lime, blood orange

    Alcachofas con Trufas ($12)
    Baby artichokes, pappardelle, meyer lemon, idiazabal

    Vierlas con Panchetta ($18)
    Diver scallops, cider demi-glace, benton's bacon, shaved apple

    Entrecote ($38)
    Sirloin steak, catalan spinach, quince paste, fresh goat cheese

    Cappuccino ($5.50) / Mighty Leaf Organic Black Tea ($4)

    Cuajada ($8)
    Toffee, black pepper custards, fig, pistachio

    Chocolate ($9)
    Smoked chocolate torte, chestnut purée, sherry ice cream

    Overall, our anniversary dinner at Tinto was one of my favorite meals in Philly. The service was impeccable - our server, Rebecca, accommodated all of my requests (turning down the heat and providing a list of the dishes served) and even went the extra mile in wishing us both a happy anniversary and offering complimentary champagne.

    While not all of the dishes were home-runs, they were all interesting and well-conceived. And frankly, the majority of the dishes, did in fact, hit it out of the park. Do get the pork belly, prawns, and scallop. Do splurge on the jamón ibérico. Please get the white sangria and the mairritze. In fact, if you're interested in cooking a la Garces, the Chef recently came out with a new book - The Latin Road Home. But enough sports references and plugging, let's just say Jose Garces did it again for me and provided the GF and I with a wonderful anniversary meal.

    One final note - I heard through the grapevine that Chef Garces will be teaming up with the Kimmel Center later this year - so that's something to look forward to as well! I'm interested to see what else he has in store for us.

In the future, I would definitely stray away from that spot during the winter months.

    We decided to start the night off with drinks, ordering a pitcher of white sangrai for the table. Sweet and subtle, it was a great way to wet out palettes before diving right into the menu. The fruit was perfect sized for sangria and the peach undertones made the concoction extra tasty.

    For our couses, I decided to go with chorizo pamplona and jamon serrano for first, pork belly montadito and moules basquaise for second, and cuajada for dessert. First course was not especially filling but pretty tasty. The sliced ham had the saltiness and flavor I love from cured meats, although it could've been a little more tender. It was hard enough biting through it with the bread that came with it. They were also served with an egg salad, which to be honest was a little bland. Not exactly sure what its purpose was.

    The second course was also fairly mediocre. My pork belly was a little tough to chew on, but the combination of flavors was spot on. I was a huge fan of the apple, giving it a nice crisp crunch and sweet finish. The mussels were just disappointing. Not only was it very salty, but to me, it just tasted like mussels soaked in a tomate-based sauce. There was not much depth to it and I was not impressed at all. Even the accompanying toasted bread was harder than normal to bite into.

    Dessert was the redeeming course of the meal. The custard was so rich and so creamy. I was slightly worried about the pepper, but I enjoyed up loving it. Specialties

Tinto features a mouth-watering selection of "pinxtos" (pronounced PEEN-choes), the Basque equivalent of tapas.  Along with pinxtos, Tinto offers a tempting array of charcuterie, cheeses, mariscos (shellfish), brochettes and bocadillos (sandwiches).  

Some of the inspired dishes include:  Duck Confit Montadito, with wild black cherries and bleu de Basque;  Gambas Brochettes, shrimp with grape tomato and sherry espelette chili sauce;  Roasted Rack of Lamb Au Jus, with truffled artichoke puree and candied lemon;  Marmitako de Ventresca, tuna toro, parsley oil, migas and shaved mojama;  Txanguro Montadito, Dungeness crab, avocado and espelette;  and Mousse de Queso, with goat cheese, calabaza, orange, olive oil, caramel and lavender tuille.

History

Established in 2007.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
