    Visiting Philly? Check out Kanella. You'll thank me later.

    Few weekends ago, my friends and I took a short trip to Philly for an Adele show.  Before heading back to NY, we agreed to eat brunch at Kanella. I remember reading about this restaurant a couple of years ago but didn't get a chance to try. And since we had cheese steaks the night before, we decided a meal not so meat laden would be perfect.

    We were greeted warmly by the hostess and were seated almost immediately at outside table. I love when positive vibes are present as soon as you walk in. We scanned the menu and thought everything sounded good. I kept changing my mind but finally settled for a Shaksouka - a tomato, onion and pepper stew with w poached eggs and grilled bread. It was perfect. It was rich and flavorful minus the heaviness. Naturally I ordered a side of bacon and a medium sized french pressed coffee. I just had to...

    It was a lovely brunch at a pleasant neighborhood. The combination of good service, simple decor,  affordable price and delicious food is what definitely put them in Philadelphia Magazine's 2011 "50 Best Places to Eat Right Now". Did I hear you say MMmmmmmmm?

    Absolutely delicious! We went for dinner on Saturday with prior reservations. We tried 4 appetizers - dips of the day, Saganaki (pan fried sheep cheese), flat bread with vegetables and Greek tappenade, and a lamb kabob special. Wow! Wow! Wow and Wow!! For mains, we tried the seasonal stuffed vegetables (bell pepper stuffed with heavenly rice, lentils, raisins etc.,), the Pouleriko a flattened whole chicken in an incredible sumac sauce, the Arni a medium done lamb with mild spices, and the Katsiki (braised goat wih mild spices in lentil). All 8 dishes we tried had distinct taste and flavors which drew upon the fresh ingredients. Serving size is just right (about the size of 2 palms).

    Wait staff were efficient, courteous and knowledgeable. Members of our party were running late, and we were reminded that reservations were for 90 minutes. Not that it was an issue as the service is quick, leaving ample time for an enjoyable dinner. It does get crowded. I was able to confirm that the chef does not like photographing the food ;-))

    Parking is a perpetual issue. Suggest the garage on 13th. Other than that little nuisance what a fantastic BYO.

    All in all I had a lovely time at Kanella.  We arrived 15 minutes late for our reservation but fortunately it wasn't a problem.  The service was good and the waiter reminded me of Alton Brown somewhat in appearance and demeanor (not a bad thing).  And I actually like that he didn't refill our wine glasses after the initial filling.  I know some of you like waiters to do that but I think waiters at BYOs don't have any idea about how the wine is being distributed at the table and sometimes people drink faster and really shouldn't have their glasses automatically refilled because then they're getting a disproportionate share of the bottle.  Here I go off on a tangent again.  I am a wine Marxist.

    We ordered appetizers of a certain kind of Amazonian fish (I guess the Greeks can cook fish from anywhere) and the trio of dips.  The dips were excellent, especially the garlic one.  A sizeable morsel of said dip remained on the plate and then a waiter whisked it away before I could tell him that me doth protest.  Those waiters are awfully quick to take your plates and ask you if they can take it *while taking it away*.  Hold on to your horses, boys.  I also almost lost my last bite of my entree this way but I was quicker on the draw that time.

    It wasn't till nearly the end of our dip feast that the waiter informed us that the fish appetizer had been sold out.  So that's fine, but why it took him that long to realize I'm not sure.  We ordered the octopus ragout in its place, which arrived promptly and was very pleasing.  The octopus was tender (for octopus) and all the flavors came together harmoniously.  I loved the little bits of chorizo-like sausage in it, whatever they were.

    I had the goat entree, which was steaming hot upon its arrival but fortunately cooled off quickly.  The portion size was decent and the meat was amply tender.  Combined with barley, greek yogurt, breaded eggplant, etc. it was a nice homey dish.  I sampled my sister's quail, constituting my first bite ever of this fowl.  Lacking other quail experience I can't comment too much on it but the meat seemed to be cooked well.  The sauce for this dish was pretty heavy on the sherry.  My sister seemed to like this aspect; I would have been more on the fence about it.  Every course of the evening was seasoned well. ++

    I don't know if I'd rush back here but would recommend it without much hesitation to anyone looking for Greek.  I'd like to give it four and a half stars.  WHY can't Yelp have a half star option?  It'd be easy to create and half stars are reflected in review averages anyway.

    My dad and sister came to visit me me tonight and check out the new apartment I just moved into around 11th and Spruce. Wandering around looking for food, I suggested Kanella, as I had just walked past it for the first time earlier today and thought it looked like it would worth the trip.

Luckily she did us a solid and got us a table in less than 5 minutes. It took a few minutes for our server to get us menus but again, huge party taking up half of the tables in the restaurant, totally understandable.

    We ordered the grape leaves as an appetizer, the quail for me, and the calamari for my dad and sister. The appetizer was only two stuffed grape leaves so if you have more than two people, make sure to order two orders! They were very good nonetheless, I wish there had been more of them!

    The entrees were out of this world. My quail was so tender and flavorful and all the side fixings--grapes, barley, mushrooms, sausage--were perfect compliments. The sherry vinegar sauce that it came in was tart in the best way possible. I had some of my dad and sisters entrees and I can honestly say that I've never had calamari that has tasted that good. Super tender, full of flavor, and it all blended wonderfully with the feta/pine nut/rice filling. My dad could not stop marveling at how good it was.

    For dessert, I got a tart with hibiscus ice cream and my sister got some sort of custard concoction(I was too busy anticipating my upcoming sugar bomb to pay attention). The tart was fantastic and the perfect portion to end a meal. The ice cream was sorbet-like and refreshing. My sister enjoyed her dessert very much as well.

It was a bit on the loud side for most of the meal but as soon as the huge party left, it got comfortable noise-wise. The prices were affordable, appetizer, three entrees, two desserts, and a soda landed us just over $100.

    I will most definitely be back, since I live right down the block, and my sister already decided she wants her birthday dinner in August to be here. Do yourself a favor, GO!

    My recent visit to Kanella was a very long ordeal which spanned 2 hours of our afternoon. Here is how the day just seemed to fly by.

    1:30PM - My family and I arrive as a party of 10 adults, 3 children, and 1 baby. We arrive together and are ready to be seated on-time.

    After about 10-15 minutes of reviewing the menu and considering the specials, we place our orders for drinks, appetizers, and entrees all at once. We couldn't help but notice that our waiter was having difficulty tallying up the drink orders (4 fresh mint ice teas, 1 lemonana, and 3 hot teas). The only reason I can remember it is because we had to repeat the order to him about 3 or 4 times.

    Taking the dinner orders seemed to go better than taking the drink orders. The waiter managed to go around the whole table and even repeated the specials just in case our guests were sitting on the fence about which dish to order.

    2:15PM - Our drinks are finally served to the table. The only conceivable reason I can think of is that the ice teas were fresh brewed and required proper time to cool to get them to serving temperature. Why they wouldn't have a few pot made prior to the incoming lunch crowd is beyond my comprehension. I find it hard to believe that the evening service takes 30 minutes to prepare beverages for table.

    2:20PM - Some of our guests had offered a few appetizers to tide us over while we patiently waited for our meals to be served.

    Malohwa (pan fried savory puff pastry from Yemen, served with a boiled egg, spicy tomato salad and Greek yogurt) - Tasted like a soggy side of spanokopita, except without the flavorful filling inside

    Kanella platter (stuffed grape leaves, tabouli, falafel, fried eggplant, spicy tomato salad, tzaziki, olives and pita) - The stuffed grape were filled with pine nuts and currants. Although I have seen recipes which have referred to this filling as "Greek," from my own experience I have always associated this type of filling as a more Turkish variant. But given that Kanella seems to have Cypriot and Armenian  influences, this point is neither here nor there and can likely be attributed to these influences as this place has never claimed to be an exclusively Greek restaurant. Not a fault of the chef, just not what I'm used to when eating dolmades.

    Halloumi - similar to fried saganaki, this cypriot cheese was probably the tastiest of all of our dishes. No complaints from anyone as this was unanimously regarded as one of the favorites of the day.

    Greek fried potatoes - basically these were fresh cut steak fries sprinkled  with fresh lemon and wild oregano. Very tasty and helped tide us over while waiting for our meals to be served.

    2:45PM - Meals hit the table.

    Rabbit kidneys with spinach pasta and white beans - I ordered this special because I really hoped that it would be a solid dish. When I asked the waiter exactly what type of sauce or broth this was prepared with, he replied that there really wasn't one. Despite my hopes of unique flavors, I found this dish to be bland. I tried squeezing a bit of lemon, adding a scoop of the Kanella's homemade hot sauce, and even a few dashes of salt ... but all of this was to no avail. This dish would not be picked up with any flavor no matter what I tried to do to resuscitate the heart beat that the kidneys should have had.

    Roast lamb sandwich with feta, dill and spinach - This was a second entree which I easily shared with my brother-in-law as it was already split in half. The biggest problem with the lamb dish was that we were left asking ourselves "Where's the beef?" When we looked inside the bread we could clearly see where the lamb was supposed to be except it seemed to be like Neo (The Matrix) dodging bullets as we actually tried to eat it. Our every bite came down feeling unsatisfying due to the lack of meat from within the sandwich.

    3:15PM - Desserts are served.

    Chef's mother's coconut, date, and hazelnut cake (with date and orange syrup) - Way way too much orange with every bite. This tasted like they used the entire orange when making this "cookie," the whole orange including the peel! The overwhelming taste of concentrated orange made this dessert inedible for most of our guests and sat there on the plate basically uneaten.

    Galatopoureko (fillo pastry filled with semolina custard) - I've had this many times before and this is a usual dessert that you'd find at almost any Greek restaurant.

    3:30PM - Check paid.

    Lucky for us the gratuity was already included as we may have had ill will against our waiter for keeping us there for a full 2 hours before letting us go about our way and continue our day. Also, the restaurant's policy is not to allow more than 2 credit cards to be used on a check. Fortunately 2 of us had enough cash on us to cover the other half of the check. Thanks Kanella, but I don't think that I'll be back again to confirm that this was an off day for you. I've had enough to be sure that it was enough.

Specialties

We specialise in cooking from the homeland of chef Konstantinos Pitsillides. Pitsillides hails from the island of Cyprus, an indipendant Greek island located in the very east of the Mediterranean. The Cyprus kitchen has many influences but can perhaps best be considered as part of the Levant. At home in Cyprus, as in his Philly kitchen freshness, simplicity, flavour and love are the standard ingrediants. Chef takes his French culinary training and applies it to the traditional recipes of his homeland. Homemade desserts, head to tail cooking, lemon and olive oil are all part and parcel of this neighbourhood joint.

History

Established in 2008.

Kanella is made up of a passionate bunch of characters from the chef on down. Being a local restaurant filled with the family spirit much of the staff have been there since we opened. Though Chef Pitsillides is oft misunderstood (and yes sometimes this is exaserbated by himself), the intent of the restaurant is a simple one... We have aimed to be a neighbourhood venue for good, honest and consitstant cooking. A place that you can afford to come to regularly and have a good meal, good service, a nice bottle of wine, and enjoy your company. We have been blessed with the neighborhood we are in and all our wonderfull customers, so many of which we have known for sometime now. We look forward to continued joyful cooking and eating!
