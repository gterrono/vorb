    Their pickles were fine and the coleslaw was actually pretty good. They also give you these nice cookies at the end, which was a pleasant surprise. The service was quick and friendly, no complaints there.

    I'd say, food wise, it's a 3.5, establishment/atmosphere-wise, a 4. So 4 it is.

    I happen to love Famous 4th Street.

    The portions are huge, the food is fresh, and the atmosphere is comfortable-- what more could a Philly girl want? Oh wait, there are also pickles.

    The minute you sit down you will be presented with a bowl of pickles, which range in color/flavor (from more cucumber-y to more pickle-y) which, to me, is awesome. I've been here a million times and usually get an omelet which is really filling (served with home fries and toast/bagel). The grilled cheese is oddly good, the latkes are awesome, and the blintzes are made with farm fresh cottage cheese!

    You just don't encounter this type of old style Jewish deli anymore, so this place is definitely a treat. I should also mention that Famous is immaculate.

    All in all, it's a great for moderately sized groups, but tends to get really packed on a Sunday morning-- so plan on possibly waiting for a table. Oh and you get a chocolate chip cookie on your way out... seriously.

    As I was sitting at our cozy window-side table at 4th Street Deli, I realized this was my first experience at a Jewish-style deli (they're not kosher, apparently).  Kinda hard for me to believe.  At least now I know it won't be my last.

    My husband had eaten here before and knew how enormous the servings were, so we opted to split a hot roast beef sandwich.  It came on rye, and we got the cole slaw side.  The pickle and vinegar slaw appetizer came free with our order, and everything was crunchy and flavorful.  The pickles were a little on the salty side, but then again, I'm not really big on pickles.

    The sandwich...huge.  The roast beef was steaming and sliced a little on the thick side, as was the hearty rye bread.  The au jus came in a little bowl so we could pour or dip as we pleased.  Great flavor, and we couldn't finish all the meat so we wrapped it up to bring home.  The cole slaw side was creamier and sweeter than the vinegar slaw, but no less crunchy and tasty.

    On a side note, we kept wondering who the young woman in all the photos was...her face was staring at us from everywhere.  My guess was the daughter of the owners.  I felt kinda obliged to smile as I ate so she'd see I was enjoying the meal.

    Our friendly (but maybe a bit curt?) waitress brought us some chocolate chip cookies at the end.  They were a little crunchy, a little soft, and the chips were semi-sweet dark and gooey.  The food was definitely satisfying and we look forward to our next visit...we plan on trying the famous corned beef.

course there's the food.  Gargantuan portions of some of my favorite comfort food, made the way my mom makes it.  When I'm sick, I order take out of their maztah ball soup it's that good.  I have yet
