    Perfect dinner in every way!

    My companion and I came here for dinner during Restaurant Week and both got the RW menu, which was five courses. We both had the hors d'oeuvres (which were housemade ricotta wrapped in roasted red pepper, duck on a salted chip and a small bean salad); all three were very good. Next course, we both had potato and olive oil soup, which was delicious. Risotto followed, which varies daily; ours was bacon, egg yolk and parmesan, and it was fantastic. We didn't think it could get much better, but it did. My friend had the ricotta gnocchi. He's a gnocchi fiend and knows his stuff -- and he was blown away by the dish. Fortunately he let me try one and it was So Good! My fourth course was the chicken pot pie. I expected something rather traditional, but got a deconstructed dish with a puff pastry biscuit sitting in a luscious thick gravy accompanied by tender chicken meat and a roasted leg. Yum! Dessert was a choice of chocolate milk shake (made with housemade ice cream), an eclair, or cinnamon cheesecake with cranberry compote. We couldn't decide, so the server let us get all three (and added a small charge to the bill). All three were wonderful.

    The drink menu features about fifteen beers on tap, lots of bottled beer, two pages of whiskey, absinthe and cocktails.

    Service was perfection. One server handled all the tables but she did an amazing job. She didn't write anything down, which always worries me, but she got everything right. She was knowledgeable about the menu and very pleasant. And finally, when we were done, she brought our check within a couple of minutes; the check was accurate, I plunked down my card and she retrieved it quickly and then brought it back within a minute or two. This is my biggest peeve with restaurants -- I get great service all through the meal, and then when I'm ready to pay up I don't see anyone for 15 minutes. That was not the case here.

    My only quibble with Time was the tables, which were way too squeezed together.

    The musical group was nice -- a two-piece playing standards. My impression is that there is a different group each night.

    Food: sublime. Service: perfection. Tables: too close together.

    This was our first time to this restaurant and we WILL be back!!

    Update: We have since been back to Time, which is quite near City Hall, and had the same great experience. Terrific food and great service!

    For an appetizer, the two of us munched on a cheese plate ($15). The cheese plate was overpriced, nearby competition typically serve more at that rate. We had 3 cheeses: a blue cheese, goat cheese and a ?hard cheese. Each one had suggested pairings: lemon for the goat cheese, dates for the blue cheese and toffee brittle for the hard (sorry! can't remember) cheese along with grapes, bread and apple slices. While we gabbed and ate our app, a jazz band set up (downstairs), played at noise level that was perfect if you want to still have a conversation. My friend really liked the blue cheese and said that the hard cheese tasted like a bandaid. I agreed that it wasn't appealing but with the toffee combination made it was much better.

    We also shared our dinner: Fried Chicken Sandwich ($13) with house made bacon, jack cheese, fries and a side order of onion rings ($5). This was definately enough for us both. The Fried Chicken Sandwich was the better part of this plate---the chicken was moist, the bacon stood out and overall a good bite. The onion rings were in fair shape, fresh onion but the batter was overbearing and flaky. Overall, a 3 star for the food.

    Our service was grizzly, at one point I wanted to just put my money on the table and hop out the nearby window. We waited, waited and then some more waiting went by. The table next to use arrived after us and had their check before we ever got our sandwich.  

    We weren't charged a cover but arrived at 8 pm.
