    This is my favorite neighborhood restaurant, home to perhaps the most underrated burger in Philly. I have heard that Rembrandt's once was bad, but everything on the menu since the new ownership is truly delicious. The half-price happy hour Rembrandt burger is a thing of the past (boo), but it is well worth $10 at full price. The pizzas, salads - excellent. My sister says Rembrandt's has the best French onion soup she has ever had. I'm slightly uncomfortable with that superlative, but it is DELICIOUS. The only real downer to the menu is dessert. It's just not good.

    Drinks wise, there are no good cocktails on the menu, and each time I go, I manage to forget how sweet and nasty the Riesling is. But the bar has a warm, inviting atmosphere. It's a nice mix of locals of all ages. Quizo on Tuesday now requires a reservation (?) but in a way, that's better than fighting for a table. I also appreciate that it's rarely so crowded that I have to wait. That's a virtue in a neighborhood restaurant.

    I might even go 4.5 stars. Rembrandt's is perfect for what it is - a family pub/restaurant with great food.

    3 stars, why? I'm glad you asked. Cause it is no secret that Rembrandt's is my favorite neighborhood go to bar and restaurant, but the menu changes have me in a tizzy.

    Since being a loyal patron for the last 5 yrs I have lived in this neighborhood, I have frequented Rembrandt's on a monthly if not weekly basis. I have brought all my friends from out of town here for Sunday brunch and I have loved Tuesday night quizzo for as long as I can remember, but when Jan sold Rembrandt's a little over a year ago, I feel the change in management came with a menu change that wasn't as welcoming as the new owner. Getting rid of the crabcake benedict for Sunday brunch was heresy. I almost had a heart attack when the crabcake benedict came off the menu. To make matters worse the brunch menu has gotten smaller and smaller, where it seems silly to claim you have a brunch menu, when I only see 6 breakfast items on the list amongst the typical burger and flatbread options. I have heard from the staff that the new chef is a pork lover, but BRING BACK A FULL BRUNCH MENU WITH MORE THAN THE CLASSIC EGGS BENEDICT WITH CANADIAN BACON, WE LIKE SEAFOOD AND CHICKEN AND SPINACH AND FETA!!!! NO MORE PORK! Another hit to my Rembrandt go-to dishes was the Paesano burger. The Rogue burger is not the Paesano burger and although the Rogue burger is delicious, I want bacon instead of a slab of pork belly on it, which I feel is insult to injury, a heart attack waiting to happen, and yet again more pork...CUT IT OUT!

    Outside of the menu changes, which have hurt my palate and made other neighborhood brunch places higher on my Sunday go-to places, I still love Rembrandt's. The staff, ambiance, and fellow patrons are amazing. The owners as well as the staff are very good to their customers and our neighborhood. They have a great beer menu that rotates regularly and overall it's still my favorite place to go in Fairmount.

    The menu just needs to be changed back to a more well rounded and justified American menu as opposed to its current Pork take over.

    Listed in Gastro-pubs in the city

    An excellent beer and wine selection, slow but pleasant bar service, and noticeable improvement in the food from when I was here last, about 2 years ago.

    We rolled into this neighborhood pub from the Rembrandt exhibit at the Art Museum.  That was kind of fitting, dontcha think?  From Rembrandt to Rembrandt's? ;-)

    There was a great draft beer selection on the blackboard, but there were only three beers on happy hour special.  Anyway, my friend had  the Pils special, it was above average.  I, of course, had to have my daily IPA fix, so I tried a sample of the full priced Mission IPA, and followed that up with more of the same after sucking that first glass dry.

    They had a few food happy hour specials, small bites.  I had the wings, which were 3 wings of great size, served with a tiny bit of fresh crispy slaw, the wings were perfectly hot, but the batter was laid on a little thick.  $3, so for a buck a wing, that wasn't really a special, but very good nonetheless.  

    We next split a Jambalaya Pizza.  My friend said it was the best thing she had put in her mouth in weeks.  No comment :-X  It was really, really good.  Think about the flavors of the best Jambalaya you could find in the area, spread it thin on an ultra perfect crispy crust.  We couldn't get enough of it, it disappeared quickly, all of it.  And it was not small either!!

    We were so blown away by that dish, we had to try something else, so we split the burger of the day.  It was a smoked pork burger.  Creatively concocted, assembled, and on a wonderful bun, but it was only slightly above average on the palate.  

    Overall, I am very happy I returned after 2 years and add it to the list of another good choice in the Art Museum area.  Nice mixed neighborhood crowd, friendly, not overly loud and a very nice happy hour ambiance if you had a stressful day.

    ~ Good food, good service, nice setting. The prices are moderate and their beer selection is great! Go for the high-end burgers and flatbread pizzas. ~

    We had dinner here on a Tuesday evening and were surprised when we were told by the hostess that we could not sit in the bar area (which has tables as well) because Quiz was going on. Apparently you need to make specific reservations for Quizo, which I may have to try on another Tuesday night; that section was hopping! We sat in another section which was not crowded at all, so much quieter.  It's really quite a large space.

    Rembrandt's has what could be described as romantic lighting and a somewhat elegant feel. And of course works of art by Rembrandt are dotting the walls.

    The food is mostly limited to Burgers/Sandwiches and Pizzas/Flatbreads, with a few other items and specials. This might seem restricted, but there are a lot of inventive menu items and everything that was ordered to our table looked delicious. I of course could not resist the Lamb Burger ($12) with feta cheese yogurt, arugula salad, kalamata olives, and smoked paprika vinaigrette. It was very very good (not quite as good as the one PYT or even Lancaster Brewing Co), and came with a nice side salad with a light but flavorful dressing.

    The presentation of the food was also well done - modern rectangular white platter plates with the burger or sandwich on one side and your side dish on the other (fries served in a mini deep-fry basket, very cute!). My fiancé ordered the Jambalaya Pizza, which was HUGE for a personal pizza - basically half would be more than enough.

    Everything was great; I will definitely head back to Rembrandt's in the future for more delicious food, or a game of Quizo and good beer.

Specialties

A friendly neighborhood bar and gastropub with banquet and private event facilities.

Rembrandt's has one of the most inviting sidewalk cafe's in the Fairmount neighborhood.

When not hosting private events, Rembrandt's provides a variety of entertainment - Quizzo on Tuesday nights is a big draw!.  Call us to schedule your private event or use our space to show off your (or your group's) talent.  We feature a great view of the Philadelphia Skyline from our upstairs banquet room and we are fully equipped for audio and video events.

And, of course, we offer casual dining in our romantic dining room, and the same menus are available at both of our turn of the century bars.

History

Established in 1985.

The brown bars and cafes of Amsterdam, the city that was home to Rembrandt van Rijn, are well known throughout Europe for their antique charm, warmth, inviting atmosphere, and friendly but stimulating conversation. Here at Rembrandt's in Philadelphia, a seventy-five year old tavern has been renovated in a manner reminiscent of the brown bars of Amsterdam. In fact, many of the interesting fixtures you will see in the bar and restaurant were discovered in Europe by the original owners of Rembrandt's, and their history may be of interest to you. Please browse our website for photos of our neighborhood gathering spot and of the people who come to enjoy our food, drink and companionship!
