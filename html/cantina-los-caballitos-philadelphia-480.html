    I have eaten here in the past and for whatever reason was not totally wowed by the food.  As a result, despite my love for margaritas and recent move to the neighborhood, I had not been back here for quite a long time.  This weekend, I had some friends in town who were craving margaritas, so I decided to give it another go.

    I was incredibly and pleasantly surprised with the quality of our dinners (and the margs!).  This place does not take reservations, and we arrived Saturday at 8 pm, so we were expecting a long wait.  Although the wait for outside was long, there was a four-top table right by the open front wall, which we snatched right up.  

    Our server immediately served us ice water and took our drink orders--traditional and jalapeno margaritas and one beer. We also ordered the guacamole starter.  While we waited for our drinks and guac to arrive, we were served with tortilla chips and two generous bowls of salsa--a garlicky tomatillo and a traditional red with a hint of chipotle.  

    The guacomole arrived with another bowl of chips and was definitely excellent--seasoned perfectly with healthy chunks of perfectly ripe avocado.  

    For our entrees, a friend and I split the chicken enchiladas and fajitas.  The enchiladas were my favorite--they came in a creamy red sauce and were topped with sour cream.  The chicken in the enchiladas was moist and delicious.  The fajitas were good.  I especially liked the variety of vegetables and other accouterments (like mushrooms) that came with the dish, that while not traditional fajita fare, added to the heartiness of the dish. The fajitas came with more of that delicious guac, but in my opinion (and that of my friend) could have also used some cheese or sour cream.

    I will definitely be back again now that I have rediscovered this restaurants.

    Bonus:  They paved the outside patio, so you no longer have to navigate through mosquito soaked gravel to enjoy your meal outside on a beautiful day!
