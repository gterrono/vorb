    I'm really picky about pizza, but Gianfranco is the best that I've had in Philly.  I realize that pizza is sort of a personal thing, so I tried to give both objective and subjective evaluations of the sauce, crust and cheese.  Maybe you'll disagree with me on how excellent the overall product is, but hopefully I'll give you a good idea of what to expect when you order from Gianfranco.

    First, the sauce.  Gianfranco's pizza is pretty saucey, which is key for me.  There's nothing I hate more than under-sauced pizza, which seems to be all too common in the Philadelphia area. (Especially in Wayne...  If you're in Wayne, go to DiMeo's.  New Wayne and Real Pizza are awful.)  The sauce's sweet/salty balance is perfect.  I don't know whether that's a function of using fresh tomatoes vs. canned or what (honestly, Gianfranco may used canned, I don't care), but it's exactly what I love.

    On to the crust.  The crust is middle of the road, thickness-wise. It's certainly not deep dish, but not New York/Jersey Shore style either. (I would say try Pete's on Chestnut if you're in Old City and looking for NY-style)  But it definitely works with the rest of the pizza.  It's thin enough to get a little crispy, but thick enough that it a) can handle the sauce level and b) gives you some bready flavor.  And it's never overcooked, at least in my experience.  I don't have strong convictions about there being a "right kind" or "wrong kind" of crust, but Gianfranco's goes perfectly with the other ingredients.

    Lastly, the cheese.  The cheese is, as many people have pointed out, stringy.  That's how mozzarella is, and if you don't like that, you don't like a pure-mozzarella pizza.  It's lightly cooked, with little to no browning.  Whether you like that is up to you, but for me it's heaven.

    If I had to describe Gianfranco's pizza in one word, it would be "fresh".  As I said before, I don't know whether Gianfranco uses 100% fresh tomatoes in its sauce or if their bread comes from a can, but something about the pizza tastes really fresh to me.  And there's a certain synergy between the sauce, crust and mozzarella that I don't often taste at a pizza place.  And in the end, I think that's what I like so much about Gianfranco.  It might not be the best in Philly (I haven't been everywhere) but I can say with some certainty that it's top tier.

    looking at the reviews... there's differences and here's why:  the shop rocks but take-out/delivery experience with francos can be hit or miss.

    Have had dozens of great lunches in the little shop ordering slices but it's true the delivery or take out of a whole pie just wasn't as good. They have to step up that part of their game.

    Maybe it has something to do with the extra large pies they make in the shop as compared to the smaller pies people usually order. Maybe it has to do with who is making what... or how the pies hold up over time...Maybe it's because if you take it out you are less likely to throw some spice on it...

    key gianfrancos tip: the slices get better with doctoring in the form of every little spice bottle they offer on the table: red pepper, black pepper, salt, cheese, oregano and garlic power. Assemble them correctly, it's like alchemy.  Eat the slice naked, that's a rookie mistake.

    Ordering can be an art. people seem to like their steaks but i'm loyal to those slices... and enjoy the excitement of eying up the talent and drafting my selections. don't be afraid to ask when the next of a certain pie is coming out.

    the tomato slice with pesto is always a safe choice, regardless of any other variable. It's not truly cheese-less there is usually shaved romano on top. Lovely.

    another tip: "Franco" the owner is more likely to be there during the week, those pies usually are all perfect.

    f you are lucky (and early like 11:00am.) you might find him making himself a pie that isn't ever on the menu. Prosciutto and roasted potatoes was one such experiment. Broccoli Rabe is often featured.

    Get that slice and another, grab a free fountain drink. Spice it up. if it's a sunny day take a walk with a paper plate, maybe go down to independence park, sit on a bench and laugh and people talking to fake Ben Franklin. This is when Gianfranco's is at it's best.
