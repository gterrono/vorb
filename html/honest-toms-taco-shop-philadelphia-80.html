    Used to go to their truck now and then, and always found it enjoyable. Today, I decided to check out the shop as well.

    I ordered two tacos, one pork and one beef. The price was right, at just $6 for the two tacos and about $1.25 for a bottle of root beer. As for the food, it was overall very good. The tomatoes and guac added some sweetness and smoothness to the tacos that contrasted very nicely with the crisp onions. I generally don't eat tomatoes on my tacos, but they worked very well here. The meat itself was probably the weakest part of the taco but it was still pretty good; the pork in particular was a bit dry, but there was still plenty of flavor to make the taco work.

    Seating was a bit cramped, but I was able to get a seat without undue difficulty.

    I'd definitely come back here. I do want to try the chicken and sweet potato tacos next time, as well as the other type of tortilla (they offer both corn and flour). Very nice!

    Listed in West Philly Gems

    I don't think I can convey in words how much I love this place. Sometimes I'll even come here several times a week. I make it a point to bring people who have never been so that all can understand and experience the goodness that is Honest Tom's Taco Shop. Really, it's that good.

    I usually pay attention to the taco specials since those are the ones that change while the burrito options stay constant. My favorite thing I have ever tried at Honest Tom's is the Al Pastor taco special. Al Pastor is shwarma spit-grilled-style pork, and the taco is topped with onions, cilantro, pineapple, and more. Do yourself a favor and like the Facebook page--keep your eyes peeled for the next time they offer this special!

    The tacos are relatively small, so when there are no good specials or I'm feeling very hungry, I'll go for the burrito. The pork and sweet potato burrito is excellent. You can combine any meat with sweet potato, which makes it doubly awesome (chicken and sweet potato is great as well). The pork is Pueblo style carnitas, and it's incredibly juicy, tender, and flavorful. The pork in the tacos is a bit oversalted, but the flavor is mediated by the sweet potato and other toppings in the burrito. I love that guacamole comes with the other toppings without extra charge, and furthermore, their guacamole is incredible. The Tamarind Jarritos soda is delicious too.

    Honestly, you can't really go wrong here. So just go. You won't regret it :)

    If not for the longer walk, I would definitely come to Honest Tom's over Chipotle for burritos any day. For 8 dollars, you get a burrito that is packed with fresh ingredients and perfectly marinated meats. Not only is the burrito more filling than Chipotle's (I think it is, and it's bursting with flavor--probably because you can't customize the condiments), but the wrap doesn't fall apart as easily either (they put the closing flap on the heat to seal the burrito's contents inside).

I definitely plan on coming again soon.
