    I don't usually write reviews with a five star rating, but Erawan Thai deserves this wholeheartedly. I had never tried Thai food until this trip, and I have to say: I LOVE IT. I'm an extremely picky eater and have shied away from anything with an excess of veggies my whole life, so finding an exotic eatery that was willing to accommodate such a picky palate was a godsend.

    First and foremost, the exterior is very classy and inviting, The interior is surprisingly bigger and more spacious than you would expect. It's great for large parties as well as for dates. All of the staff was extremely friendly for how busy and hectic it was during dinner rush hour. Without reservation, we were immediately seated. The menu prices are very fair and the portion sizes are very generous. My date and I split the steamed pork dumplings and were very impressed with how quickly the food was presented and the quality. Each of our entrees were large and fresh. It was almost like the noodles were made right before they prepared our dishes. I got the Drunken Noodles sans vegetables plus beef and a spice increase to level two and was more than satisfied. As a self-proclaimed spicy queen, even I was surprised at how spicy a level two out of three spice increase would be. Though tolerable, I probably would stick with level one for comforts sake in the future. My dates Pad Thai was everything he hoped it would be. He managed to finish the dish while I only managed half of mine. I highly recommend a visit here, we will DEFINITELY be returning.

    p.s. They offer free delivery within three blocks of the restaurant and they have coupons on their website!

    The food and prices here are decent.  Ordered the green curry shrimp, which was pretty good overall, although I was a bit surprised at how spicy it came out.   However they could definitely use some work in service training for the wait staff.

    We wanted to utilize a yelp check-in for a free appetizer for our party of 6.  The waiter was reluctant to let us order it, despite it being common practice to order an appetizer before deciding on main courses.  She said we had to decide on our main courses first because they needed to see how much it came out to.  Apparently, she wasn't convinced that 6 people would be able to order over $50 of food to qualify for the free appetizer.  After explaining that to her that we would definitely order more than $50, she finally allowed us to order the appetizer.

    Then there was the issue of splitting the check.  She first told us the rule that the minimum order for using a credit card was $15.  Then there was another rule that there couldn't be more than 3 credit cards per table.  We had order 7 entrees, 2 drinks, and 1 dessert at this point, and among 5 credit cards, each card was being charged anywhere from $15.50 to $50.  She didn't like the way we split the bill and asked us to redo it, to satisfy the rule of the 3 credit cards per table.  We asked her if she could talk to her manager to accommodate the 5 credit cards.  She refused, and after we asked her again, explaining that several people did not carry enough cash, she finally reluctantly split the check for us.  

    On top of this, they added an automatic 20% gratuity onto our bill, which I thought was pretty steep given the type of restaurant and quality of the service.  I assumed the standard practice would be 18% for parties of 6 or more.
