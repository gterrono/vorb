    Try the scrambled eggs with corned beef - excellent portion of tangy, tender slices (not chunks) intermingled with fluffy eggs.  Any corned beef fan would REALLY appreciate this dish!  The corn muffin was also quite good.

    My bride got the biscuit sandwich with veggie sausage.  She loved the biscuit and liked the sausage (crispy exterior with mild peppery taste that was pretty close to the real thing).  Fruit side dish was a bit small for the additional $1.75 cost but it was all very fresh and delicious.

    Solid 4 star breakfast overall.  The corned beef alone makes the trip worth it even if you have a $8ish one-way cab fare like we did (from Hampton Inn near the convention center).  They were very busy, but we got a table for 2 right away on Easter Sunday morning between 8:30a and 9:00a.

    Side note: Skip the standard diner coffee at Honey's and get your caffeine at Cafe la Moude instead!  It's excellent and is literally a 15 second walk down the street.

    Last months (this was a draft for a bit of time) experience at Honey's left such a sour taste in my mouth that I just can't seem to shake. I guess this means it's time for a review...

    So Honey's is known for their brunch, and don't get my wrong, it's a tasty brunch but it's nothing I would call "out of this world." My mom's Jewish cooking is much better and when it comes to a place that offers so many Jewish options (lakes,brisket, and matzo ball soup) I expected better. But if it hadn't have been for this recent outing, I'd probably cut Honey's some slack and give them the 4 star rating in regards to their brunch. Many of their omelettes are definitely above average, and their ambiance is fun and rustic.

    But now on to today and my horrible experience. It was around 1pm and my boyfriend and I decided to spend some time in Northern Niberties and since Honey's is a BYOB we thought mimosas would be a lot of fun. So we bought a bottle of champagne and headed over. Well we ended up waiting a half hour before we were seated, but that was to be expected since Honey's can get quite busy on the weekends. Once we were sat, it took the waitress a good 10 minutes to come over to our table. She seemed aloof and distant and when we told her we might be in the mood for mimosa's she said "don't you worry, I've got just the thing" and left to go get orange juice. There are two problems with this:

       1.My boyfriend doesn't like pulp in his OJ  (and there was tons of it) so I was just about to ask her when she left
       2. I assumed the OJ was complimentary because she had kept us waiting for so long and she didn't ask what size we wanted and clearly I saw there was a small and a large

    But whatever, I had my mimosa and my boyfriend had his champagne (still would give it 4 stars)  

    So when she finally showed up to take our order, I was no longer in the mood for the go-to breakfast food (omlette, eggs, etc...) and decided to try their lunch options. I ordered the grilled chicken breast sandwich and my boyfriend got the brisket sandwich.

    Let's just say I'd imagine Applebees' chicken sandwich is better. It was cold and bland and I was extremely disappointed. I actually remember trying to convince myself it was better because of the enjoyable brunches I've had here in the past. But there was no way around it, it was a terrible excuse for a sandwich..

    OK now the Jew in me is coming out, that brisket sandwich should not be calling itself a brisket anything. I'm not expecting my mom's brisket, but let's be reasonable,  It didn't even taste like brisket. My boyfriend has never had brisket before but even he could tell that it didn't taste right.

    All in all, the experience was pretty awful. Poor waitress coupled with bland food makes for a bad brunch. Over and over again I found myself saying "Their brunch is really good, I swear this isn't how they usually are." But when you have to convince yourself of that, something is wrong. The flighty waitress who never seemed to be around when I needed her, the OJ, and that brisket just ruined it for me. I don't think I'll return anytime soon. There are plenty of haunts in No Libs that  I'll still frequent. Peace out Honey's.

    In town visiting for the day, and the decision (based on other Yelp reviews) to have brunch at Honey's was made.

    First and foremost, this is absolutely a Hipster dive, so if you have an aversion to the casually ironic, stay away...it will only get your blood up and probably dull the taste buds just a little bit.

    Anyway, looking at their menu, it is clear that there is a great amount of thought placed on taste and variety, two things that absolutely make it hard to make a choice when it's your first and probably only chance to sample the offerings.

    I settled on the Challah French Toast with Home Fries and their locally-purchased bacon. Add to that an absolutely delicious fresh-squeezed Limeaid that more than hit the spot.

    First and foremost, major props for serving hot maple syrup with their pancakes and french toast, that is a special touch that you don't really get most other places. Add to that the fact that they gave a generous amount, guaranteeing that the maple flavor is absolutely present. I dug that.

    The Challah French Toast was light, airy and very delicious. Three slices of this thick bread are more than enough to satisfy even the hungriest of diner. Very good choice here.

    The Home Fries, made with peppers and onions were just as delicious, although I wish the portion was slightly larger. $2.50 for a modest saucer is a bit much; I would have happily paid an extra buck for a larger portion.

    But by far, the absolute best part of the meal was the Lancaster County Country Bacon, which without fillers and emulsifiers, made for an excellent slice of bacony heaven. Cooked just right, with a good amount of thickness, it was a highlight of the meal.

    Must admit that I wasn't a fan of the Cash Only rule, but as the ATM located in the shop had a relatively low fee ($1.50), I'm not going to let it ding the overall experience.

    One last thing: I tried a bit of the Potato Latke, and I must say...I wasn't nearly as impressed as everyone else here seems to be. It was on the dry side, and not as savory as I would have liked. If you have to choose between the Latke and the Home Fries, absolutely go for the Home Fries.

    I doubt I will have a chance to visit Honey's again, but if you are in the area, I believe it's definitely worth giving it a shot.
