    Yes, it's a chain.  Yes, I usually avoid chains.  But if you're looking for a solid "casual dining" restaurant, this is a solid choice.

    First off, who doesn't love Maggiano's Little Italy?!  No one that I know!

    My boyfriend and I decided to dine at Maggiano's on our weekend trip to Philly because 1.) good food, 2.) there isn't one in New York City, and 3) it was right across from our hotel.

    We went around 7:30pm, and there was about a 20 minute wait.  Lucky for us there were some tables open in the bar area and we didn't mind eating there, especially because the Final 4 was on and we could watch the game!

    We had to wait about 10 minutes before our waitress came by, and then her visits were few and far between.  Yes, the restaurant was crowded, but it seemed like she was slacking a little.  I mean, maybe they were short-staffed, I don't know.  

    Our food did end up coming out quickly, and it was delicious, as always.  I always order the Mushroom Ravioli and it's to die for, SO decadent, and yummy!  My boyfriend had the Eggplant Parm and he said he liked it a lot.  It kind of seemed more like Eggplant Lasagna, because there was no side of pasta and it was kind of stacked like lasagna, but it was good.

    I did notice that some of the other tables around us were getting really bad service.  The couple sitting next to us waited like 20 minutes before the waitress greeted them, brought them water, and took their order.  They were expressing their annoyance and almost left.  I hope it's not always like this, but who knows.

    All in all, a good dinner and the same kind of experience that always coincides with Maggiano's.

    You want to eat?  I mean, really eat?  Go to any Maggiano's and you definitely will.  I visited the Maggiano's in Philly this past weekend because my husband and I were there for the Philadelphia Flower Show.  We met up with my brother and sis-in-law, who had never been to Maggiano's before (they live in New Jersey, where there are many authentic Italian restaurants, so they know Italian).  I have been to the Maggiano's in Tysons Corner, Virginia, so I knew I would enjoy it.  Was hoping they would too.

    Well, no disappointments here!  Our server was attentive and friendly and we got along with her splendidly.  We had a reservation, so we only had to wait a few minutes rather than the hour and ten minutes for people walking in the door.  We ordered "family style" which is great if everyone can agree.  They have three "levels" of family style - each one progressively more expensive but entitling one to more delicious dishes.  We chose the middle-priced version, $27.95 per person.  Incredibly, we all managed to agree on two appetizers, two salads, four entrees, and two desserts.  The appetizers we chose were the breaded mozzarella and mushroom ravioli.  Both were excellent.  We all ordered a variety of chicken for our entrees (marsala, parmesan, saltimbocca, piccata).  I especially enjoyed the saltimbocca but they were all good.  For dessert, tiramisu and creme brulee.  All I can say to that is wow.  And believe it or not, you can get additional plates of food if you want seconds (or thirds....or fourths).  It's a pay one price, eat all you want kind of deal.  And....they let you take home leftovers!

    My Italian sister-in-law agreed that the food was great, and my brother (who just likes to eat) loved everything.  I think our favorites were the appetizers - the mushroom ravioli was heavenly.  It was a relaxing evening and a great start to our weekend in Philly.
