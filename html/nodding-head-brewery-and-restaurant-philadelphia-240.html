    I'm not sure where the snobbery is coming from among these many reviews. Based on the beer alone, this place is at least 4 stars in quality. That's not to mention the wonderful aesthetics of this place-- the rustic wood interior and dim lighting makes it feel like a tavern from the 1800's. I'm a real sucker for that stuff.

    My friends and I tried several of their brews as we were in quite the celebration mood. I can't recall the names but I know I tried a dark IPA, a dark ale, and something sweet, somewhat resembling a jeremiah. I'm quite the beer snob and I will say that their home brewed beer is excellent. If you don't think it's good, I implore you to name a better place in the district for real micro brews.

    Then there was the food. It's DIFFERENT and it's GOOD. The mussels are a killer deal for about 10 bucks. Mine came with a unique sort of seasoning (i think it was the moroccan?) that resembled genuine indian style curry flavor with a hint of sweet. Very unique flavors for a very savory little treat. And I love the fact that it comes in a big metal pot. The fries were excellent and were served with some sort of creamy Aioli sauce. I can't complain about the food at all. Our waiter was also pretty friendly and helpful with suggestions about the menu. I WOULD come back if I ever found myself in philly again.

    Continuing my beer tourism I saw this place right next to where I had dinner and figured, a brewery in downtown-ish Philadelphia, that's a must try for a West Coaster like me.

    My main goal and review here is for their beer selection, which I have to say is good and unique. Ordering the flight was perfect cause it gave me a taste of everything on their menu.

    Grog - This full flavored brown ale was really nice with some coffee overtones. It had a bit of nut to it and went down really well.

    Ich Bin Ein Berliner Weisse - This drink was tart but crisp and smooth, not dry. As I found out later it is a unique style of beer that's not often found, I was quite impressed with it. The tartness was smoothed out by adding woodruff syrup as tradition calls. Oddly the syrup on its own is a bit tart, but mixed in with the beer, gave it light sweetness. I was a big fan of this one.

    Picnic - A cherry ale, it had a great aroma to it and a gorgeous ruby color. The flavor was a sharp tart, which was nice, I think a sweet cherry flavor would have ruined it.

    Brotherly Suds Volume 3 - This dark amber colored beer didn't have much of an aroma that I could discern. It had a good malty flavor and a bit of a bitter after taste, but not so much that it 'bit' you like an IPA.

    Oakeller Pilsner - I'm not a fan of pilsners usually and this one didn't deviate, it had a bit of a bite in the after taste, as I find most pilsners do. To me it had the flavorings that a pilsner should, so if you're a fan you'd probably like this one.

    Bullseye Bitter - This hoppy beer had a unique earthy/dirt-like flavor just as they described. Its unique but not bad. I'd have this one with a full meal, like steak, medium rare.

    Bill Payer Ale - This one I couldn't find on their website, but it had a great hoppy aroma to it and great hoppy flavor. I would definitely drink this one again.

    Rascal - This brown ale, I expected to be a bit on the sweeter side, but was greeted with a familiar hoppy flavor. It had a solid flavor to it and rounded out my experience.

    *** Edit 2012 06 12

    We did order the Jerk Mussels, a very goodchoice. They were plump and juicy and the sauce/stew was very good. Definitely had me nodding in agreement.

    Overall, I like the beer selection they offered. I didn't find any that I would say are must have, though I highly recommend the Berliner Weisse... very unique. A good experience but didn't find that beer that would make me come back next time I'm around; but a good stop to make for sure.

    I love beer, so it makes sense for me to be a fan of Nodding Head.  It's a great place to go any time of year.  Beers are solid, as is the food; the french dip sandwich is my favorite.  There are plenty of booths to cram into with a group, and a few high tops and bar seats scattered about.  One thing I like is that in the warm months, they open the windows so you can look out over Sansom St. and get fresh air while you enjoy your beer.  Probably the best part is that if it's not too crowded you can play darts!  The second best part is that Nodding Head reminds me of several bars/pubs I went to in Belgium.  That leaves any beer connoisseur with a warm fuzzy feeling.

    The only cons are that sometimes it does get very crowded, and depending what's on tap, you may not find a beer that you love.  I am not a tremendous fan of IPAs or very hoppy beers, and there have definitely been times I went to Nodding Head and found a significant inequality in the styles of beer represented.  The solution for that is to look online and see what they have on tap before going.
