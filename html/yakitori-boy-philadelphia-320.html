    So I come here at least once every time I come back to Philly when I'm away. It has two floors: the first level is its restaurant and the second is its lounge + karaoke rooms. Both levels have a bar. It's a great place to hang out with friends for a dinner or for a night to have fun at the lounge.

    Entertainment
    This is the reason for me giving it the 4/5 rating. This is one of the only few places in center city Philly that provides groups of parties their own private karaoke rooms. The prices are very affordable, especially if a group uses the free room promotion (buy enough drinks/food that cover double the hourly fee; use to be just the same amount, but not anymore). If you don't want to book a private room, the lounge is always fun. There is always people out singing their hearts out, which makes it super fun and hilarious. The con to entertainment, though, is that now it gets so packed that you have to book a table or room during the weekends, or you won't be able to get up there. Bar selection is decent; they have many drinks and house drinks as well. Definitely try the Tokyo Tea; a few of these can get you pretty tipsy.

    Food
    The food is pretty good. The prices vary from inexpensive to expensive depending on what you want to order. The daily specials they have are somewhat cheap, but selections are very limited. I usually come on Sushi or Skewer nights, because they are mad cheap. I love the quail egg, chicken, chicken and scallion, and the pork sausage skewers. As for taste, it's not the best I've eaten around in terms of Japanese food, but the selection is huge. You can also order food from both levels.

    Service
    Hosting is great and.. controlling? If you would like to go upstairs, they are really thorough with you in restaurant procedures and policies. Definitely great at the lower level restaurant. They come just enough so they don't really bother you, but are still attentive of you. As for upstairs, it tends to be confusing. If you're at the lounge and want food, you sometimes have no idea who your server is and you just have to approach staff. Sometimes, they're no where to be found and you might just have to approach a bouncer or the person controlling the lounge karaoke. Bar staff is sometimes short, and you really have to push through to grab drinks. Having your own room is good, since there is a call button. If it gets busy though, they might take forever to come. Different servers take care of you too, so be sure not to order the same thing twice. Making reservations is also very thorough; they'd like to call you twice

    Recommendations: Order From Food Specials, Make Reservations For Upstairs Prior
    Pros: Great atmosphere, very fun, friendly people
    Cons: Somewhat expensive for things, crowded on weekends

    Yakitori Boy is a lot of fun.  This was the (planned) last stop on my sister's birthday night.  If you read some of my other reviews you'll know how the rest of the night went.  Get on that! I'd also been here once before with a larger party.

    Yakitori Boy is actually so much fun that we tried to get another hour added onto the room we had, but sadly it was already booked.  In my opinion this place is all about the private rooms.  You can get rooms of varying sizes and I was shocked by how cheap they are.  For a six person room it's only $30 an hour on weekends and $15 an hour on weekdays.  Split that between a few people and you could have a room all night without breaking the bank.

    The song selection is relatively easy to figure out and as people start drinking more and more they loosen up and become increasingly entertaining to watch.  In each room you get a long wrap around couch with a table in front and an interactive screen on the front wall.  There is a button by the door that you press for service which makes you feel about as VIP as possible.

    That said, things here aren't cheap, but who the heck would expect them to be?   There are some really delicious little bites and if you only get a few you should be fine, or if you have enough people to split the costs.  The chicken meatballs were great, the beef skewers were tasty, and there was a lot of other things on the menu I'd like to try sometime.

    There are cons of course.  It gets crowded because it's basically a club.   The main floor can be congested, but there's something hilarious about a room fool of people crowded around a bar signing "Buddy Holly" badly.  Like I said the drinks and food are pricey, but good, and it's to be expected.  They have an impressive song library, but they're definitely missing some hits that I was shocked not to find.  Don't be surprised to click on an artists and not find your favorite song.   The system itself is glitchy to navigate.  The search has issues finding songs and you're generally forced to scroll around till you find what you want.

    If the system worked better and they had more of the real music videos I'd give this place 5 stars.  But it's definitely fun, surprisingly reasonable to rent a room, and has some tasty offerings.

    I'm a big fan of Yakitori Boy. I've been here both for dining as well as in the private karaoke rooms upstairs.

    PRIVATE EXPERIENCE:

    Several of my friends have had parties here and I couldn't be happier with the experience. Our server has always checked in regularly, and food and drinks were bought in a very timely fashion. We always have one person come 10 minutes in early in order to set up a playlist for the music. I like that the rooms are truly soundproof and you can make as much noise as you want. They have rooms that can accommodate (I believe) up to 20 peole. It's always been clean and quite spacious considering.

    DINING:

    I've been here for dinner many times and I've always been pleased with the service as well as the quick service with our food. As always the menu prices are very reasonable and anything I've ever ordered has been fairly tasty. Their regular drink menu isn't too extensive but all the drink are well made. The Tokyo Teas here are really good.

    HAPPY HOUR:

    Yakitori's Boy is one of my favorite spots downtown for happy hour. They give you several options and they're all solid choices that I often order when I'm just here for dinner. Some favorites are the spring rolls, calamari and shumai or gyoza. My favorite happy hour drink is the Samurai tini, which is a lemony ginger type drink. If you're a big fan of ginger this is the way to go! The sake-tini here well is equally delicious and the cucumber flavor is very refreshing.

    My only complaint is if you come here with a party of 2 you're more than likely going to be sat over in the corner, and I feel like these tables are really just too close together with an extreme lack of privacy. Other than that, I really enjoy coming to Yakitori Boy. The waiters are also always very friendly and willing to give info or their food recommendations.     My rating for Yakitori Boy remains the same at 3.5 stars, but since I was just here for skewer night (which is on Tuesdays), I'll include more details about what I've tried:

    My fave was the Bacon & Quail Egg skewer. Bacon was crispy and the egg was soft and warm -- delicious!

    Other skewers I've enjoyed included the Chicken, Chicken gizzard, Chicken heart, Chicken & Scallion, Eggplant, which were all nicely grilled.  Asparagus was nothing special, but okay for something different and non-meaty.

    Also enjoyed the Tako Yaki which are soft flour-batter balls with a bit of octopus in the middle, topped with mayo, sauce and bonito flakes. I don't know where else to get takoyaki in Philly and these are pretty good for anyone who misses them.  It's cool to watch the bonito flakes waving around in the steam rising off the takoyaki -- they almost look like they're alive (they're not).  

    I've also tried the Okonomi Yaki (seafood pancake) which was not bad.

    Stuff I wouldn't want to order again: Ginkgo nut skewer (not bad, but not very flavourful and the gingko just didn't gain anything from the yakitori treatment), Pork Belly skewer (too hard and overcooked but okay flavour), Chicken Meatball, Chicken liver (very strong liver flavour).  Also didn't love the Shishamo (fish).  We also tried a few sushi rolls that I probably wouldn't order again.  The spicy crunchy tuna and spicy crunchy salmon maki weren't super fresh and seemed a bit soft/soggy in texture.

    Yakitori Boy's still a good place to go when you're in the mood for izakaya.
