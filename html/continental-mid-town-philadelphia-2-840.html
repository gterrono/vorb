    Place is dope. Steven Starr restaurant and the experience doesn't live his namesake down.

    Great for a one-on-one with a friend as well as with a large group of 6+.  The atmosphere is hip and fun yet endearingly intimate. The service has never disappointed me and I'm usually perceptive of things like that.

    The menu itself is worth making a visit for, as the dishes are served "tapas" style and feature items from various places around the world. Everything I've had has been delicious ranging from the calamari salad or quinoa salad to the LOBSTER MAC N CHEESE (A MUST) to the French Onion Soup Dumplings to Sea Bass to Pad Thai. I just don't think there is much that this kitchen can't cook well.  I mean I could go on but you're better off just showing up and trying it than seeing me ramble. Only thing that did turn me off ever so slightly was an over-cooked pork rib that was still seasoned and garnished nicely.

    A great spot to drop in every now and then with a drink list that isn't too bad, although my friend ordered a Pimm's Cup recently that underwhelmed. I don't think all their drinks and wines are necessarily the best, but most of them are and it's definitely a nice bar experience as well with atmosphere of the 2nd floor level. Go with friend's and enjoy.

    Call me strange, but I like the swinging basket chairs. You just have to balance yourself on your toes so you won't sway from side to side while eating.

    On Saturday (09/13), I caught up with a good friend of mine over brunch at this place. I've been here a few times in the past when I used to live in Center City, and I always thought the food was pretty good. The service? Meh, kinda hit or miss, but no one's ever been rude or sloppy, so I tend to give The Continental a pass here. That being said, I have subtracted one star for our server's performance. She wasn't terrible, and she was pleasant, but she didn't offer syrup for my friend's pancakes or water alongside our drinks. Also, she didn't actually serve us our meals, so she must have been running in the weeds.

    For our drinks, I decided to get The Socialite (champagne mixed with blueberry and lime juice), and my friend ordered the Space Age Mimosa (standard mimosa with sprinkles of Tang lining the edge of the glass). I liked the blend of sweet with tart flavors in my drink, although I had to sip it slowly because it was a little strong.

    For our main courses, I had the smoked salmon hash with poached eggs, and my friend ordered pancakes topped with bananas and berries, and a side of crisp bacon. I had the hash once before, about two years ago, but this time it was even better. The smoked salmon was thinly shredded and mixed perfectly with the finely chopped roasted potatoes and sauteed onions, and the eggs were also done well (they weren't gummy or hard). My friend loved her pancakes, but was a bit bummed she had to get up and take another table's syrup.

    Overall, it was another solid meal at The Continental. A few hiccups in the service, but the food more than makes up for it.

    I don't know if this lowers the credibility of my review when I write this, but I had no idea that this was a Steven Starr restaurant until about a week after I ate there.

    Anyway, I walked into the restaurant one regular Saturday afternoon with my trusted eating companion and we were seated on the top level.     I went here on Saturday night on 9/27. I believe we couldn't set reservations for 2 people, however, knowing that we would be going a little later at night, around 9 pm, we expected to be seated right away. We had to wait about 10 minutes which gave us enough time to head upstairs to the bar to order drinks. I ordered the Pimm's Cup and the lady order the Twizzler martini. Both drinks were amazing and tasted great. The martini was great looking and had a great taste. A little on the sugary side but that was expected. It had a rolled up Twizzler on the side of the martini glass as a garnish which was cool. The Pimm's Cup #1 was great. Simple and easy drink done to perfection.

    Now to the dining experience. We were seated by near the front door. There's a lot of action going on where we were seated. Many people coming in and out of the restaurant as well as waiters/waitresses heading to the hostess stand.

    It was my first time at this restaurant and the ambiance/decor was great. The chandelier in the middle of the restaurant is amazing looking. The hanging wicker chairs are nice looking as well and add a different element to the place. The noise level is pretty high so there are some difficulties hearing your party talk or the waitress/waiter.

    We ordered the grilled octopus salad, Thai chicken lettuce wrap, Korean pork tacos, and lobster mac and cheese. The first three items were amazing. The grilled octopus salad was great. It had spinach, roasted red peppers and a nice vinaigrette dressing that tied everything together well. The Thai chicken lettuce wrap was great as well. It was a large portion. The peanut or almond sauce that came with it was one of the best sauces I've ever had. I wish they could bottle it and sell it. The Korean pork tacos were good. They have a nice kick to them at the end so if you do not like a little spice I recommend staying away from them.

    The last dish and worst dish of the night was the lobster mac and cheese. The dish is severely over-salted. I could not taste the lobster or any other ingredient in the dish except salt. It was so salty to the point that we had to stop eating it after only a few bites. I'm not sure if we maybe received a bad batch or what, but I would not try that dish again.

    However, except the mac and cheese dish, everything else was great. The service was excellent, the food was served quickly and hot, and the experience was definitely a positive. The food prices weren't sky high so you can definitely enjoy a nice dinner in a great spot at a reasonably expected price.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
