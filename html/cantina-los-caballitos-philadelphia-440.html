    This place is solid if you're in the neighborhood. For us, the lower prices (compared to El Vez, say) isn't worth the cab ride unless we're already nearby or meeting people in the area. But I imagine if I lived within what can be considered walking distance, I would be here more often.

    The blood orange margarita is good. Strong, and pleasant flavors.

    The nachos app was just okay. Not the most mind-blowing nachos/loaded chips I've had in the city (that is shared by El Vez and, surprisingly, The Field House - kettle chips twist FTW!), but also not as frightfully bad as this one encounter in the 'burbs where they gave us cooked hamburger meat as a topping (as a former Southern Californian, trust me when I say this was a frightening take on nachos!)

    The chorizo burrito and shrimp tacos were both nicely done. The strange thing is you have to ask for cheese, the tacos only come with lettuce and tomatoes, but the waitress alerted us to this anomaly during the ordering process so no big deal.

    I'll keep this place in mind if I wander into the neighborhood in the future as I'm sure I will.

    I am granting an extra star here for sheer nostalgia, but this place is solidly three stars from an objective standpoint. I can't believe I've never reviewed Cantina! Back when I was a fresh young ingenue in the city, Cantina was my first regular foray to the Avenue, and proper South Philly. It was the summer of 2008, and there wasn't much down on the Avenue in those days. I remember it always felt like it took forever to walk there, and that we passed little of interest. Now, the entire length from Broad/Snyder all the way up to the Italian Market is bustling at every hour of the night and day. It's insane how much this street has changed.

    Anywho... I stopped coming to Cantina regularly a few years ago when it seemed the pitchers of margaritas were getting ever weaker, and the always-crappy service ever more terrible. Their food had never been anything to write home about in my book (what is with the plain white rice, guys? I don't get it) but the chips and salsa are always decent and the goat burrito is pretty darn tasty. I was showing a few out-of-town friends around the area when we wanted some lunch with drinks, which was slim pickin's on a post-Thanksgiving Saturday, but Cantina was open so there we went.

    My guests were delighted, and I was pleasantly lulled by the warm glow of nostalgia. It's been at least two years since I've been, and the goat burrito tasted exactly the same. So did the plain white rice. WTF, people? All the cheese was at the bottom of my burrito, to the extent I thought they had forgotten it for most of my eating experience. I guess if I had started at the other end, it wouldn't have been at the bottom. In any event, I ate the whole thing and was pretty ashamed about it. Guests also ordered burritos, a goat and a breakfast with chorizo, and there was much contentment. I got a special margarita with guava and cointreau in it. It was tasty and plenty strong.

    That habanero hot sauce on the table is spicy, be careful. But it's also damn delicious. Our server appeared to be high on copious amount of cocaine and/or meth. It was kind of entertaining. Ah, Cantina. You haven't changed much.
