    This is the go-to place for burritos in Market East. It has the consistent quality of a Qdoda or a Chipotle but to me the food is infinitely better, fresher, and more abundant. The burritos at this spot are generously stuffed with lots of yum. Nowadays I don't eat meat so the veggie burrito is where it's at for me but I had the chicken back in my carnivorous days and it was awesome with just the right amount of heat.

    Be sure to get here right before the lunch rush - 11:45 seems to do it -- or you'll definitely be greeted at the door, by the line. It goes quick but I prefer to avoid the hassle. There isn't much seating so save this one for a day where you don't mind hoofing it over to one of the many green spaces in the area and pulling up a patch of grass.

    Happy burrito-ing!

    Delicious. People who say Santa Fe Burrito is better than this place are insane. El Fuego is dangerously close to where I work (my co-workers are all obsessed), and while I typically hold out all week, by Friday I usually break down and march over for some burrito bowl goodness.

    Yes, the line is out of control on a sunny day during lunch hour. But they are seriously a well oiled machine... the only thing that slows them down is the people who come in with 10 custom orders for their entire office. And who likes those people, anyway? They're the worst.  

    Now, the food... it's Tex Mex, but it's fresh and flavorful. The rice has a nice tang from cilantro and lime. The beans are seasoned. The chicken....mmmm. I've never gotten the chorizo, it's just too greasy for my taste, but the beef is also very yummy. I typically get a burrito bowl and sometimes I'll have them line the bottom with lettuce to make a sort of burrito salad. My favorite thing is the three salsas... the medium salsa verde and the red spciy are both delightful...combining both is the way to go.

    My ONLY complaint would be cost... a burrito bowl with guac plus a drink runs me 11 or 12 bucks. Yikes.

    I've only ever eaten in once because it was pouring outside, but during the day it gets crazy loud and crowded so it's less than ideal. Most importantly while I ALWAYS overstuff myself I never get a stomach ache from their food, which I find to be the case with so many places like this. And it's true, El Fuego is pretty similar to Chipotle, but in my opinion it definitely tastes more homemade and fresh.

    I'm not as much of a maniac of burritos as many people are lately, but once in awhile I just get a hankering for gooey cheese, meat, sour cream and some guac. I work near many burrito options and upon a few recommendations, I finally tried out El Fuego.

    I feel in El Fuego like I do when I go to most burrito places during my lunch hour: that I'm in the Soup Nazi episode of Seinfeld. I feel that even moreso in El Fuego because the lines are so much longer than they are at Chipotle and Qdobo plus the music playing is loud so there's a little pressure there to move along. When I get into line I know I want a chorizo or beef burrito, but when it gets to be my turn to let the burrito maker know what I want as toppings & fillings, I get a little frozen. Many choices and I feel like I'll be holding up the line if I dawdle making my choice. Eventually I do, but then sometimes after I get back to eat, I wish I'd filled my burrito with black beans instead of pinto and vice versa.

    Anyway, I have now been to El Fuego twice and have tried the chorizo and the spicy beef, both which were great. I always get the spiciest salsa, which goes so nicely with all the fillings. The ingredients here are always really good and fresh. I loved, loved, loved the spicy beef and will definitely be getting that again. Whenever I get a burrito, I know that that's going to be pretty much all I'll eat that day. So yes, out of the 4 burrito places near my work, I rank El Fuego as #1, despite the Soup Nazi-feeling line.

    There's a certain amount of cliche that must be digested with every Tex Mex burrito or taco. It's sort of a bland, salty taste that passes for overseasoning while it's really your psyche rebelling at the thought of even more fake Mexican food in your body.

    Sadly, El Fuego is no exception. It's the same ingredients, the same process, the same formula as your Moe's, Chipotle, Qdoba. Let's set aside my begruntled, waistline-growing, Tex-Mex glutton self and acknowledge El Fuego for it's contribution to the Center City fare.

    The price is right, and the atmosphere is inviting, no-frills. It's about the food here, and even if it won't change your worldview, it's always refreshing to eat somewhere that's more interested in their food than their aesthetic (a less and less prevalent approach). The chicken is stand-out, the steak is fine, and the chorizo could be good (however, when I had it, it was cold).

    My only serious complaint - what almost made me give it a 3-star review - is that the food on the assembly line should be kept warmer. That minor point aside, El Fuego is a good place to grab a quick meal on Walnut (a relatively unrivaled option in that area, unless you're looking to spend quite a bit more).
