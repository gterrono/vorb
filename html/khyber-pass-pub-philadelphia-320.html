    Listed in Best Sandwiches

    The great thing about working in center city Philadelphia is you can try a new place almost every day for lunch.  Looking at the menu beforehand, what got my attention was the 3 different kinds of BBQ sauce.  Now, this is not known as a BBQ restaurant, but what it offers some "true" BBQ places lack-- and that's multiple kinds of BBQ sauces.  Living in Philadelphia, the city is by no means known for it's BBQ.  Most people in the northeast part of the country don't realize how many different types there are.  The most common kind that people know is the tomato based Kansas City kind.  Although it's good, I need variety in my life!  I was very happy to see the Khyber Pass Pub offered a KC, North Carolina (vinegar-based), and South Carolina (mustard- based).  I went with the North Carolina pulled pork sandwich and it was delicious.  My only complaint was that the cole slaw was served on the sandwich.  Put it on the side so I have the option!  What really sealed it for me was the sauce.  The North Carolina sauce is a very thin kind that packs a lot of flavor.  Pulled pork sandwiches are my personal standard for how good a place does BBQ and they passed.  I got the cheddar fries as my side and regretted that.  The fries themselves were good with some skin on, but the cheese didn't enhance them.  I prefer a cheese whiz cheese fries.   For a place that is mostly known for their beer selection, the food is very good.  I'll certainly be coming back for more.

    Date night- a Wednesday- a 12 degree Wednesday... and I want food... and beer... and minimal lettuce...

    The Khyber called and we listened!  I really hadn't been by for food since they switched from a dive bar (literally thought I could fall through the floor), to a kinda funky, layed back bar on the bayou.  I was pleased to see that the renovations made the place nice (and structurally sound), but not TOO nice... because that would have just felt fake.

    We sat in a cozy little table/booth combo, and were greeted promptly by our waiter for our drink order.  The 20 beers on draught was AWESOME.  I'm a sucker for PBC... and cider... so i was pleased to learn that PBC makes cider!  I went for that.  It was delish, and a solid pour.  I've been getting hit with pints with a few centimeters of beer missing- a real life tragedy!

    For dinner, I ordered the vegan fried chicken po boy with a side of fried okra.  As I type this i realize that's a lot of fried food... showing how cold it really was out... and how badly i wanted something soul-satisfying!  My boyfriend went for the 1/2 chicken with a side of mac and cheese, and cole slaw.  

    We chatted, enjoyed our beers, and our food came out in what I would consider a perfect turnaround time.  

    I dug into my very first EVER po-boy since they are pretty much never vegetarian, and let me tell you... Po Boys rock!  The chick'n was tasty and juicy and the bread was crispy on the outside, and soft on the inside.  The sandwich as a whole was HUGE.  It was a really pleasant experience.  I felt lucky!  Truly lucky!  Then I moved on to the orka... and felt a lot less lucky.  They used some spice... I couldn't quite put my finger on it... but the fried okra (and a generous portion of fried okra I should mention) just didn't taste that great!  The breading was crispy and golden brown.  The dipping sauce had the same seasoning that i just didn't care for.  I barely ate them, and had major orderers remorse.  I'm usually quite an okra fan too, so this was really a disappointment.  My boyfriend enjoyed his 1/2 bbq chicken, and couldn't finish it, which i've never seen out of him before!  He said it was good... but not amazing.  His reaction was the same towards the cole slaw.  The mac and cheese, however, was a game changer.  He was elated with that, and after a bite, I could understand why.  It was pretty much perfection!  Oooodles of creamy cheezy goodness, browned on the top, served warm... just fab!  

    For a bar that happens to serve food, we had a really positive experience at the bayou.  Our check for 2 with 2 drinks each and a really big meal was around $50 which i thought was reasonable.  The service was great- our waiter was on top of things!  Just a few dishes need a facelift.

    Also worth mentioning- there are 3 sauces- north/south carolina BBQ sauce, and kansas BBQ sauce.  We decided we liked the vinegar based one the best, but in all honesty, they all could use some improvement.

    Khyber Pass has become one of our go-to spots for dinner out.  They have the look of a dive bar from the outside and the main bar is dark and cramped but the dining section has a fair amount of tables of various sizes.  They have tons of amazing beers on tap at all times.  On the weekends you can sometimes wait a long time to be seated (and they don't take reservations so you have to take your chances) but we've also come in with a party of 8 on a Saturday night and been seated right away so it's not too bad.

    The food is southern barbecue inspired but it gets very creative.  The hushpuppies with molasses butter and pepper jelly are delicious and the sauces really make them a solid choice.  

    We usually skip the apps in favor of diving right into the main courses and sides.  I've gotten the fried green tomato BLT (amazing), the bbq brisket sandwich (so so tender and love the horseradish topping), and the muffaletta (freaking huge!! so much meat and cheese I actually had to take some of it off and eat piece separately.  This sandwich is a BEAST).  

    They also have a special sandwich that's been running lately that is a smoked duck sandwich and it is AMAZING.  The smoked duck is so tender and it comes with cashew butter and pepper jelly which go super well together.  It also comes with a simple veggie slaw which cuts through the richness of the sandwich.  Outstanding special and I understand why it's been on the specials board often over the past few months.

    I always get the mac and cheese as a side and it is a really classic version with bread crumbs on top.  It always comes out steaming hot and I love the contrast of the creamy cheese with the salty meats.  The smoked cheddar fries are good as well but feel a little salty with the options for main courses and get cold fast while you're chugging your meat.  The biscuit is super buttery and delicious.

    The service is usually super attentive and on point but I did have one bad experience.  Two people ordered the duck sandwich in my party, one of whom is  allergic to most tree nuts and asked for hers without the cashew butter to be safe. The server screwed up the plates and gave my friend the one with the cashew butter and she didn't notice until her boyfriend took a bite of hers and said it tasted different than his.  Fortunately she was just being careful and has a very low sensitivity to cashews but if she had a serious allergy her throat would have closed up right there in the restaurant.  The server should have been very careful with that so double check if you make any allergy-related requests!!
