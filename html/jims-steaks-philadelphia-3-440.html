    Kinda anti-climatic when I say the best cheesesteak I ever had is from a place in Philly, but it's true!

    Jim's Steaks is one of those old school place and as soon as you walk in, not only do you smell the steaks and all the food, but you also get a strong sense that there's a lot of history here.  The wall is lined with actors, actresses, celebrities, or public figures' memorabilia and signatures, expressing their love for Jim's.  Pretty cool.

    After waiting in line, proceeded with ordering a cheeseteak with whiz, onions, and peppers.  Nothing too crazy about the quality of the steak (it's actually a little dry) and the toppings, but the whiz totally did it for me.  I never had a cheesesteak with a cheese sauce, and the whiz just makes the whole sandwich tastes so much better.  It moistens up the sandwich considerably and since they spread the whiz on the bottom of the roll, your tongue hits the whiz first before the beef for that dose of cheesy goodness.  I don't think I want my cheesesteak with Provolone anymore.  Give me whiz!

    As many have mentioned, service is just horrible.  We wanted to pay as a group and when we told the guy at the cashier, he immediately rolled his eyes as if paying as a group is so troublesome for him.  Even with that sort of attitude, people still go back.  Heck, I would.

    There's an upstairs area with additional seating, but overall the place isn't really big, especially when there's a lot of tourists like us.  No fountain here for drinks, just in cans, but they do have beer here!  Corona and a cheesesteak?

    Whiz is my new love now.

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
