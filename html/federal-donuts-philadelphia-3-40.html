    Federal Donuts.  Known for fresh donuts and fried chicken.
    Their 2nd shop is now located at the City Center (half a block away from Hotel Palomar) - opened since October 2012.  Score!!

    This review is for the donuts only.  Their donuts are the cakey kind of donuts.  The old fashioned donuts.  YUM!

    They boast 2 main types of donuts:
    1) Fancy Donuts - donuts with icing on top with innovative flavors like maple bacon and french toast
    2) Hot Donuts - donuts with granulated sugar with flavors like strawberry lavender and turkish mocha

    I personally enjoy the hot donuts over the fancy donuts.  The hot donuts are served fresh... cakey but fluffy!  The fancy donuts - the dough had time to settle, so it is not as fluffy after it had some time to cool.

    If you're a fan of old fashioned donuts... this is your kind of place.  Don't walk... RUN, FOREST, RUN!!  I will DEFINITELY be back for their strawberry lavender flavor donut.  YUM!!  

    I would also like to try their fried chicken the next time.  Served with a donut... I suppose it is their version of chicken and waffles but instead chicken and donuts.  

    ENJOY!!

    I've been here twice since the center city location opened a few months ago--once for takeout and once here--and the food was delicious each time. This place might have donuts in the name, but it's the chicken that you need to get.

    The shabazzi dry rub added a nice spice to the chicken, without giving it too much heat. The chili-garlic sauce was great too, and added a nice tang to the crunchy skin. The half order comes with four pieces of chicken (thigh, leg, split breast) and all were moist and liberally coated with the seasoning/sauce. If you get a meal, it also comes with a honey donut and japanese pickles that are good, but not great. However, the chicken more than makes up for it.

    I've never been able to get there in time for a fancy donut, but I've had the hot fresh donut in strawberry-lavender flavor as well as vanilla-spice and both were great. The donuts are made on the spot, so they're still hot and soft, and the sweet spice coating actually pair extremely well with the chicken, since they're not too sweet.

    Definitely worth taking a trip into center city for, just make sure to walk home afterwards.

    Now I don't normally like donuts, but this place has changed my mind. Federal Donuts seems like your good old hipster joint, and yes, it turns out that it's pretty hip. At Federal Donuts, you can get donuts, coffee, and.....fried chicken. Now the fried chicken here isn't your KFC or Church's chicken. It's Korean styled so it's similar to your BonChon and KyoChon. But let's be real here, you're not looking to come here for the chicken or coffee. You're here for the donuts, straight up. The shop itself is pretty small. There are small little individual tables scattered along the wall for people to sit, but usually, they're all taken. I just ended up getting my donuts to go.

    At Federal Donuts, they serve up cake donuts in unique flavors. For $2, you can get a "Fancy Donut" in flavors such as: Double Chocolate, The Nutty Pistachio, Maple-Bacon, Milk Chocolate-Peanut Butter, French Toast, Cherry-Almond. If $2 is too much for you, or you're not in the mood for a fancy donut, then you can opt for the "Hot Fresh" donuts for $1.25 each. With those, the flavors that you can choose from are: Appollonia, Indian Cinnamon, and Vanilla-Lavender.

    For myself, I decided to go with the Nutty Pistachio and the Cherry-Almond. The instant I took a bite into the Nutty Pistachio, I was a true believer. This donut tasted very similar to a pistachio macaroon, and was straight up one of the best donuts that I've had. The cake itself was nice and fluffy and was not at all dry. Now with the Cherry-Almond, I didn't like that one as much. I felt that the cherry glaze was just a little too strong and on the tangy side, but that is my personal taste preference. When I was there, there were only 3 Fancy Donuts for sale. These things sell out, starting from 7am, so if you're really looking for a specific flavor, you should try and come earlier than later.

    Federal Donuts, you are quire amazing. That Nutty Pistachio donut made my day, and if I ever come back to Philly, you will be mine again.

Specialties

Cake donuts in interesting flavors and Korean-style, twice-fried chicken sold by the half and whole bird.  House-roasted coffee available hot or cold-brewed and iced. That's basically it.

History

Established in 2012.

Chef Michael Solomonov and partner Steve Cook of Zahav and Percy Street Barbecue got a wild idea to pair exotic donuts and super-crispy fried chicken. With Bodhi Coffee owners' Bob Logue and Tom Henneman's coffee expertise and professional bigmouth Felicia D'Ambrosio, the team opened the first FedNuts at 1219 S. Second St. in Pennsport, South Philly in 2011.  The Sansom Street shop followed one year later in October 2012.  We have since been cranking out fancy donuts, melt in your mouth hot fresh donuts, and truly badass chicken.  Follow us on Twitter www.Twitter.com/federaldonuts for status updates and fun contests.
