    "At sea, I learned how little a person needs..." Robert Lee Graham

    If you have watched a Rocky or Godfather movie, you've probably gotten a glimpse of this lovely antique ship. Originally named Kurt, after the owner of a shipping company in Germany, it was first launched in 1904. The vessel was utilized to carry goods between Europe, Australia and South America. Kurt was then named Dreadnought and later Moshulu by first lady, Edith Wilson. Throughout its 110 year history, it's been seized, capsized, rebuilt, bankrupt, famed, preserved and now a restaurant resting at Penn's Landing.

    Good for special occasions.

    The interior of the ship is a little disconcerting from an inner-ear perspective. Since you're inside the hull, everything is curved and tilted at angles that may take some getting used to in the first couple of minutes after you arrive. The decor is a little dated, prices are somewhat expensive, but the food is exceptional.

    We started with a beverage at the bar, and our bartender was very kind. His eyes lit up when we asked him about the history of the yacht. I felt bad about being out where folks had to work on the holiday, but the employees seemed to be happy, and there was lots of good energy all around. Be sure to check out the big book with great stories and photos of the people who used to live and work aboard over the years.

Specialties

The Moshulu, the world's oldest and largest four-masted Tall Ship offers unsurpassed views for intimate to extraordinary dining aboard this Philadelphia landmark. .  Enjoy delectable, award-winning contemporary American cuisine by Chef Anthony Bonett using the freshest, local ingredients available from the land and from the sea paired with an extensive wine list hand selected by Sommelier, Dusan Vranic.

History

Established in 2003.

The Moshulu was purchased in 1968 in Naantale, Finland for restoration and conversion into a restaurant. In the fall of 1974, she was towed to Philadelphia and opened as a restaurant on Philadelphia's Penn's Landing in 1975 until she was damaged by fire. In 1994, the Moshulu was purchased by HMS Ventures, Inc and restored in the style of a turn-of-the-century luxury liner. The Moshulu was re-christened by Philadelphia Mayor Ed Rendell on July 24, 1996. In 2002, well known restauranteur, Martin Grims, purchased the ship as she is today.
