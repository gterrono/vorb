    I can only review the dol sot bibimbap because it's so good that that's all I ever get. It's always served in a sizzling hot pot and the portion size is quite generous - it used to be a bit bigger, but I noticed a few months ago they shrunk it a little.

    Unlike other Korean places, they don't serve a sunnyside up fried egg on the dol sot bimbap, instead they throw on a few strips of omelette. It's a shame, but the bibimbap is still very good. It's a mix of fresh vegetables, seaweed and rice topped with your choice of tofu or beef, chicken, etc. The hot sauce is great, the kimchi is so-so, but if you're craving a fresh, tasty and interesting meal, the dol sot bibimbap is for you. Price-wise, this dish is a little expensive for lunch, but it seems to me all Korean places charge around $12-14 for dol sot bibimbap, so the price is average.

    I have three loves in my life.

    1. My darling husband
    2. Vodka
    3. Korean food

    Korean cuisine is a necessity in my life. At any given moment there is a huge jar of kimchi and gochujang in my fridge. Pajeon (green onion pancake) replaces a normal blueberry pancake as my breakfast and I can recite the recipe for bulgogi in my sleep. I could give up every other food and exist solely on bibimbap. Yes, even my Korean friends think I'm insane.

    Unfortunately, Philly falls a little flat in the Korean food scene in center city. So, when I get my need-some-jap-chae-now cravings, it can be tough to fulfill.

    That is, until I met Giwa.

    This unassuming little place tucked away on Sansom, packs a mighty little punch. Their bibimbap is fresh and flavorful, they make their own gochujang and the portions are huge. I also love their open kitchen, which provides me with culinary entertainment whilst waiting for my to-go order. While I feel the prices are a bit high, the quality of the ingredients and the friendliness of the staff quickly remedies that.

    Hear that? That's the sound of satisfaction.

    We ended up stopping in at Giwa by accident really. My fiance and I were on our way to Trader Joe's, and were really hungry, and at that moment we both turned and saw Giwa!

    Giwa is a bit on the small side, but still the place was noticeably packed, which was definitely a good sign. Upon scanning the menu, my fiance and I both found viable options (I have few restrictions, whereas she is vegetarian, which was not an issue since they have plenty of options which are clearly labeled).

    I've never really had Korean food before, so I wasn't sure what to get right away. However, I'd heard of bibimbap  before so I was considering this. But then, I saw an order of the hot bibimbap, and I KNEW I had to try this dish!

    For those unfamiliar with this dish, I'll try my best (as a novice) to explain. Bibimbap is essentially a dish composed of several elements, including rice, meat, noodles, and an assortment of veggies, cooked in layers in a stone pot. To the best of my knowledge, there is very little seasoning involved during the cooking, though I am not an expert. The real key to this dish is that it is then served in the same stone pot it had been cooking in (yes, it is VERY hot, so be careful if you order this)! This concept is amazing, it keeps everything hot and still cooking, and the best part is the rice at the bottom gets to the point that its actually crunchy! The dish comes served with a side of kimchee (a sort of cold, spiced cabbage dish), some kind of beans, and a "hot" sauce to add to the bibimbap (it's red, and I guess supposed to be hot, but I didn't find it that powerful...).

    All in all, we both had a great meal here (my fiance got the vegetarian hot bibimbap), and were actually full with about half the dish left, which made for good leftovers (glad it turned out this way, because it was a little pricey for fast food style Korean!).

    My only complaints were that the can of Ginger-ale I bought was not really cold, however they had no ice (might wanna think about getting fountain drinks instead of cans), and they had no other sauce to add to the bibimbap other than the one it came with, which is sad for those of us who enjoy some extra spice in our food!
