    We went to Distrito the other night for a colleagues birthday. I had been wanting to check this spot out for quite some time, since I had heard a lot about it.

    We started off by taking in some happy hour festivities at the bar. Everyone else ordered before me and got Mango Mohitos which looked really good, and were that good according to each of them.

    Well, I would not be me if I didn't at least try to be different. So, I ordered the Tequilachilada. I was in for quite the surprise. Despite its name, it is nowhere close to being in the Pina Colada family. It's a wild thing in a category all by itself. After my first sip a colleague asked how it was. My response was, "It tickles my senses in so many places." The first three ingredients listed on the menu are pretty straight forward; tequila, beer, and lime. It's the last ingredient that gets you. It just says spices, which as I discovered gives them all kinds of liberties. One such liberty was a nice dousing of hot sauce. I don't know what else was in it, but it was all kinds of wild. On one side, I liked it. On the other side, I could only take so much at a time.

    While downing our drinks, we also got some chips and salsa and Nachos Ignacio, a meatless nacho dish. Both were very fresh and super tasty. And, they helped a lot with my crazy drink.

    After an hour of effort, I finally finished my Tequilachilada. I decided to go super safe and sweet for my next drink. I got me a frozen margarita. I figured I could not go wrong here, and I was right. The flavor on tap was mango and it was yummy. Unlike the fire bomb I drank before it, I was able to suck this one down fairly quickly.

    Following the drinking festivities, we sat down and ordered dinner. I started off with a Pozole Verde Sopas, aka soup. It had pork belly and little neck clams in it and a green broth. Also, there were little grainy balls that reminded me of gnocchi and pieces of jalapenos. This was outrageously good. The funny thing is, when I ordered it, I had no idea what I was getting. I was like a deer in headlights when I realized I ordered a soup. I was glad this was one of those pleasant mistakes. And, totally blame the flaming alcoholic drink I had earlier for my ignorance.

    Next, I had a side of Platano Y Crema, which is basically sweet plantains and some melted cheese on it. This was prepared perfectly and tasted amazing.

    While the rest of my party finished off their dishes, I waited for my last item. Everyone seemed to thoroughly enjoy what they got. To my disappointment, my last item, Camarones Y Chorizo (Shrimp Tacos) did not come.

    When the waitress checked in, I told her my tacos had not arrived. She rushed to the kitchen and got them to me quickly, apologized, and gave them to me on the house. I appreciated the level of service she provided in correcting this mistake. And, the tacos were delish.

    All in all, this was a great meal. The portions are a little small, kind of like a tapas bar, so you should plan on ordered 2-3 dishes.

    Distrito is my go-to restaurant for dates or treating friends from out of town. Crotchety, boring diners won't appreciate the Pepto Bismol pink walls, glittery gold tabletops, and lucha libre masks studding the walls but it creates a visually stimulating environment and makes for great conversation. I love watching people's faces when they step into the restaurant- shock/confusion/amusement.

    The "modern Mexican" style is one of my favorites. Everything is tapas style and yes, you will need about 3-4 plates. Usually, I get the Camarones ceviche which is loaded with avocado and pretty refreshing. The tamales are cooked nicely and accompanied with juicy pork. The queso fundido is another one of my favorites- a must for cheese-lovers like myself. The margaritas are all very tasty albeit overpriced for frozen cocktails.

    I have an insatiable sweet tooth and am usually disappointed with the dessert selections at such restaurants. But Distrito's array of goodies is really suberb. The churros are nice and crispy, the panna cotta with fruits and granola is excellent, but my favorite is the flan. It is served with tiny almond cakes, the most succulent mango pieces, and my favorite, dulce de leche. Perfection.

    If you are penny-pinching, yes, Distrito will probably disappoint you. Some dishes/drinks are very overpriced but you can kind of gauge the portions and see which ones are most cost-efficient. I will continue to come back here as long as Distrito exists. My fav of the Garces group.

    The atmosphere really reminds me of Stephen Starrs Continental with a Cuban (not Mexican) flair.  We came in on a Friday night at 5:15pm with reservations at 6:15pm.  Being on 40th parking at this time (and during the summer) was very easy.  I was very pleased to see that there was no wait and many open seats at the bar.

    They serve a limited happy hour menu with appetizers, beers, cocktails and wine.  We opted for the guacamole; try it, you will love it.  The chips were perfect and guac was super smooth and very flavorful.  After a few more beers and about an hour of time we got our table upstairs (hint: if it's ever busy at the downstairs one there is an additional bar up the steps).

    The server explained how the process worked: "modern mexican...small plates...etc".  He suggested two to three plates per person; I can eat a lot of food and we ordered five plates and two side dishes...we took two thirds of the food home.  Order a bit less as you can always add on dishes if you want more.  

    We ordered two flat breads: one with wild mushrooms and the duck confit.  Both very good (if you don't like truffle do not order the wild mushroom flat bread).  Also ordered was the chicken enchilada...it was the only dish I wasn't a huge fan of (my girlfriend loved it); I was looking for a more traditional sauce.  The tamales with roasted pork are crazy...the masa is served separate from the pork (which is diced, wrapped in a banana leaf and melts in your mouth); again a more modern approach which is very good.  The hanger steak tacos were a little tough...the beef was perfectly medium-rare and the fresh tortillas were great; just a little too much chew on the beef (maybe next time I'll cut it up smaller first) but overall great flavor.  We also added the rice and corn which was phenomenal and refried beans which were typical.  

    Overall the meal was great.  Phenomenal flavor and presentation.  I love that fact that Jose Garces has enough salt in his dishes so that the flavors of the food intensify.  For me there is nothing worse than ordering food with no or little salt.  

    The service at the bar and in the dining room was acceptable...nothing special, not huge personalities but we never had to wait.  A very reserved approach which I am fine with.  

    And the best part is that we were able to have several alcoholic beverages, leave full and with leftover food for just over $100.  We will be back many more times...they show a bone marrow picture on the website; however, they are not always serving it.  I will be a pest until I can try that dish.

Specialties

Distrito is the new restaurant concept by Jose Garces, the chef behind the acclaimed Philadelphia restaurants Amada and Tinto. Distrito is a high-energy restaurant inspired by the spirited culture and cuisine of Mexico City. The Distrito menu features Chef Garces' fresh interpretations of classic Mexican fare and beverages include over 65 tequilas and margaritas made with fresh seasonal fruits. A striking decor includes a hot pink and bright green color scheme, a wall constructed from over 600 colorful, campy masks of lucha libre, or "free fight" professional wrestlers, and a booth crafted from a Volkswagen Beetle, a typical Mexico City taxi cab.

History

Established in 2008.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
