    I've been looking at Govinda's To-Go menu for months. It's just far enough from my office to make it a bit of a walk but if I feel like moving quickly enough, it's not so bad. Today, I decided to treat myself to my weekly lunch-out a day early. I walked to Govinda's in the sun at a relatively fast pace and ordered a chicken cheesesteak on a sesame roll, with soy mayo and soy cheese. The service was fast and they accept credit, WHEW. I hustled back to my office with my sandwich and dug in. The sandwich absolutely lived up to the hype.

    The sandwich artist dug out the meat of the roll, which I definitely appreciated, leaving just enough inner-softness and the crunchy, but not hard, outer sesame-seed shell. So good, so good. The chunks of "chicken" were delicious - flavorful and not that wet, stringy shizzle I've encountered on veggie chicken cheesesteaks of yore. They tasted close to what I remember chicken tasting like, only softer and way better. The green peppers were crisp, tasted fresh, and were portioned just right - not too many, but enough to still have presence. And the soy cheese & soy mayo combo was perfect. A little sloppy, but not messy, and just wet enough.

    I only ate half, the other half is bundled up for dinner. I'm wary of the bread becoming soggy, but I am hopeful yet confident that the roll will stand the test of time.

    Sometimes I wonder why I continue to frequent Govinda's. Usually it takes only a bite of their vegan chicken cheesesteak to put those thoughts to rest.

    The main draw of Govinda's is, naturally, the food. I mention this because it's certainly not the prompt service or the low low prices.
