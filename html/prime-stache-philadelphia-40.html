    Cool hipster vibe, dim bulbs  with wood paneling walls and brick., feels like a northern libs/fishtown bar.

    Eclectic bar menu, random and new selections for drafts and bottles, worth sitting down and trying.  The peach julep with change your mind on bourbon, orser one while tou're looking over the menu.

    Tomato and onion salad: light dressing, suprisingly light on onion, so dont be scared to order.

    Sweet pea and edamame eavioli: mild and flavorful, large enough to take 4 bites to finish.   Its a rice wrapper with a sweet feelig, very light on the truffle and heavy on flavor.

    Cherry pepper calamari: a little on the mild side, what you expect a fried calamari dish to taste like, does not disappoint

    Rock shrimp fry: spicey!  Lots of heat, one of my favorites!

    Meatloaf: like a rich and hearty home pressed burger with just a hint of bbq sauce, best dish of the night. Wrapped in bacon and served on a bed of pureed celery root and two glazed baby carrots.  This is the dish you'll come back for

    Cavatelli: tastes housemade.  Meat is a little bland compared to some of the heat from the apps,, couln't tell if it was pulled pork or shredded beef, its just good and filling.

    Burger is a let down after tasting the meatloaf...order the meatloaf on a roll if you want a burger...its good if you tried nothing else but after the meatloaf, you'll never look at a burger the same way again

    Lasagne:  bright and simple sauce, great flavors, large piece of eggplant...my ex was off the boat Italian and this gives her mom's  homemade lasagne a run for her money.

    Applepie: decadent and rich, smothered in caramel with what seems like a hint off sea salt.  Super disappointed its no longer on the menu.

    The staff couldnt be any friendlier.  From the bartenders to the wait staff to the chef Hee himself, this is going to be your new neighborhood favorite!

    9/12/2013

    Atmosphere: 95
    Service: 95
    Beer: 85
    Food: 95
    Value: 90
    OVERALL:  (92) A-

    OVERVIEW:
    Cool atmosphere.  Fantastic service.  Good beer.  Awesome food.  Great Value.

    ATMOSPHERE:
    Apparently everything in this bar, floor to ceiling (including drink), is made in America.  Well done, Prime Stache.

    SERVICE:
    I've been there twice within 5 days.  It was nice that the bartender remembered what I ordered when I came back the second time.

    BEER:
    An ok amount of beers on tap that are mostly from craft breweries.  They have a few seasonal options and I saw a few bottles as well.

    FOOD:
    This is the home of the best sandwich in Philadelphia.  The Ron Burgundy is a pork lover's dream.  3 types of pork (pulled pork, pork belly, and roast pork), a Coca-Cola BBQ sauce, cheddar cheese, slaw, and fried tabasco onions served with a side of homemade potato chips.  You can actually tell which pork you are currently tasting, evening if you've just taken a bite of all three. The textures and flavors are all unique, yet they work in a tremendous harmony.  The pulled pork is smokey and just falls apart, the pork belly is like candy with a crunchy exterior that just melts in your mouth, and the roast pork made with a rosemary natural au jus is similar in taste to the best Philly staple roast pork sandwiches around the city.  The onions would be a fantastic appetizer at any bar on their own.  Finally... that sauce... damn.

    VALUE:
    Worth it.  Go.

    FINAL THOUGHT:
    I just found my favorite bar in Old City.

History

Established in 2013.

On the cobblestone block of Front and Chestnut Street, Prime Stache in Old City provides an extensive American bistro style menu and carefully selected craft beer and cocktails. From the classic exposed brick, to the antique wooden décor, to the Thomas Edison style light fixtures, there is a rustic, yet modern and comfortable feel to the restaurant.

Owner Brent Celek and partner/chef Hee Chang have developed a distinctive, gratifying, and affordable menu filled with savory and prodigious entrées and sandwiches while Kevin Malvey's unique cocktail menu combines all fresh ingredients with house made infusions and bitters in an elaborate display of mason jars that will leave you curious and anxious to ask 'What's in that one?'
