    WOW.  Let me start by saying, I am VERY impressed.  

    I have been wanting to try out this place for quite some time.  Due to the fact that you almost always need a reservation, I've settled for places nearby.  Never again!  I'll be sure to make a reservation, this place is worth it.  

    When you walk in there's an amazing vibe.  Some really chill music, and a sprawling beautiful open kitchen.  It's so nice to see the food being made.  These cooks work like a well oiled machine.  

    When the waitress made it over she asked if we had been here before.  After I told her that we had not she took us through the menu in a quick but knowledgeable fashion.  They have small plates as well as entrees.  It's perfect for a group to share, or even to share with another person.  

    When she came to take our order, we told her we were ready, but asked her to give us a suggestion.  She was incredibly knowledgeable on the menu and suggested her favorite items and talked about how each of the flavors were.  A lot of her picks matched up with what we ordered.  She informed us that things are brought out as soon as they are made so the food would be staggered.  

    Our first delivery was Brussels sprouts.  Absolutely delicious!  Next came the edemame ravioli.  Another incredible taste.  The tuna tacos were a STANDOUT!  These are small portions, but with fair prices you can taste a lot of them.  

    For desert we got the mini ice cream cones which were not only adorable but delicious.  

    Plates were taken as soon as we were finished and our waitress was always on top of filling our waters and asking us if we'd like another glass of wine.  Both of us were incredibly impressed and talked about how we'll be back very soon.  If you have anyone visiting the city, TAKE THEM HERE!  They will be raving.

You could definitely tell the cuisine was asian fusion though (mainly chinese, with a bit of vietnamese, thai, and korean).     This review is strictly for happy hour, because although I've been here 4 or 5 times now, it's only been during that delightful time of day when work is over and drinks and small bites are offered at reduced prices to celebrate - ah, I do love happy hour.

    Anyway, this place is part of the cluster that makes up my favorite intersection in the city, at 13th and Sansom.  It's easy to spot because it's got a cool, modern black-and bamboo look to it, and there's a long row of outdoor seating that is always PACKED when it's nice outside.  Inside, it's dim and has a sort of edgy/romantic feel.  Happy hour is only at the bar, which is generally too small for the crowd, and so you might find yourself waiting if you don't get there close to 5pm.  Still, it goes till 7, so plenty of time to fight your way into some departing guests' seats.  There's always a $4 cocktail special, which is always something delicious, if not always strong.  I've had the shrimp and lobster dumplings (served in miso soup) twice, and they are excellent.  For $4, you get 3 or 4 dumplings, and they're far better than standard Chinese fare. Most recently I also had the shrimp fried rice, which had a nice spice to it, and my friend tried the brussel sprouts - really, really good.  Though they're "small bites", they're actually fairly large portions, great for sharing and easily made into a meal.

    Of course, I have to mention that all the same great happy hour deals are also offered in the back, at the Graffiti Bar.  To get there, you can either walk through the restaurant or enter through the tiny little alley next to it.  This place also gets ridiculously packed.  Although every time I go I am seriously concerned about the lack of acceptable fire or other emergency escapes, this is a really cool place to hang out. (Just try to be near the door. You know... just in case...)

    DUDE.

    Back from this past October, when visiting Philly for the weekend for my birthday, the beau and I were on our way out of El Vez, exploring the neighborhood a bit before heading to our bus back to NYC. He looked down an alleyway and saw incredible graffiti; being the hardcore NYC-born and raised graffiti and tag-loving kid he is, he was drawn, but we really didn't have time to stop and explore. Once I got home that night, I decided to figure out what this was and came across Graffiti Bar. Associated with that was Sampan. I immediately bookmarked both on Yelp as a reminder to check it out next time I was in Philly. Well, a few months later, we decided to come back down for another weekend for HIS birthday, and you can bet I made a surprise birthday dinner reservation for him!

    Though our reservation was at 7:30, we were easily seated upon arrival at 7:25. One, it's freaking gorgeous and lively in there, a blue tree branch-stenciled lit design glowing over our heads. Our waitress (...Daniella, I believe was her name...) was fantastic and knowledgeable about the menu and drinks. I went for the Twisted Tai: good ol' spiced rum and fruit juices. I believe Adam's drink was called the Chapter 2, which I believe had bourbon...and, well, other delicious and fruit additions to it.

    As for the food, we went the split route and shared all of our dishes: Crispy Quail and Philly Cheesesteak Potstickers, followed by a bowl of Kobe Beef Fried Rice. The quail was akin to a nice and meaty chicken wing; those quails did not die in vain, because they were delicious! The potstickers epitomized Asian Fusion: delicious dumplings filled with beef and provolone as a tangy and wonderful combination! As for the fried rice, it was some of the best I ever had. I couldn't quite explain why, because were were various elements, but the rice was absolutely perfect in terms of consistency, flavor, and stickiness. The beef chunks were magic; the beau was speechless through his first few bites!

    For dessert, we were bad ass and ordered two: the S'Mores Tart and the house-made soft serve ice cream cones. The tart boasted an Oreo base, crushed peanuts, marshmallow cream, chocolate sauce, caramel sauce, and white chocolate shavings. Need I say more? I am not a fan to peanut butter and chocolate together (...yes, I am a strange child...), but I damn near devoured that whole thing. As for the ice cream, they had house-made Vanilla Cinnamon Toast Crunch and Chocolate M & M soft serve in baby ice cream cones. Each was amazing, and we even got one one with both flavors twisted onto it!

    I left that place drifting into an amazing food coma; we literally just went back to our hotel and slept afterwards. Ha! (Big Friday night in Philly, am I right?) But it was worth it. Price-wise, also, I was shocked: two small plates, the fried rice, two drinks, and two desserts between two people only cost us $71. I was expecting more but was pleased by such a (relatively) low price for a quality dining experience plus the amount of food we got.

    Prior to leaving, I saw that Michael Schulson is the brain behind Izakaya, the only positive experience I had while staying at the Borgata in Atlantic City a few years back. Well, no wonder I liked this place so much!

    Seriously, a gem in Philly not worth missing. SO pleased! I'm sitting here at almost 10 a.m. craving those Philly Cheesesteak Potstickers again...Sampan has clearly affected me...

    Having just left a reception and not being terribly hungry, a friend and I ventured to Sampan to check out the Asian tapas. Wanting to try a little bit of everything, we ordered the chef's tasting menu ($45) which included 7 selections - 2 small plates, 1 satay, 1 cold and hot, 1 meat, 1 fish and 1 side - as well as a complimentary dessert.

    Small plates - Shrimp and lobster dumpling (miso broth, scallion, tofu), philly cheese steak pot stickers
    Satay - Kobe beef (apricot, soy, mirin)
    Cold/Hot - Crab wonton taco (avocado, pickled salad, cilantro)
    Meat - Pekin duck (scallion bun, cucumber, hoisin)
    Fish - Soy glazed chilean glazed sea bass (cauliflower puree, long bean salad)
    Side - Crispy brussel sprout (fish sauce, puffed rice and chili)
    Dessert - mini ice cream cones with rotating flavors (vanilla with froot loops, chocolate peanut butter)

    Sampan's innovative asian-style small plates were mostly very well executed. The sea bass was flaky and light with a touch of sweetness that made every bite delectable and the brussel sprouts were crispy and well seasoned. The skewered kobe beef practically melted in my mouth. Although the duck required a bit of assembly, once the duck was in the bun with a few cucumbers and some hoisin, it was a little sandwich with big flavor! My least favorite dish was the philly cheese steak dumpling which sat atop a seemingly processed cheese sauce.

    The food arrived at the table as it was prepared and in an efficient manner, although we didn't feel rushed. The ambiance was created by the dimly lit room, cozy booths and the wall of fluorescent artwork. The dining room was packed and for good reason. So, if you're in the area and looking for a light bite to eat, I recommend checking out Sampan.
