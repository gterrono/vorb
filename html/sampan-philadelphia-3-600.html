    I wasn't expecting much coming here. I thought it would be sorta like a glorified Americanized Chinese resto, like PF Chang's.

    Though it was a bit pricey, I was pleasantly surprised with the flavors, quality, and variety of Asian inspired food. We started out with a cocktail and a beer. I forgot what it was called but the cocktail was fresh and flavors mingled together well with just the right amount of alcohol to maintain flavor. We ordered lobster & shrimp dumplings, pork bao bun, korean fried chicken, and kim chee fried rice, and soft serve to finish it off. Food took time and came out in spurts, but each dish was wonderful, especially the pork bao buns. Slightly crispy and sweet outside, tender inside. Wrapped in a nice fluffy warm bun.

    Besides the yummy food, the service was on point. The decor was charming and attractive with comfortable seating. Plus, the restaurant is located in the hub of 20-somethings, so the area is great for a stroll, even late at night.

    Visiting on business from CA, love elevated Asian concepts and this one delivered exactly what i was looking for. For modern street food than tasting menu place although a 5 course was offered. Got there at 6:55PM and sat a the bar, bartender (Ryan?) immediately let me know I had 5 minutes to order off the "happy hour menu" which was loaded with exactly what a foodie-hipster from Cali was looking for. BBQ pork Bahn mi, beef short rib skewers, and a kimchi fried rice. Tell me those aren't the sacred 3 dishes by which to judge a modern Asian place by...

    Service at the bar was great, started with a glass of Grüner, and asked for suggestions on Sake, told me a little bit but then poured me a small taste of both the lowest end and highest end sake to give me a sense of the range. The food started coming out almost immediately, fist came the Bahn mi, cut into four, on what had to be half an actual baguette.  Great flavor, no clear flaws... then about 2 min later came the short rib skewers, equally loaded with flavor well beyond beef, still tender and not at all chewy. I've eaten varients of these first two simple street food dishes more times than I can count and I have to say these were really up there in terms of authenticity to what I've found on my travels and in overall flavor and conception.

    The rice took a few more minutes to come out which was just fine with me (probably less than 10 min from ordering). Even after tasting the two sake's referenced in the first paragraph I asked for his suggestion in more of the middle range of the menu. He poured me blindly the second and third on the list for which there was a huge variation in price for an 8 oz pour($17 v $34),  I told him I preferred "the one on the right" which it turned out was the pricer, he let me know the differnece in price before pouring it to be sure I was ok with that. I respect this for several reasons... For one I am alone at the bar, not trying to impress anyone, he had the sense to recognize this and offer me a chance at the less expensive pour because there was such a huge varience in price. At the same time he didn't under-sell me and respected the fact that I could discern the difference.

    The rice was great, when it arrived the runner could see I was still eating the bahn mi and asked if she could mix in the egg (although she didnt say it this is so the runny part of the egg could be mixed in and didnt cook through solid while I was eating my other dish. Again done with grace by the service staff while not making me feel rushed. How can fried rice be the best thing you eat? By being Awesome (with a purposeful capital A)... By being kimchi that wasnt just out of a jar...

    As I finished I was reminded that world cup soccer was on in the graffiti bar outside (part of the same establishment), and that I was welcome to head out there to watch America beat Ghana.  I did both (head out there, and watch America win).  Perhaps  y only complaint of the night was the smell in the outdoor graffiti bar... Maybe just being from out of town I'm not normalized but it really smelled of homeless guy piss back there. Oh well!

    Great evening! I spent $11 to get three great dishes (again thanks to happy hour and a great bartender) Kudos to the chef and the staff you are all killing it!!!!
