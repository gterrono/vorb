    Listed in Places I Hope You Don't Discover, Adam Richman My Secret Love

    My boyfriend and I are sitting here in a Brooklyn, New York hotel room noshing on Tony Luke's cheese steak subs right this minute.  We had to stop in and eat lunch on the way to NYC, and (of course) pick up some for later.  We'd PROMISED ourselves, you see.

    I made good on my intention to try the pork and broccoli rabe sandwich - and I was actually shocked, because in some ways this is even BETTER than the cheese steak I had the last time - believe me, that was GOOD, too.  But the pork and broccoli rabe was in a class of its own.  The pork was so...tender... and JUICY.... and the rabe just set it all off perfectly.  I can't even begin to describe the flavor of it - it was just a little kick of spice combined with that juicy meat... hell, it didn't even need any condiments at all.  

    LoML got a regular cheese steak - the look on his face was just pure bliss.  If anyone can be transported up to heaven, he was in that all-too-brief time... so, so, so, so good.

    We also got the salt and vinegar fries to share - not bad, though I thought them a little TOO salty, honestly.  I wish there had been some extra malt vinegar to put on them.  But not too terrible.  Now that we've been there more than once, though, I can safely say that sides are not this place's strength.  If you decide to skip them, you probably won't miss it.

    We're going to stop back on the way home and pick up more sandwiches.  They're even good cold - go figure.

    We did find out that there's a Tony Luke's branch inside of a strip club nearby us at home, but we really don't want to go in there just for cheese steaks.  We could use a location in Baltimore.  (Not-so-subtle-hint.)

    The Love of My Life and I were visiting Philadelphia today on a "day trip", and one of the things… The Love of My Life and I were visiting Philadelphia today on a "day trip", and one of the things that I wanted him to experience was the taste and sensation of eating a "real Philly cheesesteak".  Being a native of Las Vegas, he had never had the chance to experience ecstasy as we East Coasters know it.

    Oh, sure, Baltimore has cheesesteaks - but it's just NOT the same.  Nothing will do but the real deal.  So, up to Philly we drove, and we had a choice to make.  Pat's, Geno's, Jim's?  Hmmm... which one?

    Then we heard about Tony Luke's.  Actually, we'd seen it - on Man v. Food.  And I have to admit - those looked like some DAMNED good cheesesteaks.  Adam Richman sure looked like he was having a good old time, anyway.  So we said, what the hell, it can't be that bad, right?

    We were a bit nervous about the neighborhood - this looked like a real "sketch" area, right on par with some of the worst ghettos in Baltimore.  We almost missed it, truth be told, until we went just under the highway - then our GPS lit up like a beacon.  "You have arrived at your destination, you have arrived."  And, did we ever.  The line!!!  We were really lucky and snagged a parking spot right under the highway, but I can see where parking would be a real nightmare here.  There were cars literally in the middle of the street.  

    From previous stories that I'd heard, I warned my boyfriend that he had to know exactly what he wanted, and to be fast about ordering it, as the cashiers are usually harried and don't have time to deal with people that don't know what's going on.  We were prepared, but the staff didn't really seem to be that gruff.  Friendly, no, but not downright rude, so that was a plus.  

    I ordered a regular cheesesteak with American cheese (I do NOT do "whiz" on my sandwiches - I'm sorry, but, just, no - if that makes me less than a purist, too bad) and heaps of fried onions, while LoML tried a pizza cheesesteak.  We also ordered cheese and pizza curly fries as "go-withs".  

    When we got our food and dug in.... oh. my. WORD.  Heaven.  Sheer freaking heaven.  I have never in my LIFE had a cheesesteak so good.  I could feel the tears prickling my eyelids as I tasted the meat, perfectly done, the onions, the delicious meltiness of my cheese.  Oh, yeah.  This was the right choice, indeed.  Pat's, Geno's, I'll never go there again, ever.  Tony Luke's is the cheesesteak of choice, the bomb DIGGITY, the ULTIMATE in sandwich goodness.  This was worth a 3 hour trip up and back all by itself.

    So, why only 4 stars instead of the ultimate 5?  Well.... there were a few minor distractions from the goodness that is this sandwich:

    - The open-air seating was a bit difficult to deal with.  We had to flick away lots of insect creatures that happened to want to share our sandwich nirvana.  That kind of sucked.

    - The number/last name system over the intercom is VERY difficult to hear, especially when the place is crowded.  We could barely hear our order coming up because there were a LOT of people in there, all talking and laughing and... well, sharing our sandwich nirvana.  We know the staff is super busy already, we don't want to complicate the system by coming up and timidly asking where our order is, heh.

    - The cheese fries, while good, were literally drowning helplessly in a sea of whiz.  I guess that's to be expected, but it did get overpowering toward the bottom of the cup, so much that I started to kind of wish that the portion wasn't as large as it turned out to be.  Still, it's a minor beef and one I can certainly live with.

    The "Ultimate Cheesesteak Challenge" that Adam Richman did with Mr. Luke will sadly have to go untried on my behalf - the one sandwich I did eat will probably carry me through right up until tomorrow night.  It was THAT filling.  I can't imagine eating 5 pounds of it.  

    I wanted to try the pork/broccoli rabe sandwich, but alas, it was a difficult choice and the cheesesteak won out.  Next time I stop here (and I will), that pork sammie is next for sure.

    Adam Richman, you are the Jesus of food once again, sir.  I salute you.     Read more

    Cheese...Steak...Sandwich... CHEESE, it's in the name.

    After many years of going to my Father-in-laws in Philly, I finally convince him to take us to Tony Luke's today. Every other time, he says "You don't wanna go there, it's awful.", and after years of anticipation and seeing this place on all the food shows, he was half right. What a letdown.

    We stood in line maybe twenty minutes which is expected at a place so popular. Got to order, then wait another fifteen minutes because everything is made to order. So we finally get our food, and (car crash sound) wtf? Where's the cheese? I ordered beef with cheese. My ticket just said beef, so I assume the mean lady at the cash register screwed up, so I eat it anyway. It was okay, I'd say nothing to write home about, but here I am writing about it. I can honestly say that I've had better microwaved sandwiches late night at a gas station back in college. Not trying to be mean, but all the juice they showed them drowning it with on television just wasn't there, and of course no cheese. Just dry meat on day old bread.

    Then, my wife bites into hers, and (car crash sound) where's the pork AND cheese? Really, a pork, broccoli rabe and cheese with JUST broccoli? This was getting ridiculous. We looked at her ticket, strike two for the mean lady at the cash register, this was a green Italian sandwich. Huge bummer, am I supposed to stand in line again to get our orders remade?

    Next, Father-in-laws chicken with spinach sandwich. Yay, they got one right, but he said it sucked. He's the guy that sends everything back at restaurants, and eats last all the time, so I'm sure it was still pretty good. At least they got his order right.

    We all shared an order of salt and vinegar fries. They were okay, not really, someone was a bit heavy handed with the salt. We had to scrape off tons of excess. They were a good value at $4 for a huge portion, but still, strike four.

    So now I can cross Tony Luke's off my list of food destinations, and add it to my been there done that list. But really, I'm a foodie, I live for this stuff. My Father-in-law is a heart patient who watches every thing he eats. I thought he just didn't want to go there by telling us it was "awful". But he was right.

    On the good side. In anticipation for this trip, I bought a box of Tony Luke's Philly Cheese steaks from the frozen food aisle of my grocery store the other day. They were great! I watched the video on Youtube on how to prepare them, and what a surprise, even the bread came out PERFECT. I should have stopped right there. I can now equate this place to White Castle, hit or miss at the restaurant, but pretty damn good and consistent out of the box.

    So basically this trip to Tony Luke's was scarred by the mean lady who worked the cash register today (8-31-2012 @2pm), the person who left the juice off my sandwich, and the person who violently attacked our fries with a whole salt shaker.

The matter of Philadelphia's signature sandwich--a taste-treat known world-wide--is only set on proper footing if Tony Luke's is recognized and acknowledged immediately, at the outset of all talk.

    Yes, when I overhear the above, I will jump in with two feet and energetically discuss the merits of each (Pat's or Geno's vs each other). Or, I will champion BOTH of them against utter drivel like Jim's of South Street. This is just by way of keeping the issues straight; keeping comparisons fair. But never, ever, are Pat's or Geno's worth comparing to Tony Luke's.

    That would be foolhardy and naive. Jim's is far below Pat's/Geno's... and Pat's/Geno's are well below Tony Luke's. By a country mile. That is the atomic--and subatomic--and galactic--order of things, in Philadelphia. That is the nature of things to-day, Mr. Biel.

    And this is not even touching upon the sphere of all things related to pork sandwiches. For that, there is no discussion. Its all academic. Tony Luke's and Tommy DeNic's. They're not rivals. They both do superb work in the areas of pork. So, no--do not even worry about this. The hierarchy of the Philly pork sammich, is firmly settled.

    But in cheesesteaks...ahh, cheese-steaks. Well. People insist on debating this; it is a tussle which lends itself to partisanship. But demonstrably,  Tony Luke's rises above all the 'poor sammich execution issues' that give followers of Pat's or Geno's their criticisms against each other.

    For example. Tony Luke's meat is never 'gristly'. Their meat is never stringy, either. Their meat is cut into the proper size on the grill. Its not too big or too small. They put enough of the meat into every sandwich. Their cheese-steaks are made in the proper manner (there is a defined order in which the ingredients must be laid into the roll). And the meat is top quality.

    In short, all the complaints which are hurled between Pat's and Geno's do not apply here and guess what? I can give you a simple test which proves the higher caliber of Tony Luke's which you can attempt for yourself, any time you wish.

    Take a classic cheese-steak  from each of these three steak emporiums and sit down at a table. Remove each steak from its paper and hold each up with one hand, *vertically*. Watch what happens. A Tony Luke's cheese-steak will not drip a single drop of grease. Pat's and Geno's? Your hand will be covered.

    Superior quality. Q.E.D.
