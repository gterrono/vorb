    Listed in Top Restaurants

    Since my first meal at Fond, nearly two and a half years ago, I am consistently blown away by Lee's cuisine, by Jessie's breads and desserts, and by the impeccable service in the dining room, led by Tory.  

    Some highlights that define the experience of dining at Fond for me:

    The food
    Not only are the "standards" like steak & fish consistently fantastic with unexpected sauces or accompaniments, but Lee also has made me fall in love with ingredients I never thought would become part of my list of favorite foods.  Foie gras soup (so decadent), crispy sweetbreads (I wish these came in a family-style size so I could gorge myself on them), and wild boar pate (I am not sure what heavenly spices are in here, but I fought my husband for the last bite).  The pork belly needs a special mention as well - simply incredible.

    A great value and culinary treat is the five or seven course tasting menus.  For about half the price, this is every bit as delicious and exciting as the tasting menus at Talula's, Vetri, & Cyrus in Healdsburg, CA.  The only component missing is the cheese course, which is quickly forgotten once you taste Jessie's desserts.

    The breads and desserts
    I have never before been to a restaurant where I felt the bread was a necessary element of the dinner.  Jessie's hand-kneaded olive bread is so light and savory that I have to pace myself so I have room for my actual meal.  Her desserts are beautiful and delicious.  The sorbets are always excellent, and a perfect way to end a decadent meal.  I was particularly impressed with the chocolate-truffle truffle, which I wish would make it to the standard menu.  Who knew that truffles taste even better with chocolate than when shaved over homemade pasta?!  

    When I am not too stuffed (typically after either eating at home or after eating at another restaurant), Jessie's Belle Cakery is my new go-to place for a cup of coffee, perhaps a glass of wine, and the best desserts in the city.  If you are lucky, you can even snag a loaf of olive bread to take home.

    The service
    Dining at Fond, you feel like the incredibly lucky guests in the chef's home.  The staff work so harmoniously you'd think they've worked as a team their entire career.  Tory brings refined elegance to the experience.  Steve brings true South Philly hospitality.  Every staff member executes a flawless service - glasses are always refreshed, bread is always replenished, presentation of the menu, specials, and courses are inspired.  

    ***

    The balance of exceptional food with 5-star service is what makes dining at Fond so remarkable.  I would suggest dining here for special occasions, with anyone who loves food, with clients, or just because you cannot resist the specials that Lee posts on the Fond BYOB Facebook page.  In the nice weather, there is even a Chef's Table in the backyard, which is fantastic!  

    I see Fond as the future of Philadelphia dining.  I look forward to seeing how the restaurant evolves over the next 20+ years, and how its success continues to grow.

    My wife and I took a ride into South Philly for dinner last night, and based on an online recommendation, found Fond.  The seating area was small, about 10 tables, in what was once the living room and dinning room of a South Philly row house.  It kind of reminded us of some of the small SoHo bistros in New York.

    Our young server was friendly and attentive, and both the chef and one of the owners took time to talk to us.

    The menu selection was small and "interesting".  Not every item was to our liking.  We both chose a salad, which was very fresh, but small and at $10, over priced for its size.  For our main courses, my wife had the skate, which she described as excellent, and she is a tough critic.  I had the duck breast which was very tender, and cooked just perfect.  Both excellent choices and recommendations from our server, and not over priced.

    Dessert was mixed.  My wife went for a dish of malted ice cream with chocolate and peanuts.  It was good, but could have had more ice cream.  I chose the canolis.  They were excellent with Carmel sauce and sour cherries, and were from a local bakery down the street.

    Fond is a BYOB, so that kept the check to a somewhat reasonable $98 for the two of us.  Dirt cheap by New York standards, and not at all out of line for Philadelphia.

    I would definitely recommend Fond and make a return visit.  Next time, though, I would probably skip the appetizer / salad, and just have entree and dessert.

    Finally got to check out Fond last night. Had a fantastic experience from the getgo. Great staff here, very friendly and knowledgeable about the items on the menu.

    I love the menus on the individual sized corkboards. I think the selection of food for a tiny spot is pretty remarkable and innovative. It's like a halfway molecular gastronomy meets traditional American cooking. Can't beat that.

    We started with the veal sweetbreads. Absolutely phenomenal, definitely the best part of the meal. The sweetbreads were served on top of buffalo mozzarella and accompanied by some greens. Thoughtfully, they split the appetizer in half so that we each had our own plate. Very generous of the chef and wait staff.

    Even before that, a nice little token on the house was served in the form of a mushroom soup. The soup was very subtle but just right. Can't beat those flavors.

    My dining partner and I each had our choice of bread (olive or sourdough) and that was a nice touch.

    The main entrees were outstanding! The beef flat-iron was cooked medium rare and the confit potatoes were perfectly seasoned. The baby fennel also added to the palate with its unique, licorice-like taste.

    My dining partner had the seared tuna accompanied with brown butter hummus (good good good!) and diced cucumbers on the side.

    Finishing the meal was a delicious cheesecake with roasted strawberries and a meyer lemon cannoli. Cannot go wrong here.

    I really enjoyed my Fond experience and hope to make it back some time. I think it's the small places like this and Bibou that make the dining experience in Philadelphia different from other places. This joint would not work anywhere else but in Philly. Props to the Fond people, hope to dine again with you soon.

    When a friend found out I'd never been to Fond, despite living mere blocks away, she insisted we go as soon as possible. A few days later, reservations were made, wine was procured, a Napa Sauvignon Blanc, and a Burgundy Pinot Noir, and we were on our way.

    The first impression is that the restaurant is small and intimate. It was neither quiet nor deafening, and did not fall into the trap of dimming the lights so far as to prevent reading the menu. Of course, who needs menus when there's a multi-course tasting?

    We started with a sublime squash soup, something perfectly buttery and earthy to get started. Next came a hiramasu crudo over celery salad, greek yogurt, and dusted with nigella seeds, a crisp and delicate introduction to the menu, that turned out to be just a tease of the excellence to come.

    The next course was a special of the night, braised duck hearts, and it stepped up the game considerably. After cleansing our palates with a lemon sorbet, the next course was skate wing over risotto and brown butter. This was the highlight of the night, and probably the best plate I'd had in almost a year. The courses finished off with a porkbelly finished to a satisfying crisp on one side, served over excellent sweet potatoes.

    Dessert amounted to a warm chocolate brownie and a tangerine cheesecake, both of which earn high marks despite my lack of a sweet tooth, and the passionfruit meringues that came with the check punctuated the evening expertly

    Take a date here, take a friend here, take your mom here, it doesn't matter, just go here.
