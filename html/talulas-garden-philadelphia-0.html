    Come here for the ambiance.  Sitting outside in the garden is romantic and relaxing on a cool summer evening.  There are times that you can forget that you're in the heart of the city and it makes for a great dining experience.  

    The food was good, but slightly pricier than it warrants.  We started with the cheese plate, and top to bottom it was delicious.  For our entrees we had the brick chicken, which was excellent.  The chicken is marinated in the brine of the mozzarella they make in house and had a nice undertone of the cheese and notes of peppercorn as well.  The brick created a nice char on the chicken, but left it tender and juicy inside.  We also had the trout.  Looking back on it, it left a lot to be desired.  

    The service here is impeccable.  It seems as though every time I needed something and turned to my left to look for the waitress she was already headed my way.

    So you might spend a little more than you want to, and there are places in the city with better food, but you'll be hard pressed to find a more beautiful place to eat outside on a cool summer night in the city.

    Intro:

    From the farm to table ethos to the fabulous outdoor garden, Talula's is one of the finest restaurants in the city.

    Basics:

    1. Ordered the brick pressed chicken. Perfectly tender and seasoned. Bits not directly under brick was subject to a delightful charring and crunchy texture. The paired yukon potatoes were a bit heavy for my palate, perhaps a lighter vegetable side?

    2. Appetizers: Beef tartare was not the best I've had. It was not as robust in flavor as I had. It was minced very finely, and tossed with creme fraiche. Over all the texture of the creme too closely resembled the minced beef (a testament to the melt-in-mouth tenderness of the beef), giving the over all dish a one dimensional mouthfeel.

    The tuna crudo was amazing. Simply refreshing, especially in the summer. The melons were a perfect pairing with the fish. The tartness of the lime mellowed out the sweetness and brought out the brine of the fish. Wonderful.

    3. Vegetable sides: all well portioned and ready to share with 2-4 persons. Beets: great combo with the house made ricotta. Mushrooms and beans were equally as fantastic. Great variety in the mushroom medley.

    Conclusion:

    Absolute joy to go to this establishment. Some of the best food in the city. Find a seat outside if possible; the package won't disappoint.

    We bumped into Talula's Garden after a mid-summer stroll through adjacent Washington Square, one of the loveliest spots in Philly.  Instantly enchanted, especially by the lush outdoor garden and whimsical menu, we booked an anniversary dinner here.  When dinner time came, the city was beset by violent thunderstorms, almost prompting us to reschedule (thankfully we didn't), and of course the garden was off-limits.  Nonetheless, Talula's delivered one of the most memorable and exquisite meals in my memory

    The large room where we sat featured very high ceilings and an enormous, somewhat gaudy chandelier, along with scattered art and red walls.  The first morsel we sampled was a small, homemade brioche topped with a dab of sun-dried tomato butter:  hard to go wrong if that if your prelude.  Our server, though not the most warm or welcoming I've met, gave us plenty of time to deliberate over the uniformly delectable menu.

    First up were the shrimp, large, head-on, sumptuous, and seared on a plank.  The ambrosial salad of corn, bulgar and nectarines offered a unique and interesting complement.  The arugula pesto added visual appeal and a nutty undertone, and I was impressed by my first exposure to purslane, a complex-tasting green that is much maligned (considered a "weed" in the U.S., and sometimes known by the unflattering name "pigweed.")  

    Along with the shrimp came a decadent and beautiful portion of pan-fried homemade gnocchi.  The gnocchi had a deep roasted potato taste, and the outside was lightly fried until golden brown, adding some sweetness which combined well with the semi-salty shreds of ham and the halved Mission figs strewn across the plate.  The crown jewel here was the cheesy fondue the gnocchi was tossed with before serving.   A true knock-out (or should I say Gnocc-out?)

    We chose scallops, with a decidedly Thai motif, for our main course.  There was a lot going on across the plate within the circle formed by five perfectly seared, medium size scallops - a couple of fresh kaffir leaves, some seared zucchini, curry sauce with a bit of foam, sweet white corn, and a handful of cashews.  It came together like the finest symphony.

    Dessert presented a rare dilemma - six very different but nearly equally appealing options.  We continued with the well-established corn theme and ordered a blueberry upside down cake with a creamy scoop of sweet corn ice cream.  The cake was luscious and moist and warm and went seamlessly with the ice cream.  A dozen fresh blueberries and mint paired nicely as well.

    Talula's is great for a special occasion, or really any occasion.  We'll be back, hopefully on a day when we can enjoy the garden in all its glory.

    What can I say about Talula's Garden that other's haven't already said? Chances are, you don't doubt that Talula's Garden is great, so I won't try to convince you of that, but more try to describe what Talula's Garden is, so you can know whether or not you want to come here for a date, special occassion, brunch or whatever the case may be!

Talula's Garden also has an outdoor dining area that is really really beautiful, it's like someone planted a secret garden and you're invited in! Talula's manages to be classy, while also being cozy. You can tell what their restaurant philosophy is because all their waitstaff dresses in a very down-to-earth kind of way, no doubt to invoke ideas of farming or gardening.

    Service: Service at Talula's was great! Our server, honestly seemed like he was near the end of his shift, so he was perhaps a tad impatient, but he was still helpful, suggestive, and polite. It took a while for our food to come out, but an extremely large party had just been sat, and their orders taken immediately prior to my girlfriend and I, so, the wait was understandable, and not by any means unreasonable.

    Food: I didn't even remember the wait, seriously. My girlfriend and I are a bunch of brunch fiends, and we were both blown away by the food at Talula's Garden. We started off with an order of Lemon-Ricotta Zeppolis, and we were shocked. More often than not, my experience with Zeppoli's have been oily, and dense (though not entirely a bad thing). But these were different, these fried balls of joy were surprisingly light and airy! It came served with lemon curd and what I imagine was a blackberry puree, between the two citrus' even though we were eating such a heavy appetizer, the appetizer remained bright and light, whetting our appetite for our entrees. I was literally scraping my plate with my knife and licking it.

    I ordered the steak and eggs, while my girlfriend ordered a quiche. My steak was fantastic! It was cooked rare (as it ought to be), and it was served with a hash that had little pieces of pickled okra, really smart move. The pickled okra managed to brighten up the entire dish and help keep the steak and eggs from feeling to dense or heavy.

    My girlfriend's quiche was delightful. I'm honestly at a bit of a loss for how else to describe it, and I suppose that's really the secret of Talula's. The food is simple, there's no real hidden tricks, it's just great, fresh ingredients, prepared well. My Girlfriend's quiche was a great example of this, it was eggs, with some veg and meat baked into it. Nothing special, but everything tasted fantastic. There were no complex interplays of textures or flavors, but just honest, fresh food, which is really the charm of Talula's.

    Things I didn't like: No draft crafts. What? Why? It's Summer! Where is the Victory Summer Love? The Dogfish? The Yards? Talula's is all about local production, local, fresh production. Why not support the local craft beer brewers!? What's the deal?

    If I had a fault, it's that Talula's can be a little TOO polished, and finessed. When I order steak and eggs, I imagine and want, a steak sizzling in a cast iron skillet with a thick crust, tossed with some butter, with a side of potatos (and pickled Okra, seriosuly such a great combo, bravo). Instead, I got a beautiful, composed, focused plate, with sliced steak, cut at a bias for me. My steak and eggs was just a little TOO polished and clean, I could use a little less polish at times, and a little more homey/cozy.

    I'd come to this garden all day and any day... if I could afford it!

STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
