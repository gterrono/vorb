    I recently came here on a Saturday night for a friend's birthday dinner. It didn't feel like an overly-trendy Steven Starr restaurant, so that was a plus. It really does feel like a legit English pub, only bigger, cleaner and much better organized than anyplace you'd find in London. But the music selection was waaay off-base. That's the only thing that distracted from the otherwise spot-on ambiance.

    The beer selection was fantastic, and the food was amazing! Fish balls, pork pate, Cumberland sausages were all fantastic, but the pork tenderloin stole the show. Our server Mary was great; fun but professional, and great with making suggestions.

    No complaints from anyone in our party. I would definitely visit again.

    I went to Dandelion yesterday for lunch and I must say it was not bad. A little hard to find because of the 4x4 sign, and once we found the entrance, we still were lost because we went in the wrong door. Luckily, a hostess greeted us and showed us the way to our table. I'm always interested to see the beverage selection because most places in Philly are lacking with their selections. I wasn't overly impressed, but the list was better than most. My fiancé and I ordered a glass of Riesling which took quite sometime, and then the server came back and told us that she tripped over her shoes and spilled our wine and that is why it took so long. I don't know why I needed to know that, but it didn't make the wait any better.
    We started with the chicken liver parfait which was absolutely fantastic! I chuckled though when I went to dig in and had no fork. OOps. However it was so  flavorful and delicious. Couldn't have been better. And the cherries and brioche toast were a perfect complement. Next, I had the PEI Mussels which were good, but not great and way over priced. My fiancé had the fish and chips which he said was great, but who charges $20 for fish and chips. I mean honestly. We finished with dessert which was nothing to write home about it.
    I didn't find anything dramatically wrong with service, but I think that the food runners can take 5 minutes to explain the dishes instead of mumbling and running off like their in a marathon. Also, when servers place silverware on the table they should be sure to have a fork and knife and always remember the fork goes on the left and the knife on the right.
    If you happen to find Dandelion, be sure to try the chicken liver parfait.

    The good - food is spectacular.  Fish and chips are some of the best I've ever had and I haven't been disappointed by any of the entrees or apps.  I love the deviled eggs with the hint of curry in the yolk.  Also, this place is pretty kid friendly.  My brother and sister-in-law brought their one-year-old here and he had a great time.  It's just loud enough that kids won't disturb anyone.  And as cheesy as it is, I actually like the faux English pub decor and atmosphere.  Just don't pretend like this actually is an authentic English pub.

    The bad - dear lord this place is expensive!  Even by Starr standards.  The beers are particularly egregious.  $8-10 for a pint that you can get anywhere else in the city!  I understand maybe for some more unknown or rare beers, but seriously, I can get Victory, or even a Wells Bombardier at dozens of other bars for a lot less.  The drink prices are just too damn high to make it worth going to on a regular basis.

    I came here with 3 friends on a Friday as soon as the restaurant opened. When we checked Dandelion's website before going it didn't say anything about serving from different menus on different days so we expected to order some omelets and other delicious breakfast goodies. Unfortunately, we were told upon arrival that the breakfast and brunch menus are only served on the weekends (which we probably should have assumed) and were handed the lunch menu.

    As a hearty omnivore I had no problem picking out my meal, but my vegetarian friend was pretty much limited to the salad menu and one or two appetizers. We were all a little disappointed by the lack of plates from which he could safely choose, given that most places in this price range in Philly are usually a little bit more diverse in their offerings.

    That disappointment aside, everyone ended up enjoying their meals immensely. We split a deviled egg appetizer and, between the 4 of us we, wound up with steak tartare, shepherd's pie, a caesar salad, and really delicious risotto. The free coffee refills (which came frequently and in abundance) were also greatly appreciated. We also took full advantage of their "complimentary fizzy water," which was both amusing and a nice touch.

    The most striking thing about Dandelion is its building and the way in which it is decorated: the space is made up of two conjoined structures and spans two full floors. Like many Starr restaurants, Dandelion is themed and in this case each room is individually decorated and beautifully detailed. We were seated as a nice table in the main dining room on the second floor. It was sunny and quiet and lovely until around 12:30pm when it filled up with suits from the area on their lunch breaks.

    I'd go back.

    I was walking back with my boyfriend from the art museum, and we needed to kill some time before a show at the World Cafe.  I dreamt of a place where I could cozy up by a fire, drink a nice drink, and snack a little bit.  I searched my yelp app for "fireplace," and the Dandelion Pub was listed first.  Hoping the review meant Dandelion did, indeed, have a fireplace, we meandered our way to the pub.  It was around 5 on a Sunday, so it was quiet inside.  Upon entering, we found the fireplace immediately!  I was overjoyed.  No one was sitting there (thankfully to our 5 pm Sunday arrival).  

    Our server was super nice.  We ordered a pitcher of dark and stormys....she said that sounded like it'd be a fun time.  It was!  We also got a side of the swiss chard and parsnips, and the salmon appetizer.  

    The parsnips were really amazing.  I'd never had one before - they are so meaty.  I love trying new veg and being inspired to cook it myself.  The swiss chard was great too, though perhaps a bit too oily/buttery for my taste.  I never like salmon, so I was not super in love with the cured salmon app.  

    The dark and stormys were delicious too.  At $38 a pop, it's not cheap... but I was appropriately buzzed.  I'd say there were about 5 glasses in the pitcher.  

    The fireplace was well-tended by our "fireplace wench."  She added a log to the flame two or three times over the course of the meal.  The vibe inside is delightful; a dimly-lit, old English pub.  I felt transported back to the days of Charles Dickens.

    We concluded the meal with a piece of pumpkin pecan pie.  A bit underwhelming.  But I'm so in love with the fireplace that I'll return for relief from the cold that will inevitably insue this winter.

Specialties

London's culinary revolution comes to Philadelphia with this eclectic English pub. Cozy rooms and intimate bars create a storied, Old World aura, while updated seasonal takes on British food make up the menu. In keeping with tradition, cask and tap craft beers are always rotating, a proper afternoon tea service has a sweet and savory menu, and classic beef family-style roasts are served on Sundays.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
