    Jim's
    When in Philly, I guess you pretty much have to get a cheesesteak.  And I have been eating pretty close to vegan for a while.  But,cheesesteak it was going to be.  

    So, which place?  Many said that you have to go to Pat and Geno's.  But I was a little skeptical because it is actually on the touristy map they give you at hotels.  When pressed, everyone working at the Westin told me to go to Jim's at fourth and south street.  They said it was much better and a local's favorite.

    I can't tell you how it compares to Geno's but I can tell you that it was great.  You order downstairs and have a choice of cheese (whiz, provolone or cheddar) and vegetables.  I really didn't understand the whiz thing at all since I think that stuff is utterly disgusting so I got the provolone and light onions.  It was fantastic.  I also don't get the people slathering ketchup all over it.   A cheesesteak should be about the meat and the cheese (in my humble don't eat much meat opinion).

    The meat was lean and melts in your mouth. I simply cannot imagine what happened with a recent reviewer who said the meat was dry.  I may be mostly vegetarian but I cook everything and this was great. Perfectly cooked although I did add a little salt which is rare for me.  The mix of provolone and onions was fabulous.  The portion size was definitely enough to share between two women but I was alone and ate the whole thing!  I ended up skipping dinner because I was so full.

    You order on the lower floor and there are a few counter seats on that floor.  I opted for going upstairs which has plenty of decent seating.

    I walked there and back and it was a great walk.  Philadelphia is a wonderful city which such amazing history and culture.   Being in between NYC and Washington DC, I think it sometimes gets forgotten about or at least has to play third fiddle.  I will be back when I can take the time to do more exploring.

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
