    Café chismosa is a gem of a place in Northern Liberties.  I'm a tough critic, and I was won over.  

    Before I get to the food, I want to highlight the warm and inviting decor, and a layout that makes good use of the small space.  (Pizza brain: take notes!)

    We ordered two breakfasts.  I initially ordered breakfast tacos, with chorizo (sausage), beautifully prepared with what looked like pickled onion or beets.  (It's a colorful plate).  My associate ordered a breakfast panini.

    What a great idea!  Paninis keep food warm, add a nice crust and browning.  

    In fact I was still hungry, and eager to try out the Cafe, I ordered the John Wayne's huevos... Not an everyday order at $10, but definitely worth trying.  Suffice it to say, it was excellent (and the panini toast is a great touch - what a great way to keep toast warm, crunchy, and soft inside).

    I like the place, and I like the owner -- he aspires to create plates the engage the senses without becoming pretentious.

    I'm really into this place. So much so that I have been the past two Saturdays for brunch. I love that its just a few small tables and never a wait. I love a place where the chef comes out and tells you about the food too. The coconut latte is insanely good!! I could drink one every day and be happy. They also make their own hot sauces that you can choose from (the pepitas is the best!) and the food is pretty unique and quite delicious. It's not the best breakfast I have ever had or anything, but I don't need the best breakfast in the world every weekend. I think the chilaquiles I had today were definitely worth ordering again, but I wouldn't do the frittata another time. They serve Reanimator coffee (plus pour overs!) and sell it for cheaper than anywhere else you will get in the city. The mix of Asian and Hispanic decor and vibe is pretty cool and unusual, and for some reason they are always playing songs that make me nostalgic about being 16. Bonus: you can bring your dog inside!

    Me and a couple friends were in NoLibs on a Saturday afternoon and have been to the traditional brunch faves in the area and decided to try something different. We stumbled on Cafe Chismosa, which has a cute, inviting door and specials board outside. We were greeted by a friendly gentlemen behind the counter, who I think is the owner.

    I had a coconut latte, which was to die for. My friend tried the hibiscus tea, equally as addictive.

    For brunch, we tried the cous cous salad with shrimp, which was fresh and light yet filling and well balanced, the wild mushroom quesadilla, which was the best quesadilla I can remember, and the torta ahogada with chicken, a decadent chicken sandwich soaked in a rich, flavorful broth / sauce.

    The ambiance was cozy, food and drinks wonderful, and overall a great brunch / lunch. Have since returned several times for the to die for coconut lattes and have tried some other quesadillas and sandwiches, all of which were similarly impressive. I also love their housemade sauces and salsas. So happy to have found Cafe Chismosa!!

Specialties

Now open in Northern Liberties...A Latin inspired Cafe serving ceviches, tortas, quecas (quesadillas), avocado salads, home-made salsas, the freshest guacamole in the hood.  We serve locally roasted coffee by ReAnimator, espresso, smoothies, fresh squeezed juices & mexican sodas.  For the early risers we open @8,and make a great spinach, egg & cheese & pick up fresh bagels every morning. Come on by and hang out--listen to an LP from our extensive record collection while you use our free WiFi.
