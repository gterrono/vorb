    I really wanted to like this place.  For lunch, I was at Davios and had an amazing experience with their food and service.  So for dinner, I was really expecting Morimoto's to match up in respect to service.

    Service really fell short at Morimoto's.  My waitress might have been a new girl and was really awkward at times, but sweet.  She was really nice but always got really close to my face.  I sat at the sushi bar, so we were at eye level when she spoke to us.  She just made me uncomfortable.

    For the food, my date and I ordered the omakase 150pp.  The first dish was terrible.  It was a tuna toro tartar type of dish.  The tartar was sitting in a bath of salty broth.  I really really really wanted to send it back and have the chef try it himself.  It was so salty; my date and I decided to just leave it unfinished instead of sending our first dish back.

    My favorite dish was the whitefish carpaccio, it was thinly sliced with yuzu soy and hot oil.  This dish is on the regular menu.

    For the price, it was a good value.  They are very generous with their portions here, so you definitely won't be hungry after the omakase.

    I personally don't plan on returning.  The food just isn't that awesome.

    Where do I begin with Morimoto? Other then that Masaharu is one of my all time favorite iron chef! So you can imagine my enthusiasm before even trying this place.

Although the dish was delicious, I did have a hard time eating the rare duck as it was cut way too thick.     My husband & I dined here on Friday 9/26 for dinner at 7pm. We came here for our first wedding anniversary. We were seated promptly at 7.
    Service was great. We had and attentive waiter. All the food we had was the right temperature.

    Drinks:
    I ordered the white pear cocktail they have on their menu, and my husband got a Stella. I'm a sucker for anything with Elderflower liqueur, so I liked my drink a lot. It even had these pretty edible flowers floating around in it.

    My husband & I shared all of the following dishes.

    For appetizers:
    We ordered the rock shrimp tempura. Nice, hot & crispy.
    We got 2 sushi rolls: spicy salmon & yellowtail scallion. They were both really good.
    For soup, we got dobin mushi. It was served in a teapot with some chicken, mushroom, & shrimp floating in it. The waiter that brought it told us that the chef recommended to drink the broth separately from the solids. My husband especially liked the flavor of the broth.

    For entrees:
    The uni carbonara. Had sea urchin on top. I never had sea urchin before, and the flavor really melded well with the richness of the carbonara.
    Duck duck duck. What can I say about this amazing dish. The duck is cooked medium rare & tastes sort of like a steak. I never had duck cooked like this, but it was amazing. It came with a side of duck confit fried rice, topped with a sunny side up duck egg. The chef recommends to break the yolk & mix it in with the fried rice. I think it was the best fried rice I have ever had in my life. Not kidding! I could eat that fried rice for every meal. Yum.

    At the end of the night, our waiter brought each of us a glass of sparkling wine with a tiny scoop of pear sorbet, on the house, because it was our anniversary! So nice, and a nice end to the meal.

    I really can't say anything bad about this restaurant.

    I was in Philadelphia a couple weeks ago for work, and was asking some locals if there were some good places I should try for lunch or dinner.  Of course they mentioned a few different cheese steak places, but when someone mentioned that Morimoto had a restaurant near the office, I was immediately on board.  I'm a fan of the show Iron Chef, so I wanted to take advantage of trying this place while I was in town.  I was not disappointed at all.

    The restaurant isn't huge, but it has a very modern feel to it.  There are all sorts of neat decorations.  As for the food, this is mainly a sushi style restaurant, but they do have several entrees as well.  We came here for lunch, as it seems to be quite a bit less expensive to come for lunch than dinner.  I came here with one of my coworkers, and we split a few rolls of sushi.  I believe we got the spicy tuna, california, and the shrimp tempura rolls.  Everything was outstanding.  You could just tell that the quality of the ingredients was top notch.  I get it that some people aren't sushi fans, but if you want to try it, this would certainly be a good place to do so.  As you would expect from a place like this, service was also excellent.  If I'm back in Philadelphia anytime soon, I'll definitely be heading back to Morimoto.  Well done!

    We went to Morimoto for our second night's dinner. I've been to Morimoto in NYC, so I was expecting what to get and how good it would be, as I think it fit into the category, maybe slightly more than what I have expected.

    Compared to NYC one, it was much smaller, as it is only 1 floor (I guess), and it was much quieter. There was awkwardly located light in the middle of table, which made us little bit uncomfortable to share our dishes, but there was no way that we could remove it, so - whatever.

    As an appetizer, we got Toro Tartare and bone marrow spread (day's special) with ikura and shiso leaf pesto. The bone marrow was extremely delicious and the server was very attentive to get us extra baguette "before" we asked for - which we loved!

    We ordered Uni Carbonara as our noodles dish, and we LOVED IT. Nowadays there are many places that have uni pastas, but this place had one of the best flavored dish - yet it was a little bit salty.

    As an entree, we had Duck Duck Duck, as it was highly recommended by the server. One of my party was a huge duck lover and she told me that their duck dish was better than one of NYC's upscale Michelin rated restaurant.

    We also ordered $60 of Chef's Combination of sushi, sashimi and rolls. It was "okay" I guess, but as Morimoto is not like a sushi-ya that only or highly focuses on classical sushi/sashimi, it was okay-leveled. But their tamago was exceptional delicious. Tamago that I've been looking for!

    As desserts, because we didn't order too much of food (I GUESS HAHA), Yuzu Merigue Tart (which was too sweet for my guest - but she liked the freshness of yuzu and mango that brought), Matcha Tres Leches was extremely moist and deliciously sweet, and Black Sesame Moussecake, which was loved by all 3 of us and it had such a rich flavor in.

    Overall, we loved the dinner as we were pretty much satisfied with most of our dishes and as the server reallyx100 friendly and approaching. I would visit this place again if I visit Philadelphia anytime soon but I will also often visit Morimoto in NYC.

Specialties

Iron Chef Masaharu Morimoto elevates Japanese flavors and creates sushi that is sublime in this smart eatery. Enjoy an Omakase menu -- a meal custom designed by the chef -- or mix and match from the incredible menu of a la carte creations.

History

Established in 2001.

eatery. Enjoy an Omakase menu -- a meal custom designed by the chef -- or mix and match from the
incredible menu of a la carte creations.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
