    I went to Moriarty's at dinner time around 6:30 on a Saturday night.  Though there was a line to wait, it moved fast and we were seated in a matter of 5 minutes.  

    My friend and I both ordered the crab cake entree.  The whole time I'm looking at the extensive menu, I'm thinking to myself, why am I in the mood for crabcakes at an IRISH pub???  Luckily, I made a good choice and the food was excellent!

    This crab cake entree entree included a small side salad.  The greens were fresh, chopped nicely, and served prompted by our waitress.  The crab cakes were cooked just right and served hot with a good tasting tartar sauce.  The vegetables that came with the entree were also tasty, fresh and sliced nice and thin - always a winner!  A baked potato was also served with  the entree.  I did have to ask for sour cream which later, I noticed on the bill, I was charged 80 cents for requesting.  

    The bartender, Jennifer, was friendly, very sweet, and kind to us.  She was a pleasant conversationalist who made our dinner a fun one!  Overall a very good meal with reasonable prices.

    The Irish music that is played adds to the ambiance of the Irish bar!  It's not too loud and overbearing, you are still able to hold a conversation with the people you are dining with in the restaurant.

    I will admit that I had all but edged Moriarty's out of my barring/dining repertoire several years ago after a few bad service incidents.  I was grudgingly taken back by a friend last year, and have now been popping in once or twice a month for solid bar food and beers.  I don't know if they have re-staffed, but recent service has all been friendly.  

    The addition of the outdoor tables along the side street provides a great place to catch a little sun without the immediate presence of passing buses, as tends to happen with Philly outdoor dining.

    All of the food that I have had at Moriarty's has been solidly above average.  They have tasty beer-battered fries, the sandwiches are good, and the burgers are decent.  The giant steroid chicken wings do live up to the hype, but know ahead of time that there is NO dainty or polite way to eat them.  It is an experience meant to be shared only by siblings, the best of friends, or significant others who will not judge you for needing four wet-naps.  

    The beer list is quite solid, and the drafts are generously sized and reasonably priced.

    There is usually a pretty mixed clientele that varies with time of day.  It seems to be a curiously popular stop for post-matinee dining from the Walnut Street theater.  While the theme is not "sports bar" there are plenty of TVs at the bar, and a good crowd usually assembles to watch whatever the big game of any given season or day is.

    All in all, a solid bar experience!

    Listed in The Best of Philadelphia

    It is time to get serious.  

    Moriarty's has the best hot wings in Philadelphia.  This isn't my opinion.  This is an irrefutable fact.  The same way that you rely on the sun rising in the east, you can also rely on Moriarty's to serve the best hot wings in Philly.  Don't even look at the menu.  Just order the hot wings.  If you don't like hot wings, reevaluate where your life took a wrong turn and order them so that you can take the first step towards positive change.

    The flats and drumsticks are still connected and they are huge.  One order is enough to satisfy two adults.  The hot sauce has the perfect amount of heat and tang.  The wings come with celery and carrots so that you can diversify your major food group intake.  3 out of 4 of the major food groups are represented in one order of Moriarty's hot wings(these groups being meat, plant, and dressing).  The only food group that the order lacks is beer, which you can easily purchase to help wash down the wings.

    I've systematically tried most other places in Philadelphia that have great hot wing reviews and I am reliably saddened each time.  These other places that may have a more creative variety of wings(honey caribbean, teriyaki, etc.) but absolutely none of them have hot wings that stack up to those that you can consume at Moriarty's.
