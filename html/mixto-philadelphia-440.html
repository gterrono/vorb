    My friend and I decided to eat lunch at Mixto the other day. A small Caribbean restaurant located on S. Pine Street in Old City. Everything resembled paradise from the palm trees that lined the sidewalk outside to their fresh flowers and shrubs. In honor of eating in the Caribbean I ordered their "bikini martini" garnished with a thick slice of pineapple. The drink was delicious, a little pricey, but defiantly worth the splurge.  My friend and I started off with their guacamole and baked banana chips. Believe me it tastes as good as it sounds! For our entrees, I ordered the "Pollo Mareado" which combined my two favorite foods, chicken and shrimp served with an ancho chili sauce. My friend ordered the "Ensalada de Espinaca con queso de cabra" fresh spinach salad with a variety of fruits. She ate every bite! Two drinks, two entrees, and one appetizer had our bill at about fifty dollars. Not bad for a lunch trip to the Caribbean!

    Listed in The Yelp 100 challenge, You said this has HOW many calories!?, Tourist-Michael, Meat-Cheat List

    If these five stars were to rely on the food alone, they would be solid. When you combine them with the best service I have ever received, well I wish I could add more.

    Mixto is a Latin American restaurant in a nice romantic downtown strip area, and being an out-of-towner I relied on Yelp to find me a winner. Yelp came through (as always). Mixto has solid reviews and stature in the community.

    We were seated immediately in a comfortable booth. The atmosphere is romantic and lends a cozy environment while not being skimpy on space. There is a downstairs and upstairs it looked like, and the aromas wafting through the air will have your tongue tantalizing  with every other breeze on new dishes you've yet to obtain.

    The prices are very fair, figure around 25$/per person for some real quality dining. The wait staff here were very friendly - I thought I must have been on some hidden camera show with this level of service. The gentleman waiting on us kept a smile and cool-professional demeanor. When asking about the sides, he even threw in a couple of small extras that really went the extra mile as far as our enjoyment went (without us even asking.)

    We caught the end of happy hour, so I had a 5$ mojito in a Tom Collins glass. The mint was not muddled very well, but 5-6 seconds of straw-muddling easily took care of that - no problem. It was good, strong and lime-y as a good mojito should be.

    We started off with a plantain topped with ground beef and cheese which really whet our appetite for the main course. I ordered the scallops (19$ I believe - thankfully covered by my work) which came with rice and red beans, asparagus, potatoes and some other sides. The scallops were so tender and buttery it was easy to forget to even chew it! I have never had scallops so good before in my life. Hands down the best.

    Miss Sarit ordered fried plantain with pulled pork and cheese with a tangy sauce on the side. It was so good, and we enjoyed them both so much that we wound up sharing each other's plates.

    As we were brought the cheque, the waiter said "Thank you for choosing Mixto. And remember, if you're ever in the area again - we are here for you." That really sealed the deal and made this the best dining experience I have had in a very long time.

    Thank you Mixto. I will be back, and it is good to know you are there for me. :)
