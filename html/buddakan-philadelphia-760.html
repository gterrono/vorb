    Needless to say, Starr Restaurant, so the service and ambiance were top notch. Probably the best dining service I've ever had.

    Edamame Dumplings- they were supperrr squishy for dumplings like melted in your mouth but not in a good way. The edamame was a puree inside. The sauce wasn't very good either.     Dragon Tofu- OFF MENU ITEM (tell you're waiter you're vegan/vegetarian). Delicious and spicy. Fried tofu with spicy glaze on top of a bed of sauteed stir fry

    Vegetable Fried Rice with Coconut Foam- The rice was just normal coconut fried rice. Really well paired with the dragon tofu. Wouldn't get this soley as an entree. Idk WHAT that foam was.... it didn't have a taste... idk really.

    Red Dragon drink (name?)- I was tipsy after a sip probably. It had tequila and thai chili..oh man.. tough to sip through that one. Mojito for the next drink-best mojito I've had since Nicaragua! YAY.

    Like I normally say, when it comes to Asian food, I tend to like hole in the wall places in Chinatown. I would give this food a 3.5-4 but the experience a 5 so pushing them up!

    My visit to Buddakan started off weak, but they quickly turned it around!

    My friend made a reservation for Buddakan for Restaurant Week. Needless to say, it was packed... even at 9 PM on a Wednesday! Unfortunately they weren't ready for us when we got there, so we had wait for 10-15 minutes even after they confirmed we already had a reservation. We had a long sit on the comfy couch at the front of the restaurant (I guess that made the wait better). I loved the lighting near the front of the restaurant, but the interior was just dark. For some reason, the Buddha wasn't as big as I thought it would be.

    I ordered the chicken dumplings, Chilean sea bass, and donuts for Restaurant Week dinner. My friends and I made plans to hit up McDonald's afterwards because there was no way we'd be full after... Buddakan showed us wrong. The chicken dumplings just tasted like higher quality Chinatown dumplings (except with chicken instead of pork), so I'll try something more exotic next time. My friend said his sashimi was good though! Next, the Chilean sea bass was OUT OF THIS WORLD. So soft, tender, and absolutely delicious -- definitely the best Chilean sea bass I've had! This alone is the reason for my high rating... The donuts were pretty good, but I had to take it home since I was so full. My friends absolutely loved their chocolate desserts!

Specialties

Centered around a ten-and-a-half foot gold gilded Buddha, Buddakan serves family-style modern Asian cuisine. Imaginative dishes like edamame ravioli, Asian barbeque pork and lobster fried rice have made this a must-stop spot on any visit to Philly.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
