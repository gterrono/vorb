        Popped into Rosa Blanca on a Sunday night. We had been to Chifa before and we were interested to try to new place.

        Like Chifa, the place has a really cool decor. It has and old diner vibe in the front with diner booths and juke boxes. In the rear it has a nice old Cuba feel. Rosda Blanca has a lot of charm.

        On the night we were there, the restaurant was pretty empty. I hope it was just because it was Sunday.

        I had the Cubano sandwich. I order this quite bit and Rosa Blanca's was very good and not too badly priced at $9.00. The bread was good and the meats tasty. I had the soup of the day, a roasted tomato and chicken soup and that was pretty good also. My daughter had the Spanish omelette and she liked that quite a bit. It was really nicely presented. My wife order the fish of the day and that was pretty pricey at $22.00. It was just OK, and not really what we expected from a Garces restaurant. It was bland, a bit salty and fishy. We had Cafe Con Leche and as Michelle C. wrote that was served luke warm and was nothing special either. We finished with the Rosa Blanca version of Tres Leches. They do it with chocolate and banana and this was light but nothing close to fantastic. We did start with the guacamole with roasted pineapple. That was really, really good.

        Rosa Blanca's menu is a little perplexing also. There are several items that have a cool Cuban diner flare and that are reasonably prices, especially for a Garces restaurant. These include sandwiches, breakfast all day, etc. Then there are the other items, the entrees in the $18-30 range.

        I will give Rosa Blanca a try again. It hit on some things for me and missed on some others.

        We had a thoroughly pleasant experience at Rosa Blanca.   We ordered the $35 Para La Familia tasting menu so we could try lots of different things.  The best guacamole ever, with house-roasted pineapple.  A delicious, ultra-fresh frisee salad.  Fresh-baked cheese bread with guava butter.   Maybe a bit more fried food than was absolutely necessary, with both ham and bacalao croquettes, the latter served with a garlic aioli that would kill a vampire at 20 paces.  Two main dishes (!), a shrimp and rice combo and the best ropa vieja I've ever had. The shrimp themselves were so sweet and fresh, it made up for the fact that the dish was not especially interesting.   I might have forgotten one or two things, it felt as if the food would never stop flowing from the kitchen.  A spectacular custard dessert with layers of passion fruit and mango, and a mango sorbet.  We left with a shopping bag of food-- probably two more meals.  Service was excellent, though I don't know that the servers need to wear tuxedos in a casual, diner-style restaurant.  We will definitely return.

        Chifa, I miss you so very much. Truth be told Chifa was my favorite Jose Garces restaurant, so I am still a little bitter that its no longer there. That being said, the food at Rosa Blanca is very good. Its not all Cuban food, which threw me off a bit at first because I thought it was supposed to be a Cuban diner. But the feeling you get walking in the door definitely takes you to a Cuban state of mind.

        My fiance and I ordered the Para la Familia, which is an option of chefs choices for the table. We had the pineapple guacamole with yucca chips, ensalada verde, empanadas de queso, croquettas de bacalao, ropa vieja, enchilado de camarones, y espinacas. We also ordered a mojito and a Juana Rodriguez. The mojito was pretty lackluster. Especially for a Cuban restaurant, it really needs to be improved. It was strong, but the flavor of the mojito definitely needs to be enhanced. My fiance's Juana Rodriguez was very tasty. It was a drink complete with white rum, raspberry, cava and lemon, that all combined to form a delicious citrusy island feeling cocktail.

        I thought the yucca chips needed to be crispier, but the guacamole was fantastic. The pineapple really starred. The ensalada verde was a throw away dish that really didnt do much for us. We really liked the empanadas de queso and croquettas de bacalao were both very good. Whatever spice they used in the ropa vieja, whether it was nutmeg or cinnamon, was overpowering, but the dish was good nonetheless. The enchilado de carmarones was very tasty. It had a spicy tomato based sauce on it that made the shrimp really flavorful. As always we loved the maduros as well. Fried sweet plantains are fantastic! Our dessert was bomb, a delicious custard with mango sorbet. It may have been the sweetest and most flavorful sorbet I have ever had. It tasted like I was eating a frozen fresh mango. Outstanding.

        The cortadito I ordered was very good in flavor, but it was served lukewarm. If it was hot it would have been outstanding. Hopefully they can make some improvements. It has a lot of potential, there were some really high notes, but some pretty low ones as well. If anyone can right the ship, its Jose Garces.

      Specialties

      Cuban comfort food, from Chef Jose Garces' dream diner. Combining the laid-back, all-day appeal of a diner with the innately comforting character of classic Cuban fare, Rosa Blanca is the best of Latin and American culinary culture from Philadelphia's top Latin-American chef: Jose Garces.

      History

      Established in 2005.

      Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

      Meet the Business Owner

      Jose G. Business Owner

      Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
