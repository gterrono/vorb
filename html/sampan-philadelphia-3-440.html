    I'm actually quite shocked that I haven't already written a review for Sampan yet for as much as my boyfriend and I go there.

    We live close by and going out for happy hour is almost a struggle because we want to try new places but we are just so in love with Sampan that we don't want to go anywhere else!

    The food specials are what makes their Happy Hour so great. They offer so many items and they are so cheap! We end up eating enough to cover dinner and don't spend a lot doing so.

    Here are our faves:
    Korean BBQ Beef Satay - $3
    ---Soo tender and delicious!

    Spicy Chicken Wings - $4
    ---Spicy and sweet, served with some delicious carrot cucumber salad.

    Edamame Dumplings - $4
    ---You must try these! They are infused with truffle oil and are served in sake broth. Easily my favorite thing on the menu.

    Pork Bao Buns - $4
    ---Pork belly and mint with some sort of jelly? Whatever it is, it's good. I love the buns!

    Kim Chee Fried Rice - $4
    ---I'm still shocked at how big the portion is on this dish...especially for 4 bucks. It's a must if you're trying to fill up!

    They offer a $3 beer and I want to say a $4 or $5 cocktail of the day? I have noticed that the cocktails can be a little on the weaker side, so we just stick to the beer of the day instead.

    Like I said, best happy hour in town, and it's offered 7 days a week! Do yourself a favor if you're even slightly into Asian food and go to Sampan!... but get there early, the bar and outdoor seating fills up fast!

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge, Food in Philly

    I don't think I have *ever* been to a better happy hour. The quality of the food is outstanding and the prices are unbelievably low.

    I particularly loved the pork bao buns--the pork belly was deep-fried so it was crispy/crunchy on the outside and fatty/luscious on the inside. The duck egg rolls were superb--the richness of the duck mingled well with the slight tang and spiciness of the accompanying peanut dipping sauce.

    Shrimp/lobster dumplings came served in a miso broth, and the seafood was sweet, firm, and fresh. The pork dumplings were great, too--jam-packed with flavorful ground pork and topped with kimchi.

    It is easy to put together a whole meal out of the tapas-like happy hour dishes which include various dumplings, hot dogs and bao, side dishes, meat skewers, etc.

    Oh, did I mention that no food item we ordered cost more than $4?! Crazy!

    I had a cucumber-lime cocktail that was only $4 because it was the cocktail special of the evening. It was nicely balanced, not too sweet, and cooling--perfect for a sultry end-of-summer afternoon.

    I would call this happy hour an absolute go-to for snacks or dinner in the city. Next time, I want to try the kimchi fried rice. Two dudes next to us ordered that and they raved about how wonderful it was.

    I went for restaurant week - they offer their chef's tasting menu for a price of $35/pp (compared to $45 normally.) Instead of offering separate meals for everyone dining, you collectively choose a total of 7 dishes + homemade ice cream.  

    SMALL (2 choices)
    * General Tso's Soup Dumpling - one of my favorite dishes of the night.  The General Tso's flavor wasn't as syrupy/fake as sometimes Chinese food can be, but the dumpling had tremendous flavor from the soup and the chicken.  
    * Tuna Rice Cracker - Great presentation of what appears to be a little tuna meatball on a small rice cracker, with just the hint of chili oil.  Extremely flavorful.  The chili oil is a really nice touch that gives the dish just enough heat without taking over your palate for the rest of the meal.

    SATAY (1 choice)
    * Korean BBQ Beef - the beef was good - nothing outstanding.  No complaints.

    COLD & HOT (1 choice)
    * Calamari Salad - I didn't know that lightly fried calamari could ever be described as 'refreshing' - but the salad greens, apple, dressing and calamari combination made for a light and nice break after some more intense flavors.

    MEAT (1 choice)
    * Pekin Duck - really "fun" dish.  The duck comes paired with house-made scallion buns that you can use to either make a sandwich or taco with.  Even as an adult it's still fun to use your hands and be playful with food.  The duck was tender and the scallion buns with some hoisin and cucumber was a great flavor combination.  

    FISH (1 choice)
    * Spice Crusted Tuna - the way the tuna was prepared was perfect.  Perfectly seared with a flavorful and complex rub/crust.  No complaints.

    SIDE (1 choice)
    * Kim Chee Fried Rice - Richly flavored and up a few notches from your standard fried rice.  Paired nicely with both of the main dishes.

    The service was extremely attentive, friendly, and helpful.  A dirty/empty serving dish didn't sit more than about 30 seconds on the table before it was cleared.

    Great environment and decor.

    All in all, fantastic experience.

    Overall a fantastic example of what an Asian-Fusion restaurant should be. It definitely is not on the cheap side, but the happy hour prices are excellent, especially for what you get (you have to sit at the bar though, which might take some time, so plan ahead!)

    Their happy hour menu is both well priced and full of options. Their drinks (house wine, specialty beer/cocktail) are $4 while their food options run between $2 (for the kobe beef slider and street dog) and $4 (dumplings, fried rice, etc).

    Ambiance: Absolutely fantastic. The wood  trim on the bar and the wooden floor panels add a great touch and matches well with the tables and other decor. It is somewhat dark outside of the bar, but still pretty well lit.

    Food: Talk about amazing. The menu has a great variety of options. I personally had the pork buns, kobe beef slider, and the kimchi fried rice. The pork bun is very well made. The bun accents the sauce and fried pork/scallions very well. The kobe beef slider, while still good, was a bit on the dry side. The kimchi fried rice was by far my favorite, definitely try it!

    Drinks: I had the specialty cocktail and I can't complain about it at all. My friends had some of the non-happy hour cocktails and they are definitely worth the price! Each one is adventure unto itself.

    Service: Despite the amount of people there, the service was pretty good. Most of the food came out within 10 minutes, though they did mix up my friend's order, but that's understandable considering how busy it was.
