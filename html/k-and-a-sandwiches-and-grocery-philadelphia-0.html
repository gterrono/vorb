    I visited this South Philly establishment for the first time today. The sign over the door reads "Best Hoagies in Philly" and when my friend and I walked in, the narrow shop was packed with customers- a second good sign.
    Prices are good-- under $20 for two large hoagies, drinks, and chips-- and the hoagies are huge-- like seriously overflowingly huge with extra meat and cheese and generous servings of whatever else you ask for-- and delicious-- I unwrapped mine in the car and couldn't stop eating on the drive home.
    When we got to the register the co-owner Karen (K) recognized that we weren't regulars and chatted with us for a few minutes and introduced us to her husband Allen (A). She then gave each of us a free dessert as a first-time visitor special-- lemon cake for me, sweet potato pie for my friend.
    This shop is in the Point Breeze neighborhood which is a little out of the way for me, but worth a special trip if you are looking for a great sandwich.

    I was pleasantly surprised about the quality of this sandwich (this is what prompted me to write reviews about other recent hoagie experiences).

    It's a clean small sandwich shop that seems to be grounded in customer service.
    The meat is cut to order, hence it is very fresh and seems to be of good quality.

    I ordered a Karen's Special (corned beef, spiced beef, beef pastrami, beef salami, american cheese) and it was spectacular.  I also tasted a Zoey's Gobbler Special (turkey ham, turkey salami, gourmet turkey, turkey pastrami, swiss cheese) and it was pretty good.  The sandwich is the right blend of meat & veggies and very flavorful.  

    I think this is my new go-to hoagie shop!

    This place is definitely not the best hoagies ever - as someone else wrote, but it's a good sandwich, and the prices are reasonable.

    Heard about this place from a friend and finally was in the area and tried it. They make a decent hoagie. The meat was good and the portion of meat was decent. The bread is the standard Amoroso type roll (no Sarconne's roll's here). The staff didn't seem friendly at first - no greeting or acknowledgement that your standing there waiting. I had to ask if I was in the right place to order. Once the staff actually talk with you, you get the sense that their just quite as they do there work - and just don't have great customer service skills.

    All in all, I would definitely go back if I'm in the area to try one of the cheese steaks.

    Oh, the place is very clean and organized. And the woman at the register was very nice and friendly.
