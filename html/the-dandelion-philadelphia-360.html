    I FREAKING love this place!

    I lived in England for a year and became quite accustomed to having Sunday roast. I haven't really found an OUTSTANDING pub here in the states til now.  I didn't know a ton about this city when I moved here and after walking by I bookmarked it after reading all the great reviews.

    After a big group of us had been out walking all day some of us were famished and decided to head over to The Dandelion. I called ahead and asked if we might reserve a table. Once we arrived I was overjoyed to be sat at the large family table right at the window near a fireplace. On a rainy day this quite picturesque setting, Christmas decor up, was so whimsical.

    The food is devine. Is it 100% authentic, no. Is it close enough while having a much more modern twist, yes. I had the welsh rarebit and deviled eggs as a starter and the Sunday roast for a main. Everything was extraordinary. The Yorkshire pudding was nice and crisp as it ought to be, the meat tender and juicy, the veg was seasoned so deliciously and presented in a separate cast iron dish. Absolutely devine. I am indeed a happy bunny. Everyone else seemed quite pleased with their meals. They all had the salmon but I am not a huge fan of this fish so I didn't taste it.

    The only thing lacking was the service. Our waitress was friendly and attentive enough but she did at times seem checked out and off in her own world. We had to ask for bread more than once and waited quite awhile to get it.

    Def worth going again and again. The menu is vast and well worth the somewhat pricey charges.

    We came here to celebrate my girlfriend's birthday with a large group.  Ended up being 8 people.  My girlfriend said it was easy enough to make the reservation, even with a large group so that's good to hear.

    When we arrived, we were sat at the one long table in the main room.  The decor in The Dandelion is really great.  Everything seems aged well and all works together great.

    The menu is actually pretty diverse, considering it's more of a pub than anything.  As an appetizer I tried the tartare and the foie gras.  The tartare was really great.  It was seasoned appropriately and prepared very well.  The foie gras was unlike any foie gras preparation that I've had before.  It came with an over easy duck egg and some bacon.  Was it more of a take on breakfast perhaps?  Alone the foie was good, but not amazing.  All together though it really worked and I enjoyed it very much.

    For dinner our table did a good job of ordering a variety of items from the menu.  I went with the scallops.  The scallops were cooked perfectly and the accompaniments worked great with the scallops.  Like most scallop dishes, it was pretty small so I knew splitting some appetizers to start was definitely a smart move.

    Just like the relatively large food menu, there was a great selection of drinks available.  My favorite had to be the cask sampler I got.  Not many places you can get beer served that way and most of the males at the table got the sampler.  I was a big fan and recommend giving it a shot if you're at The Dandelion.

    Overall, I was pretty pleased with The Dandelion.  Service was pretty good, the ambiance was interesting and the food and drink were fun and interesting.  Everyone had a good time and I definitely wouldn't mind coming back for another party.

    With my sister FINALLY visiting me in Philadelphia for the weekend (and being in Philly for the first time EVER), I knew that I had to take her out to dinner. We only had Saturday night available, so after  checking out what options were available on opentable.com, I gave her some choices and she thought that The Dandelion sounded interesting - especially since she had just come back from England. I grabbed one of the few reservations left with less than a week in advance.

    After a looooong day exploring the city we dragged our weary feet to the restaurant, excited to enjoy a pleasant meal. We were certainly greeted by very friendly hosts, and our server was lovely and quite attentive throughout the evening. My fiance ordered a pint on draft, and my sister got a bottle of hard pear cider. We decided to split the pickled beetroot jar to start, and after chatting and waiting for a little while one of the hosts brought it over, apologizing for the delay since a waiter had accidentally spilled it so they scrambled to get another one - and therefore they comped our appetizer! It was absolutely delicious, the beets were tender and tangy, and the whipped goat cheese and olive tapenade were both scrumptious, individually and together. They were so good, we used the bread still in our bread basket to clean the little jars!

    For our entrees, my fiance got the lamb shepherd's pie, which is deceptively small because it is seriously hearty and filling (I snagged a taste, which was a feat since he cleaned that bowl out). My sister and I shared the mussels, which came in a creamy apple-mustard-tarragon sauce with a toasted baguette begging to be dipped in. We also got the sauteed swiss chard and honey glazed parsnips, both of which were delicious although the parsnips were borderline too sweet for my tastes. We were quite satiated, but wanted to try a dessert. Our server suggested trying the Eton Mess (a parfait of meyer lemon curd, huckleberry compote, crunchy meringue, and crème fraiche) since it was a light and fresh dessert - and it was a great suggestion. From the service to the food, The Dandelion provided a truly pleasurable dining experience, and we'll have to come back to try the fish and chips we keep hearing about!

    After tons of buildup and hype from everyone I knew, I finally got to have brunch here, and I have to say I was contemplating just 3 stars, but what won me over eventually was the ambience.  If you are expecting English gastropub food, well, that's what you get here.  Not many places serve it in Philly.

    The pub's interior design, while not authentic, mimics it quite nicely, in an understated fashion, from the nice hardwood everywhere to the unmatching chairs to the rambling hallways and rooms.  There's a real fireplace in the entry room and 2 bars.  There's soccer on TV.

    We all had the English breakfast, with blood sausage, beans, egg, tomato, another sausage, bacon and a big piece of what I call Texas Toast.  Not sure that you would find that in England, but perhaps the chef here is exercising a bit of artistic license.  Anyways, the English breakfast was passable.  Nothing special.

    The beer, however, is wonderful.  They have a smaller selection than many gastropubs in Philly but they have cask beer, which is served at "cellar" temperature, somewhere slightly lower than 60 degrees.  Their remaining selections are well-thought and are international.

    If you want to be transported to England, this place will get you closer.

    Came on a blustery winter afternoon for lunch.  Opening the large wooden door to a room with a fireplace was quite inviting.  The restaurant is quite large but is broken up into multiple small rooms lending to a cozy feel.  Fun kitschy decor does lend a Brit feel.  Our room had dark woods and an English bulldog theme.  The bathroom was full of cross-stitch and had old, lacy drapes.

    The beer and drink menu look excellent, through pricey.  The beer list has American beers on it but focuses on British styles like bitters, pale ales, IPAs and, of course, stouts.  They have a rotating list of 3 beers on cask.  I had a Victory double IPA on cask and it was delicious but was $10 for a 10 oz. pour!  The Yards Brawler on cask was perfectly Brit, but also at $10 was way too expensive even in a real imperial pint (20 oz.) pour.  My girlfriend got a "Queen Mary" Bloody Mary which had pickle brine in it.  Delicious, but at $13 also expensive.  Some other beer selections are more reasonably priced.  $6 for a 20 oz. Stoudt's seasonal (not on cask) was worth it.

    Initial impression was that of slow service.  I was the only one ready with a drink order when the waitress first arrived and then it took a full 15-20 minutes to see her again and in the meantime my drink never came.  The server got my friend's beer order wrong but played it off like it wasn't a problem and did not apologize for the mix up.  Unfortunately my friend (an actual Brit) was too nice a lad to call her out or argue for it to be off the bill.  At this point she took our beer/drink menu and when we asked for her to return it to order another round that also took a full 15-20 minutes.  Really?  To hand us a piece of paper that should have remained on the table anyway?

    Cheese board ($18) was delicious with some authentic British styles no doubt bought from DiBrunos across the street.  Menu is large and it was hard to choose my main.  The brunch items look good and on Sundays they offer a traditional roast for $28.  But, I went with the fish and chips via the Yelp recommendation.  The fish was a small portion - one piece of fish - but was perfectly cooked.  The fish was moist and the breading was light and flaky.  The chips were well done and the tartar was delicious, though I could have used more of that.  Despite this, at $20 for the portion was overly expensive.  Should I have ordered something else?

    My last lunch (technically brunch) in Philadelphia, a meal shared with my two lovely friends who'd driven in from New York, would actually prove to be one of the most surprising of the trip - a meal that surpassed modest expectations when we tucked in at restaurateur Stephen Starr's British themed Dandelion Pub on 18th Street.  Admittedly not a fan of 'theme' restaurants...or Starr's restaurants in general...but led to believe by many friends that The Dandelion was different from the rest what the three of us experience that Saturday afternoon was actually some of the best 'pub food' I've ever encountered and aside from a seating issue the meal was really quite satisfying.

    Noting first the space - a large house completely renovated into a restaurant and featuring rooms ranging from a large banquet hall to a sporty bar (complete with televisions showing Man U) to many small nooks and crannies each featuring distinctive seating from rustic chairs to benches and barstools - the feel of the restaurant is both cozy and inviting while service, in our case from a friendly young woman named Mary, is professional and accommodating - even when we requested to be moved because a large family with several unruly children was placed in the same section of the house.  

    Moving next to the food and drink, while I cannot comment on the two-course cask beer pairing ordered by one in our party, I can say that everything I tasted that afternoon was good while a couple of the plates actually verged on great - the enormous portion of liver mousse with buttery brioche (replenished warm, without request, French Laundry style) and the Sticky Toffee Pudding particularly stunning amongst the eight plates sampled.  Taking special note of the oft raved Cod - it is definitely as good as the rumors; a moist and flaky fish with great flavor under a particularly crunchy batter - though at nearly $20 it would be tough to call the single fillet a deal and while the Shepherd's Pie was good, in my opinion it could have used a bit more heft from the meat and a bit more salt in the potatoes.

    Perhaps not "authentic" and certainly not "cheap," and yet at the same time not entirely disingenuous or "theme park" I think one can best sum up The Dandelion as "you get what you pay for," and it certainly costs less than a plane ticket to London and compared to other 'pub' themed places to watch an afternoon soccer match I'm pretty sure none of them are going to trump The Dandelion's beer list, liver, cod, or sticky toffee pudding.

Specialties

London's culinary revolution comes to Philadelphia with this eclectic English pub. Cozy rooms and intimate bars create a storied, Old World aura, while updated seasonal takes on British food make up the menu. In keeping with tradition, cask and tap craft beers are always rotating, a proper afternoon tea service has a sweet and savory menu, and classic beef family-style roasts are served on Sundays.

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
