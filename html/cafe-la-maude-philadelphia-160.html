    Listed in Philly Favorites

    The food seems to generally be great, although I wish they had more options for picky people like me. Service is excellent, and the place is small (read: cozy or cramped, take your pick).

    Disclaimer: I'm not a huge breakfast/brunch person, but my boyfriend is. I'm also not the biggest fan of cold food, read: sandwiches. Therefore, I had a hard time finding something I'd like off Cafe La Maude's menu, but I definitely see the potential for people who like eggs, aren't lactose intolerant, aren't allergic to fruit, etc (I know, I have a lot of problems!).

    I got the steak frite sandwich, which was pretty good, but nothing to shout about. In fact, the bread was pretty hard and tired out my jaw, so I took half of it home and heated it up--it tasted infinitely better then! I especially liked the combination of steak, avocado, and mustard aioli. The fries were great and flavorful--but not in a normal, salted way! On the other hand, my boyfriend got the french toast (Berrylicious Pain Perdu), and it was so. beautiful. And he said it tasted as amazing as it looked! (Picture posted.) He definitely wants to come back for brunch again.

    The service was excellent, and everyone was really friendly. They don't really assign a specific server to you, so the waitresses frequently check up on you to make sure you have everything you need--especially before you get your food. When I was there, people came out of the back and talked to customers they seemed to know.

    The space is a little cramped, and the high-top tables in the front each have a tall chair and a spot on a long booth that goes against the wall. I imagine walk-ins are best for two. Oh, and you can definitely hear everyone's conversations.

Specialties

With the authentic look and feel of a true European café, this hidden gem in Philadelphia's Northern Liberties neighborhood serves up some of the most delicious food, coffee and pastries anywhere in the city. Café La Maude's specially brewed La Colombe coffee is known for its strong taste that's never bitter.  The French-Lebanese menu combines the exotic flavors of the Middle East with a distinctly Continental French accent and includes traditional favorites such as Brioche French Toast, Nicoise Salad, Taboule and Baklava along with more unique items such as the Grilled Haloumi, a baguette sandwich with olives, roasted tomatoes, mesculun greens and a basil-arugula aioli.  House specialties include the Café La Maude Salad of  arugula topped with warm seasoned chickpeas and fava beans.  Another popular dish is the traditional lebanese recipe of foul moudamas of marinated fava beans served with fresh tomatoes, chopped parsley and pita served with scrambled eggs. For meat lovers the cafe's marinated chicken sandwich and kebbe is not to be missed. Whether it's breakfast, lunch or brunch that brings you to Café La Maude, it will feel like Paris and taste like Beirut.
