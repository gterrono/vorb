        This is like the Chipotle of Japanese food - fast, fresh, and totally customizable. The veggies are finely cut and crispy, the meat/protein is satisfying, and the sauces add the final Asian touch. Their eggplant miso soup is perfect for the cooling weather, and actually has a decent amount of veggies - I wish all miso soups were like theirs.

        The only downside is the limited seating, so expect take-out if you go during peak hours.

        Looks like someone in Philly finally caught onto the sushirrito trend!

        I was happy to see that Hai Street was pretty cheap, but that's only because the portion is pretty small. You can build your own sushirrito or choose from their signature items. I got the sumo steak, and it was pretty good, but I prefer real Japanese ingredients (and why do they put asparagus in everything? ick)... We also tried the $2 hibiscus tea, which tasted like barely-flavored water. Thanks for that.

        Definitely wish there were more seating here. There are only a few high top seats facing the window, and everyone else goes back to work or eats outside. Would be a pain come wintertime.

        Once the novelty wears off, hopefully Hai Street will give us a little more value for what we're paying!

        I was pleasantly surprised by the sushi burrito.  I don't quite know what i was expecting but I think what I got was better then expected.

        I made my own burrito with white rice, salmon, guacamole/wasabe, red cabbage, cucumbers, and papaya salad topped with some spicy mayo and crunchy garlic.  This made for one very large sushi roll.  I have to say I feel like this was a perfect combo and if I were to go there again, I'd get this exact same thing.

        The total came out to $10 which at first I thought was a bit pricey but then again, it was filling and specialty rolls would run about that much or more so I'm thinking it might be a reasonable price.
