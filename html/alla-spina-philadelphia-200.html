    We weren't even hungry. Correction. I wasn't even hungry, so our search for dinner was sorta half @ss. We stumbled upon Alla Spina because she was craving for Oyster Rockefeller and the sexy pig head she wanted to make food porn with. We wanted 6pm reservations and they gave us 6:30.

    Once we got there, it smelled like a BBQ joint. It was more casual than I expected. We ordered the baked oysters, bone marrow and the pigs head.

    Baked Oysters - gross. I can't believe they'd serve this to their patrons. The cheese was rock hard; like it was sitting in their kitchen for 2 hours. No cream, no spinach, no bacon, not even hot. Just a piece of Polly-O string cheese on top of an oyster.

    Bone Marrow with mushrooms - a large femur bone with some mushrooms on top. Once I cut through, I noticed that the marrow was still bloody and undercooked. The kind and pleasant waitress went to ask the chef and he stated that it was perfectly fine and it was supposed to be that way. I've eaten marrow many times and no - it's not supposed to be bloody.

    Porks head - with slices of bread covered in some orange marmalade jam along side it. This was actually good. Too bad I was too full to really enjoy it. We ripped apart the cheeks and made some pulled pork from it. Meat was tender but not sure if I was crazy about the sweet orange marmalade type glaze.

    If it wasn't for the good service from the pleasant waitresses, Alla Spina would have been a 2 star establishment. The food was really subpar and not good for the amount of money.

    I drove up and down Broad Street on my way to and from work for over a year and would periodically look over at Alla Spina -- sitting patiently on my bookmarks list -- and would think, "You know, I should really stop there sometime."

    Well, I'm not traveling past it anymore because I've moved to Fairmount. Since I'm living here, it just made sense to finally haul myself in. No excuses. I visited Saturday night and really loved the set up they had going on with the open windows and fun decor.

    One look at the drink menu and I knew what to order: the Negroni. Bluecoat gin, campari, sweet vermouth? That's fantastic. Also tried a sip (..gulp. ...half of it?) of the Bellini and that was stellar too.

    As for food, I started with the tuscan kale dish. Delicious stuff. It took me until dessert-time to finish it, but I accomplished the feat! I'm someone who loathes most (97% of) vegetables, and I'm telling you that it's good. You should try it.

    For my main dish, I *was* going to order the chicken, but I had actually somehow chickened myself out this week (???) and, at the last minute, I changed my mind and ordered the mortadella hot dog. Now, this is weird, but I kept dipping it in the spicy dijon mustard. Why is that weird? Well, because I hate all things spicy -- passionately and always. Yet that spicy mustard kept drawing me back!

    When it came time for dessert (as I was finishing my kale app...), the server steered me right to the choco taco. Perfect choice. Great ending. Yep.

    I'll be back sometime soon for beer and to try the chicken.
