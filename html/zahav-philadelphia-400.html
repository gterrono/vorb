    Before my husband and I left for Philadelphia, we decided to use Yelp to help us pick a place for Saturday night. This was on top for one of the lists of restaurants we viewed. So, we had high expectations for this place. First, it was quite difficult to find. We took the subway with no problems, but walking from the subway stop to the restaurant took us about 10 minutes. Which, we were happy that we brought our scarves and gloves as it was quite chilly walking around trying to find their door(s). There are two entrances that can be used. (One in the back..which opens out to a small shopping area and an alley or the front, which faces out to a street).

    So, when we walked in, we were pointed to the direction of the host/hostess stand where there were three people. There was a party of four in front of us who did not make reservations and eventually left, but none of the hostesses/host acknowledged our presence..despite there being three of them there. When they finally got to us 10 minutes later, they took our coats and both my husband and I placed our scarves into our jacket sleeves and we were quickly seated.  

    Now, I should note that the stand a lone tables are quite close to one another. So much so, that we could hear a couple arguing next to us while we waiting to be attended to. It was actually quite awkward and marred our dining experience and we were quite thankful when they left. I should also note that the servers have to go through the tiny spaces between the tables, which made us feel like we have even less personal dining space.

    The Food:
    We decided that we would try the Tayim tasting menu each so that we could experience more. We started off with the Turkish Hummus, which was buttery smooth, creamy and oh so warm and delicious! We have never tasted anything like that before. The Laffa that accompanied this was also warm and freshly made in house.
    Next we had several small samples of vegetable salads. My favorite were the carrots, eggplant, beets and cabbage. My husband enjoyed those as well as the fennel salad, however, I am not a fan of licorice and so did not try it. For our Mezze, I ordered the Crispy Haloumi and Kibbe Naya, which were both wonderful. The fried cheese with the date sauce had a nice savory/sweet taste, while the kibbe was perfectly spiced and did not taste like raw meat. We highly recommend these two.
    My husband, for his Mezze, ordered Grilled Chicken Livers and Jerusalem Kugel. I'm usually not a fan of chicken liver, but the liver was perfectly spiced and the beef in the kugel was wonderful. (By this point we were certainly starting to fill up so we asked if we could box some of our previous dishes, which out waitress did willingly. We also had to do this because we were just running out of room at our very small table, which made it difficult to move dishes around.)
    For our main my husband had the Duck Kebabs and I had the Royal Trumpet Mushrooms. For the duck kebabs, we were expecting the foie gras to come on top of the ground duck, however, it was ground right in with the meat. And they were such small bites that we couldn't not taste the foie gras and it rather reminded us of eating sausage. Not all that special. We really enjoyed the mushrooms as it had a smokey taste and meaty texture when cutting.
    For dessert my husband ordered the Halva and I ordered the Kataifi. We both agreed that the Kataifi was the was the way to go. The yogurt is a nice pairing with the sweet chocolate and filo dough pastry and the honey sauce. The Halva did not have much taste at all.

    Upon leaving, our waitress gets our coats and as we are putting them on, I realize that my scarf is missing..a 30 dollar scarf mind you. Even though, I had put it into my sleeve when one of the hostesses took out coats. They were nice enough to have us go back to the coat area and look with them, however, it was not on the floor, not in the closet; just gone. It was a bright teal color with circle patterns in it and rather long so it would not have been easy to miss. I'm not sure if another guest walked off with it and I would hate to think that an employee would take it, but at this point I don't really know. They even had the nerve to ask me if I am sure I even had it with me. Like I said earlier, we knew it was cold out and would have to do a bit of walking, so we made sure we had our winter gear on. One hostess was courteous enough to take down my name and number and description of my scarf, but I never received a call stating that they had looked again and couldn't find anything to at least show that they actually cared/bothered to look again.

    I would have given two stars if it had not been for our waitress. She wasn't overbearing and allowed us to savor our dishes before she came around and asked if we needed anything. Overall, my husband and I will not go back to Zahav for the price and losing my scarf and seemingly not to care or try and remedy the situation.
