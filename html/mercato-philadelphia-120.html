        Got the tasting menu, and every dish was excellent.  The chicken was a standout, it was perfectly cooked and the peaches on the side added to the flavor.  I also had the portobello and arugula appetizer, the seafood pasta, and the gelatto.  My wife and I brought a bottle pinot noir from the Williamette Valley with us, as Mercato is a BYO.

        The price of the tasting menu is phenomenal, when you consider that it actually isn't a real tasting menu.  What I got was a full size portion for each course (and there were four courses).  That is actually my only gripe.  I am used to each course in a tasting menu being pared down in size.  I just wish I knew going into the meal how much food I was going to get.  But  I won't ding Mercato in stars for this.  The staff will gladly wrap up any leftovers.      

        Bottom line, Mercato should be on everyone's dining list for Philly.

        Mercato is fine dining at it's best.  It's a small restaurant with limited seating capabilities, but when the food is so flavorful and the wine flows from the very bottle you hand picked, you would not be distracted by strangers sitting near by.  Anyway, I've been to Mercato a number of times, and I always get the grilled whole artichoke and mussels/clams apps to share.  Last time around my friend also tried the tomato soup, which was so fresh it gave the impression that the tomatoes had been pureed just for him.  The soup was served with melted cheese over white bread, which was simple and a perfect balance to the acid of the tomato.  For the entree I tried the pumpkin fettucine, and he had the organic chicken.  I was at first not very impressed by my pasta, but as I ate I could taste the flavors marry and the dish became better and better throughout the meal.   I could not identify the taste of pumpkin really, it was not particularly the best dish i've had there but I'm glad I tried it.  My favorite entrees include the winter risotto and the linguine w/ seafood.  Next time I will have to save room for dessert.

        Listed in Culinary Colonialism, The Most Wonderful Time of the Year

        My first trip to Mercato happened during Restaurant Week, so some of the slips of service we had were probably due to the crazy number of people they were trying to feed. The food was stellar enough to make up for the minor hiccups, I'll definitely be heading back.

        We had a reservation for 7:30, but when we arrived the hostess informed us that the party before us had been delayed due to traffic and there would be about a 30 minute wait for our table. They offered to give us a 10% off coupon for Valanni while we waited, but we had 2 full bottles of wine with us so just opted to wait. They have wicked strong heat lamps outside the restaurant, so our wait was not too uncomfortable. There is NO room to wait in the doorway, so there was some tension when the four parties waiting outside could see that tables were being cleared/set - everyone tried to crowd in, which meant the door was open which meant the poor table seated a foot from the entrance kept being blasted by cold air.

        The restaurant is tiny and crowded. It feels very warm and homey. All of the servers who came by our table were incredibly friendly and helpful. Also very cute, in a very gayborhood kind of way. Lots of tight red tshirts. Rawr.

        I haven't seen the full menu, but I got the impression that they offered one selection from each section (appetizer, pasta or entree, dessert) from their full menu for RW instead of choosing certain selections. There was a ton of variety, so it was very hard to choose! At our table, we tried the grilled artichoke, trio of bruschettas, and the roasted portobello with arugala. All got high marks, although one of us thought that the grilled artichoke had too much oil (it might have been inconsistent, since mine was perfect) - the dipping sauce with the artichokes was delicious. We also got good bread with a delicious olive/garlic butter to start. Some minor issues with service - my bread plate was dirty (but replaced as soon as I pointed this out!) and it took a minute for them to bring us a bowl for the artichoke leaves, but I'm attributing this to RW craziness.

        Everyone's entrees were great. We tried the short rib ragu, pappardelle, and striped bass. The short rib ragu was delicious and very rich, the gnocchi had a great texture. The portion size was HUGE and it was very filling. The table next to us tried the pumpkin fettucini and lobster and shrimp, and reported that both were delicious - the lobster and shrimp looked particularly amazing.

        You should plan to save room for dessert, although doing so will be a challenge. I had the panna cotta, which was delicious - although the apples had a little bit too much cinnamon for my taste. The molten chocolate cake was incredibly rich and decadent - it would be a great choice if you manage to go a bit lighter on the rest of your food, otherwise there is just no way you'll manage it! The tiramisu was light and delicious, and much easier to find room for after the marathon meal we had just encountered.

        Cheese Plate: I definely forgot the names of the cheeses, but one was on the soft/creamy side while the other was a type of gouda.
