    I went to Industry on a weekend night and it was packed, but somehow our group was able to get table service. The layout was a standing bar area and tables. The open warehouse feel was attractive to look at. They food and drink menu was diverse and interesting. The people at my table seemed to be enjoying themselves.

    The drawbacks I noticed on a busy night was the noise. It seemed impossible to have a normal conversation at the table and shouting didn't seem to help. My ears rang with the din. Another issue was the bathroom, situated in a corner of the room. It was hard to fight through the bar crowd to get there and there only seemed to be one stall.

    Industry is an incredibly attractive place and can see why it draws a crowd. I would definitely go back, but may choose a weeknight next time.

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge, Beer Me!, I'm in Burger Heaven!

    Finally got back to the Industry and I'm happy to report that they have earned another star.

    Visited this past weekend for an early and quick Saturday dinner with my husband, his parents and his sister.  Our general rule of thumb when picking a place for an "L" family dinner is to choose a place with a decent burger, a few salad/lighter options, parking options nearby, and a place where we'll be seated promptly.  The Industry fit the bill for us in every way, so I suggested we meet here for dinner.

    Since we had talked up the burger, everyone except my husband and I got one.  We wanted to try some of the other offerings since we've already tried their great burger- I went with the whole grain salad.

    The whole grain salad was delicious- comprised of farro, peas, fava beans, and ricotta salata.  My only wish is that it had a TAD bit more of dressing on it- it was a little dry.  However, I still really liked the dish.  And at $10, it's a good value.

    Luckily my in-laws all loved their burgers (and the great beer list), so yay for daughter-in-law brownie points.  So if you are planning a gathering for people that want a great meal with some safe options (such as the burger) or interesting options (pig ear lettuce wraps? buffalo sweetbreads?) at a very reasonable price, check out the Industry.

    It's probably a good thing that Yelp exists because the amount of food conversations that I have in… It's probably a good thing that Yelp exists because the amount of food conversations that I have in real life is pretty ridiculous and usually needs to be policed.  One great example is when I have an in-depth conversation with a coworker about the best burger in Philadelphia.  An outsider may have thought our heated conversation was about religion or politics.  Nope, just about pieces of meat on bread.  So my coworker says that The Industry has the best burger in Philly.

    Oh really?  This will need to verified by Jill L, immediately.

    My husband and I headed over for a quick dinner on a Saturday night.  First thing I noticed was how open the space was.  Some of these South Philly joints feel like the walls are closing in on you.  Not here.  We were quickly seated and were given the challenge of choosing a beer off their (very good) list.  Prices seemed to be in line with nearby establishments.

    So we reviewed the menu, despite already knowing what we were getting.  The menu here is... eclectic.  If you are not an adventurous eater, you may not be happy here.  BUT you can always get the burger which is pretty cheap ($8 for a single patty) and can be customized with additional patties and toppings.  The basic burger comes with lettuce, tomato, Cabot cheddar, and pickle mayo.

    I thought the burger was pretty amazing.  The best in Philly?  I don't know.  But definitely one of the best you can get for $8.  

    So why 3 stars?  I feel like the location isn't the best.  If you have a car, you are fine because there is plenty of parking.  But I feel like it's a bit too removed from many parts of the city- including East Passyunk where I live.  The other reason is that there are too many what I call "strange meats" on the menu.  I'm a wimp when it comes to meat and alot of the menu wasn't appealing to me.

    I'd definitely be interested in putting on my big girl pants and trying some of the menu items that look scary to me, because I'm sure I'd find them delicious.  Also, it seems like a great place to get a group together for some drinks.  The Industry, I will be back and will be looking to add some more stars to my review!     I finally made it over to The Industry to see just what the fuss was all about.  This Pennsport pub, which garnered waves of praise among critics and foodies alike when it first opened, just didn't hit the mark for me this time around.  While the food was actually pretty good, it was the drinks and the service that left a bad taste in my mouth.

    Coming in for brunch, we were promptly seated and told about the cocktail specials: $5 Ghost of Mary, and $5 mimosas.  The Ghost of Mary, which is their take on the classic Bloody Mary, wasn't exactly my forte, but I figured for the special it would be ok (going forward, I will always take my Bloody Marys with tomato juice no matter what).  My burger, however, fared much better.  The meat was perfectly cooked, seasoned well, so juicy, and surprisingly, none of the ingredients (including the fried egg!) fell off the bun.  In fact, I would even go as so far to say that this is probably one of the better if not best burgers I've enjoyed in Philly for a while.

    Although our food came out fast and service was generally good, when the check came, the cocktails were shown at the regular price.  When I asked the server about the price difference, he agreed it was weird, then walked over to the service area, and came back to let me know that the discount price only operates during happy hour (between 3-6).  At no point did he acknowledge that when he offered us drink specials on arrival that it was only during a certain price window, not during brunch.  He didn't even offer to adjust the bill or apologize for the confusion (just a simple "yeah, the $5 price is only valid from 3-6, sorry).

    So to sum up: good food, weird service.  Not sure I'm going to head back soon.

Specialties

The Industry has a distinctive menu featuring homemade, contemporary american, bistro style fare. Signature dishes for dinner include Potted Trout, BBQ Duck Sandwich, Kentucky Fried Guinea Hen and Homemade Sausage. Signature dishes for brunch include Fresh Baked Doughnuts, Shirred Eggs, the Hillbilly Philly and the "Stomachectomy."  Executive Chef, Patrick Szoke is the driving force in the Industry kitchen and will continue to create new and exciting plates as the restaurant moves forward.
