    "We proudly serve Barilla pasta" proclaims their take-out bags.  Guess what, Maggiano's, so do I... in my own kitchen!  I am puzzled as to why an Italian restaurant -- promoting their pasta dishes -- would so overtly publicize that their pasta isn't freshly made, and moreover, is the same brand you can find in your local supermarket.  It shows that this chain restaurant isn't that special, just like its food.

    The grilled salmon sandwich was unfortunately mediocre, as the salmon was overcooked and dry.  The side dishes, including the veggies, were laden with oil.  Dining companions commented that their pasta dishes were just average.

    Sure, the location is convenient to the Convention Center, but you're not missing anything if you skip this place.  Head one block east to the Reading Terminal Market, support a local establishment, and you'll find a meal much, much more satisfying that Maggiano's.

    I got take-out recently from Maggiano's... and I was both happy and upset at the same time -- Happy because the food was delicious!... Upset because the customer service was pretty lousy.

    First - do note - that Pick-Up is NOT at the main restaurant. You must walk two doors down and into the separate entrance. If you don't know this, the hostesses will certainly tell you (as if you should have already instinctively known this before walking into their door).

    If your To-Go order is ready to go - then YIPPIE - unless you also ordered a salad with your hot pasta. In that case, you'll have to wait about another 5 minutes while they make it to go with the hot food.

    I ordered one app and one entree. The To-Go chic thought that I was having one meal by myself so only gave me one set of plasticware... which I had to share with my friend..."my bad"... I will be sure to clarify this in the future.

    On a positive - the food was delicious once I got it home. Crab cakes were full of jumbo lump crab, and came with two zesty tangy sauces. The garlic shrimp linguine was also D-e-licious.

    Maggianos... I think I will return... but will already know where to bee-line it to, and will know that I will be waiting and will need to ask for multiple sets of plasticware to go.

    I totally forgot about this place in my recent review spree.  I'm often in center city with my mother or other older family members.  This always presents me with the interesting challenge of finding places to eat that are nicer than the standard casual chain, but not too far out of the comfort zone of those who frequent casual chains.  It was on one of those missions that I found Maggiano's.  

    On our first visit, we had 7 people who were fresh from the flower show with a variety of food allergies, preferences and pet peeves.  Despite the fact that the restaurant was absolutely jammed with similar parties, we were greeted warmly and seated promptly.  Our server, Odie, was amazingly fast, friendly, and understanding.  One person in our party is gluten sensitive, which always presents a fun challenge in an Italian place, and he was not only offered gluten free pasta, but was given special attention in all aspects of the meal.

    The food was decent in a "you get what you expect" kind of way and, though it was not authentic by any stretch, it was satisfying and plentiful.  

    I was so impressed with the service that I actually emailed the company.  They responded by sending me a gift card and a nice letter, which I thought was pretty awesome.  It tells me that this particular chain values positive feedback as much as most respond to negative.

    I've been back since with my partner and have always enjoyed my time here.  It's not fine dining and the prices are slightly high for Italian fare, but it's a good fallback when you're looking for familiar and simple.
