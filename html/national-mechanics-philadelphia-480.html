    See everyone next Wednesday for Quizzo!

    I frequent National Mechanics for lunch with coworkers, and the food is very good. I'm a big fan of the veggie burger! I don't know what it's like at dinner time, but for lunch you want to arrive before noon. Service is very slow on a good day, and when it's busy, you will wait a very long time for everything (waiter taking order, drinks to arrive, food to arrive, check to arrive). Today, we arrived at 12:15 and put in our orders right away (knowing the kitchen is slow). We didn't get our food until after 1:00 --- more than 45 mins later! It wouldn't have been an issue, but two of my friend's lunches were cold (including fries and soggy burger bun). I mentioned it to the waitress, and she comped us a round of beer which made us feel a tad better. I knew it wasn't her fault. There are just major issues going on in that kitchen.

    I'm sure we'll be back, as I'm addicted to that veggie burger. However, we'll be arriving at 11:45 and plan on being there way over an hour.

History

Established in 2006.

Built by famed architect William Strickland in 1837 the National Mechanics Building has seen banks, churches, clubs, bars and fires pass between its walls, and it's still standing.

This beautiful building, as much a part of Philadelphia history as any tourist attraction, now houses Philadelphia's freshest new bar and restaurant.

The peculiar name for our establishment stems from the building's original incarnation in 1837 as Mechanics National Bank.

Our décor is an allusion to all things mechanical from our homemade lighting fixtures to the strange devices running throughout the space to our uniquely designed bathrooms. All of these elements combined with our laid back and off kilter philosophy make this bar what it is. A place designed with real folks in mind and molded by time, our hands and the hands of many before us. Come in and check it out.
