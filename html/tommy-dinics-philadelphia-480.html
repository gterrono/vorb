    If for any reason you had to convert a vegetarian into a pork eater, DiNic's would be a good place to start. For the record, this is only the second time I have been to the Reading Terminal DiNic's. I used to frequent the DiNic's on the black horse pike in nearby Southern New Jersey, but THAT DiNic's doesn't hold a candle to THIS DiNic's.

    After going almost 2 years without an authentic DiNic's sandwich and seeing that they were crowned "Best Sandwich in America" by Adam Richman, I just had to come back in and judge for myself, and I'm glad I did!

    For quality alone, this sandwich is fully deserving of 5 stars and when I say sandwich I am referring to the roast pork with sharp provolone and broccoli rabe. Don't get me wrong, their brisket is also worthy of mention, but there is just something about this holy trinity of ingredients that makes my mouth water whenever I even begin to think about them. This sandwich is also served on a freshly baked Italian roll, which is sturdy enough to hold in the juices and easy enough to bite into without having to wrestle the sandwich.

    Everything about this sandwich is perfect. The bitterness of the rabe along with the sharpness of the provolone, the juiciness of the pork and of course the roll, which supports everything. You are just going to have to try one to know what I'm talking about.

    I would give them 4 stars for the high prices, but I just can't bring myself to do it. Yes, they are delicious, yes, it is a damn good sandwich, and yes they are worth the $9.75 by the time you add cheese and rabe to the sandwich but $9.75 is a whole lotta wampum for a sandwich. Needless to say, it is a small price to pay for something that was placed on this earth by the gods of roast pork themselves.

    Listed in Best Places to Eat - Philly

    When my friends are in from out of town and they are looking for a place to eat, almost instinctively I say "Reading Terminal Market" because you can get just about anything here.  If they inquire further, the next word out of my mouth is "DiNic's".  Why?  Because it's really freaking good, that's why.  No wonder it was recently named the best sandwich in the United States (fact).

    Sandwiched (ha, get it?) between the busy food stalls and shops of Reading Terminal Market, Dinic's is as much of a Philadelphia institution as Independence Hall, The Liberty Bell, and the Phillies (and probably just as touristy).  Since moving to their new expanded spot, they've doubled the size of their kitchen and counter, allowing more locals and tourists to have their incredible sandwiches.  A simple menu makes ordering easy, and no matter what you get, it's bound to be delicious.

    But for me, there are really only two options.  First, there's the classic Roast Pork Sandwich.  Get it on a hoagie roll with sharp provolone and broccoli rabe for a taste of Philadelphia that's more authentic than a tired old cheesesteak.  It's juicy, savory, and you'll be in fat boy sandwich heaven all the way through your meal.  Then, there's the Italian pulled pork, which is even juicier (if you can believe it) and makes your tastebuds sing even louder.  The pork is so soft, so tender, it melts in your mouth.  You could dress it up with cheese and greens, but truth be told, the pork can stand alone on this one.

    I always opt to "eat in" as opposed to take out due to quicker service and less time waiting for sandwiches.  Admittedly a bit more touristy in recent years, but still holds more clout with the locals than Pat's or Geno's.  Nevertheless, this is always my first choice at RTM and definitely one of the reasons why I love eating in this town.
