    Fantastic BBQ in a city that's hit or miss with BBQ establishments (in my opinion).  

    Here are some of my thoughts (in list form).

    #1 - The meat. Most importantly, the meat is excellent.  The pork belly, wow, the pulled pork, even better, the ribs, falling off the bone.  A great selection of sweet, spicy, and vinegar based bbq sauce to compliment the meat is available as well.

    #2 - The sides. As others have noted the sides aren't that great.  My favorite is the pan fried mushrooms when they have them.  The bacon brownie is interesting to try but nothing over the top.

    #3 - The beer.  Great beer selection but it's a bit annoying to have to order separately.  I guess that's the best way to do it given the cafeteria style line and seating situation.  Regardless, great beer selection filled with local craft brews.  

    #4 -   A bit pricey but I think that's to be expected as Fishtown continues to grow.

    #5 - The decor.  Very inviting place.  With a mix of outdoor and indoor seating, it's a very cool all year spot.  Heat lamps are available for the winter months if you plan on sitting outside with a nice cold beer.

    #6 - The bathroom.  Two stalls in the men's room.  Could stand to install a urinal for the weekend crowd.  

    Overall 4/5.  Boom.

    Solid experience. We hadn't been, but they were happy to explain. It works like a deli, you order a la carte by weight. Non-alcoholic drinks at the counter, the rest at the bar.

    Since it was our first time there, we decided to sample and grab 1/4 lb portions each of short rib, St. Louis spare rib, brisket, burnt end baked beans, and mushrooms. It was happy hour so we also did the pulled pork "sandwich" (pulled pork with hot slaw) and chicken legs for the happy hour price.

    The best, by far, were the short ribs. Melted off the bone and in your mouth. Pulled pork and brisket were tied for second. I didn't love the St. Louis, but I don't tend to in general. As for the sides, I would have preferred a tad bit more sweet to balance out the burnt taste. I love burnt ends, but I got nothing but burnt in those beans. Still good, but not great. Mushrooms were a bit dry, but they were also ok.

    The beer selection was made up of local options, which I always appreciate. $5 happy hour Manhattan was well mixed.

    Definitely pricy, but a great happy hour option! The deals ease the wallet and you can enjoy some samplings over that discounted Manhattan or draught.

He looked up, nodded with great approval, and said, "Oh, it's very juicy today... and real good." I knew I was in for a treat. My friend Chris and I thought the difference between our two Fette Sau visits was like night and day. "Damn, we don't even need sauce for these meats," he said as he dug right in. He was right.     Fette Sau makes some tasty BBQ.  Is it the best I've ever had?  No.  Is it the best in Philadelphia?  Personally I don't think so.  I do think it's a decent spot to grab a meal and I'll be back again.

    In the warmer months, the walls open and the place becomes a very loud, social atmosphere while customers herd into the cattle shoot (ordering line) and select what they want.  They keep a glass deli counter of all of their prepared food for the day and the prices are clearly marked on the wall.  You can order by the half pound.  I chose brisket and ribs to share.  The ribs definitely won for me in that challenge.  They were very tender and had a hefty douse of smoke.  The brisket was not my favorite.  I ended up preferring their sweet sauce to their spicy.  They also offer a vinegar for all you southerners.

    For sides, I typically stay with the beans and the sweet potatoes.  I'm sure they have other options but those are my go-tos.

    They serve beer (9 taps) and keep a healthy selection of bourbon and whisky if you're into that sort of thing.  Prices are in line with anything else in the booming Fishtown area.

    In the winter (which I recently learned), the atmosphere is quite similar except those walls are closed up and the place is quite warm.  I was concerned about it being drafty but it seems like they solved that one.

    It's a decent spot to go with a group or just with one other person.  You'll leave full and your wallet will be a bit lighter but overall, it's a good experience.

    This is some of the best restaurant barbecue I've ever had... of course nothing can compare to Carolina barbecue on neighborhood grills, but it can try!

    I was confused as to how a Stephen Starr barbecue restaurant would work, considering barbecue is usually pretty inexpensive. As with most Starr restaurants, you can tell a lot of work was put into the atmosphere. A quick walk from the Girard Station, Fette Sau is more of a large-hole-in-the-wall, as I like to call it. There's a bit of an alcove with outdoor seating (with heat lamps, of course!), and then you enter through an inconspicuous door on the left, where you're happily blasted with heating (if it's 30 degrees outside like it was for me) and you feel back home in the South. I enjoyed the picnic tables and wooden booths that we got to choose from, as it made the place seem more open and communal. Fette Sau is definitely the most well-decorated barbecue place I've seen in a long while, and I appreciated that!

    So it turns out that they price by the pound--so my half-pound mix of beef brisket, pulled pork, and St. Louis ribs, in addition to a dinner roll, came out to about $15, if you're curious. The food is already prepared and waiting for you in a deli counter. I was pleasantly surprised by the relative speed of this method (versus being served), although the meat was definitely not that warm when I got around to it!

    The beef brisket was my and my friends' favorite by far. Like the pulled pork, it's soft and tender and tastes even better with the sweet barbecue sauce! The St. Louis ribs were pretty good, but they were heavily blackened and the meat didn't exactly fall off the bone.

    Contrary to even my own belief, it was hard for me to eat half a pound of pure meat without any kind of vegetable. My friends got the potato salad, and I have to warn you that it's cooked German style--vinegar and oil instead of mayonnaise. I'm not the biggest fan of mayonnaise, but I definitely prefer Southern style potato salad to German style. The dinner rolls were pretty good, and I hear the baked beans are delicious. I read about this amazing mac and cheese on Yelp, but apparently Fette Sau doesn't serve it anymore. I will definitely be back to get a taste of more of the meat, but I really wish Fette Sau would work on some sides! I feel like I should binge on salads before the next time I come here...

History

Established in 2012.

Fette Sau (German for 'fat pig') is a barbecue restaurant specializing in dry-rubbed meats, sourced from
small, local farms and smoked in-house. The menu changes daily, with a rotating selection of six meats
available for purchase by the pound. The sides are kept simple and include burnt end baked beans, broccoli
salad, pickles, sauerkraut, house-made coleslaw and potato chips. The drink list offers nine beers on
tap, plus a cider, and an extensive list of North American bourbon and whiskeys. Picnic tables are the
primary form of seating, giving the restaurant a communal atmosphere. The space has an industrial feel,
with it's reclaimed wood, cement floor and exposed beam ceiling.

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country.
