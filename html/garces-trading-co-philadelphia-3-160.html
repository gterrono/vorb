    Listed in The Squid's Picks

    A friend and I had worked ourselves up into a frenzy about trying the deep dish pizza from GTC for a couple of weeks. We finally went for it.

    The menu advises you that the deep dish pizza takes approx. thirty minutes to prepare, so we called our order in ahead. The plan was to go browse the meat/cheese counter as well and get everything to go. We headed over figuring we would have a little time to browse and that the pizza would be well on its way.

    We arrived, went to the register to tell them we had a pick up order then headed over to the counter.

    The staff are extremely helpful, friendly and attentive. The selections of meat/cheese they have are all amazing. All are labeled with country of origin, a brief description of flavor and in the case of the cheese - the type of animal milk & its firmness. They will happily let you taste any cheese you are interested in. We tasted a bunch of different cheeses before actually making our selections for purchase.

    While we were drooling over their wares, the guy from the register comes over to tell us that the slip our order was written on had accidentally fallen behind something. As such, they only discovered it upon our arrival and had just begun its preparation. He advised us that while we would have to wait an additional twenty minutes, the meal would be comped!

    On to the food...

    Cheese: We got the Cana de Oveja (semi-soft sheep from Spain). and the Piave Vecchio (Firm cow; Italian). Both are amazing in their own right. The Cana is a white, round log. It has a moldy hard rind and a soft & flaky center.  The Piave Vecchio is like a Parmesan.

    Of course, we got a baguette to accompany these gems.  

    Pizza - It was about two inches thick, gooey and robust. We got ours topped with chorizo. The whole surface was covered with sliced chorizo. As with any pizza, it cannot be properly judged unless it is sampled the day after. I performed just such an examination and it passed with flying colors. The crust actually tasted better the following day. It had a biscuit-like quality I hadn't detected the night before. Tasty as I don't know what.

    Salad - We got the Romaine Salad. I'm not a salad nut - it was a few leaves, shaved Pecorino cheese and two anchovies - but in the grand scheme of salads this one was made well. I could have used more anchovies, but that's just me - I'm a glutton like that. It was the perfect side dish.

    Special note: Do not overlook their condiments. The roasted garlic dulce de leche is phenomenal and a jar of the stuff is well worth the cost ($8). Make your own little cheese plate at home!

    I've read and heard some grumblings about it being "noisy" and "bustling". It's a cafe-type setting with an accompanying market, this is the intended atmosphere. It's not supposed to have the sanctity of a genteel or dainty dining room. Expectations folks, expectations....

    I really didn't expect to be comped, but I shouldn't have been surprised....it's another Jose Garces experience and another class-act all the way. Thank you yet again, Mr. Garces and thanks to your staff at GTC. Please keep doing what you do.

    I have been to GTC 3 times now and think I am officially ready to write my review.
    My first 2 visits were absolutely lovely. Both times a friend and I popped in at an off-time for a light lunch-mid day meal. Our impression was that this place was perfect - casual, relaxed, loud, perfect for what we were looking for.
    On both occasions we quickly purchased a bottle of chilled (inexpensive) white wine and took our seats. During the second visit we realized that they now have a liquor license so we could have ordered wine by the glass but I think we made the right choice. Again, on both occasions, we enjoyed a salad and a pizza to share along with the house bread accompanied by the olive oil that they happen to be offering that day. This was the perfect amount of food - a fresh salad with nuts, fruit and cheese and a pizza - once with prosciutto and once with pancetta (we may have a thing for ham products!). Everything tastes so fresh and crisp and both times the pizza was cooked to perfection. Coffee and treats both times too (cookie selection - more mini cakes than cookies) - both times yummy and a great finish to a light meal.

    This last visit to GTC brought me there for Restaurant Week. I know there are mixed feelings all around regarding RW but I always enjoy it. It gives me an excuse to try new places and new dishes that I might not have otherwise. GTC offered 4 courses as opposed to the normal 3. The first 2 courses we were told would be served family style and were intended to share. We ordered - eggplant antipasti, olives, charcuterie, a simple salad and 2 pizzas - mushroom and margherita to start. I don't like olives. Never have and probably never will. I tried yet again but still have to pass. The charcuterie was not impressive, offering only one type of meat where as I was expecting an array. The eggplant was special - simple and delicious; roasted with tomatoes and in a balsamic dressing that was light and yummy. The pizzas were amazing as well but if you read my other reviews you will know I am a huge pizza fan. One small thing I would have changed was the lemon flavor that was on the mushroom pizza - I don't like lemon overpowering a dish which I find it almost always does.

    My main dish was pappardelle pasta with lamb ragu. A small dish (the perfect size after all of the above) which was rich in flavor. This is something I would absolutely order again. My fellow diners enjoyed the goat bolognese and the salmon - both were pleased and impressed on their first visit to Garces Trading Co.

    Desserts were good but nothing to go on about - 2 choices: a chocolate bombe cake and a winter verrine. I had the winter verrine(pudding and mousse) which was not too sweet and a nice light finish to the meal.

    I will definitely return to GTC again and again to dine as well as pick up interesting cheese, meat and pastries. Well worth it!

    I like the concept of this little establishment Garces has. It's rustic and homey and not incredibly dark (and/or arguably romantic) like his other restaurants. It's a wonderful destination for the budding gourmand who wants to explore new ingredients. I didn't get a chance to peruse the products as much as I would have liked, but there was a great selection of flavor-infused olive oils (the porcini was great) and vinegars, available for tasting and in GTC-branded bottles.

    There were also sections for gourmet cheeses, artisan breads and cured/raw meats - presumably items featured on or integrated into the menu. For those who like the sauce, there is a wine store within, and for those who don't booze up at 9AM, they also sell pastries and coffee.

    Onto the meal (note: I went during restaurant week). The cheese plate to start was good, but the star was really the apple chutney that it was served with. I liked the little slab of stone it came on. The eggplant antipasti was a little heavy on the balsamic, but still bursting with juices and flavor, with the pine nuts providing a much-needed textural and flavor contrast.

    The Lyonnaise duck salad was a total hit. I will take a runny egg on anything, really. But I loved the richness of the egg and the tenderness of the duck against the slightly crunchy greens and potatoes. The funghi pizza was much better than the short rib, parmesan and horseradish flatbread at Amada, in my opinion. The cheese was delicious and melty, and I am quite easily swayed by the presence of truffle.

    We ran into our first hitch at the mains. At the suggestion of our server, we ordered the goat bolognese, which turned out to be pretty blah-gnese (I regret writing that immediately). The pasta was overcooked and the dish itself was just very flat and unimpressive. The linguine with clams and crab meat, however, was very good. It was light and done simply in butter, and punctuated with sweet bites of peas and fatty bacon lardons. It was a delicious dish that I will probably attempt unsuccessfully to recreate.

    The desserts were nice, but not as good as I've had at Amada. I found both the mint chocolate bomb and the winter verrine to be good conceptually and very seasonal, but unfortunately incredibly sweet.

    The sangria, however, was abysmal. I've tried it at most of the Garces restaurants and it has always been delicious, so I was very disappointed with what we were served here.

    I would come back again - both to eat and to shop.

    Just had my second brunch at the Garces Trading Co. and I was not disappointed.  

    Garces really knows how to take your momma's brunch and turn it into something fresh, delicious and good enough to crave later that evening.

    My boyfriend and I ordered off the $25 prix fixe brunch menu, which if you ask me is a STELLAR deal for a place that's BYO (we even brought our own vodka for bloody mary's once... it's just a little embarassing to pull a bottle of vodka out of your purse)...

    The thing about Garces that makes diners come back again and again is he uses fresh simple ingredient combinations that just light up in your mouth, which is just PERFECT for a nice Sunday brunch.

    I enjoyed the salmon tartar as my first course.  The creamy Scottish salmon (my Norwegian boyfriend is always upset about how chefs seem to prefer the Scottish) with the toasted pumpernickel bread really highlighted the fresh sea salt taste of the salmon.  The presentation is just so simple it's beautiful.

    As my second course, I had the speck wrapped pheasant sausage with oat risotto and a nice runny egg.  OMG I am considering marrying the man who makes this.  I don't think my Norwegian will be too happy with that but this is by far the best brunch item I've ever had.  If you like sausage, this is sausage take to infinite and beyond (to quote the famous Mr. Lightyear)... It melts and at the same time spices your mouth up and the oat risotto is the perfect complement as anything with the word risotto in it, is in my mind, delicious.  I never had risotto made with oats and it was definitely NOT my granny's oatmeal but it was so yummy I would consider converting to a fiber rich oatmeal diet if Mr. Garces would come and make it for me every morning.

    Dessert was pumpkin trifle for me and bacon ice cream for my boyfriend.  To tell you the truth I stole the bacon ice cream from my boyfriend as I preferred the salty sweet combination... YUM!

    Brunch is sooo good here I am actually thinking that my boyfriend and I need to go visit for a nice romantic dinner.  Maybe I could even talk him into getting me a nice bottle of that delicious olive oil they serve with the bread.

    PS Service here was wonderful.  My boyfriend was at least 30 minutes late so as my friend sat there drinking our bloody may's she kindly told us all about Jose Garces and his background here in the Philadelphia... apparently his wife is a local, although he's from Chicago.

Specialties

Garces Trading Company is a full-service bar and restaurant focused on Iron Chef Jose Garces' own favorite dishes from his travels to cafes throughout Western Europe, with a focus on Italy, Spain and France.  Our thoughtful menu of cafe staples is served throughout the day, and our bar offers a wealth of cocktails, wines and beers that pair beautifully with our menu.  Our on-site PLCB Wine Boutique, the first of its kind, makes a great resource either before sitting down to dinner (corkage fees apply) or to complement one of our decadent gift baskets, available in our newly redesigned retail section.

History

Established in 2010.

Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a refined, Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, a contemporary American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago, IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale, AZ and Palm Springs, CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the landmark Revel Resort. Today, in addition to our much-lauded restaurants around the country, Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
