    Cozy Thai BYO whose opening probably doubles the number of Thai restaurants located in Center City.… Cozy Thai BYO whose opening probably doubles the number of Thai restaurants located in Center City. I like how pretty much every order is customizable based on what meat you want, and there are quite a few Thai dishes unfamiliar to me that sound pretty interesting. However, a long wait even with a reservation and really frazzled and scattered service, though understandable since they just recently opened, make me hesitant to go back soon.

    We ordered the following, and they were all solid, albeit in moderate portion sizes:
    - Satay-Gai: Moist grilled chicken skewers marinated in coconut curry; I enjoyed the sauces they came with (peanut sauce and cucumber relish)
    - Gaeng-Phed-Ped-Yang (duck curry): a sweeter curry that had a spiciness level of essentially zero. I usually expect a little more heat in my curries, but the duck was tender and I liked it anyway
    - Pad-Nor-Mai-Kung (stir-fried shrimp and bamboo shoots in chili garlic sauce): also a dish on the sweeter side, despite the fact that the sauce is chili-based, almost like a sweet and sour sauce
    - Vegetable Pad-Thai-Gung: your standard pad thai dish, but I liked how they didn't skimp on the veggies - broccoli, cauliflower, carrots, bean sprouts, etc.

    I guess a recurring theme with our dishes makes me think that there is that there is probably nothing remotely spicy on the menu, but everything was tasty (and I think even average Thai food is still pretty darn good). Nothing stood out for me as exceptional, but I'm glad more sit-down Thai options have opened up - for now, in its first few weeks of operation, Sawatdee requires a very patient customer.     Much-loved local Mexican restaurant Pico de Gallo used to be on this corner location at 15th and South streets. Sadly, that place is gone now, but pleased to discover this new Thai restaurant in its place. First-time visit yesterday to try the lunch special. Very impressive choice of soups, salads or appetizers, and entrees for a brilliant bargain at $9.95 for all. I had a hot and sour soup and an amazing deep-fried tofu appetizer that was fried to perfection with a thin, crispy crust and soft, melting interior. My entree (don't recall the name) was a fresh and light vegetarian dish with wood mushrooms and rice. The exceptional freshness of all the vegetables and other ingredients really stood out, and the gentle seasoning of the entree was mild but delicious. None of the dishes were truly spicy (takes a lot of spice to get my attention, being one who regularly cooks with fresh habaneros from my garden), and the whole range of seasoning in the dishes I had was less powerful and complex than other Thai restaurants here and abroad, but subtlety in seasoning is an art form too, which, along with freshness, are the great virtues of this lovely new addition to the neighborhood's restaurants. The waiter was attentive and friendly and the prices of everything on their menu are terrific too. I look forward to eating here again soon.

Specialties

Opened in 2011 by Chef Samarn "Tou" Inchote (aka Tony) and wife Wanphen "Noy" Choosang, Sawatdee Thai Restaurant brings traditional Thai food to the Philadelphia scene.
Non pretentious and simple environment accompanies their food to create the perfect traditional home cooked Thai style meal. Our menu ranges from Thailand's National signature dish Pad Thai, through the explosion of flavors and spices of our Gaeng Thai (Curry) and the Bangkok market favorites: Satay Gai and Late Night Noodles.
Finish your meal with our delicious Asian Pumpkin with Coconut Thai Custard or any of our Sticky Rice options while sipping on one of our Thai Iced Tea or Coffees or your favorite wine (yes! we are BYOB)
During the summer months come and enjoy the balmy weather of South Street West on our outside dinning tables.

History

Established in 2011.

Chef Samarn "Tou" Inchote has been working alongside local famous chefs Kiong Banh, Susana Foo and Dimitri since he arrived from Bangkok in the 90's. It has been a dream of his to bring his homeland flavors to our patrons since day one. Along with his wife, Wanphen "Noy" Choosang, and hard work this dream came true 8 July 2011.
