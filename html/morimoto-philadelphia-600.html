    Words like "amazing" and "excellent" aren't enough. Our experience here for my birthday was TRANSCENDENT. It was without a doubt the most extravagant, enjoyable and exquisite restaurant experience I've ever had. We are finally at a point in our lives where a $400+ birthday dinner for two is not out of the question, so if you have the means, GO GO GO.

    If it's your first time or your hundreth, but especially if it's your first, I recommend the chef's choice omakase tasting menu. It is a multi-course menu where the ingredients and dishes are different every night, just whatever the chef feels like making, and encompasses the whole menu. Ours included a tuna tartare, raw oysters, a bass carpaccio that didn't even taste like fish, just tasted like amazingness, a sashimi salad with a delicate, silky yuzo mayonnaise that will be in my dreams for the rest of my life, a roasted, spice-rubbed lobster, a short rib slathered in a truly magical bearnaise sauce, a sushi platter with an array of fishes, and a white chocolate mousse dessert that was truly lovely.

    The service was impeccable, and the presentation of the food was beautiful. Every dish was a work of art. I'd been dreaming of going to Morimoto for years before it was a financial reality, and had VERY high expectations. I was concerned my expecations were so high I'd be disappointed, but every expectation was surpassed and then some.

    We will be talking about this meal for the rest of our lives!

    Was invited out to Morimoto for my birthday as a surprise, but my party wasn't aware that I had been to this restaurant before, in any case, I was happy to be back. Our server was pleasant, knowledgeable, attentive, and sincere. And as always, the atmosphere was fun, colorful, modern and all that jazz that an esteemed restaurant should be. As for the food, WOW.... We went for lunch - my first time I went for dinner - and I must say I like the lunch menu much better. We ordered the Chef's choice and the Beverage Omakase (everything was at the Chef's discretion which was the most fun part about the meal). Each dish was incredible. There were 7 dishes total, each paired with either a champagne, a wine, a sake, or a beer. First course was the Toro Tartare with Caviar and Fresh Wasabi. First time having tartare of any kind, but the wasabi definitely makes that dish. Second was the striped bass carpaccio in a hot oil. We ate it so fast we forgot to take a picture of it - that should tell you something. Third was the Sashimi Salad with Mixed Greens and Tuna Tataki in a Shoyu dressing. At this point in the meal, I'm feeling pretty adventurous. I've never eaten so many different kinds of raw-prepared fish before. I didn't think that I would enjoy it that much! Our fourth course was the Seared Scallop in a Red Miso glaze on top of marinated vegetables and rice in a Green tea dashi. Once the green tea gets married with the other flavors - it becomes amazing. Fifth was the Black Cod Miso with a Su Miso Mustard with slivers of orange and yellow peppers and pickled ginger - the peppers were my favorite part of the dish. Our sushi course included Fatty Tuna, Mackeral, Red Clam, Tile Fish, and Flying Fish. My favorites out of the five were the mackeral, the tile fish and the flying fish. And for dessert, we had the Black Sesame Moussecake which consisted of a goma mousse, sea salt mousse, flourless chocolate cake, and dark chocolate ganache with peppermint gel. Very awesome! It was quite an experience for me. I had a wonderful time and will definitely be back.

    Chef Morimoto truly is a master at what he does. If this is your first time at this place, try the omakase (chef's tasting menu) for sure. It may be a bit pricey, but I think it's worth it. By the way, sorry New Yorkers, but I think the Morimoto in Philadelphia offers tastier fare.

    The ambience is lovely, Stephen Starr is also really good at what he does. The wait staff is amazing, and you'll get probably some of the best service in Philadelphia (I can think of maybe one or two other places that match Morimoto in the service department).

    The omakase is delicious. Every dish that comes out is masterfully executed, planned, and the flavor combinations are beautiful. Some stand outs for me include the tuna tartar with roe, the shrimp, and the succulent slices of sashimi. Of course the omakase changes so it may be different, but I've enjoyed it. The second time I got the omakase, a completely different set of food came out, and my friend who's never had the omakase pretty much had what I had the first time. I appreciate that it's different the second time around (just let them know you've had it before... they'll mix it up for you).

    Well if I'm giving such a beaming review, why did I give it 4 stars? I was a bit disappointed with some of the a la carte items I've ordered there. Perhaps I was expecting the omakase level food, but the surf and turf frankly was not all that great, the teriyaki was so-so, and some of the other menu items I've had were decent but not memorable.

    Go for the omakase.

Other dish highlights were the duck duck duck, ishi taki bori bop (yellowtail), and miso black cod, all of which featured perfectly cooked protein and great flavors. Totally recommended.     For me, Morimoto is the best Japanese restaurant in Philadelphia. Easily. Possibly best restaurant in Philadelphia, although that's just my opinion.

    I've been several times. I always recommend to order the Omakase at whatever price you like. If you have food allergies, the chefs will accommodate them. Let the capable crew of this ship pilot, navigate, and deliver.

    Price be damned, the Sake Omakase is worth it. Yes, you can get some of the sake in Jersey or PA liquor stores for a fraction of the price. It's the pairing and variety that makes it a worthy addition to your meal. If sake isn't your thing, try the Beverage Omakase.

    Aside from the Omakases, there are a few less obvious items on the menu worth mentioning, especially if you want to keep the tab under control. The Wasabi Fried Rice is to die for. Vegetable sushi is the best I've ever had. Incredible. Vegetable Ramen is spectacular. The term vegetarian is meaningless here. Everyone eats well. Japanese Uni when they have it is a must-order, for those who can appreciate Uni. Uni Ice Cream as well....Yes that's right....Caramel and Uni flavors together in an ice cream I always ask about. Then there's Monkfish liver, which I've never had anywhere else. It's like God's own cheescake. Who would have thought. I could just go on.

    Make a reservation if you want a table. The sushi bar is a contrast, but a worthy place to be as well if you are a small party. I sit there occasionally for a little change.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
