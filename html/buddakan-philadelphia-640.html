    I used to scoff at the idea of "Asian Fusion" because, like most other Asians that I know, I am very proud of the food that my heritage has bequeathed upon this earth, and believe that every gram, morsel, and bite of it should be preserved in the way it's served back at home.

    So how could I say no to a restaurant that preserves all the oh-so familar qualities yet uses each ingredient in a way to bring out the best and mask the weakness of one another?

    It took me two visits to Buddakan to finally convince myself that Fusion, if done right, can be absolutely amazing and I'm glad I did; almost everything I've tried here was delectable but here's a few that particularly stood out:

    Tuna Tartare Spring Roll - Crunchy, flaky vietnamese style crust with fresh tuna that carries a spicy kick. You can't really ask for more than this from an appetizer.

    Peking Duck Buns - The buns here are fashioned in a manner very similar to momofuku ssam bar in NYC; instead of the heyuebing you get the open bao-like bun; they toned down the greasiness typically expected from Peking Duck and used slightly less-sweet Tianmianjiang so the flavor doen't overpower the complexity of the duck. I thought this was extremely well-done

    Edamame Ravioli/Dumplngs - Worth every dime and cent. I was slightly bummed out that they had replaced the ravioli with dumplings, as the chewy al dente texture of the ravioli was the perfect contrast to the mushy and paste-like qualities of the Edamame. This is one time where making an Asian dish more Asian kind of backfired XD

    Dungeness Crab Sticky Rice/Lobster Fried Rice - Beyond godly. The fried rice offerings here taste like the simple scallion egg fried rice that I used to get every morning back when I was studying Mandarin in Yun'nan province. The crab sticky rice comes with generous servings of crab, and the lobster fried rice has chunks of lobster in every bite. Quite possibly the best bang for your buck here.

    Thai Cola - Depending on who makes this, this can either be the most amazing non-alcoholic cocktail you've had in your life, or just mildly disappointing. I believe they have an alcoholic version as well.

    My SO and I had all of the above + Miso Black Cod and the bill came out to about 100 dollars. Service was very prompt and accomodating, music was a little loud, but would definitely come back out here for date night.

    Buddakan is one of the best restaurants in Philly and one that actually lives up to its hype. Chris and I came here for a Sunday dinner during Restaurant Week and let me just say, 'wow!'

    The décor and layout of the restaurant is really interesting. We sat on the upper level which overlooked the entire restaurant and had a great view of the massive Buddha statue below. The chairs had black and white images of random faces, which I thought was pretty weird and fun.

    Our server was very nice and helpful with our order. He was attentive without being intrusive. The other staff seemed friendly as well.

    If you've never had the Pan Seared Sea Bass, you need to! Chris and I both ordered the sea bass for our main course and we were both very impressed. The fish was cooked and flavored beautifully. The vegetables complemented the fish very well. My mouth is watering at the thought of that dish.

    The Dim Sum Doughnuts were fantastic.  These delightful sugary fried treats came with two delicious sauces: chocolate and berry.  I was surprised by how large the three doughnuts were and even more surprised by how quickly I was able to inhale them - they were that good. What made the dessert even more memorable was its presentation: the doughnuts came presented with a Chinese take-out box.  Definitely a great way to end an exceptional meal.

    Went to Buddakan for lunch with my wife and 13yr old cousin and it was amazing. Because it was the day before Thanksgiving it was not busy at all. They also have a free coat check which was nice.

    Service:

    It was not busy and we received amazing service. Also, the manager stopped by to chat and see how things were going. Also, because we were new, they gave us a free dessert.

    Drinks:
    Lotus. They supposedly just redid their drink menu. I thought this drink would have a kick, but it didn't. I thought it was a little too sweet and my wife thought the gin was too strong. Presentation was great, but I would try a different drink next time.

    First course:
    chicken dumplings. 5 come in the appetizer for $10. They were steamed and moist on the inside. They are above average, but I think that price point is rather high.

    I and the 13yr old got the "bento boxes". These are not actually bento boxes but a neat rotating tray that holds 3 dishes. However, this was good as you got much more than a normal bento box would provide.

    First dish:
    Kobe slider. Tender and moist and delicious. This was an amazing dish and I wish we got this as the appetizer.

    Shrimp and chicken egg roll. One of the best egg rolls I have ever had. Not heavy or overly greasy. The shrimp is big and has a nice taste. My only comment is that there could be more sauce.

    Second course:
    Vegetable fried rice. Once again very light. The coconut foam with it was delicious. It had more egg than usual fried rice I have had in the past but still did not taste overly eggy.

    Chicken egg drop soup. The aroma from this soup was amazing. The chicken was nice pieces of white meat and not stringy. The upon noodles were a nice touch.

    Third course:
    tuna tataki. The tuna was fresh and the wasabi rub and the noodles gave a nice little kick. I would potentially be concerned about the entrée version getting a little spicy. However at this serving size it was great

    Lamb chops. The lamb had a nice rub on it. Because of the 13yr old, it was ordered well done, so it was a little dry for me (no fault on the restaurant that suggested medium/medium rare). The eggplant and pesto gave it a nice touch.
    Entrée

    My wife ordered the lobster fried rice. The lobster was nice and big, but the fried rice didn't taste as light as the vegetable fried rice for some reason. This was the weakest dish in my opinion.

    Dessert
    Chocolate tear. You have to get this dessert. I do not like sweats, and I thought this was amazing. The coffee ice cream was not overly coffee flavored and the chocolate eye was warm and delicious. Be weary about letting this sit because I think if they eye got cold it would not be good.

    Chocolate bar. This was also a delicious dessert and is what they gave us on the house. The crumble and caramel accentuated the bar and it was great.

    I am definitely going back and cannot wait to try more dishes.

Specialties

Centered around a ten-and-a-half foot gold gilded Buddha, Buddakan serves family-style modern Asian cuisine. Imaginative dishes like edamame ravioli, Asian barbeque pork and lobster fried rice have made this a must-stop spot on any visit to Philly.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
