    I have never in my life had a few beers and then got a craving for bone-marrow.  Put some nachos on… I have never in my life had a few beers and then got a craving for bone-marrow.  Put some nachos on the menu or something...trust me, no one will complain.  

    I really want to like this place.  I live about a block and a half away and the area is in serious need of a good bar/restaurant.  

    First off, the place has a minimalistic decor but its nice.  Restored wood lines the ceiling and most of the walls.  The bar is simple with a chalkboard drink list.  It is small but seems to have a decent amount of seating.  The kitchen is in the back but open so you can check out their handywork.  The open layout allows you to see the two tv's from anywhere in the restaurant although I think the layout also makes the place a little loud.

    As for the beer list, it is OK.  They had about 10-12 beers on tap, a few beers were interesting and the rest were local staples, no macros except for the obligatory $3 pbr pounders and high lifes.  Seems to be a lot of love going to Philly Brewing Company as they had the most beers on the draft list.  The nonhappy hour prices are OK, in the $4-$6 range for a beer.  I'd be way more jazzed about the place if they did half off happy hours but as far as I know they only do $1 off all beers.  

    As for the cocktails, I didn't try any but they have a list of some of their own specialties.  Once again, I'd be more interested in these drinks if they would settle down in price to more of the $8 range rather than the $10-$12 range.  The group sitting next to me ordered a punch bowl called Mummer's Punch.  It's 4quarts of punch to share and looked fun.  It is supposed to serve 4-6 people and costs $45.  

    Menu wise I'm not sure what is going on here.  I think the chef designing the menu was over-ambitious, unaware of what the clientele in the area is looking for, and would prefer to be cooking at a gourmet restaurant rather than at a South Philly bar.  Basically, the only thing on the menu that you would recognize as pub food is the burger and wings.  The other options include things like bone marrow, pulled duck bbq, and tartare.  Don't get me wrong, I like all sorts of food, and I would be interested in trying some of the things on the menu but the menu should be more balanced.  For example, if you want to offer bone marrow then fine, but offer pulled pork bbq instead of pulled duck...sometimes I don't want to order food and hope that its good.  I want to order something that I know I like and then hope that the restaurant makes it really well so I end up loving it.  Price wise, the menu is a little too expensive as well. They have 'small plates' and large plates' I'm not sure how big the small plates are but they are in the $10 range and the large places move up to the $18-$22 range.  So basically, if my wife and I go here, have a beer or cocktail each, then each have an entree we are looking at spending $60 plus dollars.  That's a lot of money for a bar in Pennsport.  I'll go to The Village Belle for a dinner in that price range any day.  At least you are getting some non-bar ambiance there.

    I did see someone with the steak frites which looked really good but at $18 most of the entrees are too expensive to make this a place to go and get a beer and dinner when I don't want to cook.  

    The service was good even though the place was crowded.  And when you consider that I was there when they had only been open for 2 or 3 days, it was running very smoothly.  

    As far as the places association or their claim to cater to people working the the restaurant/bar "Industry" I didn't see it.  I don't know if they offer a discount to industry folks, but it didn't appear to be advertised on the menu or anything.  The only thing that seemed to give a nod to the industry were little bits of advice given on menu's and coasters and things such as "Tip: Never start your order with, "this is going to be a pain but...".  There is also an offer on the drink menu to buy a round of shots or a six pack for the kitchen.  I guess this caters to "the industry" but mostly just to people who work at "the industry" and not the restaurant biz in general.  Overall, I don't really care about "the industry" theme.  I'm not in the business so it doesn't have any impact on me, I just thought they might have a little more playing off the theme.  It mostly feels gimmicky

    With all of this said, I want to note that this is my initial review.  The place is young and I live so close I'm sure I'll be back.  I want the place to find an identity that is more appropriate for the neighborhood and clientele.  I'm not saying serve coors light and fill the place with Mummers.  I just mean, recognize that The Industry is not in Center City or Rittenhouse.  If you are catering to people in the "Industry" realize that you have to understand that you are a bar and have to offer a good happy hour or nightly specials along with understandable food at a reasonable price not gourmet-based food.  I'll be back, but I'm hoping for change.     Industry is another ultra-hip pub, which has paid a great deal of attention to the light bulbs, and thank God for that. As we walked in after work, we took seats at the bar, and were the first patrons of the day.  We waited more than 15 minutes before anyone from the staff decided to acknowledge our presence, but at least they had shirts with snappy saying on them! I asked for a Guinness, which they did not have, nor did they have anything comparable. Okay...how about a lager or a pilsner which isn't cloyingly hoppy? Bar owners...You Need a couple Fastballs!!!! The burger and frites were both somewhat typical, but the taste was really good (the bacon was amazing!). As for the Fried Chicken, it definitely doesn't worth $20. I do like the fact that items on their menu change based on what's on the market and what they want to make (the fish and seafood BLT sandwich for example), so I'll probably come back a number of times to see what they have to offer. Some of their food is pretty expensive for what it  looks/sounds like it should be.

    Service
