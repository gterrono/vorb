    I've been in search of a spacious coffee shop in the Fairmount area for weeks. The pickings are slim. There are only a handful of independent coffee shops and one Starbucks that can barely fit ten people. So with the help of Yelp, I found Cafe L'aube tucked away on the corner of Wallace street in the Spring Garden area.

    What is so great about Cafe L'Aube? Well for one, it serves great coffee and pastries. But mainly because it serves delicious and authentic crepes made to order. The employees are francophone and if you close your eyes you may try to imagine yourself sipping away at your cafe and crepe at a french coffee shop.

    I started off with a latte and pain au chocolate... a few hours later I found myself hungry again and ordered the ham, mushroom and emmentaler cheese crepe. Delicious and cheesy.

    So far in terms of coffee shops, this is my favorite in the city. Any one have any other recommendations?

    So delightfully French and delicieux!  Not only are the crepes and sandwiches extraordinary, but the menu, food, and coffee bring back fond memories of my Lyon study abroad. (And high school French class... "Je veux un croque monseur, s'il vous plait.")

    I've had the Savoie and La Flandre sandwiches, and both were wonderful combinations of hearty turkey, melty creamy cheese, and sweet tomatoes/fig spread on crusty baguette.  The type of sandwich that you savor every bite of and leaves you deliciously satisfied.  The bites of both sweet and savory crepes that I've tried have all been amazing as well.  Everything perfectly prepared with rich, fresh, pure ingredients.

    I'd strongly suggest the iced teas too - red vanille is my fav.  They have about 6 flavors of green, black and red teas such as fruit de forest and mint.

    I love the spacious indie coffee shop layout with extra seating in back.  And friendly, genuine waitstaff.  If only they stayed open past 6pm!  Definitely a highlight of the neighborhood eateries.  (And I seriously need to try the coffee.)

I'm not sure when it was made, but it definitely wasn't made that morning or the night before. It had been sitting there for a little while, maybe a day or so. It didn't look fresh or taste fresh. The flavor was good though and I would be willing to try one of their savory ones if I had the opportunity.

    I'm going to violate my usual sandwich method of criticism here, and just start with what I don't like: It's expensive, and it closes earlier than other coffee shops.

    Now that I've gotten that out of the way, let's discuss what I love.  The crepes are fantastic.  The combinations are inventive and I have never been disappointed in the product.  I love the complete, quotidien, prefere, and quebecoise.  The quebecoise has maple syrup, ham, and apples.  I love the whole sweet and savory in one dish thing, so this is truly a gem.  

    The atmosphere is also lovely.  The windows bring in lots of sunlight, and the whole cafe takes on this really positive vibe as a result.  It's a great way of deluding myself into thinking I'm outside, when really I'm inside weeping over a paper.  It seems that others have a similar opinion - in December, the L'aube was packed with students doing work.

    That brings me to my final point.  Jean-Luc is a lovely neighbor.  I live very nearby, and when he first opened I immediately started going in.  He has never forgotten that I was there when no one else knew the place existed.  In December he was roasting coffee beans while I and other students did work.  He sent ALL of us home with a delicious pound of his fresh-roasted coffee.  For free.  He is just so nice and positive, and I love coming in to see him.

    Frankly, the only reason I do not literally live in this place is that the food is so good, I always cave and get something.  But because it's pricey and I'm on a fixed income (shakes fist), I can't afford it all the time.  However, it is well worth the price. Between the food and atmosphere, you will not be disappointed.

    Let me first start with the words Pain aux Chocolate Almond. Take a Chocolate Croissant and lovingly dress it in Almond slivers and powered sugar. Bliss. You can get this here.

    Why? Because Cafe L'Aube is not a pretentious French cafe running on some French cliche. It's owned and operated by a West African gentleman (or so the rumor goes) from Cote D'Ivoire - Ivory Coast. And we thank Mr. Jean-Luc Fanny for his authentic contribution of a cafe that is an actual cafe. The coffee is stellar, you can get a savory or sweet crepe that's cheaper than most creperies in the city.

    The atmosphere is a combination of Euro-cozy and Philadelphia house-cafe with hardwood floors, the ikea couch, and the plain tables and chairs. The walls are painted so nicely and you can even pick up a L'Monde to catch up on your French news.

    The system for ordering and getting your food could be easier, but the staff makes up for that by being friendly and engaging. No hipsters here. You can speak French to almost all of them, because half of them are from West Africa as well.

    It's the kind of place you make your neighborhood coffee spot.

Specialties

Cafe L'Aube is a traditional eat-in French cafe. Our concept is all about sharing a little piece of the food-based French café and salon de thé culture we love with our adopted city. More than a coffeehouse, easier than a restaurant. Quality, all made-to-order fresh food, as well as coffees through our own laubetorrefaction.com. BYOB brunch, lunch or dinner.
