    For a burger and beer enthusiast, Standard Tap should be no contest. For years before my first time at ST, I'd heard about their no compromise draft only list, lack of menus and famous burger.

    And yet, when I got there, I was pretty much universally disappointed.

    First of all, though I am heavily into craft beer, I have a few friends who are not. While it's cool for me that Standard Tap doesn't serve macro crap, I do feel bad that my friends aren't able to get what they want, and thus, probably won't be drinking much, if anything, and further, that they will probably not be having much fun. Second, for a joint that has no bottle list, their tap selection is surprisingly anemic. Not so much that I didn't find anything I liked on tap, just that there were simply not enough taps. I am usually able to find a beer or two to drink, but that's really not enough for my taste in a place like this. I'm looking for an experience. If I can't have two beers I really like, if not two beers I've never had before that look good, I'm going home disappointed. ST is borderline here, and I can see them having an off night where there is literally nothing I'm looking to drink. Granted, that hasn't happened in the couple of times I've been there, but were I to become a regular, I imagine it'd be easily possible. Still, I realize that it's not fair to judge a place on a hypothetical, so we go on to the other half of my experience:

    The burger. I was not thrilled. I see this now and again and I'm always shocked that someone thought it was a good idea. It's the bread they use. Some kind of hearty, rustic bread. Possibly a ciabatta? I'm not certain specifically what it was, but it hugely detracted from the experience. With bread that tough on a sandwich, all of the other flavors are greatly diminished, to the point where I really couldn't tell if the burger was actually tasty or not, it could only whisper to me through thick, mostly flavorless bread. Very disappointing.

    While I did give this place a second chance, it was pretty much the same the second time around.

    I've written a song for the Standard Tap, and it goes like this (the lyrics at least)...

    I like the Standard Tap
    They serve the same old crap
    Like Burgers and Beer
    Yeah you can get those here

    I like the Standard Tap
    And I'll tell you no lies
    When you order the pulled pork
    It comes with fries

    I like the Standard Tap
    They're pretty friendly too
    When your food is ready
    The bar-dude brings it to you.

    Yeah, that about sums it up. Actually, I wish this summed it up. But the Standard Tap isn't a tavern, it's a gastro-pub... which is an over reaching tavern... a tavern with pretensions. Is that a good or bad thing. Well, who cares?

    I like the Standard Tap, because the place builds intellectual and decision making skills. For instance, when you arrive at the front door, you have to figure out whether the door PUSHES in, or PULLS out... and whether to grasp the plumbing work on the door for PUSH or PULL. It isn't easy. I screw this up every time.

    Upon entering, you have to immediately assess the crowd at the bar to the left. Is there anyone there you want to be seen next to? If not, you need to make a decision on going up to the second floor. You have about two seconds to make this decision in a nonchalant fashion. After two seconds have passed, you will appear deliberate and rationalistic, and your street cred will evaporate.

    This is the first big decision, and not an easy one. If you screw up opening the door, no one will know. But deciding on which floor to go to is visible to all. If you go up to the second floor, and you don't like it, you will be forced to retreat to the first floor, where you will appear to be a guy cruising for chicks... like some bad dance club scene from the 90s. You might as well leave at that point.

    But even getting to the second floor is filled with relevant decisions. You have to navigate past the jukebox, and there is a moment where you might be blindsided by a waitress streaming by. At that very same moment you will catch a glimpse of a dartboard. Mind you, I have never seen anyone playing darts there, but where there's a board, there might be airborne darts.

    Assuming you make it to the base of the stairs, you must be ever mindful of someone coming down the steps the same time you are going up... because there is only room for one person. If the person on the top of the stairs is a woman, you can be sure she will let you go first, since she won't want to risk rubbing up against you on the way down. If it is a guy, then you have a one in seven chance (so they say) of having a guy want to rub up against you. Again, these choices are not easy.

    If the second floor is no good, you can always go out on the deck if it is open and it isn't a private party and if the weather is nice and if you feel like a character in a Woody Allen movie. The deck is actually really nice, though I spend half my time staring at the glass window to one of the bathrooms, wondering why the bathroom window is so open to the public.

    The subject of bathroom windows in public places is a whole other conversation... and it's appearance here indicates a stultification vis-a-vis the topic of the Tap, and it's complex decision paradigms.

    And so on and so on.

    I'm surprised many of these reviews talk about this being a burger place. Maybe I'm just clueless, but I don't think I've ever gone with anyone who has gotten a burger. Usually we look forward to sharing some of the more unusual dishes, including things like barracuda, boar, rabbit, and octopus. I had the lamb lentil stew once, which was super delicious and made me go on a mission to figure out a way to replicate it at home. We never miss the mussels (always excellent) and french fries, and the smelts are pretty good. The menu changes every day, for better or for worse... unfortunately my favorite appetizer salad has not made an appearance the last few times we've gone.

    The beer selection is excellent and I almost always ask the waitress or bartender for their suggestion on what to drink. So far I have not been disappointed. We always hang out upstairs, usually on the deck if the weather is nice and we can find a table or if the wait is reasonable. The back dining room is nice and quiet.

    One thing I would advise, and this seems to be a common theme among the other reviews: do not go here for brunch. The brunch food is overpriced and resoundingly ordinary. We're big fans of Standard Tap dinner but made the mistake of bringing my parents here for brunch, and it was a total let down.

Specialties

Standard Tap features locally-brewed draft beer.  Our food menu both complements the beer and expands the notion of what tavern food can be.  We use only the best available produce, poultry, meats and seafood -- much of it from area farms and local vendors - to create fresh, seasonal dishes.

Our full menu is available from 4 pm to 1 am, seven days a week.  Brunch is served Sat. & Sun. from 11 am to 3 pm.

Wine and liquor also available.

History

Established in 1999.

When Standard Tap opened its doors in December 1999, our goal was simple:  to create a neighborhood tavern that featured great beer and wholesome food.  With that in mind, we offer a draft-only beer selection that highlights the vast array of styles crafted by breweries located in Pennsylvania, New Jersey and Delaware.  Our food menu both complements the beer and expands the notion of what pub food can be.  We use only the best available produce, poultry, meats and seafood -- much of it from area farms and local vendors - to create fresh, seasonal dishes.
