    Listed in Best Roast Pork

    Oh my have the lines at this establishment gotten longer and longer over the years! And for good reason, one of the best sandwiches I have ever eaten. Their bread is great, their meat is juicy and their provolone cheese is nice and sharp. In my personal opinion, though, its those toppings that sell it for me every time. The broccoli rabe, the spinach, even the roasted peppers are incredible, very heavy on the garlic and a little red pepper flake. The best greens served at any of the Philly sandwich shops. I also love the atmosphere, but unlike my wife, I am not adverse to crowds, if you are, the Reading Terminal Market will drive you crazy!

    Still, however, I have to rank it below Tony Luke's and John's on the roast pork, even though the bread and toppings and atmosphere might be better here. Its just the meat is not better, its close and really good, but not. Tony Luke's meat is a tad bit juicer and John's is a tad bit more flavorful. All in all, however, you simply cannot go wrong with a roast pork sandwich here (or roast beef)..

    What this place does have going for though is its the Italian-style pulled pork. Now this is some incredible stuff and I highly recommend it. Top it with greens and sharp provolone, just like a roast pork sandwich, and its probably one of the best sandwiches I have ever had and blows away any cheesesteak.  Before it closed, i used to laugh at the suckers lined up at Rick's for a cheesesteak, did they not know about DiNic's a few rows down?  Unlike the brisket sandwich here, the pulled pork lacks any and all BBQ/tomato/smoke flavor. Its pretty much very moist pulled pork with a hint of Italian seasonings. It is divine. I am not a huge fan of the brisket sandwich, although I know many who are. The sausage is also good, but not many order it here with so many other outstanding options. For me 9 times out of 10 its the Italian-style pulled pork... mmmm....

    TIP: If your with three people or less why not sit and eat? You don't have to wait in that long line to order, just wait for someone to get up and sit down. Also they do run out of food sometimes later int he day, best to try and get here before 2:00 if you are making a trip.

    After seeing this featured on Man v Food I knew I had to have me a roast pork sandwich whenever I could come to Philadelphia.

    So as fortune would have it, our family trip out to the DC area included a trip to Philly! I insisted on making a stop at the Reading Terminal Market and after being dropped off by one of the local cabs I went into the building eagerly.

    Not knowing where I was going I simply walked in a straight line and after seeing that wonderful DiNic's sign I knew I made it. Being a tourist I stepped in line to wait to order my sandwich.

    Little did I know that if a seat opens up at their counter, you can simply sit down and get your sandwich MUCH quicker (if your intention is to eat there). Unfortunately, the seating is extremely limited but this time I got lucky with a seat and ordered two roast pork sandwiches (one for me and one for my younger sister). They will ask you what you want on your sandwich; and me being the total noob I asked for everything (though I just wanted what Adam had on MvF). "Everything" includes the sharp provolone, broccoli rabe, and peppers.

    Be aware that they could run out of broccoli rabe, so you may want to visit early and get your sandwich before this happens; otherwise you will end up with spinach I believe. They also give you a choice of sweet or hot peppers, and being the anti-spicy guy that I am; I went for sweet peppers.

    After about 10 minutes my sandwich was ready, and I digged in. Generous heaps of roast pork and the combination of the sharp provolone, sweet peppers, and broccoli rabe made this truly a unique sandwich. Admittedly I am no longer the big eater that I used to be; and this sandwich ended up being 5 bites larger than I could handle but I still managed to finish it with nothing left but the tiny scraps of what was.

    Doesn't seem like they take credit cards as everyone around me was paying strictly in cash; so be aware of that and visit the ATM before coming here.

    For me, the roast pork sandwich totally lived up to the hype that I saw on TV and then some. It was worth every penny and I was glad that I stopped here while visiting Philly for the first time. I definitely would like to return to try their brisket the next time I am in town.

    Listed in Best Ever- Nationwide, As Seen on TV, Best Food: Anywhere USA, PHILADELPHIA, Sandwiches!

    Tommy DiNics has been a Philly staple since 1954. The modest spot within the Reading Terminal Market can be packed with a line stretching around the market, or you could get there have a seat right away. I was able to get a seat at the counter right away but the people next to me told me they tried to come an hour before and the line was so long they decided to leave and come back. By the time I was finished a sizeable line was formed again. Because it's within the market the hours are sadly not as convenient as if they had their own spot, but this just makes them that much more special. Special enough that nearly every traveling food show has featured them at one time or another (Man vs. Food, and Diners Drive-ins and Dives both come to mind).

    Onto the food. Their menu is very simple, they only have a few choices of meats and only a few toppings. I won't tell you those choices because you only need to know one combination, the one that made them famous, the sandwich that makes every Philadelphian forget about the cheesesteak: it's their roast pork with sharp provolone and broccoli rabe. There's only one possible criticism anyone could possibly have with this miraculous sandwich, the fact that they just raised their prices and now it will set you back $9.25. I still think it's a good value but it's certainly not a steal. The sandwich is huge so come hungry or split it with someone so you can eat some of the other great things available in the market.

    This sandwich was one of (if not the) best sandwiches I have ever had in my life. I had heard about Philly's love for roast pork, sharp provolone, and broccoli rabe before and had tried it at one of my favorite cheesesteak places, Tony Lukes. I didn't like it, the provolone was too overpowering, the roast pork was just ok, and the broccoli rabe wasn't drained properly so I had a rush of water making the sandwich soggy and messy. I decided to try the combo again at DiNics and was blown away. The pork was amazingly succulent, the provolone gave a nice sharp kick but didn't overpower anything, and the broccoli rabe was fresh and balanced the flavors of the rest of the sandwich perfectly. Not only is the Reading Terminal Market the best thing in Philadelphia (arguably one of the best places culinary spots in the country) but the roast pork sandwich at DiNics should be on everyone's 100 things to eat before I die list.
