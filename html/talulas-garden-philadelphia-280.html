    We had a delicious (and expensive) brunch at Talula's Garden. It was too cold to sit outside so we asked for a window seat inside and had the sun streaming in the huge window with a view of Washington Square. Our food was delicious, like the citris and beet salad with ricotta cheese, a side of sausage that you dip into sweet syrup, and a salad of flavorful greens, crunchy (maybe too hard) croutons, and rapes. For our main course we had the duck confit over potatoes with perfectly fried egg on top. The potatoes were the best thing that I've had in a while: with a crispy almost sweet crusty glaze. The duck actually was a little tough, but the potatoes were just absolutely amazing. We finished the meal with a little donuts filled with chocolate which you dip in a vanilla sauce which was also amazing; it was a great way of ending the meal.  So two starters, two sides, 1 main course, coffee, a bloody mary and a mimosa was $90.  A splurge, but worth it.  I'll be back, I really liked the food.

    Just went for the first time. Wow. This was absolutely one of the best meals I've had in Philly, period. Everything was so well balanced, showcasing local vegetables even in dishes that had strong main components (for example, the pea puree in my striped bass dish was just as good as the fish itself, which was excellent). Everything blended together so well, the service was smooth, and the atmosphere is comfortable.

    Our dishes:
    The Butcher cocktail - a SPICY bloody mary. Really good, but you better like spice
    Foraged salad - great combo of fresh greens, veggies, apples, and an awesome dressing
    Curry Tuna - the tuna was incredible. Very subtle amount of curry flavor, mostly just got the delicious tuna flavor. The green and potatoes accompanying it were fine.
    Striped Bass - Seared with a crispy skin and served on super fluffy mashed potatoes with pea puree and full peas. So good, the only complaint was that the fish was JUST barely overcooked. I would order this agian and again.
    Lamb Shoulder - would also order this again and again. Just a pot of pulled lamb, jous, and polenta mixed together. Amazing
    Gnochetti - really fresh tasting homemade pasta and vegetables. Didn't like the fern (too oily)
    Creamed Kale - Pretty standard but great
    Beets and Ricotta - really amazing, the ricotta went with the beet flavor so well.
    Donuts - Really soft, fluffy donuts, can't go wrong
    Chocolate Parade - 4 different chocolate dessert, a torte, a hazelnut bar, a pudding, and a salted chocolate caramel cookie. All of them were great, with the salted caramel bar the best

    We didn't even hit up the huge cheese menu or anythign else, but I would go back and try everything in a heartbeat.

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge, Brunch Time in Philly, Philly Favorites

    I've been here a couple times for both dinner and brunch and had great experiences every time. The ambiance is always wonderful (especially the outdoor garden!), the service is friendly, and the food comes out fast.

    The dinner menus change periodically, so I won't say much about the dishes I've tried other than that they're generally good and quite filling, and my favorite dish I've ever had here was the spiced Berkshire pork loin and rib (which I had when I came for my birthday back in October). They also have an extensive cheese menu if you like cheeses.

    The desserts here are really excellent too - on my most recent visit here, I had the little sweet ricotta doughnuts and the crisp creme brulee napoleon. The doughnuts had a chocolate hazelnut filling and were topped with nuts and a little bit of salt, and came with a little jar of vanilla anglaise on the side. The creme brulee napoleon was a stack of creme brulee and crispy caramelized wafer (basically the top of what a normal creme brulee is), and came with a side of blood orange sorbet. Both were amazing, and I'd definitely recommend them if you're not already too full from your dinner!

    Brunch is also delicious - I had the cornmeal hotcakes, which were caramel-y and cinnamon-y and came with a generous helping of candied walnuts and bananas. My friend also enjoyed his shrimp and grits.

    Talula's is among my top restaurants in Philly, and I definitely recommend it if you're looking for a place that offers great food, good service, and a rustic yet classy ambiance.

    I'll start by saying, I've always wanted to get a seat at Talula's Table in Kennett Square, but doubt I ever will.  I can't make a reservation a year in advance and ensure I'll have 10 people to go with me!  Too hard.  I wish they opened up the table a few times a year just to couples--and you could sit and dine with strangers.  But anyway, I was psyched to find Talula's Garden, since it's an offshoot of Talula's Table.

    Absolutely romantic and very unique place.  The garden terrace was gorgeous--I only wish it were warmer so we could have dined on it.  We went in mid-May for my birthday, and it was just a strangely cold day.  We had a cute table in the corner and I just couldn't stop looking around at everything.

    We started w/ the cheese platter--and let me tell you, we had trouble choosing because they all sounded great.  The cheesemonger (hilarious) was extremely knowledgeable, telling us all about our selection.  He was very attentive as well.  
    I'm glad they had cheese plates though because none of the other apps really appealed to me.

    For dinner, I had the filet.  It came out pre-sliced sort of like medallions--not my favorite presentation-wise, but it was melt-in-your-mouth delicious.  The potatoes it came with, twice baked were a great side dish to it.  It tasted lovely with my rich Pinot Noir.

    My boyfriend got the scallops and was afraid he'd still be hungry.  But he wasn't and loved the dish.  We split the green beans side dish; they were Asian-style and he HATES  vegetables (like a child) and says this all the time, "If all green beans were made like that, I'd eat them all the time!"  Cracks me up.

    For dessert, we had the ricotta doughnuts -- a delicious indulgence.  

    My one wish -- cheaper wine selection.  Half bottles were double the price of whole bottles you can get in store.  I mean I understand wine mark-up, but the cheapest bottle was about $50.  I know a lot of the wines (if not all) are organic, etc., but we 2 glasses for $28 was less than half the price of a full bottle--so we opted for that route.  

    They had a bunch of yummy sounding specialty cocktails too and a cute bar.  

    We'll DEFINITELY be back.

    Wow. Was here last Sunday and just... wow. We were a part of four, and we pulled out all the stops. Drinks first - The Day Tripper and The Tree Hugger. Pasting from their menu, here are the descriptions, respectively: Strawberry Smash...Bulleit Bourbon, Local Strawberries, and Fresh Mint; Maple Manhattan... Bourbon, Maple, and Black Walnut. Both were AWESOME.

    Cheese second - the selection process is an experience all on its own, but we did what any normal person seeking the best experience would do; we opted for the MASTER COLLECTION. Yes, it's the master collection. Tell me that name doesn't make you think it's the best one - plus it's the last on the list. Save best for last? Worked out this time.

    Beginnings (that's how they name their appetizers) - carrot soup and mushroom pate en croute (served with a fried chicken wing and a sunny-side up quail egg, making this dish a winner), AND because we wanted to splurge, we also ordered the gnochetti primavera, a main, as an app as well

    Mains - trout, duck, bass, and seafood plate (aka Garden Bouillabaisse) - I talked about the duck in my last review. Out of this world, again. A complaint on the Garden B: a little too salty and the shrimp were major offenders on that front, and they were overcooked, which gave them a mealy, dry, and chewy consistency.

    Dessert - Creamy Butterscotch Pudding and the Melting Dark Chocolate "S'mores" & Smoked Ice Cream. Have you had the salted caramel budino at Barbuzzo? If so, the pudding will be very familiar to you, which also means that you're going to love it. The chocolate dessert was good, and the smoked ice cream was interesting. In this case, I'm using interesting to mean that I wasn't such a fan. The combo worked well, I just wouldn't order the ice cream on its own.

    Overall verdict - this place just became a top three in Philly for me. Our server, Laura, also deserves a special shout out. She was great!

    Go there, hug some trees, get some cheese mongered, eat some duck... life will be good.
