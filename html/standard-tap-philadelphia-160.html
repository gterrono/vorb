    Its always so sad when you have to say good bye to a friend - especially one that's moving to bumble-frick middle of nowhere Minnesota to work on an organic farm for the next couple of months. So we decided to take him out for a last hurrah/happy belated birthday at Standard Tap since he'd never EVER been to the Northern Liberties (and yet grew up in Philly).

    Arrived a little before 7pm, perfect timing since we snagged one of the few remaining tables upstairs. The poor suckers behind us had to nurse their beers by the bar and watch us order. The two boys next to me opted to order the exact same thing - the burger - while I opted to treat myself to much needed oysters on the half shell and their beet salad. The interior is lovely, I was really digging the red painted walls and red light right above our table, and I'm always a sucker for some dark wood accents everywhere you turn. Mostly I was amused by the enormous group of guys that absconded a whole wall's worth of tables - we kept trying to guess why there weren't any girls with them.

    Anyways, on to more important things - like the food! Oysters arrived mere minutes after our waiter took our order, and I scarfed them down as quickly as they shucked 'em! I loves me some oysters. My salad arrived with the boys' burgers and I was a wee bit surprised at the...size...of the...salad.......I dunno, maybe I just need to become more familiar with their menu, but when a salad is listed on the menu, even as an appetizer, its usually about twice the size as what I got. I'd compare it to a tapas portion rather than appetizer. Still tasty, though. The burgers were huge and came with those darn tasty fries. The awesome thing about boyfriends is when they let you snag a few bites from their plate without caring :) Some things seem to be priced well, while others *cough*salad*cough* are just not worth it. But the food is definitely good, as is the beer on tap, and when your laid back waiter is nice and VERY attentive to your table, a good meal is had by all.

    Listed in Northern Liberties Livin', First you take a drink, then the drink takes a drink..., Summer Days

    LOVE their second floor deck.  Though it's small, It's one of my favorite places to drink outside when the weather is nice.  It always gets a good amount of sun during the day and at night, there is always a lovely breeze that passes through.  Before a bevy of condos and buildings started to spring up southwest of the Tap, you could see the center city skyline.

    I usually skip the downstairs space and go directly upstairs to either the bar or the deck.  In the winter months, they have a roaring fireplace that makes the place positively cozy.  In the spring, summer, and early autumn there's that sweet little deck.  I rarely go here to eat, I'm not entirely impressed with their kitchen (inconsistent and lackluster) - but when I do, I stick to their sandwiches (the burger, in particular) and some of their seafood plates (mussels, clams, octopus).

    It goes without saying (but I will anyways), that Tap is the destination for good brew drinking (some serious, and ever-local selection) - and for the hair o' the dog: their killer Bloody Mary.

    Got a spot on the deck on a gorgeous Thursday night. The deck here is one of the better outdoor dining spots in the city. It's tucked on the side of the building, accessible from the second-floor dining and bar areas. It's a cozy, bi-level, umbrella-strewn space with a bit of greenery thrown in. Neither too large or two small - it's perfect, actually!

    Even in the cooler months, Standard Tap has always been one of my mainstays, and usually is my destination of choice when headed to Northern Liberties. This place has a great beer selection and the food rarely, if ever disappoints. This place has a decided "pub" feel, so don't go expecting anything fancy. The menu is written on chalk boards hanging at various places on the walls. The burger here is one of my favorites to be had in Philly.

    Service can be a bit absent-minded at times, but not to the extent that it impairs the experience.

    I've always found the crowd here to be refreshingly diverse. Both the dining and bar areas can get pretty loud on busy nights, so I'd bear that in mind if you're considering taking a date here.

    Overall: good food, good atmosphere, good beer.

    Standard Tap is almost exactly what you'd expect when going there.  I'm sure by that point you've Yelp'd it and are looking forward to a good restauranting experience.  It lives up to that, I'd say.

    The food is spot on for a gastropub and you won't be disappointed with the selection.  I had the Double Standard Burger (12oz versus 8oz for the Standard Burger) and it was fantastic.  One of the best burgers I've had in the city so far.  As usual, I would recommend this no more cooked than medium-rare.  Very tasty, very filling.  Fries were average.

    Wife got the duck salad, which was also very good.  The duck was actually warm which is always a plus in my book, as oftentimes they throw cold meats on salads which isn't to my liking.  The duck was juicy and just the right amount of salt (read: a little too salty for some people).  The greens were...greens (sorry, the duck is really all that matters on this dish).

    Friend got the ham and brie sandwich.  I would say the best thing about this entree was the portion size.  One might expect a ham and brie sandwich to be two slices of meat with a little bit of brie and some veggies.  Wrong.  There was about 1-1.5 inches of ham on this thing.  And the flavor was great.  I'm not a HUGE brie fan, but I still enjoyed it.  Again, the fries were average.

    Dessert was peach cobbler a la mode.  This was very nice, and very different.  By different I mean that the breading was extra thick, so that your first bite has barely any peach in it.  This was actually not as bad as it may sound, because their breading is awesome.

    Beer: solid selection, but nothing to write home about.  It was "Flight Night" when we went so were were able to get two different flights of three beers each.  One was $6, the other $9.  They have a fair selection of the usual brews and plenty to keep you entertained for the night, but maybe not enough to keep you coming back to try to types (unless they rotate their list out regularly, which I don't know).

    The bad: we sat on the terrace which was great, however, they use two chalkboards for their menus (don't even have paper copies) and these were annoyingly placed where you have to be at one of two or three select tables in order to read them.  We had to keep getting up to look at the menu before we could order another round.

    All in all: I'll be back!

Specialties

Standard Tap features locally-brewed draft beer.  Our food menu both complements the beer and expands the notion of what tavern food can be.  We use only the best available produce, poultry, meats and seafood -- much of it from area farms and local vendors - to create fresh, seasonal dishes.

Our full menu is available from 4 pm to 1 am, seven days a week.  Brunch is served Sat. & Sun. from 11 am to 3 pm.

Wine and liquor also available.

History

Established in 1999.

When Standard Tap opened its doors in December 1999, our goal was simple:  to create a neighborhood tavern that featured great beer and wholesome food.  With that in mind, we offer a draft-only beer selection that highlights the vast array of styles crafted by breweries located in Pennsylvania, New Jersey and Delaware.  Our food menu both complements the beer and expands the notion of what pub food can be.  We use only the best available produce, poultry, meats and seafood -- much of it from area farms and local vendors - to create fresh, seasonal dishes.
