    Bufad's new BOGO Sunday promotion might be the best pizza deal in the city. We had two quality Neapolitan style pies here for $14. Still, even without money-saving specials Bufad's pies will satisfy the live-fired pizza craving if you find yourself in the area.

    The lack of space here could prove problematic on a busier night, but I like the intimate wood-and-brick rustic aesthetic they have. Bufad's concise menu has a decent amount of options neatly divided between red sauce and more seasonal fare (the seasonal stuff usually tastes better). The pizzas' crusts are superb, rimmed with perfect amounts of char, crisp, slightly puffy, and intensely flavorful. I dug my slightly over-salted pannocchia pie with its unique combination of corn sweetness, mushroom funk, and salty pancetta and cheese. My companion's melanzane had rich eggplant flavor, hearty sauce, and mellow ricotta, which while tasty failed to make as big an impression as my pizza.

    Still, both pies competed well with the better known Neapolitan haunts in Philly (Stella, Zavino, Barbuzzo, and the like) and were a couple bucks cheaper even without the promo. Also, many reviewers complain about the staff's douchiness, but everyone we interacted with acted friendly and courteous. Bufad renders trips to other pizza spots superfluous if you're nearby or don't want to deal with the headache of parking in more popular areas.

    I really don't understand these bad reviews.  I'm pretty picky about food and service and I can't imagine what these people had to complain about.  It's not a corner, greasy pizza joint.  It is exactly what Philly and that strip of neighborhoods (Callowhill/NoLibs/Fishtown) in particular does so well: high quality, inventive food in a fun, cool and relaxed atmosphere.  I'll take you through our dining experience.

    1) The Antipasti that day was phenomenal.  The green beans had the perfect amount of bite.  The crostini was out of this world.  Of course who doesn't like tomato and mozzarella?

    2) The Tritato was also fantasic.  Really well balanced fresh and rich components.  I forgot which cheese was in this, but it  was a really nice choice.

    3) The Rapini pizza was also well balanced.  I'm not usually a fan of spicy food but the sausage was the perfect amount.  Not overpowering and it really added something special.  Obviously it doesn't need to be said that the thin crust, wood fired pizza is awesome.

    4) The Margherita was obviously good.  Its a classic and it was done well.  I don't understand why the uncooked sauce is any different than the slow cooked.  Whatever the reason, I'm sure the chef is right because it was tasty.

    5) The Zucchini pizza was probably my favorite.  I LOVE ricotta and it was not in short supply.  As with the other pizzas, awesome crust and really well balanced.

    6) For desert the table split the peaches/honey/greek yogurt concoction and the chocolate panna cotta.  Both were killer.  The panna cotta in particular was decadent but not heavy, just a really nice finish to the meal.

    To top it all off we accidentally stumbled into BOGO pizza night.  What a deal!  Whoever is giving this place bad reviews clearly doesn't understand the type of place they are going to.  Its not trying to be a corner, greasy pizza shop.  Its trying to be a high quality Neapolitan style shop, and its succeeding beautifully.  I'll be back next Sunday for sure.

    The only thing that kept me from giving 5 stars is they haven't worked out the timing on the dishes.  The food for our party came out at all sorts of odd times.  Still amazing, but room for improvement in that department.

    This place had been on my go-to list for quite some time, and I was excited a larger group of us could finally go so we could try a lot of their flavors. Unfortunately, bigger groups (we were 6) aren't easy to seat at Bufad so we ended up sitting outside where it was a little chillier than was comfortable.

    We started with an Antipasti platter- good but not great. My favorite bite was the sformato because it was the most unique. I could have easily prepared everything else on the platter at home. Their Prosciutto salad, on the other hand, was delicious. We split it amongst 6 of us, but I could eat one just by myself.

    And for the pizzas:
    Pomodori Freschi: like a Margherita but even fresher tasting. Ours could have used a hint more salt, and the crust got a little soggy with the juicy tomatoes and creamy burrata.

    Pannochia: pops of corn were delicious sweet, little surprises in this pie. It could have used a bit more shiitake mushroom and truffle, but the combo of flavors is something I'll definitely be copying at home

    Melanzane: I had high hopes for this because I love eggplant, ricotta, and egg, but the execution of this was a bit off. The eggplant pieces were dry and flavorless and it needed more salt and more basil.

    Salsiccia: The sweet juicy grapes and salty dry sausage were a good pairing in this. I loved the almond bits for the added crunch. This was probably the best executed of the ones we tried, but it was still lacking a satisfying salty and umami-y punch.

    Overall, this place has so much potential. It just lacks in some of the execution. The crust is delicious, the flavor profiles are unique, but all of the pies need a touch more salt, a touch more herb, and a touch more veggie to really make them stand out and sing.

    There are five things (according to me and my boyfriend) on which people can judge their pizza:.
    1. Crust thickness/ taste
    2. Sauce
    3. Cheese
    4. Overall pizza taste
    5. Value

    Bufad was recommended by my human Yelp, Mindy K., but I have to disagree. We were disappointed.  Each of the five judging points had issues.

    I will say that I liked the crust thickness but my boyfriend didn't because the outside crust part was too thick in comparison to the rest of the pizza. However, we both agreed that the crust was salty. Like, noticeably salty.

    My boyfriend got a sausage and veggie pizza (I forge the name) and I got the Brussels sprout pizza without meat. The former is a red sauce pizza the latter not but it was difficult to tell because there was a serious lack of sauce. And cheese no couldn't even see the cheese on mine and there was just one tiny slice of mozzarella on each slice of my boyfriend's.

    The overall taste of each of ours was lacking. The Brussels sprouts were practically raw and would have been way better roasted. Or cooked in some fashion. The sausage was very flavorful with the fennel inside but then there was a broccoli rabe-like veggie which was overpowering in bitterness. Combined with the salty crust, he was unhappy and felt that the pizza did not mesh well at all.

    It was gourmet pizza and we paid a gourmet price but didn't feel like the value was good at all.

    The vibe of Bufad was also a little off. The waitresses did not smile and came by the table to ask how things were but then didn't stay to actually hear our answer.  It was freezing out and we had made a reservation so when we asked to sit not right in front of the door and were told we couldn't because the back tables were reserved, I was not pleased. And then NONE of the parties showed up so we were cold for no reason. I don't know how long people are hanging out at Bufad but the 1.5 hour wait seemed excessive for a half empty restaurant.
