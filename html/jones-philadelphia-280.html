    Jones, you were one of the first places I ever visited when I moved to Philadelphia.  You were the last place I visited before I left.  My boyfriend suggested going to Jones one afternoon, when we were rolling around Center City and looking for some comfort grub.  The decor was understated and sort of reminded me of a 'seventies vacation pad--tall pillars of natural rock and deeply recessed areas to lounge.  The menu reminded me of everything I wished my health food-loving mom would have made when I was a kid.

    My boyfriend had the Chicken and Waffles, which were as good as the ones in South Central.  He had a side of the slimmest, saltiest French fries ever, which were amazing to dip into the homemade chicken gravy.  I was a little more restrained with the Sesame Seared Tuna.  The tuna was just the right shade of rare with a tangy wasabi-soy sauce to compliment it.  The best part of our first visit were the drinks.  It didn't matter which drink we had--be it one of the hand-stirred fruity cocktails or the sparkling Jones soda--everything was good.  Our server was attentive and wonderful and introduced me to the Poached Pear Martini, which is still one of my favourite drinks anywhere.

    Every other visit has promised the same, whether we've crawled in there late night after hitting the city or stumbled in on a Sunday morning for a hangover cure.  We both have our favourites, but I'll sometimes get a wild craving for the French fries and chicken gravy and we know where to go.  Their Potato Pancakes are to die for, as is the Baked Macaroni and Cheese.  It's all the type of food that your grandma might make you if you visited on a Saturday afternoon, and that's okay.

    We all need a little comfort!

    The food on the other hand was a *total* disappointment. In fact, I'm not convinced anything was actually cooked there. We ordered the chicken and ginger dumplings, Tuna Steak Tacos and the Meatloaf to share. The dumplings were fine, but tasted like something I get out of the freezer case at Trader Joes. The filling to the tacos was pretty good actually, but they were served on the hard taco shells that I swear were the Old El Paso brand you can buy at the grocery store. I asked for a side of salsa--also not fresh or homemade. I asked my husband what the glaze on the meatloaf was and he described it as "brown gravy and ketchup, mixed together". Yuck! In short, there is nothing remotely memorable that gave me any indication they are doing anything in the kitchen that doesn't involve a can opener or deep frying pre-cooked packaged food. What a waste of chic place with good atmosphere. I won't go back, and would suggest you don't waste your money here.

    And I was really surprised by the low quality, given all the good reviews I've read here and for as popular this place seems. You're not going to find anything offensive here, and if you're serving picky eaters you'll probably be safe. But I won't electively go back to Jones. Save your money.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
