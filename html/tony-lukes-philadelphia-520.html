    Listed in 2013 Yelp 100 Bonanza Challenge, 5-Star Reviews, Bucket List, Philadelphia Freedom

    My business associate and I have spent a few jam-packed days meeting with clients and working on presentations during this week in Philadelphia, and he promised me a break from all of the work with a driving tour of the city and a stop at one of his favorite places to eat. So we bundled up against the cold weather and criss-crossed through various neighborhoods as he told me the history of Philadelphia, and shared some stories about growing up in the Italian neighborhoods of South Philly.

    During our journey we drove past both Pat's and Geno's, and no telling how many other famous emporiums of cheesesteak goodness as we cruised along the major thoroughfares. Finally he parks the car on a side street, under the highway, and we march around the corner to find ourselves standing in front of the original location of Tony Luke's. Now Frank has not led me wrong all week with his recommendations for places to eat, so I was certainly psyched to try a real Philly cheesesteak.

    I was freezing my niblets off in the very cold weather (after all I'm from Florida, and this frozen tundra crap is someone else's idea of fun), but sure enough we stood in line - OUTSIDE - while waiting for our turn to place our order. The menu is pretty straight forward... you have roast beef, roast pork, veal cutlet, chicken cutlet, chicken steak or rib-eye sandwiches along with a couple of other options for vegetarians and seafood lovers. At Frank's urging I ordered a Roast Beef Italian with Provolone and grilled onions and a side of onion rings.

    HOLY WOW!!!! I'm not the kind of guy to say that one South Philly cheesesteak is better than another, and then have some sort of fight-to-the-death over bragging rights. I'm just going to say that this food was awesome -- tender, tasty, and flat out amazing.

    Yeah, it's touristy and gimmicky, but when the food tastes this good I'm not going to complain.
