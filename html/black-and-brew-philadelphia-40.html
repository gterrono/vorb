    When it comes to coffee, I am the cheapest person alive.  I almost NEVER buy coffee during the week (because my office makes it for free).  When I do buy it, I go to the cheapest places available (Dunkin Donuts or Wawa... although I'm sure many people would argue that the coffee is just as good).  But I've been thinking lately how when I do make my infrequent coffee purchases, I should go to local businesses instead.

    Enter in Black N Brew.  This little spot is right in my 'hood and I walk past it almost daily.  I went once for breakfast a year or so ago, but I stopped in this past weekend for some breakfast.  I got a plain, small iced coffee (refer to the comment about how cheap I am above) and a bagel with butter.  The total was about $4, which is about what I'd pay at Dunkin Donuts for the same thing.

    I'm not super picky about my coffee (unless its that instant crap... yuck!) so I enjoyed my iced coffee.  The bagel was pretty good too.  

    I wish I wasn't so cheap because I'd really like to try all the fancy shmancy coffee drinks they sell here.  They sound amazing!  

    Space wise, it's pretty tight inside.  There are maybe 4 or 5 outdoor tables, but the one is right next to one of the city garbage cans.. ew.

    Overall, I think this place is great and is well worth a visit.  Especially if you are on your way to the Dunkin Donuts around the corner-- support your local businesses!

    East Passyunk, you've come a long way in the eight or so years that I strolled around your storefronts.

    Wedged between two fairly impoverished areas, this corner of South Philly still has a great old school Italo-greaser vibe, best evidenced in the sign for the soon-to-be-defunct King of Jeans down the street.  But the bourgeois bohemians leave nothing in their wake, so the Italian mom-and-pops that don't close will simply adapt with the times and start going upscale (and probably make a killing for the people who came to the nabe seeking something authentic, all the while squelching it through the higher rental rates that they command).

    Enough of that though.  Black N Brew is about as typical for this neighborhood as you get.  Chalkboard menu on three walls that's hard to follow but has lots of great options which are only really creative if you've never visited one of Black N Brew's doppelgangers in other East Coast Cities.  Just because it's not unique doesn't make it bad--my burrito was decent enough.  The iced tea, which was awful enough to make my friend and I question if the leaves had spoiled, was promptly replaced by the good stuff...but it does make me wonder since we got there late in the morning.  Did no one else notice, or did they think a conventional iced tea is supposed to taste that way?

    You go to a place like this for the ambiance, which, in a transitional neighborhood like East Passyunk, makes for great people watching for the patrons of the restaurant, or in the outdoor deuces for dog-walkers, bike messengers, eternal grad students, yuppies with baby strollers (and their sights on the suburbs), and yes, even the occasional aging Guido.

Specialties

Black N Brew is a neighborhood full scale coffee shop with homemade cafe style food. We prepare all of our food on premise. We believe in providing yummy meals that satisfy but don't stuff you to the gills.  We offer a creative menu with plenty of healthy options for all. There are plenty of vegetarian options as well as a few vegan treats offered on any given day. We have free wifi and are dog friendly. Black n Brew PROUDLY serves La Colombe coffee and Metropolitan Bread (comes in fresh daily). We really take pride in what we do and wanted to create a place where anyone could come and feel welcome and inspired! Also if you are into art we feature monthly local artist work on our walls and the outside of our building is a mosaic by Isaiah Zagar.

History

Established in 2007.

Black N Brew is a staple on East Passyunk Ave. Fellow business owners and neighborhood people love Black n Brew as their meeting spot! You can find, old south Philly, new homeowners, business owners, real estate agents and hipsters all enjoying coffee and a bite to eat on any given day.
