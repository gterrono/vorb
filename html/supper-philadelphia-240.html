    This is the second time we've eaten at Supper and both times I have been very happy with my meal.  We went with a couple who like to do good, interesting food.  Supper has a fun menu: it was hard to whittle down to just three appetizers.  The masa fries were good, the deviled eggs were really something else (siracha flavored one was really yummy) and the cheese puffs were very good.  

    For dinner we all debated on several dishes.  I think the risotto was the only veggie option.  I wish they had done a few more because their meat dishes were pretty unique.  I had the pork belly with the sweet potato spoon bread.  Everything on my plate was superbly balanced.  Plus, the pork belly wasn't as heavy as I had dreaded- it's a hard cut to not come out a little greasy.  This one was really REALLY good- I didn't want to give up any bites to try the duck and waffles (which was good but I liked mine better).  

    Dessert wise we tried the brown butter pudding (delicious), waffles again (I didn't have any but our friend said they were very good) and the apple cobbler (really REALLY delicious).  The wine choices were good as were the cocktails.  This was a very relaxed, refreshing evening.  I would definitely give this restaurant a shot- it's on my short list of nice places to go with friends for a delicious meal in Philly.

    I was part of a family brunch for 9 today.  I'll try to give you a general idea not only of what I thought, but what everyone else thought at the table.  

    Now, in my mind at least, my surprise to myself was that I spent the better part of the week looking over their menu, and thinking to myself that when I get there I was going to order something rich and decadent like those Red Velvet Waffles or the Banana Cream Pie French Toast.  

    So, once presented with the option, what did I go for?  The frittata with a bagel and side of bacon.  Yeah, that's pretty much what I'd order at any diner.  Silly me.  

    Well, at the last minute I craved the basics... eggs, bread, and bacon.  So how did they do on the basics?  Very well, I thought.  The frittata is pretty sizable served in the iron skillet.  I ordered sausage and gruyere in mine, and actually thought I didn't get a lot of those ingredients in there.  Maybe a slight skimp.  But still quite good since they didn't skimp on the egg.  I loved the bagel as they make their own everything bagels.  The bacon was served in 4 big hearty strips and crispy.  I thought they'd give me more potatoes, but they weren't too bad.  Overall, I was pleased if not totally overwhelmed.  

    Overall our table was pleased.  One person felt the veggies they got with their frittata were a little raw.  I heard more than one complaint about the slowness of service.  Yeah, sometimes they tend to ignore your table for 10-15 minutes.  

    Interesting note... I sampled a duck fat fry from someone's plate and was quite surprised.  I was surprised that it didn't taste completely decadent.  I've had these fries at other establishments and they come out sinfully rich.  These were almost a little on the light tasting side.  They were almost like regular ol' steak fries.  

    My wife was pleased with her frittata so much that she wants to consider coming back for dinner.  So perhaps a future visit is in order.  

    Overall, it's a very good brunch choice.  I'm not sure it's a perfect experience.  Food quality is a pretty solid 4 stars.

    I've heard good things about Supper and was excited to drop by for brunch.  I was let down.  The food was mostly good, but the service was sub-par.

    The initial greetings from the hostess was nonexistent, her attitude was far from friendly.  Our waiter acted like it was his first day on the job (maybe it was?) - we encountered several awkward moments with him while ordering and in between each dish.   Oh well, we're all awkward in one way or the other I guess.

    I had the hoboken benny with two fried eggs, taylor ham, smoked mozz, garlic pizza knots, oregano hollandaise and tomato jam.  This was quite good. Although I could have done without the tomato jam, which stuck out - flavor wise - like a sore thumb.  I had a bite of the supper dog - pork shoulder wrapped in bacon, deep fried and served with onions, beer mustard and fried pickles.  I'm not a big fan of mustard, but I thought it went well on the supper dog.  Certainly not the choice to make if you're looking for something healthy!  Luckily, both of us were in the mood to indulge.

    For dessert, we wrapped it up with the housemade piña colada doughnuts with toasted coconut and the chocolate mud pie with chocolate chip ice cream.  The doughnuts were super light yet tasty.  The mud pie was super heavy and yet tasty.  The scoop of chocolate chip ice cream was tiny in comparison to the slice of pie, so make sure you get your share of the ice cream before your brunch mate takes it all!

    Tip - if you're coming around the afternoon, make sure you don't sit in the front corner of the restaurant.  The sun will hit you in the eyes like there's no tomorrow.  Alternatively, bring a pair of shades.

HUGE WTF.  What is this the Continental Breakfast at the Holiday Inn?

    The BF had the grillades, grits n' greens.  He loved every bite of it, except the one small bite I was able to snag for a taste!  The other couple had the skillet roasted mushroom toast and the hoboken benny.  The mushroom toast was not what we expected but she said it was really good.  I had the supper frittata with cheddar and sauteed greens.  I have to say my dish was the only disappointing one out of the group!  I probably just made a bad choice, but felt limited because I don't like fried eggs and the majority of the dishes came with fried eggs.  The frittata was way too much egg compared to filling.  You only get two fillings without paying extra, which I feel is the downfall here.  I would rather have a skillet of veggies and cheese, with a little egg mixed in.  I had to slop my dish up with ketchup and hot sauce to get it down.

I felt as though I was on a farm & someone had prepared the most organic and healthy frittata this side of the Mason Dixon. The kale had a mild crispy texture that worked well with the eggs, gruyere and bacon. Each has a harmonious flavor that seasoned the egg without needing any salt, pepper or any other seasoning. The potatoes on top actually was a great substitute for bread. Now I have eaten many servings of grits in my day but hands down these were the best.     J had the strawberry shortcake pancakes which tasted so much like the actual dessert. No syrup was needed as the strawberries and cream topping were all the sweetness these pancakes needed. These pancakes are a must order because they are divine.

    Well done Supper, I will return soon.....

Specialties

Supper serves seasonal modern Amercian cuisine in a beautiful Urban Farmhouse setting.  Whenever and wherever possible we source local, organic and sustainable ingredients.  All charcuterie, preserves, pickles and desserts are made in house as well as the signature housebaked crackers for our All American artisan cheese selection.
Supper is open 7 nights for dinner service beginning at 5pm.  We are open for lunch Wednesdays through Saturdays from 11:30 am to 2:30 pm and for our acclaimed Sunday brunch from 11:00 am to 2:30 pm.
Supper is also available for private events.  We can custom design a menu for you through our catering company, The Global Dish or from our a la carte menu selections.  Please contact Sue Solomon so that we can help you plan an event to remember.
