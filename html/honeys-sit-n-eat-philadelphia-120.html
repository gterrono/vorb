    Aside from the standard praise for the breakfast, this is one of the few places in Philly where you can find biscuits with sausage county gravy. I'm not too keen on the fact  that the sausage isn't actually in the gravy, rather it's just a single sausage patty on top of the biscuits, and the sausage could be a bit more spicy than it were, but still very good biscuits and gravy.     Years ago, I lived in diners. I spent many a-night at the legendary Mayfair Diner (which is nowhere near it's former glory, but that's another review). I matured along with my palette and started branching out to actual restaraunts, and I don't mean the chain kind. But I'll always have a soft spot for diners as I've been to probably 100+ in my lifetime. And now I think I've found my favorite.

    I write this after my 10th or so visit to Honey's, which has only once disappointed (sickeningly sweet French toast with canned peaches and an endless wait for my check, but I digress...). 9 out of 10 times, however, I've loved it. And while the reasons are many, there are 2 letters that sum up what I love about Honey's:

    O
    J

    The orange juice is so freshly squeezed here, there are still seeds in the glass. It seems to good to be true to think that someone is in the kitchen literally squeezing orange after orange into a jar, yet it is the freshest-tasting OJ you'll ever have, it can be the only explanation. There's no juice that's ever come remotely close to this. It goes from the orange to the cup to your mouth in the very same day.

    And then there's the wheat pancakes. I've eaten 5 times my body weight in pancakes over the course of a lifetime, and Honey's wheat pancakes stuffed with granola and fruit are the best pancakes I've ever had, especially in the summer when they use fresh berries. I believe I'll go the grave with this opinion, because they'll never be topped.

    One last thing. If you go in the winter time, finish your meal off with Honey's homemade hot chocolate. It is as rich as dark chocolate gets, and oh so good.

    Service is a typical gripe, and I guess the service here can be flighty, but I've never been so upset that I let it ruin a great meal. And I've honestly never had to wait that long despite what most people say. So I can say with confidence that Honey's is and always will be my favorite diner.
