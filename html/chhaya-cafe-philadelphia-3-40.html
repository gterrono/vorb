        Listed in A great brunch in Philly? Where to even start, Top 5 Philly brunch

        I don't want to write a review about Chhaya. Mostly because I don't want you (yes, you reading this) to come and spoil my fun. I want to keep Chhaya all to myself, and only share it with close friends, because it's got this great feel of not being one of the major 'brunch oclock' places in Philadelphia- and I don't want you to ruin it. OK? so if you go, seriously, keep it under your hat? deal?

        All joking aside, Chhaya truly is the perfect atmosphere I'm looking for in a brunch spot. It's just an small coffee house with perfect coffees and teas, foods, and free wifi. The waitresses are super attentive and fun to talk to- like fully fleshed out characters not just interested in taking your order, but actually having a human conversation- what what?

        As for the food, dear lord, where to start. My friend had the heuvros rancheros waffles- maybe I ate like 1/4 of his plate, my bad. Let's talk about his entree first- heuvros rancheros is a delicious meal but to me I always feel like when it comes it's just beans, and that's not what I want in breakfast. Theirs is served atop cornbread waffles, with eggs, black beans, salsa,  and practically a whole avocado and it's just perfect. It's filling yet the waffles manage to maintain a pleasant lightness and the portion size isn't overwhelming, it's exactly enough food to satiate- I love a place that doesn't think they need to have HUGE food in order to have GREAT food. There's definitely a difference, and this place has got it.

        As for me I had the savory crepe of the day, it was filled with gouda, fennel, roasted tomatoes, arugula, and topped with an egg, all for only 7.50 I think- How is that even possible? It was simply the best, melt in your mouth, delightful brunch I have had in awhile- and I brunch constantly so how did they top the others? I have no idea, they just did. I think the atmosphere of the place certainly made everything just jive for the perfect brunch experience. The place is small, but it wasn't packed when I was there which was really lovely. Basically I eating here made me want to break my lease and move to East Passyunk. no big deal. Just an awesome life changing, mood lifting brunch. You have definitely created a repeat customer Chhaya- much thanks!

        Based on the fantastic Yelp reviews, I suggested this place to my family for Saturday morning. We were a party of six. They cannot easily accomodate that large of a group inside.         Listed in Lamentable experiences, Bed, Brunch, Bed

        Oh...a tiny breakfast. How quaint.

          After reading reviews extolling the virtues of Chhaya, I was anxious to witness this culinary righteousness for myself. My Lady and I agreed to blow off our usual brunch favourtites, and instead head over to E. Passyunk Street.  We walked into a dining room which was slightly smaller than our living room, albeit tidy. We were immediately informed that there is no table service on the weekdays, lest we ask for more than we are entitled to. We sat down and perused le menu.  Waffles seemed to be the speciality of the house, so we decided to try them.

            My CD sized waffle arrived at my table unaccompanied by any of the usual suspects...not even a lowly garnish. Lady Mary's breaky looked similarly lonely, but at least we each had a non-refillable $4 cup of coffee to keep our waffles company.  The flavour wasn't at all bad, however.  The sausage gravy was acceptably authentic (on mine), and nutella was used in a reserved judicious manner (on the other). The main problem is that the portion wasn't even close to meal sized. I felt like I was being punished for some unknown transgression. No table service for you Urqhart!!! And I hope you like your waffles extra small boy!!!!! MUAHHAAA!!!   We ate silently as stroller after stroller bumped into our table.  I must admit, I didn't at all agree with the rave reviews I read here; quite the contrary.  Beau Monde is to Chhaya as Miles Davis is to Kenny G.

          As an aside, we've noticed most of the restaurants in the Passyunk square follow a bit of a motif.  State Side, Plenty, Chhaya, Cantina, Birra...all entirely too hip for your own good.  Owners need to do two things: 1. Go to a good pub in Europe (that's the hard one), and 2. go around the corner to Carmen's Country Kitchen.  It's elevated, with a good deal less 'la-de-da', and one leaves feeling with the satisfaction of just having eaten a holiday meal.  I don't care that all the restaurants in the square have artisan light bulbs. I don't care that the portions are stylishly lilliputian . I don't care that the napkins are made from recycled grocery bags.  I truly, truly, with all my heart, don't care. I just want quality food (and enough to fill me up), sometimes a decent pint, at a good price.  I'm putting the Passyunk Square on my no fly zone for the foreseeable future. Off to greener pastures.
