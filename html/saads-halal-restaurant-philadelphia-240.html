    Saad's Halal Restaurant (I'm still chuckling over the specific inclusion of "Halal" in their title) was boisterous and filled to the brim with people. Which is a good indication of how popular a joint this is. Because we went as a family, we had the opportunity to try a variety of orders including their Philly cheesesteak, falafel platter, lamb maroush and chicken maroush sandwich. All of them were supremely satisfying. The platters came with bread and two sides and even with 5 people we left with enough leftovers for another two meals. As for drinks, we got the savory yogurt drink that's in the fridge next to the counter. I was expecting something sweet, but I guess I should've checked the ingredients. The staff was also nice enough to split our sandwiches into five portions so we could all share. The order took about as long as you'd expect when you walk in and see that every table is occupied and the line in front of the counter is a good 3-5 people deep.  Finally, we were impressed with how neat the place was, despite the crowd, and the full complement of take out needs by the front entrance (including plastic bags and little containers to categorize your food into).
    Oh, but don't forget that they don't accept credit cards! Debit cards are fine though.

    Every once in awhile I come across a place I cannot believe have not reviewed yet. Saad's is around the corner from my house and I have visited them many times. This is a true West Philly gem. There is no other part of town, not even you fancy pants Center City, that I have found to serve as good Middle Eastern food as Saad's.

    My staple is the Chicken Shish Tawook platter. It comes with grilled chicken, rice, and two sides. The chicken is perfectly grilled. The rice is plump and juicy. There's a white garlic sauce that's real tasty. I always want extra because it really makes the chicken and rice that much more tasty.

    My favorite side is the Tabouly salad. It is bomb-tastic. I've had this before and did't care for it, but Saad's not only makes me care for it, but love it. It is always so fresh and the juices are sublime.

    I've rotated for my second side. I've gotten hummus several times and it's awesome. Same for the baba ghanouj. I recently got the Greek salad and it too was great. I have also got fries a few times, mostly because my daughter is a fry lover. They're good fries, but this is not why you go to Saad's.

    One time I got the Chicken Shish Tawook Maroosh Way sandwich. I good lose my mind over this sandwich. There's no way this can be good for your heart, but it tastes oh so good. One day I will get it again, I am sure of it. But I must brace myself for how good it tastes and what it might do to my artery.

    Giddy-up!

    Saw the great reviews for this place while exploring Philly. I walked a mile to get here, but did not realized they were closed on Fridays from 1:30-2:30pm for prayer. Lucky me, I got there at 1:27pm. Driven by the high ratings on Yelp and a thirst to try their Chicken Maroosh, I tried again for lunch the following day with my boyfriend.

    9/20/14 (Saturday 2:30pm-3:00pm)

    This place was pretty packed when we got there, but we were greeted with a very warm smile at the counter. Order and pay after you get your food at the counter. (I was a noob and tried to pay immediately)

    Chicken Maroosh: Portion was very generous and the sub was sliced in two halves. Perfect for sharing. Sauce was very juicy, and the chicken was nice and warm. Tasted very good like the yelp reviews say, but I didn't think it was amazing like some yelpers out there.
    Philly Cheesesteak: Giant portion, overflowing from the bread. Also sliced in half, good for sharing. Cheese was melted perfectly and completed this meal. A messy eat, so you may want to delve in with the fork.

    Total: $17 + $1 for an Arizona drink

    Overall, will likely visit again when I'm in Philly. Boyfriend may likely become a regular. Good price, good portion, and good taste.
