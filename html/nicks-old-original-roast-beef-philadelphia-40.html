    I'm embarrassed to say that I've lived in and around Philly all my life, been to S. Philly for all kinds of food 100's of times - but have never been to the Original Nick's.  I've alway's known it was there, but just never got there. In the neighborhood for a free outdoor jazz concert - between sets went to Nick's for a quick beef and beer.

    I would not rate this as the best roast beef sandwich ever, but it's right up there near the top and definitely a place worth going to - whether it's for a quickie, takeout or to stay and watch a game. Place is really just an old school, south philly corner bar (with lot's of character and character's) that serves an excellent beef sandwich.  

    We stategically chose to sit at the far end of the bar, right by the carving station to see how they work their magic. (There's also a small dining room in the back). So here's the deal:

    Two guy's - 1st guy hand carve's the beef, the other assembles the sandwich.

    The Beef - Moist, tender, flavorful, sliced thin.

    The amount of beef - piled on high, but not too high. I'd say there's a 1/3 to 1/2 lb. of meat - just the right amount. Once the guy carefully positions the beef on the bun, then he drops a spoonful of gravy (au jus, debris or whatever you like to call it) on the beef, then places one slice of provolone (I wish it was sharp prov.), then drops a little more gravy on the cheese, and then lightly presses on the top and cuts in half.

    The bread - nothing special, just a fresh, decent standard kaiser roll that slowly falls apart as you eat it - so it gets messy. Make sure you have plenty of napkins, because the ones they have are so thin and cheap, you need a stack of them to keep yourself from looking like a slob.

    Condiments - Mustard, hot peppers and horseradish are self serve.

    What could make it better -  a brown spicy mustard, better hot peppers, a better roll (maybe a long seeded roll from Liscio's or Sarcone's)

    It kills me not to give Nick's 5 stars, but I'm one of those people who needs room to go up for something I'll probably never see.  I have been a Nick's customer since 1981, and have never, ever, ever, had a less than awesome roast beef sandwich there.  It is truly one of the greatest sandwiches on Earth.  They expanded the menu from about 6 items maybe 15 years ago, and the additions are welcome.  Try the gravy fries loaded with beef bits and delectable gravy.  All the sandwiches are good, but just get the beef combo and be done with it.  All others, as great as they are, are sandwiches you are eating while you could have been eating a beef combo.  A beef combo with roasted hot peppers and gravy fries is as good a casual meal as you'll find.
        Okay, now I have to mention that the place is a dive bar.  I miss the days with the separate Ladies' Entrance and the french tickler condom machine in the Men's room, but at the same time I'm a little glad they've moved into the 20th century a little.  Unfortunately the neighborhood has gotten a little shaky and parking is always rough, especially if you get double parked.  It is still worth the trip though and it will be for years to come.

    I lived in Philadelphia the first 36 years of my life, before moving to sunny SoCal in January of '08.  I never made it to Nick's while I lived here, so I made it a point to take the wife and kids here during our vacation this week.

    As you can tell by the 2 stars, I was not impressed.  First off, the place was DIRTY.  Not like "charmingly dive-y"; no, everything in the place was covered by a pretty thick layer of grease.  Second of all, there were no high chairs.  Maybe I was expecting too much, but you can't even have a booster seat for my 1-year old?  Third, the service was horrible.  We waited 5 minutes for the older waitress to look up from her textbook (what, is she on the 20-year plan??) and deign to come to our table.  And she acted like we were troubling her.  I should have left then.

    On to the food.  For $6.50 you do get a very generous portion of roast beef, on a nice kaiser roll.  I got mine "overboard", and the gravy was good. I must admit that the gravy fries were great; the roast beef in the gravy fries was better than the sandwich.

    The bottom line is that Nick's is way overrated.  Go here if you want the South Philly bum's rush.  Otherwise, get a cheesesteak from Tony Luke's or Pat's.

    Don't be put-off that Nick's is somewhat of a dive.  They take their sandwiches very seriously.  For starters, the gravy fries are amazing!  Fries smothered in pan-drippings loaded with tender chunks of roast beef...it was a meal unto itself.  I thought the fries would be soggy, but somehow, they maintained the perfect balance of sogginess to crispiness.
    My roast-beef sandwich was ordered "overboard", which gets you additional gravy, which is delicious.  It was also topped with a slice of provolone cheese.  A spicy horseradish on the table was the final compliment to the generous amount of beef in this sandwich.  By far, this was the best roast-beef sandwich I've ever had.
    If you've never been to Nick's and find yourself there some day, let your server know that it's your first visit.  They'll clue you in on what they offer and how to order.  If you consider yourself picky, then this place is not for you.  Nick's is a "what you see is what you get" establishment.  If you're looking to get a killer sandwich from an unpretentious place, this is it.

    Best roast beef sandwich around, hands down.

    This is a classic South Philly establishment.  The roast beef sandwich is perfect and here are some tips about how to order from an old pro.

    They only have one kind of cheese, provolone.  Don't ask for anything else because you won't get it.

    A "Combo" is a sandwich with cheese.  So if you want roast beef with cheese just say "Beef Combo".

    You can order "side cuts" which means the darkened meat from the outside of the roast.

    If you like your sandwich with extra gravy, and who don't, make sure you order it "Overboard".

    BTW, if you leave without trying the "gravy fries" your doing yourself a great injustice. The gravy fries are crispy golden fries drizzled with gravy and tiny beef chunks.  OUT OF THIS WORLD!

    I got introduced to Nick's just this year; my sister who has been living in Philly these past 10 or so years found out about it, visited it and promptly called me to come up and try it.  I should've written this review months ago, but now is just as good.

    Let me start by agreeing with most everyone else on here- as far as roast beef sandwiches go, you'll find NONE better; not just in Philly, but ANYWHERE ELSE.  I'm no restaurant connoisseur, but I am a big fan of foods that I like and lets face it, I love beef.  So I was skeptical about some Philly dive making the best roast beef sandwiches, it sounded preposterous to me.  Not to mention, every Philly cheesesteak I've ever had has been underwhelming- at least in Philly.

    My mother always told me you shouldn't judge a book by its cover, but I felt no confidence whatsoever when I saw where this place was situated, and even less so once I walked inside.  Its dark, grimy, the clientele seemed primarily composed of older white males, 50 and over who favored beer over any other sort of libation.  I was with my wife, my sister and one of her friends- my sister was somewhat apprehensive because she could tell I was not feeling the place.  She assured me that appearances aside, it is well worth it.  She knows how I am.

    The waitress appeared, seemed friendly enough, knew her business so to speak.  The menu's small...roast beef, turkey, pork or baked ham are the meats, fries, broccoli rabe and italian hots are the 'sides' and of course, there's the gravy.  It didn't take long for my dry roast beef sandwich to arrive...the meat had a nice look to it, firm but not too firm, with a solid and satisfying bite.  The flavor was excellent; so naturally I tried a bite of my sister's overboard roast beef (lots of gravy) and my eyes went wide.  I'd never tasted a gravy that good before!  So I ordered some on the side and as soon as I got it, poured it over my sandwich.  My sandwich just went from good to amazing, just like that.  Add some of the sliced banana peppers or horse radish that is kept on every table and take it to the next level!  The fries were just ok, made much better with the addition of that wonderful beef gravy, but by themselves they're pretty nondescript.  Reminded me of the fries you used to get at BK back when they were trying to give you the ultra crispy fry that had that coating on it and didn't taste that great.  That's what these fries tasted like, but with the gravy they're consumable.

    My sister's friend got the roast turkey, and that too was an excellent sandwich; the turkey was tender but not falling apart (a sign that it has cooked too long usually), the turkey gravy was almost as good as the beef gravy and of course, the kaiser rolls they put these sandwiches in really do make the sandwich.  Nothing worse for a sandwich than bad bread (except bad filling of course)- but these rolls are fresh with a light crispy outside and a tender, chewy inside- perfect for holding onto all that gravy and great flavor.

    I've seen a kid or two come in but by and large, this is a bar so treat it as adults-only; Thursday nights and Sundays all day are football days- if you're not an Eagles fan this could present somewhat of a problem but it need not be; the roast beef is that good!  The beer selection's limited but at least the prices are decent and there's always a beer special- beer is the perfect complement to these sandwiches.  If you love a good roast beef sandwich and you're visiting from out of town, bring a cooler- you're going to want to take some home.  I guarantee it.

    You might ask what prompted me to write a review about a place that I first visited some 8 or so months ago?  I was there last weekend, which was my 4th time going so far.  Its great to see my sister too- but everytime I do go up there to see her, we end up at Nick's.  By design?  I hope so!
