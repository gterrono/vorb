    This is an expansion of the Honest Tom's Taco truck. They hoped to open this restaurant several months ago but I guess the renovations took longer than expected.

    Not too big a place, and at the moment more a take out place than an eat in restaurant. They currently have a table that seats 4, but I think that they will soon have another table and also stools so you can sit and eat at a wrap-around counter.

    They serve either tacos (2 for $6) or burritos ($8). You can get either with chicken, beef, or sweet potatoes. They also have a small selection of drinks. I think that they are byob.

    I tried a chicken taco and the beef burrito. The chicken taco was good but I liked the beef burrito much better.

    A nice addition to the neighborhood. Their pico de gallo and guacamole are good. My only complaint is that I wish that they would make their own red and green chile that you could have added to the taco or burrito if you want something more spicy. They do have commercial hot sauces on the side counter so you can add your own, but it would be nice if they had their own in-house red and green chile.

    Listed in Mexican Meals, West Philadelphia

    The bright colors of Honest Tom's Taco Shop pop from the grays, browns, bricks and stones that make up the rest of the landscape of 44th Street in West Philly. Inside, a lone table and some counter seating are available, but I wouldn't bet on free seats given the modest size of what will undoubtedly become a popular breakfast, lunch and dinner destination.

    Since I had only tried Tom's breakfast tacos on one other occasion (and loved them), I decided to check out a chicken burrito on my first of what will probably be many visits. We beat a small rush by mere seconds and waited patiently at the counter for our food, which was up pretty quickly. My burrito was basic and yet really satisfying. There was more than enough chicken, plus rice, black beans, cheddar and guacamole. I would have dug a little sour cream but honestly, it wasn't needed. Instead, I doused the thing in that same delicious hot sauce that I had used for my breakfast tacos. I need a bottle of that for my house to add to the growing hot sauce collection.

    The menu is small but that's okay for a couple reasons. One, they just opened. Two, this isn't fine dining Mexican or a place that you are going to enjoy a long and leisurely meal. It's a quick, satisfying option. And six or eight bucks for lunch is definitely okay with me. I'm looking forward to my next visit and seeing the business settle in.

    This place is really cute and colorful. They normally serve breakfast until 1, but all day on Sundays. The first time I came here it was just past 1 on a Saturday, so we missed out on the breakfast. We walked out with their normal burritos/tacos, which were okay. They were by no means bad, but neither were they especially good, at $8 per burrito and $6 for two tacos. The chicken was a bit dry, and the pork was kind of meh. For an $8 burrito, I expect not to be able to finish it, or be totally stuffed if I do. These weren't exactly small, but I finished it way too comfortably. Burritos elsewhere are often $7, and much larger/tastier.

    Breakfast, however, is the real reason to come here. At $7, the breakfast burrito is a behemoth of eggs, potato, cheese, guac, beans, sour cream, pico de gallo, and bacon on request (at no extra charge). Unfortunately, the bacon is not crispy, but soft and fatty. I had to pick out a few solid chunks of fat. Still, this is a pretty tasty breakfast burrito.

    Honest Tom's makes a nice neighborhood breakfast shop, but I wouldn't necessarily go out of my way, especially for the non-breakfast food.

    If you're looking for Honest Mexican food, this is not your destination, but if you're looking for a burrito west of 40th St, then this may be your answer.  

    While I agree with most reviewers that $8 for a burrito is steep, the beef burrito I had was delicious and came with guacamole, so the $8 won't keep me from going back.

    This stop is certainly a truck-turned-restaurant.  Their drink choices include, rootbeer, birch beer and flavored seltzer waters.  Their menu includes tacos, burritos and chips&guacamole with filling options of beef, chicken, pork or sweet potatoes.  They also offer a breakfast burrito before 11am.  The seating is extremely limited with 2 tables and 5 stools around a busy counter.  And most importantly, this is a CASH ONLY establishment without an ATM, so make sure to stop by the CVS at 43rd & Locust to get cash back before showing up.
