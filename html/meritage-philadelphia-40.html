    I have been to Meritage several times, and have only positive things to say about it. The food is very reasonably priced, and the small plates are definitely large enough to eat as an entree for those who don't have huge appetites.     Overall, though, go for the wine and the food. Truly cannot be missed!

    It makes me sad how disappointing Meritage was.. I was so hyped up to go after I read all the amazing reviews on Yelp. & as soon as I heard that they were bringing back the Korean fried chicken entree, I made a reservation.

    On the day of the reservation, I tried calling the restaurant multiple times, but it seems as though their phone wasn't working. I told the server about it when I got there but he didn't know about it at all. Anyways, moving onto the food.

    For an appetizer (me, my sister & my boyfriend) ordered the spicy shrimp scampi flavored with lemongrass & ginger over crostini. This was the best part of the meal. The shrimp were flavored just right, & all the juices saturated the crostini making it extra tasty. Sadly this is where the tastiness ends. We ordered the Korean fried chicken, & the grilled anger steak with cauliflower gratin and sautéed spinach as our entrees to share. I'm pretty sure why the fried chicken wasn't good to me was probably because I'm Korean & I know what Korean fried chicken should taste like. It should be crispy cos' it's double fried, a mixture of sweet/spicy/tangy. Meritage's chicken tasted like it was just marinated in straight up Korean hot pepper paste. The collard greens were whatever, & the mac & cheese was okay. One of the pieces of our chicken was pink on the inside, leaning towards undercooked. We asked our server to take it back to the kitchen & he replaced it with another piece of chicken. Sad thing was, the new piece was still undercooked & the server tried to convince us that's just the way its supposed to be. For $18 I could have gone to Soho cafe & for another $2 gotten 20 pieces of Korean fried chicken.

    Moving onto the hangar steak, the steak was very chewy, like beef jerky, & the spinach was so salty, like it was completely doused with salt. The only good thing on the plate were the fried sweet potato strings.

    Again I'm just so disappointed at how bad my experience was at Meritage. I wanted so bad to love it, but in the end it was just bad. Shame on Meritage's fake me out Korean fried chicken & chewy beef jerky steak.

    My boyfriend and I were in serious need of nourishment after an 8.5 hour ride down to Rittenhouse Square from Boston last weekend.  Yes.  8.5 hours.  Had a near death experience on our ride down.  But, I digress.

    We walked out of the parking lot on Lombard St. and voila!  Meritage.  It was past 10pm, we were in dire straits, and lucky for us, they still had their kitchen open.  Phew!

    Our waitress was fantastic, for starters.  She attended to us right away.  We ordered our much-needed drinks and a few appetizers to start us off.

    We had the vegetable tempura, pork & mushroom dumplings, and kobe beef meatballs.  All were very satisfying and delicious.  Our favorite was definitely the veggie tempura (included sweet potato, summer squash, and eggplant)... we learned that the head chef goes to the farmer's market everyday to pick out fresh veggies for the restaurant.  Thumbs up for fresh produce!  

    However, our hunger was not stifled.  We ordered the duck to split.  It was cooked perfectly and was super juicy.  It almost melted in our mouths.  On the side was a sweet potato mash and cabbage - at first, we were puzzled by this mixture, but it worked.

    Finally, we split the peach cobbler for dessert.  It was tremendous and we gobbled until the bowl was completely cleaned out.

    If we make a trip to Rittenhouse Square, we will definitely be visiting Meritage again!  I can't wait to see what else they have to offer.

    The food here freaking rocked.  Like hardcore rocked.

    Up until this point, Meritage was the place that I stopped at for a glass of wine or a quick app.  I now realize how incredibly short sighted that was.  After having a meal here, I'm a believer.

    The first thing I noticed was a very nicely priced but well selected wine list.  After looking at glass and bottle prices, we decided to go with a bottle.  The Carmen Petite Syrah was a great choice to go with our meal.

    We split and appetizer special, the roast pork stuffed poblano.  This thing was battered and fried and served over a tangy tomato sauce.  This was just perfectly cooked and wonderfully seasoned.  A+

    For entrees, we went with the roasted amish chicken and the hanger steak.  The waitress politely informed me that while they were out of hanger steak they were substituting filet mignon.  Um, yeah, that will be fine for me.

    If I could make a pan sauce like what was served with my steak, I don't think I'd leave the house.  It was just perfect in every way.  It formed a velvet like drape over the perfectly cooked filet.  Marvelous.  The potato croquettes were crispy on the outside and soft and gooey on the inside.  Even the string beans were cooked to perfection and seasoned incredibly.

    In Yelp parlance with the 5 stars I'm assigning to Meritage, "Woohoo, As good as it gets!"
