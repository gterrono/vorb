    Don't really get what the hype is all about, and I've given this place more chances than it deserves. Mediocre food & completely overpriced.

    Let me describe each of my experiences to you:

    1. Dinner - Came here for the first time in fall 09 for a group dinner (all expenses paid) so there was really no reason not to love this place, but the food was mediocre. Can't remember exactly what I had, but just remember feeling unsatisfied

    2. Brunch - After avoiding this place for a few years, came here a couple months ago for brunch because a friend wanted to and I thought I should let go of my naive freshman thoughts. For about $27 per person (tax and tip included), the meal left me again wondering why Distrito is so popular. For the first course I ordered the tortilla soup - great presentation - but was just a small chunk of chicken in a lukewarm orange-ish liquid that they call soup. For the second course I ordered steak & eggs. Steak was chewy in a weird way and was too juicy but with no flavor - seriously who cooked it? And the scrambled eggs and refried beans were both completely soggy.

    3. Happy hour - Right before graduation this month, came here for the last time for happy hour, thinking it would be different. Don't know why I tried so hard. The drink deals aren't the best in the area but the mojitos and the guac are pretty good. But again, looking at the price to quality ratio, this place just keeps disappointing me.

    There are so many dinner/brunch/happy hour places in Philly - whenever I'm in the city I'll be sure to unfortunately avoid this one.

    Distrito isn't your typical Mexican cuisine - it is part Mexican and part Jose Garces.  Jose Graces adds that little twist to traditional Mexican food which makes it a true culinary adventure.  

    I want to start out with the mahi mahi tacos.  Scrumptious!  Perfectly fried mahi mahi with pickled cabbage and a lovely mysterious Jose Garces sauce.  One of my favorite items on the menu.

    All of the ceviches are delicate, tender, refreshing.  They come in super small portions though, so be wary that you will probably be fighting over the last bite!

    The cocktails are wonderful.  It is east to tell they use fresh fruit in the mix.

    Lastly, the tres leches cake is a great dessert.  I grew up in Arizona, so I have tried many tres leches cakes at many family owned Mexican establishments.  You could probably bribe me into throwing an inferior tres leches cake into someones face for it.

    The ambiance and service here is great.  Jose Garces trademark really.

    Being a student at Penn, I think I've gone to Distrito more than 10 times now, and along with my experience at Amada, has made me a dedicated follower of Jose Garces! Up next - Chifa.

    For now, I can't seem to get tired of Distrito. What I recommend: (1) Guacamole - delicious, fresh, and love the shredded cheese that tops it, overall the best appetizer by far, and right as it's being finished, the other dishes are on their way. (2) Huarache de Hongos - flat bread vegetarian dish; the mushroom is to die for, everything is so savory, soft and crunchy at the same time. (3) Ceviches - try at least one when you're there if you like raw fish, I love them all; my personal favorite is the Atun; the tuna slices are also so fresh, the sauce is sweet, chilled, with a hint of lemon; oh, and that little piece of sorbet (?) in the middle...yum, and cleanses the taste buds so well! (4) Platanos - to put it simply, it's just whole, fried plantain, but it has a sweet cream, and is the perfect dessert, and the $4 tag is just SO worth it!

    On the side, the service hasn't been disappointing. My recommendation is to go for the 2nd floor if you're not sitting in the car. First floor always looks like it's left out. The second floor has a nice area where there are swings to sit on, and if you're here for a more "serious" occasion, the other side on the 2nd floor seats normally. I haven't tried the drinks here simply because I believe that's how Distrito makes money, and why people complain about the pricey-ness of the restaurant. I've never once felt the money I paid wasn't worth it!

    Listed in Classy Eats

    Went here for the first time with friends during the UC Dining Days and was intrigued with the fine dining take on Mexican food.  Growing up in southern California with friends from Mexico (and the home-cooked traditional fare prepared by their mothers), I realize that I have incredibly high standards for Mexican food.  As far as authenticity goes, Distrito is more of a miss than a hit.  But for interesting, high end dishes made using cutting edge preparation techniques and with a solidly Mexican flavor, then Distrito is a solid hit.  

    In terms of food: the entrees for the Dining Days menu are slightly smaller than usual, however the menu allows you to choose two appetizers and two entrees in addition to dessert so it's more of a five-course meal for $35 as opposed to a three-course one.  Both myself and my friends left feeling completely stuffed and satisfied.  I began with a very spicy take on a cucumber mojito which I had to carefully sip throughout my entire dinner since it was more spicy than refreshing as I would have expected.  However it was happy-hour special price so still worth it.  For starters, I began with their guacamole made in-house with fresh avocados and served with tortilla chips.  The guac was excellent, just salty enough, creamy and smooth.  The tortilla chips were overbaked and not nearly salty enough but the guacamole more than made up for this deficiency.  This was followed by the jicama salad which was a refreshing combination of jicama, mango, cucumber, orange, and topped with a scoop of some type of melon sorbet (on a salad as an appetizer!  I'm sold on anything dessert-like appearing in the first course...).  My two entrees were both excellent.  The carnitas tacos featured juicy, pulled pork and a mild but flavorful salsa on fresh corn tortillas.  The pollo entomatado with its juicy, tender chicken, in a cheesy, smokey and flavorful chipotle-tomato sauce was definitely my favorite part of the evening (and served in an adorable little cast-iron cauldron that seemed straight out of Harry Potter).  Dessert of a tiny slice of grilled vanilla sponge cake topped by blackberry sherbet, garnished with three fresh blackberries, and drizzled with salted caramel sauce rounded out the evening.  In all, a solid menu with beautiful presentation, and excellent food.

    In terms of service: incredibly attentive and friendly.  The waiters were always unobtrusively refilling water glasses, and clearing plates as we finished.  More importantly, the waited until everyone at the table had finished the current course before brining out the next one (the way it's supposed to be done!).  This allowed us to take a leisurely, relaxing dinner without feeling rushed through all five courses.

    The decor is also incredibly unique.  A combination of fun and wacky accents with traditional Mexican decor.  The interior is a riot of color with multicolored chairs, glittering tables, and all of it surrounded by decidedly pink walls.  If it weren't for the low lighting, I would have been on visual overload.  As it was, it was instead a fun and energetic atmosphere.

    In summary: while not the traditional Mexican food I was expected, Distrito provided an excellent, tasty five-course meal in relaxed and fun surroundings.  While the traditional menu looks a little pricey for everyday, I will definitely be returning for special occasions.  Worth the price.

Specialties

Distrito is the new restaurant concept by Jose Garces, the chef behind the acclaimed Philadelphia restaurants Amada and Tinto. Distrito is a high-energy restaurant inspired by the spirited culture and cuisine of Mexico City. The Distrito menu features Chef Garces' fresh interpretations of classic Mexican fare and beverages include over 65 tequilas and margaritas made with fresh seasonal fruits. A striking decor includes a hot pink and bright green color scheme, a wall constructed from over 600 colorful, campy masks of lucha libre, or "free fight" professional wrestlers, and a booth crafted from a Volkswagen Beetle, a typical Mexico City taxi cab.

History

Established in 2008.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
