    There's one thing that I crave from Barbuzzo. It's their salted caramel budino. That said, however, the last time I had it, it wasn't as creamy or smooth as I had remembered it in the past. Maybe it was just an off-night.

    Everything else is good, but not mmmm good. I came once for their Valentine's Day menu and got the vegetarian cannelloni as my main, something that I could have easily recreated at home. Their tomato sauce is tasty, and I sopped it up with extra bread, but the other components were meh.

    Most recently, we ordered their arancini. Fried risotto balls are always yummy, but I've had better at some of the other Italian places in the city. The Brussels sprouts had potential with all of the pancetta flavoring them, but they were undercooked. The pizza is something I was looking forward to the most. They just look so darn pretty. We ordered the sausage pizza, only to be disappointed. The pizza crust had a nice char, but it was soggy and droopy in the center. I understand the less is more concept of topping a pizza, but don't place all of them in the middle of the pie.

    I haven't given up on this place, but I'll have to think closely about what I order next.

    We ate dinner at the bar at Barbuzzo, walking in without a reservation.  I only reserve five-star reviews if the place gave me the experience of a lifetime, and Barbuzzo gave me two: the best pork loin I've ever had and the best swordfish I've ever had.  In both cases, nowhere else has even come close.  

    Maybe the most amazing thing we had though was the budino with salted caramel.  We ordered it after the people next to us ordered it during their meal.  They seemed to be enjoying it so much, we had to order it too.  We weren't disappointed.  In fact, we may have even enjoyed it more than they did.

The restaurant was full, so we took our places at the bar and ordered off of the well endowed beer and wine menus, respecitvely (He went with the Allegash BLACK, which we didn't even know existed, and I went with a tasty Pinot Blanc).

    I absolutely loved the wood/brick, dimly lit, sophisticated atmosphere, and the spacious and comfortable bar brought a whole new level of intimacy and sexitude to the eating experience. We ended up sharing the roasted beet salad with goat cheese, fried greens, and grapefruit and the grilled calamari and octopus.     So much has been said about Barbuzzo by prior reviewers, it hard to improve on the info already out there. This place rocks - but it has a few blemishes as well.

    On the upside - the food is outstanding. The wine list is decent and decently priced. Save room for dessert! The salted Carmel budino is worth the trip in and of itself. The pizza is good, but i think Ostaria does it better overall in that category.

    On the downside, this place gets really crowded, really fast. Reservations at 5:15, place was overfilled by 5:30. Seating is tight - my left arm was to the "aisle" and i lost track of how many waiters/patrons ran into me on their way past.  It did get old after a while. The tight seating also makes it a bit weird regarding table conversation - be prepared to meet the couple next to you... like it or not.

    Finally, I thought their "ordering philosophy" - which is order everything at once - feels way to much like table turnover to me. I guess given its popularity i get it - but it makes the diner feel a little rushed IMHO.

    Still, Barbuzzo is fun, relatively affordable, and still on the must do list if you haven't been. Better plan ahead though - this is still a tough weekend reservation to get.
