    I think that any time a restaurant has 4 1/2 stars on yelp after over 200 reviews, it sets an unattainable standard.

    The restaurant experience here was first-class, but not particularly interesting.

    The restaurant is decorated in a hip, "farmhouse chic" style, for lack of a better descriptor. It was packed. It's  a narrow space too, you have to watch your elbows in there. Make reservations. The couple in front of us was told at 7:30 that the next available table would be at 10:45.

    For apps we ordered the sheep's milk ricotta and the marrow bones. The sheep's milk ricotta was pretty good - the bread served with it even better. The marrow bones were somewhat disappointing. I've never ordered marrow bones before, but I guess I was just expecting a bit more marrow. What was there was smoky and decadent, however.

    For mains, we ordered roasted sardines, the calamari/octopus plate, and the ravioli. The sardines were a disappointment. The bones were distracting, and it was overwhelmingly fishy. It was served with something the consistency of coleslaw, which, according to the menu, consisted of oranges, fennel, and olives. It was good, but totally overpowered by the fishiness of the sardines. Maybe some cilantro or something would have punched through the fish taste more. The calmari/octopus plate was excellent. Both octopus and calamari have exquisite texture when cooked properly, but are often overcooked and tough. Not here. The pancetta, olive, and ramps were a perfect accompaniment. Ravioli was very good as well.

    The salted caramel boudino was the star of the show, however. Salted caramel pudding over chocolate crumbles (almost like oreos, but I'm sure it was something much fancier). So good. I had, by chance ordered espresso, which I would highly recommend with this dessert, as it mutes some of the sweetness, and brings out a dark chocolate flavor.

    Total tab for all of this and two mixed drinks, $94. Not bad. Not a bargain, either though. I'd say you get exactly what you pay for at Barbuzzo.

    This place lives up to the hype, one of the best meals in Philly I've had. It is worth it to go with a group so you can maximize how many plates you sample. If you do have a group of 6, they will only make a reservation for 6:00 or 8:30 as I believe there is only one table. Warning: This table is right next to the pizza oven so it can get a bit hot during the summer. Here is the plate by plate breakdown.

    Pig Popcorn - These were pork rinds on steroids. So crunchy and flavorful, great dipping sauce, had to get two orders.
    Meat Board - Would rather spend my money elsewhere but all delicious. Chorizo was my favorite. Loved the homemade mustard but needed more bread
    Goat Cheese Board - Once again, would not get again but all these cheeses were tasty. Could have used more bread also.
    Aspargo Pizza - Unbelievable. Thank god we got two or there might have been a fight over the slices. A bite with everything was perfection, I never thought an egg could make such a difference.
    Gnocchi - I'm always a sucker for gnocchi and this did not disappoint. Just like all the dishes, there was the perfect balance of ingredients.
    Steak - Was sliced up perfectly and extremely tender. The romesco was an excellent combination. Would be a signature dish at other places but here was just average.
    Octopus and Calamari - Refreshing alternative to all the smoked meats consumed. Enjoyed the smokey grilled flavor with the oranges.
    Meatballs - LOVED these. So rich and flavorful. The combination of meats with cheese melts in your mouth and the sauce is also of the highest quality. Not sure how this is Mediterranean but who cares.
    Ravioli - Disappointing compared to the other plates. The brown butter did not add much as overall it was more on the bland side.
    Fideus - Everyone could not get enough of this. A great noodle dish with a lot of delicious seafood. The aioli served as a sauce to avoid any chance of a dry bite.
    Budino - Worth saving room for, or just forcing it down anyway. There is a reason this dessert is so highly acclaimed. You will not find a better combination of salty and sweet.
    In conclusion, go here immediately and bring friends who like to eat as much as you.

    What can I say, the wait was worth it. I had been trying to get a reservation to this place for the last three weekends, till I gave up and decided to try it out on a Tuesday evening. And even then it was packed like sardines! (side note: the logo of barbuzzo is a sardine in a bucket). This bar-cum-restaurant is a bit on the smaller side, but it has utilized its limited space quite efficiently. The nearest table to ours was only an arms length away, and yet the surrounding hustle-bustle somehow drowned out their conversation, although we could maintain ours.

    To start off, we ordered the goat cheese plate - five different chèvres served with almonds, raisins, apple slices, jelly and chutney. (Looking up the menu online, I find that the jelly was actually dried cherries, the chutney was quince paste, and the almonds were marcona almonds.) The whole array was an explosion of tastes served on a wooden board. The cheeses were delectable - my favorite was the Verde Capra, a bluish-green cheese, smelling like the gorgonzola but not tasting so, with a pasty interior which melted in your mouth. Closely following was another blue cheese, the Hubbardston blue, which had a strong flavour that burst out on your tongue. To counter these bold adventures, there was served the Pantaleo supposedly having a floral finish which I couldn't detect (what is a floral finish anyway?), and the montcabrer, which frankly was lost on me. Of course, along with these four, came good old-fashioned creamy, spreadable, young honey goat cheese from Belmont, WI which I used on the thin sliced bread.

    The next plate was the grilled spanish octopus and calamari. I had only had octopus raw before, and was pleasantly surprised by the cooked product. The texture was stringy like that of chicken, and tasted a little like it too. The calamari was much chewier than what it is when fried till death as one gets in most restaurants, and I appreciated this taste as well. The mollusks were served in a roasted garlic aioli alongside olives, bacon and grilled onions. The bacon was an interesting salty surprise (I hadn't noticed it in the menu while ordering), although I would've preferred the aioli to have slightly more zing than it had. All in all, a very delicious dish.

    Our third plate was the house special, the squash pizza which was a let-down after the two previous ones. Although tastier than your normal thin crust pizza, there was nothing to write home about, besides the basil. It was the freshest basil I have tasted in a pizza - it seemed they had plucked a few leaves backstage and slid them in the pie as it was cooking. No wonder the margherita of this place, which I was recommended but foolishly didn't order, is delicioso.

    Finally, to sweeten the deal, we ordered the honey panna cotta. People closest to me know that there are few things on a menu which can light my eyes up as much as panna cotta does. Even in the dim candle lighting of Barbuzzo, the sparkle was enough for me to convince my friend of the need to order it. So we did, with my caution that what we might be served may not be panna cotta (but some mousse-y custard; this has happened to me before - twice.) Fortunately, we did get panna cotta which, although maybe not quite as how the Florentians make it, pretty much concluded this delightful dinner with sweet satisfaction.

    Summary: Delightful place. On the expensive side, but you get what you pay for, and more maybe.  Highly recommended, especially in a group so that one can taste more dishes. Downside: didn't see many vegetarian options, and I couldn't finish the Java Head stout that I ordered. The stout was decent, but probably goes better with a cheeseburger.

    "Say ... would you happen to remember those TV commercials with a giant pitcher of some soft drink crashing through a wall--"

    "Kool-Aid?"

    "Yes!!!"

    And with that, our server, who irritatingly had vanished for a number of minutes earlier in the evening--admittedly in part my fault due to my indecisiveness in selecting a drink--redeemed herself.

    (My drink, the citrus-drenched sanguinello, reminded my dining partner of Kool-Aid. Or was it Hi-C?)

    Barbuzzo has tones of gastropub and diner with an undercurrent of funk. A slender room rich with wood and blond brick and dense with people and noise, it was positively hopping late on a Wednesday with good vibes that were irresistible. You couldn't help but enjoy the place. I've chided our server, but in fact she was professional, friendly and upbeat, helping make this a memorable dining event.

    We began with lardo--simply the best pizza I can recall. Thin and light and white, it had major char on the bottom that was a perfect foil to the almost sweet melange of artichoke and cheeses on top. It was a fairly good-sized pizza, but it didn't last long.
