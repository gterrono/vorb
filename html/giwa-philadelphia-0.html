    If you're looking for incredible fresh vegetables, clean flavors, and just the right amount of spice, then Giwa is your place.

    After weeks of eating a diet that felt almost entirely void of fresh veggies I was craving a meal that would satisfy my craving.  I ordered the vegetarian Bibim Bob (Special Rice Bowl).  It was a large portion packed with fresh carrots, seaweed, bean sprouts, green leaf lettuce, red bell pepper, julliened egg, and probably more that I am forgetting.  They serve this in a big bowl with rice that's seasoned with jut a hint of sesame, as well as kimchi, spicy sesame hot sauce, and picked vegetables.

    It's definitely a filling portion and for $8.50 I had enough left over to snack on later, which is saying something because I have a huge appetite.  My only complaint is that it didn't come with any sauce that supplied any added liquid/moisture.  A soy based sauce would have made the dish sublime.

    If you're looking for Korean food in CC- look no further. This tiny little place on Sansom is all you need- they do eat in (if you're lucky enough to grab a table, since space is at a premium!) or take out.
    I really recommend them for lunch- you can call ahead for takeout, although eating in is worth it, since their stone bowl (Dol Sot Bibimbop- a combination of rice, vegetables and the protein of your choice served in a sizzling heated stone bowl that cooks your food as you eat) is only available to on-site diners. They make everything themselves onsite, which makes for really quick service and really fresh food.
    Their tofu stew and kimchi are also good- although not for those who don't enjoy spicy food. All in all, just try it- it won't set you back too much (an entree is 7-10, lunch specials 6-10) and you might just find a new favorite.

    I live 1 block from Giwa. I have eaten there at least 5 times since August 2008. It started out pretty impressive for a quick take out/eat in Korean restuarant. The price is higher than I would like to pay for take out but tolerable considering how close it is to where I live and the quality of the food. Their seafood pancake is actually surprisingly good for being a fast food korean restuarant. My dad really liked it.
    The place is very clean and casual. The folks are friendly. It's a cute fast asian food place. I see a lot of cute couples that come in for a casual date or I guess just a quick dinner. They are all smiles everytime.

    Well after about the 3rd time, I notice they got stingy with the little side dishes. Usually at a regular Korean place your table would be so full of those little side dishes that your own entree could barely fit on the table right? Well, this time, they only gave us 2 little rimkins of kimchi and something else.  Also my Spicy KimChi stew had very few tofu and pork. The soup was saltier. The rice stayed sticky and yummy.

    Maybe it was just the winter funk. I will go back in a couple of months and check them out again. Maybe everything will be back to normal in the spring when everything is happier then.

    By the way, I have never had Korean ginger tea before until I came here. It inspired me to make my own at home. It's really easy! Ginger + Cinnamon sticks then boil in water then reduce to half + honey. Yum!

    This is the second time I've had lunch at Giwa, and I ordered the right thing this time: #3 dol sot bibimbop.

    Since I'm kind of a Korean food newbie, maybe some others of you are, so I'll describe this dish. It is basically a large bowl of rice topped with a generous amount of colorful vegetables, sliced egg, and the protein of your choice (beef, chicken, tofu, shrimp).  I had the beef.  I overheard some one order it with spicy pork, so I guess that is an option as well, though it's not on the menu!

    The great thing about this dish is that it is cooked in a stone bowl directly over the gas burner.  This results in the rice becoming slightly brown and crispy on the bottom of the bowl.  Also, the bowl is sizzling hot - be careful!    Just after cooking, the chef tops the bowl with lettuce and some green stuff that looks like finely shredded dried seaweed.

    The bibimbop comes with a plastic cup of tangy Korean hot sauce.  Add to taste and stir everything up as it sizzles and the aromas waft up to your nose!  There are also two little side dishes, one of kim chee (spicy cabbage) and one of marinated diced cucumbers.

    The browned rice adds a nutty flavor and slightly crunchy texture to the dish. You can substitute multigrain rice for additional charge, but I would not be inclined to do that as it might affect the texture and flavor of the dish.

    This is a small place where you order and pay at the counter and they bring you the food. Bibimbop was $10.25, tip jar optional.  

    I recommend this dish and I'll be back for more!
