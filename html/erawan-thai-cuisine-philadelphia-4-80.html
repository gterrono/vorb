    A recent take-out dinner from Erawan reminded me that I should update from my first review because it has been awhile and things can sometimes change. It seems like take-out is the best method compared to delivery, which can be slow.

    We're still fans of the Holy Basil Supreme and Royal Rama Chicken, although the chicken is still on the slightly overcooked side. The tofu has drastically improved so that it's no longer fried, just lightly sauteed along with the veggies and sauce - gotta love spicy! This last time we also had their steamed pork dumplings, which are some of my bf's favorite dumplings and are quite juicy and very good with the accompanying dipping sauce. Erawan provides solid Thai food in an area rather bereft of them - but I still really want to try Smile Cafe, which has a slightly cheaper menu. I love Thai food, but I also like saving money when eating out.

As my bf was finishing up ordering, a look of panic appeared and he mouthed to me, "One hour wait," but at that point, having already ordered everything, we opted to just wait even though we were starving by the time the food arrived (it took a bit longer than an hour).

    The food itself was pretty good. The shrimp rolls were the only disappointing dish; it was just too plain tasting, other rolls of that type I've had before have been far better. The chicken was a bit overcooked, and the tofu was WAAAAY too fried and not the best quality, but the sauces made up for that. Erawan doesn't get 4 stars because the food was late, and by that point a little cold.     Listed in The Fat List, Date Restaurants

    Tasty, big food in a cozy establishment.

    Oh, Yelp, you know me well enough by now. Don't you see where this is going?

    Erawan is a nice little BYO right at 23rd and Sansom, where there's plenty of parking because no one wants to be more than 1 block away from Rittenhouse Square on a Saturday Night. The place is cozy, but they lay it out so that you won't feel cramped, a surprising feat for a standard row home sized space. We didn't have reservations, but the hostess literally just peeked over her shoulder, separated a table meant for six, and seated us right away. Score!

    Anyway, my friend is vegetarian, and we always share food, so that made ordering a bit more interesting - for me, Thai usually includes a good bit of meat. For my friend, nothing ever includes any meat. Quarrel!

    Erawan has a pretty good vegetarian selection though, and we had no trouble picking things out. We started with mango salad and vegetarian fried spring rolls. Both were great, especially the mango salad. I like mangoes "al dente," if I can use that term - slightly under ripe so they're not as stringy and a little more tart. Must be a southeast Asian thing, because that's how they do it at Erawan.

    For main courses we had eggplant with stir fried vegetables in tamarind pepper sauce, and the vegetarian pad thai. The portions here are pretty big, all said and done. The pad thai is enough for two people, and the eggplant is enough for one really fat person. I suppose what I'm saying is that it's a good thing I had spent the day dancing and not eating.

    Very appetizing, and the whole meal came out to around 40 after tip and tax. On an aside, Malbec goes really well with Thai food, in my completely uninformed, non-expert opinion. Then again, I think Malbec goes well with everything.

    Pro-tip: We were lucky that we got a seat so quickly. Call ahead for a reservation!

    I often leave Erawan with mixed feelings, but I keep coming back!  

    I want to draw attention to the lunch possibilities here. It is a good time of day to experience Erawan, as dinner is often a bit chaotic: crowded, loud (bad acoustics) and understaffed (the servers are quite nice, but always seemed stressed as they run about trying to make everyone happy) and there seems to be something lost in translation about seating, and who was waiting first, and who had a reservation, etc...

    But lunch is nice.  Less people, better service.  More relaxed atmosphere.  And the food is good, and sometimes great.  It's the place to go for a real fix of good, authentic Thai food.  They really CARE about the food, and it is reflected in so much loyal patronage.  You can forgive all of the other faux pas after tasting the curry (both red and yellow) and basil dishes, and they deliver on the spicy scale, according to what you request (mild-spicy).  The lunch special with soup & appetizer makes for a nicely rounded lunch for a decent value.  Priced just at the upper limit for this kind of place, and if it were any more expensive I would pass.

    But there is something lacking in the service.  Part of it is cultural, as the one greeting patrons & seating does not seem to have a good grasp of American expectations (she seemed like a young college student who was clueless about general manners, so for her naivite she can be forgiven) and I often feel rushed, as if they want to get me out of there as quickly as possible because others are waiting.  But this is less of a factor at lunch.  And the food trumps these minor annoyances.
