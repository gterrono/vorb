    I had been hearing the hype about Paesano's for a couple of years now, but had yet to venture out to try this gem.  Wow!  This place makes a mean sandwich.  First, they got that good Liscio's bread.  Second, since there are only two people working and it is such a small place, they actually care about crafting your sandwich.  The staff was also very friendly.  I got the namesake Paesano sandwich which was brisket, hot pepper, a fried egg, cheese and horse radish mayo.  While a bit messy because of the runny egg, the yolk provided an unexpectedly good sauce, which only helped to make the brisket juicier.  It was also nicely loaded with meat so definitely got my $8 worth.  My girlfriend enjoyed her Italian.  (the Daddywad).  Can't go wrong with hot or cold sandwiches.  Can't wait to go back.  (p.s. - we tried to go to the other paesanos on 9th street prior to coming to this one, but they had run out of bread)

    Paesano's remains a local legend for stellar sandwiches that satisfy any appetite, and this NoLibs location is no exception. Think of it as South Philly's outpost but with no deep fried sandwiches (which means no Liveracce or Bolognese, unfortunately) and less seating.

    This location of Paesano's is almost a literal hole-in-the-wall. The counter seats only 8, and you will undoubtedly bump elbows with your neighbor. Don't even think about eating in with more than a party of two during peak lunch and dinner hours. Despite this crowding, the more "intimate" ambiance lets you interact with the cooks and watch them do their thing, which is a pretty cool experience.

    Of course, Paesano's would be nothing if not for their ridiculously good sandwiches, and the NoLibs store dishes out the quality Paesano's is known for. The namesake Paesano is a stoner's wet dream in the best possible way, with its rich brisket, horseradish mayo, and fried egg. The Arista kicks Dinic's pork in the crotch and laughs at it while it writhes in pain on the ground. Vegetarians will find the Giardinia can easily outmatch any other herbivorous offering in this city. Head to Paesano's, grab a sandwich, and taste what the hype is about.

Specialties

Sandwiches made with Italian inspiration and Philadelphia at-t-tude. Peter McAndrew's approach to gastronomy has to do first with full flavor, and then hospitality.  Sandwiches are the backbone of any cuisine, but here in Philly they represent the true bloodline of it's people.

History

Established in 2008.

The second concept from chef Peter McAndrew. Following the success of his Italian trattoria "Modo Mio," established in 2007, Paesano's represents authentic Italian cuisine, but made for Philly palettes.
