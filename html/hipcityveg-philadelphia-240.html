    Are you one of these people who think Vegan food is pretty much just tree bark, some nuts and a stomach full of air? Well you sir (or madam) are wrong! Vegan foods are delicious and can be filling. Filling you say? YES!

    I'm an evolving carnivore.  Non-meat options are gradually finding their way into my diet.  I've always enjoyed fruits and vegetables but, meat has always been a staple.

    I was immediately greeted by a cheerful 20 something that eagerly described some of the customer favorites.  She recommended the $9.00 Udon Noodle Salad (Chinese black bean dressing, sprouts, arugula, carrots, cabbage, daikon, peanuts, & spicy glazed "chick'n").  My eyes transfixed to an oddly written drink - The $4.50 Groothie (green smoothie with organic apples, organic leafy greens, bananas, & seasonal fruit).  I ordered same.

    I won't wait for another Sunny Sunday to enjoy HipCityVeg.

    Stopped in to HipCityVeg for a quick lunch yesterday after hearing good things about it. My BF and I are all about trying new vegetarian and vegan dishes, but one thing that we despise about veg cuisine is all of the imitation meat and dairy products.  In my opinion, processed soy products are no less evil than their carcass opponents.  

    With that being said; after weeding out all of the options containing faux 'meats', we were left with very few options to pick from.  I had the Bistro Bella sandwich and the BF had the Arugula Taco Salad.  1 out of 5 salads and 4 out of 8 sandwiches, on the menu are imitation-free.  The Arugula Taco Salad had was lovely, fresh and had a nice flavorful cilantro dressing (though I fear that may have been made with imitation dairy products - which eliminates all salads as being imitation-free).  My Bistro Bella sandwich was on a fabulous whole grain bun.  The portobella was freshly grilled and the tapenade flavor was evident in very bite.  We didn't try any of their specialty drinks, but they all looked great and fresh!  They do offer free water (water was flavored with fresh orange slices) which is always great, I can't stand when I have to purchase a $2 water bottle from a 'fast food' joint.  The desserts also looked great, I was tempted to get a cookie, but didn't.

    Neither of our meals were overly filling, the portions are perfect for someone who is a) trying to lose weight, b) lives a pretty sedentary life style c) is going to get a side dish or dessert with the main course.  Since we do a lot of walking/running/etc. on a regular basis, we were hungry within a few hours of eating lunch, I probably could have had 2 sandwiches without feeling stuffed.

    If HipCityVeg menu fit your bill, this is a great, quick, fresh spot in center city to grab lunch!  They do their thing well.  I would give my meal 4 stars but I am only giving them 3 stars because I was highly disappointed with their "chick'n" and tempeh obsession.
