    I had some family in from out of town over the weekend and on one of the hottest days of the year, they were craving a piping hot bowl of noodles.  Naturally.  So we piled our sweaty selves into a cab and headed to Chinatown.  Thank god Nan Zhou is air conditioned.

    This place is no frills.  We walked in and the waitress pointed to an open table (there were many that day because really, who wants hot soup in 100 degree weather).  Within about 30 seconds the waitress was at our table to take our order.  Good thing we already knew what we wanted.  We all opted for the hand drawn noodles.  Both the shrimp and the beef brisket were delicious.  You get a giant bowl of soup piled with wonderfully fresh noodles, savory broth and fresh cilantro.  For the 3 of us, our total was $21.50.  Service is super fast.  Don't go if you're looking for a leisurely dinner in a nice setting.  Go for the noodles, not the ambiance and you'll be happy.

    SOLID 4.5 stars.

    I have been here on numerous occasions, and it is DELICIOUS. I've lived in Philadelphia for 6 years to date. And a year ago, a foodie friend of mine asked me if I've ever had the pleasure of eating Nan Zhou.. and that's where the story begins.

    First time eating there it was a rainy day, I got the hand drawn noodles. Oh my GOD so good. First of all, the broth is just perfect. It's not too hot, you can easily eat/drink it as you may be starving like I was. But my god the broth is SO good. Not going to lie, the day after that, I went back to get it AGAIN. My friends and I joked that there was crystal meth in it because I HAD to get it the day after. But anyway, back to the handdrawn noodles. First of all, they make them FRESH. As in, you order -- the chef either slices it for the knife cut one, or hand draws them into noodles after you place your order. You can see them do it. So yes, the noodles are great consistency, fresh, delicious broth.. $6 can't go wrong! Plus they give you SO much noodles, SO much beef and enough vegetables.

    I went there a few more times with my mom and best friend and even boyfriend who doesn't like noodle soup. All of the times were GREAT, except the 2nd time I went with my mom. She even noticed that the broth wasn't AS flavorful as usual. That is why this review is a 4.5 instead of 5 stars! That one time was the only inconsistency. Other than that, DELICIOUS. The knife cut -- i swears.. has a different consistency/texture. It is more " q" aka, chewy. It also seems they give twice the amount. It's funny. Hand drawn - i eat all that shit on my own lol. Knife cut - I split with either my bf or best friend.

    Hands down - delicious food, now $6.50 but still worth it.. although I'm not a fan for the raise in price. Delicious broth, savory beef slices, home made FRESH noodles = I am SOLD

    I like, I like! Times like these, I am oh so grateful to the Yelp fam.

    I recently have been in the Philly area often for work, but haven't really found any good eats. I wanted some type of Asian noodle and came across via Yelp, Nan Zhou - I probably, otherwise, would not have.

    Their prices are ridiculous for the portions they give you. And the speed of which all is served. The noodles really are perfect - not all over cooked and swollen, or fried like ramen. They puff up a bit the longer they sit in the broth (duh) but hold up really well.

    I ordered the brisket noodle and the amount of meat in there was impressive for $6.50! It tastes like kalbi-jjim, to those who are familiar. Melty, salty and sweet, delicious.

    Also went for the steamed curry chicken dumplings and had myself a great friggin time. The curry doesn't over power, the dough of the dumpling is thin, and the chicken mounds, plump.

    I wasn't able to eat about 65% of what was served, but for $11.50 - I absolutely got my moneys worth.

    (Go around the corner for some fro-yo after! ... and then hurry home, it's a creepy neighborhood after 7:30pm)
