        Great food, professional staff, tiny portions.

        That sums it up.

        Five very hungry guys decended on Zahav at 9:45pm on a Friday.  As could be expected, the lamb shoulder was 86ed.  So we had the smaller tasting menu.

        The salatim (salads) were delicious.  We got six of them for five of us.  The problem was that each of us got less than one full tablespoon of each salad.  The laffa bread was also amazing, but three "slices" for five hungry guys!?!?  (Their refill yielded only two more.)

        We had lamb's tongues, sweetbreads, duck's hearts, chicken liver and melange of other offal.  All were impecably cooked and none was filling.

        Thumbs up to the wait staff.  They're attentive without being overbearing.  If I had to be critical, I would say they are too rehearsed, but I'll take that over poor service any day.

        This place has been on our to-try list for a long time and we finally made it for dinner last Sunday. We both had the tasting menu, so got the chance to try quite a bit.

        The salatim and hummus to start ranged from okay (radish) to superb (twice cooked eggplant, tabouleh). Six small dishes, hummus, and bread was almost a meal in of itself for two people. 4/5

        Moving on to the mezze, we tried the fried cauliflower, sweetbreads, lamb's tongue, and chicken liver. If you try one thing here, go for the chicken liver. It's amazing, assuming you like the strong flavor of liver. The tongue was okay - I think I prefer the meat to have more flavor and be less reliant on the accompanying sauce. Cauliflower didn't strike me as anything special. Our server also brought out a little plate of tuna tartare, which was excellent (particularly the orange sauce that accompanied it). 5/5

        The meat dishes were solid as well - I liked the duck but thought the lamb was too heavily spiced. Wife thought the exact opposite. All in all, very very good. 4/5

        Finally, dessert. I'm not much of a dessert person, but the semifreddo was ridiculously good. Flaky tart + citrusy custard + sweet cherries = WIN. The other dessert we tried (basboosa) wasn't as good, but still not bad. I liked the coffee ice cream because it didn't taste like sugar with coffee flavoring. 5/5

        Overall, this place jumps near the top of our list - it's a damn good combination of quality food and pleasant atmosphere for a reasonable price. We'll be back!

        Perhaps the best meal I have had in my life.  It was so good that I don't even know where to begin with this.

        First, we got the desert rose cocktails.  They were highly refreshing on a humid evening, and perfectly mixed.  Later, I went non-alcoholic with the mint lemonade which was also perfect and refreshing.

        We ordered the Mesibah, which is the tasting menu that comes with the slow-cooked lamb shoulder.  You do not get to pick your mezze plates with it, but they are extremely accommodating and make sure they don't send you something you would like.  Every single thing we ate was delicious.  Every.Thing.  The hummus and pita is to die for.  Their hummus is just perfectly smooth, not bitter, and just lovely.  

        All of the salatim was amazing.  I really did not expect to like things like a beet salad, moroccan carrots, or radish salad, but it was all delicious.  I am really not a fan of beets, and their beet salad was perfectly seasoned and delicious.  

        For the mezze, we were given the house-smoked sable, fried cauliflower, and crispy haloumi.  I'm not a big raisins fan, and the Haloumi came served with raisins.  Never fear.  Somehow they were tasty and a perfect accompaniment to the cheese.  

        The lamb was delightful.  Tender, bursting with flavor, and a true pleasure to eat.  By the time it got to us, we were pretty full, but were still able to enjoy some if it before we had to give up.  The lamb portion was HUGE.  They essentially gave us a mountain of meat.  Never fear, they let us take the leftovers home.

        I can't tell you exactly what we had for dessert, other than that it was delicious.  By the time we left, I was pretty full but not on the verge of explosion, which I appreciated.  As a word to the wise, I think they will send out more hummus and pita if you ask for it.  I recommend resisting that urge so you can get through and enjoy the rest of your meal.

        The next day, we went to reheat the lamb and discovered (to our delight) that they had removed all of the bones for us.  The lamb was still amazing the next day, and it provided us with a delicious, filling, second meal from Zahav.  

        This restaurant is delicious and unique.  Sure, it is pricey, but you won't feel like you're getting hosed.  Go here!

        I live in Israel for a year and became a snob about Israeli food -- I try all the Israeli food I can find, hoping I'll find something worth eating again, and am almost always disappointed.  I finally succeed with Zahav.  This was one of the best meals I've ever had, and certainly the best Israeli food I've had outside of Israel.

        To be fair, most of the food is not "authentic" Israeli food, but food with an Israeli spin or flavor.  Everything we had was delicious, fresh and interesting. The hummus, lafah (middle easter flat bread) and many of the salads are very authentic.  The hummus is probably the best hummus I've ever had.

        I'm a vegetarian, and there were plenty of vegetarian options.

        We did the tasting menu, which was a great way to sample many different dishes. The plates are small, but there was so much as part of the tasting that we ended up taking a couple of things home.

        The service was excellent -- the staff checked up on us without being annoying about it.  I always drink a ton of water, and my glass was never empty.

        This was probably the best dining experience I've ever had.

        I saw Zahav mentioned on a restaurant blog that I follow, it's also been praised on a message board I frequent, and a couple of our close friends raved about it. When my husband and I were trying to plan a special event for his birthday, I suggested that we drive a couple hours to Philadelphia to try Zahav and he agreed. We invited four friends to join us and we made a reservation a few weeks in advance, noting to the hostess that we wished to try the "Mesibah/party time" tasting menu with the lamb shoulder.

        When the day came, we arrived on time and were immediately given a very nice table. Four of us ordered the mint lemonade, which was delicious and refreshing. The waitress confirmed that we were all doing the Mesibah menu, and also asked if anyone had any food allergies or dietary concerns that they should know about, and they were able to substitute something for a planned tartare option since one person couldn't eat it.

        Since there were six of us, everything came out doubled so that we could all have enough of it. We were given small dishes of pickled vegetables (celery, mini eggplant, etc.) to snack on while we waited for the first course of small salads - roasted eggplant, spicy green beans, beets, tabbouleh,  an Israeli cucumber salad, and pickeled fennel, all of which was wonderful. We also got big bowls of fresh hummus and plates of warm laffa bread, and we had to get a refill of the bread because we gobbled it up so quickly.

        The next course consisted of several small plates ... one was a roasted heirloom tomato salad with fresh, delicious ricotta cheese. Another was a plate of fried cheese, the taste of which reminded me of the cheese curds I've had in Montreal. We also had fried cauliflower with labaneh (yogurt with chive, dill, mint, and garlic, I believe), which my husband LOVED. And then there was a piece of challah bread topped with smoked sablefish and with a runny egg underneath, which the waitress cut open for us ... oh my ... I saw two of the guys' eyes light up when "runny egg" was mentioned.

        Then came the lamb. Two pieces of lamb between the six of us, actually. Holy cow. GIANT portions, wonderfully tender with a crispy skin, and laying on top of yummy crispy rice. The chickpeas had soaked up the wonderful pomegranate flavor. We were so stuffed from the salads and appetizers that we absolutely couldn't finish the lamb, so the waiters packed up three separate containers so that each couple could take home the leftovers.

        The waiters then came out with the desserts, and they thoughtfully added a candle to one plate for my husband's birthday. My favorite dessert was a trio of small white chocolate cakes resting on top of a delicious vanilla(?) custard. Another dessert was a bed of phyllo dough (which resembled a large puff of Shredded Wheat cereal) topped by a scoop of Turkish coffee ice cream. The third dessert was a scoop of vanilla ice cream topped by a piece of phyllo dough and dressed with a warm cherry compote.

        The guys in our party shared a pot of French press coffee, which came out with a small sand timer so that we'd known when the coffee was ready for pressing. The presentation of the dishes and the little details like the timer made everything feel so elegant and special.

        And I noticed that all of the other tables around us received their courses around the same time as us. When I called to make the reservation, my only choices were 6 p.m. or after 9 p.m., so I figured it was just packed since it was a Saturday night ... but once we got there, I realized that they must prepare all the food at the same time in order to increase efficiency. Fine by me.

        The service was phenomenal. The waiters cleared our plates and utensils between courses to bring us clean ones, and wiped down the table and took away any empty bowls ... they left behind any bowl that still had food in it, so that we could still pick if we wished. Our water glasses were constantly refilled, and we were repeatedly asked if everything was OK and if they could get us anything. All with a smile. Wonderful!

        The final bill came out - the tasting menu was around $50 per person, and we also had eight lemonades, one beer, and the pot of coffee added to that. And we had to figure in a tip. So we walked out of there paying $145 a couple ... are you KIDDING me??? That's an absolute steal for what we got - the amount of food, the quality of the food, and the great service. I've always wanted to do a tasting menu but we live near New York and tasting menus usually start around $100 per person, so it was really nice to be able to have such a great experience for such a reasonable price. One of our friends commented that this was among the best restaurant meals she's ever had.

        I'm BEYOND pleased with our experience at Zahav, and I'll gladly go back myself plus recommend it to anyone who'll be in Philadelphia.
