    Philadelphia Pho!

    After knocking off Delaware at the 2014 Delaware marathon, my marathoner friends, whom both ran the full distance with me, and I came here to Saigon Pho to celebrate. It was at the exact same complex @ Ruby Buffet that we got our carb loading on and now, it was a chance to unwind and relax after a challenging day in Wilmington, DE across the river. It was a quite day, since everyone else seemed to be out at fancy restaurants celebrating Mother's Day, but here we were at Saigon Pho. It was a nice place to hang out and relax. The overall experience was nice, although there was air condition, and it was your typical Vietnamese restaurant in the heart of South Philadelphia. Nothing you don't want that's applicable.

    Pho Saigon is located at 1100 South Columbus Avenue in south Philadelphia, enclosed within a strip mall. I guess get what you can find with yelp, and this place kind of fit the bill exactly, especially with having sustenance after knocking off 26.2 miles and a new state. The parking often is a hit or miss, since there are plenty of shops that anchor this stretch of paradise, but we were lucky and found a spot, so we headed on in. It was Mother's Day 2014, after all, and both of my friends were moms, so it was nice to hang out with them with having Pho on this special day for them. They do accept credit cards, so that's always nice to have. For this trip to have Pho, it was nice to have quality friends time here after running the marathon and to unwind and relax. We came here around 3:30pm and there was a couple who wore their Sunday best, since it was Mother's Day, but at a Vietnamese restaurant. I was a little surprised at that fact, but I guess wherever they want to eat, is fine by me.  They directed us to the corner near the entrance, which was cool. We could see the outside while we were eating. The entire meal took about 45 minutes before we had to leave for the airport, which was kind of fast, but not really. Pho is fast and inexpensive for the most part.

    Prices are reasonable here. One bowl of Pho was about $9.00 with tax, gratuity not included. The service here was friendly and fast. The ambiance of Pho Saigon was very calm and quiet, almost like a library, since it was a Sunday afternoon. Everything was more or less laid back and restful. The city is like that sometimes, so it's nice to just take a step back and enjoy the weekend and be calm about it.  The overall experience was good. Priced right and very much delicious. I had ordered the Pho with all the meat ingredients, since they did not really have a combination type of Pho, aka the Pho bac diet. They should've though. I also had some appetizers, which were the fried rolls, which I haven't had in a long time. It was warm and crispy, which tasted really good. So good in fact that it just melted in my mouth after chewing on it a few times. Just like M&M's, sometimes.

    The Pho was nice and the broth seemed to be a little watered down, but it tasted pretty good. After a marathon, I would literally eat just about anything. Anything good and delicious anyway. :) Still, it was a nice place to have Pho. Recommended if you enjoy Vietnamese in south Philly.

    Support local!

    We all have that special food that we will go out of our way to eat and try no matter where we are because it's familiar and we always want to see how it compares to our favorite.  Pho is that food for me.

    Pho Saigon has shown me another angle in which to view pho as an international dish which has become a great food phenom for all sorts of races and cultures.

    As far as pho houses are concerned, I usually order the same type of item because it makes it easy to compare and it has all the tasty bits that others tend to shy away from.

    The house special, the deluxe, the 'everything' normally consists of tripe, brisket, raw flank steak, tendon (either crunchy or fatty), and beef meatballs topping off a healthy portion of noodles, cilantro and slice onion.  Throw in some thai basil, some sprouts (if I'm feeling healthy) and a 5 second squirt of sriracha and I'm in business.

    Compared to my normal bowl of deliciousness, i think that Pho Saigon's broth leans towards the spicier side of the spectrum.  Not heat spice, but star anise, cinnamon and ginger spiced.  It's very delicious and as with most places served quickly and always hot.

    The summer rolls are delicious (a bit noodle heavy IMO) but the sauce served with it is superb.

    If you're in the area and looking for a quick, hot bowl of pho, stop in and give it a try.  You won't be disappointed.

    This was my first time at Pho Saigon and I must say it was rather tasty!

    Since it was summer, I usually skip pho soup because that seems like a fall and winter type of food.  All that hot broth really warms up your gut, and who needs that on a muggy July night?  So I ordered the bun vermicelli with beef skewers and fried summer rolls.  Very nice portion size, and the beef was really yummy.  They also skipped the bean sprouts which some Vietnamese diners will pile on because it's a cheap filler.  I also ordered side order of summer rolls and they were plump and wrapped tightly so as not to "explode" when you bite into them.  I wasn't blown away by the peanut dipping sauce, since I prefer my sauce to be heavy on the hoison so it has am extra kick.  Overall, not a big deal.

    I called ahead for a pickup order and I was pleased at how easy it was to get from the front door to the counter to pick up my food.  Most Vietnamese diners are very small and I feel like I have to weave in between tables to get to my order.  For what it's worth, this place was jam packed with a lot of Vietnamese customers on a Saturday night, so that should tell you they make good food.  At least, I would assume Vietnamese people are the most critical judges of their native food, right?  I also liked the fact it's located in a small strip plaza with lots of free parking.  There is even a craft beer shop right next door --- and beer seems to go well with Vietnamese grub.

    So stop being stupid and check this place out.  Your belly will thank you as you long for those warm nights on the beaches of Saigon.
