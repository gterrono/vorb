    Listed in The Places I Go Out

    On a cold, rainy Monday night in early spring, my friends and I were out in search of a good place to hang out when we spotted the Good Dog.  On of my friends said that she'd been there before and liked it, so we all piled inside to get out of the rain.  Inside was cozy, dim, decorated with pictures of patrons' dogs, and packed full of people - on a Monday night at 7 p.m.  Since the downstairs is tiny, there was no room for us at the bar or anywhere else, so my friend managed somehow to get us a table upstairs.  Upstairs was also pretty crowded, but our table was spacious and comfy, with low lighting and more pictures of adorable hounds on the walls.  This place is nothing fancy, but it's exactly what you'd want in a neighborhood bar - cool, shabby chic, packed with a young crowd, and a place to grab a good beer and some grub.

    Once my friends and I settled in, we asked for menus, which the snarky waitress eventually provided to us.  According to my friend, there's one particular waitress whom everyone thinks is rude and somewhat bitchy - lucky us, she was our waitress that night.  Although the waitress seemed very annoyed to have to bring all 8 or 9 of us beer and food, and she took quite a while in getting things to us and in coming back to see if we wanted more drinks, the check, etc., I thoroughly enjoyed myself.  I threw back a few cold pints of Magic Hat #9 for $4 a pint and had a $9 grilled cheese.  While that price might seem steep for such a sandwich, it was actually really worth it.  This was no simple grilled cheese sandwich.  Instead, it was brioche stuffed with fontina, asiago, and gruyere (my favorite), thinly sliced smoked apples, and applewood smoked bacon.  I've honestly never had a more enjoyable grilled cheese in my life.  And the rest of their menu is equally fancy, so I had a very difficult time choosing what to eat.  My friends all seemed to really enjoy their food, as well.  Check out their menu at gooddogbar.com (and enjoy the barking web site).

    Although I was stuffed from my sandwich, I couldn't resist ordering dessert - a rice crispy treat sampler for $5.  It included one each of an original, peanut butter, and chocolate treat with chocolate and caramel sauces.  This is genius!  Why don't more restaurants serve up comfort food in such a gourmet way?  Seriously, I plan on going back and ordering this dessert again sometime very soon.  

    I highly recommend this place if you're looking for some good bar food and a cold pint.

    Every time I come down to Philadelphia, I have to make a trip down to the hole-in-the-wall bar known as the Good Dog Bar. This is not a very large bar, so it can get crowded pretty fast. When walking in, it may seem long and narrow, but there is also additional seating upstairs. One of the waitresses serving the tables in the front will frequently walk by and ask if you want to be seated or to have drinks at the bar. Be forewarned, that this place is not made for large parties. I would recommend 4 max.

    Every time I go here, my mind is made up and I always wind up ordering the Good Dog Burger which is a 1/2 lb. of house ground sirloin, stuffed with Roquefort cheese and topped with caramelized onions, and served on a toasted brioche. On the side, is a large serving of mixed fries (french fries and sweet potato fries) with just the right amount of salt to make them mighty yummy. There is also a serving of aioli sauce to dip the fries in but I usually stay away from it as it is not much to rave about. And now to the Good Dog Burger. The buger is just INSANE!! As soon as you bite into it, you can taste the flavor of the grilled marinated meat. Soon to follow is the gushing Roquefort cheese which makes it out-of-this-world. Don't forget the caramelized onions that just makes this burger go over the top. I have never had such a perfect burger in my life. This is why the reason so many people come back time and time again. This burger is definitely worth the $10.

    The beer selection is very good (about 15 or so at a time), with many local and not-so-mainstream beers on tap. For those desiring a mainstream beer (Budweiser, Rolling Rock, Miller Lite, Coors Light, etc.), you can order by the bottle. All beers are reasonably priced at $3-5.

    Every time I come, the service is good as the waitress always comes back to make sure everything is alright. I probably go empty on a glass of beer at most, 1-2 minutes, and that is during the busiest times, 7-10pm. The music selection (jazz box) is good and is never too loud so that you don't have to shout.

    This place can not go missed. Every time I go to Philadelphia, this is my top-pick, surpassing all the more desired places such as the cheesesteak restaurants (Pat's, Gino's, Jim's, etc.). If you are in town, definitely make the effort to check this place out.
