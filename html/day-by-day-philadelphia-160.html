    Day By Day is our absolute favorite place for brunch (although I'll admit we haven't tried a ton of places because that would mean skipping Day By Day!). It's not cheap but it's worth it and the coffee is good! Everything we've tried on the menu is solid. We love the breakfast sandwich, and our friends are obsessed with the oatmeal pancakes. When the stuffed french toast is lemon curd with berry compote, do not pass it up (the "stuffing" rotates monthly). I tried the quiche for the first time on my most recent visit and it was amazing, plus it came with a perfectly dressed mixed green salad, which was just the right amount of acid to offset all of the buttery goodness of the quiche. The huevos rancheros is also good, and big enough for two to share. If you're with a group, the "bread basket" of baked goods is a great deal (but too big for two people, unless you're going to take most of it home). The service is also always good; I don't think the wait staff has ever kept us waiting excessively. The only admittedly nit-picky thing we've found wrong with this place is you have to ask for jam with your toast--it's not on the table.

    If I had a reason to be in Rittenhouse on a Sunday afternoon, chances are that reason would be to eat at Day by Day.  HONESTLY!

    Today, all of the pseudo-sub-zero temperature woes were forgotten once I stepped into Day by Day for brunch.  Of course the inside of the building is much much warmer than the outside, for sure!  But that was not what swayed me.  Yes, it's super busy inside - hustling and bustling and munching and crunching and conversing and on and on and on - and yes, we had to wait for 40 minutes to get a table (but that's because the place is so tiny compared to the average number of customers that go to the establishment!)...but, oh, the food was memorable.

    I had the sundried tomato, goat cheese, and spinach omelette.  When was the last time I had goat cheese in my omelette?  Um, don't think I would have been able to remember the last time, so...never!  I've had feta in my omelette and that cheese, by far, is my favorite cheese to my omelette :-)  But goat cheese?  Oh, move aside, feta!  Goat cheese has taken your place!  Creamy goodness over and over again with each bite.  

    Ok, so, what can I say?  Day by Day just got lucky with me - I tried something new, and it could have been the opposite of everything that I had just said...but it really was just my luck that I tried it at Day by Day and not anywhere else before it.  Well played, Day by Day.  Well played.  But I also do love the small establishment - the character is definitely there :-)
