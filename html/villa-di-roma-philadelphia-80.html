    I've been there twice in the last month, and have been very pleased with the meals. The first time I went, I had the Veal Dominic, which is veal in some sort of sauce. It was delicious, though a little salty. My date got chicken parm, which she reported was very good.

The veal was tender, telling me it was good veal. I've had restaurants try to pass off low quality veal, and I can tell the difference. My date got spaghetti and meatballs, which tasted homemade. Another reviewer here called them "mushy," but that wasn't our experience. They were good.
    Villa Di Roma is just a good, old-school "red sauce" Italian restaurant. Nothing trendy, no celebrity chefs. Just good, classic Italian food in ample portions. Mangia!

    This place serves up some really tasty, authentic, no-frills Italian food.  The red sauce is great.  Meatballs are huge and delicious.  Someone near me ordered the escarole and it smelled so good that I had vivid fantasies about stealing it.  Eggplant parm was also drool-worthy.
