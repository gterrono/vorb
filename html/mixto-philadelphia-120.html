    I was severely craving Cuban food and near despair because I know the choices are pretty limited in the Philly area, at least for authentic restaurants. Before I go trekking to Tierra Colombiana, I thought I'd give Mixto a try. I have to say, I'm impressed and craving satisfied... for now!  I sucked down that 1st mojito so fast, even before my husband came in from his search for a parking space. Parking is a little challenged since it's more residential and there's not much turnover for the street spots. We went on a Friday evening, nearest lot was $17. Hands down, this mojito is the best I've had in Philly area so far. I don't know how Cuba Libre continues to get away with serving their watered down version. For dinner, we chose to sit at the bar because we didn't want to wait for a table. Even for a raining night, the restaurant was full. The Churrasco Argentine was delish! The steak was tender, cooked to a perfect med-rare and served on a sizzling plate. However, there was a giant single asparagus on the platter. It was nearly raw and I'm not sure if that was garnish or what, it was odd. My dinner came with a side of yummy black beans and rice, and then there was another side of plaintain chips with guacamole. The plaintain chips were a little stale and the guacamole was tasteless - the dinner was great without it - Mixto, nix the guac!  My husband had the paella. It was pretty tasty and not dry at all. The seafood were cooked perfectly. He is not a Latin cuisine fan and only came here out of sympathy for me.  At the end of the night, he was very happy and full - and can't wait for next time. I hope we can snag a sidewalk table or a table by the French doors for a different experience next time.  Can't wait!!!

    Coming from Miami, I often find myself craving some serious Latin food. I'm talking palomilla with arroz y frijoles, maduros, cafe con leche, yuca, mojitos, ropa vieja, arroz con pollo...where to go in these fair parts? Mixto.

    Listed in Memoirs of a Bean Queen

    My experiences at Mixto have been ... very Mixto.

    In other words, uneven.

    It can feel like a fun place to go, but to me it's like the white man's version of Latin food. It's bland, even when there's seasoning. The main seasoning seems to be salt, and it's not artfully applied.  The margaritas are wildly unimpressive. The rice and beans are notably bad. Food or booze, there are no real standouts.

    A good analogy: wedding food. Nice-enough looking plates you could take or leave.

    No question: Mixto has a role to play, and it plays it OK. It's handy for late nights and for entertaining big crowds at reasonable prices. It meets a lot of needs across a wide cross-section of people. It's the kind of place your boss might take the team to celebrate a job well done.

    For this particular person, however, who savors the Latin flavor: it's a big No, gracias.
