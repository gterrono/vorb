    I remember loving this place a few years back but I just hadn't gotten around to stopping in recently. Last night my group was looking for a place to sip a drink and enjoy the outdoors, and the Devil's Alley balcony tables came to mind. There was a table on the street level available when we rolled up so we decided to sit there instead.

    I was going to be perfectly content ordering a Tröegs Perpetual IPA for $5.50 until our sever informed us that all bottles were $3 for craft beer Tuesday. Then she pointed out that Victory's Golden Monkey counted towards the special. I love this girl! She then continued to blow our minds with their daily drink specials. I believe it's margarita Monday, craft beer Tuesday, rum punch Wednesday, Jameson Thursday, Absolute Friday, Sangria Saturday and Yuengling Sunday. The prices vary per day but were really excellent based on each beverage.

    We weren't planning on eating but then she told us the specials. There was a duck quesadilla with goat cheese and a Mac n Cheese with jalapeño, bacon and crab. Both sounded phenomenal but my friend wanted the Mac n Cheese and I definitely needed an eating buddy since I wasn't even hungry. Amazing call! It was creamy and you got at least some topping in every bite. The jalapeños could have been a little hotter but maybe it was better not to make the dish super-hot.

    Of course inside the restaurant they had the lights and talks trees that make the ambiance cool. I didn't love how the tables overlooked a dying section of Chestnut Street but it's not Devil Alley's fault this once vibrant neighborhood is now a little desolate.

    With such great all day drink specials, I'll absolutely be back soon. Even if it's just for one drink before moving elsewhere, who can resist?

    On a recent Friday night after several glasses of wine at the apartment, my friends and I were STARVING. But where the heck could we go around Rittenhouse that wouldn't involve standing around for an hour? Then I remembered that Devil's Alley does Open Table and voila! Table booked for 9pm.

    They have things as indulgent as a crab imperial burger (um YES, this Marylander gives it two pinchers up) but also a few "healthier" options like salads.     Which reminds me: they have a lovely beer list and cocktail list, too! And they do cocktail pitchers for select drinks which is always fun.

    My only major gripe is that it's been pretty loud both times I've been there, but hey... it is a bar...

    We came for afternoon drinks because they actually had some semi outdoor seating. The upstairs area has 3 tables by the balcony and are right next to the full bar on the 2nd floor.

    Drinks:
    - 1907: St. Germain, gin, strawberry purée, lime, and grapefruit. I got it without syrup, but it still wasn't tart enough for me. The gin was pretty strong, and you could definitely taste it (not one of my favorite flavors)
    - Tarragon Gin Fizz: lots of fresh tarragon, fresh, more citrusy than the above. It's also fun based, but the combination of herbs, lemon, and champagne made this one much more pleasant to drink

    To their credit, drinks are nice and strong. My friend really liked his raspberry mojito (warning - it's pretty pink).  

    It's not a super fancy place, but drinks are strong and pretty solid with nice outdoor seating.

    This place is very OK. The food is OK. The selection is OK. The atmosphere is OK (can be good, if you get one of the tables for two right next to the open area on the second floor). The service is OK. The value is OK. I've come for brunch, dinner, and drinks - all were OK.

    My wife likes coming here, but I usually veto it - not because there is anything that bad about it, but I'm usually looking for something different.

    Brunch - some pretty usual fair, and decent but not what I'd consider great prices.

    Dinner - nothing that ever jumps out as memorable, though the food usually tastes pretty good, with portions that seem a bit small for the price and quality. OK.

    Drinks - I've only come here for drinks once (were were also getting appetizers/dessert, but were mostly killing time until we could get into someplace more interesting, like  1 Tippling Place or Ranstead Room, which are infinitely more interesting places to drink). The service was horribly slow, and inflexible. They gave us a special menu (maybe restaurant week?) even though we told them what we were there for. It listed root beer floats, which I decided I was craving, and they insisted they could not do. I had to order ice cream and root beer separately and combine myself. I understand customization can be a hassle at restaurants, but with the "almost a chain" feel you get here I didn't think it would be a problem, especially since they were offering that exact thing anyway, just on a different menu that they also gave us unsolicited. Not the end of the world, but it reinforced the "eh, OK" feeling I had for this place.

    I'll probably be back. I really want to like it - it would make my wife so happy. I also convinced her to start a Yelp account recently - maybe she'll give it 4 or 5 stars to counter my review. Or maybe eventually I'll find the right food combination that I really enjoy here, and then become a happy regular.

    Ambience - Yelp is asking me for what it qualifies as, listing every combination they can think of. I can't really say it qualifies as any of those. Maybe they need a "feels like it should be a chain" category. Casual + touristy might be the closest, but I can't imagine a tourist heading here. I guess I'll flag those anyway.
