    We just happened upon this place when a nice lady saw us looking for another bar that had shut down (I was shattered when she told me)...but she recommended Race Street, and I have to say that we were all pleasently surprised!

    There is a great atmosphere, good amout of seating space with a bar. They have a great little fireplace up near the front and a street lamp right in the middle of the floor, so cool.

    Thier drink selection was great (didn't see much by name for wines, just standard Chard, Merlot, Malbec etc.) but they have a great beer selection.

    Their food was amazing, the wings, sweet potatoe fries, calamari (with jalapenos fried up with it) and the turey burger were just delicious! The service was really great, and they were really busy right after we go there, but our server did her best and we had a great time

    I will definitely be back as I just love these out of the way, laid back, cool to hang out and talk kind of places.

    The interior of Race Street Cafe was nothing like I had expected. Even in the middle of the day, the place is a little dark with a focus on the bar, which is the first thing you see when you walk in. I wasn't expecting that. I had heard such good things about lunch at the Race Street Cafe, I thought it would be more like a Wedge + Fig kind of place.

    Anyway, I loved my lunch there. A generous portion of the special salad, but mixed greens were topped with roasted beets, bacon (the guiltiest pleasure of the day), hard boiled eggs, tomatoes, fried goat cheese, red onion (which I gracefully tossed to the side) and a simple champagne-y vinaigrette. Service was casual- I think the one dude in the place held responsibilities of bartender + server but i didn't mind a bit.  

    The menu was not particularly veggie-friendly, but Michelle C's buffalo chicken salad (Zack H's fave) looked delicious. I'll be bringing my boy to try it out in the near future.

    A 5 star rating would actually take something away from this place...I mean this is more like a cozy neighborhood pub than a cafe...and I like it that way.

    So I know you want to have a great meal in a unique building that was once a Farriers, right?  Don't go running to Google yet, I'll fill you in.  Back in the days when Ben Franklin was walking the streets of Olde City a Farrier was a blacksmith that also had some veterinarian skills. This is where you brought your horse for new shoes.  Today you bring your hungry/thirsty self for delicious craft beer and inventive food.

    I'm a sucker for restaurants that are rustic old buildings with lots of wood and heavy beams.   Olde City has quite a few and this is one of the best.  The front of the building has two huge barn doors (to bring in the horses of course) that can be opened during the summer.  This creates a unique experience that should not be missed.  It the winter there is a wood burning stove that's very nice.

    These folks have a full bar but they specialize in unique draft beers.  They have about 15 taps and on a recent visit I enjoyed a delicious Troubadour Belgian Pale.  

    So what about the food?  I've visited for lunch many times and dinner twice.  The lovely wife and I have also enjoyed Sunday brunch so I think I have a good handle on the food.  A lunch sandwich is going to cost you about $10.  It could be a burger or chicken or pulled pork, or even fish tacos.  There is a long list of extra toppings that go for $.50.  Of course the sandwiches come with good fries.  The Spanish Burger - $10.50 is to die for. A combination of ground beef and chorizo sausage with melted Manchego cheese and fries topped with sautéed onions, jalapenos and cheese.  Add a craft beer and I'm in heaven.  

    Expect your entrees to be in the mid-teens.  I've enjoyed a pork loin dinner for $16.50.

    When I was at Sunday brunch I tried the Huevos San Lucas - $10 which is three fried eggs served over house made salsa, grilled andouille, and nacho chips topped with melted cheese and fresh avocado. Delicious!

    A word or two needs to be said about the service.  Several reviewers have commented on negative experiences.  My experience has been that while sitting at the bar my service has been excellent.  When at a table my experience has been mixed.  It appears that the servers are overworked and a bit undertrained.  

    When I rate a restaurant I look at atmosphere, adult libations, food, and service.  Even though the service is sometimes a little off the overall experience is so great I'm going four stars.

Specialties

Stop by for lunch, dinner, a late night snack or brunch to sample our diverse menu and ever-changing specials board. To quench your thirst we offer 15 constantly changing draft beers including a hand pump as well as many quality liquors and wine.

History

Established in 1999.

Sit inside the low-key café and you'll feel like you've settled into a rustic bistro: heavy wood beams hold up the ceiling, a wood stove warms the room, and a gas street lamp throws dim light on exposed brick. During the warmer months you are likely to find the large barn doors on the front of the building propped wide open looking out onto the sidewalk dining and passers by. Friendly bartenders and a broad selection of 15 beers on tap, with one hand pump, help you wet your whistle.  The menu is simple but accomplished with a blackboard list of specials and a focus on seasonality of local products. From housemade hot and cold soups to an extensive sandwich collection and entrees from a Fish of the Day to a Grilled New York Strip Steak you are sure to find something for everyone
