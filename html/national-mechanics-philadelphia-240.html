    Last Saturday at the unofficial elite event after party I learned that in Old City it is in fact possible to visit a bar on a busy night and not only can you find a seat and receive service within mere minutes of cozying up to the bar, but that you can also be taken care of without being overbearingly asked how you are doing. With four bartenders there was never a wait for anything, given the noise factor between the crowd and the DJ it was nice to not have to repeat an order over and over, every bartender got it right the first time. All of these wonderful qualities and a decent crowd to boot, none of the douchemongers you see in places like Mad River or Lucy's.

    With an overqualified beer selection, I had a hard time deciding what to order. It was in a moment of hesitation that Daniel C. took the reigns and introduced me to what will now be my summer staple, RJ Rocker's Son of a Peach.

    Less than a week after my first visit I was summoned to partake in an evening of Quizzo with a fellow yelper. Against my better judgement on a weeknight I went to test my fuzzy brain skills (which absolutely failed me on a Chumbawamba question that I'm still kicking myself over). The MC was alright, a few of his ramblings left people like us who were situated rather far from him wondering what the actual question was, but for a team of two with no fancy phone to cheat with, we held our own on topics ranging from sports and science to music and movies.

    Oh hey, guess what else I learned at National Mechanics? That varicella is the scientific word for chicken pox and not the commonly mistaken term for nose hair. All in all this an absolutely decent spot any night of the week and one that I will be visiting again and again.

    I have been here many times. The beer list is pretty solid, not amazing - but they have some good local ones. They always have yummy sounding fancy drinks like a jalapeno margarita.

I had two Bells Oberon with my meal and my friend had two Bloody Marys from the make your own bar. You pick your tequila and choice of rim, and they bring it out to you - and you use the mix, horseradish, hot sauce, steak sauce, slim jims, etc to make a pretty tasty drink. And my water glass never got under 1/2 full .... super attentive service.

    That doesn't add up to four stars, so I'm giving them two more stars for the building and atmosphere. I like the outside of the building and the inside too! :)

    Like all good friendships, I remember the exact moment I became acquainted with National Mechanics.  My boyfriend dragged me here to meet up with a bunch of law school students (now lawyers) for the Wednesday night Pub Quiz with the rowdy host, Irish John.  "We've got to get there early or there won't be anywhere to sit," my boyfriend urged me.  I thought he was kidding.  Surely there would be some place to sit.  Merde, was he right.

    Quizzo was jam-packed with people, old and young, ugly and cute, all waiting to jockey for the winning position.  We quickly found a spot, made up some lame team name, ordered up a batch of drinks from Annie, one of the NM staples, and proceeded to win the game.  Winning meant we got $50 off our tab.  The second place team received a lesser amount, but still got some money to take off the tab.  The host cursed at people for cheating ("Don't try to look up the answers on your Blackberries.  It's bullshit and pathetic," he yelled.  Amen.)  Rinse and repeat almost every Wednesday for about three years.  

    What's not to love?  There are orchids and terrariums filled with exotic plants, artwork of a scary clown hammering a nail into his own face, resin greyhounds on the walls, an old bank vault for the building, and a spooky-ass centipede hung on the wall by the bathroom.  The bartenders are good about pouring the beer right and there's always some strange menu item (a jalapeno margarita?!) to intrigue you.  NM also offers plenty of vegetarian and healthy options if you're not wanting heavy pub fare.

    There were many Quizzo games, countless drinks, and endless attempts to find a parking spot in Old City, but National Mechanics was always there.  We had daily specials and burgers, Frito pie and pretty much every other item on the menu.  Sometimes it was great.  Sometimes it was not-quite great, but we always had the NM family there.  The servers, Liz and Annie, in particular bonded with us.  It wasn't unusual for one of the girls to buy us a round of shots or drinks after we won a game.  For a group of six guys and one girl (me), NM was the closest we got to having a Cheers experience.

    The funny thing, though, is that for a long time, I hadn't tried the brunch at NM.  Finally, we went there to see what it was all about.  I loved the make-your-own Bloody Mary bar and the bacon-flavoured vodka.  It was like drinking a BLT.  The prices are friendly, the food appropriately greasy, and the atmosphere understated.  The dimness is welcome after you've been walking the streets in harsh sunlight.  NM really is a place where they know your name.  Now that I'm not a Philadelphia resident any longer, it's one of the places I'll think of with nostalgia.  But like with any good friend, the memories are fond and many.  Thanks, NM, for everything.

History

Established in 2006.

Built by famed architect William Strickland in 1837 the National Mechanics Building has seen banks, churches, clubs, bars and fires pass between its walls, and it's still standing.

This beautiful building, as much a part of Philadelphia history as any tourist attraction, now houses Philadelphia's freshest new bar and restaurant.

The peculiar name for our establishment stems from the building's original incarnation in 1837 as Mechanics National Bank.

Our décor is an allusion to all things mechanical from our homemade lighting fixtures to the strange devices running throughout the space to our uniquely designed bathrooms. All of these elements combined with our laid back and off kilter philosophy make this bar what it is. A place designed with real folks in mind and molded by time, our hands and the hands of many before us. Come in and check it out.
