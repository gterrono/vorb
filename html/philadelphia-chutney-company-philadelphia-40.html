    Supremely disappointing.  I love southeast Indian food, and this dosa - even the traditional aspects - was unlike any other I have had.  Maybe the entirely westernized dosas are better.

    First, the texture of the dosa itself was more like a dry crepe than the spongier consistency I expected.  

    Second, the potato filling was not only dry, but also there were huge chunks of mealy potato.  Not appetizing.  The chunks of veggie chicken were good, but not really why I ordered a masala dosa.  

    Third, the mango chutney tasted more like mango ice cream - it had a fake flavor and lacked the depth that real mango chutney has.  

    I asked for my dosa to be spicy, and there were big chunks of spicy sauce glopped onto the dosa that were overpowering.  Judging from other reviews, the restaurant is inconsistent with what they mean by 'spicy.'  

    The service was fast and friendly, and the staff was happy to offer suggestions, but it's not enough to make up for the terrible food.

    Running errands in a car in Center City on a cold day while hungry is a terrible way to spend a weekend.  I ditched the car in a parking spot and just started walking because traffic was at a stand still.

    Enter Phila Chutney Company to my right.  The windows are a little steamed up from all the warm spicy goodness inside and someone sat right by the window and started in on a plate of dosa, which was more than enough reason to go in and give this place a try.

    Ordered the classic masala dosa - friendly fast service, clean dining area.  The kind of place that makes it easy to grab a quick bite and go, by yourself or with a couple of friends.  The food was made well, the dosa was nice and crispy but not overdone, and the filling was flavorful and spiced traditionally.  I got the mango chutney, which wasn't memorable, but wasn't terrible either.

    Would love to try some of the other things on their menu.  Would def be on my list of "places for a quick bite when I'm busy."  Especially when running errands in Center City, since this is smack in the middle of my square mile of errand-running establishments.

    The Philadelphia Chutney Company is a hip, funky take on the Indian cuisine in the heart of Center City.  If you're only idea of Indian lunch fare is endless, cheap buffets, think again.  This place not only serves up enormous dosas (traditional stuffed Indian crepes) in myriad combinations, but everything on the menu is vegetarian friendly and surprisingly delicious.  In addition to dosas, Chutney also serves Uttapa and Samosas.  Yum!

    The best part about Chutney is their attempt to take ingredients and combinations from other cuisines and blend them into traditional Indian cooking.  While the results do not look entirely appetizing, the taste is still there.  My dosa with veggie chicken, spinach, roasted tomatoes, and goat cheese looked as if someone threw them together on an over-sized dosa haphazardly, but when eaten together tasted incredible.  The same could also be said about their condiments and drinks.  My mango chutney and my mango lassi didn't look particularly appetizing, but both tasted fresh and delicious.

    Chutney needs to work on their presentation, especially since the food is delicious and the decor is modern and fresh.  Otherwise, if you're stuck in the same old lunch rut, Philadelphia Chutney Company is a terrific alternative to the Center City lunch scene.

Specialties

At the Chutney Co., we have a fun, Indian-fusion concept that we're taking to locations all around the city. Our Southern Indian fusion menu (which you'll see on our cool chalkboards in our bright blue, orange and green stores) is lighter and healthier than the heavy food most people associate with Indian cuisine. And on top of that, our food here is organically sourced, health-conscious, low fat, natural and 100% vegetarian.

History

Established in 2010.

In visionary cities like London and Paris, you can find a fast-food Indian boite bustling on every corner. But in the States, our take-outs options are restricted to only Mexican, Chinese, pizza or burger joints. The Philadelphia Chutney Company is looking to change that and bring Philly up to speed.

Meet the Business Owner

Nirav M. Business Owner

This project of passion was fueled by Nirav Mehta, a former technology entrepreneur/attorney with dreams of the dot-com era to create something special, and Baldev Singh, a former attorney whose chef dreams led him to open Aman's restaurants and eventually become the boisterous "cooking guy" behind the scenes at Chutney.
