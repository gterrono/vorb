    2/6/2014

    My parents, brothers, and I were staying in Center City for the weekend. We came to Nodding Head for a casual dinner on Friday night around 6:30pm. We walked upstairs and were seated immediately. I found our waiter to be very friendly and attentive. My dad had a Grog, which is a dark beer. Dark beers aren't my thing but I did enjoy the after taste of chocolate. My mom and I had a Solstice, which is a light, refreshing beer. To start we had the sampler. I suppose with a few more beers I could have enjoyed the platter of fried goodness. The fried mac n cheese was creamy and rich and the onion rings had a tasty batter. For dinner my dad had a meatloaf sandwich. The meatloaf had a hint of spice and the gravy was delicious, especially on the fries. My mom had the rasta man chicken sandwich. The sweet plantains and Spanish fries (fries with grilled onions and jalepeños) were a great compliment to the spicy, jerk chicken. My youngest brother had the pulled pork sandwich. It had a ton of meat on it and the potato wedges that were served with it were his favorite. My other brother and I had the Monk burger. The creamy blue cheese with the grilled, sweet leeks are delicious toppings on the moist burger. The seasoned fries however were what made my meal even better. They were cooked perfectly and were seasoned heavy handedly. Overall I found this place to have a great atmosphere. The dining room we were sitting in could have been a bit more lit, but that is my only complaint. When I stay in Philadelphia again I will come to Nodding Head for a beer and some delicious, wallet friendly food.

    Listed in My Own Private 2014 Challenge, Beertopia, Breweries and Brewpubs, Brotherly Love

    Being the beer nerd that I am, after booking my stay at the Latham, the first thing I did was look for the closest brewery and/or brewpub, and discovered that Nodding Head Brewery and Restaurant was just a block or so away. I went to their website for some research and was greeted with very colorful branding that had a dated/throwback feel more suited for an old school neighborhood joint than a modern brewpub. And that's the enigma of Nodding Head. It's an old upstairs city bar that looks and feels more like a locals hangout than a brewpub. It's classic wooden interior, old-style bar and kitschy decor - Nodding Head refers to the hundreds of bobblehead dolls that adorn the interior - offer the warm and inviting casualness of your local dive bar.

    I would expect any beer brewed in a place like this to stick with the basics ales and lagers. This is not the place where I'd expect a lot of variety and creativity. But you know that old saying about looks ...

    Nodding Head was one of my final stops in Philly. It was our last night and the wife was getting tired when I reminded her that I still wanted to check them out. She graciously allowed me to go while she rested, so I headed out and arrived to a mostly empty bar at 11PM on a Saturday night. I grabbed a seat at the bar and grabbed a beer menu. As others have noted, the service here isn't exactly the warmest, but I've come to expect that from places that give off the "locals bar" vibe.

    I was surprised to find a wide variety of beers on tap, offering beers that showed some deep knowledge of beer styles and brewing techniques. I started with a "flight" of three - their Berliner Weisse, Pepper Pale Ale, and Summer Nights Sour Brown Ale. Their "flights" are served in a full-sized white wine glass, and can be ordered in any amount or combination of their brews. The sours were very good, and I enjoyed the subtle peppery kick (which comes from white pepper) of the Pepper Pale Ale.

    I knew from my interwebs research that they had a Berliner Weisse here, but I was pleasantly surprised to see two sours here. I was also pleasantly surprised to see a cask ale here (Bill Payer Ale - a hoppy pale ale) and an aged beer on tap. Their aged beer was their "BBA BDG" - a Bière de Garde (a strong ageable farmhouse ale similar to a saison) aged in Burgundy Barrels. The barrel-aging imparted very complex wine notes and added a bit of pleasant tartness to the beer.

    I didn't have food here, so my review is a bit incomplete in that aspect. But if you are coming here just to try the beer, don't judge the book by the cover. If you can get past the dim dive bar vibe and the kitschy bobblehead decor, you'll find that Nodding Head offers a selection unique and complex well-crafted brews.
