    Sabrina's is a sure-fire bet for an excellent breakfast or brunch for two or three people.  The cozy former townhome (two of them, actually) provides a warm and bustling environment for small groups, if one which is a bit crowded even during off-peak hours.  On the weekends, it's absolutely mobbed, and you can usually expect a minimum half-hour wait to seat two or three people, but during the week the pace is much more sedate.

    My aunt and I stopped in midday yesterday for brunch before doing my pre-semester food shopping in the Italian Market (I need my cheese...)  We were greeted with a fairly unusual sight in our experience: Sabrina's with free tables!  Thus, we were promptly seated and our (very friendly) server dropped by with water and a carafe of fresh coffee.  Her patience was impressive, as she stood around for at least five minutes explaining various details of the specials and chatting with us about her favorites before my aunt and I had come to our decisions.  The service here is one of the best things about Sabrina's, probably the single feature (food aside, of course) which pushes it from merely great to truly excellent; servers are attentive, fast, and almost preternaturally on-the-ball.

    Anyway, onto the important parts: the food!  Having not had Eggs Benedict in a few months, I opted to order the Eggs Benedict Florentine.  I was not disappointed; from the slightly cayenne-spiced Hollandaise sauce to a pair of perfectly poached eggs to freshly-sauteed wilted spinach, this dish was a perfect blend of flavors and textures.  By far the best Eggs Florentine I've had in a long time.  My aunt went for "Gabby's Flip Side Fritatta," an egg-white fritatta with a vegetable medley, topped with goat cheese and a caper cream sauce.  I had to try a bite, of course, and found it to be light but crisp, with the caper sauce and goat cheese both lending a bit of sharp counterpart to the relatively lightly-seasoned fritatta.

    On a side note, this is definitely a place where you can except to get a good cup of plain, black coffee.  No one in their right mind would come here *solely* for coffee, but I find that brewing ordinary coffee is increasingly a lost art form in all too many breakfast joints.  Not the case here; strongly but cleanly brewed La Colombe coffee and constant warm-ups are enough to make any coffee lover a very happy, if hyper, person.

    I live two blocks from Sabrina's.  I go there often and I order take-out. And yet, every weekend, I walk by and I feel sorry for the massive clouds of lemmings waiting for two hours.
       It's French Toast people.  Seriously. We are only on this earth for a short time.   Come back during the week, or later in the evening.
        That said, if you do come at a normal hour, everything on the menu is solid comfort food.  A few suggestions
      a) The polenta fries have a serious kick to them.
      b)  Order the Mel's with mayo, because it's dry.
      c) The crazyasspancakes with Butterfingers or whatever they have that day will be great for about 5 bites. And then it's just too sweet.  
       d) Ditto the French toast.  
       e) The secret is for one of you to order something normal (burger, veggie sandwich, cali Turkey)  and the other to order the crazy and then split it.

         g)  The coffee is La Columbe.
        h) Be careful with take-out orders. They've gotten mine wrong several times now.

    The only good thing about being "that girl" who has to ask for everything made in a special way (this on the side, no that but this instead, you know) is that the food you receive is usually fresher and hotter than it might be otherwise. At no time is this a bigger bonus than when you order breakfast potatoes.

    The potatoes at Sabrina's rocked, as did my build-your-own omelet with cheddar, spinach, and tomatoes with a side of sausage. There aren't a ton of gluten-free options here, but eggs and potatoes are always a solid choice. My one gripe is that you can't add meat to the omelets, however I decided on that particular day a side of sausage would fit the bill better anyhow.

    B's stuffed french toast was just insane. I ate two huge bites, knowing full well how sick it would make me, and when I was lying on the couch clutching my stomach hours later, I still wanted more of it. Like dessert on a plate, with the cream cheese and banana filling adding just enough tang to keep it from being cloying. The bread is so fluffy, and it's so enormous! She barely managed to eat half.

    On a Saturday afternoon around 1:30 we waited probably 15 minutes for a table. This place is literally always packed and it's for good reason. Be prepared to wait, but know in your heart that it will be sooo worth it. In the past I've had their more lunch-type offerings as well and they were also solid. Everything is homemade with good quality, fresh ingredients. Sabrina's is my favourite brunch place in the area, hands down.

    Only in south Philly...

    Our recent experience with Sabrina's was anything but dull. As we waited for a table, we listened to statements being made to police, as a woman's purse was stolen from her table (outside).  The commotion cleared out a few patrons (and tables), so our wait time was pruned considerably. Lady Mary and I took our seats in the outside section. It was a beautiful day, so al fresco dining seemed to be just the ticket.

    No sooner did we sit down, when a crazy old wench accosted out table with suggestions on purse security. Our waiter asked her politely to leave, which only served to provoke her into a rousing chorus of "She has a right to know this!!!" & "I'm not interrupting", and other brunch favorites.  I also asked her to politely take leave with all available dispatch, to no avail.  A Charlie Manueleqse belly bump and a retort, "You ARE interrupting my meal" sent her on her wayward way.  

    All that before needed to be related first, or nothing wonderful may come of this story.  Kidding...joke for any Dickens geeks. Let's talk about the food for a moment, shall we? I had a pork sandwich, which used sweetness in a most eye-openingly intelligent way. The meat had been marinaded in apple and pear juice, and served with thin slices of said pear & roasted peppers. The sweetness managed to get IN to the flavour, rather than being put onto...like just adding sugar, syrup, or some traditionally sweet component...really top notch. Lady Mary had a solid, if not spectacular, mexican style brunch. Eggs, some heat, cream, corn...all the usual suspects done well, with fresh ingredients.  It was above average, but I would brave no perils for a chance to eat it.

    Lastly, there was an apparent celebrity sitting behind us. We deduced this because the gaggle of hipster girls at the table in front of us couldn't stop whispering to each other, and taking very conspicuously rude pictures with their iPhones. I suspect he was an athlete on a team I don't know, of a sport I don't watch. Either way, we let the man eat his meal in peace, as there had been enough disturbances in the force that morning.

    Sabrina's should be at least four stars.  I will need to go back on a less chaotic day to rate it any higher however.
