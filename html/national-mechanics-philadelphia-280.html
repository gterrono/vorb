        National Mechanics is an Old City staple. They have decent food at decent prices, great waitstaff, ample space and I love the decor. The terrariums and orchids are killer and the place is always decorated for the season. Right now they have a Christmas tree hanging from the ceiling. They offer a good variety of drafts and bottles and also have cocktails.

        I mostly go here for:

        Lunch
        I work in Old City and 'Nat Mech' is our go to for lunch as a group. They aren't open on Mondays for lunch, but you can't beat a good burger (veggie or beef) or my favorite, the popcorn chicken. Always have plenty of seating room, good service and lunch is fairly priced for the area.

        Happy hour during the week
        Happy hour Monday through Friday is good with a manageable group. They have $1 off drafts from 5-7pm. Pro tip: Check the board, not the table card for drafts on tap.

        I don't go here on:

        Weekends
        I try to avoid National Mechanics on Friday and Saturday nights. It's not them it's me! They get swamped with people coming into the city for their night out and I'd rather avoid that crowd.

        Listed in City of Brotherly Love

        Your experience with National Mechanics starts with the outside. From the outside, National Mechanics looks like a museum, which certainly easily fits in with the surrounding aesthetic in Old City. The steps up to the bar actually remind me of the Metropolitan Museum of Art in NYC. Once you're inside, the wooden interior half reminds me of a study in someone's mansion. One that I've always dreamed of having "when I grow up." Needless to say, National Mechanics may very well be the closest I'll ever get to that dream. National Mechanics has several different "events" every so often, ranging from a weekly trivia night (aka "quizzo" in Philadelphia) and live music, but the first event that brought me to the bar was the "Bibliodiscoteca" fundraiser my friend in the library science program invited me to. For the aforementioned reason, the location just seemed perfect. Obama was in his first year in office and they had a drink dedicated to him: some lovely concoction that was part beer and part some sort of chocolate liqueur. It remains one of my favorite drinks to date. Last time I went, there was a margarita using jalapeno-infused tequila. It wasn't my favorite - for the spicy drinks, I liked Franklin Mortgage and Investment Company's Jalapeno Martini much better. Their beer selection seems pretty awesome (even to a non-beer aficionado) - if I remember correctly, they have Yeungling on tap (which of course is pretty much standard for the Philly area), drafts from the other local breweries like Yards and Victory, and notably Lindemann's Framboise which was basically my gateway beer and very hard to get on draft in any bar, as well as many foreign bottled beers.

        Quizzo - it's a pretty tough Quizzo. I went with two other fairly intelligent people but there were just so many detailed questions in genres we sucked at. It gets pretty full fast so get there early!

        People seem to enjoy the food there but I haven't sampled any of the food myself.

        Philly is home to a lot of great bars that have a way of feeling like a neighborhood bar to a lot of people. National Mechanics is definitely one of them.

      History

      Established in 2006.

      Built by famed architect William Strickland in 1837 the National Mechanics Building has seen banks, churches, clubs, bars and fires pass between its walls, and it's still standing.

      This beautiful building, as much a part of Philadelphia history as any tourist attraction, now houses Philadelphia's freshest new bar and restaurant.

      The peculiar name for our establishment stems from the building's original incarnation in 1837 as Mechanics National Bank.

      Our décor is an allusion to all things mechanical from our homemade lighting fixtures to the strange devices running throughout the space to our uniquely designed bathrooms. All of these elements combined with our laid back and off kilter philosophy make this bar what it is. A place designed with real folks in mind and molded by time, our hands and the hands of many before us. Come in and check it out.
