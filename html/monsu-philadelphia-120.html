    Monsu offers a monsoon of flavour.

    This gem of an eatery is situated at the most northern point of the Italian Market, at 9th and Christian streets. A commitment to authentic Sicilian cuisine makes it's proximity to the market more sensical than some of it's Mexican counterparts. This dedication to honest food made Monsu a brunch destination on a fare spring morning.

    To start, Lady Mary and I shared a calamari appetizer.  The balance among all of the components of the dish was carefully constructed, and very successful. Crunchy walnuts tempered chew from the calamari. Notes of citrus and fennel were skillfully added, to create a bright, yet subtle dish. This course was, needless to say, very effective.

    For entres, I opted for the bucatini special, whilst my lady selected the crab hash.  Although my pasta was good, the hollandaise on the hash elevated her dish to a very different level. We were forced to suspend our conversation for a few moments, as we voraciously attacked our meals. The perils of socialism would have to wait until we finished the course.

    Worth mentioning is the fact that my service was downright enjoyable. I appreciated our server so much, that I almost wish she could have joined us for brunch. Her clear passion for Sicilian cuisine was well matched to an effervescent personality, and a simply beautiful smile. Cheers from the less ebullient Englishman.

    P.S. - If you please, go to the photo section to see shots of the meal.

    I was looking for a nice place in my new neighborhood to take my parents and Monsu totally fit the bill: A cute little cash-only BYOB with a charming atmosphere and attentive waitstaff. I couldn't believe we were able to walk in on a Sunday night with no reservation!

    The menu is extensive and there is a 40$/4 course fixed price option, which is really nice. I had a hard time choosing a main course because there were so many interesting options. They also gave us a complimentary tuna amuse bouche in addition to their house-made focaccia and tapenade, which was a nice touch. I don't eat meat, but my parents both enjoyed it. We got the caprese salad, the green salad, and the fried ricotta to start. All the ingredients were fresh and tasty, but the fried ricotta could have used either a sauce or a crispier batter in my opinion; just something to add contrast. For my main course I had the ravioli with goat cheese and basil, sweet yellow peppers, and extra virgin olive oil, which was rich, decadent, and beautifully presented. The plates were just the right size too. Overall a great dinner!
