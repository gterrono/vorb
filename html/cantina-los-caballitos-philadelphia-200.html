    Chorizo tacos.

    It's been years since I have been to Cantina.  I came here once before when I didn't even live in Philadelphia.  It was odd that my friend from New York heard about a place with great Margaritas in South Philly.  After a phillies games, we investigated.  So glad we did.

    Fast forward a few years.  I know live in walking distance of Cantina.  I know plenty of people who are regulars.  It's been on my list for a year now and I finally went after a run one night.  Delicious.

    I love this space.  It's got such style.  It's theme is so approachable and there is a soul to this restaurant that makes you feel good.  The chips and salsas (two of them) are both delicious and make it hard to leave room for your actual meal.  These tacos were amazing.  The tortillas were perfect and the chorizo had great texture and spice.  I know I could have eaten 10 more of them.

    The menu is so reasonable and the place is just fun and inviting.  Our waitress was extremely personable and I know that I'm gonna become a regular here.

    I'm torn on Cantina, but I'm reviewing tonight's meal, not the whole idea of the Cantina.

    Typically when I go in, my backup plan is the tacos and his is the enchilada, unless we're swayed by the specials.  I live on the specials.  Ambiance aside (ok, one note, so loud that our waiter was standing over my husband's shoulder and still screaming the specials at us), this was a really great meal.

    We started with the fried avocado (they can do that?!) stuffed with chicken.  I was cautious about the sweet sauce it was topped with, but the texture was incredible.  It wasn't super-flavorful, but who cares when you have this lucious, creamy avocado in front of you, coated in crispy fried goodness.  

    I ordered the vegan buffalo wings - cautiously, after confirming they were seitan and not tempeh.  We recently had a bad experience with buffalo-style tempeh nearby.   These were truly awesome.  Like, I'm biting my tongue (fingers?) not to start swearing right now about how good they were.  I'd pick this over chicken any day.  Crispy seitan in a hot (but not so hot you couldn't taste it) sauce with a tangy creamy sauce that I can't believe was non-dairy.

    He got the enchiladas, and he was jealous.

    So Cantina wins again.  Sure I'll fight it off for a while, but I'll be back and I'll be honed in on that specials board.

    After all these years, all my trips to the Cantina, and yet I have not written a review on the Cantina. Why? With so much to say, I think reverting to bullet points will serve this review best.

    The food is a hybrid of authentic Mexican and Tex-Mex American. Inventive, really good stuff usually. Highlights include the fajitas, nachos, corn on the cob and Mexican hot dog. However, the plates seem to have gotten smaller in the last year whilst prices have not.

    As for the drinks...
    Really, really the best margarita in the world. They will KNOCK YOU ON YOUR A** if you arent careful. I prefer the passionfruit or guava, but really, there are about ten to choose from. Amazing, reasonably priced tequila flights. The best drinkery in that regard in South Philly. Beer selection though is a bit myopic, focusing mostly on Mexican beers. Sort of makes sense. Sort of.

    The environment:
    If there is one thing to get out of this review, its this: Inside is LOUD AND DARK. I mean... really loud. And really dark.  I do not like it at all. A bit cramped in the non-booth table. I highly, highly recommend sitting outside when the weather suits.  The outside seating is comfortable and perfect for people watching, and its a beautiful neighborhood to just relax outside.

    All in all, my feelings on the Cantina are split. Good, not great food. Totally worth it to sit outside on a 65 degree day and drink the nite away. Otherwise, if you have to go inside, its a whole nother ball game.
