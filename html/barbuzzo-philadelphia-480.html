    yum! made a trip here for my birthday dinner. we started out with the pig popcorn and sheeps milk ricotta. the popcorn was ok...not really my thing and kind of greasy. the ricotta was amazing! i could eat it every morning for breakfast. a little sweet, a little savory, and ultra creamy and delish. i think i dreamed about it last night.  we also had the skirt steak and gnocci. the steak had a great sauce and was cooked perfectly. i really enjoyed the gnocci. it was very rich but not overwhelming. the wild mushrooms added a nice flavor.

    Far and away the best meal I've ever eaten. Say what you will, I may not be a big Yelper (but I'm trying!) but I've eaten in Sydney, Paris, London, New York, NEW ORLEANS (home of my previously favorite meal - Mr. B's Bistro - braised Kobe beef short ribs over polenta with a reduced red wine sauce), Los Angeles among many other cities. We started with the olives, simple enough right? Well, these little jewels were special. Several varieties served with blanched then roasted almonds and a sprinkling of roasted rosemary. Jesus, these were tasty. Then came the Charcuterie plate with Apricot Mustard/Tapenade/Crostini. Yum. Next, the spicy meatballs. My Italian best friend must've come into the kitchen while we weren't looking because these suckers were fricken' to die for. About this time, the next dish is served - Bone Marrow! Who knew? Only complaint, I wanted seven more. Greasy, salty, fatty deliciousness scooped onto a crispy crostini. Finally, the coup de grace, the squid ink pasta with crumbled sausage and clams. O-M-G. Mouth orgasm. This dish came with think soft bread that had been broiled with butter. Rip apart the bread, dip in broth inside this bowl of good and start crying just a little. It was THAT good. Did I mention the cocktails? Basil smashed like its an ingredient in a mojito with Vodka and homemade lemonade. It was basil-y and tart. The lemonade was not sweet but fresh and given just enough sugar to make it fit perfectly in this drink. Mind you, we came to eat here because we were stuck in Philly because of Hurricane Sandy. They were open and they came to cook in the kitchen that day. Bar none, the new best dinner I've eaten. GO!

    Barbuzzo perfects the art of unpretentious dining with small plates that quite literally blow your mind within the first few bites.  

    While everyone, including your correspondent, loves the salted caramel budino, the dark horse is the butternut arancini - muddled cheese and squash balls, fried to perfection.  I was fighting my girl for the leftover pesto sauce!

    The warm beet salad should not be missed, there are few places that get it as good as these guys do.  I've always loved citrus in my salads, and the whole thing goes very well with pretty much anything else you're consuming.

    The calabrian greens were aight, not particularly inventive.  I just like super dark greens, so it was a personal fix.

    Clearly, there's much more exploring to be done.  The location is very cool, a great first date option in my opinion.  And even if you don't like the somewhat cramped quarters, your date might get the hint and snuggle up, yaknowwhadimean?

    We ordered drinks, both very good, and two dishes-- one of which was the sauteed gnocchi.  It was very rich and memorable.  In fact, the other dish was slightly less memorable, as I can't remember what it was, though it was fairly good tasting.     Quite possibly one of the best meals that I have ever had the pleasure of savoring.

    We had a girls night out in Philly to celebrate a friend's birthday during Restaurant Week. First of all, if you've been dying to eat at Barbuzzo like I have, then make a reservation 2 weeks in-advance. You'll thank me for it!

    Everything at Barbuzzo was just pure perfection! From the décor to the food.

    We had a 9:30pm reservation during Philly's Restaurant Week, and were seated promptly. The waiter seemed frustrated that we couldn't decide whether we all wanted to get their Restaurant Week dinner special (4 courses for $35), or if we would rather order dishes off the menu. We decided the latter.

    I had the Roasted Beet Salad. It was so light and fresh, and completely worth the hype! If there's one dish that I wouldn't mind ordering again at Barbuzzo without the guilt, it would be this one!

    An an entrée, I ordered their Rosemary Tonarelli. Yes, the pasta is fresh -- which should be expected from an establishment like this, but it was so heavy! All I could taste was the butter and lemon. I wouldn't order this dish again.

    My friends ordered:

    Sheep's Milk Ricotta -- GET THIS. It's smooth like butter. So creamy and light in texture. The bread that they serve it wish is freshly made, rustic and crusty, grilled, toasted, and buttered to perfection!

    Grilled Mediterranean Octopus Salad -- For $13 and considered a "tapas" dish on their menu, the portion was huge! The octopus was so tender that it tasted like lobster. We weren't sure if all of the flavors in the dish went together (the roasted peppers with the sopressata.

    Butternut Squash Ravioli -- This was a special entrée for the fall season. So, it is no longer offered. For $15, we were expecting a little more than just 6 ravioli, but we guess that it's quality over quantity, right? Really, really fresh from the pasta to the butternut squash flavoring.

    Uovo -- personally one of my favorite dishes from that night. As much as it can be made vegetarian (I'm a vegetarian), don't. That bacon fat is absolutely necessary to the flavoring of the toppings. So much as you can pick it off, do just that. The crust, the white sauce, and that signature truffled egg in the center danced beautifully in my mouth. This, I would order again, but perhaps with a carnivorous friend to eat the bacon...

    Budino -- Lives up to the hype! Honestly, I only ever wanted to go Barbuzzo because of this dessert. The contrast of the salted caramel with the dark chocolate crust at the bottom is just amazing! Pure perfection! I'm a sucker for salted caramel. So, this really hit the spot for me.

    Warm Lancaster Apple & Raisin Bread Pudding -- From the one spoonful that I had, I would have ordered this, too. It tastes just the way bread pudding should taste.

    Overall, I would come to Barbuzzo for lunch, but not for dinner. All of the dishes that I have liked are also offered during lunch. Lunch tends to be less busy as well.

    What to order: Roasted Beet Salad, Sheep's Milk Ricotta, Uovo, and Budino. YOU WON'T REGRET IT.

    Atmosphere: I agree with previous yelpers' reviews that at night, the restaurant can be quite dark, but I guess that it adds to the intimate atmosphere..

    Let me start by saying: Barbuzzo was everything I dreamed of and more.

The bartender doesn't mess around when it comes to liquor! M had a microbrew, which he enjoyed.

    To begin, I ordered the sheep's milk ricotta and M ordered the bone marrow. The ricotta was TO. DIE. FOR. I used to be vehemently against ricotta cheese -- I never liked the grainy texture or bland flavor -- but when dating an Italian you're pretty much forced into it.  This ricotta was different. It was barely grainy, with a smooth texture. The bone marrow was also delicious. I loved the concentrated beef flavor, and the salad served with it was perfect to cut through the oily substance of the marrow.

    Then, I ordered the Paccheri al Forno and M ordered the Torcheitti "al Telefono". The lamb in my dish was slightly gamey (but in a good way!), and the sauce was delicious. M's al Telefono took the cake, though. I never thought pork in a tomato sauce over pasta would be so unreal. The pork fell apart, was juicy, and went perfectly with the sauce. Yum!

    For dessert, by the urging of fellow Yelpers, I got the Budino. I don't think it lasted more than a full minute on the table (and I ate it all myself. M's not a huge dessert fan). The center was delicious, the crust was amazing, but the salted caramel made the whole thing perfect. The salt was the last thing to hit my tastebuds, and it was wonderful.

    The only thing that I could say I wasn't totally wild about with this place was how the seats are all so close together. You're practically bumping elbows with your neighbors. The people sitting next to me kept commenting on my food to each other, which was kind of awkward for me.
