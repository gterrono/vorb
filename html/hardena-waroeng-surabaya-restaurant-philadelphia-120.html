    Hardena offers authentic Indonesian food in a home-style setting. Note the food here is out in food warmers, and the owner scoops everything for you.

    From the outside, it may look dingy or even closed, but don't be scarred - inside you'll find some delicious food.

    For like $8 you get two entrees, a side, and rice. Highlights include:
    - Beef Rendang - this is really the star of Hardena, so juicy and flavorful
    - Collards - cooked Indonesian style (not Southern style lol)
    - Fish Curry
    - Tempeh

    Anthony Bourdain gave this place his approval, so check it out.

    Note - if you are looking for a more cooked-to-order Indonesian experience, there are like 5 other Indonesian restuarants in the Point Breeze area.

    After moving to Philadelphia, Hardenas quickly became my favorite place to eat!
    The buffet style is perfect, and there's usually at least one dish out that I haven't had in a while, which keeps things interesting.

    My two personally standbys are the collard greens and tempeh tofu, but everything I've had here is great. The service does tend to vary depending on whether or not the two daughters are working, and they are the only two who know how to take credit - but I've gone in on an occasion when they weren't there and they still took my order and simply told me to pay later! On rare occasions the flavor of certain dishes will be a bit subdued or dry, but everyone is very friendly and always seems happy to be serving the food that they are.

    Take out containers are very filling, with a ton of rice and three generous sides; eating in tends to be smaller portions.

    After moving away from the direct vicinity, Hardenas is without a doubt what I miss the most! Go there NOW!
