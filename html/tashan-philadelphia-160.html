    I've been aware of Tashan for a while but I hadn't made it a priority to visit mainly because I'm not that into Indian food, but based on the high rating I thought it was finally time to give it a try.  I'm glad I did.
     
    The menu is influenced by various global cuisines.  I found it to be very accessible, even for someone uninitiated to Indian flavors and spices.  

    The best dish by far was the Butter Chicken, which I get the feeling is not really an authentic Indian preparation, but whatever, it was tremendous.  The tomato cream sauce was amazing, and we used it as a dipping sauce for pretty much everything else we had on the table.  The Crab Cake and Palak Chaat were both quite good.  The Kobe Chopan Kebab was the only miss - it was more like Kobe sausage which I wasn't really expecting, but I'm willing to write that off to personal preference.  I rarely eat dessert but there was crème brulee with a little Masala donut in the center which I couldn't pass up, and it was awesome!

    Our waiter was friendly and generally attentive.  The only misstep of the night was an unusually long wait for my glass of wine, which he remedied by providing it gratis, which I considered a fair trade for the minor inconvenience.

    Also free valet parking, which I didn't learn until after I left :(

    Will definitely go back soon!

    My wife was super excited to come here, and I am always happy to follow her excellent restaurant instincts, but, unfortunately, our experience left us confused and upset.

    Let's begin with the entrance.  I was impressed with the cool, hip decor as I walked in, but I was unimpressed when we had to wait in front of the unstaffed hostess desk for a while before someone eventually came to seat us.

    As we began to look over the menu, we reminded our waiter that we would be using a Groupon for the brunch.  We specifically asked if we would be allowed to order anything off the menu, and he assured us that we could.  When it came time to order, though, a different waitress came over to take the order.  When she learned that we would be using a Groupon, she claimed that our brunch choices would be made by the chef.  After some confusion and arguing, she gave in and allowed us to make our choices from the menu (note: the Groupon fine print says that we could pick from any item on the menu).

    The ordering process was our first sense that we would be treated differently because we were using a Groupon.  We later watched as tables around us received their food before us, despite the fact that we had placed our order much earlier.

    As our food came out, it looked and tasted delicious, which matches what many other reviews here say.  I can't complain about that.  What I will complain about is that when they made a mistake by bringing out an incorrect bread, they replaced it with such a tiny new piece.  We had received the goat cheese naan instead of the black truffle naan, and they replaced it with the correct black truffle naan...but were they trying to trick us with the cheaper option.  I'm willing to give them the benefit of the doubt, but after the dessert trick they pulled on us later, I'm not really sure if this was an unintentional mistake.

    Like I said, the food was all very tasty, but the final awkward, annoying, and frustrating part of the meal was dessert.  We ordered the Fruit and Berries dessert which was described as having tandoori pineapple along with various berries.  For an $8 item, all we received was a platter of cut mango, watermelon, cantaloupe, blueberries, and blackberries with a small cup of yogurt dip.  There was no tandoori pineapple or cardamom syrup as described in the menu.  One waiter told us that it would come sauteed in a cardamom syrup with star anise, but they said the chef didn't know how to prepare it that way.  Instead, this was what we received.  Our original waiter returned, asked how the dish was, and, after hearing our confusion, was surprised to hear that we were expecting something different.  He claimed this was the actual preparation of the dish.  When we argued that it did not fit the menu description at all, he became confused and offered to appease us with free coffee or another dessert.  It was all very strange and awkward considering how it felt like this waiter was either knowingly lying to us or was very unaware of what the dish should have been.  We should have spoken to the manager at this point, but we just wanted to get out of there.  We were the final people left as brunch was ending, and we didn't want to stick around any longer.

    My final thought: if they seemed so annoyed by Groupon customers, why did they offer one in the first place?!

    Great dining experience here on Friday night. I have been out of the groupon game for a while as it normally will get you the 2nd class citizen treatment. Still, I have wanted to come here for a while so we gave the tasting menu a chance through groupon and boy I am glad we did.

    Inside was not what I expected, very trendy vibe and the dining room was packed on a Philly summer Friday night - impressive. The only real complaint is I was not impressed with the cocktails. Both the broad and catherine as well as the mumbai were unimaginative and did not complement the first rounds of food. However, there is a great glass of shiraz that has an indian spice finish I was glad I found.

    As for the food you will not go home hungry if you go the tasting menu route. My favorite like many others was the butter chicken. Hands down one of the best cream sauces I've ever had and the chicken was super tender. The GOC naan is the perfect vehicle to make sure no sauce goes to waste. Also worth a try is the Palak Chaat baby spinach salad. Super fresh, nice balance of textures and a perfect palate cleanser between courses (also I'm a sucker for yogurt sauces so this was a winner from the start). The Naga Beef Sümi was a great flavor combination between the wagyu beef, soy reduction and relish although I had hoped the meat to be more tender. There seems to be many other fans of the tandoor shrimp and mine was cooked well but could have used more sauce. The Gol Gappa hollow durum puffs "shots" were definitely unique, worth a try with the flavorful cilantro water. If you're into heat, the Mangalorean Sausages would be up your alley albeit a little dry for me. In addition we had a plate of roasted vegetables in the middle also, really nothing memorable there. The standard 3 item dessert tasting finished off this feast and the creme brulee with a hidden donut treasure underneath was the showstopper.

    This place definitely leans Indian-fusion more than authentic but I appreciate the uniqueness. The regular prices are steep but in my opinion justified and I already look forward to coming back. Service especially throughout the meal was excellent, another integral piece of the overall experience. Keep up the great work Tashan.

    Ambiance
    The restaurant design is pretty posh and surprisingly large inside.  You can get a pretty good view of the kitchen by sitting in the main dining room.

    Food
    Some really clever fusion dishes going on.  I've liked everything I've tried here, and the butter paneer might be my new favorite "Indian" dish.   The gulab jamun creme brulee also rocks my world.  Palak Chaat is another stand-out, which is sort of like a twist of a North-Indian dish that has fried spinach, except this is made into a really tasty salad.

    Price
    The lunch menu is a really good value.  It's currently $12 for a samosa, naan, rice, and main dish.  I'd recommend lunch as a budget-conscious way to check out Tashan. For dinner, the 3-course menu from 5-7 is also a really good deal.  It comes with an appetizer, main dish, and desert for $35.  Dinner can also be had a la carte, in which case, the prices will be higher.

    Service
    Excellent.  Servers are really knowledgeable and willing to lend some recommendations.  They're good at doing things like filling water without being intrusive.     Read more

    The waitress did give a nice explanation as to the type of food and seemed to have good knowledge of the menu. But I was disappointed at how they honored the deal. The living social deal only listed 1 menu item that was not part of the deal. But before we ordered, the waitress informed us that there were 3 menu entrees that we could not get under the deal in addition to them being out of 2 other entrees. That literally cut our options almost in half. I really think they should honor what is on the promotion and not make changes later, but we didn't make a big deal of it.

    I ordered the truffle naan and LLT Club (lamb sandwich with fries). My boyfriend ordered the chili naan with an omelet. My entree came out about 15-20 minutes before my boyfriend's entree and we were not planning on splitting the entrees. The LLT sandwich was delicious. The lamb itself and the sauce were awesome. The fries were another story. The fries tasted like McDonalds fries sprinkled with chili powder or some other spicy powder. I think they would be better off with a side salad versus the side of fries.

    My boyfriend's omelet (called the Amlet-e-Tashan) was awesome and one of the best omelets I have tried in a long time. It was huge and filled with lots of cheese, caramelized onions and mushrooms. It also had a small side salad with tiny pieces of fruit.

    The naan came towards the end of our meal. We both didn't really care for the truffle naan and felt that its sweetness didn't really go with the bread. But we both really liked the chili naan- it is very spicy. After we had our naan, other staff members came over with more naan which was not for our table. Fortunately, the quality of our meal suggested otherwise, and I'd gladly return to Tashan if I could afford it under normal circumstances.

    First, to clear a common misconception, Tashan indeed serves fusion fare, but the flavors of the dishes are truly rustic and respectful to real Indian cuisine. Think authentic flavor profiles wrapped in elegant, well-executed packages and you'll have an idea of what Tashan does well, which scored major points with my Indian dining party. Their butter chicken struck the essential balance between tomato brightness and creamy richness, with discernible aromas of fenugreek and other spices. The pieces of chicken themselves had tenderness with a bit of bite, and indeed Tashan's rendition of the classic ranked among the best I've tastedt.

    My lamb chops, in contrast, failed to make an impact. Though the quality of the meat was quite good, the flavors weren't anything I couldn't receive from a reasonably authentic Pakistani joint (like Kabobeesh in UCity), so the chops were subsequently a major rip off. Naans had the appropriate levels of fluffiness, and I enjoyed the earthy funk of my truffle version.

    Dessert, a creative play on creme brulee featuring gulab jamun, had the proper level of sweetness without dulling the palette. Tashan pleasantly surprised; its refreshing to see well-executed Indian food outside of the hard to find hole-in-the-wall places. If they'd decrease the prices a bit, they'd win many regular customers. Unfortunately without Groupons in hand, Tashan is too much of a splurge for the Indian craving. Consider it a special occasion place for the Indian food aficionado.

History

Established in 2011.

Tiffin started as a web only and delivery only concept out of a small store front in the Northern Liberties area of Philadelphia and soon evolved into maybe the first multi platform restaurant in the country, offering a full ecommerce website, delivery and carry out on the phone and dine-in, all from the same location. The successful formula led to the opening of the second location in Mt. Airy followed by Elkins Park, Wynnewood and Bryn Mawr, sandwiched around the opening of Tiffin etc., an Indian street food concept. The very latest from tiffin is a 5,000 square foot theatrical ethnic restaurant. The focus of this first-class restaurant will be on sophisticated Indian fusion grills using time honored Indian grilling methods. Unique and unconventional to the east coast, this groundbreaking restaurant concept will transform the Philadelphia dining scene and thus serve as both an amenity to Philadelphians and as a destination location for people throughout the tri-state area.

Meet the Business Owner

Munish N. Business Owner

Munish Narula, a former Investment Banker and a Wharton MBA is the founder and president of Tiffin Indian cuisine. After earning a Bachelor of Science in Hotel Restaurant Management and a MS in Hospitality Administration from Johnson & Wales University, he started working for a full service chain restaurant where he quickly moved up the ladder to reach the position of Area Manager with 11 restaurants under his management. After earning a MBA degree in Finance from Wharton and a stint with bulge bracket Investment Bank, he came back to the food service business when he founded, the now nationally acclaimed, Karma restaurant in Philadelphia. It was during his time with Karma that he identified the need of providing fresh nutritious Indian food to people at affordable prices and so the idea for Tiffin.com was born.
