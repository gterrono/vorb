    CatahouLOVE. This place is awesome. It's really too bad that it's tucked away in such a strange spot because I never would have known about had a friend not told me.  She had a good experience here once before so she decided to purchase the recent Living Social deal to try it again. We stopped in on a Friday night around 11, and although the place is really small (4-5 tables and seats at the bar), it really wasn't that crowded.

    The menu is creole, Louisiana style soul food. Much of it is fried and or is cooked in some kind of animal fat, but I was ok with it. The menu has a lot of different offerings, the basics were soups, salads, appetizers, po boys, small plates, flatbreads, other sandwiches, and large plates. We both had a really hard time deciding since we agreed we would eat anything on the menu, but we settled on cornmeal crusted fried  oysters (over sauteed greens with tomato jam), a shrimp/gouda/andouille sausage/hot sauce flatbread, and three small plates (cornbread, hush puppies, and bacon braised collards). The oysters were amazing. Salty and sweet and I could have eaten the tomato jam by itself. The flatbread was genius - they used Crystal hot sauce as the "tomato sauce" on it, and combined with the smokey gouda and spicy sausage, it was my favorite thing. My friend cried from the spiciness, but I was happy because that meant more for me. The small plates were all solid and well done. Prices were fair, and you could easily have a cheap meal or decide to go all out.

    They don't have that many beers on tap but when we were there they had one of my favorites, Flying Fish Grand Cru, on special. We didn't try the desserts because we were so full but they looked tasty as well.

    Our server did a pretty good job considering he was the sole FOH employee there at the time. Our water glasses were always full, and he came to check on us often. I will definitely spread the word about this place!

    Quality food and drink in a great part of town.

    Catahoula has a creole/cajun theme thing going on: po' boys, gumbo & such. The food is good. Real good. You won't go wrong with their "My Burger". It can go up against most, if not any, burger in the city.

    Hush puppies. I personally did not  like them. They had too much garlic or some other spices in them. I find hush puppies should not be altered. Ball it up, fry it up and give it to me. No need to go fancy here - they taste just damn fine by themselves.

    I would actually give this place a 3.5

    My friend and I shared the crawfish bisque, fried green tomatoes as a side dish, duck jambalaya, and the shrimp and grits. Even though we were both stuffed, we ordered the brioche bread pudding with banana foster.

    Breaking it down, the crawfish bisque was a little too salty for my taste, but the crawfish flavor was good with a surprising amount of spice. Oddly, I did find a small piece of a blue rubberband in the bisque, which was probably attached to the green onions that were garnish. Not that big of a deal, but still a little strange.

    Fried green tomatoes: good, but not the best I've ever had.

    Duck jambalaya: the duck was really well cooked and tasted delicious. I wasn't a big fan of the rice because again, I found it too salty. I think it had too much BBQ sauce mixed in, which just overpowered the taste of the andouille sausage and ham.

    Shrimp and grits: The shrimp was again, delicious, as were the bacon braised collard greens. The grits were a little too bland (maybe just compared to the saltiness of everything else), and the mushrooms got kind of lost in them and didn't add much to the flavor.

    Brioche bread pudding: EASILY the best part of the meal... and that's saying something, given that I was already stuffed from the meal. It was just SO good... the best bread pudding I've ever had.

    I also had an Abito IPA that was on tap. Solid IPA.

    If I were to come back to Catahoula, I would probably come back just for the bread pudding and drinks.

    Stopped in on a warm weeknight and got an outside table right away.  The outside tables are set in a little lot next to the bar.  Strings of lights hang overhead between the bar and the building next door.  It's a perfect little nook for pretending you're somewhere else.  However, within a few minutes, one of the servers came out and opened two cabinets attached to the building, revealing two flat-screen TVs playing the Flyers game.  I found this strange and it definitely killed my atmosphere enjoyment a bit.  I'm not sure what their angle is here.  Are they trying to make it a cajun sports bar?  I sure hope not, but that's just me.  Not that I wouldn't come back if it was sports oriented, that just isn't at the top of my list of favorite eating/drinking atmospheres.

    However, the food was delicious.  We started with the Creole Remoulade Shrimp.  Delish.  I had the duck jumbalya.  It was awesome.  It is served as a whole duck leg set on top of the rice/sausage/veg mixture.  This wasn't what I expected, but once I tasted it, I didn't care. Rich, deep flavor, yummy as hell.  My partner got the blackened trout, which was also really good.  I'm not a huge fish person and I rarely order it out, but I would order this next time I visit.  We also get a side of cornbread - butter, honey, delicious, done.  We finished with the bananas foster bread pudding which was ridiculous.  Dense, gooey, sweet, HUGE!  I had some serious guilt after eating it, but it was worth it.

    Service was good, but a little...frantic?  It seemed like there were only two guys bartending and waiting the tables and there was a lot of running back and forth.  Each seemed to be waiting on our table and were often crossing each other's paths with drink orders.  But, they were very nice and food and drinks came out quickly.  Maybe they just need a little time to get into a rhythm.

    Bottom line = check it out and I will be back!
