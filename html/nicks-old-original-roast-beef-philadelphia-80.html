    We took megabus to Philadelphia and were on a mission to try a cheesesteak. Funny thing is, we never did try the original sandwich with cheezwiz (which I do appreciate for its history and "unique" consistency). I guess it's similar to the time I looked everywhere for Dirty Rice in New Orleans, and never found it except at a Popeye's... in Mississippi.

    Anyway, after going to Nick's, we figured this is probably better than the typical sliced beef sandwiches. The sandwiches here come with thick, moist slices of flavorful (aka fatty) roast beef and provolone. Since everyone suggested the gravy fries, we got one order, and ordered a second one because it was so good. They take the drippings from the roast beef, maybe whisk in a little flour, and pour it over fries. How can you go wrong?

    There was only one server for the whole seated section, so service was slow, but we didn't care. The food was worth it.

    Really 3.5 stars. As everyone's mentioned, the atmosphere is super dive-y. It's dark, dingy, and definitely not a great place to spend a nice afternoon. That being said, the staff is nice, the food comes out quickly, and the prices are just right. I've been a couple times but only had the beef combo and gravy fries at this point. Both are very tasty, but possibly not as mindblowing as the hype here suggests. It's probably a good idea to go in with slightly lower expectations, in which case they'll most likely be met or even exceeded. The roast beef is definitely very good, the cheese is nice and sharp, and the sandwich as a whole is really satisfying (especially if you slather on some ketchup, mustard, and horseradish). The gravy fries are a gloppy, satisfying mess as well. Nick's doesn't make the best sandwich in Philly, but it sure beats most of the touristy cheesesteak shops and is absolutely worth a visit. It's a true Philly establishment, and despite my reservations keeps me coming back for more.
