    I'm surprised a the spectrum of reviews for honeygrow...I had an experience today that was nothing short of awesome. Perhaps, like every restaurant ever, they had some hiccups in their first few months, but I found it to be a smoothly-run place with great food and service.

    It ain't easy to find clean, fresh food made fast. I have disease-related diet restrictions, and feel my best when eating a fresh, plant-based diet with lean meats and no gluten. If you're mindful about what goes in your body, you know how hard it is to get takeout or any food on the go. I was absolutely delighted to find a place that offers food I can eat (what a treat to not have to cook a meal!). I also find the locally-sourced food aspect to be a major selling point.

    I enjoyed using the touch-screen menus. The graphics are cute and modern, the pages are easy to navigate, and it's nice to be able to control each ingredient in your meal. Plus, you can swipe your credit/debit card yourself, which is so convenient.

    After I ordered, I waited maybe five minutes for my food, and I was able to see the person making it. I like the transparency of honeygrow's food line; there's no hiding gross ingredients or lack of cleanliness (neither of which are a problem here, everything looked great).

    I got the cobb salad, which has romaine lettuce, hardboiled egg, bacon, apples, grape tomatoes, avocado, grilled chicken (free range!), bleu cheese and a white balsamic vinaigrette. The bacon was perfectly crispy, the avocado was fresh, and the dressing was delicious (and just the right amount was on the salad). I loved every bite - it had nice texture, was hearty but not too heavy, and filled me right up. It was $10, and worth every penny.

    It seems that all of the negative reviews are coming from people who just aren't honeygrow's audience. If you're looking for a huge, cheap meal, then you're in the wrong place. If you're looking for human-sized proportions of quality food, then you've hit the jackpot. I'll definitely be back to try the stir-frys and smoothies!

    Dress codes set an expectation. There's a way you imagine that night's food when you lace up a tie, and there's a way you envision it with a pair of Chuck Taylor's beneath your jeans. Walking into Honeygrow, you half expect the computer ordering system to list protein shakes and energy bars to the crowds donning compression shorts and Asics.

    The Five Spice BBQ with pork was a satisfying, though not dazzling display of wokery.
    Onions, bell peppers, broccoli, egg white noodles, and Five Spice BBQ sauce  round out the contents of your cool little Chinese carry out box.

    Five Spice BBQ sauce is typically a rub comprised of Sichuan, fennel seed, cinnamon, star anise and cloves, but I thought it worked decently in its semi-solid food state. The pork is stylized close to the char sui fashion that dominates Chinatown menus, but was done without the deft hand. Fatty, tough pieces were a problem and the pig didn't pick up the Five Spice flavors like the noodles.

    Lululemon's cafeteria in another life, Honeygrow is a respectable option for stir fry that tends be a little lighter, and maybe a little more expensive than its Asian brethren. I can't say for sure the food is healthier, but it gives off that appearance.
