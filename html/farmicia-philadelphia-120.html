    Don't bother.

    The good: our attentive and well-informed waiter, who is clearly really into the place, creative cocktails and wine list, and the ambiance (especially the plants and simple table decorations).
    The bad: the food.  You know.  The main reason you go out to eat at a restaurant.

    The takeaway here is this: if you're interested in high quality, local ingredients, prepared in a creative and skillful way, you want to be eating...somewhere else.  Maybe Pumpkin on South Street.  They source locally and do a lot of organic stuff and are always very seasonal.  Bonus that their chef actually knows what he's doing unlike, apparently, the guy behind the wheel at Farmicia on Tuesday.

    We came here for an early dinner and planned to go all out -- appetizers, salads, entrees, and dessert.  By the end of our entree, we were so disappointed, we skipped dessert.

    Started with two appetizers: the fiddlehead ferns with green garlic and hazelnuts and the vietnamese crispy squid with sweet and hot tomato dipping sauce.  The fiddlehead ferns would prove to be the highlight of the evening.  They were tender crisp. bright green, sauteed and seasoned to perfection.  The green garlic was the perfect sharp complement to their springy freshness.  The vietnamese crispy squid on the other hand, would prove to be a harbinger of the doomed remainder of the meal.  Is the fryer too warm or is the kitchen just totally failing to let their fried stuff get a sufficient rest before sticking it on a plate and letting the grease accumulate in an unappetizing pool?  Unknown.  Either way, my date rightly proclaimed it to be a fiasco.  The dipping sauce, while a little ketchupy, was reasonably pleasant but couldn't possibly redeem the soggy grease balls that it accompanied.

    The salads.  Tuscan grains with chopped vegetables, lemon basil vinaigrette, arugula, and ricotta salata for me.  French lentils with baked goat cheese, greens, and sherry dressing for him.  If there was any dressing on either of our salads, it was woefully over oily and under acidic.  Hello!?  Vinaigrette!  Meaning with vinegar!  Let me say, I love salads.  I eat a ton of vegetables and I typically enjoy a simple presentation that shows off the flavor of some high-quality produce.  However, this does not mean that I want to go out into the garden, yank out some lettuce, dunk it in some oil and then stick it in my mouth.  While I can appreciate a few bites of plain, adorned greens to get in the appreciation of their quality, ultimately I want said greens to have some kind of dressing  Some fat, some acid, some salt, some herbs, some.. something.  Seasoning of any kind seemed to have taken a vacation from both these dishes.  The cheeses were the highlights and to say that they were used sparingly would be an understatement.

    The entrees: boneless honey-garlic grilled pork chop with maple mashed sweet potatoes and bacon braised cabbage for him.  Duck breast with crisp skin, sun-dried cherry glaze, native grains pilaf and sauteed snap peas with scallions for me.  At this point, I'm wondering if we somehow just managed to pick all of the stinkers on the menu.  Fortunately, when we ordered, I asked our waiter to help me choose between the salmon and the duck.  Without hesitation he steered me toward the duck, complimenting the cherry compote and suggesting medium rare on the temperature.  We also accepted his recommendation on medium for the pork chop.  Alas, the pork chop was decidedly NOT medium (try medium well at least) and the duck was also drifting toward the well side of the scale.  Our waiter was correct on at least one front: the cherry compote definitely the highlight.  The "native grains pilaf," which looked like a mixture of wild rice, hominy, pumpkin seeds, and maybe some other random stuff, seriously tasted like it might as well have been Uncle Ben's.  The cabbage, while not completely inedible, was boring at best and offensive at worst.  After taking a bite, my date immediately regretted having put it in his mouth.  

    ...

    While it feels good to get this off my chest, mostly I'm just disappointed.  I have heard such wonderful things about this place.  Their menu looks great, they're associated with Metropolitan Bakery, which is one of my favorite places to get bread (and pizza and snacks and coffee...).  The giant open windows, high ceiling and other aesthetic details are all so pleasing and perfect.  I had really high hopes and I feel like they were just completely dashed.  It's certainly possible that they were just having an off night or something, but we left feeling like we just wasted $100+, 2 hours, and how ever many calories.  Usually I leave a good restaurant having cleaned everything of my plate in a totally unladylike fashion and feeling like someone needs to stick me in a wagon and wheel me home.  Tonight we were so unsatisfied we were able to walk the 20+ blocks home in total comfort.  Sad measure, but there it is.
