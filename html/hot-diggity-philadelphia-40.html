    Going to Dapper Dog left me eager to try some more additions to the gourmet hot dog renaissance. And the topping heavy additions, potentially AMAZING fries, gourmet sodas, and then some made diggity the perfect candidate.

    While I was weirded out by things like the Philly variation coming with a homemade fishcake and brown mustard (which is very unPhilly like), and the Seattle variation coming with chopped tomato and garlic cream cheese (Thanks Steve! - i1187.photobucket.com/al…), my mouth was watering over plenty of their other varieties. In the end I knew that I absolutely had to have the Saigon Fusion which paid homage to the Banh Mi; and the Big Kahuna in all of its guava mustard, grilled onion, pineapple salsa and habañero aioli filled glory! While the hot dog could be slightly bigger for the buck, the main attraction is supposed to be the toppings, and the Sabretts showcased them beautifully. Both dogs where truly an explosion of their dedicated flavors. It should also be mentioned that all hot dogs can be served veggie style!

    Despite going for 2 serious dogs I had to try the fries as well, and the small cone ended up being a mega large! the spuds were large, golden brown, and absolutely delicious. Add in one of their 15 dipping sauces (curry mayo, Sriracha ketchup, and chipotle malt vinegar for the win!) and you have some big options.

    Hot Diggity also has some pretty crazy specials. when I was there they had a head cheese hot dog, and now they're featuring a hot dog and sauerkraut filled egg roll. I was disappointed to not see the Baked beans with pork belly that Adam Erace mentioned in his review (citypaper.net/food/2011-…). But the poutine will definitely have me coming back.

    Add in things like their craft soda collection and Bad Movie Nights, and Hot Diggity is definitely a step beyond you're average dog!

    Listed in Youse Guys Have Spoken - RotDs, LevelUp Up Up, South Street, Society Hill, Queen Village, Bella Vista, Getcher Wieners

    In New Jersey there's this time honored tradition where you order your hot dogs "all the way", this consists of a dog smothered in mustard, chopped onions, and chili sauce; there is no other way to order, it's kind of a way of life in the dog-loving community...this is the only way I know how to order. I am admittedly resistant to change, it scares me, I like things the way I like them...what can I say? I'm a little OCD at times, but why mess with a good thing?

    So fast forward through years of my limited ordering abilities to the opening of Hot Diggity. I had just moved to the neighborhood and was already having some minor anxiety about leaving my old 'hood behind, quite literally though just steps from my buildings door was a storefront with windows covered in paper and a sign saying "Hot Diggity Coming Soon". "Oh good", I thought to myself, "I'm not the only new kid on the block". Then the wheels start turning, could this be the taste of home that I have been missing in the last five years of living in the city? I waited patiently for that paper to come down, but when it did I was packing up to go away for the weekend. I wanted to be the first reviewer, I wanted so badly to stake the claim on being the first to tell the world about the magic going on in my new neighborhood. Instead, I watched from Jersey as the reviews started to flow in, my hot dog loving heart was crushed and I could just hear the Price Is Right fail-horn blasting in my head. It hurt.

    Fast forward a little over 7 months after their opening and my move to the neighborhood, and you know what doesn't hurt? The awesome business that HD is doing. It makes me happy to see a full house because it means people are enjoying things like a Seattle Grunge with its glob of cream cheese, the Southern Comfort with its abundance of cole slaw and creole mustard, the Windy City and its mouthful of fresh veggies. On the flip side full houses can make me bitter too, like the attempt I made to try the December dog-of-the-month after mustering the courage to try headcheese only to hear that they were sold out. Sell outs are great for Keith and the gang over at HD and my backup Fiesta Dog did not disappoint, but missing my first dog-of-the-month in three months was a little sad. Don't worry guys, we're still cool.

    One single dog can be a meal, but if you're going to do the HD experience right, you're going to have to grab a friend or two to help you split an order of the belgian cut fries and go to town with a couple of dipping sauces. Don't ask any questions, just do it. If you're having trouble picking between sauces, just order both and thank me later.

    Hot Diggity has become a staple in the rotation amongst friends especially on Monday nights pre-quizzo, I hope it sticks around. As for me, your change-resistant hot dog loving reviewer? Well, I've found that change isn't such a bad thing around these parts...in fact, it can be quite tasty. Maybe one of these months, we'll get a Jersey style dog on the menu, but until then I've got about 8 regular menu dogs and months worth of specials to keep me occupied.

    As a connoisseur of the art of tubular meats, Hot Diggity has been on my list of places to try, so when I found myself in a 5 mile vicinity of it, I made a bee-line for it.

    Upon entering, I noticed a sign for poutine being served this month - SCORE! Next was the difficult decision of which dog or dogs to order. Shall opt for the spicy and refreshing Saigon Dog, a cross between a Bahn Mi and hot dog, with lightly pickled cucumber, onions, jalapeno, carrots and cilatro, or the classic Texas holdem, perhaps a Mexican style Fiesta dog or stay local with the Philly Surf and Turf? Oh decisions...decisions. Luckily, my mind began to recall the delicious looking and saliva inducing images of the Saigon Dog from FoodSpotting, thereby sealing the deal.

    The dog was spectacular, nice natural casing crisped up on a griddle to give that characteristic snap and burst of flavor while the jalapeno gave it a slight kick which was immediately cooled by the pickled cucumber, carrots, onion and cilantro.

    The poutine was also spectacular. Super crisp, skin-on, hand-cut Belgian fries which were drizzled in umami inducing demi glace, topped with Rosemary and fresh cheese curds which provided a different texture and almost acted as a palate cleanser.

    Hot Diggity is the closest think Philly has to the famous Hot Doug's in Chicago, minus the Foie Gras dog, duck fries and long lines.

    My only disappointment was that I did not sample their eclectic sodas and more dogs like the Hawaiian style Big Kahuna dog or the Southern Comfort, but there is always next time.

    It's not difficult to say that I love Hot Diggity, but it is difficult to justify all the reasons why. Street dogs are one thing, but these guys make the hot dog an artwork (literally. Peep the Hawk Krall pieces for each dog.)

    I have had everything on the menu here, and it's honestly hard to decide on a favorite. Depends on my mood, really. Want spicy? Try the Saigon Fusion, which includes jalapenos, thai chili vinaigrette and Sriracha. Super hungry? Grab the Cincinnati Skyline. The chili, cheese and white onion are super hearty piled on top. The Philly Surf and Turf is a must have if you're looking for all around goodness. It's a fish cake wrapped dog with pepper hash and mustard. Solid all around.

    Your best bet here is to get two dogs the first time and skip everything else. Next time, pick a different dog and go with fries on the side. If you're a hot dog enthusiast, it will take you a little while to eat through their menu, but it's totally worth it.

    Everything about this place is designed to give you a good experience, even before you take your first bite. It's pretty easy to understand the menu based on the drawings above the counter. They make it easy to pick a craft soda, with each bottle on its own shelf presented with a short, enticing description. The tables have perfect round cutouts to put your fry cones in. You can pay with LevelUp. Honestly, the only thing I don't like about the setup of the place is that it's a long walk to the bathroom.

    Keith, the owner, is also one of the nicer dudes you could encounter during a lunch break. He'll spend some time chatting with you if that's your thing, and he definitely remembers the regulars.
