On the other hand, the waiter was very helpful and did not make us feel rushed.

    Food: We started with the hummus which had really great flavor but was drowning in olive oil. For our main dish, we both ordered the shakshouka which I really enjoyed. As a side we got the fried haloumi. I much prefer the haloumi preparation at Zahav. The cheese didn't have much flavor so we tossed it in our shakshouka (which was actually a delicious idea).

    I can't say I'll be back for brunch because I did not think it was worth the cost, but I definitely would go back and give their dinner menu a try.

    Delicious Greek cuisine.  

    Very pleasant staff and servers.  They have a good sense of humor, and are well trained and take the extra step such as clearing crumbs, making sure your water is always filled, and grabbing a bucket of ice if you have a white wine.

    I hesitate to assign 5 stars to Kanella, however.  The owner is probably a little crazy, as he has a very off-putting sign outside his kitchen stating that people who leave negative reviews are somehow in the wrong and weak, since they should confront the staff and chef himself if there is a problem.   First off, Kanella receives great reviews i.e., the 4.5 rating on Yelp, and second, not everyone rates on only how good a restaurant is at what it does but other particulars.

    For instance, the guiding light at Kanella is fresh, well cooked ingredients.  Essentially executing Greek dishes to perfection with a Cypriot twist.  That said, this is not a gourmet dining experience in the sense that you're receiving large portions, the food is about the food, and not necessarily about the artistry of inventive combinations, breaking boundaries, and the execution of the plates in terms of color, texture profiles, etc.  I'm more of a fancy pants, so I gave Avance a 5, while Kanella is a solid (and very pleasing!) 4.

    Here's what I did:

    1) Fresh-baked bread with tahini (5/5) -- Surprisingly strong start.  They bake their bread fresh every day and it's welcome to be served with tahini as opposed to the usual mess of olive oil (so 2007)

    2) Kanella Platter (4/5) -- I split this with my DP.  THis is often reviewed and is excellent for its mix of ingredients.  The tomato and fig salad was my favorite.  While the Haloumi is delicious, it's more about the Haloumi itself here, where as at Zahav its cut in smaller pieces so they're hotter and you're enjoying more of the texture.  That in a microcosm is Kanella vs Zahav

    3) Carrot Salad (4/5) -- My DP did this, and it was very good.  Very fresh, high-quality carrots in a bed of greens.  I found the dressing heavy on the oil, which isn't unexpected but it was difficult to enjoy the flavors of the greens when your mouth is coated in slime

    4) Grilled Octopus (5/5) -- My favorite course.  Two tentacles cooked PERFECTLY.  So tender and succulent.  They're served in a earthenware pot with a tomato base and some large white beans

    5) Whole Fish (4/5) -- I have the Sea Bream this particular evening.  They grill it in grape leaves, so it gives the fish a little more texture.  You know they're serious about fish here, but it's not as traditional as some Greek restaurants where you're able to inspect and pick the fish you'd like

    We skipped the desserts, although they looked fantastic.  The whole fish is a large portion, and the earlier appetizers are quite feeling.  I felt stuffed and delighted with the experience except for the insane note.

Specialties

We specialise in cooking from the homeland of chef Konstantinos Pitsillides. Pitsillides hails from the island of Cyprus, an indipendant Greek island located in the very east of the Mediterranean. The Cyprus kitchen has many influences but can perhaps best be considered as part of the Levant. At home in Cyprus, as in his Philly kitchen freshness, simplicity, flavour and love are the standard ingrediants. Chef takes his French culinary training and applies it to the traditional recipes of his homeland. Homemade desserts, head to tail cooking, lemon and olive oil are all part and parcel of this neighbourhood joint.

History

Established in 2008.

Kanella is made up of a passionate bunch of characters from the chef on down. Being a local restaurant filled with the family spirit much of the staff have been there since we opened. Though Chef Pitsillides is oft misunderstood (and yes sometimes this is exaserbated by himself), the intent of the restaurant is a simple one... We have aimed to be a neighbourhood venue for good, honest and consitstant cooking. A place that you can afford to come to regularly and have a good meal, good service, a nice bottle of wine, and enjoy your company. We have been blessed with the neighborhood we are in and all our wonderfull customers, so many of which we have known for sometime now. We look forward to continued joyful cooking and eating!
