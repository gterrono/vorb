    Appetizer/Soup: As the majority of the other reviews can confirm, the French Onion Soup was excellent. The soup came out hot, the broth was impeccable, and best of all, there was a colossal amount of cheese on top. I must note, however, there wasn't much caramelized onions in the soup I was served.

    Entree: I had the Rabbit Papperdelle, which went with pasta and Pioppini mushrooms. The rabbit meat, moist and tasty, was plenty in the dish, and the small Pioppini mushrooms was succulent, but my fiance's Steak Frites blew my rabbit out the forest. The steak was tender and juicy with a seasoned butter and gratifying fries. He thought the garlic in the steak was a bit overwhelming, but I thought it was flavored with garlic just right.

    Dessert: The $9 Milk Chocolate Pot de Creme is basically a chocolate mousse with whip cream and chocolate wafer balls on top. I'd recommend this if you want a light and basic dessert. Unfortunately, I didn't find it to be anything special in terms of a dessert.  

    Service: The service was prompt. I wouldn't say our waitress was overly friendly, but she did her job accordingly by taking our order correctly and making sure our glasses were filled.

    Environment: This restaurant has a pleasant Parisian-cafe feel, especially with the interior decor and dim lights. It's also very large and compartmentalized nicely.  

    My fiance made a dinner reservation at this restaurant for a Sunday night, and there was definitely a crowd. So, unless you want to attempt to find a spot at the bar and/or wait awhile to be seated, definitely make a reservation.

    Overall, this place is worth trying. The dinner menu has a lot of offer, and I cannot wait to try the mussels and dip their pommes frites in its white wine-based sauce!

    My absolute favorite restaurant in the city. I have never had a bad experience (and I've been here about 30 times, give or take). The service is always perfect, accommodating and polite.

    My favorite items on the menu are: steak tar tar, warm shrimp salad, and the baguette provencal. The lamb shank is also to die for. However you really cannot go wrong with anything here.

    On one occasion a group of four of us ordered the petite plateau (a compilation of raw oysters, clams, mussels, jumbo lump crab meat and king crab legs) and it was just absolutely delicious. Amazing. Superb. Order it!

    If you're not into the fare (which I'm not quite sure how that's possible) then go just for the ambience and order a drink at the bar. Awesome cocktail menu (though pricey, as expected). Adequate beer list, most of the good stuff is offered in bottles and the more common stuff is on tap.

    Sara had the onion soup, I've heard a lot of people say they are not impressed with the soup. The cheese is not perfectly bubbly and crisp. it is certainly oozy! She said it was ok, but not so hot.

    Our waiter was perfectly content with the fact we were just having snacks. He even brought us a basket of bread! Their butter is lovely. The bread with the walnuts and cranberry is delicious. Their breads are great. you can buy baguettes at the door! The brunch breakfast menu looks really great. Will have to do stop back in!

    FANTASTIC.  Will be number one on my list of places to return to on my next Philadelphia trip.

    Came on a perfect day for brunch. This place is beautiful, sunny, and huge! Had a reservation for 11:30am, was seated early at 11:15am.

    First order of business for any brunch:   Mimosas. Parc offers them Tableside and if you have enough people to split a bottle of champagne I recommend it.  They brought a carafe of orange juice, and came buy and made them on the spot.

    The first thing you need to order from Parc for brunch is the bread basket. $10, offers a healthy portion of muffins, scones, and a delicious apple square.  Split everything up and share it all. Excellent.  If you don't get enough carbs, they will then bring another bread basket too of regular bread.

    We also ordered a cheese plate of three cheese, and they were excellent. Each came with nuts and various honey to pair with them. $15, and delicious.

    For my main I had the steak and eggs $19 and they were great. Eggs came fried on a piece of bread with cheese and a sauce, just the way I like them. Also a side of potatoes. There was very little left at the end.

    Overall, it was an excellent experience. The staff is a bit pretentious, but they are also prompt and responsive so I didn't mind.  In the end I loved this space, location right off Rittenhouse Sq, and most importantly the food!

Specialties

This stylish addition to Rittenhouse Square pays tribute to French cafe culture, serving breakfast, lunch and dinner seven days a week. Ideal for a quick bite or a leisurely meal, the menu is filled with classics: onion soup, escargots, steak frites and towering plateaus de fruits de mer. The baguettes are made in-house and  the wine list is expansive. Sip a Kir Royale at the zinc bar or enjoy a cafe au lait while basking in the sun.
