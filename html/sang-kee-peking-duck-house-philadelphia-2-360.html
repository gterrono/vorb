With our recent experience, there is no reason to go back there anymore.

        We wanted something different in Philadelphia and decided to hit Chinatown.  If you've never been to Philly's Chinatown (I hadn't), it's really nice. Tons of options.  Fun to walk around.  I've been to Chinatowns everywhere (San Francisco, Oakland CA, Victoria BC, Chicago, NYC, DC (DC's sucks), Honolulu... and even Hong Kong and Kowloon), but somehow missed Philadelphia's until this past weekend.  I recommend it if you're in town. We were looking for Peking Duck and this restaurant seemed the most recommended.  It was boisterous and busy... 3 floors... we waited for about 15 minutes for a table.  But the service was friendly once we were seated.  My son wanted dim sum, so he ordered veggie/pork steamed buns (that came out hot and fresh).  He gave them a thumbs up. We also got "Golden Shrimp with honey coated walnuts" (yummy if you like this dish... it has a mayonnaise sauce).  I wasn't crazy about the fried shrimp  here. I like a lightly battered fried shrimp over this cheaper-seeming crunchy fried shrimp.  But it was nice enough and the rice was perfect.  We also got 1/2 a Peking Duck.  The flavor was incredible.  Truly delicious.  And it was served cold/luke-warm... which I find to be fairly typical in many Chinese restaurants... but my son was disappointed.  He prefers it hot and fresh like at Peking Gourmet Inn in Seven Corners, Virginia (near DC). Still, the flavor of the duck was truly awesome.  The pancakes were a bore... I prefer homemade, smaller ones.  But overall, we loved the experience.  I'd probably try a noodle place in Philly's Chinatown next time, but for Peking Duck, this was great.

        This is our favorite place for Peking Duck in Philadelphia.  Sang Kee never disappoints with crispy duck skin, juicy meat, delicious sauce and scallions.  They use the tortilla type pancakes, so if you are looking for more of the steamed bun wrapper, you will not find it here.  We took a big group and got a whole duck - mmmm!
        We also got a ton of other tasty dishes - fish ball noodle soup (savory and delicious!), shanghai rib special, spicy flounder and tofu (really spicy - yes!), mushroom, pork and crispy tofu (really great fried tofu, but eat it quick, as the sauce on top will make the fried crispiness less so), a second dish with the remaining duck of green beans and duck (my favorite way to enjoy green beans I think), and a magnificent walnut shrimp  with walnuts bigger than I've ever seen!  We also got chicken fried rice, which could have used a little more time in the wok and was my least favorite, but everything else was spot on!
        They serve a good selection of beer, as some places in Chinatown don't serve alcohol.  And I guess since everyone is comparing, yes, I've had Peking Duck in Beijing many times, but if you're in Philadelphia, this is the place to go.  Yum!

      Specialties

      Welcome to Sang Kee Peking Duck House located in Chinatown.  We offer dining on three (3) floors.  Sang Kee serves Hong Kong cuisine in a family style setting.  Our speciality is Peking duck, dumplings, noodles, and a wide variety of other traditional Hong Kong style dishes for your enjoyment.  We've been voted "Best" by countless making us a success since 1980.  Make sure to consider our prix fixe menus, our banquet menus, and our catering menus.

      History

      Established in 1980.

      Sang Kee Peking Duck House offers a taste of traditional and authentic Chinese dishes to customers. With a 30 year history, many have voted Sang Kee Peking Duck House in Chinatown - the best in Philadelphia. Our approach includes the use of high quality ingredients and freshness. We designed our menu specially for you that has been time tested to be the best at a reasonable price. When dining with your family and friends, you can taste the experienced authentic Asian cuisine style cooking techniques. Our interior decoration is simple and clean. We have the perfect table for your party whether its 2 or 100+. Our place is perfect for gatherings where you can pass around the dishes of exotic Asian foods over any occasion or casual dining. Sang Kee Peking Duck House in Chinatown has become an institution in the community and the Philadelphia area for good food.

      Meet the Business Owner

      Michael C. Business Owner

      Executive Chef Michael Chow started Sang Kee in 1980 specializing in Peking style duck and Hong Kong cuisine in a modest 20-seat rented facility before moving to the current location on 9th Street. Chef Michael, originally from Hong Kong, made his transition to the Food industry in the 1970s. He studied the intricate method of making Peking Duck, a method passed through apprendiceship, which he has now perfected. Peking Duck is now reknown as a culinary masterpiece worldwide originally from China. Chef Michael is a celebrated Chef in the Philadelphia area. His current collection of restaurants continues to grow beyond Philadelphia's Chinatown.
