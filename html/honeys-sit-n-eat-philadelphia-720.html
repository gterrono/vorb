Thank the Challah bread  french toast that this place has a selection that can quell even the most delicate of breakfast food connoisseurs.

    So I've been to Honey's twice in the past week... My first experience there was so amazing that I couldn't wait to go back - so I didn't. If I had written my review after my first time there, I would have given it a definite 5 stars, but after my second time there, I started to notice some things that gave me doubts.  Don't get me wrong, I still love this place and will definitely be going back, but I may be checking out some other infamous breakfast/brunch spots in Philly before then.  Anyway...

    So the first time I went to Honey's I got the Honey Cristo: smoked ham and swiss cheese sandwiched between two pieces of challah french toast.  And to top it off are two sunny side eggs (I asked for over easy, and they adjusted my order).  It was a lot of food, but oh my god it was delicious.  I usually don't like meals like bacon egg and cheese sandwiches - I find the flavors too overwhelming, but this was just perfect.  The runny yolk gave the sandwich some moisture to aid in the digestion of all that dense goodness! And the mixture of ham and cheese and french toast was the perfect combination of savory and sweet.  The salad with lemon and salt dressing on the side was refreshing as well, especially when you want to combat all that heavy food with something light.  It was a huge meal, but I definitely will be having it again (probably splitting it with someone next time).

    The second time I went, I really wanted a biscuit (I had tried my friend's before and it was delicious), but I also wanted something else with my meal.  Although I wanted to try to the biscuit sandwich sandwich, I opted out this time and got the Breakfast Bomb, which is described as a giant buttermilk pancake stuffed with scrambled eggs and bacon.  I was intrigued by the logistics of a stuffed pancake so I ordered it with the intention of getting a biscuit on the side.  The meals labeled with an asterisk come with a side of a variety of things (or so the menu says) such a latkes, grits, OR BISCUITS, but apparently I already had my share of carbs with the pancake, so I wasn't allowed get a biscuit on the side.  But I could order a latke. Aren't latkes made with potatoes??? I really didn't understand why I couldn't get a biscuit as my side especially when the Breakfast Bomb is one of the more expensive items on the menu (sorry about my bitterness for lack of biscuit).

    I also tried to modify my pancake by getting over easy eggs on the side (I really like over easy eggs) instead of scrambled, and only having the pancake stuffed with bacon.  This too became difficult because the meal is made with bacon bits and so it would essentially become me ordering a pancake with eggs and bacon on the side...  Once I got the meal, it was literally just a "bacon bits omelette" that had a pancake folded around it. So much for the stuffed.  And I got a latke on the side that was ok... not the best I've had.

    So basically, I think Honey's is great when you want an exact item on the menu, but if you want to modify it at all, you're either gonna have a difficult time doing so or pay the (unnecessary) price.

    I can't stop thinking about the huevos rancheros at Honey's!

    I came here with a few friends in the afternoon after shopping at the Brooklyn Flea Market. We were seated instantly (since we arrived at an awkward hour). I didn't think the menu appeared to be that inventive compared to some of the other brunch places in Philly, perhaps because I'm more partial to two kinds of brunch options--sweet and fruity or Mexican--and the menu seemed sparse on both. Though I guess they're not trying to be or selling themselves as inventive. Most of the options were pretty standard fare--my friends ordered a biscuit and eggs, an omelette, and a pancake wrapped around eggs and bacon. I decided on huevos rancheros but was unpleasantly surprised when I learned that it did not come with guacamole and in fact that adding guacamole would cost $4. Even more ridiculous than Chipotle. I was briefly indignant about this injustice, but my displeasure was short-lived once my entree arrived.

    The huevos rancheros had nice and crispy tortilla chips in the center, topped with black beans, rice, shredded pepper jack cheese, a fried egg, and a large roasted red pepper on top. Surrounding this centerpiece were small piles of jalapeno peppers, sour cream, and a salad of julienned lettuce, tomatos, and onions. All the ingredients tasted fresh and light, and the runny yolk really tied the whole dish together and made me forget about the lack of guacamole (cost-effective strategy on their part, huh?) The dish was a nice portion size and felt light and refreshingly healthy, which is hard to say about most huevos rancheros dishes I've had. Loved the combinations of textures. Will definitely be back to order this again sometime very, very soon.
