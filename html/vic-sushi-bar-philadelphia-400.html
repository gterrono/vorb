    This place deserves a serious update from me-3 to 4 stars is easy for me this time.

    Since my original review, this place has quickly become my favorite, go to eat in, delivery, and to go sushi spot. This has only been supplemented by the fact that I've since sampled the other delivery sushi places in the area. Sumo, tsuki, makiman, etc. This place is definitively better than the rest of them.

Turnover is fast, so even if you are waiting with like a ton of other people for a spot, you shouldn't be waiting that long. Also, since its BYO, you can just finish a beer or two while you're waiting and it'll all be ok.

    Listed in Philly Eats

    Awesome place for a relaxed, low-key, delicious meal with groups of 2 to 4. You could even come here by yourself and eat comfortably as the seating is around the sushi bar.

    The sushi here is extremely well-priced and very fresh and tasty. I also really enjoyed Vic's tuna and avocado dumpling appetizer (2 dumplings to an order - though they are humongous!). These dumplings don't have any dough wrapping around them; instead it is avocado wrapped in raw tuna, and shaped somewhat like a xiao long bao (soup dumpling). Absolutely delicious with the sauce that they add to the dish.

    Once they hand you your sushi, the tea and water is self-served through a hot water dispenser, which I actually prefer because I didn't have to wait for someone to come around to re-fill my cup! Definitely an informal place that won't wait on you hand and foot, but you will get excellent sushi for a great value here (under $20 per person for four rolls, 4 salads, and 2 orders of tuna avocado dumplings)!

    They don't take reservations and have seating probably for no more than 10-12 people at a time.

    Really good and cheap sushi.  It's extremely difficult to find this quality of sushi at this price anywhere in the Northeast, so I would highly recommend people eat at this restaurant if they live in or visit Philadelphia.  I recommend getting the tuna tartar or the gyoza for an appetizer, and experimenting with the $11 10-roll special for the main.  The stand-alone rolls are pretty good, but none are really worth the extra price.  Out of the rolls available within the 10-roll special, salmon avocado and spicy tuna are by far the best, although the other ones are pretty good too.  My most favorite aspect of the sushi here is the size - American sushi restaurants tend to stuff too much rice and large chunks of fish into each roll, which ruins the delicacy of the sushi meal.  The chefs at Vic do an excellent job of serving small, well-made pieces of sushi that don't just resemble a cut-up seafood taco.

    The restaurant space is VERY limited - it only fits around 7-8 people at the counter and 4 people in a table.  During the week, the restaurant is fairly empty around lunch and dinner time, but it tends to get pretty crowded on weekend nights so make sure to get a spot early (before 7ish) if you can.  This is a great place to get take-out, even during the weekends.  They usually have your meal ready in 10-15 minutes.
