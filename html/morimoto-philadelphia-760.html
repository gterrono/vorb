    A four year anniversary deserves a special romantic anniversary dinner IMO. So in Iron Chef fashion, I hit the Yelp talk boards and asked fellow Yelpers to compare Morimoto and Buddakan. I got some funny responses (like "Morimoto all day erryday")  in the "battle" but  seriously Morimoto was the clear winner. So...we made a reservation for Sunday (last night) at 5pm. The restaurant was nice and slow and our waiter was extremely attentive. We were seated in one of the trendy little booths and loved the contemporary decor. The waiter's initial introduction reminded me of the ones I'd give during presentation intros in my grad school classes. He took his time to explain the menu and questioned us about dietary restrictions and our tolerance for raw vs. cooked items. The restaurant acknowledged our anniversary in a few nice ways. We received a small lobster tasting on the house prior to our appetizer dishes (rock shrimp tempura and sushi). Man oh man - all of the appetizers were slap yo' mama good. My boyfriend and I ordered the oolong and lychee cocktails and got the Morimoto beer to go. All were very good. He ordered the seafood tobin yaki and I had the surf and turf. Now my surf was a bit of an experiment for me: sea urchin. Yeah I know, you're like "what? you tried sea urchin" but I did and it was pretty good. For dessert I ordered the brownie and ice cream and greedily finished it. He ordered the sorbet trio. We learned that all of the ice cream was made in house and when they brought our plates out they had written "happy anniversary " in chocolate and put candles on them. If you think that's corny you probably don't like kittens either. Touched my heart :)  5/5 for Morimoto; One of the best meals we've ever had. Thanks again to my fellow Yelpers for the recommend; sharing is caring and you guys never dissappoint.

    Listed in The DMV Yelp 100 Challenge, My Favorite Places Where I've Stuffed My Face, Philadelphia...Ring My Bell!

    For almost as long as we've been dating, the girlfriend had been raving about Morimoto.  I finally got my chance to see what all the raving was about as we had dinner reservations for an early Sunday evening.  We arrived at the restaurant at our appointed time.  Morimoto is a striking looking place with its wood floors, undulating wood ceiling, and ultra-modern furnishings.  We asked to be seated at the sushi bar in order to have front row seats to see our food being prepared to which the hostess graciously agreed and led us to our seats.  The sushi bar in the back of the room is in stark contrast to the rest of the place in its simplicity.  For what it's worth, the sushi chefs were actually Japanese and speaking their native language as they worked behind the bar.  I don't know if that made them any better at their craft, but after watching "Jiro Dreams of Sushi", I figured it didn't hurt.

    We opted for the omakase meal.  To accompany our meal, we wanted a bottle of sake and after looking over the short, but quality, list, we opted for the Matsukura Dewatsuru Tokubetsu Junmai.  It was dry, crisp, with marshmallow notes.  Pictures being worth a thousand words, here's what we had...

    First Course:  Toro Tartare with fresh wasabi and osetra caviar. yelp.com/biz_photos/mori…

    Second Course:  Kumamoto Oysters with Japanese orange salsa, citrus cilantro ceviche & Thai fish sauce with jalapeno. yelp.com/biz_photos/mori…

    Third Course:  Scallop Carpaccio with sea urchin, garlic, ginger, mitsuba & chives, dressed with yuzu citrus soy. yelp.com/biz_photos/mori…

    Fourth Course:  Chef's Selection of Sashimi. yelp.com/biz_photos/mori…

    Intermezzo...Sour cherry-blossom soda

    Fifth Course:  Lobster epice:  Chef Morimoto's Eight Spice Lobster with citrus crème fraiche and fresh chives. yelp.com/biz_photos/mori…

    Sixth Course:  Japanese Wagyu seared a la plancha with a frisee salad and Asian pear. yelp.com/biz_photos/mori…

    Seventh Course:  Chef's Selection of Sushi. yelp.com/biz_photos/mori…

    Dessert:  Cherry-Blossom Cheesecake with a green tea-graham cracker crumble. yelp.com/biz_photos/mori…

    We closed out our meal with a pair of cocktails.  I had the Kabocha Bourbon and it seemed to be their twist on a Bourbon Swizzle using kabocha squash puree.  It was a nice drink.  The girlfriend's Karai was a silver tequila-based drink make with Calpico, grapefruit juice, and jalapeno syrup.  It, too, was a good drink, but I liked mine better.  yelp.com/biz_photos/mori…

    The omakase meal was very pricey, but it was certainly the way to go to get the full Morimoto experience.  From the quality of ingredients (i.e. real, fresh ground wasabi, A5 wagyu), the freshness of the fish, the flawless execution, and the attention to detail in its beautifully refined, but not overdone presentation, and impeccable service, I felt like a judge on Iron Chef America.  Did Morimoto live up to my girlfriend's hype?  In my mind, that would be a big "HELL, YEAH!!!"

    Came here for my mom's birthday with the family.  This place doesn't look like much from the outside but once you step in, you feel like your in the future forgetting that you just left the parking lot from outside and the bright sun.  We were greeted and seated right away cause we came in before the dinner crowd.  When you sit down and look around, you see how beautiful the place is from the walls to the seats against the wall that look like you are having dinner in a movie.

    Our waiter was really friendly and nice.  She did not mind that we were a traditional Asian family that likes to share all of the dishes and gave each of us small plates and bowls.  There was a special going on, $20 for 4 tiny strips of Kobe Beef.  They bring it out with a hot stone and you can grill it yourself.  It was literally the softest beef I had in my life.  You can cut that beef like paper with a plastic knife.  Great experience but prob wouldn't drop $20 again for a measly amount of food.  For appetizer, we shared the 10 Hour Pork Kakuni.  I never had better congee in my life.  The sauce mixed in well with the congee and the pork was so tender.  Just wished their was another piece in it.  For entrees, we got Duck Duck Duck, Wild Striped Bass, Lobster epice, New York Strip Steak and Ishi Yaki Bop with the Yellowtail.  They were all good but some dishes needed more on the plate.  The Bass just came with a a piece of fish.  At least it could come with some vegetables or carbs as well as the lobster.  The steak and lobster weren't anything special and I recommend spending your money on dishes more adventurous.  For desert we shared the Matcha Tres Leches.  Thanks to the waiter for putting a candle on it for my mom's birthday.

    Do yourself a favor and make reservations. My party and I made reservations but still had to wait a bit before being seated. Fortunately, the decor is super cool and "swanky" so we didn't mind rolling out to the bar for a few drinks before dinner. They have the usual beers and wines but they also have SPECIALTY DRINKS. I had a lemongrass gin and really enjoyed it. They infuse the alcohol themselves and the lemongrass smooths out the gin. Some biddy I was there with got the Yuzu Julep, it was also great. It is basically a mint julep with a nice citrus kick (thanks to the yuzu).

    RAW BAR!!! They have Kumamto oysters (big and beautiful), market oysters, and king crab (fresh and succulent). They can be served with either Japanese salsa, cerviche, or Thai fish sauce.

    My gluttonous friends and I ordered the Kumamto oysters (with the Thai fish sauce) and the king crab (with cerviche). The Thai fish sauce smelled less fishy than the store bought brands and had more mellow taste. It was delicious and complemented the oysters perfectly. After we finished the oysters, I spooned up the sauce at the bottom of the plate and then proceeded (to the disgust of my party) to use my finger to get the last drops. Nom nom nom. The slight tang of the acidic cerviche complemented the crab very well. The crab was hearty and fresh and I was sad when it was done.

    My table shared a bunch of sushi rolls. The highlights were the spicy tuna, spicy salmon, and yellowtail-scallion. The yellowtail-scallion was my favorite. The scallions were thinly sliced and fresh and the yellowtail meat was a perfect pale pink. The tuna was really dark and wasn't mashed up with spicy mayo like it usually is. The fish was cut into thick chunks and so retained its enjoyable color and texture. The spicy salmon was similarly prepared.

    I ordered the Seafood Toban Yaki. I highly recommend it. It's a sort of Asian cioppino and served in a wide, shallow clay pot. It contains lobster, king crab, huge prawns, and scallops in a rich miso sake broth.

    The seafood here is a work of art. It looks amazing and tastes just as good. The wait staff is super attentive. It's a bit expensive but you get what you pay for.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
