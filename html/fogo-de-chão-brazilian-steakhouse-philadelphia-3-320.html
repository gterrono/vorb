        Fogo de Chao is more than just a good meal, it is an experience all in its own. At any given moment you have 5 ninjas (servers) flying across your table with huge spears of meat and sharp knives. I love it because it is so different than your stiffy steak houses where they exert extra effort on being "classy". Fogo is fun and a great place to go as a group.

        Thank the Brazilian gods I finally found a place that honors a reservation on time. Our reservation for a party of 4 was at 8:30 and we were sat at 8:30 promptly. Almost unfortunate because they have a nice little bar right when you walk in with amazing Brazilian mojitos and caipirinhas.

        Our server came by to explain the concept and take drink orders then it was all on. I don't think we saw our server again because they work as a team. This is a pro  - you don't have to wait for your server whose name you already forgot to come back to ask for anything. The salad bar was good. I did not overload on vegetables and other fresh items since I knew I wanted to focus on the 15 different meats. They had everything you could want though as a starter.

        Finally, after letting my party finish the salad round I flipped my card to GREEN and said bring on the meat. Now, this is the 3rd time I have had Fogo and will admit it was better in the past, but the meat was still delicious. The fillet and bottom roast in particular had a ton of flavor and melted in your mouth. Our lamb was a little tough and I had one cut of rib eye that was still mooing. We sat a little  bit later so I am wondering if you're there during peak times if the meat is better. You don't have to worry about your meat getting cold with the servers whisking around you; many of the cuts still sizzled on my plate. I think I tried 8-10 of the meats (I skipped all chicken).

        Sides - fried plantains, mashed potatoes, palenta - don't waste your time. they were all average at best. You don't go to Fogo for the sides though, you go for the meat.

        Desert - creme brulee- awesome. nuff said.

        Overall it is only 4 stars. $51.50 a head is expensive considering the quality of the sides. The meat is delicious and I think if they lowered the price point, they would get more regular and return customers.

        Tip: Don't bring a date here. You are going to eat a lot to get your money's worth and that is never sexy.

        The only churrascaria I had ever visited was in Kenya and I had my fair share of delicious exotic game meats.  Though Fogo de Chao did not have zebra or crocodile, they had an excellent mix of steaks, chicken, sausage, and lamb.  

        We made reservations a few days before without any trouble. The service was stellar from the time we valeted our car until the time we walked out.  The venue itself is exceptionally beautiful with high ceilings and great lighting.  

        As mentioned in previous reviews, they have an extensive fresh salad bar.  My 8 year old nephew particularly enjoyed the huge bowl of bacon.  It can be a bit overwhelming as evidenced by the look of fear and confusion on my mom's face as the servers whizzed by offering various meats. You also receive sides of mash potatoes, fried polenta, pao de queijo (fried cheese balls) and fried bananas (not plantains). I could have lived without them.  In fact I did.  I ate meat, and lots of it.  To the point that I felt dizzy.  My 4 year old nephew, who has the metabolism of a hummingbird, fell asleep and plopped his head down on the table by the end of dinner.  

        It's a bit  formal but you can certainly pull off wearing nice jeans and a shirt.  You can save your tux for another day.  It was my dad's birthday and they decorated and brought out his cake with a candle. It's a bit steep but kids over 10 eat half off and kids under 5 eat for free. Plus, it's well worth the experience.

        I went to Fogo with my family on the evening I graduated college, and I'll never forget how ridiculously amazing it was.  It's the first churrascaria style steakhouse I ever went to, and now whenever I pass one on the road, I can't help but salivate.  A lot.

        At the risk of making myself overly hungry while I write this, I'm going to try to keep the flavor-describing adjectives to a strict minimum.  The quality of the meat is almost unfathomable.  How the waiters can continuously walk around with their giant skewers of meat, table to table, back and forth from the kitchen, and maintain the rich, juicy, tender - agh, okay, I can't do it.  Seriously, as a proud carnivore, I can confidently say that I have not eaten any better beef than I have here.  That includes everything from the zesty kick of the pepper steak, to the classic bliss of the filet mignon, to the succulent simplicity of the sirloin, etc.  There are so many different kinds of meat, including chicken, pork, and even lamb.  All of which are unbelievable.

        The salad bar is a great supplement to what inevitably will be a very filling meal.  Lush greens, steaming rice, moist potatoes - there's no end to the pleasurable tastes inside this place.  A veritable treasure trove of food porn, if you will.  I'm not even sorry - it's true.

        The walls are stacked with wine bottoms, creating this chic, smart atmosphere in which you can completely breathe easy and use your mouth for a more pressing purpose... Okay, seriously, the porn comment was ages ago.  Simmer down.

        It's a little expensive, but considering what you're paying for, it's not absurd.  If you are an avid eater of meats - all shapes, sizes, textures and species - you need to try this place.  I've been to a slew of Brazilian steakhouses like this one since, but Fogo stands high above the rest.

        Have been here three times, and thankfully, never on my bill. I love the food, but not sure if I could justify to pay for myself.

        Fogo is more than a restaurant, it's an experience. They go all out with the presentation and the service. It's quite a show to watch the steak servers wielding their skewers of delicious, succulent meat. Love the red/green service flip card, indicating when you are eating and when you are resting. Their side dishes are a great accompaniment, helping get rid of that lingering iron taste in your mouth from meat overdose. Their cheesy bread is also out-of-this-world, would kill to learn the secret behind its puffiness.

        The meat itself is awesome, although I wouldn't say every cut is my favorite. If you love variety, there are over 12 different types of meat to choose from: steak, chicken, sausage, lamb seasoned in multiple ways. My favorite cuts are the bacon-wrapped filet mignon and the top sirloin. Both are oh-so tender, and juicy. I'm also a big fan of rarer meats and I have found these cuts to have been the most towards my liking.

        Their salad bar is also quite the sight, with so many options to cut through the greasy meat. I love alternating between steak and mozzarella balls, asparagus stalks, some smoked salmon, and string beans. But as other reviewers have mentioned, beware of over-stuffing on greens!

        Overall, a unique experience I would suggest anyone to partake in. But maybe you should try and get someone to pay for you. Or go for lunch.

        Pros: experience, lots of variety, unique, beautiful interior, great service
        Cons: price

        Dishes to try: top sirloin, filet mignon, chicken (it's quite tender)
        Dishes to skip: pork loin, sausage, iceberg lettuce

        Listed in Mmmmmm...

        I went to Fogo for my bf's birthday last week, and it was very, very good! We got there 15 minutes after our reservation time (oops), but they seated us right away (it was a Thursday night and not horribly busy.) The servers came over right away to explain how it works. Basically, you have a small disc that is red on one side and green on the other. When you are ready to eat, you flip it to green. If you need a break, you flip it to red, and they won't offer you anything. There are chefs circling the tables, each with a different type of meat on a giant skewer. If you want that meat, they'll cut off a piece for you. You can do this as many times as you want. It's almost like dim sum, for those familiar with that type of dining.

        I love that the food service began immediately, because everything was pretty much already cooked. They brought out the sides, which were polenta (just eh, but I'm not really into that), mashed potatoes (ok, but nothing special), and fried bananas (sweet mother of fried fruits, that was GREAT.) Just based on the sides, my review wouldn't have been that stellar, but then the meats started going around...

        We started with the Parmesan-crusted pork, which was very salty but really good. I got that one a couple more times later on. I also really liked the bacon-wrapped filet Mignon. Boyfriend was practically hunting down the chef with the Picanha, which is a top sirloin and the house specialty. I didn't like that one as much, but he was getting a little obsessive about it. Everything else was good but not incredibly memorable.

        I should also mention that I ordered 2 Caipirinhas during the dinner service. It's the national drink of Brazil. I wasn't crazy about the first one--it was just the original recipe and kind of strong in an acidic way. The second one was the Passionfruit version, and that was delicious. They were both very strong, alcohol-wise, which is how I like my drinks!! They were also pretty darn expensive, but that's to be expected, I guess.

        Finally, dessert. We got the chocolate molten lava cake with ice cream. There's really no way you can go wrong with that, and it was delicious. Just make sure to save room for dessert after all that meat.

        One thing--it's expensive (over $150 just for bf and me), but it'd be pretty hard not to expect that going into it. The only thing is that there really isn't a menu, so you can't keep track of how everything adds up. Overall, though, we had a really great experience and will probably be back. I also got my dad (another steak-lover) a gift certificate there for Father' Day.

        Fogo is a great place to eat, especially if you're not the one paying.

        My brother graduated from Drexel this past Friday, so my mom took us all out for dinner at Fogo afterward. The service was exceptional, the atmosphere was classy, and the food was pretty spectacular.

        First of all, we started with the salad bar. "Saladwise", it was very generic. I stayed away from that so as not to fill myself too much. My sister and I then wandered over tot he other end of the salad bar, where there was a pretty nice selection of meats and cheeses, including a very large wheel of some very nice parmigiano reggiano. So, instead of salads we created our own little charcuterie plate to start off our meal.

        I also ordered a caipirinha, their signature drink, and it was pretty awesome. It was very much like a margarita, but a little sweeter and lighter in flavor. I enjoyed it very much.

        Now, on to the meat. First of all, as you probably already know, you are given a card with one side green and one side red. You get where I'm going with this. Anyway, the gauchos come around to your table to bring you all different cuts of meat. What I really liked about this experience compared to some of my other Brazilian steakhouse experiences was that they were able to describe in detail which cut you were receiving, as well as choose your temperature. It worked out great for me because I like mostly rare meats, of which there was no shortage. My personal favorites were the Picanha cut (their house cut) and the smoked sausage. The sausage had a ton of flavor and the picanha had a lovely layer of fat that tasted like meaty butter. It was the highlight of the meal- so much so that I want to find a butcher that sells that cut of meat so I can make it at home!! The only thing I didn't care for was the parmesan crusted pork. It was way overcooked for me and the cheese tasted like it came out of one of those green plastic shakers from the grocery store.

        I had flan for dessert, which was really excellent. It was as good as any of the best flan I've had, and was accompanied but a very light homemade whipped cream. I usually can't stand whipped cream, but this one I enjoyed very much.

        For 5 of us, the check totaled over $400. I picked up the drinks and my mother paid for the food. I don't know if I would go back if I had to pay for myself. I'll stick with Picanha on Castor ave for a fraction of the price.

      Specialties

      We are an authentic Churrascaria (Brazilian Steakhouse) from Rio Grande do Sul in southern Brazil which features superior service, unrivaled cuisine and a unique dining experience.

      You will be pampered by our authentic Gaucho chefs who come to your tableside to carve a variety of succulent meats right off their skewers.  Our featured meats have been slow-roasted over an open flame and lightly seasoned to preserve the unique taste of each tender cut.

      Adding to our unique dining experience, you will be treated to a sumptuous, gourmet salad and vegetable bar featuring over 30 items including imported cheeses, cured meats, fresh vegetables and more.  We complement your meal with traditional Brazilian side dishes such as caramelized bananas, crispy polenta, seasoned mashed potatoes and our world-famous cheese bread (pao de queijo).

      No meal is complete without trying a refreshing caipirinha, something from our award-winning wine list or one of our decadent desserts.
