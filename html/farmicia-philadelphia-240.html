    Famished after a long touristy day, we stumbled upon this place during off peak meal hours on a Saturday. Only the bar menu was offered, but it provided more than enough options for even the most hungry of patrons, like ourselves.

    Farmicia gets four stars for each delicious, half price drink I had during their brunch happy hour.

    That's right--BRUNCH HAPPY HOUR.  How could this place earn fewer than four stars?  The food wasn't the best thing I've ever put in my mouth, but everything was delicious.  It's common for restaurants to mess up little things, like putting some poor quality lox on the eggs, or serving sub-par fruit in the fruit salad.  Not the case at Farmicia.  My eggs were perfectly runny, the tomatoes actually tasted like tomatoes, and that applewood smoked bacon was the bomb!  After four cocktails I started feeling a bit nauseous, but I would gladly have thrown up this meal to taste it again.

    Speaking of cocktails, let's discuss getting brunch drunk.  We came to the restaurant with a dream of being wasted by 3 pm, and our dream came true at Farmicia.  Although my white peach sangria was woefully devoid of fruit chunks, it actually tasted incredibly fruity, and got me tipsy on an empty stomach.  The Pimm's cup, with chunks of cucumber and apple, was crisp and refreshing--a great primer for the two martinis* I washed it down with.  Speaking of (fake) martinis, thanks for making them so strong, Farmicia bartender!  It was as though you could sense that I was still vaguely sober and wanted to do everything you could to fix the situation.

    We ordered enough food and drinks to keep a shocked and disgusted expression on our waiter's face the entire time, and our bill was still under $100 (3 people, 4 entrees, sides, and a million drinks)--I'll definitely be back for brunch at Farmicia.

    *Fruity drinks with lots of alcohol served in martini glasses.

    NYer in Philly - wandered into farmicia, curious about promising fresh/farm to table hints in the name and curbside menu.  Lots of stuff i think I'll enjoy.

    In I go!  Solo, so I head for the bar.  Interesting and varied wine list, but I play it safe with an argentine malbec.  A dozen things I'd like to try on the menu, so...appetizer-tapas time!  Gazpacho, Vietnamese crispy squid, and trio of cheeses.  Eclectic you say; yes but all would work well with my silky malbec.

    Decor is a comfortable crafty mishmosh.  Service is impeccable, both from behind the bar and the waitstaff.  I should repeat the service was impeccable.

    So imagine my surprise.  Gazpacho watery with chucks of sweet peppers as the only texture/mouthfeel.  Not untasty but reminiscent of the juice at the bottom of a can of pomodori pelati.

    Great sour dough whole wheat bread.  Fresh, perfect crust even in the pre-hurricane 300% humidity.

    "Vietnamese" crispy fried squid.  Well (light, dry, good tooth) fried frozen calamari.  Ok they were good calamari rings, but let's explore the Vietnamese part.   Whatever I was expecting, (sriracha? sambal?) these things were served with a barely disguised ketchup.

    The cheese plate was fine, too much thinly sliced yesterday's baguette "crostini" and a huge mound of indeterminately sweet chutney, but the smoked "fresh" goat cheese was one of the best and most distinctive things I've tasted this week.

    Did I mention the service was impeccable?  It really was.  Right there, helpful, and then out of the way.  No pretense.  A big credit to whomever is hiring there.

    A little more effort on delivering the promise of the menu and this is a great place!
