    Waited on line for about 25 minutes. Arrived at about 4:30pm on a chilly saturday. Only a few minutes of my wait were spent outside. I ordered a cheesesteak with provolone and peppers. The service was friendly and the guys behind the counter clearly have a rapport. One cracked an extremely funny and innapropriate joke about encouraged by my husband's choice of raw onions as a condiment. So, the service was great. After paying for the cheesesteaks, and 2 waters, (about $20) we went upstairs and were able to sit down to eat. The indoor seating was actuallly the reason I opted for this establishment vs. Tony Luke's or Pat's.

    On the food:
    The meat was lukewarm. Surprising, because it went directly from the grill, into the bread and was eaten just 1 or 2 minutes afterward. The provolone, 2 round slices that were placed underneath the meat, was bland and only half-melted. The peppers were good. The sandwich as a whole was underwhelming and dry. Maybe I should have opted for the wiz? I would not return.

    I should probably preface this by saying that I am not a big fan of cheesesteaks in Philly. It might have something to do with the bucket of whiz and heaping mounds of greasy beef and onions on the grill resting behind a greasy glass wall. Nevertheless, I do have out of town visitors from time to time and it's hard for me to tell them NOT to get cheesesteaks - since, you know, they're in Philly and all.

    So with that said, I'd have to say despite my forgivings, Jim's is my favorite place for cheesesteaks here. It's not really a BIG sandwich (but rest assured that the calories are there). I think what makes this place stand out is the beef, which is pretty well flavored. Compared to Pat's, I definitely prefer Jim's.

    Make no mistake: don't come here for the service. They're not terribly nice here, as it's really just about speeding people through the line as quickly as humanly possible. And it's not exactly the cleanest of places either (if you're eating upstairs).

    When I told the next guy who was prepping items for here or to go, I apologized and said I wanted onions.  Ironically, although much of their business comes from tourists, the employees apparently have zero patience for them.  This person then repeats his original question, "for here or to go?" louder and condescendingly.  Fine, I get the 'cash-only-we-hate-everyone-too-cool-for school' thing.  At this point, I'm still intent that Jim's could get 4 or 5 stars.  

    Then Jim's made their fatal error.  The guy reaches over and grabs 3 or 4 pieces of onion, which were totally raw, and decides that is the delicious oniony treat I was looking for.  I don't care if tourists annoy you, don't sacrifice the quality and taste of your food because of your control freak attitude.  

    The food:

    The meat is not fresh, as there is essentially a huge pile of meat sitting on the grill.  As a result, you can imagine just how moist the meat is by the time they slop it on your dry roll.  They were also stingy with the wiz, and the onions were just awful raw like that.

    If you're reading this review, this is a comparison (and the same review for Pat's Steaks, Ishkabibbles, Jim's Steaks, and Geno's).  

    I came to Philly with, what I believe are, pretty realistic expectations regarding cheesesteaks.  Yes, I knew coming into it that Genos and Pats were tourist traps and that their might be better cheesesteaks elsewhere throughout Philadelphia.  But at the end of the day, after trying four different places, a cheesesteak is a cheesesteak and it didn't really live up to the hype at all.  With that said, and amongst the 4 places, I would have to rank these in the following order:

    1) Jim's Steaks (3 stars):  Best place to sit and eat. They had the largest dining area with an upstairs area.  It was not air conditioned though.  As for the cheesesteaks, it was the best in terms of flavor, service, bread consistency. Bread wasn't too chewy or hard.I had it prov/wit (provolone cheese and onions) and mushrooms.  Better service than Geno's and Pats.

    2)  Ishkabibbles (solid 2.5 stars, maybe 3):  Very comparable to Jim's but there's absolutely no where to sit with the exception of 6 barstools.   The gremlin drink is overrated too...but I had to try it.  I had it wiz/wit (cheese wiz and onions) and mushrooms.  Better service than Geno's and Pats.

    3)  Geno's (1.5 stars due to lack of smiles/service, but I'll round up to 2 for the hype and flourescent lights):  I like the bread but the meat was pretty flavorless.  Meat was in big slices and not chopped up.  I had it both with wiz/wit (cheese wiz and onions) and prov/wit (provolone chesse and onions).  This was, by the way, the first place I tried.  They have outdoor seating so if it's cold, it's not the best place to eat there.  

    4)  Pat's (solid 1.5 stars.  I won't round up to 2 since it's the least favorite of the four places):  The bread is softer and the meat definitely had more flavor than Geno's. The meat was chopped up.  BUT it was sooooo greasy that it gave me an upset stomach.  I had it with prov/wit (provolone cheese and onions).  They also have outdoor seating only.  

    All in all, and if I'm in Philly again, I'm going to try Tony Luke's, Campos, Dalessandro's, and maybe a street vendor next time.  

    BOTTOM LINE:  Skip the hype at Genos and Pats.  One of them may have started the cheesesteak craze, but the other establishments have made it better. It's time for Genos and Pats to catch up to the 21st century.  Word of advice to Genos and Pats:  SMILE and appreciate your customers!

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
