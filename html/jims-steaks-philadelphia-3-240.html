    I flew into Philly for only a couple of hours but stayed long enough to try an infamous Philly Cheese Steak from "The City of Brotherly Love".

    When I opened the door, I was hit with a pleasant aroma of steak and onions cooking on a grille.

    The menu is easy to read and offers you sandwiches with the basics of Steak, Mushroom Steak, Pizza Steak and a couple of other options. You have the choice of adding Onions, Peppers, Cheese, and Banana Peppers. The establishment was very clean and pleasant.

    I ordered the Mushroom Steak w/Provolone cheese and Onion/Peppers. Service was quick and orderly while walking through the line. I ran into a slight problem when paying because they do not accept any credit cards. I was a tourist for a day and never carry cash. Luckily, my host had cash with him and purchased the sandwiches.

    First bite was like heaven in a bun. The first taste was of peppers and mushrooms and the steak with cheese was filling behind the flavor. The sandwich was not greasy but more of a refreshing feel compared to the other Cheese Steak sandwiches that I have eaten in Florida. The peppers were delicious and everything seemed to complement each other. Not one item on my sandwich was overbearing. I immediately imagined myself coming back to Philly in the future just to order one of these.

    This was the only place that I tried a "Famous Philly Cheese Steak" in Philly and compared to any of the others I have eaten in Orlando it is by far the best I have ever had.

    Everyone in Philly has got their own "best place for cheesesteaks" location and their reason for their decided spot.  I'm just telling you that they're full of crap, because there is only ONE.  Jim Steak's on 4th and South.

    You want to talk about no frills, this is the place.  If you know what you're doing the staff are perfectly friendly, but order something that isn't there and you've walked into Phily's version of the Soup Nazi's Soup Kitchen.  But think about it, the first guy you talk to and that you order from is the guy working over the hot grill.  It's hot back there dummy so of course if you say something stupid you're going to get an earful and at the very least a less than helpful comment.  So be prepared.  Know what you want.  You'll be fine.

    Yes, there is notoriously a line here.  And yes, it can take up to an hour at bad times, but it's worth it.  But if you really have a bug up your a** then take a walk around South Street and make your way back and the line is usually a little shorter, but sometimes not.  

    Bottomline is the cheesesteaks are world class.  The location is convenient and if you can get a seat on the 2nd floor overlooking South Street then it is awesome for people watching.  

    But like I said, people will tell you their place is better.  And sure, go and try out the competition.  Go try out the overrated and overadvertised Geno's and Pat's.  Go try out Chubbs.  It's all more of the same, so-so.  When you're done wasting time, I'm sure you'll come back to Jim's.

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
