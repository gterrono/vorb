    3/28/2014

    I had for many months longingly looked at this place wanting to go inside - I just loved the vibe from the outside. During a loved one's recent hospitalization I found myself going there once for dinner and twice for lunch. Each time the food was fantastic (although some minor food complaints shortly and a bigger complaint about the apparent lack of a real dishwasher person or machine coming up soon).

    I love flavorful food - and everything I got was packed with delicious, harmonious bites. The first night I got a cup of chili and the roasted beet and goat cheese salad. The chili was perhaps the best I've ever had - served in a mug (I liked that little touch) and topped with sour cream and guacamole. As a relatively new guac lover and someone who never put sour cream on chili before - I was immediately taken in by the texture and flavor. The chili itself was packed with different beans and sizable pieces of meat (not sure what kind).

    The chili was served with these adorable petite cornbread muffins served with whipped jalapeño butter. My only complaint here was I couldn't really spread the butter on the muffin without it crumbling apart. Also surprisingly, I didn't really get a lot of jalapeño flavor or heat from the butter - but I still dug the attempt. The salad was surprisingly large for a salad of this nature - and I wasn't able to finish.

    My second visit - the next day - was for lunch. I couldn't resist the chili despite so many other great options to choose from on the menu. This time I got a bowl of it. (A cup warrants two muffins, a bowl three!) The chili was good - but not as good as the night before. It seemed a little mushier this visit - but again, still great flavor.

    My third visit was today for lunch. I got the 1/2 portion of smoked chicken quesadillas. Once again I was met with great flavors!! My complaint here was the quesadilla was VERY greasy - to the point where grease was all over my hand and pooled in one area on the plate. But all in all, it had great flavors - a good tomato salsa with sliced-in-half grape tomatoes and a jalapeño corn salsa - that was yummy although not hot at all (at least to me).

    Now onto the dishwasher comments - my first visit saw a lipstick smear on the water glass, my silverware wrapped in a napkin (so there was a chance for quality control) both had dried food bits on them, when I reached over to the next table for silverware - one of the two utensils in that bundle had food on it too. I pointed this out to the waitress who apologized but another utensil slipped through the cracks on my second visit. I really started questioning the cleanliness of the place - but thankfully today saw clean silverware and a clean water glass.

    To me, this place could easily be 5 stars with just some attention to the little things like that. Hats off to the chef for serving up delicious fare!

    One other note - I live about an hour away from Philly and easily spend $10 on lunches at Panera or the local diner. To get this high-quality food for the same price - if not less - in a big city - makes me wish I worked in center-city Philly!

    This was my fourth or fifth time at Smokin' Betty's in 2014, and I have to say, the buzz and hype have finally worn off. I don't have any specific negative comments -- food, service, atmosphere were all adequate -- but I don't have any high marks to share either. I ordered a simple bowl of chili, and it was, well, a simple bowl of chili. One of the primary reasons I've returned so often (aside from its convenient location) is to see what all the hooplah is about; I still can't figure it out. It's all so "meh" to me.

    I've had their tacos (both fish and rib), their burgers, and the salmon on different visits. And while the meal is always fine and satisfying, I'm just never dazzled. I don't understand the fuss people make over this place. There is much better bbq in Philly: Sweet Lucy's in the northeast, Percy Street downtown, and even Famous Dave's. Again, there was nothing wrong with my meals on any of my visits. I just always leave there thinking, "So what?"

    A comment re: staff and atmosphere (which I maintain were not bad, just "adequate"): I was admittedly turned off by the saturation of hipster customers, possibly spilling over from MilkDud (or whatever that place next store is called). But in 2014 Philadelphia, they are becoming inevitable. They've infested the staff too. They make for lousy waiters/bartenders/food runners/bussers/hosts, and Smokin' Betty's staff is no exception. Though nothing went wrong, I'm really unimpressed when the median age for front-of-the-house staff is 22. So I'll cop to my bias, but it would have been refreshing to see a grown-up manager observe the staff once in a while.
