    Does Time now represent Jazz in Philadelphia?

    Time is one of the only bar/clubs left in Philadelphia where one can hear jazz most nights of the week. Theoretically, this is a good thing, and I'm happy to support it. Paying a cover is not a problem for me, and I enjoy sitting and listening to real music. However, I seem to be the in the minority. There is very little respect paid the music. Time's patrons talk through the set, make odd requests of the musicians (sometimes while they are playing), and take their drinks precariously close to what looks like valuable instruments. The lack of respect shown to the music can sometimes be off putting.

    The food stays in line with modern gastro-pub criteria. There is nothing amazing, or offensive that I've tried. The whiskey list is fine. My private collection is better, and no one talks through 'Sketches of Spain' at le maison du Urqhart. Time makes an effort, but in the end, it fails to create an atmosphere in which one can absorb, show reverence to, and reflect on Art. On the other hand, it's a hell of a step up from a DJ Fake Musician.

    Music:   Awesome & eclectic selection of live music (usually Jazz, with tinges of Blues, Funk, Rock, Latin & more) from 10 until 2-ish most nights of the week. Sundays thru Thursdays are usually pretty chill, easy to grab a table or seat at the bar and enjoy some food and drinks with some of the best local musicians providing an excellent sonic atmosphere.  Friday & Saturday nights have legit DJ's upstairs (Trap / Hip Hop, EDM / Dubstep, Throwbacks, Etc.) while there is also a live band playing downstairs.  Weekend days are a bit more energetic and alive, better for going out with some friends to have a good time checking out the music or dancing than for relaxing and hanging out.  Def tends to get pretty packed on weekends.

    For musicians, there's an open mic/jam every Tuesday night. Pretty diverse as far as genres go, very free-form.  There's also a closed jam on Sundays. They're more straight up jazz and seem a bit more structured (standards, etc).  Both nights have some very talented musicians from the local scene.

    Food:   REALLY good selection of food (from Wings and Burgers to very well composed house specialties. IE: Roasted Duck Breast w/ Confit Leg, Sweet Potatoes, Brocolli Rabe, Picked Chili, Jus) and they serve until 1AM Sunday thru Thursday, 11PM Friday and Saturday.  Get there a bit before 10 on the weekend if you want a better chance of grabbing a table or seat at the bar.

    Drinks:   These guys always have an epic selection of about 20 craft beers on draft, which are rotated frequently, in addition to a great bottle selection.  Bartenders are very friendly and make some awesome cocktails as well.

    Bottom Line:   If you like listening to really good music played by great musicians, want to get some delicious food served late in the city, want to go out on the weekend and hit the dancefloor with a decent DJ or get rocked by some funky grooves in a fun crowd, allthewhile having access to a great selection of drinks and good vibes all around:

    GO TO TIME!
