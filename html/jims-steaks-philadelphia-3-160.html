    Jim's serves a solid three star product, with an extra star added for quality control. You can spend a half hour in line here knowing that at the end of the trail you're going to be served the same tender mouth-watering steak you had on your last visit. It's not easy to maintain that quality year after year, trust me. The thing that makes Jim's sandwich truly unique is the way they slice the meat - it's so thin it literally melts in your mouth. Remember the scene in Goodfellas where they're all in prison and one of the guys is slicing the garlic with a razor blade so it will liquify in the pan? I have a mental image of the same guy sitting in the back room of Jim's with a razor blade and a big slab of beef. Like they say in the movie, it's a good system. I don't know if I would visit Philly strictly so I could eat at Jim's, but I do know that no trip to this city is complete without a cheese steak from this Carnivore's Paradise.

    So, I'm taking on the all time "beef" (literally) between the best Philadelphia cheese steak joints that are actually IN Philadelphia. Being a Philadelphia native - born AND raised - I felt it my duty to write this review and I hope my fellow 'Illadelphians can respect it and not jeer at me and throw stuff...

    Jim's Steaks has, for a VERY long time, been my absolute favorite place to procure a pure, honest and delicious cheese steak in the city. I have always felt that Geno's and Pat's just don't measure up - especially with all the hype (and the garish lights that Geno's insists on polluting their corner with). It's hard to explain to a non-Philadelphian what exactly the difference is between these 3 places without sounding obsessed and slightly crazy. My summary is as follows:

    ***Most cheese steaks that are authentic contain chopped meat, not slabs of it. I have found that Pat's and Geno's BOTH give out slabs of cooked meat and not chopped. This is a sin, in my book.
    ***The quality of service is totally different. Now that I am a pescetarian, I always get a "meatless" steak (which usually includes the peppers, mushrooms and onions in place of the steak itself). At Jim's, they ALWAYS do this for me without a side glance - as long as I don't stumble on my order and know exactly what I want by the time I get to the cook. Their veggies are always scrumptious and they add just the right amount of each.
    ***For some reason, Jim's Amoroso's bread always seems to be softer and crisped on the outside perfectly compared to Geno's and Pat's. The latter 2 are usually packed with people waiting in lines around the block so they tend to rush the whole steak process and the bread suffers.

    All in all, Jim's is the place I will forever take my friends and recommend to anyone who asks. I could probably go on forever about this place and I decided I couldn't go another day without reppin' it on Yelp. Can't let Pat's and Geno's get all the press! I hope "y'uns" get a chance to try this stellar joint and hopefully see what I'm talking about.

    Don't forget, they only accept cash and there is usually going to be a line so be ready to wait (obviously if you were thinking of going to Pat's or Geno's, you were intending on spending some quality time in line). They are not "Soup Nazi" with their ordering style but they don't tolerate slow decision making or a lot of questions so be prepared by the time you order. Also, there's seating upstairs so don't fret if the downstairs looks like a cattle call and you can't go outside because of rain...

    There are multiple locations. I just got back from a weekend in Philadelphia and visited the location on South St. Benjamin Franklin rocks.

    On WTR!!!

    Menu:
    Simple and straight forward:
    Cheesesteak with either wiz, american or provolone, comes with onions, you can add peppers, mushrooms, pizza sauce, etc.
    They also have egg-n-cheese sandwiches along with their variants.
    Bonus: They also sell beer.  I fell off my chair when I took a bite of the standard American Cheesesteak with onions. Holy cow!

    Quality:
    Juicy, flavorful meat. This stuff AIN'T steakums... this is thinly sliced eye round that is just PERFECT for this sandwich. Served on this perfect hoagie roll (sub roll for us in the NYC area) that has the right amount of crispy crust and chewy inside. The gods eat this.

    Service:
    This is fast action please order right - and we'll treat ya nice kinda place. One guy flips the meat, one guys deliver the grub, another guy charges ya. Its simple and its blue collar perfect. You want a maitre d'? - Look elsewhere. 40 people served in 20 mins. I am impressed.

    Decor:
    The place is filled with all sorts of famous people portraits with handwritten 'shout-outs' to Jims. It's a bar-stool kinda place that is just an edge above any fast-food place. You're not here for the decor. Period.

    Ambiance:
    LOL - ambiance - hungry people eating greasy food. Yeah. No candles, no waiters. Just table cleaners and grill masters. That's all you need really - you can go here with a date who likes greasy yuminess. You do not propose here... although.. there is a pic of a wedding couple going here for afterparty munchies.

    Price:
    $7.50 for a standard heavenly cheesesteak. $2 for a can of heineken. Additions add a few cents to the sandwich. I heard the other locations are a little cheaper - but not bad for the bar section of town.

    Parking:
    Um.. parking? LOL - i parked at the hotel and walked a few blocks. It's tough around here. Be prepared for traffic on weekends. Lots of it. So the parking is based on that fact - more cars equals less spaces.

    Location:
    Right on the corner of 4th and south. You can't miss it. Its black with silver shimmering linings. It will be the building with the line of people inside. A few blocks from Old City.

    Conclusion:
    I tried the wiz. I tried the American cheese. This place is great for anyone that has never had a real cheesesteak. I'd recommend this above all others in the vicinty. Do no be fooled by Pat's or by Geno's - this is better. I now know this.

    Pros:
    Best Damn Cheesesteaks
    Not to expensive
    Greasy Fun

    Cons:
    No parking

    H-Cane's Final Spin:
    Wow. Just Wow. I learned what a cheesesteak really is this weekend. Everyone from Philly: Props to you! People- go here and love it. It's awesome.

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
