        I came here on the recommendation from a friend that the Shanghai style dumplings here are better than the ones at Dim Sum Garden. I'm afraid that was not the case. The dumplings at Sakura were inconsistent in size. The skin was thicker and the filling tasted bland in flavor. There was soup/broth in the dumplings but it tasted almost like water. There were only 6 dumplings instead of 8 at Dim Sum Garden.

        The dumplings at Dim Sum Garden are way better, especially the flavor in the broth. I admit the filling of the dumplings at Dim Sum Garden are filled with pork higher in fat content and loaded with more salt, hence the more flavorful broth but this is the way it is suppose to be. The dumplings at Sakura tasted like a health/lite version of dumplings. I guess maybe some people would prefer a healthier dish but soup dumplings, and Chinese food in general, are in no way supposed to be health foods. The filling aside, the skin of the dumplings at Sakura was just way too thick.

        The vinegar they give you at Sakura Mandarin is also the wrong kind of vinegar, not the kind used in Shanghai with dumplings.  It wasn't sour enough, dark brown/black, and tasted more like soy sauce where as the right vinegar is suppose to be brown/red in color. I also thought it was inconvenient they brought out one small dish of vinegar to share between two people. They should have just had a bottle at the table.

        I also tried a classic Sichuan dish as well, the spicy beef and tripe. It wasn't even all that spicy and covered in peanuts. It was lacking some other spices usually found in spicy beef and tripe.

        The food here isn't bad but it's light in taste and lack the appropriate seasonings for traditional Chinese cuisine. I would say the quality is average.

        In the end, I was so disappointed with the boring dumplings I had (tried both the regular AND the crab one) so I went over to Dim Sum Garden and got some more dumplings same night.

        Oh and this place is maybe $1-2 more expensive than Dim Sum Garden and the portion sizes are smaller.

        I just wanted to point out that on Yelp site Sakura Mandarin and Sakuza listed like two different restaurants. In reality this is the same place and under the same address.
