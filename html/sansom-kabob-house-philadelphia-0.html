    I have to write a review for this place, even though I moved out of Philly. It is my favorite, cheap restaurant in the city. It is run by a very nice little family. It's really a small, hole in the wall kind of place but the service is excellent and the food is freshly prepared.

    Some of the food can be a little greasy, depending on what you order (but it's relatively healthy compared to most restaurant food).  It is all spiced delicately to moderately.  I've tried all the vegetarian dishes, which are yummy. The Afghani bread is homemade and delicious. The rice is tasty too. Overall, my 2 favorite dishes are the eggplant and spinach. Try the tea, which is very refreshing. Dessert's nice too. Of course most people go there for meat kabobs, which I don't eat. I've heard that they're good too though!

    I've never had to wait for a table, even on weekends. I don't know why this place never gets crowded, but in a selfish way I liked it!

    (And for comparison, this place is a million times better than a popular Afghan restaurant in NYC's East Village. Philly does have good ethnic food!)

    First off, what a charming place this is. I found it intriguing that one descends into this place, as it is below ground. It's nestled in between the many shops and restaurants on Sansom Street. My waitress this evening was a cute little girl of no more than ten, who took her job quite seriously! And to boot, she's a far better waitress than 90% of the other restaurants I've been to. It was amusing how serious she was at ringing up my bill. But on to the food.

    I have to say it's nice to have something other than banal Middle-Eastern food prepared with little to no imagination. SKH puts thought into their menu, though by  no means was my experience a cerebral one. I ordered a lamb stew that was cooked in a sauce similar to a curry, though a bit sweeter and it had onions and pepper. It was served with a light salad with a yogurt dressing as a starter. Then came the brown rice and lamb dish with Afghan bread. The lamb was like butter in your mouth and the sauce was fantastic. All the food complemented itself. And they also brew a wonderful tea. My only negative detail was that I found the sauce to be a tad bit salty. As for one who likes to keep an eye on the salt consumption, this was a minor detail. I'll see if this was a fluke or a regular practice next time. If you find yourself in Center City, by all means, visit the Kabob House on Sansom Street.

    Sansom Kabob House is fantastic - I've been several times for lunch and finally tried it for dinner as well. The menus are fairly similar, regardless of which meal you go for, although dinner portions are slightly larger and more expensive (~$11 per entree for dinner vs $8 for lunch).

    My personal favorite kabobs are the ground spiced beef kabob, whose name escapes me, and the Sansom (which is a lamb, chicken, beef combo). I also tried the spinach chalaw this last time, which was good but given my general preference for meat, next time I'll go for the kabobs again or the kufta chalaw, which are spiced meatballs in a bean sauce.

    It gets a little smoky and the use of space is strange - you go downstairs to enter, then through a slightly odd hallway with stools and tables, but the real tables are in the back. It's really small in the back and service is occasionally slow when they're busy, but generally they're very nice.

    And you can also order kabob sandwich (a kabob wrapped in the flatbread) to go. Perfect for eating lunch at your desk..

    I absolutely ADORE Sansom Kabob House! I've probably eaten in or picked up 20-30 times, and the food's always fantastic. I'll admit that the bread sometimes seems a little past its prime, but I can't not give them five stars.

    The decor's charmingly tacky, and the restaurant itself is half underground. Their lunch portions are a little smaller (I think), and definitely cheaper.

    Enjoy!
