    Drinks
    There was a house red and white for $4 or 5, a beer for $3 and a sake and house cocktail for $4 or $5, and all their other drinks were a dollar off.

We even added 2 people to our party halfway through and they shifted some seats around to accomodate us.     All in all, I see why this place is packed with young people at happy hour. You can get a drink for $5 and an appetizer for $4, the lighting is cool, and it's right near a lot of offices so it's perfect if you need a quick place to hang out with coworkers right after work. But it is not a worthy destination within itself, IMHO

    I made reservations for Sampan at 9:15pm on a Friday night for a party of 12. We walked right in at 9:15pm and didn't get seated until 10pm. That's a 45 minute wait so the host had a few appetizers waiting for us on the house.

    They served us edamame dumplings and tuna rice crackers- edamame dumplings, I would give a 1 out of 10 (10 being the best), there was absolutely no texture to the dumpling. It was like eating baby food. I think if they added some crunch it would be decent. I did, however, enjoy the tuna rice crackers.

    We had a party of 12 so I got to taste everything. I ordered the filet mignon with a side of brussel sprouts and the pork bao buns. I enjoyed the pork bao buns but would much rather go to Chinatown and have the duck buns. The filet seemed a bit rubbery, I wouldn't order it again but the fries were good (portion size was big enough to share with 3 people). The brussel sprouts were overly fried and could barely taste the veggie. It was also too salty for my taste. My friend ordered the spice crusted tuna and it was decent. The horse radish sauce did not compliment the tuna at all so I just ate it plain with the potato. It was a weird mixture of texture going on in this dish- wouldn't order again.  

    My other friend ordered the short ribs with lo mein. I didn't like this dish at all and I usually love short ribs. The Asian spices just didn't blend well together- the lo mein was okay but it was just an odd combination. Out of all the dishes, I would say the Kimchi Fried Rice was excellent. I'm Korean so I'm picky about Asian food but this was really good.

    Overall, I wouldn't come back for dinner but perhaps Happy Hour- I really liked the blue/modern decor of the restaurant. The food was okay and even after waiting for 45 minutes for my reserved table, we had two main entrees NOT come out while everyone else had theirs.

    Not a place for a big group of folks. 2 stars for the mediocre food and poor experience.

    I came here yesterday for happy hour with friends and I really liked it and now came another story on why I had stayed away from Sampan for years and never came back.

    When they first opened up, there was a highly advertised event for free Banh Mi and I do love free food. Back then, the Banh Mi didn't impressed me at all so I've written this place off as one of those faux Asian place catered to non-Asian people. I didn't write them a bad review or anything but when my friend recommended this place for happy hour, I wasn't thrilled but then I'm always up to giving a second chance.

    I do like the bar set up, The main bar is cute, but I always love seating near the kitchen to see how they prepare their food. The bartender is friendly, she came to explained the happy hour menu, which is full of goodies in a low price. I enjoyed the cocktail special which was made of blood orange juice, vanilla vodka and pineapple juice.

    I love satay. The Korean beef satay has all the flavors in Korean BBQ but the meat itself is a bit chewy that has more to do with the cut of beef they use. The chicken satay is fried to a crisp and it comes with a slightly spicy sauce that goes very well with the sweetness in the chicken. I can eat five of these. We also ordered the duck egg roll and the Cantonese spring roll. I really like the duck egg roll, which was like having Peking duck in a roll and deep fried. The Cantonese roll was crispy and came with a nice sauce but I didn't taste the shrimp or mushroom.

    The two dumplings we ordered were the edamame dumplings (best item on the happy hour menu) and the shrimp and lobster dumplings. Now I did not know that all the dumplings are cradled in broth and it makes sharing a little awkward if you don't know the person that well but I guess it can be overcome. The shrimp and lobster dumpling has the satisfying crunch of cooked shrimp, I was expecting the edamame in the dumpling to be individual beans but it was a puree and the rich truffle flavor comes across some luxurious. Also the sake dashi broth was so clear and clean. It goes perfectly with the dumpling. Definitely must have.

    The sliders were satisfying but not too, too special. The description of the brussel sprouts reminded me of the ones at Momofuku and they were just as awesome. The kimchee fried rice comes with a fried sunny side up egg with the server will break it apart at your table which reminds me of bibimbap. There are lots of nice sized shrimps and goes great with the lightly spiced kimchee.

    Honestly there not one item I tried that I didn't like and they are all so reasonably priced, I was there around 5 and I was full throughout the night. I don't usually do happy hour but I will find an excuse to go back. I'm really happy that I stepped back in, now when I pass by Sampan I wouldn't have to feel like I'm bumping into an ex-boyfriend.
