    I had wanted to try this place as soon as I had heard it even existed, you see as a teenager, I used to go dancing there on Friday nights.

    The space is interestingly decorated (with skeletons of animals placed on high shelves, and lighting oddities, and many a terrarium), I thought the location was a great stop while attending First Friday at the galleries in Olde City.

    The menu is pretty standard for this type of place- wings, burgers, nachos, calamari, fish and chips, hummus, that type of thing, but there is an addition of a few different varieties of shrimp, a selection of sandwiches, and steak. The most interesting items on the menu are from the "nostalgics" which include Tomato Soup and Grilled Cheese (who doesn't love that) Corn Dogs (!!! At a restaurant ?!) and something they call Frito Taco Extravaganza-which the menu states is a bag of Frito's opened up and topped with chili and shredded cheese. They also have more substantial dinner fare, but I caught them during their special-all burgers (veggie and meat) along with fries are $5 from 5-7 pm Tuesday-Sunday (they are closed Monday)

    Id have to say they have THE BEST veggie burgers I have ever had! Shredded vegetables make up the patty, even the roll is yum. PLUS they have cider on the menu and a large selection of beer (seasonal and otherwise), friendly staff. In October be sure to try their pumpkin soup, one of the best I have ever had...

    Definitely do not skip it if you find yourself in the Olde City area looking for a meal!

    It amazes me the number of different "scenes" that one can experience in this cool historic space depending on when you go.  

    I will confess up front that this old lady almost always finds it too loud at night, but I have had raucous good times when I was in the mood for them.  They keep a nice rotation of draft beers including local micro-brew favorites and some more exotic choices, as well as solid, basic beers (e.g. Yeungling, Sam Adams, etc.).  I have had some good mixed drinks as well, but the bartenders and waitstaff can get a little overwhelmed when it's packed, making beer seem like the easier and kinder choice.

    My favorite National Mechanics experience has to be brunch.  It is quieter, more mellow, and the diffuse sunlight streaming through the antique stained glass windows really highlights the grand space.  The rough-hewn wood tables and little touches like carnivorous plants under glass bells on the window sills stand out.  All of the breakfast and lunch dishes I have tried (turkey sandwich, Breakfast!, monte cristo, and a couple of salads and omelettes among them) have been quite good, and a HUGE amount of food for the price.  

    They also do a spectacular bloody mary bar even though I am generally not into these. The times that I am out seeking therapeutic early-day food with liquor are usually the times I most want someone else to do all of the work.  However, the generous pours of customizable vodka (or tequila), and absurd variety of hot sauces and extras really make it worth the effort and the money.

    Residing in a former bank built in 1837, National Mechanics blends in perfectly with the look and feel of Olde City. After spending the earlier part of our night pub-crawling at sea level, we stumbled up the steps of this beautiful work of architecture and I was nearly convinced I'd be seeing neon and strobe lights begin to pour out the front door, hearing house music pound the foundation. If this were a building in New York, my prediction at this point would be dead-on. Instead, I was more than pleasantly surprised to walk inside and see a bar - not some club, not some dance hall, not some over-priced lounge, but a bar.

    Almost immediately, I knew we'd be here the rest of the night. There was this great party atmosphere, stripped of all pretension - the kind of setting that feels like the worst type of peer pressure - you know, the type where you're actually welcoming the pressure and not just succumbing to it? Anyway, National Mechanics is very dark, which, in my opinion, is the only way a bar should be. The music was a little hit or miss, but when the drinks are drank, the drunks will still dance, right? There were times the DJ had everyone moving, then by the next song, he'd lost me. No worries - the lapse in momentum was simply the cue to get another adult beverage. The mixed drinks were around $7, nearly half alcohol, and served right until the stroke of 2AM - the bartenders were on top of their game, for sure.

    This is my brand of bar - deceivingly gorgeous on the outside, and a devil of a good time on the inside. If I lived in Philly, National Mechanics would be my go-to spot in the neighborhood. Next time I'm in town, you know where to find me.

History

Established in 2006.

Built by famed architect William Strickland in 1837 the National Mechanics Building has seen banks, churches, clubs, bars and fires pass between its walls, and it's still standing.

This beautiful building, as much a part of Philadelphia history as any tourist attraction, now houses Philadelphia's freshest new bar and restaurant.

The peculiar name for our establishment stems from the building's original incarnation in 1837 as Mechanics National Bank.

Our décor is an allusion to all things mechanical from our homemade lighting fixtures to the strange devices running throughout the space to our uniquely designed bathrooms. All of these elements combined with our laid back and off kilter philosophy make this bar what it is. A place designed with real folks in mind and molded by time, our hands and the hands of many before us. Come in and check it out.
