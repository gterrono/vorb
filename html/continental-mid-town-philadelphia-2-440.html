    I've been living in the city for almost two years and if I had written this review a year ago it may have been a little more positive.  In Continental's defense I have been there numerous times, enough to start seeing the flaws of the restaurant.

    I'll start by saying that the food isn't bad and the french onions dumplings are both innovative and delicious.

    On to what I see as a problem not only with the restaurant, but with Starr Restaurants in general:

    1) They have expanded so quickly that they haven't been able to keep up with the training and development of talented managers.  I've seen plenty of good managers at their establishments, but I've also experienced more than a couple that seem like a deer in headlights when faced with disgruntled customers.  I think that Starr needs to take a minute and re-focus their efforts on the overall experience their guests are getting.  They seem to be settling for just being good these days...too many people around them are doing better than good.

    2)  They don't empower their waiters to make decisions.  I was in there one Sunday after a Philly half marathon.  They clearly weren't expecting the influx of people and were overwhelmed by the numbers.  I will let the being understaffed go (even though they should be aware of major events going on, especially a restaurant group like Starr with so many establishments!), but their inability to offset their deficiencies was striking.  We were on the roof, where it was particularly underserved, and I literally saw a guy ask for mayo six times before getting it.  I literally though the guy was going to murder his waiter.  Then when he said he couldnt eat his meal and wanted it taken off his bill the waiter had to wait an additional 30 minutes for the waiter to find his manager to take the sandwich off.  You have got to be kidding me, give your waiters the power to comp a meal (its not that hard to monitor it on the back-end if you are so worried about them comping for friends).

    3)  I hate seeing people settling for mediocrity when they have potential (i.e. Continental has a friggin sweet roof venue and it is barely a good spot to hang out).

    Anyways...like I said, I think the food is fine and I have obviously been there many times.  I guess I am just disappointed that Starr isn't capitilizing on the good-will they have developed with their patrons.  Hopefully they realize this sooner than later or that goodwill bank account will be a negative balance.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
