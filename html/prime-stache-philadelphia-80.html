    Listed in Philly.. from the West to the South to all around, Bar Keep.. Beer me !!, Ye Old City!

    I came into Prime Stache for a "Yelp Elite " event in April of 2013, my very first event mind you, I had to come back.  Almost a year later I still remembered about this spot, saying to myself  "OK tonight lets get to Prime" well finally I got to it. This time I brought a friend and fellow Yelper Jason K. with me.   He has heard me mention numerous times about this spot , to where we just decided to make it a go.

    This was the start of our night in bar hopping, we were gonna party like it was 1999. Well minus Prince and his crushed purple velvet ass cheeks hanging out get up, with the white ruffled shirt.

    Instead of ordering a main course(s) we decided to do an all appetizer approach, with different drinks that the libation gods would approve of. From a few Brier Patch orders that had a nice little spice to it , to wine was consumed.  Even dived into a few Fig Sazerac concoctions with Absinthe and Vodka being the main ingredients.

    Our menu for this evening, again this is all appetizers  was :
    - Fish taco's . With the fish being tilapia this tasted pretty good.  Good size portion to it, the tilapia we had it prepaired in a cajun style. It could have been more tilapia to it, but still had a great taste to it .

    -Rock Shrimp. These little morsels were simply out of this world, I love a good spicy dish. These little bad boys mixed with an Asian sauce made it just right with every bite. Nice flavorful kick to it that

    -Cherry Pepper Calamari - Now seeing I am a fan of all dishes spicy these cherry peppers were cut in sections and stuffed adding a nice kick to when you are enjoying calamari. A lightly breaded little guy to be enjoyed with fresh lemon squeezed over top with a dijon style dipping sauce.

    -The Ron Burgundy Sliders.(2 total) 3 types of pork (pulled pork, pork belly, and roast pork),  BBQ sauce, all packed under a brioche bun , slaw, and seemed to be caramelized onions with a side of homemade potato chips.

    -Edamame Ravioli.  Nice sized ravioli with snow peas in a homemade broth. This was OK ,  not sure if something has changed since the last time I had these, but it wasn't what i remembered.

    -Sashimi.  A special they were doing that evening was a Salmon and King Fish duo. This was on point, a homerun that made your taste buds go in a frenzy. Nicely portioned cuts  in an unbelievable house made marinade with soy and wasabi on the side for you to enjoy.

    -Stache Sliders .(2 total) This was something we didn't order, but given to us. Hey I will not turn away anything free. Juicy little burgers with melted cheese and cuts of bacon in a homemade BBQ sauce.  

    A great spot to go in and enjoy an evening of drinks and great food. Great service all around from the bartenders to the hostess making sure everything was to your liking. The manager running it that evening was talking to all the patrons, giving  insight on how the dishes are prepped. That is comforting I feel if you are not certain which path to take.

    Go in and enjoy, you never know who will pop in like Jason Kelce enjoying a meal and talking to you. For those of you who don't know he is the center for the Eagles, which the owner is Brent Celek a tight end.

    Listed in Reviews for 2014...to 800 or bust.

    Stopped in recently about a half hour before closing on a weeknight.  Although not at all crowded, the warm brick/old wood ambiance mixed well with the several customers hanging on into the night.  The exposed brick and long bar anchored the room, while the wooden tables and chairs offered the sense of an Old City establishment that could have existed at any time since the late 18th Century.  In fact, the foundation was that old.  Walking in from cobblestone streets completed the impression that Prime Stache doubled as a time machine.

    I sat briefly at the bar waiting for my companion to arrive for a late business discussion.  No pressure to order a drink after explaining my purpose, and the bartendress was friendly and attractive.  I had a chance to settle in and look around.  I liked everything I saw.  The bar menu looked appealing, and as a former barkeep I appreciated the fresh fruit and infusion jars resting around the work area.  I was tempted, but knew I needed a clear head.  Still, I have a strong desire to try something creative next time I come.  

    My contraparty arrived and we moved to a table.  She was hungry and ordered Turkey Sliders.  An app of Sweet Sautéed Carrots arrived first.  They were small and thin and obviously a local produce.  Nice snappy sweetness.  We discussed our businesses while awaiting the main course, and switched to questions that we could answer for each other while she enjoyed the mini burgers.  Really wish I was more hungry, but observing her face as she ate was a clear indication of high enjoyment.  I continued to sip away as our comfort level with the space and each other grew into a more detailed and tangential discussion.  

    We ended up closing the place.  All the other chairs were up on their tables when we finally walked out.  

    Prime Stache is known as an American Bistro.  The quality of the environment, drinks, and the food, makes it something more than that. While the word, "Stache," suggests both a Golden Age of American history, as well as a slightly out of date image for a modern resto, it successfully sets itself apart by a consistency of atmos, service, and product that liberate from the constraints of time and place, and bring you into a timelessness that is compelling.  

    I am excited about returning here again, and that makes me a fan.

History

Established in 2013.

On the cobblestone block of Front and Chestnut Street, Prime Stache in Old City provides an extensive American bistro style menu and carefully selected craft beer and cocktails. From the classic exposed brick, to the antique wooden décor, to the Thomas Edison style light fixtures, there is a rustic, yet modern and comfortable feel to the restaurant.

Owner Brent Celek and partner/chef Hee Chang have developed a distinctive, gratifying, and affordable menu filled with savory and prodigious entrées and sandwiches while Kevin Malvey's unique cocktail menu combines all fresh ingredients with house made infusions and bitters in an elaborate display of mason jars that will leave you curious and anxious to ask 'What's in that one?'
