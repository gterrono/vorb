    Also went here for restaurant week.
    The menu looked great, and a few of the plates were huge hits, but others were big misses. We went because a member of our party is a vegetarian and they offer specifically vegetarian options. She said she really liked what she got!

    Main: I got the Tuna and I would say it was the winner. Cooked great, lots of flavor and I loved the sides.     Dessert: All desserts were pretty good, none stood out. I liked my creme brule but have had better.

    Came here with my best friend (who has gluten sensitivities) for dinner during Restaurant Week. I was a little nervous from the reviews- seems the general consensus is that their brunch is way better. But the dinner was actually pretty good!

    Apps:
    Crab cake- I had to restrain myself from licking the plate clean. Very tasty!
    Farmicia Salad- looked so yawn to me and she doesn't eat lettuce, so I didn't quite understand that choice but she liked the other veggies. I have this thing where I like to finish other people's plates (hate seeing food go to waste) so I ate her lettuce. The herb vinaigrette was okay.

    Main:
    10oz rib eye- we both got this. I'm not into the popular kale trend going on right now but they happily let us sub it for Swiss chard, yum! Mine was great and was definitely Swiss chard... hers was NOT. Remember when I said I finish other plates? She offered me whatever it was. I tried it and was grossed out. I believe it was spinach which I usually love, but it was completely overly seasoned. The steaks were done just as we asked (medium). DEM MASHED POTATOES, so good! And that blue cheese butter... love.

    Dessert:
    Flourless chocolate cake- my first time having a flourless pastry. Now I can't help wonder why we even use flour! So unbelievably decadent and just lovely. If you don't like rich desserts, stay away. This was the kind of dessert you had to wipe your mouth after every single bite because you just know there is chocolate on your lips.
    Creme brûlée- this usually comes with a sugar cookie which they did not include per her request. She loved it. I wish I thought to steal a bite.

    Drinks: she got the St. Germaine cocktail and enjoyed it; I got a standard glass of shiraz. All drinks were half-off for happy hour :)

    Service: it was fine. Our waiter was very friendly and helpful, but he disappeared by the end. Waited a while for our desserts and for someone to grab my card.

    They sat us at the table right against the front window, which I thought was neat... until some jerk sat in his Jeep and revved his engine for a solid 10 minutes. Since these windows open up, the smell seeped in and it was disgusting. The manager walked out and looked at the driver, but ended up just walking back in.

    Would I come back? Definitely to try the brunch and we'll probably come back for happy hour, given that we haven't been able to find many GF options in the city. I wouldn't come back for dinner with someone else who can tolerate gluten though- too many other great places.

    A few of my colleagues and I decided to walk from the convention center back to our hotel.  We were looking for a spot to grab dinner as well, and Farmicia was one of the few places that could accommodate our group without a reservation.

    Farmicia places an emphasis on local and seasonal ingredients, so the name of the restaurant fits well!  Nice feel to the place too.  After looking through the menu though, I noticed that the entrees were on the higher price point and nothing really stood out to me, except for the crabcakes.  Good thing for me that they offer the crabcake as an entree so you get two instead of one, so I went with it.  In fact, you can do that with several other appetizers as well.

    When my crabcakes came, it was two huge crabcakes sitting on a plate.  At first, I was a bit annoyed that the cakes were the only things on the plate, but wow, those things filled me up.  Nice crabcakes with lots of lump crab meat.  Green goddess sauce underneath is a nice compliment.  It was actually one of the better crabcakes I've had and I liked the sauce so I asked if I could get more.

    However, that's when my whole experience here went the other direction.  When I got the extra sauce, I didn't think much about it, but when I saw the bill, I saw that they had charged me for it!  It was 50 cents, which isn't much at all considering I am already paying $25+ for the crabcakes, but I just don't like how the server didn't tell me there would be a charge.  In fact, when my colleague asked for more dressing on the side, they charged for that as well and didn't inform us.  Again, would've been no problem if they had just let us know.

    While I did like my crabcakes, my colleagues also complained how even with the entree items, you literally just get what you see on the menu.  Probably wise to order some appetizers to share for the bigger eaters.

    It was also a bit tough to wave down our waitress, but luckily the busboy was able to help us a few times.  

    Ideas and atmosphere's great, but price and service is not desirable.
