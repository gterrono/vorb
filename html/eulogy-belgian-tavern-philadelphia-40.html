    Came here at my brother's suggestion, along with his friend and our cousin. This was on a Wednesday night. The place was fairly full even on that weeknight, but no problem getting a table. We went upstairs first to have some food, and then migrated downstairs when the upstairs closed. It did thin out a fair bit by around 11:30 or so. My brother said it was a lot more of a zoo when he was there on a Friday night.

    My brother clearly wanted to come to have muscles at least as much as for the beer. I tried one and they were tasty (but not cheap). We also had some fries that were really excellent. The came with a couple of sauces that were not bad, but sort of drowned out the tastiness of the fries if you're not careful.

    There were about 20 taps, and good ones too. They also have an enormous bottle list. Everything is in the right glassware. I really liked the heavy glass goblets they have, they're pretty excellent. I recall having a Delirium Tremens, St. Bernardus Abt 12, Piraat, Bell's Jahva Stout, and a Ruination. The selection of Beligan brews is phenomenal, both in the bottle and on tap - worth going out of your way for. The selection of domestic mirco beer is solid, but not quite as phenomenal. They also have a pitcher system where you can order a small pitchers wort, and they also give you several appropriate glasses. This is somewhat more economical. Just interesting since I've never ordered a pitcher of, say, Abt 12 before. A pitcher of Abt 12, which I'd say was I'd guess a little more than a liter, was I think $17. Which for a beer of that quality is quite reasonable.

    The service was generally good and prompt. I generally prefer to stick to what's on tap when out, and I was at least vaguely familiar with most of the taps. So I wasn't asking really looking for detailed advice.

    All in all a quite positive experience.

    3.5 stars

    I couldn't pick a better spot than Eulogy for some good beer.  The selection is absolutely unbelievable, though the draught menu could be more extensive.  The server was also pretty knowledgeable and provided some recommendations and his thought on each one.

    My complaint is that when we said we were there for beer and might only order an appetizer, we were told that if more people showed up we'd have to leave.  This irritated me quite a bit.  The menu is so minimal, it seems more of a way to feed you while you drink than to really be a showcase.  How can you possibly offer a selection of well over 200 Belgian and craft brews, and then shun people when they don't want anything from the minimal food menu?  I just don't understand the difference between four people spending $20+ each on beers and four people spending $20+ each on food.

    When we first arrived it wasn't crowded at all (a huge improvement over Monk's) with a great laid back vibe, and we eagerly started scouring the beer list.  As soon as we were "encouraged" to order food, we were turned off for the rest of our time there and felt rushed.  We had about two rounds, and probably would've ordered more if we had felt welcome.

    Eulogy is easily one of my favorite "beer bars" in the world.  And I have been to A LOT of beer bars.

    I visited Eulogy for the third time or so since moving to Philly because I had some out-of-town visitors in for a concert.  We wandered aimlessly around historic Old City for a while before settling in for a few drinks.

    Everyone in the group was pleased with our orders and the bartender even recommended what he considers to be the finest american saison.  He may or may not be right, but it was certainly delicious.

Specialties

This is Beer Heaven, give a "high praise" Eulogy for your favorite beer after you consume it.  We also prepare our food in the authentic Flemish Belgian Style as opposed to other Belgian restaurants who choose the French Walloon Belgian style.  This means our fries are thick enough to truly hold a double fry technique, our fries go "crunch, goo" because they are the right thickness, the wimpy skinny fries elsewhere go "goo goo goo" because they are too skinny and the double fry technique does not work.  Also our mussels are larger than the PEIs other restaurants use, this is because our mussels are the authentic North Sea size like in Belgium.  The more meat the more flavor the mussel can hold in our ala minute pan technique (all ingredients added fresh, never ever ever any pour over sauce on mussels).  Also our handburgers are award winning based on seven secret spices and our fish and chips and other entrees are as good as the beers ! Make a reservation at www.eulogybar.com

History

Established in 2002.

My family is from Brugge, Belgium, I am passionate about Belgian beer, food and heritage, this adds an authenticity to the business.  I got the building in 2002 and built the restaurant myself using antique stain glass behind the bar from an old Philadelphia church and a chandelier from an old church as well.  The bar was constructed in Fishtown in Philadelphia by a local carpenter using Honduras Mahogany I imported and the design of the bar was based on a blueprint I drew.  
We take our beer very very very serious, our beer starts in a beer cooler and is then delivered via dual gas blenders using co2 and nitrogen blends for the proper carbonation technique.  Most of our draft beer never travels more than six feet but along that journey there are glycol filled lines keeping the beer at 40 ish degrees until it hits your glass.  We have about 200 different glasses we keep in stock because most of the taste for beer is accomplished through your smelling it, yes your nose!
