    Listed in 2013 Yelp 100 Bonanza Challenge, 5-Star Reviews, Bucket List, Italian, Philadelphia Freedom

    Dante & Luigi's, was established in 1899 and is one of the oldest existing Italian restaurants in the United States. Situated in the heart of the Philadelphia's Italian Market district, it was once the destination of Italian immigrants arriving in Philadelphia.

    The restaurant is housed inside of two converted town houses / brownstones at the corner of 10th Street and Catharine Street, and it reflects much of the Old World charm from that era. Walking through the front door you see row upon row of dining awards including a 5-Star from Zagat's and several Best of Philadelphia awards.

    The menu is a stunning mixture of homemade Italian favorites with fresh seafood, and some amazing daily specials. The wine menu is a very pleasant blend of red and wine Italian wines, competitively priced to add to the enjoyment of your meal.

    We were there for dinner, and started with a bottle of Italian Pinot Noir and freshly-baked Italian bread. Next was a small house salad (Romaine lettuce, tomatoes & olives) with a light oil & vinegar dressing. For my main course I ordered Veal Saltimboca which was medallions of veal with fresh sage, proscuitto, mushrooms & provolone cheese in a sherry sauce, and that came with a small side of penne pasta in Italian gravy (marinara sauce).

    We finished this excellent meal by splitting Tira Misu which is the classic Italian dessert of Lady fingers, mascarpone cheese, creamy egg custard, flavored  with  Ameretto, Espresso & Kahlua. Oh yes, please.....I'd like 7 or 8 of these to go!!!!!

    Lovely restaurant.

    The lady at the front (it seemed like she was right at home here) was very sweet and made me feel right at home as well.  When I told her that some of our party of 6 (driving in from Jersey) had run into traffic and would be late, she smiled and said it was no problem.

    Many of the past reviews mentioned impolite waiters, but I wanted to walk in without any bias on the matter.  The waiter was very sweet, even splitting the check for each couple.  However, we got the impression they wanted us out of here very quickly and made us feel somewhat uncomfortable.  Perhaps they're not used to having guests younger than 30.  Perhaps they were annoyed that we didn't order any wine.  Overall the atmosphere is very nice and homelike.  Great for an date!

    There were some people in suits, but there were others in tshirts and jeans (multiple groups actually)  Not sure if they underdressed or this is how the customers usually dress.  The inside of the restaurant and the fact that they have valet parking ($10) make me think it's too classy for tshirts and jeans. The boyfriend and I took the train/walked so we didn't need to use the valet, but the man running it seemed like a sweetheart.

    The waiter started off by giving us a rundown of the soups, fish, and specials of the day.  He was also happy to give a recommendation to those who were unsure of what to order (will get into the actual food in a bit).  They gave us fresh bread (and bottles of balsamic vinegar and olive oil), giving us more as soon as it was almost gone, which was nice and shows how attentive they were.  

    Appetizers:
    Eggplant Parmagiana: delicious, with chunks of actual tomato and not too salty like some Marinara that I've had.  Eggplant very nicely cooked.
    Fried Calamari: very good for the first few bites, but a bit too greasy after a while to finish.  Squid was very thinly sliced, and breading was also thin.
    Stuffed Portobello Mushroom with Crabmeat: A bit difficult to share as crabmeat tends to fall apart easily.  My friend said that it was good but there was more mushroom than crab.

    Entrees:
    Perciatelli Genovese: This is what I got.  The sauce was very creamy and tasty (lots of meat!) but the pasta was a bit too thick for my liking and was a bit difficult to eat.  Big portions! Took the rest home because I was just so full.
    Fettucini Filetto: YUM. They don't skim on the meat in this one.
    Veal...not sure but I think it was the "T-Bone" Veal Chop: friend enjoyed it
    Gnocchi Romano (?): now, this was the recommendation of the waiter.  My friend isn't into very doughy pastas, so she did not like this at all.  The sauce, however, was very good with the bread.  It's a bit unfortunate that it wasn't to her liking.  Said it tasted like mac-n-cheese

    Desserts: YUM. Great presentation
    Dark side of the moon: This is what the boyfriend and I got because he loves chocolate.  Very good choice on his part.  The icing was dark chocolate so it wasn't disgustingly sweet like some icings you eat.  Fluffy and nice, just the right amount of of sweetness to end the meal on a good note.
    Lemon Sorbet: Large glass, no outspoken praises or complaints from the couple who got this
    Ricotta Cheese Cake: Apparently more batter-y and cake like than cheese creamy, somewhat like the cheese cakes you would get at Chinatown.

    Next time, will definitely try the rum cake and tiramisu.  Was far too full for that though!  

    Also, don't forget to bring cash or a check! (3.5% charge for using a credit card)

Specialties

Established in 1899.. Dante & Luigi's is Philadelphia"s OLDEST Italian restaurant; and one of the oldest existing Italian restaurants in the United States; specializing in all homemade Traditional Italian dishes. Specialty items such as OssoBuco, Homemade Lasagna, Crepe Manicotti , Homemade Gnocchi, a 20 oz, TBone of Veal, along with fresh seafood, veal, chicken, and ofcourse, traditional Italian Gravy and Meatballs. This is a Philadelphia landmark, and truly the most authentic and delicious restaurant in the city. Lovely "Olde World " atmosphere in their 150 yr. old townhouse setting.

History

Established in 1899.

Philadelphia's Oldest Italian restaurant. Italian Immigrents would arrive at the docks, unable to speak english, with the name of the restaurant pinned to their lapels. They would then be brought to the restaurant to live on the upper floors, which was then a boarding house, and work in the Italian Market district or in the restaurant itself. Today 111 yrs, later, the Philadelphia landmerk continues to serve only the most authentic and homemade dishes. The tradition continues.

Meet the Business Owner

Connie L. Business Owner

Dante & Luigi's is owned by Michael & Connie LaRussa. Michael, born in Sicily, learned traditional italian recipies handed down for generations,and oversees the preparation of these dishes, by his chefs , in the restaurant. Connie coordinates the running of the business and staff.
