    Stopped by here after checking out some local bookstores and strolling around. Looked like a nice place to sit, read some newly-acquired books, and people-watch.

    Was surprised to find some attempts at fusion on the menu, including Korean BBQ duck tacos and banh mi with french fries.  I asked my server which one he would get and he emphatically recommended the banh mi.

    The sandwich that arrived lacked any resemblance to banh mi as I know it.  The bread was a fluffy roll, rather than a crispy baguette.  The shredded carrots and slices of pepper tasted like leftover cole slaw.  The sriracha mayo had no bite.  The meat seemed to be sauteed pork.  No fresh herbs, spicy zing, or rich meaty flavors.

    That said, the bartender made a fine cocktail, the service was good, and I contentedly read and watched passers-by on South Street.  I'd just recommend ordering the classic items on the menu (burgers, etc.).

    A few friends and I had decided to stop at Bridget Foy's for a late night dinner. Walking in on a whim and having a big appetite for yummy in our tummies, we happily walked in and were seated in a booth for a group of five at around 10:45

    At the beginning of our service, he was a fairly good waiter. He was polite, cracked a few jokes, and was pretty attentive to serving our group. We had put in our huge order consisting of cheese steak croquettes, duck tacos, lamb meatballs, four different kinds of burgers, and the mac and cheese. The food was good and it was all very delicious. The duck tacos had a Vietnamese banh mi taste to them because of the pickled vegetables, the croquettes were little fried balls of Philly's essence served with jalapeno ketchup, and the lamb meatballs came in a hot plate that was complemented with small motza balls, goat cheese, and tomatoes. This all came out within ten minutes of ordering, it was quick and delicious. Five minutes after finishing the appetizers, our entrees came.

    The burgers were yummy but could have been a little juicier, but the buns definitely complimented the burger itself, being fluffy but a little crispy at the same time. The mac and cheese came in a hot plate, topped off with a potato chip crust. It was gooey, savory, salty, and crunchy all in one, it was definitely the star out of all of the dishes. At this point into the service, our server started to slack a little seeing as it was close to closing time. Our drinks were refilled less frequently and it took a little while for our plates to be taken. But that's really not a big deal, the big deal comes later on into the night.

    We decided to order dessert even though we were stuffed beyond belief. We had ordered the croissant bread pudding topped with Bassetts ice cream. It took about fifteen minutes for the dish to arrive but it was gooey and delicious. Although the pudding itself was good, the ice cream made it taste so much better and you really had a hard time enjoying the pudding itself without the ice cream, so it could've used a little bit more ice cream on top, but still a good dish in itself.

    At this point, we had finished our dinner and waited for the check. It took a fairly long time for our waiter to come and give us the check. Four out of five of us didn't have cash so we had to pay a little bit of the bill with cash and then split the rest of the bill four ways. I understand, this can be a real big pain, but as a server you still have to get the bill paid in some way, shape or form, and seeing how they already tacked on a gratuity, our server was already making 18% of our bill so he shouldn't have a big problem at all. (And also, I really liked his service at this point and I was ready to tack on a few more dollars into the tip) He comes over and I politely ask him to please split the bill between the four cards and I apologized for the inconvenience. He looks at the cards with an angry look and scoffed "Sure."

    After all is said and done, he comes back with the check book and throws it in front of me and storms away. I barely even got in a "thank you" before he was already at the other end of the room. I sat there in disbelief that this guy had the nerve to throw something at a customer. I've never had any one be more disrespectful than this one guy. We sat there and basically said to each other that if we could subtract the gratuity, we definitely would. But we couldn't so I just left him a little note, telling him how rude he was and we just left.

    This experience started off as a great dining experience and it just went down hill at the end. I would've easily given four stars but I believe service at a restaurant is one of the biggest factors in a dining experience and obviously, being scolded at is not great service. So I'm taking it down to two stars, it's a lot, I know, but I believe it is fairly reasonable.

If you are into food, other places in the neighborhood trump this one.
