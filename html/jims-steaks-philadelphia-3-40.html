    Little did I know that I was in for a treat. I was taken to Jim's Steaks by a native-Philadelphian. I asked what makes the Philly cheese steak so unique.     On a first-time Philly visit, we decided to try out Pat's, Geno's, and Jim's... and agreed that we liked the sandwiches at Jim's the best.

    Pat's had the most meat, but the bread wasn't very good. Geno's had less meat, but a bit better bread. The bread wasn't toasted at either places, though.

    Jim's had the tastiest meat, toasted bread, and much fresher ingredients overall than either Pat's or Geno's. They also have upstairs seating (the other places have outdoor seating).

    The one major drawback here is the wait. We had to wait 45 minutes... something that was worth it for a first-time or occasional visitor, but probably not for a frequenter. They only had one person doing the actual cooking on one grill, and the line would stall at intervals while he waited for the meat to fully cook. Pat's and Geno's, on the other hand, was incredibly efficient. The lines were much shorter (when we were there around lunch time), but they also went very quickly overall (we had about a 5 min. wait). Still, on another visit, I'd probably return here, but not to Pat's or Geno's.

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
