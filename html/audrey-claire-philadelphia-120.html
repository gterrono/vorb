    Listed in BYOBs in Philly, 68 and counting..., Rittenhouse revelry, Places to eat and drink outside, No reservations needed

    It's a simple BYOB, in a good way.  The decor is minimalist... plain white walls, wooden tables, plain glasses for water and with smaller versions of the same glass for your (BYO) wine.  The food is also pretty straightforward, but without being ordinary.  

    The chicken Milanese topped with arugula, lemon, and roma tomatoes was delicious. The chocolate lava cake with strawberries and home made whipped cream was good, but a bit bland for my taste.   Last time I visited, I had the baby romaine Caesar salad and the pomegranite chicken, which were both tasty.  The menu online is just a sampling, but fairly representative of what they offer on any given night.

    Prices are reasonable, considering it is mere blocks from Rittenhouse Square.  Many entrees are $18-22.

    They are cash only, and take reservations during the week.  We didn't know this, and thus did not make a reservation tonight.  Luckily, we got the last 3 seats at the kitchen counter area.  Every seat in the place was full!

    Light, healthy, and minimal.

    I could just stop the review right there, but let's explore this a bit more. Audrey Claire was the go-to spot for my girlfriend's birthday with all her friends. We had a reservation during Restaurant Week, which means three course meal for $35. We also had some drinks, and there was no
    corkage fee, awesome!

    Light - Audrey Claire makes dishes that, I feel, I could eat on a bed of clouds. They don't strive for BOLD IN YOUR FACE flavor, but instead, they marry a couple of ingredients very perfectly. It is a nice change of pace from all of the high powered dishes I've had lately.

    The night started off with a round of appetizers, and mine being the Bosc Pear and Gorgonzola Flatbread w/ Toasted Sunflower Seeds. Flatbreads are the "in" thing lately, and this version pleasured my taste buds with great justice. It was crispy from the thin flatbread, and sweet from the cooked pears. The Gorgonzola cheese added that earthy stank to the dish, which was amazing. The seeds added a subtle crunch. My favorite dish of the night, easily.

    Healthy - The method of cooking these dishes were really clean and healthy. There was never a sign of excess fat or oil, and it felt good to eat, although their portions are bigger than I had expected!

    Next, I enjoyed the Shallot Crusted Lemon Sole w/ Crispy Sage and Caper Butter. The beautiful crust was really crispy, and I enjoyed that very much. The sole was cooked well and flaked perfectly upon my fork. I was not too fond of the sides accompanying the fish, just because I am not a big veggie eater. The dish was still worth it.

    I also had a chance to taste my girlfriend's pork chop. Really tasty and still juicy, great job.

    Minimal - The team at Audrey Claire strives for simplicity. You can see by the plates that having minimal amount of ingredients really push the natural and fresh flavors of each.

    For dessert, I had the banana bread pudding? I was really full at this point and barely finished it. This could have been better.

    Overall, the experience was dandy. A fun night and the service was great. She answered all the questions we had and was super friendly.

    Audrey Claire was the first restaurant I visited in Philadelphia when I came here on vacation about five years ago.  I had the pappardelle with portobello mushrooms (which continues to be my favorite) and when I returned years later I scoured the streets for the "tiny BYO with the open kitchen and big windows and the perfect papardelle."   I was thrilled when I re-discovered the restaurant on Spruce Street and found it unchanged in charm.  

    The service is classic:  polite but not overly chatty. The menu is seasonal, so any vegetable, root, or fruit on the menu is at the apogee of ripeness and full of flavor.  This time around, we started with a pumpkin, beet and goat cheese antipasti - vegetables which we spooned like ice cream.  I opted away from my favorite pappardelle dish to try something new and ordered the potato crusted ahi tuna.  I thought it was good, but it did not approach the perfection of the ahi tuna steak at Amis.  Though perhaps this was in part due to the fact that I paired it with Chardonnay (the one downside to BYO's is you choose the wine first, and feel somewhat confined in ordering dishes), I still think the Amis version superior.  The pasta at Audrey Claire, however, remains a favorite.  Also, the maple-brined pork chop that my boyfriend ordered was absolutely delicious--I might even say the best pork in town.  I fawned over my boyfriend's choice and stole as many bites as I could.  

    I would highly recommend this restaurant for dates -- as the tables are so close together and intimate that you feel like you are spilling into your neighbors --lending itself to great people-watching, conversation-fodder and no awkward silences.  Also a great place to go with friends for lively chatter, but no business dinners here.
