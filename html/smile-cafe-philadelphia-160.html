    I was *this close to giving Smile 5 stars. First 2 deliveries and eat-in's were nothing short of amazing. The food packs much more flavor than you expect and will blow your taste buds away. The prices are exceedingly reasonable and service is good. Far and above the best Thai I've had in Philly. So why not 5 stars?

    The last time I got take-out the spicy noodles were unbearably hot. The only other time I've had food this hot was at an Indian restaurant where I asked for the spice level to be "as hot as you can make it" and then sent the dish back for lack of spice. The dish that returned could start a wildfire. So the takeout from Smile was ordered to be spicy, but it was so ridiculously over-spiced that I drained the noodles 3 times and still couldn't eat it. Hopefully it was an anomaly but I have to ding them a star.

    Their spicy noodles are to die for (if it doesn't kill you first) and their curry is exceptional. You won't find a better bang for buck in center city.

    We ordered delivery and it was absolutely TERRIBLE. My dinner would have to be free or all the other options would have to be closed for me to try this place again. It was a complete waste of money.

    We ordered coconut soup, papaya salad, pad thai, pad see ew, and seafood fried rice. Here's the breakdown:

    Coconut soup - The broth was flavorful enough (slightly over salted), but the shrimp was inedible. We hypothesized they may have even used old shrimp because it was just that bad (very tough and tasted off) .

    Papaya salad - This may have been dressed with water. There was no flavor whatsoever.

    Pad Thai - The noodles were so overcooked that they were turning to mush. The flavor was also unrecognizable. You can't just throw rice noodles, a dried lime, scallions, bean sprouts, and chopped peanuts into a plastic container with a weird brown sauce and tell me it's pad thai.

    Pad See Ew - I'm pretty sure this was covered with watered down soy sauce. There were barely any vegetables or meat, so it was really just another helping of mushy, bland noodles.

    Seafood fried rice - This was edible, not delicious by any means, but edible. OR maybe we were just so hungry at this point that we were giving SOMETHING a pass. I really don't know, but this was the only thing that we even made a dent in eating since it had SOME flavor and most of the rice were still recognizable grains.

    It was SAD to see my favorite cuisine being passed off in this way. I don't think you can call this Thai food or even "modern" Thai food unless you're trying to be disrespectful. Though the process of ordering over the phone was pleasant enough (spoke to a friendly-sounding person who didn't rush through the order), the quality of the food we received was just so bad that I won't be a customer here again.

    We came for a late dinner and very much enjoyed the food and ambiance.  It is a tiny restaurant but cozy and our dishes were great:
    - The garlic and black pepper stir fry was light and savory, although I did not see the black pepper.  
    -The red curry tofu was solid with a spicy and creamy broth, and the pad see ew was perfectly seasoned and served with a six jumbo shrimp.  
    -The chicken pad thai was very good as well with plenty of chicken and perfectly cooked sweet and savory noodles; the portion size was quite generous and is enough to last two meals, at least.  
    -The grilled calamari appetizer was also a hit as it was tender with a slight char and served with a tasty slightly tangy peanut dipping sauce.  
    -The chicken satay was decent but pretty standard.
    -The coconut soups were a hit but the tofu vegetable soup was literally 5 tiny cubes of tofu in a vegetable broth.  

    Overall, the meal was great and service was fantastic.  We ended up talking until 10:30 before we realized that they actually close at 10 pm since they were kind enough to not even ask us to leave!

    I ordered take-out for lunch because my favorite go-to Thai place was closed.  I was super hungry and all I was craving was noodles...my expectations were not that high.  Yet still the food managed to disappoint.  

    The drunken noodles seemed a bit dry to be "drunken".  There wasn't any sauce to be seen or tasted, except the occasional pocket of oil.  The noodles were mushy and stuck together in one giant lump, more like potatoes au gratin.  They were lukewarm, so I started eating without worrying about it being too hot, except suddenly I found myself scrambling for water because I hit a hot spot so hot it burned the top of my mouth.  What this tells me is that this was sitting around and microwaved when I ordered.  On top of that, the steamed dumpling was doughy and tasteless, despite that it was floating in sauce and had some sort of filling (pork??).  I have never in my life thrown away a steamed dumpling, but today was a first.

    I don't see any point in going back.  This was hands-down the worst Thai food I've had.

We finished off lunch with fried bananas and ice cream. It was pretty darn good! I love my sweets and it wasn't greasy at all.  I would come back again. 3.5 stars!     I really wanted to give Smile four stars--I really and truly did. Went here on a date last night, after not having gone in at least a year. I had used to go to Smile a lot with friends before seeing a show at First Unitarian, right around the corner (rest easy, old friend), and remembered it as a really solid Thai spot. Which it certainly still is!

    I'm a huge fan of the Pineapple Fried Rice (no Shrimp), and will get it every time. When we arrived, my friend and I were the only people in (around 6:00pm), and got the great seat by the window. Our server was speedy in the beginning, but then as the night got longer, his visits to our table came less and less--to the point where other diners who had arrived after us had gotten their to-go boxes and gone, all while we were clearly waiting on the server. A mild annoyance, but still, could have been a little more attentive.

    I'll definitely still be coming to Smile, it's a great little place that's off of a lot of people's radar for being located where it is. Three solid stars, nothing to be ashamed of!
