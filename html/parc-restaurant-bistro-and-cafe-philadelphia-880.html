    Well, you're paying for the view and location as much as you are for the food, but the food itself it was very good.

    A friend and I went for an early breakfast on Sunday morning (that is, right when they opened). We got a window seat overlooking the park. Even though it's early spring and there wasn't much to see yet, it was still nice because the sky was clear and blue, and we could people watch.

    I had the Eggs Benedict, one of my favorite dishes of all time, and it was very good compared to other Eggs Benedicts that I've had in other cities. The eggs were cooked perfectly--when I broke the yoke with fork, the yoke ran all over the toasted English muffins and ham.     The coffee was very good, and we got refills often.

    I can see how some people might think that the waitstaff are a little snooty, but it didn't bother me because it didn't seem to affect how often my coffee was refilled or how good the food was.

    If you haven't traveled to Europe and dined in a Parisian bistro-- then go dine at Parc, its honestly the next best thing. Now I may be bias since I live next door to the restaurant, but I've never been more impressed with the consistency I've enjoyed at Parc both with the food and the service. I day dream about their French Onion soup and I bring all my friends here to try it :) The location on Rittenhouse Square is perfect, they take advantage of the location with outdoor seating basically year round. The outdoor heat lamps are turned on when it gets chilly and in the spring and summer the floor to ceiling windows are opened making the entire restaurant fill with fresh air. They open early and stay open. I love coming in for breakfast or brunch on the weekends or grabbing dessert and a drink late at night.
    The prices may be more than you are used to spending, but there is a nice balance on the menu of lower priced items and all the food is exceptional so when the check arrives you'll gladly pay the price.

    For breakfast I always enjoy their Eggs Benedict, the eggs are poached perfectly and the side of potatoes are always cooked just right. Not too much hollandaise either so you won't feel too guilty afterwards. Otherwise the breakfast pastry basket is a nice small sampling and the French omelet is light. Quiche is always a special treat and the French Toast is one of the larger sized breakfast items.

    For lunch I adore the Chicken Paillard Salad or the Warm Shrimp Salad. More affordable lunch items include the Croque Madame, Macaroni Au Gratin, French Onion soup, Cheeseburger, or Mozzarella and Tomato Baguette.

    For dinner the Beef Borguinon is so tender you can cut it with your fork. The Moules Frites are a wonderful combination  of salty, crispy French fries with garlicly buttered mussels. The Steak Au Poivre and Steak Frites are classic favorites. And if you're watching your wallet the French omelet and Cheeseburger are also offered for dinner (as is the famous French Onion soup!)
    Hopefully you save room for a small but delightful dessert such as the Profiteroles or Apple Tart Tatin.

    Parc is one our favorite places to go when we want to sit outside and enjoy the scenery. Located and facing Rittenhouse Square the seating is just amazing, even in the fall months when you're a bit chilly - the outdoor heaters push you to take off the jacket and show some skin.

    This is one of Starrs' flagships, pushing 100s of covers on a daily shift - brunch, lunch, dinner. This must be one of the greatest cash cows in Philly and making him a bundle. This man can't lose! No wonder Philly magazine has graced their cover with his likeness and rated him one of the most powerful men in Philadelphia. He has revolutionized the restaurant scene and without him this town would be worse off.

    So now for Parc.

    Ambience:
    French Brasserie with incredible decor. The bathrooms, the bar, the seating arrangement, the everything. This place is amazing. The detail and style goes above and beyond what you want from any restaurant and is hands down my favorite American/French style restaurant in Philadelphia.

    STAFF
    Servers:
    Now here is where I will offer some constructive criticism. I've been served by the same waitress three times and every single time, she has been an absolute mess: RUDE, untimely, pretentious, and not very nice to a younger looking crowd who flashes some dough. She usually wears some weird 50s throwback white Raybans - look ridiculous btw - and doesn't seem to take pride in her work. Her and the GM seem to be buddy buddy so that always helps when you suck at your job.

    Bar:
    Bar staff is always a pleasure to talk to and anything you need just ask they'll give it to you. No complaints. Bartenders are always impeccably dressed and trained out the wazooo, so don't be afraid to ask questions.

    Management:
    There are a lot of rumors about the GM, or maybe the old GM (saw some recent ads for GM/Manager on CL). She is known to party and isn't susceptible to dealing with bullshit which i must say, isn't the most admirable quality of any manager. A GM must be able to throw on the hat, deal with anything that comes their way with a smile and a great attitude. I've seen her stumbling about town a few times - which isn't bad, but let's not let it affect your work. As for floor managers, never had a bad experience.

    Busstaff:
    Some diversity of ethnicity would go a long way for the perception of the restaurant. Seems to us that they're only hiring one ethic group, and that doesn't sit well with us.

    FOOD:
    Pretty f*in' amazing! It's simple but put together with some artistic flare. LOVE IT. A previous chef went over to Talula's Garden when they opened and that place is flourishing, not just with his help (name will come with the TG review) but with the help of the amazing management and co-ownership. Try anything. We love it all!

    HOST STAFF:
    There's a couple that just have attitude problems when you request specific tables. And hey, when we've been sitting at the same table every single time we go in, then for gods sake, is it that hard to change your seating chart when you return your incredibly easy job! One of our staff members even met one of the hostesses at an art event - she didn't even remember. Bad form. Starr is notorious for hiring incredibly beautiful host staff. I think that's all that matters to the organization. Brains come second.

    Overall:
    Great place but could use some attention when staff is concerned.

    Check it out, have a drink, and enjoy the scenery.

    Happy eating Philly!
    BEST - KS

Specialties

This stylish addition to Rittenhouse Square pays tribute to French cafe culture, serving breakfast, lunch and dinner seven days a week. Ideal for a quick bite or a leisurely meal, the menu is filled with classics: onion soup, escargots, steak frites and towering plateaus de fruits de mer. The baguettes are made in-house and  the wine list is expansive. Sip a Kir Royale at the zinc bar or enjoy a cafe au lait while basking in the sun.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
