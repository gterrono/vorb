    Not a bad place to hang out and have a few beers and even a bite to eat.  Three of us stepped in to get a bite, we read the menu that was posted in the front window, and it was quite easy to find something that each of us would enjoy.  The burgers and sandwiches were around $8 and there were some dinner fare around  $18, so something for different price ranges.

    The beer selection (which takes up more of the menu than the food) is pretty extensive.  Tons of Belgian beers, but also beers from other parts, including extensive micro brew options from across the U.S.  The menu even jokes they have some of the more traditional U.S. macro brews, but with a menu as deep and servers willing to help you find a beer that suits your taste, be adventurous.  

    The food was good, the service slightly surly, and the interior was nice...with a nice eclectic mix of tables, chairs and even a nice disco ball in the back of the upstairs dining area.  Not handicap friendly, but all others welcome.

    At first I was hesitant to pen a review as it is early on a Sunday morning and I should be contemplating more sleep; but one yelpers resent of what was maybe my favorite and most cynical detail of Eulogy prompted me to get to typing.

    Eulogy is tasteful in every sense of the word. As far as the food goes, the three cheese grilled cheese accompanied by their belgian frietjes was the perfect tavern din for myself. And considering it's in the heart of Old City I consider ten bucks a steal for a simple and yet decedent meal. My friends ordered a pound of their tasty mussels covered in some sort of dijon and white wine sauce. I'm not sure if the pricing is consistent year round, but they ordered the dish and were served a pound for nine bucks.

    As far as the atmosphere is concerned--it's dim and a little compressed; but certainly not uncomfortable. Our service was great and we happened to really luck out and get seats immediately (thanks to some kind group who decided to cancel, we were spared a 40 minute wait). Since the place is prone to a long wait (though totally worth the wait); especially on a Saturday night, I'd suggest calling in ahead of time.

    Now for the beer, OH SWEET MERCIFUL CRAP DO THEY HAVE A LOT OF BEER. I knew coming into Eulogy that they had a reputation for being beer heaven. I knew I should expect fine imports and good American beers; but I didn't know I'd be staring at three mouth watering pages of everything from lambics and gueuzes, to sexy stouts and "the TV AD beers." My friends and I didn't read the disclaimer about the tv beers as pretentiousness. This is a tasteful beer place, I suppose there's a degree of prestige in any specialty drink restaurant; but I think the intent is to humor you. Also, they totally mark the price of the crappy beers up to the point that they're almost the same as some of the other options; so you really have no choice but to experiment if you're unfamiliar. And if you honestly come to Eulogy looking for PBR and are offended at that statement; the Khyber is merely blocks away.

    Tired, hungry, and cranky from sitting in traffic for hours, our spring break trip with the fam was starting out pretty crappy. We dropped the bags at the hotel and commenced to wander down Market St.  looking for some grub.

    Walk...look, walk...look. Not too much open on Easter Sunday at 8pm. Decided to turn down a random street and try my mobile yelp. Lo and behold, Eulogy Belgian Tavern was tops on the list. We look up and see it across the street.

    Hesitant at first since we had the kiddos and it was a tavern, we looked at the menu posted on the window. One look at the beer selection and Yelp reviews of mussels with frites, and we were in.

    There was no wait, and we opted to sit upstairs. It was a little dark and hard to read the menu by candlelight, but it fit in with the whole tavern vibe. The beer bible was huge and pretty overwhelming, but we must've looked like the lost tourists that we were, so the waiter came over and gave some great recommendations. We ended up trying some Czech beers and a Belgian wheat beer. We also ordered 3 different types of mussels, an order of frites, and some chicken wings for the picky one.

    The mussels were fat and swimming in sauce. We tried the white wine, red wine, and beer/cheese/potato kind. All were great, but our fav was the standard white wine, garlic and butter variety. The frites were hot, crisp, and very good, especially dipped in the mussel broth. Ample bread was provided to sop everything up.

    There is no kid menu, and this place obviously caters to the drinking crowd, but we did not feel out of place at all. Food, drinks, and service were all fantastic. It was a great find to start off our Philly trip.

    After hearing a lot of good things, I decided to go to Eulogy this past Sunday. I was not disappointed.

    The wait staff was polite, friendly, and helpful. We got our food fast enough, but not too fast. In my opinion, the timing of the servings was perfect.

    The beer selection is nothing short of astounding. If I remember correctly, the beer list takes up 5 pages of the menu, which is AT LEAST half. This, my friends, is beer nirvana. Know what you want? Perfect, they probably have it. Don't know what you want? No problem, your waiter will be probably be able to make some good recommendations if you tell him or her what kind of beer you like.

    I have to admit, the food was a bit of a surprise. I honestly wasn't expecting amazing food seeing as how the star of Eulogy is the beer. My expectations were shattered: The burger, fries and wings that I had were pretty damn good. The burger was big, cooked right, and was full of flavor. The wings' flavor was on-par with most other wings I've had, but the chefs at Eulogy seemed to have the spice level nailed down. It's right on the money. The fries were a littler better than average. I just wish I got more of them.

    The prices are pretty reasonable I think. The price of beer largely depends on what you get, so you could either spend a lot of money, or relatively-little money. The burger/fries were $9, but they were damn tasty. I don't remember how much the wings were, but I don't think that they were that much.

    If you're looking for a cool place to grab some simple (yet delicious) food and get some different and unique beer, Eulogy is your place. I highly recommend it to everyone.

Specialties

This is Beer Heaven, give a "high praise" Eulogy for your favorite beer after you consume it.  We also prepare our food in the authentic Flemish Belgian Style as opposed to other Belgian restaurants who choose the French Walloon Belgian style.  This means our fries are thick enough to truly hold a double fry technique, our fries go "crunch, goo" because they are the right thickness, the wimpy skinny fries elsewhere go "goo goo goo" because they are too skinny and the double fry technique does not work.  Also our mussels are larger than the PEIs other restaurants use, this is because our mussels are the authentic North Sea size like in Belgium.  The more meat the more flavor the mussel can hold in our ala minute pan technique (all ingredients added fresh, never ever ever any pour over sauce on mussels).  Also our handburgers are award winning based on seven secret spices and our fish and chips and other entrees are as good as the beers ! Make a reservation at www.eulogybar.com

History

Established in 2002.

My family is from Brugge, Belgium, I am passionate about Belgian beer, food and heritage, this adds an authenticity to the business.  I got the building in 2002 and built the restaurant myself using antique stain glass behind the bar from an old Philadelphia church and a chandelier from an old church as well.  The bar was constructed in Fishtown in Philadelphia by a local carpenter using Honduras Mahogany I imported and the design of the bar was based on a blueprint I drew.  
We take our beer very very very serious, our beer starts in a beer cooler and is then delivered via dual gas blenders using co2 and nitrogen blends for the proper carbonation technique.  Most of our draft beer never travels more than six feet but along that journey there are glycol filled lines keeping the beer at 40 ish degrees until it hits your glass.  We have about 200 different glasses we keep in stock because most of the taste for beer is accomplished through your smelling it, yes your nose!
