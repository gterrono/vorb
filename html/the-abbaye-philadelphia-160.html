    Swung by here recently with some friends for some drinks/snacks.  I've been here a few times, and happen to quite fancy the ambiance, affordable prices, awesome servers, and VEGETARIAN FRIENDLY gastropub goodness.  On this particular evening, i had tired of beer (a rarity in my world) so i opted to drink the Abbaye Sangria.  It was $5 a pint, and quite tasty.  It was white Sangria which i tend to steer clear of, but this was well worth the exception.  The fruit wasn't just floating around, but it had been blended up and so my Sangria has the consistency of pulpy OJ.  I could totally see how someone would hate this, but I really dug it.  Fiberous booze!  My mother would be proud.  

    That fruit sure packed a boozy punch too.  After a bit, I decided to get an order of the vegan wings.  They were REALLY good.  I loved how they were crispy, and saucy, and really really tasty.  That sort of texture is another tough one to find amongst the vegetarian community.  I've had the seitan cheesesteak here before too and I must say its equally as delicious.

    All in all- great little watering hole with lots of fun booze and food!

    This place is really climbing into my list of favorite bars in the city.  Each time I go it is better than the last.

    The food here is the star.  The portions are huge, so come prepared to take something home.  Vegetarians or vegans are treated too nicely here; the menu is very veggie friendly.  Not to say meat-eaters won't be happy.  I looked over and saw some guy had ordered a fried chicken entree which was enormous.  The breading looked perfectly done - not too thick, but very substantial.  My favorite item is the Seitan Cheesesteak.  I get it with cheese (at no extra cost), and it is THE best vegetarian cheesesteak in town!  Hands-down the closest thing to the real thing.  Blackbird gets a lot of love, and I do love Blackbird for their vegan wings and pizza, but Abbaye's cheesesteak is more authentic.  It's greasier, the bread is crispy on the outside and chewy on the inside (Blackbird's is too crunchy throughout), and the "meat" is cut thin and chopped; just like what you might see at Dalessandro's (the best real cheesesteak in Philly).  My wife didn't like it as much as me because the greasier, meatier texture turned her off, but for more recent vegetarians (like me) or for people who want the true experience and not just another dry cube-cut seitan cheesesteak, this is the best one around.

    I love the service at Abbaye.  The bartenders are friendly and attentive and the waitstaff have never let me down.  There is usually someplace to sit, even on weekends, because the bar has a fairly large inside seating area in addition to its sidewalk tables.  Service is key - if your service is rude it doesn't matter how good the food is.  In addition, the draft list is well done with selections from all over the US and always features a good local craft brew.  Abbaye doesn't disappoint.  I will be making this a regular stop from now on.

Not only are the servers/bartenders here kind, but you can really tell that these people work hard. They make sure that your glass is always full, and that you have just what you need at your table. They are also great because they are always willing to work with you if you come with a group. On more than one occasion they have moved tables out of their normal position to accommodate my group, and I really appreciate that because many places will not do that.

    While the menu is a bit small for my liking, everything on it is a good choice. I've had the wings, mussels, crabcake sandwich, Mac and cheese , and a few other things from the specials menu here, and I've always been impressed.

    Last but not least, this place is affordable. Their happy hour is awesome (although I wish it was 5-7 rather than 4-6) and their features brewery pint nights are also a great deal.

    I like that the Abbaye is a tad out of the way of the Piazza-goers so it can still be somewhat of a hidden gem for neighborhood folks only!
