    I know you are waiting of an awesome review about this place but unfortunately chef garces did not hit the mark with this place.

    The place is nicely designed; cheese and deli meats on the side; coffee/desserts on one corner and a wine store on the right.  I was told there was a huge discrepency about the BYOB policy and then having a wine store inside the same restaurant.  How does that make any sense?! IF you decide to buy a bottle of wine at the restaurant, the money spent on a bottle is still going into chef garces pocket. VERY cleaver ;)

    We were at the cut mark to try out the $25 tasting menu for lunch and had to do the $40 dinner tasting menu and also included the $20 wine pairing.

    the $40 dinner tasting menu included the following:
    (please ingore the names of each dish as I'm trying to recall each dish)

    3 cheeses and prosciutto platter with pairing sauces - Nothing wrong with this one but disliked one of the musky goat cheeses offered.  

    Baby radishes served with butter and olive oil- I can't believe this miniscule dish was even called a dish.  The 9 baby radishes were sooooooo tiny that I started to think I was going to get ripped off by the main entree.  

    Octopus with fennel - I love octopus but this one was so salty, I thought I was eating a salt lick.  I'm assuming there was no quality control in the kitchen on this dish...not edible at all.

    Dumplings with ricotta cheese filling:  I might be misinterpreting the name of these dishes but this one was pretty good.  My only compliant is the portion sizes - way too small to share!

    French chicken thigh/legs (smoked/bbqed?!) - at first, this dish looked amazing and figured I was getting my money's worth. As I started to taste it, it reminded me of eating a turkey hotdog. Both chicken thighs/legs were way under cooked.  As we got to the center of the meat and bone area...you can definitely tell the dish needed a few more minutes in the oven.

    Dessert - Asst. macaroons, chocolate brownie? etc: Did not dislike anything from this platter.  Can't go wrong with macaroons...the salted caramel was my favorite.

    The $20 wine pairing was pretty good - 1 sparkling, 1 white and 1 red was well worth the $20 considering how much they would charge per glass.

    I would advise to steer away from the tasting menus and stick to an entree given on the menu.  Maybe my experience would 've been better but unfortunately this place did not hit the mark for me.  This is where my chef garces dining experience ended in Philly.

    Listed in Celebrity Chefs are Overrated

    Very large party went for a birthday dinner.

    Note: Any group larger than 10 must get the tasting menu.  We opted to have 2 separate groups instead.

    Interesting concept with cheese, coffee, and wine sold inside the restaurant.

    Service is attentive without being intrusive.  We went on a weeknight, so the place was not busy at all, so stress level was not very high.
    Corking fee is $10/ bottle.  

    Food:
    Bread: soft, homemade, one basket had fresh garlic in it.  Very good extra virgin olive oil.  Simplicity at its best.  

    1.  Chef's pairing cheese plate with 3 cheeses and 3 accoutrement.  Manchego-like spanish hard sheep's milk cheese:  my favorite of the 3, served with a rhubarb preserve
    A domestic cow's milk cheese similar to brie, which name escapes me.  served with a very good mustard and
    Roquefort:  French bleu cheese: salty and surprisingly tasty, even though i'm not a huge fan of bleu cheeses.  

    Dinner:
    I had the Orechiette di Mare, little shells with a pesto-like sauce with seafood and plum tomatoes.  Pasta was al dente.  Seafood was flavorful and fresh, but not too much.  The sauce was a little too salty.  The tomatoes were very fresh and tart, but I like that.  I can see others being turned off by it.  

    Other dishes we tried:  
    Deep dish pizza: the sauce is amazing.  I want to go back for this.
    Verde pizza:  fresh and interesting.  

    Salads looked very fresh.  
    Charcuterie selection looked wonderful from across the table.  

    Dessert:
    I had the Assiete de Chocolate, which is 3 different layers of panna cotta, extremely decadent chocolate sponge on top, and white chocolate on top with a macaron and chocolate covered praline, all served in a little dessert cup.  Like a party in your mouth!  
    Each layer of panna cotta is unique and complements the other.  The bottommost layer is tangy with a slight fizz, almost like yogurt.  The middle layer is smooth and rich.  The topmost layer is chocolate, which is slightly salty, bringing out all the flavors.  The white chocolate on top is light and sweet.  

    Conclusion: will go back to try the deep dish pizza and charcuterie and drown myself in the Assiete de Chocolate.

    There's a bit of a disconnect between what Garces intends as "a casual sit-down meal" and the $50 pp you'd have to drop for an appetizer/soup/salad, an entree and a glass of wine.

    That being said, the food was amazing and worth the $50 I dropped. The ambiance is mixed as part of the place is a deli style market, with a sit down cafe portion. This food is certainly worthy of a classier dining experience.

    I started with the Vichyssoise Chaude which was served table side. A shallow bowl with potato, leek, scallop, bacon, and truffle shavings, and then the server poured the hot chowder into the bowl. It was rich, complex, and amazing (though I still contend that using truffles is culinary cheating).

    I also had the Pappardelle With Lamb Ragu--long and wide pasta with slow cooked lamb with sunchoke puree, peas, and piave vechio (which is Italian for fancy parmesan). Lots of great flavors if you like lamb, but not too heavy. Each bite dances a little in your mouth with layers of flavors playing off each other.

    Honorable mention that I almost got, was the Sunday night special of ox-tail and braised short rib lasagna.

Specialties

Garces Trading Company is a full-service bar and restaurant focused on Iron Chef Jose Garces' own favorite dishes from his travels to cafes throughout Western Europe, with a focus on Italy, Spain and France.  Our thoughtful menu of cafe staples is served throughout the day, and our bar offers a wealth of cocktails, wines and beers that pair beautifully with our menu.  Our on-site PLCB Wine Boutique, the first of its kind, makes a great resource either before sitting down to dinner (corkage fees apply) or to complement one of our decadent gift baskets, available in our newly redesigned retail section.

History

Established in 2010.

Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a refined, Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, a contemporary American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago, IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale, AZ and Palm Springs, CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the landmark Revel Resort. Today, in addition to our much-lauded restaurants around the country, Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
