    What can I say that hasn't been said here before? Consider this my love letter to Kanella's brunch.  I start by way of confession -- I don't love brunch.     Listed in Philly Favorites!

    Succulent Food.  Brilliant service.  Comforting decor.  BYO.  Favorite all-around restaurant in Philadelphia.  5 stars for sure.

    I've come here dozens of times in the past few years and have yet to have a bad experience here.  Everything on the menu is made with fresh, organic, natural ingredients.  I find that many restaurants can not produce high quality across the entire range of proteins, however, fish, lamb, goat, chicken, beef, octopus and vegetarian dishes are superbly cooked and burst with exciting flavor combinations.  I tend to try the specials, which can range from rabbit stew to bull testicles.  For the less adventurous, the Cyprus breakfast or fisherman's stew are tasty.  Try the ice cream.  And the French Press.  And.. everything else.

    Service is equally excellent.  Orders are taken promptly, with daily specials rattled off from memory.  The team always seems happy and they never rush you out even when they are booked solid.  They seat about 40 guests and with warm weather, some outdoor seating is available.  While the space isn't large, I don't feel cramped or want of more space.

    Wonderful for dates, special occasions, or when you simply crave excellent food.  Reasonably priced for all of this.  Did I mention it's my favorite?  Cheers!

    I'm upgrading my review to 5 stars because I surely just experienced some kind of culinary transcendence last night at their Sunday night meze family supper.  

    This is how Kanellas's brilliant Sunday meze supper works.  You show up with your favorite bottle of wine, and they'll sit you down without a menu. You sit in delicious anticipation, and then one of their wonderful servers will come tell you about some highlights of the menu with a fresh loaf of bread and a lentil- or chickpea-based dip. Then...for the next two hours or so, let go of your preconceived notions of what you like or not like to eat, and just go with it.  If at all possible, go with half vegetarian and half meat option so you get the greatest variety. At $35 (meat) and $30 (veg) pp, I think this is the best dinner deal in town.  

    I can't list all of the dishes, but I think there were about 16 in all...offered in two sets (appetizer and main).  

    I'm sure the dishes change with the seasons but some highlights: succulent, braised chicken legs, mussels with rice, fragrant roasted carrot/okra/squash salad, eggplant/potatoes, and the BEST GREEK SALAD I've ever, ever, ever had stateside.  I was so enamored with its bright, acidic bite that I requested a second helping, which was granted with a smile and no extra charge.  The second set of dishes (main course) was no less amazing--a side of wheatberries and lentils, so simple but so good because it was cooked and seasoned perfectly.  Housemade rabbit sausages with Cypriot couscous, fresh grilled sardines (not for everyone, but oh, YUM!), the most juicy Greek meatballs, and on, and on, and on.  

    Another highlight for me, though not part of meze, was their housemade herbal teas (I was nursing a sore throat and their sage/cinnamon mix was just the ticket).

    Oh and dessert!  Only $5, you must try the Galatopita.  A fluffy, milky semolina custard, enveloped in phyllo dough, baked hot and laced with candied orange and syrup.  It's kind of my favorite thing ever.  

    I love this restaurant.  I also loved my waiter who recommended me the sage tea and the galatopita.  I'm sorry i didn't catch your name, red-headed bearded guy, but you were the best waiter EVER.

Specialties

We specialise in cooking from the homeland of chef Konstantinos Pitsillides. Pitsillides hails from the island of Cyprus, an indipendant Greek island located in the very east of the Mediterranean. The Cyprus kitchen has many influences but can perhaps best be considered as part of the Levant. At home in Cyprus, as in his Philly kitchen freshness, simplicity, flavour and love are the standard ingrediants. Chef takes his French culinary training and applies it to the traditional recipes of his homeland. Homemade desserts, head to tail cooking, lemon and olive oil are all part and parcel of this neighbourhood joint.

History

Established in 2008.

Kanella is made up of a passionate bunch of characters from the chef on down. Being a local restaurant filled with the family spirit much of the staff have been there since we opened. Though Chef Pitsillides is oft misunderstood (and yes sometimes this is exaserbated by himself), the intent of the restaurant is a simple one... We have aimed to be a neighbourhood venue for good, honest and consitstant cooking. A place that you can afford to come to regularly and have a good meal, good service, a nice bottle of wine, and enjoy your company. We have been blessed with the neighborhood we are in and all our wonderfull customers, so many of which we have known for sometime now. We look forward to continued joyful cooking and eating!
