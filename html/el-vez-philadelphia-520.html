    I've been going to this place for years. The best thing about El Vez is you always know what you're going to get. It's a Stephen Starr restaurant so you know that you'll get an interesting atmosphere and well thought out, trendy decor. You'll also get pretty good food and lots of good drinks. The circular bar in the middle is always a happening spot, and the noise level can get pretty loud here. But it's a great place to unwind, drink, eat, and be as loud as you want (sometimes you do just want to yell and laugh heartily without the next table over looking at you like you just insulted their mothers).

    The blood orange margarita is a must have. I've spent many a night over a pitcher of this with a few friends being very very happy.

    As for food, what really stands out is the guacamole. All of them are good but my personal favorite is the Bazooka Limon. I love goat cheese, I love avocados, and I also love pistachios. Put them together, add some citrus and salt, and it's a winning combination.

    The Queso Fundido con carne is also really good. It's usually sizzling hot when it arrives at the table, and the saltiness of the chorizo and cheese is so good with the tortillas.

    Other favorites include the pibil pork tacos, the mahi mahi tacos, and the classic red chile and chicken enchiladas.

    Now after you're stuffed and drunk, head over to the photobooth and take some incriminating photos to end the night.

    I'm a big fan of this corridor on 13th and Sansom, and El Vez being one of the original stops has yet to disappoint.

    You really can't miss their guacamole.  They used to make it front of you, but on my most recent trip they didn't, so I'm not sure if they changed that.  We had the "bazooka" which has goat cheese and pistachio.  I've had it before, and it is delicious.  They also do a fantastic nacho, and very substantial, which is the case with most of thei foods.

    Now I have to tell you, I had their cristpy mahi mahi tacos on this visit, and they were perfect!  The flavors complimented each other very well and if you add some of their fresh salsa and house guas (which is on the side with almost everything) and you'll get close to food-gasm.  The only reason I'm not giving it five stars is because of other experiences, but if it were based on this trip it would be a five-starer.

    My boyfriend (he'll be in most of my food reviews, just to be prepared) had their chorizo and eggs, only available on their brunch menus.  He loved it, and said "this is how chorizo should be."  He also had enough to take home for his breakfast.  Money well spent.  

    I would also suggest enjoying either their sangria or their margarita.  My personal favorite is the frozen blood orange, no salt.  Drinks can get a bit expensive, especially their pitchers, which are good for three drinks and cost just as much if not more.

    The major reason for the reduced star count is the noise, which can be terrible if crowded, the table placement, which leaves people sitting staring at people's butts, and the wait, which can be significant if you don't plan ahead.  This is a must visit though, and you can bounce from place to place either before or after thanks to the wonderful "midtown village" area.

    I have been to El Vez many times over and have yet to see the place empty.  

    Cocktails/drinks:  The cocktails are slightly expensive, but worth it.  The margaritas are always good, but my favorite is the zapata.  It's an intriguing mix of vodka, strawberries, and basil.  All the cocktails are made to order and they use fresh herbs and fruits, so it can take some extra time to get your drinks.  They also have an impressive tequila selection.  The bar is usually jam-packed, so don't expect to be able to get a stool right away.  

    Brunch: Brunch at a Mexican restaurant can either be really great or really awful.  Fortunately, it was the former.  My friend ordered the huevos rancheros, which looked and smelled amazing.  I stuck with a burrito and was highly satisfied.

    Lunch and dinner: We order guac for the table when we go and it's very fresh and flavorful.  Service can be slightly slow since it's always so packed, but the food is worth the wait.  The pork tacos are by far the best item on the menu, in my humble opinion.  Three mini tacos come out with marinated pulled pork, avocado, and a pickled cabbage of some sort.  The portion is actually perfectly sized.

    Dessert: I'm always too full to order dessert...but, I would be truly surprised if it wasn't good.

    Best time to go here: Anytime really, but Cinco de Mayo at El Vez is super fun.  I'm talking free sombreros for everyone and ridiculous specials.

    Last, but not least, the photobooth: Bring $3 and take some pictures to document your drunken Mexican restaurant experience.  Enjoy.

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District.
