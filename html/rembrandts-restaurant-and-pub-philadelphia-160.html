    After hearing about this place for quite a while & studying the menu online for months - we finally decided to give this place a shot.  

    We got there just in time for the Stanley Cup Playoff Game 2.  We were enjoying ourselves and were pretty impressed with the place until they turned off the sound to the hockey game and turned on a techno new wave electro synthezied pop song that was awesome a few years ago.  The good news was, this cleared out some of the people that stayed after the Craft Beer Bar Crawl a little too long & were becoming obnoxious.  Our bartender was great - she changed the sound back to the hockey game when I asked, she poured our beer even though we only ordered bottles & cans, and was very friendly and attentive.  

    The beer selection was still great even though they had been cleaned out from the bar crawl right before we got there.  As for food, we ordered the flatbread pizza special (asparagus, artichoke, pancetta, white sauce, maybe some other delicious things), loaded tots, and the french onion soup.  It was all very delicious, which almost validates the steep-ish prices. The french onion soup in particular was amazing and was definitely worth ten dollars!

    Overall, the food was great, our service was great, and Rembrandt's seems like a great place for all ages to enjoy themselves.  The prices are a little too high to make this our regular bar - but I would definitely come back again!

Specialties

A friendly neighborhood bar and gastropub with banquet and private event facilities.

Rembrandt's has one of the most inviting sidewalk cafe's in the Fairmount neighborhood.

When not hosting private events, Rembrandt's provides a variety of entertainment - Quizzo on Tuesday nights is a big draw!.  Call us to schedule your private event or use our space to show off your (or your group's) talent.  We feature a great view of the Philadelphia Skyline from our upstairs banquet room and we are fully equipped for audio and video events.

And, of course, we offer casual dining in our romantic dining room, and the same menus are available at both of our turn of the century bars.

History

Established in 1985.

The brown bars and cafes of Amsterdam, the city that was home to Rembrandt van Rijn, are well known throughout Europe for their antique charm, warmth, inviting atmosphere, and friendly but stimulating conversation. Here at Rembrandt's in Philadelphia, a seventy-five year old tavern has been renovated in a manner reminiscent of the brown bars of Amsterdam. In fact, many of the interesting fixtures you will see in the bar and restaurant were discovered in Europe by the original owners of Rembrandt's, and their history may be of interest to you. Please browse our website for photos of our neighborhood gathering spot and of the people who come to enjoy our food, drink and companionship!
