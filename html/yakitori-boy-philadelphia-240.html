    Definitely have made some good memories at this place.

    Food wise, the food is serviceable and plentiful.  You can either order a bento (meal for one person) or you can go tapas style.  The tapas menu has a large selection, and the only thing I remember is that my dining party pretty much chose a bunch of pork tapas dishes (nothing wrong with that).

    The song selection seems to be up to date whenever I stop by, and they do have a good selection of Japanese songs (hey, you gotta pay yo respects).  Sometimes the mikes are spotty, but what does that matter when everyone in the room is singing along to the awesome song that you chose?!?!  The more the merrier, and many a merry time have we had, with many more to come.

    The first time I visited Yakitori Boy I must say I wasn't exactly sure what to expect. The whole idea of singing aloud in front of other people is quite a nerve racking thing, .let alone to do so on such a popular night like wednesday "karaoke" nights. I was very surprised and pleased, to say the least. The atmosphere is so relaxed (as can be in a karaoke bar with laser light shows) you sometimes forget its a stage for people, possibly you, to get up in front of strangers and sing a song. There are private rooms you can rent for a reasonable price, as well as booth seating in the upstairs loft to merely take in the musical stylings of others, while still remaining in the general public. My favorite aspect of Yakitori Boy may also be the most surprising one. When you first walk in you have the option to head upstairs to the karaoke section, or by pass that to sit in their secluded dining section. I suggest the latter, if for nothing else than the unbelievably fresh Sushi you can find at the bar. The menu is original and eclectic, ranging from california rolls and shrimp tempura, to the not so in the box sauteed chicken hearts. YB is a great place for a private party ie: Birthday or bachelorette; or the simply let off steam as many people in the restaurant business do on a regular basis(Inquire about their Monday Industry Night).
