    I was downtown today around lunchtime so decided I would grab a bite to eat. My handy Yelp app (yay!) let me know I was in the area of Garces Trading Company, and since I'd heard good things, I decided to give it a try.

    The restaurant itself is very strikingly decorated with a great sleek, modern, yet welcoming ambience. I'm not sure it is a place I would come for dinner but for lunch, it looked great.

    I was dining alone and was annoyed that the hostess kind of... hid me! I was the only person eating by myself and I got stuck at a table back by the kitchen. Usually I would ask to be seated somewhere else but I decided to let this one slide, as my seat was not sooo bad. Still, it rubbed me the wrong way.

    However, my server was very friendly and she was also attentive. I ordered the white wine sangria and the burger. The sangria was really not great. I ordered it because it had pisco, which is one of my favorite things, but you could hardly taste it. The sangria was just bland and watered down, not good.

    The burger however was delicious. Perfectly medium-rare as I had requested it, delicious toppings, and the duck-fat fries were so tasty and decadent, especially with the creamy sauce for dipping. The burger was also served with some pickled zucchini slices that were so tasty!!

    I feel like at $8/glass, it was inexcusable that the sangria was not delicious. At the same time, $12 for the burger was a total deal.

    I'd come back if I were in the area, but probably not to eat, especially not as a single diner. I'm definitely looking forward to checking out the deli and going home with some pickles and charcuterie, though.

    Listed in Lunch Places in Philly, Trendy Dining, Europe in Philadelphia

    This is a place where you'll want to arrive a little early. When you arrive,
    -- Sample some of the oils and vinegars they have to offer. I recommend the truffle oil and the herbs de provence oil.
    -- Then, make your way to the wine shop to purchase a bottle or two for your group. Note: Garces will open your first bottle free, $10 for each additional bottle.
    -- If you don't drink or are on the run, visit their dessert and to-go counter-- it's pretty fancy.

    Garces uses a small-dish/tapas idea, and it feels a little long in the tooth to me.  If you go during restaurant week, you'll most likely be disappointed.  For example, my girlfriend and I received bland or overly salted courses, stale macarons, and a raw (not rare) duck breast that must have come from the tiniest duck.  I suspect it's high volume in a place that wasn't expecting it. Either way, a bit disappointing for a meal that was expected to be much better.  I did like their cauliflower dish and the cold tomato soup dish.  

    I think Restaurant Week misleads consumers or misleads restaurateurs at times. Instead of putting their best foot forward, it leads them to lower quality products that probably aren't representative of the restaurant. I'll try Garces Trading Co. one more time. If it fails me again, the update will not be so nice.

    I have mixed feelings on restaurant week. I don't think it's a deal, considering most of the time you spend the same amount ordering each course off the regular menu, and usually for RW the portions are half the normal size. Sometimes you'll find a gem where this is not the case, but Garces Trading Co was not one of those spots.

    For the first course we all shared manchengo cheese with a truffle-lavender honey, a simple green salad, and a pork rilette. The cheese was good (when is cheese not good?) but the honey had a strange flavor. The salad was ok but nothing special and the pork rilette had the texture of tunafish which was off-putting.

    For the second course I got buckwheat polenta with creme fraiche, which was actually very tasty and rich. The perfect comfort food. I tasted my friend's sausage pizza which was good but once again nothing exceptional and a cold tomato soup with cheese and egg which was very good and flavorful.

    The third course was scallops. The scallops were sweet and cooked perfectly, but I'm still torn on the sauce they were in. I don't remember the name but it was a thick, sweet sauce, the consistency of hummus, that they said was a mix of garlic, grapes, and almonds.There were dots of what tasted like sriracha on the side of the plate, but it didn't mix well with the other sauce. It was definitely different, but not necessarily in a good way.

    Dessert was a layered chocolate cake, with caramel somewhere in there. I like the flavors but one of the layers was very gelatinous (that's a word, right?) which once again struck out on texture.

    The final verdict? Meh. The atmosphere is cool, you get to dine in the middle o a cute little market. But food wise, nothing was bad, nothing was amazing, and I don't think I'd order anything again. The one plus is that they don't charge a corkage fee for your first bottle, so if you're a small party it's pretty much BYO. Although that said, if the waitress pushed the $5 sangria and harder she would have been pouring it down our throats. Be prepared to combat some attitude if you do tote your own vino.

    Like many of my food blog reading brethren - I came for the all you can eat steak frites! And let me tell you - amazing.

    Just to recap though - Iron Chef Jose Andres' GTC is set up as a boutique wine / cheese / salumi / pastry shop within a restaurant. This restaurant is BYO with $10 corkage after the 1st bottle. A great deal for a restaurant of this caliber. The olive oils are fruity, fragrant, fantastic. Sample the various offerings before you dine!

    But we're here for the steak frites - we'll ignore the margherita pizza and the salmon (nicely cooked delicious) with brussel sprites (toothsome, well seasoned).

    Hangar steak - extremely well seasoned and comes out with a choice of three sauces - bordelaise (wine), bernaise (butter), and au poivre (peppercorn). Huge platter of accompanying duck fat fries which were delicious but a bit too salty for my liking.

    Now - here's how the all you can eat really works. You'll see a decent platter of steak on the same dish as the frites - but you DO NOT need to eat all of those fries to get your next lip-smacking serving of steak! GTC is not trying to gouge you by carb-loading you with fries. How do they get around this?

    They bring out the steak on a serving platter and serve it to you table-side! They came back twice for me - and it was delicious every time - perfectly cooked to a medium non-bloody rare. For any novices ordering their steak well-done ... just try it .. pllllease! (FYI: they don't ask for your temperature preference - probably because they know how it SHOULD be cooked).

    Final note: they don't bring out additional sauces - which really isn't necessary. They might - but I never asked.

    Service was very good - as usual. Slightly annoying that water glasses were not refilled after paying the check - we were sitting around talking for about 45 minutes after dinner (mind you - we were the last seating for that table and there was no other diners waiting to be seated).

    The amazing steak frites deal trumps this however - resulting in the 5 star review - I'll be back for this steak frites deal - Hope to see you there!

Specialties

Garces Trading Company is a full-service bar and restaurant focused on Iron Chef Jose Garces' own favorite dishes from his travels to cafes throughout Western Europe, with a focus on Italy, Spain and France.  Our thoughtful menu of cafe staples is served throughout the day, and our bar offers a wealth of cocktails, wines and beers that pair beautifully with our menu.  Our on-site PLCB Wine Boutique, the first of its kind, makes a great resource either before sitting down to dinner (corkage fees apply) or to complement one of our decadent gift baskets, available in our newly redesigned retail section.

History

Established in 2010.

Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a refined, Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, a contemporary American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago, IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale, AZ and Palm Springs, CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the landmark Revel Resort. Today, in addition to our much-lauded restaurants around the country, Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
