    Listed in Oh Hipsters!

    Though I love Wawa and, in a pinch, 7-11, both of the big convenience stores are severely lacking in vegan options.  It killed me when I didn't eat meat/dairy, because I'd wind up aimlessly wandering the aisles, trying desperately to find something I could hodge podge together into a meal.  

    That was until I stayed with a friend who lived around the corner from Jay's.  

    I swear, that place kept me alive.  Now, they don't have the most amazing selection of vegan goodness, but they practically always have some sort of vegan sandwiches available in their refrigerated case.  Plus, they usually have hummus and pita chips.  Yum.  One thing that should be noted is that their vegan sandwiches are NOT made on site; they're the same standard brands found in most convenience stores that carry pre-packaged vegan sandwiches.  OH!  And I almost forgot!  I'm pretty certain they also carry a small selection of Amy's microwavable foods.  Basically, they make sure the veg*ns are taken care of!

    They have a pretty wide variety of food for a corner deli and, more than once, I relied on them for something quick and yummy to cram down my throat or because I had an "OH CRAP! WE DON'T HAVE ANY _____" moment.  I remember the prices being pretty reasonable, especially considering that they sell much more upscale sandwiches than what you'll find at either 7-11 or Wawa.  I recently tried a roast beef wrap and inhaled that within a few minutes, so it seems like their meaty sandwiches are also on par with the veg*n stuff they carry.  

    The only complaint I have is that they seem to have arbitrary hours.  I think they basically close when the crowds start to consistently die down for the night, which is a little bit of a pain.

    Overall, it's definitely worth checking out, especially over the nearest 7-11 (which is a little scuzzy and lacking in selection) or the nearest Wawa (which is right across from Jefferson and therefore, a pain to get in and out of during the day).

    Listed in Sandwichpedia

    Wedged inside a building as non-descript as its place in the popular Philly sandwich hierarchy, the sandwich counter here churns out reliably good work. These are not cute concoctions of foie gras on a jelly donut with bull's penis meant to snag blog headlines, they're thoughtful, well-executed sandwiches. You know what you're going to get and you're going to like it. A lot.

    The P1, turkey breast and swiss panini with bacon, tomatoes, red peppers and Russian dressing ($6.29) is fantastic. Russian dressing on turkey sandwiches is as indispensable of a combo to me as nasty cheese on nasty meat is to our lovely cheesesteak city.

    The S5 features prosciutto and mozzarella with red peppers, spinach, olive oil and pepper on focaccia bread ($6.19). It was good, but I'm not big on focaccia bread, so I liked the P12 better, closely related with prosciutto and mozzarella with roasted peppers, sun dried tomatoes, and pesto.

    The P2, turkey, ham, American cheese and tomato pesto is the only dud I've had. It tastes like bad pizza.

    It's also important to note that while the convenient store is open late, they stop making hot food around 8:30 or 9, depending on business.

    Jay's takes great ingredients and makes them their bitch, frankly.
