    Lucha Libres, acid pink decor, and modern Mexican... yes, I have finally arrived to my first Garces eatery.

    Cue Distrito. As mentioned above, this spot is bright pink all over; think about an over saturated bubblegum pink. The downstairs was where the cantina (bar) was located at. A keen eye will spot the car table, yes, a table inside of a car. Sadly, the car is hidden in the front of the restaurant and next to the front doors and bathrooms. Who really wants this seating anyways? Moving upstairs, we were greeted with Mexican wrestling masks along the way. I found this to be a really nice aesthetic to the place, creating an extravagant pattern on the wall. The top level was filled with more brightly woven seats, along with the hanging kind. Again, a pretty cool feature, but definitely more for show.

    The menu design was interesting and refreshing at last. Lately, menu designs have not been intriguing to me, but Distrito had a voice. All pink type on white, the menu was unique with its many uses of different typefaces; very fun, circus-like, and met with the restaurant's specific funky style.

    As for the food, there was much left to be discovered. We had the Esquites, which was sweet corn, queso fresco, chipotle, and lime. Presented nicely in a tall glass, the dish was pretty good. It was very tasty, but compared to its grilled corn counterpart, it wasn't anything special. Next on the menu were the Carne Kobe Tacos. I had high hopes for these tacos, but they fell a bit short due it its non-tender cut of meat. I had to really wrestle my taco here, and it was not expected from kobe beef. The skinny yukon fries added a wonderful crunch to the well seasoned beef. Lastly, we enjoyed the Los Hongos Hurrache. For those who do not know, this was a mexican flatbread with black truffles, queso mixto, and forest mushrooms. This dish alone, saved the restaurant for me. The distinct and earthy flavors all worked together so well. I fell in love with it, and would definitely get this dish again and again. It was that good!

    Bathroom area was interesting, with its co-ed bathroom with respective individual rooms. I am a fan of bathroom design.

    Service was really consistent, even when the restaurant was filled. Props to their team.

    I really want to try this place again. The first time was with a large group and I don't believe I experienced the intimate connection I would like to have with my restaurants. Next time, Los Hongos for sure along with some bone marrow tacos! See you again Distrito/Garces.

    Two five star experiences, two three star experiences, one four star experience.  Avoid restaurant week if you're just going out for a quick bite, and avoid the cantina hours unless your preferred dishes are on the cantina menu.

    I visited Distrito five times while I was in Philly.  Two awesome experiences, and two mediocre experiences.  I'm still going with four stars because no matter what, the food is delicious.

    My first experience was with a friend of mine who is a vegetarian.  I happen to love most meat products whether by air, sea or land (mmm bacon), so we were both pleased at the menu selection having adequate vegetarian dishes that were quite tasty.  In fact, one of them (huarache los hongos) was a repeat order for me on almost every visit (the 'almost' is a reason for one of the mediocre experiences).  Service was fantastic, food was incredible, overall experience was a five star.

Specialties

Distrito is the new restaurant concept by Jose Garces, the chef behind the acclaimed Philadelphia restaurants Amada and Tinto. Distrito is a high-energy restaurant inspired by the spirited culture and cuisine of Mexico City. The Distrito menu features Chef Garces' fresh interpretations of classic Mexican fare and beverages include over 65 tequilas and margaritas made with fresh seasonal fruits. A striking decor includes a hot pink and bright green color scheme, a wall constructed from over 600 colorful, campy masks of lucha libre, or "free fight" professional wrestlers, and a booth crafted from a Volkswagen Beetle, a typical Mexico City taxi cab.

History

Established in 2008.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
