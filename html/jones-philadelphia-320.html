    I've only been here for brunch. Food gets three stars, but overall experience gets four. Here's why:

    1) "Food your grandmother served in the '70s" is not really a genre I'm into, but these people kind of make the best of it. Being vegetarian should preclude me from eating much of the aforementioned genre, but Jones has their "ALT" - avocado, lettuce, and tomato. I find it tasty. I also like the monkey bread a lot, despite not being much of a sweet breakfast person. The bloody marys are too expensive, but okay.

    2) Atmosphere is just ... unique. The order pad for the aforementioned bloody marys is one reason. The creepy bathroom stalls are another. On the "delightful" side for this place is the giant photo of the ladies in swimsuits with red balloons and ... shag carpeting. (I asked about how this texture works with the inevitable server spills. It's professionally cleaned once a month and replaced with frequency.)

    Worth going once or twice, and covers a few "strange craving" bases. For example, "OMG, I want tater tots, but the oven's being used for storage."

    Remember Lyon's Restaurants, out west? Or Googie architecture? The inside of Jones looks like the outside of a '60s Lyon's -- except in Jones everything is at right angles. So maybe it's more like Joe's of Westlake/Marin/San Jose, etc. But with servers in t-shirts instead of tuxedos.

    Oh, and there was food.

    It too was straight out of my childhood going-out experiences, though of higher quality. I ordered the Thanksgiving dinner and the two hefty slabs of turkey breast were not mere diner quality. The same can be said for the silky mashed potatoes, the dense, old-fashioned gravy, the candied carrots (if you like your shafts of carrots at just the right um, firmness, this is the place), succulent, moist stuffing and fresh cranberry sauce that was off the tartness chart. My date's chicken and waffles were superb -- as she's stated, the dish was salty, however. I figure that's Lord Starr *really* bringing back a by-gone dining era, though in fairness there is modernity and whimsy on the menu as well. Just think brute portions served on massive plates with a light touch.

    Other notes: Our server was exceedingly friendly. The bar was exceedingly dead. (Apparently, everyone drinking was at the opposite corner of the intersection of Chestnut and South 7th ... but that's another review.) The joint has a fireplace and a coat check and matchbooks and no TVs and you go there to have a good time, not to score status points.

    Jones won't blow you away, but it's good. If you've been wary of the place, I say give it a shot.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
