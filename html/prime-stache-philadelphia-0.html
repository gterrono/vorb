        First to Review

        Ambled up to the doors, not sure what to expect from a mustache hanging above them but obviously intrigued.  We were greeted by a great staff that was excited to seat us and get us started with some drinks.  After taking a seat at the bar and evaluating the beer menu we settled on the Golden Bear and Oberon Wheat.  The draft menu was small but had several great selections that kept us happy for several rounds.

        We decided to try the sriracha wings as an appetizer.  Well made and not too spicy; they went well with the drinks.

        I ordered the Ron Burgundy and my friend had the Stache Burger.  Neither of us were in any way disappointed. The green chiles are a must on the stache burger, taking it to the next level of flavor.  The chips that come along with both of the items were the perfect compliment to each of our meals.  Wonderfully fried and seasoned to blend well with the rest of the meal.

        We met the owner and chatted with the chef for a bit and learned about their polenta fries.  They were not on the menu yet, but are a must order for every meal.  Fresh out of the kitchen they posses a creaminess and crunch that is unmatched on the menu.  Highly recommended by all who tried them at the bar.

        The staff was very outgoing and friendly, rounding out a phenomenal night out!  Kevin, the bartender, was very open to suggestions and obviously interested in creating the best possible experience for each of us at the bar.  Brent even took the time to introduce himself to us and ask how our experience was.

        Truly a great experience with some excellent prices.  We will definitely be back this weekend and look forward to the brunch offering in the coming weeks.

        Fantastic. During the new mustache craze I had to give this place a try and there were four of us so it was a perfect opportunity to try a wide variety. The hanging mustache and rustic decor give this placw a great first impression.

        NOTHING is imported, which caught my attention immediately considering it allows you to eat local ingredients. They do not even carry tequila because it is not made in the US. Dishes use minimal ingredients and everything is fresh which is always a plus!

        The drink list is very sophisticated and simple. All of the drinks we had were awesome-not too sweet and very well-balanced.

        First we tried the sriracha wings which had the perfect amount of spice. Next, the edamame ravioli and meatball sliders which were both outstanding. Being italian, I am pretty critical when it comes to meatballs and these were very flavorful.

        Next we tried fish tacos which use the fresh fish of the day (tilapia) which were very tasty followed by the pork belly mac and cheese (AMAZING). Whenever there is a good mac and cheese on the menu I have to try it and it was incredible. The pepper steak with Polenta fritters was by far my favorite. The polenta fritters literally melt in your mouth. We each had a few bites of the ron burgundy sandwich which was difficult to share but I dont think it is meant to be shared. The pulled pork had the perfect amount of sweetness.

        All in all this place is great and I cannot wait to go back!

        Prime Stache was an awesome choice for a girls night out over the weekend. I made a reservation in advance and we were seated right when we arrived. We were all in the mood for martinis and I asked for my usual - slightly dirty with olives. Prime Stache serves only American products so my choices were Tito's Vodka and Penn 1681. I'm down with Tito's so I went with that and my drinks were perfect all night; never too dirty, just right.

        One of my friends is gluten-free but she was able to find a few menu items that worked. We started with the house salad which is all veggies and vinaigrette, and my other friend and I ordered the edamame and sweet pea ravioli, which we both loved. The kitchen sent out a second round of the ravioli and an order of fish tacos; two of us had already ordered the tacos for our mains but our gluten-free friend was able to eat the fish which was great, since she couldn't have the ravioli. I personally thought the tacos could use a little bit of sauce; some type of spicy aioli or sour cream would elevate these tacos from good to great.

        What I did like is that a lot of the food we ordered was fairly healthy and didn't feel too heavy. There are more decadent menu items for sure, but it's really nice to have a lighter option for once when dining out. Something for everyone.

        We skipped dessert in favor of another round of drinks, and when all was said and done, our bill was reasonable for a girls night out.

        I've missed my fair share of Elite Events for the sake of paying it forward to other Elite's worthy attending the awesomeness that transpires during them. But out of all the events that I've found myself missing for just such an occasion; missing the opportunity to hob nob table side with some fellow elites during a feast of 3x pork filled sandwiches, debonair sounding cocktails, and then some inside a new Old City restaurant owned by a Philadelphia Eagle was that one occasion that had me yelling a cinema sized Nooooooo (youtu.be/Eal4fep7pK4)!!!!!

        After finally being able to fully cope from such an event, I found my sell invigorated and ready to enjoy what Prime Stache had to offer on a special occasion. And what could be better than enjoying a sandwich named for Anchorman extraordinaire Ron Burgandy and some good brews with your dad on Father's Day? And for that occasion Brent Celek's American Bistro delivered a touchdown.

        With classics like a whiskey sour and peach julep, and innovative cockatails like a Pomegranate Hibiscus and Honey Lavender Lemon Drop I could just have easily gone that route. But it was hard to ignore good brews from Stones, Firestone Walker, Bells, and more. Again things like sweet pea and edamame ravioli, bacon wrapped meatloaf, and the green chile optional Stache burger loaded with bacon and white American cheese were all pretty hard to pass up. But the Ron Burgundy sandwich fully loaded with pulled pork, roast pork, and pork belly was Beyond impossible to pass up. and it was a shot of scotch and jazz flute serenade away from being the perfect Anchorman tribute.

        Its pretty easy to respect Prime Stache as an American Bistro that delivers fresh ingredients good sized sandwiches, classically inspired cocktails with a new school twist, and a widespread selection of draft and bottled beers from some of the better recognized microbrews across the country. It also wins out for having a roomy interior, a nice wooden decor, and one of the best mustache logos ever!

      History

      Established in 2013.

      On the cobblestone block of Front and Chestnut Street, Prime Stache in Old City provides an extensive American bistro style menu and carefully selected craft beer and cocktails. From the classic exposed brick, to the antique wooden décor, to the Thomas Edison style light fixtures, there is a rustic, yet modern and comfortable feel to the restaurant.

      Owner Brent Celek and partner/chef Hee Chang have developed a distinctive, gratifying, and affordable menu filled with savory and prodigious entrées and sandwiches while Kevin Malvey's unique cocktail menu combines all fresh ingredients with house made infusions and bitters in an elaborate display of mason jars that will leave you curious and anxious to ask 'What's in that one?'
