    To celebrate my brother and sister-in-law's 25th anniversary, we went to Noord.  Three of the four of us gave it a 5 star rating and the other one a 4 star rating so it certainly lived up to its reputation and promise of great food.  We were met outside the restaurant by Chef Joncarl Lachman who poured us our BYOB wine sidewalk-side as we waited for our table to be set.  Chef Lachman was friendly, chatty, welcoming and within minutes we were seated at our table.

    The homemade Dutch rye/oat bread with roasted garlic and butter was amazing - truly delicious.  We ordered a number of appetizers to try: the cheese and pickle platter, the bitterballen, the smorrebrod, the Holland Sliders "Broodjes Haring" and the Mustard Soup with Seared Diver Scallop and caviar.  The pickles were fresh, crunchy and brightly acidic - perfect counterparts for the smoked fish, scallops, the various cheeses, the bitterballen and the herring sliders.

    I loved the mustard soup and could have eaten a gallon of it with the oat bread and been perfectly happy.  It was garnished with a beautifully seared scallop and caviar on a rye toast raft but as I am not a scallop fan; my sister-in-law (who likes scallops) ate it and she said it was perfectly cooked.  Seared to golden perfection and bathed in butter on the outside and creamy, sweet on the inside.

    For our entrees we tried the Konijn in het Zuur (rabbit leg confit) in a delicious cream sauce, the braised pork Uitsmijter, the pan-cooked whole Sea Bass and the very Dutch "Lekkerbekjes" on Snert, a crispy beer battered whitefish, ravigote sauce, split pea, bacon, smoked sausage, malt vinegar aioli.  

    Everything was perfectly cooked, perfectly delicious and the portions are generous.  After our round of appetizers; two of us were unable to finish our entrees and had then boxed to go.

    We finished with a couple of cafetières à piston and for dessert, the Dutch butter cake and the dark chocolate bark.  The butter cake was an almond marzipan-y cake (reminiscent of a financier) with crème anglais and whipped cream and the dark chocolate bark was melt-in-your-mouth slices of dark chocolate embellished with nuts and some fleur de sel or other coarse salt.

    Our waitress was good - not as memorable as the matching tattooed upper arm sleeves she wore - but adequate enough.  

    The star of the evening was what it should have been, the food, the ambiance and the experience which were all 5 star.  I have a Dutch brother-in-law and when next he is town, we will be going to Noord.  Heerlijk!!

    Noord is a sleek and innovative spot that's the ideal gateway to the East Passyunk area of South Philly, a budding hipster enclave with a lively restaurant and bar scene, great people watching, and terrible parking.  Made me feel right at home given I spend many of my days in the trendy Mission District of San Francisco.  

    Our dinner companions were the veritable first couple of South Philly, the life of the party, tight with all the restaurant staff and beloved of every passerby.  And when you are sitting in a fully open-air restaurant, big windows flung open to the sky, the passersby become part of the fabric of the meal.  Camaraderie and community Philly-style.  

    Everything we ate here was very rich, very hearty, and very delicious, not to mention unfailingly unique.   My first taste of a mustard soup went down smooth as I grasped for a bite of the tender scallops bobbing in the middle alongside semi-crunchy rye croutons, the whole shebang swirled with a thin ribbon of tarragon oil.  The stuff of brilliant invention!   As were the "clams and zuurkool," a stew of littleneck clams with braised cabbage, bacon and sausage, and caraway beer broth.   The ultimate tonic for a Scandinavian winter, no doubt.

    Next came the gut-busting Bitterballen, gigantic pork meatballs perfectly seasoned, uncharacteristically moist, and unrepentantly deep-fried.  We approached these caloric orbs with trepidation, given the likely health consequences, but ultimately found them the most sublime aspect of the meal.

    Our entrees, which arrived long after fulfillment and just before the onset of gluttony, consisted of the braised pork uitsmijter, a remarkable sandwich atop a grilled brioche with a sunny side egg and pickles, along with its gastronomic opposite, the Surinam seafood curry, a mild, tomato based Indo-Scandinivian bouillabaisse loaded with shellfish, potatoes and carrots, by far the most health-conscious course of the evening.  

    All of this was washed down with copious amounts of BYOB, a friendly touch that kept the bill from reaching astronomical proportions.  Because we had to give dessert a whirl, we chose the almond cake, which was intensely imbued with almond extract and surrounded by clouds of vanilla whipped cream.  We couldn't stop until the plate was clean, even though our minds and bodies had long since thrown in the towel.

    I was thrilled when Joncarl Lachman opened Noord last Spring since it was advertised that the menu, although Dutch focused, would feature Scandinavian specialties as well. Being a Norwegian living in Philly, there has been slim picking in the Scandinavian cuisine department through all the near 15 years I have resided here, resorting to getting my fixes at Ikea's Swedeshop. For one reason or another it took until May 14 this year before I had a chance to visit the restaurant. My girlfriend and I had recently moved to South Philly and she had booked a table for my birthday.

    Full of anticipation we entered a near-full, buzzing restaurant on a Wednesday evening. We got greeted by friendly wait staff and shown the best table with street view and open window. Joncarl came by with delicious Dutch rye bread with roasted garlic and butter. So far, so good.

    Quite frankly, I have not much experience in Dutch cuisine and even my Dutch friends and colleagues tell me that the local food is not what you travel to the Netherlands for. So I was curious to experience this place, which has got such raving reviews.

    For starter, my girlfriend chose the Bitterballen (fried pork meatballs) with and nutmeg, mustard. I got the special asparagus with eggs and butter sauce. The combination reminded me of the traditional Norwegian spring ritual of asparagus and "Sandefjordsmør". Perfect with the brought-along "aquavit".

    For her main course, my girlfriend ordered the rabbit leg confit with smoked sausage, lardons, baby turnips and zuurkool. "Zuurkool" was reminiscent of what Scandinavians refer to as "Parisian sauerkraut", which is a sweeter variety than the German.

    I ordered the crispy beer battered whitefish, ravigote sauce, split pea, bacon, smoked sausage and malt vinegar aioli. The "ravigote" sauce is another Scandinavian staple and the homemade version in combination with the split pea mash provided the prefect acidic accompaniment to the fish.  

    After such a feast, we had no desire to stop so we went for the recommended Noord dessert sampler, including the delicious Dutch butter cake, cranberry almond bread pudding and dark chocolate bark.

    Needless to day, I love this place and my ambition is to try all the courses on the menu. No doubt it's a perfect match for the Northern European taste buds, but I bet many born Philadelphians will be appreciative as well of the virtuous concoctions Joncarl is cooking up from the North Sea and flatlands.

    3) Whole grilled sea bass (5/5) -- Outstanding!  This is the real deal, a full fish grilled and accompanied by an assortment of vegetables in a light broth.  The fish was grilled perfectly with a crispy outside and tender interior.  The vegetables are Noord's secret weapon; they're extremely fresh, cooked expertly and matched very well to the dish.  This was a special, but I think they always have whatever fish is freshest available

    My partner did the roasted acorn squash, which was served with lentils, the tastiest sweet carrots (smaller than regular, but not baby!), amongst some other well chosen vegetables.  This is the ideal vegetarian dish.  They probably would do well to have another veg dish on the main menu, however

    4) Butter cake (5/5) -- This is served with an almond-based sauce as well as a chocolate.  The butter cake is basically the best pound cake you can imagine, except with a crispier outer layer, chocolate chips, and a dusting of powdered sugar and a touch of delectable creme sauces.  Yep.

    My partner did the bread pudding, which is very European style (a la made from the brown bread) except it is topped off with some additional cranberry sauce and a nice sprinkle of cinnamon

    Portions at Noord are on the large size, and it's BYOB which is a win for me.  There's a valet in the square.  Restaurant was sold out all night, so I imagine reservations on the weekend are highly recommended.

    The only critique I have is that they should put a little WD-40 one of their oven hinges as it made a noticeable squeak from time to time in the dining room.  ;)
