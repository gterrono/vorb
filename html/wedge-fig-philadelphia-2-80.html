    Visiting from out of town and found this place in a warehouse/gallery district.  Had a lovely Sunday morning brunch all the way from the cappucino to the cream cheese/apple compote stuffed French toast and a little side of sheep's cheese with fig paste.  I like both sweet and savory for breakfast--can't have one without the other and luckily my boyfriend ordered Eggs NOLA which was two poached eggs on cornbread and Tasso ham along with lemony, parmesan-dusted fried potatoes.  We also tried the sausage and bacon to round things out--a lot of good food--all very clean and healthy tasting and feeling.  Reasonably priced, very nice and helpful service. They serve French press coffee in-house--to-go I got an Americano.  Decent inside albeit a little chilly in temperature. Would definitely return again for a snack, dinner, or another brunch.

    Came here with friends for a late brunch one Sunday. It's a tiny little spot but it didn't feel cramped. The wait for our food was fairly long, and probably seemed longer because we were all starving! We ordered a bunch of salads and a side of mac and cheese.

    My salad was DELECTABLE. I've been fantasizing about recreating it at home ever since. Spring greens, toasted halved almonds, perfectly cured prosciutto, and  marinated figs, and manchego. It was delicious but I do have one complaint--one of my little manchego slices was from the waxy, not-that-edible part of the wedge. That is cheap and annoying.

    Most of my dining partners enjoyed their salads, although one of my friends said hers was not that great. Also, the mac and cheese was strangely bad! Strange, because this restaurant has an entire cheese counter and specializes in delicious cheeses. But the mac and cheese was bland, needed salt, did not have a good blend of cheeses, and generally in terms of texture, appearance, and taste, seemed like it was made at least two days in advance. I really should have asked the waiter to take it away but by the time we each had a bite of it, it was half gone, so I felt like that would be too awkward.

    Seriously, Wedge + Fig, fix your mac and cheese. It's embarassingly bad for a place that is supposed to be priding itself on cheese selections.

    I also tasted a salad that had chickpeas and quinoa and it was delish! So I'd definitely recommend this place for tasty, inventive salads.

    A good place for a cheap meal in Old City. We had a bowl of lentil soup and two sandwiches  for $22 plus the tip.  

    The sandwiches were tasty, I give 2 thumbs up to the "Blue Fig" which combined blue cheese, fig spread, and a generous layer of proscuitto between two slices of pretzel bread. I really enjoyed the contrast of sweet and salty in this sandwich but it was also very rich.  

    I shared half of my Blue Figs sandwich with my husband, and in return got half of his the Shackmaxon, which I thought was less successful.  The turkey was a bit dry, the mustard overpowering so that you could not really taste the brie on the sandwich.  I didn't like the mango slices with this combo either.  The highlight of that sandwich was the challah bread.

    The lentil soup was tasty if a bit over salty.

    I understand that they have a nice courtyard in the back for  open air dining in warmer weather. As it was below freezing, we ate indoors, however, the front door had a tendency to swing open letting in blasts of freezing cold air.  If you can, don't sit by the door, or it will be your job to close it every time it swings open.

    The service was slow, there was only one waitress, we were hungry and it seemed like the food took forever to come.

    Overall, I gave it 3 stars, I think I would prefer it for lunch rather than dinner.

    This place is where brunch foodies' dreams come alive and triglycerides go awry. The vibe here is casual and hipsterish. The saying, "If you're good, people will tell you that you're good" is quite evident here. There is no unnecessary showing of largeness or grandeur. The quality and innovativeness of the food speaks for itself.

    After a brisk walk in the cold, we were comforted with a pot of hot pomegranate oolong tea (really good). They also have delicious French press coffee! Where else in the city do you see that? I really appreciated the simplicity and thoughtfulness of how they serve tea and coffee here. Thoroughly impressed.

    These two items were demolished:

    EGGS NOLA
    A quintessential brunch dish perfected. The poached eggs were covered with unbelievably creamy hollandaise sauce and served on top of Cajun spiced ham and house-made cornbread. It's well worth the nap you'll need after you eat it.

    BACONS-LATTICE MAC & CHEESE PIE
    Yup, this may be the best mac n cheese I ever had. Love the creativity and quality of ingredients. Made with house-made cheeses and laced with bacons inside a delicious flaky crusted pie, I may consider this one of my last meals on earth.

    To the owner, Captain Kirk, I salute you sir. I look forward to another exciting adventure for the chance to explore your awesome food.

    Since my last review, where I waxed glory over the Jawn sandwich, I've continued my regular wanderings to Wedge + Fig.  So far, I've been back to try their Coronation Chicken salad and Blue Figs panini for lunch, which are equally delicious and less foodcoma-inducing than the Jawn (which is now on their regular menu!).  Also, if you ask really nicely and give them a week's notice, they do yummy pumpkin pies and pielettes on special order for the holiday season.

    Many's the time I've been perched out back in their lovely urban garden, either studying for an exam with a French press coffee, or sharing an intimate brunch with a friend. If you're not a fan of the occasional flower drifting to your table from the huge catalpa tree, stay indoors and enjoy the rotating artsy paintings hanging on the walls.  Either way, I'd recommend their omelet special, or better yet, the Eggs NOLA.  The cornbread is an unusual twist, but it's so damn good with those darn parmesan potatoes on the side.

    Honestly, what keeps me returning is the down-to-earth service.  Unlike the neighbourhood they're in, there's no pretension here -- the staff take great care to make you feel at home and remember your likes and dislikes.  Better yet, each time either of us notice a mistake (whether that's a long wait for food, an ordering mistake, or a billing error) they go above and beyond to rectify the issue, which is something that I've found increasingly rare in my eating adventures.

Specialties

Old City's Cheese Bistro, featuring dinner, lunch and weekend brunch.  Sample our chicken pot pie, mac & cheese and scrumptious salads in the evening.  For lunch, try  our unique sandwiches, salads and soups (take out available for most items).  Omelettes, strata, cream-cheese-stuffed French toast are some brunch favorites.  Dine under the stars in our secluded garden.  Bring a bottle of wine or ale and we will pair it with a selection of cheeses.  The perfect romantic evening!

History

Established in 2006.

We rescued Old City Cheese Shop just before it closed.  We gave it some badly needed upgrades, changed the menu, re-named the store to wedge+fig.  And we stocked the cheese case with cheese!

Meet the Business Owner

Kirk N. Business Owner

Cheese lover from an early age.  Lived on a sailboat for 15 years.  Used to play guitar.  Now I own a restaurant.
