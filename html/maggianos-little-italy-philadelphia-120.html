    It's been years since I was last here travelling alone on business.  I recall having a fabulous experience eating at the bar and this time, travelling with my wife and son, suggested that we try it out.

    The bar experience continues to stand out.  The bartender with the least seniority is Evan and he's been there 10 years.  I remembered him from my last visit and can unequivocally say that he sets the standard for world-class bartending (and I've frequented more than a few in 30+ years)!  Prompt, polite, helpful (held our seats while the three of us stepped away from the bar), accommodating, and knowledgeable.  He even knew how much time we had left before our table was supposed to be ready.  It it had just been two of us we would have eaten at the bar just so that Evan could have continued to serve us.  I just cannot say enough good things about this man.

    After lingering at the bar for nearly 90 minutes I checked with the hostess about our seats.  She remembered taking our reservation, but couldn't understand why when the appointed time came and went there was no notice.  No worries, we were immediately seated.

    I ordered sausage flatbread as an appetizer and it got three thumbs up.  Crispy crust with flavorful sausage.  Two of us ordered house salads which were fresh and tasty.

    For entrees, we ordered lobster carbonara, shrimp scampi, and chicken parmesan.  The lobster carbonara was hands down the best, followed by the scampi.  I'd ordered the carbonara previously at another location and it was just as good.  The chicken parm was a serious dud though.  It was tough and the sauce tasted like it came out of a jar as well as being too sweet.  My wife has a demanding palate and couldn't finish it.  I have to agree with her - it was forgettable.

    Our server was gone for long periods of time and was unmemorable.

    For dessert we shared a slice of cheescake - outstanding!  Finally, the house red was a combination of cabernet and sangiovese.  Also outstanding.

    Bottom line - I would without hesitation come back to this restaurant for the bar experience and Evan's service.  For an Italian dinner and great dining room service, probably elsewhere.

    Three to four stars for food. One to one and a half stars for service. Really three stars overall,… Three to four stars for food. One to one and a half stars for service. Really three stars overall, but the service really left a bad taste in my mouth.

    ~~

    Maggiano's offers 'family style,' which is the way to go if you have four or more people in your party. They have three different options which range from light, classic, and chef's choice. The higher ranges offer more choices, which may include entrees such as lobster carbonara.

    We went with the classic menu which was $28.95 per person.

    Highlights of the meal include:

    Spinach Salad
    Surprisingly, this salad was one of my favorites of all the dishes. The onions, bacon, and gorgonzola brought a nice, funky savoriness, which was balanced by what were yellow peppers (though the description says red) and apple cider vinaigrette.

    Crispy Onion Strings
    These were perfectly sliced - probably with a Mandolin. Very fine - and came out perfectly crisp. Somewhat greasy and heavy.

    Sausage & Peppers
    The sausage was well seasoned and had a nice snap.

    At this point, service was decent, however our waitress was consistently behind on refilling our drinks and more often than not, did not even notice our glasses were empty. They were refilled upon reminder however. To be truthful though, she never took an initial drink order - we had to ask after she had left upon taken our food order. I did appreciate that they fired the mains after the appetizers were semi-finished - though this is most likely because there would have been no room on the table.

    We ordered the Chicken Parm Taylor Street Baked Ziti, Four Cheese Ravioli, and Fettuccine Alfredo for our mains.

    After our first go at the mains, the men at the table were still hungry so re-ordered all of the mains. After a thirty minute wait, the manager stopped by to inform us that all of our dishes had broken and the food was thus contaminated and we would have to wait some more. Understandable and fine with us. We appreciated the notice.

    Interestingly though - the food came out no less than 2 minutes after he left. So I'll leave the conspiracy theories to you - but it's obvious we either received contaminated food, the original food was fine, or the manager was extremely delayed. In any case, understandably the 2nd round of dishes had slightly less food for some dishes (one less chicken breast for the parm and two less ravioli). We were able to finish about half of this second round and had the rest wrapped to take home.

    For dessert, we consumed the Chocolate Zuccotto Cake and the Creme Brulee. Both really great.

    Now onto the service. The busboys are clearly busy and the runners do a great job. However, our waitress' service was downhill from the start. I can somewhat forgive the lack of attention to our soda refills. Even when we asked for refills, it took awhile for them to come. Granted - we were there the weekend of Thanksgiving and while it's not the busiest I've seen this location, it was hopping.

    At the end of the meal, I was prepared to pay with gift cards (I had $200 in denominations of $25). Despite the lack of consistency in service, in consideration of the busboys and runners, I was prepared to tip over 30%. I gave the waitress 7 gift cards ($175) - expecting that I would get another receipt back to add the tip.

    We waited for said receipt for at least 30 minutes. After thirty minutes of not looking our way and purposely avoiding eye contact, I got her attention and she stopped by. I asked for our bill - and she mentioned that she was confused. I asked what she was confused about. She said that with gift cards, she could only "verify that the cards had the amount listed on the gift card and that they would be processed at the end of day." I mentioned that this was ludicrous and clearly something was amiss.

    Honestly - did she expect a 40% tip? Service was nowhere near that good. I asked for one of the cards back, leaving the staff with exactly a 25% tip before taxes. Not exactly chump change, but less than what I had wanted to leave. Fine with me considering the inconsistency in service, but this left an uncomfortable taste in all our mouths, despite the decent food we had just eaten. Especially since she should have at least informed us rather than hiding from us - it was clear that she knew why we were waiting.

    Overall, Maggiano's delivers if you're looking to go with a crowd and are seeking out a variety of options. The food is consistent and decent. It's more of a touristy-chain restaurant and due to this, service can get overwhelmed. In this particular instance, it was not necessarily a case of being overwhelmed, but clearly something was amiss. Hopefully not the norm - regardless - there are better Italian options in Philadelphia if you're not looking to be a glutton.     Read more

    Review intended for a PRIVATE PARTY:

    I organized our office holiday party for 30 people at Maggiano's this past weekend and had a fantastic experience. Party planning was made easy by choosing the Milano package ($35 food and $40 open bar per person). We were in the Napoleon room which was very pleasant with a nice classy touch. The service was absolutely phenomenal and definitely added to why the party was such a success.

    Food Line Up:

    Started with the bruschetta & sausage peppers and onions served on a buffet. The bruschetta was great! I was pleasantly surprised to find cannellini beans in it! Turned out to be very tasty.
    Got the Maggiano's house salad & caesar salad served to the tables family style. I recommend both but to request less dressing as it was a little drenched and I had some guests complain.
