    When five separate people all told me I had to try Village Whiskey in Philadelphia, I knew this place had to be something. And I was right! If you love whiskey, this is your spot. It's a bit small, but I've been back twice and lucked out with a barstool every time.

    We started off with the whiskey flights, there's three options, we went with the Rickhouse and were quite satisfied. I've had the Village Burger, and it's a good burger, but it won't rewrite your universe. What might, are the duck-fat fries. I live in a different city also known for fantastic food, and as much as I've scoured, we do not have fries cooked in duck-fat. I should open a food truck and serve them, because they are amazing!

    Finally, there's a huge menu of whiskies. Try the Redbreast if you need a place to start. Any time I've ordered it, the bartender has always been impressed.

    Next time I'm in Philly, I'm definitely headed back to Village Whiskey.

    My sister and grandmother were in town the other weekend and wanted to meet for dinner. I turned to Yelp for advice, because I really don't get down to the city much for dinner. I stumbled upon Village Whiskey with excellent reviews. As a whiskey lover myself, I thought why not give it a try.

    We arrived at  5:30pm on a Saturday night. They weren't kidding when they said it was small. Since they don't take reservations here, we put our name in and was told it would be about a half hour. We were seated at table about 20 minutes later. My sister ordered a glass of pinot grigio and I ordered the Old Fashioned. This drink is awesome especially if you like bourbon. The following day I tried to replicate it at my house, but to no avail.

     We decided to share the crab cocktail for appetizer. It's a rocks glass full of lump crab meat. No skimping here, just big chunks of crab meat and the glass was full.
    For dinner, my grandmother had the Pulled Pork Sandwich, my sister had the Lobster Mac n Cheese and I had the Village Burger with Cheddar. I love their simple menu because it is easy and fun. The burger was a perfectly cooked medium well.
    the meat was juicy and flavorful. I kind of wanted a second one, but thought  better of it. I had a bit of the pulled pork and the lobster mac. Both tasted great. We did get an order of duck fat fries. First time any of us had them. They were really good.

     For dessert, we split the chocolate cake with ice cream. My sister had a shot of espresso as she would be driving my grandmother back to New Jersey after dinner.
    I tried pay for dinner, but my grandmother wasn't having any of that. The total bill with tip was $145.00. Taking into consideration that my sister and I each had 2 drinks, I thought check was right on par for three people eating in center city. I will definitely be back.

    Try to arrive early if you can, because Village Whiskey doesn't take reservations and gets PACKED after people get off work on weekdays. My friends and I were luckily able to come for an early dinner at 5:30 on a Thursday, so there were still enough empty seats for us to get seated right away.

    Having read about the famous burgers here, of course that's what I ordered. Not wanting to break the bank with too many burger toppings, I added only caramelized onions to my Village Burger. The meat came slightly overdone (i.e., no pinkness whatsoever for something that was supposed to be medium), but was tender and juicy enough that I didn't even notice until my friend pointed it out. With the thousand island dressing and the caramelized onions, the burger was messy, but very delicious.

    The duck fat fries, however, weren't quite as exciting. I found them too salty, and while the cheddar sauce was good, it could probably be made better with a kick of spice.

    Overall, worth coming back to? Definitely yes for the burgers!

    Listed in philly phaves, tipsy, carnivores galore

    it's been two years since i last dined here. perhaps i've overhyped this place with my memories, or perhaps i've just had a lot of really delicious burgers in those two years.. but the village burger isn't what i remember it to be.

    it is still a solid burger in its own right -- medium rare cooked the way it's supposed to be, delicious homemade thousand island sauce, and a nice greasy sesame bun. but there wasn't anything MAGICAL about the burger this time around.

    and since they've raised the price of this burger to $12, i'm almost inclined to go across the street and get two shake shack burgers for the same price (same medium rare meat quality, similar greasy delicious bun).

    i also tried the duck fat fries this time. and while they're good, they too are also nothing to write home about.

    but what maintains this place at 4 stars is the whiskey (whisky?) selection. with almost 200 bottles of whiskys behind the bar, village whiskey has the largest single collection in all of philadelphia. if you're craving anything -- be it bourbon, rye, single malt, american, asian, irish, scotch -- chances are, you'll find it here.

    the fact that it's two blocks from my apartment also doesn't hurt. you can bet i'll be back here at the bar nursing a glass of single malt whisky again soon.

Specialties

A ground-to-order Angus Beef Burger, a Veggie Burger, a Bison Burger or an Vienna All-Beef Hot Dog with all the trimmings are each served alongside duck fat French fries and Sly Fox cheddar sauce.  Burgers are topped with Village Whiskey's house-made thousand-island dressing, Bibb lettuce, beefsteak tomato and a potato-beer bun, with optional toppings including bacon, caramelized onion, truffles, artichokes and a selection of cheeses.

As one of the most comprehensive collections of its namesake spirit available in Philadelphia, Village Whiskey features a veritable library of 80 to 100 varieties of whiskey, bourbon, rye and scotch from Scotland, Canada, Ireland, United States and even a few Japanese options.

History

Established in 2009.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
