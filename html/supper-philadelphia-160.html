    Listed in Favourite Date Spots

    Savored.com lured me into joining their sham with promises of 30% off my bill if I book a table through their website.  Turns out, that also includes a $10 booking fee and the selection of restaurants isn't great so I pretty much ignored it after I signed up.  Until, that is, I got an email saying they'd waive the first booking fee.  Well in that case...

    Obviously the best strategy here is to go with a pricy place to get your savings' worth.  Supper it is!  We came in at 9 on a Tuesday night and the place was fairly quiet.  We were seated promptly at a two-top in the corner of the front windows, which made for a nice view but a bit of a draft on a chilly night.  I warmed up with a Village Green Preservation Society cocktail because I couldn't resist the hilariously waspy name and it had gin.  Tasty, but slightly too sweet for my preference.  E got an IPA that came in a freakin huge can with a frosty beer mug.

    We started by sharing an order of hush puppies and the relish tray.  The hush puppies were the best I've found in the city so far and really close to being absolutely perfect.  Three big fluffy balls of cornmeal batter with the perfect crisp fried exterior, they were pillowy little morsels of comfort and the BBQ sauce on the plate really made it happen in my mouth.  The hush puppies themselves could have used some more seasoning but overall they were delicious.  Relish tray included pickled okra with red onions, a whipped ricotta with garlic oil and chives, and an eggplant caponata with mint and feta.  I couldn't get enough of the caponata, it was soooo good.  The okra was also outstanding (E even ate some and he's not an okra fan) and the ricotta was luxurious and pungent.  My only complaint was I wish they had served it with something more substantial than thin crisp wafers of crostini bread.

    Between the apps and our entrees we enjoyed the warm buttered garlic crab dish, which was possibly the best item of the night.  It came in a little cast iron skillet and was comprised of a big portion of crab mixed with lemon, garlic, capers, and butter, served with a garlic-cheese bread on the side.  There aren't words for how tasty this was.  We demolished it while we waited for our entrees, which took a little longer than would have been expected on a quiet night.  I went with the duck and waffles, always a lover of kitsch, and he got the scallops as per usual.  The duck and waffles was excellent- perfect confit leg, impeccable balance of sweetness from the maple glaze and offset by the pickled cabbage.  I ate everything on my plate, including all the cabbage which isn't usually my bag.  E's scallops were well-seasoned and the plate was thoughtful, but the scallops had inconsistency in their cooking.  They were all different sizes (and only 4 scallops, rather small at that, is a rip-off for $26)  and it seemed as though they had all been cooked the same length of time so some were slightly overcooked and others were perfect.  It was late and maybe the line cooks were ready to get the eff out of there, who knows?  Everything else was awesome.

    Service was unobtrusive and perfectly satisfactory, but something to note: a 20% gratuity was added to our bill.  I don't know if this is because we used savored.com to book the table (nothing on that website mentions an automatic gratuity), but it's something to watch out for.  The damage wasn't too bad considering all the food we got and our drinks- $100 exactly before the discount.  I would not recommend ordering less food than we did, however, as I can see how someone could easily leave this place hungry.  2-3 apps and an entree each for two people is the perfect meal here.

Specialties

Supper serves seasonal modern Amercian cuisine in a beautiful Urban Farmhouse setting.  Whenever and wherever possible we source local, organic and sustainable ingredients.  All charcuterie, preserves, pickles and desserts are made in house as well as the signature housebaked crackers for our All American artisan cheese selection.
Supper is open 7 nights for dinner service beginning at 5pm.  We are open for lunch Wednesdays through Saturdays from 11:30 am to 2:30 pm and for our acclaimed Sunday brunch from 11:00 am to 2:30 pm.
Supper is also available for private events.  We can custom design a menu for you through our catering company, The Global Dish or from our a la carte menu selections.  Please contact Sue Solomon so that we can help you plan an event to remember.
