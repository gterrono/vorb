    It seems lately that a major city cannot put itself on the culinary map without having a high-end, Asian-fusion-whatever-you-want-to-call-it type of restaurant.  Buddakan does not disappoint and puts an otherwise uneventful city on the map for me.

    The interior is clean, modern, spacious and classy.  The 30' Budda sitting serenely mid-restaurant seems out of place in this busy, boisterous eatery in which the food seemed to come almost instantaneously.  

    Our waiter was typical but very nice.  Not too pushy and offering guidance when asked.  Just right.  The food is the main attraction (naturally) and did not disappoint.  Our group of 5 ate mostly appetizers and a few entrees shared amongst the group.  Note:  ORDER SLOWLY.  The meals are VERY large and can easily be shared by 3-4 people.  Plus the food comes at an outrageously fast pace, so no need to worry that ordering later will tack on an additional hour to your experience.

    And now to the food:

    Edamame ravioli - perfect in every way conceivable.  This was my favorite dish of the evening.

    Tuna pizza - my least favorite of the evening.  Not enough ingenuity to merit taking up 1/5 of my stomach space.  Must save room for the others.

    King Crab Dumplings - sublime. A must try.

    Chicken and Ginger Dumplings  - better than the King Crab (but order both for comparison  . . . .the taste differences are hard to explain).

    Asian chicken caeser salad - a literal Mount Fuji of salad.  Terrific.  Might have been slightly better if served toward the beginning of our meal.

    Pad Thai (chicken) - The wait staff's accomodation for swapping shrimp for chicken was very much appreciated.  Although the pad thai was "great" by my wife, it certainly was not amazing.  Our definitive best pad thai of all time in Bangkok.  I felt the executive chef needed to revamp this dish so the level of quality matched the other items.

    And to wash it all down:  cold sake and Japanese beer.  For all of these items the total bill clocked in at about $40pp.  An excellent value and a pleasurable experience.  I would certainly come back if I ever stray as far as Philadelphia again.

    Listed in Phabulously Philly

    Buddakan was an absolute treat... not being an actual Phillyphyte I often have to look to Yelp and local suggestions when I'm entertaining clients in town. Unfortunately in the past I've felt a bit let down by the likes of Roy's and Morton's and started to feel Philly cuisine as a whole may be somewhat hopeless, save for those cheesesteaks of course.

    Clearly I couldn't have been more wrong, I just wasn't looking in the right places. Buddakan hit the spot for every criteria I look for when dining out well or putting on my host hat.

    * Fabulous décor - a modern, lofty, dramatic space with tons of beautiful lighting, candlelight, asian inspired touches and of course, that tremendous and gorgeous gold buddha sitting over the main room. The atmosphere generates an electric buzz that immediately lets you know that you're somewhere special. This is a hot date spot - take note Philly boys!

    * Great Service - we were thoughtfully attended to throughout the evening. Our group of 8 was a bit rowdy once the drinks were flowing and our server got in on the fun, which we loved. His suggestions were on point and a few freebies were even thrown our way which always scores huge points (and heftier tips) in my book.

    * Fabulous Food - The menu is robust with inventive and fusion inspired selections. Not one dish hit a sour note and made for a very impressive showing in front of my clients. When the restaurant can put a feather in my cap this way I love to play along... make me a hero! We went for many of the apps, a salad, a rice and individual entreés for each diner. I don't want you to hit the snooze button on me with a run-down of each item so let me just toss out the highlights......
                       - Buddakan-style Hamachi sashmi; citrus soy, sizzling oil
                       - Pacific Prawns; crisp noodle blanket, spicy tomato
                       - Scallion Pancake; braised beef shortrib, soybean hummus, apple
                       - Crab fried rice; lump crab meat, wok fried egg, ginger
                       -  Pan seared sea bass; truffle jus, butternut squash, maitake mushrooms
                       - Asian BBQ Pork; chinese broccoli, giant panko onion rings
    Is your mouth watering yet? You won't go wrong with any of the above selections so dig in. Artfully presented, aromatic and hands-down tasty, Buddakan does it right with complex flavor and quality ingredients.
                       
    * Decadent Desserts - As if all of the above wasn't already gluttonous enough, right? Now you must make room in your belly for a little taste of something sweet. We shared a medley of tasty treats, each one better and more sinful then the next. The chocolate bento box, 'dip sum' donuts and banana tower were group favorites and some of the best layers of sweet flavors I've had in Philly, ever.

    Three cheers to the big buddha for changing my mind on dining in this monumental city... let's hear it for life, liberty and the pursuit of dining perfection for all!

The main dining area downstairs is louder and has more "buzz," as conversation, groovy music and people watching all meld together. Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
