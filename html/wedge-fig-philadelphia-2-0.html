    We stumbled upon this Olde City hole-in-the-wall and decided to betray our traditional Sunday, cheese-centered, brunchy-lunchy establishment to give Wedge & Fig a shot.

    I am glad that we did.

    The atmosphere and staff are arguably the best aspects of this restaurant. We were immediately seated in the garden, a small courtyard in the backside of the restaurant, shaded by a large tree and high brick walls. There were several other tables with diners, but the small space was quiet enough to enjoy reading the newspaper.

    Onto the staff. Our waiter was awesome. In addition to being extremely attentive and friendly, he was both knowledgeable and honest about dishes and cheeses. Rather than give the traditional cow/sheep/goat description, he took the time to describe the various cheeses in terms that all sorts of diners would understand.

    And finally the food.

    1) This may sound simple, but having French Press coffee at a small establishment always makes me smile. Nice touch.

    2) The potatoes were fantastic. Slightly roasted, with lemon zest and a dash of cheese gave these a lighter taste than your traditional breakfast spuds. No ketchup needed here.

    3) Paninis are apparently a recent addition, and a good one at that. As other reviewers have mentioned, they are wonderfully creative. I don't even remember what my girlfriend ordered, except for that it was the special of the day and that she ate every bite.

    My panini presented the only drawback - I had the Blue Fig, a seemingly delicious concoction of fourme d'ambert, fig spread, and prosciutto. The cheese/meat combination was fairly strong, with a bit of a salty punch. Only problem? I couldn't pull apart the prosciutto, no matter how hard I tried. Interesting taste, hard to eat.

    Definitely stop by and give Wedge & Fig a try. Fantastic atmosphere and staff paired with a creative menu make this a great addition to the neighborhood.

The garden was the perfect setting for brunch, and you can even watch them make your food through the picture window into the kitchen. We started with the large french press coffee, which we were told would be more than enough for three people, but it ended up only filling our three mugs 3/4 of the way.     I had a really hard time deciding on what to order, since the whole menu looked really good. I decided on the omelette of the day, which  included bacon, some "tangy french cheese", leeks, and a charred corn and tomato salsa. The lemon parmesan potatoes it came with were sooo good, and there was a little side of dressed greens as well. The omelette was amazing, I wasn't sure of the flavors at first (corn and eggs?) but it went together perfectly. My only wish would be for a little more cheese. The portions were pretty small compared to behemoth standards set by places like Sabrina's and Morning Glory, but I didn't mind because I didn't feel like I was going to die/slip into a permanent food coma after. Prices were fair, about $9-$11 for brunch/lunch items.

    Another high point was the service. I wish I remembered our server's name so I could give him a shout out, but we loved him and his charming dry humor. I noticed they are BYO, so I can't wait to return with some wine and nom on some delicious cheese.

    Listed in All Things Old and City-like, Proving New Yorkers Wrong, Bringing My Own

    Wedge & Fig might be one of the best local businesses in Old City. I really love everything about this place!

    The staff is friendly and seem very invested in the success of the overall business. The food is amazing (I've been for both brunch and lunch). For brunch, they have a bread pudding special that each and every time sounds more delicious than the last. Their sandwiches are fun and inventive, especially their specials. If the panini of the day is not your scene, try the Tamanend, you won't be disappointed. Their mac & cheese is wonderful and always changing; they use various cheeses from their case so it is different from one day to the next.

    Wedge & Fig also has some wonderful events and pairings. Chocolate & Wine, Cheese & Beer, etc. The venue also is BYO, and on weekend nights you can bring in a bottle of wine and get a cheese plate to match.

    The location is also a cheese shop; they carry quite the assortment of cheese and are always willing to help you narrow down the selection.

    The actual place is quite small. They have a wonderful outdoor garden in the back (with a heater for cold days) that makes you feel like you've escaped to a small European city.

    If you're ever on N 3rd, be sure to stop in here and grab a bite to eat, I'm sure you don't be disappointed.

Specialties

Old City's Cheese Bistro, featuring dinner, lunch and weekend brunch.  Sample our chicken pot pie, mac & cheese and scrumptious salads in the evening.  For lunch, try  our unique sandwiches, salads and soups (take out available for most items).  Omelettes, strata, cream-cheese-stuffed French toast are some brunch favorites.  Dine under the stars in our secluded garden.  Bring a bottle of wine or ale and we will pair it with a selection of cheeses.  The perfect romantic evening!

History

Established in 2006.

We rescued Old City Cheese Shop just before it closed.  We gave it some badly needed upgrades, changed the menu, re-named the store to wedge+fig.  And we stocked the cheese case with cheese!

Meet the Business Owner

Kirk N. Business Owner

Cheese lover from an early age.  Lived on a sailboat for 15 years.  Used to play guitar.  Now I own a restaurant.
