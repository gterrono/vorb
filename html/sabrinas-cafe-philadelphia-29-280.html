    Listed in When I want to munch on brunch!

    I haven't been here in years but I wanted to add it to my brunch list. I prefer this location to the Christian Street location because parking is better and I always felt cramped at the Christian Street location. The food is the same though.

    Sabrina's is a really good brunch spot, but I really reallly think it's just a bit overrated. Sorry. I would go more if it wasn't so damn popular. I know. I sound like a hater. But I don't care. The hype is just too much for me.

    On to the food. The food is yummy. I was a lacto veg when I first started eating here, so the tofu scramble was a dream for a non-egg eater. It came with toast and homefries which were also winners. Years prior, I tried the stuffed french toast which was a favorite of friends of mine but not me-too sweet. I've had a big waffle as well as pancakes. Both good enough. I don't know if it was for brunch, but the fish tacos are damn good too.

    When the boyfriend got sick I even ordered a really good cup of chicken noodle soup from Sabrina's. Cause you know I wasn't about to make it.

    ZOMG this place is good. I'd heard about Sabrina's from coworkers and friends, but it was only this past weekend when fortune saw fit to bring me to the neighborhood for one of the legendary brunches. We were told to expect a 45-minute wait, which actually turned out to be about 5 minutes. There's an extra star right there.

    The place was completely slammed, as expected, but that didn't stop our server from taking prompt, efficient care of us and our food. We each had an entree and split an order of the stuffed challah French toast for the table. It was heavenly, though I kind of wish we'd gotten the plain version as I'm not the world's biggest cream cheese fan. My eggs and turkey bacon were very satisfying, and the bites I stole of my friend's buffalo chicken salad were equally enjoyable.

    I'd highly recommend this place if you're in the mood to eat an entire army's worth of food for a reasonable price.
