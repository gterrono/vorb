    Unique beers, love the bobble-heads, decent food.

    Was in town for the MeetIn.org convention and decided to check out some local brew with some folks from the group. We did the "flight", a sampler of all the beers on tap. Their beers were very unique. I wish I could remember the names. But, there was one that looked dark like guiness, but didn't taste heavy like a guiness. I figured the weizen was a wheat beer, nope, it tasted like apple juice! I would definitely recommend you do the flight at this place to try all the beers. Some you'll love, some you'll hate, but either way it's an experience.

    Had the chicken pasta. I try not to order anything too creative in a bar. But, it was quite good. If I ever find myself back in town, I may try their quiche of the day.

    They lose one point for ambiance. The place is pretty old and run down, not in a charming way, but in a dirty way. The booths are cute, but it makes me wonder when they were last cleaned. But, the bobble head collection is a thing of beauty, you must head into this place for that if nothing else.

    Nodding Head is pretty much the old standby for a chill night of beer, pub food, and philosophizing.  I've been going here for nearly a decade, so it's interesting to compare my experiences to the other reviews.  The positive reviews are spot-on:  delicious and creative brews, chill vibe, yummy food, friendly staff.  The negative reviews are also things I've experienced on occasion, but not often enough to call it a pattern:  twice I've had microbial-tasting beer, the kitchen has had an off-night now and then, and sometimes your server just has a bad day.  Oh well.  Nobody's perfect.

    But really, Nodding Head is everything you want a pub to be.  It's friendly and cozy, and there's usually a table or spot at the bar available.  You can bring your Model UN of friends and they'll all find something to like on the menu.  And they brew their own beer, which deserves a level of respect in itself.

    The restaurant sits atop a flight of narrow, steep stairs that I would hate to walk down after a long night with their delicious beers.

    The place has real character. It's dark, it's old, the tables have been gouged by patrons over the years and its even a bit smoky (which I'm not sure is legal!) The bathrooms only hold one at a time, there are few "bar" qualities, and yet its somewhere I would want to spend many nights should I be lucky enough to live nearby.

    The baked brie with mango chutney is simply one of the best two or three items of food I have ever put in my mouth. It's creamy, its offset by the sweetness of the chutney, and served with warm, freshly baked pita wedges. I could eat this every day of my life and never be sick of it. The mufaletta sandwich is very tasty, although slightly overpowered by the herbal pesto generously applied to the fresh bread. Combined with the roasted red peppers and sun dried tomatos, and it is difficult to pick up the ham and pepperoni inside. My friend's jerk chicken was likewise lost amongst other flavors and it tasted little more than chicken. The fries were crispy and very, very, very delicious.

    Be careful if you come here alone and order a sampler. You will be greeted by seven or eight glasses filled with at least six ounces of liquid. But they are very, very well done. The oatmeal stout was especially good, and the "Grog" was an excellent English-styled nut brown. But the true star was the BPA, a British Pale Ale, that was somehow not hoppy up front but explosively floral and intricate and hop taste throughout the experience. Plus it went perfectly with the brie!

    Three words: Monkey Knife Fight. This beer sums up what I like about Nodding Head's brews: they have creative flavors without always relying on the crutch of heavy stouts or porters with high alcohol content served in a teeny glass. I think Nodding Head's lighter beer creations set it apart from most microbrews in the city, and that's a good thing. The price per beer hovers around $5 which is no deal but also not extravagant.

    The food is fairly mediocre across the board, but they have a few strong points. I'd recommend the French Dip if you're particularly interested in eating food, but I think Nodding Head is a better drinking bar than a place to go for a meal.

    One nice thing about Nodding Head is that the bar area is a good size for their usual crowd, so you can usually find a place to sit, many times at the bar without much trouble. It's also not particularly difficult to get a table and there are a few cushy booths reserved for bar dwellers that you can jump into without hostess intervention. I'd recommend popping by Nodding Head immediately after work around 5 or later at night in the ten to midnight hour.
