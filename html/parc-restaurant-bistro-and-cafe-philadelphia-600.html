    Listed in In and around Ritten-wait-for-it-HOUSE, my house, The "50 List" 2013

    Parc is the perfectly situated tourist trap of Rittenhouse Square. It's enormous, cold atmosphere and the machine-like churning of people in and out of this place make it unmemorable and unwelcoming.

    The servers are for the most part super rushed, non-attentive, and some just have poor attitudes, as if their customers are burdens to them. Sitting outside seems like a really good idea for people watching/getting bumped into by everyone walking by, but you will wait twice as long to be served or paid attention to since your server can easily hide inside. I had to go inside and find mine once.

    The drinks, oh the drinks. I have been here many a time for drinks alone and I don't know why it took me so long to figure out that they are underwhelming and way over priced. They have what appears to be a decent selection of classic and craft cocktails, some of which are ok, but it appears that these bartenders have memorized the small list on the menu because when presented with the request to make something off menu (an old fashioned, mind you) the product is made incorrectly and you are charged $13 for it. I appreciate their absinthe selection, and their absinthe service, but that is about it.

    I am not sure why I keep coming back to this place. I guess it is location alone. It is a place I can take people from out of town with the pretense that "it is in a great location and is great for people watching, but don't expect too much."

    Its a Steven Starr restaurant, so you know it's going to have a certain level of authenticity when it comes to food and atmosphere. Parc is supposed to enliven the Parisian Bistro.  Though I have yet to get to France,  I was feeling pretty Parisian as I dined in the cozy atmosphere with good food and drinks.

    I had the moules frites (mussels + a side of french fries), and Onion Soup. And, is it just me or is everyone who works there beautiful?

    Great place for a date or with a group of friends.  I would love to return and sit outside, the bistro tables look like a great place to enjoy the weather and people-watch at Rittenhouse Square-- all while enjoying a cheese plate, some wine and some good company.

    Parc is great for the obvious reasons: Steven Starr-style trendy atmosphere, quality service, quality food. The location is the best part, and the people-watching right off of Rittenhouse Square really is INCREDIBLY entertaining.

    As far as Steven Starr restaurants go though, this is not the highest on my list. Most entrees I've had are 70% bread, and while bread might just be my favorite food, it's too much. (Although the cranberry/walnut bread they give you in the bread basket is pretty awesome) For some reason, the French bistro atmosphere feels somewhat false and lacks the original touch that you find at most Starr restaurants.

    Best time you'll have: go when the weather is fine around 3 or 4 PM to avoid crowds, sit outside and get a bottle of white wine and the warm shrimp salad.

    ***Great, Classy French Dinner***

    Note--this review is only based on one dining experience for dinner.

    Positives:
    -Great location: the restaurant is situated right on Rittenhouse Square. Must be nice to enjoy a meal outside (to see and/or be seen depending on your goals) when the weather is permitting.
    -Moules Frites: Excellent entree. The best part was all the leftover sauce from the mussels that you can liberally sop up with the bread. There were a good amount of mussels and the fries were really tasty, although I could have stood them coming out a bit warmer. Since this entree was one of the cheaper options, I'd highly recommend it.
    -The Bread. It is delicious and they will be happy to bring as much of it as you desire.
    -Milk Chocolate Pot de Creme: just about as rich as a pot of creme...absolutely chocoliciousness. Highly recommended for all chocolate lovers.
    -Service is very professional and well done. No qualms.
    -Parc matchboxes. Make sure to pick one up before you leave for the novelty.

    Why Not Five Stars:
    -From the small sample size of what food for two people, it seems they use Cheesecake Factory-esque portions of butter in their dishes, much to the same effect (of making them delicious). And of course, the ingredients are better here, but by no means think of this as a health meal for most orders.
    -Bathrooms could have been nicer. One of my metrics for a really nice restaurant is how nice the bathrooms are. While they were very clean, large, and elegant enough, I have been to slightly cheaper restaurants whose bathrooms are a good bit fancier. Unfair as it may be, to get into 5-star territory, I need to be all-around wowed, and for the price of this place, I expect luxurious bathrooms.
    -Trout Amandine: My friend ordered this...it looked good and that's what he indicated, although I don't think it blew him away. The haricots verts (aka green beans) were really good though.
    -Fromage Blanc Cheesecake: good but not great. Again, if you love chocolate, go with the pot of creme.
    -It ain't cheap.

    It's definitely an older crowd here with a few families, but it still seems like a nice place for a classy date. Yes its big, and perhaps for Philly residents slightly touristy, but I would recommend Parc to anyone looking for a nice dinner.

Specialties

This stylish addition to Rittenhouse Square pays tribute to French cafe culture, serving breakfast, lunch and dinner seven days a week. Ideal for a quick bite or a leisurely meal, the menu is filled with classics: onion soup, escargots, steak frites and towering plateaus de fruits de mer. The baguettes are made in-house and  the wine list is expansive. Sip a Kir Royale at the zinc bar or enjoy a cafe au lait while basking in the sun.
