    Came here with some friends on a rainy friday afternoon.  We chose to get the restaurant week menu.  I did notice that the menu consisted of the cheapest items that they served.  NO fried icecream.  The soup was actually very good and it had actual scallops and mussels not the typical tofu like other joints. The starter consisted of some sort of steamed shrimp dumpling (similar to something you get for dimsum).  That was my favorite part of the meal.  Next came a salad which was literally iceberg and some arugula thrown  on  salmon eggs.  The main dish was a combo sashimi platter.  Everything here was mediocre, nothing special, the fish wasn't too fresh but not bad.  I really couldn't complain about it but I am never going to crave fuji mountain.  The dessert was red bean ice cream (self explanatory).

    Bottom Line-  So-So sushi,  Great tea, nice atmosphere, not the best but not the worst.

    I'm sure that we weren't the only ones using a Groupon, seven-course tasting here this weekend, as I got a feeling of deja vu when the waitresses kept walking by with the same plates.  I haven't been thrilled with the sushi experiences here in Philadelphia, but this one was pretty good.  No fancy rolls, mayo infused sauces, or soy splattered plates, just the good stuff with a few nice Japanese dishes to accompany it.  Not to mention, the service was better than most at a sushi restaurant.

    The seaweed salad, typical but good was an afterthought compared to the perfect seafood miso soup.  I've never had miso soup quite like that, but it was the show stealing dish of the night.  Perfect balance and a nice big bowl (could have easily finished off two of them).  The sushi appetizer was nice, and I must say that I've never had cuddlefish liver before.  I'm always up for trying a new one, and it was very pleasant.  

    The kitchen appetizer was middle of the road.  The chicken, soy glazed, blah balls were not worth a second tasting.  The sushi sampler on the other hand, was excellent.  Great rice, nice ratio, and very fresh fish is all it takes to impress on this course.  The final kitchen entree was also surprisingly good.  Miso glazed white fish over some kind of Japanese inspired sweet potatoes.  Wasn't expecting to like it, but I did.  

    The negatives (other than the aforementioned chicken balls):  terrible dessert, which I'm docking a full star for and extremely small portions.  This is another Groupon that makes me scratch my head...was it really worth the cost.

Specialties

Authentic Japanese Cuisine specializing in high quality & fresh sushi. We are an award winning sushi oasis offering lunch, dinner, late-night & private karaoke parties

Our fourth floor karaoke room is ideal for private parties, and can accommodate up to 30 people. It offers the same full menu as our other floors, and is a great place to let loose and enjoy the night. Direct access to the bar is only a few steps away, and European bottle service is available. The room can be rented anytime from 6PM - 2AM daily, and reservations are strongly recommended.
