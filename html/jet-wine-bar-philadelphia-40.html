    Listed in Best Happy Hour

    I hesitate writing this review and giving away what I think is one of the best kept wine secrets in Philly, but I will take my chances. To start, the decor and vibe of this little nook appeals to a younger crowd than what most Philly wine bars have tried to cater to. I think this is wise, as many young people are developing an appreciation and education for good wine. The music was fun and funky, and the open air seating by the window enriched the experience with people watching (we were approached by a real-life bubble fairy!) and fresh air.
    Service was handled solely by one sweet, fresh -faced bartender who was on the shy side, but attentive nonetheless. I can't speak for his wine knowledge however, because I didn't ask questions, but he hooked us up with 2 free pours after alerting him to the close-call of the owner getting a parking ticket. That gesture alone would make me return.
    As for the wine, by far, the most eclectic and interesting list I've seen in Philly. Some folks complained that it was short, but I disagree. I tried the Tjokali Spanish white exhibiting toasted, smoky orange favors that was totally exotic. I'm an educated oenophile and have never even heard of this grape, whic I found refreshing and exciting.
    They have daily wine flights, which is scribbled at the bottom of the menu and I didn't notice in time...woulda done it had I seen it.
    I also like that they have daily activities planned, like a viewing of Spirited Away in the evening. The fact that they even know that movie made me realize these were my kinda peeps....
    And finally, they have daily happy hour which a few wine bars think they're too good for. We tried one, the montepulciano, which is a standard house wine, and it was good enough. But to be honest, I was so intrigued by some of the selections (sparkling rose Pinot grigio....WHAAAT?) that I passed on the happy hour deals and enjoyed what the list had to offer. Even the app HH selections were great...a truffled tremor cheese plate with plentiful baguette, grapes, blueberries, and Cajun-spiced cashews, for $4.50? Incredible.
    Kudos, well-done in every respect, I found my new favorite South Street hang out, just in time for summer. Look out...I plan to be there at 4:30 to get the good seat at the window.

    Listed in We Love Our Neighborhood!, Drinking in Philly, Philly Favorites, South Street is my new Broadway

    In the past year, Jet has become much more than a wine bar to us. It's a place in the neighborhood where we can congregate, meet other neighbors, hang out with the bartenders, and on top of that enjoy some wonderful wine and food. I've really come to appreciate the eclectic wine selection at Jet, which emphasizes uncommon wines from around the world. Sure, there are other places in the city where you can enjoy fine wine from the usual suspects, but at Jet I can have obscure wines from Brazil and Lebanon, and even some excellent Pennsylvania wines that I never would have tried otherwise. The wine menu changes frequently, as the owner Jill finds more new and interesting wines to share with us.

    One of my favorite things at Jet is their Tuesday night flight night, where you can get three generous pours of any wines on the menu for only $10. It's a total steal, and a great way to try new wines. They also have some nice happy hour specials, with great prices on bar snacks. Have I mentioned I love their bar snacks? Their house-spiced nuts, house-spiced carrots, olives, and deviled eggs are all delightful. I also really enjoy their bruschetta and their sharing platters, particularly their vegetarian platter and meat & cheese plates. Sometimes a cheese plate hits the spot, which comes with a choice of any three (or five) of their cheeses as well as a selection of fruit and nuts. Martini Mondays are another great night to come to Jet, where bartender Amanda often features concoctions made from house-infused spirits.

    All in all, I love the food and wine, but it's the excellent service and neighborhood/community vibe that make this 5 stars.

    Jet is my local neighborhood wine bar, and I love it because that's precisely what it feels like:… Jet is my local neighborhood wine bar, and I love it because that's precisely what it feels like: neighborhoody and friendly. Kind of like the Cheers of wine bars, to use a cliché. I love going to a place where we are recognized, and when you look around the bar, you see a bunch of other locals who all seem to know each other and the staff. They'll really try to help you find wines that you like, even sometimes bringing by other things to try, and if you don't like it, no worries, they'll get you something else. As far as food, they have some very tasty and reasonably priced nibbles. I've never been for brunch, but I do think it's rad that they have Monday brunch. They usually have some fun movies streaming on Netflix, or sometimes sports. I prefer it during the afternoon when it's nice and peaceful; at night it can get a little loud for me, but as long as it's mellow and not too crowded I'll have a good time. They do have a full bar as well as a decent bottled beer selection, but I generally stick to wine when I'm here. I also appreciate that they have an espresso machine, as a lot of bars really drop the ball when it comes to coffee. Specialties

At Jet Wine Bar, we ask you to think globally, drink locally. In the mood to send your taste buds to Santorini, Sardegna, or beyond? Jet Wine Bar can take you there.

We offer a revolving menu of small plates. Jet offers meats, cheeses, sandwiches, and snacks to complement our diverse wine and beverage selection.

You don't need a passport to sample wines and tastes from around the world, just set your course for Jet!

Every day is special:
Martini Mondays
Tasting Tuesdays
Wednesday DJ
Thursday Movie Night

History

Established in 2010.

Jet Wine Bar opened in November of 2010 as the love child of an archaeologist and an engineer.  We love wine, we love bars... we love wine bars!  We wanted to bring a friendly, accessible wine bar to a great neighborhood.

Meet the Business Owner

Jill W. Business Owner

Jill comes to the restaurant world from... archaeology.  She has worked in Syria, Turkey, and Armenia since 1992.  1992 is also the year she came to Philly to start graduate work at UPenn.  She fell in love with the city (and her partner, Evan), and has been here ever since.
