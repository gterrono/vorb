    Came here for a cocktail and an appetizer while waiting for a table at another restaurant and was not disappointed. The darkish corners and warm candlelight made it obvious why this was clearly a great date night locale.

    I had a cocktail with vodka, passion fruit and fresh thyme. Sometimes savory additions can be a little TOO savory (know what I mean?) but the little hint of herbacious freshness perfectly balanced the bright beverage.

    I went with the chef's plate, which included two cheeses and two meats, plus accompaniments. I let the bartender select the cheese and meats and was not disappointed. In addition to two cured meats - lomo and one I can't remember - and the seasonal cheeses, the platter included an olive and caper berry, marcona almonds, baguette, two kinds off house-made crackers, stone ground mustard and house-pickled beets, red onions and carrots. It was more than I expected, and well worth the $15 or so.

    After a long turkey-filled Thanksgiving weekend, we were craving something that wasn't poultry and sleep-inducing. Paella was our main goal, so we opted to try out Jamonera after a quick search on Yelp indicated that this was a good place to come to.

    We arrived just a little too late on Sunday night to get a table, but we were seated at the copper-topped bar and were served by a friendly bartender who gave us suggestions from the menu.

    The two of us managed to fill up on the seafood paella, the brussels sprouts, and the meatballs. The meatballs were incredibly flavourful, and I enjoyed the olive tapenade topping and melted cheese combination. The seafood paella was delicious, albeit on the wet risotto side of the spectrum. Sprouts were okay, but nothing memorable.

    Often tapas leaves you hungry and broke, but what I really enjoyed about Jamonera is that it was the exact opposite experience: dishes were generous in serving portions, while prices weren't a hit to the wallet. This restaurant is definitely worth revisiting.

    Jamonera, you cheeky gal. You offer tapas, your servers suggest 2-3 per diner, and I come hungry. Between the two of us, we low-balled for 5 total dishes and two drinks each. And what we got.... well, it felt lik the equivalent of 6-8 normal tapas.

    I'll go ahead and say that this isn't NECESSARILY a bad thing. I enjoy generous portions, and these are certainly a bang for your buck. However, when the waitress requested that we order all our dishes at the front end (instead of our initial idea of "let's try two and see how we feel afterwards, go from there") I felt a little hoodwinked. I think that if we were two ravenous, bulky fellows, this would've been a solid suggestion. But my date was a tiny lady, and I, despite possessing Amazonian stature, was unable to pick up the slack alone.

    The speed that the dishes came out was worthy of praise, as it seemed like we'd only just received our drinks when our first dish arrived. Medjool dates, wrapped in bacon, stuffed with valdeon, THIS was the only real feeling of a traditionally sized tapas. Two dates per gal, really whet our appetite. When the Papa Frita was brought our way, though, I was shocked at the volume of 'taters inside. This actually ended up having a negative effect on the dish, as the potato to aioli ration was skewed (due to many of the potatoes below the top level being unsauced). The ones with sauce were quite tasty, but they were in the minority.

    Following the advice of several Yelpers, we also ordered the Berenjenas (eggplant fries) which were quite tasty when accented with the truffle honey and zamarano cheese. Again, though, there was simply too much food on our table to eat them fast enough, and towards the second half of the (large) dish, they began to get cold and lose some of their appeal.  A special Spanish Breakfast (different from the one usually featured on the menu, but similar) arrived at the same time as the eggplant fries and woefully was nearly untouched due to the timing and massive amounts of food on our small table.

    Unfortunately, the chef's board of meats and cheeses arrived last, and at this point my date was struggling to swallow another bite. I managed to enjoy a few pieces of cured meat and cheese, but the damage was already done.

    The ambiance is beautiful, dim lighting and very romantic, and initially, all the plates we received were delicious. In my previous experience though, tapas are better served as small bites, easily shared and not the type of meal that leaves you feeling bloated afterwards. Jamonera does what they do well, but they could consider shrinking a few of the dishes OR informing the patrons that these are fairly large "small plates" in order to better estimate the meal. If I ever go again, I'll try to do so with a larger party to better accommodate the portions.

    We stopped in for a drink at Jamonera and really enjoyed the space--can't wait to come back and get something to eat. The decor is very warm and welcoming (minus the Manhattan-like sidewalk anteroom), with nods to a Spanish aesthetic (including a vintage Real Madrid soccer ball above the bar) without drifting into the realm of caricature.

    We sat at the bar and were served promptly. I had the red sangria, which was spectacular. It's usually dicey ordering a sangria in Philadelphia (I once saw a bartender at another establishment pour the juice from maraschino cherry dish into some wine that had previously been flavored with a dazzling array of "liqueurs" from the Pucker series!). But the sangria here is legit. They also have a white sangria, but I always stick with red. My wife really enjoyed the cocktail that she ordered too.

    The food menu looks amazing, and I was sitting next to someone who ordered the paella. I had to fight off the urge to engage in a less-than-decorous manner by posing the query "Yo, you gonna eat all that paella, brah?" Next time I am here, neither hell nor high water nor high hell nor water alone nor another other permutation of hell and water will prevent me from ordering that paella.

    The bartender here was also very pleasant and attentive. I especially appreciated that he gave us water as soon as we sat down, without us having to ask. Bonus points for this.

    All in all, this seems like a fine establishment, and I look forward to returning.
