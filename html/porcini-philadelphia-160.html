    Porcini is our favorite local dinner place and we hope to be considered regulars soon. It's is owned and operated by two brothers and I love how personable and attentive they are. We are always greeted at the door by David with a handshake, hug and a big smile and great to see you! And you can usually see Steven in the front of the house by the end of the night too!
    The ambiance is very quaint and tiny but in an adorable and very familial way. Everyone feels like family and that's how you are treated. Reservations are helpful but waiting on Sansom street is pretty and not too bad either.
    The food is absolutely out of this world delicious. I often have a hard time getting something different because of how much I'm craving whatever I had last time (typically the ravioli in pecorino sauce) but when I try other peoples dishes I realize I absolutely cannot go wrong. The bolognese, oso bucco, caprese, creme pastry are all to die for. I've really never tasted anything here without uttering the words "that's delicious!" Immediately after.
    We love Porcini and cannot wait to go back as frequently as possible!

Specialties

Porcini was on the cutting edge when it opened in 1996.The first BYOB stlye Italian  restaurant took it to another level,with no liquor or wine," the entire focus is on the food and service". Porcini's upscale casual feel is seen in many restaurants today. Chef Steve's fine dinning training and brother Dave's energetic bistro style service, give's their guests a unique and memorable experience.
Focusing on fresh delicate made pasta prepared in diverse way's,Chef's knowledge of Itailan cuisine will ensure you get authenticly prepared meals. Request a dish,and if the ingredients are avaiable he can prepare your
favorites.Special menu's and private  dinning is avalable for groups up to 35.

www.porcinirestaurant.com

History

Established in 1996.

Steven and David Sansone founded Porcini in the spring of 1996. Their rich Italian background is represented in the cuisine that is served at the restaurant daily .Their grandparents arrived in America in the 1890's.
The inspiration comes from the food that they grew up with from Sunday dinners to holiday feasts.

Meet the Business Owner

Chef Steve S. Business Owner

35 years in the restaurant business including 10 years at the Hyatt hotel.
The last 17 years as owner and executive chef of Philadelphia's original Italian  BYOB
