    The service experience at Garces made a greater impression on me than the food, which is generally not a good sign. The hostesses were very accommodating for a party of 10 on a Friday night and our server was cheerful and talkative (good or bad depending on preference). She was, however, quite pushy when we tried to only order 2 flatbreads for the table instead of the 3 she recommended, telling us repeatedly it wasn't enough (it was enough since we only wanted a small bite). Our entrees also took an extremely long time to come out (over 30-40 minutes), which really dragged the meal. Not even the bread and olive oil & vinegar selection could hold us over.

    Overall impression of the food was ok. We shared 2 flatbreads (margherita and funghi) - small but good for sharing as a light appetizer. The romaine salad was fine (lettuce could have been crisper) and the pasta e fagioli wasn't memorable. Pasta and entree portions were small but heavy. Multiple people, including myself, found the drinks to be too sweet (specifically the Italian Sour, Rosemary Greyhound, and the French Limonade).

    Overall, the atmosphere was lively and fun for a Friday night, but I wouldn't return for the food.

    BRUNCH REVIEW:

    My sister and I stopped in on a rainy afternoon for some post shopping brunch. She rarely comes to the city so I know I had to pick somewhere good, and I'm happy to report she approved!

    DEAL
    There is a $22 brunch special which includes unlimited coffee, pastries, and 2 dishes, but we decided that (although a great deal) that would be way too much food. The waiter said it would only really be worth it for the coffee and pastries because otherwise it's not a deal.

    FOOD
    We split the fresh mozarella as an appetizer (delicious). Pro tip: try not to eat all your bread before the mozarella comes. Warning: that will be difficult. Next up we split the baked eggs in spicy tomato stew which came with toast with olive tapenade. Not a big olive fan but I really enjoyed this. Try to save some bread for the stew too. This was really good and I definitely recommend getting it. Kind of like shakshouka without the peppers + cheese and guanciale, but we don't remember any meat really, so it must not have been much. We also split the Tarte Flambee flatbread (eggs, bacon, white onions, creme fraiche) which was also delicious. We took home leftovers and felt very satisfied.

    Service was good, but not overly friendly (going for that authentic french bistro feel maybe?) Not too crowded and they take opentable reservations which was nice.

    Definitely recommend for a fancier brunch! It was a bit pricier than Sabrina's/Day by Day/Green Eggs and some of my other go-to spots but definitely worth it!

    Listed in Starr v. Garces

    I'm a big fan of Garces - a number of his restaurants are some of my favorites in Philadelphia. GTC is definitely another addition to this list.

    The service here is great - they give you just the right amount of attentiveness. Their complimentary bread basket is decent, but it's their extra virgin olive oil that's the star of this dish. It's very fruity and fragrant, which is a nice alternative to your normal bread and butter.

    We shared the house-made mozzarella as an appetizer - it's an exceedingly simple dish. Just quality mozzarella, olive oil, salt, and pepper.

    I had the pappardelle with lamb ragu for my entree. They're very generous with how much meat they give you. The tender English peas really brightened up the heavy pasta. I thought my plate could have used more of the Sunchoke purée though. The pasta was perfectly cooked but it did start to stick together and became somewhat of a doughy mess by the end of my meal.

    My friend had the vichyssoise chaude and a side of their famous house duck fat fries. The warm potato leek soup was poured and presented at the table, which was a nice bit of unexpected dinner entertainment. The fries were delicious and lived up to the hype.

    I'm definitely going to come back to try their lyonnaise duck salad - it sounds AMAZING.

Specialties

Garces Trading Company is a full-service bar and restaurant focused on Iron Chef Jose Garces' own favorite dishes from his travels to cafes throughout Western Europe, with a focus on Italy, Spain and France.  Our thoughtful menu of cafe staples is served throughout the day, and our bar offers a wealth of cocktails, wines and beers that pair beautifully with our menu.  Our on-site PLCB Wine Boutique, the first of its kind, makes a great resource either before sitting down to dinner (corkage fees apply) or to complement one of our decadent gift baskets, available in our newly redesigned retail section.

History

Established in 2010.

Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a refined, Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, a contemporary American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago, IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale, AZ and Palm Springs, CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the landmark Revel Resort. Today, in addition to our much-lauded restaurants around the country, Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
