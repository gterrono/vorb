    Time out: let's stop the extreme reviews and indulge in a brief moment of honesty.

    Without Geno's next door, Pat's wouldn't exist. They are like two greasy forces that summoned each other into being, their illusory rivalry little more than a ploy to attract us. Geno's sucks considerably more, but Pat's still sucks. Yet we need both.

    Geno's gives us something to revile and judge, yet we must patronize to judge. Pat's is defined in opposition to this: it's less bad, not to mention venerable and unpretentious next to the tawdry neon facade of Geno's. But in a universe without Geno's (dare we fantasize?) Pat's would have to be considered on its own merits--an unremarkable, overpriced, dive trading on nothing more than a boring heritage.

    Pat's can boast that they actually put a decent quantity of fillings in their sandwich. They can swagger around knowing that they actually saute their onions rather than merely steaming them. Even in the category of snot-like synth-cheese, Pat's delivers a thicker and more robust slime... they're like a 20W-50 to Geno's 5W-20. The steak at Pat's is also obliterated a bit more, which makes it less leathery and almost flavorful.

    None of this is to say that Pat's serves a good sandwich--they don't--but, thanks to Geno's, we come to realize that it's a tolerable sandwich.

    Hey. It's PAT'S. What do you want? One of the things I miss and crave the most from home, and one of my guaranteed stops when I'm home for a visit.

    As many have mentioned, it's right across the street from Genos, and those of us from Philly are pretty loyal to one or the other. For me, Pat's steaks are just more flavorful, better made and more delicious. I also think their fries are better. And they serve Coke. And, when you look at the two businesses, it should be apparent which one is better. Genos must need all that neon for a reason. #Justsayin.

    One note to all of the reviews about rude behavior: It's part of the thing. Call it ambiance or whatever, it's part of the experience of getting a cheesesteak in South Philly. At Pat's you are expected to be ready to order, to know HOW to order and to not hold the line up. If you can do those three things, you will get what you ordered lickety split and enjoy a delicious meal. It aint fancy, but it's not supposed to be. If you don't like it, go to the Four Seasons and leave the rest of us alone.

    If you are new or just visiting and are nervous, that's understandable. We're not unreasonable. So ask the people around you while you wait in line. Or follow my handy guide for newbies...

    1. There are TWO lines. One for cheesesteaks and one for fries/sodas. You can't get fries in the cheesesteak line. You can't get steaks in the fries line. It is best to bring at least two people. The cheesesteak line is almost always longer, so the person in charge of fry ordering should wait until their partner is fairly well along in the cheesesteak line before stepping up to the window so that you both come out with food at the same time.

    2. Fries. You can and should get cheese fries. They are drenched in Cheez Wiz and are AWESOME.... the type you have to eat with a fork and the type where there's a big pool of cheese down in the bottom of the cup.

    3. CASH only. Don't ask about credit cards. You'll look stupid. Like the Soup Nazi on Seinfeld, it's best to have your cash out when you order.

    4. Ordering. Do it right. They have gotten a bit more lenient in recent years, but there was a time in which a bad orderer would be sent to the back of the line. You say two words and two words only: the type of cheese followed by WIT or WITOUT (ie, with or without fried onions). Yes, you can get other cheese and other stuff but I highly recommend Wiz Wit for your first time out (see next point below). The only other words you need are numbers (as in "TWO Wiz Wits").

    5. Wanna be branded as an outsider? Order something other than Wiz on your steak. Yes, there are other cheese options, but orders with Provolone are like announcing you're not from around here. You traveled all the way to an odd corner in South Philly to sample a REAL Philly cheesesteak. So suck it up and order a REAL steak -- WIZ WIT is all you need to say.

    6. Tables. Red picnic tables line the sidewalk all the way around Pat's. Ideally you should bring a third person whose job it will be to troll the area for an available table and snag it while your other two people do the line thing in point #1. I've gone to Pat's at all hours of the day -- steaks for midnight snacks, steaks for breakfast, and it's extremely rare to go there and find a bunch of empty tables. At the very best, it's not jam-packed. So having a table scout can pay off big time during busy times.

    7. Parking. Pat's and Genos are located pretty much right in the middle of a neighborhood, separated by a neighborhood baseball field. The only option is street parking. It's tight and hard to find sometimes. If you suck at parallel parking, bring someone who knows what they're doing. Not the place for rookies. Or take a cab from a safer locale.

    That's it! Good luck and happy cheesesteak!!!
