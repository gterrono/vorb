    I had one of the best meals of my life at Buddakan last summer.  My boyfriend was visiting from CA, and I wanted to take him to a nice, romantic dinner.  Although I made reservations over a month in advance, I was still only able to reserve a 9:30 pm reservation for 2 people on a Saturday...okay, for me this is a sign that this place is popular and hopping late into the night.

    First - ambiance and décor.  This place is beautiful, with a fountain and rock wall greeting you when you first enter.  Modern furniture, low lighting, and fancy light fixtures all make for a very fun drinking and dining environment.  Service was great, no complaints there.  Actually, no complaints about anything!

    Second - cocktails.  I wish I could remember the name of the cocktail I had, but for the life of me, I cannot.  So I will describe it in all of its fabulousness:  martini style, either gin or vodka, and the key ingredient - CILANTRO.  So strange, right?  I honestly didn't know if I would enjoy it when I ordered it, but it was one of the tastiest (but not sweet) and most refreshing cocktails I have ever had.  Perfect for a hot, humid Philly night.

    Third - food.  We ordered a noodle dish and the butter poached lobster, and we shared.  When I tasted that lobster, it was like I had died and gone to heaven.  The noodles were good too, although definitely overshadowed by the lobster.  We also tried an ice cream creation type dessert - while I don't remember the specifics, I remember being blown away by how tasty it was.

    Definitely a must try in Philly, and in my opinion, one of Steven Starr's best.  On the pricey side, but well worth it for the quality of the food, drink, and overall experience.

    WARNING: Lotta words up ahead.

    As soon as my boyfriend and I walked through the door, it was just wow. The decor was bold and had a nice modern/lounge feel to it. We were seated upstairs where it wasn't as loud as the main floor. Throughout our meal, our waiter was friendly and attentive. The manager even walked around a few times making sure the patrons were happy. For drinks, I had a glass of Life and a glass of Zen-gris, which were both crisp and fruity but not overly sweet. My boyfriend had Power (which ironically came in a martini glass) and was very strong. I thought it smelled kind of like a lemon jolly rancher/hard candy but for me it smelled better than it tasted. Now for the food...

    Tuna Tartare Spring Rolls: Wonderfully seasoned and delicious. My boyfriend wanted to tasate more of the tuna in it but I thought they were fantastic.

    Edamame Ravioli: We had to try these of course since everyone was saying how great it was and they were great. I don't know if they were as amazing as other people made it out to be but they were definitely worth trying. To me it was prepared perfectly but to me it tasted like a light ricotta cheese filling with a hint of edamame.

    Peking Duck Buns: If you've ever had peking duck at a Chinese restaurant, you know it's a slab of duck/crispy duck skin and you add your own sauce, cucumber and scallion to it. With that said, Buddakan's were basically the same ingredients presented in a different way. The duck was chopped up and seasoned with hoisin sauce in the bun. My boyfriend absolutely loved these and said that he could really taste the duck but I felt the sauce overpowered the duck. Still, they were tasty.

    Wasabi Tuna Tataki with ginger somen noodles: The tuna perfectly seared and sliced with a wasabi coating that was just enough kick. The noodles were a delicious complement.

    Pan Seared Sea Bass with butternut squash, maitake mushrooms and truffle jus: This was probably one of my favorite fish dishes ever. The sea bass was perfectly seared and gave the outside a nice crunch and flavor whereas the inside was tender and melt-in-your-mouth perfection. The veggies that were in the dish were delicious as well but I do wish the pieces butternut squash more evenly cooked (some were hard/undercooked and others were okay).

    Wasabi Mashed Potatoes: This was just the perfect complement to the entire meal. At first bite, we were hoping for more wasabi flavor but after a few more bites, we realized it was just enough without being over powerful. It was absolutely delicious.

    Chocolate Bento Box: The bento box was 6 desserts. There was a small chocolate ice cream sandwich-ish tower, a cashew candy bar, a warm chocolate cake, a white chocolate passion panna cotta, chocolate almonds and a banana tower. They weren't super sugary or overly rich so it was was pretty great. My favorites were the candy bar (fantastic crunch and basically an adult twist on a child's favorite) and the panna cotta (with the wonderful tart raspberry mousse to complement the sweetness of the white chocolate).

    Overall, it was a great meal but for the price, it's a special occasion kind of thing.

    The service was good and the venue was nice. Unfortunately the food fell short. Like I've said before I dislike "fusion" Asian restaurants - they are jacks of all trades, but experts in nothing. In the end, Buddakan seems more driven to be fashionable than providing great food.

Specialties

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
