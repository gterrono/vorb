    After trying this pho and trying several other places after, I've come to conclusion that Pho Ha has the best pho out there. I normally never give 5 stars. But I make special trips for this place so it deserves 5 stars. I want to address that is very difficult finding a place that serves pho ga. Most places do not serve it and it's extremely frustrating because I don't eat beef. Pho is the perfect comfort food in my opinion. It just makes everything better. This places gives you a huge serving that is sometimes hard to finish. They fill it to the edge with broth, noodle, and big pieces of chicken. They do not skimp out on the ingredients. The broth is perfect - flavorful and delicious compared to other broths which are just bland and taste like water. The noodles are the perfect size and I have no trouble eating them with chopsticks compared to thin noodles I've had in other places. (Still a newbie with chopsticks). The chicken pieces are perfectly cooked and the perfect size, which is surprising because most places only add a few small pieces of chicken. I add in a bunch of cilantro, sprouts, jalapeños and a few basil leaves to flavor the broth. I also add the brown sauce (don't know what it's called) and sriracha to add heat. I top it off with a splash of lime. At the end, what I'm left with is the perfect bowl of pho ga. It's a shame that I don't go that often but whenever I do, it's worth it. It helps that this place is reasonably priced. And you know it's a good place because it located next to a few other pho places, which are always empty.

    This is the most authentic pho place I've found in Philly so far!

    (And places I've been to include Vietnam Cafe, Vietnam Restaurant, Vietnam Palace, Pho Cali, Pho and Cafe Saigon, Nam Phuong, Nam Son...)

    The portions are huge and delicious. Since I'm not a particularly large eater, I usually go with the kid's size pho bowls, which are pretty filling on their own, but the regular sized bowls are a great deal if you are really hungry. The ingredients are fresh and come in generous amounts. I highly recommend the flank steak pho, which comes with tender, thinly cut slices of steak. What really makes the pho authentic here is the broth. They definitely know how to make a good pho broth.

    This is more of a casual place than a restaurant where you would go to enjoy the ambiance, but the food comes out very quickly, it's reasonably priced, and extremely delicious! Definitely my go-to place for authentic pho. If this restaurant wasn't so out of my way, I would probably come here every day!

    Best pho in town? I don't know, but I definitely like Pho Ha. Fast, efficient service and solid food in a super casual, low-key kind of place.

    Pho comes in pretty large bowls (the small was quite generous) for about $6. The broth is really good; this soup tastes way more flavorful than what I remember from Choo Choo's or Nam Phuong. They give you a lot of noodles, the variety of meat offered is great,and the numbering system is totally weird. Also - they bring out fewer vegetables than every other pho place, or they've really run out of bean sprouts right before closing.

    Other notes
    - they have this "Make Your Own" kind of thing. It looks like you can choose the vegetables and choose the meats of your meal? It looks a little complicated, but if you're seriously control-freaky about your meals, this is the place to go.
    - a couple frequenters suggested that I ask for fatty broth next time. Sounds good!
    - cash only! But there's an ATM in the restaurant.

    Among my friends, this place is known for being ridiculously fast. The food comes out immediately. After walking in (past the incense burning in the foyer area, which I really hate), you're seated immediately at whatever free table, and you're given a menu. You order, and then the food comes out within 5 minutes. 5 minutes is probably the longest you'll have to wait. And not just for pho - my friend ordered some kind of chicken over rice dish, and it came out before our pho did. How do they do it!?

    The food itself is great. Although not my favorite in the city (I really prefer Pho Saigon in terms of taste), I won't argue if anyone really wants to come here. Undeniably great broth - you really don't need to add anything (I still do, though. I need the lime).

    This place is like your standard, shabby pho place - but with better food.

    Bring cash to avoid an ATM fee!
