    My wife and I went here this past weekend and absolutely loved it.  We're always so happy to get new restaurants in the neighborhood and decided to give Hickory Lane a try.  We're thrilled we did.  The decor was great and it created an elegant, but cozy and down to earth feel.  I don't know exactly what an American Bistro's decor should look like, but this seems to fit the bill well.  The menu had a number of well thought out items on it and we found it difficult to pick the few that we were going to try.

    Hickory Lane is like a hidden gem in Fairmount without being hidden at all.  It is BYOB--always a winner in my book--and has outdoor seating, which is perfect for this time of year (well, except when the humidity is higher than the temperature and I end up looking like Monica from Friends with my fro... I digress).  

    The man-friend and I started with the Steak Tartare which was deeeeeeeelicious.  Love me some raw meat (that's what she said).  Only complaint was that they only gave you a handful of chips to eat it with and so I ended up having to ask for more.

    Between the two of us we had the King Salmon and the Hickory Burger.  Just as a general comment for both, we had ordered both to be cooked "medium," however both came out to us very rare.  Not a major deal, because I don't have an aversion to rare meat (see my comments on my appetizer choice).

    The Salmon came on a bed of polenta with asiago cheese.... and this really stole the show for me.  So creamy, so delicious, so everything-i-could-want-in-life-or-at-least-in-my-­mouth.  The Salmon was good as well, but that Polenta.  Ohhhh the Polenta.

    The Hickory Burger was killer.  It came with perfectly hand cut fries and a pickle, which I truly loved--a burger should always come with a Pickle, no matter what restaurant you're at.  The Garlic aioli was sooo delicious.  The super-thick-slice of cheese on the burger really made the dish, because it added depth and tons of flavor but didn't overpower the meat.  

    All-in-all, this is a great spot with awesome ambiance--perfect for summer dining in Fairmount.

    Step 1: Come here before Hickory Lane acquires a liquor license.
    Step 2: Order the burger and fries.
    Step 3: Order a side of extra fries.

Oh yes, and you'll need more fries to consume the garlic aioli (see below).
    4. Because that burger is pretty damn good and if you order it medium rare, you'll find yourself indulging in one of the most awesome burgers in Philly. Juicy, tender, and a scintilla crisp on the edges, this is one cow patty you don't want to pass up. And the garlic aioli that comes with it is so perfectly tangy.
    5. Because no meal is complete without an extra large slice of brownie.
    6. Because that extra large slice of brownie is moist, gooey, chewy and the quintessence of what a good brown should be. It's crackly on top, fat and oozing in the middle, and topped with ice cream.

    Classic. 'Merican. Meal.

    Hot Friday Night in Mid Summer w/ no idea what you would like to eat, but you would probably eat a burger.  Head to Hickory Lane on the Corner of Corinthian and Fairmount.  But before you head to this great BYOB, stop around the corner at the local Wine and Spirits store for a bottle of chilled white wine.

    This is how my night ended up about two Friday's ago with my girlfriend and I not knowing what to eat.  Although I had heard the whispers of a new burger in the Fairmount area that has the ability to rival Village Whiskey, Pub & Kitchen, and Royal Tavern.  

    I had no clue what to expect other than the noticing the long interlocking windows and facade of the restaurant.  The atmosphere of the restaurant can be pretty loud if you are inside and the windows are closed, so we decided to sit outside in the shadow of the Eastern State Penitentiary.  

    We decided to get three things and split them: Hickory Lane Salad, Steamed Mussels, and the Hickory Burger.  All three were exceptional.  The Hickory Lane Salad was made with Baby Frisee, Roasted Potatoes, Crispy Pancetta, Poached Egg, Sea Salt Focaccia & Red Wine Vinaigrette. We cracked the poached egg and it melted into the vinaigrette and soaked into the potatoes.  

    The Mussels were great, I just love to dip the crispy baguette into the broth.  Yum....didnt taste like the ocean and had great flavor w/ the rosemary, fennel, garlic, and lemon.  

    The Burger was most likely the best burger I've had in Philadelphia and I have had a few of the best.  It didn't require anything but the meat and bun.  The Meat was delicious as it melted in your mouth, mind you I got medium...not sure of your temperatue preference.  My girlfriend does not like her burgers pink in the middle but this one was surely that and she thoroughly enjoyed it!!  The lettuce and tomato were very fresh adding the crunch and a bit of juiciness, not like it was needed with the melt in your mouth concoction of ground beef just oozing on the plate.  Also it was accompanied by a thick slice of sharp white cheddar, which was very light.  Overall the burger was composed to be just that, a burger and the fixings we there to accompany the taste of the burger but not intrude with the tender beef.

    We deciding to get dessert and we got the rice pudding with fresh donuts, a personal favorite of mine and it was delicious.  The donuts were the pastry style and had cinnamon sugar on them....you could tell they were made fresh.

    Great dinner overall. I will be back

    Service was very quick and helpful, the waiter even stopped to have a chat about the composition of the burger...I won't share please go try it out.

Specialties

Hickory Lane American Bistro's cuisine is founded in exceptional ingredients prepared with expert techniques by its chef, presenting a dining experience and atmosphere that is highly energized.  Hickory Lane offers dinner and brunch in a casually elegant space in the Art Museum/Fairmount neighborhood of Philadelphia, right next to the Eastern State Penitentiary.

Ideal for a quick bite, leisurely meal or an exceptional memory,  Hickory Lane's menu will change constantly to capture seasonal and exceptional ingredients.

Great food deserves great drink and Hickory Lane offers both with a short but well rounded wine list that is available by the glass, carafe or bottle. The beer list hovers around 15 selections from around the world with a focus on local craft beers. Spirits are the heart and soul of the bar at Hickory Lane. The spirits are selected for their wonderfully different qualities and their applications make fun and soulful interpretations of classic pre-prohibition cocktails.

History

Established in 2011.

Hickory Lane American Bistro's cuisine is founded in exceptional ingredients prepared with expert techniques by its Executive Chef, Albert Alvarado, formally of Green Eggs . Hickory Lane offers nightly dinner and weekend brunch in a casually elegant space in the Art Museum/Fairmount neighborhood of Philadelphia. Ideal for a quick bite, leisurely meal or an exceptional memory Hickory Lane's menu will change constantly to capture seasonal and exceptional ingredients.

Great food deserves great drink and now Hickory Lane offers both with a short but well rounded wine list that is available by the glass, carafe or bottle. The beer list always hovers around 15 selections with a focus on local craft beers. Spirits are the heart and soul of the bar at Hickory Lane. The spirits are selected for their wonderfully different qualities and their applications for making fun and soulful interpretations of classic pre-prohibition era cocktails like Martinis, Manhattans, Sazeracs and punches.

Executive Chef Albert Alvarado was raised in and around Vineland, NJ. After graduating from Vineland High School, Albert pursued a degree in Culinary Arts & Sciences after being accepted to Johnson & Wales University in Providence, RI.
