    I've been wanting to try Nomad Pizza Co. for a while because um, I love pizza and beer and finally found the time (and someone willing to foot the bill) to make it happen...and yep, this place definitely deserves its hype.

    We were seated upstairs which I was initially nervous about because I've heard it's literally 9 bajillion degrees up there and seeing as how it's approximately 8 bajillion degrees outside, I wasn't excited about possibly melting part way through my meal. But not to worry, it was fully air conditioned and had a really nice ambiance - chill and laid back but kind of hipster or something like that. Also, most of the tables downstairs are communal (aka you could end up sitting next to a stranger, which might be awesome but might be terrible), and my two friends and I were at our own 4-top, which was nice.

    Oh, you want to hear about what I actually consumed there? Fine.

    Beer: Stone IPA, perfect temperature, good price. Love it. They do have a really nice tap selection and a good amount of bottles as well, so beer drinkers, you're definitely taken care of here.

    Salad: Arugala salad and roasted beet/carrot salad. Both were delish and actually felt healthy, as opposed to some places where the salads are swimming in dressing and topped with all the cheese one cow produced in a year. I have to say, the beet salad wasn't the most "summery" thing I've ever eaten, but its taste more than made up for its off-season vibe.

    Pizza: I continue to be a vegetarian and there were a ton of options for me and my also-veg companions. We ordered the margarita/Buffalo mozzarella pizza and the Shitake mushroom pizza. Loved both. I died a little bit more when I ate the mushroom pizza (caramelized onions totally know the way to my heart), not gonna lie, but the margarita was far from your average "plain" pie. All the pizzas are 12-inches, so if you're totally ravenous, you could probably eat one by yourself, but they're perfect for sharing and as a bonus, I didn't walk out of the restaurant feeling like I ate a house. Woo!

    Just things to keep in mind: 1) Pizzas come out literally one or two at a time, so if they're busy, be prepared to sit for a while. Hope you like your dining companions! 2) They don't slice the pizzas in the kitchen, but do give you pizza slicers so you can either bravely do it yourself or wait for your server to take care of it for you tableside. 3) It's kind of loud and echo-y, if you care about knowing about that kind of stuff ahead of time.
