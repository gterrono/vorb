    Definitely a gem in the West Philly treasure trove of small eats.  Honest Tom's Steak and Yucca taco as well as their Sweet Potato taco make my mouth water just thinking of them. Extremely fresh, diced tomatoes tossed with herbs and chopped red onions cover the just-made and perfect guacamole. And underneath that bed of goodness is a very generous layer of juicy steak pieces and soft, starchy yucca; its texture is similar to a potato but slightly sweeter.  If you get the sweet potato taco, the soft chunks of sweet potato have the same fresh toppings, which you can see being made in the open kitchen behind the counter.  It's a fun shop, laidback, colorful, and with a friendly environment.  It's a tad pricy, but for the quality of the food you're getting, it's worth it!  So glad they've expanded from the food truck to their own shop now!

    After months of hearing about this West Philadelphia taco joint, I finally made the trek to Honest Tom's.  This small, dare I say "hipster" taqueria on hipper-than-thou S. 44th Street does serve up tasty tacos (as well as burritos) with standard fillings (such as chicken, beef, pork), and a few creative ones (like steak and yucca).  Everyone here, staff included, wears the same uniform: skinny jeans, ironic tee, hoodie, and is talking about that time Animal Collective came to the Mann Center last month.  But I'm not here to converse with the Philadelphia offshoot of Thought Catalog: I want tacos.

    For $6, I get two tacos: one pork, one steak and yucca.  They're not particularly big tacos, but they do come with all the trimmings on top (including guacamole).  Both of them were really good, and made even better with the addition of hot sauce.  To be honest, I really couldn't tell difference between the steak taco from the pork taco, but I do know that the tacos were definitely better than average (or one that you would get from a certain fast food franchise that rhymes with "shmipotlay").  And even though you can't get a brewski to go with your food, it's convenient that Bottle Shop is just a few doors down, and the staff is like, so cool to let you bring in your own PBR.

    My only complaint is the pricing strategy.  I think it's just a bit expensive to charge $6 for two tacos.  One reviewer suggests putting in a 3rd taco, which makes sense, or you could charge $4 or $5 for two tacos and add a surcharge for guac.  Apparently, the burrito ($8) "is enough for two meals" (and at that price, seems more than fair).

    Honest Tom's also makes "amazing" breakfast tacos, with eggs, potatoes, cheese, and guacamole.  I'm so going back to try these.

    For University City, Honest Tom's is awesome. In general, though, it's not ~the greatest thing ever~ for me.

    To start off, I'm a big fan of Mexican food. Burritos are, like, the best thing ever for me. The fact that Honest Tom's has HUGE burritos that are better than Chipotle's AND they are a BYO already has me sold! Though the price is comparable...

    However, their burritos--at least their vegetarian options (sweet potato and rice & bean)--are still a bit on the bland side, for me. Maybe it's because I'm a big fan of spices and flavors, but I find Honest Tom's burritos to just be a bit... lacking. Pouring on some of their many hot sauces definitely helps, though, so that's a plus. Nonetheless, the burritos are STUFFED with fresh ingredients, and you can taste the care put into the burritos. Good luck trying to finish one in one sitting!

    The staff is extremely friendly, and the color scheme is bright and inviting, which is really fun, and makes trips to Honest Tom's more exciting. The seating is EXTREMELY limited (there are like, 2 tables total, with about ~13 seats total inside,) so I would reccommend getting your food to go unless you're prepared to have an awkward, cramped BYO inside (more power to you if you are...)
