    The Industry is way overpriced for what it is.  I'd never been to Pennsport, so I never would have known about The Industry if it wasn't for Yelp.  The area is mostly residential and a street full of row homes.  My husband and I found street parking pretty easily in the middle of the street.  We headed over to the Industry and couldn't get anyone's attention when we walked in after standing there for five minutes, so we just found a couple of open seats at the bar instead.  

    The cocktail menu is written on a chalkboard above the bar.  I ordered a tequila based cocktail.  It was tart from the citrus and similar to a margarita, minus the salt.   I can't recall the name, but that's probably because it wasn't a memorable drink. I thought maybe I just ordered wrong, so I ordered a cocktail with a peculiar name, "No balls, you won't", for my second drink. It was described as a strange combination of tequila, espresso, sherry, and hot peppers.   It tasted like creamy, boozy, espresso.  The heat from the pepper was very faint.  Nice creamy texture from the egg white, but I guess I was expecting a more interesting flavor from a cocktail with such a crazy name.  The cocktails were priced from $9 - $13.  The pricing seemed a little steep considering that there were no garnishes or amazing flavors, and the atmosphere in the bar was really casual.  I expect more when you're charging those kinds of prices.  The Industry is no Franklin Mortgage.  

    As for the food, we ordered the cheese plate to snack on.  It wasn't bad, but again at $14, I guess I was expecting more.  I liked the idea of a cow's milk, a goat's milk, and a sheep's milk cheese combo.  Everything was ok, but nothing really stood out.  The sheep's milk cheese tasted exactly like one I'd bought at Trader Joe's the week before that I wasn't that impressed with.  The cows milk cheese was bland, and the goat's milk one wasn't my thing, but my husband liked it well enough.  The presentation was boring, and it came served with a spoonful of honey, jam, and a few slices of green apple.  The pieces of sliced baguette it came with were okay, but I like a crisper crust.  I could have made myself a much better cheese plate at home by randomly grabbing a couple blocks of cheese and a baguette from Whole Foods.

    The service here was fast once we were seated at the bar.  Overall, there are far better gastropubs in Philly and they don't come with this hefty of a price tag.

    The Industry has definitely grown on me as I've come here more and more. I've found the service to be relaxed, consistently friendly, prompt without being rushed, and super helpful if you need help choosing a beer. The attitude in this place makes it a great neighborhood hangout.

    I like the fact that it's a chill, quiet place I can have a conversation. Although, to be fair, I haven't been here that late at night.

    The food is good but nothing mind-blowing. Brunch is better than a diner but not in the same league as Honey's or Green Eggs. The burger (4oz patty, though you can order 1 or 2 additional patties) and fries is a solid choice. Everything looks delicious and portions are what you would expect.

    I do like the fact that The Industry tries to be a little fancy, but I'm a little turned off by some of the price points. Specialties

The Industry has a distinctive menu featuring homemade, contemporary american, bistro style fare. Signature dishes for dinner include Potted Trout, BBQ Duck Sandwich, Kentucky Fried Guinea Hen and Homemade Sausage. Signature dishes for brunch include Fresh Baked Doughnuts, Shirred Eggs, the Hillbilly Philly and the "Stomachectomy."  Executive Chef, Patrick Szoke is the driving force in the Industry kitchen and will continue to create new and exciting plates as the restaurant moves forward.
