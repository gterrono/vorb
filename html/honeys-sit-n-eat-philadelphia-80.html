    This the place to go to for breakfast!  You can get it at anytime of the day as long as they are open.  Crave breakfast for dinner; here is your place.  This place is setting the standard for breakfast and with reasonable prices it's really a win, win place to go and grub.  After your meal you will not only feel satisfied but it's like you went home and got a home made breakfast from mom (that is if your mom can cook...I mean really cook...not the buy it and toast it breakfast!)  This place really compares to another breakfast place in San Francisco called Mama's in Washington Square.  

    Ambience:  Honey's is located on a corner.  During the summer time it's great to sit outside and eat.  Especially with all the plants and mural.  Usually on the weekends you have to wait in line for a seat.  Inside is very cozy and at times when it's really busy you get to know the tables next you very well too!  During the winter months sometimes you can go and wait at another restaurant while your number comes up.  The staff has a unique personality and are very friendly.  

    Parking:  Street parking only, you have to go around the block a few times before getting a spot especially on the weekends.

    Menu/Pricing:  There are a variety of items on the menu.  I usually get breakfast and have tired many of the omelets.  I did not like the pickled jalapeños in the omelets and was offered by my waiter to use fresh ones which made the omelet taste much better.  As I said before they are willing to make changes so that you enjoy your food.  The pancakes are great and you can add whatever you wish.  I think their biscuits are  amazing; they come "pre-sliced" and buttered.  It cannot get better then that.  Slap some home made blueberry jam/sauce on it and call it the day!  Prices are very reasonable and you can stuff yourself silly without going broke or even close to it.  The chorizo and goat cheese omelets are great and only fresh avocados are used.  I have tried the soft tacos once and found it to be simple and okay tasting.  Nothing spectacular.  I feel this place is great for breakfast items.  

    Waiter/Service:  Everyone is friendly and are eager to accommodate you.

    Would I return:  I am here almost every two weeks or so when I am off.

    Negatives:  No credit cards, that's about it.  Waiting in line at times can get a little frustrating but once you have the food you forget all about your wait.

    Without a doubt the best breakfast (and lots of other cool eats) in Philly. My gal suggested we go here last year.  She's lived here all her life and never went prior to then - imagine that!  Anyway, the love we both feel for this joint is sky high and now I'll tell you all the fine reasons why.

    1. The food.  Duh.  It's priced amazingly and tastes even more amazing than the most superlative adjective I can throw down here for it.  Biscuits and gravy with the "Honey" sausage are killer.  That huge-ass po' boy samitch is enough for two folks, but you'll be able to handle it on your own, I know you will.  The tostadas are impeccable, the pancakes and french toast are super ginchy, and my oh my, the roasted corn cobs are food gold, people.  Did I mention the best fresh-squeezed orange juice I've ever tasted as well?

    2. The staff.  I don't get the reviews I've seen on here where people have complained about the people that work at Honey's.  I've never had an experience there where I wasn't seen to by more than one person.  Always friendly even when busting their asses and more than happy to help you out with your order in any way.

    3. The decor.  The inside of Honey's has a notch over hole in the wall ambiance, and it's fantastic.  Big steel beams span across the ceiling, a strange shade of green/blue is slathered across the walls behind the counter service, there are interesting knick-knacks here and there, and the tables are literally placed so you're pretty much shoulder to shoulder with everyone else.  Dig the communal atmosphere!  Make friends with strangers chowing down and discuss what each of you had and compare notes!

    I'm not going to lie, there's usually a wait at Honey's.  There have been the couple of weird times we've gone when it was lined up outside and we gave our name and were actually able to be seated instantly at the counter because no one wanted those spots.  Hey, I'm, not picky when it comes to great food!  But be prepared to wait upwards to 30 minutes or so on busy weekend mornings.  I promise it's worth every second, though.  Do not miss out on this one of a kind groove.

    I love a restaurant that has options like "Vegetarian Chicken Fried Steak" on the menu. It's very thoughtful. Roomie had an omelet with spinach, salsa & cheddar w/ turkey bacon, toast & latke, plus OJ. I had the "Big Bowl o' Miso Soup" and pancakes with banana and almond with coffee. That soup was so good I wanted to cry. I honestly had to ask Kyleen to pull it away from me so I didn't tilt the bowl to my face & drink the last of it. Pancakes were great, fluffy golden & perfect - none of that banana goo crap you get at iHop. The coffee wasn't amazing, but free refills are good.
