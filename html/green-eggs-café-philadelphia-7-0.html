        First to Review

        So it looks like there's a new brunch spot here in Midtown/Center City, which I'll admit, I'm pretty excited about!

        I live right around the corner from here, and the other day I was walking by and saw the sign for Green Eggs Cafe. I've walked this way several times and never noticed it before, so I'm guessing this is brand new (plus, it seems like I'm the first one reviewing it on here, which tells me something is up!). I was instantly excited to see this opening, as brunch is quickly becoming the new obsession for my fiance and I. Upon further inspection, we saw that there are actually two other locations for Green Eggs Cafe, one in South Philly and one in Northern Liberties.

        We dropped by around 11:30 on a Sunday, figuring this place was new so it shouldn't be to busy, but boy were we wrong! They must have created some sort of buzz about it, because when we got there it was packed and we actually had a short wait for 2 (around 10 minutes). Given it was Sunday, which is the unofficial "National Brunch Day", but still, we were surprised by how busy this place was!

        I got the 3 eggs cooked my way, which was medium over, and it came with home fries, toast, and a side of meat (I went with bacon). I also got a coffee. My fiance got the vegetarian eggs benedict, a vegetarian spin on the classic which replaces ham with an assortment of veggies, and comes with a side of home fries.

        Overall, we were both pretty pleased with our meals, I especially liked my home fries, and the toast was from Le Bus bakery. A few complaints I had though, which were pretty minor but caused me to take one star off the rating: my fiance asked for the signature home fries, but instead they gave her the rosemary potatoes (she didn't wanna complain, but didn't really eat any of her home fries), and I had to ask twice for salt and pepper. It seems to me that it would be easier to just have salt and pepper on every table, but you actually have to ask for it...kind of odd. Also, this place is cash only, which is just strange in today's age of using credit everywhere (but they have an ATM on-site if you need it)!
