    If a friend in need is a friend indeed, then Honest Tom and I are BFFL. Stranded in West Philly one scenic Philly Beer Week Sunday, surrounded by too many hops and not enough huevos, I opted to step away from the offerings of IPAs and sours and find solace in Honest Tom's.

    Honestly?

    Tom tore it up. It, of course, being "my tastebuds".

    Breakfast taco options only, despite it being about 1:30pm, but despite my initial disappointment that I couldn't nab some fish tacos, my concerns were quickly assuaged by two tacos chock-full of eggs, cilantro, onions, and (for a QUARTER more!!) bacon. All the hot sauce I could handle.

    At first, I wasn't sure if the juice was going be worth the squeeze. Two tacos? From SOME taco spots, that's basically two bites. These were stuffed to the gills, though. All the fixins were fresh and tasted like high quality ingredients. The service was truly nothing to write home about, at times a little too laid back. I had to ask two employees to empty the trash before I was finally able to add my refuse to the eventually-empty bin, and the water jug was empty when I was there. But clearly the cooks are paying attention, and that's exactly what I needed.

    LOVE the outdoor seating, would bring folks back for brunch in a heartbeat.

    Thanks, Tom. You saved the (very hungry) day.

    Much of my burrito palette is built upon my frequent--almost too frequent--trips to Chipotle.  Although Honest Tom's and Chipotle both sell burritos, they cannot be compared.  Their styles are completely different.  

    Being the half vegetarian I am, I decided to get the sweet potato burrito.  It was delicious and flavorful.  The sweet potato provided that sweet-and-savory combination I love, and everything was warm and toasty inside (the cheese was gooey and melted).  The one thing that surprised me about this burrito is how much food it is.  At first glance, it looks like a pretty average-sized burrito, but it is dense!  The burrito is packed with so much goodness, that it's hard to even finish one.  We came around 11AM for our first meal of the day, and it kept us full for a few hours.  The guacamole was also really good and the ingredients were really fresh, but it was a little overpriced, given its portion size.  There's also a cute popsicle shop next store--perfect for dessert.  I definitely want to come again when I'm in the area.

    I'll begin this review by saying that I've been visiting this place for the last couple of years, and up until 6 months ago, I would have given this place a 5 star rating without a second thought. But not any more.

    I remember the first Burrito that I had here: Fresh, filled with guacamole, sour cream, flavorful and filling. Then about a few months ago, they raised the prices. "Fair enough, inflation hits everyone", I thought. But I wasn't prepared for the drop in quality. The burritos now are usually dry, too salty, overstuffed with beans more than anything else. The guacamole is still in the burritos, but in token proportions, while the sour cream seems to be on a permanent vacation from this place!

    Just today, my girlfriend ordered a burrito from this place. She specifically asked them to go easy on the beans and for extra sour cream. She got a burrito chock full of beans and with no noticeable sour cream. She took apart the burrito and sure enough, there's no sour cream anywhere in the burrito.

    To sum it up, a burrito from this place is dry, salty rice, cheese, lots of beans, a token amount of guacamole, with hit or miss meat (misses more often than not).

    If you're looking for a place that USED to be great, then this is the place you're looking for. As for me, I'll be walking the four or so more blocks to the Qdoba when I fancy a burrito from now on. At least they don't skimp on the guacamole and sour cream even if you do have to pay extra (and hey, even with the extras, it's cheaper than this place, with their $9.75 steak burritos!)
