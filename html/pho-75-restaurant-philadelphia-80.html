    Gimme some mo' pho bitches!

    This is my favorite pho joint in the DC area, and I am glad to see the Philly franchise is alive and well.  I remember this place used to be in a tiny hole in the wall in Chinatown.  I'm glad they moved to these bigger digs, which remind me of the one I usually go to in Roslyn, VA.

    The food here is just as good as there - perfectly cooked beef in a light but flavorful broth with rice noodles that have the spot-on texture needed for that perfect bowl of pho.  There's not much to say beyond what the place delivers - the most awesome bowl of Vietnamese beef noodles that many of us New Yorkers can only dream of due to the serious dearth of authentic and inspired pho restaurants in NYC.  Here's something that l'm definitely jealous that us big apple peeps don't have.

    Oh and beyond the beef noodles, definitely hit up the excellent Vietnamese iced coffee here.  You can't go wrong.  

    Otherwise this place is about as charming as a hospital cafeteria and boasts clean bathrooms.  The servers are friendly and the price is reasonable.  What are you waiting for?

    I couldn't have picked a better place to lose my pho-ginity.  This was always the first place that came to mind when I asked my friends where I should have my first pho, so I went with it, and I'm so happy I did.

    This place is definitely no frills - they only serve pho, no appetizers, and the dining area feels a lot like a cafeteria.  The waitstaff is not overly friendly but will promptly take your order, bring it to you faster than Jimmy Johns delivers, and will refill your water and tea without being asked.  Which is fine, because I was quite preoccupied with devouring the bowl of deliciousness in front of me!

    I was rather adventurous and ordered a beef bowl with all of the extras, and I will say that you really shouldn't be afraid of the tendon or tripe.  The texture and flavor that they add are worth it!  Load up your bowl with the sides of fresh thai basil, bean sprouts, jalapenos, and whatever sauce your little heart desires, and enjoy!

    Ps - I have a hearty appetite and the small size was enough for me.  The portions are very generous.

    Don't forget about the iced Vietnamese coffee!  Order it as soon as you sit down, because it comes in a little drip pot and can take forever to be ready to drink.  Oh and pro tip, pour fast.  Otherwise it'll all end up on the table.

    Can't wait to come back!
