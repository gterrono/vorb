    Listed in Sushi Soul Searching

    Shiroi Hana is so unassuming from the outside that I managed to completely forget about it. I love sushi, and I managed to wait about 2 years before trying this place again after an ok first visit. The Subaru Cherry Blossom festival is in town this week, which means 20% here and a few other Japanese restaurants around town, so it was the perfect time to come back.

    First of all, let me say that SH does not do happy hour. I asked the waitress if they did and she stared at me like I had three heads before gesturing to the full price drink menu. The glasses of wine started at $7, so I passed on the drinks. We started with the steamed shrimp shumai ($5). They were ok, not the best I've ever had, and the portion was on the small side for the price. I wanted to order a salad with my two rolls, so I asked if I should get the small or large. The waitress told me the large was so big it could feed two people, so I ended up getting the small and it was verrry tiny but the dressing was really good. If you're getting this as a main part of your meal definitely go with the large.

    I ordered the tuna club and island specialty rolls, and they both turned out to be a decent size. The tuna club roll ($13) is unique in that is shaped like a mini club sandwich, with layers of rice as the "bread" and two layers of chopped spicy tuna with a sheet of nori in the middle. It was good and actually spicy which I liked, but not really much different than the normal spicy tuna roll which is half the price. The island roll ($10) however, was amazing. There were chunks of raw salmon on the inside with some spicy crunch, topped with more raw salmon and ripe mango. I keep specifying 'raw' since there seems to be an affinity towards using mostly cooked/smoked/tempura-d fish in specialty rolls lately.

    Overall Shiroi Hana is an ok sushi spot. The fish tasted fresh and the portion sizes were decent for the rolls, but I've seen better specialty rolls elsewhere. The prices were also higher than most sushi spots for both food and drinks. Service was just ok as well.

    Shiroi Hana, as many have commented, has beautiful interior with a calming, comfortable ambiance. It is memorable for the way it is minimally decorated and extremely clean.

    The food:

    Gyoza: liked the flavor of the filling, but the wrapper wasn't crisp/light

    Tempura: beautiful presentation with light batter. Very well-done and great portion/variety.

    Tonkatsu - Lightly breaded and tender cutlet. For its price, you get two cutlets worth of katsu, and I think the real treat is the katsu sauce with kewpie mayonnaise. Really lovely.

    Sashimi regular- For $25, you get a 12-piece sashimi dinner. Extremely fresh fish, and I love the shredded daikon beneath. The salmon and the yellowtail were exceptional. Filling alongside salad and rice.

    Hamachi don- Again, beautifully fresh hamachi don presented on rice with black sesame. (Chirashi must also be wonderful)

    I am looking forward to trying more of their traditional dishes, and I was very taken with their service. Absolutely a gem in Philly.

    -

    Edit: Surprised with my second visit. Had the chicken teriyaki, which was terribly overcooked chicken breast with a very thin dressing as the "teriyaki sauce." Not moist or flavorful at all. Also, the agedashi wasn't very good, either. Tough tofu and the flavor was a little off.

    Shame on me for not singing Shiroi Hana's praises earlier. The one day I stopped by here, it was for lunch, after a stressful national standardized exam.     Seriously, come to Shiroi Hana for a great sushi experience. The fish is fresh and well cut. The sake is well-poured.

    A highly recommended experience for excellent Japanese food in a comfortable environment.
