    Lazy Saturdays with my husband are rare, so when I finally had a Saturday off from work there was no way I was cooking dinner. We had such delicious Asian cuisine when we were in Canada (crazy, huh?), that we decided to check out some place here in Philly's Chinatown. We stumbled upon Terakawa Ramen, and since my boy loves ramen the decision was made and inside the small establishment we went.

    The menu is small (thankfully compared to many other area restaurants), and we ordered pretty quickly. He got their signature Terakawa Ramen (Natural Heritage Berkshire Pork bone soup topped with roast pork (charshu), bamboo shoots, kikurage mushrooms, red ginger, chopped scallions and a seasoned boiled egg), while I got the Miso Ramen (Chicken and pork miso soup, topped with bamboo shoots, roast pork (charshu), bean sprouts, chopped scallions, kikurage mushroom and a seasoned boiled egg) and we shared the pan fried gyoza and a side of the charsu onigiri. Both ramen were delicious, the broths flavorful and noodles tender. The gyoza were also delicious, and the onigiri as well (not alot of pork in it, but its supposed to be mostly rice - and yummy seasoned rice, too). We ended our meal with warm, full bellies (his more so since I couldn't finish my ramen!).

    Service was very friendly and swift, and prices aren't bad since the portions are quite generous. We'll likely be back - but we've got a lot of other restaurants in Chinatown to check out now!

    I need to write this down before I started to forget! The trip has been a month ago :)

    I came to Philly not to eat Ramen but man oh man, it made me want to revisit!
    (I also went during Government Shutdown so I have a perfect excuse to go back - even though it really is because of Ramen)

    Yelp took me to this place. Cute cozy store in China Town of Philly.
    Staffs and Chefs are very friendly. I believe they are actually Chinese Owned so I didn't get my hopes up high.

    We went there on Wednesday after lunch so the place is not packed but still have few more tables occupied when we arrived which is good because I was so hungry!
    We got the Terakawa Ramen ( with extra chashu- must do and with extra noodle that I couldn't finish), The Chashu onigiri (Rice Ball), The Chashu Bun, Takoyaki and the small chicken curry bowl.

    The Ramen has a really good broth, flavorful and tasty. I should have stuck with original noodle type but I changed it. Don't follow me! I think it would goes better with smaller ramen noodle as originally prepared.
    The highlight of the Ramen is that EGG! Oh my goodness! I don't know how they cook it. The white is very flavorful and firm. The yolk is still a bit yolky  just the way I like it and it is tasty too. I would just eat a bunch of those with rice!
    Ramen comes with half the egg and there were two of us, the chef saw that and he was super nice to bring out the other piece for us so we wouldn't need to split the half!! How generous :)

    The rice ball is good not superb but good solid simple food.

    Takoyaki is soft on the inside and very yummy!

    Chicken Karaage curry rice is small so we love it. A nice touch to have variety of food without having to order a large bowl and a full price. Curry taste good but I personally like the tonkatsu with curry  better.

    Pork Bun is A MUST order!
    So heavenly goodness. Well marinated meat and so tender. Works so well with soft bun and crisp veggies.

    A very delightful meal at Terakawa. Will definitely re visit when back in Philly!

    Cute place tucked away at the edges of Chinatown.  If I hadn't passed it on foot, I'd have never known it existed.  There isn't a terrible amount of seating area - maybe 8 tables or so and some counter space, but at the same time, it doesn't feel cramped and elbow-to-elbow.  The chairs are small wooden benches, with a cute indentation for your bottom.

    Terakawa used to be Ramen Boy and I suppose owners change and along with it came a name change to avoid the bad reputation from Ramen Boy.

    First off, improvements in customer server went up with the new ownership so this is really good. The waiters are prompt and the food arrives quickly, so quickly perhaps that I think it was already pre-made... Like lighting quick. My dish came out in like 2-4 mins after ordering it.

    I had the original Terekawa Ramen, I can't recall the name. It was good. Not spectacular, not bad, just good. Like I wouldn't write home to my mom about it, but I'd probably confirm it's existence if someone brought it up. The Ramen was a little on the salty side for me and in comparison to all the other Ramen joints in Philly, I'd say it's about in the same league. So far, no Ramen in Philly has really stood out compared to other cities, but I believe Philly will reach that point eventually.

    I also tried the Takoyaki, which was 90% breading and 10% squid. I'd say this is consistent with all the other Takoyaki's I've tried in Philly which is disappointing.

    Overall, going to give it a 3, which is much better than the 1 I gave to Ramen Boy back in the days. Things are better here, but not all that much better where I will make a trip out here. But if I'm in the area, sure I'll grab a bite if I am really craving Ramen. The issue is that this is in Chinatown and in Chinatown, there are so many other better restaurants that you can go to.

    My charshu onigiri appetizer was heavily seasoned and delicious. The entire onigiri tastes like delicious pork stock with bits of meat, sesame seeds, and nori (seaweed).

    I also ordered the takoyaki. Octopus is very chewy and bouncy, a texture that is very popular with some people, but I am NOT one of them. I loved the doughy coating, it practically melted in my mouth, and it was topped with fish flakes, mayo, and a tangy BBQ-like sauce. I personally don't enjoy the texture of the octopus center, but I would still order this again.

    My main course was chicken katsu don. Katsu don is a bowl of white rice with caramelized onions, a fried egg, sliced chicken cutlet, and a sweet soy sauce. (It was also topped with a bit of green onion and nori.) The rice, caramelized onions, and sauce create a sticky sweet base for the crispy chicken. While the onion and fried egg enhanced the savory aspect of the meal. Each ingredient was delicious on it's own, but the dish is at it's best when you have everything combined.

    I love this place and I can't wait to head back into the city for some more.

    I go to Terakawa at least once per month and definitely consider it to be the best ramen in Philadelphia.  The house Terakawa tonkatsu ramen is very tasty, with high quality slices of chashu pork, menma (bamboo shoots), wakame (seaweed) and soft-boiled egg.  The quality of the pork used in their ramen is a big step over what is typically offered in U.S. ramen shops.  Having been to Tokyo many times, I can say that Terakawa's quality compares favorably.  When ordering, it is possible to choose the softness of noodles as well as add various extras.  I've also really enjoyed the chicken katsu and chicken curry dishes.  Service is prompt, courteous and helpful and prices are reasonable.  Interior is well-designed unlike most restaurants in Chinatown.  BYOB is welcome, which is also something not usually offered in Chinatown.  Highly recommended!
