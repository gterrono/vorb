    If you're a carb loader or bread-lover, go to Fork. And I mean this in a loving manner. Seriously, Fork is like a gourmet bakery course disguised in a gourmet meal.

    If you want to eat the House Menu, which we did, get ready to eat a lot of bread! This involves you choosing individual appetizers, entrees, and desserts, and the whole table had to agree on a pasta to share amongst all members (which came after the appetizer and 2nd bread course).

    Despite not being the type of people to heavily indulge in complementary bread, the servers at Fork explained the intricacies of each rolls in such detail that we were compelled to at least have a bite of all types of bread provided. The complementary breads included:

    - rustic olive roll, cheese-stuffed Philly pretzel bite (before the appetizer)

    - baguette loaf to share (before the pasta)

    - squid ink sourdough spongy bread, sunflower seed/vegetable roll, a beef-fat brioche (after the pasta, before the entrée)

    - at the very end after dessert, a complementary mini-donut. Any donut cravings I had beforehand--FULFILLED!

    All of these tiny rolls were about a bite large, but thanks to the variety, that was quite enough when added to the sumptuous meal presented before us.

    And each bread came with a butter/jam that particularly complemented each type of bread. SERIOUSLY, bread at Fork is SERIOUS BUSINESS.

    Uh, the pasta, appetizers, entrees, and desserts were amazing, too. But you can read about them in someone else's review. No, this review is...

                                                   *~*\\an ode to bread//*~*

    (...Well, I have to recommend the eggplant cake for someone who doesn't have a huge sweet tooth but still loves smoky goodness.)

    I have never written a review on Yelp.  However .....

    I went to Fork Saturday night for the first time in six or eight years.  My girlfriend and I both agreed that it was the best restaurant meal either of us had experienced in years.

    The decor is understatedly hip/elegant.  A deep single room, high ceilinged, warm, nice bar in front.

    The waitstaff was smart and attentive.  They first brought us small, warm, homemade, onion bialys with their own version of cream cheese (more a crème fraîche).  Fantastic.

    We then had grilled carrots.  At sixteen dollars, I was questioning my friend's judgment, but I can honestly say that it was perhaps the most intense, complex, and delicious vegetable dish I can remember.  Caramelized, undertones of balsamic vinegar and cream, a simple carrot puree into which to dip the dark smoky carrots.

    Fresh bread next.  Thick, hard as a rock, almost charred crust, light and  inside.  Homemade butter at room temperature.  Wow.

    She had Brazino en croute, though the en croute was really just an open wallet sized thin buttery toasted something that was unreal.  The fish was light, in a tamarind broth with little sautéed rice cakes and blanched artichoke stems.  

    I had Pastrami Lamb, which I can't begin to explain.  Suffice it to say that it married the comfort aspect of pastrami with impeccably roasted lamb (tenderloin, shank, chop), with a sauerkraut, rye, chard salad.

    Memorable meals are something unique and special.  I will take this night in my memory forward, and hope that I can replicate it before I'm old in the tooth.

    Fork has been around a long time, and from what I have heard, it has been all over the map in terms of food and service.  Well, I am pleased to report that its current incarnation is top-notch.  Fork is in my top five restaurants of Philadelphia.  Chef Eli Kulp is doing really inventive, fun things without being too fussy or pretentious.  Portion sizes are, for example, great!  No skimpy haute cuisine portions here!

    We each had a specialty cocktail, both were great.  None of them have names, so I um, can't remember what I had!  The bartender, however, was a real sourpuss.  The wine list was interesting and had a lot of unconventional things on it.

    Our waitress was very warm, friendly, intelligent about the whole menu, the wine, and a whole lot more.  Service was paced perfectly.  Several different people took care of us, which is the mark of a serious restaurant.  

    Decor:  very sophisticated and very cool.  A tree in the middle of the restaurant, original murals on the walls.  Beautiful.

    Food:  Grilled carrots with ramps, yogurt, coriander.  Beautiful to look at, excellent marriage of tastes and textures, and nice and large.  It made you actually like carrots!  These are not your mother's carrots!

    Ramp gnudi.  Wow.  Hurry and get this because ramps are about done for the season.  Beautiful, subtle flavors, and and although there were a lot of elements that I had never had together before, it just gave the sense of familiar comfort food.

    Razor clams with radish and apple.  Very good.  Tasty tender clams.  Fun to eat.

    This was our first time here, and since we were enjoying everything, the waitress said we HAD to have the duck feast special.  They only do 2 of them per night!  Wow, we made a great choice.  Duck done 4 ways!  Confit with broccoli rabe, salad with duck prosciutto and duck hearts, duck meat and duck liver meatballs, and finally, duck breast with a simple reduction sauce.  Wow. Wow. Wow.  Stuffed to the gills!  All of the 4 duck preparations were great, but here's the kicker:

    Earlier this evening I went to Zahav for a cocktail, and I tried a few appetizers too.  One of them was their duck hearts!  Wow, I've never even HAD duck hearts and I had 'em twice in one night!  And now, the comparison:  Fork's duck hearts completely blew away Zahav's.  Much more tender and possibly because they were smaller.  (and better cooked)  Wow. Wow. Wow.  

    Folks, if it's been a while, get back here.  Now.
