    Oh, the food here is good.  I got carnitas burrito the first time, and it was the best carnitas I've ever had.  The pork was crisp and delicious.  It was so good I had to get the carnitas tacos next time I came, instead of something else. The salsa was full of flavor and added just the right bite to the tacos.  But then my associate ordered a fish burrito to go and after watching them fry the fish I almost regretted that I hadn't sampled more of the menu.  I have to find the strength, somehow, to tear myself  away from those carnitas.  The grilled chicken looks and smells delicious too.

    I advise against takeout.  A lot of Mexican food is mushy and soggy when you get it, and it does not suffer when you take it home in a bag.  This food is not like that.  You will want to eat it while it is fresh and hot.

    Tacos and burritos all run about $6-7, plus tax.

    Addendum 2010-04-10: The hot hot sauce is much better than the medium hot sauce.   It has a lot more flavor, and it is not unbearably hot.  I think it's mostly made with chipotle.

    Jose's Tacos is a great, authentic, low-key place to go for a solid burrito or taco....or a nibble of both.

    Jose's is a bit hard to find -- it doesn't stand out, doesn't use neon signs or take up a large amount of real estate. It's small and hot, but delivers some great food.

    I got my usual burrito order -- beef, cheese, rice, lettuce, peppers. It tastes great, and I had no complaints.     So I was looking for a place to get good street tacos in Philly and this is what I found to be recommended on Yelp.  I'm not sure why it's rated so highly, and maybe I ordered the wrong stuff.  At any rate, this place wasn't that great.

    Firstly, the tacos are served with iceberg lettuce, sour cream, and cheese.  I was expecting an "authentic Mexican" place to air more on the side of cilantro and onions, and not Taco Bell style.  I asked that I have these specifications to my tacos and they said no problem.  Then when I got home, they hadn't done this, so my tacos were drenched in sour cream and lettuce.  Dang.

    Next point of order: the guac.  Sub-par at best.  It was essentially just smashed up avocados.  There was no black pepper, no salt, no lime, no jalapenos, and no onions.  Boring guacamole with very little flavor.  It was served with what I can only describe as a pic de gallo / tomatillo hybrid sauce.  This was almost better than the guac (I ended up mixing them).

    I'd also like to comment on the hot sauce, arguably the most important aspect of a Mexican restaurant.  I order the "hot" option to go with my tacos.  It was not spicy and the flavor was not good.  It was almost a smoky flavor, more similar to Tabasco Chipotle Sauce.

    So anyway, I'm wise enough to not completely write a place off based on one bad experience, so I'll edit the review and my rating if I go back and enjoy the food more.
