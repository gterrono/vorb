    Woohoo! Where else can I get two full meals for $3? As a vegetarian the soups are out of the question, but those gloriously chewy noodles are still fair game. The peanut noodles are a big tangled pile of yum, add some chili oil and you're set with plenty more left for lunch the next day.

    My carnivorous counterpart has enjoyed many of the soups, but a favorite of his is the duck. A plate full of jellyfish is also a must (personally it looks blech), and at ~$8 it's one of the more expensive dishes on the menu.

    Cash only, but with these prices that makes perfect sense. Kudos to Nan Zhou for upgrading their space, it's so much larger & cleaner than the original spot.

    I find myself torn over Nan Zhou because while I generally like the food and the price of entrees, I do find the service a bit off putting.  There really is no decorum from the staff.  The servers (and I've had several) all have the same disinterested tone.  They aren't rude, they are just dealing in volume and it's apparent.

    You can get a decent size plate of food here for a good price.  Clearly, someone back in that kitchen knows what they are doing.  I like the shaved noodles with vegetables because of the price and the way I feel after I eat it.  It's enjoyable.  I do wish I had a bib because undoubtedly, I will get some sauce on my shirt.

    So, if you're in the mood for a variety of hand drawn noodles made in tasty ways give this place a shot.  Just don't expect them to ask you how you're doing.
