    Have you ever had a kale salad?  I never knew people ate kale salads!  PF makes a refreshing, light but still filling one that has chicken, almonds and some kind of hummus-like dressing.  I think if you're looking for something heavy or super flavorful for lunch, go grab a sandwich at Famous 4th's or a food truck, or a meal at any of the Stephen Starr restaurants across the street.  The point of the food at PF is that it's fresh, uses lots of healthy ingredients, and uses very little salt or other unhealthy additives that are yummy but high in fat/calories/etc.

    The tandoori chicken sandwich and the turkey w/ gorgonzola and pear were both filling at $7.50 each, but I preferred the turkey.  Both meats were of high quality and tasty.  (Although I found the chicken in my kale salad overly dry--maybe it has to do with the cut, sliced versus cubed...?)  The tandoori sandwich used a lot of mint in its spread which was a bit of a surprise!

    The kale and ricotta frittata was ok--much more eggy than I thought it would be but I'm sure they heavily scale back on a lot of the bad-for-you ingredients like cheese and cream that I'm used to at bakeries.  You can really taste the fresh ingredients like tomato and red onions.  For 300 calories, it was not bad, but pricey at $4.50.  I wonder how their spinach and cheddar frittata is tho...

    i also invested in a bag of coffee beans--pretty pricey at $10.26 per 8-oz bag, but the medium roast is delicious, esp when made with my new pour over drip kettle!  PF seems to mostly carry Ethiopian beans--there were 3 medium roasts available and deliveries are Sunday and Thursday, I believe.  If you are willing to spend more on a cup of joe, their coffee is excellent to go.

    There were 3 soups available and I tried the carrot & ginger and their green soups.  Both were very good but if you're looking for heavy and salty soups like the ones from Hale & Hearty, you will be in for a big surprise.  Again, the ingredients are extremely fresh and the soup is not overly seasoned so they stay "refreshing" and light.

    I thought the zucchini noodle salad w/ sesame dressing looked amazing so I'll try that next time as well as the butternut squash, beets, kale, with some type of beans-salad.  The tuna salad looked not so interesting but as you can see, there is quite a bit of variety of salads and sandwiches.

    The calorie counts are helpful for the super health conscious.  You also need to be willing to spend a little more than you would at a local deli.  If anything, this place inspires me to start cooking with some of the "unique" ingredients I see at the grocery.

    Listed in lunch break - eat, drink, shop, explore, ritten's in the HOUSE, philadelphia goes vegetarian., a votre santé!

    It's 12:33PM and the stagnant scent of burnt coffee is becoming too much to handle. I am in dire need of a cubicle escape. A "cuscapé", if you will.

    Impetus comes in the form of a craving. A SOUP CRAVING.

    Where to go where to gooo? I mean Au Bon Pain is just yards away, Cosi's not much farther. In fact, there's an entire food court brimming with stale, over processed, over-sodiumed varieties of soups a mere half block from my office. But I don't want any of that.

    I want something Fresh. Healthy. Delicious. Something in the realm of pureed kale, spinach, and cauliflower, seasoned with hints of cumin, cilantro and curry.

    YES, such delights exist in center city!

    It's called PureFare's green goodness soup and you would be insane not to worship its innate ability to satiate without overfeeding. I enjoyed this alongside a quinoa blueberry muffin (which, I am glad to report, contains WHOLE blueberries bursting with juicy goodness).

    Good things to know:
    +Seating is limited but it never seems to be overwhelmingly packed - there's one large shared center table, some stool seating along the windows and a couple of tables outside for the warmer months and/or those with polar bear skin.
    +Free wifi.
    +Service is a bit slow but I don't mind - I come here to get away from the 16-17-18 st. hustle bustle, anyway.
    +Prices are a little steep (specifically for the specialty drinks) but if you stick with a salad/soup/sandwich it's really quite reasonable.
    + The drip coffee looks legit, although I haven't tried it.
    +Interesting baked good options include olive oil/thyme muffins & avocado chocolate chip cookies.
    +They track your purchases through their website - after 12 visits you get a 20% off coupon & the site also doubles as a calorie tracker, if you're into that sort of thing.

    PureFare is definitely worth a visit, plus it's an excuse to eat a healthy meal! (although I have a feeling that homemade twix bar of theirs is rather decadent....).

    We have come here many times to pick up our CSA from Crawford Organics, but the other day was the first time we came for lunch and coffee.

    They serve Blue Bottle coffee, and the pour-over preparation is exactly the way they do it at Blue Bottle at the Ferry Building in San Francisco, including grinding the beans fresh to order for each individual cup. They also sell the Blue Bottle beans, and considering they came from the other side of the country, the roasting date on the package is remarkably fresh (within the week).

    I was pleased with the soup and sandwiches we ordered for lunch. The soup was lentil, butternut squash, and coconut milk, which was an interesting combination, and very hearty. We got there early enough for breakfast sandwiches; they had several types but we got one with eggs, leeks, and spinach on multi-grain bread. It took them awhile to make it and bring it out, but other than that, no complaints, the sandwich was very good. They also have plenty of pre-made sandwiches, salads, and sides. We sat at the big communal table in the middle, but there is also some bar-type seating near the windows. A couple near us got some of the salads, and they looked very good.

    I would definitely come again, for coffee alone, or for soup and sandwiches.

    Sometimes you have to pay a little more for a healthier meal option, I get that, but at the end of a lunch meetup here with a few fellow yelpers found myself more jazzed about the company than my sandwich and the price I paid for a lackluster lunch. Pure Fare, I want to love you, but "meh" is much more on-point than "A-Ok" ... please help me love you.

    In general, I really like the idea of this place. It's a CSA pickup point, the decor is cute rustic-chic without being too kitschy, the fresh and natural menu is a great alternative to a lot of the other junk options in the area, but in the end and after all of the positive praise it has received, I was still left a little underwhelmed.

    First off, maybe it's just me but I think if you're going to spend $8+ on a sandwich ($10+ for a pre-made sandwich and bottle of water) it should be a little more bulky and if nothing else come with some sort of a side; even Panera shells out for an Apple! My turkey, gorgonzola, and pear was on the "meh" side; a little dry, and not enough of that strong blue cheese taste came through to balance the other key components of the sandwich. A friends goat brie and fig sandwich was just OK to me even after she asked for a side of fig spread to amp up the dismal amount that was soaked up into the bread. The description on the wrapper vs what was inside the bag looked like two completely different sandwiches, but she was assured by the employees that the order was correct. Well ok then. Next point on food, I understand there isn't a large area to prep food, but when did you make these sandwiches? You didn't make it in front of me I know that much. Because of the fact that everything is pre-made, I'm wondering if there is any way to customize any of their menu options to accomodate health needs; it doesn't look that way, but I may be wrong.

    Second, the space. Cute as it may be, the seating is not condusive to visiting with friends. With the handful of us that made this our lunch spot we were cool with being separated, some at the communal table, and others at the window...the problem arose when I was approached by two ladies with strollers of kids who were nowhere near the table saying that they were sitting there already. My thoughts: "Um, no you aren't, you walked away from the table and haven't even ordered, just because you have tykes in tow doesn't mean you can claim a table from 10 feet away." Instead, I graciously allowed them to take the table. I choose my battles wisely and I wasn't looking to pick a fight with Rittenhouse moms, quite frankly I did it for the kids...it's always for the kids.

    Outdoor seating is limited, but not a bad option. By the time we all got our acts together our little group had made it outside and around two tables to enjoy a little sunshine on our break...so much so that I ended up taking a 2 hour lunch so as not to rush. Again, this was much more attributed to the company than the sandwich that sat there getting eaten at a very leisurely pace.

    Will I hit this again? Maybe. The price-tag + mediocrity = mild reluctance. I doubt I'd go back for another sandwich, but I like knowing that the healthy alternative is available in soup, salad, and fro-yo form; I'm hopeful that one of the other options can change my mind for the better at some point in the future.
