    It took awhile for Hip City Veg to grow on me. Hip City Veg is basically vegan fast food. I love that its vegan, I love that it tastes good, but at the end of the meal I am left feeling like I paid $9 for a chicken sandwich, fries not included.

    I've tried exactly 4 things from the menu. The Ziggy Burger, which I love because the tempeh gives it a nice smokey flavor. The Hip City Ranch tastes exactly like a chicken sandwich and isn't overly ranchy. Give it to any meat eater, and I doubt they will know the difference. The Buffalo Bella is perfectly spicy (and messy!) but the celery slaw was a bit overwhelming for me. Lastly, the Groothie. It was filling and great for a meal on the go, but had a bit too much banana for me. Disclaimer: I don't love bananas.

    I'm slowly making my way through the menu, but so far so good.

    HOLY CRAP.

    I just moved back to the Philly area after being gone for 2 years. Many things have changed since I left, so I was asking co-workers for good lunch spots to hit in the area. One of them knew that I was vegetarian, and recommended that I check out HipCityVeg -- and BOY am I glad that he did!

    I have gone back to this pace literally every single day since I found out that it exists, and I don't see any end in sight. I've tried most of the things on the menu, and they are all amazing. My personal favorite is the fajita wrap. They somehow create this chipotle crema that is just a bit spicy and blends super well with the fresh veggies and chick'n. Incredible.

    If I had to put any type of negative, it's that the salads are a bit overpriced. $9 for a medium sized salad that is mainly lettuce just seems a bit steep -- but it's far from out of control.

    I have been and will continue to recommend everyone to check this place out -- meat eaters and veggies alike. You just can't go wrong!

    HipCityVeg is a great place for meat lovers to try something outside their comfort zone. I love meat, all kinds of meat, so I didn't think I'd be satisfied with an all veggie sandwich. Govinda's is good, but after eating their chicken cheesesteaks again and again, you'll realize that you're just eating bread (seitan) with bread (the bun or wrap) with a small helping of peppers. The focus is not on the seitan or soy meat but on all of the delicious, wonderful ingredients that are married to it. For fast (or speedy) food, this is really good. The Crispy HipCity Ranch is also delish. The Bella Bistro is a big portabella mushroom sandwich. Many vegetarians will claim that portabella mushrooms taste almost exactly like beef. It doesn't. It's good, but to call it beef would be insulting to both beef and mushrooms. The Bella Bistro is a nice, hearty sandwich filled with plenty of red onions and artichokes, a couple of slices of tomato (from possibly a small beefsteak tomato), arugala, and olive tampenade all in a big multi-grain roll. Be forewarned, your breath will stink of onions when you're done, but it's sooo good. The Fajita Wrap with its blackened soy chicken, peppers, onions, pico de gallo, and avocado is a little more forgiving. The use of intense flavors in both sandwiches masks the fact that you're not eating meat. The Buffalo Bella is another yummy. I find myself ordering the Philly Steak quite often these days because of its delicious mix of soy meat, mushrooms, and onions. The Jerk Caesar Salad is my only nonfave so far. There's too much salad compared to the protein portion. Give this place a try. The servers are friendly, which makes the wait in the occasionally long lines bearable. It's not exactly a sit-down place as there is limited seating, but on a warm, sunny day, I can think of few places around Rittenhouse Square that can provide you with a more satisfying sandwich that you can enjoy while sitting in the park people-watching. My only gripe is they took away the Kid's Steak from the kid's menu. Now I can't really introduce my little ones to the wonderful sandwiches here since they're not really sized appropriately for kids.
