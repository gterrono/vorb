    We had a very pleasant dinner at Effie's.  We went on a Friday evening in the summer and sat outside.  It wasn't crowded and we accomplished exactly what we came to do, catch up, have a few bottles of wine, and enjoy a nice meal.

    We started with the Meze Plate, which was delicious and plenty for four to share as we sipped our wine and chatted.  They gave us plenty of out-of-the-oven sliced pita bread to soak everything up.  For our mains we had Moussaka, Makaronia, Lamb Souvlaki Plate, and the Lamb Lemonato.  I ordered the Makaronia and wiped my plate clean. Sometimes I get in the mood for pasta, and the combination of bacon, spinach, and mushrooms alone is enough for me to pull the trigger.  Add in some Greek cheeses and I am sold.  I was able to taste some of each plate, and in my opinion everything was very flavorful.  I thought the moussaka was especially delicious, even when heated up in the microwave the next day.

    The service was also great.  We had a nice young lady as our waitress who was exceedingly polite and understanding.  At no point did we ever feel rushed, even as the outdoor area was nearly full up as we were finishing our wine.  The prices were reasonable as well.  All in all, a very nice experience and would return when in the mood for a relaxing evening and some good Greek cuisine.

    The main thing this BYOB has going for it is the ambiance.  It has a sweet little courtyard, a private oasis in the city, and that great carriage house space at the rear.  We didn't sit in the carriage house because there was a large group in there, but it looks like it would be a good space for a group even though other reviews also mention it as a romantic table for two.  Food-wise, your better bet is to try to get a table at Kanella, which is leaps and bounds better.  Effie's is just ok.  The saganaki is pretty weak.  It doesn't come out in flames (which I only care about for the drama of it), but it's too dense and less flavorful than other Greek restaurants I've been to (Symposium in New York has the best saganaki, Mythos in West Chester, PA is a close second).  I had the Shrimp Santorini with feta, scallions and a nice tomato sauce, which was good - I liked it, but I was disappointed with the portion for how much I paid for it.  For $23, I would have hoped to have gotten twice as much.  Take your $23 a few blocks away to Kanella if you really want Greek, and get yourself something (everything is delicious) that'll blow Effie's away.

    Opa! A decent Greek experience! I went here with some friends recently on a Saturday night, and was happy to find that it smelled pretty great inside.  We were seated pretty fast (which doesn't really say much - considering that it was a Saturday night should be packed - but I digress).  We were seated fast, and the service the entire time was great.  The hostess, the owner, and the servers were all super nice.  We ordered a variety of things from salads to chicken to lamb to humus to saganaki.  Most of them were solid.   I tried the greek salad (the grilled feta was well grilled and tasted good), the humus was really garlicky but well made otherwise. It wasn't out of this world by any standards, but it was pretty darn good.  The pita was warm and grilled and one of  my favorite things I had.  The saganaki was well fried, but I prefer it when it's been flambeed right in front of you, which is how it should be done in theory, and would make it taste better.  However, it was well fried, and had a good consistency. I did also like that they provided you with your own lemon slice so that you could apply the preferred amount for yourself.  The salads I tried were all delicious.  Not sure which ones were ordered but they were great. I obviously didn't try the chicken or lamb, but they smelled great. Additionally, we tried some tzatziki which was delicious and well made.  To finish off the meal, we tried the baklava and another filo dough / custard like desert.  The baklava was good, pretty good in fact compared to most I have tasted in Philadelphia - the right consistency of filo dough, honey, and other such things.  The custard based desert was to die for and made me want so much more of it.  However, I restrained myself to a few small bites.

    For about $20 a person, we all ate a pretty hearty, good, full meal.  I'm a fan of this place.  Not totally sure I'd go here over Dmitri's, but it's a good, slightly cheaper alternative.  I also like the location and ambiance. With the kitchen flowing right into the seating area, it seems like you are literally eating in a Greek friend's home, which I enjoyed. The smells coming in from the kitchen were all amazing!
