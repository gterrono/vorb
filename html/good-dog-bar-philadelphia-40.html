    Hype can really set your expectations high.  From all the reviews that I've read and the word on the… Hype can really set your expectations high.  From all the reviews that I've read and the word on the street, I felt confident that I would be making the right choice by choosing the Good Dog for dinner on a whim.

    I'm going to be honest, and I hope that I don't lose Yelp friends and make enemies at the Good Dog, but I was highly satisfied in some aspects and disappointed in others.

    First: The GOOD!  The atmosphere is great in this place!  Bar tenders, waiters, decor, music, EVERYTHING is just so friendly and down to earth about the place.  There are booths on the first floor and tables on the second. (I didn't actually see the second floor because the restaurant is closed up there on Sundays.)  The beer selection appeared good and there was plenty of top-shelf liquor too.

    Second: The things that were not up to my expectations.  I want to say first of all that the Sausage and Tomato soup was excellent!  But, upon ordering a dinner sandwich I was sorely disappointed by the portions for the price.  The quality of taste was superb, but with the sandwiches you get a plate that is 80% french fries/sweet potato fries, 5% coleslaw, and 15% sandwich.  Again, the actual food tasted GREAT, but the portions for the price was off.

    I think that a better way to approach eating here would  be to order several of the "Little Bites" (appetizers) and get some drinks.  There are also larger entrees ("Big Bites"), but then were $15 or more.

    I still gave the Good Dog four stars because its food does taste much better than other bar/restaurants and the service and ambiance is amazing.  I just hope that portions are reconsidered for their price.  I'd pay less to have fewer fries so I could get a "small bite" as well.     Read more

    Listed in The Places I Go Out, Top Restaurants

    Good Dog is one of my favorite places to go late at night after work.  Their changing beer list is always top notch and they have a good selection of liquor as well (not too many bars that I know of carry Hangar One vodka, which is my favorite).
    They have rotating food specials, such as flatbread pizza of the day and quiche of the day, although it seems like they often rotate between the same 3 choices or so.  Still delicious.  Their menu staples are great also.
    Definitely try their wings, which are dripping with a sweet and spicy buffalo sauce, my favorite wings around.  The macaroni and cheese with corn flake crust is also excellent.
    They also have an 'in the biz' discount for servers, bartenders, and other restaurant workers which I definitely appreciate, being a bartender myself. This discount is available sundays through thursdays, I believe.

    A few minor complaints:
    -Because of the size of the restaurant, it's sometimes hard to find a table and even harder to find a table to accommodate more than 4 people, especially on a weekend.
    -Sometimes the service is hit or miss.  Servers Liz and Angelo are great, friendly, talkative, and attentive.  Others who I'm not sure of their names are a little less friendly and attentive.  Nothing that wouldn't make me come back.
    -Their fry sauce isn't anything to write home about.

    All in all, one of my favorite places in Center City.

    Working nearby, I've been to many a bar in the area; Good Dog remains hands down my favorite place to go in the city for a couple pints and one mean-ass burger.  

    Atmosphere neither posh nor dive, the joint is nice and clean with black & white photos of dogs adorning the walls; think wood everywhere and red accents.  Low lighting but not too low that you can't read a menu or the dog bone shaped chalk boards informing you what wonderful craft brews are currently tapped.

    Oh the beer; American-brewed micros flow from all taps, and the selection changes so you don't ever get bored with their selection.  Also a great selection of bottles (Bells Two-Hearted Ale, Flying Dog selections) and cans (Sly Fox variety, all cans 1/2 price on Wednesdays).

    You've already heard the legend of the burger filled with Roquefort cheese accompanied by a mix of sweet potato and yukon gold fries, believe the hype.  The rest of the menu looks great but I really haven't even gone there; I have sampled some of my girl's veggie cake and as a meat eater I can say it's the finest non-carnivorous sandwich I've tried.

    Last but certainly not least the service here has been second to none in my experience.  I go here way too much and the staff has been fantastic top to bottom.  The beer and food are great but they keep me coming back for more.  They just celebrated their five-year anniversary and I know there are plenty more to come; see you on the second floor.
