    Great food.  Absolutely horrible service.

    Had a belated Mother's Day dinner here with my family.  If this review was for the food alone, I'd give them a solid 4 stars, possibly 5.  The Figs in a Blanket, Chicken Empanadas, and Tuna Tartare were all top notch and I'd recommend them.  The seafood paella was fantastic, I'd even say it was better than the paella I had at Amada a couple months ago.

    That said, the service here is atrocious.

    A couple lowlights:
    - We arrived 20 minutes before our reservation.  We weren't seated until 20 min after our scheduled reservation time.  And even then, it took me going from the bar to ask them about our table for them to seat us.
    -  We asked our waitress to space out the small plates so they don't come all at once, but at the same time we'd be finished before the paella came out.  That didn't work.  4 plates come out right away...the last comes out with the paella.
    - The brought all our food out...but didn't have any plates on the table.  And they didn't even get plates right away!!  We sat there and watched our hot food while waiting for plates.
    - Finished my water before any food came out, didn't get a refill until after our plates were cleared.

    I went to Valanni for dinner on a Friday evening with a friend. I had to change the reservation time three times due a bad combination of traffic & weather that evening, but they were completely accommodating. The decor is slick - real slick. Surprisingly, I felt like I was in a spaceship-slash-techno dance club. Mirrored walls alternate with white paneled walls ingrained with wave-like textures. The bar's in the center, illuminated with neon blue lights. On the other side from where we sat, there are booths furnished with white leather couches.

    I ordered the vegetarian paella, which is offered in portions for either 1 or 2 people. The single portion I received was enormous, and I was convinced they'd sent me a 2-person one instead. They didn't! It's just a giant serving. I ended up taking more than half of it home, so keep in mind that it's large enough to share with at least one other person. My friend ordered the following tapas: chickpeas & chorizo, brussel sprouts, and one of the oyster dishes. She loved them all. Our drinks were delicious - though her gimlet was much stronger than my brazilian pop. Service was attentive, though not overbearing. I'm looking forward to going to their happy hour sometime this summer.

    Listed in Delicious Vegetarian Food

    There are only two words you need to know about this place:   Brussel Sprouts.  Seriously, if you go here you have to order them.  They are crazy delicious and cooked with balsamic vinegar and apples and are a little crispy.  The texture and the taste was just amazing!  I never would have thought this, but I would come back just for that alone.  
         I made a reservation on open table on a Saturday night about an hour before we ate at this place.  I was craving tapas, and after doing a little research, this place seemed to have a good selection of vegetarian dishes.
          We got there and the place seemed pretty packed.  We were seated quickly by the window.  We ordered one of all the veggie dishes, which came in a set.  It was $75, but a whole lot of food.  You always expect to spend a lot when you get tapas, and sometimes you do that and don't even get full.  This wasn't the case here.  We actually had to get a take out box because we were so full!  We made it through all the dishes until we go to the antipasto platter which was huge.  It was very delicious though as it contained all these grilled veggies, olives, and legumes.  Nothing we had that night was bad.  I give it four stars though because the only things that really stood out were the brussel sprouts, and the antipasto platter.  Everything else was good, but not that memorable.
       However, both drinks I had were really different and delicious.  One drink was made with balsamic vinegar and reminded me of an Asian vinegar drink from Taiwan.  It was really tasty.  The other drink had Thai Basil leaves muddled in it, which was good, and quite different from the usual taste of mint, which is frequently muddled in drinks.  My boyfriend had a Manhattan, his favorite drink.  He's very particular about them, and said this one was the second best one he ever had, and only topped by Bar Alulea's in Hiroshima, Japan.  I  would also go back to this place just for cocktails.  
         Overall, we really enjoyed our evening out here, and I plan to go back sometime.

History

Established in 2000.

Valanni restaurant opened its doors in September of 2000, introducing "Medi-Latin" cuisine to Philadelphia. Situated right off of the "Avenue of the Arts," Valanni has become a pre-theater ritual and place to meet for a night-cap for many who live in and around the neighborhood. The bar at Valanni keeps up with the creative cuisine by offering an assortment of sangria, mojitos and other specialty drinks. The kitchen is open until 1 AM daily, serving Tapas and a Mediterranean inspired lounge menu.
