    The hummus here is the best hummus I have ever had... and I've been to both Israel and Greece! It's the perfect consistency and doesn't taste too bitter or too sweet. Plus the flatbread they give you for dipping is also delicious.

    If you're looking for a place that has unique tastes and gives you a friendly, ethnic (middle eastern) dining atmosphere, you MUST go to Zahav. I went during restaurant week and we got lots of food for a great price, but I'd go here anytime I have friends in town that I want to impress, or if I'm looking to splurge on something tasty before seeing a movie at the Ritz East.

    Two things you must eat -
    * the appetizer sampler (not sure what it's called on the menu) because it is full of amazing little salads made with several delicious ingredients like beets, cucumbers, peppers, and more.
    * the fried cauliflower because it tastes like a french fry and the sauce they garnish it with is amazing

    The rest all depends on what you're in the mood for - there's a lot of variety on the menu. And it's served tapas-style with small portions, so you can share things and not limit yourself to one meal.

    All in all this is a GREAT place, and I'm glad Philadelphia magazine recognized that!

    I couldn't ask for much more than what I got when I finally made my way to Zahav's.  The concept is unique, the food was spectacular, the portions were well sized, the atmosphere was relaxed but trendy/hip, and the prices were good.  The only strange/offputting thing was that the waiters wore israeli military dress as their uniform.  

    My girlfriend is vegetarian and I trend in that direction, so we did an all "dairy" meal.  We started with the small appetizer platter (minus the hummus), which includes 8 small dishes ranging from baked eggplant to tabbouleh to spicy chickpeas to beets and tahini.  All were delicious and a great size.  It was also super fun to taste 8 different plates right off the bat.

    We then moved on to the regular plates, which are tapas sized portions.  We had 4 between the two of us, which, combined with the appetizer, was the perfect amount of food.  The fried houmli (sp?) was decadent - different than what I ever imagined and oh so good.  The fried cauliflower was perfectly fried and not burnt with a nice lebaneh sauce.  The grilled eggplant (galil) was really tasty but a bit oversauced and a bit overwhelming.  Finally, the stuffed pepper was tasty, but not nearly as special as the rest of the meal.  The best part - with all of that food (12 dishes in all, plus some wonderful bread), we only paid $55 with tip.  No alcohol makes things pretty cheap of course, but $55 with tip is a pretty amazing price for such well made food.

    A nice side bar is that we went (just two of us) without reservations and were able to get a spot at the counter overlooking the kitchen without a wait.  The counter and the bar looked to always have two spaces open even though we were there from 8-9:30, prime eating hours.   Go!  Enjoy!  As my jewish grandmother said, "eat something, it's good for you!"

    Bottom Line: Zahav was completely delicious. It far surpassed my expectations and redefinied what I expect of the best restaurants. The menu for restaurant week was phenomenal, but it's absolute worth a visit at anytime.
