    Listed in Nibbles

    We went here yesterday for brunch, and on our way back home, we made plans to return today. Sampan totally exceeded our expectations. Craving Asian food, we were on our way to Chinatown when we passed it. We looked at the menu, thought it was going to be too trendy to deliver on flavor, and walked about a block before we both agreed we should give it a chance. After all, it was cold and Chinatown was still several blocks away.

    We sat at the bar because we always like to watch the chefs in action. Suffice it to say that with all of their bao making, rice frying, and dumpling pinching, they kept us entertained. The highlight was watching them make the Swedish fish frozen yogurt base for that night's service.

    We loved everything we got. When restaurants try to do fusion, they usually smack you in the face with the concept and the flavors. But here, it's subtle, and it all works beautifully.

    Here's what we got:
    - General Tso's soup dumplings
    - Edamame dumplings
    - Wild mushroom dumplings
    - Kimchee fried rice....we ordered this, devoured it, and ordered another one only to devour that one
    - Shrimp and lobster dumplings
    - Frozen yogurt of the day (Tang and Chocolate Cinnamon Toast Crunch)

    Everything was soooo good, but if you're looking for the most unique and could only pick one dish, go with the edamame dumplings. The goat cheese and truffle flavors are just subtle enough not to overpower the earthiness of the edamame.

    Next stop: Sampan's happy hour

    In the year or so I've been going to Sampan regularly it has solidly become my favorite restaurant in the city.

    Sampan consistently pleases everyone of my senses.

    When you walk in you're greeted by a usually friendly hostess and the vibrant decor.  I really like the lighted walls and the great outdoor decor.  It's easy to recognize the wood paneling on the outside.

    I've only had one bad waitress in the many times we've been here, but haven't seen her the previous two visits so perhaps she's not there anymore.

    Every other server has been prompt and courteous.  A couple times we've sat at the bar next to the kitchen.  This is not as intimate as the tables, but is definitely worth trying once.  The various cooks in the back will usually chat with you about what they're making and it's really cool to see all the amazing food being prepared.

    What I really love though is the food.  The general tso's dumplings are delicous and you just can't beat the various skewers.  My favorite though are the brussels sprouts.  If you think you don't like brussels sprouts, trust me, these will change your mind.

    I'm personally not a big fan of the "tasting" menu because you have to pick out a certain number from each category.  I'd rather it be changing every couple days and be a true chef's choice menu.  But it's good for new comers to be "forced" to try a variety of new things.

    Other than that you though can't go wrong at Sampan.  Get an amazing bottle of sake, sit back and enjoy the excellent Asian fusion.
