        I came here for the Sunday night tasting menu.  By accident.  Not sure what to expect, we got one vegetarian tasting and one meat tasting.  10 courses. wow!  I was super nervous about the bill...

        On the whole, I think that ordering a la carte from Kanella is better.  The vegetarian tasting came with too many cold dishes - carrot salad, bean salad, etc.  They were good, but not something you want to warm your heart on a cold winter night.  The meat tasting was better than the vegetarian, but definitely had some things I wasn't crazy about - duck hearts, anyone?  

        Dessert was the real standout.  They are an extra $5, but are much larger than the tasting courses so you really only need one after all the food that comes with the tasting (it's a lot).  We got these sweet fried balls, which reminded me of gulab jamun but was much better.  

        At only $30 for the vegetarian and $35 for the meat tastings, the price is really right.  If you're into tastings, I'd check Kanella's out.  If you're just trying to try Kanella, I'd say avoid Sunday nights and go for the a la carte...that will surely not disappoint!

        Dined here recently at the suggestion of a friend.  At the suggestion of another friend, we got a bunch of appetizers to share.  We also shared a nice bottle of white wine my friend brought.

        Our server started with a recitation of the evening's specials and, to his credit, noted the price of each one after describing it (side rant -- what's up with all the *other* servers/restaurants that rattle off a bunch of specials and never mention their cost?  What, do I look like I'm Louis XIV?  Does their restaurant look like the Palace at Versailles?).  While we debated the merits (and demerits) of the various dishes on the menu, our server got us wine glasses, filled them with wine, kept the bottle on ice nearby, and got us water, as well; nice.

        First, we got some tasty sourdough bread and the day's dip -- tahini, on the house.  The tahini was okay, but I think the traditional olive oil would have worked better.  Next, we had the 3-dip app, which came with fava, beet, and eggplant dips and nicely grilled pita pieces.  All three dips had a pleasing combo of texture, oil, and seasonings, though the fava one was our favorite.  The pita, incidentally, went better with the tahini than the bread did.  Third, we had the bourek, a warm and yummy cheese-filled filo pastry drizzled with honey and accompanied by a beet salad.

        Fourth, we had the mixed plate, which included more pita, fine fried haloumi cheese, okay lamb sausage, unremarkable olives, and forgettable tzatziki.  The fifth dish was a savory and toothsome dish of octopus in an herbed onion veggie sauce (more of the sourdough bread would have been better than the pita for soaking it up; just sayin').

        Finally, for dessert we tried the day's special of hazelnut (?) cake with some butterscotchy pudding and some ice cream.  It was okay, but didn't really work for me.  

        Overall, tasty food, good service, fair prices, and pleasant ambiance (and it's a BYOB).

        Like half of Philly, brunch is my thing and I am a brunch snob. So I don't say this lightly: Kanella has arguably one of the best brunches in Philly.

        The Cypress breakfast is downright decadent: Eggs fried in olive oil, with a crusty bread to soak up the yolks, a salty fried cheese and slice of meat, a garnish of greek salad. The French press coffee is smooth and perfect, and the Greek coffee is an experience if you're used to American watered-down coffee (read: it's tiny, and thick - I ordered medium sweet and it was just right for my taste). If you like bacon, or have jumped on the BACON EVERYTHING trend, order it. It's thickly cut, fatty, and amazing.

        But what makes Kanella one of the best is not just the food - though, clearly, I think it rates - but the combination of food, service, and atmosphere. Kanella isn't a kitchy joint. The decor is simple, airy, and comfortable, and sitting inside feels like a lazy weekend morning, even when the place is packed and the servers are constantly back and forth from the kitchen. And the servers! They can obviously handle the rush and the somewhat tight arrangement of tables, maintaining grace and humor the whole way. I've experienced nothing but the best service from Kanella.

        As if all of that wasn't enough, the entire experience is topped off by one very useful fact: they take brunch reservations.

        Kanella looks very basic from the outside. It's got a big blue awning and it's on the corner of 10th and spruce right across from Varga bar.

        I went around 8:45 the day after valentines day and the place was packed. When you walk inside the place looks like an Italian family dining room. It's got closely spaced tables and copper cooking wear hung on the walls. Very non-pretentious for the food prices. It reminds me of hole in the wall italian restaurants in Rome surrounding the piazzas.

        The hostess was very friendly and seemed happy to be working.

        Once we were seated our server carefully explained all the specials and ingredients that were in each plate. He was very articulate and particularly passionate about the food.

        Based on other yelp reviews I decided to go with the shotgun approach and order a bunch of appetizer plates tapas style.

        I am glad I did because we were able to try a plethora of their signature dishes without getting too full on any one item.

        We got the dips of the day, cyprus plate, lamb pieces with hummus and arugula, vegetable soup with cheese cubes (special), and the Greek salad.

        Everything was fresh and carefully seasoned but my favorite combo was putting the lamb/hummus/arugula combo on top of the pita pieces and mixing in a bit of the beet mash on top with a touch of tahini sauce drizzled on top (I used the one that came with the bread).

        This combo was amazing and I highly recommend you try it. I suggested that they should even make it into its own dish.

        The vegetable soup was also superb and had the consistency of split pea soup. It also had chunks of roasted halloumi cheese.

        In the end, my waiter and I decided that we should cook together sometime.

        The experience was first-class.

        a philly friend made a reservation for brunch at this quaint restaurant, decorated with wood furniture and the blue and white of the greek flag. while i may have been surprised at the idea of a greek place whose forte is brunch, it took but one glance at the menu for any reservations i had to evaporate.

        there was the shakshouka, two poached eggs floating in a cumin-tomato stew; the kedergee, boiled eggs in a nest of spiced rice and smoked fish; and the special of the day, an omelette with mixed mushrooms, spinach, and a little greek yogurt for creaminess. i opted for what turned out to be their most popular dish - the cyprus breakfast, which is two eggs fried in olive oil, lounza (greek ham), peperonata, and the taste worm i can't get out of my head (read my nando's peri peri review!) - halloumi cheese. honestly, the lounza was nothing special to me, but the eggs, peperonta, and halloumi were one of the best breakfasts i've ever had. kanella is BYOB, so we came prepared with bottles of champagne, and were provided with small pitchers of freshly squeezed orange juice.

        the only downside to kanella is the size - small. our reservations were definitely necessary, and at the end of the meal, as we were finished eating and just drinking, they actually asked us to leave so they could prepare for an upcoming reservation. now, chugging my mimosa isn't the most ladylike thing i did that day, but i get it - they're super popular because the food is really good!

      Specialties

      We specialise in cooking from the homeland of chef Konstantinos Pitsillides. Pitsillides hails from the island of Cyprus, an indipendant Greek island located in the very east of the Mediterranean. The Cyprus kitchen has many influences but can perhaps best be considered as part of the Levant. At home in Cyprus, as in his Philly kitchen freshness, simplicity, flavour and love are the standard ingrediants. Chef takes his French culinary training and applies it to the traditional recipes of his homeland. Homemade desserts, head to tail cooking, lemon and olive oil are all part and parcel of this neighbourhood joint.

      History

      Established in 2008.

      Kanella is made up of a passionate bunch of characters from the chef on down. Being a local restaurant filled with the family spirit much of the staff have been there since we opened. Though Chef Pitsillides is oft misunderstood (and yes sometimes this is exaserbated by himself), the intent of the restaurant is a simple one... We have aimed to be a neighbourhood venue for good, honest and consitstant cooking. A place that you can afford to come to regularly and have a good meal, good service, a nice bottle of wine, and enjoy your company. We have been blessed with the neighborhood we are in and all our wonderfull customers, so many of which we have known for sometime now. We look forward to continued joyful cooking and eating!
