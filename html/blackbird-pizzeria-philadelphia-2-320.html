    Listed in Vegan Jamz in Philly and Region, 6 Stars, Quintessential Philly, Pizza in Philly (and region): Some places are somewhat adequate!, Five Favorite Locally Owned Philadelphia Businesses

    What can be said about Blackbird that hasn't before?

    Apparently something because I'm updating my review.

    First and foremost: this place is not healthy. It is similar to the beloved now-defunct Brooklyn vegan restaurant Foodswings in that it offers awesome taste from some very unhealthy food.

    Having established that, I recently tried some items from their menu for the first time and have to share:

    Cheese fries: Just like Foodswings used to have! Very tasty. Not overly cheesy.

    The Ringer: Finally, they have a sandwich that fills the void on the menu left by the departure of the Chickpea Parmesan. This is a great take on that sandwich. Another one of the best sandwiches in the city coming from Blackbird!

    The Cheesesteak: Their new cheese whiz is fantastic. Great caramelized onions. I think this might be the best cheesesteak in Philly!

    It should be noted that Blackbird makes its own bread now. To die for!!!

    The Patate Pizza: It finally happened: I found something at Blackbird I don't like. My wife ordered it and I tried some. Not good. Just a bunch of grease. It did not work.

    The Popeye: Surprisingly good. Blackbird makes this wonderful pumpkin seed pesto which shines here.

    If you're in weight-loss mode, avoid Blackbird. Otherwise, enjoy one of the gems of Philadelphia!

    I have a new favorite pizza joint!

    It is a shame it opened now and not in the mid 00s when I lived…     I have a new favorite pizza joint!

    It is a shame it opened now and not in the mid 00s when I lived in walking distance. Back in the day there was an establishment there called Gianna's. I liked Gianna's. Their eggplant parmesan pizza was fantastic. They were Vegan friendly. Hip, somewhat grimy storefront. A highlight of living in the area.

    Fast forward to 2012. Long since moved to Glenside and I've finally realized that I have to cut back drastically on dairy and entirely on meat. So I thought my pizza days were done. Then my wife, Erin M. of Yelp fame, told me about Blackbird. I had to try and finally did when I had a day off on the Friday of President's Day weekend.

    First impression: the storefront is like Gianna's just a tad of spiffed up. The people working there were very friendly. The pies look like pizza. I ordered a slice of veggie supreme and a slice of "sausage." The slices are huge. I would order one next time. Maybe try one of their Vegan sides with it.

    The taste is great. Its a great alternative to pizza. Its not pizza. The crust is the same. The cheese is what is different. Its not pretending to be cheese and has a different texture/gooey-ness. The taste is slightly different as well but still very pleasing.

    When it comes to pizza, this is the way to go. I just need to start pestering them about opening location number two in walking distance to my house.     Read more
