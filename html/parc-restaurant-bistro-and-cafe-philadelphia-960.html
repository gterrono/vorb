    Every time we visit Parc, the Mister remarks that it reminds him of the Titanic.  I reply that I hope that the Titanic's denouement does not befall this lovely bistro.  (LOL) Parc is at the top  of "our places" list, and we can't visit too often.   We love being transported to 1920's Paris, without having to board a plane.  The aged mirrored surfaces, L-shaped zinc bar, tile floor and white-coated bartenders all add to the ambiance.  Meanwhile, the beautiful golden glow of the lighting and walls can make almost anyone look like they are ready for their close-up.    Indeed, it is such a favorite that our anniversary ritual is to meet our dear friends at Parc for a couple of cocktails al fresco before heading over to another Steven Starr restaurant for dinner.  Great people watching here, too, both inside and out.  

    Unsurprisingly, the service has been impeccable during each of our visits.  Because our experience here has been limited to the most excellent cocktails (especially the Romarin) and apps, I am withholding the fifth star until we have a chance to explore more of the menu.

    There is no doubt that I feel entirely confident giving Parc all five of its deserved stars. Cuisine, cocktails, and décor are top-notch all the way, every day. I've visited this establishment perhaps an alarming (!) amount of times, but every visit leaves me and my companions happy!

    Breakfast options include sophisticated takes on classics--the oatmeal, eggs & soldiers, and yogurt parfait (who doesn't love parfait?) are perfect bites to start your day off right. Additionally, the brunch offerings are decadent and sooooo worth the caloric intake. The coffee is always sublime and the staff is always knowledgeable and friendly. Their bellinis are a tasty way to start a celebratory brunch too!

    Lunch is great for people watching and catching up with friends and family, especially since it's just a few blocks from my mother's office! We have shared many happy memories over delicious offerings. If you're feeling ambitious and famished, the burger is incredible, as are the various sandwich offerings and ALWAYS the warm shrimp salad.

    Finally, dinner is an experience not to be missed! Beautiful entrées comprised of classic french dishes, such as the moules frites or the half roast chicken, are wonderful ways to spend an evening. One of my close friends is a decisively critical eater when it comes to French food, mostly because he's so well versed thanks to the French side of his family. Nonetheless, even his discerning palate was truly impressed by Parc's offerings!

    Don't miss dessert here either! The menu offerings are excellent, like profiteroles, or the house-made macarons that are a secret off-the-menu option (not anymore!). Just ask your server for them, plus they are also available to go.

    This beautiful restaurant always takes us back to our engagement in Paris and is a location full of happy memories from our excessive visits! Can't wait to come back soon :)

    I treated my BFF to a fancy brunch here on a recent Saturday afternoon to kick off her bachelorette festivities. Had to get her out of the apartment so other bridesmaids could set up copious amounts of penis paraphernalia and pin the junk on the hunk games.

    But we're classy broads, so we had a lovely time enjoying a Saturday brunch at Parc! We gave the hostess a bit of a run-around with the constantly changing size of our party but they handled it like pros. Typical of a Stephen Starr restaurant, service was impeccable the entire time.

    We started off with the complimentary bread basket, which is one of the best free bread baskets I've ever received and we asked them to box up the leftovers in order to supply appropriate booze-cushion for the rest of our day. We each got a French 75 cocktail, possibly my favourite cocktail of all time, and they were delicious. I liked the touch of adding a sugar cube at the bottom. We had 2 each and were pleasantly tipsy by the end of brunch.

    Being that we were feeling celebratory and both rarely eat rich foods, we split an order of the mac and cheese for an appetizer. Holy cow. It was freaking fantastic. I'd be hard-pressed to name a place in the city that does a better mac and cheese rendition. We barely ate half of it, and took the rest to go.

    For my entree I went with the asparagus and poached eggs, with a side of sausage. She got the brioche french toast, which was seriously epic in proportions and deliciousness. My breakfast was just the right amount of food, loved the grilled baguette to sop up the yolk and sauce, and the side order of sausage was substantial. It could have been enough for two, which is nice because it was pretty pricey. She got a side of bacon, which was also quite large. If you come for brunch, try to find someone to split your side-order of meat with, it's a lot. She was very happy with her french toast, although the enormous quenelle of pecan butter on top was a little unnecessary. It was a LOT of butter for one plate.

    The meal was delicious all around, but incredibly pricey. It was about $115 including tip on a meal for 2 before dinner time, which is kind of nuts. I was prepared for it to be expensive, and the drinks added a lot to the tab, but daaamn. However for a special occasion like this, it was totally worth it. Come for a splurge, or when someone else is paying!

Specialties

This stylish addition to Rittenhouse Square pays tribute to French cafe culture, serving breakfast, lunch and dinner seven days a week. Ideal for a quick bite or a leisurely meal, the menu is filled with classics: onion soup, escargots, steak frites and towering plateaus de fruits de mer. The baguettes are made in-house and  the wine list is expansive. Sip a Kir Royale at the zinc bar or enjoy a cafe au lait while basking in the sun.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
