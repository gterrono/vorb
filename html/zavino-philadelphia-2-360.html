    Ambiance: 4. Small, cool mini-bar vibe.  Somewhat dark and a little above "moderate" noise, which was a nice feel on a cold winter night.  Felt like it could be a jazz bar --

    Cost: 3. Actually not 100% sure on this (not from area), but pizzas seem reasonable priced in low-to-mid-teens.

    Food Quality: 4. Zavino lies in the Northeast corridor "pizza belt" and doesn't not disappoint.  I had the Polpettini (veal & ricotta meatball is the key topping), but the Diavolo and some meat boards were around and looked awesome.  Pizzas come out fast which is certainly nice if you're there for a quick happy hour or snack.

    Service: 4. Very prompt, not pushy. Can't ask for much more.  Warning though they do not take Amex.

    When to return: Happy hour when you want one a bit different. Quick bite to eat. Pizza/meat board craving. Lower key date night.

    I came here while I was visiting Philly and I have to say I absolutely loved this restaurant! It's very unassuming and casual. The menu is simple - pizza, pastas, salads and small plates.

    I had missed lunch because of a busy afternoon and came here for an early dinner. The waitress was friendly and she quickly sat me down and gave me the menu. I ask her what could be made vegan and gluten free in the pizza options and she mentioned that they could do the Kennett without the béchamel and replace it with red sauce. The Kennett comes with crimini, oyster and shiitake mushrooms, roasted onions and rosemary.

    The service was so quick! Within 10 minutes of ordering my pizza - it was fresh and ready on my table. This was by far the best gluten free pizza I have ever had. And I have tried plenty of places - the gluten free pizzas typically are thin but something you just want a thick crust. And this was exactly that! The toppings were a great matching with the base.

    My one word of caution is to eat the gluten free pizza fresh here rather than taking out and reheating. It just doesn't taste the same.

    I would definitely come here again and recommend others to come here as well. The atmosphere is quiet and relaxed and it's a great place to enjoy a delicious meal with friends and family. My only question to them is - when are you opening up in NYC??

    Listed in Strive for Five, Philly Phavorites

    Anytime I'm in Center City during happy hours (5-7pm), I'm drawn to the intersection of 13th and Sansom, a serious treasure trove of discounted drinks and delectable bites.  Although there's quite a bit to choose from, Zavino still stands out, offering the best pizza and wine on the block.

    Tuesday evening was warm and sunny, so I originally thought my boyfriend and I would hang out in Graffiti Bar, the open-air backyard of Sampan.  By the time we got there, almost 6pm, the space was full, and so we settled in at the bar at Zavino. Huge plus - even though we were on the later side for happy hour, there were still open seats at the bar.  We sat down by the open oven, which was warm, but pretty cool to see your pizza cooked right in front of your.  For $5, I got the featured wine, a sweet white blend, and my guy ordered a $3 draft - not a bad deal.  We first ordered only one of the $8 margherita specials (regularly $14), but ended up ordering a second - it was just too good. The dough is light and crispy, the basil fresh and flavorful - making myself hungry by writing about it.

    If you're craving great pizza in Center City, this spot is worth a try - bonus if you get there during happy hour and take advantage of the discounts!

    Pizza and wine? What could be a more perfect combination! I've walked by this spot many times and Zavino is always packed. I rushed over after work to try and snag a table since this spot is ridiculously tiny inside with 4 indoor tables and 3-4 outside. There are stools at the bar also, but beware the restaurant is narrow and not suitable for groups of more than 4.
    Happy hour is Monday-Friday, 4:30-6:30 pm. Featured specials are $3 select draft beers, $5 select wines by the glass and $8 margherita pizza. They had a pinot grigio, chardonnay, pinot noir, and merlot special when I stopped by.

    We tried the margherita pizza and fettuccine. I was impressed to hear that pasta was made in house and spying on other tables I noticed the gnocchi also looked extremely appetizing. The pizza came out quickly and had a nice bubbly crust from the wood fired oven. I loved the chunkier sauce with crushed tomatoes, fresh mozzarella, and shredded basil. Normally the margherita pizza is $13 which is still a decent price, but keep in mind the pizzas are thin crust and more of a personal size (ideal for 1-2 people). The smaller size is nice for sharing, but you need more than one pie to fill you up. Prices range from $13-16 which I think may be steep considering the atmosphere of the restaurant, but most patrons probably come for the drinks and not the food.
    I do applaud them for having some unique pizzas like The Stache pizza (whole wheat crust, pistachio pesto, mozzarella, parmesan, lemon vinaigrette, baby arugula), The Kennett pizza (oven roasted crimini, shiitake and oyster mushrooms, mozzarella, béchamel, roasted onions, rosemary), and The Garden pizza (chef's seasonal inspiration).

    Overall I would love to come back and try some small plates and salads. After all, good wine goes well with more than just pizza :)
