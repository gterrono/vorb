    Listed in My Neighborhood

    Over the last few years, Circles has come a long way.  They've gone from quick-n-dirty (in a good way) takeout place to full-on dine in experience, and I have to say, I like it.

    Don't let the neighborhood fool you, Circles isn't located along a business district, restaurant row or hotspot central.  They're the little engine that could, that neighborhood secret that's whispered from transplant to transplant like that hot local band.  That is, if it's possible for a band to taste like spicy curry.

    But I digress, Circles is like a secret wrapped in a surprise inside a foodgasm.  I've gone there for brunch, dinner and takeout late at night.  I've never been disappointed.

    One thing I've always noticed, is the food there tastes fresh and "clean."  By clean, I mean that in a lot of restaurants the food tastes like the oil it was cooked in.  Here, everything seems made to order, like in much more upscale places, but at very decent prices.  It seems to bridge that gap between cheap, "fast" asian food and the pricy exotic kind.

    Their dumplings are perfectly chewy, the pad thai & the drunken noodle dishes sometimes tend to go light on actual noodles and heavy on the veggies, and other times you get the opposite, but that little inconsistency isn't really a problem because they're A+ on flavor.  I can't comment on the vegetarian dishes, but they seem to have an alternative dish for almost everything available.  Every time we've eaten in, service has been excellent.  Occasionally the chef will make a visit to your table to ask about the food, and it's great to see people who care enough about their product to ask.

    If you aren't that adventurous, they have a selection of no-so-thai food that's fairly americanized without compromising creativity or flavor, such as thai-bbq wings, cheesesteak spring rolls and a variety of soups and rice dishes that can be altered according to taste.  They're all perfect after a few drinks, if you know what I mean.  Personally, I'm a hot food wimp and prefer the "medium" flavor range.  Let me say they nailed it, and I got exactly what I was expecting.

    They recently started serving brunch on the weekends and their peanut butter french toast will be something you'll fondly dream about many days after eating it.  That is, if their fantastic thai coffee isn't keeping you awake.

    At only a 10 minute walk from the Passyunk corridor, if you're in the area and have a hankering for something other than pizza, cheesesteaks and overall south philly culinary nastiness, you owe it to yourself to try this place.

    I love Asian food, okay? And between myself, who waitressed at Chinese and Thai restaurants in college and my boyfriend, who studied abroad in Beijing, we eat a lot of it.

    A friend met me at Circles this past Friday night for dinner at 7:30. I had heard amazing things via Yelp- that it was South Philly's favorite take-out spot and the establishment had recently opened a dining room. I hopped on the bus with a half-empty bottle of wine (classy, right?) and kept my eyes peeled for Circles.

    The place was empty. Strange, I thought, for a Friday night. It didn't scare me though- I knew the food would be good. And boy, was it.

    We started with summer rolls. Three fatties stuffed with fresh ingredients and wrapped in perfectly chewy rice paper. Drunken noodles with chicken and panang curry with tofu (ordered "white girl spicy") followed shortly after. They were the BEST drunken noodles I've had in Philadelphia, hands down- chock full of veggies and not too greasy. The panang was also excellent, though I wish they used string beans instead of peas.     Suddenly it was 10pm and my friend and I were still finishing our wine. Oops! We were the ONLY customers in the restaurant for two and a half hours. I felt terrible- they could have closed early if we had left shortly after eating. The owner was super friendly and send us home with take-out menus and 15% off coupons.

    Go to Circles, now! We gotta keep this place open! If you're lucky enough to live in the Queen Village area, you might find a door hanger flyer with a $10 off voucher. Love those coups.

    I've been ordering from Circles since I moved to South Philly in January, and the frequency has increased -- is it me, or do they keep stepping up their game? From my first so-so spring rolls to a great meal in their dining room the other day, I have become a convert.

    One big improvement in recent months is the addition of a nice little dining room. We ate in for the first time last week, and oh boy, it was even better than I've remembered it. The papaya salad is so, so good -- crisp, acidic and a bit spicy. I've also had great curries with tofu -- the one with pineapple is particularly delicious -- and they are happy to modulate the spice for you. As in, they will actually make it spice if you legitimately want it to be. Thank goodness.

    After an unfortunate incident with bland Pad See Ew in January, I was afraid to try it again, but recently I took the plunge, and it was perfect -- sweet, spicy, sour, savory, with toothsome noodles that stopped short of being chewy. YUM.

    I feel disloyal to Vien Tiane, but this is now my go-to spot for Southeast Asian food in Philly. It's also BYO, and the servers are really nice!

    The thin slices of chicken were slightly tinged with flecks of wok-y char... the wide noodles too grabbed the taste. It's all the difference in the world! Circles pulled off a very very decent Pad kee Mao. Plenty of my favorite Thai basil throughout...and bell peppers and chiles.

    I appreciate that Circles heeded my semi-desperate pleading to make my dishes, "Thai hot... Really, Thai hot. Extra, extra, OK?" They sent me some extra nam pla on the side (Thai bird chiles + fish sauce), as I had requested.  I like a little explosion here and there of added bright chile heat and pungent salt on those drunken noodles. So ummm...are you picking up that the Pad Kee Mao was enjoyed? It was.

    We also split the pumpkin curry which was a tasty plate. It's really filling; dense and creamy pumpkin chunks are suspended in rich, sweet and spicy, velvety curry. Husband was crazy about this curry. I liked, he loved.
