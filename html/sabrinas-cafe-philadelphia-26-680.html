    This is Such an adorably quaint cafe! With it's original hardwood floors and finishings in tact along with unique wall Hangings, the vibe is welcoming and homey. I also really dig their music that plays softly in the background.

    The food is delicious as well. The stuffed
    French toast is decadent and filling and even the hungriest of diners would probably find it to be a challenge to eat the whole
    Thing. The egg dishes are especially tasty and the potatoes are really unique- not dripping with grease, yet perfectly crisp on the outside.

    We really enjoyed our breakfast on a cold, rainy weekday morning where there were hardly any other diners. I am
    Not sure I would wait several hours to have breakfast here on a weekend. Oh, and parking is always a challenge in this part
    Of town, no matter what day of the week or time of the day you are visiting so be prepared.

    Plenty of folks love Sabrina's, so I'm sure they won't miss me as I go on break from them for a while. I've always avoided the place on weekends because of the crazy waits, but I've occasionally hit them up for a midweek breakfast or lunch and it's always been pretty good.

When the plate arrived, the size of the frittata was laughably huge. I ate about two thirds of it and was absolutely stuffed. I didn't make it far into my salad, however, because I found a plastic tab (the kind that you'd find on a bag of bread) mixed in. Stuff happens, I didn't think it was that big of a deal, but I did mention it to our server. The plate was removed from our check which I thought was a nice move on their part.

    Personally, I'm more interested in quality over quantity and I don't really feel like I get that at Sabrina's. The food is okay but I feel like they rely on their gargantuan serving sizes more than the taste and quality of the ingredients. I'm off to explore other options for a while.
