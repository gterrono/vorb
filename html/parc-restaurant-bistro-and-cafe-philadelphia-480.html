    This is probably my new favorite restaurant in the area. I would've given them 5 stars except my entree came out luke warm. I think they planned poorly and mine was finished before my sister's was and they came out at the same time. Otherwise, the food and experience was great.

    I've been here in the past and it was definitely a 3 - 4 star experience then, but yesterday the food was just that great. Maybe I just ordered superbly, so maybe I should get that last star. I'm a super star.

    Anyway, the complementary bread is great. No complaints. You get three times, some type of cranberry nut bread, a thicker moist brownish wheat or something bread, and a hard sourdough style bread. All great for dipping if you order an appetizer or main course with dippableness.

    For my appetizer, I ordered the escargot. It was probably the best pot of snails I've ever had. Generally when you order snails, they come drenched in heavy oily butter and/or cheese, which kind of leaves you feeling a bit disgusting. The snails here came in a lighter hazelnut butter with a lot of garlic and three slices of croutonish hardened garlic bread. The snails were extremely tender and the butter flavorful without being heavy. It ended up being heavy only because I took the previously mentioned table bread and started sopping up the butter sauce. I felt so disgusting afterwards, it was marvelous.

    By the time my entree came out, I was pretty heavily gorged on bread and garlic butter sauce. Like I mentioned at the start, my beef short ribs came luke warm. It was off the bone already, but the meat was tender and flavorful, perfectly cooked. The mashed potatoes and compliments were all good as well.

    My sister had the steak frittes, medium rare. This was cooked perfectly also and had a great garlic butter type of spread on top. The flavor was delicious. The side that accompanied it was fries with some sort of dipping sauce which worked out well also.

    All in all, the food was amazing, the experience was great, the problem was just the luke warm entree. I didn't care enough to send it back, im sure they would've heated it up, but I was so full I only ate half anyway.

    My husband and I went to Parc for the first time this past Wednesday for our wedding anniversary. We could not have been happier with the place!

    I arrived before my husband (I work in the city and he was driving down and having trouble finding parking!), and they allowed me to be seated without him; it was nice to be seated after walking over and not have to stand and wait for him to arrive.

    Our waiter, Patrick, introduced himself immediately, poured me a glass of water, and asked if I would like a cocktail while I waited for my husband to arrive. He recommended their very popular Basilic and the Romarin, and I decided on the Romarin; it was deliciously tart, sweet, and fruity all at once and I thoroughly enjoyed it. My husband ordered a Sazerac when he arrived, which he said was very good, too.

    To start our meal, we ordered 2 appetizers: the Tuna Carpaccio and the Escargots. We were blown away! The tuna is pounded so thin it boggles the mind that it doesn't tear (I would bet you could see through it if you ever held it up), and it's dressed/marinated in the most deliciously light and complementary vinaigrette and garlic; a slice folded onto a piece of the crostini it comes with is perfection. And the escargot, oh my! So tender and melt-in-your-mouth; I would have tried escargots a long time ago if I'd known it would taste like this! The hazelnut butter/garlic sauce they come in is absolutely heavenly; warm and comforting with that bite of garlic, I could easily have made a meal of this sauce in its cute little cast iron pot with a loaf of French baguette.

    For our entrees, my husband ordered the Steak Au Poivre done medium rare with a side of the Cauliflower Gratin rather than the Garlic Spinach; the steak was done perfectly and certainly lived up to its name of being well peppered! The cauliflower was warm and tender with a deliciously melty layer of cheese on top, but we both picked up a bit of a horseradish taste, and I for one do not like horseradish, so I'd pass on this side for future orders. I ordered the Wednesday Specialty du Jour, Lobster Americane. I could not recall what Patrick told me when into the saucing of this dish, but you get the lobster tail and claw meat in nice chunks over top nice fat flat noodles and sprinkled liberally with nice firm cooked peas (nothing even remotely mushy about these peas!). The sauce was rich, creamy, and buttery, all the other flavors melding and hinting at their presences. The lobster was fresh and firm and done perfectly in this saucy pasta dish.

    During the meal I ordered a second cocktail, the Basilic this time, and loved it! It's refreshing, herby, and a little effervescent; the muddled cucumber and basil create the most wonderful aroma that floats up the back of you throat and settles in your nasal passages with each sip.

    For dessert we split the Raspberry Chocolate Tart, with it's crumbly cookie-like crust and decadently dense raspberry chocolate middle, with 3 wonderful fresh raspberries on top! This with a cup of Cafe au Lait, and my night was complete!

    While the tab for a meal here might be steep, I would not hesitate to return for other special occasions and celebrations. Not only are you enjoying expertly prepared dishes, but the entire atmosphere and the staff create the most perfect experience that you simply can't replicate at locations that lack the acute attention to detail Parc has. Patrick seems to thoroughly enjoy his interactions with the guests, and more than once we received attention from the manager as he made a point of interacting with the patrons and ensuring all was well.

Specialties

This stylish addition to Rittenhouse Square pays tribute to French cafe culture, serving breakfast, lunch and dinner seven days a week. Ideal for a quick bite or a leisurely meal, the menu is filled with classics: onion soup, escargots, steak frites and towering plateaus de fruits de mer. The baguettes are made in-house and  the wine list is expansive. Sip a Kir Royale at the zinc bar or enjoy a cafe au lait while basking in the sun.
