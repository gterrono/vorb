    Devil's Alley I thought to be nothing special and out of the ordinary. I come in on a saturday night with a party of 10 and getting a table wasn't too much trouble which was nice. I do enjoy the trees in the middle of the restaurant, you don't really see that in a restaurant often.

    So I ordered the devil burger (1/2 of angus beef, topped with onion rings and fries) medium rare. Now when the burger came out, the presentation had that "wow" factor much like PYT burgers and I didn't know where to start. The temperature of the meat was a little more on the well done side. I did take notice that the brioche bun did not lose it's integrity and kept it's shape, however that could be due to the meat being on the dry side. The fries weren't too great but the cheese sauce was pretty good with them. The onion rings were nothing special and I was expecting overall the burger with a higher standard.

    Would I go back? Sure, but not for the burger.

    Devil's Alley is a great "all-purpose" bar/pub/restaurant.  Its got something for everyone, which is why I usually recommend this bar for out-of-towners.  They have a great drink menu, from unique cocktails, to sangria, to craft beers on tap.  The food also never lets you down.  There are many choices of bar munchies, finger foods, or full meals.  The quesadillas are great, the guacamole is pretty good!  The ribs are fantastic, nice sweet BBQ sauce on the ribs.  The mac-n-cheese is really cheezy!! I love that.  The pulled pork sandwich is probably one of the best I've had in a long time... its juicy, sweet, and stacked with pulled pork.  The side of coleslaw is great when you pour it on top the pork!  The nachos are great, loaded with cheese whiz.  

    The service is always good, very happy waitresses and bartenders, they are accommodating with large groups also.  I love the upstairs bar the best, there is more room and I love sitting at the table tops near the wide open windows when the weather is nice out.  

    Go here, they also have Happy Hour specials.  All around great bar with a great, fun, open atmosphere.  I recommend this place all the time to people who don't know where to go in Philly.

Later she got a pomegranite sangria and mine was something goofy that came in a feminine glass. Was happy to be seated upstairs and away from judging eyes.

    Shortly after placing our second drink order, our third course showed up. I was 3/4ths  full by now, which is unusual, but I soldiered on. I got the swordfish, which didnt taste smoked like it was described, but definitely was grilled. Maybe a little overcooked, but it had nice flavor, was flaky, and not too dry. The rest was described as "Fingerling potato, leak & cured pork belly hash, bourbon Dijon beurre blanc, fried shallots" The bourbon dijon was a nice sauce around the whole plate, and the cured pork belly really heightened the hash. Wish I wasnt so full for this.

    My girlfriend was sort of disappointed with her duck breast. A braised short rib girl through and through, she got burned for being adventurous. The duck didnt have a sauce and was kind of rubbery, and the brussels that came with it werent crisp. The maple bacon glaze was tasty as was the celery root puree.

    By the time dessert arrived I wasnt hungry in the least. She picked at her triple layer chocolate cake, which was plated nicely but was really just a thin piece of cake on its side. We poth enjoyed the anjou pear tart, which tasted almost like an apple fritter. The gorgonzola ice cream was really good and wish they served it by the pint as take out. There was no cheesecake offering on the menu like the website said. Fucking liars.

    Overall the pacing may have led to my fullness by the main course, but I thought service overall was good and the place has a nice atmosphere. None of the stuff I mentioned is really on the regular menu, so we might have to go back and check that out.
