    Tried out Valanni's for the first time last week and I definitely enjoyed the experience.  The atmosphere is pretty cool - it seems somewhat  upscale yet is casual.  I had seen some reviews online claiming it to be "business casual" but I wore jeans and felt perfectly comfortable.

    It was a Tuesday evening and I had made reservations "just in case" but it was unnecessary - we showed up a half hour early and were seated immediately.  We were seated in the back corner and while there were many other diners, even those seated next to us didn't feel intrusive.  Our server was fantastic - very attentive, friendly and helpful.  We took a long time over drinks and reviewing the menu so, after coming over several times only to be turned around, he jokingly said to just give him "a look" when we were ready.  I really appreciated his not wanting to rush us yet still be attentive.

    For dinner we decided to order some tapas to share and he suggested 2-3 would be plenty - we were pretty hungry and everything on the menu looked good so we ordered four - fried calamari, trio of spreads, spanakopita and the caprese flatbread.  Everything was delicious.  And we definitely over-ordered and had leftovers, they are pretty generous with the tapas plates.

    I ordered dessert - I just have to say, if you come here save room for the oreo beignet.  The menu description doesn't even do it justice, it is pretty much a little bowl of fried heaven.  I would come back just for dessert.

    I would definitely recommend to a friend and can't wait to get back here again.

    Valanni just underwent a sort of face-lift, and seems to be having a bit of an identity crisis as a result. Most people I've spoken to know Valanni as an upscale restaurant with a sleek, modern but classy set-up. The new Valanni (or "Valanni Social", as the new signs indicate) is more of an upscale gay club/tapas bar. While there are still tables for dinner and such, the main draw now seems to be to the plush front room that faces Spruce Street, with white couches and a fancy changing-light wall. It also appears that there's a DJ there full-time pumping dance remixes into the restaurant.

    You won't find Valanni on any list of gay bars in Philadelphia but it's definitely become that -- moreso a place where you and friends can go for some moderately-priced cocktails before hitting up bars like Woody's or Tavern on Camac. I also can't really tell what the server situation is there. Some people seem to get waited on in the lounge area, others not.

From the moment we stepped into the restaurant, things went downhill. Since today is an unusually warm day in the city of Philadelphia, the restaurant was hot and stuffy. We were seated in the back, right next to the swinging kitchen doors. We eventually got settled and was greeted by our pleasant server. Two coffees, one tea, one iced tea, and one orange juice were ordered. I was highly disappointed when the server appeared with two cups of coffee with no French press in sight.  We asked the server what happened to the French press that used to be placed onto the table along with the coffee cups. We eventually found out that the restaurant had issues with patrons breaking the mechanism so there were not enough to go around.. Free refills were presented to us, after informing the server that our cups were empty. On the third refill, I was presented with tepid coffee that was not brewed properly.
    A friend of mine waited about five minutes for a tea box to be presented to her...that didn't contain breakfast tea! When asked for more hot tea, the server informed us that she would be charged another $3 for a tea bag. NOTE: Bring your own tea bags next time. Our friend, who ordered an iced tea, was presented with a $5 bottle of iced tea, not even freshly brewed. The Orange juice was less then stellar, which also came with a $5 price tag.  The glass was filled with ice with a splash of orange juice barely covering the juice.
    After waiting about 20 minutes for our food to arrive, and wondering if the kitchen staff had ventured out back to slaughter the cow to prepare the filet someone ordered, we received our food. The Huevos Rancheros was enjoyable, as was the filet, and the truffled provolone omelet. The cinnamon bun French toast was the topic of conversation at the table- and not in a good way. Two of my friends had ordered this "popular item" and was presented with two raw cinnamon buns topped with orange glaze and currants, swimming in a pool of syrup. If one did not have a cavity before, they would after consuming this monstrosity. The description of the cinnamon bun French toast was deceptive.  One of the plates were sent back, but not before the server telling us that we would need to talk to the manager first to see what she would say. Gordon Ramsay would've thrown a fit if he heard this! The customers' plate would be swept off the table with a flourish and immediately presented with a menu asking them to choose another item. The other plate of cinnamon French toast sat dejectedly on the table, untouched while another item was ordered.
    After asking for salt and pepper, I was presented with a dusty pepper mill and a tablespoon of salt in a creamer container. I was thoroughly confused. I think that Kitchen Kapers sells mini salt and pepper shakers for about $12 for a set of 6. Maybe Valanni would consider invested in some of those?
    The bill was presented to us with 20% gratuity added for a less than average experience at Valanni. I am sad to say that I will not step foot inside this restaurant again. We dappled with idea that maybe after our brunch at La Croix two weeks ago, we didn't give Valanni a fair trial.  After careful consideration, we decided that this wasn't the case. Devil's Alley, Mixto, and  Marathon would be other choices for brunch in the weeks to follow. Atleast they don't try to cover their costs for their new décor by serving tepid coffee, charging $3 for a tea bag and $5 for bottled iced tea and watered down store bought orange juice.
    I think the highlight of brunch was the company and the upbeat music! And I think it's safe to say that Gordon Ramsay would NOT be pleased with Valanni.

I also got the coffee, which they bring out in a french press. It was some of the best coffee I've had a restaurant in a long time, and I'm very particular about my coffee.     I had a couple, albeit minor, issues which prevented me from giving Valanni 5 stars. First, the server was not that great. Our waitress was pretty good, but she did not serve our food. Two slightly larger pet peeves of mine, and the first is admittedly me being a little over the top, is when the server cleared my tapas plate, which had my fork on it, he took the fork off and put it on the table, instead of just taking it and bringing me a fresh fork. History

Established in 2000.

Valanni restaurant opened its doors in September of 2000, introducing "Medi-Latin" cuisine to Philadelphia. Situated right off of the "Avenue of the Arts," Valanni has become a pre-theater ritual and place to meet for a night-cap for many who live in and around the neighborhood. The bar at Valanni keeps up with the creative cuisine by offering an assortment of sangria, mojitos and other specialty drinks. The kitchen is open until 1 AM daily, serving Tapas and a Mediterranean inspired lounge menu.
