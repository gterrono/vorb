    Listed in Fillerdelphia

    I dropped by Distrito for a quick power lunch while visiting the Penn campus.

    First thing that came to mind as I soaked in the hot-pink, faux-Mexican façade: "Damn, they didn't have *anything* like this when I was an undergrad."

    Second thing that came to mind once I scoped out the interior: "Damn, Jose Garces totally ripped off the look of El Vez.  Stephen Starr must be pissed."

    Needless to say, I had a little trepidation.  Most of it melted away when I took a seat at the long and comfortable bar and made some friendly smalltalk with the ridiculously good-looking bartender.  I then saw that Distrito offers a two-course lunch special (drink included!) for just $15.  It sounded like a good deal money-wise, but the food quality was decidedly mixed.

    To start, I had a bowl of sopa de calabaza (butternut squash soup with crab, pepitas, and crema).  This I liked a lot.  It was warm and comforting and satisfying - perfect on a bone-chillingly cold Philly day.

    For course #2, I gave the huarache de costillas (flatbread with short rib, queso, and radish) a try.  This dish wasn't good at all.  The flatbread was soaked in so much grease that the other flavors were indiscernible.  Also, the short rib was massively overcooked, tough, and stringy.

    Yeah, so.... Distrito is a solidly average spot.  That soup gives me hope for future improvement, though.

    Generally speaking, I had a good experience at Distrito. I went with my sister when I was visiting her in Philly and the two of us split the following dishes for Sunday brunch: salsa and guacamole, huevos rancheros, and chocolate torte.

    Overall, the food was good--nothing mindblowing (I've definitely had better huevos rancheros, for instance), but for someone who doesn't like brunch normally, I was impressed with the food.

    Distrito first caught my eye with the pink decor and the car table in the first floor window. I also love the seating on the second floor, and the whole restaurant is buzzing but quiet enough.

    As for the food, the aesthetics are perfect, and the food tastes as delicious as it looks! Most items are on the small side, tapas-style, so make sure to order a good number if you want to leave full. In addition, the food is prepared well and comes out on cute platters. My favorites include guacamole, queso, chorizo, chicken tacos, and the shortrib flatbread!

    The service here isn't always the best, especially as they get pretty busy during dinner hours and private events.

    Definitely come for some sort of special--Taco Tuesday or Happy Hour. I didn't realize that the guac and tacos were the same size for the specials and regular hours (edit: I think they've now downsized for Happy Hour), but for about double the price. When I come out and split food with friends for Happy Hour, my meal comes up to around $20 including tax and tip, which is amazing--and so worth it!

    The review is for Taco Tuesday.

    I came to Taco Tuesday last, well, Tuesday for the second time in three weeks, and I have to say that I am in love. It's every week from 5pm-7pm, where they sell $2 and $5 guac and salsa.

    They offer three kinds of tacos: pollo (chicken), carnitas (carnitas with black beans and salsa), and hongos (wild mushrooms with potato). The carnitas was my favorite, but I love the combination of mushrooms and potatoes on the hongos--it gave a certain depth of flavor that (I thought at least) was slightly lacking from the pollo.

    We also ordered the guac and salsa. It was delicious! The chips are a wonderful texture. Not overly greasy, not overly dry. Just very crispy, practical, no-nonsense chips.

    After tax and tip, I paid $12 for the meal. Not bad for a Jose Garces!

    Mexican HEAVEN in University City. I'm so glad my boyfriend was feeling generous and suggested we go here for dinner last week (he doesn't like Mexican food).

    I thought Distrito might be crowded on a Tuesday since they have their weekly Taco Tuesday deal, but arriving at 5 pm we found ourselves in a relatively empty restaurant. We were seated immediately and greeted by an exceptionally friendly and enthusiastic server, Javas. He placed a bowl of complimentary spicy peanuts in the middle of our table (not before asking about potential allergies) and took our drink orders. Throughout the meal Javas was quick to offer suggestions and help us navigate our way through the substantial menu. Every dish was served promptly and came with an explanation about what we were about to eat. I think the easiest way to do this is in list form, so here's the run down of what we shared:

    -Guacamole and chips - Excellent guacamole. Ripe avocados obviously freshly mashed, topped with cotija cheese (which I love). We were told this was tapas size (like the rest of the menu) but I disagree... it was a huge portion... not that I'm complaining.
    -Carnitas tacos - Succulent, perfectly cooked pulled pork. I thought they could go a little heavier with the pineapple, though.
    -Carne Kobe tacos - The steak was cooked well but my boyfriend was NOT a fan of the tomato sauce. The crispy yukon fries on top was a nice idea but they were very bland.
    -Huarache de costillas- Short rib tacos... these were good but nothing really stood out about them.
    -Enchilada de pollo - Succulent chicken enchilada served in the cutest little cast iron skillet.
    -Platano y crema - I'm already a big fan of plantains but this was TO DIE FOR. The large plantain was lightly covered in a savory black bean sauce and drizzled with Mexican crema - The absolute perfect combination of sweet and savory.
    -Esquites - I also happen to be a big fan of Mexican street corn. This was served as a parfait in a glass. It was a little watery but the flavors were INCREDIBLE. My mouth is STILL watering thinking about this.
    -Kahlua coffee cheesecake - I barely had room for dessert but, since it was tapas size, I decided to push through. SO GLAD I did. The flavors of the cheesecake and ice cream accompaniment were absolutely out of this world. My boyfriend was laughing at how often I whispered "mmmm" and "OMG" while eating it.
    -Frozen lime margarita - Very refreshing! Strong flavor but not skimpy on the alcohol, either.

    This was easily my favorite meal I've eaten this year. I CANNOT WAIT to go back here and explore the rest of the menu.

    My family and I came here to celebrate a belated Father's Day.  Dad requested Mexican, and I was afraid of overdoing El Vez, so we headed to Distrito.  We were immediately seated upon our arrival (right across from the man himself, Jose Garces--there with his family and watching the World Cup) and promptly served water and told the specials.  The frozen margarita on our visit was peach ginger, and it was delicious.  More ginger than peach, but that was fine with me! No one will accuse the bartender here of going light on the tequila.  I could taste its salty goodness throughout the drink.  

    For our appetizers, the table the:

    Guacamole: This guacamole is one of the better ones.  It contained huge chunks of fresh avocado (but not too huge) and was fresh and light.  

    Salsa:  This was made with fresh, super bright, red tomatoes, jalapeno, garlic, cilantro, and onion.  The peppers gave it a kick and the fresh tomato cooled you down.  

    We had six people and this was enough for us to share.

    For my entree, I had the:

    Enchilada de Verduras: This vegetarian enchilada contains toasted quinoa, black beans, cooked kale and is topped with chipotle crema.  The crema adds a little spice and is incredibly rich and indulgent.  This is the first time I've tried this dish, but it is probably my favorite thing on the distrito menu.  It has a little bit of crunch as well, which adds a nice texture.  Someone I was with wanted a bite, but I refused to share.  I could not spare a square (inch) of this delicious enchilada.

    Pollo Tacos:  These come with chicken ropa vieja, queso fresco, crema and are topped with crisp matchstick radish to add some crunch (if not much flavor).  I nickname these tacos "Old Reliable." They are consistently good, if not exciting, cheap at $8, and filling.

    Note:  All enchiladas come in single serving portions.  You get one.  The tacos come in sets of three, so keep that in mind when you order.

    Everyone in my party was very pleased with their meal, and I will continue to make a trip over to University City to get my Distrito fix.

Specialties

Distrito is the new restaurant concept by Jose Garces, the chef behind the acclaimed Philadelphia restaurants Amada and Tinto. Distrito is a high-energy restaurant inspired by the spirited culture and cuisine of Mexico City. The Distrito menu features Chef Garces' fresh interpretations of classic Mexican fare and beverages include over 65 tequilas and margaritas made with fresh seasonal fruits. A striking decor includes a hot pink and bright green color scheme, a wall constructed from over 600 colorful, campy masks of lucha libre, or "free fight" professional wrestlers, and a booth crafted from a Volkswagen Beetle, a typical Mexico City taxi cab.

History

Established in 2008.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
