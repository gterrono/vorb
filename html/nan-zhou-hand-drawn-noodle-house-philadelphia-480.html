    This goes to the top of the list of the best cheap eats in Philly. This is a popular place for great noodles (especially the 'knife cut' noodles, which you could say is very thick and doughy, reminding me of a European style 'dumpling').

    Tell me what's better than rich broth with tender meat (my favorite is the ox-tail) and pickled vegetables. At around $6, it can't be beat.

    Folks should be aware that this place is pretty small. Also, this is a more 'Northern' style of Chinese food, quite different from the rest of Chinatown which is still pretty heavily Cantonese influences.

    These are the most amazing noodles EVER. I'm from NYC but have yet to find anything that even comes close here. Ever since my friend introduced me to Nan Zhou Noodle House four years ago, I insist that we come here every time I visit her in Philly. The noodles are my main motivation to go to Philly, but shhhh, don't tell my friend that =)

    They have two types of noodles, which they make at the restaurant: hand-drawn (like spaghetti) and shaven (wide flat noodles). I prefer the beef brisket noodle soup with shaven noodles. You can also add an fried egg into your soup, which is pretty tasty. The broth is so homey and delicious. They also have dry noodles but I think the soup is better. The proportions are HUGE so it's hard to even come close to finishing it. On top of that, it's SO cheap. If you're feeling adventurous, I would also recommend starting with the beef tendon on top of pickled radish.

    This place is a must try and worth the trip (from NY) to Philly. It's THAT good.

    Came here last night with the BF while trying to find a place that was in center city, open late, and cheap.  We strolled in around 10, with most of the wait staff eating dinner at the end of their shift but someone was nonetheless happy to serve us.  Based on tips and recommendations, we each opted for the Roast Duck hand drawn noodle soup and a shaved noodles with peanut sauce to split.

    The duck soup was really good: chewy yummy noodles in broth with juicy, flavorful pieces of duck that just fell off the bone.  Perhaps a bit too strong in the cilantro category, but still incredibly good nonetheless.  However, it didn't hold a candle to the peanut noodles.  Soft, chewy, tender, and bursting with flavor, these noodles were surprisingly filling and incredibly savory.  They tasted even better with the hot chili oil drizzled on top, and is hands down one of the best things I've eaten in this city.

    And the best part, both of our dinners were less than $10 per person.  Cheap, filling, and incredibly delicious?  You bet your ass I'll be coming here again soon (but maybe try something other than the duck soup).

    Cold days and Nan Zhou soups - the French would call this un mariage parfait.  I am totally addicted to these quality noodles and their chewy, toothy consistency, be they in soups, sauces, or stir-fried.  

    Spicy Pig Ears is a must appetizers (don't give me that surprised "eek"!) - pretty, flowing ribbons doused in chili oil, resting on a bed of cool daikon radish salad - a spicy ode to porcus maximus, great in texture and taste.  Peanut noodles: must have a shaved noodle and hand drawn noodle version at the same time to appreciate the difference in texture.  Another appetizer I like is shredded kelp, with its taste reminiscent of ocean.

    You can count on the familiar dishes like snow pea tips, spinach with garlic, etc. to be reliantly good but slurping more noodles from a big bowl full of one of the many different types of soups (beef, beef brisket, ox tail, roast pork and even more interesting ingredients for the more adventurous eaters) is a special pleasure.  Coming with a group of friends allows for tasting many different dishes.  The bill will always be very low.

    Service is efficient and friendly (with some exceptions), the restaurant bright and comfortable.  Go try!
