    I have eaten at Peter McAndrews two restaurants, Modo Mio and Monsu, and loved them, so I had high hopes here. It certainly does not disappoint.

    The brisket sandwich is not at all what you'd expect. Definitely not bbq style. It is tender and flavorful and aided by the roasted tomatoes, sharp provolone and fried egg. Really enjoyable.

    But the star has to be the pork. A friend said it was the best in the city so I was skeptical. I think roast pork is the true Philadelphia sandwich. I will take a pork over a steak 99 times out of 100. I have always considered John's to be the best with honorable mention to Tony Luke's, DiNic's and Lenny's (in Conshohocken). But Paesano's certainly belongs at or near the top. It is kind of a cross between shredded and chopped pork, so there is little to no gristle to bite through. Add in some peppers and sharp provolone and you are in heaven. I've had dreams about this sandwich.

    If you haven't been, go. Go out of your way to get there if necessary because this place is a true joy.

Specialties

Sandwiches made with Italian inspiration and Philadelphia at-t-tude. Peter McAndrew's approach to gastronomy has to do first with full flavor, and then hospitality.  Sandwiches are the backbone of any cuisine, but here in Philly they represent the true bloodline of it's people.

History

Established in 2008.

The second concept from chef Peter McAndrew. Following the success of his Italian trattoria "Modo Mio," established in 2007, Paesano's represents authentic Italian cuisine, but made for Philly palettes.
