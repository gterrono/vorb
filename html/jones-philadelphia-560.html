    If I had to live on nothing but Southern eats for the rest of my life, I'd be perfectly content.

    Jones intrigued me for quite some time -- the storefront seemingly standing out whenever I'd walk by it. Finally, last Sunday, a friend and I decided to stop in for a late brunch. We saw people waiting outside, but since we were a small party, we were quickly seated.

    I had a perfect view of the kitchen from my chair. It was a curse. Despite the fact that we had been seated just three minutes before and hadn't even ordered yet, I wanted my food that instant. I don't think I've ever been as impatient at a restaurant than I was at Jones. It's amazing my friend didn't hurt her neck considering how many times I urged her to turn around/strain to look at plates being produced behind her.

    We decided to have a sober brunch, so I started with a Jones cream soda -- mostly because of the irony and the fact that I didn't think I had one since I was 13. Still good stuff.

    For entrees, my friend got the meatless cheese sandwich and ate every bite. She loved it. I got the fried chicken and fries basket, and despite a truly valiant effort, it looked like I didn't take a bite by the time I was finished with it. I'm fairly certain they had an entire chicken squished into that basket, sans feet (thank you for that, Jones) and head (also thank you for that, Jones). It also came with cole slaw, which was exceptional for when you're comparing it to other slaws.

    Needless to say, I'll be back. I could only fit so much chicken in my stomach on that day -- and there are so many other menu options still on my in-brain list to try!

    This is the restaurant I'd have if I opened a restaurant -- someplace warm, comfortable, and bright, which makes home cooking without a literal "greasy spoon" :-) .  I see Jones as an upscale diner serving good old American home cooking in a warm, comfortable setting.  The food is very good across the board, but especially the vegetables.  (Sorry, kids, but you really should try some!)  There's also a large menu of drinks, both alcoholic and nonalcoholic.  The atmosphere is set with caramel-colored woods, huge windows on two sides of the restaurant, high ceilings, and thick grey carpet in the middle section of the main floor and in the small upstairs dining area.  I also enjoyed the funky, artistic light fixtures.  The seating is on cushioned bench seats and what I thought of as somewhat old-fashioned (in a good way!) red barrel-type chairs. (I pictured the '60s and '70s eras for the chairs.)  There is plenty  of seating on the main level, and there are several additional two-person booths upstairs that overlook the rest of the restaurant.  The staff is warm and helpful.  (At least the wait staff -- the hostesses when I was there didn't seem as warm as at most places.)  The one hiccup was that the unisex bathrooms -- I think there are three individual ones -- have a regular line of waiting 'customers,' so plan your needs accordingly.  

    I will find my way here again!

    My friend and I went to Jones for brunch on a beautiful Sunday. Although it was during Father's Day lunch time, the restaurant does not seem overly crowded. The interior decoration captured my attention soon as I entered the restaurant. Jones provides three types of seating options: 2 person table, 4 person booth and group booth.

    Food -
    I ordered chicken with waffles and a side of tater tots. Unfortunately, the quality of the dish did not meet the standard that all these 4 & 5 stars reviews have set. When my food came out, it was 'simple' looking. Two pieces of fried chicken with half of a waffle and a side of gravy. The gravy was in room temperature, waffle was slightly soggy (before I put the gravy on) and the fried chicken was dry and in room temperature. Note that the even the dark meat portion was dry too. All of these indication made me think that my dish was sitting around for a little while before they brought it out.

    My friend ordered an ALT sandwich. There's no way that server was waiting for her order to complete before she brought both dishes to our table.  

    Drinks -
    My friend and I both chose the bottomless bellini option, $21 per person. We got a bottle of house selected champagne and three flavors of mixers - White peach(decent), mango(delicious) and orange juice(neither of us used it). The mixers came in a mini mason jar which was really cute! I'd say if you have a few hours to spare and a good company to chat with, this is your option.

    Definitely will try their bloody mary(vodka based)/maria(tequila based)/derby(whiskey base). Plenty of interesting choices for garnishes.

    Service -
    This was probably the only aspect of this meal that was up to par. Our waitress was very friendly and responsive to our requests. Thumbs up for her!

    Overall, it was somewhere in between meh and a-ok, more like 2.5 stars.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
