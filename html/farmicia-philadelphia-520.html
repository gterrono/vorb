    We went for brunch on a rainy Sunday.

    Coffee was good and they had happy hour pricing throughout brunch. This meant half off mimosas (which were bubbly and delicious).     Basically, Farmicia has something for everyone. I hope to get back there soon!

    With the exception for the Sampan reservation I made for his birthday dinner, the beau picked out the other places we ate on our most recent Philly trip. He knocked it out of the park, this place included!

    On Easter Sunday, there was no wait when we arrived at 11:30. It was quite full and had a bubbling atmosphere. Our waitress with her awesome long purple hair in a long ponytail quickly took our orders and informed us about an awesome thing: Brunch Happy Hour! From 11 a.m. to 2 p.m., all brunch drinks are half off, so the beau and I each got a $5 cocktail. (The fresh-squeezed O.J. cost $6.50, for comparison! Ha!)  I got the Farmer's Daughter, which was strawberry vodka, grenadine, and Sprite. It tasted like a Shirley Temple; nice childhood throwback! I'm drawing a blank on the name of Adam's drink, but it was basically an alcoholic Arnold Palmer: lemonade mixed with tea-infused vodka. Both were phenomenal!  

    The beau went simple and got a stack of pancakes with real maple syrup, and they were simple and perfect. They were perfectly spongy and light with a good chew to them. I went a little crazy and got the French Toast with warm pecan-praline sauce & whipped cream. YOWZA! I couldn't finish the last two bites because I was overwhelmed by sweetness. I tried to channel my inner Adam Richman and Man Vs. Food it*, but alas. I went for my side cheese grits instead. I was a bit disappointed that they weren't creamier, but that was my own fault for not eating them right away. (I make cheese grits often enough for dinner sides. I know they cool down and seize up fast!) Regardless, they were bursting with sharp cheddar flavor!

    I'd absolutely have brunch here again if I was in Philly. This place is a charmer! You can't go wrong with a Brunch Happy Hour; I'd get the French Toast again too, but go for one slice instead of two. I think it's great they offer an option for just one slice! With all of it's toppings, one is certainly enough.

    * - Yes, I used "Man Vs. Food" as a verb. Y'all know what I mean.

    After a long day of kid-friendly activities (I took one for the team) I was ready to eat somewhere of my own selfish choosing. That's when Farmicia appeared, like a ray of light, as we walked down a random Philly street.

    The food here is great - no doubt about it. I had a crab salad with avocado and it was deliciously fresh. The dressing was tasty and was appropriately tossed (meaning not drenched).

    My husband had a the turkey avocado sandwich with fries and my daughter had a cheese sandwich from the children's menu (love that it came with a side of fruit instead of fries). They both enjoyed their food as well.

    The service here is friendly and the food came out quickly. Ambiance is great too.

    So why the 3 stars? Well after two bites I saw that my salad came with an extra ingredient: a piece of hair.

    This kind of thing happens, I totally get it, but I couldn't find our server so I had to go inside and let the hostess know. She retrieved my salad and said she'd have a new one made for me, which is fine.

    Here's what troubled me though: there was no apology. I mean, I had to sit and watch my husband and devour their hair-free food while I waited for a new salad to be made (assuming they made me a new salad and didn't just remake the old one) and nobody at the restaurant seemed to think that was a problem that deserved an apology.

    Bottom Line: if I'm paying $18 for a salad I expect it to be hair-free. If it does come with hair I expect an apology or maybe even an adjustment on the bill. I don't think that's too much to ask.
