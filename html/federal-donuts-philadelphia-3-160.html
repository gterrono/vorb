    All the fancy donuts (minus the watermelon lime) were out by the time the boyfriend and I got there (around 3:00).  So, to repeat, GO EARLY.

    The restaurant itself was much smaller than I imagined, but that added to the old, authentic feel of the place.  Gave off the feeling that this place wasn't here to mess around and trick you with the ambiance (like some restaurants you see nowadays) - it was here to serve you some freaking delicious food. And did it?  Oh my, yes it did.

    CHICKEN
    A good tip for those who enjoy sampling multiple flavors and tastes and can't bear to just choose one: You can get a two halves with different seasonings/glazes for the price of a whole chicken.  Why choose, when you can have both!  The boyfriend and I ended up getting one half chili garlic and the other honey ginger.

    We managed to get there at a good time; there was no line/wait.  The chicken was served to us immediately, still nice and hot.  As soon as I leaned in, I could smell the chili wafting up from that heavenly chili garlic half.  Actually made my nose and eyes water a bit.  As someone who has been bred to eat spicy Korea food without flinching, I was quite impressed by those first sniffs.  First bite, DELICIOUS. Although it's considered a glaze on their menu, it was more like a sauce (a bit thicker, think barbeque sauce on barbeque).  It became a bit un-crispied due to the sauce, but still nice and hot.  After a bit, however, it tasted a bit repetitive and pretty much similar to chili/garlic sauces you would find elsewhere.

    HOWEVER.  The honey ginger.  Oh. My. God. Wow I could probably would have ordered another whole chicken with just this seasoning.  It wasn't as wet as the chili garlic - more of a glaze, as expected - so it was remained crispy while I was eating.  It had this light fresh mouthwatering taste that you just can't get sick of, no matter how much you eat.  It didn't taste repetitive or sickening at all.  The ginger was light and subtle.  Definitely try this if you can.  Will be back to try the other flavors.

    The honey donut they give you definitely compliments the meal with a slight sweetness (similar to that of chicken and waffles), and without being overpowering or sickly sweet.

    DONUTS
    Didn't expect the hot fresh donuts to just be the honey donut with sugar sprinkled all over them.  Thought the flavor would be infused into the actual dough but, oh well, still tastes good.  Loved the strawberry lavender because you could actually taste the strawberry and lavender and didn't have to search for it.  Same with the vanilla spice, though a bit more subtle.

    Definitely will be back!  And hopefully early enough to grab a dozen of those fancy donuts.

    The combination just sets itself up for success - fried chicken and donuts - you kiddin me???

    We were racing from Virginia Beach actually, trying to get to Federal Donuts before they closed for the day. And luckily we made it! In sadder news, all the fancy donuts were gone :(

    As depressing as the lack of the fancies was, the brilliance of the chicken and of the hot fresh sugared donuts made me happy.

    Now for those unfamiliar with this place who think I'm talking gibberish right now - Federal Donuts has two different categories of donuts. One category is known as the fancy donuts - with flavors like cookies n' cream, sticky bun, watermelon cucumber/lime, chocolate peanut butter, banana cream pie, and strawberry ginger. The other category is considered their hot fresh donuts that are essentially sugar-coated cake donuts of three different flavors: vanilla spice, strawberry lavender, and cinnamon brown sugar. On top of all these donuts, Federal is also famous for their fried chicken and wings that comes in multiple varieties as well. Your chicken can be dry spiced/rubbed in flavors like za'atar, buttermilk ranch, or ballpark bbq OR it can be glazed in chili garlic or honey ginger. Your pick.

    As sadly mentioned, I was unable to try any of the fancies, but I did have the vanilla spice, strawberry lavender, and cinnamon brown sugar hot fresh donuts, plus the za'atar 1/2 chicken.

    Everything was incredible. The donuts lived up to their name and were both piping hot and wonderfully fresh. The flavors shone and were each unique in their own right. Now the chicken was just silly. I couldn't pinpoint the flavor, but it was outstanding. And the meat was cooked beautifully.

    I CANNOT wait to be back in Philly to run to Federal Donuts and order every one of their fancies (before they sell out :/ )!!!

    Much like one has to bank up some cash to visit Vetri, one must bank up unused calories to visit the caloric extravaganza that is FedNuts. If you get here around lunchtime, I recommend getting the fried chicken, preferably in the chili glaze. I would say that it's probably the second best fried chicken I've ever had (after Cafe Soho's wings), as it's done in the twice-fried Korean style. Definitely worth checking out.

    If you're here for breakfast, or if you want to experience "the full monty," then you should definitely check out the star of the show, the donuts. There are some pretty creative flavor profiles, some of which are spectacular and some of which are a little much. That said, I don't think there's any way to predict which donuts a particular person will like. Last I was here, we tried a watermelon-cucumber-lime donut, which I thought was a little out of balance, but my friends loved. I loved the cookies-n-cream donut, but others were not as crazy about it. So my advice would be to go with some friends, order one donut each, and share them. You may be surprised at which donuts you like and which you don't.

    The space is pretty small, so if you want to eat there, get there early. The staff is nice enough, too. Overall, worth visiting.

Specialties

Cake donuts in interesting flavors and Korean-style, twice-fried chicken sold by the half and whole bird.  House-roasted coffee available hot or cold-brewed and iced. That's basically it.

History

Established in 2012.

Chef Michael Solomonov and partner Steve Cook of Zahav and Percy Street Barbecue got a wild idea to pair exotic donuts and super-crispy fried chicken. With Bodhi Coffee owners' Bob Logue and Tom Henneman's coffee expertise and professional bigmouth Felicia D'Ambrosio, the team opened the first FedNuts at 1219 S. Second St. in Pennsport, South Philly in 2011.  The Sansom Street shop followed one year later in October 2012.  We have since been cranking out fancy donuts, melt in your mouth hot fresh donuts, and truly badass chicken.  Follow us on Twitter www.Twitter.com/federaldonuts for status updates and fun contests.
