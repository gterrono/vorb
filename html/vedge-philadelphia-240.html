        I had a terrible day yesterday. My building prematurely switched from heat to AC, I hadn't slept well, I was having dizzy spells all day, there was a work emergency while I was getting dressed for this reservation my amazing sister-in-law, Nikki, made over a month ago, & just, ARGH!

        But then, 7pm arrived, and I found myself, after so many months of YEARNING, finally at Vedge. And everything was right in my world again.

        The atmosphere & decor are lovely. The service was phenomenal. Thank you, Megan for being such a great server, for being just as enthusiastic as I was, & for even being cool about high-fiving me over desserts. Thank you, also, to the host who picked up my peel n' eat lupini bean that went flying & said "It happens all the time" (after which Nikki & I turned to each other & said "Pretty Woman").

        We loved everything we had, I posted some pics, but they don't even come close to doing our plates justice. Here's the rundown:

        To drink:
        I enjoyed the Pomegranate Sangria & Nikki enjoyed the Creole Gent. We both enjoyed them twice.

        From the 4/23 Dirt List:
        Ramps
        Nebrodini Mushrooms
        Cauliflower
        (loved them all, the cauliflower melted in my mouth, the mushrooms were my fave of the three)

        Small Bites:
        peel n' eat lupini beans ("slippery little suckers" (I had to say it!), but so worth the effort to open them!)

        Plates:
        sweet potato paté
        salt roasted gold beets
        funky kimchee stew
        spicy grilled tofu
        roasted maitake mushroom
        (again, loved them ALL...I think Nikki's faves were the sweet potato paté & the tofu, mine were the kimchee & maitake mushroom (probably b/c it included 2 of my favorite things ever...celery root & leeks!))

        Dessert
        Saffron Cheesecake
        Chocolate Über Chunk
        (I could pretty much have licked both of these plates clean, but I didn't because I try to have just a little class in public)

        Wow. I just realized how much we ate. I'm really looking forward to my dad & step-mom's next visit, because we will definitely be taking them to Vedge!

        Closed-minded omnivores, you best open your minds & get yourselves to Vedge. You have no idea what you're missing. Or fine, don't, because then more for me.

        Seriously. I don't think I've ever had a better meal.

        I met a friend of mine at  Vedge yesterday for happy hour and it was a great time!  For drinks, we tried the spring collins, summer buck, and pomegranate sangria.  The two mixed drinks were fantastic!  Both were mixed to perfection with liquor that tasted top shelf (but priced reasonably at $6 during happy hour).  I also have to comment on the colors - the spring collins was a lovely purple and the summer buck, a bright orange.  Loved them both!  The sangria was pretty basic, but loaded with fresh fruit.

        After over a month's wait, it finally arrived -- the reservation for dinner at Vedge. My partner in crime and I beared the early evening drizzle and 23 bussed it from East Passyunk to 12th and Locust to the Philly media's new darling, the seemingly Mecca of Vegetarian bliss, Vedge.

        We arrive at the restaurant with ten minutes to spare of happy hour, so I reach for the happy hour menu. A drunk woman at the bar looks at me crossly and says something like, "I could have handed it to you!" and then rolls her eyes. Gave off a stinky, snooty vibe, but I can't judge a place off of one obnoxious patron.
        So I order 2 pomegranate sangrias. The bar tender takes longer than expected to prepare the drinks but that's ok, since I've waited longer for sangria. However, this sangria doesn't really do it for me. It tastes as if it's awash with apple juice, and not nearly enough of a kick to make it stand out. Not so much a kick in terms of alcohol level, just a spice, or flavor, or something different to make me think, "Wow! This is good!" it's good, just not special, and makes me glad I was able to pay the $5 happy hour price for it instead of the full priced $8.

        We get seated and bring our sangrias to the table. There's no place to hang my coat or put my umbrella, so I leave the coat on the couch cushion I'm sitting on and the umbrella on the floor. A waitress comes over and explains the menu to us first-timers. She then scrams and we ponder over the menu for about ten minutes until she returns to take our order. I get the sweet potato pate and the seitan steak, my date orders the funky stew and radishes. We also split the fries.
        Shortly after we order, a man with a bread tray tongs one slice of bread in front of me, one in front of my date, then prepares a small olive oil dipping arrangement with what I believed to have been anise. It's good, but that' it. Just one slice for each of us. I quickly devour the bread, and, like a lapdog, eagerly wait for him to come back, but he never does. Oh well. It keeps me hungry for the food I ordered, but I long for the Italian restaurant style of having a big 'ol breadbasket on the table and keep it coming until I can't eat any more.

        First comes the sweet potato pate and the radishes. My first thought is "This is it?" Two little cutting boards with the vegetables lined up nice and pretty, but the substance is definitely lacking. The sweet potato is a tiny wedge of the root whipped up in a fancy pate than you use to rub on tiny pieces of bread. The radishes are lined up with a glob of avocado in the middle that doesn't really make sense, and a metal bowl of soy sauce on the side for dipping. It's grilled and interesting and absolutely tasty, but this feels more like a complimentary appetizer the chef would offer, not a $9 dish.

        We finish up the apps, the server whips away the empty plates, and the main dishes arrive. These are much better. The kimchee stew is nicely portioned and has a fantastic swirl of flavors. It just does kimchee right. The seitan steak is delicious, and the swiss chard it came with tastes good too. Still, it went too fast, and I would have enjoyed a slightly bigger portion or more sides on the plate. I actually feel a little bummed because when I first eyeballed the plate I mistook the swiss chard for more seitan. Luckily, we also ordered the "fries" off of the dirt list, and those complemented the seitan nicely.

        We skip dessert, and bid Vedge adieu. All in all it was a very nice experience, but I doubt I'd return. Our server was friendly and aside from the snooty drunk lady at happy hour, it had a pretty chill vibe. I would have liked more big portion options, but the small portions they did serve all tasted very good. You can tell that lots of care went into the menu, and that means something. It's refreshing to visit a restaurant that doesn't have the need to douse food with cheese to make it good for customers, and also fun to have the option to order anything off of the menu without worry of health or dietary repercussions. Still, I left Vedge with a longing desire to go back to FrancoLuigis and order a big plate of eggplant parmigiana, where the bread keeps on coming.

        Vedge succeeds at making the simplest things taste complex and amazing.  Most people don't believe me when I tell them that I had this crazy amazing dish that was meatless and based around beets, but if they had tasted this, I would be shocked if they didn't agree.  My dinner at Vedge was most definitely a five star experience.  

        The cocktails I had at my previous visit at Vedge were good, but didn't compare to the ones I got off the regular menu during my second visit.  The Ides of March is earthy and complex with flavors of fennel, cucumber with a slight hint of sweetness that is blended into the dry vermouth that is used as a base.  The other cocktail I had was tequila based with a hint of ginger.  It was refreshing, but not as interesting as the first one.  My husband was in love with his whiskey based cocktail, and although I'm not a whiskey fan, I found it to be rather smooth.

        The server explained the menus to us, and made some recommendations.  He talked us into the beet salad that didn't sound that amazing to me, but it turned out to the best thing I've eaten in a long time.  It was beautifully presented, and to be honest, I wouldn't have know it was mainly composed of beets from tasting it!  On the menu it's listed as salt roasted gold beets, avocado, smoked tofu, rye, capers, and creamy cucumber.  The combination of the thinly sliced sweet tender beets with creamy avocado and cucumber with hints of smokey and salty flavors was pure heaven.  I would go back for this dish alone.  The board with the homemade pickled veggies, olives, and lupini beans, was a good deal. Olives are my favorite food, and it was even too many olives for me, and they were the good kind as it even included castelvetrano (the really, really green looking ones).  I actually had them box half of the olives up because it was just too much for one sitting!  They were delicious the next day as well. The lupini beans had a nice firm texture and were coated in a garlic oil.  

        The curried cauliflower soup had a wonderful texture as the cauliflower was tender and not too soft and chopped into tiny pieces.  It was flavorful due to the saffron.  My second favorite dish of the night was the spicy grilled tofu, gochujang, edamame,
        smoked miso, yuba cracklin.  Honestly, I ordered it because it said yuba which is the skin off the top of the tofu.  I used to eat it all the time in Japan, but I don't see it much in the states.  However, I fell in love the texture of the grilled tofu, the flavorful sauce, and the smokiness of it more than the yuba.  A similar thing happened with the roasted maitake mushroom, celery root fritter, smoked leek remoulade.  I ordered it because I love maitake, but the hot crispy celery root fritter and the rich smokey remoulade were the stars of the show in that dish.  I really liked it, but not for the reasons that I thought I would.

        The two dishes that I wasn't really feeling that night were both off the Dirt Menu (a daily list of seasonal and slightly smaller dishes).  One was turnip greens, and while they were good, they weren't anything special and could have easily and cheaply been prepared at home.  The other was kale with an XO sauce. The XO sauce was way, way, way too salty.  I couldn't even eat it, but my husband was okay with it, so I let him have it.  I'm wondering if the saltiness is due to whatever the vegetarian substitute they use in it, as XO sauce traditionally has some seafood in it to flavor it.  

        Dessert more than made up for the failures off the dirt list.  I decided to try two flavors off the fresh ice cream and sorbets.  I got the rosemary olive oil ice cream and the cucumber mint sorbet.  The rosemary olive oil was rice and buttery with rich rosemary flavor.  The cucumber mint was like a refreshing East Side cocktail without the gin.  These were so unique and fresh, and great for someone like me that doesn't like overly sweet typical desserts.   My husband had the Chocolate Über Chunk which had a pretzel peanut crust, malt custard, and stout ice cream.  It was very sweet and rich and on the other end of the spectrum from the dessert I had.  It's not something I would normally order, but I have to say, I took a bite and the combination of crispy pretzels and nuts with the rich, creamy custard was quite decadent.  

        The service was excellent and the courses were spaced out nicely.  The waiter was also very knowledgeable about the menu, and the items he recommended were the best ones.  I actually had wanted to go to Vedge for a Valentine's Day dinner, but by the time I got around to booking it in mid January, the place was already full every weekend until the end of March.  My husband and I decided just to make that our Valentine's Day since that's where I wanted to go, and I'm so glad we did.  Make sure you make reservations well ahead of time!  This place is quite popular, and for a good reason.

        We sat in the lounge area which was nice and sunny, and had comfy bench/couch like seating.  I started with the Pomegrante Sangria for $5 because I'd remembered reading about it on Yelp.  It was good, but not the best Sangria I've ever tasted.  One of my friends ordered the Blood Orange Gimlet.  It was pretty, pinkish, and actually tasted like lemonade, but all of us agreed it was refreshing despite it not really tasting like we'd imagined it.  The best drink I had was the Spiked Cider Fizz.  This was a concoction of rum, apple cider, lemon, and soda and it was fizzy and full of apple flavor.  I would order it again in a heartbeat. It was totally worth the $6.

        I tried everything on the happy hour menu that day, which consisted of four different items.  The plates were only $4, but they are small.  As others have said, the Korean Seitan Taco is slightly spicy, and has a great texture.  If you only get one thing for happy hour, get that because everyone liked it.  I was the only one who enjoyed the smoked mushrooms and white bean and sauerkraut puree.  It was very smokey, which turned off others in my party, but for me it was a plus. The pickle and olive jar was just okay.

        I didn't want to order the curry wedge fries because other reviewers had talked about it coming with only three fries.  Yes, there were only three fries, but they are large and the curry sauce is bursting with so much crazy amazing curry flavor, that surprisingly this was probably my favorite.  My friend wanted to order another order of it because of the sauce.  We did and then proceeded to clean the bowl of curry sauce with our forks.

        I think I can talk my non-veggie friends into coming back here based on the curry sauce.  Yay!  I'm hoping to check this place out for dinner as well in the near future.         Read more
