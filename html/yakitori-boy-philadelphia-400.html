    I work in Chinatown, so I've walked past this place several times. I noticed that they have some awesome-looking weekly specials, including a SUSHI special on Wednesdays. Now, sushi has to be my favorite food of all time. Their Wednesday happy hour special is $2.50 per roll, plus $3-4 beer specials. I knew I had to try this place out.

    I had my chance last week. I brought a date there.  The inside is pretty nice, and the waiter took us to a fairly secluded table. I have to admit, I was skeptical about the food. I figured they would only offer their worst-selling rolls..I mean, come on. $2.50?? That is soo cheap.

    I was wrong. They had a fairly extensive menu of $2.50 rolls. I didn't feel limited at all. And the food was good!!!! I think we tried nearly every roll on the menu and loved them all.

    We both totally gorged ourselves on sushi. I had a Sapporo, my date had 2, and we got out of there with a $33 bill!! Even better, my date picked up the check :)

    Thanks Yakitori Boy!

    My bf and I are huge yakitori fanatics and were super excited when we discovered this place during restaurant week. We ordered a good amount that time and since have come back a few times for the great yakitori deals on Tuesday ($1 yakitori). The sushi is subpar to me except for the really tastey spicey yellow tail carpaccio which is a dish hard to do wrong at any sushi restaurant. The menu is extensive and offers amazing variety from small dish appetizers, salads, sushi rolls to entrees so you will definitely find something for everyone here I feel. I wish the sushi was a bit better in quality but am excited to try out all the different daily specials they have and will eventually give the sushi a second chance on Wednesdays when they have their deals on the rolls.

    Trendy bar with nice flat screens where I witnessed a fan cry when Brazil lost a game in the world cup. Fair-sized list of cocktails for HH in which I haven't found my go-to drink yet.. these tend to be a bit too syrupy and sweet for me. My roommate raved about his Sapporo draft with shaved ice on top.

    Yakitori isn't cheap generally so this is a decent deal on Tuesdays. Takoyaki here is actually decent, too but don't burn your tongue like I did. Can't wait to try more on the huge menu, the daily specials, and also the karaoke!

    I went to Yakitori Boy for karaoke only and was pleasantly surprised to find that there was no cover at 11 on a Friday night, which was a surprise.

    Most of my party was fairly bombed by the time we got to Yakitori (unfortunately I was DDing so this was all very clear in my mind), and we shuffled upstairs to the very crowded bar area where the open karaoke was going on. I saw a lot of party groups there (bachelorettes and such) and all of the private rooms were booked when we first arrived. Everyone got really into the songs that were onscreen--enough so that the actual people singing the karaoke were drowned out (which I suppose was good for the bad singers). It was a cool concept, because it meant that no one really got embarrassed.

    At some point early in our time there, one of my party managed to get us a reservation for a room... at 12:30. Waiting that long for a room meant that my party was even more hammered by the time we got in. The private room was cool, but my party was on the wrong side of rowdy. I will say that the staff were absolute angels, walking us through the system multiple times and cleaning up all of broken glassware without batting an eye. Very much appreciated.
