    Not much of an atmosphere, no thrills or frills but really great Pho. Best I've had in the city to date.

    if you want to know what Pho is - en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phở or an excerpt:
    Pho is a viatnamese dish served as a bowl of white rice noodles in clear beef broth, with thin cuts of beef (steak, fatty flank, lean flank, brisket) .

    They close kind of early about 7 to 9 bucks should cover your meal.

    When I moved from Boston, home of the largest Vietnamese population in the northeast, to Philly, I was afraid I was going to have a hard time getting good Vietnamese food.  I went to a couple of places that were lackluster, at best, and Pho Hoa is always a standard OK, but this was the first place I found that really compared to my hometown's Pho.  

    The pho here is delicious, the broth is rich and savory and just a little on the salty side, which I love.  I usually go for the beef and beef flank, or something with meatballs, and I've never been disappointed.  

    The only thing about this place, and I think someone else mentioned it, is that they only have pho.  I love a range of Vietnamese foods, and I wish I could share some bun or chicken salad with friends if I go in a group, but I suppose that's one of the reasons they do their pho so well.

    So, I technically never tried pho before, but when my bf had a craving for some we turned to Yelp for inspiration - and thankfully didn't have to look far for Pho 75's listing. We opted for this location since I wanted to drop by the Italian Market after and enjoyed a lovely stroll all the way down there.

    We weren't surprised by the lack of decor and rather spare cafeteria-like layout. Simple, standard tables and chairs everywhere. The majority of patrons seemed to be Vietnamese themselves, a very good sign. My bf ordered a large (and boy do they mean LARGE) Tai, Nam, Sach (slices of eye-round steak, well-done flank and beef tripe), and I just had a taste of the noodles while he went to town with all the meaty goodness. The noodles were fantastic, perfect texture. He couldn't get over how delicious the pho was, the meat was sliced thinly and melted with eat bite he took, but the tripe was definitely the favorite ingredient since its been awhile since he's had any.

    It may be no frills, but Pho 75 has amazing pho, and considering how large their portions are and the quality, the prices are great! Large was just about $7. We're definitely coming back for more soon!
