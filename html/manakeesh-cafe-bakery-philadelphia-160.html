    Listed in Coffee, Chocolate, Dessert, Cheap Fast Food

    The place is an absolute gem!  Whether you are looking for dinner (classic manakeesh - Lebanese flatbread with olive oil, thyme, and sesame), breakfast (freshly squeezed mango juice and hazelnut crepes!) or a snack (the best baklava in the city!), Manakeesh has it.  I recently returned here for dinner and was, as always, very pleased.

    The flatbread is crisp on the outside and chewy on the inside with a warm, buttery flavor and you can choose from a variety of toppings (some vegetarian, some not).  I ordered the combo manakeesh with hummus (olive oil, thyme, sesame and cheese with added humus on flatbread).  They also offer an assortment of vegan fare for the more eco-conscious palette.  For dessert, I topped off my meal with three decadent squares of honey-goodness that is the baklava here.  Light, flaky, covered with honey, and topped with a dusting of pistachio grinds, this is hands down the best baklava I've had (it's available for purchase in boxes now too!).  My dinner was wonderful, and very well priced (under $10!).

    There is also a juice/smoothie/ice cream bar in a side portion of the restaurant which now features a mini-mart like set-up where you can purchase snacks including Lebanese spices, health food, baklava and other assorted goodies.  I'm itching to return another time to check out the fabulous fun of this area.  Perhaps this weekend!

    In all, if you're looking for a cozy atmosphere, good Turkish coffee and some lovely baklava, Manakeesh is the place to go.  Love it!

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge

    I moan and groan about the gigantic sweet tooth I inherited thanks to my Mediterranean heritage, but when I choose to indulge it, especially with Mediterranean sweets, its quite a bit of fun (oh right, and SUPER delicious). I had an intense hankering for baklava, but there are so few places in CC that actually make baklava for take out. A Yelp search brought me to Manakeesh Cafe & Bakery, and although its location is quite out of the way, my yoga classes on the weekend bring me far enough into the area that I decided to make a side trip there before heading home.

    The large cafe and bakery is designed so that it looks like two different sections/businesses, with the cafe on one side and the dessert-y, smoothie, ice cream bar area on the other. I quickly spotted the sweets I was after since they are displayed right at the cafe's counter, and after a bit of a wait since this is a popular place, I ordered almost one of every single type of baklava and sweet that I saw. It was quite a large order, but we were having friends over so I figured it was safer to get more and then people can choose what to eat (and I would get the leftovers ;D). Our little party had fun trying things (I was shocked that someone had never had baklava before!):

    Semolina cake: this almond semolina cake was very dense and sweet, especially with the sweet sugar syrup that's poured on top. The almond flavor is very light and complements the semolina perfectly.
    Phyllo sweet cheese pastry (can't remember the name of it): yummy! The shredded phyllo itself was not very sweet, but it adds a great crunch, and the sweet cheese filling was delicious. The piece they cut was topped with a dollop of more of the filling with pistachio pieces on top.
    Malmouls: the pistachio one had a mellow toasted pistachio filling, and the semolina shortbread was still a bit moist and corn cookie-like. The sweet date filled one was delicious, too. The walnut one had a very pronounced toasted walnut flavor - it was so good, it might be my favorite, and I had assumed pistachio would be since I love that little green nut so much...
    Assorted baklava: these were absolutely delicious, not soaked in honey the way most baklava you encounter is, and the phyllo dough is super light, flaky, and buttery. All of the different toasted nuts were finely ground and held their own flavor against the sweet honey. Some of the best baklava I've ever had. Manakeesh is now my go-to for Mediterranean sweets - thank god for my sweet tooth its such a hike to get over there!

Specialties

History

Established in 2011.

Owner Wissam Chatila's dreams of having Lebanon's famous flatbread sandwiches close to home have been realized in the form of Manakeesh, a Lebanese cafe and bakery, serving up the famous sandwiches after which it is named in over 30 varieties.

In addition to the manakeesh, expert Chef Wissam Zayat whips up the best Middle Eastern sweets in the area, including award winning baklava.

Manakeesh is open for breakfast, lunch, dinner and dessert!

Meet the Manager

Abd G. Manager

A self described 'people person', Abd brings his upbeat attitude and energy to Manakeesh, where he can be seen greeting customers and making sure their experience is quite memorable.
