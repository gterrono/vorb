    I was almost hesitant to give this place a try, as I'd heard so much hype (oh, the red velvet pancakes). That much hype makes me a little nervous in Philly.

    HOWEVER. I was here on Saturday, slightly hungover and therefore late for the brunch rush. I called ahead to see what the wait was, and the host was so nice and honest. We got there to a very small (10-15 minutes?) wait, got a fantastic table for myself and 3 of my best friends, it was warm, not super-squishy (like at Honey's where you have to sit on top of each other?) and we could hold a great conversation.

    I got the pork belly benedict, as I love bacon and I love eggs benedict. I was very happy with my meal, rosemary potatoes were awesome, maple 'hollandaise' sauce was delicious. The pork belly was a little dry, but other than that, great meal. Friends got the creme brulee french toast (loved it!), a soup-sandwich special and of course, one order of red velvet pancakes (not my thing, but she loved them). The portions (aside from the pancakes--which are incredibly large) were very hearty but not like, sickening.

    Our server was great, the service itself was timely and just right (I did mention we're 4 girlfriends who haven't seen each other in a few months, right?), and I left feeling comfortably satisfied and content.

    I thought the prices were reasonable, and the only reason they get one star off is well, because no one is perfect, and my pork belly was overdone. And cash-only drives me nuts.

    I highly recommend you give it a try, wait until around 1:30 so you don't get all hungry and snippy about waiting for an hour, and see if you, too, believe the hype. Green Eggs is great!

    Most of the restaurants Sam has fallen in love with in Philly are strictly for foodie carnivores, but I was delighted that his favorite brunch spot was veg-friendly and we could enjoy it together.

    There was a wait, but that's to be expected just before noon on a Sunday.  Better idea - grab a group of friends and make a reservation.  I think the minimum is 5 people.  We did our due diligence, grabbing coffee elsewhere to kill time and keep warm, and soon enough we made it inside.

    I had a hangover to cure, and nothing sounded better than a tofu scramble.  If you have a sweet tooth to conquer, they also have vegan french toast!  I had done a little too much damage to my body the night before to justify piling on the non-dairy whipped cream, but it'll happen next time.

    The scramble was just what I needed.  Lots of veggies and flavor, complete with tofu that wasn't too mushy or grainy.  There was a chunk of sausage that wasn't really up my ally, but I crumbled some of it into the scramble for a little something extra.  The seasoning sort of reminded me of scrapple, but that's a far off distant memory so don't hold me to it.  The rosemary homefries were just fatty enough to ease me into recovery, and I had to resist clearing my plate - they were VERY good.  I ended up not touching my toast since the butter that came with it was dairy (PS why are you serving butter with a vegan entree?), but seeing how I could barely finish my scramble, I'm sure it was for the best.

    It's hard to believe a hip place with multiple locations is cash only, but alas, there's the rub.

    Get a credit card reader, get some Earth Balance, and you'll be an absolute gem.  The food hit the spot and I'll be back.

    I've been here twice. Both experiences were generally positive.

    The first time I came here, I had a tomato soup, a side of fries, and the Buffalo Chicken Mac & Cheese, which could easily feed five hungry children.

    The tomato soup was good, but it had a weird after-taste. If you generally don't mind weird after-tastes, then you probably wouldn't mind this soup. The fries were a bit soggy, but after I took them home and re-fried them myself, they turned crispy, so problem solved.

    The Buffalo Chicken Mac & Cheese is a beast, served in a deep cast-iron skillet, sizzling with aged cheddar and crumbled blue cheese, pulled chicken, and a bit of hot sauce. It is a perfect comfort food for a cold, hungry day. I took three bites and was so full I could barely walk. Yet they were the most satisfying 1000-calorie bites I've ever had. I took the rest home and was all set for the week.

    During my second visit, I tried the Crème Brulee French Toast, topped with mixed berries and Chantilly cream. There was probably half a loaf of bread in there, but I couldn't stop eating it. For anyone looking for a place that offers a solid French Toast in Center City, look no further. Green Eggs Cafe has it. (Warning: This delicious mass of carbs will completely destroy any diet.)

    My boyfriend tried the Kitchen Sink, which sounded interesting, but the interest waned when we realized that it consisted of mostly potatoes, and the biscuit on top was kind of stale.

    We were here for a Saturday brunch with a large group of friends and had a great time. Though they don't take weekend brunch reservations, we didn't have to wait too long to be seated once our party was complete. I would recommend calling ahead to make sure there is space. As other reviewers point out, there is something for everyone on this menu.

    Things to keep in mind:
    ---This is a CASH-ONLY establishment.
    ---There is no dinner service.
    ---They charge 25 cents per to-go boxes.
