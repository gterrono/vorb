    How does one rate the experience of Honey's? Is it really fair to hold it to the hype it's gotten? Perhaps if it were an eatery in my own back yard, or someplace I could go on a regular basis, I might give it the benefit of the doubt. But given my position in the city and the often impenetrable wait, getting into Honey's became something of a Homeric epic, one that I hoped would pay off with a meal for the ages.

    Not that Honey's is "bad" by any measure; on the contrary, it's pretty good. I just can't say it's everything people have told me. Sure, maybe it you haven't eaten at Sabrina's or Carmen's on a regular basis, it might seem like something exceptional. Frankly, though, nothing about it struck me as better than the places I can go to in my own neighborhood.

    Would I eat at Honey's again? Sure, though I would probably bring a longer crossword to help with the wait. I'm just not going to tell anyone that they "have to, have to, HAVE TO eat here!"

    From its rustic pine plank floors to its gingham-covered counter stools, this Northern Liberties diner oozes rustic style. The menu is a combination of Jewish and Southern comfort cuisine.

    You can't go wrong with any of the breakfast choices, including the Challah French Toast, Chicken Fried Steak, Potato Latke, the bacon is delicious and especially the Banana Nut Pancakes are the hit.

    However, since I live around the corner the latter complaint is not an issue.  Also, there has been plenty of celebrity sightings at this spot, making it a place you must stop by if you're in the hood.

    Cream cheese? In an omelet?! With lox?!?!?!

    I ordered it with a latke and some coffee. You wouldn't think this omelet was good just by reading the combination of ingredients, but lemme tell ya, IT WAS DELICIOUS.     All in all a totally great place!!

    I had the Challah French Toast (~$7.25) which came as advertised. It was very basic for the price.  Real maple syrup made this dish one of the best french toast I've had in quite some time.  To add to that, the blueberry preserves on the table complemented the meal quite well.  However, I would have appreciated a potato latke or some other side as the meal was... basic.

    My wife got an omelet of modest size but smaller than I'd like, which tasted very good.  Their redskin potato home-fry was rather poorly made.  It tasted too much like olive-oil and was seasoned with WAY too much rosemary.  A decent spread but I couldn't help but feel like I was paying a bit for the experience as the food left a little lacking.

    Bottom Line:  A great spot for a nice breakfast, but not quite worth the price for the taste and portion.
