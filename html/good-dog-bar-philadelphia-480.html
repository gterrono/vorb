    I came here a few years ago with a group and liked it so I stopped in the other day on my way through Philly when I was hoping for a tasty burger and it was as good as I remembered.

    I actually ended up getting the appetizer of 3 pulled pork sliders which is definitely enough for a meal. They come topped with an awesome cole slaw which was a nice treat. My boyfriend got a gigantic burger with the fries that are a mix of regular fries AND sweet potato fries. The beer is good, the food is better, and its still stands as my favorite burger spot in a town where there are some very good meat and bun options.  My in-laws were in town, and after a few hours of sight-seeing, we had worked up an appetite.     I came, I sat, and I stayed.

    Good Dog is a solid neighborhood bar.  I love the endless photos of dogs, and the bartenders are pretty chill and friendly.  I love that I can stroll in here and sit at the bar alone without feeling awkward or fearing getting sandwiched by The Roxbury Guys.

    If it were less pricey, I would probably come here every evening for happy hour and dinner.  People keep alluding to this bar as a dive; but in actuality, this is just a normal bar.  The vast majority of seats are intact, the bar is pretty clean, and the other patrons are neither hipsters nor scary alcoholics.  (For a dive experience if you happen to be in Denver, check out Lost Lake, which has surprisingly not been shut down for health code violations).  

    The food here is quite delicious.  I loved the chicken dish (I'm a sucker for the accompanying pan-fried brussel sprouts and the creamy mashed potatoes).  The duck pot pie was quite excellent: you don't get the BS of only having a top crust - you get your stewed ingredients completely encased in buttery flaky goodness ... and it comes with ... an arugula salad (getting back to my previous point, dives do not serve arugula).  The beer selection is decent, although I would probably go to Monk's if I were in a more serious beer mood.  Happy hour is not a bad deal with $1 off drinks, but it's not dirt cheap either (some of the beers are $6 to begin with) - hence the deduction of one star because the prices could be a bit better.  Once I've burned off the 3000 calories from tonight's duck pot pie, I'll be back to try the burger and the cheesesteak empanadas.  

    Saturday nights are busier, with failed attempts x 2 to find a seat for imbibing (granted, I arrived there around midnight both times I showed up).

    This establishment does have me wondering, though, what a bar with endless pictures of cats would be like ... maybe that would end up being a strange hipster dive? ::shudder::

    I like Good Dog Bar and Restaurant because I like dogs and I like food.

    The decor of Good Dog is relaxed and unpretentious and the food is good. Not "really good," not "very good," just "good," which I suppose is why they didn't name it "Very Good Dog Bar and Restaurant." Good Dog reminds me of a bastard love-child between a high-priced gastropub and a neighborhood bar. I mean bastard in the literal sense and not as a negative reflection on Good Dog. The high-priced gastropub is already married and was merely "slumming" because it's marriage had become a loveless and passionless.  

    I went to Good Dog for the burger since it was rated as one of Philly's best burgers in by Philadelphia magazine. The Good Dog burger is reasonably priced ($12.5) and delicious. It's "1/2 lb of house ground sirloin stuffed with roquefort cheese & topped with caramelized onions, served on a toasted brioche bun." That's right, it's stuffed with cheese! The roquefort cheese is soft and creamy with a distinct tanginess that goes well with the fattiness of the burger. The burger comes with a side of fries (half kennebec (normal), half sweet potato). I was hoping for steak fries with a distinct flavor but instead got thin and bland fries. They were underwhelming.

    The biddy I was with got the mussels. They are "sautéed with capers, shallots, garlic, chipotle peppers & fresh herbs then steamed in magic hat #9." They were also "good." The sauce they came with was pretty flavorless and bland. The sautéing and steaming dulled (and destroyed) the sharp flavors I expect from capers and smoky-heat I expect from chipotle peppers. I would not recommend this dish.

    I will go back since I live 2 blocks away, like the decor, and they have a salmon reuben and a lamb belly sandwich on the menu which I want to try. The burger gets 4 stars and the rest of the food gets 2 stars.
