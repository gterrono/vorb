    Not much to add to this heavily and favorably reviewed restaurant, I'll just
    give my take on the specific dishes we tasted.

    Margarita (regular, no fruit): Well balanced, extremely fresh limes, good balance, sneaky strong.

    Guacamole: as good as I can make at home, which is fine by me, fresh and with the right amount of salt and lime.  Relatively small portion for $10.

    Salsa: $5 for salsa? Its tasty, but to me, paying for salsa at a Mexican restaurant is unamerican.

    Pato Huaraches (flat bread with duck, manchego cream): Very tasty, delicious fatty and flavorful duck, crispy flatbread, amazing cheese.  

    Camarones y Chorizo tacos: A must try.  The shrimp had a very unique flavor and the chorizo was a great contrast. Must try.

    Pollo Tacos: Very good choice for less adventurous eaters.

    Enchiladas de pollo: one per order, relatively filling, thought the sauce was a bit on the sour side, but the chicken was cooked and seasoned well, not dry at all.  

    Arroz con crema: MMMM.  Must try, creamy, cheesy, pilloughy, comforting, and unbelievable tasty.  Filling.

    Platano y crema: Its a plantain with a light cream sauce drizzle.  Plantains are awesome.  If you like plantains, get it.

    Enjoy!

    Remember that movie, Atonement? Where someone did something bad and they have to like, atone for it?

    Well that's what I'm doing now, with Distrito, except instead of atoning for fraudulently accusing someone of rape, I'm updating a restaurant review on a website. These things are the same.

    The first time I ate here, I wasn't ready.  The year was 2008 and my powers of consuming and writing about consuming were not well developed. I wasn't prepared for the price and I wasn't familiar enough with the tapas concept to care. I was expecting a typical Mexican restaurant where I could get a chimichanga the size of a gorilla's fist and limitless nachos.

    I was too hard on Distrito the first time I went. Now, things are different. Distrito rules.

    The galabaza soup is excellent. It is simply brimming with hazelnuttyness. It is a winner and I prefer it greatly to the tortilla soup.

    All of the ceviches I've tried are great and a solid bet.

    I prefer the hamachi tacos to the chicken and kobe beef ones. The chipotle remoulade that comes on them is super tasty.

    The esquites is a very interesting menu item. It is served in a way that is kinda like a sweet corn lime parfait.

    The los hongos and costillas huaraches are great. Garces knows how to make bread in the shape of flat. He knows how to make it taste amazing. These items are larger than most of the other tapas and their high carb content will go a good way towards filling you up.

    and finally...

    The spiced tres leches is the best tres leches cake I've had. This is coming from a man who has scoured Costa Rica, Venezuela, and Florida for the best tres leches. It is here. I've found it. Get it. It rules.

    On the action-movie-parallel-satisfaction-scale, this place is Predator 2.

    Today, my friend and I had lunch at Distrito. I had been wanting to eat there for ages, so I was thrilled to go.

    Although you cannot eat decor, let's be honest, decor is important. Distrito is decorated to make you feel like you are dining al fresco in Mexico. The tables are decorated in a way that reminded me of ornate Mexican tiles, but are smooth. I liked those tables so much if I could have stuck one in my purse, I would have been tempted. There are wicker seats, with cushions for comfort, as well as a wall of luchador masks adding to the overall effect. And, yes, the famous taxi is available for reservations!

    Now onto the food!

    We had the Blue Devil lunch special (30.00 per person) which covers a starter and an entree. We also each had a margarita and dessert, which were a la carte.

    I had tortilla soup for my appetizer and was delighted by the sensuous smoky chipotle flavor. Often I find with chipotle, people miss the smoky quality of it and just lay on the heat. This was not the case with this soup. It also had a few nice sized chunks of queso fresco in it, which added a burst of flavor and gave the soup multiple notes of flavor and texture.

    My friend had the nachos. I am not exaggerating in the slightest when I say these were the best nachos I have had in an extremely long time. The chips were clearly housemade, topped with cheddar, queso fresco, micro greens, and, believe it or not, pickled peppers and - wait for it - radishes! They were amazing! I would be happy to go back to Distrito just for nachos and margaritas!

    I had the jefe margarita, which was tasty, but, for the price, a little small. My friend's margarita was actually a bottle of Mexican lime soda and a shot and half of tequila which was brought out in components. Hers was definitely more bang for the buck.

    My friend had the himachi tacos for the entree (plates are a good size for sharing). The fish was lightly battered and fried and not at all greasy (death for a fish taco). It was very good, but as far as fish tacos go, I think I will stick to San Diego and Key West for them. Fish tacos, in my opinion, are a regional delicacy, much the way a cheese steak is. I mean, no one goes to San Diego and orders a cheese steak. You will just get a sandwich. But they were not without their charm.

    I had the huitaloche (mushroom) and corn flat bread. This was a vegetarian (NOT vegan) dish and a nice size. The mushrooms, of course, had an earthy savory element and a great texture. Fresh kernels of corn, were scattered throughout, and lent a nice toothsome texture to the dish. There was a white sauce, delicately drizzled on top, which lent the dish a surprisingly sweet note, but it was not cloying. A delicious and satisfying dish.

    For dessert, my friend had the flan and I had the tres leches cake. I did not taste her flan, but I did taste the accompanying sorbet and it was fantastic! The server (very professional, and well trained) said it was apple. We thought it was pear. Regardless, it was excellent and boasted a nice tang and a strong cinnamon flavor.

    I have to come clean on tres leches cake. I make one myself, and, um, I like mine more! This was good, but, well, I don't know. It was served with cranberry sorbet which, while tasty on its own, I did not think it went with the cake itself. I will bet if I had never had tres leches before, I would have liked this more.

    Overall, I really enjoyed lunch there and will definitely go back.

    I *must* have those nachos again!

    I've been here 3 or 4 times now. The servers are always fantastic and I for one, like the slightly flip decor and wall of luchador masks. The green taxi reminds me of dodging traffic on my visit to Mexico City years ago.

    The food is excellent, and I really enjoy the different ceviches and nachos (with fresh, not canned vegetables!). The flan and side of sorbet is out of this world.  I think the portion sizes have increased since the opening, and I have never left hungry. The drink prices are truly out of line for the size of the product you are given. A  single of the standard Margarita is $12 for a juice sized glass.

    Beware of the layout of the 'Blue Demon lunch' special menu. I don't think they are trying to intentionally deceive, but it is easy on the fly to look down the list and order one item in the 5 sections on the menu when you only get three items included on the special price. I would not have ordered the extra black beans or dessert that I hardly ate and that really raised the lunch price tag. The bill has always been more then I have estimated when I eat here.

    I don't think the prices are out of line for the tapas of this quality, but I do wonder if perhaps a restaurant with this type of bill isn't fit for the neighborhood. UCity has some great restaurants, but they are usually priced more modestly. It gives one a bit of a sticker shock unless you frequent old city.

    OMG! FANTASTICO! DELIGHTFUL! SCRUMPTIOUS!

    Is that enough descriptive words? I have been here 3 or 4 times now and each time it has been fantastic. The only thing I have ever ordered here was the Chef's Tasting Menu. For those of you who don't know what this is, it's a mixture of the some of the chef's specialties (like 5-8 courses). In this case, we are not big seafood people so we asked for no seafood. If you like seafood I highly recommend keeping the seafood in the tasting, it looks fantastic!

    Funny thing to start, we had the same waitress we have had the past two times. Very funny but we like her a lot.

    Only FROZEN Margarita they had was the Pomegranate which was very good and just tart and sweet enough.

    Before you even get a chance to look at the menu the table is served spicy warm peanuts with fresh lime zest on top. This is by far one of the best little table compliments. First courses to come were a pomegranate salad with oranges and other great compliments - it was fabulous and a new addition that we had never tried before. Another salad came with candied pecans, frisee lettuce, cranberries, and a lime vinaigrette with some sort of light mild cubed cheese that complimented the whole dish nicely. Also, in addition to the two salads, we were served warm tortilla chips with fresh guacamole and verde salsa (green) - both equally amazing. I could come here and just keep ordering guacamole until I am stuffed. Also served with the apps were pulled pork soft tacos - yummo!

    Now for my favorite part, the mushroom flat-bread. Now let it be known I am not a mushroom kind of gal and this has made me a mushroom lover! Crisp flat-bread with woody mushrooms, micro greens, light sauce and fresh veggies on top.

    Next were the main entrees, the most amazing steak with a chimichurri sauce on top with crispy tomatillos and a creamy corn risotto which is out of this world. We ate this up so fast, the risotto was perfect and the steak was something I would come back for day after day. I can't imagine every getting tired of this dish.

    As we are all sitting there thinking of how full we all are, another dish appears on the horizon. Chicken Enchilada with a habenero red sauce (not as spicy as you would think at all). OMG, I wish I wasn't so full. The enchiladas were fantabulous and was certainly one of the highlights.

    Wait - not done! Dessert, duh! Brown sugar flan with toasted hazelnuts and roasted apple on the side. Small portion but a great sweet way to end the meal.

    One bit of concern, the chef tasting menu used to be $35 a person, and now the price has increased to $45 a person. This can become a bit steep when you include drinks. They may have over priced a bit.

    A fantastic experience and a must go

    My girlfriend treated me to my 1st Jose Garces experience at Distrito for my birthday. Upon arrival were greeted by a Technicolor lobby and a friendly hostess.  I loved the atmosphere, bright colors, pink walls, and playful décor. As you make your way to the 2nd floor, you walk past a brightly colored taxi cab dining booth as well as a wall of wrestling masks that  brought a smile to my face.. I hate when places take themselves too seriously. We took our seats and I noted "Marquee" style signage adorning the dining room advertising the Chef's menu, margaritas, etc.

    As we picked up our menus, our waitress set down a bowl of spicy peanuts; a nice compliment to the meal. A Pineapple Margarita for myself, and a Hemingway for my little lady. My margarita was balanced, sneaky strong and had none of the terribly sweet sour mix that plagues so many bars. My girlfriend didn't care for her Hemingway but it piqued my curiosity. It's a Chile infused Tequila drink mixed with grapefruit juice, and served in a margarita type glass. WHOA! When they say chile infused, they were serious. You can definitely taste the smoky spice of the chile but I must say, spicy cocktails are not my bag. I can't say I would order this again, but I'm glad I tried it.

    After 5 minutes with the menu I was set on the "Diego Rivera" tasting menu and girlfriend had 4 or 5 dishes as well. I placed my order and as my girlfriend went to order our waitress interrupted and informed us the only way to do the tasting menu is to have the whole table order from it. After assurances that they could accommodate some of the entrees we both had wanted to try, we ordered the "Diego Rivera" ($45/pp) which is reflective of the best items on the menu.

    Our first course was a Traditional Guacamole with Cotija Cheese ($10), Salsa Verde ($7), and fresh chips. I was always put off by the color and consistency of Guacamole (Read: baby poop) but in the spirit of the moment, I tried it. With the grated Cotija, I found it to be mild in both taste & flavor. It's not something I would pay for, but if it came on a dish in the future, what the hell, I'll have some.

    Course two brought us a Camarones Ceviche ($9) of fresh baby shrimp, tomato, avocado, and plantain chips. This was by far and away, the most delicious, flavorful dish of Ceviche I've ever had anywhere. I would come back JUST for this dish, it was that good.

    At this point I was expecting the entrees to arrive.. I never had a tasting menu with more than five courses before.. but no, a large Chilango Chop Salad  ($8) arrived with Romaine, baby arugula, green apples, cranberries, spiced pecans, and a mild honey lime yogurt vinaigrette, The salad was light and a nice palate cleanser from the heavier Ceviche course.

    A simply fantastic Costillas flatbread ($10) (or Huaraches) of short rib, 3 chile bbq sauce, queso mixto, poblano, crema, and radish followed the salad. Yelp only gives me 5,000 characters to work with and I can be long winded, so let me say this. If you come here for one dish, let it be this ethereal Flatbread. Trust me, you will thank me later.. unless you don't eat meat.

    Yes, MORE food was on the way! We were treated to some Hamachi "Estilo OG" Tacos ($8). It consisted of fried yellowtail tuna topped with a chipotle remoulade, avocado, red cabbage and lime. This dish hit on all cyclinders.. even though it sounds like it may not.. it truly worked.

    The same did not hold true for our entrees, which were the sole reason Distrito doesn't have five stars from me. A half Lobster was served in the shell with tomatoes, capers and olives in a flavorful tomato broth. The vegetables and broth were perfect and very flavorful. The tiny bit of meat we managed to salvage from the shell was indeed, tasty. However, this Lobster was the meat equivalent of having Kate Moss for dinner. Between us, we only had 3-4 tiny seafood forks of Lobster.

    I thought to myself "I hope that's not how we end this meal" and bam! "Carne Asada" ($32)! A 12 oz NY Strip Steak with Chimichurri sauce, adobo and a creamy poblano corn rice that just melted in your mouth arrived with a side of Refried Beans ($3). I did not have a single piece of fat on my end of the steak, but my girlfriend ended up with a large chunk of fat on her end of the steak.

    Our waitress apologized for the fatty steak and anemic Lobster, promising they "will make up for it with dessert". Did they! There was Tres Leches - a cornmeal cake with roasted pineapple and coconut sorbet and Flan with cider caramel, local gala apples, toasted hazelnuts, & a roasted apple sorbet. Both dishes were a delicious, sweet way to end the meal.

    This was a superb overall experience. The food was delicious & inventive. Service was executed flawlessly; the timing of the dishes' arrival and departure was impeccable, and water glasses seemed bottomless. The tasting menu was the way to go. Do yourself a favor and get to Distrito! I know I'll be back!

Specialties

Distrito is the new restaurant concept by Jose Garces, the chef behind the acclaimed Philadelphia restaurants Amada and Tinto. Distrito is a high-energy restaurant inspired by the spirited culture and cuisine of Mexico City. The Distrito menu features Chef Garces' fresh interpretations of classic Mexican fare and beverages include over 65 tequilas and margaritas made with fresh seasonal fruits. A striking decor includes a hot pink and bright green color scheme, a wall constructed from over 600 colorful, campy masks of lucha libre, or "free fight" professional wrestlers, and a booth crafted from a Volkswagen Beetle, a typical Mexico City taxi cab.

History

Established in 2008.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
