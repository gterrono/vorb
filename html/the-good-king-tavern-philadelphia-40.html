    Came here a couple weeks back and totally forgot about it already.  Usually when I do that I throw the place three stars and say it wasn't memorable.  But when I looked back at the menu, remembered what I ate and drank, I was reminded "oh yeah, I need to go back there!".

    The good:
    - Wine - Love the "good, better, best" approach to glasses and bottles.  They go $20-$25-$30 for a bottle so even if you get the "best" you're spending less than most restaurants charge.  Was the wine any good, do I know what I was drinking?  Yes, I actually enjoyed the "better" pinot noir I had, especially for $25 a bottle.  I don't really care what brand it was.  More places should do this.
    - Steak - I had the Diane, my girlfriend the frites.  Hit the freakin' spot.  Both under $20 and plenty of food to satisfy even a big boy like myself on a Friday night (the 3 glasses of wine helped too).

    The not so good...but really not that bad.
    - Service - bristly.  The words the waitress was saying were nice but the WAY she said them wasn't particularly.  Get what I mean?
    - Steak - I thought you said it was good!  No no, I said it hit the spot...but was a little gristly.  Which is OK for a less than $20 steak, but was tough because they didn't offer proper steak knives.

    This is a solid spot I see myself coming back to many times.  The negatives really weren't that bad, the place was packed yet not overwhelming, and could easily bump to a five star spot.

    Since our first visit we've been back and had a chance to try the Duck Confit, which is out of this world.

    Not one for frequent southern French cuisine, I enjoyed a very pleasant birthday celebration at the Good King Tavern.  The restaurant is surprisingly small, but the indoor seating is close and intimate, and the recent fine weather allows for sociable dining outdoors.  To the less-experienced, such as myself, ask questions about the rotating menu because some unfamiliar French dishes are meant to be shared as appetizers and others as full entrees.  The socca is an easy choice as a starter--a sweet and savory chickpea crepe served with fresh ratatouille and lemon-dressed lettuce.  Our party ordered different meat-and-potato entrees--the morteau sausage was deemed to smoky and barely touched for the rest of the evening. The duck confit with fingerling potatos and mahi mahi were equally pleasing, but the spicy and flavorful falafel and rich frites were the crowd's favorite.  At dinner's end, the restaurant staff overheard us celebrating the birthday and they happily and generously served chocolate pot de creme as a complimentary closer.  We are damn certain to return, if not only for this sweet dessert, but for the oft-talked of brussel sprouts, falafel and flatbread, and robust French menu.

Specialties

The Good King Tavern is a cozy neighborhood spot located in the heart of Bella Vista at 7th & Kater.  With a casual French-inspired menu, daily chalkboard specials, classic cocktails, draft beers and wines by glass, bottle and pichet, there is something for everyone to enjoy.

History

Established in 2013.

The Good King was established in November 2013 by father-daughter duo Bernard and Chloe Grigri. With roots in Alsace and Provence, the Grigris developed the casual tavern-concept driving The Good King today. Chef Paul Lyons has led the kitchen since day one, cooking up classics like duck confit and steak frites, as well as more imaginative French-inspired dishes featured on our ever-changing chalkboards.
