    I went with my 7 year old son for a schools out and Dad is off fun day.

    It took a little convincing that this would be a fun place to go. But, once he was there he was happy.
    The appearance is a little like a Starbucks location. Just that they are selling chocolate items instead. Kind of a hip feel to it.

    The Secret kids menu is awesome for the most part. Not only do they combine foods kids like to order when out, they add fun components to it.
    My son had a do it yourself hot chocolate. It comes out with chocolate in a dish, a straw spoon, and a cup with a candle under it. You mix in as much chocolate as you want and stir. He loved it. It had extra warm milk on the side.
    He also ordered the standard Mac in Cheese. I did not read the menu carefully to know that he would get a syringe of marinara sauce to squeeze in as he liked. He squeezed some in the crock and he also squeezed it directly into his mouth. His only complaint, he wanted more sauce. Yeah, he wanted to play with it more.

    So my order was straight forward as well. I ordered an Alice Cup (a milk shake) and loved it. You pick between the various varieties and I went with a basic 80's shake. It was chocolate goodness with every sip. As it came out it had a small scoop of ice cream with hardened chocolate (magic shell) on the top. It really was delicious.

    I also ordered a Brenner Burger. It was a large size and I could hardly finish it. It had some spice hit to it. It was well balanced in flavor. It came with a small side of delicious waffle fries and a specialty spicy type sauce. I'd like more fries and less burger with the meal. But, that was just me. No complaints about it.

    I did not order dessert, I was already stuffed. And I knew my son would order and not finish one himself. He had a Chocolate Pizza. Oh my, this is a child's menu item? Is it allowed to taste that good? It was two large slices of pizza crust with milk and white chocolate on top, slightly warmed and gooey. It came with marshmallows to put on top and gummy bears. A small size of vanilla ice cream. Of course, my son had the gummy bears and ice cream, barely touching the pizza. We took a slice home in fact. I ate most of the other piece.

    Excellent experience overall. My only complaint to the place. I think the waitress could have been a little more friendly. A little more attentive at times, like when we had dirty dishes sitting on the table even as the next order came out. She did show my son what he was supposed to do. It took time to get the bill as well.

    It is a little pricey as well. The kids menu for example, everything had a price to it, instead of a package deal like other kid friendly places.
    But, it was a fun chocolaty experience for a day.

    This is a lesson in how to ruin a restaurant.

    Ate here many times soon after they opened. They had AWESOME chocolate desserts. The chocolate sauce they called "fresh" was unlike anything I had ever tasted--thick, unctuous, and powerfully flavorful. Not surprisingly, the place was always packed--if you didn't have a reservation, you waited 45 minutes for a table.

    Returned last PM after a 2 year hiatus for desserts. What a disappointment!

    First clue should have been the sparse crowd--the place was way less than half filled. The menu was also very different with far fewer choices. We ordered: a chocolate heart cake (AKA lava cake) was overcooked, tasted burnt and had no melted chocolate in the center. A request for a scoop of vanilla ice cream with the aforementioned "fresh" sauce resulted in a 1/8 cup scoop of almost completely melted ice cream. There was--no exaggeration here, no more than 1 tablespoon of actual ice cream in the bowl floating in melted ice cream. It came with their "milk chocolate ganache"--I was told they stopped doing the "fresh sauce." The ganache was thin, watery, and lacking in chocolate flavor.

    What happened to this place?

    Some questioning of the staff solved the mystery. About two years ago, the Brenner organization underwent some sort of reorganization. It sounds as if the rights to the Brenner name along with the existing stores in the United States were sold to an independent restaurant group. (Presumably not a franchise...had Brenner franchised their name, they would retain control over food quality).

    Since then, according to the staff, the new company has been "making some changes"--quite a few by my reckoning. And the changes coincided with our menu selections. The "fresh" sauce was a casualty of the new management--outright eliminated from the menu, and the recipe for the molten cakes had also been altered.

    Based on last night's experience this exercise, presumably to reduce costs, has been done at a HUGE sacrifice in quality. Staff training is apparently not a priority, either. If it were, my waiter, who claimed nearly 2 years experience at the site, would never have served a dish of melted ice cream.

    AWFUL, AWFUL, AWFUL and what sheer foolishness, both on the part of the Brenner organization for allowing their name to be used this way, and for the investing company who purchased a successful and amazing concept and are now in the process of destroying it.

    Unless I hear something to the contrary, this place is off my list and unless they get their act together, I'd wager that the place will be out of business within 2 years.
