    Jake's Sandwich Board often gets the short end of the stick when the Philly roast pork greats are considered. Sandwich aficionados often refer their advisees (rightfully, I may add) to Paesano's or Dinic's for their fix of this staple Philly food. Frankly, I do not understand why Jake's gets ignored; their product is on par, if not superior to, the ones offered at those aforementioned joints. To be clear, my allegiance for best pork sandwich lies with Paesano's Arista, but Jake's is a close second.

    This mainly stems from Jake's roast meat, as it offers a tender, unparalleled distillation of porcine flavor. Pork sandwiches here are customizable, and toppings range from the requisite long hots to the uncommon cracklings. The rolls are standard Philly fare, not quite as deliciously crusty as Liscio's or Sarcone's product but better than whatever mush Dinic's employs. The brisket is tasty too, check out the brisket cheesesteak for a superlative rendition of the Philly tourist classic. Do skip their standard cheesesteaks though, these fall flat in comparison to more dedicated shops.

    Jake's also slightly edges out their competition in two other regards: $5 pork sandwiches on Mondays (eat your heart out, Subway), and having a deep fryer. $2 nets you a nice taste of delicious fried pickles, and the other, slightly more expensive deep fried offerings make for pleasant munching. You can even get sandwich toppings on fries instead of bread just in case you want to shave a couple extra years off of your life. Head to Jake's and get a pork sandwich, some fried pickles, and a few extra napkins for the juices that'll inevitably drip down your chin. You likely won't miss the more popular options.
