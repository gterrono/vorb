    We stopped in this past weekend for lunch while walking around Washington Square. Having loved Talula's Garden, I imagined the food would be just as fresh and local, and the decor just as cozy. Both were true of Talula's Daily.

    We ordered a few things to split...beet soup, tomato and mozzarella salad, and honey mustard chicken. They were whisked away to the kitchen to be heated up, and were brought over to our table. All were very tasty, despite the absence of mozzarella in our salad.

    The idea of the space is great, prepared goods to be taken out or eaten there, with a variety of gourmet groceries filling the shelves. A very unique spot combining a gourmet grocery store with a casual dining experience. Prix fixe dinners are also served most nights, full of seasonal ingredients...a more affordable and timely alternative to Talula's Table.

    On the way out, I had a bit of a coffee miscommunication and was given a free cookie for my troubles. Staff is friendly and accommodating. A great spot in the beautiful Washington Square...more spots like this should open in the area.

    I think I finally figured out why they call it Talula's Daily. It is because every day there is something new you haven't tried or something you want that is missing.

    One day there are cupcakes then the next, no cupcakes. After making the mistake of splitting their awesome red velvet cupcake with my bf, I journeyed back the next day to purchase another red velvet cupcake. Turns out there were no red velvet cupcakes or any other cupcakes for that matter. After asking one of the staff, I was told that what baked goods are made for the day are determined by the chef. There is no schedule. So if I wanted a few red velvet cupcakes, I would need to order some. Big sigh. Pretty much every two days, I would go looking for my red velvet, and left with something else instead. Here is a review of a few things that I am a fan of:

    1) Chocolate chip cookie: When served warm or a few hours out of the oven, it is one of the best ccc I have ever had. I have seen them wrapped in plastic recently on the side. However, after a slight squeeze of the plastic wrapped cookie, I know that they cannot compare to that fresh from the oven taste. These used to be $1.50 but now they are $2.00...

    2) Red velvet cake: Unfortunately Tartes Bakery loses the trophy for best red velvet in the city. However, at least I can count on Tartes if I am having a rvc craving.

    3) Quiche: They have all different kinds. I am not even sure if they make the same combinations of quiche daily. At $4.50, it is a breakfast steal. I split this with my bf and inside the quiche was packed with turkey bacon, turkey breast, arugula, and cheese.

    4) Bear claw: Delicious, but if I only have 3 dollars to spend for breakfast, it is not my first choice.

    5) Carrot Cake: Down home goodness. Used to be $2.50 now it's $3.00 argh...

    6) Pumpkin Bread: Snacked on this for a few hours. Great flavoring. A subtle punch of pumpkin flavor. Crisp on the edges and goes along well with their cappuccino.

    7) Scones: Apple and Ginger, Raspberry and Lemon, Mixed Berry...all great. Mixed berry is my personal favorite just because lemon in the morning with milk doesn't sit well with me. The scones  taste even better when you get one of the day old scones for $1.50 instead of the $3.00 that they usually go for.

    8) Mocha Cupcake: Usually I am not a frosting eater but it was so smooth and creamy, I didn't even notice that I was eating it.

    Things that I am over:

    1) That sandwich and salad bar! Listen, it tastes fresh but I just can't get with paying $8 and up for something that is pre-made.

    2) Where do I sit? Where do I stand? Where do I order? When it gets very crowded in here, I have no idea how this works. i just throw myself in a line or at a table and hope no one growls at me.

    Haven't written a review in a long time... but this place stopped me in my tracks!

    Accidentally stumbled upon Talula's "DAILY" with my boyfriend and mother after valeting our car with the intention to dine at Talula's Garden, two doors down.  Unfortunatlely, to our unpleasant surprise, it was not open until 4pm.  Next time we are in Philadelphia, we do fully intend on returning to Talula's Garden during their operating hours because the place was so aesthetically pleasing and I've read a plethora of wonderful reviews.... so, we can't wait!

    So.... (Back to Talula's Daily)....We came to a consensus that our hunger could not wait until 4pm, and my boyfriend and I needed to catch a flight later that afternoon, so we decided to walk to Talula's Daily.  At a first glance from the front entrance, it 'seemed as though' our only options would likely be hot tea and coffee with pastries and possibly a pre-made deli sandwich.

    Boy... were we wrong!!  Once we entered this adorable little cafe... we soon realized that it offered much more than just coffee, tea, and pastries.  The options in the deli case were so enticing and mouthwatering that it was hard to narrow it down to just a few items to share! We ordered the kale and watermelon salad (which i fell in love w/ so much - I now make this salad at home with my own little revision), a mushroom quiche, grilled salmon, a sauteed vegetable medley, and the carrot cake.

    We chose a cute round table in the back, grabbed our silverware, sharing plates, and some cups of water from the self-serve area under the window sill, and waited for them to heat our food while we oo-ed and ahh-ed over how cute and trendy the decor was... with so many vibrant colors, unique trinkets, kitchen and cooking essentials for sale with a mini market (selling fancy pastas, jams, crackers, etc).... My boyfriend and I were recently in Paris, and the decor and feel of this spot reminded us so much of our favorite little hidden treasure in Paris~!!

    After the chef adorned our table with the dishes we ordered, which were served in all different colored and shaped serving bowls, it was displayed so beautifully, that we could not wait to dig in!  We indulged in a little tasting feast of each dish and barely even spoke to each other during the experience, because we were enjoying the food so much... haha... each item we ordered tasted fresh, gourmet, well-balanced, and light.  Not what you would "normally expect" from food at a "café style" fast dining luncheon, nothing tasted greasy or like it's been on the shelf a day too long... and we were pleasantly surprised and satisfied to perfection.  The carrot cake was a great way to finish off our meal... not too sweet, just enough icing, & perfectly cakey ... viola!

    Please do yourself a favor, and stop by this cute little spot conveniently located at Washington Square.... it is sure to satisfy your appetite, and please your palate!

    Cheers!!

Specialties

Talula's Daily is a hub to shop, eat, drink, and congregate over real, wholesome food and ingredients. Market-goers are greeted by the scents of crusty, warm bread, freshly roasted coffee, and local farmstead cheeses. An assortment of organic juices are squeezed on-site, and a coffee bar serving Atelier La Colombe is the perfect place for a pick-me-up or to linger at one of the café tables. A counter and refrigerated section offer creative sandwiches for vegetarians and omnivores alike. We feature heirloom recipes, such as kasha & bowties, stuffed pork chops, monkey bread, spinach pie, stuffed shells, and old-fashioned tapioca pudding. Quick takeaway meals such as vegetable lasagna, and grilled fish are also prepared for enjoying later. The Daily offers catering and a picnic program of baskets filled with seasonal goodies. When the market closes, the restaurant opens. Chef de Cuisine Scott Megill and his team cook up a family-style supper for guests of one to twelve.

History

Established in 2013.

Talula's Daily opened in July, 2013 and is adjacent to Talula's Garden in the historic Ayer Condominium. The market builds upon the Talula's dining experience as an easier-going alternative, as well as the opportunity for customers to bring the Talula's way of eating and cooking into their own homes.

Meet the Business Owner

Aimee O. Business Owner

One of the originals in the farm-to-table movement in Philadelphia dining, Aimee Olexy truly understands what hospitality means.  Aimee wanted a way to showcase cheese and wine in beautiful Washington Park, near the Liberty Bell, in the great historic area of philadelphia.
