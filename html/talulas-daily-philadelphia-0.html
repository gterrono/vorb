    Listed in Brunch-a-Licious, Yummy in my Tummy, Caffeinated

    Monthly Suppers here are perfection.  That's the long and short of it.   You want to have an amazing meal, fantastically paced with service that is just beyond?  Come here for their nightly secret supper clubs.  It truly cannot be beat. $55 for five courses, $35 for beverage pairings.  You shall lick your plate clean.  You shall leave in the happiest of food comas. You will want to come back and back again. Done and done.

    Since I have had a kiddo, I definitely do not go out as much as I used to, and so when I do go out for a dinner, I want to go somewhere that will deliver.  Talula's Daily is it.  It's a bit pricey, admittedly, but it's an experience that never, ever disappoints.

    Seasonal tasting menus change monthly.  They gladly accommodate any and all dietary restrictions without batting a eye.  Gourmet and elegant to the nines.

    Eating here, feels like you are at home, having a private chef cook for you as you linger and laugh with friends over amazing food.  It's a leisurely delectable procession served on lovely china with Laguiole cutlery, amidst a beautiful table setting.  The beverage pairings are exemplary and right on point.  For example, a cuvée Béatrice paired perfectly with a brocoli gruyere soup topped with horseradish, while a Washington sauvignon blanc elevated a sweet potato gnudi with brussels sprouts, apple and cabbage.  The menus here are always well executed.  

    Not to mention, last night I had a pumpkin marscapone cheesecake (with pumpkin seed brittle) and salted caramel that left me nearly orgasmic.  Food at Talula's Daily will do that to you.  What are you waiting for?

    Count me in for one of their prix fix menus every month.  It has become our favorite restaurant in Philly in no time.  I think this is because it is a magical restaurant where you don't have to make any decisions and the menu changes monthly.

    My friends and I did the May Supper tasting a few months back and had a great time!

    We loved the intimate atmosphere, adorable décor (all anthropologie), and service was very attentive and super friendly. The waitress even insisted on taking our picture once she heard we do this rotating dinner series to make sure to make time for each other. We've had some great meals through this arrangement (each friend picks a spot when it's her turn) and some tough contenders (Serpico, Vedge) and this was one of the best experience we had. I had also done the $75 course tasting the night before at Fork and preferred this one!

    The food was excellent. Every course was delicious (see pics for what we ate, highlights for me were the ramp pesto pasta and the halibut, and duh, the cheese plate).

    Some Tips:

    --Check out the menu. It's seasonal so if you're not into root vegetables, maybe wait until the spring.
    --If you're into cocktails, they can bring over the Talula's Garden menu (and those drinks are delish).
    --Make a rezzy. There's not much seating so spots fill up!
    --Be ready for the money to add up $55 is great, but with 2 drinks, tax, and tip, it's gonna be about $100. (That's my main gripe with this place-with so many great tasting menu options, this one is a bit on the pricy side).

    Highly recommend for a special occasion or if you're a baller like that!

    Lunch review: quick service, good selection, wonderful atmosphere.

    As this is an European style of dining, it may be kind of tricky at first. All salads, sandwiches are packed in to-go containers. So that's really easy if you're taking it to go. If you're staying to eat, they can heat up your sandwich/soup or toss your salads for you. Just bring your food to the cashier, pay for your food, ask for service you would like and they'll bring it out to you. Grab your silverware from the table and pick your seat.

    Seating is limited, with a long community dining table, a few smaller tables and a bar along the wall.

    Food selection: superb. The salads are great (I always go for the turkey/cheese with green goddess dressing). They always have a nice selection of daily specials next to the register. I've tried their roasted cauliflower salad and fig crepes. Both are incredible.

Specialties

Talula's Daily is a hub to shop, eat, drink, and congregate over real, wholesome food and ingredients. Market-goers are greeted by the scents of crusty, warm bread, freshly roasted coffee, and local farmstead cheeses. An assortment of organic juices are squeezed on-site, and a coffee bar serving Atelier La Colombe is the perfect place for a pick-me-up or to linger at one of the café tables. A counter and refrigerated section offer creative sandwiches for vegetarians and omnivores alike. We feature heirloom recipes, such as kasha & bowties, stuffed pork chops, monkey bread, spinach pie, stuffed shells, and old-fashioned tapioca pudding. Quick takeaway meals such as vegetable lasagna, and grilled fish are also prepared for enjoying later. The Daily offers catering and a picnic program of baskets filled with seasonal goodies. When the market closes, the restaurant opens. Chef de Cuisine Scott Megill and his team cook up a family-style supper for guests of one to twelve.

History

Established in 2013.

Talula's Daily opened in July, 2013 and is adjacent to Talula's Garden in the historic Ayer Condominium. The market builds upon the Talula's dining experience as an easier-going alternative, as well as the opportunity for customers to bring the Talula's way of eating and cooking into their own homes.

Meet the Business Owner

Aimee O. Business Owner

One of the originals in the farm-to-table movement in Philadelphia dining, Aimee Olexy truly understands what hospitality means.  Aimee wanted a way to showcase cheese and wine in beautiful Washington Park, near the Liberty Bell, in the great historic area of philadelphia.
