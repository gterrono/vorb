    I came here with my parents on a weekday afternoon. A delightful brunch place with a semi-long wait of 30-45 minutes. The lines may go by quicker if people leave (3 or 4 groups didn't show up during our wait), but don't count on it!

    We got a nice window seat inside the restaurant where I got some nice lighting of our food. The restaurant is infamously busy--they could afford a couple more waiters/waitresses.

    I ordered the Vegan French Toast ($12) slathered in raspberry coulis with strawberries, whipped cream, and maple syrup. The french toast was done in a soy-based batter. The toast was a little dry for my taste. I didn't eat the "crust" of the toast. I did enjoy the sweet and tart raspberry coulis, though. I'd probably order pancakes next time.

    My parents shared a Three Egg Omelet ($10) with choice of bacon, spinach, and mushrooms. It comes with a side of toast and homefries, which we ordered in the signature. Signature homefries are cooked with onions and green pepper. The potatoes are cut into small slices rather than diced into large pieces. They were super tasty!

    Also got the coffee ($3) which was nice and strong and a small pineapple juice ($2).

    I know they're a busy restaurant, but I wished they didn't hand us the bill before we got our food...
