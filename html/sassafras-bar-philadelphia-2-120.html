    In a neighborhood characterized by the trendy, the bright, the loud, the obnoxious, Sassafras is a diamond in the rough. Warm lighting sets a rosy glow to the inside , unassuming from the outside. In fact, were it not for the suggestion of my date, I likely wouldn't have given this spot a second glance. Thank goodness for the experiences of others (though I'm sure Yelp would've steered me here eventually....)

    A Friday night around 11 pm wouldn't seem like the ideal time to suddenly decide to switch bars in OC, but Sassafras welcomed us with open arms on a not-so-chilly second day of Spring. Though there were a number of small parties dining and drinking, there was more than enough space for us to sidle up to the bar and snag some stools. The drink menu was diverse without feeling like a novella, offering a little something for everyone, from their take on a Manhattan served in a martini glass (I steered clear from that one, though it smelled lovely, since bourbon and I aren't too tight nowadays) and a Dark  & Stormy with a decent pour, an excellent bite from the ginger beer, and a smooth lime finish. The bartenders lent expertise and sophistication to the atmosphere, looking dapper and dandy. The prices weren't outrageous, especially relative to some other quality spirits spots. It was obvious that my fellow patrons felt at ease, shooting the breeze with the employees bringing steaming plates of burgers and fries to their tables. We were just there for a well-crafted cocktail, but I did spot a fair-priced draft list of quality craft beers.

    Whether it was the dark & stormy or the location itself, I left Sassafras feeling refreshed. And even though it was to emerge into the hustle and bustle of midnight in OC, it carried me home with a smile.

    REVIEW: The monthly meeting of the Facebook group BURGER CLUB PHL (yes, we are that geeky!) was held at Sassafras Bar in Old City this past week.

    Actually, I had never even heard of Sassafras before, despite having walked by it many times on the way to nearby Khyber Pass.  However, it is definitely not the new kid on the block, having been around for more than 35 years!  Bars don't last that long unless they have something going for them, but still, Sassafras appears to fly a bit under the radar, at least for most of the douchey after-work Old City bar set.  Which is just perfectly fine by me.

    A long, thin, small bar and dining area (seating perhaps only 25 or so) decorated in dark wood and tap list-painted mirrors, Sassafras feels cozy and intimate.  Not stuck-up-and-pretentious intimate, but more of a neighborhood bar that has some real old-fashioned style.  Just siddle up to the bar for a perfectly poured Guinness (complete with shamrock-emblazoned head!), or a short list of microbrews from Irish bartender Neill Laughlin.

    Sassafras has a  casual but somewhat "gastropuby" menu, modest in length, with sufficient diversity to keep you interested (hummus, wings, escargot, fish & chips, steak frites, Mongolian dumplings, caesar salad...you get the idea).  Of course we were there for the famous burgers, which one of my fellow club members remembered as being the best burger she tried at the Philly Burger Bash.  And having read many positive Yelp reviews, I knew exactly what I wanted to try (see photos):

    OSTRICH BURGER ($14): Medium rare, with extra applewood-smoked bacon ($2), sunny side-up egg ($1), on a brioche bun, and included sweet potato fries and lemon aioli.

    Yes, OSTRICH MEAT!  Never having had ostrich in any form before, I couldn't pass this chance up.  But for the slightly less adventuresome, Sassafras also serves Bison, Lamb, Sirloin, and Veggie burgers, all of which get rave reviews.

    My burger was perfectly med rare, and surprisingly flavorful, well-seasoned, with a good sear.  Thank God, ostrich meat turned out to not remotely resemble chicken (my fear going in).  My only slight criticism was that ostrich is apparently very lean (so don't order it over medium, and preferably med rare), and although not dry by any means, I wish it was a bit more juicy.  Perhaps next time I'll try one of the optional sauces, such as Bordelaise (they also have a good selection of gourmet cheeses you can top your burger with). And BTW, personally, I prefer a good brioche bun for my burger, and this was a nice one, so don't give me any flack for that.

    But, as good as the burger was, the sweet potato fries almost stole the show.  Although sweet potato fries always taste great, the limp, undercooked versions that most bars serve generally disappoint me.  These, however, were decidedly crispy, not too thick, and came with a creamy lemon aioli for dipping.  Some of the best I've ever had!  Not a large serving with the burger, so next time I'll definitely order extra.

    Service was friendly and efficient, although we did have to wait a bit for the burgers, but not excessively so.  Parking is easy, if you don't mind paying $16 at the open-air lot next door, and there are plenty of other lots in the area.

    Oh, before I forget, another unique feature of Sassafras is the live music on Tuesday - Thursday.  Last night, music was provided by a pair of instrumental jazz guitarists, who adeptly playing away in the background, making conversation easy and most enjoyable.

    Great burgers, live music, and a well poured Guinness?  But no douchebags???  Sign me up Sassafras!

    Listed in These Are a Few of My Favorite Things

    Sassafras is hands down the best bar in Philadelphia. Over the course of my two years in Philadelphia, I have logged many hours at the bar, sipping cocktails, listening to live music, and eating delicious meals. Allow me to break down some of the many wonderful things I love about Sass:

    The Food: I have yet to have a disappointing meal here. My go-tos are the calamari and the fried pickles, because the batter is just amazing, and the sauces - aioli for the pickles and something sweet and tangy for the calamari - are fantastic every time. Do ask about the specials, though, because there is almost always something creative on the menu that will make even the least adventurous eaters feel like foodies, and sometimes they come with a side of sweet potato fries, which I swear I could eat all day long.

    The Drinks: Owner/Bartender Neill created a drink just for me. I'm not going to tell you what it's called because I'm selfish like that (and I'm also afraid you'll spoil the ingredients, which I would prefer remain a blissful mystery). Suffice to say, it is magical, and I order it every time. I also love the large selection of cocktails they roll out for special occasions. Keep an eye on the Facebook page, because I'm sure one of the autumn concoctions will tickle your fancy!

    The Place: Sass is undeniably warm and inviting. The real gem (in my opinion) is the ladies' room, which features a beautiful mural on the walls, a comfy couch, and a full-length mirror to aid in the prevention of wardrobe malfunctions.

    The People: The best part of Sass is truly the wonderful people who work there. I had only to tell them "Neill makes me this thing that's pink and fruity" and each of them came up with their own unique version - all delicious. They are friendly, warm, welcoming people who are always good for a laugh and/or a slice of wisdom, and they always go the extra mile for their patrons. Just last night, Angie poured a bucket of ice water over my head out front for the ALS Ice Bucket Challenge.

    But why take my word for how incredible this place is? Go see for yourself, order a drink and a plate of sweet potato fries, and strike up a conversation with your bar tender. I hope we meet at the bar!

Specialties

A fixture on Second Street for over 35 years, Sassafras is the Grand Dame of neighborhood bars. Sophisticated. Timeless. Classic. Serving dinner every evening from 5 to 10 p.m.,with cocktail service until 2 a.m. A classic, cozy bar in the heart of Olde City.

History

Established in 1976.

A fixture on Second Street for over 35 years, Sassafras is the Grand Dame of neighborhood bars. Among the bright lights of Old City lies this old world pub with modern flavor. Sassafras is like your father's bar but in a good way. A warm and inviting atmosphere with bartenders who know how a good drink should be made. We boast a splendid selection of Single Malt Scotches, Whiskies and Bourbon.

Be sure to check the mirrors for our constantly changing list of hand selected beers and wines.

Our eclectic menu has something to satisfy every palate - whether it is our traditional French Onion Soup, award winning burgers or smothered steak sandwich.
