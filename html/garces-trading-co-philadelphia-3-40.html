    I've been here twice.  The first time (April 1st) was more just to check things out--we sampled some oils and got some pastries--madeleines, caneles, maybe some macarons.  The pastries were very good and reasonably priced.  Having the taste of some of the oils lingering in the back of my throat for most of the night was less enjoyable, but I suppose that's what you get for doing a shot of truffle oil.  Coffee was also pretty good.

    On the more recent visit, April 25, we got some more pastries (I think the price may have been $4 for half a dozen small items on the first visit and $6 the second time, although that may be my imagination), which were good again.  We ordered the chef's charcuterie selection, a deep dish pizza, the baba au rhum, and the spring verine (a panna cotta with grapefruit and nuts).  The pizza was simple and well-executed, with nice sweetness from the tomatoes, plenty of mozzerella, and a buttery biscuitlike crust.  The charcuterie plate seemed a bit pricey for what we got.  I didn't love the meats we got, but that's because I'm not a huge cured meat fan.  The baba was good and the verine was good, although the ramekin it was served in made it hard to get at the different layers.

    I'm always conscious of value, and I think it's possible to get great food at great prices here if you're careful.  I did notice a jar of Nutella in the market area that was marked up quite a bit, and I can imagine spending quite a bit on cheese, oils, meats, etc if you struggle with self-control.  (I struggle, but this time, at least, I won the battle.)

    Listed in Places that make you feel good and smart, East of Broad, Best Neighborhood (ever), Yelp Approved!, Business Friendly Lunch Spots

    Strolled in on a breezy Thursday night and my 2 friends and I found ourselves subjected to a reasonable 20 minute wait. No problem - it gave us a chance to sample the olive oils and vinegars, peruse the wine selection, and gape at the meats, cheeses, and waiters. The pomegranate vinegar was outstanding, the vinos muy barato, and the servers well-toned and hunky.

    There's an endless number of awesome things about Garces Trading, but more than anything, I love the accessibility of gourmet items. The carefully selected wines are all priced within reason (our Tempranillo was only $10), the $28 take home dinner for two (coq au vin) made me wish I had someone to cozy up next to and feed hand-to-mouth style, and the dine-in menu keeps things creative.

    It'd be easy to compare this place to Tria, but unlike my favorite wine bar, Garces dishes out the most ambrosial spaghetti and meatballs in the city! You can always go the wine and cheese route if you'd prefer to leave pleasantly satiated, but if you'd rather roll outta there, order the Perchitelli & Meatballs - stuff is le good!

    Garces has made a name in Philadelphia as the poster child for boutique cuisine, and this stop is not a bad choice for a quick lunch.

    I have to admit for the record that I only did take-away from the market, but the food was extraordinary. We had ordered a bit from each genre. The italian sandwich with lemon confit was too die for. I would have never thought to add lemon to that overly salty mix of cured italian meats (bresaola, hot coppa, melted taleggio) but the contrast blended amazingly well to please the palette. The Pappardelle with Lamb Ragu was unbelievable. I never cared much for lamb, but the braised shredded lamb that arrived with the perfectly cooked handmade pasta was unreal. It melted in my mouth and was euphoric.  The cous de gras was the Cannelle's which are the most underestimated desserts for their lack of flair. It was like eating air, which this subtle deluge of banana flavor.

    It was worth every penny, and would return any day.

    Garces fan for life! I think this has been my favorite Garces restaurant so far (haven't been to Tinto yet, so I can't really include that one). Trading Co was beyond delicious!

    The place looks fabulous. It is located on 11th and Locust, an absolute charming part of Philly, especially during this time of the year (spring). They have tables inside, ranging from small ones (seating 2 people) to larger tables that can seat a large party or several parties together. They also have seating outside that looked absolutely fabulous! We sat inside because it was a little too warm!

    When you walk into Garces Trading Co, you can either sit down at a table or walk around and look at the cheeses and meats, the desserts and the wines. We decided to buy a bottle of wine to have during our  meal and we were immediately helped by this amazingly knowledgeable woman. Her recommendation was spot on, and best of all, the wine was not expensive and very tasty.

    Our waiter was very attentive. Not once did our water glasses or wine glasses go empty. He took his time to recommend and explain things on the menu, and he even made a little bit of conversation.

    We had the chef's selection of three cheeses with condiments. Don't exactly remember what the cheeses were but one was soft, the other semisoft, and the last one hard. The condiments went perfectly well with each cheese (amazing how they know how to pair them). My favorite was the cherry fig marmalade, but the lavender honey and the fennel apricot mostardo were great as well.

    Next we had the chef's selection of charcuterie. This was great as well. Everything went great with the wine.

    My boyfriend had the Colorado lamb chops served with heirloom potato salad and an amazing glaze. Wow, I will never stop loving lamb. I had the funghi pizza, which was incredibly rich and flavorful. Simply delicious. (Look at the pictures I posted)

    Finally we ordered dessert and coffee, both excellent. We ordered a small coffee press that wasn't really that small. In fact, the bf had two cups of coffee, I (who normally don't drink coffee) had one cup, and there was still enough in the fancy little pot to fill another cup and a half. The raspberry marjolaine went great with the coffee and was light enough to finish off an already amazing meal.

    I think that what I liked best about Garces Trading Co was that you could either come in for a full meal, or just have a bottle of wine and some appetizers and still have a great time.

Specialties

Garces Trading Company is a full-service bar and restaurant focused on Iron Chef Jose Garces' own favorite dishes from his travels to cafes throughout Western Europe, with a focus on Italy, Spain and France.  Our thoughtful menu of cafe staples is served throughout the day, and our bar offers a wealth of cocktails, wines and beers that pair beautifully with our menu.  Our on-site PLCB Wine Boutique, the first of its kind, makes a great resource either before sitting down to dinner (corkage fees apply) or to complement one of our decadent gift baskets, available in our newly redesigned retail section.

History

Established in 2010.

Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a refined, Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, a contemporary American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago, IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale, AZ and Palm Springs, CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the landmark Revel Resort. Today, in addition to our much-lauded restaurants around the country, Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
