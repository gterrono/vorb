    Distrito gets one of its four stars for this sole, rare bonus:  I've never had a weak drink there.  Every other place in the city seems to under-tequila or under-brandy their magarita/sangria.  Distrito doesn't.  

    Now, that may be because I've only ever ordered the pricier version (the Jefe, 15.00), so I am defitinitely paying for that extra tequila goodness.  Still, a bonus point.  

    I give it four stars instead of five for two reasons:  it's a bit loud, expensive, and cavernous for my taste, and the hoards of Penn students mean that if you forget your ID, you WILL be carded and there will be absolutely no wiggle room re: your partner ordering drinks that are for you, etc.  

    The food has always been excellent and fairly imaginative.  It doesn't quite measure up to the best haute Mexican I've had (that would be in Houston), but for being in Philly, not bad at all.  I recommend the tasting menu, the tuna ceviche, and anything involving mushrooms or huitlacoche.

    This place was busy on the (Wednesday) night I went.  The hostess, servers, runners, bussers, etc. were all very friendly, helpful and courteous.  It's expensive, but its definitely worth it considering the quality of the product and their emphasis on detail.

    The idea behind this restaurant is presenting very traditional mexican flavors and products in the form of tapas (or small plate items).  

    The food was tasty, however, a few criticisms:  The hamachi ceviche was not seasoned well.  I expected it to be have more salt and acidity.  The sorbet was a nice touch though.  Although, it was actually physically difficult to eat with a knife and fork...a little awkward.  

    Also, one of the dishes I got with hanger steak was cooked to the chef's desire.  However, it was VERY rare and VERY chewy.  I thought they could've done better with that one.

    Also, the toritillas were a little redundant on the menu.  I know that tortillas are pretty common in mexican cuisine, but it was a little boring after a while.  Like I said, though, the food was all very tasty.  The margaritas were awesome.  I had two and got totally smashed.

    Although I'm skeptical of these superstar chef/entrepreneurs monopolizing the Philadelphia restaurant scene, I'm happy Jose Garces opened up this spot in "University City".  Its bringing business to a place outside of Center City and changing the geography of the Philly scene.

    If you're looking for a place to have a good time, eat amazing food, and be in the presence of an ambiance so distinguished and unique that you will not believe your eyes, then you MUST eat at Distrito. This restaurant opened roughly a year ago, and I have loved it so much that last night was the 4th time I have gone since it has opened. This restaurant has the same dining style as the rest of Garces' restaurants in that the plates, or at least most of them, are served tapas style, which means small plates for the table to share. The food is explosive, the building itself is neon-lit and full of vibrant additions like Mexican wrestler masks on the wall and a viewing of "Tres Amigos" at all times.

    Decor - (5/5)
    Service - (5/5)
    Plates:

    Nachos (Encarnacion) (4/5) - These nachos come filled with skirt steak, beans, and a plethora of different cheeses. This is a great starter, although it is very filling, for those who don't like to eat much early while dining.
    Flatbread Pizza (Los Hongos) (4.5/5) - This flatbread pizza is a mushroom-filled one that comes bursting with flavor. The mix of the cheeses and the mushrooms makes for a delicious little treat between nachos and tacos.
    Flatbread Pizza (Guisados) (5/5) - This flatbread pizza comes topped with shreds of boneless short ribs and a bbq sauce that is undeniable. This pizza is mind-blowing. Beware: you will want more than one.
    Kobe Beef Tacos (Carne Kobe) (5/5) - Yes, you will die and go to heaven after biting into these. They are out of this world, and the texture of the meat is so fine that it'll simply fall down your throat with ease.
    Rabbit (Amarillo Moles) (4.5/5) - This tapas in very different in that it comes in a small cast-iron with a bed of rice and its sauce. This is more difficult to share, but it is still very excellent. I suggest this if you are a rabbit fan.
    Duck Breast (Poblano Moles) (5/5) - This tapas is similar to the rabbit in terms of presentation. Nonetheless, the flavor packed into the duck is out of this world. Each piece is equally rich. Get this and the kobe beef tacos, and you'll be happy as you can possibly be.

    Overall, I could write about more plates, but that would just take forever. This restaurant has been voted Best of Philly New Restaurant this summer, and rightfully so. Come with a bunch of friends, and enjoy. Remember, due to the small portions, you have more control of what your check looks like, so choose wisely...Oh, yeah. 5/5. No doubt about it.

Specialties

Distrito is the new restaurant concept by Jose Garces, the chef behind the acclaimed Philadelphia restaurants Amada and Tinto. Distrito is a high-energy restaurant inspired by the spirited culture and cuisine of Mexico City. The Distrito menu features Chef Garces' fresh interpretations of classic Mexican fare and beverages include over 65 tequilas and margaritas made with fresh seasonal fruits. A striking decor includes a hot pink and bright green color scheme, a wall constructed from over 600 colorful, campy masks of lucha libre, or "free fight" professional wrestlers, and a booth crafted from a Volkswagen Beetle, a typical Mexico City taxi cab.

History

Established in 2008.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
