        I know many have already reviewed this Philly high end hot spot, but I just wanted to add my two cents worth.  It consistently shows up on best in Philadelphia lists, and well that it should.  For years now it has been one of restaurant impresario Stephen Starr's many successes.  What is especially noteworthy is this restaurant's consistent quality and service.  We have been there at least a dozen times, and have had our favorites over and over again.  Each time they are better than we remember.  The calamari salad is in a class by itself; first courses are huge and filling.  We like the Pad Thai and the Lobster Fried Rice - both great bargains, with perfectly cooked shrimp in the former, and plenty of Maine lobster in the latter. Outstanding entrees include the salmon and any fresh fish.  Always leave room for dessert; the Chocolate Box is a classic, and the "Dip Sum" donuts will assure your return.  The staff is friendly and attitude-free.  I say this place is one of the best deals in town!

        This place is quite a scene - beautiful inside (think dimmed lights, lots of red tones, a big gold buddha - kind of reminded me of Tao in NYC) and quite noisy and packed with people.  Upscale, trendy crowd.  The cilantro martini was delicious (cilantro, grey goose, lemon-lime).  The service was great - very quick.  We split the calamari salad, edamame ravioli, tea-smoked spareribs, and the black cod in miso paste.  The salad was very large and it was tasty - not spectacular, but good.  The edamame ravioli was interesting - good - again, good, not spectacular.  The tea-smoked spareribs were basically falling off of the bone - these were spectacular.  The black cod was very fresh, tender and perfectly matched with wasabe mashed potatoes.  But, the salad and the spareribs filled us, and we had to stuff down the other food - the plates are big here, and everything is family style so be careful when you order.  For dessert, we split the banana tower and the chocolate pagoda.  Both were beautiful - the banana tower is basically a banana custard/pudding inside a caramel cookie shell, topped with whipped cream.  It was good - not the best banana cream dessert that I've ever had.  The chocolate pagoda was an ice cream sandwich of cake, mousse, and chocolate in some other form.  It was good - not spectacular.  But, very pretty.  Overall - I really enjoyed this place a lot - the food was very solid, the service great, and the crowd is loud and young.  It's pricey.

        I'm not too familiar with the Philly dining scene, but if Buddakan is indicative of the quality of food in the area, then Philadelphia is a foodie force to be reckoned with.  Buddakan is totally a scenester restaurant with a flashy ambiance centered around the gigantic Buddha in the middle of the dining room.  I'd read that Hollywood types frequent this Stephen Starr restaurant when in Philly, and I can definitely see why.  The lighting is very dim...very clubbish to me.   Thankfully the Buddakan experience wasn't purely superficial.  The quality of the Asian fusion dishes was fantastic.  I would highly recommend the Japanese Black Cod with wasabi mashed potatoes.  The buttery fish was perfectly cooked and seasoned with a miso glaze.  My dining companion had the Cantonese Ginger Sole.  It was steamed and flavored nicely--reminiscent of the steamed fish my Grandma used to make but with a much higher price.  When ordering I would recommend you carefully read the menu because some of the entrees don't come with sides on the plate.  The sole was one of these dishes which and be problematic if you aren't eating "family style."  Service was polite and professional, but a bit slow.  I would recommend using the valet parking if you are planning on ordering multiple courses.  I went earlier in the evening and parked on the street.  However dinner ran over 2 hours so my parking meter expired, and my rental car was graced with a $26 parking ticket.  Had I known this was going to happen, I would have done the valet which would have been a little bit cheaper in the end.  Although the price point was a bit high, I would recommend Buddakan for a splurge night out.
