    And for the 100th review on my Yelp 100 list: Cafe Lift....

    I decided to go with Cafe Lift for my 100th review in the list because I believe that it comprises the single best breakfast bite that I've ever had. I have been thinking about this single bite since it occurred and running a computationally intensive comparison to every other breakfast bite I've ever taken. As my brain is a slower model of processor than most, it took some time to complete the calculations. The compute job just completed and I can now verify that, indeed, best single bite ever.

    I'm talking about the cannoli french toast special. Try to process this: vanilla-and-cinnamon dredged challah bread, stuffed with cannoli cream, topped with fresh shaved chocolate pieces, fresh peaches, and walnuts soaked in maple. It was spectacularly decadent and delicious. I should note that there is a reason that I said "the single best bite" and not the best dish. This is because the dish is so rich that you cannot each much of it. I was able to eat about 1/3, which seemed like a heroic feat. That said, you should get this. I recommend getting a side of bacon or something salty to cut the sweet, though.

    Other items of note:

    - our waitress was super nice, as was the host.
    - the coffee, from La Colombe, is excellect
    - the space is very nice and has great artwork
    - cool crowd
    - they don't take reservations, but were able to walk in at about 10:30am and get a table for 5. They told me that it starts to get crazy around 11:15, and sure enough, at 11:15, there was a wait. I recommend getting here early.
    - the lemon ricotta pancakes are also quite special, as is the nutty monkey crispelle (a crepe stuffed with nutella and bananas).

    Highly recommend. One of the best brunch spots around, and I've been to many.

    Cafe Lift is one of the more enjoyable places I've had brunch in the city.  The staff was very friendly and the food was quite delicious.

    This restaurant serves brunch only from 9a-3p.  They are part of the Prohibition Taproom and Bufad Family (PGRQ).  Also, probably most importantly, this restaurant is cash only.  They have an ATM on site, but if you avoid fees like the plague make sure to stop at your local bank or Wawa on the way over.

    Housed in an old warehouse of some kind, the cafe is lofted on essentially an old manufacturing floor.  It gives it an interesting feel with the exposed interior utility lines/pipes and the cement slab floor.  The space is warm and inviting if not only for the wonderful array of people that brunch together on a given day.

    My lady friend and I entered the restaurant without checking the menu.  We'd overheard many people talking about the cannoli French toast.  We'd also observed the specials board and were taken aback by the variety offered.  I was impressed.

    After greeting us warmly, our waitress took our drink orders and fielded questions about the menu.  We decided to order the regular French toast to share and we each ordered a special.  I got the steak and eggs (Grilled local grass fed beef served with either eggs over easy or scrambled eggs. Served with corn and tomato salad, roasted potatoes and toast).  She got the eggs florentine (Poached eggs, sauteed spinach, crab meat, atop toasted Italian bread with truffled hollandaise).  The prices being reasonable, we assumed portions were smaller.  Boy were we wrong.  HUMONGOUS!!

    I enjoyed myself and got more than my fill.  I only have two suggestions.  They (1) didn't know the specific cut of beef used for the steak when asked, and (2) didn't make me aware of the cash only policy before walking in the door.  The manager was great and told me about the cow sharing plan the restaurants use and told me she was texting the on-staff butcher (so cool!!!) to find out.  They should definitely sort that out before service.  And the cash only policy is just a pet peeve of mine knowing that me, nor many of my friends, carry money and hate to pay an ATM charge.  I'll return again, but this time with cash before I walk in the door.

    We came in with 6 on a Saturday around 10:30 and expected to wait about an hour but only sat around for about 20 minutes. For the uninitiated, that's great for popular Philly brunch joints.

    Service was fast, friendly, and efficient, no complaints.

    As for the most important part, the food! I got huevos rancheros as per another Yelp recommendation and I was blown away by the flavors, especially their homemade hot sauce that they serve on the side gratis. My only teensy complaint was that the eggs were fried a bit too much for my taste, but I can't blame the restaurant as I didn't specify. The herbed potatoes on the side were delicious too. Highly recommended.

    Girlfriend got a plate of pancakes, in which the kitchen graciously folded fresh blueberries, served with fresh strawberries on the side and a generous portion of perfectly sweetened homemade whipped cream. The pancakes were thin yet dense, an unexpected change from the traditional fluffy pancake. Still, these hotcakes were awesome.

    We also tried a single lemon-ricotta pancake. Now THESE are something special. Next time I go back, I'm getting myself a big plate of these wonders. Dense yet light (if that makes any sense), perfectly flavored with just the right amount of tart lemon and creamy ricotta, topped with a little honey, fresh strawberries, and their oh-so-good whipped cream. Try them.

    Friend got the infamous cannoli stuffed french toast. Definitely comparable if not better than the legendary red velvet pancakes at Green Eggs, but bit too rich and sweet for my taste. A single bite was all I needed. Definitely recommended for the table to share, but it might be too much for a single person to order.

    The rest of the crew got eggs benedict, eggs florentine (on special), and a panini of some sort. I didn't try them, but they all looked amazing and were gobbled up.

    Don't mind the neighborhood; it looks a little sketchy and it isn't the best at night, but during the day it's totally fine. Think post-industrial.

    Get there on the early side to beat the rush. All in all, this place is outstanding.

If you are in the mood for a sweet breakfast, then I would highly recommend that you go to Cafe Lift because they has all sorts of specialty crepes and stuffed toasts that looked heavenly. I am way too sensible to use up all my calories on the first meal of the day, so I stick to eggs, but if  cannoli filled french toast is your jam, then head over to Cafe Lift.

    Knowing the portions are large after reading the reviews we ordered meals for two for the three of us. We had a mixed mushroom frittata and the challah French toast with fruit. The meal including tip and tax was $11 per person which was a bargain considering the amount and quality of the food we were served.
