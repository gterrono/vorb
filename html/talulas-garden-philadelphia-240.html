    Family members suggested that we try Talula's Garden because of the great food, fresh ingredients, and awesome selection of cheeses. So a friend of mine and I decided to try it out on New Years Eve, and we opted out of the 8 course prefixed menu.

    While the menu held so many tasty treats that were tempting, We ended up ordering the scallops appetizer along with the Fois gras. Both were absolutely delicious. The scallops were tender and warm and melted in our mouths, and the fois gras was served as a pâté with a toasted baguette. Some of the best Fois gras I've had.

    For our entree we split a beef special. It was served with kale (which I'm not a big fan of) that to my surprise was actually quite delicious. The meat was high quality meat which I appreciated, but I would have liked something more with it. While testing fresh and succulent, I just needed something else with the dish.

    For dessert we had one of their fantastic cheese trays which I highly recommend for anyone who enjoys good cheese. The selection was astonishing and the presentation was perfect.

    I'm very happy I tried talulas garden, and really want to go back. Our dinner total was 160 including tip, and as a college student I will need to wait a bit before I return, but I'm counting down the days.

    After returning a second time, everything was fantastic again. One meal came out way to salty, but the staff and manager handled it very quickly and the issue was resolved. Still a five star experience.

    My boyfriend surprised me for my birthday dinner at Talula's Garden. I have been dying to come and it was absolutely the perfect choice. From the minute the cab pulled up, I was ecstatic. The garden outside is lit and spectacular and the restaurant is gorgeous. Natural wood, flowers, butterflies...perfection.

    Even though there were only two of us, they sat us at a round 4 seater near the cheese bar, which was amazing. Our waiter brought us both a glass of champagne to celebrate my birthday which was very much appreciated. We felt like royalty already.

    To start we ordered a cheese plate and told them to make something they thought we would like. Everything was delicious. The plate had a variety of cheeses along with freshly baked bread and crackers, and accompaniments...soaked raisins, salted caramel, local honey, candied walnuts, truffle and apricot chutney the list goes on. The cheese was definitely the best I've ever had. Standouts were the honey goat cheese, the cranberry orange goat cheese, the dulce gorgonzola, and the cheddar which had a note of caramel.

    For our appetizers we got the Hot Dixie Prawns and the Orchard Pear Salad. Both appetizers were good, but not amazing. The prawns and romesco were great, but the rest of the dish was filled with spicy peppers. The salad was my least favorite of the night. Too many large pieces of pears.

    Next we got another gift! Freshly baked brioche with thyme butter. Unbelievable!

    For our entrees we split the Muscovy Duck Breast with Crisp Chickpea Crêpe with Sweet Apricot and Duck Rillete and the Roasted Barnegat Scallops with Celery and Celery Root "Chowder," House-cured Bacon, Little-necks, and Meyer Lemon Clam Broth. Both of these were amazing. The duck was unbelievable and I am now craving it. The scallops were great as well.

    We opted out of dessert, but they brought us little chocolate bites with a candle for my birthday. So nice! And delicious.

    This was the perfect night. Great service, amazing food, amazing cheese, beautiful space. My new favorite restaurant. I can't wait to come back.

    As a wedding gift, we were generously given a $150 Steven Starr gift card and immediately decided this would be the place we would use it- and we wouldn't question the price of anything ordered- just go all out for an amazing meal, and that is absolutely what we did.

    Dining here is truly an experience in itself. The waiters and waitresses are so knowledgeable of the entire menu, that you forget that theyre talking about the food you are about to eat vs a piece of art.

    We started with "World Travel" cheese board... and the presentation and explanation of the cheeses when they arrived lasted about 5 minutes. The talk they have to give every time someone orders one of the many cheese boards offered makes them deserving of a 20%(+) tip - no questions asked. The cheeses were all amazing... and went together so well.

    After ordering the cheese, we placed our order for appetizers, entrees and sides. We thought the plates might be on the smaller side and we came in hungry and didn't want to be disappointed in portion size.

    For our next course, we each ordered an appetizer to share- we got the Sweetbreads and the Scallop dish- the Sweetbreads were out of this world- the broth, the braised beef - all balanced each other perfectly and made me wanting more. The scallops didnt do it as much for me - although perfectly cooked - were bay scallops and very tiny, which didn't give them the sear they needed to take the dish to the next level. However, the flavors in this dish were great- the combination of homemade pasta with the cream sauce.

    As for the entrees, my husband ordered the steak special for the night, and for $42 was very good, but we decided for about $10 less he could have gotten a more unique entree. I ordered the swordfish which was some of the best I have had in some time- it had the perfect crisp to it and the plate itself looked beautiful.

    Apparently our eyes were bigger than our stomachs - we also decided to order 2 sides to go with our entrees , which were completely unnecessary, although very good. However, for $8 each , we could have done with just one or none at all.

    We decided to finish off the evening with one dessert to split,  which was the Chocolate Parade...and Ohmygoodness. tt was the perfect finish to an amazing meal. This dessert was served with 5 mini desserts- each was the perfect amount to split for a bite size amount per person.

    So for $250 total, which included a cheese board, 2 cocktails, 2 glasses of wine, 2 appetizers, 2 entrees, 2 sides, and 1 dessert, tax & tip... maybe this isn't going to be in our regular rotation of places to go out to eat, however, next time we are looking for someplace to celebrate a special occasion, we will absolutely be back.!

    Talula's Garden may be my favorite place to eat in the city. Yes...it's a bit pricey but honestly thank goodness...otherwise I would probably be there every night faster than my paycheck can keep up.

    The Visionary - the addition of cinnamon syrup to the rum punch was clutch. The freshly squeezed blood orange juice didn't hurt either.
    The Optimist: Huckleberry lemonade. So sweet and refreshing that it may have disappeared a little too quickly
    The Butcher: Best bloody I've had in a while. They aren't afraid of horseradish...and neither am I! Rawr!

    Appetizers:

    King Carrot Soup- Interesting take on carrot soup. The pickled salad was awesome and actually worked very well. However, I would suggest they ditch the mint leaves. A little too overpowering and a texture problem with the rest of the soup
    Hot Dixie Prawns- YUM. The nutty, spicy sauce they sit on top of is incredible.
    Exotic Mushroom Pate- Plated so beautifully. The mushroom flavor is tremendous and nicely complimented with the fried chicken wings. A must have if you're a mushroom freak
    Handmade kale cavatelli- TO DIE FOR. Seriously this is the ultimate. I could eat this every day for a week and not get sick of it. Comfort meets classy

    Entrees:

    Scallops- Cooked to perfection. The "Chowder" aspect was a nice touch. The server told us that you would be ordering something closer to a stew rather than a  traditional scallop plate. He was right and I'm a fan
    Porgy- Server told me this was his favorite...so I got it. One of the best cooked whole fish I've ever had. The side of broccoli rabe was out of this world. It was cooked in garlic and the tomatoes...almost like it was stewed. Delish
    Risotto-This is the one dish that noone at the table was a huge fan of...but then again we are all serious carnivores so ordering a Veggie entree might have been a stretch

    Sides:

    Creamed kale & roasted mushrooms. The kale was simply amazing. I've never had creamed kale before and was worried about the bitterness but it balanced out exceptionally well. Very impressed.

    I know this is a hefty review...but really Talula's deserves it.
    I'll be back ASAP

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
