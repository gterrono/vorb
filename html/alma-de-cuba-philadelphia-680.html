    This one of my top five favorite restaurants I have ever been to.  I loved this place so much the first time that I came back the next day! I was visiting Philadelphia and found this place on a happy hour app.  The ambiance is very cool, it has three different floors.  The first floor has a lounge feel, the second floor is more for dining, and the third floor is a little bit brighter and more casual.  The service here was prompt and attentive on both occasions.  There are multiple happy hour drinks, but I ordered the $5 daily special mojito (the flavor changes every day).  I had Guava and Mango, both excellent.  They had a light flavoring, with real sugar cane garnish.  I've had plenty of mojitos and this place has some of the best (especially for the happy hour price).  We got happy hour spinach empanadas which were also $5, but it was a pretty small portion.  The empanadas themselves were excellent.  Crispy, but soft enough that you don't crumbs falling out every time you take a bite.  

    I wish they had this where I live.  I will definitely return to Philadelphia, and when I do this place will be my first stop!

    This is my second time here (the last time was around three years ago), but I think my memories of the last time I was here set the bar too high for my most recent visit.

    We were seated immediately, on the second floor. The lighting is dimmer than most restaurants, with a red tinge that makes it almost impossible to take pictures of your food. Still, the ambiance is beautiful and very suave. Our table unfortunately had a little wobble, but it wasn't anything other than slightly distracting.

    Our server was very nice and aggressively friendly, but everything he said seemed so pointedly scripted. He was still very polite, extremely knowledgable about the menu and drinks, and he didn't check in so much that it was annoying.

    For drinks, I had the strawberry sparkling mojito (which was $15! I wish I had stuck to the suave mojito, but I love berries in my mojitos..), which was decent. I didn't think it was that strong, but it came with a stick of sugar cane and was still delicious. My friend had the Walt whit, which was fine. The complimentary bread + olive oil mixture is to die for. You could smell the butter, and the herby mixture was great. Another server (I think the busboy?) came by and gave us more bread! Awesome!

    We had the smoked albacore tacos for appetizer - last time I was here, it was marlin - which were great! Not as great as I remember them being, but perhaps it's because they are great along side the stuffed dates? Next time, I'd recommend the appetizer sampler (and I see they changed the wording on the menu so that it's not ambiguous that the price is per person), just to get more variety.

    I had a ceviche tasting of 3 for my entree, whereas my friend had the vaca frita - we decided to share everything. The vaca frita was tasty, but a little dry or over cooked, which was disappointing. I wished we had ordered the pork dish instead. The ceviches were good - we had the lengua al fuego, yellowtail kingfish, and the rainbow. They were all fresh and citrusy. The 'lengua' was extremely spicy, but I think it's more because the first two bites weren't spicy at all, so I pretty much took the heat of the entire dish at once. I wished the heat was more uniform throughout the dish, but still, it was delicious and I think my favorite out of the three, especially with the kumquats. My friend's favorite was the rainbow - which includes salmon and tuna - especially because of the variety of texture that the crunch and the orange gave. The yellowtail one was also good, but not my favorite. The ceviche also came with a mixture of popcorn and corn nuts which was awesome - salty, crunchy, warm; a perfect contrast.

    For dessert, we shared the chocolate cigar. The waiter lit the fake matches on fire - I wasn't sure of the point of that because when the fire went out, it was burnt black, so we avoided eating that.. What a waste. But the cinnamon-y cookie that the matches were attached to was a good addition to the surprisingly dense, chocolatey cigar. The dulce de leche ice cream was also a great compliment. I would order this dessert every time I came here (although I was sad to miss the dessert of the day, which included blood orange). When we received the check, it also came with a small box of petit fours. I tried the cinnamon covered marshmallow and didn't like it, but the others were good, especially the jelly.

    Anyway, in short, the meal was a good experience but fell short of my expectations, mainly because of the overcooked skirt steak. I wanted to rate this place a 3.5, but still, the service was excellent, the ambiance is beautiful and sexy, and the drinks and food were good. I would return for a special occasion or if I just wanted to blow through some money, hoping that the meat is not normally that dry. (Thanks for paying, friend!)

Both portion sizes were large enough to satisfy our appetites.

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge, Philadelphia Center City Restaurant Week

    My last couple visits to Alma de Cuba didn't live up to my first.  The second time I went, my chicken was very dry, but the service and other food was really, really good. I chalked it up to making a bad menu selection as I had immensely enjoyed the fish on my first visit.  However, my last visit during the most recent Restaurant Week in January was terribly disappointing and I ordered fish.  

    I know a lot of people hate Restaurant Week, but honestly, this only the second time I've gone somewhere during Restaurant Week and been disappointed.  The service is what bothered me the most.  We felt extremely rushed.  One course would come out before you'd even finished the previous one, and they'd start taking your plate away! I also had silverware dropped on me a number of times, and a couple close calls with food as well.  

    The food didn't make up for the service.  The bread wasn't warm like it had been in the past.  The scallop ceviche was just kind of there.  I've had way better ceviche before.  My husband's black bean soup was way over-salted.  My main course was a small whole fish with a couple of tiny slivers of potato on it.  Last time I ordered the fish during Restaurant Week it was filling, as it was larger and came with a decent portion of well-seasoned potatoes.  This just made me sad.  Also the amazing flavor wasn't there, as the sauce was a few drizzles of a cream that was rather bland. The chimichurri sauce that I'd had previously on fish there was far superior.  The dessert was the best part, and not even because of the flavor, but because I was starving at that point and it was a large portion.  It was coconut milk with tapioca.  It was like a sweet, cold soup.  Not bad, but not something I would seek out again either.

    My mojito needed some more muddling.  It mostly just tasted of booze.  I like that they didn't skimp on the booze, but I still wanted it to taste minty!  

    My husband had a more pleasant experience food wise, as he ordered the pork for his main course.  He got an overabundance of food.  I joked that they gave him half of a pig.  He actually had leftovers for lunch the next day.  I really hate pork, so I didn't try it, but the skin apparently was really crispy and the meat fatty which he loved.  

    I'm giving Alma three stars since my second visit was quite good, and nothing tasted bad on my last visit, just not spectacular.  I still do love the decor and atmosphere there.  However, I don't think I'll be seeking it out any time soon when I want to go out for a nice dinner.  There are other nice places in the neighborhood that have been far more consistent.

    So far the first restaurant week of 2013 is proving to be the best yet!  I had a fantastic meal here… So far the first restaurant week of 2013 is proving to be the best yet!  I had a fantastic meal here last week for restaurant week.  It was snowing, and the traffic reports seemed nightmarish, so even though my husband and I left early, I thought we might still be late for our reservation.  However, traffic was fine, so we arrived at the restaurant one hour before our reservation time.  The hostess took our name, and told us we could wait at the bar, and she would come get us when the table was ready.

       The service was excellent starting with the service we received at the bar.  I started off with a tequila based cocktail with tamarind in it.  It was good, but not amazing.  I then got the El Jefe Mojito (the difference from the standard mojito being the rum used), which was much better, well muddled, and contained the right amount of sweetness and was garnished with sugarcane.  I usually walk away disappointed when I order a mojito from a bar, because often the mint isn't properly muddled, but no complaints here.  The bartender keep our water glasses full and came back to check on us to make sure we were enjoying our cocktails.

       When our table was ready the server offered to get a tray and carry our drinks up.  That wasn't necessary, but I thought it was nice that they offered.  

       The restaurant is a lot bigger than it appears as there is an upstairs area, and possibly another floor on top of that (there was another staircase, but I didn't go up it).  I love the dimly lit orange lights and the white modern furniture and tables.  As others have said, this place has a loungey feel, but in a classy way.  It would be a great place to go on a date.

        We were brought fresh yuca bread, which I didn't know we were going to get.  Lots of times with restaurant week, the restaurant makes cut backs like the free bread.  Not here though.  It was hot, doughy, and starchy and served with a chimichurri sauce, which is basically oil, parsley garlic, and spices.  It wasn't what I was expecting when I bit into it, but I liked it.

        For the first course of my restaurant week meal I had the bass ceviche.  It said it was corvina online, but they must have ran out.  I was a bit disappointed since I'd eaten this fish while in Panama and fallen in love with it, but the bass was tasty, and I liked the hints of vanilla in the sauce.  Maybe I can get the corvina next time.

       For my main course, I ordered the tilapia with chimichurri sauce.  When the food was dropped off by someone who wasn't our server, mine didn't look like fish.  I stared at it for about five seconds before our waiter said, it wasn't the fish and was in fact some kind of beef and took it away.  She immediately brought me out the crab and vegetable salad appetizer on the house, the one my husband and I hadn't ordered, to tide me over while they worked on correcting my entree.  It wasn't her mistake, and that was excellent service on her part.  I wasn't bothered that I had to wait, as I got the chance to taste another dish.  The crab was really good, but the salad tasted a lot like coleslaw.  I tasted my husband's shrimp and grits.  I usually hate grits because of the texture, but these were smooth and delicious.  The tomato sauce was rich and savory.  I thought for a minute that maybe I'd made a mistake in ordering the tilapia.

         My fish came about about ten minutes later, and let me tell you, it was so worth the wait.  Usually tilapia isn't impressive, but it was the whole fish and the sauce was so good, and the fish itself was really quite flavorful.  I can't stop thinking about it!  The potatoes it came with were really tasty too.  I was getting full though, and I was worried we would leave hungry.  Usually that's what happens when I go to Steven Starr restaurants, with the exception of some dishes at the Dandelion.  Thankfully, that was not the case this evening.

       Dessert came and I was full, but ate the Cuban cookies filled with dulce de leche and with a scoop of banana ice cream on the side anyway because there is always room for dessert! The ice cream was full of banana flavor and not too sweet.  The cookies surprisingly tasted like my mom's Christmas cookies, which wasn't a bad thing.  They were very buttery.  I tasted the flan, and it was well, flan.  It's hard to go wrong.  The red berries made for a pretty and colorful garnish.

      I can't wait to go back here and try other dishes and drinks.  Alma did not disappoint with food or service.  I will be back!     Read more

    I had some of the best food of my life at Alma de Cuba... I will definitely be back! though I hope the service is better next time.

    We made reservations, and for that I am very glad, since it was a Friday night. The place was packed. Unfortunately, this also meant all the servers and busboys were extremely busy and therefore slow. It took quite a while for waiters to notice that we were done with each course and clear our tables. I also noticed that our waters were empty at several points in the night, which doesn't usually happen at restaurants of this caliber. We asked to have our receipt split onto two tabs, but they forgot. I will try to return on a less busy night to get some faster service.

    I still have to give Alma 5 stars due to the tastiest food! I, for one, am actually glad with the cuisine style, as it seems to be more like Americanized Cuban. Firstly, the rolls were amazing, and I wanted more! I'm not usually a fan of empanadas, but these spinach ones were delicious. The vaca frita was extremely soft and easy to tear, and I loved the mojo that came with it! At this point, I was already decently full, but also tried a pretty tasty sorbet sampling (raspberry, pineapple coconut, and vanilla) for dessert. The food is pricey for Cuban food, but not for a Stephen Starr restaurant. At least you're full at the end!

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
