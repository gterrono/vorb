There was a mixup on my soda, but I got it, so no harm no foul.  I sat down at one of the aluminium tables next to the order window with my sandwich. The roast pork sandwich was so so good.     You can't go to Philly and not indulge yourself in the classic sandwich this city made famous, the Philly Cheesesteak! Last time out, Nui and I grabbed  a quickie at Rick's Cheesesteak in the Reading Terminal Market and it was good, but not great as I had hoped. This time around, she delivered with Tony Luke's.

    It's literally a drive up joint that looks like it's a mobile home. There's one window where you order and you pick up your food and they have limited outdoor seating on some picnic tables. We were there on a Friday night, around 9ish and it was hopping. We're talking people double parking and parking on the median (which appeared to be perfectly acceptable behavior). You step up to the window after a short wait in line, place your order, and pay. Then they call your name out when the order is ready. There were a lot of people there that night and more than one Tony so I got a little thrown off.

    Tony Luke's has an extensive menu which includes burgers, hotdogs, hoagies, sandwiches, breakfast items, beef, pork, veal, chicken, and of course beefsteaks. I wanted the authentic original topped with (what else?) Cheese Whiz.  Nui went for the Roast Pork with Sharp Provolone. I got a side of curly fries sans the cheese, a small nod to avoiding cardiac arrest. I like my cheese steaks meaty, which means the bread should not overwhelm the meat. In this case, there was just the right amount of bread in each bite coupled with just the right amount of fatty meat. I also topped my cheese steak with whole pickled peppers which added a nice zing to each bite. The piece de resistance was the Cheese Whiz. I have no doubt it's not made from cows, or cheese for that matter, but no matter what this heavenly goop is made of, it made each bite of this Tony Luke's original melt in your mouth. I had some of the Roast Pork with Provalone, touted as their own secret recipe. The recipe seems to call for copious amounts of pepper to be liberally interspersed into the thinly sliced pieces of pork. Still, it had a kick to it and with the sharp provolone, had me wishing I had gotten a second sandwich.

    Now I just need to find a way to get them to open a stand out here in San Francisco. My heart probably thanks you that there isn't one yet.

    After having read my fellow Yelpers' rave reviews for Tony Luke's I swore I would eat a cheesesteak from here before the year was out. During my last trip to Philly, a gastronomical upset foiled my plans to indulge. So this time I made a day after Thanksgiving trip by driving an hour and a half to go eat at Tony Luke's.

    Upon reaching I saw a line of about 30 people ahead of me who had just gotten out of a sporting event wearing Philly Jerseys, so I knew this place was endorsed by locals. I took my place in line and patiently waited for my turn to order what some call the greatest Philly Cheesesteak of them all.

    After about 20 mins my turn came, I ordered a cheesesteak with Cheese Wiz (the recommended option) and fried onions, a cheese fries, an order of curly fries and one order of the sauteed mushrooms.

    The cheese fries were awesome, they had an unhealthy buttery goodness to them which made them delicious. The sauteed mushrooms were undercooked, and the cheesesteak was lacking enough cheese. Honestly, if it wasn't for the excellent hot peppers available at the peppers and pickle bar I probably wouldn't have been able to enjoy my sandwich.

    Now I am not saying the cheesesteak is bad.. no no... what I am saying is that Tony Luke's food is a victim of its own success. In their rush to serve the hordes that come to it seeking the ultimate cheesesteak they no longer care about making a perfect cheesesteak. They make them fast and without care. The meat maybe undercooked for some, not enough cheese in others, in my sandwich only one side had some semblance of cheese while the other half was completely devoid of it.

    I will say that there probably is tons of other food that still make this place a great place to eat, like the cheese fries, but I do not believe that they deserve a five star rating or the rave reviews posted here anymore.

    So I plead to the restaurateurs of Tony Luke's... please make the Cheesesteak personal again. Make each one with love and care that made your cheesesteaks famous. I stood for 20 mins... I will gladly stand for 40 if I know that you took the time to give me the perfect cheesesteak.
