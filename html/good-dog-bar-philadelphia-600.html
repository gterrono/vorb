    Good Dog might be the epitome of my favorite kind of Philly bar. Anywhere else it would just be a hole in the wall with 6 domestic taps and a menu that makes you want to cross the street to Applebees. But not in Philly. Here, we've got a cozy unpretentious bar with great beer and food. They've got 3 floors to enjoy, with a long bar and booths at ground level, more tables on the above and a pool table and dartboard on the 3rd.

    The beer list isn't as long as other area bars, but it's a well curated variety of craft beers. The food is excellent and creative. There are great appetizers; the roasted onion hummus with house-made veggie chips is a nice change from pita wedges and and a ball of bland chickpea paste, just as the cordon blue tenders will trump everyday frozen chicken strips. The cheesesteak empanadas and mixed sweet/regular fries are also standouts. The roquefort stuffed Good Dog burger is one of the best in Philly, but their other sandwiches prevent it from being an automatic order.

    This bar isn't ruff!  It's bow-wow!  (oh man, sorry guys)

    Having been to Good Dog a number of times throughout the years, the primary reason to come is the food, which is amazing.  It is far above and beyond typical pub cuisine and features one of the most distinct and tasty burgers in the city.  They also do a flatbread pizza of the day, which never disappoints.  The blueberry cornbread is also terrific.  

    The beer/liquor selection is everything you need on a night out.  The bartenders and servers are great: Friendly, efficient, and knowledgeable.  Can't say I've ever had a bad experience with any of them.  They also have decent taste in music - you won't hear garbage music on the jukebox for very long.  The bar on the first floor does get a little crowded and loud, but that's where the third floor comes in handy.  Up there is a game room/large hang out spot featuring a pool table, darts, and everyone's favorite creepy game: erotic photo-hunt.

    A final note/suggestion:  Depending on the time you come, plan to wait a while to get seated.  It is the type of place where people linger at tables even after they finish eating.  Be careful that the hostess is straight up with you about the wait, because on at least two occasions, my group of friends were told there would be a long wait even though certain tables looked available.  Can't fault the bar for these isolated incidents.  This place is well worth the trip!
