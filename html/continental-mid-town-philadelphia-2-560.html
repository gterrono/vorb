    Ah, Continental... a Philly classic.

    Some of the global tapas are delicious (say yes to the Lobster Mashed Potatoes and Grilled Octopus), with masterfully blended flavors and ingredients.  And some of them are just... *no.* I'm not a fan of the Indian tapas but I'm admittedly very critical of Indian food.  However, I think the place is generally more appreciated for its drinks than its food.

    Let me start by saying I live in the Fairmount district of the city, so I have been there, tried that, with most restaurants in the city.  Having ventured into Continental Mid-town many times for late night drinks I have always enjoyed the atmosphere and stiff drinks.  

    Yet this review is not based upon these intoxicated nights.  Rather, a recent trip for dinner with a friend of mine.  

    The atmosphere is very unique and gives this place even a different flavor from it's Old City counterpart.  With plenty of space, getting seated never seems like it would be much of an issue at Mid-town.

    Once seated I took a close look at the menu and realized that the plates where just as  unique as the atmosphere.  My friend and I settled on a few appetizers and main courses.  We both left feeling as though the food had satisfied both our stomachs & our wallets.

    However all this being said, I must say the most pleasant part of the trip was our server.  Molly was quick to serve us upon being seated and I never felt rushed or bothered.  Her recommendations were spot on and seemed to genuinely come from a honest understanding of all items on the menu.  

    If you are on the fence of going to Continental Mid-Town....DO IT....just hope you get Molly waiting your table.

    Fantastic.  This is the place you take your out-of-town, or new-to-town, friends for a funky-chic dining experience where you can feel like you are "out" without actually having to "go out."  

    I love the huge booths on the ground floor,  The second floor swingy-chairs are great  for just drinks and people watching.  The upstairs booths are a bit grungier, but still afford the polka-dot-swank vibe.

    If you want lots of food for your money (read: value), go for the pad Thai: slippery, soft noodles nestled in bib lettuce, with thick chunks of chicken and crunchy soft bean sprouts.  I also go for the calamari or continental salad w/ chicken.  Absolutely huge.   The Thai lettuce wraps are a good bet, and the Philly cheese steak egg rolls are legit grease splurge.

    Most of the drink options are sickeningly sweet, and Starr-quality (read: much $ for little alcohol) so choose carefully.  My suggestion?  Bring a flask ;)

    This is my second time at Continental Mid-town. I had loved it the first time, but this second time just blew the first out of the water.

    Continental Mid-town is a fusion-y restaurant, both in terms of decor and menu. It's not a classy restaurant; rather, it has more of a fun and retro vibe. There are huge globe lights dangling from the ceiling despite the restaurant being dimly lit, hanging basket chairs (definitely request for those seats!), cool-looking booth seats complete with headrests, and a nice bar area.

    During our first visit to the restaurant, we were seated in the hanging basket chairs (I think we requested for those). This visit, we were seated in the upstairs booth area since we didn't have a reservation. We were downtown already and decided to pop in for brunch on a Saturday morning.

    Our server was very friendly and offered us complimentary pomegranate lemonade aquas frescas as palate cleansers. They were so refreshing that we decided to order a full glass. I ordered a BLT (bacon, arugula, tomato) omelette, but I asked to substitute spinach for the arugula, to which our server graciously obliged. My boyfriend ordered The Big Bang, which consisted of pancakes, breakfast potatoes, toast, turkey sausages, and bacon.

    The BLT omelette was AMAZING. It was probably the best omelette I had ever had. It was so soft, but not under cooked, and the bacon, tomato, and spinach were cooked perfectly. I don't think I've ever finished an omelette so quickly. The Big Bang was a very very generous portion, and we enjoyed that too (the pancakes were so fluffy!)

    Stephen Starr has never disappointed, and this brunch at Continental Mid-town is one I won't forget.

    Let's not make Continental what it's not. It's not a gourmet, high-end establishment with any extremely fresh ingredients to speak of. It's a trendy bar, with pretty delicious food and a neat atmosphere (hello throwback Stephen Starr). And the champagne-o-rama, complete with a lickable sugary rim.

    Continental is a tried and true spot. I've been countless times for countless different reasons. Brunch, lunch, happy hour, dinner, late drinks, take out udon noodle soup for a sick day etc. The only thing I could possibly complain about is that if I find myself going too frequently, I need a break.

    I have probably had the entire menu at one time or another, and I've been to every location. I will say, the AC Continental is quality. And as you find at most other Stephen Starr's, solid food, solid service, solid atmosphere.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
