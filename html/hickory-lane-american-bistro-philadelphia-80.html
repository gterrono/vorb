    Hickory Lane has actually improved since the new chef took over.

    The original chef Zagorski, the famous creator of the popular burger here, was very good and I had eaten here once while he was still manning the kitchen.  We came, as most people did, to try the burger.  Well let me tell you that it was EVERYTHING it was cracked up to be.  Bibb lettuce, brioche bun, tomato, cheese and meat.  Nothing fancy, because it didn't need to be.  This was the most delicious, decadent hamburger I had ever tasted.  I brought my whole family in a few weeks later to try it but the restaurant's supplier had left them hanging so the burger was off the menu.  I never had it again.

    The problem with Zagorski was that other than his burger, nothing else on the menu excited me at all.  The choices were haphazard and boring.  The new chef at Hickory Lane has reinvigorated my desire to come again, though, because he revamped the menu and included more veggie-friendly items.  He also built a stronger menu with more of a concept.  The burger is still on the menu, but I know the specific blend of meats Zagorski used was proprietary so I doubt it is exactly the same.  I can say, however, that the cuts of meat are listed on the menu and those do appear to be the same cuts that Zagorski used, so perhaps he handed down his recipe.  I have since become a vegetarian, so I cannot rate this new burger.  I can rate the rest of the menu, though.

    I recently came for brunch and had the Tex-Mex Plato, which was awesome.  I substituted some soy bacon for the sausage, which was a good choice because the meal would otherwise have been too mushy in texture.  The sausage, I'm sure, adds the crunch.  Still, it was a great inventive meal.  My wife had a stuffed French toast dish with pineapple and cream cheese that was heavenly.  It was the best stuffed French toast I've ever tried.  We also had two great fruit-flavored mimosas (pear and pineapple) that were excellent.  Subtlety sweet with a decent extra-dry champagne.

    The service was also exceptionally friendly.  They brought out a water bowl for our dog and kept it filled and seated us under and awning as it looked like rain was on the horizon.  The location and view you get at Hickory Lane is also almost unmatched.  People watching on the street with the Penitentiary behind them!

    The kitchen took a while to get our food out to us; that is my only complaint.  I could hear them rushing to get orders, and perhaps making a mistake that made the wait longer.  I also noticed a table of four girls that was seated before us take a very long time to get their food, so I knew we were in for a long wait as well.  If they pick up the pace, they'd easily be 5 stars.

    I wanted to try a new place in Fairmount for brunch and a friend recommended Hickory Lane.  We made reservations for 12:30 on a Sunday in August.  We arrived at 12:30 and our table was ready.  We opted to sit inside but they do have several tables outside on the sidewalk.     The Quiche came with potatoes and a salad.  My aunt said it was fantastic.  The Hickory Burger was so fantastic I have moved it into the top 3 of my top burger list.  The patty was made of Filet Tips, Deckle (fat from a Ribeye) and a custom brisket blend.  The patty was topped with Aged Cheddar, Garlic Aioli, Tomato, Bibb Lettuce & a Pickle on a Challah Roll.  I added house cut fries.  The Belgian Waffle was light, fluffy & my Fiance loved it.

    I don't recall the exact total but I thought it was very reasonable for our meal.  I will be back.

    Another fine choice due to the Yelp Nation.  First, if you do a Yelp check in and receive the pop up voucher for a free happy hour app (with purchase of two happy hour drinks) know that the eligible apps are on the bar menu.  The bar menu offers a small but diverse selection, which we further narrowed to two choices:  the cheese plate and the mussels.   We went with the former, and the plate included three artisanal cheeses, mustard, fig preserves, grapes, raisins and nuts.  We had great fun playing around with flavor combos while enjoying our Sungria and beer.

    Our entrees included the crab salad, which was beautiful salade composee.  The crab salad was perched on and between three stacked crispy wontons, which made for a delicious textural contrast.  The pulled pork sandwich, our other entree, was dressed with a S. Carolina-style, vinegar-based sauce and topped with a lightly dressed slaw.   The flavor juxtaposition was superb and the sandwich was sufficiently rich that 1/2 was a filling meal.  The accompanying sweet potato fries were crisp, flavorful  and not greasy.  They needed no condiments and the portion was ginormous. We ate perhaps half of them.

    Finally, the Sungria--mentioned by other Yelpers--is worth a whirl (especially during happy hour when it is $2 off the regular price.  On a hot day, it was a very thirst-quenching treat (a sweeter version of Sangria).

    We look forward to a return visit.

    It makes me so happy that Hickory Lane is in my neighborhood. I've been here for dinners, lunches and brunches and have yet to be disappointed by any of their offerings.

    I've also yet to meet an employee who has been anything less than extremely friendly. If they have room, they even let you sit down and wait at your table before the entirety of your party arrives. I think that's great.

    Food Highlights:
    - Hickory Burger. I haven't actually liked cheeseburgers for long (less than a year!), but in that time period, this one has soared to the top of my burger-list. It is so, so good. Made just right. Huge. Now I want one. Shoot.
    - Cookies & Cream French Toast. This was a special during brunch earlier in the summer. Oh man. It was also huge, and creamy, and amazing. This should just stay on their menu always. Who doesn't love cookies & cream stuff?
    - Grilled Chicken Sandwich. This has bacon and avocados on it. I realize it's a basic option, but theirs is pretty great. It's also a perfect choice for picky eaters.
    - Red Velvet Crespelles. These are red velvet pancakes, rolled up with cream inside of them. They have whipped cream and chocolate chips on top of them. They are delicious and sweet. Also surprisingly filling.
    - Tahiti Vanilla Gelato. Another basic offering but fantastic nonetheless!

    Hickory Lane is also really welcoming. I've never felt rushed during any of my visits. Plus, they have a perfect location on Fairmount Ave. next to Eastern State!

    Needless to say, I'll be back.
