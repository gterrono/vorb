    It looks like most reviews are for brunch, but I've only been for weekday lunch. When we want to venture out beyond University City for a sit-down lunch, sometimes we hop on the trolley to 22nd St to steal away for some comfort food at Day By Day. It's a fairly comfortable space; it can get somewhat crowded but we haven't had to wait for a table. The service can be slow, so I don't recommend it if you're in a hurry, but it is friendly. Usually we get specials from the chalkboard, but there are still plenty of things on the regular menu that sound great. I finally had their grilled chicken & provalone sandwich, which gives the Mel's chicken cutlet at Sabrina's a run for its money. The food that we've had so far has all been very good, from soup to sandwiches to salad to pasta. My only complaint is that sometimes the food comes out a little heavier/greasier/fattier than what I expected. Luckily, though, their portions are not too large; they are enough for me to be satisfied, but not stuffed. Overall this is a pretty solid spot for lunch.

    Maybe not THE best brunch place I've ever been, but surely one of the solid options for brunch or lunch in philadelphia.

    They had this 'crab & avocado omelette' when I first visited here, and I became a regular here ever since. (Well, I live on 21st and Walnut, so the location is also one of the factors that made me a regular here too LOL)

    However, sadly that was one of two best dished I've ever had in Day by Day. (The other was 'spinach & shrimp Quiche', and it was legendary.)

    Can't complain about their food; they serve really good dishes here, and I'm yet to be disappointed by any food they serve.
    However, I do not like how they always change their menu, cuz I always try their daily specials, and I usually can't get the same thing the next time I come. (When you liked something last time, it hurts to know that you can't get the same thing this time.)

    Anyways, highly recommended brunch/lunch spot, but expect to wait for a long time if you come here during weekend lunch time. My cheat is to come here either early or late and avoid the peak hours (They open at 11am~2:30ish during the weekdays and 10am~3ish on the weekends, so it's not actually a bad idea to come around when they open or close).

    To sum up,

    1. Solid brunch/ lunch place
    2. Keep changing the daily menus, so don't expect to get the same thing twice if you order from the daily menu. However, whatever you choose to have, it's hard to fail - so don't be afraid.
    3. Don't come here during the weekend peak hours unless you like waiting in a line for at least an hour.

    Day By Day catered our recent wedding and they were wonderful, and I recommend them without any reservations.  The food was outstanding - fresh, varied, beautifully prepared - and served perfectly.  They bring a grill and prepare the food right before it's served.  We were aiming for a casual but nice outdoor wedding - we got a lovely, elegant event without any pretension.  The staff was excellent, and very well-managed.  They timed everything perfectly and our day could not have been better.  They even packaged the left-overs and helped us load our car.
