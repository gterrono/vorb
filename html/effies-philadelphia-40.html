        So, I've been to Effie's a lot over the years because it's close to my mom's house and is a byob.

        I feel it's consistency is slipping. Ben and I went a few weeks ago because we had a bottle of wine and wanted a casual meal. I ordered grape leaves and the octopus tomato salad and asked for them to be brought together. Ben ordered the navy bean soup and a pasta dish. His soup was barely better than canned stuff-- a simple slightly gelatinous tomato base with beans. Certainly hardy and filling. But really not worth serving at a restaurant. Next, his huge (and delicious!) pasta dish and my octopus arrived. Since I was nibbling on his food and the grape leaves still hadn't come, I asked the AMAZING WAITRESS to take them off the bill which she did with pleasure.

        I am an adventurous eater and have had octopus many times before. This was not good octopus. It had a fishy metallic taste, wasn't grilled nicely and was drenched in balsamic and tomato which only highlighted its weird flavor. I barely ate any of it. After we'd nearly finished our bottle of wine, our AMAZING WAITRESS came around to ask how everything was. Usually I lie and say it's "great" but after the wine and because the octopus was SO NOT GOOD i just couldn't lie and told her it was so-so. And she agreed with me! I couldn't believe it! She took it off our bill and offered us free baklava to take home. She explained that because the Eagles had won that day, the kitchen staff had been doin' a little celebrating so it might be an off night. Now, I can appreciate hometown pride, but I really wanted better food. All in all our waitress was the only delightful part of the meal. Kudos to her!

        Went to Effie's last night with a group of 10 friends. The food was pretty good (the moussaka is insanely large and hearty) and the service during the meal was fine. Ambience is nice - I especially like the garden in the back.

        The reason that I left the meal rather unhappy was the sequence of events that occurred when we were paying the bill. First of all, the menu said "18% gratuity included for parties of 8 or more". When it says that on the menu, you would expect that it would be the *actual* policy. So they brought us the bill (which was basically the tape reel of a calculator) and we figured that the tip was included because IT SAID IT WOULD BE ON THE MENU (am I wrong here, people??)

        After we paid, the server came back all puppy-eyed with the bill and some change and asked if there was something wrong with our meal. She insinuated that our tip was insufficient and then explained that they didn't apply the policy they had clearly printed on the menu because it was a slow night or something. That pissed me off. If you have a policy printed on your menu, customers expect you to enforce it (again, please correct me if I am wrong here). Or you should tell us when you give us the bill that tip is *not* included like it says on the menu.

        I liked Effie's before last night, but I was left with a rather poor taste in my mouth (and pocket) and don't know if I'll be going back anytime soon.

        Recently we had some time to waste before a show, and what better way to waste time than by eating? Effie's has been on our to-try list forever, so it was the perfect opportunity.

        My dining partner and I shared the Spanakopita appetizer and Lamb Lemonato entree. Both satisfied the taste buds, but neither wowed me. I judge the quality of a meal by how memorable it is. Will I remember this meal fondly when I'm 95 and someone is spooning pureed vegetables into my mouth at the nursing home? Maybe as an afterthought, as in, "what was that place we went to that wasn't as good as Zorba's?"

        Effie's wins points for having friendly, prompt service, and for being a BYOB. But Effie's loses points for their website. Go ahead and look at their list of "Entree's." Apostrophe misuse is a double-fail, so the good points are negated, and I can only grade this rating based on the food.

        I had a group of 6 here for my birthday dinner on Friday.  We had a semi private round table upstairs - it really felt like we were in someone's living room.  A good thing, in my book.  The whole place is cozy and intimate.

        I've been on a Greek food kick motivated by my recent visits to Kanella's, which is just down the street on Spruce St and 10th.  Of course, given the proximity and both being BYOBs, it's hard not to compare the two.  It always comes down to the food for me, and I think Effie's falls short here.  The food isn't as inspired or nuanced as Kanella's.  It could be due to the Cypriot regional influence (think middle Eastern) of Kanella's versus Effie's, which is more mainland Greek, but the food is less spiced, a bit bland and not presented in a way that's inspired.  

        I will say that our dinner table lingered for 2.5 hours and enjoyed many drinks over the span of several courses, and we never felt rushed or unwelcomed.   I think that speaks to the nature of the inviting atmosphere at Effie's.  Definitely a great place for romantic date.

        I don't like giving 3 stars, because that is a concession to having an average experience - (and who wants to be average?) - but as mentioned above, Kanella's gives Effie's a run for it's money and they'd do well to step up their culinary game.
