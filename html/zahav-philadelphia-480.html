    Friday Night 9pm - 3 people

    A bit of an odd location on top of a small hill, next to offices, what seemed to be a condo building, and a small strip mall. The restaurant itself is beautifully decorated - a modernly Mediterranean theme with romantic lighting. Service was attentive but not overbearing and great food/drink recommendations were given. I appreciated that the manager stopped by to ask how our meal was going.

    We opted for the Tayim - Taste of Zahav which enabled us to enjoy: salatim (daily selection of salads) & hummus with laffa, 6 mezze plates (small plates), 3 ha'esh plates (large plates), and 3 desserts for the table (39 a person). The menu seemed a little plain but the food that came out was anything but that! The flavors were wonderful and exciting without being overly salted.

    Salatim & hummus with laffa - tabouli, *roasted beets, curried carrots, Israeli salad, & roasted eggplant. my favorite was the beet dish. all of the salads were tasty and could be eaten with the hummus and laffa.

    *Crispy haloumi - peach-amba puree, local corn, almonds - the crispiness was surprising and good

    Fried cauliflower - labaneh with chive, dill, mint and garlic - great texture

    *Cured salmon - pickled watermelon rind, cantaloupe, grapefruit- juicy and fluffy salmon

    *Grilled duck hearts - date tehina, crispy onions, gooseberries - flavors exploding in your mouth - delicious

    Stuffed grape leaves - beef, pine nuts, raisins - too much leaf and not enough stuffing - get something else

    Kibbe naya - raw lamb, walnuts, sumac shallots - tasty

    --

    Duck kebab - ground duck, pistachios, saffron - good but did not stand out as much

    *Hanger steak - eggplant two ways, sesame, pine nuts - tender and smoky flavored

    Salmon - couscous, spicy tomato puree - it was good - probably would swap this out for something else

    ---

    * Vanilla semifreddo - pistachio cookie, strawberry, blueberry - the best - flavors and textures worked well

    Konafi - ricotta, peaches, labaneh ice cream - unique

    Rugelach - hazelnut praline, date puree - dry - get something else

    Overall a great experience and I would love to go back if I am in town again.

    Listed in My Visits to Philadelphia

    Dined here on a Friday evening over the Labor Day holiday.  We made reservations via Opentable which was key, as the place was packed and we never saw a vacant table.  

    The main dining area is attached to a relatively open kitchen.  The dining area has extremely high ceilings and is dimly lit.  The tables are somewhat close together, however you don't feel like you are sitting on top of each other.  It's a good date spot.  

    The boyfriend and I each decided to do the $39 per person tasting menu.  Before getting to the food, I must mention the drink each of us got and enjoyed immensely.  The "Smoke on the Water" was absolutely fabulous.  Complementing the liquor was a devilish combination of pepper and lime.  This drink is smooth (and potentially dangerous).  Luckily, I was able to keep it to two during dinner.  

    Before the mezes arrived, the salatim and hummus arrived at the table with a seasoned laffa.  The beets, the carrots, and the eggplant were standouts for me.  The hummus was probably the best I've ever had.  

    The mezes were all wonderful.  The fried cauliflower was so unique as these golden nuggets sat in the bowl, you wondered what type of fried taste it would have.  It's a surprisingly light dish.  Another standout was the watermelon and feta salad.  I also loved the chicken liver mousse on challah bread which had been lightly grilled.

    For the main course, I had the duck kebabs.  I've never seen it on a menu and was excited to try it.  It did not disappoint.  The ground duck was perfectly grilled as kebabs and came with rice.  After the main course, we were both nearing max capacity, but definitely wanted to sample some dessert.

    We each got a dessert.  The stone fruit came with a nectarine sorbet was a great palate cleanser.  We also got the chocolate almond cigars with chocolate mousse.  It was hard to go wrong with either of those.

    Overall, it was a wonderful dining experience.  Two small knocks on the place.  
    1.  It was absolutely freezing at our table - bring a sweater just in case.
    2.  The background music was a tad too loud.
