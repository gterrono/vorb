    Listed in Favourite Date Spots

    Gosh, where to begin?  I came here many years ago in the old location and never forgot about my amazing meal.  Just as I was thinking I should go back again, it closed!  My heart was broken, but now that they have reopened I felt this would be the perfect Valentine's Day spot, and boy was it.

    First off, yes it is miniature and crowded, there is no bar or waiting area so make a reservation and show up on time to avoid awkwardness.  Our table was ready when we arrived (+1/2 point because it seems like that never happens anymore).  They took our extra beers to the fridge to keep them cold and poured us glasses of our first.  The bread they bring out is delicious (I'm thinking unseeded Sarcone's baguette?), it was very fresh and they give you a little pot of absurdly addictive chimichurri to go with.  We tapped that baby out by the end of the meal (they keep bringing bread throughout the meal, also a +1/2 point).  Service was good even though it was clearly a big night for them.

    Ely got a couple of oysters to try, one East Coast and one West, and liked them both although the West Coast was his favourite.  He started with the rock octopus and I had the scallop.  I fully expected to get just one big diver scallop since the menu referred to it in a singular manner and that seems to be pretty typical of mid-high end restaurants, but to my delight I received two beautifully seared scallops on my plate!  They were perfectly cooked, the apple puree was just the right balance of sweet and savory, the bacon lardons were delightfully crispy/chewy, and overall it was possibly the best scallop dish I've ever had.  The octopus salad was nearly identical in ingredients to at least 4 other octopus salads in town, but this was definitely the best rendition.  The char on the octopus was just right and everything tasted super fresh.

    For an entree I went with the tilefish and he got the black cod.  We both enjoyed the dishes so much neither of us ended up picking off eachother's plate, we were too busy eating our own.  The portions are nicely substantial and we both had a few bites left on our plates that we were too full to finish.  The only criticism is that I did find two tiny bone pieces in my tilefish, but it was a crazy night for the kitchen so I'll let it slide this once.  We were too full for dessert so we got the check and went on our way.  The total damage for both was $100 on the nose including tax, which I would consider fair for the amount and quality of the food we got, although it's a little pricier than before they relocated.

    One important note:  do not wear something that will require expensive drycleaning!  The ventilation of the open kitchen could be a little better and the cooking smell will come home with you in your clothes.  That being said, the decor and ambience were quite nice and the whole experience was overwhelmingly positive.  We will probably be back someday soon.

    We had gone to Little Fish on our first date at the old location, so a visit to the new one seemed quite appropriate for our anniversary. Still very cozy, but somewhat more polished than the old location. Our waitress was attentive but not intrusive, and even accounted for my fiancee's nut allergy by bringing us olive oil with the bread rather than the house pesto.

    Listed in My Kind of Town, Philadelphia

    Now at the corner of Fitzwater & 6th, Little Fish is about as large on taste as it is small on space.  Simple decor and the gentle sizzle of meals in progress sauntering from the open kitchen build your anticipation for what's to come.

    Menu is written on the wall, which is actually quite useful for referencing once you begin eating.  We opened the wine and started with king crab in hot & sour broth and the rock octopus.  Both were excellent, octopus was flavorful with nicely sized, perfectly cooked chunks.  Wife thought the king crab was delicious, though she had a bit of buyer's remorse after indiscreetly leering at the neighboring table's scallops later in the night.

    Both entrees were prepared in a style that seemed very similar to sister restaurant Fish, the result of which is a sear on the top and bottom of the fish with a ridiculously moist, pretty much perfect, interior.   Wife ordered the golden tilefish.  It was soft and flaky, almost crumbling under the pressure of the fork.  The rockshrimp and grits gave a great texture to go with the andouille kick.  She loved it.  My black cod was the best seafood I've had since, well, Fish.  Incredibly moist, thin flaked fillet on a soybean, mushroom, sunchoke, and urchin base.  I can't describe this in a way that really does it justice, so I'll simply say that it is an edible form of happiness.

    Things were going well, so we kept it rolling with dessert.  After listening to the options, we decided to order one of everything.  It seemed like a rare opportunity to use this phrase without incurring substantial doggy bags.  Wife enjoyed the banana cake, while I downed a pistachio / chocolate mousse creation.  Pretty good stuff.

    I don't dish out 5 stars on a whim, but Little Fish earned it.  You should eat here.

    Thirteen years after I first darkened their doorstep, Little Fish hasn't lost any of its mojo.  The ratio of creativity and culinary artistry to cooking space must be the highest on the planet (or perhaps outside Tokyo).  In this tiny space (maybe ten tables, and a 5 x 10 open kitchen) the chef turns out expertly prepared seafood adorned with the freshest local ingredients, in presentations that rival Bar Lyonnais (where I dined the night before) for aesthetic appeal.  

    Our meal consisted of a starter of scallops on an unbelievably fresh, lovely pea puree that we practically licked off the plate, followed by the miso black cod and halibut with morels and aspargus on homemade gnocchi (hats off to the gnocchi maker, lightest I've had in recent memory).  All the dishes were delectable, and the service was just the right mix of warm and professional without a hint of obsequiousness.  The wine list is whatever is chilling in the fridge at the nearby State Wine Shoppe, this being a BYO (as many of the smaller restaurants are in Philadelphia).

    Little Fish is a great choice when you're seeking a top-notch dining experience in a laid-back, casual (and, it must be said, minimally decorated) space that borders on hole-in-the-wall.  A unique experience not to be missed if you're a foodie visiting Philadelphia.
