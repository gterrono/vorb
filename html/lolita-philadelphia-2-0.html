    NEVER GOING BACK AGAIN.

    Lolita, the previous version, was my favorite restaurant in Philadelphia. I would go there with friends, bring a bottle of patron and enjoy an amazing dinner.  

    Since it reopened, I was clamoring to go back.  I was thoroughly disappointed and rather disgusted. The menu is completely different. They had this amazing Salmon Large plate on the former menu that was delicious.  Now, it's primarily small plates for "sharing".  I use that term loosely because the plates are so tiny.

    The guacamole was $11 for a tiny cup barely enough for two people, where across the street at El Vez, you get an enormous bowl that 6 people could easily enjoy for $13.  

    We ordered three small plates- fish tacos, enchiladas verdes and shortrib chilaquiles.  Fish Tacos are one of my most favorite dishes, and we were sorely disappointed with what we received. There was very little fish in the taco and the tacos were so small you could eat them in one bite.  The taco itself tasted like a piece of paper.  I ended up scraping off the "fish" and just eating that.  But there wasn't much flavor.

    The shortribs had the most potential. It was flavorful, but again, a very small portion.  Smaller than the size of a woman's hand. certainly not enough for 2 people to share.

    The enchiladas verdes was mediocre. More flavor than the Fish tacos, but they came 45 minutes after our other small plates. We were starving, so we ate them regardless of the flavor.

    And the margaritas.  Our margaritas were made with Patron, which shouldn't be THAT big of an upcharge.  We were charged $85 for a pitcher of margarita mix with a dash of patron.  Probably the WORST margaritas I have ever had.  

    Overall, we were charged $420 for dinner and drinks for TWO people. It was a WASTE of time and money.  

    Next time I want a good mexican dinner, I'm going to El Vez.  Good Meals, Great Vibe, Better Service.  

    I will Never, Ever go back to Lolita.

    I went to Lolita for date night.  It was a great venue for that.  It was not super loud (which is often a miracle at these tiny 13th street restaurants) and it was dimly - dare I say romantically - lit.  There are a few large plates, but most of the dishes were small.  The tacos come in threes and are the same size as you would get at el vez across the street.  

    We started with the tradicional guacamole--it was a few (not many) dollars cheaper than the guacamole at el vez, but it was about 1/4 of the size, and did not come with salsa.  It was generally tasty, but not mind blowing.  I prefer thicker chunks of avocado in my guac and this was almost a little mushy.  The chips it came with were great--the majority are corn chips, but you also get a few plaintain chips and malanga chips.  I've never had malanga chips before, I'm still not sure what they are, but those were my favorite.  Very light and crispy.

    For my main course, I had the duck fat tamal.  It was a decent-sized portion for a "small" plate and with the guacamole I was full at the end of my meal.  The taste was okay.  It says it comes with a peanut mole, but I did not taste any peanut.  Also it states that it comes with a jicama corn salsa, and my salsa was mostly red pepper.  I didn't mind the substitutions.  But I was not wowed by the dish.

    For my cocktail, I went with the cucumber jalapeno margarita, which comes on the rocks with salt.  The taste of the cucumber was strong, and the jalapeno very subtle (as was the tequila--frankly, I couldn't taste it at all).  If you don't like spice, but like the taste of jalepenos (although I'm not sure you would have figured that out), this is the drink for you.

    The service was meh--the dishes came out way to quickly (my main dish came out about 30 seconds after the guacamole).  30 minutes after we got there, my drink was empty for about 10 minutes and we were out of water, and no one came by to refill us. Our server offered us dessert while we were still eating our food and then brought us the check immediately, even though we still had half of our guacamole on the table.

    I felt rushed, even though the restaurant was empty.  We were in and out in 40 minutes.  If you need a fast but relatively fancy dinner in center city, this is a good bet.  The competition for this type of cuisine in Philly is pretty fierce, and this would not be my first place to go.

    I expected great things of Marcie Turney's revamped Lolita, a Mexican-style mirror image of Barbuzzo. Lolita has the same hardwood tables and floor, steel bar, and long, narrow setup with a ton of people in it. It's crammed and bubbling and loud and there's a great atmosphere.

    They didn't have a table for us outside immediately and since it was a nice day, we went to the bar to grab a drink whilst one became available. There, we found Marcie herself running the kitchen, so we expected something spectacular, and were disappointed.

    My Margarita glass was only half full (what a wonderful metaphor for the evening) and both of them tasted unbalanced and dull. No more mixed drinks for us that night! The guacamole was very average, though the plantain chips were out of this world. If you want great guac, go down the road to El Vez!

    From there we were bustled out into the street, where you can sit in the warm Philly evening watching they gayborhood go by. People watching is always fun!

    We're (largely) vegetarian and there is an excellent choice for vegetarians and vegans alike, which was very pleasing. So much choice that we struggled to choose our dishes and looked to our (friendly and helpful) server for some advice.

    We started off with the tuna tacos which were probably the highlight of the meal - light, tasty, raw sushi grade tuna finished with scallions and spice. From there to the queso fundido, which again looks to compete with El Vez and fails. It was over salted, somewhat bland and the cheese had turned back into a block by the time it got to us, so we had to put it onto the tortillas in lumps.

    From there to the quesadillas, which were even more heavily salted and rather bland. I can't put my finger on what was wrong with them, but the presentation was also off with them.

    And then the octopus, which was the lowlight of the meal. Presentation was excellent, very reminiscent of the octopus at Barbuzzo, and it looked to be the highlight of the meal, but it was so heavily salted that it returned to the kitchen, half uneaten.

    After that, we looked at the dessert menu, found nothing inspiring and contemplated heading next door to Barbuzzo for the AMAZING salted caramel budino. But we couldn't face any more salt by this point, so we just sat back and guzzled the filtered tapwater.

    I think that more than anything, the biggest problem with Lolita is that there isn't a compelling reason to go back. The food at El Vez is so much better, it's not BYO any more, and the menu is not differentiating.

    My boyfriend and I made a last minute decision to check out Lolita on Sunday evening.  We had walked past it a few times before and it seemed to be drawing pretty good crowds.  We were quickly seated inside and were told that it was the first night of DIY tacos.  This deal is $35/person and comes with three courses served family style.  First course consists of pre-selected salad and chips and salsa.  The salad was delicious, a huge portion for 2 people and had a light and refreshing vinaigrette.  The chips were all made in house and were a mix of traditional tortilla chips and veggie chips with two salsas.  We asked the server to take them away since they were completely addicting.  For the tacos, I selected fried mahi and my boyfriend selected pork shoulder (the special for the day). The two meat options came on a huge platter and were accompanied by pico de gallo, cheese, a light cole slaw, grilled corn (amazing) and other taco toppings.  The mahi was heavily battered, but the fish itself was tender and flaky.  The pork was served cool, but had a great flavor as it was served in a marinade with pineapple--perfect for a summer meal.  Finally, dessert was a tres leches cake.  I always avoided this cake since I thought I didn't like it.  Since there was only one option I was forced to try it again.  Thank goodness I did! It was fluffy and delectable.  I honestly thought about ordering another slice.  Overall, the service was excellent and the interior was small but cozy.  Next time I would try to sit at the bar to see some of the action in the kitchen.  Definitely check this place out!

History

Established in 2004.

Lolita is a Modern Mexican Restaurant located in Philadelphia in Midtown Village. Originally opened in 2004 by restaurateurs Chef Marcie Turney and Valerie Safran, it underwent a major renovation in 2014 and reopened with a fresh look, a new Mexican street food-inspired menu and a full liquor license.

The extensive full-scale renovation highlights the addition of a bar and chef's counter looking into the open kitchen. Lolita seats 45 guests with additional outdoor sidewalk seating in the warmer months. Reservations are accepted every day for lunch and dinner, while seating at the bar and chef's counter is on a first come, first served basis. We have a full liquor license and accept all major credit cards.
