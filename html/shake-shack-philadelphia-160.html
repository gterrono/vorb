    Listed in I'm in Burger Heaven!

    Finally got around to visit Shake Shack!  My husband and I headed over for a late lunch on Labor Day- the holiday and the rain held off the crowds- we only waited about 5 minutes to order.  I went with the Shack Burger, he went with the Smoke Shack and we shared an order of fries and an Abita Root Beer. Total order was about $18.  Wait time for our meal after ordering was about 10 minutes.

    I'm going to start with service- the employees here ROCK.  And I don't use that praise lightly because many fast-casual establishments have employees that act as though they'd rather get run over by a truck than serve you your moderately priced lunch.  But at Shake Shack, everyone seems really excited and friendly.  It was refreshing

    Now I didn't try the shake (will do so on my next trip- I promise!) but the burger was worth the trip.  Super juicy and flavorful, great meat-to-bun ratio (I like when the patties are on the thinner side) and tasty toppings.  The lettuce and tomato were nice and fresh and crisp.  The fries didn't wow me.  They were over-salted versions of the fries my mom would cook for me in the oven when I was a kid.  Tasty, sure, but wouldn't make me go out of my way for them.  The Abita root beer was great but at $2.65, I wish I had realized they had free tap water in the back.  Dang!

    Overall, this spot is pretty legit.  Not sure if I'd wait in line for an hour for it (I'd probably head over to 500 degrees if I saw that line) but it was a nice visit and the food was good.

    So yeah, a lot of people were looking forward to this place, it's apparently a big hit in New York.  The first 3 times I passed by here, it had lines out the door.  Today, no line at all.  NONE.  I am quite frankly very puzzled at how anyone can think their food is even as good as Five Guys.  Here's why:

    The burger bun was so soggy, it was like eating a wet paper towel.  The bun, if dry, was nothing remarkable.  The "Shack Sauce"  was so tasteless, I couldn't tell what was in it.  I don't know, maybe they didn't put enough of it on the burger?  Tomatoes and lettuce added a "fresh" component, but little flavor.

    The fries tasted frozen.  For a long time.  They tasted like they weren't wrapped up real perfect in the freezer and had that kind of taste you get when the food picks up all the other flavors of random ickyness in a freezer.  They had a good outer texture, however.  Crinkle cut.  Salted  the right amount.

    The shake, vanilla, you could of course argue doesn't have as much flavor as chocolate or strawberry, but I struggled to find much vanilla flavor at all.  It tasted like frozen cream.  I've had much tastier shakes at McDonalds, when I used to eat there.  After watching all those scary films about fast food, I don't eat there anymore.

    The price for the burger 4.55 and fries 2.65, ok, not terrible.  The shake?  $5.  Good grief.

    This whole meal was so mediocre it makes me weep that so many people seem to like it.  Here's why Five Guys is better:
    1. burger toppings, a dozen of them, build your own, you choose
    2. fries are cut fresh in the kitchen, you can see the potato skin on them still, and cooked in peanut oil
    3. flavor, flavor, flavor

    This place doesn't have it.  You come in here, you are paying for the buildout, basically.  Nice wooden walls, stainless steel everywhere, spotless kitchen full of more stainless steel.  Looks impressive at first sight, but that is it.
