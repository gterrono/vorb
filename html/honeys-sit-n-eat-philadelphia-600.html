    Listed in Best Places to Eat - Philly, Brunchadelphia

    Honey's Sit 'n' Eat is one of Philly's better known brunch spots.  Somewhat of a cross between your Jewish grandmother's kitchen and a down home Southern diner, Honey's serves up everything from latkes and applesauce to biscuits and gravy.  I've been here a few times and the food has never disappointed.  The kitschy decor and on point service also adds to the experience, and definitely makes up for the long waits that one endures to eat here.

    On my most recent visit today, a Sunday morning at 9:30 am, the five of us still had to wait about 20 or so minutes.  When we were finally seated, I only had one thing on my mind: chicken fried steak.  And in the true spirit of Honey's, my brunch was not only delicious, but the portions were enough to feed at least two people.  I got a heaping portion of steak with country gravy, a cheese potato and onion latke, buttered biscuit, and scrambled eggs.  I didn't even need the side of bacon that I ordered out of impulse (delicious, Lancaster County bacon, by the way).

    Everything tasted delicious and savory, perfect for my tired, hungover this morning.  I couldn't even finish my food.  I did wish that my steak had a little more steak and a little less "country fried", but otherwise it was really good.  On another occasion, I've also had the macaroni and cheese pancakes (buttermilk pancakes with macaroni and cheese in the middle), which was a great mix of salty, savory, and sweet (especially with maple syrup drizzled all over everything).  I still have yet to try their non-brunch menu, but I'm sure it lives up to the reputation of the brunch fare.

    Honey's also offers a very cheap breakfast special during the week from 7-9 where you can get a full breakfast for under $4, something I'm looking forward to taking advantage of when they finally open their South Street location.  And other than the long waits that one has to endure no matter when they decide to come to Honey's, the food is great, the service is terrific, and it's definitely one of the better brunch and breakfast spots in this town.
