    Everyone knows how important--not to mention expensive and exhausting--it is to keep in step with the ever growing list of all the new must-try dining destinations.  Equal attention should be paid to trying out the city's mainstay restaurants like Matyson, that helped set the bar for BYO-excellence back before Passyunk Avenue was the new gayborhood and when Stephen Starr's empire was just barely beginning to cross Broad street.  These guys have been doing their thing for quite some time and it shows.  It's a mystery that I've lived in Philadelphia for so long and never tried Matyson until last night.  

    If I had to do things over again, I'd probably go with the 5 course, $45 tasting menu which changes every week.  Last night's edition featured oysters, foie gras and filet mignon, to name a few ingredients.  We skipped it because I'm not a fan of the foie and my guest doesn't do oysters.  Plus, with so many amazing sounding appetizers and entrees on the ala carte menu, there were plenty of options to choose from.  Everything looked so good, we narrowed it down ingredient-by-ingredient, process of elimination style.

    We settled on escargot (served over polenta with pancetta and poached quail eggs) and a truffled burdock root ravioli (with black trumpet mushrooms) for appetizers.  Burdock root, a little known veggie, is something similar to salsify and tastes something like a nutty potato or a spicy turnip.  When you add truffle to the mix, it doesn't really matter though, does it?  For mains we started with a coffee rubbed duck breast served over cheddar (grits/potatoes?--idk, but they were delicious) with an ancho bbq sauce.  The duck was a perfect medium-rare and the dish itself was perfect winter comfort food.
    Next up were the scallops, served over maitake mushrooms and wild rice.  The sauce was scented with lemongrass and a good match for the earthiness of the mushrooms.

    Service was fantastic.  Our server coursed out our food in a way that let us enjoy each dish (and each other's company).  Her menu knowledge was encyclopedic and her enthusiasm for the restaurant very clear.  We skipped desert, but got to try the champagne sorbet.  Highly recommended if you want something slightly sweet but not at all filling to end your meal on.    

    Word on the street is, they'll be updating their decor fairly soon which is another reason to justify a visit or revisit.

    On a side note. . . The menu information listed on the website is a bit out of step with the menu served in the restaurant.  I got myself all excited for stuff I saw on the webpage only to find a different menu at the restaurant.  Again, there were no shortage of interesting sounding (and ultimately delicious) dishes to choose from.  However, updating a webpage only takes a few seconds and I think it's a step of service akin to the napkin refold.  Most of the time, when you get up to use the bathroom you return to find your napkin refolded.  If it's done, you probably won't notice.  But if it's not, you just might.  

    (For full disclosure, the bathroom here is very nice and I found my napkin refolded upon upon returning to the table).

Specialties

One feature that sets us apart from the pack is our weekly tasting menu that showcases whatever is fresh and in season. The themes can range from lobster to vanilla. Five courses for $45 Monday through Thursday. Check the menu page for this weeks tasting menu.

History

Established in 2003.

Matyson opened its doors in the fall of 2003  and became an immediate success under the leadership of Matt & Sonjia Spector. Matt & Sonjia installed the framework for what Matyson has become today. After four great years the couple left to pursue another restaurant in the Napa Valley named Jole, but they left the restaurant in very capable hands. Younger cousin, Ben Puchowitz, whom had been working at Matyson as a line cook since its opening, assumed the role of Executive Chef, and his father, Butch Puchowitz became full owner. Ben has instilled his own personality into the food, without changing the original concepts. The staff of Matyson has continued to win awards and high acclaim for their refined cuisine and cozy atmosphere.
