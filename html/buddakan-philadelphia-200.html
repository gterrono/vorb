    My husband & I had dinner here for our 5th Anniversary on Friday and we had such a wonderful time. The service was great, the food was amazing and the atmosphere was really cool - it was more of a lounge feel than a quiet upscale dinner feel.

    We arrived about 5 minutes late b/c we were waiting on our sitter for our daughter so I called Buddakan to notify them - they were really courteous and told us they'd hold our table for 15 minutes which was a nice gesture. As soon as we arrived, they seated us in a nice cozy booth across from the bar. We were given 3 choices for water (ice, spring or sparkling) and we also ordered some nonalcoholic beverages. For appetizer we ordered the Shrimp Dumplings which were really yummy - better than others we've had. We also ordered the House Salad with Ginger Dressing which was enough for the both of us to share and it was so light and refreshing! For entrees we ordered the Pad Thai w/ Jumbo shrimp and the Pan Seared Chilean Sea Bass. Both dishes were wonderful but my favorite was the Chilean Sea Bass. It was so "melt-in-your-mouth" flavorful and buttery. Makes my mouth water just thinking about it! For dessert we ordered the Crying Chocolate and the Ginger Ice Cream Sandwich. Both were delicious but we preferred the Ice Cream Sandwich b/c it also came with a side of fried cinnamon sugar apricot with a wonderful glaze filling! I also had a cappuccino afterwards which was nice :) We definitely plan to go back again but maybe not splurge as much - I hear their lunch is really nice and affordable!

    I came here for the first time a while ago for a friend's birthday and i LOVEDIT!!!!! My three friends and I shared the tuna pizza which was pretty good, but nothing spectacular in my opinion.

    For the entree, I ordered the delicious tea-smoked ribs. They were fall off the bone tender and the smokiness and spice tingled on my lips and tongue as if smoking a joint and the sauce was amazing. It was served with a cooling green mango salad, making it a perfect pair, but heck I'd eat the ribs plain.

    My friends ordered the sea bass with maitake mushrooms. It had a nice crispy crust, tender and flaky meat and the sauce on the veggies and mushrooms tasted like a slightly sweeter version of Chinatown's sizzling beef. The sauce with the mushrooms gave the fish a decadent, meaty flavor, that enhanced the fish immensely. We ooh-ed and ahh-ed as we ate it along with everything else. The duck breast with spoon bread was of course, also cooked tender with a crispy skin. The sppon bread made the dish. It was oddly familiar but I couldn't quite put my finger on it. The sirloin steak with wasabi mashed potatoes was the least innovative and unique of all the dishes we had that night, but still quite good.

    For dessert, we ordered the chocolate bento box. By this time, we were all stuffed but enjoyed the different flavors paired with the chocolate and the different textures of each dessert held within each square. It was an awesome meal.

    I've been back for the bento box special twice, and the food wasn't quite as good each time. ::Sigh:: if only dinner was more affordable! But I'll definitely be back again, and again, and again.

    The food is fabulous when you order the right things!  In particular, the Edamame Ravioli appetizer and the Miso Black Cod entree (which is the same delicious fish as a butterfish) were both incredibly flavorful.  They both had a smooth richness that was really wonderful.  The Guilt cocktail was light and bubbly and delicious - no guilt or remorse there.  

    Other apps: the Calamari Salad was well-seasoned with tender rings of calamari - another great dish.  The Buddakan Sashimi was pretty good too, but the House Salad was forgettable.

    I sampled the Sea Bass, which had a great flavor too, but would definitely still pick the Black Cod over the Bass.  Wasabi Mashed Potatoes were tasty, but a little disappointing...I want a little more kick out of something with wasabi in the name!

    For dessert, I got the Milk Chocolate Caramel Tart.  Expectations were high after the my delicious apps and entree, but sadly,  the dessert was not worth finishing.  Overly sweet (and I can handle sweet!) and odd chocolate consistencies dragged down this overall rating from achieving 5-stars.

    I was pleasantly surprised that they had a shellfish-free menu to accommodate allergies.  Great wait staff too!
