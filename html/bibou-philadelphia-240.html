    Average meal. Tasty but nothing mind-blowing. We went here on a Friday evening. I was expecting the small sized dining room given other reviews, so I was not surprised to see that it is indeed a VERY small dining room. Appetizers: chilled summer squash soup, gravlax. First taste of the soup was extremely bland, but once mixed properly with the tomatillo/jalapeno jam on the bottom of the bowl tasted much better. The gravlax was not particularly memorable. Tiny cubed smoked salmon with a somewhat overpowering turmeric creme fraiche. Wish they had at least one salad option on the menu, which I think another Yelper also mentioned. Mains: roasted veal medallion, and the infamous pork foot. Our cut of the medallion was very uneven and poorly plated. One larger piece placed slightly askew on top of a tiny silver dollar piece (upon glancing over at our older neighbors who also ordered the veal, they had a much nicer/larger/even cut of meat). The meat itself was still cooked well and was tender and tasty. Natural jus on the plate/spaetzel was a bit too salty for me (and I love salt.) The pig foot was probably the best part of the meal - crispy, gamey, and very savory however does feel more appropriate as a heavy fall/winter dish. I really enjoyed the accompanying French lentils. Dessert: Grand Marnier soufflé. Apparently what I missed on the menu was the "frozen" part. So when I anticipated a lovely soufflé, I received ice cream. The chef decided to make rounds in the dining room (which I admittedly hate) while we were eating said soufflé, so I took the opportunity to ask him about it when he approached our table to inquire about our dining experience. He apologized for the confusion and offered to bring out another dessert. Nice gesture. Later, we realized that the dessert had been comp'ed from our bill as well. We had a fairly pleasant meal, but probably will not be back unless we see something particularly appealing show up on the online menu.

    I forgot to mention a jarring detail -- the butter. Oh man. Not good. Not good at all. Prepackaged in a tin foil wrapper brought out to the table cold, i.e. purchased in bulk like we're at a diner? Okay maybe not a diner -- but definitely not befitting of the type of restaurant I'm sure they're striving to be.

    So, I finally had dinner at Bibou after a few of my friends had suggested that I should go here.  I understand the appeal, and we had a nice meal, but there were a few misses.
    We started with three appetizers to share.  The foie gras with a date jam, the escargots, and a special chicken liver mousse.  The foie gras and the mousse were very good and we enjoyed them both.  The escargots with some brown sauce, nuts, and beans were not so good.  
    Then came the main course and we had the famous/infamous pig's foot stuffed with foie gras over lentils and a special red snapper cooked in parchment.  The pig's foot was very good and I was pleased since this came highly recommended.  The snapper was also very good, moist, tasty, but the vegetables which accompanied the fish did not seem to go very well.
    Then came dessert, a frozen Grand Marnier souffle and a pistachio creme something.  We ended up not finishing the souffle, as we were both stuffed.  Some espresso and some macaroons from the chef and we were all done.
    The staff was pleasant and polite and genuinely helpful.  
    Upon leaving, we both agreed that we had a nice meal, but the missus did say that the wine we brought outclassed the meal quite seriously.  I agree with her.

    In truth, I have had quite a few rustic-type French meals in all sorts of different places and this was good, but not amazing as I had been led to believe.  Would I recommend?  Yes.  Would I recommend over other places in Philly for a great meal and experience?  Probably not.

    A Small dining area accompanied by French music, we have been to Bibou twice. Both times we had the same knowledgeable waiter. He was on point with describing anything on the menu in case you are unsure what they are serving. He also was familiar with how light or rich each dish was. The first time we went to Bibou, we were able to order the chicken liver mousse, escargot, lobster bisque and foie gras for starters. Each dish was extremely flavorful! The second time we went, we ordered a cantaloupe salad and braised beef tongue. The cantaloupe wasn't anything to die for but still tasty nonetheless. The beef tongue was light and tender. We also had the bone marrow entree, filet mignon and purple potato encrusted halibut. All dishes were cooked to perfection and flavorful with each bite. We finished off dinner with frozen chocolate made with Grand Marnier. Each bite tasted like orange liqueur followed by rich dark chocolate. If you plan on dining here, plan ahead of time as reservations need to made a few weeks in advance or hope for any cancellations.

    I was in Philadelphia for a day with my mother-in-law, and we decided to eat in town rather than fight traffic, so I quickly scanned for a highly-rated restaurant nearby, found Bibou, and went with high expectations.

    They were not met.

    Bibou seems a little schizophrenic. I think they try to be French, at least judging by their music, but you couldn't tell it by the menu - a couple of dishes were French, the others not so much. Walking in the place settings seemed really upscale, and our server did the put the napkin on our lap thing some high-end restaurants do, but on the other hand when they cleared our appetizer plates they didn't clean up the bread crumbs on the table. Also the servers/bus boys were a little strange, they were like "your dish was good?" rather than asking us how the food was.

    We only had the foie gras duo, a bass dish, and the bone marrow  (I would have liked to try more but we didn't realize they didn't get credit cards until we got there, and were too lazy to find at ATM to get more cash from). The foie gras was done correctly and was tasty, the duo part was not particularly enjoyable. The bass was a piece of fish on a plate with some sauce thiings, tasty but nothing special. The bone marrow was OK, but was served with potatoes you'd see at a typical diner and some leaves with sauce - it didn't look as if the chef had put a lot of thought into the dish.

    All in all, not bad, but not particularly good either.
