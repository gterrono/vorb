    Casual pub with a friendly vibe. Went for dinner Saturday night. It was pretty vacant so service was quick and attentive. We had the calamari which was pretty pricy for the quality and the amount. For dinner, I had the shepard's pie and it was delicious, with the right amount of flavor. The portion was larger than I anticipate! My boyfriend had the filet mignon sliders and he said the meat was very flavorful. I was not impressed with the fries though. They tasted like cardboard and were too overdone. I was happy they had dogfish pumpkin beer on tap, though! I would come back again, especially since their brunch menu looked promising.

    The only reason why I'm giving Black Sheep 4 stars and not 3 is because I was really craving mac and cheese the day I went and theirs definitely hit the spot. Black Sheep is just like any other pub, nothing special about it. We ordered mac and cheese as our appetizer to split between 3 people and then I ordered it again as my main dish too. My friends ordered vegetable quesadillas. Our total was around $30 so $10 per person...pretty cheap. One thing that I disliked was how uncomfortable the booths were- I kept sliding off. This isn't a restaurant/pub that I would list when recommending places to friends. I would probably go back again if I'm walking by and in need of some mac and cheese but wouldn't go out of my.
