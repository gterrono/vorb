    First to Review

    Went here last night. Great renovation of what used to be a pretty dingy bar. This portion of south street is really improving.

    The food menu was very interesting, big focus on the apps & sandwiches. I like that it's not trying to be a gastro-pub and restaurant at the same time. We ordered the Nachos, they were fresh cooked and nicely presented.

    On the downside, the beer list has some strange choices. I mean Corona light? I don't care if it's on draft, I didn't go to a high end bar that boasts about it's beer menu to drink corona available in any dive bar, why not substitute a good light craft beer? I ordered an oyster stout, and it came with a 1"-2" head. It was a good beer, but just badly poured.  It wasn't a huge glass to start, so not only did I feel robbed, it was difficult to drink and enjoy. Hopefully it was a learning curve issue, as it's been open only 2 days, but in a place that focuses on beer, they need to improve pouring it.

    This place has huge potential, but it's got some way to go in my opinion.

    Also, apparently not open for lunch, not sure why seen as the food is perfect for a lunch menu.

    it's generally frowned upon to review a place that's been in business for less than a week, but the cambridge has done a great job managing its very busy first few days -- and they deserve some credit.

    sunday dinnertime was packed, but staff was going with the flow. the owner made a point of stopping by our table to chat, and we appreciated the friendly hello when we knew he had a million other things to worry about.

    beer list is large and varied and looks like it changes often. we only tried a few items off the menu:

    burrata baguette: liked it, but beware if you're lukewarm about anchovies. they dominate the flavor in this, big time.

    grilled peach & pecan salad: loved it. probably not large enough for an entree if you're hungry, but a very solid salad. pair it with an app.

    uncle reuben: great. it's served with salad instead of fries, so you don't feel too gross after inhaling it.

    additional two cents: i've seen a good bit of online commentary bemoaning the abundance of gastropubs in philly and criticizing the cambridge's opening. i don't get it. business owners are trying to make a living, and as the cambridge has illustrated in its opening days, philadelphians flock to gastropubs (and this particular neighborhood is not one of the pub-saturated ones in the first place).

    you can't criticize a couple for pursuing a business model that clearly works. this place is going to succeed. i had nothing against tritone, but there's a reason it went under -- and we still have bob & barbara's, which isn't going anywhere. i hope everyone can start making peace with change, because so far, the only thing worth complaining about with the cambridge is the noise level (it is very loud)!

    Listed in G-Ho In the house! My house, that is., Pour me something tall and strong, Gastropubs - The perfect combo of good food and good beer

    This block of South street has a lot going on recently, and I think the Cambridge is a nice addition to the neighborhood. As any place that has just opened, I'm willing to accommodate for mishaps and mistakes right up front, but so far my experience here twice has been decent to pretty good.

    Good things:
    - Beer list: extensive and has some very unique choices along with some not so unique ones (corona light) which honestly offers something for everyone. Note: not everyone is a beer snob!
    - Atmosphere: the old Tritone is well redone with the rustic, 'aged' decor. Lighting and space are also a plus. Does not seemed cramped even though it is a smallish and crowded space. There is an AWESOME outdoor patio, though when I visited on a slower night it appeared to not be open.
    - Cocktails: the list seemed really well done, had some awesome originals, and of the three I tried were very delicious. The "main line" was great, with the tequila based one being my favorite. I was impressed with their execution of the classic cocktails as this seems to be a problem with a lot of so called quality bartenders in Philadelphia, and everywhere for that matter.

    Decent things:
    - Food: As a foodie at heart I always want this category to be in the very good section of a bar/restaurant but it just wasn't for me. I had the nachos that I really really didn't like. Everyone else seemed to have a decent meal though, so I'm hoping this category levels up with my subsequent visits.
    - Service: I had two opposite experiences, one being extremely accommodating and exceeding expectations, and another being average and slightly dismissive. Hard to judge up front with two entirely different experiences.

    The bad:
    I guess I'm not the only one to notice (thankfully) that this bar is sweltering hot. I noticed it on the first night I went (a Saturday) which was rather packed, and then again on a weekday which was not packed at all. Equally as humid and hot. I'm not one to complain about this kind of problem, in fact I'm the one who's always cold, so I knew that it was actually a problem. They kinda have to do something about this. I get that it's a old building but so are the majority of businesses on the same street.

    Overall, I am quite happy to welcome this place to the neighborhood and I am hopeful that they will meet expectations after being in the swing of things for a few months.

    Having walked by The Cambridge at least a dozen times since it opened, I decided last night was as good a time as any to check it out for dinner.  Since it was a beautiful night, we chose to sit outside on the little patio in the back, which was lovely.  

    Me and the BF got there right before 6pm, so we were able to enjoy happy hour prices for our first round (beers priced at $4 for happy hour from 4pm - 6pm).  The beer list here is pretty exciting and they already had some pumpkin beers on the draft list, so I was a happy camper.  (The draft list lets you know what's on tap and what's next on the list if that keg is kicked....very nice!)  I ordered the Longtrail Imperial Pumpkin while he got the Ayinger Oktoberfest - both beers were delicious.

    Now on to the food!  My roommate ate here last week and told me the food was "eh" (that's a direct quote), so I was interested to see what they had to offer.  For the app, we ordered the shortrib mac & cheese.  Oh my god.  Holy moly.  So good!  (Even better with a couple hits of sriracha).  I definitely recommend this!  For entrees, I got the Sansei Kris (Asian pulled pork, slaw, Mongolian BBQ sauce goodness) and he ordered The Cambridge Hall (beef burger, pancetta, wine poached pears and brie).  We were blown away by how juicy and delicious everything was.  The Sansei Kris is one of the best things I've had in quite a while.  It was full of flavor, the pork was perfectly cooked, and the slaw was so so good.  The only criticism is that I could have used some more pickled jalapenos, but I probably could have gotten more if I asked.  Nevertheless - it was a great sandwich.  On the flip side, the the burger was equally as awesome.  Super juicy, cooked a perfect medium rare, and the pears/brie combo was a perfect topping.

    I promise you, I will be back here again soon!  Service was great, beer list is rocking, and food was mmmm!

    Side notes (aka my only complaints...for now):  (1)  If you look at the menu, there is a sandwich called the Philly Chicken BLT.  If you look at the description , you'll notice there is no B, L, or T listed.  Very interesting.  I overheard a convo at another table where the girl asked if there was any B on the sandwich and the waiter told her yes.  Why none of this is on the menu is beyond me.  (2) As we were walking through the bar on our way out, I noticed it's super dark in here.  Not sure why they keep the lights so dim!  I definitely liked it outside better.
