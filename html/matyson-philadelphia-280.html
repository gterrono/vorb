    Celebrated a special anniversary with Matyson this week, so we went with the tasting menu ($45/pp)... with a corn theme. The tasting blew us away. Overall, we experienced excellent presentation, special/creative cooking, and great service. Add that to BYOB (we chose well) and it was a perfect evening.

    Course 1: smoked corn vichyssoise, served cold. Delicious flavor, with great texture coming from the fried leeks and pickled corn kernels. Started the meal with a yummmm.

    Course 2: corn-encrusted soft shell crab, served over a Mexican-style corn salad and huitlacoche paste. Strong flavors, can't go wrong with soft-shell crab done right. Only complaint: only half a crab per person.

    Course 3: corn and lobster risotto, with mascarpone and peaches. The risotto (which was a bit loose for a true risotto, but I digress) was laden with chopped tarragon, which gave an entrancing, anise-like bounce to this otherwise sweet-tart dish. Excellent combination of tastes.

    Course 4: roasted veal chop, served over sweet corn puree, with smashed blackberries. This is the best veal we have ever had: perfectly prepared, tender, delicate, laced with briny flavor. Great contrast with the sweetness of the corn and the tartness of the blackberries.

    Course 5: corn & black pepper ice cream, with caramel corn. This was the only underwhelming course. Not nearly peppery enough, and not enough caramel corn.

    Would definitely swing back through here the next time we are in town.

    This place is nothing short of amazing. I came for a friend's birthday dinner, and we decided to go for the tasting menu - DID NOT REGRET THIS DECISION ONE BIT.

    It's BYO, but probably the classiest BYO in Philly. Nothing like Don Memo's, Banana Leaf, or the other BYOs Penn students frequent to get outrageously inebriated. I'm fairly sure that if our party had become raucous, the staff would not have taken it too kindly. We brought two bottles of red wine that went splendidly with our meal.

    That being said, the ambience of Matyson is intimate and upscale. You push aside a curtain as you walk in, and there's mandatory coat check (and your coat check card is an actual playing card from a deck, how cute is that?). There are candles on every table, and the lighting is low and romantic. It's the perfect place for a date or a quiet dinner out with friends.

    Onto the food: the ingredient for that week's tasting menu was greens, and each dish was perfectly laid out. Though the portions were tiny, we left feeling full, perhaps because there were five courses. I'm going to go ahead and say that each course was delicious, because it was.

    1. Split pea soup with poppyseed creme fraiche, country ham, and mint
    2. Egg noodles with greens and a poached egg on top
    3. Striped bass with blue basil, artichoke, fava beans, and tomato
    4. Braised veal breast with mascarpone risotto (my favorite course)
    5. Chai tea creme brûlée

    It's a bit pricey but worth every penny. For a party of four we paid around $200, plus tip.

    I have heard wonderful things about Matyson, so my roommate and I decided to give it a shot. I found it to be unexpectedly intimate with delicious food in reasonable portion sizes and very pleasant service.

    Ambience: We were seated at a small table in the middle of the restaurant, with a row of larger tables on either side of us. The space is relatively small, but I still felt far enough from the tables around us that we were able to have a private dinner with personal conversations. The room was a little too darkly lit, so I didn't really notice the decor, but that probably contributed to the intimacy we felt. I noticed that each waitress and hostess had her own unique style that she seemed encouraged to express--a small detail, but it gave me the impression that although the restaurant was up-scale, it was not stuffy, and I thought it was consistent with its innovative and modern theme.

    Service: My roommate bought a bottle of wine at a shop around the corner right before the dinner and upon our arrival, our waitress took it to be chilled, reporting to us the status of the wine bottle once or twice. In general, our waitress was attentive, but not overbearing, and helpful while I was making my entree selection, taking into consideration what would go best with the appetizer and wine. The food came out very quickly even though it was a Saturday night and the restaurant was pretty full.

    Food: We ordered the banana bread foie gras to share, and for entrees, I went with the striped bass with artichoke and tomato mussel jus while my roommate selected the Long Island duck breast.

    I thought the foie gras was phenomenal. A winning combination of textures, between the crisped skin, the spongy banana bread, and the soft and creamy foie gras in between. The sweet and savory flavors, given even greater depth by the addition of the nutty flavors in the bread and the hazelnuts, blew me away. Beautifully executed. Believe the hype and don't miss this dish.

    The bass was excellent as well. The fish was extremely tender with nicely crisped skin. The fish lay across a bed of lightly pickled artichokes, of which I was a fan. The pieces of mussels in the sauce was a nice little surprise since I wasn't expecting much of it. My only complaint would be that the tomato jus was a little overwhelming for my tastes. Portion size was more generous than I expected, with a pretty sizeable strip of bass about seven inches long. I tasted my friend's duck breast, and it was very tender and juicy, but I don't think it was quite as good as the duck I had at Fork a few months ago.

    Overall, I enjoyed my experience at Matyson, and recommend the foie gras as a MUST HAVE.

Specialties

One feature that sets us apart from the pack is our weekly tasting menu that showcases whatever is fresh and in season. The themes can range from lobster to vanilla. Five courses for $45 Monday through Thursday. Check the menu page for this weeks tasting menu.

History

Established in 2003.

Matyson opened its doors in the fall of 2003  and became an immediate success under the leadership of Matt & Sonjia Spector. Matt & Sonjia installed the framework for what Matyson has become today. After four great years the couple left to pursue another restaurant in the Napa Valley named Jole, but they left the restaurant in very capable hands. Younger cousin, Ben Puchowitz, whom had been working at Matyson as a line cook since its opening, assumed the role of Executive Chef, and his father, Butch Puchowitz became full owner. Ben has instilled his own personality into the food, without changing the original concepts. The staff of Matyson has continued to win awards and high acclaim for their refined cuisine and cozy atmosphere.
