    My company had our holiday party here.  After calling quite a few places in the neighborhood, we decided on Wedge and Fig as it was the most accommodating to our requests and our budget.  Plus, we couldn't say no to cheese.

    There were around fourteen of us, and the owner was ready for us when we arrived.  They opened our wine for us and brought out two very large beautiful cheese plates.  There were around seven varieties of cheese on each slate.  The cheese plate was amazing!  If I was reviewing Wedge + Fig on the cheese plate alone, it would receive five stars hands down.  The cheese was arranged from mild to strong with delicious treats to go with the cheese lining the sides of the board such as honey, walnuts, and fig paste. I usually hate brie, but there was this triple cream brie there that was like creamy butter.  I preferred the cheese on the milder end of the board, but almost everything was good.  The only one I didn't like was the blue cheese, but I never like blue cheese anyway.  I honestly would have been happy with just eating cheese all night.  It was that good.  The staff kept our bread bowls full so we could finish off the cheese.

    For my second course I had a nicoise salad with tuna.  It was exactly what you expect from a nicoise salad with olives, eggs, and capers.  My main course was a chicken breast with a red sauce served with a side of green beans.  The chicken was properly cooked and juicy.  However, I did not care for the red sauce.  I just didn't like the flavor.  The beans were a little bland.  It was disappointing after the amazing cheese plate.  

    For dessert we had a variety of brownies.  They were way too sweet for my liking.  Many people liked the pecan pie.  Again, I'm just not a fan of pecan pie in general, as I don't like desserts that are that sweet.

    The cheese plate really wowed me, but everything else was forgettable.  However, I would come back for the cheese plate any day though.  I wouldn't mind having another party here either if next time we just focused more on the first course and the salad and skipped the main course and dessert.  I also would like to return when the weather is nicer and check out the garden in the back.

    Listed in Bed, Brunch, Bed

    Wedge & Fig is one of the better brunch options in the city right now. Every dish that I have tried has been successful. From their weekly special omelet, to Eggs Nola, to their Shakamaxon sandwich; all smack of fresh, French country style cooking. Scones are made in house, rendering my inner Englishman completely defenseless against them. I haven't been able to resist ordering them even once. Served with traditional clotted cream, jam is not needed; as the sweetness of whichever fruit is being used is more than enough. Artisan blend coffee is available in French press form...have some. One might even opt for a cheese board before, after, or instead of the traditional brunch.

      A glass cased refrigerator displays 20 or so odd cheeses which are used in various dishes, and available for purchase...nice.  The cast is familiar, but solid. Prima Donna, Valdeon, Taleggio were all invited to the party...we know those guys, don't we? Potted plants and a water feature reside in the front window, and work from local photographers festoon the walls. I've seen better work...but who's judging?  One should be aware that space is at a premium at W & F, so conversations tend to be quasi-private, at best. Lady Mary and I see this as an opportunity to commit minor mischief against would be eavesdroppers. We make up completely outrageous stories, talk through them in all seriousness, watch people next to us try not to gasp, and go home laughing about it for days. 'You killed Steve, Francis?!?'  'Couldn't be helped my love, he was talking to the Russians.' What fun!  Outdoor seating in the summer is a lovely option if one arrives early enough for a table.

      The staff is friendly and knowledgable. We have been able to talk to everyone of our servers intelligibly about food (or life), and they indulge our eccentricities. Well done. They may, in fact, be host to our favourite overall staff in the city. Cheers from the original Urqhart.

      If Wedge & Fig is on your list of possibilities to try, do move it up a notch or two, and have the scones!

Specialties

Old City's Cheese Bistro, featuring dinner, lunch and weekend brunch.  Sample our chicken pot pie, mac & cheese and scrumptious salads in the evening.  For lunch, try  our unique sandwiches, salads and soups (take out available for most items).  Omelettes, strata, cream-cheese-stuffed French toast are some brunch favorites.  Dine under the stars in our secluded garden.  Bring a bottle of wine or ale and we will pair it with a selection of cheeses.  The perfect romantic evening!

History

Established in 2006.

We rescued Old City Cheese Shop just before it closed.  We gave it some badly needed upgrades, changed the menu, re-named the store to wedge+fig.  And we stocked the cheese case with cheese!

Meet the Business Owner

Kirk N. Business Owner

Cheese lover from an early age.  Lived on a sailboat for 15 years.  Used to play guitar.  Now I own a restaurant.
