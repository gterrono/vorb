    Listed in Yelp 100 Challenge - 2013, Get Out Of Town! Philly Edition

    A bar after my own heart. I found a home away from home in Philly. It reminds me of a place, Daily Refresher, in Rochester. Speakeasy-style, white and black tiled floors, candles lit on every table -- they have an eye for style, I say.

    It's a comfortable place for a Friday/Saturday night drink -- a bar that's not too loud and has good music playing in the background. Yes, you can still hear what your neighbor is saying.

    They have a solid drink menu -- cocktails, draft beer and bottles. A few local options and a nice variety like ciders, stouts, porters, and sours. It's pretty awesome to see that there's something for every kind of drinker / beer lover -- well-rounded, indeed. Unfortunately, when we were there, they were out of the most popular draft brews like the IPAs and porters. I think they're new and still working the kinks out. Not to fret, I found a tasty cocktail to warm me up -- the Autumn Blossom. It was a citrus (orange) base, but a hint of warm spices - like cinnamon perhaps? I'd order it again.

    We were hungry so I squealed like a giddy little girl when I saw they had French onion soup. I had been craving it that week, and was set to order it. It was wonderous. My dreams were realized. Obviously it came out pipping hot, the broth was super flavorful, rich, and dense as it was absorbing the crostini and cheese. It was comforting and just enough to fill me up. We also ordered the perogies for the table. I tried one and it was yummy. They were big like half moon raviolis and super seared on the outside. The mashed potato filling was garlic-y and creamy and the applesauce to top was a great savory-sweet combination. 'Twas an evening of comfort food.

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge, Brunch Spots, Bloody Mary Morning, La Colombe Friendly

    My boyfriend and I trekked all the way to NoLibs for brunch at the much-talked-about Jerry's Bar on Saturday. We had a late start and arrived around 2, so there wasn't a wait for a table which was great as I was getting pretty hungry by that point. The bar is really pretty. Lots of very cool touches with regards to decor and a beautiful outdoor patio adjacent to the dining room.

    We started with a round of bloody marys and coffee. The bloody mary came with an Old Bay rim and a skewer with an olive, cornichon, cherry tomato, banana pepper and a gigantic shrimp. Pretty delicious. The coffee was very tasty as well, I'm thinking it was probably La Colombe which is my fav.

    I've been on an eggs benedict kick lately so I went with that, and shared a side of delicious thick-cut bacon. The eggs in my benny were perfectly poached and served over tiny toast rounds with thin slices of ham. The hash browns alongside were ample and delicious though pretty greasy. But, you know, hash browns. My boyfriend had the croque madame which looked delicious though he thought it could have used more cheese.

    Overall, we enjoyed our experience and I'd really like to get back there for dinner sometime soon.

    Listed in Brunchie Munchies, Philly Staycation

    I think, no I know, that I have a new favorite brunch spot in this city and it is nestled in a really odd little corner of Northern Liberties. Sunday morning brunch is a staple event in my life, the best sweet and savory meal to be had shared amongst friends with the addition of pre-noon alcohol consumption without stink eye, really...it's quite perfect. Vinny P., Leo I. and myself had a delightful outdoor brunch that will soon be repeated if I have any say in it, oh! and I do because I plan brunch club. My first visit here with the boys was absolutely lovely, the weather was great and we sat in the finished alley still within earshot of the beautiful jazz music being played from inside, but quiet enough that we could still have a nice little conversation. My eggs Jerry was amazing, I'm a sucker for all brunch things that include smoked salmon so this order was a no-brainer. Paired with a hashbrown and baguette toast for dipping into the drippy eggs and a mug of La Colombe, I'd say this was a darn near perfect meal.

    My second visit here was with my main hoe Aaliyah P. and her yelp love Zachary H. where we initially asked for a table, but ended up settling for seats at the bar near the open front door and just across from the bass and guitar players. Bar seats are honestly not a bad way to go if you have only 2-3 eating together, you get an awesome view of the bartenders making nicely crafted cocktails while catching the light cross breezes of the restaurants space. Speaking of space, this place is beautiful...hard to believe that it once played home to a super divey bar. This visit started as all should, with a beautiful Hendricks cocktail followed by a shared plate of bacon, a shared walnut cinnamon sticky bun and then onto entrees of which two of us ordered the shirred eggs while the other ordered a burger. The shirred eggs with mushrooms and spinach and that delicious truffle oil was a great choice, my only qualm was that the eggs were slightly overcooked and not quite as drippy as I would have liked. It wasn't serious enough that I would send it back and ask for it to be done over again, but it might make me think twice about ordering it again. While most might say that's a bold statement to not order a second time for something easily fixable, I say it's just as well because the rest of the menu looks just as delicious that I don't know if I 'd ever want to order the same thing twice.

    I asked the waitress on our first visit what would happen to the alley in the winter months and she said she wasn't exactly sure except that there was talk that an outdoor fireplace/fire pit and/or heat lamps may be brought in to extend the outdoor dining season a bit longer which I am absolutely excited for. I debated for a long time sharing this review so as not to find myself in a position of not getting a table at my new favorite brunch place, but I'm in a continuously generous mood these days so run, don't walk here. You're welcome.

    As diehard fans of the former Cafe Estelle, my friends and I had Jerry's Bar on our radar after it came out on the Philly restaurant newsfeed. We finally made the journey to NoLibs for brunch and were completely pleased by the experience.

    Jerry's Bar has a live jazz band and a second floor to people-watch, which was where we ended up. It was really cozy to be the only table up at the top; it was also wonderful that we could listen to great music without having to shout for a good conversation.

    The scrambled eggs and gravlax were a special at the old establishment, so I was hugely pleased to see that they'd made it to the main brunch menu at Jerry's. The eggs were gently but perfectly cooked and the pickled veg was a great compliment to the dish.

    The Bloody Jerry (a Bloody Mary with an Old Bay rim and a shrimp garnish) was out of this world. I wished I'd ordered my own, but had to settle for sneaking sips out of my friends' drinks and nursing my awesome cup of coffee.

    Jerry's Bar has proven that it deserves all the rave reviews that it has been receiving, and I can't wait to come back in the near future for more awesome food and some of their inventive cocktails.
