        Listed in Yelp 100 for 2013, Lunch Places in Philly, Bars in Philly

        An Irish bar with an extended menu,  Black Sheep sits just off Rittenhouse Square on 17th St.   A friend and I decided to have lunch there on a Friday around 1:00pm.  It was fairly empty-- maybe six other people were either at the bar or at a table having lunch.  

        We just wanted food, so we ordered the roasted veggies, salad, and a pulled pork sandwich.  The roasted veggies plate was large, had a great variety, and were seasoned well.  The only downsides-- pretty salty and probably reheated rather than freshly roasted.  Still, it hit the spot.

        My pulled pork sandwich, that I thought was slightly overpriced at $11, was giant and on a nicely toasted and soft French loaf, and it had a nice side salad. Well done there. The flavor of the sandwich was one note-- typical bbq with lots of salt. One problem I had was that there were two giant hunks of fat and gristle in the sandwich. Not cool.  Overall, was the sandwich worth $11?  Probably not, but a decent effort.  

        My friend enjoyed his caesar salad, but if you mess up a caesar salad, then there are issues.  

        The bottom line: The Black Sheep is an Irish bar in a nice location that has a sizable menu with decent selections. It's a little pricier than I'd like, but it's worth trying at least once.
