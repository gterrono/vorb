    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge!, Asian Persuasion

    I came to Sampan on Saturday following my sister's college graduation. There were 6 of us, including my 8 year old brother who is Celiac. I mentioned this when making my Open Table reservation, upon entering the restaurant and several times throughout our meal. With Celiac, it's not a lifestyle choice or even an intolerance. If my brother ingests anything with gluten, it could be potentially life-threatening.

    Our server was very gracious. He explained the menu items pretty accurately and seemed to have an understanding about my brother's condition. We got the 9 course tasting menu, which was really a great deal for $45, and got Kobe beef skewers and Pad Thai from the gluten free menu for my brother. I also asked him before we had ordered anything if he could put our cocktails on a separate check, so that I could pay for my sister's drinks.

    The tasting menu was excellent. You get to choose your items from the menu, and just about everything was really amazing. My favorites were the hamachi ceviche, pork belly bao buns (better than Chifa's, which I didn't think was possible), Kobe beef fried rice, and the skirt steak. The skirt steak was served atop a fried rice that I wasn't nuts about, but the steak was perfection. I also discovered that the rice also had bits of yummy chorizo in it, which sadly got lost in all the other muddled flavors. The dessert was amazing too- little miniature ice cream cones for the table, one with vanilla Cap'n Crunch ice cream, and the other with chocolate Nutella ice cream. So awesome. We were absolutely stuffed!

    This is when the experience starts to go downhill, and fast. My brother was having a hard time finishing his pad thai, so we asked to have it wrapped to go. There were also a few other items left on the table that he offered to wrap for us as well, so we accepted. We also realized we only had about 15 minutes left on our meter, so we asked for the check as soon as he was able. First, he brought out one check and forgot to separate them. Then, he brought out the second set of checks correctly. I gave him my credit card to settle my portion of the check, and he came back with a charge draft for the amount of the entire check! I was pretty annoyed by this, especially because each time he was gone it was for quite a long time. I have worked in restaurants and I know how long it takes to run a credit card and close out a table. Finally, it's corrected and we race out of the restaurant to avoid getting ticketed.

    This is the part that I simply can not and will not accept. Because we ran out of the restaurant so quickly, we didn't have a chance to check the food we had wrapped to go. When we finally did, we realized that they packaged my brother's gluten free pad thai in the same container as everything else that WASN'T gluten free! First of all, why would you do this in ANY situation?? But when there is a health issue that you are more than aware of, what would possess you to do something like this? I was furious because he had taken about two bites before he was full because of the skewers he'd eaten. He did like it though and wanted to eat it later that night. Trying to explain to an 8 year old that they can't eat something they enjoy is heartbreaking, and Celiac kids have to deal with this on a daily basis.

    DON'T give the perception to your patrons that you understand how gluten free actually works if you don't actually understand what steps need to be taken. This carelessness makes me wonder if they even take the necessary care in their kitchens to avoid cross contamination. It's a terrible shame, because this serious negligence instantly turned a 4 star review into a 2 star review. The only saving grace was that the food I ate was truly well-conceived and delicious.

    We were greeted with a very vibrant and lively atmosphere. Tastefully decorated and very pleasant aromas coming from the open kitchen made us even more hungry.

    The theme is small plate Asian fusion and draws from mostly from Chinese,Korean and Japanese cuisines. You can say it is a type of "upscale" dim sum. The food is served community style and is meant to be shared.

    The tuna rice cracker arrived first and was a creative version of a sushi. Raw finely chopped tuna was well-seasoned and served on top of square crackers. Delicious and very refreshing.

    Next came the Vietnamese roll and had tender well seasoned pork belly served with fresh crisp julienne carrots and glass noodles wrapped in rice paper.  All ingredients were fresh and tasty.

    Then came a Korean fried chicken satay which contained very moist and flavorful battered, deep fried chicken with a sweet and spicy chili dipping sauce. The chicken was nice and crisp and just mildly greasy, but nothing to really detract for the overall good execution of the dish.

    The Philly Cheesesteak was one of the most unique take on a cheesesteak I've ever had. It was mildly spicy barbecued beef on a bao bun which was sitting on a layer of sriracha sauce and topped with aged provolone shreds. Outstanding flavor combinations.

    Finally, we finished with the shrimp and lobster dumplings in miso broth with tofu pieces. The dumplings were tender and the filling was superb. The miso broth itself was average to good

    We thoroughly enjoyed our visit to Sampan and service was warm,friendly and efficient. Our meal with 2 top-shelf cocktails and 5 small plates came to just over 55 bucks which is a decent value considering the good and consistent quality of the food.

    Sampan is worth a visit if you are in center city and in the mood to try something different and refreshing with a vibrant and lively atmosphere.
