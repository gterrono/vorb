    We stopped in on Saturday afternoon, mainly to escape the 100+ degree heat. Our bartender Celeste from Hotel Palomar also works here, and she suggested we stop by.

    We didn't order much, but what we got was excellent. The Polpettini pizza was perfectly baked and absolutely delicious.

    Something about sticking fingers in fires makes skin blister. Luckily, if you're a round of wonderful, yeasty, slightly sour, slightly sweet pizza dough spun by the chefs at Zavino and you get shoved in a 850 degree wood fired oven, you're going to blister. But you're going to taste wonderfully delicious, because instead of leaking puss, you'll ooze creamy Mozzarella. And in lieu of blood, you will be coated in a thin layer of slightly sweet, slightly caked red sauce. All of this, of course, will be to the delight of diners craving warm, pizza with just enough charcoal to appease their inner carcinogen lover.

    Protip: Unless you've got a particular affinity for waiting in line, do not attempt to manger at Zavino at any reasonable dinner hour.
