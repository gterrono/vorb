    Although I can't speak to the restaurant's other location in center city, this Sabrina's Cafe is a hidden gem on Drexel's campus. It is located in an old building in a well landscaped and well-kept neighborhood. As soon as you enter there is a certain charm and warmth one feels as if instead of entering a restaurant you're entering someone's home. Although it is true that the lines can sometimes be a bit long - even though I have yet to wait more then 20 minutes - it is definitely worth the wait. Moreover, the building has multiple floors that offer places to charge your devices, places to study, a place to play pool, or area to watch t.v. on very comfortable couches that are becoming of the colonial style of the building. Once you're ready to be seated, prepare yourself for one of the best meals. I personally recommend the stuffed challah french toast. I am not a breakfast person but you'd be missing out, so don't pass up the opportunity! This portion is huge with two very thick slices of challah bread - almost half a loaf - with a generous serving of a cream cheese and banana filling and to top it off just enough of a rich, sweet vanilla bean syrup drizzled on top. I have to be honest, I've never ordered anything else because it is just too good to forgo. However, I have gone with friends in which we've split dishes in order to make the most of our visit. I highly recommend that you do the same and choose a hearty meal to balance the sweetness of the french toast. Hearty dishes that I recommend are the Huevos Rancheros and the 100% Black Angus Burger.

    The Huevos Rancheros is the perfect blend of freshness from the guacamole and sour cream, smokiness from the chorizo and spiciness from the jalapeno.
    *note: I generally don't like spicy food but this was not overwhelming at all

    The 100% Black Angus Burger was perfect. The brioche bread was soft and buttery and perfectly complemented the juicy patty. The burger is customizable and you can add toppings like grilled onions, sun-dried tomatoes, mushrooms and other unique toppings. As a side choose their parmesan fries or sweet potato fries: both are great and are sure to have you licking your fingers.

    OVERALL: charming and quaint restaurant that has a bright and homy feel. The service as well as the food are always great. Although I have not tried everything in the menu, I can confidently say that anything you order will be good. And for the adventurous eater each week (or month....I don't know how often) there is a new extension to the menu with temporary options. They include creative dishes such as Oreo-topped french toast with wacky, themed names such as "I'm Dreaming of a White Omelette". As I said before, it is must-go place and one of the first places I take anyone who comes to visit me in Philadelphia.

    I'd heard about Sabrina's SO many times, but as a Baltimore brunch snob (nothing beats Miss Shirley's), I hadn't made it here after a year and a half in Philly.  Finally, I decided to venture to the University City location on Sunday morning . Despite the warnings about long waits, my group of three was seated after only 15 minutes (nothing compared to the 2 hours at the aforementioned Miss Shirley's), and I was happy we got a table on the enclosed porch. The old building is beautiful, and I loved the big porch windows.

    The "special" menu here is the most intriguing, and I had to try the Ron Burgundy-themed Pancake Party - 3 giant pancakes with a crazy mix of add-ins and toppings.  I asked for some on the side, which helped tone it down and taste things one at a time. The pancakes were pumpkin-based, with peanut butter oatmeal mixed in - pretty darn good.  My friend ordered the same, declaring them the best pumpkin pancakes she's ever had, and my boyfriend was pleased with the french toast.

    I have to admit, it still doesn't compare to my Baltimore brunch spot - but it's topping the list in Philadelphia.

    I think that the perfect way to describe Sabrina's is as an upscale diner. It is the perfect little brunch spot in University City (they have another location in Center City, but I've never been there). My boyfriend lives really close so we stop here pretty often to grab a bite to eat. The food has always been fantastic and we love that they have new specials so often and how personalized the chef makes them!

    Some rules:

    1. If you like french toast, you have to try the Stuffed Challah French Toast. It is to die for and I don't say that lightly. It is just that stinking good. The portion is HUGE (it's really enough for two people) and I always wind up bringing some of it home. I like ordering it with a side of sausage and turning it into 2-3 meals. The stuffing is sweet with just a hint of sour to make it jive really well with the vanilla bean sauce. (Can you tell I'm a fan?!)

    2. Don't be afraid to try the specials! My boyfriend almost always orders something off of the special menu and its easy to do - they change them frequently enough that you truly do have a limited amount of time to try them.

    3. If you want a more relaxed experience, avoid peak meal times. The place is hoppin' and if you go around a normal meal time you may find yourself waiting quite a while, or crowded into a small table because that is all that they have available. We like to go for more of a brunch time and there is usually much less of a crowd. We rarely go on the weekends because it is just packed.

    Overall - LOVE Sabrina's. Only place I have ever found that compares is Miss Shirley's in Baltimore, MD.

    The last time I went to Sabrina's Café, it was kind of a going-away brunch, as I prepared to depart Philadelphia for New York. I knew there was no leaving this city without stopping in to eat at Sabrina's, so my boyfriend and I walked over on my last Sunday morning here. It was busy as usual, but since there was only two of us, we were seated almost immediately in the back of the restaurant, the sunlight streaming in through the window overlooking the neighborhood.

    A server approached us after a few minutes, and we ordered some coffee as he left us to look over the menu before deciding. The café brews La Colombe, and the coffee is bottomless, which is something my boyfriend and I both appreciate. When the server returned returned, my boyfriend selected the classic Steak 'n' Eggs Breakfast ($13.89), and I chose two egg whites with fruit and multigrain toast ($8.64). I'm always disappointed that the café charges $1.75 to substitute an average-sized cup of fruit for the fried potatoes (or homies, as they call them), but oh well.

    About 15 minutes later, our food was ready. I have no complaints about my meal - the eggs were fresh, the fruit was ripe and sweet, and the toast was hearty. Likewise, my boyfriend (supremely) enjoyed his food; in fact, he's usually the one who encourages me to choose Sabrina's when we go out for brunch. According to him, his steak 'n' eggs were delicious - the steak was very well cooked, and the roasted plum tomato chimichurri was a unique and flavorful touch.

    When I return to Philadelphia in the future to visit my college friends, I'll be sure to take them to Sabrina's Café. The food is good, the environment is pretty and traditional, and the service is pleasant and attentive. A must for any Philadelphia resident in search of a quality morning meal!
