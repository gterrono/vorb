        3/28/2014

        This restaurant opened in November, pretty much under the radar. The exterior isn't particularly inviting - it's on a corner between an alley and a parking garage. It wasn't even on Yelp until recently. But oh my word. It's so good. Everyone needs to go there and keep it afloat.

        It's mostly Malaysian, with a little Thai and assorted pan-Asian to soften the confusion of Philadelphians who don't know what Malaysian is. (Try it. You'll like it.)

        I tend to get the roti canai, whose coconut curry sauce is so good I finish it with a spoon. It's like a wispy griddle-fried bread you dip in the sauce. The first time I went, I also had char kueh teow, a kind of soy-based wok noodle dish. Yummy. Today I had "masak chicken," which was a blind pick on the menu. Super delicious sweet-ish brown sauce (described as "special sauce" on the menu) and meat that was fresh tasting with red bell pepper, ginger, carrot, and onion.

        I can't overstate how really excellent the quality is for cheap Asian takeout. I've never seen the place busy, which concerns me. Because I want to keep going there for a long time.

        Oh, and there is a counter where you can sit and gaze upon a dumpster-filled alley, but no tables. It's primarily take out and delivery.

        Ordered take out for lunch. I would give this place 3.5 stars.

        Pad thai was really good, decent sized portion, and inexpensive. I like how there is NO weird ketchup like red sauce in it or too much sugar which is a good sign. The dish was filled with eggs and strips of tofu as well. Overall, I'd say it's fairless authentic. I would definitely order the pad thai here again. I got chicken this time and will have to try the shrimp one next.

        On the other hand, I don't know why people are raving about the roti canai. I would not recommend it. The pancake was a cross between naan and a flour tortilla and it had a chewy consistency. The the coconut potato curry sauce had no meat or potatoes in it; it was just liquid. I've had roti canai elsewhere and it was NOTHING like this. The roti canai I had before was a flakey, pastry-like pancake and the curry had chunks of meat and potatoes in it. Maybe I ate it the wrong way but since the sauce here was so incredibly thin, I could not even get too much on the pancakes. I ended up with a cup full of curry sauce after I had eaten all the pancake. What was I suppose to do with the rest, drink it like soup?  Maybe I could have broken the pancake down into little pieces and just soaked everything in the curry. Seriously though, this dish had no substance what so ever. I would not consider the pancake substance. It was too thin and too chewy, too tough to even eat.

        My co-worker got mango chicken and they seriously put a whole sliced mango in his dish. There was more mango than chicken. It looked decent though albeit probably really sweet because it was drenched in some orange sauce.

        Overall, I would say the pad thai is a solid 4 stars so stick with that. The roti canai is like 2 stars. The prices is 4.5 stars, very inexpensive for the type of cuisine/center city!
