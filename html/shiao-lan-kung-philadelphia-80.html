    If you asked me about Shiao Lan Kung about 5 years ago....I would have hands down said this place was a 5 and was THE BEST Chinese Restaurant in Philly.......but since there has been a new owner and dishes have changed it's not the same restaurant that I have been eating at since I was 10 years old.

    First thing to order...get that hot and sour soup! This still tastes the same so they must have kept that secret recipe!! It's pretty sour and my family LOVES that and we still will go here to this day to get it. Salt baked combination is delicious as well! The portions for this have decreased dramatically, but the flavor still remains pretty much the same. We used to order the pachen tofu seafood hot pot from here, but again, they have cut back on a lot of the ingredients and the portions are very small as well compared to the old times. While the taste for most of the dishes that I have always gotten remain the same, the portion size has shrunk significantly.

    The biggest difference other than the food is the service. I no longer feel at home here since almost everyone has been replaced. Now whenever I go there, it just feels like they are trying to get rid of us as soon as we sit down so they can give the table to other patrons that are waiting in line.

    Pros:
    - Delicious dishes!! Just wish that they were bigger portion sizes like old times!
    - Service is really quick! Dishes will be at your table within minutes of you ordering!
    - Authentic Chinese recipes......lots of people order lo mein, but try something that's REAL Chinese food people!

    Cons:
    - The staff their could be more friendly...I always feel so rushed there the moment I sit down!! I know that they want to turn tables over quickly, but I think they can hide their impatience better
    - Wish this restaurant could expand!! I've been saying this for years, but I guess this gives Shiao Lan Kung its charm...plus it feels awesome when you grab a table!!
    - Groups larger than 5, expect to be squeezed into a table!!! You'll get to know your friends a lot better since your elbows are basically touching

    Why I gave it a 4.....
    This once was my favorite restaurant, but with the new ownership, I dislike how they try to rush you through the meal the moment that you sit down. The taste is still pretty much the same if you know what to order (stay away from that lo mein...this isn't a Chinese take-out place!) but you don't get that bang for your buck like old times :( But I still love you Shiao Lan Kung!

    Walking out of Hop Sing in the wee hours on Sunday morning, we asked Lee for a recommendation for late-night Chinese and he rattled off a couple places. We walked past the line at David's and into the nearly-empty Shiao Lan Kung.

    My klutzy self had suffered a minor incident on the one block walk (heels are dangerous and even moreseo after several drinks) and when we arrived my left knee was looking a bit questionable. A lady who worked there came over right away with some first aid stuff, which was much appreciated.

    If you want to continue your drinking, you'll have to BYO as Shiao Lan Kung does not serve alcohol. We saw several late-night parties walk in after us with beer. That said, leave the bar before last call and secure your table. The place got a lot busier as the night went on.  

    We started with fried dumplings which arrived in a soy sauce with fresh chopped scallions. They were booze-soakingly delicious. Next up was some perfectly sticky white rice and chicken in black bean sauce and pork in garlic sauce. Both were awesome.

    I'm still impressed that my motor vehicle skills allowed me to use my chopsticks successfully. I definitely owe this place a more sober visit so I can properly assess the food.

    Listed in Chinese Take-Out

    Ever since our neighborhood Chinese restaurant closed, we've been on the prowl for a new go-to restaurant. This weekend we tried out Shiao Lan Kung. How did we pick it, you ask? Well, because they're one of the few Chinese restaurants that actually have their menu online (philadelphia.menupages.c…).

    Here's everything we tried:
    - Steamed dumplings ($5.50)
    - Egg rolls ($1.40)
    - Spicy shredded pork with red pepper ($8.00)
    - Dragon And Phoenix Nest ($14.50)
    - White rice (yes, you have to order it separately - $1.00)

    I was really surprised by the dumplings and have to say that they're some of the best dumplings I've ever ordered at a Chinese restaurant. They were big, covered in scallions, and not at all like the wontons you get in soup. I've been to lots of places where their dumplings are just straight-up wontons from their soup, and it annoys me to no end. I really liked the ones we got here, and for $5.50 for EIGHT of them, you really can't go wrong.

    The egg rolls were also really good. They were filled with tiny shrimps and pork, and by the time I was done with the dumplings and the egg rolls, I was full! I continued to eat, though, for the sake of this review.

    The spicy shredded pork with red pepper was a big surprise for me. My husband was the one who ordered it, and I wasn't too excited because I thought it sounded plain, but it was mighty tasty. It ended up being my favorite part about the meal.

    As for the dragon and phoenix nest, which is described as this: jumbo shrimp and white meat chicken sauteed with broccoli, asparagus and snow peas in a hot & spicy sauce blended with garlic and pepper................I was let down. Everything that was in the dish tasted like it was boiled and then covered in sauce. And the sauce, might I add, wasn't spicy at all - and that's the primary reason I got it.

    The next day, though, I combined all of the leftover entrees, reheated them, added a little chili oil, and it was one of the most delicious things I've ever tasted.

    I'd certainly go back to this place, but I think our search will still continue.
