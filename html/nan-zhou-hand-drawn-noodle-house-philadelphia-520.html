    I could eat here every single day.  It's seriously THAT good.  

    I didn't want soup the night we went so I ordered the stir-fried hand drawn noodles with roast pork.  For $7 I wasn't expecting the mountain of noodles that came out on my plate, but I was certainly happy about it.  My first bite was the stuff of legends and I swear I heard a heavenly choir singing while I ate.  The noodles were so perfect and chewy, the pork slightly sweet, and the sauce clung beautifully to everything.  I got to try my friend's knife-shaved noodles and I definitely liked the hand drawn better.  The shaved are "toothier" as others described but I preferred the thinner hand drawn.  

    I got less than halfway through my plate before I had to throw in the towel, but that meant I got to see how the dish held up to being refrigerated and reheated.  It was even better the next day, if that's possible.  The noodles soaked up more flavor, but didn't feel dry when reheated.  

    This was definitely a great meal and an incredible price and totally worth going if you haven't already.

    FAVORITES:
    Beef brisket noodle soup - 4 stars - have ordered this soup multiple times and tried with both the hand drawn (thin egg noodles) and thick (wider knife-shaved noodles (yelp.com/biz_photos/nan-…)).  I prefer the soup with thinner noodles, although both are fresh and good.  The broth is okay, but I mostly order this dish for the beef and noodles.  The cilantro in the dish works well, and adding an egg to this soup makes a great combination.  

    Noodles with pork soy sauce - 4 stars - another great option is this traditional Chinese noodle dish  (non-soup).  The pork is very flavorful and the hand drawn noodles go well with the ground pork.

    APPETIZERS:
    Cold shredded potatoes - 4.5 stars -  great option.  The vinegar flavoring is really good the dish comes with carrots and cucumbers.  You can't even tell you're eating potatoes.  

    Chicken dumplings with curry and coconut - 4 stars (fried version), 3 stars (boiled version).  Definitely go with the fried (unhealthy) version if you order this.  Chicken is not optimal for dumplings but with the curry it was pretty good.  You can't really tell that there's coconut in the dumplings though.  

    Diced cucumbers in sauce - 3 stars - typical cucumbers with minced garlic.  I think this dish was a bit too salty and as a result the cucumber flavor was somewhat lost.

    OVERALL
    Expect to pay ~$12/person for a large bowl of noodles along with a few shared appetizers.  Good portion sizes and definitely a very affordable and good option in Chinatown.

    I really like to eat la mian (hand-drawn noodles) and i think this is the best spot in Philly to indulge this taste.  La mian are famous all over China and indeed Asia for being the centuries-old style of churning out noodles in the city of Lanzhou in Northwest China.  This is where China begins to meet the Gobi desert, Mongolia, Russia, and the Asian Republics - inotherwords a real trade route type of locale.  Well these noodles spread around the world and now pop up in Philadelphia with all their freshness and taste.  You can watch cooks through a small window in the rear of the restaurant working the dough and producing the hand-drawn noodles.  You can then eat them in one of their famous soups (I really like the duck) or like a sphagetti dinner in the awesome Pork Soy platter. The atmosphere is only Chinatown Plain and service isn't that attentive - perhaps because it's usually really busy here.  Communicating in English is often a challenge if you want something beyond a basic request.  But hey, maybe that's part of the reason that this is overall just a very good place to eat!
