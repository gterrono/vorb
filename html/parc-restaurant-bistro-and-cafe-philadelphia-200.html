    Listed in Celi Philly, When Brunch Can't Wait

    Celiac-friendly while surprisingly authentic and brunchable!

    I am on the side of the Starr debate that finds the food at his various restaurants to be at worst, below the quality of microwave dinners (Jones) and at best decent, but over-priced compared to other similarly situated places elsewhere in Philly (Buddakhan), I now make an exception to my Starr aversion for Parc.

    Atmosphere:  Yes, some nights of the week it has that atmosphere that plagues so much of the establishments lining the square: the mid-life crisis crowd, tucked, pulled and tanned into looks that range from local news-anchor to slightly South Jersey Chic. If you can get past the air of desperation or just go when the divorcee crowd seems to be having their custody nights/ weekends you will clearly see a clean almost Disney land like version of a Parisian Cafe. The elements of which are arranged into the American Socratic Ideal of a French Bistro as envisioned by Pottery Barn.  Don't expect the mild grit and the french brand of slight haphazardness here.

    Food: While the decor may be a slight (but delightful) caricature of a Parisian cafe, the food is fairly close to what you get at the corner restaurant within the central arrondissements of Paris. Simple, yet perfectly seasoned, sauced and spiced food. The one disappointment here was a cafe au lait that I had with brunch. It could have been a fluke but I essentially received a coffee with a few drops of milk.

    Service: While the food may transport you back to France, the friendly and attentive service will remind you that you are definitely still in America. The servers are always remarkably friendly and are incredibly knowledgeable about the ingredients and Celiacs/food allergies. In addition to being enthusiastic about double checking ingredients, they always offer to alert the kitchen. Despite the care and awareness that many places now have of gluten and food allergies, I still accept stomach aches as a common risk of eating out. I have eaten at Parc 3 times and have never felt the least bit sick afterwards.

    BRUNCH! Likely because of the large amounts of seating, even on Sunday the brunch wait is only about 30 minutes, plus they let you make reservations and can usually fit you in within a half hour to hour of when you call. Sitting inside or outside and looking onto the park on a Saturday or Sunday morning (weather willing) is as delightful as the rest of the restaurant.

    So I'm not one of these people who wait with bated breath for the next Starr or Garces project to open. I've had great meals in places with two booths, one cook, and a bathroom the size of a broom closet.  With me its food first, service second, ambiance/atmosphere third.

         All this being said, my mother and I both enjoyed our visit. I started off with a Weihenstaphaner Hefeweizen. It's one of my favorite of the German wheat beers, second only to Franziskaner. Amazingly its only $6. 6 bucks is an amazing deal on this beer, since its a non-American standard bottle(I'm guessing 14-16oz). Especially amazing since Scudweiser can run you $6 in some of the Center City bars.

      I ordered the beef bourguignon and my mom had the seared sea scallops. My beef was amazingly tender, falling apart with just a light application of the fork(But it wasn't mushy). The beef was cooked in a very tasty wine reduction sauce with mushrooms. The mashed potatoes were excellent, as were the carrots. The turnips, however, I didn't like. But I just don't like turnips, so no big deal. My mothers sea scallops were cooked perfectly and tasted very fresh. They had a breading/crusty substance on top that gave it additional flavor and texture and was very very savory.

    The apple tarte I had for dessert was delicious. The tarte was light and flavorful without being overly sweet and gooey. Perfect execution. My mother had a chocolate confection that looked like a Hirshhorn gallery sculpture but was very tasty.

    Service was good. Our waiter was right on top of our order and he refilled out wine, beer, and water just enough to make sure we weren't parched without being annoying. He was very personable. Also, a man who appeared to be the GM/Manager stopped by to enquire about our meal. He was happy that we both liked the sea scallops and was also very nice.

    All in all, a perfect way to end my mothers first trip to Philly since 1976.

    My friend had been telling me about Parc for a while now, and he finally took me there last Friday night. We sat under the awning-- sort of inside/outside at one of the little bistro tables. It was a lovely night, and we had a beautiful view of the park.

    Since so many reviewers have discussed the food and service, I thought I would spend the first part of the review on the atmosphere. Sure, the decor and atmosphere aren't "original"-- but it's a nice way to bring a little bit of Paris to Philly, and the restaurant is pretty. The lighting, I thought, was a bit dim (we tried to take photos, and they came out very dark), but there is a soft glow to the place, and several of the design details add a nice touch-- the tilework on the floor is lovely, and of course, there is the zinc bar. The atmosphere is romantic but not overkill-- you wouldn't feel uncomfortable here on a first date or dining with a friend-- and the restaurant, while upscale, doesn't feel stuffy.

    The only quirk about the atmosphere is that it is dog-friendly, which I know adds to the city bistro personality, but that could be problematic for some. There was a beautiful Rhodesian Ridgeback at the next table who wandered over a few times to say hello. The owner did have the dog on a leash and under control, for the most part, but if you have a problem with dogs, maybe you should sit inside.

    As for the service, our waiter was professional and friendly. He was very helpful when I couldn't decide what to order, and he seemed very knowledgeable about the menu.  The only unpleasant aspect of the service (and a few reviewers have mentioned a similar experience) was that when the table busser cleared our cheese plate away, he spilled some of the honey on the table and made a sticky streak there, and then continued to spill the honey on my friend's pants. We had to ask for a rag to clean the honey off the table (which a nearby waiter did immediately and with an apology).

    As for the food, I was absolutely thrilled with my almond-encrusted trout in lemon butter with green beans on the side. It was the perfect portion size for me. My friend had steak, and he was quite happy with his dish, though even I thought the spinach on the side looked a bit unappetizing. We were too full for dessert, so we each had a coffee drink to finish the meal, and I have been thinking about my delicious Vietnamese coffee (a layer of sweetened condensed milk, a shot of espresso and light froth on top) for days!

    I've already recommended Parc to several friends and would definitely dine there again. Next time, I may try the breakfast/brunch, thanks to the descriptions of a few of the reviewers here.

Specialties

This stylish addition to Rittenhouse Square pays tribute to French cafe culture, serving breakfast, lunch and dinner seven days a week. Ideal for a quick bite or a leisurely meal, the menu is filled with classics: onion soup, escargots, steak frites and towering plateaus de fruits de mer. The baguettes are made in-house and  the wine list is expansive. Sip a Kir Royale at the zinc bar or enjoy a cafe au lait while basking in the sun.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
