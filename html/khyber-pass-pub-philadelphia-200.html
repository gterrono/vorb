    After numerous times coming here for Happy Hour I finally broke down and tried the food...WOW, is all I can really say. The catfish with hush puppies and tater tots were on par with something I would find back home in Arkansas, which speaks volumes, but when I paired it with the Mac and Cheese the meal truly was on a level that is hard to put into words. My fiancee had the roast beef po boy and had a smile on her face the entire meal (I'm sure the strong whiskey drink probably had a little something to do with that as well).

    Speaking of drinks, their beer selection is quite extensive and all the servers are extremely knowledgeable about pretty much everyone of their craft offerings. When I told the waitress what I was going to order she was able to steer me to the perfect craft beer, a beer I now order the majority of the time I go out. Also, their liquor selection is just as large as it is for their beers and they are very generous with their pours, which is always a huge plus.

    For the sake of keeping this review brief I'll say this and be finished, if you enjoy good food, good drinks, a good atmosphere, good service, or any combination of those four, Khyber Pass is a place you must visit at least once in your life, it seriously is that awesome.

    oh, Khyber Pass, how I love you!  I've been here for drinks only, lunch and dinner.  and every visit has been spectacular.  Great bartenders with that lovely ability to remember you -- this is one of those things that just makes me smile because my own family barely remembers me, so it's better than coming home.  lol.  

    The food is almost always delicious and very well prepared.  Try the fried green tomato BLT and the sammie po-boy (fried chicken pieces paired with ham and swiss, served on a good crusty roll with tomato, lettuce, pickles and a yummy creole mustard).  No, this may not be the best place for a diet, but the long leisurely walk through old city that you will be lulled into taking after one (or three) of their lovely sazoracs will work off most of the naughties.

    Decor is nothing too spectacular, but I do love a tin ceiling, and the bar is comfy (if your date doesn't slam his knee into a bag hook.)  It's not a huge place, but I've never had trouble getting a spot at the bar or a table.  Prices are very reasonable.

    I tried out the Khyber Pass Pub last week with the boyfriend before seeing a movie at the Ritz. We went on a Monday night around happy hour ($2 off drafts!! :D) and the place was understandably pretty quiet except for a few groups eating and a small crowd at the bar.

    I was very pleased with the music, which was a good selection of classic rock-- quite a few songs I heard at KPB are somewhat obscure and don't get played on the radio much, so that was a plus. I was only unhappy with the volume on the music; at times, it was too loud and I could hardly hear myself or my boyfriend speak.

    The beer: large and varied list, with many less well-known brewers. I ordered the Green Flash Grand Cru (mostly because of the name) and I was not disappointed. It was a little darker than Flying Fish's version, but it went really well with my dinner.

    The food: my BBQ brisket sandwich with a side of fries is the reason why this pub gets five stars. I am no BBQ connoisseur, but this sandwich was the BBQ I've ever had. The meat was tender, juicy, covered in the smoky BBQ sauce, and was piled in four layers on my bun. The bun itself was buttered (huge plus) and there was a glorious amount of horseradish on the side that I could spread on my sandwich. The fries were quite good-- nice and crispy without being hard shells, and with the right amount of fluff. But the most amazing thing on the table was the boyfriend's side of mac-and-cheese. It was heavenly, I tell you. We asked the waiter what cheese was in it and he returned to tell us that they used a bechamel sauce. Oh God. I could die. I've had the best pub mac-and-cheese on this earth. Seriously. Go try it.

    Overall, this place was really cool, with great food and beer and friendly service to boot. I hope to come back soon for more and in the meantime, this is definitely going on my favorite bars list!
