    Hearing a lot of talk about the 13th street corridor I decided to bring some out of town friends to Sampan. We sat at the bar around 8 on a Saturday night, plenty of space and despite it being a busy night VERY friendly bar service.

    The decor was trendy with blue backlit walls under scribblings of trees. The restaurant was sectioned off enough that despite being a packed house there were no pockets of loud chatter.

    Our server/bartender multi-tasked whipping up cocktails for the restaurant and efficiently taking our food and drink orders without missing a beat. He suggested cocktails which properly suited our tastes. Our dinner being informal we ordered 7 items from the small plates section of the menu and it wasn't long before they started coming out.

    Not to be missed:
    General Tso soup dumplings
    Lobster Gazpacho (comes out single servings on spoons, so don't be afraid to share!)
    Hamachi Ceviche
    Mini Soft Serve Cones (they were brought out as a nice surprise for my birthday by the staff)

    Our bill came to just under $80 for THREE(!) and we were all pretty satisfied.

    I can assure everything on the menu is fantastic, but we aware if you are very hungry you may want to order larger plates. A friend with a much larger appetite recently ate here and his only concern was portion sizes.

    Wow, went 2 for 2 in terms of 5 star dining experiences here in Center City!

    Sampan was an awesome dining experience of which we won't soon forget. On a comfortable early September evening, we choose to eat out meal outside. Sampan is more or less tapas style, however there are a few items on the menu for those with a big appetite. Another tip, they even have a separate vegetarian menu, just ask your waitress for one!

    The fiancé ordered the edamame dumplings, tofu tacos, and kim chi fried rice off of the vegetarian menu. The dumplings came without the broth (which is cooked with fish oil), and was pretty good but probably would have been better with the broth. The tofu tacos seemed pretty good, she certainly enjoyed them. I thought the best item off of the vegetarian menu, though, was the kim chi fried rice. It came out piping hot with a sunny side up egg on top, which the server then cut up at the table! The rice was really tasty, and came in such a large quantity for only $6, definitely one of the best bargains on the menu.

    Meanwhile, I ordered the Korean BBQ Beef Satay and the "side" of noodles and sausage off of the regular menu.  The noodles and sausage was very good, it came out in a pretty large amount and seemed much like a main dish than a so called side. But the star of the show, without a doubt, was the Beef Satay! This is definitely some of the tastiest beef I've had in a while; it was savory, full of flavor, and cooked to a perfect medium. The satays were also covered with some kind of unidentifiable toppings, but in no way did they hurt the meat, only enhanced it. I highly recommend ordering the BBQ beef satay if you come here.

    Finally, for dessert, we went with the mini ice cream cones, which they apparently make different flavors for. This time around, they featured a Cinnamon Toast Crunch 'chocolate' and a Cap'n Crunch 'vanilla" cone, both of which were really good and surprisingly tasted just like the actual cereals!

    Funny coincidence: we decided to watch Limitless later that day (the movie filmed in Philly with Bradley Cooper and Robert De Niro), and at the end of the movie they have lunch in what is clearly Sampan!

    Sampan is, by far, my best Restaurant Week (RW) experience, though I know I'd enjoy this place on any given day since the RW menu was the Chef's Tasting for $5 less than usual.

    Happy Hour/Drinks: 4 stars:
    We got there a little early, so we sat at the bar for a little while.  If I weren't there for RW, I'd have just stayed at the bar and made use of the happy hour deals!  They have a creative drink menu.  The sampantini is so refreshingly delicious!  I'll definitely be going back to partake!  

    Service: 5 stars
    Our waitress was wonderful.  She knew the menu really well, gave great tips on what to order and was very attentive.  Not only that, she was very personable and friendly; she kept us laughing (and hopefully vice versa!)  All waitstaff that delivered food was also friendly and personable.

    Food:  4 stars
    The mouth-watering aromas coming out of the open kitchen made our empty stomachs demand instant gratification.  So we initially started with the Philly Cheese Steak.  Wow!  This was kick-in-the-butt delicious.  The rest of our order was as follows:

    -Edamame Dumplings: beautiful texture, subtle flavors.
    -Pork Bao Bun
    -Suckling Pig Satay: the bing cherry went surprisingly well with the savory/spicy pig-on-stick.
    -Beef Short Rib
    -Crab Wonton Taco: The wonton taco was good, but the crab was a little too mayonnaise-y for me.
    -Lobster Stir Fry
    -Crispy Brussel Sprouts: why can't all mothers make brussel sprouts like this?  Dogs will never get those generally despised vegetables tossed to them under the table again!

    Ingredients were fresh.  Each dish was well put together and artistically presented.  If I were to re-do the experience, I would have the Philly Cheese Steak after the Pork Bao Bun; it over powered the subtleness of the edamame dumplings.

    The Chef's Tasting includes soft serve.  The flavors of that day: Nutter Butter or Apple Jacks.  This soft-serve doesn't have chunks; it's pure soft-serve texture all the way.  How awesome is that?!  It's FREAKING awesome, I tell you!  Our waitress told us that they also have cinnamon toast crunch, peanut butter & jelly swirl and starburst.  This is OMG-worthy!

    I can't wait to go back!  (Oh!  And this is my 100th Yelp review!)

    This has quickly become one of my favorite places to eat in Philadelphia. The concept isn't over-the-top or in-your-face, the staff runs like a well-oiled machine, and the food is excellent.

    I brought a friend here last night who was visiting from out of town and wanted something "different", and I knew Sampan was the place. For an 8pm reservation on a Friday night, the place was crowded but didn't seem packed to the brim, nor did the staff seem on-edge or too busy at any point during our visit.

    Our server was pleasant and well-spoken, and was clearly full of knowledge about the menu and restaurant's concept. She quickly sold us on the $40 tasting menu without sounding pushy or like she was trying to sell us something we didn't need or that would benefit her, which is often how such pitches seem to me in other restaurants.

    Our selections included the General Tso's Soup Dumplings, Chicken Bao Buns, Kobe Beef Satay, Charred Thai Chicken, Crispy Rock Shrimp, Roasted Broccoli and the Soy Glazed Chilean Sea Bass, rounded out with a dessert course consisting of two mini soft-serve cones: Chocolate Coconut and Vanilla Rice Krispy Treat. Food came out one dish at a time, at perfect times, and empty plates were cleared promptly (a few times by what were obviously managers, who were chatty and friendly - an excellent touch). At no point did we feel inundated with food or plates. All of our dishes were excellent, but stand-outs were the General Tso's dumplings, the Kobe satay and the Sea Bass, which pretty much melted in my mouth. It was also refreshing to enjoy a tasting menu, drinks AND dessert and leave the restaurant feeling full, but not stuffed.

    The food is excellent, the service is flawless, and the restaurant itself looks awesome. I highly recommend the tasting menu, ALL of the specialty cocktails, and Sampan's awesome Happy Hour menu (one of the only places I've seen that has Happy Hour on a Sunday!). The Graffiti Bar out back is oddly out of place in terms of concept, but it's a cool little space for some quick drinks and appetizers. I'll definitely be bringing all of my out-of-town friends here from now on.
