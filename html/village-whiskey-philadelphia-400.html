        CLARIFICATION:  I know that Village Whiskey is NOT a Steven Starr restaurant.  What I am getting at is that it is much better than any of the Steven Starr restaurants I've visited.  There's a lot of hype around his restaurants, and I want people to know - the hype SHOULD be around Village Whiskey!

        ------------------

        Hands down, the BEST restaurant in Philly.  I don't care what anyone says about the Steven Starr restaurants.  I've been to lots of different places, but this still always remains my favorite.  Village Whiskey is the best burger I've EVER had, no doubt.  Also some of the best alcohol-forward, "Prohibition" style cocktails I've ever had.  Not to mention their whiskey list is MILES long.  

        Trust me on this - order a Village burger, add the aged cheddar and an egg.  Yes, an egg - this is the crucial ingredient.  I can't speak for the fois gras - I don't eat it.  Anyway, do the above, and order it cooked M...it will be pink and juicy and heavenly.  Don't forget an order of the duck fat fries (a must), and if you're really feeling hungry - try something on the pickled menu.  I love the cherry tomatoes.  

        As far as cocktails go - you'll be good with just about anything, but my personal favorite is the Sazerac.  Village Whiskey is SMALL though, really not much seating, and they don't take reservations.  So basically, it gets super packed.  Be prepared to wait, or venture in during non-peak hours.  If you get a chance, I also recommend sitting somewhere you can watch the bartenders make the drinks - it's seriously a work of art.  Keep doing what you're doing VW - you have a lifelong fan in me!

        We sat outside, thankfully they offer outdoor seating because the inside, although traditionally decorated, was quite small and we hate waiting for food when we're hungry. I ordered a Rum, Cucumber, Lime and Sugar drink and my boyfriend (let's call him Bill from here on out) ordered an Old Fashioned.

        The drinks were awesome, although Bill's was a little strong it was a good stiff mans drink. We set out to order the Village Burger with the addition of carmalized onions and fancy cheddar cheese, the BBQ Pork Sammy and the infamous Duck Fat French Fries.

        As we impatiently waited for our food (we were starved and all the food going to the other tables was teasing us) we kept commenting on how great the drinks were.

        I spotted the waiter carrying our food out and I giddily whisper "It's HERE!" as too sit up straight making ample room for the food to be placed in front of my face.

        The burger was as amazing as one could ever imagine, complete with 1000 Island dressing on a homemade Brioche Bun...that glistened like stars in the sky (albeit was probably an egg wash). The BBQ Pork Sammy put every Pulled Pork Sess pool mess you've ever eaten to shame. It was composed of roast pork sliced neatly and lovingly dressed with their Whiskey BBQ Sauce, not doused.       From the business

      Specialties

      A ground-to-order Angus Beef Burger, a Veggie Burger, a Bison Burger or an Vienna All-Beef Hot Dog with all the trimmings are each served alongside duck fat French fries and Sly Fox cheddar sauce.  Burgers are topped with Village Whiskey's house-made thousand-island dressing, Bibb lettuce, beefsteak tomato and a potato-beer bun, with optional toppings including bacon, caramelized onion, truffles, artichokes and a selection of cheeses.

      As one of the most comprehensive collections of its namesake spirit available in Philadelphia, Village Whiskey features a veritable library of 80 to 100 varieties of whiskey, bourbon, rye and scotch from Scotland, Canada, Ireland, United States and even a few Japanese options.

      History

      Established in 2009.

      Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

      Meet the Business Owner

      Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

      Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
