    I have to give Nomad Pizza five stars because they have the single best pizza I've ever tasted... The Spicy Sausage with italian tomatoes, fresh mozzarella, renaissance spicy fennel sausage, caramelized onions, basil, organic olive oil, organic parmesan and sea salt  - WOW. Definitely a hip destination for college types - we are in our 40's with kids so its more about the food.

    As I am on a constant search for good pizza, it was only a matter of time before I found myself here.  I've seen the trucks at various locations around town and I've been dying to try Nomad.  We sat upstairs in the dining room and it was so cool!  The bar looked great, although it was empty on Saturday at 6:30 (I'm thinking it must draw a better happy hour or crowd later) In the dining area there was a huge projector screen playing old movies and large communal tables along with some 4 and 2 tops.  

    I personally enjoy communal tables, especially when seated next to fun and interesting diners, but being a party of two they put us at a two top.  There was a large party of older adults that didn't seem too pleased at having to "share" their table, but we wrote them off as curmudgeons.  

    We started with the Winter Caprese Salad-- The roasted tomatoes had an excellent flavor and they used burrata which added a lucsious quality-- perfect for February!

    Then we had the Spicy Soppressata pizza and it was really well done.  They added a bit of honey to balance the spice of the meat and the sauce/cheese ratio was spot on!  The crust was charred JUST enough.  Then we heard the dessert selections and went with the house-made pound cake slightly pan fried with bourbon vanilla ice-cream and a caramel sauce that was the perfect ending.

    I will be back (hopefully with a large group) to try some of the awesome pizza combinations we didn't get a chance to!

    After reading several stellar yelp reviews, we decided to give Nomad a try. We figured we'd order a couple different pizzas to see what all the fuss was about.

    I should say that Nomad really does customer service right. From the initial phone order to the pickup, everyone I encountered was friendly, professional, and accommodating. To me, the initial phone interaction usually sets the tone for the experience and this was definitely a good start.

    Now on to the pizza. We are huge fans of pepperoni but dislike the additives and preservatives in traditional pepperoni so I appreciated Nomad's use of organic and more natural product (Applegate farms is sold at whole foods and we like their products). So, we ordered the pepperoni and also got the marinara.

    When I went to pick them up, within 15 minutes as told and it was done on time - not sitting there, but fresh out of the oven as I paid :) always a plus. It was 28.80 for two pizzas (which for gourmet pizza, I don't mind). However, the size is that of a personal pizza. I could've taken down the pepperoni on my own and the two pizzas were not enough.  The pepperoni was amazing!! Honestly that deserves 5 stars - seriously it was that good. The marinara left much to be desired. It's basically a tomato pie. It was OK but I wouldn't order again.

    We will be back for the pepperoni for sure and to try the margherita!

    Overall, 4.5 stars and Nomad is definitely recommended :)

    Was looking for a good pizza because I've heard people from Philly claim to have the best pizza in the US.   I'm from Chicago and I remember reading a Yelp review from someone from Philly visiting a Chicago pizza parlor saying that she was "skeptical about trying Chicago-style pizza because she's from Philly".    Of course no one outside of Philly has heard of Philly-style pizza.

    We checked the Yelp reviews for pizza in downtown Philly and Nomad came up as the highest rated in our area.   When we stepped inside, I thought "I'm pretty sure this is not what Philly-style pizza is supposed to be" based on how fancy they looked on everyone's tables.   We were hungry so we decided to stay.

    The pizza is excellent.   All the ingredients are top-notch.   It's done brick-oven style and the crust is evidence of that.   Crispy on the outside and chewy and airy on the inside.  The sauce has an herbed spice that is nice kick in the pants.  Not too much cheese so that the gourmet toppings are highlighted properly.

    This is definitely artisanal pizza and not the Philly pizza locals claim is the best in the country.   That's fine with me, because we tried Philly pizza later during our visit and it was not something to be proud of.   However, Nomad is.

    Love Nomad Pizza Company! If you like Pizzeria Stella, then you will love Nomad, too.

    I've dined with friends here multiple times, and over the course of those visits, we've had the arancini (sp?) for the starter, and have ordered the following pizzas: Truffle Pecorino, Margherita, Spicy Soppressata, Shittake Mushroom, Spinach, and the Arugula con Prosciutto. The crusts are light and perfectly crispy, and the toppings are plentiful and fresh, especially the veggies. I loved the shittake mushroom and the spinach, and can't wait to order them again. While the rest of my party loved the Truffle Pecorino, it was very rich - I'd recommend having a slice of it, but it's not something I could eat a lot of.

    The pizzas are a good size to share; when I've been with one other person for dinner, we've shared the arancini appetizer and two pizzas, but we ordered two specifically to have leftovers for the next day. One pizza per two people is a great size. The beer and wine selection is solid and reasonably priced.

    I enjoy the atmosphere of Nomad Pizza Co. much better than that of Nomad Roman on 13th. The waitstaff here is far more experienced and much friendlier; they are attentive, well-versed in the restaurant's offerings, and are great at describing the food and offering recommendations. The decor is really nice - warm paneling, light and high ceilings. The upstairs does get pretty warm, especially if you're sitting by the bar, but the upstairs tables are comfortable.

    I will continue to dine at Nomad Pizza Co and will definitely choose it over Pizzeria Stella - it's a locally-owned, sources fresh ingredients, and is just a cool place to eat and relax with friends. I haven't attended one of the movie showings, but I hope to do so soon.

    Thanks Nomad!
