    The food here, in my opinion is a little better than so-so, but their classic mojitos, OH MY GOD!  So delicious!  I've been twice, both times in the early evening (7-ish) and both times sat outside.  The location is GREAT for people watching, and I prefer the lit sidewalk to the dark, dark, interior.  It's a sexy dark, but you still can't see anything, and I like to see the people I'm with, as well as the food I'm eating.  Also, it's very loud inside, which detracts from the sexiness a little, so outside for me.  I've ordered the calamari salad, the sampler (I forget what it's called, but it's on the appetizer list), the chicken dinner, the shrimp dinner, and the black dough lobster & crab empanada.  The calamari salad is served on a divided plate, with calamari on one side and the salad on the other.  The calamari is good, but the salad is warm, oily and kind of hot/spicy.  Not really my cup of tea.  The sampler was served on a 3 tier rack, with 3 different dishes, and you can choose to have enough for 2, 3, or 4 people.  All 3 of the dishes were delicious, but I can't remember what they were.  The chicken dinner was very good, very moist, very tasty, the shrimp dinner, not so much.  The shrimp are huge and kind of tough, I remember having a hard time cutting them into smaller, more manageable pieces.  The black dough empanada was good.  The lobster/crab filling was absolutely delicious, and the outer black dough looks like it has been burnt very badly, but it's just the color of the dough.  It doesn't have much taste to it, and I didn't eat much of it.  The mojitos though, are absolutely to die for!  They server them with a sliver of sugarcane, which is fun to suck on, and adds to the sweetness.  You can't taste the alcohol very much, but that doesn't mean they aren't strong.  My mom had two and was pretty well done for the rest of the night, and my boyfriend had one and it definitely loosened him up more than a bit.

    Listed in Are You Buying?, My Love Affair With Starr

    It's times like these that I wonder how I managed to score my boyfriend.

    For my birthday gift, he planned out an entire weekend just for the two of us, and Saturday began with lunch at Alma de Cuba. His family are fans of the Starr restaurants, and after my birthday dinner last year at Morimoto, I too have become a convert and the two of us are determined to try each and every Starr restaurant. A birthday lunch at Alma was therefore the perfect way to begin our day.

    The interior is really nice, modern decor with clean lines. My boyfriend preferred the warm lounge on the first floor, but I enjoyed the second floor's more light and airy feel. Our waiter was clearly new, but he was very nice and extremely attentive. The menu is fusion Cuban cuisine, and damn is it good! We didn't have drinks, but their mojito seems to be popular.

    We shared their ceviche sampler (which everyone should definitely try), that was a tasting of each of their ceviches: Vuelve La Vida: shrimp, scallops and calamari tossed in a an aji amarillo sauce with cherry tomatoes; Hamachi Tiradito: yellowtail with red onion, cilantro, lime juice and a touch of aji; and Ecuadorian Shrimp: shrimp with roasted tomatoes, chives, avocado and corn nuts. The ceviches were incredible, each one spiced perfectly to accompany the seafood, although I wish that the hamachi had a lighter sauce because I really couldn't taste the fish (which I personally prefer), only the lime juice.

    We also shared two entrees, a special halibut one with both smoked and grilled halibut over a salad of mesclun, yellow beets and cherry tomatoes, and a cuban-sounding dish that was spinach and cheese layered with plantains and wrapped with zucchini with tomato sauce on top, served over a black bean and pesto spread with an onion-jicama slaw. Damn, damn good, but the plantain dish was waaaay too heavy for me. The halibut was perfect (except for the fact that I don't care for smoked fish EVER), a nice light dish for the hot summer. We both loved the food, but unfortunately didn't have enough (or any) room for dessert, which just means that we'll have to go back again in the near future!!

    Ohh, Stephan Starr. I've really really tried to avoid your restaurants, let alone LIKING one of them, but alas. My plan has failed horribly.

    I recently spent my 22nd birthday here with my family and had an ABSOLUTELY PHENOMENAL meal. My sister and I would've literally licked the plate clean because the sauce that came with it was super tasty but, thankfully, we have manners and settled for using bread. The empanadas verde were also really good and light. I enjoyed the oysters rodriguez but I found that the potatoes or whatever they were that were under the tiny ball of fried oyster to be too sweet and kind of overwhelmed the main point of the dish - the oyster. I didn't get to taste the bacon-wrapped dates because everyone else got to them first but clearly they were delicious too.

    In terms of entrees, I decided to be a spoiled birthday girl and chose the most expensive dish on the menu - the sirloin. In some ways I regret it because even though it was really good, it didn't blow my mind. Perhaps it's because the other dishes were a lot better but I suppose I kind of expected a $36 dollar dish to be super delicious. The papas that came with it were good, slighly greasy, and the other aspect of it was just some cut up tomatoes with bleu cheese on it. Not creative, but not bad either. Now, my boyfriend's tenderloin on the other hand was great. It had a really good sauce and came with yummy asparagus. My dad's pollo was super amazing and tasted just like the street chicken you would find in Mexico or some other South American country (that's a good thing). Although I will say that the fennel tamale that was served with it was gross, but I hate fennel. My sister got the maple-glazed tuna, which was served almost like tuna pops. I enjoyed it but I found it weird that a glazed tuna was served with a red sauce. Both were delicious, I just would've preferred them separately. The absolute BEST dish that we had all night was my mom's, and she got a special sea bass that was an addition to the menu. Holy hell, I kid you not when I say that it literally melts in your mouth. I've tasted some good seafood in my lifetime and this was LITERALLY the best dish I've ever had. I wish I could have that sea bass at least once a day for the rest of my life.  My dad actually agreed and said that this was the best meal that he's had in years, which is huge because he is also a restauranteur and is usually way too critical to really enjoy most dining experiences.

    The service was superb. Our waitress was super attentive and made me realize that I could neverever work in a fine dining restaurant because she took care of so many minor details that I would never think of doing. I will say though that I found the hosts to be way too fake nice. I understand that you have to be but seriously, a plastered on smile never left her face and it was just so obviously insincere that it really rubbed me the wrong way.

    All in all, I would definitely go back here if it was a special occasion or I somehow came into a lot of money. Seeing as the latter won't happen any time soon, you'll find me here on an anniversary or on my birthday next year.

    We arrived 30 minute prior to our 8:00 pm reservations to check out the ambiance.  The decor is reminiscent of the Delano or Shore Club in South Beach---sophisticated sounds and luscious on the eyes. The place is two-level, with first floor lounge-vibe, bar and trendy couches. The second floor is all dinner tables.

    I have to agree with others about the "little bread rolls"----they are addictively good. It's a shame patrons must pay extra for side orders of sweet plantains and black beans/rice---essentials to any cuban plate.

    Dessert: Portions were more than adequate so we skipped the sweets
    Service---friendly and superb; timely and accomodating. We had reservations, showed  up on time and no wait.
