    Expect nothing fancy here- just delicious food with exotic flavors. If you're tired of the usual Italian BYOs and like to share plates and taste as many things as you can, go to Banana Leaf!

    They're a little eager on wanting to take your order, but they're helpful in guiding you along if you can't decide. Luckily, I went with a group of eaters all willing to do the family-style thing so I didn't feel like I was missing out on any of the dishes.

    Favorites: stuffed roti with chicken curry, beef rendang, basil noodles, haianese chicken, sizzling beef, and their banana leaf kari ayam. Some may find their chicken a little plain, but I loved how moist and tender it was.

    Skip their white rice and order some of their coconut and haianese chicken rice to soak up all of the sauces.

    We got a their pisang goring- fried bananas with coconut ice cream for dessert and their fried ice cream. Both were delicious but too similar so I would do the pisang goring and their coconut pudding next time...

    Audrey's Party Order Guide

    Banana Leaf has always been a reliable spot for throwing 20+ people birthday parties. We always leave stuffed and happy/buzzed by sharing several dishes family style and bringing our own alcohol -- usually costs $15 - $20 a person, tax and tip included.

    This is what I order for groups every time we go. I'm writing this because when I'm on Yelp looking up new/unfamiliar spots, I just want to know what to get and be done with it. I'm sure the other stuff is good too, but these are my favs.

    Appetizer:

    - Roti Canai (1 to share between every 3 people)

    Entrees (as many entrees as we have people -- for example, since there are 7 entree picks, if we had 21 people, I'd get 3 of each dish):

    - Indian Mee Goreng
    - Clay Pot Pearl Noodles
    - Seafood Scramble Egg Chow Fun
    - Japanese Sizzling Tofu
    - House Special Pork with Yam
    - Banana Leaf Kari Ayam
    - Kang Kung Belacan (because you need fiber to poop eventually)

    Then I ask who wants rice and we get that number of white rice bowls. If people want more (it happens every time), we just ask the servers to bring extra bowls later.

    The things that stuck out to me most at Banana Leaf were: a) the service-- the waiters were very helpful and willing to give direction on the best items on the menu (especially for those of you who prefer not be surprised by spices!), and b) the flavors. I ordered the mango chicken, which only increased my love for that fruit. Fresh, fruity, and wonderfully prepared/presented-- the meal was a good introduction to Thai food and at a cheap price compared to more upscale restaurants.

    Location: only a couple of blocks from Market East and just outside of the Trocadero, so it's very convenient. Even though it doesn't look like much on the outside, definitely try Banana Leaf, because the food is worth the trip.

    I think they have the nicest servers out of all the restaurants in Chinatown.
