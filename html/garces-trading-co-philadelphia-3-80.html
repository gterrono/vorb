    If you don't already know, service is a BIG deal to me. Be friendly and make me feel like you want me in your establishment.

    Ok, so now onto my review of GTC. If this rating were based solely on food, GTC would receive an easy 4.5 stars from me. I ordered the De Prosciutto pizza with the sausage de Toulouse - DELICIOUS! The flavors were great, the crust was soft enough (I'm not really a fan of thin crust pizza), and it was just generally amazing. For dessert I had a pistachio macaroon (*singing* YUM!!!), and the raspberry marjolaine, which was ok. My friends and I washed it down with a tasty bottle of Moscato. Ben, our waiter, was attentive, and he gave great recommendations. We were all very pleased with our food.

    Sounds like a fantastic meal, right? Normally I would say yes, but even before we sat down, a damper was placed on the evening by a rude hostess. There were three hostesses; one was friendly, one was relatively inconsiderate and one was out and out rude. When I first got there, I gave one hostess (the friendly one) my name, and she told me the wait would be about 45 minutes. That was no biggie because it was a Saturday night. So an hour later, I go in to see how much longer our wait would be, and a different hostess (the rude one) tells me that the wait will be more like the 45 minutes I was originally quoted. Confused, I told her that 45 minutes had already passed. She curtly replied that according to her clock, only 30 minutes had passed, and that she'd let us know when the table was ready. Now I'd been timing our wait, and I knew it'd been longer than 45 minutes, but how are you going to argue with a chick who's working in a restaurant that you've REALLY been wanting to try? I wasn't about to get into it with her, or her jack-leg time telling skills.  The appropriate response would have been "I'm sorry, but it's going to be a few more minutes.  We'll let you know as soon as the table is ready," *insert apologetic look here*.  About fifteen minutes later, she comes outside where we were waiting to tell us our table was ready.

    Once seated, and after having given our orders, a different hostess (the inconsiderate one) comes over and tells me that she needs the chair next to me (our purses were sitting in it), and that I could put my purse on the table. Now, I'm not one of "those girls" who thinks it's bad luck to put her purse on the floor (because frankly, it doesn't matter if your purse is on the floor or on your lap - if it's empty it's empty, and the location of said purse has nothing to do with that.). I will not, however, place my purse on the dinner table. You put YOUR purse on the table! We were seated at one of the higher tables, so I just pulled up another chair. Now don't get me wrong, I can understand you needing the chair, but there were other chairs of the same type not too far away, so she could have grabbed one of those. I just felt inconvenienced, and I shouldn't feel like that as a restaurant patron. She was apologetic, but whole thing could have been handled better.

    So yeah, the food and Ben's service are really what afforded this place and this experience more than a single star. I would have had a much more enjoyable experience sans the rude and inconsiderate hostesses.

    That said, I won't let this experience hinder me from trying more of the Garces restaurants, but I probably will not return to GTC.

    I adore Jose Garces and he has yet to disappoint me. Garces Trading Company exceeded my expectations. I expected it to feel like you're eating in the middle of the store, but it wasn't like that at all.  They did a good job of separating the two. They have a wonderful wine cellar with a wide selection of varieties and price ranges. The restaurant is byo so I highly recommend buying a bottle of wine there to enjoy with your meal.

    A friend took me here to celebrate my birthday, so we definitely splurged. We ordered the Chef's tasting cheese plate that was amazing. Delicious cheeses paired with the appropriate accompaniments are one my favorite things. I'm sorry I don't remember the names of the cheeses we got because they were all so good. One was a bleu cheese that was better than any I've had before and was paired with a lavender honey. The two together left me literally speechless, which is not an easy thing to do. Next we ordered the roasted eggplant with 150 yr aged balsamic. Gorgeous! We also tried the breakfast radishes with goat butter. I had my doubts on this one. Why would I want to eat a radish with butter? That just sounds weird. But man, was it delicious. Lightly flavored radishes, a dollop of goat butter all drizzled with olive oil, sea salt and chives. I recommend trying it. Then came the salami you could see through. It was so flavorful and served with house made whole grain mustard. We followed all that with the funghi pizza. I should have drank less wine and paid more attention to the ingredients so I could accurately describe this to you guys. The crust was thin and crisp and rumored to be prepared with duck fat. It was topped with a slightly sweet, buttery cheese, a variety of mushrooms and microgreens. I loved it. I would definitely order it again.

    I can sincerely say I savored every bite that night. I was too full to go for dessert but did take the opportunity to peruse the menu. They offer the usual from lemon meringue tarts, chocolate mousse cake, etc. All promise to be delicious.

    One thing I like about places such as this is that you can go for a simple, moderately priced meal or you can go all out and really treat yourself. I will absolutely go back there. Although we ate a lot, there were so many things I didn't get to try. They have beautiful pasta dishes I'm looking forward to trying and all the other pizzas. And more wine of course!

    Went over to Garces Trading Company after numerous suggestions from friends. Tried the chef's selection of charcuterie, the vichyssoise chaude and the tarte flambee pizza. We also picked up a bottle of wine for a reasonable price at the in-house wine shop.

    The atmosphere was enjoyable. It felt warm, friendly and casual with a dash of sophistication. We were seated almost immediately and our waiter was very helpful and informative.

    After deciding on what we would try from the menu a friend went to the wine shop to select a bottle that would pair well. She, having worked at wine retailers for years in the past, noted that Garces Trading Co. has surprisingly little mark up relative to what she would have expected.

    As far as the menu we thoroughly enjoyed all of the food. Portion size was slightly underwhelming but alternatively the prices are reasonable.

    To start we enjoyed the chef's selection of charcuterie with house mustard. Out of the three selections given to us (two salamis, one ham) the only one that really spoke to us was the saucisson sec basque which had espelette in it. It was one of the more unique and notable salamis I have ever eaten. In hindsight we wish we had gone with the cheeses or another dish or two entirely.

    We followed that up with the vichyssoise soup that had bay scallops and thick cuts of fried bacon. The waiter brings out your soup, minus the actual soup, and you will see all of the perfectly prepared scallops, fried bacon and potatoes. You'll want to devour them right away in their unaltered state. Then it's virtually "ruined", the waiter pours in the vichyssoise. It was very rich without overwhelming the delicate ingredients. Certainly something I would recommend. It's hard to go wrong with cream, bacon and scallops.

    Shortly after our pizza arrived, the tarte flambee with large cuts of smoked pork belly, spanish onions and creme fraiche. The crust is wonderfully baked, salted and at the perfect crispness. I could have easily eaten another half.

    I am excited to return and give the much heralded brunch a try some time in the future.

    So far, so good...

    My friend and I came for lunch on a Saturday afternoon.  We split a chicken sandwich and the margherita pizza.

    The chicken sandwich was pretty good.  The bread had a great crust and the flavors were solid.  Only complaint is that there wasn't much chicken on the sandwich.

    Margherita pizza was pretty good too.  Tomato sauce was fresh and flavorful and the cheese was tasty.  Nothing to write home about though (pizza at Zavino is much better).

    Although the service was a bit spotty (our waiter asked us for our order right away, and our order came out approximately 2 1/2 minutes later -- also had some trouble with the water refills), it was overall pretty good.  Hostess was friendly.  We weren't rushed at the end of the meal.  Definitely a pleasant dining experience.

    My favorite part about this place is the fact that they give you a generous portion of bread with great olive oil, plus a little container of sea salt and a pepper grinder that you can add to your own taste.  Seems not many places these days are too generous with the bread, so this was a nice touch.

    The prices were pretty reasonable, although I think we ordered the cheapest things on the menu.  It did seem you could spend quite a bit without really thinking about it.

    Overall, GTCo and I got off on the right foot today.  Although I was a bit hesitant to try the place (seems like much in the "marketplace" is overpriced and riding the "Chef Garces" high (i.e., $7 pot of herbs etc.) so I was a bit skeptical on how a meal would be), I was very pleased with my lunch experience and am now ready to put my toes in the water a little further and try dinner.

    i am also not a fan of automatic gratuity and here they charged 20% for the six of us.  while i am sure we received 20% worth of service, i prefer tips being at my discretion.  while they made up for it as best the possible could, i am not sure that i would wait over an hour to eat here again, or even longer than 20 minutes for that matter.  i would just buy the wine and food to go and enjoy it elsewhere.

    In this article, the author suggests an approach to Garces Trading Co. that results in maximum utility at minimal expense, ideally $0.00.  The author believes it is appropriate to use a "trading company" as a platform to discuss theories of economic efficiency.  To these ends, the example of a "date" [fn1] is employed.

    Dates are typically very expensive, owing to a variety of reasons: desire to impress one's date, purchase of alcoholic beverages, etc.  In many cases, these are also high risk/high reward scenarios, and not necessarily a wise investment when one is possessed of limited financial resources.

    Funds expended during the course of a date are comprised of both tangible items (food, drink, entertainment, taxis) and intangibles (atmosphere, things that inform conversation).  Garces Trading Co. is designed in such a way as to negate the need to expend funds in pursuit of the majority of these items.  

    One need not engage its services as a restaurant, with the attendant expectation that one order food in exchange for the opportunity to spend time inside the location, since it is also a retail outfit.  One may simply browse at one's leisure, in full view of the dining area.  The background on its origins/reasons for being, assuming one is aware of such facts or willing to make them up for the benefit of one's date, provide ample fodder for conversation.

    As for tangibles, Garces Trading Co. strongly encourages taking samples of their many fine meats, cheeses, olive oils and vinegars.  If one takes full advantage of these free offerings, this may fulfill the food requirement of the date [fn2].  Along these lines, discussing the food items sampled with the employees also functions as a kind of entertainment, assuming one is entertained by discussing food with knowledgeable persons.  Located in the heart of Center City, it is within walking distance for most city residents or otherwise easily accessible by cheap public transportation.  To the author's knowledge, there is no way to procure alcoholic beverages free of charge, but the on-site PLCB outpost supplies wine at below typical restaurant rates.

    Employing this approach, it is possible that one could avoid trading money for goods and services at Garces Trading Co. but still go home and trade bodily fluids with one's date.

Specialties

Garces Trading Company is a full-service bar and restaurant focused on Iron Chef Jose Garces' own favorite dishes from his travels to cafes throughout Western Europe, with a focus on Italy, Spain and France.  Our thoughtful menu of cafe staples is served throughout the day, and our bar offers a wealth of cocktails, wines and beers that pair beautifully with our menu.  Our on-site PLCB Wine Boutique, the first of its kind, makes a great resource either before sitting down to dinner (corkage fees apply) or to complement one of our decadent gift baskets, available in our newly redesigned retail section.

History

Established in 2010.

Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a refined, Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, a contemporary American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago, IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale, AZ and Palm Springs, CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the landmark Revel Resort. Today, in addition to our much-lauded restaurants around the country, Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
