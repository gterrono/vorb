        In the city known for their Cheese steaks you will never be disappointed if you select Jim's. First lets clear something up - Pat's and Geno's great places, both unique and with their own style from the way the meat is served up to the bread it squished in to. However, Jim's just takes it all the way home!

        My fav location and unfortunately a trendy spot for tourists is this South Street location. When I visit Philly I visit Jim's. I love going home just to spend that time in line anticipating the juicy steak sandwich that is coming my way!

        In order to not embarrass yourself or have instant anxiety be prepared to shout the order and don't forget KISS (keep it simple stupid). For example leave out the um's, ugh's and what's. Just tell 'em how you want it dressed. For example: provolone, peppers and onions, and hot peppers. That's it. You don't have to say "I would like a cheese steak with lettuce, tomato, fried onions and peppers and cheese whiz" Just say "in the garden, fried onions and pepper w/ whiz" That's it! Then be prepared to devoured the best greasy but tasty sandwich you've ever had!! :)

        Once you weave your way through the line, oh and enjoy looking at the pictures all over the walls of celebrity status people who have have the privilege to dine at Jim's. Once you have food in hand, the issue becomes where to sit. You might have a bite before your butt hits the chair! You can venture to second floor where you will find condiments like mustard and ketchup. There will be about 10 tables or so and the windows overlook South street down below. Be safe on these stairs, a bit steep!! If sitting on the second floor is a no go and the little bar style seating on the first floor is another dead end, grab your food head outside to the curb, sit down and EAT!

        BTW they ship!!!! I have yet to try this feature unfortunately I like the real deal straight off the grill!

        Normally, if you'd ask me to stand in a line in excess of 100 people for in excess of an hour for a sandwich, I'd tell you go take a flying leap (or words to that effect) but I did, and man, am I glad.

        I didn't know a sandwich could be that good.  Oh I've had the Italian Beef's of Chicago and , they still hold a special place in my belly, but the Philly Cheesesteak at Jim's is to die for. Order it "wiz wit" (with Cheeze Wiz), mushrooms and onions and my friend, you have a wonderful slice of heaven in front of you.  

        The order line snakes around through "cattle herders" inside the building, then out the door, around the corner and down the sidewalk a good 50-60 feet.  

        Once inside, the seating is not plentiful although there is an area upstairs if you're lucky enough to find a place to plop down.  It's often standing room only.  For that reason it will will never rank 5 stars in my book but let's face it, no one is there for the ambiance.

        Check out South Street in Philly anyway because it's a cool area with lots to offer but whatever you do, don't leave without one of Jim's steaks!  Trust me, it will be worth the wait!

        Pop Quiz - which of the following locations is most likely to be visited by out-of-towners in Philadelphia?

        A) 4th & South
        B) 9th & Wharton
        C) Front & Oregon
        D) The easternmost reaches of Snyder Ave.

        I thought a good tactic with Jim's would be to not review it at all, just ignore it altogether. However, I can sit by reading reviews deifying this place no longer.

        Some locals throw their support behind Jim's and I must ask, why? You know there are better steaks* around town to be had. I don't blame folks from out-of-town for going to Jim's, but locals - stop it already.

        Jim's is a tourist attraction, plain and simple. Scroll through the list of reviews & glance at the home city of most of the reviewers. CASE CLOSED. My advice to visitors: take a few extra minutes in the cab and head into South Philly.

        Jim's is the kind of place you go to just to say you've been. I get that. I think for many, local and visitor alike, their experience with Jim's is much like the first time a youth helps themselves to their parents' liquor cabinet - when discretion or wisdom is lacking.

        This is how thirteen year olds wind up swigging Creme de Menthe from the bottle. This is how people find themselves at Jim's.

        Let it be noted that this is not the type of place that should keep its patrons in an enclosed indoor space. There are reasons other sandwich shops use an open air format. One of those reasons being so you don't emerge smelling worse than a thousand armpits.

        Let it also be noted that long lines aren't necessarily indicative of a quality product. There are lines for shitty movies, concerts, etc all the time. The tastes of the collective shouldn't be given too much weight. (i.e. - the success of chain restaurants)

        For most Philadelphians, Jim's is an afterthought. One whose existence and purpose are at worst, vexing - at best, absent from our consciousness.

        If you answered 'A' to the opening question, you've identified the secret behind Jim's popularity and the principal factor in its success. There are better steaks* to be had elsewhere in Philly.

        * - Note to non-Philadelphians: We often shorten "cheesesteak" to "steak" for the sake of brevity. This is not to be confused with an actual cut of beef as in Porterhouse, Filet, Ribeye, etc.

        Every time someone comes and visits me in Philly they want to try an authentic cheesesteak. I don't eat them myself, and there are so many decent places around town that its very overwhelming to figure out where to take them for a gooey bite of steak and cheese. Since most big name places are in South Philly (and I have no car access), I have to choose from what's in CC.

        I've ended up taking two people on separate occasions to Jim's Steaks for their requisite cheesesteaks; once during the summer with a friend, and another time in the winter with my brother. Summertime sucks on South Street because its PACKED with all the high schoolers (and even middle schoolers) who have waaaay too much free time on their hands, all the summer tourists who go straight to the big name places, and then the poor people who live and work within the vicinity. Jim's always has a gigantic line around the block, and I did wait on it with my friend so that she could have a cheesesteak. Winter seems much more reasonable since kids are in school (yes!!!!) and its not tourist season - but after I got in line with my brother inside Jim's, the line behind us increased exponentially and did end up outside. Get there early, or prepare to wait.

        Both my friend and brother LOVED their cheesesteaks, and finished them almost instantaneously. My bf (who hadn't had a cheesesteak in quite a long time for a Philadelphian) also enjoyed his, although he now has a favorite place near Rittenhouse. The juices flow freely from them so arm yourself with napkins! But for me, the main reason to go to Jim's is just for the show. I love watching the guys behind the grill, seeing all the pictures of people who have been there before, and the line does make it more of an event than just food. There are better places out there, but this place is fine too.

        Cheesesteak bliss.

        This is one of those tourist spots that actually has earned its stripes (unlike say, Pat's or Genos).  The lines are around the block long but they are definitely worth it if you are craving a cheesesteak or want to see what the cheesesteak is all about.

        Out of towners can enjoy seeing pictures of various famous people plastered and signed all over the walls while they wait for their food to be done.  My advice is to take the steak to go with extra condiments and take a walk around South Street to get a real Philly experience.

      Specialties

      Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

      History

      Established in 1976.

      This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

      In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
