    I am shocked how bad my dining experience was based on all of the stellar reviews.  I go out often to Philly for dinner and have had many experiences with restaurants on all levels.  I was extremely disappointed on how the night turned out.  Here's what happened:

    We arrived for our 5pm reservation on time.  There were several tables already sat and a party ahead of us at the door. A 4 top wanted to move from the table by the door and it took about 5 minutes for the staff to decide where to put them.  There weren't too many choices since this is an extremely small restaurant.  None of the other staff stepped up to seat our party and the one in front of us, so we waited until the other seating arrangement was decided upon.

    We were finally taken to our table and offered water.  No server came over to explain the menu.  I heard the server at the table next to us explaining how to use the regular menu for Restaurant week and advised of 2 additions.  After waiting for about 10 minutes our server comes over and asks what we wanted to order.  No introduction, no explanation of the menu and did not tell us about the additions.  She had blond hair with a long bang that covered one of her eyes.

    Our appetizers came out quickly.  I ordered the grilled octopus which was a disappointment.  The octopus itself had no flavor.  The dish was heavily salted.  The portion was large and the vegetables were fresh.  My partner had the artichoke, which was alot of work and took him twice as long to eat it as it took me for my dish.

    From the time I finished my appetizer it was about 30 minutes before the entree arrived.  I started to worry a little before 6pm.  I had told the restaurant when I made the reservation that we had theater tickets at 6:30pm.  I advised the server of this and she went towards the kitchen, but still the clock ticked.  Finally the food arrived.  Not what I expected.  I got the Lobster and Shrimp pyramids.   I could barely taste the seafood.  The pyramids were chewy and the inside was bland.  The sauce was good, but got heavy soon after I started eating it.  We had to shovel the food in order to try to make it to our show in time.  The server never returned to check on us and see how we were enjoying our meals.  I guess she did not like that I made a comment when she delivered the dinner and she placed my dish in front of my husband.  She did the same thing with the appetizer.  It was annoying especially since she only had 2 other tables in addition to ours and they were 2 or 4 tops.

    Two other servers came over and offered to give us a credit to come back and have dessert.  They also offered to wrap something up for us.  At this point we had no extra time.  They were kind enough to remove the desserts from the bill.

    Also this place it cash only, which I don't mind, but they do make you give a credit card to keep you from no showing.  If you take a credit card for that, you should also offer as an option for dinner.

    I came to Mercato tonight with very high hopes.  I love restaurant week and look forward to new dining experiences at a reasonable price.  

    I knew Mercato normally doesn't take reservations so I was pleased when they took a reservation for 6 people at 7:30pm.  Before taking the reservation they required a credit card number to hold the spot (an odd fact considering they are a cash-only establishment) and I was informed that if I canceled less than 24 hours in advance I would be charged $25 per person.  This all seemed strange, but I gave my credit card number, pleased that at least  I could make a reservation.

    Tonight we arrived on time (if not a bit early) for our reservation.  As the restaurant is small, we had to wait outside.  It's was a nice night, so we didn't mind.  However, we waited and waited and waited to be seated.  It wasn't until 8:10 (40 minutes after our original reservation) that we were seated.  Unfortunately this rushed our experience as a member of our party had a train to catch.  For being so preoccupied about my being on time for my reservation, enough so to require a credit card "deposit" to hold the reservation, they weren't particularly concerned about keeping us waiting.

    As for the food...  I started with the whole grilled artichoke.  This was incredibly overcooked.  The middle was a brown mush, while the outside was burnt to a crisp and completely inedible.  The only saving grace was the the tangy aioli, which was quite delicious.   I had the striped bass as a second course which was decent.  The sauce was tangy and complemented the fish nicely.  The mushrooms however, were rubbery.  For dessert I ordered the molten chocolate cake.  What came out was neither molten nor cake.  Instead it was a ramequin of what resembled room temperature hot fudge.  I made it halfway through before I felt incredible ill.  Disappointing in the face of all the wonderful reviews I have seen for this restaurant.  

    All in all,  for $35 dollars it was a decent amount of food.  I left feeling full, but not particularly satisfied.  You would think that restaurant week is an opportunity for restaurants to woo new customers and that they would want to make a good first impression.   I will not be returning again, for restaurant week or any other week for that matter.

    My fiance and I went to Mercato last night for Valentine's Day dinner.  We were very excited having been to Mercato before for a great meal.  Let me preface this review by saying yes, I understand it's Valentine's Day....but- a few things:

    -Their Valentine's Day 4-course Prix Fixe menu was $65, and they were bold enough to have that and the normal price for their 4-course Prix Fixe ($48) listed on the website at the same time.  I was not told at the time I made my reservation that the Prix Fixe was the ONLY menu option for Valentine's Day.  In looking at the selections, there really wasn't anything that special about the Vday menu vs. the regular tasting.  The things they are best known for (meat and cheese platter, diver scallops) were not an option on the Vday menu.

    -Upon our timely arrival for our 7:30 reservation, we were told it would be a 15-20 minute wait. Ooookay? If we had a really late reservation, say 8:30 or 9, I could see being backed up already, but 7:30?  Come on.

    -We waited outside for FIFTY minutes, with no heads up from the maitre d about when exactly we would be seated.  Thankfully, they had heat lamps and it was unseasonably warm for February otherwise this would have been miserable and we probably would have left.  The only consolation prize was a server coming out and bringing complimentary champagne at one point.

    Finally, we're called inside.  Tables are elbow to elbow, but that is the standard at Mercato and I actually sort of enjoy that.  There is one water glass and one wine glass on the table.  The young lady who seated us apologizes and says sorry, we are running low on wine glasses (even though I can see rows of them on shelves in the kitchen.) and promises to bring some glasses.  We wait another 5 minutes, she returns with the glasses and comes to take our order.

    Another unpleasant surprise- out of the three dessert options (cookie plate with espresso, cheese plate, and a chocolate torte), the cookie plate and cheese plates are only given in pairs, so if you got that option, you only got one dessert.  Excuse me?  You jack up your prix fixe menu prices almost $20 and then at the table, sneak in the fact that one of the courses isn't really included?  Would it have been so hard to cut the platter in half?

    Anyways, onto the first course.  He had the carpaccio salad, I had the cauliflower soup, which was thin, dull and tasteless aside from the random cracker smeared with bleu cheese on the side and the piece of pancetta hiding at the bottom of the bowl.  Maybe that is just the nature of cauliflower soup, but I would have attempted to give it a little more flavor somehow.

    Second course- papardelle with rabbit for me, pork with broccoli rabe and pasta for him.  Absolutely stunning.  One of the reasons I gave this review two stars.

    Third course- my rack of lamb is cold, maybe lukewarm if I was feeling generous.  His steak, potatoes and asparagus are cooked perfectly and absolutely delicious.  At this point, I'm crafting the Yelp review in my mind.

    Dessert arrives.  Cookies are great, espresso with ice cream in it even better.  We pay the bill and leave.

    Overall, I might be willing to return to Mercato on a non-holiday night (and certainly non-Restaurant Week as I can't imagine how they handle a gazillion tables of screaming 21-year-old girls toting magnums of Yellow Tail)  Their food is pretty delicious aside from some technical issues, but they clearly don't have their act together under pressure.
