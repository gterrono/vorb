    When you think Mexican food, you don't really think elegant.  You think big-portions, inexpensive, and lots of heat and flavor.  Distrito doesn't necessarily deliver on the first two (this is tapas, after all), but it sure is DELICIOUS.  

    I've been here four times now, and have never been disappointed.  The guacamole is creamy with just the right amount of spice.  The skirt steak nachos are good too.  When you go, you MUST order one of the ceviches.  I've had the yellowtail, and the sangrita sorbet was a light, tarty surprise with the fish.  The forest mushroom huraches were very, very rich --- a mushroom lover's paradise.  The tacos have a complex set of flavors, and the corn tortillas themselves have a fresh, corn-y taste.  The whole grilled fish is also delicious, with grilled smoky flavor that doesn't overwhelm the fresh taste of the meat.  I feel like you can't really go wrong with anything on the menu.  Best of all, you don't feel weighed down.  Just full.

    Out of the three Jose Garces restaurants I've been to (Amada, Chifa, and Distrito), I have to say this one is my favorite.  The balance of flavors and textures is perfect --- the spices and sauces complements the meats and vegetables instead of overwhelming them.  It's not "authentic" Mexican food in the traditional sense, but hey, that just means  Garces has reinvented Mexican food so that it is light, complex, and refreshingly delicious.

    Distrito is a stylishly decorated restaurant with a lot of space. They have a pretty big bar downstairs and a big area to sit and eat upstairs. As you go up the steps you see luchador masks adorning the walls, which always makes me think  of Jack Black for some reason. There's a lot of lime/light green and pink, which I don't get but I guess it works. And yes there is a table with a VW bug interior in it downstairs.

    The food here really isn't traditional Mexican, but more Mexican inspired. That being said, it's delicious. I have never truly had anything bad here.

    The guacamole is truly delicious. However it is expensive so be forewarned. Is it worth it? Probably, but at $10, its on the edge. Similarly the Ignacio nachos are really good and loaded. But at $9 its also on the expensive side for nachos.  

    They had a lobster soup that was delicious, even after it was made vegetarian for my girlfriend. She loved it. I barely got a word in edgewise while she finished the soup.

    Something to note is that the portions of the main menu items are not real big. It's kind of like Mexican tapas. So its best to order more than one dish and share.

    My favorites from the main menu include the Los Hongos huarache, which is a pizza like creation with mushrooms, black truffles, corn, and cheese on it. It's ridiculously good. So much so that I ended up finishing my girlfriends portion when she couldn't eat anymore the first time. I actually liked it more than the Chilango, which had chorizo and ham on it. I also like the carnitas tacos. There's a lot of flavor packed into them, although they are on the small side.

    The service is always very attentive and the food comes out at a decent pace. They servers were very helpful in trying to find vegetarian dishes that my girlfriend could eat.

    Overall the flavors you get here are really worth the price tag. I don't go here very often, but I savor each trip.

Specialties

Distrito is the new restaurant concept by Jose Garces, the chef behind the acclaimed Philadelphia restaurants Amada and Tinto. Distrito is a high-energy restaurant inspired by the spirited culture and cuisine of Mexico City. The Distrito menu features Chef Garces' fresh interpretations of classic Mexican fare and beverages include over 65 tequilas and margaritas made with fresh seasonal fruits. A striking decor includes a hot pink and bright green color scheme, a wall constructed from over 600 colorful, campy masks of lucha libre, or "free fight" professional wrestlers, and a booth crafted from a Volkswagen Beetle, a typical Mexico City taxi cab.

History

Established in 2008.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
