    Great new addition to the neighborhood!  

    Since I get into work so early in the morning, I take a very early lunch and usually get to places right after they open.  I had checked out the menu and knew exactly what I wanted: schwarma.  Unfortunately, even though they'd been open for 15 minutes, the schwarma wasn't ready so I had to order something else.  I went with the chicken kebob plate with rice, cabbage, carrots, and hummus as my sides.

    The chicken was very tender and flavorful with a nice char from the grill, the rice it was served with was cooked perfectly (not overly cooked or mushy) and salted well.  I was most pumped about the carrots but they were actually my least favorite thing and I left almost all of them on my plate.  The cabbage had an acidity that I wasn't expecting but was pleasantly surprised with.  The hummus was very smooth, maybe a little bit too smooth, but had a really great flavor and the pita was soft and pillowy perfect.  

    I got a lot of food for the price and it was all very fresh and flavorful.  This place is definitely a great lunch spot and I know I'll be back often!  Hopefully I'll be able to try the schwarma next time!

    Having recently moved to this neighborhood, I can envision sandwiches from Hummus joining my regular takeout rotation. Prices at this outpost aren't, say, food cart cheap, but I'll gladly pay a couple more bucks for the spotless kitchen/dining area, superior eats, and friendly service.

    All three sandwiches we ordered arrived overflowing with our fillings of choice and all the fixings. In fact, the thick, doughy pitas burst at the seams due to a combination of a lot of fillings (good) and a lack of toasting (bad). Each variety of pita sandwich tasted great, however. I dug the shawarma's ideal combination of crispy, charred bits of chicken with more tender morsels. The Hummus staff fries and seasons their falafel fritters better than does Maoz around the corner, with each bite suggesting earthy chickpea and grassy parsley. The baba ganoush carries smoky notes and roasted vegetable sweetness equally, as it should.

    Of course, each sandwich benefits from a hit of pickled hot sauce or herbed tahini from the counter. The staff acted friendly even by non-fast food (and non-Philly) standards, and the premises were immaculate as previously mentioned. A welcome option for a quick, hearty lunch in Wash Square West.

    Bang for the buck, plain and simple.  Portions are big and the food tastes as if it were twice as expensive.  I usually go for the shawarma or beef kabob platter.  The shawarma maintains its juiciness, while still having some crisp to it from roasting on the spit.  The beef kabobs are cooked on sword-like stakes (according to tradition) and have a distinctive flavor.  My favorite sides are the eggplant, hummus, and tabouleh.  

    The concept of the restaurant appeals to me as well: to serve great Mediterranean food cheaply, without all of the cheesy decor and music you get at some more traditional places.  If you are looking for a greasy, hole-in-the-wall place, look elsewhere.  The space is well appointed, and the service staff are friendly and welcoming.  Hummus has become one of my go-to places in Philly for a tasty, quick, cheap, satisfying meal.  Would highly recommend.
