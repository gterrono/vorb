    Hands down the best hot sandwiches in Philadelphia. From the perfectly seasoned meat to the cheese to the veggies to the rolls, it doesn't get any better than this. I feel like I'd never really had a cheesesteak before tasting theirs, and I won't even touch a chicken cheesesteak from anywhere else. All this from a tiny little shack by the train tracks between an old factory and a bunch of South Philly strip malls.

    Be prepared to wait around lunch time. The line's almost always out the door, and snakes around a bunch even once you make it inside. Get there early, both to avoid the lines and to ensure you get a sandwich; once they run out of rolls, they close for the day. Oh, and don't even think about making the trip on the weekend or anything even close to a holiday (Flag Day, etc.): they'll be closed.

    Pros: Cheese steak!
    Cons: CASH ONLY, chemical smell while sitting outside

    So we couldn't pass up getting a Philly Cheese Steak while passing through Philly. Being we don't know the places to go and after recommendations from Philaians...Phillytonians...Philaers, whatever you call people from Philadelphia, finding it on almost every Top Philly Cheese Steak list, and the rep from the Inquirer, my wife and I made this our stop of choice while passing through Philadelphia. This would be our second time in Philly and our second Philly Cheese Steak.

    We arrived, it's a small shop on the corner with parking on the street and side parking lot. It looks like every other sandwich shop, small and simple. We went on a Thursday in the middle of the day, so there wasn't much of a crowd or line, just a few people in front of us. Everyone seemed to be ordering the pork sandwich, although looking on the grill, the array of mixes looked DELICIOUS!

    We ordered our sandwiches and realized that we could only pay with cash. This is just an FYI. Luckily we had cash, and they have an ATM inside. It only took a few minutes for our sandwiches and we headed outside.

    Now I have to say, we have only tried one other Cheese Steak in Philly. This may be a strong statement but it seems like most Cheese Steak sandwiches I have tried aren't significantly different. Some have a different flavor, some have a different type of break but they all are pretty close in all aspects. This one was good size and delicious, I definitely recommend it.

    The one downside that we experienced while visiting was after we grabbed our sandwiches, we sat at an open table outside and opened our delicious goodness, we took our first few bites. As we enjoyed every juicy/cheesy bite a slight breeze picked up carrying a very strong and heavy odor coming from a business plant of some kind across the street. Although it didn't make sitting outside enjoyable, it was completely out of the control of John's place which kept this place still at a 5 Star level! I recommend eating in your car or at another place.

    5 stars, period. No arguments accepted.

    Cheesesteaks - Crusty roll, in a good way, with chopped up chicken or beef and loads of cheese. Seems like 5-6 thick slices if American, and they do a good job of mixing it into the steak so its not all globbed up in one spot. A lot of places screw this up.

    No whiz should be noted. Not sure why but really don't care myself.

    Roast Pork sandwiches are mind blowing, best in the city. I don't even need cheese on mine, juicy and flavorful enough as is.

    If you're with a group - time your sandwiches. Cheesesteaks can take 7-12 or so minutes, while the roast pork will be done basically as soon as you can get your order taken. So wait a few after your friends order steaks then get the pork, so you can all eat at the same time.

    The chemical plant across the street is part of the authentic experience. Sniff some fumes, get a little buzz on, and enjoy some mind blowing sandwiches.

    My favorite sandwich place on planet earth. Bow down. We are not worthy.
