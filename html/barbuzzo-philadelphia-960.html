    I have this eerie feeling a lot of people are going to disagree with me. Oh well. Barbuzzo, Barbuzzo. Did I overhype you in my mind? Was I expecting too much? Probably. But an average 4.5 stars with almost 1000 reviews... That's a lot to live up to. Frankly, it disappointed. This is more like a 2.5 star review. It pains me to say it, it really does.

    Our party of 3 dined on a Sunday evening. We ordered 5 plates and dessert. We started with the Sheep's milk Ricotta: creamy and yummy at first, but kind of dull and unenthusiastic thereafter.  It needed salt. Next was the foie-mousse, which needed either spice or salt to cut the extreme fat and richness. I wanted SO badly to love this, but didn't. Next was the gnocchi, which was the best of the evening. I disagree with the other reviews that said it was too heavy. For a gnocchi, it was filling but light. Must be eaten when it's hot, and it lost all flavor when it cooled. We then had the stuffed meatballs. If you're going to have meatballs on your menu as a standalone dish, it better be the best meatballs you can put forth. I've had better. The peppers in the sauce were a nice touch, but the texture of the meatballs and flavors felt incomplete. Last plate of dinner was the asparagus/guanciale/truffled egg pizza: goopy, one note, and mushy. This pizza had no textural contrast. If the crust was crispier, it would have been successful. Perhaps they could have grilled the asparagus. I loaded on tons of the house oregano and chili oil to try and make it better. No one wanted a second slice.  

    Dessert menu looked OK so we ordered the peach bread pudding and ginger ice cream & tiramisu. Husband ordered a coffee. The bread pudding was DRY and poorly executed. Such a shame. Tiramisu on the other hand was fantastic. But then, the coffee was burnt.

    Won't be back.

    Came here late on a Saturday night prior to the Philly half. Walked right in and was seated immediately. I did the interior of this restaurant because it is dark and cozy. The menu was explained to us as being a "tapas" style or small plates restaurant. The food comes to the table as the dishes are completed. The menu had a lot of great choices that looked outstanding to me. It was mostly Mediterranean style food including some pizzas. The beer list is pretty good as well, I went with Anderson Valley Gose beer. Absolutely fantastic sour brew.

    We started out with the cheese plate. It was a great cheese plate and everything was paired properly. I went with the Lima bean and lemon Arancini. This was a 10! The lemon in the dish was so bright and refreshing and the earthyness of the Lima beans was awesome. I also ordered the beat and kale salad. Again this was awesome. The kale was tossed with a pistachio pesto and the goat cheese was smeared on the inside of the bowl so you could grab a bit as you are through the dish. My wife got the margharita pie and said it was awesome. We finished the evening with the chocolate tasting. Holy smokes was this awesome.

    This was a great evening overall with some great food. The service was spot on and we got to sit right next to the open kitchen area. I would highly recommend coming here if you are in the area.

    Let me just say that I have had to listen to my flatmate rave passionately about this place for the past three months, so I came in with sky-high expectations.

    We snagged a 9:45 PM day-of reservation online, but happened to be in the area and dropped in an hour early. We were luckily able to grab two seats at the chef's counter instead and shared four items between the two of us.

    A quick rundown:

    1. Fava bean and preserved lemon arancini. Yes. YES. YES. This was amazing and done perfectly. The risotto balls, paired with the watermelon radish and lemon aioli, were heavenly. Unfortunately, they were so good, my friend had to put up with me trying to convince her that we absolutely needed to another one of these (spoiler: she didn't give in).

    2. Mortadella pizza. This was decent, and I probably would've loved it more if I weren't still having fits of ecstasy about the arancini when this came out, so it admittedly was at a disadvantage.

    3. Pan-seared gnocchi. By this time, I had mostly recovered from the arancini and was a little more cognizant of what I was eating. The charred corn was a genius touch and was perfect for the dish.

    4. Salted caramel budino. SWEET BABY JESUS. I bombarded the Snapchat servers with my love for this dessert. I know everyone has said how perfect this is, but I have to say it again: this is perfect. A tip from one glutton to another: be brave and dig deep, so you get the dark chocolate crust at the very bottom together with everything else in one bite.

    Food aside, Barbuzzo has one of the best sangrias I've had. The service was also impeccable and attentive, with the plates timed perfectly.

    On the whole, I have to say that I love the concept behind Barbuzzo. So many things on the menu looked appealing--can't wait to visit again and to try its sister restaurants as well.

    Barbuzzo is without a doubt one of my top three favorite restaurants in Philadelphia, and I dreaded having to write this review because of the finality of it all (I've relocated to NYC for work). However, the sheep's milk ricotta, pizza (any of them, I promise), and salted caramel budino are three seductive Barbuzzo menu items that will have me crawling back to good ol' Philly even years from now.

    I've been for lunch, dinner, and have even stopped by for budino take-out. The restaurant is rather small and can be cramped, but they accept OpenTable reservations, so be smart and make a reservation!

    If you're really just stopping by for the budino take-out, the wait is short and the process relatively painless as long as you don't get caught inside the black curtain they have in their entrance/waiting area.

    All in all, Barbuzzo is one of the many delightful gems on 13th St. Make a reservation and order the budino! Your taste buds will thank you.

    Oh, during Valentine's Day they also offer budinos in bulk for purchase, so feel free to buy a bunch for your loverd ones (or yourself) and share the love.

    So my husband and I decided to try Barbuzzo after a 6:30 show at the Suzanne Roberts Theater. Even though it was a Tuesday night, I called ahead to make a reservation and they said they could accommodate us at 8:30, which they did immediately when we walked in. the restaurant was crowded on a Tuesday night because it is a very small narrow space. I had read great reviews about this restaurant. The tables are very, very close and the restaurant is very dark with a small votive candle on each table. Even though we have excellent eyesight, we still had to hold the candle over our menu to read it. We were approached promptly and I ordered the sangria and my husband ordered a dark beer on tap. The weird thing was they served his beer (which was very good) in a wine glass which was probably only about 5 to 6 ounces. The sangria was just ok. It tasted very sweet with little fruit and almost tasted like cranberry. We were advised that the plates were small so we should order 2 to 3 per person but we are not huge eaters so we chose 3 to share. We chose the roasted and raw beet salad which was delicious but not many beets, heavy on the kale. My husband ordered the bronzino and it was perfect. I ordered the rosemary pork loin and it was also very good. It was brined to perfect tenderness and the white bean salad accompaniment was delicious. We were very full after 3 dishes but decided to share the budino after reading so many reviews and it did not disappoint, rich and creamy with a cookie base crumb. The restaurant was very loud for conversation and our seat was closest to the narrow aisle where all the customers and waitstaff pass so we were constantly being brushed against. This is not a place to go if you want to have an intimate conversation with a companion or friends. That being said, the food was delicious and we would return.
