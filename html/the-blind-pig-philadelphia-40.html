    I've been to Blind Pig 3 times now, and each experience was great.

    The first time was for their soft opening private event.  It was the first week they were open and everything was really delicious.  They came by and asked if we had any questions or comments, and they took notes!  

    The 2nd time was with a larger group of people and we sat at the large table in the back (a great table).  I didn't order much except beer since I had already eaten dinner, but service was good and my friend enjoyed the fried oyster sandwich and the thanksgiving balls (my fave).

    A total of 6 of us visited the Bling Pig last weekend and it was fantastic.  They reserved us the "Gordon Ramsey" table!  The service was impeccable, and it was like the staff were our family members.  Really respectable yet laid back.  It's the perfect atmosphere to just kick back a few quality beers and munch on "balls" and poutine.  Their mussels were great and I am in love with their fries.

    The canned beers come with little plastic handles, which helps in keeping your beer colder longer (since your hands aren't wrapped around them).  

    Oh! Before I forget, the chef's own BACON JAM was awesome. It came with a cheese plate, and while cheese normally is the star of this plate, we were sucking down that bacon jam.

    Sometimes the bar has a stash of local beef jerky (like gourmet slim jims).  They are awesome... and sometimes they have the spicy one.  OMG.  

    If you haven't been there yet, you have to check it out.  I mean... Gordon Ramsey came from London to eat there (and also to film).  So should you.

    My first visit to the Blind Pig was only a week or so into their existence, and I wanted to give them another shot.  There have definitely been improvements, but it's still not the revelation I was hoping for.

    Our server was friendly and very pleasant, and the drink special we got, mostly Root liquor and ginger beer, was fantastic (repeatedly!).

    - The Poutine: Pure heaven in a giant, giant bowl.  I will be happy to eat this again, and likely again and again after that.  Delicious fries, crispy and perfectly salty, an excellent fry-to-gravy ratio... just an excellent poutine.

    - Bleu Balls: Way too much mashed potato, all of the other flavors were completely drowned out.  There was a hint of bleu cheese and a totally reasonable amount of short rib... but the mashed just overwhelmed everything.  Maybe if the bleu had been mixed into the potato this would have been more what I was expecting, but it was just bland with the lack of cheese and ton of mashed.

    - Lamb Sliders: These were absolutely good.  The small sliders keep the meat from getting dry, the onions and pickle gave it some pep, and the little brioche was both cute and tasty.  I just don't think I can go so far as to call them 'great.'

    But the Blind Pig has a lot going for it.  It's in a great spot, it's got a great name and ambiance, the waitstaff has been really nice, and some of their dishes have been delicious (poutine!).

    I am so sorry to have to give this place two stars.  You name a place The Blind Pig, you give it an… I am so sorry to have to give this place two stars.  You name a place The Blind Pig, you give it an awesome logo, you open it right near where I live... I was beyond enthused.  But despite the build-up, The Blind Pig just did not deliver.

    So let's start with what was awesome, which happens to also work out chronologically.  The place has great ambiance, it's warm and cozy inside and has a certain comfort to it.  The menus are cleverly done as manila folders (which led to lots of "let me check my files" jokes... which worked better out loud).  The beer list was great, not standard even for NoLi, and the beer-can-handles were fun and different.  

    The Good:  The Thanksgiving Balls... huge huge success, and I'm not even the biggest Thanksgiving fan.  They're the size of tennis balls, they're fried perfectly (which to me means a thick and crunchy crust), the inside is filled with turkey and mashed potatoes and stuffing which just work perfectly together with just the right amount of each.  You get two dipping sauces, one of gravy (which was light in the right ways) and one of cranberry sauce (which was spot-on and not too tart).  I could have eaten a lot of these, and I'm thinking of just coming here for Thanksgiving and ordering 50.

    I also heard the lamb sliders were delicious, but my buddy chomped them all down before I could get a bite.  I think that's a pretty good review of those!

    The Moderate:  But sadly that's where the good times ended.  The oyster sandwich was passable, with the oyster flavor pretty much nonexistent, hidden completely by the deep fry, the tomato, bacon and mustard.  The BBQ pulled pork sandwich was... ok.  I'm not putting it down too hard, it tasted fine, the pork was tender and well-cooked, even if the barbecue sauce was way too subtle... but at a place called The Blind Pig, I was expecting this to be explosively good, and it was just ok.

    The Bad:  The biggest disappointments were the mussels and the mac & cheese.  The mussels were very fishy tasting, which is disconcerting as fishy mussels don't tend to be fresh.  Then the mac & cheese... I had such high hopes, but there's a flavor in there that just soured me on it immediately, a kind of acidity that tasted like vinegar... it just didn't taste good and then left a bad taste in my mouth after each bite.

    The Blind Pig is brand new, and I don't think it's a bad place.  It's got a great idea, and they can iron out the issues with the dishes above.  I will absolutely be back to try some beers, the poutine and the house-made-chorizo quesadilla.  I just hope they live up to the Thanksgiving Balls!     After months of attempting to convince my fiance to check this place out with me, we finally made it out with another couple for a double date of sorts a few Fridays ago. I'd heard many great things from different people in my life - from how amazing the Thanksgiving Balls are to their great, extensive canned beer selection. It's so close to home it's amazing we hadn't made it over sooner.

    While I'm very eager to try The Blind Pig again, I think we all left a little disappointed. Our waitress was informative and attentive (while seemingly working the entire room!), but the food was just average. A very interesting menu yes, but not one of us was WOW'ed by anything we tried. Maybe we caught the chef on an off night, perhaps? The burger I had was cooked as I asked, and was no doubt a good, satisfying sandwich, but wasn't particularly memorable. The T-day balls, which our group shared, were good, but not bursting with as much flavor as I'd hoped. They did have their "Blue Balls" (pulled pork with blue cheese) on special but we didn't try them, I wish we had. I should mention the food came out very fast!

    However, I walk by the spot almost nightly and they never seem to be very busy. I almost feel as if its the actual location responsible for this as the 3-4 restaurants to occupy this address prior to BP all opened and closed in a short amount of time. The space itself is cozy, the decor is nice and all staff we dealt with we liked, and as mentioned the menu is creative so I will make a point to stop back in. Seems like a great place to sit at the bar alone and have a beer and a sandwich.

    While we don't need another gastro-pub in the neighborhood (my first thought upon hearing of BP's opening), something about it makes me want to give it another try. And I will.... if only for some of those blue balls :)

    Our Order:
    1 Blue Ball
    1 Lamb Sliders
    1 Cubano
    1 Canned White Rascal Beer
    1 Twisted Ice Tea Beer

    Everything was really solid and fairly tasty. Unable to channel our inner holiday spirit, we opted to try the blue balls over the Thanksgiving balls. These blue balls were handcrafted with short-ribs and gorgonzola cheese and lavishly surrounded by mashed potatoes, breaded and deep fried. Our socks weren't knocked off but we thoroughly enjoyed these delicious balls. The Cubano wasn't the best we ever had but certainly wasn't the worst. Meanwhile, lamb sliders were well cooked and packed a nice flavor. I would recommend it if you're undecided between this and the regular burger.

    We often complain of the horrible service that ruined our experience when eating out. But all too frequent we omit a compliment or two of a place for their professional and friendly attitudes.. So yeah, Bling Pig's service was excellent.

    Final thought: Go out of your way to make the trip here. You won't be disappointed, and your wallet won't be either. 4 stars.
