    I enjoyed Doma.  It was clean, friendly, and BYOB (without a corking fee).

    I came to Doma with my wife and two of our friends on recommendations from Yelp.  While our meal was good, it wasn't great - and it couldn't compare to other sushi restaurants we've tried (Ooka, Kumo).

    The service was good, but a bit slow.  It took too long to get our orders and even longer to get the check.  The staff came by to refill waters and tea but once the place filled up they seemed overwhelmed.  They were very friendly, however, and spoke great English.

    The tea was disappointing.  It was too bland and had no uniqueness to it.  The food was the best part of the experience (and most important).  We ordered the edamame and the agetofu for appetizers.  I was a big fan of the agetofu but my wife seemed to care less.  The edamame was "meh;" it was like any other.  I really appreciated the creative Korean dishes added to the menu.  While I did not personally try any, my friend had the Korean Tacos and enjoyed them.  Having Korean at a sushi restaurant was a neat addition.  The hand rolls were disappointing to me because Doma does not offer brown rice, only white.  I have never been to a sushi restaurant that did not offer brown rice.  I still find it strange.

    All in all, I still give Doma 3 stars because I enjoyed the food enough to go back.  The BYOB is a big plus and makes it a restaurant worth going to for dates or out with friends.

    Seems every city I travel to on biz or pleasure, the sushi restaurants have sold their souls to attract the millennials and hipsters with thumping house music and foo-foo exotic $20.00 cocktails.

    Bless the proprietors of Doma for creating a dining experience where the food is the feature, but where the ambiance is understated chic without all the pretense.

    Dining with our party of 4 on Labor Day weekend, we thought we'd have a wicked long wait. Happily, there was one table left and we were seated immediately by a very affable and friendly host.

    The ambiance: It's very white, very minimalist with some pretty cool lighting. What struck me rather funny were the chairs... all clear lucite. I thought they would be murder on the tush, but they were very comfortable.

    Service: Sushi restaurants, save for high end joints like Nobu are notorious for sup par service and marginally friendly serving staff. The service at Doma was very attentive, but not pushy. When asked about choosing between various appetizers, he was very passionate about his favorites along with the apps the regular diners tend to favor.

    The Food: I am a sushi snob. There, I said it. I love sushi, and probably hit 2 sushi joints a week on average. All too often the offerings are uninspiring and rather insipid, not unlike most Americanized Chinese takeout. Here at Doma, the food was in some cases daring, and even borderline radical. I like that.

    Appetizers: We opted to share 3 apps for the table: "Jumbo Shrimp Shumai" - a Japanese staple. Most shumai tends to be small, pasty balls with minced shrimp. not here. The dumplings were large, fluffy and filled with actually jumbo shrimp with an unusual, but very tasty dipping sauce. Next, and by far the best, was a very unusual app called the "Tuna Flatbread" - A remarkable blend of razor thin sliced tuna, a dab of guacamole, a thin slice of real jalapeno ( not that pickled garbage you get a chipotle ) red onion, topped with a sweet glaze AND spicy mayo on top of an ultra thin tortilla. The mix of textures and tastes was incredible. One of the best sushi apps I've ever had. Last, in the timely and literal sense, were the fried oysters. I love love LOVE oysters.... these oysters were god awful. So deep fried that any resemblance to what the oyster used to be was long gone. On the bright side, we were able to use them to stabalize the slight wobble on our table by putting them under the base. Lesson Learned: No fried oysters at sushi restaurants.

    Main Courses / Sushi:

    Being communal eaters, we ordered 2 rolls and a lot of sushi to share. The rolls were epic. The Ooki Eel was great. Eel, avocado, cream cheese, lightly battered with spicy tuna, tempura crunch, scallion and tobiko with a sweet glaze. The best roll was not exactly a roll... but a daring attempt at mixing up the ordinary. The Naked Salmon Roll... was not really a roll. It was spicy salmon tartar and avocado on a square of sticky rice with masago and a thin jalapeno slice on top. This was beyond epic... the perfect mix. It was like sushi crack....  The other sushi we ordered on an individual basis, ikura ( with quail egg ) white tuna and some other staples were all fresh and well portioned.

    Dessert: Are you kidding? No room...  stick a fork in us, we're done. May be I'll get to dessert next time.

    Cost Factor: 4 of us at and drank ( And drank well ) for under $180.00. Everything here is very fairly priced.

    Conclusion: One of the better sushi restaurants I've been to throughout the US. Only reason they didn't get that 5th star from me was that horrific fried oyster fiasco. I try to be fair, but that definitely knocked them down a notch.

    Other than that, chow away!

    -peace-

    GW
