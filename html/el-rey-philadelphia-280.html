    So? Go here on a weeknight, unless you routinely answer your cell phone by saying "What's going on, bro?"

    In a town where new restaurants are opening and subsequently closing every month, El Rey is probably old news. Add to this, Starr's designers did little to update the former Midtown IV dive-diner. What they did do, which includes vintage Mexican movie posters, embroidered "El Rey" booth seat backs, and vintage mismatched thrift store lamps and pendants, gives you the sense that you are entering a long established family owned Mexican restaurant that could possibly be found on the Baja Peninsula of California. The restaurant's interior is pure kitch: stone grotto walls, rec room style faux wood paneling, original terrazzo floors and circa 1950 patterned Formica. This is all charming, until you remember that this is like visiting "Mexico" in Epcot Center.

    Where this restaurant excels is where Starr excels; finding chef talent who really knows authentic Mexican food and then developing consistency of food and drink product. Consistency in general. The tortillas and chips are handmade, the sauces authentic, and the dishes are delicious and reasonably priced. This is Mexican comfort food abuela style. Where else in Rittenhouse Square area can you have authentic food at a fair price and an experience that lacks pretension otherwise known as Rittenhouse attitude?

    This is the kind of place, where I'd like to go after a night of bar hopping to scarf down carb ladden, fried, smothered cheesy goodness in an attempt to keep the world from spinning too quickly. Please Starr, if you do anything, figure out a way to keep this place open past 2 a.m., and extend the 90 min Happy Hour to the standard 2 hr time slot.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
