    Ok, let me take a stab at Yakitori Boy/Japas. I like Yakitori Boy but I hate Japas. Since they didn't separate this establishment into two businesses, I have no choice but review them as one business and give it the lower average score.

    Philadelphia lacks izakayas and it's definitely business savvy for someone to fill the space. The thing is though that traditional izakayas are pretty inexpensive since the items they sell are usually cheap to make but since the concept of having an izakaya here in Philly is still exotic and they pretty much have a monopoly, we as consumers are paying primo for it. It's fine. I do enjoy having a huge menu of dishes to choose from and the food in general is pretty good, so I don't particularly mind.

    I generally get pretty good service accept on Tuesdays when they have the $1 yakitori and beer deal, the place would be packed wall-to-wall, then I know the attention span of the wait staff might suffer. One of the friendly waitress told me that the sushi chef has 30+ years of experience and the sushi is good. Again, the price might be a bit high for various items, so usually I do go on Tuesday.

    I'm sure we all have our favorite izakaya items but If you are a beginner, I recommend trying okonomiyaki, takoyakis, get a small bowl of udon/soba, and try the myriad yakitori according to your comfortable levels. Any chicken, beef, pork is good. I also like shishamo and chicken gizzard. Eat to your heart's content and be merry. 4 stars (And here's where my review of Yakitori Boy ends and my review for Japas begins)

    In the same establishment there's a karaoke bar upstairs named Japas where there is about a dozen of karaoke room of various sizes and a medium size open karaoke bar. You can other all the food items from Yakitori Boy and eat at the tables in the open area or in the rooms.

    Being the only decent karaoke bar in town, you can never get a room without booking hours or days ahead of time during weekends. But I'm not going to complain about their good fortune. What I dislike it's their asinine rules. You cannot seat at one of the table at the open bar unless you guarantee that you'll spend a certain amount of money there per hour. It's like a strip club, for a table of 4, you'd have to guarantee you'll be spending $60 an hour on food and drinks. It's easy to spend that amount when everything is overpriced but that's not it.

    A friend invited me to go to a gathering he's throwing at the karaoke bar. There's approximately 12 of us and some of us got there early,  so we sat at the bar, ordered a lot of food and drinks, and when the rest of the party comes, the waitress informed us that the food and drinks we ordered at the bar cannot be counted toward the guideline of the table because we didn't order that at the table. We ended up spending way more than their requirements, but I certainly didn't feel welcomed so I didn't have any fun. Customer service is non-existent when your guest feel like their constantly being monitored.

    Drink-wise is way more over-priced than the food was at Yakitori boy or any place else I've been. 12 of us spent around $1000. It's a tourist trap with unfriendly rules. I can't in good conscience recommend it to anyone, unless you have money to burn. 1 star
