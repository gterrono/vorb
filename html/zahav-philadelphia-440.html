    Listed in Philadelphia, je t'aime (Or best of the best), Old City/Society Hill Hangouts, Little candle on the table

    Five stars just doesn't cut it.

    I visited Zahav for the first time on a Friday night, and the overall dining experience was completely exceptional from start to finish. The restaurant is small and intimate with large windows, clean decor, and a decidedly non-fussy vibe.

    The service was completely professional and on-point the whole night, and everything was timed beautifully to create a slow pace that allowed you to fully enjoy each course before the next one arrived. Our server was attentive and did a wonderful job of explaining the menu to first-timers. Each dish was presented to the table and thoroughly explained, and every single person who we interacted with obviously knew the menu to a T. Our water glasses were being refilled in such a careful and non-obtrusive way, and when I left the table I came back to find that my napkin had been folded for me. This is something that you do not get just anywhere.

    Both my boyfriend and I started off with a drink. He got the Rumble (this is a pink drink, my male friends, be warned) and I ordered the the Desert Rose, which seemed to be very popular with the ladies. Both were strong and delicious. We both opted for the sampler ($39), which takes you through the shared salatim (eight salads), hummus and laffa, two mezze (small plates), one al ha'esh (entree), and one dessert.

    We decided to share everything and opted for the Turkish hummus, which was exceptional. Our mezze choices were the fried cauliflower, sweetbread schnitzel, stuffed eggplant, and baked kibbe. The fried cauliflower was a must and the baked kibbe was a surprising standout. Our al ha'esh selections were Moroccan chicken and hanger steak, and while my hanger steak was served with pine nuts and absolutely delicious the Moroccan chicken was also wonderfully seasoned and got major points for being served with a fried egg. Our desserts were rugelach and kataifi, and while the rugelach was warm and delicious the kataifi was wonderfully rich and an obvious favorite.

    All I can say is that I was actually smiling throughout this meal. Everything was unbelievably delicious and I was introduced to both new tastes and unexpected combinations. The final bill was more than I usually spend on a normal meal for two, but we were there for a well-paced two hours (not waiting, but actually at a table) and everything was so exceptional that I felt glad to pay for that experience.

    Hm.. let's see if I can think of something that hasn't already been said. (Though, as an Israeli, perhaps my judgment is more relevant in certain areas.)
    Went with a friend on a Saturday night, 10pm reservation, were seated at 10:15ish. We only felt the tiiiiniest bit rushed towards the end, but with no real detriment to our meal experience, and the build to the incredible finish was worth it...
    We shared the tayim tasting menu.
    The Laffa was served warm and fresh, and though thinner and smaller than you might find in the shook in the Middle East, was respectable, with a light zaatar coating. We got the standard hummus spread with garlic, lemon, olive oil, pine nuts, cumin, etc. It was good, typical hummus presentation.
    I was a bit tired from traveling earlier that night so I zoned out a bit for the salat explanation but it was a nice selection of fairly standard colorful Israeli salads... some eggplant, some beets, etc. etc... presented nicely, all fresh, crisp, flavorful, and fairly authentic.
    For the 4 shared mezze plates we had:
    haloumi, fried cauliflower, stuffed eggplant, and baked kibbe (I think it had lamb instead of chicken for some reason?)
    We were sad that the Haloumi wasn't paired with date paste, as we had anticipated from other reviews, and the waiter confirmed that this pea/ramps "Spring" version was inferior to it's sweeter predecessor. The cheese, however, was delicious. Warm, crisp,  flavorful, and though savory, the dish was great.
    The fried cauliflower was not as magical as I expected, but was still delicious. The labaneh might have been better on the side, as it quickly warmed and seeped into the hot cauliflower and I might've preferred to dip it on my own.
    Stuffed eggplant was wonderful, exactly like my mom's at home, very flavorful.
    Something about the kibbe didn't sit well with me, maybe it was the texture?
    For the al ha'esh dishes we ordered the hangar steak and the kofte. The hangar steak was presented nicely and the eggplant preparations were delicious.
    Maybe I was too full at this point, or maybe the kofte really was a bit dried out (it was almost 11pm at this point...) but I didn't eat much of it.

    Blah blah blah.. onto the best part.
    THE DESSERT WAS INCREDIBLE.
    I can't write this part without salivating. Pathetic.
    The kataifi was rich, creamy, and chocolatey, with just the right crunch and grainy texture for the coating. The slightly tart labaneh ice cream was perfectly complementary to the deep Valrhona flavors, and the mango was a masterful addition.  

    The roasted apricots with homemade turkish delight and apricot sorbet was magical. MAGICAL. So tangy, fresh, so many textures and flavors. Oh man. It was simple yet inspired and inventive.

    I would go back JUST for the apricots. But, aside from that, my previously uninitiated friend was impressed by my native cuisine, and though she did say she might've enjoyed a bit more "kick" (she loves spicy food), she enjoyed the colors, flavors, and presentation of this modern take on Israeli cuisine.

    Listed in Best Places to Eat - Philly, Philadelphia Date Night

    Located just steps from the Society Hill Towers, Zahav is a fantastic Israel-Mediterranean restaurant that sets the bar for fine dining in Philadelphia.  Not only did all my friends who ate there love it, but it lived up to all of my expectations.  A recent pick for a date night with the BF, the atmosphere was warm and inviting, with great views of the surrounding neighborhood.

    The menu is a little overwhelming at first as it's served "tapas style" with many little plates, but I highly recommend ordering the Tayim tasting menu, which allows you to try their amazing selection of salads (salatim), hummus, and house-baked laffa bread, along with two mezze, one grilled meat plate, and dessert (so if you're dining with a partner, you get to try a large chunk of the menu).  The hummus and salads tasted so fresh and perfect, a great way to start off the meal.  For the mezze, we tried the fried cauliflower, kibbe naya (their take on a lamb tartare), crispy halloumi cheese (my favorite dish of the night), and the stuffed grape leaves.  I'm not one for stuffed grape leaves personally, but the way they created it with the ground veal and raisins made it taste that much better and more elegant than standard Greek fare.  Our main plates, the duck kebabs and beef cheeks, were also cooked to perfection.  And dessert, which we barely had room for, was also incredible: sweet enough to signify the end of the meal, but not too sweet.  I really enjoyed the warm valrhona chocolate inside the crisp Kataifi, as well as the homemade Turkish Delight in the Roasted Apricot dessert.

    The service was impeccable and was able to not only make good recommendations, but provide an easy way to read and decipher the menu. Zahav also has an expansive and terrific cocktail list, and the two of us ended up splitting a pitcher of their signature cocktail, the Lemonnana (essentially, lemonade blended with herbs and bourbon).  Overall, I greatly enjoyed my experience here and it truly deserves to be among one of the best restaurants in the city.  Definitely worth it for a special occasion or for elevated Middle Eastern cuisine.

    Listed in My Kind of Town, Philadelphia

    Great, unique restaurant with excellent service.  It had been on our list of restaurants to get to for a long time.

    After scanning the menu in the well decorated, bustling dining room we decided to go for the sampling oriented Tayim prix fixe since it seemed like the easiest way to try a bunch of dishes.

    Wine list is really nice.  The more Bekaa Valley wine that finds its way into Philly restaurants the better.  From the few opportunities I've had, it's been an unadulterated, fantastic product.  My glass here was no exception, and the wine list has a solid number of Lebanese options.

    Salatim and Hummus with Laffa was very good.  Maybe eight or so different creations accompanied by their delicious hummus and flat bread.

    Fried cauliflower with labneh, mint, garlic, and chive was one of the best dishes we had.  So savory, the tips were literally bursting with flavor when you bit in.

    Crispy haloumi was interesting, but not on the same level as our other dishes up to that point.  It's a lightly fried cheese with a consistency I'd liken most to string cheese, accompanied by english peas, pine nuts, and tendrils.   Worth trying at least.

    Hanger steak with eggplant two ways, sesame, and pine nuts.  This is why I love middle eastern food, they know how to do red meat.  Fantastic.  Layered, complex flavor from a nicely cooked steak.

    Fish, on the other hand, did not work out so well.  Crispy branzino was somewhat mediocre and too salty.  The lone blemish on an otherwise excellent meal that finished up with some delicious desserts.

    In the end we kind of regretted not taking the plunge for the Mesibah prix fixe with its phenomenal sounding lamb shoulder instead.  Oh well, there's always next time.

    -The rest of the food that we got was all 4 stars some things maybe 3 1/2. While being tasty, non of them were "Holy MOLY!!! Best things I have ever had!!!"
