    This one is easy - Sabrina's a must when in Philly. With a spectacular atmosphere, great food, and a helpful and attentive staff it's always worth the long-ish wait.

    I've had a number of dishes here - a Mexican egg special with black bean grits, lox and onion omelet, spinach and egg sandwich, and even a taste of the stuffed challah French toast.     Everything I've eaten here thus far has featured eggs. And that's okay with me. Breakfast of eggs "homies" (read: homefries, or fried potato chunks) and toast, and then a few weeks later, lunch of huevos rancheros. I've been lucky enough to enjoy Sabrina's on weekdays at off times (so no lunch rush and breakfast crowds to battle) and have never experienced even a moment of wait to be seated.

    I will say, however, that it took a surprisingly long amount of time for my breakfast foods to arrive on my first visit. The second trip, for lunch, took an equal amount of time, but I was with a larger party and there were more complicated orders. Breakfast, though, was scrambled eggs, toast, and potatoes. Not too tough, right?

    They've got an abundance of seating, different little nooks and crannies of the warm and inviting space, so each experience can feel a little different. I LOVE the gorgeous, interesting drawings on the chalkboard (drawn by an employee! Not to be missed) and the tiny Frank's hot sauces, as well as the regular outpouring of ketchup on demand. The flavors of the huevos were AMAZING, lots of crunch and spice and some lime sour cream (AMAZING!!) topped with a charred jalapeno....  wow.

    That said, the other foods (potatoes and the scrambled eggs) were pretty standard. Large portions, but I would try and order something with wow factor, don't waste your time on the basic options.

    Almost every time I'm in Philly, I go to Sabrina's with my boyfriend. They're famous for their stuffed challah french toast, and for good reason (see picture).     Knowing how Philly brunchin' is on a Sunday..i.e. wicked crowded at most spots, the bf called at 9am to check the wait.  After we were ready to head out around 10:30am we called again and put our name down and told them we'd be there at 10:45am for a party of 4.  Hostess that answered the phone said that when we got there, we can come to the hostess stand to check-in and we could get seated almost right away.  The two friends that met up with us got there a bit earlier and checked us in.  
    They have a seating system that is based on the party size and not first come first serve.  Those in the hospitality industry know that there are pros and cons to this method and usually the cons of having very unhappy customers outweigh the pros of this system.  They also color coordinate where the waiting parties are located: inside or outside the restaurant.  Seems organized but NOPE!  Failed for us.  We were "Kevin Party of 4" waiting OUTSIDE.  What happened you ask?  
    "Kevin Party of 4" was called and ANOTHER Kevin with a party of 4 took our spot because they were waiting INSIDE!!!
    We even checked up on our reservation twice and the third time we did, they told us they sat another party thinking it was us.  FAIL.
    Waited about an hour before being seated.
    One hostess was nice to apologize so I shook it off but my other friend was pretty upset about it.

    -------------------------------------------

    FOOD:

    THE BAD:
    Flanders' Eggs Benediddely Florentinarino with Lox and Tomato ($1 extra for tomato)
    Not worth it.  Whatever that sauce was on top was definitely NOT Hollandaise.  It was straight up butter that kind of curdled with whatever else was mixed into it.
    Not enough lox for the price of the dish. One egg was poached, and one egg was overcooked. Overall nothing good to say about this and will not be ordering this again.

    THE GOOD:
    Huevos Rancheros:
    When all components of this dish are combined, it forms such a phenomenal hodgepodge of flavor!  I really don't have to say more than..it's a Mexican party in your mouth!  

    Barking Chihuahua:
    I cannot give a review of this based on tasting this myself.  I can only say that my friend ate the ENTIRE THING faster than I could ask for some to try!
    He basically said "hurry up and take a pic so I can devour this!"  
    That's how good it is.  'NUFF SAID.

    THE IN BETWEEN:
    Homer's "Mmm...Stuffed French Toast"  (Brunch Special)
    Waaaaaaay tooooo much going on here!!  My tastebuds tripped out on the sweetness of this dish.  Knowing this I would choose to get this without the pumpkin puree.  Or I would not get it stuffed and get it with the pumpkin puree (because I LOVE PUMPKIN)  The stuffing is super creamy and rich.  The toast itself could be the star of this dish but it gets lost with all the stuffing and toppings.  
    If you're a simple french toast person, go for just their challah french toast and add a topping if you wish.

    --------------------------------------------

    The table service made up for our initial experience and after going into a food coma, I've forgotten all about the latter.
    Definitely want to come back and try more off their extensive menu.  
    I love their creativity with naming their Brunch Specials.  Although the names change, some of the food under the specials stays the same.  They do change it up with the seasons and often, which makes it exciting to know there's always something new to try.
