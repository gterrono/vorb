    Sabrina's is a cute and crowded place for brunch on weekends. I arrived to put my name on the list for tables, and was told they couldn't seat me until my brunch companion arrived, since they were busy. Nothing about the meal really stood out as remarkable, though, which makes me wonder...

    Delicious and diner like, but please please share a plate if you go with company.  The portions are massive.  No, really really massive.
    This was our first breakfast in Philly during our 3 day trip.  It came recommended by a local friend and it was a great recommendation.  We were not quite sure what to expect of this place, but it was diner like (a much much nicer version of an Ihop if you must).  We were sat immediately and browsed the menu for a little while (there were too many great choices).  
    Our server was wonderfully pleasant and made sure we were taken care of during our entire visit.  I ended up ordering the Egg White Turkey Bacon Fritatta (OMG, SO DELICIOUS) and right up my alley.  It came with an option for toast/I ordered the whole wheat, but I unfortunately was unable to eat not one bite of it!  I had also told Dan (our server) that I also wanted a small portion of the french toast because I could not decide which entree to eat and I desperately wanted a taste of the French Toast.  He suggested that we order a half portion of French Toast which I jumped at!  The husband ordered a wrap (I believe it was called Yellow and Black something?) but it was basically an egg wrap with a side of home fries/potatoes.  The potatoes were fantastic, but I only had room to eat 2 bites of them.  
    My fritatta was wonderful, light, HUGE, full of yummy veggies and the husband ate his wrap so quickly he had to tell me afterwards that it was delicious.  Now, the HALF order of french toast was MASSIVE!!!  It was a normal person's full order of French Toast, but alas, we somehow ended up eating it (I had to not eat my potatoes or toast), but it was yummy.

    All in all, everything we had was wonderful and we are glad that we ate here.  (BTW this is the Sabrina's in Fairmount/Art Gallery area).  
    Dan was great and we were happy and full upon leaving.

Specialties

Based off the same love for food, quality ingredients, and good friends that flourished Sabrina's Cafe in the Italian Market; Sabrina's Cafe and Spencer's Too hopes to satisfy even the loneliest of tummy's with behemoth portions and decadent fare no matter what time of the day it is. You can take that to the bank.

Checkout our Breakfast, Brunch, and Dinner. And did you know that we Cater to?

History

Established in 2007.

Sabrina's Cafe was born out of Robert & Raquel DeAbreu's desire to create a comfortable, welcoming place that served comfortable, welcoming food but with a twist. In May 2001, the first Sabrina's Café opened and was appropriately named after the couples new baby, Sabrina Isabella. With the help of impassioned regulars, Sabrina's began to outgrow it's modest upbringings and a second location was added in 2007 named Sabrina's Cafe & Spencer's Too, after their son.
