    We were walking by on the sidewalk and the woman gave us samples of stuffed baked potato. It was very good, so we decided the three of us should split one (we had eaten earlier). We went in, and the man made us three potatoes and charged us about $3 each; then the woman gave us a cookie to try and it was super delicious; then she gave us two more. We wanted to try one of her cakes, so she gave us a giant slice and asked $2 for that. One us us later asked for water and they just gave us tiramisu (and water).

    Everything we ate was delicious. The place is run by this very friendly [Greek?] couple and their daughter. They make everything. They seem to still be figuring out prices, or they don't believe in fixed prices. He said for the potatoes they usually ask $5 or $6, but he'd charge $3 for us.

    The place is attractively decorated and has a nice atmosphere. They have a table on the sidewalk too.

    Besides potatoes, they have prepared salads, about 8 flavors of ice cream, coffee and tea, many beautiful deserts, and an eclectic cooler of drinks.

    Highly recommended: very friendly people, delicious food, great atmosphere, good prices.

    I live on South Street and always like to check out/support neighborhood businesses.
    The owners of this coffee/sandwich shop are two of the sweetest people I've ever met, and, after trying a few of their generous samples of  homemade baked good, I'm completely  hooked on their oatmeal raisin and chocolate chips cookies! Delicious, fresh-baked, and more than reasonable for what you get, they've now become my weekly guilty pleasure (sacrilege that it is, the oatmeal raisin ones rival my mom's secret family recipe)!
    There's also a large well air conditioned seating area in the front with floor to ceiling windows, so it's the perfect place to escape the heat and enjoy a li'l South Street people watching.

    I have mixed feelings about Evil Eye Cafe. It seemed like the perfect spot to stop for a quick bite while roaming around South Street the other day, so my friend and I decided to pop in for a quick drink/snack.

    The lady behind the counter was very friendly, and the food was homemade and authentic, for sure!

    However, I was a little uncertain about the freshness of everything that was covered in plastic wrap (and looked like it may have been sitting in the refrigerated cases for a few days). Some of the desserts like the tiramisu looked a little floppy; the berries had left stain marks on the cheesecake, etc. I went for the baklava, which was really delicious and seemed like it wouldn't have been potentially spoiled like the dairy desserts.

    The place is really cute, but it was also slow and empty for a happening Sunday on South Street. I hope that the nice owner here can continue to keep her business profitable while serving fresh and delicious meals and desserts.

    I love this place. We usually eat brunch/lunch about 2-3 times a week from here.

    Our standard lunch is a chicken salad sandwich and some red lentil soup (with a squeeze of lemon). You can't go wrong if you order this. The chicken salad has tiny chopped pieces of chicken, celery, and herbs...and its one of the best I've ever had. Red lentil soup is delicious, but also unique. Can't say I've had a soup like it before, but really like it.

    The Turkish coffee is a perfect mid-afternoon pick-me-up to help you get through the day. It's a tiny cup of coffee that packs a big punch and tastes WAY better than expresso. Turkish tea is also delicious and a bit more appropriate if you're sensitive to caffeine.

    Also...the woman running the counter is the sweetest lady of all time. It is EXACTLY like being in your 'third aunt, twice removed's house.' She is an absolute doll.
