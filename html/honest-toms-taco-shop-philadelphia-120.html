    Only been here once, but I'm confident that I'll be boosting my review to five stars after my next trip.

    I was originally wary of Honest Tom's because I'd recently moved from Boston, where a local Mexican joint was throwing down $2 tacos STUFFED with fresh ingredients (including guacamole, cilantro, sour cream, and much more) and made to order, while most other places are charging $4+ for a store bought tortilla filled with a couple ounces of chicken and MAYBE raw onions on top. Fearing Honest Tom's would fall into the latter category, I tentatively ordered dinner here with some family ready to be disappointed.

    Instead, we all left happily surprised and planning our next meal here. Tried the fish, chicken, and beef tacos ($6 for two tacos), as well as a pork burrito ($8) and chips/salsa/guac (I think $4 for all three). Hands down the best fish taco I've ever eaten, plus very respectable chicken and beef tacos. The burrito was a little expensive compared to the rest of the menu, but it is insanely large and well worth the price tag. Another great surprise? These people actually understand that a burrito shouldn't be all rice at one end, all beans in the middle, and all meat on the other. They actually mix everything evenly before rolling up your bundle of joy for maximum satisfaction. Guac was solid, salsa was quite tasty, and the chips were fine.

    Hope this dinner wasn't a fluke... gonna go back soon to make sure Honest Tom's is the real deal!

    Pro tip: Hit up Lil Pop Shop next door for some gourmet popsicles as dessert.

    Honest Tom's Taco Shop is one of the best places to go in University City for a quality burrito. I have ordered the sweet potato burrito from here 10+ times and it never fails to disppoint. The burrito itself can sometimes be somewhat bland, but the array of hot sauces available more than makes up for this. The ingredients are fresh and it is a nice alternative to the Chipotle on 39th and Walnut.

    That being said, the one downside is that the location remains inconvenient for the majority of students at UPenn. Honest Tom's used to offer delivery services but discontinued delivery earlier this year. If they restarted this service or at least partnered with a delivery service like GrubHub, Honest Tom's would be a much more viable food option for Penn students. Till then, Chipotle will be my go-to burrito spot on campus and Honest Tom's will remain the occasional treat.

    Been here twice now and Honest Tom's is the real deal.  I love these independent neighborhood… Been here twice now and Honest Tom's is the real deal.  I love these independent neighborhood taco/burrito joints.  They always seem to do it right compared to the places that mass produce their product.  

    Food (9.0/10) = Been here twice, and had the Steak and Yucca burrito twice.  For $8 you get this massive burrito packed with steak, yucca, rice, beans, cheese, salsa, guacamole, and sour cream.  I'm always hesitant about ordering "steak" in anything unless it's an actual steak dish, because of the risk that it comes overcooked and chewy.  The steak inside Honest Tom's burritos are juicy and tender enough for me.  Other meats you can have in their burrito include chicken, pork, beef, sweet potato, or just rice and beans.  They also have tacos with similar meat options as the burritos.  For $6, you get 2 tacos.  Another cool feature with this joint is how they carry real sodas.  The Mexican coke...the stuff not made with high fructose corn syrup.  They also have the tamarind soda.

    Ambiance (8.5/10) = cool, open kitchen, allowing you to watch the folks construct the masterpiece that you will soon devour.  Great outdoor seating available too.

    Service (8.0/10) = service with a smile.  Always a great plus to local businesses.

    Overall Opinion = A very special place in West Philly. Although I've only had the steak and yucca burritos, I'm willing to bet that the other fillings, and the tacos are heavyweight champions too.  Keep being honest with your customers, Tom...honestly freakin' delicious!     Read more

    Many cities in our vast country boast delicious Mexican fare especially the West coast and the Southwest. So imagine my surprise when I found unparalleled burritos and tacos hiding out in a small shop in West Philadelphia.

    I know - you're discouraged by the $8+ price tag for a burrito. Don't be, though...the sweet potato burrito provided me with three separate meals and could easily be shared with another person. I imagine the chicken, steak, or beef varieties would also stretch quite far. Even without the rice, the burritos are still likely to weigh in at about a pound. Unlike chain joints like Qdoba and Chipotle, Tom's flavors are really clean and distinguishable from one another. The ingredients haven't been sitting in metal pans on a steam table which means that their flavors don't muddle together.

    Speaking of sweet potato, it makes an excellent burrito filling when roasted to tender perfection and combined with fresh black beans, pico de gallo, cheese, sour cream, and guacamole. I hold the rice - too much starch in one tortilla if you ask me.

    The freshly prepared tortilla chips that they serve with pico or guac are out of this world - crisp and a bit salty without that cardboard texture that you often find in commercially prepared varieties. The guacamole is as fresh as it gets and the avocados are left to speak for themselves instead of being masked beneath too much garlic, onion, or jalapeno. Plan on digging in and finishing off the container in one day.

    Seating is sparse (one large table and counter seats), so I recommend grabbing your order to go, especially if the weather is nice and people are flocking to the shop. Spring water is on the house  via the water cooler or you can grab a Coke or Fanta in the old-fashioned bottle among other options. Tacos are also available and can be made with soft flour tortillas or the usual crunchy corn options. I hear that the breakfast options are equally delicious, especially if you overdid it at the bar the night before.

    If you're hankering for a taste South of the Border, skip the large, conveyor-belt operations and treat yourself to a meal worth splurging on.
