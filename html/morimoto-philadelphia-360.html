    Every time I visit Philly I am sure to pay Morimoto a visit, which is only 3 with 2 years in between each visit. It's probably time for me to go back again =)

    I love the architect and design of the restaurant, very modern and clean. It is also a reflection on the food as well. The dishes were light, tasty, fresh and flavorful. I can't say I remember everything I ate but nothing bad or even mildly dissatisfying stands out in mind. However, the best thing I possibly ate in my life thus far, (other than my mama's cooking), was the kobe beef bibimbap. The mixture of the vegetables, egg, raw kobe beef you cook on the sides of the sizzling pot and the sauces were just phenomenal. It was the best most awesomest foodgasm I've ever had, even just thinking about it sends shivers down my spine. You may not find it on the menu because it was the special that night so hopefully on my next visit the'll have it again.

    Good(s):

    Bad(s):
    Morimoto Soba Ale - Pretty meh for a $16 beer.
    Pork ramen - Too subtle for my palate, the only really developed flavor I got was the salt.  I used to eat ramen every day so I know good ramen when I've had it.
    Black cod miso - On the plus side it was almost perfectly cooked, maybe a little under.  But it was syrupy sweet (maybe I just don't like these Japanese fusion flavors?  But I love most Japanese foods...)  
    Cha-soba - Next time I feel like soba I'll buy the $4 package of green tea soba and the $5 bottle of soba dipping sauce at Hmart and it'll taste exactly the same.

    Sorry Masaharu, I'm sticking with Jose Garces for special occassions

    I was told by a coworkers that Morimoto was a must-have experience as someone who makes frequent trips to Philly. He wasn't kidding.

    I can't ever recall being in a sushi restaurant with a menu such as this one. From fish to beef to oysters and sashimi, Morimoto truly is an experience worth having.

    We started with the octopus carpaccio, which had a citrus sauce delicately spread across the dish and was very tender and tasty. We had a dozen oysters, 6 each from Washington State and British Columbia, respectively. They also had 3 different sauces, one being a jalapeno sauce, one being a thicker fig sauce, and the third having a strong and sweet citrus flavor.

    We then did the $55 sushi chef platter that offered everything from tuna and mackeral, to salmon and suzuki.

    Great space, food, and service. Definitely a place to bookmark in Philly if the price doesn't scare you away. $200 sans tip for 2 of us.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
