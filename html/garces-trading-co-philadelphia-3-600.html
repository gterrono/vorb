    garces somewhat recently changed their lunch menu! before i remembered they had those daily specials like carbonara pasta and steak frites on certain days of the week.  now those 2 choices are permanently on the lunch menu! this is good news for me who can't seem to enjoy the simplicity of just munching on a sandwich for lunch.. garces added several pasta choices meanwhile keeping most of the old menu of pizza and sandwiches.

    so on my most recent visit, i went big on a tues and got steak frites for lunch!  it was a good choice indeed! we started out with a green salad in light vinaigrette dressing that was included in our lunch.  then the star of the meal came! they offer 3 sauces, wine, butter or au poivre.  i chose the wine sauce which had a light sweet taste to it... super nom!! i had a terrible experience with the au poivre once which left me scarred.. however, i did try a bit of it since my dining buddy ordered it and surprisingly, i liked it a lot more! lol loved the spicy zing to it~
    i wish the steak was accompanied by a little bit more sauce~ but no matter! i picked up some truffle oil on the side and drenched it ALL over my steak lol it was soooo scrumptious!  i think that's def THE perk of dining at garces, you can get yourself a sample of their truffle oil and just put it on EVERYTHING you eat at the restaurant lol

    needless to say, that was definitely one of the nicer lunches i've had~ and VERY satisfying like holding-my-stomach-full by the end of the meal.  will def return when im feeling steak frites again!

    Excellent service, high-quality ingredients, nice ambiance.

    I'm a fan of their airy, somewhat minimalist interior. On my most recent visit we had two outstanding "salads" (lightly sauteed cauliflower served a la Waldorf, with something small and green and leafy; and endive with mandarin oranges, nuts, and something else yummy that was sort of like golden raisins).

    The bread was outstanding and the olive oil very high quality and flavorful. We were both amused that house policy is apparently that the wait staff needs to pour the oil out for you -- perhaps customers were confused as to why this bottle of olive oil was on the table?!

    Our entrees were good but not quite as good as the salads. I had branzino with some kind of pea sauce, which TBH was a distant second to a similar dish I had at the Oyster House recently. The skin was not well crisped and the pea sauce was very bland. The fingerling potatoes were pretty delish, though.

    For dessert we enjoyed a nice selection of cheeses and the bouchons (aka two very dense chocolate-chocolate-chip tiny cakes).

    Overall we were very happy. And did I mention the service was excellent? Never waited a moment for anything.

    GTC may have great food (get the burger if you're there for lunch!), but service is unexcusably slow for a restaurant with such a reputation and price. The setup of the place is pretty nice, with wooden tables and cocktail tables.

    Came for lunch and was seated pretty quickly since the restaurant wasn't even full. I am the slowest drinker in the world, and I finished 2 sodas before we got our first course. I got the Santa Maria EXPRESS, yet I counted about 30 minutes until we got our bread, 50 minutes until we got our appetizer, and 70 minutes until we got our entrees! I did notice all the servers were running about, so GTC: please hire a bartender so my waiter doesn't have to spend half their time making drinks! And while you're at it, bring on another server per shift! Or maybe it's the kitchen, who knows. Some "express" meal. Can't even imagine what a normal dinner would be like!

    My server didn't know the menu very well -- forgot to ask me a few things, like how to cook the burger and what drink I wanted with the Express deal. Luckily I read the menu carefully before coming, otherwise I may have missed something.

    Can't argue with the food though -- and thank goodness, or else this rating would've been in the dump. The potato leek soup was pretty solid and completely creamy (personally not something I love, but I could definitely could stomach for this yummy soup) with bits of ham and tiny potatoes in it. Luckily my waiter warned me that the flatbreads were pretty small, so I opted for the GTC burger and my boyfriend tried the Spaniard sandwich. His sandwich was decent, but oh my goodness.... that burger. So thick and juicy! I couldn't finish it after the drinks, soup, and chips. Their tomato conserva adds an interesting, sweeter twist on a classic burger.

    TL;DR: Don't come here if you're in a rush or even with a normal time frame. Do come here if you want a burger.

Specialties

Garces Trading Company is a full-service bar and restaurant focused on Iron Chef Jose Garces' own favorite dishes from his travels to cafes throughout Western Europe, with a focus on Italy, Spain and France.  Our thoughtful menu of cafe staples is served throughout the day, and our bar offers a wealth of cocktails, wines and beers that pair beautifully with our menu.  Our on-site PLCB Wine Boutique, the first of its kind, makes a great resource either before sitting down to dinner (corkage fees apply) or to complement one of our decadent gift baskets, available in our newly redesigned retail section.

History

Established in 2010.

Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a refined, Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, a contemporary American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago, IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale, AZ and Palm Springs, CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the landmark Revel Resort. Today, in addition to our much-lauded restaurants around the country, Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
