    One of my last meals on earth would be the moules dijon (w/ extra bread) and Beneluxx (insert trademark) Belgian Frietjes from Eulogy. I simply die!

    Moules - fresh and plump. Dijon sauce is dotted thick with mustard seeds with good tang yet balanced with cream. If you share, you'll fight over the rest of the sauce. If you eat the sauce all by yourself you could possibly end up with a tummy ache (in a happy way).

    Frietjes - best fries I've had in Philly! If you're into steak fries, you're into these.

    Do I need to mention the beer selection? As if you didn't already know. Milk Stout from Left Hand Brewing Co. and an interesting Chestnut brew from Italy that I've always been curious about trying (can't get over the $16 price tag).

    How did I end up here?  Well, on a beach trip to fire island, we were all discussing the best cheeseburger we've ever had.  Two people in the room, neither of whom lives within 100 miles of philadelphia, named eulogy.  If that didn't pique my interest enough, the words "Belgian tavern" strike a beer-soaked chord with me.

    So upon my return, I called up a friend in philly, and we picked a date for dinner.

    My burger-pilgrimage involved a two (or more?) hour trip on various trains, but eventually I arrived, met my friend, and off we went.  Possibly the longest I've ever travelled for such a simple food item.  It's in a nice neighborhood, and the place itself has a great vibe.  We were seated upstairs, and presented with a beautiful beer list containing a mix of rare old favorites and a few close friends, and most importantly, exciting new things to try.  So I started off happy.  After some beer and snacks, I ordered this cheeseburger, and I will say it was one of the better burgers I've had at any pub.  Juicy and delicious.  Doesn't quite match park tavern (see -that- review), but it's close enough that if you're in philly already I'd wager it's your best choice.

    So, five stars for a place to which I can make a two-plus-hour each way pilgrimage and be happy that I did.  And once again, my compliments on the tap list.

    Listed in Favorite Philly Spots

    Places like Eulogy probably undermine my credibility as a yelper since I cannot help but give establishments like this a five star-rating.

    My criteria for a five-star rating is actually quite simple:

    Does Eulogy offer something you can't get anywhere else?
    Yes, they do (Ardbeg-aged Embrasse; Harvest Ale Lagavulin/Calvados; A crap ton of "craft beer" and extensive rotating tap)

    Does Eulogy offer the best of something you've ever had?
    Yes, they do (Dijoun moules, and hands down the best Belgian Frites I've had in my life)

    Is the atmosphere pleasant enough to make you want to visit again?
    Absolutely (The last two times I was here, I was able to strike up a casual conversation about Islay whisky and different types of Geuze and Trappiste beer with some of the patrons and the bartender)

    For those that want a more objective review, there probably isn't a Belgian beer that isn't imported/available in these Continental United States that you cannot find between Eulogy and Monk's. Eulogy does get crowded almost every evening, and probably isn't very conducive for a simple meal, but there is always something new on tap that will keep you distracted from this fact if you're an avid drinker.

    Even if you're not a craft beer aficionado, the staff here is very friendly and knowledgeable and will help you get started on your beerventure times.

    Schol!

Specialties

This is Beer Heaven, give a "high praise" Eulogy for your favorite beer after you consume it.  We also prepare our food in the authentic Flemish Belgian Style as opposed to other Belgian restaurants who choose the French Walloon Belgian style.  This means our fries are thick enough to truly hold a double fry technique, our fries go "crunch, goo" because they are the right thickness, the wimpy skinny fries elsewhere go "goo goo goo" because they are too skinny and the double fry technique does not work.  Also our mussels are larger than the PEIs other restaurants use, this is because our mussels are the authentic North Sea size like in Belgium.  The more meat the more flavor the mussel can hold in our ala minute pan technique (all ingredients added fresh, never ever ever any pour over sauce on mussels).  Also our handburgers are award winning based on seven secret spices and our fish and chips and other entrees are as good as the beers ! Make a reservation at www.eulogybar.com

History

Established in 2002.

My family is from Brugge, Belgium, I am passionate about Belgian beer, food and heritage, this adds an authenticity to the business.  I got the building in 2002 and built the restaurant myself using antique stain glass behind the bar from an old Philadelphia church and a chandelier from an old church as well.  The bar was constructed in Fishtown in Philadelphia by a local carpenter using Honduras Mahogany I imported and the design of the bar was based on a blueprint I drew.  
We take our beer very very very serious, our beer starts in a beer cooler and is then delivered via dual gas blenders using co2 and nitrogen blends for the proper carbonation technique.  Most of our draft beer never travels more than six feet but along that journey there are glycol filled lines keeping the beer at 40 ish degrees until it hits your glass.  We have about 200 different glasses we keep in stock because most of the taste for beer is accomplished through your smelling it, yes your nose!
