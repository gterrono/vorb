    4/16/2012

    Went for brunch on a Saturday late afternoon.  We had a party of 6 and were seated immediately, no wait.  The space is very bright and nice; a French theme.  As you enter on the left is a little market specializing in cheese, charcuterie and olive oils.  On the right is the seating.  We were seated right away but water, drinks, ordering was pretty darn slow considering how many tables were empty.  We also never got bread to try the tempting bottle of olive oil at our table.  (Other tables had it so I assumed we would get some.)

    I order a bloody mary.  A bit steep at $9 considering the multitude of places in the city I can get a tasty bloody mary for under $5.  But it was well mixed and garnished nonetheless and packed the right little punch of spice.  We had considered a pitcher of sangria for $32 but were told it would only make 3-4 glasses.  Not a great bulk deal...  The other brunch drinks were a "carajillo" for $10 and a mimosa for $11.  They also had Pimm's Cup which I heard was delish.  Cheers to that.  Beers were more reasonably priced and primarily a selection from Europe, however they did have Stone IPA and Allagash White on tap at the time.

    I ordered the steak & eggs ($14).  The hangar steak was delish and cooked perfectly.  The bearnaise sauce was tasty.  The "eggs" was actually one poached egg.  It sort of disintegrated in the sauce, which was both a pro and a con for me.  The potatoes were a little undercooked.  I also tried the side of gin grilled bacon.  Nice grilled flavor, but very thick cut, more like a piece of ham.  A friend allowed me to taste the duck fat frites. Tasty!

    Ultimately, a good experience overall based on ambiance and food.  Reasonably priced but smaller portions.  One star comes away for cheerful but very slow service.

    Listed in Best Places to Eat - Philly, Philadelphia Date Night

    The setup: myself, the BF, and another couple hastily put together a last minute double date in Center City.  Barbuzzo demanded a two hour wait.  I'm in the taxi heading over to midtown village.  OpenTable shows a 9:30 pm table for four open at Garces Trading Company.  Perfect, because I love Jose Garces' food and not spending the kind of money that one drops at Amada or Chifa.  Also, lavender truffle honey.  Done.

    The four of us meet up and arrive at the restaurant, which looks like a deli that's been doubled in size to accommodate a makeshift dining room in the middle.  But the atmosphere kind of works given the setup.  Unlike his other restaurants, you feel like you're eating in the chef's personal pantry, with display cases of meat, cheese, and wine surrounding you as you dip your crusty bread into the incredibly light and crisp olive oil on the table.  The menu, which is mostly tapas style with a huge selection of meats and cheeses, overwhelmed us, but we ultimately decided to split a chef's selection of cheeses and a bottle of Montepulciano D'Abruzzo.  They ordered the steak frites with bernaise.  The BF opted for the Duck Salad Lyonnaise and the Spaghetti Vognole, while I went with the Vichyssoise Chaud and the Pappardelle with Lamb.

    The cheese plate was terrific.  We had no idea what to get so the chef put out a selection of three cheeses: one hard, one semi-hard, and one soft, with honey, jam, and butter.  So good, even if I don't remember exactly all the names of the cheeses.  My hot vichyssoise was easily my favorite dish of the night: a creamy potato and leek broth with bacon, scallops, and fried onions right there in the soup made it taste incredible and added some terrific layers to the dish.  By the time I got to my pasta, I was already pretty full, but I soldiered on with the lamb pappardelle.  The pasta was perfectly cooked and the lamb was very tender.  Overall, all the food that we had here was delicious and well executed.

    I'm still not entirely sure that this deserves a five star review; it wasn't PERFECT, but it was pretty damn close.  Excellent service, excellent food, and a price point that doesn't break the bank (although it is by no means "cheap", but more affordable than his other ventures).  Definitely worth it for a more casual version of an upscale dinner.

    We also had the duck frites, which is one of the heaviest things I've ever eaten. It's served with a heavy, creamy Bearnaise.     A turn down Locust from 15th st lead my cohort and I to a classy joint called Garces Trading Company (garcestradingcompany.com). After a quick google search on the handy Droid, we determined that it is a type of cheese...cream? Cream cheese?

I need to load up on iron so I can give blood (drexel.edu/giveblood) this week! My cohort, Phil, ordered a Scotch Egg with House Made Salami.

    First off: Let me tell you about Grilled Gin Bacon. Grilled Gin Bacon is legit bacon, except almost half an inch thick. Now, I'm a bacon worshipper. I was loving the candy of meats way before it was so lolrandom or splayed across merchandise at the Hot Topic. I can hands down say that this was some of the best bacon I have ever had the pleasure of putting in my mouth. It was like eating steak. It was like eating bacon. It was steacon. The actual steak was a hanger steak, which was often kept by butches because it's the most awesome and flavorful. Tthe steak was very tender and seasoned perfectly. It was medium rare and cold...so cold that I suspected it was meant to be cold. The egg was...interesting. The yolk was kind of gelatinous? It wasn't runny and it wasn't solid, but it was delicious over a dollop of roasted potato.

    Phil's Scotch Egg was perfect, definitely superior to the ones at the Maryland Renaissance Festival. The outer-fried layer was perfectly crunchy compared to the sausage and egg within, creating the perfect blend of textures. I'm pretty sure this egg made Phil see and taste Jesus, as he would stare wistfully off into space throughout the day while uttering "Man...that scotch egg..." The salami was a bit comedic, laid out in 8 paper-thin slices on a slab or rock and accompanied with a tiny thin trident to pick them up. If you stacked all of the slices together, they would probably equal two regular slices from any sausage-tray. This tasting sausage came with a gritty yellow mustard that I did not taste, but was assured was delicious.

    We also split a basket of the duck fat fries. The fries had a perfect amount of crunch on the outside to go with the flaky inside. There wasn't much seasoning to them, allowing you to taste the difference that duck fat has to whatever the normal greases are. Specialties

Garces Trading Company is a full-service bar and restaurant focused on Iron Chef Jose Garces' own favorite dishes from his travels to cafes throughout Western Europe, with a focus on Italy, Spain and France.  Our thoughtful menu of cafe staples is served throughout the day, and our bar offers a wealth of cocktails, wines and beers that pair beautifully with our menu.  Our on-site PLCB Wine Boutique, the first of its kind, makes a great resource either before sitting down to dinner (corkage fees apply) or to complement one of our decadent gift baskets, available in our newly redesigned retail section.

History

Established in 2010.

Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a refined, Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, a contemporary American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago, IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale, AZ and Palm Springs, CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the landmark Revel Resort. Today, in addition to our much-lauded restaurants around the country, Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
