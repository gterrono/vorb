    Govinda's and I go waaaay waaay back. In 2008, I was living in Chicago for almost a decade when I decided to get back in touch with my "skipping high school to hop a bus from Harrisburg to hang out on South Street" day trip enthusiast roots in Philly. I booked a fight, a hotel, and planned: hanging out in Philly as an adult with money from my big girl job was going to open up a whole new side of the city. Woo!

    But, first things were first: where to get a seriously good vegetarian cheesesteak? Not only did I feel inauthentic for growing up near the city and never trying one (kind of tough after voluntarily giving up meat at age 15), I was so sick of Midwesterners familiar with Philly's forever cheesesteak rivalry asking me about my favorite cheesesteak place and having NOTHING to say about it. If I could find a place that could sell me something similar and soy or wheat gluten-based, I could finally belong to this weird society of sloppy meat sandwich eaters, even if it was in a once-removed kind of way. I would have loved to respond to "Jim's or Gino's?!" with some obscure name, in true hipster fashion, as I was. Govinda's would do.

    And I think this was the first time I ever seriously used yelp to recommend me the best vegetarian cheesesteak in Philly. I searched for a little bit, but over and over, Govinda's was reported to be the best. So, on the list it went! I would later work this into some evening plans and experience one of the best vegetarian sandwiches of my career as a vegetarian.

    The thing I enjoy the most about Govinda's vegan cheesesteak is the balance of ingredients. It's just enough cheese, just enough mayo, and the "chicken" to bread ratio is great. The texture is there and the taste is decent for being a meat substitute product. Now that I live a few blocks away from Govinda's, I realize how diner-y the place is itself. When I was visiting and, a few years later, very, very new to Philly as a resident , I used to shyly order my food and go hide in the back dining room. Now that I'm over that awkward newb phase, I realize, that the front part has a really comfy seating area where, in most places, you can watch the magic happen on the grill. There's a pretty nice view of Broad and South from there , too.

    Have I tried anything else on the menu? No. I'm mostly interested in/have the budget for finding the perfect vegan cheesesteak in Philly, and I continue to return to Govinda's over and over again. It's been a fun hunt but, as of now, this is the place to go! I love having them live down my street, even if I'm just walking by watching college students bond over wheat gluten and soy. Sincerely. I heart Govinda's!

    Govinda's has been the bulwark for Philadelphia vegetarians for years now.  They are consistent and everything is delicious.  I've had about half the items on their menu and all are good, but anyone who's from Philly can tell you : cheesesteaks are king!

    My last visit was 2 days ago and it pretty much exemplifies the Govinda's experience.  I went for lunch and briefly waited as the worker at the register took some orders.  I got a vegetarian chicken cheese steak, though cheese-less with mayo on a spinach wrap, it's one of the best freakin' sandwiches in Philly.  The mayo is vegan and they do have vegan cheese for y'all vegans out there. I happened, on this last visit, to be introducing Govinda's to a visiting carnivore from California and he was shocked at how good the food is.  It's why I try and bring all meat-eaters I can there, to show them that mock meat can be awesome and satisfying.  I also got a samosa, which was pretty spicy but fresh and a fine counter to the creamier qualities of the wrap.

    They overcharge for drinks and have a pretty small selection, but they do carry Hank's sodas which is a great local soda outfit.  They have vegan desserts as well, but also sadly, quite overpriced.

    I take a star off for mainly mechanical reasons.  The lack of air conditioning in the eating area and how small and cramped the space is, is daunting.  I've bumped into way too many people and there really is no place to wait if there's a line, which there frequently is.  Also, they can be slow, they do operate in a small space, and if you call ahead that doesn't mean your food will be ready when you show up a half hour later.

    The food though, yo that's 5 stars.

    Consider Govinda's a lesson in how meat is a completely useless food group.  

    OK so a little backstory:  I was raised eating meat, but at about 14 years old (and after disecting a baby pig for school) I began a life-long journey into cruelty-free living.  Now, as a vegetarian for a really really long time, I've bought and made tons of over-priced meat substitutes of various permutations of tofu, seitan, tempeh, and textured vegetable protein.  I can safely say that I have NO IDEA what the people at Govinda's do to make their fake meat so freakin delicious.  Who knows, maybe they were blessed by the yogic gods due to their extreme non-violence practices and given the modern day version of manna to serve to well-faring Philadelphians.

    Oh. and did I mention that this is a Jain restaurant?  I don't have official confirmation of this, but if you look at the menu there is a marked lack of garlic and onions, and they use spring mix instead of whole lettuce heads.  Talk about ahimsa all the way!  So badass!

    Other than the fact that you'll probably have to wait for what feels like a hungry eternity to get any kind of food (I mean seriously, 15 minutes for samosa? I can see it right there and you don't even have to heat it up!), what you get in the end is totally worth the wait.  

    You have to try the classic chicken-cheesesteak (I like it in a wrap with tomato and spring mix) or the kofta sub (veggie meatballs with marinara).  And save room for their Vegan Treats cakes and cookies.  You really haven't lived until you've had a vegan whoopie pie and a slice of peanut butter bomb cake.

    In the United States, 7 billion chickens are killed per year.  Seven billion.  That's 19 million per day.  And people eat their carcasses.  So make the choice and go forth and eat, the non-violent way!

    I have yelped about this place for years, describing my on-again, off-again relationship with it.  For a period of time, the service was unbearable and the selection was limited. However, over the last year or so, Govinda's Veg has gotten its act together.  The employees are very helpful and eager to please. The food is a delicious assortment of naughty and nice. Consider, for example, the veggie salads or veggie sandwich followed by a Vegan Treat like The Bomb.  Not very few places offer this wonderful assortmnet of vegan and vegetarian foods.  Since I spend about $50 a week at this place, I thought I should give you my run down:  

    conveniently located in Center City Philadelphia on a major through-way; off street parking nearby almost always for free or a low kiosk price; great seating in the front for quick ingress and egress but lots of quieter spots in the back should you and a group want to unwind and actually taste the food you will devour. i have sat in the back eating one cookie treat and answering phone calls and emails.  Govinda's has the city's best assortment of vegan treats (brought to you by the now world famous Vegan Treats in Bethlehem), workers who are attentive and work as fast as they can to handle the crowds they attract, and moderate prices.
