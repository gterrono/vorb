    First to Review

    I love the Dmitri's in Bella Vista.  I have only lived in Philadelphia for less than a year, and while I constantly have delicious meals, I think of Dmitri's as my go-to, favorite restaurant.  So, when one opened up closer to Fishtown, I was thrilled.

    My parents came into town and we were going to an art opening in Northern Liberties for the earlier part of the evening.  I thought it was the perfect time to try the new Dmitri's.  And I was right.

    Unlike the Bella Vista location, we did not have to wait at all.  The menu was exactly the same and, yes I am confident, just as delicious.

    We order the hummus, the babaganoush, the greek salad with greens, and the sugar snap peas (as a group of 5, that was a lot to share for starters).  My mother and I couldn't decide between the grilled octopus or the grilled squid, so we shared them both as an entree.  100% the grilled squid was our favorite.  The octopus was fine, perhaps a little too dry.  But the squid is just out of this world.

    Remember it is BYOB and they do NOT ACCEPT CREDIT CARDS.  So glad it moved into the neighborhood.

    I think the original reviewer must've wound up going into Long John Silver's on Girard and mistaking it for Dmitri's (ok, thats obviously a joke, as the LJS closed a few years ago...)

    What to say about the newest Dmitris? Fresh seafood, prepared simply, but perfectly.  The recipes and delivery are similar to the other Dmitris, so who can complain. Everything is under a 20 spot, so the price is right. Last night, we went tapas-style and ordered a couple of small plates. The snap peas (wouldnt normally expect this at Greek place) with marinara and feta are delish, and the meatballs are rich goodness in a bite sized delivery. And the squid... well, it's fresh and prepared perfectly. You feel like you can eat an entire plate and not feel guilty, as it isn't overly greasy or salted.  

    If there is a knock, its that Dmitri's in the NoLibs can get loud and can get crowded. However, the staff is smart and will allow restaurant-goers to leave a cellphone number for a 'heads-up your table will be ready in ten minutes' call. And always interesting is the seating at the bar, which affords the diner the opportunity to watch the chefs do their magic. This place is definitely a welcome addition.

    I was strolling the 2nd Street festival today and noticed a long line in front of Dmitri's.  With time to kill before my friends arrived, I added myself to the queue without bothering to find out what was on the other end first.  Turns out it was hummus with parsley, paprika and olive oil, served with grilled pita.  And it was free.  Good marketing on their part, because while munching on my free and good (not Zahav good, but definitely better than your average) hummus I realized that 1: their menu is almost entirely inexpensive seafood, 2: this is the same Dmitri's as the other two locations (I'd never put two and two together) and I've been dying to try them anyway, and 3: I was craving octopus.  So a couple hours later, when my friend showed up at the festival and the skies unleashed some cold summer rain, we ducked into Dmitri's for some snacks and to wait out the rain.  We sat at the cool (and cramped!) open kitchen.  My friend ordered more hummus, I got my octopus.  I think the head chef was training a new guy, but they still did a good job grilling the octopus, though I might have removed it just a second or two sooner.  I LOVE me some seasoned grill marks on octopus, though, and this place had just the right flavor on the grill.  It comes in a very simple olive oil and red wine vinegar sauce with some parsley and salt, so tasty.  Some peppers and olives on the side.  And for $12 the portion is gigantic.  It's on the appetizer menu, but no reason to not have this as an entree.  Had some simple and yummy rice pudding for dessert, and a strong little Greek coffee.  

    My only (small) complaint is the service... it wasn't unfriendly, but ever-so awkward and rushed.  They asked if we'd like anything else as I was finishing my dish.  I was taken off-guard, so I probably said we were fine.  A few moments later they dropped the check.  Then I said we might like some dessert, and she was almost off again before I managed to squeak out I wanted coffee, too.  Then they cleared my plate as I was finishing my olives!  Only minor complaints, though, the rest of the service was smooth.  I definitely enjoyed my food and watching others' food prepared in the open kitchen, and I'll definitely be back.  Good job with the free hummus table at the 2nd St festival, Dmitri's.

    Listed in Philly BYOBs, Greek/Mediterranean

    Went to Dmitri's this Saturday for Valentine's day dinner. I love seafood and I love BYO, so when I checked out the menu, I thought Dmitri's sounded like a perfect fit. The restaurant is on the corner of 2nd street with nice big windows that I'm sure are opened in the warmer months, where people can sit outside and have lunch or dinner. There's a bar set up around where the chefs are cooking if you choose to watch them prepare the dishes.
    We sat at a table and ordered the hummus which came with a heaping pile of pita. Along with the pita came a piece of bread and olive oil which had a mixture of garlic and something else that was really good! The amount of hummus they gave us was enough to feed a family of five so we weren't able to even attempt to finish it or the pita bread. The waitress was nice enough to wrap the hummus up for us and throw in an entire container of fresh pita to take with us.
    For dinner I had the steamed mussels. They were topped with stewed tomatoes (I think), garlic and escarole. I've never really had escarole outside of escarole soup, but I really liked it steamed with the mussels. My boyfriend had the full grilled fish of the day which was bass. It came with a  side of  escarole and roasted potatoes. The potatoes were delish! My favorite part of the whole meal was the desert though. My date had the rice pudding which he loves and I had the chocolate pot, which was a creamy chocolate mousse in an individual-sized ramekin, which was the perfect amount of sweetness to end the meal. After all of that, our bill came to about $56 before tip. I would recommend coming here if you enjoy grilled fish and don't want to break the bank on dinner.

    i tried dmitri's for a second time last nite and the food was every bit as fresh as i remembered it. our table of 4 started with 2 appetizers: the mediterranean plate and the sizzled cheese. the mediterranean plate included an ample amount of lentils, hummus, baba ganoush, tzatziki cumcumbers and beets (as an aside, i've ordered the beets on their own and would probably get an additional order next time). a basket of warm pita bread and some dipping oil accompanied the plate and at $15, it was more than enough for the whole table and presented a great variety for every kind of eater. we also couldn't resist ordering the sizzled cheese, a stringy but not runny cheese that was drizzled with olive oil and fresh rosemary. melty on the inside and crusty on the outside, it was delicious. aside from the mediterranean plate, most of their apps, salads and sides are between $7 and $9.

    as i did in my first visit, i ordered the grilled squid which came out fresh, and cooked just right so you could get some char from the grilling, but without a hint of chewiness that comes with overcooked squid. it also came with a suprisingly spicy tomato sauce, but that's right up my alley. they have plenty of grilled or pan-fried seafood options, and the rest of our party ordered grilled salmon, grilled scallops, and the shrimp pil pil (fried shrimp in a tasty and not too spicy crushed red pepper sauce).

    the one change from my first visit to this one is that the "main dishes" did not come with a side of escarole (by "main dishes", i mean the seafood we ordered, the entire menu is a la carte). it's as shame too, b/c the escarole is delicious, with just enough garlic to get it's taste without overpowering the greens. to make up for this, we ordered a side of escarole for the table, and at $7.50, it fed all of us with some to spare.

    2 bottles of wine and $100 (including tip) between the 4 of us, we all left full and very satisfied. the food here is exactly what you would expect from greek food...simple flavors with a lot of freshness. the fact that it's a BYOB is a bonus. i would definitely go back again and look forward to my next meal there.
