    I don't eat out for breakfast a lot and when I do it really makes me wish I just stayed home.  Things opened up well with friendly service and great coffee.  Most restaurants serve colored water so right upfront this was a huge plus.  

    I ordered the biscuits with gravy and a side of eggs for $6.00 and wasn't disappointed.  The gravy was rich and flavorfull and I almost ordered another biscuit to "sop" up it all up but managed without it.  The biscuit was freshly made, didn't have an out of the box or frozen flavor.  My side of eggs were light and fluffy opposed to that junk you get at a Denny's or standard diners.  

    Solid gem in the land of cheesesteaks and soft pretzels.

    2 weekends ago, my husband said he wanted Breakfast, not Brunch, Breakfast. He didn't want to wait for an hour, he didn't want a menu to be so big and pretentious that you can't even process the items before blurting out "just bacon and eggs!", and he didn't want to spend a fortune. He just wanted breakfast. I remembered seeing Jim H's review on Grubhouse and told him I didn't know anything about it, but if Jim liked it, it had to hold some clout. We got in the car and took the 5 minute drive from our house to Grubhouse and were relieved when we walked in and were greeted with a friendly "hello" and a "you can sit anywhere". Not only do they have a vast menu with breakfast and lunch options, but there is a special board, and all the specialty soda's and coffee flavors are listed on the walls as well. I will get to what we ate in a second, but before I do, we were so happy with the first experience, that we came back the following weekend and had an equally good experience. Dining here is easy. You come in, you order, you eat, you go home.

    What we ate: The 1st trip I had the grande burrito, which came in a perfect burrito that you could actually pick up and eat. I am cutting back on breads so I just cut it open and ate the eggs, sausage and veggies that were inside. My husband had the biscuits and gravy which he had no complaints about. On the second trip, I had the omelette with onions and peppers, a side of bacon and tater tots (free thanks to Yelp!). My husband had the cuban. He was not crazy about the banana mayo, and thought more pickles would have helped, but that's just him. I did not try it so I cannot give an alternate review. My omelette was damn perfect. The hash browns are probably the best I have had in the city. What do you have to do to get crispy hashbrowns in this city?! Go to Grubhouse! All the potatoes here are excellent, whether they are fries, tots or hashbrowns, you are in for a delicous side dish.

    So you know how I said I cut back on bread? Well I may need to break that rule only for Grub House. I am dying to try the pancakes and french toast. My husband really wants to try the burger, so we will obviously be back. Probably next weekend.

Specialties

Breakfast & Lunch. Mix of innovative and traditional fare. Best of Philly award winning bacon pancakes!  Burgers, dogs, melts, pulled pork. Eat in, take out or delivery. Online ordering at grubhousephilly.com

History

Established in 2013.

Established in March 2013 in the spot long occupied by Cousin's Luncheonette. Still focused on breakfast & lunch, we have added many unique items to the menu such as Bacon Pancakes, Root Beer BBQ pulled pork, Salted Caramel French toast & Bacon Wrapped Jalapeno Tots. We have been featured in several publications including Philadelphia Magazine, Zagats Philly, and Today.com- the official website of the today show.
