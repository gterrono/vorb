        First to Review

        The Spot food truck is amazing!  I've been here at least 5 times since they opened, which was about 3 weeks ago.  The guys who work here already know my name and what I'm going to order.

        The prices are pretty good.  It's a little more expensive than a normal food truck, but it's well worth the price.  You can get a burger with unlimited toppings for $6 (I do avocado, grilled onions, cheese, bacon, black pepper, and mayo) with great fries and a drink for an extra $3.  I don't think $9 for all that is too unreasonable.

        In my opinion, the only place that rivals the spot in terms of burgers in the rest of University City is Bobby's Burger Palace, but the same thing will cost you around $14 and a lot more time.

        In terms of the cheesesteak, it is one of the best I've ever eaten.  The cheesesteak is made from thick, juicy steak and you can get whatever you want on it.  I haven't tried the spot dog or pulled pork.

        Overall, this place is probably the best food truck that I've tried on Drexel's campus.  Go here and try it!

        Best burger ever!  It's a food truck so the service is quick but the bonus is the friendliness.  Gosh, I feel like they're my BFF's everytime I place an order.  Guy who owns the truck cooks all the food and really seems to enjoy what he's doing.  Never too stressed to give a shout out, say thanks for the business or crack a joke when appropriate.  That's a rare skill when the orders are rolling in at such a fast pace.  

        A few (very) different styles of burgers along with a BYO option.  My fave is the Roquefort which features balsamic reduction and grilled onions (licking my lips) but I've also tried the Umami which has pickled daikon.  They're all delish.  Just try them all!

        I think it's cool that they have one person on the street taking orders while the owner cooks in the back.  It makes for more of an experience and less transactional when the person is standing there on the sidewalk chatting with you as you order, pay, and wait.  Big plus there!

        They're usually on Drexel U's campus so it's a hike if you're not already in the area but it's worth it.  There's street parking right in front of the truck so no hassle there.  

        Best part:  All the meat is ground in the truck.  The result?  The most juicy and succulent burger your mouth has ever experienced.  Even the buns deserve a shout out (woop woop to the Conshohocken Bread Company)!  And the fries are tasty too.

        Spot became a quick favorite in my office when it appeared here sometime earlier this year.  We all love it!   You will too.  I just know it.

        It's all about the specials - a whole lot more than burgers! Keep an eye on @spotburgers Twitter and Facebook for the lowdown on the wonderfully interesting things happening at this tiny little cart.

        Though I'm a regular at other food trucks on 33rd & Arch, I admit I hadn't paid much attention to Spot until recently, because I incorrectly assumed they just did burgers, and I'm rarely in the mood for a burger at lunch time.

        A few weeks ago, I saw on Twitter that they were doing SLOPPY JOES as their BOTW (Burger of the Week). I love sloppy joes, so we arrived at Spot as soon as they opened, got our joes, and devoured them. The sloppy joes were pretty much perfect - delicious flavor, a bun that was soft yet kept its integrity despite all the juicy goodness, and of course, wonderfully sloppy.

        That same week, we saw they were doing lobster rolls on Friday. I couldn't wait! Unfortunately, by the time we got to the cart, the lobster rolls were already sold out.

        But, we tried their burgers - an "Umami" burger for me and a "Rizzo" burger for my partner. The burgers were pretty good, and I thought the char gave them a real homemade backyard grill kind of flavor. We were impressed by the variety of toppings available for all their different burgers. On another visit, we also ended up trying their roast pork and their cheesesteak, which were quite respectable.

        The following week, I was finally able to try the special LOBSTA ROLL and it was everything I had hoped for and more - by far the best lobster I've had in awhile. Tender chunks of wild-caught Maine lobster, steeped in tarragon-shallot scented butter, mingled with local grape tomatoes and sweet corn, topped a few snips of freshly cut chives, all wrapped in a soft potato roll. Absolutely delicious! On top of that, I was impressed that the following week they cooked down the lobster shells and made a Thai seafood chowder with lobster stock and locally caught seafood. I didn't order it but it looked and smelled wonderful.

        This week they made their own hot dogs with natural casings, and they're promising something special* for next week - I'm glued to their Twitter now, just to see what they're going to do next.

        ----

        *Update: the special for the following week turned out to be "EL BURRITO," a burrito containing fresh ground sirloin, pork, black beans, jasmine rice, house-made guacamole, hand-shredded cheese, and pico de gallo. It was the best damn burrito I've had since moving to Philly from California, and I'd eat it every day if I could!

        When you get Craig Laban's attention, you know you're good and while I don't always agree with Laban, I bow to his knowledge of the almighty burger. Imagine if you will what your idea of a fantasy burger might look like. Now be prepared to be amazed as some dreams do come true. Chef Josh Kim, owner of The Spot, grinds the meat himself and uses the finest ingredients in each meat-lover's masterpiece. Huge, pillowy soft buns, generously thick slices of tomatoes, juicy, slow-cooked spinach, tangy teriyaki sauce, or whatever strikes your burger fancy... it's all here and, frankly, I don't know where they store it all. The Spot is cute, too, with its freshly painted yellow with red accents. I've always believed that a limited number of menu items ensures specialization and the highest quality because if you're not good at making those three or four things, you may as well not be in business. The burger prices are a little higher than your standard food cart burger, but you're getting a restaurant-quality burger at The Spot. I walked my burger five blocks to return to my office at Penn and it still kept its thickness. Now I'm jealous that Drexel has better food carts than Penn! 10/16/12 update: I got a Sante Fe burger with avocado, onions, peppers, pepperjack cheese, and spicy sauce. It was really good, too. The Rizzo is still my fave. The fresh-cut fries are good, too. I think I gained 3 pounds from my meal. 1/22/13 update: The Rizzo is still great, especially on a cold day. Well worth the walk from Penn's campus where there are way too many "me-too" burger options. Congrats to Chef Josh for making delicious restaurant-quality burgers. 1/24/13 update: The Roquefort (bacon, bleu cheese, onions, mushrooms) is also awesome! 3/11/13 update: Whatever Chef Josh creates is always solid gold. I have pencilled in my Fridays as Spot Fish Day. His fish and chips are fantastic. Half a pound of Atlantic Cod expertly fried for your enjoyment. The Ponyo (a cod fish burger) is even better. It's one of those sandwiches that you finish and dream about eating another. On another trip, I had the pleasure of trying Chef Josh's cheesesteak made with delicious hand-cut sirloin. Today's special (it's Monday) was a Mexican Fajita with 7 ounces of hand-cut sirloin seasoned in lime and spices. All of the vegetables (tomatoes, avocado, green peppers, onions, jalapeno) are freshly cut. The oversized fajita pita is lightly fried on both sides and covered with sliced cheese. This belly-busting sandwich could be served in any high-quality restaurant for dinner. It was so big I had to eat mine with a knife and fork. Five stars and a big thumbs up for everything on the menu. 5/11/13 update: Spot's Balboa burger gives a serious knockout punch with its grilled onions, peppers, provolone cheese, fried sopressata, and sunny side up egg. Another awesome burger from Chef Josh! 5/23/13 update: The basic Spot burger with bacon, cheddar, coleslaw, pickles, and Spot sauce is really good. You really get to taste the quality of the meat. I like the potato bun vs the previous white bun. I refrained from getting this from the start because there are so many other wonderful burgers on the menu, but I'm glad I got this today. Everyone... I always order my medium burgers medium rare. Meat this good should NEVER be well done!

        Walk up to the cart and there should be one or 2 order takers on hand...Give them your order, your money, and sit back and wait...Prices are pretty good btw, 7$ seems fair for a burger of this quality\quantity.  Beverages are 2$.

        Overall, given the quality of the burger and the price, I'd say they deserve 5 stars.  The reason I did NOT give them 5 stars is due to the long wait for food!  This is not a food truck, but a cart.  There is room for one small person inside and one small grill which looks like it can hold about 8-10 burgers at a time...which is fine if you only have 8-10 customers, but when you have a long line with 30+ people and more coming the wait times become almost unbearable.  Some people waited over an hour to get their lunch today...I waited till they were all gone(literally placed the last order of the day) and my order was done in about 10 minutes, however they had also ran out of cheesteaks by this time.
