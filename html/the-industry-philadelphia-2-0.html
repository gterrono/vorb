    "If you dont have enough money to tip, you dont have enough money to drink at a bar. Go to the liquor store." So says a handout from The Industry Bar. Funny thing- I might never have enough money to drink there...

    After a loooooooong time waiting for this new place to open, I was absolutely ecstatic to try it out when I heard about the opening on Wednesday. My first meal was brunch on Sunday. It didnt go so well.

    My wife got the espresso martini. Delicious drink, but seriously, $10? It seemed slightly smaller than a typical martini to me, and though delicious, wow thats an expensive drink.

    This was the theme, it seemed. I ordered "The Stomectomy." This would seem to be a signature item of glutinous wonder, ala the famous Kitchen Sink at Green Eggs. Three pancakes, with bacon, sausage and housemade scrapple, all topped by a single fried egg.

    One problem- it didnt have scrapple. I realized this about halfway thru my meal, and by the time I received said meatstuff, I was 90% finished. Furthermore, the pancakes were literally undercooked and almost runny. The egg? Well, it was overcooked and stiff- what should have been yolky goodness dumped all over the massive concotion instead was a hardened yellow thing, with a brown, rubbery bottom. I hate over cooked eggs.

    It should be said that my party loved their pancakes, and I can say wihout a doubt that they were delicious.

    The space is beautiful. Modern but not loud, in terms of both volume and design.

    The staff was overworked and not able to keep up with the demands of their tables, but I get the feeling that they were busier than expected. They get a pass for that.

    Four people at brunch, only one alcoholic drink cost over $70. That's too much, I think.

    My instinct is to give Industry 2 stars. However, it was one of their first brunches, and I feel like they deserve another chance. I need to try the burger. I want to see a dinner service. Still, I feel like the menu and pricing as it stands will not succeed in the blue collar Pennsport neighborhood. It seems like a serious, serious miscalculation.

    As a resident of the 'hood, I too was psyched over the arrival of The Industry. Hope was they would bring the pub food Good Dog was known for down to South Philly.

    I went opening night with a few friends. We sampled several items from the menu - some were great (pork nuggets! whipped tofu! fried green tomatoes!) and a few items missed the mark (burger, pig ear wraps) but - being it WAS opening night, I gave the flaws a pass.

    Since then my partner and I have gone for three dinners, two brunches and one happy hour drinks/apps.

    I've not gone back to the burger - only because I was working my way through the other items on the menu. The pig ear wraps are far better than they were opening night; not nearly as chewy. The scallops were perfectly done (and I agree with another review as I would have expected at least 4 scallops) as was the Kentucky fried hen.

    Steak frites was prepared just as I had asked - and came with an addictive bordelaise. The seafood BLT was great (soft shell crab) but I'd consider a different bread; the loaf is a tad overwhelming.

    The cheese plate is a bit dull - it can easily be livened up with seasoned nuts from DiBruno's or some such. Might suggest including a sliced meat on the cheese plate as an option for the diner as well.

    The beer selection is decent - I can see some being disappointed, but I've been pleased with the options. I've not tried the specialty drinks as $10 is a bit Center City price for this residential area.

    Service has been really good - every server has taken time to introduce themselves and have been attentive. Opening night wasn't as good - but again; it was crazy packed and the night gets a pass.

    I love that they de-witched the place. As a Witch regular, I was hoping whoever took the space would gut it and start fresh. These guys did a great job with the room - can't wait for the outside tables!

    My only complaint - and its one that is hard to fix - is the decibel level. When packed it's almost deafening. Then the music tends to go up a bit to compete and .. well you get the idea. However, happy hours and early dinners/brunches ... it's fine.

    Just don't go for a quiet romantic dinner - haha!

    Other reviews here seem to be lumped into a few categories.  

    First, the "oh it's not the Witch so I don't like it group".  If the Witch was so great why did it go out of business?  

    Next, the "oh the burger here isn't very good" group.  Ordering a burger here is like getting a Bud from Monk's and complaining that it wasn't served at the proper temperature the commercials tell you it should be served.  Go outside your comfort zone a little bit and try something new.  If you want a burger go to one of the crap corner bars on Two Street.  That's not what this place is for.  Please do NOT add a chicken or roast beef sandwich to the menu.  Stay different.

    Last, the "oh this place is 'obnoxious' with their prices" group.  You are at the very least poor and should not be dining out.  Since you utilize Yelp I assume you have an internet connection, whereon The Industry has a website with all prices listed You must be oblivious as well.  Again, if you want 25 cent wings go to Dee's Place or one of the dive bars a block over.  This is not the place for you.

    Now to the review.  Drinks.  Started with the Pennsport Fizz and was underwhelmed.  Didn't seem particularly strong as others suggested, lacked much flavor or anything at all.  Nothing I'd order again.  Had the Brotherly Suds collaboration beer next.  Was quite good, which I'd expect for a beer that was apparently worked on by every brewer in the Greater Philadelphia region.  Overall the drink selection is very good, and a reason to come back.

    Now to the food.  Overall it was a disappointment.  As previously stated I love that they had the balls to put new things on the menu despite the obvious distaste for adventure by many of the neighborhood denizens.  BTW I live three blocks away, so I'm not some Center City slicker rolling up thinking I'm all high and mighty here.  

    We started with the pork nuggets and crispy pig ear lettuce wraps (imagine such a thing!).  Great ideas, but poorly executed.  Now I love Frank's Red Hot.  I put that shit on everything, as they say.  But the pig ears, as well as the guinea hen I had later were way too heavy on the stuff.  Masked every other flavor.  When I had a piece of either that somehow eluded the spice they were very good.  Pump the breaks on the Frank's, you guys.

    We had the guinea hen, like stated, as an entree as well as the veggie fritters.  Guinea hen was a sizable portion of meat virtually indistinguishable from chicken except for it's shape and size.  What skin I could taste other than Frank's was crispy and very good.  Too bad I couldn't taste much of it under a sea of orange sauce.  Meat was a bit dry but not off-putting.  Accompanying Mexican street corn was interesting and something I'd eat again though.  Fritters were dry but the arugula pesto and leftover Frank's in moderation helped.  Nothing we'd order again.

    Interior was nice, service was good so no complaints there.  Just gets two stars removed because of the over-abundance of Frank's on the pig ears and guinea hen and because nothing really stood out here, although I will return to try more options here since this seems to be the best neighborhood option at the moment above For Pete's Sake and Ugly American.  At least it's different.  Different is good.  Embrace it.

Specialties

The Industry has a distinctive menu featuring homemade, contemporary american, bistro style fare. Signature dishes for dinner include Potted Trout, BBQ Duck Sandwich, Kentucky Fried Guinea Hen and Homemade Sausage. Signature dishes for brunch include Fresh Baked Doughnuts, Shirred Eggs, the Hillbilly Philly and the "Stomachectomy."  Executive Chef, Patrick Szoke is the driving force in the Industry kitchen and will continue to create new and exciting plates as the restaurant moves forward.
