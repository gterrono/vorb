    So I know a lot of Philadelphians think he's overrated, but I'm one of those people who believes that Stephen Starr is the best thing to come out of Philly since the Declaration of Independence.  The truth is, the most memorable meals I've had in the city have been at his restaurants.  And yes, my Philadelphia bucket list included a visit to every Starr restaurant in the city.  Naturally, I had pretty high expectations for Parc.  

    I brought a skeptical out-of-town visitor in an attempt to impress him with Philadelphia's food scene.  Luckily the Starrs aligned (pun intended) and it was a beautiful day outside, so we sat in the open indoor section closest to the sidewalk.  It was the best of both worlds-- the view of Rittenhouse Square to one side and the charming Parisian-inspired decor to our other side.  The Parc experience begins the moment you step foot inside the doors and you instantly feel as though you've just entered a cafe straight from the streets of Paris.  Sure, it's theme is kitschy but it's not overdone.  

    The food lived up to my expectations and not only was my omelette beautifully presented, it was light and flavorful.  The highlight came at the end of our meal, when we split the tarte au citron with toasted meringue and blackberries.  It was creamy lemon-y heaven and of course, pretty to look at.  

    Overall the service was great, food was fabulous, and ambience was impressively unique.  5 stars for Starr.

    First and foremost, the food at Parc is outstanding (thus the stars given), and it pains me deeply… First and foremost, the food at Parc is outstanding (thus the stars given), and it pains me deeply to have to give them such a low rating, but the quality of service we received on the night of my 25th birthday was of lower calibre than that of a late night diner.

    I will give praise where it is due before moving to the negative. The architecture and ambiance of Parc is so closely to that of a Parisian brasserie, that I would have believed you if you told me I was back in Paris. My husband and I ordered the charcuterie plate and the escargots for our appetizers, and the trout amadine and lamb loin for our entrees. Everything across the board was amazing and authentic, a favorite being the hazelnut butter in which the escagots were presented.

    And now onto le cœur brisé, so to speak. When the reservation was made, my husband was asked if we were celebrating something. He informed them it was my birthday and that we would be bringing special champagne flutes for a toast.

    We arrived for our reservation at 8:30 and were promptly seated at our table. Our server approached our table quickly and was polite, but appearance-wise was disheveled (untucked shirt, loose tie, a Phillies plastic bracelet on his wrist). Our flutes were filled with champagne, but we weren't asked what we were celebrating (another server later asked, a gesture that wasn't even necessary considering he wasn't even vying for our tip). We ordered and kept the wine list so we could order a bottle with our entrees.

    The apps were brought out quickly. We were talking and enjoying our wonderful food and champagne, when about halfway through (let's say about 10-15 minutes), a food runner comes out with our entrees. Excuse me? My escargots were taken out from under me without asking if I was done (I still had my fork in my hand!) I fortunately asked for them back before they were whisked away. Our bread plates were stacked on top of our charcuterie board (literally on top of the remaining cured meats and pate), and our entrees begrudgingly thrown in front of us, without even an offer of fresh silverware. We weren't even half way through our glasses of champagne.

    Never in my life have I ever experienced this, not even in the lowliest dives, albeit a high-end restaurant.  I was so taken a back and upset, I hardly made it through my fish. Uh...happy birthday to me? I could have understood if we were sitting there for hours or were the last people in the place, but you cannot market yourself as a French brasserie and have this occur. Eating is an event for the French, who often take hours to dine with many separate courses.

    We ate our apps/entrees and immediately asked for our check as my husband could tell I wanted to leave, and salvage the rest of our night. On the way out, he had a long chat with a manager, whom, according to him, is going to "take care of us" the next time we come back and when/if this happens I will update my review, but until that day, this was one  of the (if not THE) most disappointing birthday dinners in my lifetime. Specialties

This stylish addition to Rittenhouse Square pays tribute to French cafe culture, serving breakfast, lunch and dinner seven days a week. Ideal for a quick bite or a leisurely meal, the menu is filled with classics: onion soup, escargots, steak frites and towering plateaus de fruits de mer. The baguettes are made in-house and  the wine list is expansive. Sip a Kir Royale at the zinc bar or enjoy a cafe au lait while basking in the sun.

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
