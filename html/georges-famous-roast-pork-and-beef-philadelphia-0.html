    I'm actually shocked that George's Famous Roast Beef and Pork has only garnered two reviews in 3 years. It is a dynamite breakfast and lunch spot, and pretty darn cheap. In addition, others have commented about the lack of friendliness of the staff. I strongly disagree and have a hard time thinking of what they could do to be friendlier short of giving away their food!

    It's located in the Italian Market at 9th and Carpenter, just south of Esposito's. I heartily, strongly recommend the Hot Roast Pork (something obscene like 7 pounds of tender, savory, melt in your mouth chunks!), the Meatball Parm, and the Hot Roast Beef. Their Hoagies are also good and they have daily lunch specials (like a cheesburger, fries and a soda for $5.00). Pretty much the only thing that i *don't* recommend are their french fries (they are shoestring and kind of flavorless).

    The number one thing about George's is their breakfast sandwiches. They are delicious and  cheap! These days, they run you $3.75. The other wonderful thing is that you can get stuff like 1 pancake as a side (only $1) and morning stalwarts like home fries ($1.75), in addition to their awesome breakfast specials! My favorite special is 3 pancakes and 3 pieces of sausage/bacon for only $4.25! For those who are a little hungrier, you can get 2 eggs, 2 pancakes, 2 pieces of bacon AND 2 pieces of sausage for only $5.10!!!!

    I cannot explain how George's has escaped the notice of all the hipsters down here these days, but I am glad of it. I almost don't want to write this for fear they will invade it now....

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge

    Move over Dinic... and Tony Luke.  There's a new contender in my top 3 roast pork sandwich spots.

    George's is a little hole-in-the-wall, luncheonette type of shop with all of 4-5 sparse tables to squeeze into.  By the looks of it, there hasn't been much, if any renovations, since it was opened 65+ years ago.  Not to say it's dirty.  It's just worn.  But with it located in Italian Market, that's part of the charm.

    One bite of the roast pork sandwich and all of that was forgotten.  It ain't pretty, and it ain't frilly.  It doesn't need to be.  The pork was so juicy and succulent.  Flavorful without being overwhelming.  Not heavy on the stomach.  I haven't tasted anything like it.  The experience gave me a new appreciation for pork - the other white meat.  Yeah yeah... why does it always have to be a color thing?  That's the thing.  It isn't.  This sandwich transcends color and you're just experiencing meat on a different level.

    The second time I went, I got it with broccoli rabe and sharp provolone - South Philly style.  My mouth is watering as I reminisce.... *slurp*

    The others who say that the staff isn't friendly, I'd have to disagree.  They kept asking if we were enjoying our meals.  Within a few verbal volleys, the lady at the counter was giving me the full history... the secret recipe is being passed down through the family.  

    I'd say this experience is priceless.  But you can get this for less than $10!  What's not to love.  It's a true Italian Market/South Philly experience.

Yes, I have had both DiNic's and Tony Luke's...both are good. The one I received from George's was delicious. I had "Roast Pork Italian" with Broccoli Rabe and Provolone cheese.
