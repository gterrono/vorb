    I'm not sure if I've ever eaten at such a flexible restaurant. It's a different sort of flexibility than one is afforded at one of those diners with menus the size of textbooks. Nope, here at Barbuzzo, they accomplish flexibility by offering a small yet varied selection of options that are billed as Mediterranean but different than any other place I've ever been to with that description. The menu is small plates and served family style and you can really make this into whatever sort of dining experience you want. Hungry, order a lot. Looking for a small bite prior to heading out elsewhere, stick to the snacks and spreads. A casual date becomes salad and pizza (wash it down with a cocktail or beer where they offer a small but impressive selection of draft options). And a more upscale dining experience can be built on larger plates such as the bronzino. I was even impressed by the serving size of the fish for a "small plates" sort of place.

    In terms of dishes I had, I thought the uovo pizza, even without the guanicale was great. The chestnut ravioli was pretty fantastic as well in a melts in your mouth sort of goodness. I was less impressed by the sardines and the bronzino. I wouldn't order the sardines again. The bronzino just wasn't what I was in the mood for that night but again I was pleased about the size of the plate.

    I would come back here and I bet it would be a totally different experience. And that's a great thing.

    You know when all the stars are aligned and every ingredient in your mouth tastes like perfection? Well, yes, that is what I experienced at Barbuzzo.

    Not only was the service incredible (great suggestions from our waitress and water was frequently refilled despite a full restaurant), but the food is life-affirming. I have never experienced beets with kale and citrus tossed so delicately.  The sauce never overpowered the ingredients, and the cheese was subtle yet provided an extra oomph to the dish.  

    The uovo pizza, which I think is a big Barbuzzo specialty, was spectacular! The smell of the pizza arriving at the table was tantalizing. And once I cut a small piece and plated it in my mouth, I was really blown away. This was after 3 people hyped up this pizza for me. And normally I am grossed out by egg yolk, but it provided a delicate balance with the cheese and artichoke.  The crust was fluffy yet thin, which is my favorite type of pizza crust.

    I had some of the chicken sandwich, which I think is only offered at lunch time. Somehow, Barbuzzo made such a simple dish delectable.  Their soups were also hearty and well-flavored.  Unfortunately, I didn't grab a drink because I had a full afternoon planned, but next time, I want to try their cocktails.

    And apparently they offer a fun late night menu as well. Yay! I haven't been blown away by restaurants in Philly for a while, but this restaurant reminds me why Philly is such a great foodie town.

    Ambiance:  It had a good vibe, cozy but hip.  I was there on a weekend night so the noise level was pretty high, but not intolerable.

    Service:  Not remarkable either way, nothing super bad or good.  Waitress was upbeat and checked in on us a few times.

    Food:  This is the real reason that you should come here, exceptional food.  Some dishes were homeruns and others were just okay.  Homeruns:  Roasted beets (the flavors and textures of this dish just leaped off the plate); Salsiccia (scrumptious pizza, the crust was perfect and the meats tasted incredible); Stuffed meatballs (all sorts of delicious flavor) and the best thing at the restaurant - SALTED CARAMEL BUDINO (this dessert epitomizes the delights that come from playing with savory and sweet flavors).  Just okay:  Pig popcorn (which is such a disappointment because I was so excited for this dish, it was slightly elevated pork rinds); Uovo (yummy, but not the best pizza there); pan seared gnocchi (unfortunately nothing special and too dense)

    Overall:  I would absolutely recommend this restaurant.  Bear in mind that you should make a reservation well in advance for busy times.  Another option if you do not have a reservation is to sit at the bar.

    My first impression, wow this place really is small. It is very appealing visually with a farmer's house vibe to it. But, it is slightly wider than a couple of bowling lanes. So, this is not a place for a large group as most table space only goes up to four people. Reservations I would say are a must unless you want to sit at the bar or kitchen bar seating.

    They have a nice beer selection of on tap, bottle and cans. I had a lovely belgian tripel for the evening. Do not expect anything but a fine list of import and hand crafted local beers.  They also have a small but nice selection of wine.
    The waitress was attentive but not overly intrusive. Could have been slightly more friendly. She answered questions about the menu aptly.

    This is a place I want to return to. The menu has enough interesting things on it that I wanted to try, but too many for one meal.
    For appetizers I ordered the pig popcorn, which is fried pig skins. It went well with the beer I selected. The sauce that came with it was scrumptious and a perfect compliment. The skins were slightly greasey and could use some spice on it if you ate it without the sauce.
    My wife ordered a local green salad that we found out from the waitress warning is sauteed. I tried a few bites of the salad and thought it needed some texture in it. The lardo was an excellent compliment to the bitter greens, but the garbozo beans could have been crisped to add texture I think.
    For dinner I ordered pork and absolutely loved it. I do not normally order pork in restaurants. I think of it as the home cooked overly dry pork chops of my youth. That was not the case with what was delivered. It was succulant and well prepared. The flavor initially was lemony and slightly tart. My only problem, I wanted more of it than I was given. This is on the smallish side for a main course I think. As my wife was still working on her main course I was tempted to order another appetizer or a pizza. But, she got full quickly.
    My wife ordered the Fideau. It came in a nice bowl and was of ample portion. It is very filling. My wife enjoyed the course. I had some of it with pasta and sausage, while my wife ate the seafood. It was slighly spicey but not overwhelming. I'm not a seafood lover so I cannot comment on the mussels or shrimp or calamari that came in the dish.
    Dessert seemed to be the hilight of the evening though. It is a dessert I WILL remember. It was memorable. We both ordered a salted caramel budino that everyone raves about. And now I know why. It is balanced perfectly. You get sweet some sour and salty tastes. I wanted more of this dessert immediately. The only thing I suggest, do NOT mix it like I did half way through. You end up losing some of the flavors when you do. The caramel is outstanding and the salt just pops out in intervals. This is the first dessert in about a year that has really stood out as a star.
    This is a wonderful place and not overly expensive for center city. It is not overly loud and a romantic charming setting. It is a definate go to again restaurant because of the excellent meal and affordability of it.
