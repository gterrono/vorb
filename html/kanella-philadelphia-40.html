    I came here at the suggestion of a friend who loved it. My friend and I came for brunch one Sunday. Kanella has great good, but lousy service. My friend ordered the Cypriot breakfast and I ordered the shaksouka. I am now in love with shaksouka. It was a wonderful, rich, tomato based stew served with two poached eggs and some sort of whole grain grilled bread with a green paste on it. Oh my god. I can't believe how delicious the dish was. It was filled with tomatoes, green peppers, onions, scallions, leeks and lightly seasoned with cumin and garlic. My friend's Cypriot breakfast looked good. The meat on his plate tasted much like Canadian bacon and the houllumi cheese was an usual, but good addition. It is served pan fried and has a salty taste. We also ordered their regular coffee in a french press. I was pleasantly surprised to find that it was strong coffee. A big pet peeve of mine is going out to brunch and being served mild coffee. The service was awful in that it's slow. If we were in Europe, the speed of their service would have been acceptable. Also, not once did our waiter stop by and ask how we were enjoying our meal. Negative points for that one. Go to Kanella if you want good food, but plan to be there longer than you might expect.

    after hearing many great things about the restaurant, i tried kanella this past Friday for the first time.  it took some time for my party of four to get our plan together, so we ended up making a reservation for 9:45p.  Upon arrival, we had a short wait; I noticed that there were two walk-in parties that had to be turned away, so i would definitely recommend making a reservation.

    i think one of the reasons i've yet to try kanella is because of the menu -- very meat heavy.  so we all decided to just try all of the appetizers and make it a tapas night.  

    to start things off, our server was passionate and patient in describing the items on the menu.

    as michelle c. mentioned, the dips of the day possess a pungent garlic taste.  also, the chef did not go easy on the lemon either.  but, i think you've got to try the dips of the day if you go to kanella.

    the dolmades were on the bland side, but i enjoyed the accompanying cabbage salad.

    i was not quick enough to sample the bureki, so i cannot comment on that.  i did, however, snag a roasted beet and it was really tender and tasty.

    i was a big fan of the fried halloumi cheese.  granted, it seemed to be drenched in lemon, but paired with my sauvignon blanc, the two flavors balanced each other out.  so, i enjoyed the dish.

    the cod fish and chips were just too salty for my taste.  mr. h, however, said his portion was seasoned just fine, but then he had a bite of mine that he could not finish because of the sodium.  absent the salt issue, i really would have enjoyed the appetizer.

    i didn't love the calamari, but that's because i tend to like my calamari battered and fried.  that's just me.

    also, i didn't love the cyprus sausage.  it was kind of a loose sausage.  so, it was a bit weird for me.

    i think my favorite appetizer was the koubes, which is bulgar wheat stuffed with spiced ground lamb and pine nuts served with tzaziki.  i am not even a huge lamb fan and this item was very pleasing to me.  

    overall, kanella it is not what i would consider destination dining. it is a fine byob to go to with friends or during the week if you just want a decent meal or new place to try.  i can't say it would be a great romantic spot on the weekend because it was very noisy inside.  

    i might try kanella again to try the whole fish.  our server informed us that they will fillet the whole fish for you tableside, which is nice, because i don't even know where to begin with a whole fish.

Specialties

We specialise in cooking from the homeland of chef Konstantinos Pitsillides. Pitsillides hails from the island of Cyprus, an indipendant Greek island located in the very east of the Mediterranean. The Cyprus kitchen has many influences but can perhaps best be considered as part of the Levant. At home in Cyprus, as in his Philly kitchen freshness, simplicity, flavour and love are the standard ingrediants. Chef takes his French culinary training and applies it to the traditional recipes of his homeland. Homemade desserts, head to tail cooking, lemon and olive oil are all part and parcel of this neighbourhood joint.

History

Established in 2008.

Kanella is made up of a passionate bunch of characters from the chef on down. Being a local restaurant filled with the family spirit much of the staff have been there since we opened. Though Chef Pitsillides is oft misunderstood (and yes sometimes this is exaserbated by himself), the intent of the restaurant is a simple one... We have aimed to be a neighbourhood venue for good, honest and consitstant cooking. A place that you can afford to come to regularly and have a good meal, good service, a nice bottle of wine, and enjoy your company. We have been blessed with the neighborhood we are in and all our wonderfull customers, so many of which we have known for sometime now. We look forward to continued joyful cooking and eating!
