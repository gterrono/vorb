    This is one of my favorite restaurants in Philly.  I have been here numerous times with friends and am so sorry I haven't written a review yet.  Every time I go in I feel like I am transported to another time zone.  I recently took a friend who really knows Spanish style food and ambiance- he was so impressed with all the "little details" that were accurate.  

    I have little experience with the drinks because I am a boring mojito kind of gal.  The food, though, I have consistently been happy with.  Maybe I should say that I have dined here numerous times over several years and found the entrees always amazing.  This particular night I had the veggie option, the lentil cake, which was superb.  I have had the salmon, steak and ceviche in the past and have been quite happy.  Everything is always well seasoned and perfectly cooked.  

    We passed on dessert here so I can't speak to that on this occasion.  This is a great place for a nice date night as well as a fun place to chill with good friends.

    Had dinner here with a friend during our visit to Philly as this place was in close proximity to our hotel and shopping.

    PROS
    -Lovely ambiance!
    -Good food!

    CONS
    -Fusion-esque, so a little disappointing if you're looking for authentic.
    -Not a very Vegetarian friendly menu.

    I had tried their house mojito, having been told it's a "Must Try" on the menu. I did, and I wasn't impressed. Wasn't very minty, and it was flat.

    Looking for a good old cuban (gourmet) sandwich. It wasn't on the menu and the only alternative to that was an appetizer that came out as an egg roll. It was good. It was different, a bit disappointing but if I didn't have my heart set on a good old sandwich, I may have been pleasantly surprised. The beet salad was delicious! Service was mediocre.

    Yelp rates this place as pricey but I found it to be moderately priced, not fine dining.

    I'd eat here again, now knowing what to expect.

    I was sadly pretty disappointed with my experience at Alma de Cuba.

    I went to Alma for Restaurant Week--in which you get an appetizer, entree, and dessert for $35. Upon entering the restaurant, it's clear that Stephen Staff & Co. put a lot of work into cultivating the ambiance--it's a gorgeous space with low lighting, beautiful imagery, and lush furniture. The ground floor is for drinks only, and the upper floor is for dinner.

    My party of 4 were ushered to the upper floor and seated promptly. Service was great throughout our time at Alma, with waitstaff consistently at our table asking us if we needed anything, refilling our water / AMAZING BREAD BALLS, and clearing/cleaning our messes.

    Before we ordered our food, I ordered a Mango Martini, which I was a big fan of--it tasted and felt almost like an alcoholic mango smoothie, and for $10, it was certainly worth it.

    However, as great as the service, drinks, and interior were, I can't say the same about the food.

    The Restaurant Week menu did not have a vegetarian entree option, but my waitress said the chef was willing to substitute the Crisp Rice and Black Lentil Cake for the meaty options, which I was grateful for. For an appetizer, I ordered Gloria's Black Bean Soup which was decent--it just tasted like... black bean soup I can get anywhere. Nothing to write home about. Soon afterwards, my Crisp Rice and Black Lentil Cake came out and was really just... not at all appealing in any way. The mounds of rice and lentils were all right, but the essentially-mashed vegetable mess on top just didn't taste or look good. It was all just really bland, chewy, and was a burden to eat. I still took the dish home because I was unable to eat it there in hopes that it would somehow, like, get better with age or something, but that didn't work out...
    For dessert, I ordered the Coconut Tapioca Pudding, which was also just mediocre. It was good but not phenomenally amazing. The flavors were present but didn't really go well together. Overall, I'm not really sure any vegetarian should pay the $35 for this really mediocre meal, even if the portions were big--my omnivorous friends loved their dishes, though.

    In conclusion, I really won't recommend Alma's dinner to any vegetarian. The drinks and lounge space, maybe, yes, but I was sorely disappointed with my dining experience there.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
