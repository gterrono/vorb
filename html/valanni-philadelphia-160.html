    If it weren't for the service we got here, I'd give this 5 stars. The food was delicious and the cocktails were great. Our table of 4 split 8 small plates and paella. All the tapas were great. We loved brussels sprouts - delish! The spicy potatoes (they weren't called patatas braves, but that's what they were) were perfectly spiced. The calamari was crisp and tasty, and the lobster mac and cheese was rich, creamy, and had huge pieces of lobster. The ambiance was fun for a girls night out. The only problem with the seating is that some of the chairs are low, padded stools, which would be uncomfortable for some. We didn't mind them, though.

    The issue was with the server. He seemed very "salesy" and rushed us quite a bit, which took away from our relaxing evening. First, he came over multiple times in quick succession, though we weren't ready to order. We had decided on ordering a handful of small plates to start and then continue ordering. This is usually acceptable at tapas restaurants, since the food comes out so quickly. However, he wouldn't let us do this and said the food all had to be ordered at one time. Okay, fine, we need more time to figure out what we want. So, we decide on 2 tapas per person...he comes back and we order, and then he has a problem with the order we made and essentially insisted we order even more food. Granted, the advice, had it been given in a nicer way, was probably valid. However, again, we felt a) stupid for our "bad" ordering and b) completely rushed to randomly select more menu items, just so he'd leave us alone. We ended up ordering paella, which was really delicious, but far too much food, and much of it went to waste.

    I can understand how restaurants want to feed many people in the night, but there has to be a better balance between rushing people out and letting them relax, ordering multiple rounds of tapas. Other tapas restaurants seem to be able to handle this concept. Not Valanni, that's for sure.

    As I said, though, the food and drinks were worth going for, and the waitstaff as a whole might be more respectful of his/her patrons!

    Alright. Check it.

    Valanni's about as eclectic as the glitter section in a party store. It's a wild fun place bustling with the most unexpected amalgamation of people - kind of like high school, but this time the preps, jocks, nerds, geeks, and metal heads all mingle together. Valanni's a giant cesspool of love, and it's really kind of heart warming.

    We decided to order two platters to sample from the tapas menu, one from the Vegetarian (mostly cold) and one from Medi Latin (mostly meat/hot.)

    My boyfriend ordered a tasty Gimlet made with basil and lime and my girlfriend and I offered a bottle of Prosecco. Others stuck to beer and whiskey. In what seemed like only minutes (probably 10) our first few plates came out, and then they didn't stop coming out for what felt like a straight hour. So much food!

    A majority of the veggie tapas incorporate feta cheese in some way (which I love) but with multiple dishes ordered it was a tad overkill (our bad.) The feta itself was amazing though, oftentimes warm with fresh char marks. The olives and peppers popped and the the trio of spreads weren't your average store-bought hummus. Each was savory, creamy and pretty exceptional. My absolute favorite was the warm brie...just order it! You will not be disappointed! We were already a little full when we realized we still had all of the Medi Latin tapas still to come, so we used restraint and placed some to the side.

    The rest came firing out one after another and while all were really delicious, I have to be honest, I had no idea what was what! (Our server quickly announced each as he found table space for the plates but with six people passing around...forget about it.)  Anyway, highlights of what I actually identified were the spicy chicken empanadas, the lamb merguez, tuna tartare, spicy potatoes and ham croquettes. I laugh because the very last plate came out solo and it was chick peas. I had a nibble to be polite but we were so full by that point, it didn't get much love.

    Bottom line, the food was really the star of the evening. What a refreshing change to some places where you pay for the ambiance.

    Things I've learned:

    - Their tapas portions are above average than most.

    - Pick and choose tapas from the Veggie section instead of the sampler (unless you love feta as much as me.)

    - Try the paella. If they do tapas exceptionally, I am sure the paella that everyone raves about is actually that good. (I will be back for it.)

    - Good for groups, despite its size. Six is probably the max and just make a reservation ahead of time...the place gets crowded!

    Valanni, you really are a surprising new favorite for me. I will be back!

History

Established in 2000.

Valanni restaurant opened its doors in September of 2000, introducing "Medi-Latin" cuisine to Philadelphia. Situated right off of the "Avenue of the Arts," Valanni has become a pre-theater ritual and place to meet for a night-cap for many who live in and around the neighborhood. The bar at Valanni keeps up with the creative cuisine by offering an assortment of sangria, mojitos and other specialty drinks. The kitchen is open until 1 AM daily, serving Tapas and a Mediterranean inspired lounge menu.
