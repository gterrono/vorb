    The Nodding Head never crossed my mind as a brunch destination, until one day the bf and I were wandering in search of eggs and saw the chalkboard sign outside their door with two words:  Jazz Brunch.  

    I love live music, and it is generally absent from the Philly dining scene.  The artists played soft, funky jazz in the background which set the scene for a nice little Sunday meal.  I had south of the border eggs-- the eggs were fluffy and liberally sprinkled with jalapenos and cheese.  They sat on top of well-seasoned potato pancakes (usually a throw-away item at brunch places, NH gave them adequate attention and care).  Added a mimosa (bf had a dark beer) to the mix, and it was a very pleasant meal.

    This place is a standby for me, especially when I'm entertaining out-of-towners who like beer (as opposed to out-of-towners who like wine or mixed drinks). If you're creeped out by bobble head figurines, avert your eyes at the top of the entry stairs. Atmosphere is otherwise cosy and pleasant, if unusually dark.

    I haven't explored the whole menu, in part because of the veg. thing, but also because I can't get past the polenta puttanesca. I eat it pretty much every time I go - it's delicious. Service isn't outstanding, but it's friendly and low-key.

    The beer is what's most special, probably. Monkey Knife Fight, Grog, etc - all fun and tasty. This past holiday season, they had this stuff called Sledwrecker Ale (or something similar) that was wicked, wicked tasty. The bartenders are their friendliest staff, I'd say

    I've come here with any company from preppy academics to Navy boys, and it's always been well-received. I even came here on a first date with my beau. We've been back quite a few times since, and he recently pointed out that despite the business name, it doesn't seem like a place where a passing out level of drunkenness would really be tolerated. Despite that limitation, I say go and enjoy.

    Listed in Best Happy Hour, Stop by for a beer, Good First Dates, Breweries, Cash only

    I've been here plenty of times before, but I found myself here twice this weekend - and for brunch, which was delightful.

    1. The beer is always good. I was here Friday for drinks/dinner and Sunday for brunch. By Sunday, the beer list was different. I had some fabulous O-Tay and Pho-Tay and was quite happy. Not your typical belgian beers. The Belgian Chocolate Stout? delicious.

    2. I had a bad run-in with a veggie sandwich here about a year ago, but I have since chalked it up to a faulty stomach (mine), and I'm no longer holding it against them. The meatloaf sandwich is awesome. I personally prefer to pour on the gravy myself, because I like to freshly coat every bite, but to each his own.

    3. Brunch was awesome! Great jazz music (maybe a tad too loud), yummy bloody marys (and not just the run of the mill ones - fancy ones), and great food. I had south of the border eggs, which were great. I like the latkes a lot. Plus,  they give you a basket of mini muffins. How can you not like a place that gives you baskets of mini muffins.

    4. I've taken several dates here over the years - some good, some not so much - but the beer is good. The food is good. So even if the date isn't good, you've got something. :)

    5. Happy hour is pretty good - they have a few beers and wines for $4, and I think they have a drink for $5 or so as well. Good vibe around happy hour time especially.

    There is a $10 or $15 minimum for credit cards, but there is an ATM.

    Prior to attending Opening Tap for Philly Beer Week on Friday, it was absolutely imperative to get a solid base going with an early dinner. After talking about the usual spots, my boyfriend and I decided to go outside the box and meet at Nodding Head.

    I've never been a huge fan of Nodding Head's beers, but considering the fact that it had been about two years since my last visit, I was willing to try again. There were five or six beers priced at $4 for happy hour (which went until 7) and I opted for the Zephyr, a mild, slightly hoppy brew that was pleasant enough for repeat orders. There were some food specials as well, so we ordered a $4 plate of mussels to start.

    The mussels, steeped in a delicious broth with bacon, were every bit as good as Monk's and we polished them off very quickly. For dinner, I pulled a wild card and went with the vegetarian chili after seeing it delivered to a nearby table. The rice base was perfectly cooked and flavorful, and the medley of spicy beans and squash was awesome. Cheddar topped off the hearty plate, and a side of sour cream was served with the dish.

    I'll definitely consider Nodding Head again for happy hour and a bite.
