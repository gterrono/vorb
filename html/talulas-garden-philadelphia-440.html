    Visited here from the suburbs twice in a week--the experience was so great the first time I couldn't wait to get back.

    The ambiance is perfect.  The garden is obviously the place to be, but even if you can't get a seat out there the restaurant has not slacked on the inside either.  I thought the bar area was particularly nice but there really does not seem to be a bad seat in the house.

    Service was outstanding on both visits.  Both servers were very friendly, prompt, and knowledgeable about the food and drink offerings.

    The beer list is pretty cool.  It's not especially long but has a really nice, almost eclectic selection of beers.  I tried a cherry sour beer and a ginger beer and loved both.

    The food is outstanding, down to the bread they bring to the table when you first sit down.  On my first visit I got the tuna carpaccio as an app and trout as the entree.  The tuna app was small but delicious.  The trout was really filling and excellent-- some of the best fish I've ever had.  It was served on top of some sort of noodles which were great and meshed well with the fish.  Pretty damn close to perfection.

    On my second visit I tried the hot house tomatoes with boquerones (anchovies in oil) which was also awesome.  This time I tried the halibut, which while still good was not as good as the trout I had tried a few days before.  Still, it was a great meal.

    The menu seems to change every month (judging by the fact that the current month is written on the top) and most (if not all?) ingredients are sourced locally.

    Yes, it is a bit pricey, but I feel like some of the reviews are exaggerating that aspect a little.  While Talula's Garden was definitely a splurge for me, I never felt like I was overpaying and got more than a great value.  Everything is really top notch here from beginning to end.

    Parking-wise, the valet outside is expensive (~$18).  There is a garage (Walnut Towers) 2 blocks away which is also not cheap but with the $2 validation from Talula's comes out to ~$13 (pro-tip: you have to insert the original ticket first, then the validation ticket from Talula's-- or else feel the wrath of one of the grumpy parking garage attendants).  Street parking is limited but probably possible to find if you spend a few minutes looking for it and are willing to walk a few blocks.

    If you're trying to impress a date, have a romantic evening, or just spend time with friends and loved ones, Talula's Garden is a perfect place to do it.

    A high-energy atmosphere permeates the various parts of Talula's Garden, the result of animated conversations from full tables and content diners.  The scene is watched over by the accomplished Aimee Olexy, a large and well-trained wait staff and, most importantly, a kitchen that is reliable and working on all cylinders.
     
    The Garden shines when it comes to freshness and seasonality of the dishes, many stellar ingredients sourced locally.  This is evident on the delightful marinated tomatoes and fresh garden herbs plate, or the roasted asparagus with smokey farm bacon.

    The fresh pastas are cooked perfectly.  This was true for the pappardelle with rabbit sausage and the squid ink linguine with blue crab and crab sauce.  We liked every main dish we ordered:  the sweet, moist, absolutely luscious wild spring halibut with chick peas, the seared local brook trout (what a great fish trout can be!), and the chicken under a brick with crispy Yukon gold potatoes.  The beautifully cooked bird tasted rich and, pressed flat by the brick, could be eaten like a fowl steak.

    Of course we ordered a cheese plate.  The Master Collection offered something for everyone, my favorites were the quadrello di bufala, the local Spring Brook farm "raclette" style cheese, and the ripe Epoisses (but who does not love that cheese?).  Something I do not like are "truffled" cheeses and indeed, the overly strong chemical ruined an otherwise fine Gouda for me.  Since I listed a dislike, I will quickly add another, not specific to Talula's Garden but to restaurants in the US in general:  the insane pricing of wine.  The prices of wine-by-the-glass exceeding the retail price of the bottle.  Wrong!

    We ended with the little ricotta donuts on huckleberry jam and excellent French-press coffees, then left content, as almost everyone departing from Talula's Garden does.

    I was really excited about dining here, and I had to reschedule our anniversary reservation several times because of our scheduling conflicts. So when we finally ate here recently for our 15th anniversary, we were surprised that we didn't like it as much as we had hoped.

    The setting is gorgeous. The garden is a city oasis, and the dining room inside is majestic yet comfortable.  

    We went on a Tuesday night, which you'd think wouldn't be very busy. Wrong. It was packed at 7 and didn't slow down. It took a few minutes to be seated, and we were seated at a little table smooshed up against a wall. It felt a little tight.

    The location on Washington Square makes it a popular after-work place, which is surprising given how expensive it is, and that I always thought it was more of a special occasion restaurant, not a "let's go out and have a $200 dinner after work" kind of place. About 1/3 of the people looked like they came from work, and there was a steady stream of well-dressed couples pouring in all evening.

    The menu looked amazing. I could have ordered almost everything on it. But surprisingly what we ordered just wasn't WOW. The little puffed bread with herb oil/butter that the waiter served us in the beginning was one of the best things. I could have had five of those.

    I started with the mushroom soup in a creamy parmesan stock, which was not a creamy mushroom soup like most that you order in a restaurant, but it was a broth laden with a "misestra of exotic" mushrooms, which was just too much mushroom for my taste (but what should I expect if I order a mushroom soup?). I also ordered a small plate of the buttery potato gnocci. I think I should have ordered the ribbon pasta with squash and ricotta instead. The gnocci was good but kinda mushy. I know authentic gnocci is softer than poorly made gnocci, but it just seemed a little too doughy. I also had the seared trout entree, which was delicious. It was a good-sized portion and perfectly cooked. Great crispy skin.

    My husband was less impressed with his dinner and the entire experience. He ordered the marinated tomatoes with ciabatta appetizer and the swordfish entree. He thought the portions were too small, and he could have eaten another entire meal to be satisfied. He thought his dishes were tasty but nothing that original or exciting. He also wasn't a fan of the ambiance. He thought it was kind of pretentious, and the service too slow between courses. Our waitress was really nice, and very good, but the service did seem a bit overly formal.

    I also ordered the mint stracciatella ice cream with chocolate chip cookies for dessert. The ice cream was really good, almost like a gelato. The cookies were small, hard and round and too dense for me. I like thin, crispy chocolate chip cookies.

    My husband says he wouldn't go back again. The food isn't interesting enough to warrant the price, and he doesn't like the service. He doesn't mind paying a lot for a fabulous meal, but he didn't think this was fabulous. I'd go back, probably for lunch, but I think I'd try other dishes.

    Talula's Garden earns distinction for not only its chic décor, delicious seasonal menu, wonderful outdoor display, and international cheeses but also for their astute staff and stellar service!

    Upon the introduction to the cute brioche buns with sun dried tomato butter, my dinner mates and I bit into a puff of heaven: buttery (but not overwhelmingly so!) pieces of warmness that just melted onto the palate. We were awestruck for a moment but after we recovered we just kept glancing at all the plates passing us by since we were seated at the communal table near the cheese bar and kitchen. We saw a plate with at least 10 buns with envy and were clearly ogling and contemplating: should we shamelessly ask for more buns (ala Oliver Twist style of course!) and lo and behold the waitress dressed in quaint country frock immediately placed another plate, smiled as us, and left us to devour the buns gleefully!

    The second example of their amazing service happened when one of the dinner mates felt increasingly tired and sick to the point where she was thinking about leaving before the entrée came out. We asked for hot water with lemon to hopefully revive her and it arrived promptly. Additionally when she finally decided to leave right after the entrée arrival, the waitstaff quickly doggy-bagged her crab pasta along with 2 complimentary brioches! How thoughtful!

    Now onto the food:
    - the mushroom soup was amazing symphony of earthiness and nuttiness with the Kennett Square mushrooms and pine nuts that sang well together.
    - the lamb meatball was not gamy and complemented the roasted eggplants harmoniously
    - the crab pasta was not overwhelming and lightly done so that one can keep eating the perfectly al dente pasta forever
    - the scallops was artfully arrange with the roasted potatoes, carrots, and parsnips and swirled with a delightful sauce that made it light and filling at the same time

    The food, décor, and staff were all thoughtfully done that just works well to give a diner the best experience possible in all areas!

    What can I say about Talula's Garden that other's haven't already said? Chances are, you don't doubt that Talula's Garden is great, so I won't try to convince you of that, but more try to describe what Talula's Garden is, so you can know whether or not you want to come here for a date, special occassion, brunch or whatever the case may be!

    Environment: Talula's Garden is a casual upscale sort of place. Think, Cheesecake factory, without any of the tacky, gaudy elements of it. Talula's Garden also has an outdoor dining area that is really really beautiful, it's like someone planted a secret garden and you're invited in! Talula's manages to be classy, while also being cozy. You can tell what their restaurant philosophy is because all their waitstaff dresses in a very down-to-earth kind of way, no doubt to invoke ideas of farming or gardening.

    Service: Service at Talula's was great! Our server, honestly seemed like he was near the end of his shift, so he was perhaps a tad impatient, but he was still helpful, suggestive, and polite. It took a while for our food to come out, but an extremely large party had just been sat, and their orders taken immediately prior to my girlfriend and I, so, the wait was understandable, and not by any means unreasonable.

    Food: I didn't even remember the wait, seriously. My girlfriend and I are a bunch of brunch fiends, and we were both blown away by the food at Talula's Garden. We started off with an order of Lemon-Ricotta Zeppolis, and we were shocked. More often than not, my experience with Zeppoli's have been oily, and dense (though not entirely a bad thing). But these were different, these fried balls of joy were surprisingly light and airy! It came served with lemon curd and what I imagine was a blackberry puree, between the two citrus' even though we were eating such a heavy appetizer, the appetizer remained bright and light, whetting our appetite for our entrees. I was literally scraping my plate with my knife and licking it.

    I ordered the steak and eggs, while my girlfriend ordered a quiche. My steak was fantastic! It was cooked rare (as it ought to be), and it was served with a hash that had little pieces of pickled okra, really smart move. The pickled okra managed to brighten up the entire dish and help keep the steak and eggs from feeling to dense or heavy.

    My girlfriend's quiche was delightful. I'm honestly at a bit of a loss for how else to describe it, and I suppose that's really the secret of Talula's. The food is simple, there's no real hidden tricks, it's just great, fresh ingredients, prepared well. My Girlfriend's quiche was a great example of this, it was eggs, with some veg and meat baked into it. Nothing special, but everything tasted fantastic. There were no complex interplays of textures or flavors, but just honest, fresh food, which is really the charm of Talula's.

    Things I didn't like: No draft crafts. What? Why? It's Summer! Where is the Victory Summer Love? The Dogfish? The Yards? Talula's is all about local production, local, fresh production. Why not support the local craft beer brewers!? What's the deal?

    If I had a fault, it's that Talula's can be a little TOO polished, and finessed. When I order steak and eggs, I imagine and want, a steak sizzling in a cast iron skillet with a thick crust, tossed with some butter, with a side of potatos (and pickled Okra, seriosuly such a great combo, bravo). Instead, I got a beautiful, composed, focused plate, with sliced steak, cut at a bias for me. My steak and eggs was just a little TOO polished and clean, I could use a little less polish at times, and a little more homey/cozy.

    I'd come to this garden all day and any day... if I could afford it!
