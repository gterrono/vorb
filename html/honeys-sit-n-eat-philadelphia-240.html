    People were always talking about Honey's so we finally met a friend there for breakfast/brunch in the summer. And I was impressed mostly. The menu offers plenty of options for vegetarians and meat-eaters alike and the food is top-quality and yummy. I think it's a bit pricey but I guess you pay for the locally grown high-quality ingredients. I had pancakes and they were very tasty. I would recommend Honey's if you want a good hearty breakfast and are in the area, but my favorite breakfast spot in the city is Morning Glory, hands down!

    4 stars!

    I'd heard Honey's name floating about for a while, so on last Saturday morning we went as a group of four to check it out.  I also knew from prior word-of-mouth that it was no reservations and that the line might be long.  When we got there there were people waiting outdoors.  I really liked the fact that Honey provided these heater lamps for the line outdoors - it was about 20F out.  We luckily got to wait just inside the door.. our wait was about 40-45 minutes.  Our waiter filled our coffee really quick and kept it filled our entire time.  Great service.  It was really busy but I never felt like our waiter was overwhelmed.  The menu is extensive and there are lots of options for vegetarians.  Lots of dishes allowed you to sub in tofu or vegetarian sausage.  The portions are generous and I really enjoyed my Enfrijoladas.  Others in the party got the Eastern Omelet, the Sausage with biscuits, and the Breakfast Quesadilla.  I think that everyone agreed that while everything was good, they liked the Quesadilla and the Enfrijoladas the most out of what we got.  I also got a small side of Brussels sprouts with almonds... yummy..

    Honey's is also very affordable!  As a side note, I've also heard some things about 'hipsters' going to here often.  I don't usually pay attention to the other guests.. as long as the food is good, I don't care who else is really there.  I don't know if hipsters really make an issue, however, there was some middle aged lady in a fur coat who was also in line and was threatening to kick all the diner's butts, the ones that were leisurely enjoying finishing up their brunch, chatting and drinking coffee.  Then there was another group of 9 (3 were babies) who started giving the host some attitude because they had been made to wait about an hour.  Honey's lay out is such that it's not exactly the best place for large groups.. it's doable but I expect to wait.  There were only about 2 spaces I saw that were really meant for larger groups.  

    In short, I'd recommend anyone to come here, but be patient with the possible and likely wait. I'd love to come back sometimes.

    My waist line is really glad that I didn't come here sooner. My tastebuds, however, are not.

    I've heard a lot of hype about this little spot and I have to say that I agree with many of my friends who consider Honey's to be among their favorite eateries. The menu is a bit overwhelming with the array of choices they have spanning breakfast and lunch. The place was pretty dead on a Thursday afternoon which made for a more relaxed meal.

    I settled on the fried chicken fingers and hush puppies (I'm sensing a trend here). I also had a mug of their steaming hot chocolate with a hefty dollop of rich whipped cream. They definitely don't serve you that crap that comes out of the Swiss Miss packet. The chicken fingers were fantastic! I don't know what exactly they are seasoned with but they were tender, juicy, light, crispy - just completely yummy. The hush puppies were perfect little orbs of cornmeal goodness - fried to a superb crisp on the outside and delicious with a bit of honey. Unfortunately, I should have fasted several days before because the portion was rather hearty. I ended up taking my hush puppies on the road.

    A certain Yelper coerced me into sharing the fried banana split. Yes, you read that correctly - fried banana! Heaven in a bowl people! A lightly fried banana that still managed to remain un-mushy topped with our choice of mint chocolate chip and strawberry ice creams, heaped with whipped cream, chocolate sauce and slivered almonds. Totally worth the $8.

    I also got to try a bit of their latkes from my lunchmate's plate and was pleasantly surprised since I've had all manner of poorly attempted version of this food. All in all, a great meal for $30 - two entrees, a hot chocolate, and a huge dessert.

    If only our server had been a bit more warm/fuzzy, I'd throw in the fifth star.

    Get thee to Honey's, but I'd recommend avoiding the place on the weekend as I'm sure it's a mob scene and this place ain't that big.

    Edit: Come to think of it, I don't know if I'd throw in the fifth star just yet. I was pretty skeeved out by some of the crowd we dined with. There was definitely a guy who looked as if he hadn't bathed/washed his hair in weeks. The waiter with the huge hold under the armpit of his shirt didn't make matters any better. I just don't get the whole hipster scene. Personally, I enjoy being able to breathe when I wear jeans. And Professionalism is always nice.

    It's all about the mexican breakfast here.

    A few years ago I was coerced into an enlightening moment. My friend forced me to try her huevos rancheros at Honey's. At first I was hesitant about mixing breakfast and mexican food... but I have been hooked ever since. Since my eye opening experience, I have been converting people left and right to put beans, salsa, and the likes into their breakfast staples. Mexican breakfast has ultimately changed my life.

    Having started my mexican breakfast experience at Honey's -- they remain my number one place for this breakfast genre. Their huevos rancheros are TOP NOTCH. I'm not sure how they do it, but I think it has something to do with their bean and cheese concoction. Tip= If planning on ordering the delicacy, try to get seated at a place where you aren't to be seen. This will be a messy but wonderful experience - that should almost be private! (hint #2: stock up on some napkins!)

    The huevos are my honey's staple, but I have also ventured outside of the box. I'm a big fan of their breakfast burrito with turkey sausage and enfrijoladas - both good choices. Other mentionables include their latkes, onion rings, turkey burgers, turkey meatloaf, and green bean casserole.

    On mornings where you are a bit *ahem* under the weather, their fresh squeezed orange juice is the perfect cure. A little pricey, but its worth every sweet gulp. I love watching their machine work its magic. You'll see what I mean.

    Good food, not great, very friendly people.  I like this place but I feel like this place is probably overrated by the hipster/vegan/vegetarian crowd.  It's a diner in an old building.  The menu was huge, but really it was breakfast.  We went for lunch after getting out of class.  I got the breakfast bomb, it was an egg and a breakfast meat (I chose their homemade sausage (tasted like sausage)) inside a buttermilk pancake with a starch on the side.  After reading so much about them, how could I not chose the latka.  The latka was excellent, everything I was promised!  I was starving though and thought the portions could have been bigger.
    My dining partner ordered hot chocolate and the deep fried chicken fingers.  I ended up finishing them.  The portion was more than enough for one person.  Quality white meat chicken, homemade breading.  I could see this dish as a keeper.  But it came with hush puppies and to be frank, they were the best part.  After writing this review it's dawned on me that perhaps the best part of Honey's is their sides.  That's something to think about...
    We decided to share a fried banana split for desert, it was pretty amazing.  The banana was deep fried with a soft dough, think corn dog instead of KFC Original Recipe.

    Why would a place like this not accept credit cards?  Is it because they own the ATM in the corner of the restaurant and want to bank the surcharge?  Who cares, I payed cash.  With a twenty to be exact and for some reason there was some trouble splitting the bill.  The waiter couldn't separate the checks evenly.  We both payed with a twenty.  It was not higher math.  He had bills for change in his pocket like a parking attendant.  This added to my confusion.
    One last thing to note, this place did not seem especially clean, not especially unclean, just a little scuzzy.  Maybe it's because I just came from the hospital, but I just got the feeling like there could have been more hand washing and mopping.   I'm also a fan of a clean head of hair from the people I buy food from.  Oh, and one server has a hole in the armpit of his shirt.
    I know this review won't make me popular, but after all I heard about Honey's I just wasn't overly impressed.  Perhaps I hold diners to high standards.  I'd go back, but never on the weekend.
