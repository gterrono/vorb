    The first time I tried to eat here, I discovered the hard way that John's has very... unique hours. They close around three, and I tried to eat there at five (so much for spontaneity!).

    When I tried again (after researching the hours properly this time), I was absolutely blown away. My companion and I knew the cheesesteaks were supposed to be amazing, but I was really in the mood for a roast pork sandwich, so we got a large one of each and set to. Oh my God. They were heaven in wrappers.

    To start with the cheesesteak: the cheese was well-melted throughout and quite gooey. We chose the mild provolone for this sandwich and it was a perfect choice-- the flavor of the provolone cut straight through the onion and meat and was a perfect complement. The meat and onion had a good ratio and both were well-chopped and blended together (I hate hate stringy chunks of onion, so this was a plus). It was moist, but the roll held up pretty well until we overloaded it with ketchup. A 5/5.

    The roast pork: dare I say, it was as good as DiNic's? They don't roast the pork on premises, I believe, but it didn't matter. The pork was cut into perfect slices and cheese was again melted well. I chose the sharp provolone, which weirdly enough had less flavor than the mild... but that may have been my tastebuds that day. They don't offer broccoli rabe, but they do offer spinach. Now, not being a fan of spinach kept me from putting it onto the sandwich, but I'm sure it would have added an extra element of awesome. Still a stellar sandwich with enough juices to be easy to munch. 5/5.

    The service was fast and the place was tiny-- I'm glad it was not a cold day or a long line, because the only seating is outside. I am definitely coming back here-- even though it's a trek.

    John's hits all five of my requirements for a good cheesesteak which are as follows:
    1) Good bread. I prefer hard seeded rolls like Sarcone's or Liscio's but even if it's soft, it better be fresh.
    2) Cheese has to be a mixed all through the beef not laid on top or laid on the bun. Each bite should contain cheese (and onions, see #3).
    3) Onions should be cubed (diced) so they blend in with the meat and cheese.
    4) onions must actually have a little char on them. It's not enough to just have a big pile on the grill where most of them don't actually touch the bottom.
    5) Decent quality meat. The standard gray, tasteless meat is unacceptable. Too fancy is a concern too. I think John's Roast Pork hits the right note here. Good steak but not high end steak house quality.

    John's is still in first place IMO.  However, my two most recent visits are the reason for the missing star.  On both occasions, the meat was a little chewy and dry and the cheese was not thoroughly mixed like it usually is.  We all know that consistency is key and it was a hallmark of this joint for decades.  Hopefully my next visit will confirm greatness rather than continue an alarming trend.
