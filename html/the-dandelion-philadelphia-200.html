        Great spot for this time of the year.
        Tried to sit by the fire since it was 30 and windy out, but everyone else wanted the same spots so we sat upstairs in the dog pound. I guess it's really called the Best Friend Pub, but it's a nice room with a cozy bar.

        The Fish and Chips were excellent. The Haddock was flaky, moist and perfect. The fries that came with it were super hot and quite filling.
        The burger was good, but had to be sent back as it was ordered medium and was served rare. Once they got the temperature correct it was juicy, although not a large patty, it was tasty. It also came with an order of the piping hot fries.
        Service was prompt and friendly and would go back again.
        Fish and Chips are $19.50 and the burger was $16.50 so it's not inexpensive for lunch, but a nice spot if you can sit by the fire.
        I'm not a beer drinker, but they do offer a nice selectionn of English beers and ales.

        Anytime someone starts hatin' on Yelp, I can't help but wonder if they have a ton more expendable cash than I do. When I go out to eat I want to stack the deck in my favor for getting something worthwhile for my money. Whether this means good, cheap food, an interesting experience or an extravagant meal, I like to analyze as much data as possible before investing in some calories. And what if you are new to and/or visiting an area? How do you decide where to eat then!?

        Take for instance: Dandelion Pub. Friend in town, staying at the Embassy Suites. Even when I lived in the city I lived in Fishtown, which is...different. So it was 9pm (her flight was delayed) on a Thursday and we were hungry and tired. Where to eat that would be worth spending some cash and wouldn't require more than 10 minutes to get there? Yelp to the rescue! But enough about Yelp, onto the Pub.

        The decor: Um, I kind of loved it. It was completely overdone in the best possible way. I felt like I had entered some strange British drama where people sat around in dim spaces talking about what the next best step is to avoid certain death as a metaphor for their relationships. Have minor fantasies about sitting around their fireplace, looking demure and sipping something mysteriously.

        The service: Quite lovely. Helped us figure out a way to squish all the plates onto our teeny table. Plenty of water.

        The food: If you don't like British pub food, there is no reason to expect you'll like this. It may be "upscale" but it is still fish and chips and bangers and mash, etc.  I, however, do like British pub food, and was quite pleased with all of our dishes. The lamb shepherds pie was rich, and even though not a GINOURMOUS helping I was pleased my friend & I decided to split it.

        The price: Not too bad actually. We split a couple of things, and were quite full (didn't actually finish it all). I think if you were going to come for a appetizers + meal+ drinks + dessert it would add up quickly.

        I'm always impressed by the ambiance and decor of a Starr place. I'm usually unimpressed by the food, however. While I always appreciate a small, intimate gem of a restaurant, I can also get into the Disney World-esque details and theme driven establishments of Stephen Starr. It's novel and it's fun. The food is usually just ok, but I forgive it because there are distractions--interesting ambiance and great service.

        We went to The Dandelion for dinner on Super Bowl Sunday. The place was quiet and nearly empty. We were seated upstairs in a cozy booth in a dog themed Victorian pub/dining room. The restaurant is made up of many small, separate dining rooms that all offer a different Victorian vibe. After dinner, I walked around and checked out each one--again, the place was empty. And I was curious. I love the way it looks and feels in there.

        My boyfriend and I started with a couple beers (they offer a pretty extensive drink list) and the pickled beetroot jar and the dandelion salad. The beet plate came with a good-sized jar of pickled red and golden beets, olive tapenade, goat cheese and a few small crostinis. This is hard to get wrong, but it was very tasty anyway. Only complaint is that we were given four very small crostinis and a good deal of spreadable things. I'd like a few more, please. The dandelion salad was good too. I eat dandelion only once in a great while, so it's nice to see it on a menu. Nice, simple salad with a lemony dressing (had cucumber and avocado too). Entrees: the vegetarian had the vegetarian entree--braised celery gratin. Note: he was prepared for a British-insprired menu and so was not expecting too many veggie options. The celery gratin was good, like a warm salad. A tad too overpriced though. I had the lamb shepherd's pie. Yummy comfort food. It was rich and flavorful and filling. The browned, cheesy top was the best part. Next, dessert! I was so full, but had to get the sticky toffee pudding. Toffee bread pudding covered in warm, toffee, rum sauce and sided with date ice cream. Oh my heavens. It seemed familiar, then I remembered I had had this exact dish at Kennett some time before. Who cares? Dandelion's was better.

        Anyway, one of their selling points is they have 3 rotating cask ales, as well as a small but good tap selection of English and local beers. They have superb pub food - my favorites so far are the fish & chips, lamb shepherd's pie, and rabbit pie. Last time I was there, we had at least 5 different people come by and ask how everything was, which was actually kind of nice. It was a rainy day, and it was just nice to sit here at lunch and look out the frosted glass windows and enjoy being inside with nothing better to do than enjoy a leisurely afternoon.

        Walking in for lunch has worked well for us, but for dinner I'd definitely recommend a reservation.

      Specialties

      London's culinary revolution comes to Philadelphia with this eclectic English pub. Cozy rooms and intimate bars create a storied, Old World aura, while updated seasonal takes on British food make up the menu. In keeping with tradition, cask and tap craft beers are always rotating, a proper afternoon tea service has a sweet and savory menu, and classic beef family-style roasts are served on Sundays.

      History

      Established in 2010.

      London's culinary revolution comes to Philadelphia with this gastropub from restaurateur Stephen Starr.
      Cozy rooms and intimate bars create a storied, Old World aura, while updated seasonal takes on British
      food make up the menu. In keeping with tradition, our casks and tap craft beers are always rotating, a proper
      afternoon tea service has a sweet and savory menu, and classic beef and chicken family-style roasts are
      served on Sundays.

      Stephen S. Business Owner

      Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
