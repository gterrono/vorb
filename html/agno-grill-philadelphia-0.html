    I've been so excited to try out Agno Grill since I first heard about it. After all, it's Mediterranean food, owned by the same people as Pure Fare, and it's super close to my apartment.

    Maybe all my excitement set myself up for disappointment, but I was really let down by Agno. The concept itself is really cool: fresh, healthy, organic mediterranean food. And the set-up is very Chiptole-esque where you tell them what you want at a counter and they make it right there. You start out by selecting a base: spinach, black (!!!) rice, or a wrap. I'm not usually a rice person, but I had to go with the black rice. It was so unique that I had to go for it.

    You then choose a protein (chicken, steak, falafel, tuna, grilled veggies), 3 toppings, and 2 sauces. Except the toppings are interesting... they're more like mini portions of sides that top your base (ex. quinoa tabouli and broccolini fennel salad). In the end, I chose to add the quinoa tabouli, the cucumber tomato salad, and a mix of olives and feta to my base, and chose the tomato cumin and spiced eggplant sauces.

    Out of everything I had in my bowl, I LOVED the quinoa tabouli. I seriously wish I just got an entire bowl of that (which I don't think is even an option, anyway). But my huge issue with everything else is that the taste is very bland. This includes the sauces. The tomato cumin sauce was alright, but the spiced eggplant sauce added no flavor at all.

    In addition, this place is kind of overpriced for what you get. I ended up paying about $10 with tax. Maybe that's the price you pay for "organic" and "fresh"?

    Agno might still be perfecting the recipes since they recently opened up, but I doubt I'll be returning here anytime soon.

    On a random note, the dessert looks amazing. But at $4 for a pistachio butter cup the size of a Reese's, I had to pass...

    Chestnut is such a weird street. If it were a food I'd liken it to an Italian stromboli. The Schuylkill and the Delaware are the doughie ends but after a few bites the ish really starts to hit fan. Between Front St. and 24th you'll find everything from a spitting, insult slinging homeless girl,  to a high end department store with valet parking for your Bentley. You'll also find my office at 16th and inside you'll find me on a soap box talking about the dangers of GMO and pesticides and how there needs to be more organic options for lunch. Well complain and you shall receive.

    I read about Agno on Foobooz and after some "misunderstanding" about what was for dinner boo and I ended up here. I had a nice long discussion with the lovely young lady standing outside about what she recommends and learned a little about the process. First off everything is organic, gluten free, and there are vegan options as well. Boom, the holy triumphant of 2013. Also everything is made fresh as needed. Getting slow on chicken, time to cook up some more.

    I went last night for dinner and today for lunch so here's the rundown, both days I got my food on a bed of lettuce, last night I got the beef, braised in a tomato based sauce to perfection, today I got the chicken, juicy and delicious. With the beef I got the carrots with mint, sounds weird but amazing, the quinoa tabouli, and white bean salad, great but blended in a bit with the other flavors Then hummus and lemon tahini sauce on top. On the chicken I got kale salad, it was sweet and delicious, roasted cauliflower, and broccolini with fennel salad then topped it with tzatziki, it was such an amazing flavor.

    Hopefully that wasn't too overwhelming but both meals were very filling and this is coming from a guy that eats 30-40 buffalo wings in a sitting (assuming there's a Flyers game on). Inside it looks really cool and modern, like a mix of Shake Shak and Fette Sau. The workers are so nice as well and not pretentious in the least. Such a great addition to the city lunch quick food scene!!

    Listed in $10 Paleo Meals - Center City

    We need one of these in every neighborhood.

    Super quick, filling, reasonably priced (~$10), real food.  Love it.  You can even hang out there with a bottle of wine.  This is a place where vegetarians, vegans, paleo, and plain old normal people can unite.  Who the hell doesn't like Mediterranean food??

    There are a couple big circular tables and long bench style tables.  Cool spot for a group.  Also -- peeps are often there with laptops or books -- seems like a decent place to study or do some work.

    The concept is like a Mediterranean Chipotle:

    1.  Choose your base:  Baby spinach, black rice, or gluten free wrap.  I dig the spinach.

    2.  Choose your protein:  Braised shredded steak (awesome), chicken, tuna (think tuna steak, cooked all the way through, broken up, and served cold), baked falafel (damn good), or just veg.  Extra protein is under $2 -- not always necessary, but great when extra hungry.

    2.  Choose your veg:  Load up with three toppings.  There are hot and cold.  I love the cauliflower (hot), roasted carrots (hot) and beets (cold).  I fell for the broccolini several times but was continually disappointed that it was very "stemmy".

    3.  Choose your sauce:  You get two.  I'm stuck on lemon tahini + tzatziki.  The balsamic is great too.

    Overall places like this make the world (at least Philly) a better place to live.  Cooking can be annoying.  This is pretty damn close to home cooked.
