    Listed in The Highlight Reel, Top Restaurants, Philly Bars, Burger Time, Fries

    With Village Whiskey coming up on so many people's list for best burger and fires in the city, its ALWAYS been inevitable that I'd be coming here so that I could add it to my lists for both on Yelp! And both along with the service and pickles more than exceeded my expectations!

    Village Whiskey is one of those places that I've been trying to get into for close to 2 years now. But for just about every single occasion that I've previously tried I couldn't bear to wait for the 30-60 minute wait that  would have been involved to get in. But it finally took the good karma of a lunch bonding session with my dad to get in, and as soon as we did I understood the reason behind the waiting. Between the few marble tables, and classic wooden barscape, the interior of VW is pretty freaking tiny and packed uncomfortably together; which is the only reason that it gets 4 stars.

    Other than the packed interior the first thing that I noticed was the attentive service that comes with every Garces establishment. From filling water to giving good suggestions and information they never missed a beat. And from the start of sampling the house selection of pickles which was Salsify, red onions, and a fresh kalamata olive spread to go with the bread; we quickly noticed the excellence of the food.

    From the get go my intention was to savor a burger and their incredible duck fat fries. But I didn't start things off with their legendary Maple Bourbon Glazed Cipollini onion and foie gras topped Whiskey King just yet. Instead I took full advantage of the customization that the Village Burger yielded by adding applewood bacon, Rogue Bleu Cheese, and truffled portebello mushrooms to created the perfect bacon blue burger. And the end result added up to my 2nd pick (Still undisputed champion - yelp.com/biz/sketch-phil…) for best burger in Philly.

    Add in one of my top 5 contenders for best Philly fries, an awesome raw bar selection, and a killer selection of whiskeys and cocktails; and you have a Garces destination that's worthy of a visit!

    Honestly, the food is good, but was definitely outweighed by the long wait time, mediocre service, and tiny restaurant. Perhaps it was my mistake for coming on a Friday at 6 PM. Thank goodness I only came with one other person though, otherwise the experience probably would have been even worse!

    So here goes my own rant about the wait (I know almost everyone has one at this point!)... My friend and I were rushing to get here before 6 since we figured a lot of people would come in then, but the place was already packed. The hostess estimated our wait time at 60-75 minutes, and she offered to take our mobile number so she could text us when a table opened up. So my friend and I decided to walk to Center City and shop around a bit and then head back in 45-60 minutes. I was in the middle of trying on shoes when I checked my phone and saw I had a text from Whiskey Village only about 20 minutes after we had left! The text said I had 15 minutes to confirm, and I read it 12 minutes late, so I immediately texted back that we were coming, but I don't know if they saw the text. By the time we had rushed back, they had given the table to someone else. The hostess said she had called my phone and not gotten an answer--except that she said she would text us and that we could text back within the 15 minutes, and also she texted back way earlier than we had expected! I've never seen a wait time be so off. So she told us we could wait another 30 minutes for a spot, even after we had texted her and ran back. We decided to just stick around this time in case we missed it again. About forty minutes later, we finally got a seat at a table.

    Village Whiskey is very long and narrow and overall just extremely tiny and overcrowded. There are perhaps 15 seats at the bar and 12 seats at the high top booths, and then a bunch of people stand and wait at the back counter where they will probably serve you water (whoop-dee-doo!). You have to "excuse me" your way through the place, but at least the booths are extremely comfortable!

    The service is extremely meh. I know the hostess likely does not enjoy being the bearer of bad news, but she seems desensitized and not extremely apologetic. The servers have a reasonable number of people to take care of, but I guess it's pretty difficult to get through the restaurant with all the people crowding at the sides and back. Their system just doesn't work because they save tables for 15 minutes and sometimes it's just a waste of time and people don't end up coming back when there are people waiting in the back for those tables. They should allow people to pre-order or something! I also saw a decent amount of wasted time as the servers did not quickly handle the credit card payments of multiple tables. At a place as crowded as Village Whiskey, I would have imagined the service to be more efficient in order to maximize customer flow and volume... At least they were decently attentive with water at the beginning though.

    Okay, finally on to the food. I cannot deny that the food has potential, but we definitely did not enjoy everything we got. The servers are pretty used to people sharing food, so they at least ask you whether you would like to split each item--and the chef cuts it for you in the back. My friend and I ordered the Whiskey King burger, BBQ Pork Sammy sandwich, and the Duck Fat Fries. I really enjoyed the Duck Fat Fries, buns of both sandwiches, the fried pickles from the BBQ Pork Sammy, and the meat patty of the Whiskey King burger. Everything else was overrated, and we also did not enjoy some of the things we ordered. I highly recommend that you LOVE whiskey when you come in because I didn't realize the taste of whiskey to be so strong in about half the bites of my burger (plus, they serve a countless number of bourbons at the full bar). We also found the Whiskey King burger to be expensive, especially since we ended up not liking the foie gras. It's a good burger, but I've had better.

    If you are adamant on trying this restaurant and judging for yourself, I suggest you get here as early as possible and either drink at the bar or plan for some shopping. In my opinion, it's only worth it if there is no line, you get a booth, or you really enjoy whiskey.

    I had wanted to get a burger at Village Whiskey for quite some time. It's just one of those things where desire and opportunity had not met.

    The night started with meeting up with a buddy for some drinks. It was one bar than another. No, not that kind of bar hopping. I actually only had two beers and it was only two bars. Two of the guys took off for dinner reservations and left three of us to figure it out until we met up later.

    As I often do, I pulled out my iPhone, opened up my Yelp ap, and looked around to see what was in the area. And there it was. The Village Whiskey. The ten blocks would have seemed a lot closer if it was not raining pretty hard. But I was not about to let a little water get in the way of my chance to introduce my desire to this opportunity.

    Lucky for me, the other two guys followed my lead. I didn't exactly disclose how far it was. In fact, I just said, it's a few blocks, not that far. Am I bit of a selfish jerk? Maybe. But sometimes you need to lead your friends to what is best for them. (Later, my friends thanked me for the awesomeness I introduced them to.)

    Upon our arrival, we started off by entering the wrong bar. Our stay there was short lived and we made our way over to the Village Whisky.

    Then came the bad news. It was a two hour wait for seating. What what what? And, then, the good news. But you can try the bar, which is first come first served. We found one seat. The guys were a bit skeptical, but I convinced them to give it a few minutes.

    As I made my way to the bathroom, it became clear why the wait was so long.  There were hardly any tables. When they went into business, clearly their business model did not expect such crowds for dinner.

    By the time I got back from the bathroom, the bartender who had us on watch, let me know three seats were opening, and we quickly shuffled over to them. The stars were now aligned.

    I had a beer and some whisky, both which were very good. They had some nice brews on tap and quite the selection of whisky. While waiting for my burger to come out, we had some deviled eggs. They were delish.

    I had fancied myself the Chinese New Year Burger. It's made up of char sui glazed beef, Chinese bacon, asian slaw, and spicy cucumber aioli. We got some fries to go along with our burgers. The fries were tasty, but this review is about what makes this place special, the burger.

    It was like slow motion, the good kind of slow motion, like in a romance movie when two lovers first make eye contact and they just know. There it was, on a white platter, my burger, my new love.

    The meat on this burger was mouth watering good. It practically melted in my mouth. It's the kind of burger you don't really want to swallow. You just want to chew and enjoy it's tastiness forever. If you like to burn your burger, don't insult this burger delivered from heaven. Take your burning business somewhere else. But if you like to actually taste your meat, lick your chops and head over to Village Whisky.

    We went on a Wednesday evening and got right in (but don't expect that on a weekend!).

    Decor/atmosphere: Very narrow room with a handful of tables at the entrance and a long bar. Dark, mysterious, sets the speakeasyesque 20's vibe it's going for.

    Drinks: I had what is now one of my favorite beers on as their special draught. The cocktails were interesting and very delicious as well. Prices matched the norm for the surrounding area.

    Food: To start, the duck fat fries with short ribs and cheddar cheese sauce. Verrrry rich and delicious. Real cheese sauce that nearly had the consistency of the fake stuff (which is a glorious thing and impossible for me to reproduce).

    Secondly, the burger. Ohhhhh the burger. The special burger in November was the thanksgiving burger. A perfectly seasoned and cooked medium slab of the best burger meat I have ever had sat atop a perfectly soft, flavorful, absorbent bun that somehow managed to hold together perfectly under the weight of the juicy burger. Sage, some sort of cranberry relish, and a heavy dose of crack induced mouth f'ing finished off what is certifiably the best burger I have ever had. This burger ruined me. I have thought about it at least once a week (usually more) for the past 4 months. I couldn't even eat another burger for 2 months and they will never taste as good knowing what exists out there in village whiskey fantasy land.

Specialties

A ground-to-order Angus Beef Burger, a Veggie Burger, a Bison Burger or an Vienna All-Beef Hot Dog with all the trimmings are each served alongside duck fat French fries and Sly Fox cheddar sauce.  Burgers are topped with Village Whiskey's house-made thousand-island dressing, Bibb lettuce, beefsteak tomato and a potato-beer bun, with optional toppings including bacon, caramelized onion, truffles, artichokes and a selection of cheeses.

As one of the most comprehensive collections of its namesake spirit available in Philadelphia, Village Whiskey features a veritable library of 80 to 100 varieties of whiskey, bourbon, rye and scotch from Scotland, Canada, Ireland, United States and even a few Japanese options.

History

Established in 2009.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
