    This family-style restaurant was just what my boyfriend and I were looking for last night for dinner. The cocktails were strong and delicious! I got the caipirinha, which had a watermelon ice cube in it. The drink had a great sweet finish. My boyfriend had the birch beer and the acai daiquiri. The birch beer tasted just like a root beer, and the daiquiri had great strawberry flavor, but the homemade grenadine had a strong flavor too.

    For dinner, we started with the Szechuan Shoestring Fries. They were more like potato threads, perfectly fried (not overdone despite their tiny size) and the hot mustard sauce was very good. This was the one dish I would have liked to see elevated more, however. The fries were great and it was impressive to see them stacked almost a foot high, but the mustard tasted like the hot mustard I get at my local Chinese take-out.

    Definitely get the French Onion soup dumplings and the cheesesteak eggrolls! The hearty flavor of the French Onion soup (sweetened just a touch with brown sugar) balanced the stronger Gruyere cheese flavor. The eggrolls were different than a standard Chinese eggroll, but they are worth a walk on the adventurous side. They held a lot of steak, cheese, and a few onions and peppers for flavor. The fried onions and Siracha ketchup added just the punch this cheesesteak eggroll needed!

    Overall, it was a great dinner, and I can't wait to get back to Philly to go again!

    Some buzzwords for you: trendy. Asian-fusion. Diner. Drunk food.

    These and other thoughts were running through my mind when I stopped at Continental on the late end of a night out (like the "ten minutes before the kitchen closed" kind of late). Our IDs were checked at the door and were seated right away (of course, because it was late). We were told that the kitchen would be closing in ten minutes, but we weren't rushed in any way to get our food orders in, which was nice. I ordered the udon noodle soup, which came out steaming hot and just the right size for a late-night dish (too small for dinner, but larger than a cup). I thought the dish was A-OK and the hummus my friend ordered was pretty good as well.

    Overall, it was a pleasant end to the night, but it did take longer to settle the check than usual, even though the place was nearly empty. I very much enjoyed watching the drunk people stumble out of Cuba Libre, so I wasn't complaining much about that.

Specialties

This stainless steel 60's-style diner offers an exciting menu of global cuisine, including crab pad thai, cheesesteak eggrolls and lobster mashed potatoes. Famed for its sensational cocktails, the extensive bar features a wide variety of libations and over 15 types of martinis.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
