    Listed in Definitive Beer Guide

    My first experience at Nodding Head Brewery & Restaurant many years ago was not very stellar, however, all of my subsequent experiences in recent years have been great.

    Most recently, stopped by with 2 friends for some lunch and brews before heading over to work a venue at the Philly Roller Girls. We really enjoyed the hand crafted beer which at 5$ and change is a decent price given the location. We had 2 pints and enjoyed the Grog, BPA, and Daryl the most. For food, the vegetarian amongst us had polenta puttanesca which looked good. I had the chicken wings with a special of the day sesame teriyaki sauce. For 10$ and change it was a good amount of wings and served wet (not dry like most places). My other friend got the buffalo sauce on his wings and it looked like he may have received 2 or 3 pieces of chicken less than me. Its also important to note that in the past, their wings were complete huge pieces of chicken - leg and thigh, etc. Now their wings are standard sized 'buffalo' wings, that was a disappointment. However, the wings were tasty and I would have them again. Water service was stellar and we always had enough liquid after walking around the historical areas of Philly beforehand.

    Further, the atmosphere is really cozy and it was great seeing into the kitchen where an employee was pulling pork (wonder was dish this is used in).

    BEER: 4 Stars

    SERVICE: 3 stars

    The beer here is thoughtfully crafted and the beer menu rotates relatively frequently. There's always something on the menu that gets my taste buds jumping. The prices are reasonable and I really love the ambiance here. Lots of tables, room to move, and dim lighting that makes for a great casual atmosphere. There are also loads of bobble heads throughout the place, hence the name.

    The food is sub-par bar food. Nothing memorable, but alright. I certainly wouldn't go out of my way to get food here.

    Server was pretty inattentive, but I eventually got everything I needed. ::shrug::

    Beer is the real star of Nodding Head, as it should be. If you're not into beer I'd probably bring your patronage elsewhere.

    My husband and I visited Nodding Head on our one day to spend in Philly. We came here intending to only grab a drink, but ended up staying for lunch because the food looked so enticing! My husband tried the Zephyr and the 3C microbrews. He enjoyed the Zephyr and said it was one of the most interesting beers he's ever tried with the herbal finish to it. He's also a big hops fan so the 3C was right up his alley.

    For food we ordered some chicken fingers and the Old Bay Mussels. The chicken fingers were good... a nice crispy outside to them but alas they were just chicken fingers. The Old Bay Mussels now.... holy crap they were amazing. $9.95 and you get an entire POUND of mussels steamed in beer, garlic, butter, and Old Bay Seasoning and it also comes with fries and bread for dipping in the left over sauce. The mussels were perfection. Garlicy, buttery, beery deliciousness. I ate every single one and licked my fingers clean. I wanted another pound of them but I restrained myself. There must have been an entire clove of garlic in those but I happen to be a huge garlic lover so this was not a problem for me. However, vampires beware. The fries were also very tasty and I really loved the dipping sauce that came with them. Not your standard ketchup variety. There are a few other mussel orders you could try, like a jerk seasoning one and few others. But I highly highly recommend the Old Bay. They have to be the best mussels I've ever had.

    Our service was great too. Attentive, nice, and gave great recommendations. It was pretty slow in there on this particular Friday afternoon (Black Friday) so we had our pick of tables and sat right by a window with a view of the street. Everyone, including customers seated next to us, was very friendly and we enjoyed chatting with them.

    I highly recommend a visit to this microbrewery if you are in the area. It's a fantastic time!

    I am less impressed every time I go here. I think it was the first brewery restaurant that I went to in Philly, so it was at the top of my list of pubs for a while; but now I think it is a big let-down whenever I end up there.

    First, it always smells like an old dishrag in the restaurant. My friend told me that is probably because of the beer brewing, but I'm not so sure; I feel like every time I sit down at a table, it was just wiped down with the last dishtowel on Earth. It is all I can smell. Not appetizing at all.

    Second, they really crap out on the vegetarian options. They just aren't good. The appetizers are OK (although the edamame is way too greasy), but the few meal options they have are sub-par. I've said it before and I'll say it again: roasted red peppers do not a vegetarian sandwich make. Every other restaurant I visit seems to have this in mind: "I know how to make a vegetarian sandwich! I'll take out the meat and put roasted red peppers there instead"! Please, please, PLEASE, spare me the roasted red peppers; They are gross.

    My final complaint is a new one for this place. I was there the other night, and intrigued by the offering of "house made caesar dressing," I ordered a caesar salad. The dressing was disgusting. It was like they just dumped a bottle of mayonnaise over a bed of lettuce. In case there are other cooks reading this: you DO NOT make caesar dressing with mayonnaise. Caesar dressing does have egg in it, yes, but that is not the same as mayonnaise.  Mayonnaise is not the quick solution to a good caesar dressing. Because of that, it was probably the worst caesar salad I had ever had.

    The only thing that redeems this place is the beer. So go there for Happy Hour, maybe order some fries and hummus dip, but avoid sitting at a "clean" table and get your meal someplace else.
