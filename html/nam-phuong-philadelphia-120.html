    So, I walked in Nam Phuong really skeptical because I'm a huge Pho Hoa fan (across the street). I really wanted to try something different so I journeyed in, sat down and looked at the menu- cheaper than Pho Hoa (about $2 less on some meals). So, I ordered the chicken rice vermicelli bowl (with broccoli added) and I must say it was surprisingly good! I wouldn't say it's any better or worse than the same dish from Pho Hoa but it was very tasty. I also decided to get a Vietnamese iced coffee and that was just as delicious!

    I was talking to 2 ladies eating at the table next to us and they told us that their dishes were outstanding, I'm not sure what exactly they were eating but it's good to know that others enjoy their food as much as I did.

    The only reason I didn't give this restaurant 5 stars is because some of the servers could have been a little nicer, they could have cranked the heat up a little and their free hot tea had NO flavor.

    Overall I'd recommend this place to anyone in the philadelphia area looking for some good, authentic Vietnamese food!

    Based upon all of your glowing reviews, Mike and I stopped into Nam Phuong for dinner a few weekends ago. WTF is with the portions here? SO HUGE. We started off with some veggie spring rolls which were pretty generic, but tasty. Although their fried texture but cold temperature was dubious - were they sitting out or something? I prefer the mouth-scalding temperature Vietnam's spring rolls are served at, but I digress. We also split a vegetarian crepe which tasted like Vietnamese hashbrowns with lots of veggies and tofu. It was the size of a large calzone. Like, ENORMOUS. For an entree, I went with the sweet & sour broth bowl. I was presented with a giant bowl filled to the brim with piping hot broth, veggies and floating bits of firm, silky tofu. I was only able to finish about a third of the bowl, but I felt totally satisfied. It wasn't the most amazing dish I ever had, I wasn't crazy about the flavor of the broth, but whatever. I strayed from what I would typically order and that's always a crapshoot.

    Their vegetarian menu is pretty generous considering it's hard to find a ton of super veg-friendly establishments on Washington. The ambiance is just so-so, and I will prefer my go-to (sup Vietnam) but the prices and portion size are what make Nam Phuong a solid destination for Vietnamese food in South Philly.

    This is not only my favorite Vietnamese restaurant in Philly, it's my favorite outside of Houston. Heck, it's up there with my Houston favorites.

    Everything in incredibly fresh - the salads are divine, and the spring rolls wrapped in lettuce leaves are crispy, slightly spicy and addictive. They have an extensive menu, and almost everything I've tried has ranged from very good to awesome. There are a few straight forward stir-fries on the menu that while good, are not as exciting as their other offerings, but most dishes are home runs. Their family meals are a great value, and showcase some stand-out dishes - their sweet and sour fish soup has a great balance of flavors, and their clay pot fish is like buttah.

    I forgot to order he "Three Delight" Ba Vi, and now I'm fantasizing about it. It's a perverse amount of meat, but it's crazy good, and a great excuse to bring a group of friends down to Washington Avenue.

Welcome to Nam Phuong restaurant.

For the last 20 years, Nam Phuong restaurant has transformed itself originally from a small restaurant into a well-known restaurant today in the north-east region of the U.S. At Nam Phuong, we know how important our guests are, and we always strike our very best to make our guests come back time after time. And for the last 20 years, we always carry one single goal: best quality with best services and best prices.

Located south of Philadelphia, Nam Phuong has been famous for Vietnamese delicacies and authentic cuisine. Whether it is appetizer, meat, sea food, or vegetarian, our recipes always contain the best quality and healthiest ingredients that are carefully selected from the best sources on the market. Our wide variety of delicious Vietnamese dishes combined with our services will meet your highest expectation in Vietnamese dining experience.
