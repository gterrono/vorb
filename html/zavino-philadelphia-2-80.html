    Love the space, food and drinks. Go to Zavino, you'll love it.

    There, I summed it up for everyone.
    But if you insist, here's a brief reason why I love this place.

    Their pizza is totally mouthwatering just thinking about it. Margherita and Polpettini to be exact. Your order is fresh baked to order right before your eyes(if you are sitting at the bar, then this is even more true). Love, Love the texture of the pizza dough. It's soft but not soggy, with just the right amount of crispyness. YUM!

    I don't even want to get into how much I love the drinks here. Whether it's their seasonal cocktail with perfect garnish or a glass of featured wine(yesterday's featured wine was Silver Oak 2005...the best!). My particular favorite cocktail is the peach sangria - it's perfect for the summer time.

    It's a good place to go for lunch, after work, late night...you get the drift.

    P.S. I know I have plenty of love for Zavino as you all have read but this all started with their Roasted Beet salad...it's a MUST try!!!

    This is the most recent restaurant I've crossed off of my list of places to try. I went with my boyfriend for our anniversary, a spontaneous suggestion by moi.

    Zavino is tiny, very tiny, so when we got there, it was packed. BUT it was great because the host took my name down (in his iPad) and my phone got a text message. "Your wait time is 20 minutes. We will text you again when a table is ready." What?!? Awesome.

    My boyfriend and I continued to walk around the neighborhood, and waited for a text. We went back to the restaurant out of bordem, so I never got the text, but we sat at the bar and ordered some wine.

    We sat down at our table and I ordered the butternut squash soup (which I order from anywhere if they have it on the menu) and he ordered pita with olive oil for starters. Delicious!

    As for the pizza, we went with the plain jane option and ordered a Margherita. It was the perfect size for two, and absolutely delicious. Brick oven pizza is the way to go.

    All in all, we spent about $20 on wine, and $20 on food. It's a great spend and GREAT find if you want to eat cheap and delicious.

    I've been to Zavino twice and both times have been divine. Their menu changes seasonally, but their pizzas seem to remain constant (and consistently good, from what I hear).     I would have been happy to write it off there and simply say the food tasted awful and nothing else made up for it so I would never go back, but when I saw Kai's review on this site, I knew it was more than a coincidence and I have to affirm her experience - a little later that same night I had an interesting bout of gastrointestinal distress, which I must add is also incredibly rare for me.  I always make sure to have a couple glasses of wine or alcohol in some form when I go out to try and stave off the possibility of such things happening, but whatever was going on here was more potent than the ability of alcohol to neutralize it.  Which is really quite terrifying when you think about it.  

    I'm not even sure where to start at guessing what the sources of the problems for this place are, but I can tell you the result is that this place is nothing special at best and at worst a place to be completely avoided.
