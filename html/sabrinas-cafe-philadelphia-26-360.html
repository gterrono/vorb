    Oh, Sabrina's. What happened? The first time I went here several years ago, I fell in love with this place. The atmosphere was great, albeit on the verge of being overly hipsterish, the food was amazing (I had never had an omelet stuffed with sauteed apples and melted cheddar before), and the service was top notch. I would go back now and then, often trying a delicious salad off their specials menu. I still remember this one amazing salad I had one spring day with strawberries, all sorts of fresh greens, and a homemade raspberry vinaigrette.

    But these days, something has changed. I don't know if they have new cooks, but the food just isn't as good. The apple cheddar omelet I once loved is now made with some rubbery yellow substance that used to be eggs. The apples, which once were sauteed to the point of tasting like those found in an apple pie, are now just a bit softer than raw. The onions on the homefries that used to be dark and caramelized are now barely glossy, as though they were an afterthought.

    When I was there this past weekend, the service was still quick, and the portions were still large and reasonably priced, but the atmosphere was undesirable. A group of waiters and waitresses stood around chatting right near our table, discussing their previous night's activities and the like. One waiter well within earshot described a place he had gone to that was so gross, he'd have rather licked a toilet seat. This is not what I want to overhear when I'm eating food that is already far below what I expect from a restaurant I've been to several times before.

    I'm not saying I won't go to Sabrina's again--in fact, I'm sure I'll end up there again at some point. But they are no longer my go-to restaurant of choice when a friend from out of town visits and asks, "Where's a great place to eat?" Because, frankly, I no longer consider Sabrina's to be great. They're just okay.

    Listed in Can I Get Some Dinner with That Brunch?

    After cross-yelping different lists for brunch along the eastern seaboard, I landed upon Sabrina's and reviews of its amazing, mountainous stuffed french toast.  I hungrily tore through review after review extolling the french toast: the size of it, that it was dessert under a different guise, the totally-worth-the-wait value, the cute vibe and decor.

    I went in a group of 5 with a baby.  Because we forgot to call ahead and put our name on the list, we had to sit through a wait of decent length while one of us had to find parking for a bigger-sized car and the rest of us tried to keep the baby from getting too restless.  After the fraught wait, we were seated in the front indoor dining area next to some really bad BO.  Normally, my nose is too clogged to pick up on such things but the lack of open windows did us in.  

    Despite all the circumstances leading up to our meal, my friends enjoyed their orders.  On our table, we had 1.5 orders of stuffed french toast, huevos rancheros, the pancake special, fish tacos and a dish with toast and eggs that I can't recall.  The only party pooper turned out to be me!  What is there not to love about the ridiculous version of one of my favorite foods, you ask?  That would be the ridiculous part.  The sweet cheese on top of the overly sweet challah bread made each successful bite harder to swallow.  It was all way too rich for my blood.  The ginormous size quickly went from something delightful to something to be feared.  No matter how many pieces I gave away and how many bites I took, it didn't shrink in size.  Dare I say that it was a french toast nightmare?  Never!  In my book, french toast can do wrong. Overzealous dressings of sugar and fat can.  

    So, wise Yelper, take these high praises of Sabrina's ridic french toast with a grain of salt.  You just might be the second person to drink the bah-humbug haterade.  If you insist on finding out for yourself, make sure you order other things, preferably savory, as well.  Despite all my protests, I wouldn't mind going back since: 1) the specials are always changing, 2) that one bite of fish taco I had was ridic (in the good way), 3) I'm curious about their vegan dishes, and 4) the vibe/decor is indeed cute.
