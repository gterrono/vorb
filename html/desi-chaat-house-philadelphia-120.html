History

Established in 2009.

Chaat is Indian and Pakistan street food. It is a blend of sweet and sour, and salted to absolute taste. People enjoy the Chaat so much that it has become an integral part of the menu in star hotels. Chaat is a favorite snack of all young and old Indians.
The chaat variants are all based on fried dough, with various other ingredients. The chaat is a mixture of potato pieces, crispy fried bread Dahi vada or Dahi Bhalla ("Bhalla" in Hindi), gram or chickpeas and tangy-salty spices, with sour home-made Indian chilli and Saunth (dried ginger and tamarind sauce), fresh green coriander leaves and yogurt for garnish, but other popular variants included Aloo tikkis (garnished with onion, coriander, hot spices and a dash of curd), bhel puri, dahi puri, panipuri, dahi vada, papri chaat, and sev puri.

Desi Chaat House proudly serves you combination of classic and traditional favorites. Most of chaat chat adopted to cater to modern trends and healthy eating.
