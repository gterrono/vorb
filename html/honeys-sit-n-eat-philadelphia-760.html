    What do you get when you cross a 1950's country barn diner with hipster southern Jewish hospitality? It's hard to say exactly, but Honey's is the result. As many reviewers have noted, it is predominantly known and celebrated as one of the "go-to" brunch spots in Northern Liberties.

    Why? Well, for one they serve bottomless cups of La Colombe coffee, plenty of southern breakfast staples with a twist, and just breakfast classics in general. In the two times I've gone, I've tried their Eggs Benedict, which was perfect with a side of home fries and their Toad in the Hole. I was sorely tempted by the Honey Cristo, Biscuits and Sausage with Gravy, Challah French Toast, and Breakfast Quesadilla. My boyfriend ordered very wisely the "Breakfast Bomb" which is an omelet stuffed with choice of breakfast meat and cheese, then wrapped in a pancake. Seriously, that has to be the best creative breakfast item, because if you can't decide on either an omelet or pancakes, hello you get both in one delicious dish!

    Also appreciated are the amazing vegetarian options, bagel platter and made by your bubbe potato latkes. This is one original place, but don't feel as if you are not cool or hipster enough to enjoy this treasure. Everyone feels welcome and the ambiance is very communal and friendly. Honey, give me some "sugar" because I'll be back soon.

    P.S. Love the rustic wooden caddy with not 1 but 4 different kinds of hot sauce.

    Tip: Cash only, but ATM conveniently located inside if you forget. Also be sure to get there early as this place has a line out the door most weekends after 9 a.m. You can take a nap later.

    Came in at around 11 on a Saturday and the wait was about 40 minutes.

    The three stars I'm giving is really being awarded to the potato latkes. The outside is fried to a crisp, deep golden brown complexion. The inside has CHEESE! SO good! You can never go wrong with anything involving cheese and potatoes! (TIP - When you order any brunch dish, it comes with your choice of home fries or latkes, and white toast, wheat toast or challah).

    Sadly, the rest of my dish could not compete with the latkes. I ordered the chicken fried steak with gravy. It was so unbelievably bland. Not even the mound of salt I dumped on it was able to save it. It reminded me of school lunch - both the texture and the flavor.

    Upon the recommendation of one of the employees, we also ordered the mac n' cheese pancakes. It's (a tad spicy) mac n' cheese mixed into the pancake. It sounds weird and it was. The mac n' cheese was not cheesy enough. It was interesting though. It's probably not a dish I'd order again if I went back but if you've never tried it, you should.

    The two stars I knocked off were because of all the flies. I'm not sure if it was because we sat right next to the kitchen but there were flies EVERYWHERE. We spent the whole meal swatting them away. If I have to compete with insects for food, it's definitely not my idea of a relaxing Saturday brunch.

    In terms of food, Honey's is my favorite brunch place in Philly currently. I take one star off because on a nice sunny day, I like natural light with my brunch and the interior of Honey's is severely lacking of any natural light. The other option would be to sit outside, which I did, but they've had an issue with a potent sewage smell nearby for nearly a year according to the waiter. I don't fault them for the smell as it's out of their control but it sucks to be in a dark place on a Sunday for brunch when it's 75 and sunny outside. It also doesn't have an effect on the interior either.

    Now for a nice transition from the potent sewage smell to Honey's delicious food. It was a special when I last ordered it but it definitely belongs on their regular menu. You must try the Mac & Cheese Pancakes if they serve them. We got them as a share plate for four people and we devoured it. It was amazingly delicious and I don't even like pancakes or sweets that much. The Honey Cristo and Toad in a Hole were also really good. The Challah bread that they use is deliciously soft and soaks up egg yolk perfectly.

    After having Honey's once, it instantly became my favorite brunch place followed by Green Eggs Cafe. My next step is to try the lunch menu and hopefully have the Mac & Cheese Pancakes again. I'll be sure to sit inside next time to avoid the smell, given that our noses place a large part of our taste.

    Listed in Biscuits & gravy, Get Out Of Town! Philly Edition

    If I lived in Philly, Honey's would be my breakfast spot. Ok, so I know technically, Honey's is the only breakfast spot I've been to in Philadelphia, but it was just *that* good. I guess it'll do that it's my sister's breakfast spot. She can have that.

    We waited about 20 minutes for a table. Then prompted served coffees that never had less than 3 sips out of them for the rest of the meal on account of the super attentive servers giving you refill after refill. I was happy to be caffeinated, and bonus, the coffee was delicious!

    My sister had a bevy of recommendations for us. I settled on Jeb's Open Face Biscuit Sandwich -- veggie sausage & gravy atop a split biscuit and a side of grits. The eggs were cooked over easy. Gosh, do I love a broken yolk over my sausage & biscuits. It added a bit of moisture to the biscuit that was needed. This is a dish where you want a piece of everything with each bite -- a bit of gravy, some egg, yolk on the biscuit, and a nibble of sausage. Mmm. Breakfast heaven. For a second, I thought wait, am I eating real sausage? Not that that's a bad thing, but it tasted so meaty, probably due to the seasoning which was a hint spicy. The grits needed a bit more salt and I think I realized I prefer them with cheese.

    Stuffed and happy is how we left Honey's...

    And then we came back the next day.

    It didn't matter that we were just visiting for the weekend. We wanted nothing but Honey's before we hit the road. They came through again. This time I ordered a simple  two eggs over easy with a potato latke, cornbread, and pork sausage. Woa, lots of food again. I thought I was keeping it simple. They kicked the traditional breakfast order up a notch with their unique sides. The cornbread was amazing, crumbly, and delicious, cut in half and warmed on the flat top grill. Slap on a little butter, and voila. Favorite of this meal -- the latke. Super crisp on the outside and mashed potato-y goodness on the inside. My sister happened to order the applesauce so I stole a spoonful to add to the latke, just to see what it's like and I loved it. I'll be remaking that at home.

    Honey, you're my girl!

Yes, I hate to have to admit, that I stayed away way to long. The only difference between my first and recent visit was the line. The food was just as good as I remembered.

    My friend and I were on our way to another eatery in Northern Liberties for lunch when we decided to make a detour to Honey's. Having already had breakfast earlier that morning, I considered going with the brisket I read about. Suddenly something came over me, as the cup of coffee I ordered brought back memories of that Sunday brunch two years ago; so I decided to go with breakfast. I love breakfast more than any man. I could eat it for any meal of the day but if walking into your establishment makes me want to eat breakfast just a few hours after already having breakfast, then you know your food is good.

    The biscuit sandwich was awesome. How pleasant it was to eat a fresh biscuit sandwich that was not created in a microwave at a fast-food chain. My friend ordered the Breakfast Bomb, a giant pancake filled with scrambled eggs and breakfast meat. My issues with syrup food mixing with other foods prevented me from ordering that dish. But I have to say it looked mighty scrumptious and quite filling.

    I need to visit this place a little more often. I hope to try the lunch and dinner food options someday, but I already have my eyes set on a few other breakfast items on my next trip.
