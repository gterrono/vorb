    After doing Yelp! research on Philly it was determined that Sabrina's was a must-try, and I'm so glad we did.  We didn't make it out there until late one evening, it was a surprise trip, we had a long day and decided we wanted something to eat before we headed back to our room.  We called prior to be sure that they would be open until 10pm and the perky voice on the line assured us they were, so we drove the hour to what we hoped was to be our food haven and got there around 9:15-9:30.

    Normally when you get to a restaurant and they're preparing to close the staff isn't happy to see you walk through the door.  Not here.  The hostess was happy so we thought perhaps that was the person who answered our phone call, but then we were served water and that person was happy and perky so we thought maybe she was the one on the other end of the phone line, and then our exuberantly smiling waitress came... we decided they were all happy and perky and not at all disturbed that we walked in so late.  

    Whomever was the happy perky person on the other end of the phone had already let us know that the brunch menu was not available at that hour, so that wasn't a surprise to us.  Although there were some very interesting looking items on the brunch menu we had more than enough selection to choose from and went with the barking Chihuahua, the French toast, a veggie burger, and an omelet.  Everything came out quick and hot and wonderful.  As there were three of us and we had gotten the French toast to share there's always a chance that someone's not going to enjoy their meal and hog all of the French toast but that was not the case here - we all loved our main dishes so much and the portions were so large that we ended up taking out the rest of our meals and an almost entire serving of French toast.  And yes, even though we were there eating past closing, no one was grumpy, tired or even mentioned a word.  The staff was tremendous and we enjoyed our experience all the way till the very end.

    We heated everything up the next day and had it for breakfast.  Heaven part II, this was it.  No wonder there are three Sabrina locations in Philly, if I lived here, I would keep them in business as well.  A tasty must-eat!

Specialties

Based off the same love for food, quality ingredients, and good friends that flourished Sabrina's Cafe in the Italian Market; Sabrina's Cafe and Spencer's Too hopes to satisfy even the loneliest of tummy's with behemoth portions and decadent fare no matter what time of the day it is. Checkout our Breakfast, Brunch, and Dinner. And did you know that we Cater to?

History

Established in 2007.

Sabrina's Cafe was born out of Robert & Raquel DeAbreu's desire to create a comfortable, welcoming place that served comfortable, welcoming food but with a twist. In May 2001, the first Sabrina's Café opened and was appropriately named after the couples new baby, Sabrina Isabella. With the help of impassioned regulars, Sabrina's began to outgrow it's modest upbringings and a second location was added in 2007 named Sabrina's Cafe & Spencer's Too, after their son.
