    This was my first time at Distrito and oh. my. god. is it delicious!  I had heard good things about the atmosphere, the decor, and (most importantly) the food, so I was really excited to try it - Jose did not disappoint!  

    My date and I decided to take advantage of the Chef's Tastings, the Frida Khalo option, which, at $55 per person, was expensive, but I had a gift card, so we went for it!  Worth every freakin' penny.  It was like neverending food, but in the perfect portions so that we were still hungry by the time the third course rolled around.

    This is what it entailed:

    1. Chips & guac - delicious, but I've definitely had better (hey, I'm a guacamole snob)

    2. Jicama (some kind of mango, honeydew salad with lemon sorbet) - light and refreshing
    Tuna Ceviche - I wasn't a huge fan (I don't like raw fish), but my date loved it!
    Then arrived a plate with 2 (yes, just 2) meatballs.  Let me just tell you that they were probably the most delicious meatballs that I have ever eaten. They were tender and in some kind of sauce that had a hint of chocolate in it. To die for.
    Pork Flatbread - I don't even know what this was, but it was so freakin' good. It was basically flatbread with pulled pork, cheese, this tart fig jam. Again, so delicious!

    3. Lobster (in the shell) - while this was really good, there wasn't a whole lot of meat, but it was a good portion for how much other food we had!
    Lamb Chops - Perfect. I am only a recent fan of lamb, and has me hooked. They were cooked to perfection, and again, the sauce had a hint of chocolate in it - amazing.

    4, Let's not forget dessert!  
    Frutas Con Crema - A rich, custardy dish with fruit & oats in it - sounds kind of blah, but trust me, there was nothing bland about it!  
    Churros -- OH. MY. GOD. With 2 dipping sauces: one chocolatey, the other caramely.  You will never be the same again. SO GOOD.

    I can't wait to eat here again - loved almost everything about it (the white sangria was so-so, but the watermelon margherita was YUM)!! I am hooked!!

    For the price I give Distrito three stars; let me explain.

    To begin, our server was very friendly, helpful, and professional.  He didn't intrude awkwardly while we were in mid-conversation as others might, and as we seemed to be seated in his section he was always around when we had questions for him.  

    The tortilla soup was delicious, and its presentation was fun-they actually pour it into your bowl right in front of you. Ok, maybe I'm just easy to please when it comes to this but I highly recommend it anyway.

    The atmosphere was fun, colorful, and a bit edgy. It made my wife and I feel like we were roughly 3,000 miles away from West Philly in some upscale Mexican restaurant in L.A.  However, it was also quite loud.  If you're looking for romance then this is not your place.  We sat upstairs and the acoustics there were in no way conducive to easy conversation.  Maybe we should have looked elsewhere for a quiet, romantic evening (next time we certainly will), but regardless the cafeteria-esque noise level was a bit of a turn off.

    The rest of our food was a bit bland and kind of greasy.  I've had very good Mexican food in the past that was not greasy but was still very flavorful.  So, both for the price we paid and Garces's reputation, I thought Distrito was rather unimpressive.  The tortilla soup and the free seasoned peanuts were tasty, but I'm pretty sure the rest of the food we ate was priced so high just because it was "a la Garces."  I'm glad we went, but I don't think we'll go back.

Specialties

Distrito is the new restaurant concept by Jose Garces, the chef behind the acclaimed Philadelphia restaurants Amada and Tinto. Distrito is a high-energy restaurant inspired by the spirited culture and cuisine of Mexico City. The Distrito menu features Chef Garces' fresh interpretations of classic Mexican fare and beverages include over 65 tequilas and margaritas made with fresh seasonal fruits. A striking decor includes a hot pink and bright green color scheme, a wall constructed from over 600 colorful, campy masks of lucha libre, or "free fight" professional wrestlers, and a booth crafted from a Volkswagen Beetle, a typical Mexico City taxi cab.

History

Established in 2008.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
