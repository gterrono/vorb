    We wanted a casual restaurant where we could (hopefully) sit outside on one of those crazy beautiful nights in early April. We got exactly what we had hoped for! Varga Bar does not take reservations, but we were pleased to find a few open tables outside around 8:30 on Saturday night. They even had heaters above the seats for when it got a bit chilly. We ordered drinks (I really enjoyed the Miss April, but it was very sweet) and then ordered a caesar salad, veggie burger, and soft shell crab sandwich (special for the night). The polenta croutons on the salad were PHENOMENAL. We could have used a bit more anchovy flavor, but we are weird anchovy-lovers. The veggie burger was also fantastic - the sauce that came with it truly made the sandwich. We weren't very happy with the soft shell crab sandwich - it was SUPER fried and greasy (to the point that my stomach was churning a bit after). For $17.50, I would've preferred a higher crab to batter ratio (even a smaller sandwich if it meant less breading). We will definitely come back and I can guarantee the caesar salad (with extra anchovy) will be on our order list.

    Listed in Drinking in Philly, Philly Favorites

    Varga Bar is freaking awesome. I like to think of it as a beer bar with a wine bar vibe. The decor is classy, it's got big windows overlooking the street, and it is open feeling, rather than dark and enclosed. They have a great rotating beer list, most of which is available in growlers. I like the organization of the beer list into quadrants - golden or dark, session or strong. I think that makes all kinds of sense in terms of deciding what kind of beer you're in the mood for. The food is fantastic. I've been once for dinner and once for brunch, an everything was delicious, with fresh, seasonal, high quality ingredients. I'm especially a fan of their Varga salad; the specific ingredients change seasonally, but I had it last in Fall, when it consisted of roasted butternut squash, arugula, mint, red onion, toasted pumpkin seeds, shaved parmesan, and cider-clove vinaigrette. Their weekend brunch starts at 11am; we arrived promptly and were surprised and pleased that it didn't get too crowded. We enjoyed a few of their brunch cocktails, including the "Miss January" (sparkling wine, ginger and champagne liqueurs) and "Miss March" (rye whiskey, blood orange puree, simple syrup, orange bitters). We've found their service to be friendly, and I like that they are attentive to social media. One time I tweeted about being there, and the bartender noticed and sent over a free beer. Overall this is definitely one of my favorite bars in Philly.

    Everyone was really friendly at Varga from the hostess, to the bartender, to the waitress.  We only had to wait about ten minutes for a table outside on a Friday night  with beautiful weather even though the place looked pretty crowded.  

    There is a large beer list to choose from, and a small, though interesting, cocktail list that seems to change with the seasons.  I started the evening off with Miss June, a Varga Julep, composed of bourbon, campari, fresh mint, simple syrup, and grapefruit juice.  I usually don't like bourbon alone, but with this combination it was refreshing and didn't have too much bite.  Later I tried the Miss August, Pucker Up, composed of apple vodka, watermelon pucker, fresh lime, and club soda.  It wasn't bad, but didn't wow me.  It seemed a little weak, and I was hoping for more of a jolly rancher flavor that I've had in similar cocktail combinations before.  

    To fill our tummies after a long work day, we started off with the fried pickles since they were so highly reviewed on Yelp.  They were hot and crispy.  I also think I prefer them to Memphis Tap Room's as the ones at Varga are smaller but not slices and there is some tasty spice or flavor in the batter that I like, but can't quite detect what it is.  For my entree, I had the crab-cake sandwich with crab fries.  The sandwich was topped with crisp fresh coleslaw and the crabby patty had big hearty chunks of crab-meat in it.  The fries were just basic shoestring fries with old bay on them, but they came out hot and fresh. My significant other had the short-rib sandwich, also a generous portion, and he said it was very tasty.  We left stuffed and happy.  

    I agree with fellow Yelper Vichet O. that the place does look expensive, but the menu is surprisingly reasonable.  I'm looking forward to going back and trying other menu items, especially the veggie burger which sounds amazing.
