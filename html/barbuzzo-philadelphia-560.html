    Came here on my own (I was visiting the city, and found this place on Yelp), so I just sat at the Chef's Counter. It was actually a really neat opportunity to watch your food get cooked! And all the chefs and servers are super friendly. The restaurant was very small (I'm surprised how many tables they could fit in there!), and didn't have much lighting, but I liked the general theme and décor.

    Ordered the Pappardelle Pasta, which had wild mushrooms and a buttery sauce. It was sooo delicious! And super rich (I watched them put spoonfuls of butter in the pan haha), but not too heavy. They basically let the mushrooms and other condiments simmer in the broth to really bring out the flavors. I also watched them cook gnocchi, which looked like it would be delicious as well! For dessert, I ordered the Salted Caramel Budino (it's like a pudding) because it was recommended by one of the chefs. I loved the layer of salted caramel, but the dish as a whole was a little too sweet for me. (Probably because I was eating it by myself haha) Definitely order this to share with someone else, because it's a lot for just one person!

    Overall, I had a great experience, and I would definitely come back if I ever visit Philly again.

    We (specifically I) have wanted to try Barbuzzo for AGES. I've heard so many great things about… We (specifically I) have wanted to try Barbuzzo for AGES. I've heard so many great things about their food from friends, acquaintances, and even The Food Network, which clearly accounts for how hard it is to get a reservation there! And trust me, especially on a weekend, you don't want to try just walking in for a table because it likely will be an hour plus wait (as I witnessed multiple times during our meal).

    I made our reservations over a month in advance to celebrate my fiance's birthday on a Saturday night - the only night we're currently able to dine out on due to hectic schedules. We were promptly seated at the front windows, which would be nice IF the weather was warm. The space heaters did not help every time the front door opened, letting in a large draft of cold air, which was quite frequent. The interior is definitely cramped, but I would've preferred literally rubbing elbows with fellow diners instead of needing to drape my coat over myself for most of the meal.

    Our server was nice but very busy with all the packed tables. I got a lively glass of white wine (menu descriptions are spot on!) while he had beer as we started with their famous 'pig popcorn,' so freshly fried that we could still hear it popping and crackling. Crispy, salty, lightly spiced, they were delicious with the horseradish aioli, although the pickled mustard seeds had formed a tight ball and we couldn't mix/scoop any up to taste :( Next up came two veggie dishes - the seared brussels sprouts were delicious (but let's be honest - pancetta will make almost anything yummy, as does cheese, and that caciocavallo is YUM), as were the sauteed greens, especially with the spicy coppa pieces hidden underneath.

    But it was the pastas that were the true stars of the meal. The sauteed gnocchi with wild mushrooms, shaved chestnuts, pancetta, olorosso, and smoked mascarpone was soft, delicate, and filling without being heavy. Our favorite, though, was one of the evening's specials: tortiglioni with bone marrow sausage, pork cheek ragu, and smoked goat cheese on top that had melted into the sauce - a wonderful combination of flavors (PUT IT ON THE REGULAR MENU!) that while creamy was also not heavy. My fiance loved it so much that I MAY have let him eat most of it ;) We didn't have dessert there...because I had picked up two of their infamous caramel budinos three days earlier on Valentine's Day at Verde as a surprise to enjoy at home. He doesn't usually like sweets, but THANK GOD I got one for each of us! He demolished his and then started eyeing mine - so perfectly delicious, next time I may have to pick up a dozen...     The most perfect blend of delectable food and a fun, trendy vibe can be found at this amazing little tapas-style restaurant. This place is a treat.

    You MUST get a pizza. And then your heart will officially belong to Barbuzzo. Don't worry about which kind you pick, trust that they'll all be winners (but if you must know, someone in that kitchen does wonders with mortadella). If that wasn't enough, the pizza is accompanied by sprigs of herbs and an addictive chili oil.

    After you're finished swooning over the pizza, go straight for the arancini. Stuffing varies. The butternut/fontina with pesto on the menu right now is delicious, as was another more traditional mushroom/mozz/tomato sauce I had a couple of years ago and still haven't forgotten about.

    Those two options really stand out, but I can't fathom how anything here would be less than great. Really, try anything. For the experience and the extent of satisfaction the food alone will bring you, your bill will be a steal.

    Bottom line - a fantastic choice for any reason, as long as you're okay that it gets a little loud because it's a small space. Highly recommend for a date, but a special one - can't share that budino with just anyone!

    It's a shame - the menu looks great, but the execution just isn't there.
