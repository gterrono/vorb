    I love this place.  The interior is nice, cozy (in a good way and also in the, you're sitting right next to the other table way) and smells great when you get in.  I've been twice, both times my girlfriend got the "Uovo" pizza with truffled egg (no real truffles here, just the truffle flavored oil but still good), both times it was excellent and changed a little between visits (Brussels sprouts changed out for asparagus).

    Both times we started with the bone marrow appetizer, which the second time around was a little underdone, the fat hadn't fully rendered towards a particularly deep end of the bone but they quickly fixed that with a quick second trip into the oven.  

    The "Mia Pera" pizza currently on the menu is out of this world good, they change their menu frequently as any good restaurant does but I hope some iteration of this stays.

    The pastas are quite good, I tried the one with the "six hour smokey ragu."  As any good pasta, the flavors were fresh and clean, though the pasta could've been cooked a little less in my opinion but that's only a small niggle.  I can't say it enough, the pizzas are phenomenal here.

    Deserts, eh, not my favorite, they seem a little messy, unfocused and sometimes a little over the top.  But did I say the pizza's are amazing???

    Eat here, its one of the best places you can go in Philly right now.

    I wish you could give 1/2 stars, cause I would give it an additional 1/2 star.

    We just came home from here after going for restaurant week; 4 courses for $35. Dinner starts with 2 crostinis courtesy of the chef. One is a Smashed Fava-Goat Cheese Crostini with preserved lemon, mint, extra virgin olive oil and the other is a Smoky Chickpea Crostini with roasted garlic, cumin, smoked pimenton. I personally liked the goat cheese one better, but they were both ok. Nothing ground breaking, but good enough.

    For an appetizer, my friend and I got the sheeps milk ricotta while our ladies got the meatball slider. The ricotta was served with some balsamic and olive oil and was pretty much tasteless. The ONLY flavor I was able to pick up was the balsamic. I wound up mixing everything together to try and coax some flavor out of the app, but I just wound up giving up halfway through. The meatball slider was good, not great and came with sweet potato chips.

    Dinner is where Barbuzzo got on track. I had the grilled bronzino, the misses and my friend had the pan seared gnocchi, while his wife had the House Extruded Tufoli, which is a spicy pork pasta dish. Beside the fact that I was expecting a whole fish instead of a filet, the fish was fantastic! It was delicious and the skin was nice and crispy. It was served with an artichoke-mushroom farro that gave a nice vinegary note to the fish. I thought the entree portions were a little on the small side, but as far as taste, it was a homerun. The Tufoli was delicious as well and had a nice spicyness that perked it up. The gnocchi I found to be a little doughy but it wasn't bad. The Bronzino was definitely the star.

    Then, came the heavy hitter; the Salted Caramel Budino. This dessert is everything its billed to be. AND, it was quite a bit bigger than I had anticipated. I was thinking a shot glass-esque portion, but there was a decent amount of Budino to be had. It is VERY rich, but its so tasty that you will most likely finish it anyway.

    They have a nice size wine list and the Sangria was tasty, if a little spicy. It reminded me of Christmas actually.

    The meal, drinks and tip ran us about $60 per person, but remember that those are Restaurant Week prices. Eating here any other time would be quite a bit more.

    I'd say the meal started out slow and picked up steam as it went on. Our server was excellent and even refolded my napkin when I went to the bathroom. Its a small place but it doesn't feel like it. There were some movement issues when it started to get a little packed but nothing too annoying. I'd recommend checking it out, if for no other reason than the Budino.

    Listed in Cheese, Cocktail Bars, Pizza in Philadelphia, Wine Bars in Philadelphia, Restaurant Week

    I'm not really a prophet of Restaurant Week or anything. I can take it or leave it. Mostly leave it, since I feel like I can have a better experience for the same price (sometimes less, sometimes more if we're all being honest here) any other night of the year. That said, I do dig RW for one thing - lunch. Because if you can score a table at one of the few gems on the list that runs like a well-oiled machine, you are all but guaranteed a fantastic meal at a price that can't be beat. Barbuzzo is one of those gems.

    I entered the restaurant at 12:45, my reservation time, and my lunch date and I were taken to our table right away. No waiting! If our server was unhappy to be working this shift, he certainly didn't show it. He took care of us for the duration of the meal and offered helpful suggestions when we asked for his opinion. I started with a crisp glass of white wine which went perfectly with my meal.

    To start, the vegetable board, which had a piece of buttery oven-grilled bread, roasted peppers, brussels lightly scented with truffle, glazed carrots and some broccoli rabe. It wasn't rocket science but it was a healthy and satisfying start to the meal. The sheep's milk ricotta across from me was wiped clean from the plate. Next up for me was a chicken dish, with two thin cutlets topped with parmesan and a little prosciutto. This was served over some braised greens and mashed potatoes with a little bit of gravy around the bottom. It was very nice, not too heavy and packed lots of flavor. My companion had the pizza. Folks, if you haven't had Barbuzzo's pizza, get on it, because it's some of the best in the city. Last but not least, the caramel budino. I somehow managed to restrain myself to only half, which is honestly plenty for me since the flavors are so rich and sweet.

    Surprisingly, this was probably the quietest meal I've ever enjoyed at Barbuzzo. Everyone around us seemed to be seated on time and there were no waits at the door. I guess when a restaurant is used to having a full house on the regular, Restaurant Week is no different from any other day.

We had decent seats away from the drafty door. The place is tiny, slim, the seating is cramped. You are going to hear your neighbors convo and when they leave they will make apologies for their size 4 butt they claim is huuuge. Oh honey....i've got three zip codes on ya!

    We both started with the greens and grains chicken soup. IT IS PHENOM! It is so good I've been craving it ever since. Its got this wonderful nutty faro in it that just makes it almost stew like or risotto-y deconstructed yet homey and lovely.

    We then shared the Antipasti plate which had lovely roasted  brussel sprouts and other vegetables, cheese, and beets to die for.

    We had the pugliese pizza cause our other friends don't eat sausage. The crust was thin, the cheese was perfect, the sausage was moist and not dried out, and there were some lovely olives on it that gave it a perfect piquant-ness to balance the sweetness of the tomatoes. I definitely want to go back to try more of the pizzas.

    We both had the cherry lemonade, it was refreshing but a bit too tart for me, I think I will just stick to water next time.

This budino is happiness in a glass jar. Salted caramel, whipped cream, crunchy bits, vanillay custard everything good and not too sweet. As Goldilocks would say it was just right.

    The service was great. We were able to have a long leisurely friday ladies lunching. We never felt rushed or like they needed our table. There was never any lags in the service either with our items which is always a plus in my book.

    Totally deserving of the write up in the New York Times and the James Beard nod. I love Barbuzzo. Make a reservation as this place fills up quickly. I have been lucky a few times, usually weekdays, to walk in and get some counter space (it gets hot up there though).

    When you walk in, there's that distinct smell of truffles that wafts through the air. And just like those pigs that smell out the truffles, I can't resist the temptation.

    - If you're like me, and have tastebuds, do not miss out on the Uovo pizza (this was mentioned in the NY Times piece). It's topped with a lovely truffled egg, and covered in white sauce. The leaves on top of the pizza give it that fresh bite to it. I also love that they give you dried herbs still on the twig to crumble over your pizza. Yum.

    - The pan seared gnocchi is also very good. Buttery, light pillows paired with braised lamb shank.

    - Roasted bone marrow is delicious. I actually don't really always love bone marrow as sometimes it can just be too gelatinous. But this one is good, and eat it with that crispy crusty crostini.

    - And save room for dessert, because a trip to Barbuzzo is not complete without the salted caramel budino. It really is like some sort of illegal street drug because just writing about it makes me want some. It's served in a small mason jar with a dark chocolate crust at the bottom. Make sure when you're eating it, you stick your spoon all the way to the bottom and pull up some dark chocolate goodness along with the salty creamy caramel pudding. Seriously. Please find some way to package this stuff and sell it in large quantities.
