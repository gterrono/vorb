    Because of the location, you inevitable find yourself comparing the Rose Tattoo to Sabrina's.  It may not be fair.  But as you pass Sabrina on the way there, it's going to happen at least subconsciously.

    Here's where The Rose Tattoo is better than Sabrina:
    - Sitting wasn't a problem for lunch
    - I found the décor and atmosphere of the Rose Tattoo to be very pleasant and calming
    - The noise level was such that you had no trouble hearing everyone at your table
    - Kitchen service was better.  I've been to Sabrina's several times where they couldn't make something off the menu

    But Sabrina is better for:
    - The food is simply better at Sabrina's.  The biggest factor why is they have a real vegetarian menu options.  You can order tofu, seitan or tempeh at Sabrina's.  The Rose Tattoo had a few narrow options for vegetarians.  But nothing for vegans unless you count a stripped down salad.  So while the food was good at the Rose Tattoo, it just couldn't compare.

    So when comparing The Rose Tattoo to Sabrina's, it wins on every point except the food.  And when it comes down to it, that's the most important aspect to a restaurant.  Since I gave Sabrina four stars (it lost one star to the lack of sitting at peak lunch hour,) I've kind of backed myself into giving The Rose Tattoo only 3 stars.  That's probably not fair of me, but neither is waiting 30+ minutes for a table at Sabrina's for their addictively amazing food.

    The Rose Tattoo is an obvious place to catch a drink if you are attending a program at the Free Library.  The bar is dark with lots of wood and cool old Tiffany glass doors used as partitions.  I'd love to get the story on those.

    Wines by the glass are nothing special, and there was little on the food menu for vegans, but we were happy to hide out in the cool air for a bit.  The staff, particularly the blond hostess, were very nice.  It looks like there are a lot of regulars.  An atmosphere like this does not need a tv, but it is baseball season and it's Philly, so the game has to be on.

    The dining room of this old-school place is something to see, open to the second story and filled with amazing plants.  The air must be ripe with oxygen from all the foliage!  The bar is in the middle and looks welcoming in the shadow of trees and vines, with skylights above.  Someone works hard to keep all these babies watered and alive to such a dramatic extent.  The tables in this area are behind old-timey iron fencing, kinda romantic and good for privacy.  There are lots of rooms off to the side, where they probably serve small parties.

It took a very long time for our entrees to come, and the restaurant was almost empty. I'd hate to see how long it takes when they're busy. Our waitress didn't come to ask us if we needed more drinks. She finally came to the table to let us know our food would be out shortly, and I was able to quickly ask for another drink before she ran away again. I was under the impression that waiters and waitresses typically WANT you to order more cocktails to bring the bill's total up and therefore bring their tip up. Not this one, apparently.
    Our food finally came, and two of my friends had ordered the tortellini. They were both brought some other pasta dish. We knew they had told the waitress the right thing when they ordered, because she repeated back "two torts".  We knew it was a bad sign that she didn't write the order down. She apologized and brought them the right entrees. They both liked the tortellini a lot. My fiance had a steak special, and said it was good, but very under-seasoned. I had the mushroom soup and the goat cheese and roasted beet salad. The soup was good. The salad was not so good. It was very dry.
