    I've been to Vernick a few times now: once for drinks, once for dessert, and last night for dinner.  Each time has been fantastic, with the staff, drinks, decor, and food excelling all the way.  With its modern decor, trendy feel, and amazing array of signature cocktails and tantalizing dishes, the young Vernick is poised to become one of Philadelphia's best restaurants and one of the city's best places to be seen.

    On my most recent trip, I went with the BF and one of our best friends for a late Thursday dinner.  We sat upstairs and gossiped over delicious Maidstone cocktails.  This was followed by an order of the sea urchin and scrambled eggs, which was light, fluffy, buttery, and savory.  My main dish, a sea bass with broccoli and fire roasted tomato sauce, was terrific.  The fish was juicy and flaky, the broccoli just a bit crisp, and the sauce was well seasoned, flavorful, and garlicky.  And of course, the desserts were phenomenal as per usual.  The three of us split the date cake, chocolate custard, and the meyer lemon mousse.  While the mousse wasn't our favorite, the chocolate custard was rich and carried just a pinch of heat, while the date cake, as I remembered it, was moist and not too sweet, perfectly accented by the scoop of vanilla bean gelato on top.

    With friendly, congenial service and a warm atmosphere, Vernick also makes you feel important, at home, and welcome.  It's definitely pricier than your standard dinner out, but the food is worth it and everything shines.  Glad to have a place like this in Philly.

    I was very tempted to give Vernick 5 stars, but after giving it some thought, it seems fair to give it "only" a full 4.5 stars.

    This place offered an amazing dining experience from the moment I walked in the door.  Everyone on staff was extremely friendly, and there was a very down-to-earth vibe about the place.  We were seated upstairs at a window table - we made no special request, and we were even a bit late to a pretty full restaurant, so it was a nice touch on their part.

    We started with a few appetizers.  The beef tartare toast was very good, if slightly on the salty side, and I also highly enjoyed the tuna poke.  But the sea urchin with warm scrambled eggs is on another level.  I don't use the phrase 'melts in your mouth' much, but that is exactly what it does: it melts in your mouth.  The combination of the two yields a tremendous flavor that you must experience to understand.

    For the main course, we split a seafood roast, which included shrimp (large and small), squid, clams, oysters, eel, and fish.  I cannot stress enough how well the dish came out.  I'm a huge fan of seafood, but am often disappointed by how seafood medleys turn out at other restaurants.  Vernick does it the right way, with not too much seasoning or spices, allowing the natural flavors of the seafood to dominate the dish.

    Finally, our dessert, a roasted date cake, came.  It was a perfect combination of savory and (mostly) sweet, cold and warm, easily one of the top 5 desserts I have ever had.

    A few closing thoughts: The food is served relatively quickly compared to other places.  If you're in a rush, or simply not in the mood to dawdle around and force a conversation for 3 hours, then this is a good place to simply experience good food.  Also, a lot of places have popped up touting their local and sustainable food initiatives. Vernick has some real substance behind it, and it doesn't even need to advertise them - it's a way of life here.
