    I really, really like Barbuzzo. I only gave it a four because it's difficult to get in there unless you plan quite far in advance or are particularly interested in eating after about 9:30 at night. They also have the tiniest little tables all squished in there together to the point where it's hard not to eavesdrop on your neighbor. The little tables also aren't as conducive to the sharing of their delicious small plates.

    My complaints end there -- Barbuzzo's food is top notch. We've been twice now and I will say that we order too much food every time. I think they recommend two plates per person and that is more than enough, especially if one of those plates is going to be one of their pizzas.  The serving sizes on the other items are generous as well.

    We had the olives (we ended up getting five plates because we thought that would be small, but it could have stood on its own for a plate), the roasted french feta, the beet salad, the fungi pizza, and the caciocavallo meatballs. If I had it to do over (which I will, because how could you not go back once you've been here), I would skip the olives and the pizza to try something new (both were fine, but nothing too special), but the rest of the things we picked were crave-worthy, especially the meatballs.

    We ended the meal (in fact, we left two slices of pizza behind so we'd have room) with the salted caramel budino. It's a tiny little thing for $8 and I could totally house about three of them, but they're a perfect meal ender and perhaps one of the best desserts I've ever had. Someone I know had them for dessert at Thanksgiving, so I think you can somehow order them to take out as well ... which could be dangerous ...

    Wow!! This place is a gem.  With so many reviews, the secret has been out.  I'm just late getting the memo.  My review is probably a continuation to what so many reviewers have already stated, but I will still add my two cents.  

    The restaurant decor is nice, but the space of the restaurant is very small, so small that the woman next to our table apologized in advance if she sat in my lap by accident while she was being seated.  

    The food is served tapas style.  I was worried that I would either leave hungry or spend a lot of money to be full.  Fortunately, neither one of my fears were true.  The Sheep's Milk Ricotta was served with toasted bread.  It had a creamy-velvety texture; I enjoyed sopping up the Ricotta with the perfectly grilled pieces of bread.  This was the first dish to come out and it was a great way to start the evening.  The roasted and raw beet salad came out shortly after the Ricotta.  The salad was good, but I wasn't blown away, only because I've had my share of beets and raw kale salads.  The Mortadella pizza was excellent and was a lot bigger than what I expected!  Who would have thought that a bologna pizza would taste so great?  I wish I would have thought of this when I was a kid eating so many bologna sandwiches.  I digressed.  The tagliatelle was wonderful.  The pasta was al dente.  The lamb was moist and tender.  Desert was a no brainer; I had to try to salted caramel budino.  My desert lived up to all of the lofty YELP reviews.  

    The service was excellent and every dish I had was stellar!  Thank you Barbuzzo for a memorable dining experience.

    Ladies, a note of caution: if you're also looking to boost your ego and self-esteem from the attention you're likely to get from male patrons at a restaurant, Barbuzzo might not be the destination to do so...

    Dining with a group of friends from out-of-town, exchanging flirtatious banter while seated next to a fairly attractive group of women, we embarrassed ourselves thoroughly in a 30 minute time period with the accentuated oscillation of our necks that rotated like fans as the eyes scanned an arc from the entrance to the reception - while taking note of every single attractive female (from all age groups, physical descriptions, hair types, and ethnicities) entering and exiting the restaurant. Needless to add, the table next door was sorely disappointed by the ADD we had exhibited, and didn't make eye contact with us for the rest of the meal. But, I can honestly say that I haven't seen such a large number of extremely attractive girls in a single restaurant ever - thousands of meals in major metropolises like NYC, Washington, San Francisco not withstanding...

    Given that context of my first meal at Barbuzzo, it was extremely difficult for me to judge the restaurant without rose-tinted glasses - and yet I still exercised discipline by holding off reviewing till another couple of trials. That, and the desire to make sense of the crazy popularity of their Salted Caramel Budino... yes, the famous salted caramel budino you will see mentioned in almost all reviews - for very good reason. I really have to struggle hard to recall a contemporary phenomenon like that in any other city where a single restaurant dessert rules the hearts of the diners. Worried that I wouldn't be able to sample it at my first visit, at the start of the meal, I asked the server to save one for me. Not to worry, the server assured me, we make hundreds of servings a day. Judging from the number of people that show up just to order that dessert for take-away, I wouldn't be surprised if that number is in the Thousands instead... The Salted Caramel Budino at Barbuzzo might well become a Philly institution and certainly deserves that honor over the heart clogging cheese steaks that define the City of Brotherly Love. It certainly is one of the better desserts I've had (5 stars) despite the elevated expectations and hype.

    The rest of the (Mediterranean) tapas-style menu is executed fairly competently. My usual staples are the Sheeps Milk Ricotta (extra virgin olive oil, sea salt, grilled country bread) and Chicken Liver-Foie Mousse (with plum jam, almonds, and crostini) to start, followed by Octopus (without the Sopressata) and then either the Tagliatelle (lamb, favas, leeks, pecorino) / Pan seared Gnocchi (mushroom, tomato, corn, truffle butter - without the Guanciale) or pizza (Uovo: egg yolk, truffle, cheese, greens or Margherita). They have a good menu of cocktails, and I often pick between the Basil Lemonade (w/vodka) and the Gin with cucumber drink.

    I have always found the service gracious, and still remember the courtesy of a harried manager (at peak hour) in accommodating a different table when an additional guest joined us during our meal.

    Beyond that I seem to have stored no distinct memories. But, you can't recall anything should your initial concentration and focus have been challenged in any way. Barbuzzo will do that to you, but you'll still leave with a smile - not simply for the Salted Caramel Budino :)

    Good but not great.

    For Philadelphia, this is a very good restaurant.  Compared to restaurants in…     Good but not great.

    For Philadelphia, this is a very good restaurant.  Compared to restaurants in other big foodie towns, it is ok.  I adore this type of food: lots of citrus and Mediterranean flavors.  A good dose of fresh vegetables.  Some dishes were very good others are great in theory but execution of flavors was off.  Tastes did not marry as they should.  

    Pros:
    * Very friendly wait staff.
    * Kitchen counter seating was entertaining.
    * Prices were reasonable for this kind of restaurant.
    * I felt full, which I don't always at a "plates" restaurant.
    * Huge portion of chicken liver foie mousse.
    * Some dishes (buratta and carrots) were fantastic.  The olives were great - fried rosemary was spot on texture.

    Cons:
    * I am a salt addict but halibut was so overly salted it ruined the dish.

    * The ingredients made so much sense together but just didn't sing.  They need help with their pickling, their juices, their ratios of tang, and high quality condiments like plum jam, which was completely lacking and could have turned the chicken liver into something sensational instead of just good.

    * I love marcona almonds.  But come on, a quarter of the dishes had them in it and not in any subtle way.  They feel a bit one trick pony.  I want to love this restaurant but they need to step it up to make it great.

    I will go back again because I have a hard time finding restaurants in Philly that I like much better.

    I've been to Philadelphia a few times, but this particular time it was for my sister's birthday.  I had never eaten downtown before.  My sister goes downtown quite frequently for dinner, as she likes good food like myself, and has her go-to's.  I asked her to pick a restaurant that she had never been to before so that we could all try something new.  She had always heard good things about Barbuzzo, and the price looked reasonable, so we decided to go for it.

    We made a reservation ahead of time, and when we arrived, it was crowded.  I imagine the wait time would have to be 30-45 minutes.  We asked our waitress what was popular on the menu and ordered four dishes for three people based on her recommendations: sheep's milk ricotta, octopus, asparagus pizza, and gnocchi.

    The food came out extremely fast--almost suspiciously fast, and it makes me wonder how much of the dish is prepared ahead of time. We started off with the ricotta--smooth and creamy cut with a tanginess from the balsamic.  It came with grilled pieces of fluffy bread.  The ricotta was perfectly seasoned, and the balsamic was high quality--very deep and flavorful.  The octopus had a nice texture and was not overly chewy.  The pizza came with an egg cracked in the center whose yolk ran all over the pizza.  It was thin and very crispy, and the yolk/asparagus/parmesan combination was classic.  The truffle oil was very tasty and we were all fighting over the pieces that had the most truffle oil.  Lastly, the gnocchi was light and fluffy, not heavy at all, and came in a delicious creamy sauce.  I would recommend any of the dishes. My only qualm with Barbuzzo was that the octopus and the gnocchi were pretty salty.  I'm hoping that it was just a one-time accident.  

    Although the dishes looked small, we left the restaurant surprisingly full with happy tummies and happy wallets as well.  The dinner came out to about $23 per person, which I thought was great for a good  restaurant in downtown Philly. While we all enjoyed our dinner very much, I am skeptical whether or not I would return to Barbuzzo.  There are many other great restaurants in Philly that I have yet to go to, and there wasn't anything particularly enticing on the menu that I still wanted to try.  If you're not sure what to order though, definitely try the ricotta and the asparagus/egg pizza.

    Cheese: Super super stretchy and elastic! Not evenly distributed on pizza, and the slices were cut way too thickly. The cheese factor made the pizza a bit hard to eat actually. Basil: Not enough! I still don't understand why restaurants are so skimpy on the basil!
    And last but not least the budino. I really wanted to like this, and really thought I should since the restaurant is known for it, but I just didn't. To be fair, I think I've only had a budino one other time, and I don't remember it looking or tasting like this one. So this budino came in a wide short glass jar with chocolate on the way bottom and lots of cream on top, with salted caramel in the middle. So the chocolate on the bottom tasted like oreo cookie. The cream was supposed to be vanilla flavored but I hardly tasted the vanilla; in fact it tasted very bland and similar to whipped cream. And the salted caramel was way too salty. I also thought budinos were supposed to be more of a custard or pudding and this definitely resembled neither. So I'm not quite sure what everyone is raving about, but I guess my taste just differs from others.
    Service was good! But restaurant itself was so dark I could barely read my menu, and was also very narrow and cramped. Not much space.
    I'd be curious to try one of their main dishes.
