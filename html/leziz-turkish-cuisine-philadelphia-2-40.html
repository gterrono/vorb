    I have been here a few times, so I'm surprised I have never reviewed it.  

    The first time I tried Turkish food was in Germany, when I was introduced to doner kebabs, which are lamb meat cut from this vertical rotating pole, stuffed in a flatbread served with cabbage, onions, cucumber, and sauce.  Now when this food first touched my lips, I knew I had never truly lived before this.

    S & H's lamb doner kebab is not served in flatbread, but it is just as tender and delicious as the first time.  They serve it over two different kinds of pilaf, one white and one orange, both seasoned well and very good.  It is also served with cabbage, tomato, pepper, and a yogurt sauce which compliment the meat very well.

    The ONLY negative that I have is that when we first started going here on the weekends for lunch, they would have a "lunch special" for an amazing price (not sure exactly how much, but less than $10), which included soup, salad, or appetizer, with your entree.  For some reason they changed this the last time we went and now only offer the special Monday-Friday, not on weekends.  It kind of sucks to pay more than $20 each for lunch on the weekend (especially when you aren't even boozing), so I guess we will probably only be going here for dinner now.

    Standing outside the little restaurant, or seemingly small venue on a Thursday evening, the crisp evening offsetting the flavor of tobacco in my mouth, I gazed across the street at the S & H Kebab House. I have always been one for Mediterranean/Middle Eastern food, especially Turkish and Persian, and the revelry within warmed my heart and brought forth an interest to maneuver slightly closer to the place. I had just been down this street the evening before for a birthday dinner next door, and had initially noticed it because of the amazing aromas surrounding its location. As the full menu came into viewable distance, I knew one thing: I had found a diamond in the rough.

    Crossing the threshold of the establishment my olfactory center went into extreme fever overload. The scents emanating from this place were amazing. We were very quickly seated, and as I passed other tables I could not help but to look at the variety of fantastic looking kebab dishes, as well as the occasional other traditional Turkish meals that people were enjoying. Once seated, a magical dance began of people moving all around us, bringing us water, pita (made-in-house), and menus, as well as a visit from the owner.

    It took me a while to determine what exactly I wanted to eat, but the menu is rich in choices. Not only do they have kebab meals that would satisfy even the most hardcore Turkish food enthusiast, they have many foods that are very interesting in the fact that they are their take on an adapted recipe. Having had such a large dinner the night before, I opted not to get anything except a main meal, and went with the Chef's Recommendation; the Tandir Kebab....a slow roasted lamb kebab in spices, served with long-grain rice, spicy rice meal, and fantastic greens.

    To pass the time that it would take for my kebab to be prepared, I busted into the nice sized basket of fresh, hot pita on the table and went crazy on it. It was amazingly soft, and just the right consistency. Now I only have one complaint about this place, and here it goes.... They served the pita with butter wedges. I have never had pita with butter before, it seems more appropriate to have it with hummus, yogurt, or something like a shirazi salad, but although only butter was available, I asked our fantastic waitress if I could possibly get some sumac. She eyeballed me kind of strangely, but said with a big smile that she would return momentarily with something special.

    That something special was a whole little ramekin of sumac! As well as an awesome mini-spoon to spread it with. I was in heaven. Not only is sumac one of my favorite spices, it also made the humdrum palate of the pita with butter into a much more 'Turkish' experience! Satisfied that I had conquered the challenge presented by the butter conundrum, I ordered a Coca-Cola, and waited. But I did not wait long. As soon as the beverage arrived, my kebab was accompanying it. Like some kind of strange marriage, both items were set in front of me, creating wonderment that I fail to even describe accurately.

    The rice was phenomenal, as were the greens, cooked just enough that they maintained their crunchiness, but were not anywhere near raw. The spicy rice meal was indeed that; spicy! Not burning the mouth after the fact, but just enough heat to give one the heightened senses caused by the intake of capsaicin. For lack of a better term; it was awesome! I surveyed the kebab, which would have had no chance staying whole, or even a relative shape, and the pulled lamb sat in a beautiful pile amongst the other plate items.

    To my surprise, there was just so much of the stuff, I was worried that I would not even be able to finish the dish, however, the righteous might of my stomach ruled the day. The lamb was succulent and cooked perfectly, with some slightly grilled brown, the rest soft and falling-apart, making the ultimate contrast of the dish. It was however, slightly salty, but once again I was able to offset this with the sumac.

    Everything here was fantastic, and I would highly recommend this place to anyone looking for a great place to have dinner, regardless if they have an interest in ethnic foods or not. The only reason it is not a perfect score was because of the saltiness of the lamb, but this may not be the same for everyone. The staff and ambiance is great, and the owner is genuinely concerned about your happiness. Please visit S & H Kebab House, you will not regret it...........unless you're some kind of extremely unhappy person, or maybe a zombie who can only be satisfied by living flesh, and in that case, I don't think kebabs are going to help you.

    This is a good, authentic Turkish spot to get your grilled meat fix. Lunch specials are cheap. A little pricey otherwise.

    Appetizers include babaganouj and tarama (a dip made of carp roe). The babaganouj has a distinctly fresh eggplant flavor that is balanced by the sharpness of good olive oil. My tarama had a salty, fishy taste (as it should) and also seemed pretty fresh. Both were served with pita bread, which was the low point of the meal as it resembled supermarket pita.

    The doner (gyro) is authentic and superior to the standard ground meat found in most places. Here, rather than being mushy, it eats like a thinly sliced, juicy steak. The shish kabob was tender, perfectly charred, and robustly spiced. Falafel was some of the best I've had in the city with an earthy flavor and hearty texture. The pilaf served with entrees is well-spiced and is a good al dente; it almost outshines the meats. Just be careful with the grilled jalapeno, it's demonically spicy even for a chili enthusiast like myself .

    On Friday and Saturday nights they have hookah and are open until 3am, which is cool considering it's a short distance from South Street bars. Service is impeccable, our waiter was attentive and accommodating.

History

Established in 2009.

This is family owned restaurant, specializes in Turkish, Middle Eastern and Mediterrenaen cuisine. It also features live entertainment with belly dancing every Friday and Saturday.

The story of S& H Kebab House begins many moons ago in New Jersey, as these stories always do. The Garden State is like fate's corner bar; it carefully orchestrates serendipitous encounters from frumpy Turnpike rest stops, rigorously landscaped Wisteria Lanes and many, many Cheesecake Factories. That's how Sal Kucuk met Huseyin Yuksel 13 years ago. Fate, you could say.

The kebabs, made with chicken or leg of lamb ground in-house, skewered on metal spits and cooked over open charcoals that imbue each stick with a smoky, almost sensual undertone. Turkish pepper paste stokes a fire in the long, sausage-shaped Adana kebabs, while the classic, familiar shish kebabs bring cubed lamb and chicken that had been marinating for days in yogurt, onion juice and tomato paste.

Meet the Manager

Huseyin Y. Manager

Head chef, Huseyin Yuksel, has brought the intricate layers and flavors of traditional Turkish cuisine to South Street.  Born in Samsun, Turkey, Chef Yuksel lives the food that he prepares, and cooks as much by instinct as he does by recipes.  Having run kitchens in New Jersey, New York and, most recently, at Anchorage, Alaska's Istanbul Cafe, he brings years of experience with him as he sets out to make S&H Kebab House (Huseyin is the 'H') a Philadelphia culinary staple for years to come.
