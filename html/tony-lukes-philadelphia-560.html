    No, I haven't been to Pat or Geno's. Stop looking at me like that.
    I don't need to have tried anyone else's cheesesteak to know that Tony Luke's is freakin' delicious.

    This place is kinda off the beaten path since it's not anywhere near the city center, but it's definitely an easy 7 minute drive from there. Tony Luke's actually has a pretty vast and extensive menu that isn't limited to the classic cheesesteak. You can get chicken variations, pizza ones, veggie ones, salads, hoagies, sides, etc.

    I ordered the regular cheesesteak which I was disappointed to learn didn't come with onions by default, you had to ask for them. Oh well. The bread is baked fresh daily on premises and definitely tastes that way. Soft and fluffy, it is actually narrow enough to make eating it pretty manageable. The beef itself couldn't have been more perfectly cooked. Sliced thin and quite tender and juicy, this was everything the meat in a cheesesteak could hope to be. I ordered American cheese on mine but it wasn't all that flavorful. Just melty and creamy, which was fine. If you're hungry, you'd definitely be able to finish one on your own, so don't be too intimidated by the size, it's actually perfectly portioned. As a note, there isn't much in the way of seasoning/crazy flavor on this cheesesteak. Just meat, cheese, and bread. Done.

    I ordered a side of mozzarella triangles which are really just like mozzarella sticks but it flattened, triangle shapes. These are outstanding. They are perfectly fried to be crispy on the outside and goopy, stringy, and melty on the inside. Also, the seasoning in the breading is flavorful as heck. Absolutely a must-buy if you like fried cheese in any form.

    Additionally, I snagged an order of Chipotle BBQ flavored french fries. They're new, and pretty good! The seasoning on these tastes EXACTLY like Utz or Lays sweet barbecue flavored potato chips, but definitely are on the salty side.

    Tony Luke's has a perpetual line that is, at the very least, to/out the door. You should also know that there is NO bathroom here. Have fun finding one.

    Tourists and locals alike should seriously consider leaving behind those more popular, big name cheesesteak places because Tony Luke's should be well worth it.

    I often make the mistake of blindly attempting everything Adam Richman tries or has tried on Man v. Food (back when he was not on the brink of reaching morbid obesity) The obsession started with Ike's Place and La Cumbre in SF, which were both great but not awesome; I tried the Four Horsemen Burger at Chunky's in San Antonio and the giant cinnamon roll at Lulu's Cafe which was again, great but not awesome.

    So by now, my expectation for anything with Adam Richman's seal of approve was ingrained in the back of my mind as "Great, but not awesome" and this place falls right into that category.

    I came here for my very first meal in Philadelphia with a friend who'd been living here for three years that had never once tried it. He too, had heard rumors that this place served the "best cheesesteak in philly" and was somewhat curious to try this place as well--it was even more exciting for me since my friend wasn't a foodie and his source of information regarding Tony Luke's was from word of mouth.

    First of all, Tony Luke's redefines the idea of a "hole in the wall." Like every good tourist that is unfamiliar with his surroundings should, I opted to look for the restaurant with GPS assistance, which led me to circle the area at least three times until I realized that the restaurant is the "little diner-looking thing right next to the gas station under the bridge"

    The atmosphere of the restaurant, however, does scream "locals eat here"; it's not by any means fancy inside, but is designed to get you in and get you out--once you pick up your meal, it's a  "walk over to the red picnic tables on the side and hope to God you don't pick up a disease" kind of deal.

    And as always, the meal. My initial impressions of eating this cheesesteak was the same as those that came to mind when I first took a bite of Lays' new Sriracha-flavored Potato Chips: it's not by any means mind-blowing or paradigm-shifting, but it's still the best I've ever had.

    Tony Luke's offers sharp, provolone, cheddar, and whiz. Always go for whiz, there's a reason why the original has whiz; the sweet peppers don't do much other than to mask the richness of the juicy rib-eye, so unless you're a fan of simple flavors, I wouldn't get those on your hoagie either.

    I've heard that the locals in Philly get the Pork a lot too. I'll have to try the next time I'm back.
