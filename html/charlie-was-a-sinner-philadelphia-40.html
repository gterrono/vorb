    What a lovely hidden gem. I ended up here after a few other restaurants I had planned to visit were closed, and I am glad I did! The exterior is unassuming, but as soon as you walk in, you realize you are in a place where every single detail has been attended to. The ambiance is cool, classy, but not pretentious, with teal walls, chandeliers, and abstract videos projected onto curtains on the wall for a cool artsy look. (Hard to explain but very cool to look at.) The menu is entirely plant based, but not in that boring, uber-healthy way most vegan restaurants are. The waitstaff is utterly delightful, friendly and super helpful in navigating the menu (they help you pick a drink that perfectly suits your tastes while still surprising you, and our waiter sneakily pointed out the most ample dishes on the menu so we could get the most bang for our buck.) We sampled a few drinks, my absolute favorite was the Boddhisvhatta, a creamy blend of coconut milk, coconut yogurt, rum and cardamom, and a soda made with jalapeno agave, pineapple and lime (we had ours spiked with vodka). The food is surprising and delicious--standouts include a toast with spicy grilled peppers and olives, "crab" cakes, deep fried mashed potato croquettes, and "meat"balls served over bucatini.  For dessert, we had a chocolate cake with strawberry ice cream and cocoa nibs. All in all, the food was unusually sophisticated, especially for vegan food, with bold, complex flavors and perfect execution. Kudos!

    Okay, Charlie. You haven't been the best boy, and here's why.

    I'm obliged to be honest, no matter how much I want new vegan restaurants to succeed.... We really wanted to fall in love with this place! I'm vegan and another vegan friend recommended we try it. We came in around 7:30 on a Friday night, so we expected to wait a bit longer, especially since it's a new place.

    The beers we had were wonderful. My husband had the Dortmunder and I had the Ephemere Apple. Sparkling water with mint, adorable. Everyone was friendly and the service was great up until we actually ordered.

    I was very, very excited about the food, because the entire menu is vegan. Yay, vegan! ... I guess I forgot to remember that I also cook vegan food, and I do it well, because I expected this (not cheap) restaurant food to be better than mine. And I have to admit, it wasn't. In fact I can pull off a better meatball, a better mushroom, and UNQUESTIONABLY a better potato (the confit potato cup with mushroom was, to be frank, not good.)

    We ordered five plates: the chickpea fries (so, so salty, but they were in the "salty" category on the menu, so I guess I can't complain, other than that, good); charred Caesar salad (good, but I have a weird penchant for wilted greens, my husband wanted more crunch); peppernotta on toast (very good), bucatini with meatball (pretty good, but again mine is better) and the potato confit (no, just no. The potato tasted like microwave.)

    We waited so long for anything to come out that the waitress comped us two more beers, which was nice, but it didn't inhibit our lack of satisfaction with the food, especially having waited so long. What upset me was that my standard for vegan food is high, because I know it can be and should be incredibly delicious, and I want people to give vegan food a chance, but if they think this food is the high end of vegan food, they'll be chowing down on a steak by the end of the night.

    The bottom line: people are lovely, atmosphere is lovely, bartender is top notch, but the food? Keep trying, guys. If I can make better at home I'm just coming in for drinks.

    After a long work day in the city, my wife and I will occasionally look for a chance to unwind at one of the many happy hour spots available to us in the Center City area. Yesterday, as we got out of work a bit late, we were trying to catch the tail end of a happy hour that runs until 7PM and we found one at Charlie Was a Sinner!

    With both of us having worked in food service (bartending, waitressing, etc) in previous jobs when we were younger, we can truly appreciate the components that make a bar/restaurant really worthwhile. Our experience here was beyond what we expected.

    Some highlights of our time: Incredibly friendly staff that clearly had no reservations about what is considered "their job" and what isn't. We saw a hostess take an order, a waitress seat guests, and a bar manager helping with a food menu and greeting guests. Everyone was very attentive and helpful, from food choices to following up on additional plates/drinks. The drink menu is incredibly inventive and included a cocktail mixed with beet juice (unique but refreshing) and a very tasty vodka and cucumber cocktail that tasted like strawberry lemonade. The plate portions were perfect for tapas style sharing and the food tasted very fresh.

    There is no question that we found our new happy hour/SIPS location and I highly suggest you venture out to this section of 13th St and find out about this gem!

    Let me set the stage:
    It's easy to find with its long awning and quiet signage. The decor is dark, sexy and with masculine undertones. A thick velvety curtain meets you at the door. It looks like a great place to meet a date. The dining room is mostly empty at 6 when I was there, but fills up rapidly and noisily by 7. The tables are quite small. Behind the tables is a gray curtain upon which a black and white movie is projected. The angle of the projector is such that the movie cannot really be viewed by patrons sitting down. The effect can be jarring sometimes during action scenes when you get a strobe effect.

    Drinks:
    The drink menu for cocktails is large and complex. I like cocktails but I like them basic. Many of the drinks included ingredients I didn't recognize. They make them strong.

    Food:
    The tricky part about small plates and small tables is that if you want to try a bunch of different things, you inevitably get overwhelmed with plates and there is zero room on the table. Be open for trying new things. There were plenty of cheese-like items. I did not miss the meat. I especially liked the ricotta toast and the fig salad (sold separately but eaten together). Many of the dishes were messy. Maybe not a good lace for a first date, until you like that sort of thing.

    Best Advice:
    Come for happy hour and save a bundle.

    Great vegan menu? Check.
    Cool ambiance? Check.
    Super nice staff? Check.
    Mind blowing culinary experience? Womp womp.

    I have to say, I love Hip City Vedge and was so excited to finally eat at its hot younger brother restaurant. I had a 6:45 reservation, which seemed necessary even on a Tuesday night because this place is tiny and gets crowded. Entering the space is a little strange because there are cocktail tables literally right next to the door, in what you initially think is the waiting area - so you have a moment of "Oh, hello hostess on my right and hello, couple on an awkward first date on my left, glad we're all hanging out here together." But then the hostess is just really friendly, so it works out.

    I was seated right before the end of happy hour and my waitress was kind enough to let me order my first drink off that menu, which ended up being a bargain - $6 for a cocktail that would usually cost $12. And I have to say, I'm glad I got to pay half price because I'm not sure it was such a splurge-worth bev. My dining companions (ok fine, my parents) arrived at approximately 6:57, and the waitress also let them order off the HH menu, which was a nice gesture.

    CWAS is a small plates restaurant, so the dishes are meant to be shared. Per usual, my family ordered what seemed like enough to feed both ourselves and the two couples sitting next to us - the toast with roasted red peppers and olives, grilled Cesar salad, buccatini and meatballs, crispy tofu, and chick pea fries. Everything was good, bordering on awesome, but there was just something missing - I wasn't as blown away as I was expecting. The idea of the menu is awesome and as vegetarians, it's so fun to be able to order anything off a menu (party party party!), but I think I was expecting more from this hot younger bro.

    Food aside, the music was super loud, even well after 8:00pm, which made it difficult to have a real conversation (especially with two slightly older folks - sorry mom and dad!), we were missing one setting of silverware, and we had to wait forever for our food. We ordered immediately after the table next to us, but they were served all of their plates and were almost writing the check before we had anything. Then, we were served our food all at once, which was good for our appetites but bad for our table that was just too small to fit the 18 plates. And maybe the most disappointing of all was that by just after 7:00, the kitchen had run out of the Maryland crab cake :(  For a vegetarian, the idea of being able to order crab is awesome, and it was legitimately sad that we weren't able to try this dish, especially so early in the evening.

    Overall, I will go on a second date with CWAS - he definitely deserves another shot, and maybe he will give me crabs this time. (Ba dum chh! Drops mic.)

Upon entry we were courteously greeted and made our way to the packed bar to get a drink until our table was ready. We were promptly seated and greeted by our friendly server.

    We started our meal with e delightful fig salad. It was light and refreshing and the vanilla bean vinaigrette was amazing. After that we had the "ricotta." I understand the limitations of ricotta with vegan meals, but they had used way too much lemon in the tofu mixture. The texture was there, but it honestly tasted like Trix cereal. Sorry, vegans, Trix are for kids.

    From there we an order of the chickpea fries. We were warned ahead of time that the portions would be tapas style meant to share ("to gain the full experience"). The fries came out on a plate that looked like someone just smeared aioli with their finger across it. The fries were stacked perfectly like a little chickpea log cabin... All ten of them. They had the taste, texture, and appearance of Lincoln logs. There was barely enough aioli to cut down the dryness of what I can only assume was reclaimed fried hummus.

    To cap off our meal we had baby leeks with romesco (Which admittedly I was excited to read as romanesco... Not the same thing) and the heirloom beans. Both tasted wonderful but were laughably small, even as tapas.

    Throughout this experience we sampled many of the drinks, all of which were served in glasses barely half full, is there a liquor shortage we're unaware of? Is this place supposed to be prohibition themed? No, they actually don't seem to have a theme. From their crystal chandeliers to the green lamps above the bar to the LED lights on the bar and book shelves, from the teal painted walls to the curtained wall with classic black and white films projected on loop, there's a whole lot going on without much to say.

    Charlie was a sinner, but he also probably had the appetite of a small bird. Guys, I think Charlie was a bird. It is their logo after all.
