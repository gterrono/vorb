    Eulogy is one of my favorite pubs in the city. Their lavender chicken is heavenly and the beer list is remarkably comprehensive. All of the waitstaff I've had were able to make fantastic beer recommendations based on my mainstream preferences.     The food? Tasty. It's not "holy shit that was an explosion of awesome in my mouth" but it does the trick aka sopping up the alcohol for hangover prevention. I have tried the mussel pot with bacon (good), a steak dish of sorts and the fries. All good.

    Had heard about Eulogy many times, and was happy to come across it while walking around in need of a bite and a drink. It looked like a great funky bar, the menu looked interesting, and we were pretty pumped for a good experience.

    The beer list is huge. It's unbelievably expansive. It's exciting to want to dive in and find something really excellent, obscure, or that you've wanted to try. But that experience is actually sort of frustrating because the list is simply alphabetical, so, if you feel like a Saison, you can't just turn to the list of Saisons, see what they have, get excited about trying one (or several) and order. Sure, you can go down the pages with your finger looking for all the Saisons, or Quads or whatever, and try to remember them all to choose what you want (it's a long list, have fun with that). And yes, you can ask one of their staff, who have a really great knowledge of the list, and they'll recommend something. Our recommendations were fine, not a 100% what we wanted, but fine. That just wasn't as much fun as really exploring that selection and finding something we were excited about.

    Foodwise, we started with some mussels (of course!). They were great, and had us looking forward to round two, burgers. They were not great. The challah roll and fixings were excellent, but the burger itself was a small and tall 8oz hockey puck that was rather smaller than its roll and must have had a lot of 'stuff' in it as it tasted less like a burger and more like a meatloaf sandwich. Not enjoyable at all. Kept not enjoying it throughout the rest of the day, too.

    Summary: Mixed experience. Great beer selection but not that much fun. Will likely walk by next time.

    Listed in Bars in Philly, Philly Happy Hour

    Eulogy is my favorite place for beer and fries in the city. It's has a decent location, (though the interior is a little on the small side), the draft list is incredible, the bottle list is nearly unmatched, the fries (frites) are the greatest in the land, and it's just a good place to hang on a cool spring/fall/winter day.  

    I first started coming to Eulogy five or six years ago.  I didn't drink beer too much, but this place apparently had a great draft list of beers with immense flavor.  It did not disappoint.  If you go to Eulogy, you'll fall in love. It might be with St. Bernardus, Gulden Draak, or Lindeman's Lambic.  If you go to Eulogy, you'll nosh on their twice fried Belgian Frites, absolutely crispy on the outside, and nearly mashed potato on the inside-- and they come with a moutarde (whole grain mustard) dipping sauce that you won't be able to stop eating. If you go to Eulogy, order the moussles (mussels) in one of their incredible varieties. While these are not the best in the city (that's Monk's honor), they are darn good.  

    If I have a complaint it's the size of the space and the price for some things.  It can be hard to get a seat since the place is very cozy (you can come and sit for hours sipping a beer, eating food, and having a good time). The beers can get a little pricey at times, but they shouldn't cost more than $7 for a draft and the bottles depend upon rarity and ABV.

    The bottom line: Eulogy is a great place to have a beer, eat good food, and relax with friends as long as you can get a seat or two.  You should definitely check it out.

Specialties

This is Beer Heaven, give a "high praise" Eulogy for your favorite beer after you consume it.  We also prepare our food in the authentic Flemish Belgian Style as opposed to other Belgian restaurants who choose the French Walloon Belgian style.  This means our fries are thick enough to truly hold a double fry technique, our fries go "crunch, goo" because they are the right thickness, the wimpy skinny fries elsewhere go "goo goo goo" because they are too skinny and the double fry technique does not work.  Also our mussels are larger than the PEIs other restaurants use, this is because our mussels are the authentic North Sea size like in Belgium.  The more meat the more flavor the mussel can hold in our ala minute pan technique (all ingredients added fresh, never ever ever any pour over sauce on mussels).  Also our handburgers are award winning based on seven secret spices and our fish and chips and other entrees are as good as the beers ! Make a reservation at www.eulogybar.com

History

Established in 2002.

My family is from Brugge, Belgium, I am passionate about Belgian beer, food and heritage, this adds an authenticity to the business.  I got the building in 2002 and built the restaurant myself using antique stain glass behind the bar from an old Philadelphia church and a chandelier from an old church as well.  The bar was constructed in Fishtown in Philadelphia by a local carpenter using Honduras Mahogany I imported and the design of the bar was based on a blueprint I drew.  
We take our beer very very very serious, our beer starts in a beer cooler and is then delivered via dual gas blenders using co2 and nitrogen blends for the proper carbonation technique.  Most of our draft beer never travels more than six feet but along that journey there are glycol filled lines keeping the beer at 40 ish degrees until it hits your glass.  We have about 200 different glasses we keep in stock because most of the taste for beer is accomplished through your smelling it, yes your nose!
