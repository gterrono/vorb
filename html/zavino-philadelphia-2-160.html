        Zavino is located on a bustling corner of the revitalized 13th street stretch.
        I love coming here in the wamer months, because all the windows and doors open up and the sounds of the busy city reverberate throughout the small space.

        The food is very simply and made fresh, the staff is very friendly and quick!  The Kennett pizza is a great thin crust pie and the various kinds of fungi, don't overwhelm the pie or give it a strong  'too mushroomy' taste. The combination is quite nice.

        Margherita is always a classic choice and you can not go wrong with that choice.
        The Crisped Octopus plate, even though 'small' as the header suggests, is a tiny portion, maybe I always crave more when I order it, but they could up the offering just a smidge.
        The variety of cheese and charcuterie is nice and compliments of the wine selections well. I've always been very happy with everything that we have ordered here.

        The space is fairly small and seating is limited, but the simplicity and execution of the pizzeria adds a wonderful pulse to the numerous and diverse eateries popping up in this section of town.

        Admittedly, I'm a cheap lay for Neapolitan style, live fired pizzas. There's something about that beautifully blistered crust, delicately fresh mozzarella, and artisanal toppings that sing sweet melodies to my palette.

        As such, Zavino mostly represents what I love about pizza, particularly in their crusts. The crusts here have exceptional character, they're like snowflakes in that no two are alike.  A nicely charred, razor thin, superbly crispy exterior gives way to a soft but not pillowy interior that bites back a bit, making for a pleasant chew. The flavor is surprisingly tangy without being aggressive, enhancing the pies' flavor. Zavino's website promises no burnt crusts or soggy middles, and the pies keep their word save for the occasionally errant slice. Tomatoes carry a brightly fresh, subtly sweet flavor without a hint of acridity, and the fior di latte (cow's milk mozz) has a firm, perfectly chewy texture with a clean, sweetly milky flavor. For the toppings, the meats shine due to their artisanal sourcing and high quality more than the vegetables do. For example, the Berkshire pork sausage on the Joey contains a seriously porky, slightly spicy-sweet taste whereas the spinach on the same pie failed to leave an impression.

        On a different note, many reviewers' qualms about limited space are quite well-founded, I can imagine during the winter seating is at a premium. It's not as bad during the summer, when outdoor seating doubles the restaurant's capacity, but still go during off hours for the best experience. The ambiance is somewhat intimate and trendy, with great service.

        I've also been to Stella for Neopolitan, and I strongly prefer Zavino's crusts to Stella's. For crust aficionados and lovers of quality meats, nothing beats Zavino.

At one point our server even asked if we'd like more, and that almost made us chuckle. Perhaps the board is a bit pricey at $18, yet we both wound up feeling it was a good deal considering the quality and quantity.

        There were a few minuses. Zavino is a small-scale restaurant, as much of a wine bar as a dining destination, but I expected more than one dessert item (tiramisu) on the menu. We passed, partly out of stuffedness, partly due to the lack of choice.
