    "Filly? I didn't know she was from Philly. You know, I really like those Philly Cheesesteaks. Actually, I like any kind of cheese. Actually, I like any kind of food from anywhere. " -- Racing Stripes (2005)

    Load me up with all the fixins and I'm happy.  Having been to a competitor on my last trip up, I was sure I was going to hit up Geno's this time around.  But the rain and lack of indoor seating made that an undesirable stop for me and my coworkers on this night. So I happened to get a suggestion from a friend, then reinforced by a check-in tip from that other app, then reinforced by the guys at the front desk of our hotel - Jim's Steaks.

    The place is not huge, but they have plenty of seating upstairs. You walk in and either head to the grill man to place your order, or walk to the other end to order a hoagie (do people really come in here to order hoagies?)...  Tell the man your cheese of preference (Wiz, American, Provolone) and what accoutrements you want with it (sweet peppers, mushrooms, onions, egg...).  And before you know it, you've got yourself a sammie.

    You walk to the register and get yourself a soda, water or beer, some chips if you like and pay (cash only, folks) and grab a spot at the bar seating, hear upstairs or take it to go...

    The seating upstairs is nice - lots of photos and signatures on the walls showing off the history of the joint while you enjoy the delightful snack you've just been given.  They have mustard and ketchup in pumps for you to add if you like.  

    I ordered mine with sweet peppers, mushrooms, onions and provolone cheese - having had wiz the first time i came to Philly.  I also ordered a side of plain chips and a Barq's - something about a cheesesteak screams Root Beer to me...

    After getting half way through my cheesesteak, I think it needed more cheese.  Things were falling off and it just was not gooey enough.  Maybe a little mustard added to the party would have helped, but it just seemed a bit dry.  The meat was delicious - nothing sinewy, good flavor. I just was missing more cheese and a little tang.

    I'll come back again - a definite step up from Pat Kings.

    CASH ONLY, FYI.  My first authentic Philly cheesesteak experience and Jim did not let me down!

    My mind was blown away from the first bite. I closed my eyes and enjoyed the flavor of the hot, juice dripping beef with the glorious cheese and onions on the warm, soft roll. This sandwich was the best sandwich I have ever tried in my whole life! I couldn't stop squealing about it or contain my excitement.     I inhaled the WHOLE sandwich! And even when I was stuffed, I kept thinking about that beautiful creation of meat, cheese, onion, and bread.

    Sadly, I am back in California and thousands of miles away from the cheesesteak.

    I would definitely consider going back and eating another, delicious sandwich.

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
