    i'm a little surprised i haven't reviewed this place yet. over the past 2.5 years, i've come here (and dreamed of coming here) numerous times for their brunch. sabrina's sits right at the top of my yummy but accessible list of philly brunch places.

    the wait is LONG. but i love that sabrina's allows you to call ahead and put your name on the list. sure cuts down on the amount of time you have to stand around waiting in the cold.

    french toast has been and always will be my go-to brunch item of choice. and nobody makes it like sabrina's. their challah french toast is fluffy and warm; their stuffed challah french toast is about 5x better, but almost 100x sweeter, which can wear on your sweet tooth about half-way through.

    over the years, i've also tried their other breakfast items, ranging from the standard egg and toast breakfast to the omelets to the eggs benedict (and other monthly brunch specials). the quality and yum factor varies, but any breakfast/brunch item here is generally a solid choice at the very least.

    but here's the kicker. this past weekend, i discovered something new: their huevos rancheros. if anyone can make me love mexican food, they deserve 5 stars. 'nuff said.

Specialties

Based off the same love for food, quality ingredients, and good friends that flourished Sabrina's Cafe in the Italian Market; Sabrina's Cafe and Spencer's Too hopes to satisfy even the loneliest of tummy's with behemoth portions and decadent fare no matter what time of the day it is. You can take that to the bank.

Checkout our Breakfast, Brunch, and Dinner. And did you know that we Cater to?

History

Established in 2007.

Sabrina's Cafe was born out of Robert & Raquel DeAbreu's desire to create a comfortable, welcoming place that served comfortable, welcoming food but with a twist. In May 2001, the first Sabrina's Café opened and was appropriately named after the couples new baby, Sabrina Isabella. With the help of impassioned regulars, Sabrina's began to outgrow it's modest upbringings and a second location was added in 2007 named Sabrina's Cafe & Spencer's Too, after their son.
