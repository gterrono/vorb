    My boyfriend and I recently took a weekend trip to Philly and made reservations for this place our first night there.  It was within walking distance from our hotel and sounded pretty good from other reviews I read so we thought, why not!  We were really disappointed...  We started off with the Alma Sampler: Oysters Rodriguez, Smoked Marlin, Royal Palm Dates.  The only thing I liked were the dates - everything taste better with BACON!  We also ordered the Yucca with Creamy Mojo - gross!!!!  The creamy mojo should have been called "the sticky, flavorless, unappealing mojo".  We also ordered the WORST tasting Sangria I have EVER had in my life!!!!  And a pitcher of it at that!!!!  I had two sips of it and just couldn't do it.  Tasted like I was drinking "Christmas" in a glass.  Like those horrible smelling Christmas candles (apple, cinnamon, very HEAVY clove tasting).  Let's just say that their sangria is WAY..... out of the norm of what Sangria's are - and NOT in a good way.  My sweetheart didn't want to let it go to waste so he drank mostly all of it.  I felt so bad for him.  When the waitress tried to refill my glass because she saw it was empty, I practically leaped out of my seat with a "NO"!!!!  Seeing my reaction, she asked if everything was okay.  I said, no and that I didn't like the Sangria at all....  She asked if I wanted something else but after calculating what the rest of the dinner was going to cost us, I decided to just stick to the water.  For dinner, I had the Lechon Asado which is the crispy roasted pork, congri rice and sour orange mojo.  The pork was really tasty I didn't like the rice and they definitely need to get RID of the SOUR orange mojo in this dish - YUK!  My boyfriend had the Vaca Frita "Fried Cow" which is the
    twice cooked crispy skirt steak with onions and Cuban oregano with black beans, white rice and tomato escabeche and he liked it as well.  Oh my goodness, how could I forget about the bread that they give you before your meal - SO SO SO TASTY!!!!  I would go back JUST for the bread and the bread ONLY!!!!  It was amazing!!!!  For dessert, my boyfriend had their award winning chocolate cigar and I must say that the presentation of it was great!!!  The matchbook that they placed on the side of the cigar was all lit up when it arrived to our table and I thought that was genius!  I don't remember the taste of it knocking my socks off, so I guess it was nothing special.  I had the Traditional Caramel Flan and didn't like it at all...  It comes with an apple ceviche that in my opinion, doesn't go well with the flan.  Definitely NOT traditional.  Sad, because dessert is my favorite part of any meal.  The decor was pretty and romantic.  Dim lighting is always a plus in my book.  I'm giving this place only 2 stars because YELP perfectly describes my experience there - "Meh. I've experienced better".  It was really really expensive and you just DON'T get what you pay for there!

    Listed in Great date spots, Five thumbs up, Style and substance, Meet the Parents

    Well slap my uncle and call me Starr-struck: Alma de Cuba rocked!  Going in, I was expecting to be impressed by the decor but underwhelmed by the food. My belly and I are pleased to announce that we were wrong on only one count.

    Alma really is beautiful. It's dimly lit and full of warm tones - a perfect fifth date spot, if-ya-know-what-I-mean (wink wink).  There's a lounge area and a bar when you first walk in, where you can order drinks and appetizers, and the restaurant is upstairs.  The whole enterprise is elegant and classy.

    All of this I was expecting. The food, however, surprised me.  My party of 4 decided to share three appetizers, two entrees, and a dessert, and it was the perfect amount of food.  (We were four ladies, though - your linebacker friend will probably need his own app and entree.)  The Japanese snapper ceviche was delicious, and the dates were rich and mouth-watering. I didn't love the chorizo sliders - I found them a little mealy - but my dining companions enjoyed them a lot. We had the salmon as one of our entrees, which was delicious, moist, and complemented very well by the seafood and rice stew-type-thing on which it was served.  The cuban cigar erred a little bit toward style over substance, but it was still enjoyable. Add in coffee (but no drinks), and our bill was a wholly reasonable $130, plus tip, split four ways.

    Alma de Cuba, I had you pegged as style-over-substance and not worth my time. I was wrong, and if you can forgive me, I'll be back... and this time I'll try your famous cocktails.

    Listed in Center City Dinner Spots

    Its been a while since I felt like "yelping" about a restaurant- but Alma de Cuba was so good last night it deserves a review!

    It was a Sunday night, our kids were both away on school trips, so my husband and I were in the mood for a romantic dinner.  Alma ded Cuba was the perfect setting- dim lighting and soft music.

    We started off with some fun drinks- he had a Dark N Stormy and and I had the Alma Colada. The colada was served in a cocunut shaped bowl topped with strips of shaved coconut.  It was intensely flavored with passionfruit an coconuit.  I tasted the Dark N Story too and it was delicious and refreshing!

    The appetizers we ordered were both amazing- the Oysters Rodriguez, a Cuban take on Oysters Rockefeller, consisted of four battered and fried oysters sitting on top of mashed plantains and spinach, each served in an oyster shell. Possbily the best oyster dish I've ever tasted!  The Rainbow ceviche, a mix of three kinds of marinated fish, topped with crispy sweet potato shreds tasted fresh and citrusy.

    Main course were good, though not quite as memorable as the appetizers- My husband had the salmon asopado with mussels and clams; I had the seared sugar cane tuna which was seared and served on top of hearts of palm rice. The servings were large- there were three really big chunks of tuna I could only eat two of them.

    For dessert we shared a banana semifrio which was so yummy- intensely banana flavored with a marshmallow sauce and macadamia nut crisp. I'll never eat another banana dessert again!

    I would highly recommend Alma de Cuba for a romantic evening and great food!

    On our journey to Philly, we used Yelp to identify places that we should eat.  Alma de Cuba had a high rating and a very high number of reviews.  Can't go wrong, right?  Right!

    We made reservations and were promptly seated once we arrived.  We were about to be taken up a monstrous flight of stairs when I objected, citing my sister's medical condition as reason to remain on the cool, romantic first floor, which was almost empty.  The hostess conferred with her manager and off we went.

    The seating was very comfortable, being composed of couches and chairs.  It felt like I was sitting in a dark, candlelit, Cuban living room - very cool.

    We started off with drinks.  I had a Robusto Mojito, which was sweet and delicious.  It was served with a real piece of sugar cane, I believe, which was tasty to chew on.  My wife ordered a Pina Colada, which was served with real coconut shavings, but the plastic cup was something a kid would drink out of.  My brother-in-law ordered a traditional Mojito.  My sister ordered water and all was well.

    We were served some fresh bread roles and some green dipping sauce, which was delicious.  Then we ordered appetizers, which included our choice of three ceviches.  We opted for Rainbow (Soy & lime marinated tuna, salmon, black bass, and jalapeno), Ecuadorian Shrimp (fire-roasted tomatoes, orange juice, avocado and corn nuts), and Japanese Red Snapper (Key lime, red onion, chili limo and sweet potato).  The ceviche was straight up delicious.  Always order this!

    Finally, we ordered dinner.  Unfortunately, everyone except my sister ordered the same dish - Vaca Frita.  This is a twice cooked, crispy skirt steak with onions and Cubano oregano with black beans, white rice and tomato escabeche.  This dish was interesting.  It had complex sweet and sour flavors, but the steak was overcooked and flavorless.  It wasn't nearly as good as some of the amazing Latin skirt steaks I've blissfully ocnsumed.

    My sister ordered the Chicken Combo, which included marinated grilled breast over rice with shredded chicken, manchego cheese, peas and olives.  The flavor of this dish was intense and very rich - almost too rich.  But the chicken was cooked perfectly and tasted awesome.

    With tip and drinks (three of us drank a mojito and one glass of wine), the total bill came to $263 for four.

    Recommend!

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
