    I was in Philly for a wedding weekend and met up with one my college friends, a Philadelphia local. Of course, I requested he take me for a great cheese-steak. Now, every tourist knows of Geno's and Pat's, but my friend, and some other locals I spoke with shook their heads when I mentioned these travel/food network channel destinations and suggested Jim's.

    A short walk from Independence Hall and the myriad of historical sights seen there is the South Street/Society Hill area which has a bunch of funky looking shops, art galleries, bars, and restaurants. We got to Jim's at about 1:45 pm on a Saturday and it was pretty crowded. The line was not out the door, but was only about two people shy away from being so. However, the line moves quickly.  While waiting in line you see the meat being sliced and boy does it look fresh. There is constant beef and onions on the griddle to make the operation run smoothly, and it does.

    I ordered a cheese-steak with provolone, sweet peppers and onions. (Cheese whiz does not do it for me, although it is an option here, as is American cheese). We ate in the upstairs dining room which is totally no frills. The cheese-steak, in my possibly inexperienced cheesteak eating opinion, was fantastic. The meat is savory and flavorful and the provolone was truly outstanding and smokey, unlike the provolone you so often get at places like, say Subway. The sweet peppers were also fantastic as were the onions which were also very sweet. The meat retained juice getting the bun a little soppy but not too mushy. I did not want to finish this sandwich I was enjoying it so much.

    My only real complaint with it though is that it couldd have been a bit bigger. Now, I had been walking around for about four hours that morning and might have worked up a large appetite but the other diners I was with were also not totally satisfied and we probably could have split a couple of more sandwiches. For about ten bucks you want to be full. This is why I need to deduct one star. Please note though that for many people, this sandwich will be more than enough, but it just was not for me and my friends.

    Overall though, I really liked Jim's and am jealous of Philadelphians who get to have cheese-steaks like this on a regular basis.

    So far this is my favorite cheesesteak place. I was lucky enough to come in when it was not very crowded as it is known to have the line snake across many times and sometimes out the door.

    You can clearly see them cooking and serving up the steaks through a glass window that is occasionally steamed up along the sizzle of the cooking of the steaks. They are all friendly here, and they even have a rotating video of an explanation of how you order the steaks in Philly.

    I like to get mine like the locals -- "whiz wit." There's two different ways they serve it with wiz in the city, one slathered over the meat and onions, and another with the wiz spread over the bread with the meat/onions on top, and Jim's is the latter. Which I like more.

    They don't take cards here so be prepared with cash, and it's a bit of an assembly line ordering process. Order a steak with the cook and watch them pass it down to finally the ordering guy and grab that can of Birch Beer while you're at it to fully enjoy the local experience.

    Jim's steak is chopped -- and also marinated with juicy flavor. The Amoroso bread is so fresh and soft, it feels like heaven when you bite into the sandwich. You have to walk up stairs to the seating area room, which also gives you a great second floor view of what's happening on South Street.

    While everyone always bickers about what cheesesteak they like better, you should definitely give Jim's a try on your cheesesteak-tour if you decide to embark on it, you won't be disappointed.

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
