    This place has such a great ambiance. They must have attracted a few strange looks at the hardware store buying gallons upon gallons of bubblegum pink paint, but in the evening with the lights turned down low, hoppin' music playing out of a jukebox, and alongside glittering tabletops, a backlit tequila bar, and a wall of shiny Mexican luchador masks, the pink is the icing on the cake.

    The spicy peanuts that our waiter started us with were really nice. Just the right amount of spice, and you could see little shavings of lime zest. They were the perfect teaser for all the flavors to come. I also started with a white sangria as my beverage, and it was so fruity and delicious!

    We started out with the refried beans as an appetizer. They were quite nice-- not the absolute best I've had (but then again, the best I've had came from a little Mexican hole-in-the-wall in Chicago where I'm sure they stirred a bunch of lard-- you can't beat the magic of pork fat!).

    We ordered the duck mole and the costillas huarache (a shortrib 'pizza'). The duck mole really delivered on flavor, but I'd have to say that the huarache takes the prize for my favorite dish, just because it was such a creative take on the idea of pizza. The crust in particular, which tasted of corn, was a perfect backdrop to the tender shortribs and crema on top.

    Finally, we finished with the churros as our dessert. I thought they were quite good-- a tad greasy, but the dipping sauces that came with them were really well done. One (the "cajeta crema") was a lighter, almost carmel-y sauce with goat cheese, and the other was a very dark and spicy Mexican chocolate sauce.

    Service was also terrific from start to finish. I wish I could have remembered our waiter's name to sing his praises here!

    Listed in Worth A Special Journey, Garces Obsession

    So how much do I love Distrito?  Between the lovely wife and me, together and separately, including lunch and dinner, with and without guests, we have accumulated twenty (20) visits!  This was the first Garces restaurant that turned us on to Jose's inspirational cuisine.

    In the interests of brevity I'll just focus on dinner.  Virtually every visit we have chosen the tasting menu and never regretted it.  Actually there are two tasting menus with $45 and $55 price points.  Your wait person will tell you that the difference is the emphasis on seafood.  We have always selected the $45 version.

    Something we appreciate is the ability to tweak the choices.  We have always found the wait staff to be very accommodating with respect to likes and dislikes.

    Our tasting menu journey usually begins with the amazing guacamole.  It's almost embarrassing to watch us fight over every last tidbit.  Next is often a salad.  Every time it's a delicious blend of fruits or vegetables and a unique dressing.  Many times we have enjoyed a flatbread often with delicious mushrooms.  Carne Asada is common and it has always been cooked perfectly for us.  A side of savory black beans and rice is routine.  One time my wife enjoyed a rabbit casserole.  She talked about it for days!  It just seems that the course go on and on with desert included of course.  It's an excellent value.

    Some folks call the cuisine at Distrito modern Mexican.  Others call it Mexican fusion.  I just call it delicious!

    Listed in West Philly, Best Philly

    Saying that Distrito is my least favorite of Jose Garces's restaurants is a bit like saying that Hershey's is my least favorite kind of chocolate.  It's true, but in the end, it's still chocolate.

    I didn't like Distrito at all for a few years, mostly because I had only been for dinner.  The dinner menu is expensive, the portions are small, and the flavors and textures just don't live up to what I expect from JG.  The margaritas are weak, too, which adds insult to injury.  However, I recently discovered two things Distrito does very well: happy hour and brunch.  If you go at one of those times, you can expect to enjoy the kitschy-but-classy bar area, spend a completely reasonable amount of money, and leave happy.

    TL;DR: Skip dinner, go for brunch. Get the Torrijas (dulce de leche french toast).  Thank me later.

    As a visitor to Philly, this was a cool place to check out but I don't know that I would return unless for a kitsch occasion or to show someone the decor.

    Enter Distrito and the color splash immediately screams contemporary chic meets Candyland or Wonka's factory.  I went last week for my second visit bringing several friends for their debut for this "Modern Mexican" experience.  The downstairs or lounge area is vast and could almost suffice as the entire restaurant with booths and about a dozen barstools.  Although it was enticing to dine downstairs in this lounge area, with a crew of 5 people we agreed to follow our most affable hostess upstairs and were comfortably seated in a huge wicker booth.  Enroute to our table there were floating hammocks and swanky seating selections at the onset of the dining room but our corner booth was ideal as it made conversing with one another easy; this is ideal for a quaint meal in what is otherwise a cavernous second floor room.  The whole place reportedly seats 250 patrons!     The drinks were rather pricy and weak by potency standards, but the seasonal margarita pitchers ordered were said to be tart yet tasty.  The conventional cocktails of vodka club and jack and coke were scant pours.  

    The ceviche was nothing short of terrific.  With a shellfish allergy at the table, we avoided the shrimp, but two orders of the "Atun" didn't stand a chance and they were devoured immediately hence our need to order a third order.  The "Chilango Chop" was one of the better salads I've ever tasted with the perfect blend of spiced pecans and cranberries accompanied with what is undoubtedly a house made honey lime vinaigrette.  "The District Chicken" Entree was spectacular: slow roasted, tangy and tender- cooked to perfection- well worth the $24 price tag.  As for the tacos, the tapas or small portions are no doubt revealed here, but the prices warrant heftier or at least adequate amounts of the itemized order. Specialties

Distrito is the new restaurant concept by Jose Garces, the chef behind the acclaimed Philadelphia restaurants Amada and Tinto. Distrito is a high-energy restaurant inspired by the spirited culture and cuisine of Mexico City. The Distrito menu features Chef Garces' fresh interpretations of classic Mexican fare and beverages include over 65 tequilas and margaritas made with fresh seasonal fruits. A striking decor includes a hot pink and bright green color scheme, a wall constructed from over 600 colorful, campy masks of lucha libre, or "free fight" professional wrestlers, and a booth crafted from a Volkswagen Beetle, a typical Mexico City taxi cab.

History

Established in 2008.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
