    Eulogy is one of the few places in the city I visit on a regular basis. The beer selection is enormous, the bartenders are always helpful and friendly, and the food is top notch.

    I highly recommend ordering some Buffalo wings and fritjes. The wings are meaty and covered in a unique creamy sauce that I haven't found anywhere else. The Belgian fritjes are something you must experience and don't be surprised when you find yourself craving them weeks later. This may explain why I find myself coming back for more.

    You will love the beer, even if you don't like beer.. give their Busty Blonde a try, it is brewed especially for them.

    In my opinion, Eulogy is without a doubt, the best Gastropub in Philly.

    I've been to Eulogy twice now, and both times have been at the upstairs bar.  My most recent visit was during Philly Beer Week, so it was understandably crowded.  My beau and I were able to grab two seats at the far end of the bar near the windows and fawned over the beer list.  Coronado Brewing's Mermaid Red was my first choice, which was good, but not nearly as tasty as Tom's K-O Blond.  Appropriately named, the 10% ABV was dangerously drinkable with hints of honeysuckle, apricot, and lemon.  

    I have yet to sample the food here but it's on my to-do list.  Their offerings seem very similar to Monk's.  I should also mention that the bartender (didn't catch her name) at the upstairs bar was absolutely fantastic.  One of the beers we wanted was downstairs, and she didn't mind running down to grab it for us.  Great customer service, and super friendly to boot!

    Listed in Philly Gastropubs

    Another craft beer mecca in Philadelphia.  Eulogy has been one of my favorite places in the city to find really rare craft beers, especially sour ales which I love.  They have 300-400 different bottles, and a handfull of tap lines that always have something to please.  The bartenders are awesome, just ask for their recommendation of a beer you like and they will pick something good for you.  My new friend Greg helped recommend some rare bottles that he pulled from out of the cellar down the basement!

    As for food, the honey dijon mussels were my favorite, although I haven't had them in a long time.  But last week I came here for a barrel-aged beer tap takeover event, and I ate the veggie burger which was fantastic. So tender and thick, lots of good sauce on it also.  Last week we also had a pastry roll stuffed with duck, mushrooms, and cheese.  It was one of the best meat pastries I've ever tasted!  

    The bar is small and dark like a typical Belgain pub.  But there's upstairs seating also so that  helps.  I can't comment on the table service becuase I ALWAYS sit at the bar.  Don't come here if you like to drink watery-light beers, there's not enough bar stools for that!  This place is definatly made for beer enthusiasts.  If you love a huge variety of beers and can't get a seat at the bar, there's another larger craft pub across the street called Bierstube that I also enjoy very much!  Cheers!

    Listed in Let's go drinking!

    Star #1 is for the mussels. This is THE spot for mussels in Old City, maybe in all of Philly (well, if it weren't for Monks). We tried the Dijon variety, and couldn't believe how large and sweet the meat was and how incredible the sauce was. We couldn't get enough bread to sop it up.

    Star #2 is for the calamari. Or really, the flavorful aioli. Yum.

    Star #3 is for the atmosphere and location. Cobblestone on 2nd and Chestnut leading to a perfect mix of dim and twinkly lights with music that's not too loud and a crowd that's only a little rowdy.

    Star #4 is for the staff. Attentive and friendly, we had a great experience even though we took up a 4 person table for 2 of us and didn't order proper dinners. They kept us happy!

    Star #5 is for the BEER. Oh the beer. The list I looked at first was just the draft specials. AND THEN I OPENED THE MENU. Craft beer lovers, unite, and visit Eulogy. You are guaranteed to find something you love and probably many you haven't tried yet.

    This Old City beer bar brings together all the aspects that I appreciate in a place that provided me with pages and pages of beer from which to choose. In Philadelphia, Monk's receives the majority of the spotlight in the Belgian Beer Bar category, but Eulogy has always been on an equal level for me. The beer list provides extensive information on each beer; origin, alcohol, type, etc. The building is thin and the bar can get cramped, but this is how I like a Belgian beer bar! The cramped, stuffy atmosphere creating a hubbub of chatter as everyone discusses their current, past, and future beer choices. The servers know their beer and can provide a wealth of helpful information, so I know a excellent choice will always be made. A worthy bar in the Philadelphia beer arena.

Specialties

This is Beer Heaven, give a "high praise" Eulogy for your favorite beer after you consume it.  We also prepare our food in the authentic Flemish Belgian Style as opposed to other Belgian restaurants who choose the French Walloon Belgian style.  This means our fries are thick enough to truly hold a double fry technique, our fries go "crunch, goo" because they are the right thickness, the wimpy skinny fries elsewhere go "goo goo goo" because they are too skinny and the double fry technique does not work.  Also our mussels are larger than the PEIs other restaurants use, this is because our mussels are the authentic North Sea size like in Belgium.  The more meat the more flavor the mussel can hold in our ala minute pan technique (all ingredients added fresh, never ever ever any pour over sauce on mussels).  Also our handburgers are award winning based on seven secret spices and our fish and chips and other entrees are as good as the beers ! Make a reservation at www.eulogybar.com

History

Established in 2002.

My family is from Brugge, Belgium, I am passionate about Belgian beer, food and heritage, this adds an authenticity to the business.  I got the building in 2002 and built the restaurant myself using antique stain glass behind the bar from an old Philadelphia church and a chandelier from an old church as well.  The bar was constructed in Fishtown in Philadelphia by a local carpenter using Honduras Mahogany I imported and the design of the bar was based on a blueprint I drew.  
We take our beer very very very serious, our beer starts in a beer cooler and is then delivered via dual gas blenders using co2 and nitrogen blends for the proper carbonation technique.  Most of our draft beer never travels more than six feet but along that journey there are glycol filled lines keeping the beer at 40 ish degrees until it hits your glass.  We have about 200 different glasses we keep in stock because most of the taste for beer is accomplished through your smelling it, yes your nose!
