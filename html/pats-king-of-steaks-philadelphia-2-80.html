    LET ME GET ONE WHIZ WIT!!

    Translation?  I want a cheesesteak with cheese whiz and grilled onions.  There's a kitschy little sign that will explain things if you're about 10 people back in line and are looking for how to order.  Granted, the folks here won't make fun of you if you spell things out and say, "hi, may I have a steak with cheese whiz and grilled onions?"  I think they get enough tourists to understand.

    All three time that I've gone were usually just to say we had "been there, done that" before we skittered off to Atlantic City in a vain attempt to double our pay checks.  Overall, it's another JDAM to the gut - it will keep you full and tastes even better while you're foraging for drunk food.

    Top it up with sweet peppers, a little bit of spicy relish, and use liberal amounts of ketchup.  I also recommend baby wipes so you're keeping your fingers clean after - that grease has a way of persevering over most napkins.

    I'd be lying if I said I wasn't going to do it again so I won't - but man, it's not the first stop in the quest for a good steak!

    It's a tradition.  We pack into cars, drive down route 1-- and enter Philadelphia.  We go shopping, walk around South Street and as a reward-- we go to Pat's for some grub.

    Pat's is across from the famous Geno's (I always call it the Boyz II Men cheesesteak place), which in comparison looks awfully boring.  But to me-- is yummier.

    It's quick, you order what cheesesteak want (say it right or get sent to the back of the line) from one window, pick it up at the next window. Then if you want a drink or fries, order it at the window after that.

    I'd give it 5 stars for quickness and um.. deliciousness (anything with Cheez Whiz owns me).  But the lines are sometimes ridiculously long (especially when it's cold out) and outside it's filthy.

    After Geno's, we headed over to Pat's to make the comparison. Service was a bit more brusque here, but they handled the crowd very efficiently, considering it's smaller and was a little more crowded than Geno's. Once thing I liked straight off was that you could see them cooking the meat from the window where you ordered. I just love the sight of raw meat.

    I snagged a table from a girl who was leaving; she had just tossed her entire partially eaten sandwich into a trash can and loudly complained, "worst cheesesteak ever!" Uh oh, that kind of worried me.

    We didn't do an exact comparison, since we got provalone at Geno's and American at Pat's, but overall we really liked Pat's a lot more. The difference was subtle, but we really noticed it in the meat - Pat's was more tender, a little more medium/rare, and had more flavor. Same delicious bun, good onions, and I'd have to say that American was a better cheese choice than provalone, it really makes for a great combo with the meat. Speaking of meat, I love that both Pat's and Geno's had whole slices of meat that were not chopped up into little itty bits like I've had back home. Besides the quality of the bun, the integrity of the meat was the main difference that I noticed between genuine philly cheesesteaks and the ones I'd had elsewhere. Oh yeah, and I hate peppers, so I was relieved to find that the default cheesesteaks just came with onions, no peppers, which is also unlike what I've had elsewhere.

    The only real bummer about Pat's was that they only had Pepsi, no Coke. :(

    My real goal was to try Jim's, but unfortunately I got lost trying to find it, so that comparison will have to wait until next time.

    Pat's is a toughie. If you grew up in Philly, you likely have a soft spot for it. But that also might blind you a little to all its shortcomings. I grew up stopping at Pat's before Phillies games, so its hard for me to separate the nostalgia from the reality. But I try.

    Let's start with the obvious - the cheesesteaks suck. I think we all can mostly agree on this. if you actually like them, you need to experience Steve's or Chinks or Delassandros or Tony Luke's or hell, even Jim's does it way better. And for insult to injury, its a tourist trap so they charge you 8 bucks a steak. And the whole soup-nazi thing, please. It might be funny as an urban legend, but when i see tourists get sent to the back of the line because they took too long to order, I cringe for our collective image. Shitty food and shitty service is about as great a combo as swiss on a cheesesteak.

    There are things to like about Pats though. The scene is a good one. I like the South Philly vibe, the outdoor seating, the baseball diamond and bball courts across the way. Its a bustling little city corner that is fun for any fans of people watching. They have really good birch beer. I don't know why I think this, I'm sure it's nothing special, but god damn that fountain birch beer is delicious!

    But here's the one thing that you need to know about Pats. Pats is all about the CHEESE FRIES! They have the best cheese fries (of the whiz-and-fries-only category, not fanciness with bacon or ranch or 3 cheeses) in the whole city. I swear by this. Plus, they are priced more reasonably (3.50 is still a lot, but way better deal than the steak). There also is never a line at the fry window, so you don't have to wait, and they are soooooo good. My fiancee insists that they taste so good because they are fried twice. I respond with "you can fry things twice??" These fries are fat kid tasty, yamsayin? Seriously, do yourself a favor, next time you go to Pat's just bypass the steaks, get some fries, and soak in the vibe. All the crappy stuff happens at the steak window.
