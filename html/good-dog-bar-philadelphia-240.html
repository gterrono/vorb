    My friend and I went to check out Good Dog after hearing that the place has empanadas (check) filled with truffled cheesesteak meat (check, check). We had a dinner and drinks date and were totally pleased with the venue, the food, and the people.

    I really liked the atmosphere, it had a super easy going feel, just a place to enjoy some food and drinks without a lot of hard thinking. Stress free with lots of friendly faces. We didn't catch the bartenders name but he was super cool and very accommodating. We got our drinks very quickly and the food came out at lightening speed as well.

    We ordered a half size fries, love the mix up of the regular and sweet potato fries, you get the best of both worlds. An order of the calamari cups: essentially just fried calamari over some iceberg leaves, which I tried to roll into a crispy calamari lettuce wrap; it was pretty good, but we stuck to just eating them by hand. We also ordered the cheese steak empanadas, which I was pleased with. If you are a truffle lover, you need to try the empanadas, they are quite simple but the truffle flavoring lingers on your tongue.

    The couple next to us was chowing down on some burgers, which looked incredible and from what they were saying tasted even better.  
    Good Dog seems like its a great place for groups, lots of booth space and a pool table on the 3rd floor for after dinner playtime.

    If you're looking for some good eats, good vibe, and an affordable price range...check out Good Dog!

    Came (sat, stayed) here this weekend with a fellow Yelper, more or less with the express purpose of adding to the torrent of Good Dog rave reviews.

    I arrived a little early and scoped out the rest of the place. There are two more floors apart from the main floor, one of which has a couple of pool tables. Downstairs was pretty packed on a Saturday afternoon, but finding a table was easy, and upstairs was pretty much empty anyway.

    I'm not usually a fan of winter ales, but I liked the sound of "Elysian Bifrost" rolling off my tongue, and it turned out to be a great pick for an erstwhile amber ale devotee.

    The Good Dog burger looked too good to pass up, and was it ever! The brioche bun put up a good fight against the roquefort-filled 1/2 lb. sirloin patty. The mix of regular and sweet potato fries, which arrived shriveled up and healthy looking, turned out to be amazing. I did my best, but ultimately left a sizable pile on my plate.

    I look forward to coming back to try some other stuff. The pork belly salad and truffled cheese steak empanadas sounded delish.

    More like Mediocre Dog.

    First and foremost, let me say that the fact that I am not an animal person, let alone a dog person, has zero impact on this review. Even if the walls were covered with pictures of stuff I love, like cupcakes or babies or Mark Ruffalo, I still wouldn't think it was that great.

    My boyfriend and I were seated in the corner of the wall during my first visit to Good Dog on a Monday evening. It was more crowded than we had expected, and the restaurant somehow made a two-seater out of an awkward space in corner. I faced a wall, and Zack faced me on a diagonal. The beer served to Zack was flat, but they gave him a new one, no problem. I ordered a veggie burger because other Yelpers said it was the best. Zack ordered the Good Dog Burger, which he enjoyed although the roquefort was a bit overpowering.

    Although I was impressed that the veggie burger was homemade, with rice and lentils and mushrooms and all that good stuff, it was mushy and tasted of nothing except cumin and my #1 Least Favorite Taste - 'effin cilantro. Once it's in your mouth you can't escape the flavor, am I right?

    The fries had great potential (a mix of sweet and regular) but were lukewarm and soggy, like they were from the bottom of the bag or were sitting in the warmer too long.

    Three days later I found myself at the Good Dog again to meet friends. There was a line, on a Wednesday at 7:30, and we were ushered to the third floor to wait for our names to be called. As soon as we got upstairs, I felt like I was in high school, sipping beer in someone's poorly-lit finished basement. Groups of people were sitting on the carpeted floor, or in chairs set up in a circle. Only this time, I was drinking some fancy IPA and not a Coors Light.

    We hunted down the hostess after about 20 minutes and she decided that yes, it was time for us to sit down. I got the beet and goat cheese salad, which did not taste fresh and light like a salad should. The goat cheese balls were breaded and deep fried. I'm all for "lightly fried goat cheese" like the menu stated, but these balls seemed like the kind you could pick up in the frozen section of Trader Joe's, only to be disappointed when they don't taste like you thought they would.
