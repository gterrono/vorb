        Visiting family in PA and my Yelp Elite father was on a quest to impress his daughter with fabulous meals.  He succeeded!

        Enter Spasso.  We were picked up from the Philadelphia Flower Show and a short drive later we came upon Spasso.  Also, nice to note that only one or so blocks away we found street parking for only $4, that's pretty great for Philly if you know what I mean.

        This is a larger restaurant than you first expect when first entering.  Lots of rooms and separate areas for larger parties.  We were seated in the back rooms next to some very large parties.  With that said this may not be the perfect place for romantic outings since it can be very noisy with close quarters to your neighboring tables.  Not a very intimate setting for those "I love you moments".  

        It must be said that our waiter was out of this world.  Super heavy Italian accent, sweet smile and an excellent sense of humor.  A little flirty as well, always a plus with an accent like that.

        The bread and chunky vegetable spread was a great start to the meal, and I will admit we went through two bowls of the spread as well as two bread baskets.  The menu offers a little bit for everyone, some seafood, veal, beef, chicken, and straight pasta if you like.  I love places that take the time to make their own pasta in house.  It really shows their commitment to greatness.  Our entrees were boasting great flavor and I was delighted to see the generous portions.  I decided on the Chicken Fontinella that came with asparagus, a side of perfectly grilled vegetables and I asked for a special side of homemade pasta (I couldn't resist).  Everything was delicious and I cleaned my entire plate.  The pasta was a home run, I definitely recommend any dish with their pasta.  Mom and Dad enjoyed their entrees just as much.  It was a truly splendid experience with top notch Italian food.

        With hit-or-miss (mostly hit) food and impeccable service, Spasso garners a 4-star rating.

        To clarify, Spasso proudly advertises that they specialize in "Italian comfort food." What this means is that you're not going to taste mind-blowingly-creative versions of Italian fare or palette-challenging specialities. You will get ample portions and properly prepared Italian classics.

        Meals start out with lackluster Italian bread but absolutely scrumptious roasted antipasto veggie dip. My Fetuccine Shrimp was a disappointment, the noodles were delicious and well-textured but the "pesto cream sauce," although pleasantly light, was one-dimensional in the flavor department. The "rock shrimp" promised were tiny, overcooked, and lacked the lobstery-flavor I love about rock shrimp. I wouldn't order this dish again. The Gnocchi al Asparagi, however, was a delight. Gnocchi here are pillowy and light, like eating a cloud. The asparagus was crisp and fresh and the sauce was a complex, flavorful harmony of tangy and savory, as it demanded to be mopped up with bread. The Penne Spasso Primavera (a vegetarian custom creation we requested) had perfect pasta and fresh veggies but again suffered from a one-dimensional pink sauce.

        Service, as I mentioned, was excellent. My waiter helped my picky, vegetarian dining companion order the aforementioned Penne Spasso Primavera, which isn't on the menu but was to his liking. She was very attentive to our needs and accessible. Prices were quite reasonable, most of the mains are under $20. All told, worth a stop for the fabulous gnocchi alone, but not a bad option on a small budget for Italian comfort food.

        A little off the beaten path on Front Street, it's not one of the first places you think of when going out to eat in old city.  The interior is exposed brick with booths along the far wall and the kitchen semi-open in the back.

        Food review:
        Grilled calamari: excellent.  Charred and moist at the same time, just a squirt of lemon and its perfect, order a plate right when you sit down.

        Caprese salad: basic 4 slices of tomato over shredded lettuce with olive oil.  Nothing fancy and totally forgetable.  No balsamic, no sea salt, couldn't even find any basil.  The cheese was a generic low moisture mozzarella, the olive oil had no flavor to it at all.  This should be a staple at an Italian restaurant!

        Veal parmagiania with housemade linquine.  The veal was tender and pounder paper thin, you could cut it with your fork.  The breading was solid and crunchy, but could have used a touch of salt.  The sauce on the veal and pasta was a little bland and tasted "cooked", at a place like this I was expecting a bright and fresh tomato sauce.

        Theclams came with a mildly garlic white wine broth (but could use some hearty bread), but the dish itself was excellent.

        Papadele wih porchini cognac sauce and chicken had large and rich noodles but the sauce was  little weak and we had to make a game out of trying to find the actual chicken pieces in the dish.

        Serviec was friendly and attentive and the place itself was clean and welcoming.  Easy parking outfront on Front  or the attached lot next door.

        Could be potential 3.5-4 stars except the bread is a total let down and overall quality is closer to an Olive Garden (with blander food) than the neighborhood gem that cooks like your grandmother does.
