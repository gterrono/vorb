    Went there on a weekday.  Walked right in and had the option to sit indoors or out but, since my sunglasses were stolen last weekend, I opted for indoors.  We were sat immediately and I was instantly impressed by the atmosphere.  Even though theres nothing fancy about it, the fact that they have large windows that let in plenty of natural light and the simplistic decorations gave me the impression that it was just a simple eatery off of some main street in some European city.

    Anyways, on to the food.  I ordered the scallops which came on a bed of risotto.  It was perfect, scallops were just the right firmness and the risotto was not overly heavy so I didnt feel stuffed by the end of the meal.  My date had the pappardelle which was basically linguini with a red sauce and what seemed like a pound of ground veal, pork and beef.  It was delicious and had a really nice comfort-food quality about it.

    As for dessert,If you like sorbet, they make thier own homemade version in weekly flavors. This week was strawberry-orange and it was delicious.

    Plus its BYOB!!! That means you can have a great meal and drinks for two and still get outta there for under a $100.  I will take that anyday.

    Our experience was delightful at Mercato.  We read the reviews online and also called about how they work reservations.  Apparently on Fridays they do take reservations between 5-6:30 for "theater" patrons, even if you aren't going to the theater.  Any time after this they do not take them.

    Our wait staff was excellent- we were given water and had our wine open very quickly, plus they came to take our orders before our first glass was empty (the place was packed, so I expected the service to be off, but it wasn't AT ALL!).  

    The food was very good- I had the strawberry and goat cheese salad which is perfect for a warm summer evening.  Also had the cioppino, which is a seafood stew, and it was really delicious- that bowl was chock full of seafood.  It was heaven.  Not so perfect for a warm summer evening, as I was sweating when we left the restaurant, but it was worth it for the yummy sea morsels.

    Mercato is the kind of place 5 yelp star reviews should be reserved for. This Italian BYOB has some of the best food I've ever tasted and I can't believe it took me so long to try it.

    I went here for restaurant week and was blown away with all three courses. The famous artichoke lived up to the hype, I would gladly bathe in that sauce, but the calamari stole the appetizer show. The meat was so tender yet smokey on the outside which just went perfect with all the other components (especially the different kinds of olives). It was served like a salad and a bite with a little bit of everything was to die for.

    I tried both the short rib ragu and the pyramid pasta, both very rich and delicious. The gnocchi was shaped differently than I'm used to but those little ricotta balls were some of the best I've ever had. Amazing sauce that I was sure to soak up 100% with the table side served bread. You can't go wrong with the combination of brown butter and lobster/shrimp, I could eat those pyramids every meal.

    Dessert was the perfect ending to such a great meal. Both the cheesecake and chocolate cake won't win originality points, but the taste speaks for itself. The chocolate cake was warm and moist and served over ice cream, completing the knockout punch for my food coma.

    Our reservation was for 7:30 and we were seated promptly. We were on the somewhat rushed side but I was quick to forgive them as the combination of restaurant week chaos and torrential downpour shook things up as the outdoor tables were out of commission. Nonetheless, the service improvised and did a great job considering how small this place is.

    Can't wait to go back and try everything else.

    we became a fast fan of mercato as soon as the grilled octopus salad was set down on our table.  do you know how fantastic a salad has to be for my husband to say that he would eat the salad even if it didn't come with the grilled octopus?  the man acts like vegetables causes cancer so it has to be pretty freaking fantastic.  the grilled flavor of the octopus, which was still quite tender in the middle, was perfectly complemented the fresh veggies, cannelloni beans, capers and light vinaigrette dressing.  generally we opt out of eating tomatoes in a salad, but these were obviously fresh, vine ripened and were delicious.

    my husband ordered the short rib ragu with gnocchi and it was a great selection for the cold an wintery night.  the ragu sauce was heavy but had depth and flavor.  the short rib was tender.  he was kind enough to share so i also enjoyed it as well.

    i ordered the special: a lamb shank with lentils and braised carrots.  not that it was a light dish, but definitely not as heavy as the shortrib gnocchi.  the lamb was tender and not at all gamey.  i shared as well and the plate was returned to me with exactly two bites left... because he promised not to eat it all.  thanks.

    we ended our meal with coffee and trio of housemade gelato.  since moving to philly, i've been very disappointed with gelato and have stopped ordering it all together.  one bite of the dulce de leche and it was everything i have been missing about gelato and more.  the nutella scoop had bits of toasted hazelnuts and the peanut butter clearly was made from fresh, natural peanutbutter.  it was everything gelato should be: dense with flavor (i mean it was clear what flavor it was, i didn't have to guess what it was suppose to be), slightly lighter than ice cream but not milky or flat like low-fat ice cream.

    service is attentive and sweet.  the only downside to mercato is that it's small, cramped and they don't take reservations.  the reservations part i can deal with since our regular date night is schedule for during the week anyway.  however; my chair was accidentally kicked by the wait staff several times because there just isn't any space between the tables.  i felt like at one point we were sharing the table with another couple because there is literally 2 inches of space that separated us.  even with all of these annoyances, they still earn a 5 star rating for me and i would gladly tolerate the loss of personal space for another dinner here again.
