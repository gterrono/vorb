    Vic's seems to be the holy grail of sushi, at least by reviews and ratings on yelp. I've heard from a bunch of people that this place is amazing, but somehow I haven't been able to try it until last weekend. Maybe it's the fact that it's literally a sushi bar, with only one table squeezed in by the door. The seem to run most of their business on takeout, but a lucky 10 people or so can sit at the bar if there's a seat.

    We came early on a saturday (6pm) to get be those people and there were actually a decent amount of seats available. About 20 minutes in however, it really started to fill up. It's BYO so we brought a bottle of white and they gave us wine glasses right away. If you want water, it's in the corner with paper cups and you get it yourself. Be warned, it can be very tricky getting in and out of the chairs at the bar, since they are so many packed in together.

    The beautiful thing about Vic's is that they have a $11 for 3 rolls special, and even though you choose from the "basics" they put a lot of fish and not too much rice on each one. We each got the 3 rolls and one special roll, which was the perfect amount of food (and maybe even a little too much). The special rolls are pricey, but they are pricey everywhere (hence being special) and they are HUGE. I got the red dragon, eel, avocado and cucumber with tuna on top and i think it was $12. They had some special roll additions on the white board, and they all looked amazing although they were more like $14. I can't wait to go back to try more of them! Service was great because the chefs were right in front of you - you order with them and pay with them - no waiting!

    Overall, with tip, dinner came to about $26. Not bad at all for the quantity and quality of what we got.

    I really do think I have a minor addiction to Vic Sushi Bar. Reading through the reviews below, most of the things I want to say have already been mentioned. But why not add my voice to the mix? At the very least it would reinforce some things that have been said.

    I've only eaten at inside the restaurant once. It's very very small. Without a reservation, chances are you aren't getting a table, or you're eating past your bedtime.

    What I usually do is order delivery, or take out... usually delivery. They have a decent sized delivery radius in Center City so make sure to check if you live in the area. I don't actually live that close to it.

    The 3 roll special is a really great deal. 3 rolls for $11. The quality of the fish is very good, not the greatest, but very good as sushi places in Philly go. I certainly have no problem gobbling it up.

    What takes the cake are the tuna dumplings. They are SO GOOD. If you know me, I don't use capitalized words lightly. It's sort of like a California roll (imitation crab, avocado), but instead of rice and seaweed, it's wrapped into a purse with delicious tuna! Then they drizzle that addicting Japanese mayo, sweet soy mixture onto it, and really is divine.

    This is what I do: I eat the tuna dumplings and savor the flavors along the way. Then in my sadness at the fact I have no more tuna dumplings left, I take consolation in taking the 3 roll special and cleaning up the sauce the tuna dumplings came in with them. Then when I'm done I look down at the damage I've done, and wish I ordered another order of tuna dumplings.
