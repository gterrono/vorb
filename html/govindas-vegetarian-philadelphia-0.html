    Govinda's Cafe (Gourmet to Go) is downstairs and the main Govinda Restaurant restaurant is upstairs.  Fancy upstairs, casual downstairs.  I've heard good things about both.. though I've only been to the cafe, so this review is for Govinda's Cafe (Gourmet to Go):

    Govinda's Cafe- Gourmet to Go-  is a hip, cool Philly cheesesteak joint... there is an atmosphere of fun here.  The best part is subtle:  it's all vegetarian!   Their cafe features vegan and vegetarian soups, sandwiches, and other deeelliccccious items. I can't rave enough about them.  

    They offer soy or regular cheese on their sandwiches, as well as flavored tortilla wraps instead of bread.  Tip: go for the soy cheese and wrap on your sandwiches for a tastier edge.  I find the chicken versions to be tastier over their other meat soy renditions.  The items are grilled fresh on the spot-- and each cook adds their own touch:  I find each one has their own "specialty"-- they make one or two items more standout.  So one item can be extraordinary one day-- and the next just good.  Therefore, you have to know the cooks and their niche too.. to get that extraordinary touch.

    Their sandwiches are unreal.  Most items are large portions-- perhaps even large enough for two for some.  Their chicken cheesesteak is so much like the real thing- my favorite.  You cannot tell it's not real chicken.. it's so tender!   They have a range of sandwiches, each having its own fan base... i.e. tuna, pepper cheesesteak, ham, etc.   Some rave over the Subji Wrap, which is more of an Indian style veg wrap.  Others rave over their Chicken Salad sandwich- it's really super good.  They even have an awesome chicken Mexican quesadilla.  It's really an international type grill.

    Sort your selections and choices as you would in any non-veg place:  it's not all necessarily healthy, non-oily, yoga-happy stuff (though some items are).  It is a good decent place for good decent grills, which just happens to be veg.   The place draws quite a crowd, so do be prepared to wait at times.  It's a sure shot winner, each time.

    ..................................................­..................................................­..........................
    Update several months later:  Still fantastic sandwiches.. I go there all the time.  One thing though.. I noted recently that the portions (sandwiches) have perhaps got a bit smaller lately (?!).. not too happy about that.   Aaah.. inflation.. :(  .

    I recently tried their new platter dishes, which they added.. and definitely prefer their sandwhiches over the platters.  I hear they will be adding this/that.. sometime... but I still think their orignal grill stuff is the star.  My mouth remains happy as the sandwiches keep getting tastier and tastier.  I tried their ham and cheese (soy) hoagie just yesterday.. it was unreal.  A winner.

    I've decided to update this review and add a fifth star.  Upon further visits, I've concluded that Govinda's selection is alright and I simply wasn't giving all of the menu items a chance.  I had previously ignored soup and chicken tender options, wrongfully assuming that they'd be out of the can in the case of the former and out of a Morning Star box in the case of the latter.  I was wrong.  The soup was homemade and the chicken tenders were amazingly original.  They even came with a halfway decent BBQ sauce.

    PLUS -- Govinda's keeps relatively late hours on the weekdays, especially for a small, takeout restaurant on South Street.  I can't remember off the top of my head what time it closes, but I'm pretty sure it's around 11pm (I don't want to give an exact time that's wrong and mislead people, so let's just say it's late).

    Govinda's is a five star pleasure for your health and taste buds; that's a hard take to accomplish.

    Listed in good eats for vegetarians

    as a vegetarian living in center city, sandwich options are severely limited. philly just does not seem to be a sandwich town. instead, we have to make due with hoagies, subs, whatever you call them.

    govinda's is the go-to place for vegetarian and vegan friendly fare. but i have to say, i've become a little disappointed over time.

    i used to rave about this place, but recently i've noticed that portions have gotten smaller and prices have gone up. i realize that shadow inflation is happening all over the place and our corporate media is afraid to talk about it, but it hurts even more when prices go up while quality and quantity go down.

    when i am in the mood for fake meat sandwiches, i get either the pepper steak sandwich or the chicken cheese steak. others have described the latter in detail, so i won't go into it. the former, however, is equally tasty in a completely different way. the gooey, saucey fakey meaty concoction lights up your taste buds and glazes your fingers into something even a veg might contemplate nibbling on.

    sometimes i also get the chicken fingers, which are inappropriately named. i don't know what to call them because they're somewhere between a finger and a nugget. maybe chicken mini fillets? anyway, for fakey chicken they are tasty, but somewhat overpriced at $1/mini fillet. when i say mini, i mean it.

    golden tofu is recommended by others i've eaten with, if your compelled to like that sort of thing. i am not. i suck at tofu.

    desserts are of the vegan variety and even though i hear good things, i can't bring myself to eat $6 dairy-free desserts. damn wisconsin upbringing.

    finally, get a tattoo in a visible location before eating here if you don't want to feel out of place. this advice is for the two of you that live in philadelphia without a visible tattoo.
