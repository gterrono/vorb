    Before I moved to the Philly area, everyone always told me "the best cheese steaks are at Pat's and Gino's - you have try both and decide for yourself."  Well, I tried Pat's and Gino's, and then I took a local's advice and went to Jim's on South Street.  While there are several Jim's locations throughout the city, I've heard the South Street location is the most infamous and has the best cheese steaks.  And I concur!  South Street is also a fun place to go and hang out for an afternoon, do some shopping and take in the crowds.

    I recommend ordering your cheese steak the classic way - whiz wit.  This basically means cheese whiz and grilled onions.  It's DELICIOUS.  I know cheese that comes from a can is a little scary, but you have to trust me on this.  However, you can always opt for provolone cheese, grilled peppers, etc.  Be prepared for a long wait and a filling, greasy meal.  If you want to avoid tourists and the crowds, go during "off peak" hours, and avoid weekends.  Also, don't forget that Jim's is cash only.  Happy cheese steak eating!

    Listen, I'm from Chicago...We have Kuma's and Hot Doug's. People wait 2+ hours for a burger or hot dog, respectively. I knew exactly what I was getting myself into visiting Jim's and I'm glad I gave it a try!

    But you go because you MUST try these places. They have a wait and draw tourists for a reason as a local specialty.

    In Chicago we have a place, Philly's Best, that I first discovered 10 years ago and it was love at first bite. That was my first introduction to a Philly Cheesesteak. A sandwich epiphany of sorts. My taste buds were all like...now THAT'S a steak sandwich!!!

    Of course when I found out I was heading to Philly...cheesesteaks were on my mind. I hoped to try all 3 tourist traps during the weekend, but due to convenience and a time crunch...Jim's was the winner and only cheesesteak I tried while in Philly.

    When I arrived on a Sunday afternoon, the line was only 2 people out the door. By the time I was able to order, the line extended down the block. I was lucky that I had good timing. After a 20 minute wait we were able to order our sandwiches.

    At Philly's Best I always get my cheesesteak with American cheese. I had to do the same at Jim's, that was the control group combo :)

    My sandwich was good. It was pretty much what I was expecting. I really liked the bread (much softer than the imitations we have in the Windy City), but I would have liked my onions a little more well done.  

    I don't have much to compare Jim's to, but as a tourist with few cheesesteak experiences under my belt...I'm happy I tried Jim's. If you're a n00b like me, I recommend doing the same.

    Dear Philly: I promise I'll try more places next time I am in town!

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
