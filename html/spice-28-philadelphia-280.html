    I had such a great experience the first time I went that I brought another group of colleagues two days later on a four-day trip to Philly! It was worth the 2nd time back as well!!

Make sure you get to the Happy Hour- they make fantastic drinks for $6 a pop and have an excellent array of appetizer choices...We enjoyed our first round of apps so much we went for a 2nd (Duck crepes and Chili oil Dumplings). They made the experience excellent!

    Definitely will come back here when I am in town!

    Don't be fooled by its exterior, take a step inside and you'll be glad you did. Spice 28. From the food to the service this restaurant exceeded my expectations. From the somewhat tacky sign out front and being located on Chestnut Street (which can be ghetto in some areas), I am happy to report that I would gladly return for a date or nice dinner and I plan to order delivery  in the future. The interior of the restaurant is actually much nicer than you would expect. It's a little darker with glowing mood lighting along the walls and sleek modern furniture. There is a decent sized bar to the left when you enter and the main dining area is fairly large- although I would cap large parties here at 6-8 or it may be tight.
    The menu is described as Szechuan and Asian fusion. I would say there is a nice variety, but the menu doesn't qualify as "fusion". Overall I would say this restaurant is most comparable to Han Dynasty with slightly smaller portions. I would recommend individuals order their own dish as opposed to trying a family-style dining approach.
    Happy Hour is Monday-Friday 5-7pm at the bar. Cocktails $6, Wine $4, and Beer $3-4. Appetizers are offered for $3-5.
    For starters I loved the wontons in chili oil, spicy cucumbers, Indian pancakes, and satay. I also enjoyed the shrimp and mango salad (although with 4 small shrimp this is not an entrée sized salad).
    For our main course my brother enjoyed the beef in XO sauce, chicken pad thai, wok fried rice, dry pot style chicken, dan dan noodles, and string beans with minced pork.
    The service was very friendly and fast. I definitely think the overall experience here is very good, on both occasions I have seen what looks the the manager, present and overseeing things. Although the portions could be larger I do like that this restaurant participates in SIPs happy hour over the summer and offers free delivery (both of which Han Dynasty does not).
    Full service bar, but I haven't tried the drinks yet.
    Staff was very helpful when redeeming my LivingSocial voucher.

Specialties

Spice 28 specializes in Szechuan and Asian fusion cuisines with a full bar that offers signature cocktails and a wide selection of wines and beers. Our Cuisines feature traditional authentic Szechuan cuisines, and fusion of Japanese, Malaysian, Thai and Chinese cuisines. Not only our food are rich in flavors, and spices, they are also authentic and creative. You will find some of the most authentic dishes in modern plating, but you will also find the unique cuisine that you will not find elsewhere. Also, the important is that the restaurant was designed by an architect and newly built. The atmosphere is very hip, modern and vibrant. The service is prompt and friendly. You will not be disappointed.
