        ROTD 2/8/2010

        One of my coworkers has taken on the title of Happy Hour Coordinator (doesn't every office have one of these?), a position of little lucre but great utility for the rest of us. After many long hours researching in the field, his diligence lead us to crown Valanni our Office Happy Hour Spot. Valanni is dead, long live Valanni.

        With half priced drinks and apps, a big, square bar and cozy little tables in nooks nearby, this is an accommodating place for a gaggle of people. They make a mean mojito, with lots of mint and aesthetically pleasing rock candy. When it's cold, I often opt for the frequently-changing seasonal stout (Bell's Expedition and Dogfish Head Chicory, for representative examples). My favorite embarrassingly-named cocktail looks like a trainwreck with three ingredients too many on the menu page, but with a splash of balsamic vinegar to cut its sweetness, it's different and delightful.

        The patatas bravas and other bar snacks are a cut above your typical happy hour fare, and the lobster mac n cheese makes my colleagues rave. At a catered party in the back room (which is a nice spot for a party), Valanni really showed its stuff, with an extensive Mediterranean-inflected selection of hors d'oeuvres, plated in single bites on spoons and presented alluringly on a tall rack. Everything I tasted - hummus, white beans with pesto, vegan flatbread, cucumber salad and especially this one feta dip - was well-executed, and I appreciated the balance of vegetarian and dead animal options.

        Service here tends to be either exemplary or execrable, and occasionally, that can result in a truly abominable drink. I recommend having your Happy Hour Coordinator spend some time getting to know the bartenders, who now start muddling and mixing as soon as they see our guy walk through the door.

        Mmmmmmm Valanni....

        Out of all the choices that Center City offers, I chose to come here for the first week of Winter Restaurant Week. If my salary was higher, my rent was cheaper or I had a Sugar Daddy, I would have chosen a dozen more restaurants for the first week of Restaurant Week, but that is not the case. I chose Valanni because I had heard good things, the menu online looked very appealing and it's close to where I live.

          Upon entering this Gaybourhood gem, I was immediately delighted by the atmosphere. I especially LOVE the encased metal sculpture of  V's behind the hostess stand. In fact, I wish I could buy it or create one of my own. After I ventured to the bathroom I discovered that this restaurant is actually quite large, consisting of different sections that are almost hidden throughout the place with the large round bar in the center as a focal point. The interior of each has a slightly different theme and feel. I would love to bring a big group to dine in the designated big group room (2010 Birthday Ideas--hint hint ).
          We were seated promptly and it was great to see that their version of restaurant week menu was not watered down like it is at many other participating places. With so many menu choices, I had a difficult decision to make, but I settled on the halibut, the duck breast and the rum bananas for dessert. The truth is I am a sucker for duck. Anytime I see it on a menu, it's an instant attraction. I was so glad I had ordered this because WOW, this duck dish was amazing! The duck was cooked to the temperature of my choosing, the cranberry sauce was full of flavor and a wonderful compliment to both the duck and the mashed sweet potatoes and the haricot verts were cooked perfectly. This was one of the best winter dishes I've had at a restaurant. My friend, who was a duck virgin, ended up loving it as well. I was so glad that her first taste of duck was from such a well-executed dish. She had also ordered the baked brie which was also very  well executed. My other friend had the mussels and the Kobe burger...both were very good, though nothing to rave about. However, I really enjoyed the skinny, perfectly crispy fries that accompanied the burger. My order of  halibut and white beans was good, though nothing special. They both could have used more flavor. The rum bananas were awesome. I think it's usually hard to find outstanding desserts, because no matter what, even a crappy chocolate cake or a prefrozen apple pie can still be pretty damn good. It's like what they say about pizza and sex. But the rum bananas were outstanding and the oreo beignets even outdid them! If heaven had a flavor and you wanted to try it, then I suggest you order the oreo beignets. Imagine funnel cake wrapped around oreo crumbles and accompanied by ice cream.
        Life doesn't get much better than that.

        Last night of Restaurant Week and on a friend's recommendation I made reservations at Valanni for me and my bf. I had passed it numerous times without fulling knowing what it was (at first I thought it was just a bar - I mean, you can't see inside, at night there's usually a bunch of people smoking outside...it made sense). Once we actually entered on Friday night I learned yet again that appearances can be deceiving.

        Valanni is much bigger inside than you might initially think - it seemed like there were 3 or 4 'sections' to the space, but it was hard to tell as we were led towards one of the back areas, where a second bar was (I have no idea how many bars there are in this joint, but it seemed like more than one. But I could very well be wrong). For obvious reasons it was pretty packed when we arrived, but our waitress immediately showed up, gave us menus and explained the RW deal (which was NOT obvious because they just handed us a regular menu), then took our drink orders. That gave us enough time to glance at everything and realize we needed more time to decide. They have quite a few dishes to choose from, not too surprising since its mostly small plates, but overwhelming when you can only choose one!

        We ended up ordering: grilled spanish octopus with fennel, and sundried tomatoes, lamb shoulder with foccacia dumpling and roasted vegetables, sesame and pepita crusted tuna with crispy potatoes, baby bok choy, wasabi and a ginger-balsamic reduction, and the seared porcini scallops with asparagus, crab meat, shiitake, white truffle jus and garlic mashed potatoes. Everything really tasty, but it wasn't 'knock your socks off, amazing, oh dear god when can I come back and have it again?!' cuisine. You will definitely have a really good meal here, but compared to some other places it just doesn't seem like something to brag about. There was just something missing...

        The desserts though were fantastic, very probably our favorite part of the meal. We had the oreo beignets with vanilla ice cream, which was what I imagined deep fried oreos to taste like, they came out WARM too!! Bonus points for warm, fresh beignets straight to my belly. Also ordered the caramelized rum bananas in a crispy phyllo shell (tasted exactly like churros cuz it was covered in cinnamon sugar! MmmMmm good!!!) with toasted coconut, walnuts and vanilla ice cream. These desserts were seriously good, I could've ordered more of both. Taste buds are sad that I didn't, but my waist-line is thanking me for holding back (at least a little).

      History

      Established in 2000.

      Valanni restaurant opened its doors in September of 2000, introducing "Medi-Latin" cuisine to Philadelphia. Situated right off of the "Avenue of the Arts," Valanni has become a pre-theater ritual and place to meet for a night-cap for many who live in and around the neighborhood. The bar at Valanni keeps up with the creative cuisine by offering an assortment of sangria, mojitos and other specialty drinks. The kitchen is open until 1 AM daily, serving Tapas and a Mediterranean inspired lounge menu.
