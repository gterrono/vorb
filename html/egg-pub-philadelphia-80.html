    To make a very long story short, this business engages in deceptive business practices.

    The longer story is that I used to enjoy my time at EGG, until a couple of weeks ago when I noticed that they pour their beer into what is commonly known in the hospitality business as "falsie" glasses. These are glasses specifically designed to look like a real pint, but with a much thicker bottom so that the glass holds two less ounces of beer. This is crappy tactic that has been written about extensively, and Michigan even tried to outlaw. However, it is usually something that is relegated to places like Applebees and Olive Garden...not a place that claims to be a gastropub.

    When I contacted the owner, Ryan, to express my displeasure in a very respectful email, I was pretty shocked at the result. The owner said, "every bar is not required to sell pints," and that these short pours are justified because "we allow our customers to have a 2 oz sample if they want to" and that there is product loss in pouring and clearing the beer lines. Yes, every restaurant in the world has the exact same losses, and yet most still manage to not use deceit to make people that they are getting more than they really are. As I said in my email to him,  when you see that a business is trying to get away with a deceptive shortcut, you wonder what they think about their clients and, frankly, what else they might be trying to get away with that you can't see.

    The owner ended his email with, "Hopefully you will give us a second chance if you can tolerate drinking a draft and not a pint. : )" Fortunately for me, there about 5 other places within a couple of blocks of EGG where I can have a draft AND a pint, so I think I'll be going there in the future.

    Relaxed atmosphere, great food, good beer.

    Came here with my wife for dinner on a Saturday. We were originally going to eat at another neighborhood joint but came here because it was quieter. Not that it wasn't busy, but the atmosphere here was more conducive to conversation. Sat at the bar and had a few drinks. Good beer selection, not intended to overwhelm, but to sufficiently provide an array of interesting pairing choices.

    Likewise the menu is small and well thought out. Good options for whatever the mood or dietary restriction. I went with the hangar steak grilled medium rare. It came sliced and drizzled with a traditional chimmichuri, paired with roasted fingerling potatoes and a side salad. Perfectly cooked and well portioned.

    This isn't the place you take your out of town friends to wow them into comas about how awesome Fishtown is. This is the place you go with close neighborhood friends to enjoy yourselves, chat and avoid the weekend warriors.

Specialties

East Girard Gastropub is located in the heart of Philadelphia's Fishtown neighborhood. Chef Rob Holloway creates seasonal, American cuisine of fresh, local ingredients for a savory, dining experience. The bar features microbrews, hand-picked wine selections, specialty cocktails, cozy hot-toddies, and a variety of stellar wine, beer and drinks.

Come and enjoy!
