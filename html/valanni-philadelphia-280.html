    I've been craving paella, and after a quick search on Yelp, I decided to give this place a try. First and foremost, the service here is top-notch. My boyfriend and I were warmly greeted and seated by an open window, and a waiter quickly appeared with drink and food menus. After the waiter brought out the wrong drink for me, I mentioned it to the hostess, who apologetically made the switch and comped it. It was a tiny mistake, but the restaurant went above and beyond to fix it. I really appreciated that gesture.

    On to the food. We ordered the spicy pulled chicken empanadas and the seafood paella for one to share. That was a lot of food! The empanadas were so delicious! The pulled chicken was tender and flavorful, with just the right amount of kick. The paella came out in a large paella pan, topped with all kinds of seafood: scallops, clams, mussels, shrimp, calamari, and half of a lobster tail. While tasty, I thought the dish could have used a tad more seasoning. It did have a nice crust on the bottom. Nonetheless, it satisfied my craving, and I left with the intention to return soon.

History

Established in 2000.

Valanni restaurant opened its doors in September of 2000, introducing "Medi-Latin" cuisine to Philadelphia. Situated right off of the "Avenue of the Arts," Valanni has become a pre-theater ritual and place to meet for a night-cap for many who live in and around the neighborhood. The bar at Valanni keeps up with the creative cuisine by offering an assortment of sangria, mojitos and other specialty drinks. The kitchen is open until 1 AM daily, serving Tapas and a Mediterranean inspired lounge menu.
