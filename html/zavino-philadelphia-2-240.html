    So it was Saturday night and my husband and I were hungry from a long day of Christmas shopping. We sat on the couch trying to figure out where to go eat. Generally I avoid center city on Saturday nights, but I wanted both fancy pizza and a good dirty martini. After perusing the yelp app I found a review that mentioned both of these items so I put on some lipstick and we headed to the El. We expected a wait, but were met with a lovely (and adorable/cute shoes wearing) hostess who informed us a table was leaving so our wait would be about five minutes. Awesome sauce! They had a pretty good beer and cocktail selection, but I was focused on a good dirty martini. I've had some really crappy ones along way, all vermouthy and gross. This was not one of them. It was by far one of the best dirty martinis I have ever had, which is why I had two. We also ordered the special Tartufo pizza and the Stache pizza along with some olives for munching.  

    The Tartufo lacked the truffle goodness I was expecting, I think I've been spoiled by Stella. The Stache was delicious though, with a pistachio pesto and fresh arugula on a whole wheat crust. I'd order it again in a heartbeat. They also have pasta and salads and I'm chomping at the bit to go back and try some of the other options, with of course at least one dirty martini to wash it all down. The service was pretty good, a little slow, but I appreciated it as we were given plenty of time to enjoy our pizzas and not rushed at all. It was such a relaxing experience I never would have expected on a Saturday night. Thanks Zavino!

    The pizza was kind of an in-between size.  It would have been a lot for one person to have for a meal, but not quite enough for two people to split.  That was OK since we were just there for a snack, but if you're going for pizza dinner, consider a salad or appetizer.  They serve a thin crust pie and it would have been better if the center was just a bit more crisped, but it was very tasty.

    The service was fast, attentive and polite.

    A good stop if you're in the neighborhood.

    Let me tell you a love story.

    There once was a girl who longed to be splendor, but could not find the way to have amazingly delicious foods without compromising her calorie budget. On a lovely fall day, she and her fabulous sister sat down at the bar in Zavino.

    Zavino has always had quality, delicious foods, and if gluten were this girl's problem, she would have settled here before. But alas, no, it is not gluten, and as delicious as Margherita pizza here is, it did not fill her up.

    For $3, amazing assorted, marinated olives are at her beck and call at Zavino, and olives are her favorite. But even that plentiful dish was no more than a peanut bowl when it came to wanting satisfying calories.

    She tried the chef selection charcuterie at an easy $18, but just did not WANT to share it deliciousness. Alas, charcuterie is for sharing.

    And then there, above her, on the blackboard specials, she saw a spaghetti squash appetizer. $7, she'd try it... It came in a beautiful short cylinder, a 1 cup serving on a pool of seasoned red sauce.

    Her fork folded neatly into the mound, layering the squash with red sauce, and she took a bite. It was then that she knew what happiness was. This was the day a lady fell in love with a squash.

    Some say it's not a meal, and to that she sometimes offers up the beet salad, but she keeps dreaming about that squash. She makes it at home now, but it's just not the same. So on cold days, she likes to walk to Zavino again (or park in the paid lot across the street), saddle up to the bar, and check the blackboard specials. And maybe try the seasonal Winter Fashioned cocktail next time.

    I started out this review with 1 star I had such a bad experience.  And then after having the other 3 at my table try a bite of my pizza without any warning, they made me say something to the server it was so horrible.  It was quite posssibly the worst ever pizza I had ever tasted. I ordered the Joey, and then to see how they handled the whole Gluten-free fad (although I have no Gluten allergies...), ordered it "Gluten free".  The crust crumbled and fell apart and tasted like sawdust.  It had the consistency of bad Dominos Pizza when it's been reheated the next day after sitting out all night...only without any of the flavor of the grease of their nasty cheese.  The  Sausage was suprisingly bland and I couldn't really detect any distinct flavor or seasoning at all....but then again that thick hockey puck of sawdust and tap water probably was affecting my tastebuds.  So why then 3 stars?

    I rarely give a complaint about quality unless it's something simple like a steak not cooked to the right temp or a dish being cold.  When food sucks, it just sucks.  But in all of my eating reviews, I've had plenty I didn't "like", but could still appreciate that others would like it.  This however just plain sucked!  Sucked so bad I had to ask the waitress about it.  So, the story is, everything there is made in house...except for the gluten free crust.  Due to recent demands, they have had to bring in outside made gluten free for those who have an allergy.  It's pre-packaged and sitting in a walk-in waiting to be thawed out and reheated in their nice wood burning ovens sucking out any flavor from whatever comes in contact with it.  She did tell us they've experimented with several suppliers, and have yet to find something suitable and tasty, but they still have to offer this as a dietary option for those with allergies (why I don't know, as 5 years ago pizza shops flourished without offering GF options!).  She also told us she usually warns people that it's not the most appetizing option and that they don't make it in house.  We somehow missed this warning as I would have never ordered it if it came with that kind of review.

    Fortunately, we like to taste food so there was plenty more on the table to eat.  

    We started off with the house cured olives.  Excellent.  Great flavor, texture and a distinctly different flavor/spice profile between the different olives.  Order this when you sit down and before you even look at the menu.

    The veal meatballs are probably the best thing on the menu.  Although on the smaller side, the overall dish is a really nice size appetizer and could actually be a meal if paired with a salad or another side.  The sauce is bright and fresh with a great summer tomato flavor with almost no seasoning.  Tastes like a great italian restaurants sauce in the summer.  Meatballs are light and cooked perfectly enough to still be moist on the inside.

    The rock shrimp pizza (on the traditional crust!) is spicy as promised with a nice portion of shrimp and arugula on top.  The cheese I wasn't crazy about, but there was so much heat from the shrimp, it didn't need to shine.

    The Bronzino special was cooked well with the skin on, but the brussel sprouts had no distinct flavor despite the nice carmelization on them.

    The hostesses were very nice and friendly when we walked in and the waitress was great as well (despite her forgetting to warn us about the GF!).  Her 5 minute frank discussion with us on the disappointing crust, concerns of the chef and management, and her friendly attitude saved the meal and took this 1 star review I drafted while sitting through the meal to a 3 star post meal.

    Don't get me wrong, there's still no excuse for the kitchen putting out that GF crust.  If you have a GF allergy, you should just order sauce and cheese melted on the plate, or order the fish.  No one is so desperate for a pizza experience that they'll accept this.   I was torn between a 3 Star and 2-star rating due to this, but based on the fact that not many will even consider ordering GF (especially after reading this review), I went with the review as if I hadn't tried it; 3 stars.

    For me, the other dishes were solid enough to come back and try more on the menu, and the staff and ambiance was nice enough to recommend the place for friends to try it.  Look for at least 1 follow-up review on their other dishes in the coming weeks...

    Question: How do you impress a group of fussy, high-maintenance family members who demand excellent-tasting food but refuse anything that sounds remotely exotic or unfamiliar?

    Answer: Take them to Zavino and feed them pizza.

    That's what I did with my fambam, and the meal was a resounding success.

    I brought everyone here for a long and leisurely lunch on a Saturday.  Zavino is very small and doesn't take reservations, so I made sure that we showed up just as they were opening.  Thankfully, they were able to accommodate our group without any problems.  The interior is very cute, with wooden benches and an impressively stocked wine bar.  (We skipped the wine this time around, though.)

    I started lunch with the beet salad, which was excellent.  I love colorful foods, and the yellow and purple beets were just gorgeous to look at.  I didn't hurt that they were also cooked superbly.  This was one of the best beet plates I've had in a long time.

    Next up, I had the Kennett (mushroom) pizza.  I always bemoan the lack of good pizza back home in San Diego.  Philly, on the other hand, is overflowing with excellent pizza places, and Zavino is a fierce contender.   The Kennett pizza had a great char on its crust, and the overall flavor was excellent.  However, I didn't really enjoy the amount of grease that the mushrooms were bathing in.  Honestly, it was hard to taste the mushrooms at all through the grease.  (My take is that a pizza is a showcase for the toppings, so indiscernible toppings always leave me bummed.)

    Zavino's staff couldn't have been nicer.  Our server was very gracious and prompt, and she didn't bat an eye at my family members' barrage of questions about which foods might interfere with their convoluted dietary restrictions and allergies.

    Zavino, you done good.  I'll be back for more real soon.

I could go on and on, but really, just go and try it for yourself - it's AMAZING.
