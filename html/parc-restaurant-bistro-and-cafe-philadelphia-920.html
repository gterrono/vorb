    The best thing about this place is the atmosphere.  If it were a little smaller, I would think I had somehow teleported directly to Paris.  Even the chairs look like chairs that line the sidewalks of Paris.  For this reason alone, I would return.

    The menu, including drinks and food, is definitely inspired by France, which I obviously love (French food being my favorite).  Of course it is still Americanized.

    The bread that is served before the meal is spectacular! One of the highlights.

    The menu is interesting with a lot of unique offerings.  The food was very good but after getting through half our meal, we realized that what made it so good was either cream, butter or oil, two easy ways to make a meal taste good.  It lacked some creativity and culinary genius.  But it was certainly edible, don't get me wrong.  We cleared our plates.

    Unbeatable ambiance.  Vibrant, chic, and delightful.  A good choice if you are visiting Philadelphia.

    If you go to Parc, please please get the French 75.  Gin, lemon juce, orange cognac, and champagne.  It is the most heavenly drink.

    I went here for a celebratory dinner and the hosts treated us to Parc's 3-course pre fixe menu.  Guests choose from a menu of appetizers, entrees, and desserts.  Three options per course.  Hosts get to customize the menu!  

    The scallops were superb.  The escargot was a soup of butter, but that's how it's supposed to be.  The portions the were small, but that probably wasn't a bad thing as most dishes were rich.

    Parc is the happenin' place to be on a Saturday or Sunday morning. It's in a great location, lively and exciting ambiance, al fresco dining, what more could you ask for? I made a reservation for three at 10:15 on a Saturday morning. We must have arrived before the rush, because there were plenty of open seats.

    We weren't seated outside, but just inside the restaurant next the open window/doors. Each of us ordered drinks, the Bellini, the Kir Royale, and the Mimosa Lorraine. We  passed our drinks around and were grateful we ordered the ones that suited our individual tastes. I ordered French Toast, dining buddies ordered Eggs Benedict and the Spinach and Goat cheese omelet. Our food arrived in a timely manner,  but instead of my french toast I was presented with pancakes. The pancakes looked good, but I told the food runner there had been a mistake he apologized and said it would be out quickly. My waiter also returned and apologized. My french toast came out about 5 minutes later than the other entrees. The reviews from my dining buddies were "pretty good". After our food, we all ordered another round of drinks. We weren't rushed which was a nice because the place was starting to get busy. It got very loud and I suppose it's unavoidable when people are having a good time. When we received the bill my french toast charge was missing, I suppose that was an extra apology for the mix-up.

    This is a great place to meet up with friends for a boozy brunch, but I've had better food elsewhere. I've never been to Paris, but imagine the cafe atmosphere would be similar to what they do here. Everyone raves about the breadbasket. We weren't given one and figured it was just because we only ordered brunch food. Who knows, I feel like I missed the best part.

Specialties

This stylish addition to Rittenhouse Square pays tribute to French cafe culture, serving breakfast, lunch and dinner seven days a week. Ideal for a quick bite or a leisurely meal, the menu is filled with classics: onion soup, escargots, steak frites and towering plateaus de fruits de mer. The baguettes are made in-house and  the wine list is expansive. Sip a Kir Royale at the zinc bar or enjoy a cafe au lait while basking in the sun.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
