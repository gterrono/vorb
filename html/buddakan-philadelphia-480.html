    Finally got to Buddakan for Restaurant Week! I have been wanting to try this place for many years and it was just as great as I thought it would be.

    The atmosphere is wonderful. Dimly lit, very unique decorations and a lively ambiance.

    Our appetizers were the Ginger House Salad and the Shrimp and Scallop Spring Rolls. Loved every bite!

    For entrees, we ordered the Sea Bass platter and the Braised Beef Chow Fun. The Sea Bass was served on a bed of veggies and was one of the best seafood dishes I've had in some time. Light, flaky, crispy...YUM! The Chow Fun was a larger portion than I had expected. Tender beef with thick noodles and some veggies. The sauce was extremely flavorful, very savory.

    For dessert, we had the Dip Sum doughnuts and the Crying Chocolate cake.The doughnuts were served with chocolate, raspberry and cream dipping sauces. The Crying Chocolate cake came with a fruit sorbet and was SO rich!

    I was very, very pleased with our entire meal. On their regular menu, I noticed that they have large, family sized servings for certain menu items, so I think this would be a great place to go with a small group for a special occasion.

    I would love to get back there again sometime! LOVED it!

    Having perused the glowing reviews of Buddakan before I went, my expectations were very high.  For the most part, they were met.  I think had I not gone during restaurant week, I would have given Buddakan 5 stars.  Their restaurant week menu is quite small and they do limit their portion sizes, hence the 4 stars.

    For the first course, I ordered the shrimp and scallop spring rolls.  It was by far my favorite course.  They were lightly fried, so that they weren't too heavy, and the shrimp and scallops were perfectly tender and seasoned.

    For the main dish, I had chicken and cashews.  It was very yummy, but not extraordinary.  For restaurant week, it was a decent portion size.

    For dessert, I ordered the crying chocolate, which is to die for.  It is rich and you may have trouble finishing it, but if you are a chocolate lover, it is a must.  It is served with a lovely scoop of mango sorbet.

    The ambiance is very pleasant.  The decor is fascinating, the noise level is fairly high, and everyone there is very trendy.

    We went for my friend's birthday and they served her dessert (dip sum doughnuts) with a sparkler in it, which was very fun.

    I would definitely reccomend Buddakan, but if you are willing to spend a bit more, avoid it during restaurant week.

I wonder if this restaurant only hires beautiful people, because ever waiter/host looks as if they belonged to some fashion magazine.
    Asks if it is our first time at this restaurant. It is! Great! Gives a 5 minute introduction about Buddhakan and why it's unique (it's because the food is more oriented towards sharing than individual entrees)

    9:50 - Jennifer comes by and offers her apologies for our long wait. Gives us a complimentary Edamame Ravioli(yelp.com/biz_photos/budd…). Aweeesome! Came with three pieces... I was hoping this wouldn't be indicative of the portions of the other dishes. Was pretty good, though. The consistency inside the ravioli was as good as can be.
    Friend and I also decided to order after Jennifer stops by a couple times. Asks us if we want Fiji water, sparkling water, or ice water. Ignorant on my part, I thought this was complimentary. Apparently not. My fault.

    10:00- Friend and I decided to order one restaurant week course for the both of us, including a couple extra desserts. After telling Jennifer, her face freezes for a moment, and recaptures her composure after a second. *Did I say something wrong?*.

    "Absolutely!"
    That was the last word I heard from her.

    I don't know why she stopped talking to us, but maybe she found out my friend and I are simply commoners and would maybe not be able to afford a separate course for each person? Maybe we wouldn't be able to give as large a tip as we would otherwise? I mean... the food is meant to be shared, so why not share, right? What happened to the lovely facade she was putting up? She just stop talking to us. No you're welcome after she poured us our water. As in... nothing. I was a bit offended. I was NOT expecting such a change in demeanor. Bleh.

    10:10 Our first entree comes! Char Siu Bun!(yelp.com/biz_photos/budd…) I know what good Chinese food tastes like, and this was NOTHING like good Chinese food. For $12, we got two buns of Char Siu, that barely had any pork in the filling. The pork was also not sweet as it should be. The one thing that was different from normal Char Siu was the the outside was a bit fried. Good touch, but the bread wasn't chewy or soft. The pork was warm, not hot. What on earth happened? No, no, no!

It comes with a tablespoon of mango (or other fruit) sorbet top with three flowers, and a fudge-filled chocolate cake topped with pearls and some kinda stem. I don't know these things.     This place is great.  I hadn't been there in years and was wondering if it was still good.  It's still just as good.  I don't know if this place was the first Asian Fusion restaurant in the country but I know it was the first that I went to.  Sharing is strongly encouraged so I got to try 4 different dishes and the flavor combinations on each of them were layered and coherent.

    - Wasabi Tuna Tataki - thinly sliced (almost see thru), tender as butter, and the wasabi didnt overwhelm the taste of the tuna.

    But if you're a fan of the Asian Fusion genre, you will love it.  Its also a cool looking place so its a good place for a date, or to bring out of town friends, or a special occasion with the wife, etc.

Specialties

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
