    I have resided in Chicago, Los Angeles, Pittsburgh, Philly, and Boston for extended periods of time. And although there is still a countless number of amazing restaurants that I've yet to visit, I have dined in some of the top eateries in those areas based on Yelp reviews. And I can confidently say that Alma de Cuba is my absolute favorite restaurant - an establishment that gets 5 Stars for its taste/service, creativity, atmosphere, patron appreciation, and location.

    1. Taste/Service (dinner for two): Complimentary yucca rolls were warm, fresh, light, sweet, salty, and came with a very tasty dipping sauce. The waiters refilled our rolls and water almost instantaneously so the service was quite impeccable. I didn't even look at the menu yet and already I had never experienced bread/service like this before. Ceviche: ordered the Long Island Fluke - this was our first time eating ceviche and it was divine. The fluke was incredibly delicate yet was able to grasp the sweet aromas of the yuzu/soy/ginger combination. Wow. Appetizer: Royal Palm Dates - almond-stuffed dates wrapped in bacon and with blue cheese. The name of the dish was fitting as I slowly savored each piece feeling like a King. Main Courses: Rum Cured Duck Breast - perfectly cooked duck. The flavors were def. unique to my taste buds but not entirely exotic because the perfectly cooked meat was still the highlight of the dish. My GF ordered the Maine Diver Scallops - again, perfectly cooked and the oregano butter really tied the whole dish together. Drinks: My GF ordered the Suave Mojito which is probably the signature drink on the menu. It was the quintessential mojito with its own sugar cane/fresh mint in a tall, inviting glass. I ordered the El Jefe - a stronger/bitter version but still damn good. Our waiter was very helpful and knowledgeable, and to this day, I regret not trying the dessert menu (because we had plans to get dessert at Max Brenner afterwards with a friend).

    2. Creativity: With its modest/average offerings of main entrees, Alma de Cuba makes up for this (and then some) with an eye-opening array of appetizers and ceviches. The chef who created our Long Island Fluke and Royal Palm Dates is brilliant. For first-timers reading this review, please do yourselves a favor by ordering both the Ceviche Tasting and Alma Sampler. My GF and I did not and I highly regret it.

    3. Atmosphere: I've been to many posh/upscale restaurants in numerous cities. And although Alma de Cuba's decor does make it fall into that category, it also has a very charming feel to it. The restaurant has seating on multiple levels, each with its own unique vibe. Thus, you'll never feel like you're eating in a crowd of people with stares darting left and right. The only people watching you'll do is viewing your date across the table and admiring the awe and joy he/she feels from this unmatched dining experience.

    4. Patron Appreciation: I added this category because of the feedback we received from our neighbors sitting next to us. Again, due to the amazing food and setting, my GF and I really didn't pay much attention to anyone else. But as we politely declined dessert due to our pending migration to Max Brenners, our neighbors made the effort to suggest the Chocolate Cigar. They seemed really happy and nodded in agreement at each other. I've never had that type of reaction from a neighboring couple at an upscale restaurant before. Usually, couples remain very private and isolated in their own dining adventures. But we did appreciate their kind recommendation, which proved that we weren't the only diners who were really appreciative that night.

    5. Location: On Walnut street in the heart of downtown Philly and walking distance to Rittenhouse Square/downtown shopping. There really isn't a better location in Philadelphia.

    Alma de Cuba = truly perfect all-around and one of the few things that make Philly worth visiting.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
