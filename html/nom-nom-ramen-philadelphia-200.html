    Nom Nom? Nom Nope.

    I'm not sure where to start, so let's just breakdown my two ramen dishes, I had the Shoyu Ramen, and the Karai Miso Ramen.

    Shoyu Ramen, was weird. Look, it's shoyu ramen right? So it's supposed to be salty, I get that, but it's supposed to be a pleasant saltiness, one that whets the appetite, and makes the glory of a beautiful noodle all-the-better. But this absurdity was just salty, I mean it was... Japan-would-die-of-sodium-levels-if-their-Hakata-­Style-Ramen-Was-Like-This. In addition, their Tonkatsu broth, a Hakata style specialty, lacked balance. You could taste the richness of the pork (which is a good thing), but it was just TOO oily and fatty, I mean, it felt like drinking the bacon grease leftover from that mornings cast-iron skillet (and if you're not cooking bacon on a cast iron skillet, you're not cooking bacon. Bacon is cooking you. Unless you're cooking large quantities in the oven for a party). And the flavors never combined, it didnt taste like Tonkatsu broth flavored WITH Shoyu, but it tasted like Tonkatsu broth, WITH Shoyu, without any of the redeeming qualities of Shoyu, except the saltiness of the soy.

    Karai Miso, this was certainly better than the Shoyu, but it's not saying much. Look Karai Miso (Spicy Miso), is usually made by blending chili peppers together with Miso, and Miso's beauty lies in it's subtle depth and complexity. So why the heck do I feel like I'm drinking the blood of a cardiac-arresting Satan? The heat from the Miso is deceptive, there isn't a pleasant back-of-the-tongue burn that you'd expect, and desire, but rather a much more sinister, kick you in the larynx oily burn. It tastes as though they mixed miso and chili oil with salt. (Saltiness is a theme here). Very unpleasant, I couldn't finish my broth (that's a terrible sign from someone like me, a noodle/soupivore).

    The noodles, were good. That's it, just good. They had a nice bounce to them, but honestly, both broths were so distracting I couldn't even enjoy that bounce you get from alkanized noodles :/

    They even messed up the egg. Okay that's a bit exaggerated... The Egg was wonderfully cooked, exactly how it should be. Solidified outside, with a slightly yolky, but-not-yet-liquid, shimmering middle. But, the egg was served to me, as though it was just shelled, and served, i.e. it was not cut-in-half. Now look, I know that sounds nit picky, but it's the little things that count, like having booths that don't sink 2 feet below the table so you look like a hobbit at Gondor (Nom Nom is guilty), or having noodles with just the right amount of bounce.

    What's wrong with not slicing the egg in half? Because when I try to eat aforementioned egg, it's filled with delicious, yolky, lava, and Chopsticks are not the uteinsil of choice for cutting an egg. It's also much more difficult to eat a whole, boiled egg. I want a bit of egg, to accompany my bites of ramen, that means a bit of egg white, with that rich yolk. Instead, I get all egg-white, and then some yolk only, and a bit of white and yolk, look the point is, you can't even appreciate the beauty of how well the egg is cooked. On a functional/practical level, i.e. the egg is too hot, and annoying to pick up with chopsticks, (Even for an asian man who grew up using chopsticks), and on a more culinary level, where the balance between yolk, white, and broth is ruined.

    Phone charger was nice though.
