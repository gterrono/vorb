        I came for dinner one time over a year ago and I have no idea why I never returned until now. I came here with girlfriends on Sunday for brunch. I recommended Jones because of its mention in Travel + Leisure as being one of the top 25 brunch spots in the US. Well, they were right and I was suprised! We were a larger group and waited about 25 minutes for seating. In that time, we watched all the delicious food go by as it was being delivered to nearby tables. I had the ALT-avocado, lettuce, tomato-sandwich and it was as good as it looked. I also had a blood orange cosmo that I thought about all day long. I was also able to sample the grilled cheese and tomato soup and the fried chicken and waffles. All of it was grand and I will return. This may be my favorite brunch spot in Philly ?! Yes, after only one time. When the check came we were floored! We regularly dine as a group of 5 and the bill was under $100 AND some of us had alcohol. I really enjoyed our experience. I was getting a familiar feeling as I sat in our booth and then it dawned on me (much later) that Jones does have a sort of Continental vibe. *Not in a bad way* It is more upscale and not as touristy. Loved it!

        I work right around Jones, and when my boss is jones-in' for a lunch out, she often brings us in tow to Jones.  Having dined here 4 times or so now, I have to say, it's not even close to my favorite Steven Starr place.  The food is good, but it's just kind of boring.  If you are a restauranteur, I suppose you need a place serving the typical American fare.  I just find it uninspired.

        I often get the make-your-own salad, which is a feature I enjoy.  Unlike other salad places, you can pick as many toppings as you want.  It is a huge salad, too.  I've also had the chili and avocado sandwich.  They are good, but again, nothing distinctive about them.  

        I'll continue to dine here, I'm sure (hey...when the boss is payin'....) but on the days where she seems open to suggestion, I might try to steer here a bit further afield than Jones.

        If nothing else, order the Cheese "Hold The Beef" Burger. Instead of a beef patty, you're presented with a hunka, hunka, chunk of grilled halloumi cheese, topped with fresh tomato, avocado, romaine and lemon aioli. It's auh-mazing. (Plus, "halloumi" is just a fun word to say!)

        This is truly a case where, "the cheese stands alone!"

        Seriously, this was one of the best burgers (although it was kind of a non burger) that I've had in a long time.

        It's hard to remember what else was ordered other than that. In fact, I had to look up the menu on Jones' website just to jog my memory that I also ordered the Calamari Salad. Not too much to be said about it, since it's pretty much how you would imagine - fried calamari on top romaine with corn, onions and a spicy chipotle. The calamari was fresh though, and the batter not too heavy. The salad is HUGE.

        But in the shadow of my halloumi sammich, the salad meant little to me.

        Overall, I really like Jones. It's almost like a sophisticated diner - it's open late, serves comfort, Americana and is a quick, cool and casual spot for just about any occasion.

        After visiting the Constitution Center we wanted to stop by somewhere within walking distance for some lunch. Having looked at several reviews we settled on Jones based on menu selection. While at Jones we were met with prompt seating and quality service. The waiter was comfortable making suggestions and did his best making sure that we had a good lunch. The drink selection was good and we were always taken care of with respect to that.

        We started our lunch with the nachos. We ordered these plain without any meat option and they were fantastic. Each chip was properly seasoned and featured a fantastic amount of beans, cheese, and additional accompanying toppings. The appetizer was good.

        Following the appetizer we were given bread. The bread was warm and it was crispy on the outside and soft and buttery on the inside. For our lunch we had the grilled cheese and tomato soup and a made to order market salad. The grilled cheese was toasted to perfection, wasn't too greasy, and the cheese was still very hot and stretchy. The tomato soup was very flavorful and had a great thick consistency. The taste of the soup was fantastic. The beauty of the market salad is that you can pick and choose what you want from a wide range of options to make a really hearty salad. There are roughly 15-20 different toppings that you can place on the salad with no limitations. The only suggestion that I could offer with the Market Salad is to get the dressing on the side so that you can regulate the amount of dressing put on the salad.

        The decor in Jones is meant to perpetuate a homey feel. The space is wide open and you can see directly into the kitchen. Overall we had a very nice lunch at Jones. We would consider going back but with the crowded food scene in Philly likely won't.

        I highly suggest if you are looking for a good, home cooked meal, stop in!

        Its the kind of place which if it was conceived, designed, built, and owned by a mom&pop operation--and kept to half the size--we would all become a steady fanbase. But that is not what 'Jones' is. Cozy eateries of the kind I describe, exude natural charm and warmth. But that is not what 'Jones' does. 'Jones' is a restaurant hoisted aloft by a wealthy restaurant tycoon; run like a cruise ship; and frankly, it fails at invoking any such homespun appeal. Its far too self-conscious and 'affected'. Like a hip trust-fund kid deliberately wearing Keds, dungarees, and riding a Schwinn 'Crimson Avenger' bicycle with whitewall tires; all to pretend that he was brought up at a much lower income level. Its 'faux'. Its got 'persona' rather than 'personality'.

        Everything is a mis-match. Incongruous with itself. Execution doesn't match concept. The food is alright --painfully overwhelmed by the frantic, elaborate service--but would you really come out and pay these prices for simple "mac'n'cheese" anywhere else but at a 'see-and-be-seen' type of place which this is? I mean, let's not beat around the bush here. If it wasn't a restaurant run by you-know-who; most folks would never give it a toss. It would have failed its first year; due to huge overhead.

        I've sat at the bar here plenty of nights, marveling at it all. The whole concept is really kind of disgusting: "hey, let's take a mom & pop 'style' place --home-cooking--and make it ultra chic, snobby and elitist"

      Stephen S. Business Owner

      Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
