    As with some others here on Yelp I'm old enough to remember watching dozens of crowded, sweaty and smokey live music shows here.  It was pretty interesting when the Khyber surrendered their throne as the King of live music in Philly to other environs.  I was very curious how their new persona would play out.  Having been several times now, I can say that it has certainly worked out.

    The Khyber was always a place to get good craft beer and it remains so.  They always have a huge and interesting tap selection of about 22 beers.  They also have a great cocktail list.  Some beers were fairly expensive at around $9 or so, but also rare and likely warranting the price tag.  I opted for a cheaper yet still interesting beer (Victory Wild Devil) for $6.

    Did I mention smokey before?  It was still a bit smokey in the Khyber on my most recent visit.  We stepped down to where the live bands used to play and were hit with the most delicious barbecue smell.  The Khyber now focuses on southern food and I was very overwhelmed by the size of their menu and added specials.  Tons of barbecue options as well as New Orleans inspired dishes like a muffuletta sandwich (on the regular menu) and jambalaya (special).

    After floundering for a bit in choosing we succumbed to the barbecue smoke and ordered the "2 Way" to share, which includes (not surprisingly) two heaping choices of hickory barbecued meat (pulled pork, half chicken, pork ribs or beef brisket) and 2 sides.  We chose brisket, pulled pork, sweet potato fries and barbecued beans.  For $26 this was plenty of food to stuff us both with leftovers to spare.

    I have to say that the meats were cooked perfectly.  Tender, flavorful but with some bark on the exterior to add texture.  I also quite enjoyed that they bring three different sauces to the table for you to enjoy: ketchup-based Kansas City-style sauce, vinegar-based North Carolina sauce and the little-known mustard-based regional South Carolina sauce.  I like sauce on my barbecue and I equally enjoyed all sauces.  Delicious meats + delicious sauces = happiness.  I think they might even beat Fette Sau and Percy Street out for barbecue.

    Overall, we've quite enjoyed each time we've returned to the Khyber.  I'll likely be back whenever I'm in Old City.

    Let me make one thing clear; my friend and I would have NOT known about this place if it were not for our host from Couchsurfing who knew everything about Philly! Kyhber Pass Pub, is an authentic Cajun Gastropub founded in 1970. It's one of Philadelphia's oldest bars and the menu features a satisfying selection of craft beers, hickory-smoked barbecue, authentic New Orleans specialties and more.

    My best friend and I ordered the small size of the "Nachos" which consisted of fresh corn tortilla chips, jack cheese, green tomato salsa, scallions and sour cream. My mouth is literally watering right now as I remember the burst of flavor this appetizer packed. All of the ingredients were fresh which enhanced the flavor. They were simple nachos and I think that's what allowed the flavors to really stand out. Most nachos are usually piled with heaps of cheese, sour cream, meats, etc. With so much food everything gets lost and some ingredients mask each other, but every ingredient in this dish was tasted and savored.

    Entrees

    My friend ordered from the Hickory Smoked Barbecue section of the menu. All entrees there are served with 3 bbq sauces: Kansas City (ketchup based), No. Carolina (vinegar based) and So. Carolina (mustard based). He ordered the "Sampler" which allows you to choose two or three meats and two sides for only $17. He picked pork ribs and pulled pork as his meats and chose collard greens and mac and cheese as his sides.

    I opted for the "North Carolina-style BBQ Pulled Pork Sandwich" topped with spicy vinegar barbecue sauce and coleslaw which was $10. It came with one side so I also chose mac n cheese and added a side of collard greens for $4. I can honestly say that I never felt such a rush of emotions from biting into a sandwich. My friend and I pretty much ate in silence. We were so amazed by the food that when there was a second or two that food was not in our mouths we were too breath-taken to talk anyway. We just looked at each other with big bright eyes and exchanged nods of approval. I was impressed with the culinary skill and the passion that went into making this food. Not only that, but the service was above and beyond. My friend and I never had an empty water glass or empty dishes on the table; our waitress was there taking care of our every need.

    I have to admit that I will be taking another trip down to Philly solely for Khyber Pass Pub. I have never had nachos and collard greens so good anywhere else so I am sure those two will be repeat dishes.
