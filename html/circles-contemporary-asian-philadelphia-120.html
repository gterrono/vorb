    I went with a group of 5 on Saturday. Three of us absolutely love spicy food and didn't think that  there was great Thai in Philadelphia, and we were all pleasantly surprised by how amazing Circles is. We had reservations that we made a few hours earlier. There was no wait and they had a few empty tables.

    The pumpkin curry was insanely delicious! It's probably the best Thai dish I ever had and the people at the table that love spicy food also thought it was amazing and the best dish out of everything we ordered. The Red Curry with Chicken was one of the best curries I ever had. We also had shrimp the summer rolls, calamari and another appetizer that we ordered that we all very good and fresh. My friend's General Thai Chicken dish was delicious and they made it the exact level of spiciness that we asked for. My boyfriend ordered soup that was very spicy and so delicious!

    I don't understand what other reviewers were talking about as far the cleanliness of the place. Maybe they recently renovated or cleaned up, but I thought the place was very clean, especially the bathroom.

    I felt that the food came out at a perfect pace. We brought a couple bottles of wine and the food came out timely but not too quickly that we felt rushed. And it was later in the night and they were fine with us hanging out for a bit until we finished our wine since no one was waiting for the table. We had no issues at all with the service and it was very good. And they accept level up so I got $3 off the bill and after spending over $100, I now have a $10 credit for a future visit which I will probably do in the next week.

    For the price, I think this is one of the best restaurants in the city.

    After Yelping restaurants close to where I live, I discovered this Thai restaurant that is within walking distance. I ordered for pick up and trekked my way down to 15th and Tasker after 45 minutes, like they told me. The location of this place seems a bit shady, but I'm assuming that the place started out as a take out only and slowly expanded across the street to a full dining area. I peeked in the dining area, and it actually looked pretty nice inside despite the reviews about how the kitchen/pick up area is awkwardly placed in a separate building across the alley.

    The kitchen area was jam packed even at 10 PM. There were bags upon bags of food waiting to be delivered, and a full kitchen staff going at it. There were also a few people waiting to pick up their food. I ordered a chicken pad thai, massaman curry, thai tea and a bottle of water for about $30. For the portion size, I felt like it was a bit pricy, but the quality of the food was pretty good.

    I had asked the girl on the phone about the spiciness levels, making sure to inform her that I was Asian and that I could handle spice. She went on to inquire about my exact ethnicity (Chinese), and informed me that I could probably handle the hot, but not the maximum hot level. I was pretty satisfied with the spiciness of the Massaman curry, although I probably could've handled the max just to show her how it's done!

    I wasn't too sure what the difference between Penang & Massaman curry would be, but I was satisfied with the tenderized beef and potatoes in a yellowish curry to cure my cravings. I want to try the Penang and Pumpkin curry next, since that's all the rage. The pad thai was standard, came with a bit of veges, peanuts, noodles, and whichever meat you choose.. squeeze in the lime and you're all set to go! There were even a couple slices of mango with it. All in all, the food was pretty good, but portions weren't as big as I expected. Maybe you get bigger portions when you sit, we'll see next time.
