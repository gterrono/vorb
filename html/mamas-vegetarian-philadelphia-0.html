    Similar to other reviewers, I had avoided this place for years simply because "Vegetarian" was in the name.  Curiosity finally got the best of me about a month ago, and I'm so glad it did.  I'm am now filled with falafel on weekly basis.

    Rundown:
    Get the Mama's sandwich
    Whole wheat or regular pita
    Get it hot or not
    A scoop of creamy hummus layers the pita
    3 heavenly falafel balls line the bottom
    Tomatoes, cucumbers, pickled cabbage next
    2 more of those delicious falafel balls on top
    I get extra hummus on top instead of the tahini

    Outcome:
    Delicious, crispy falafel balls that are soft on the inside and not dry.  This is mixed with the perfect crunch from the fresh cucumbers and the pickled cabbage, which also provides a nice vinegar taste.  The contrast of textures and flavors is simply amazing.

    Just raised the price to $7, but completely worth it.  Cash only.

    You can also sometimes snag a free piece of fruit from the counter. Boom!

    I was disappointed the Falafel Bar on Chestnut was closed for the summer, so we came here instead.  I've always walked by this and never came inside.  So happy I finally tried it.

    If you want a cheap, filling, healthy, and fresh lunch, definitely come to Mama's.  Not only was it the speediest service I've ever encountered (which is crucial during a lunch hour on a busy work day), it was a larger portion than Falafel Bar.

    I think I got the general $9 or $10 Mama's platter, which has a little bit of everything- falafel balls, latke, fried eggplant, warm pita, and a gigantic heap of cucumber-tomato salad covered in a delicious tzatziki-ish sauce.

    The falafel was delicious and crunchy and the platter offered plenty of veggies to load up and make a pita sandwich with.

    The only thing I didn't like at all was the latke- it was like a really dense sweet potato sponge with no redeeming flavors or textures.  I didn't let it affect my review, though.  I simply ate around it.  Plus, it might just be personal preference.

    Will definitely come back here again.

    If you don't get here early, be prepared for a serious line.  There's a reason for that.  It's because the food is very tasty.

    As advertised, it's a vegetarian place (big surprise) but it's more than that.  With lock-step efficiency, the crew behind the counter works quickly to bring you a mountain (yes it's that much) of food.  At around the same price point as most sandwiches, you can score a platter with falafel, latka and eggplant along with hummus, pita, some cucumber salad and all the hot peppers you can handle.  At first glance you may be inclined to think "this is way too much food for one person" until your first bite.  Then you eat a bit more, talk with your friends, lather rinse, repeat.

    Strangely, the food evaporates, you're full but not overstuffed and you've not had any meat whatsoever.  Brilliant!

    For a little less food, get a falafel sandwich.  That at least looks like a reasonable portion size.

    In summary, I like Mama's, I just have to remember to duck out at 11:45 to beat the rush.
