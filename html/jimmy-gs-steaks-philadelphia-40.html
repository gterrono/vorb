    Second visit.  Again, I would say this place is just OK.  The steak is actually quality so they have that going for them.  The rolls kinda suck though and that is really what's holding it back.  Last visit mine was chewy and tasted like a microwaved frozen roll.  Had it been on a better, slightly crusty roll (like Steve's) it would have been excellent.  Ditch your bakery and that could make these 100 times better.   If you look at the user pics posted here you can actually see the texture of the rolls - that shiny, chewy style.

    They go too light on the cheese for me and last visit ignored my extra cheese request ($1 wasted.)  I watched my steak being made and saw that no extra cheese was put on mine compared to the other 5 I just saw come out.  It was exactly the same amount.  When my steak was done, before giving it to me, the guy at the window asked the cook if he put extra cheese and although he knew he didn't, he kinda shrugged his head "yes."  That kind of shit is so annoying.

    The fries, although not the best in Philly as they proclaim, are really good.  They are a thick, twice cooked style.

    As it stands, good enough for a late night drunken cheesesteak but could use some improvement if they actually wanna hang with the classics and have some longevity.  Nothing bad about it but nothing special either.

    I think all the 5 star reviews here must be new to Philly since the North Broad area is like that - also out of towners going to Temple.  This is NOT a 5 star cheesesteak, but again, I want to stress it's not bad either.  A better roll would up it to 4 stars easily.  5 stars is reserved for the best of the best which Jimmy G's could not hope to approach anytime soon.

Specialties

At Jimmy G's Steaks we don't have a menu a mile long filled with mediocre options. Our steaks are 100% Grade A Rib Eye. We don't chop our steaks either in TRUE Philadelphia fashion. Along with our 100% rib eye steaks, we also developed the tastiest roast pork recipe in the city. Simply put, Jimmy G's specializes in authentic Philly Sandwiches served up with the best fries in the city.

History

Established in 2013.

The vision of a quick service cheesesteak restaurant at 695 North Broad St was first realized by the owners of the building, Glen and Nick Strafella in December 2011.  It was known that North Philadelphia needed a genuine cheesesteak restaurant and they believed Broad and Ridge would be the perfect location for it.  Over the next several months the partners dedicated their time to develop the idea.  The first step was to surround themselves with qualified people to make the restaurant a success.  Next would follow countless hours of creative meetings, operational meetings, taste tests and field tests.  The end result would be the first true north Philadelphia cheesesteak restaurant concept, Jimmy G's Steaks.
