        All I can say is that Jamonera is a wonderful dining experience.  Sure, you will need to make a reservation way in advance.  And sure, it won't be the cheapest meal you've ever eaten.  But, trust me, it'll be spectacular.

        First, the location.  Perfectly situated in Center City- close to lots of shopping, bars, and public transportation.  A perfect location for meeting up with friends who live all over the Philly area and the perfect location for making the meal into an entire night out on the town.  Next, the decor/ambiance.  You really do feel like you are in a tiny tapas restaurant in Spain.  It feels cozy and intimate without feeling like you are dining in a cave.  And then we come to the service.  Top Notch.  For sure.  They are extremely helpful in making sure every table's needs are met- especially when it comes to the inevitable question of tapas  "how much/many do we need to order?"  

        Now the real reason you eat out:  the food.  I'm just going to list what (I can remember) we ordered because our group of 6 ordered a whole lotta yum yums:

        PAPA FRITA- fried potato wedges with a spicy aioli.  The serving size was huge.  A must try

        IBERICO HAM CROQUETAS- fried croquettes stuffed with cheese and ham.  They were very delicious.  Not the easiest thing to share.

        CALAMARES EN SU TINTA- squid fried in its own ink.  Not one of my favorite dishes- it was a bit salty

        CHEF'S BOARD- pretty standard selection of cheeses and meats, with accompaniments.  I thought it was a bit pricy ($17) for what it was.

        AGUACATE- avocado salad.  I really enjoyed this one. I liked the mix of the savory avocado and the sweet and sour of the oranges and grapefruit

        ALBONDIGAS- meatballs.  These were one of my favorites.  Pretty simple but very very tasty.  Good for sharing.

        LUCIO'S DIRTY EGG- shortrib with potatoes on the side and a fried egg on top.  This dish was amazing.  I could of ate the entire dish by myself.

        PAN SEARED SKATE- probably my favorite dish.  Fish was cooked perfectly and the rice on the side was crazy good.

        BUNUELOS- this was one of our desserts.  It consists of donuts with caramel and chocolate dipping sauces.  Make sure you order enough orders for everyone to get a couple of these donuts.  They were amazing.  

        There were a few more dishes that I can't even remember.  Our total damage was about $100 a couple, and that included a few beers/cocktails/glasses of wine for each person.  I think if it was just me and my husband, we could have spent less- maybe $75 total.

        I really enjoyed my visit to Jamonera and would recommend it for a date night or a couples get together.  You are guaranteed to have not only a great meal, but an overall great time.  A must-visit for Philadelphia!

        A newer place that seems to be overshadowed by the bigger stars in the immediate area, but still worth going in for a meal with friends.

        The decor is trendy and modern.  Great for a small group of friends, or an intimate date.  

        Nice selection of wines, their glass of cava is 2 full pours anywhere else.

        Foie gras was short on the foie gras, and it was an underwhelming dish.

        Deviled egg was excellent, but with only 2 halves, you'll need to order a few plates for the table.

        Grilled octapus was a little tough and with a strong fishy and citrusy initial taste, but a nice strong char aftertaste that came close to saving the dish.

        The scallops were perfectly seared and seasoned, served ontop of a lightly dressed salad and with a nice portion of porkbelly.

        Manchego cheese appetizer came with a full glass of beer to compliment it, and was a perfect pairing.  This is more of a drink than an appetizer dish.

        Fried goat cheese salad had a nice, aged slice of a quality goatcheese, lightly fried with an excellent thick crust.  This was not the usual grocery store processed white log used by most places, definatley worth ordering one per person.

        Mushrooms on toast was a little strong on the flavor, overpowering the toast and any other seasoning.

        A little on the expensive side, but nice service, nice decor, and an overall good dining experience.

        Located right in the heart of Washington Square's entrepreneurial restaurant haven, this place is fantastic for tapas style dining and cocktails.

        I can't ever decide which one of the Turney/Safran dining hubs wins my heart the most, but Jamonera is starting to put up a decent fight for number one..snobby service aside, of course.

        Jamonera has been on our list of restaurants to try and we stopped one Saturday early evening hoping for seats at the bar to grab a drink.  We walked into a long bar and tight and snagged two seats at the bar.  The bartender was friendly and working hard.

        I ordered the Bizet cocktail (spanish vodka, passionfruit, clementine, and thyme). Amazing! What an awesome drink! I don't like cocktails that are too citrusy-y or orange-y and this was spot-on.  Very refreshing for a spring day.  

        For food we ordered the papas fritas, which were fried potatos that were really taste.  We also ordered the calamari which is cooked in the squid sauce, so the calamari is still black. The calamari was good but there are probably other more interesting and tasty options on the menu. The bartender was knowledgeable about which items are gluten free and could be made gluten free. When I called ahead to make sure they had gluten free options the hostess said "many! and I am gluten free."  So that was reassuring.

        The restaurant is a lot smaller than I expected, so make reservations.  It would be a great date place.

        As for the meal, it was the wife and I splitting all the dishes.  We went with some sangria, several beers, and the following 5 items...

        QUESOS ARTESANOS - excellent selection of cheeses
        CHARCUTERIA - a selection of meats, somewhat greasy, but very good
        PORK COCA - a flatbread with cheese and pork on top, delicious sans coleslaw
        CAMARONES AL AJILLO - tender shrimp with a nice sauce, a bit spicy for the wife
        MUSSELS & CAVA - delicious, lots of mussels, good broth

        The only reason I couldn't give this 5 is because their sister restaurant Barbuzzo across the street is a step above Jamonera in variety and deliciousness.

        As to food, we tried the bacon wrapped figs which were amazingly good, you must try these!         After these appetizers, we were practically full so had salads for our "entree."  I recommend the salad with the crispy fried goat cheese. The salad with the ham and melon was not as good.,

        Finished off the meal with some spanish style donuts (bunuelos) which were quite yummy coated in cinnamon sugar and served with a salty/sweet caramel/coffee flavored sauce.

        There is a nice selection of Spanish wines by the glass.

        Overall a good meal here.
