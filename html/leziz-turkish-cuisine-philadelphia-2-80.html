    S & H Kebab house is a true gem. From the name and almost South Street location, I was expecting a plain looking takeout type place but it is a beautiful BYOB serving up excellent food with great service.

    We started with the feta and dill rolled puff pastries, an old favorite, and they didn't disappoint. For dinner, I ordered the Adana kebab, ground lamb and beef. It was cooked perfectly, spiced perfectly, and the portions are very substantial. The kebabs come with rice, a small salad, and some charred tomatoes. For dessert, a caramel pudding (a flan-type experience) and Turkish coffee. Everything was delicious.

    The service is where the restaurant really stands out. The owner makes regular visits to each table and he truly cares about your dining experience. He is willing to go the extra mile whenever possible to ensure that the food and service is excellent. All of the servers were friendly, knowledgable about the menu, and quick.

    Can't wait to come back!

    I had a wonderful meal here while a friend and I caught up.  The waiter was helpful and available, but also gave us as much time to decide and chat as we wanted, even with a dining room that was quickly filling up!  Our waiter could be described as nothing but caring, humble, and concerned, switching out my friend's menu when he realized there was a trace of food on it.  The owner went around asking how things were and even lent a hand by filling up water glasses as the place got busy.  Again, he was attentive and kind, but not in your face.

    The food was quite tasty.  My friend and I shared the pan-fried zucchini cakes for an appetizer, which were flavorful.  I ordered the Lamb meatballs, which were spiced ever so deftly.  It came with two types of rice, one plain white, one yellow rice that had some kick to it.  There was a cabbage-like salad on the side which wasn't bad, but I would have preferred something with a bit more brightness to it, like a tomato/cucumber salad.  My friend ordered another lamb dish, which was also delicious.  On the side, we ordered the yogurt sauce appetizer as a dipping sauce for our lamb, which I definitely recommend!  

    We had the baked rice pudding for dessert, which to our surprise wasn't served warm, but had amazing flavor and was everything I wanted in a rice pudding, plus the addition of a dulce-de-leche type caramel flavor on top.  Our waiter forgot to put this on the bill, and feeling guilty, we mentioned it to him.  However, when he saw his mistake, he said it was on him!

    Overall, the food was excellent and my experience was put over the top by what I would cite as the best customer service I'd seen in a long time!

History

Established in 2009.

This is family owned restaurant, specializes in Turkish, Middle Eastern and Mediterrenaen cuisine. It also features live entertainment with belly dancing every Friday and Saturday.

The story of S& H Kebab House begins many moons ago in New Jersey, as these stories always do. The Garden State is like fate's corner bar; it carefully orchestrates serendipitous encounters from frumpy Turnpike rest stops, rigorously landscaped Wisteria Lanes and many, many Cheesecake Factories. That's how Sal Kucuk met Huseyin Yuksel 13 years ago. Fate, you could say.

The kebabs, made with chicken or leg of lamb ground in-house, skewered on metal spits and cooked over open charcoals that imbue each stick with a smoky, almost sensual undertone. Turkish pepper paste stokes a fire in the long, sausage-shaped Adana kebabs, while the classic, familiar shish kebabs bring cubed lamb and chicken that had been marinating for days in yogurt, onion juice and tomato paste.

Meet the Manager

Huseyin Y. Manager

Head chef, Huseyin Yuksel, has brought the intricate layers and flavors of traditional Turkish cuisine to South Street.  Born in Samsun, Turkey, Chef Yuksel lives the food that he prepares, and cooks as much by instinct as he does by recipes.  Having run kitchens in New Jersey, New York and, most recently, at Anchorage, Alaska's Istanbul Cafe, he brings years of experience with him as he sets out to make S&H Kebab House (Huseyin is the 'H') a Philadelphia culinary staple for years to come.
