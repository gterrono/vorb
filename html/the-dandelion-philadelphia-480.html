        Sometimes a good looking happy hour draws me into a restaurant I normally wouldn't go to, and that is exactly what happened here at The Dandelion.  I've never been a huge fan of British Pub food, but we've had excellent luck as of late with this place and Victoria Freehouse over in Old City.  

        This is a beautiful building inside and out, and while I agree that the kitsch can get a little strong, it does play nicely with the vibe of the place.  The downstairs area is set up very well, and there's enough room to sit, mingle and people watch.  The happy hour specials are pretty stellar (deviled eggs and bar nuts are top notch), and I liked both the white wines they had on special.  I never realized how nicely a full bodied white goes with bar nuts!

        I debated on three or four stars, but after seeing the duck and liver parfait next to us, we agreed on four stars and a return visit.

        Had a lovely brunch here with an old friend.  I liked everything about the place, from the decor to the service to the menu.  

        We were shuttled upstairs into a quiet dining area.  This is an old, well decorated space that has a great vibe if you like kitsch.  I, personally, am a fan of some fun personality.  My friend and I were able to get comfy and chat in relative quiet (though I know the bar downstairs and the main dining area can get a bit loud).  

        The menu was decent for a gastropub.  You had some fun, British themed offerings.  I loved my cheese plate (HIGHLY recommend it, this was a wonderful treat) and my friend had the chicken sandwich.  The drink menu is why you come, though.  I should have gotten the beer, instead I opted for sangria.  The beers on tap are pretty good, plus you can get tastings.  

        This was a fun place to have a nice lunch.  The staff couldn't have been any nicer or more attentive.  It is a gastropub and a well done one at that, so don't expect 5 course gourmet.  I can't wait to go back and sample more of the menu and try the taps.

        Come for the great food. Leave before the ROACHES show up.

        I visited The Dandelion a couple of weeks ago for a family birthday party. We were a party of six and had a lovely meal. The drinks were delicious- definitely a fan of the Dark and Stormy but avoid the Pimm's Cup unless you're a fan of bland, lemony drinks. The appetizers were uniformly good, with the pickled beetroot jar and the curried crab, cod, and chili cakes standing out as particularly memorable. The entrees were excellent; I highly recommend the lamb shepherd's pie and the rabit pie.

        You may wonder why I've rated the restaurant so low when I've had such a pleasant experience. Well after dinner, while waiting for our dessert, my brother-in-law noticed that there was a very large ROACH on the wall opposite him, just above where a young woman was enjoying her meal. We quickly sprang into action to alert her and the restaurant staff so as to avoid any more unpleasantness than was currently occurring. Once the restaurant staff dispatched of the ROACH and the young woman was reseated, our dessert was served. As you can imagine, we had little appetite. What was truly upsetting was that the restaurant staff did not acknowledge the incident in any way. They didn't apologize or thank us for alerting them of the incident.

        As we left, we saw another ROACH at the threshold. It is too bad that such a beautiful restaurant with such high quality food could be so disgustingly dirty and have such a notable lack of customer service. We will never eat here again and will notify everyone of all the ROACHES so that they don't have their meals spoiled by the same experience.

        So I warn you all too. Avoid this place unless you're a fan of ROACHES.

        SUMMARY:
        1) Decent portion sizes
        2) Cool, thought-out ambiance
        3) Good for groups

        Nice place for dinner with a large group or a close friend. I went with a group of nine and we were seated upstairs. Food was good but some dishes were getting pretty pricy. I would give this place somewhere between 3 to 4 stars.

        The chicken and duck liver parfait itself was delicious, but I wish I had been forewarned by the waiter that the chicken and duck liver parfait was A LOT and should be shared. I guess I was expecting it to be a small portion size, and possibly in a cup, but that wasn't the case. It comes on a wooden board in a HUGE serving the size of a chicken breast, along with three large slices of brioche toast, and small servings of grape chutney and cornichons (miniature pickles). I slathered the parfait thickly on the three slices and had to request extra brioche toast because I still had quite a bit remaining. However, the parfait was complex in flavor and incredibly creamy in texture, and was well complemented by the pickles. The grape chutney was a little too sweet for my tastes. Would recommend to be shared, but be forewarned that the brioche toast is very filling.

        I enjoyed the seared sea scallops (but I think I would have enjoyed it more if I wasn't so full from my appetizer). The dish comes with three large scallops, about five pieces of gnocchi, spring peas, asparagus, and lettuce. Both the scallops and the gnocchi were nicely seared and very rich. The asparagus was good but few in quantity, and I didn't think the lettuce added much. The lettuce was bland and flimsy, contributing nothing in terms of texture. I guess it was for the lettuce chervil sauce, which was nice and light with the scallops.

        The restaurant's ambiance is very quaint and the decor is great albeit extreme. I thought it was impressive that they could create the sense of being in someone's home given such a large space. We sat in incredibly comfortable plush upholstered fabric chairs. It was classy and homey at the same time, well-suited for a group of friends hanging out or a date. Service was attentive but not overbearing, and our waiter gave me solid suggestions.

        In an effort to keep from inflating reviews, I'm giving Dandelion a very solid 4 stars.  I'm not sure if the management can take credit for the exterior of the building, but they can take credit for not letting the facade go to waste.  It is one of the most interesting buildings in that area, to be sure.  The interior is definitely British with the dark woods, hidden corners, partitioned rooms and small chairs.  There was a decent lack of "British Pub" decor, which is a welcome change.  Of all my years in the UK, there was only one pub that pulled off having a wild and eclectic range of decorations and each of those had a story.  All too often pubs, both British and Irish, in the US, often appear as though they purchased the look of their establishment through some kind of pub emporium warehouse catalog.  The Dandelion, thankfully, does not.

        We sat upstairs for lunch.  I guess it's the "Dog" room, adorned with porcelain puppies of all shapes and sizes and various other monuments to the canine.  The tables and chairs all felt very Anglo.  My girl and I were seated and served promptly with classic rock and blues in the background. A nice ambiance for lunch.

        My girl had the Dandelion salad with dandelion leaves and avocados while I ordered the salmon with unknown greens and mashed potatoes.  She said the salad was delicious with the lemon vinaigrette and cucumbers.  My salmon was not overcooked (read: delicious), if nothing to write home about.  The potatoes were whipped and frankly hard to mess up.  I also had a half pint of the cask stout which paired surprisingly well with the salmon.  All in all a very enjoyable lunch and someplace I'd take a guest or visiting family.

        The downside:  I found myself wishing I'd have gotten the burger instead when I saw another table had ordered one.  I find this to be a negative reaction.  It's one thing to want to try another dish at a restaurant, it's another to see your own food and wish you'd ordered something else.  I found the whole place a little on the pricey side for what we actually received: a six ounce cut of salmon, a salad with no meat, mashed potatoes and a half pint of stout for $41. We turned away from another restaurant not wanting to have a $40 lunch and wound up with one anyway.  Our fault, to be sure, but I think they are about $8 too expensive for lunch.  Maybe I'm being critical or cheap, but maybe not.  The wait service was ok, but if you start at 15% gratuity for basic/prompt/courteous service, our waiter didn't warrant more than that.

        This is a place I would like to try again and perhaps spend another hour trying the different beers and wines.  So, I think I will.

      Specialties

      London's culinary revolution comes to Philadelphia with this eclectic English pub. Cozy rooms and intimate bars create a storied, Old World aura, while updated seasonal takes on British food make up the menu. In keeping with tradition, cask and tap craft beers are always rotating, a proper afternoon tea service has a sweet and savory menu, and classic beef family-style roasts are served on Sundays.
