    I've done some crazy things for food before and waiting in line for over 30 minutes for a cheesesteak is probably one of them.

    I was glad that Jim's Steak has their a/c on because it would've been horrific if there weren't any. The line takes about 30 minutes from the door to the front counter where you order. The guys behind the glass cooks the steaks right in your face so you know it's fresh and hot. I went for the classic, Cheesesteak wiz wit, which includes the steak, cheese wiz, and onions.

    The sandwich by itself was a bit bland for me but good amount of steak and tasty onions. I had to put some mayo and ketchup (don't judge me!) to bring up the flavor a bit.

    Was it worth the wait for me? Not really, but I'm glad I got to try it out and be the judge of it myself.

    So much disinformation surrounds the question of "best Philly cheese-steak". Everyone's got an opinion; you'll get people preaching from their little soap boxes and spouting off as if they're experts--but never going out beyond the scope of their own specific taste. Yet they love to imply that 'everyone agrees with them'.

    Some reviewer elsewhere in this thread actually has the gall to proclaim what he assumes 'everyone knows'. Yeah? So (to hear him tell it) a dinky little johnny-come-lately steak shop on TOURIST-THRONGED SOUTH STREET is the authority for cheese-steaks? Really?

    Is he even a resident of South Philly? No, because he would have to acknowledge that South Philadelphians never come up to South Street to get steaks from Jim's (the Yelper in question asserts otherwise, but lives in Florida). I'm surprised he even admits that "no, Jim's does not have the history of either Pat's and Genos" --this is the first fact that he should have offered. But he does go on to state that 'everyone knows Jim's is pound-for-pound the best' ...all without a single shred of supporting evidence (or common-sense) for his argument.

    Of course; (I can hear you say) taste always comes down to what each person--as an individual--likes. Its all a matter of 'personal opinion'. But--think about it--if you've only ever had "Jim's steaks", you are basing your opinion on limited experience. Right? You should of course go and sample the steaks from Genos/Pats. THEN, if you still think the half-hearted-feeble-little-concoctions-from-Jim's taste better--well then fine--but the argument then moves into the realm of, "Who else agrees with you?" and "How many people agree with you?" Certainly, you might cite all the tourists who keep Jim's alive--but since they themselves never go down to the Italian Market (much less Tony Luke's) is that 'credible' support? Their opinion is narrow and limited. They just don't know.

    Let's point out some not-so-obvious facts and let them speak for themselves. Pat's and Geno's--pioneers of this sandwich--have been around for decades; in constant operation **right in the heart of the Italian Market**. They serve local residents. Italian families and blue-collar folk. NOT tourists like Jim's does. They run around-the-clock; every day of the year. You can find a line of people outside even at 4 am--no matter how cold or rainy it is. Devotion. A constant throng made up of local people. Tourists don't come down to South Philly late at night; so who do you think is standing in those lines? People who know good food, that's who.

    And now think: what does this constant high volume allow Pats's/Geno's to do? For one thing, offer better-quality meat than Jim's can dream of doing. And they offer better rolls. 'Economies of scale', folks.

    Jim's steaks are puny; like his whole operation. The meat is tough; they're prepared without authenticity. Again: strictly for tourists who don't know any better. Bottom line: all you chest-thumping suburbanites: bear in mind the unshakeable facts I've alluded to above and let it temper your urge towards foolish, extravagant, empty praise for Jim's. Try the real mcCoy first, and then speak.

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
