    Green eggs will definitely have a long wait, but my group agreed that it was worth the wait.

    I split the short rib eggs benedict and the red velvet pancakes.  The short rib benedict was excellent -- I'd definitely recommend it.  People always say Green Eggs is known for its sweet breakfast items, but everyone at my table who got savory items was happy with their decision.  I also tasted the pork belly benedict (a special), and the kitchen sink, which was huge.

    The red velvet pancakes were a tad too sweet for me, and a really large serving -- it was like ordering a full cake.  Someone at my table got the creme brulee french toast, and he raved about it.

    Bottom line: I'd highly recommend it, but come ready to wait and come hungry -- the servings are massive.

    I tried going here on Sunday and the line was out the door. It reminded me of the "Fisherman's Wharf" brunch episode of Portlandia.

    However, since they offer an all day breakfast menu I was able to try their food today with no wait.

    I got the red velvet pancakes and the portion was HUGE. The three huge pancakes were slightly crisp with chocolate and marscapone cheese filling. Topped with strawberries and whipped cream, they were some of the best pancakes I've had. It was so big I had to take half of it home. I also ordered a side of home fries which were cooked with onions and peppers. Excellent. I'd say the whole plate could very easily have been shared...even if you have a big appetite (like me)!

    My fiance got the turkey panini which I was able to snag a bite of. It was also quite delicious. They have a lot of excellent options on the menu that I'd love to try in the future, including birthday cake french toast, tiramisu french toast, and the vegetable skillet.

    One thing that was a little odd is that they charged us 25 cents for the take home box. Not really a big deal, but why not just raise prices a little so that its not so obvious that you are nickel and diming people?

    Overall great value and service for the amount of food you get. All those people waiting in line aren't wrong :) I'll be back again!

    LOVE THIS PLACE. Probably one of my favorite brunch places in the city. I'm a huge sweets fan, and the French toast here is impeccable. Their specialty, red velvet pancakes, are to die for if you have a heavy sweet tooth - the portions are huge though, so definitely don't hesitate to ask the servers if you can share with someone... Or get ready to take home a hefty amount of leftovers.

    I recently became dairy-free, and I love that Green Eggs has a lot of vegan friendly options. I had the vegan French toast today, and it was amazing! The flavors and presentation for all of the dishes that I've tried and seen are on point.

    Our server today was also very congenial and friendly and helped us to truly enjoy our experience here. The wait for the food also was pretty reasonable. Arrive early if you don't want to wait for a table though, since around 10am, the place gets really packed. Since there are no reservations allowed on weekends, the line extends outside of the building, so be prepared to wait for a while if you don't get there earlier.

    A must try if you're in the area!

    Oh. My. Brunch here was the highlight of our quick overnight trip to Philadelphia -- high praise from someone as loyal to Monk's Cafe as I am! We're already scheming to get back as soon as possible.

    Thanks to Yelp, we knew about the two major inconveniences of eating at Green Eggs -- cash only and a long wait -- and came prepared with a game plan. We took turns waiting outside (no room to wait inside -- really, in freezing weather?!) and exploring the adorable shops that line 13th Street. These problems are the only reason the place doesn't merit five stars, as the service and food were amazing.

    When we finally got our table and saw the menu, we had to hold hands and do some deep breathing to stave off panic. EVERY DISH on the menu sounded perfect. How would we ever decide? Finally, we settled on three:

    My sister, who often takes five minutes to prepare her plate before taking the first bite, chose the Kitchen Sink. There were so many options in one bowl -- eggs, cheese, sausage, gravy, potatoes, vegetables and a split biscuit -- that every bite was of a different composition, providing endless variety. It really was amazing that such a combination didn't overwhelm -- everything was cooked to such high standards that it was easy to discern the different flavors and textures of each ingredient.

    My husband has an incorrigible sweet tooth and a personal policy of ordering creme brulee every time it appears on the menu, so the creme brulee french toast was an easy choice for him. It was so rich and sweet that I could only handle a bite or two, but that was a pretty transcendent bite: eggy bread topped with fresh berries and drizzled with Maple syrup, and creme anglaise and creme Chantilly to push the borders of outrageous.

    But I have to say, as much as I loved their choices, mine was really the best: crispy fried chicken on buttermilk waffles with poached eggs and Hollandaise spiked with hot sauce. It was an homage to Harlem with a curtsy to Cajun cuisine. The rosemary-roasted potatoes that accompanied them were delicious, but I couldn't finish them after such a glorious entree.

    All of us wanted bacon, so we ordered a side for the table. I was disappointed, as it arrived meltingly chewy instead of crunchy the way I like it, but the flavor was delicious and my husband, who prefers tender bacon, was happy.

    Our server had a great sense of humor and was helpful without being irritatingly familiar. We loved the experience and would return in a heartbeat!

    Second: Breakfast burrito.  We ordered without any meat or substitute, due to one friend's aversion to faux-meats. It was good, but nothing remarkable.  I was a little confused as to why they wouldn't accommodate faux-meat products on the side for this one.

    Third: Huevos Rancheros, also good. Unconventional in that the sunny side up eggs are served over taquitos (which they made filled with black beans and cheese for us but usually come with pork), and a side of potatoes (we had the regular ones with these).  I think the version at Sabrina's is better, though.  

    Lastly:  Red Velvet Pancakes.  These were HUGE (and square).  We asked the waiter to recommend between these and the tiramisu french toast, and he said that while the french toast is his personal favorite, this was better for sharing as it is larger.  I'm not a huge pancake person, but had been having a craving for like, a few bites of pancakes recently, so this hit the spot--even with the unorthodox flavor.  It comes with a mascarpone cream filling between the pancake layers, with whipped cream and strawberries on top, and there was some chocolate sprinkled in a few places.  Honestly I preferred the bites without the chocolate.  It was tasty, but a whole order would have been overwhelming--even a quarter of an order was almost too much.  This is what we had the most leftovers from (along with a bit of the tofu scramble).  

    We also had a large grapefruit juice to make mimosas, which turned out to be the perfect amount to make not-too-strong mimosas using up one bottle of champagne (we ended up taking the second one home). We also got some iced coffees, which were good but they leave sugar cubes on the table which WHY WOULD YOU PUT SUGAR CUBES IN ICED COFFEE THEY TAKE LONG ENOUGH TO DISSOLVE IN HOT COFFEE DON'T YOU PEOPLE UNDERSTAND CHEMISTRY I mean eventually it was fine but just frustrating for a few minutes.

    Service was inconsistent.  We asked for extra plates to facilitate sharing when our food was dropped off, and they didn't show up until we asked like 4 more times, of various waiters walking by.  Then our waiter stopped by a bunch to check in on how the food was as we were just starting to eat, then nothing for a long time (no refills on water, etc), we had to wait a while for to-go boxes and the check.

    The place is cash-only, but there's an ATM over by where they make the coffee and stuff.  The ATM fee is only $0.99, which is the lowest I've seen that I can remember.
