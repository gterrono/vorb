    Listed in Cheap eats

    Now these are sandwiches.

    Eating here is like being punched in the face by a sandwich, then having it scream at you about how badly standard chain places suck in comparison (I'm looking at you, Au Bon Pain).

    $8 a pop (no tax!) will stuff you until you want to throw up, but you won't be able to stop until every last juicy morsel is gone, because it hurts so good. The brisket was super juicy (and more than a little messy), and the spicy chicken was delicious. The flavor combinations are fantastic. The $1 fountain drinks with funky soda varieties are totally worth it too.

    For the record, they now take credit cards for orders +$25. You know, in case you want to take a couple home with you for later. And trust me, you will definitely want to.

    Having my nostrils assaulted by the loving stench of a near-by fish market and the bloody embrace of a next door butcher shop would normally deter me from enjoying any food...after all, olfactory senses are so intricately intertwined with taste, and smell is perhaps as important as texture or flavor itself (...durian, anyone?)

    But I digress.

    Paesano's II is a miracle of a sandwich shoppe.

    The Panelle features a "Sicilian Pancake" which tastes something like a long, fat rectangle of fried tofu, only with more seasoning and less "ugh." There's some roasted veggies scattered for good measure. At a place known for meat, that it even offers a real vegetarian option that doesn't consist of lettuce in some flat bread is a wonder...that it tastes awesome is a miracle.

    Listed in Washington Square West and surroundings..., Philly Takeout Favorites

    Hands down, my favorite sandwich place in Philly. If only they were open later during the week so I could visit more often!  (They close at 5pm on weekdays)  The menu choices are pretty slim if, like me, you don't eat beef, pork, lamb, salami, or prosciutto.  However, the two vegetarian options they do have- the Giardina (roasted veggies, mozzarella & pesto) and the Panelle (chickpea pancake w/ tomatoes, fennel and gadzuki sauce) are delicious.  I've also tried the Diavlo (Spicy Chicken Breast, Tomatoes, Broccoli Rabe &Provolone) and it was excellent as well.  Note- the Diavlo typically comes with salami, but was not a problem to order it without.  The new location they moved to is a bit further down 9th street and not as comfortable to sit down and eat (the old location where Monsu is now was much better for this).  We've found its more convenient to call up and order food for take out.  I've never had to wait long for my order and service has always been super polite.  One thing I do miss is the tilapia sandwich they used to have...too bad it is no longer on the menu.  

    Overall- Excellent food, good service, cheap prices.  Well worth the trip, I only wish they had more convenient hours- this would be an excellent weeknight dinner takeout option to have in the neighborhood.

    Paesano's is arguably perfect in every conceivable way.  Every bite lets us experience the real essence of Italian food...Patient, simple, harmony.

    I love the unassuming, and unapologetic nature of the place. It's no frills interior says, in the most selfless way possible, 'Yeah, the food is that good.'  The extra-simple Paesano's doesn't need gimmicks, either on the floor or on the menu. The Arista, Zawzeech, and Paesano are all fastballs, right down the pike.  They invite you to, at the least,  the ultimate sandwich experience; which can easily elevate to a transcendental communion with anyone who loves food.  The trial, error, passion, and, tradition of real Italian cooking comes through loud and clear in every bite of every sandwich.

    Being a non-hipster (maybe even and anti-hipster), I love the fact that there are no substitutions. You can't get gluten free, there is no vegan option, and everything is cooked in glorious pork fat.  If any of these facts offend you, then this isn't the place for you. It would literally be a shame to let trendy clamorings disturb a very real bit of perfect.

    Recap: Perfect Italian sandwiches for people who love food, at a price everyone can access. 5 Stars.

Specialties

Come taste our distinctive Philly Style sandwiches with authentic Italian inspiration. Created by Chef Peter McAndrew's of his celebrated flagship Modo Mio Trattoria,  and Monsu' restaurants, Paesano's offers a robust culinary experience in a more casual setting. From house-made lamb sausage to Sicilian Style chic-pea pancakes our menu affords true Italian creation, but with Philly style gusto.

History

Established in 2010.

Tired of having to add  'gusto' to other sandwich shop offerings, Chef Peter McAndrews decided to open a shop of his own. With the help of partner Nathan Baynes, Paesano's quickly went from a hole-in-the-wall pretender to a multi-award winning contender. "It's like a dream come true," say both proud partners. "You start with this idea about how to make something better...then all the sudden boom! They say we're the Best of Philly, it's been very satisfying."
