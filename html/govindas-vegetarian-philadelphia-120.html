    9/11/2010

    For those of you who don't know, this establishment is divided into 2 sections.  On the corner of South and Broad Streets you will find Govinda's Gourmet Vegetarian To-Go.  If you walk several yards west (on South Street) of the To-Go establishment you will find a building with an awning that reads "Art Gallery and Cafe"...this is the location of their fine dinning restaurant.  

    Firstly...The To-Go establishment:  
    With the To-Go on the corner we were easily able to find it, but were at a complete loss for  finding the part of the restaurant that we were looking for.  Starving and slightly confused we decided to get ourselves some sandwiches from the To-Go.  I got a cheese steak and my fiance go a chicken cheese steak.  The bread was wonderful...you were given a choice between whole wheat and seeded white, I went with the whole wheat. They toasted the roll on the griddle so that it became crisp and crunch and filled it with mock beef and peppers.  Although the sandwich was good, I did not feel that it resembled a real cheese steak and because of this I was disappointed.  My fiance's chicken cheese steak was incredible...I would have guessed that it was chicken.  The texture of the substitute was spot on!  The two steaks (which weren't very big) and a single bottled drink were almost $20. Ouch....But still, I would definitely go back to try their chicken cheese steak again.

    Secondly...The Gourmet Restaurant (the real reason I'm writing this review):
    Simply Incredible! After finding the restaurant we went inside and were promptly greeted by a server.  This restaurant is divided into two sections; there is a gourmet buffet downstairs and a fine dining seated section upstairs.  We opted to sit upstairs and enjoy a nice quite meal.  After being seated we were immediately given water, which seemed to have a faint trace of lemon...the water was delicious.  After our waiter gave us our menus and left the room, I took the time to look around.  The upstairs dining area was pretty.  It was fairly small, with only 8-10 tables at most, but had a very quaint and cozy feel.  Our table was decorated with silverware and long stem glasses for both water and the BYOB wine that we forgot to bring.  In addition the table, and rest of the room, were decorated in fresh cut flowers.  

    Appetizer:
    Bok Choy Wrapped Scallops with Horseradish Tomato Emulsion $8

    They were presented on a long rectangular plate, which was decorated beautifully.  They were delicious... Did the taste like scallops? Yes, but the texture was slightly different (more spongy) But, nevertheless, they were fantastic.

    Salad:
    Caesar Salad with vegan cheese and dressing $7-8

    This is one of the things that I miss most since becoming a vegan/vegetarian.  Caesar dressing has anchovies in it and fish, I'm afraid, are no longer an option for me.  When the salad was brought to the table we both quickly realized that the salad was intended to be an entree, it was huge.  The salad was very good.  The croutons were definitely house made, which was a very nice touch.  Even though, I don't really care for vegan cheese, the salad was very good.  

    Entrees:
    Vegan Mac N Cheese with Steamed Broccoli and BBQ Shrimp $15

    My fiance got this dish because she wanted to see what mock BBQ shrimp tasted like. Her dish was good, but as I mentioned before, I don't really care for vegan cheeses.  She loved her food and said that the shrimp were just like real shrimp and that the texture was very similar.  The portion size was very large and she wasn't able to finish all of her meal.

    Pan-Roasted Duck with Sweet Potato Puree and Peanut Ginger Sauce $19

    I ordered this. OMG! This was the best mock meat that I have ever had in my life.  It tasted exactly like duck.  The texture was perfect.  It even had a skin on it!!!! I am not kidding you when I say that I asked our waiter if they had given me a real piece of duck by mistake.  I am so glad that I got this dish.  It was incredible.  My fiance took one bite and told me that she didn't want to eat anymore.  When I asked her why, she said because it tasted so much like duck that she thought it was scary and that it bothered her.  YESSSSS. The price of this meal is well worth how delicious it was.  Now, i'm not saying that I'm going to start going every week...wish I had the cash to do that!.  But I will definitely be back soon.  Completely ignoring the fact that it was a mock meat, this was one of the best meals I have had in a very long time.

    Go here!!! As my fiance pointed out, being vegan makes it hard to go out to a fancy restaurant for dinner.  How often can you get a 4 course meal and remain vegan? Really? Go here and reward yourself with a beautiful, elegant meal in a relaxed, romantic atmosphere!

    Ohh, and the fine dining restaurant in only open on weekends for dinner.

    Oh my gentle Jesus. I moved from the Philly area to Chicago a month ago, and I'm reviewing Govinda's in a fit of homesickness.
    I've never eaten in here; my boyfriend and I always picked up or had it delivered, so we ordered from the to-go menu. There aren't enough positive adjectives in the English language for how much I love this food. I'm a vegetarian, so I didn't go for vegan cheese or anything (even though I'm sporadically vegan), because I love hot gooey melted cheese, because I'm human (not to knock vegan cheese; I've eaten it and enjoyed it as well). That said, here's what I ate the numerous times we ordered from here:
    Philly Chicken Cheesesteak--ridiculous.
    Ham and Cheese Hoagie--my go-to. I love this sandwich.
    Fish Sandwich--how they do fake fish justice is beyond me, but if it tastes like this I'll take it.
    Seafood Salad Wrap--again, this is so delicious and fake how? If I could eat anything meat again, it would be seafood, so this was a fantastic discovery.
    And the grand finale, for our fall-of-the-Roman-Empire-type days, was a side of twelve fried scallops and cocktail sauce. Oh. My. God.
    Here's what makes Govinda's so perfect: it's vegetarian comfort food. Because there's nothing like unwrapping that foil and biting into a hot, cheesy, greasy miracle, and why should non-carnivores be deprived of this? And this stuff tastes pretty genuine--and when it doesn't, it's still delicious. I was never not in the mood for it.
    And I have to give them props again for that non-seafood. Bravo, Govinda's. Bravo.

    Listed in Vegan Foodie, Home Sweet Philly, Solo Dolo

    I am on the search for the perfect vegan cheesesteak.  So often, I get a gourmet impostor that might be a delicious sandwich, but is in no way a cheesesteak.  For shame.

    But Govinda's stepped up to the plate and played ball.  I got a hot, messy chicken cheesesteak wrapped in foil, and it was excellent.  I might've even moaned a little when I first took a bite.  It wasn't trying to pretend it was healthy, and it was effectively comfort food.  It was pretty large, too, and I was able to save half for dinner the next day.  It held up!

    A caveat: both the chicken and the cheese are soy-based.  They had good texture, and the cheese was surprisingly creamygooey*, so I'm not suggesting a total overhaul.  Just maybe experiment in the kitchen with some seitan or nut cheese and see what happens.  I don't get fussy about some soy here or there (it's certainly better than its animal counterparts), but diversity is awesome.  And less likely to kill us.

    I loved the atmosphere of the take-out section, totally chill and not fancy at all.  I could've been in any meat-serving joint in the city, and I wouldn't have noticed the difference, besides the menu.  The service was great - pleasant, but genuine.  The prices are more than fair, especially considering I made two meals out of one sandwich.  I will definitely be back, I'll just make sure not to eat anything with soy for the preceding and following 24 hours.

    *no casein, though, in case you were wondering.  They even say so on their menu, which is considerate of them.
