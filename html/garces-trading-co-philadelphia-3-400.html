    I'm writing an update for the steak frites special that they've been running all fall. This $25 dish includes endless steak, and who doesn't love endless steak? The steak is deliciously seared and cooked to juicy perfection. We tried the red wine sauce and the green peppercorn sauce, both of which were rich and flavorful. The red wine sauce has a nice sweetness to it, while the peppercorn is more savory. We didn't ask for extra sauce and don't know if that's an option with the extra helpings of steak, but it may be worth inquiring about. The steak special also starts you off with a well-dressed frisee salad, and comes with some (slightly too) salty frites (that are pretty good nonetheless).

    The side of brussel sprouts was tasty and cooked with pieces of delicious bacon.

    For the rest of the food/pricing/service/ambiance, still 3 stars. But the steak special definitely deserves 4.

    Iron Chef Jose Garces at an affordable price tag. Sure, why not?  I've been here for lunch and dinner and had pleasant experiences both times.  Being from California, I didn't realize how difficult it was to acquire a liquor license.  Most of the restaurants in Center City, from my experiences, were BYOB. What sets Garces apart from other places is their separate, yet well integrated wine shop located smack dab in the middle of the dining room.  

    Had the wine and cheese tasting.  Not a big fan of cheeses (I find the aroma to be quite noxious) but a fan of indulding in new experiences.  They offered a wide variety of spirits and paired well with the cheeses from different regions.  The server was great and explained to us where the cheese and spirits are from, what aspects we should pay attention to, and why the pairings make sense.  I was pleasantly surprised at how much I enjoyed the flavors.

    The chicken liver mousse was very light and was spread on a light, air bread that didn't leave you thirsty as some breads do.  

    Their fungi truffle pizza was delcious and my favorite at this restaurant.  Most truffle pizza's I've had in the past utilized truffle oil.  Not to be snooty but truffle shavings on this pizza laugh at truffle oil.  It's thin but it was nice to have a pizza that didn't make me feel bad about myself afterward.

    This is definitely a happening place! I came here for a date during restaurant week, and we couldn't get a reservation until 9pm. Even then, the place was packed.

    One of the cool things about Garces for restaurant week is that because they traditionally offer "small plates," you actually get 4 courses instead of 3. Personally, I don't think their plates are all that small. Also, they are a BYOB, so that is always a plus in my book. And there's a great selection of wines in their state wine store that is directly attached to the restaurant!

    For my meal, I chose the cheese plate, lentils, cod, and chocolate cake. I was stuffed by the end, and I really enjoyed my dining experience. I thought the cheese plate was a little lacking. They gave me a somewhat large chunk of a delicious semisoft cheese, but I  would have preferred receiving 2 smaller chunks of different cheeses. When they say "cheese plate," I assume that would be more than just one piece of cheese on the plate, but maybe that's just me... The black lentils had a great flavor, but I felt like they had too many large onion chunks in them. The cod was well prepared  -- not too flaky but not too rare either. They cooked it with small chunks of ginger, and although I am not a huge fan of sweet-salty combinations, it really surprised me in a good way. Finally, the dessert was awesome. It consisted of a caramel-flavored mousse on top of a flourless chocolate cake. I also had a taste of my date's salad and pizza, and they were both delicious. At least for restaurant week, I feel like we got a lot of food for a different price. I will definitely be back to try it out on a regular night!

    Came here last night for Valentine's-- incredible. There was a $55 course and a $65 course, each had the option of a $20 wine tasting. We opted for the $55.

    Our meal started with chef's compliments--some balls of something wrapped around ricotta and fig and something else.. but it was very good and a sweet gesture. And then the free bread. Boyfriend and I love free bread everywhere and anywhere. And this sourdough treat came with some rich olive oil and sea salt. We enjoyed the bread throughout our entire meal up until the desserts came.

    First course we had jamon Serrano with a pear/horseradish spread, a delicious cheese with a fig marmalade, and baby artichokes. I personally don't like ham/prosciutto, but the jamon Serrano was still pretty good so that's saying something. I absolutely hate horseradish, and I didn't know it was in the spread so that killed me. I am usually not a fan of chese, but Garces does their cheese right and I LOVED it. (wow I'm really sounding like I don't like a lot of foods.. but it's not true!). The baby artichokes were in a very nice light but tart/vinegar-y sauce and were delightful.

    Loved the second course. My favorite. Trout salad and a funghi pizza. Get the funghi pizza. The taste is unique and incredible. The truffle flavor is just right. But I couldn't decide whether I liked that our the trout better. Okay, back to my theme of i-hate-BUT... my boyfriend despises all seafood. He can't stand fishy tastes. I was enjoying the trout so much I made him try it.. and he didn't mind it. Okay that doesn't not sound like a compliment but it's hard to get that form him regarding anything that swam that is now on his plate. I loved it loved it. It was seared trout on brioche, looked nothing like a salad, I think there were like 5 of them too so a loot of food.

    What there's a third course?? I was full after the second and ready for dessert. We had potato puree and duck salad. The potato puree to be honest was not that impressive. I think I was also put off because later I saw a neighboring table pulling cheese off theirs and the menu said something about mozzarella.. but ours had none. Just potato, oil, and some chives. It was good but your average potato. The duck was great. I've read reviews about the ducks being undercooked and I can see why people say that. Pretty sure it's supposed to be that way because it's delicious. So soft, tender juicy. The skin could bear to be crispier, but maybe that wasn't their intent. The sauces were also paired incredibly well.

    Dessert was a small chocolate torte/mousse/chantilly/knibs + macaroon. It was GOOD. We each looked at the dessert and were like "wow that's kind of small". And then because it was so heavy, we both struggled to finish ours. Had a chocolate flavor with a perfect bitter/serious overtone. Undertone? I'm not a fancy review writer but I know good food when I eat it.

    I only write yelp reviews when I feel compelled. I felt compelled. Most restaurants rip you off for Valetnine's day because they know you have no choice but to go out and spend money, so they only offer non-worthwhile fix prixe menus. Garces was amazing. We made reservations 30 minutes before we came in and they still had openings.. why were more people not coming to Garces when everywhere else was booked?? The only reaso nI can think is that the ambience is not incredibly fancy. A marketplace behind you, wine store in the restaurant. But it is, after all, Garces "trading company". It's what they do. The waitress was incredibly sweet and helpful and we had a bit of good conversation. The portions were incredible. My boyfriend and I went home and LITERALLY passed out from a food coma. We've had food comas before but never actively sleep-inducing food comas. The food was delicious. I've had many dishes I enjoy but not many that if I were to return to the restaurant, I would order the same thing. I could say that about the funghi pizza, the trout, the cheese, and the duck.

Specialties

Garces Trading Company is a full-service bar and restaurant focused on Iron Chef Jose Garces' own favorite dishes from his travels to cafes throughout Western Europe, with a focus on Italy, Spain and France.  Our thoughtful menu of cafe staples is served throughout the day, and our bar offers a wealth of cocktails, wines and beers that pair beautifully with our menu.  Our on-site PLCB Wine Boutique, the first of its kind, makes a great resource either before sitting down to dinner (corkage fees apply) or to complement one of our decadent gift baskets, available in our newly redesigned retail section.

History

Established in 2010.

Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a refined, Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, a contemporary American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago, IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale, AZ and Palm Springs, CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the landmark Revel Resort. Today, in addition to our much-lauded restaurants around the country, Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
