    This is my place to go to grab a tasty banh-mi sandwich in the Chinatown area.  I should note that they do have other foods on the menu, but I don't know of anyone who's tried them.  Also they do have a couple of tables and some counter space for eating in, but I usually grab a sandwich to go.

    My favorite sandwich is the sardine banh-mi (it's the only place I know that serves them).  Other favorites are the tofu ones -- either lemongrass or mushroom/onion.  Friends who've had the lunchmeat and pork ones enjoyed them.  They also have chicken and beef sandwiches.  The sandwiches are lightly toasted, so they're both warm and have that satisfying outer-edge crunch to them.  Inside, you've got your protein of choice plus shredded/sliced  veggies and sauce.  It's flavorful and filling without leaving you feeling heavy.  And they're a bargain at $5-6 each.

    They have an interesting assortment of drinks, but the only one I've gotten so far is the tasty (and rather strong) Vietnamese coffee.  Staff is friendly and may even remember you if you become a (semi) regular.
