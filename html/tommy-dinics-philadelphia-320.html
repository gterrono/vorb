        Had the pork with cheese and rab.  Its one of the 2 different sandwich choices you have when you approach the counter.  Beef or pork, its that simple...I only wish it was that simple for the herds of people online in front of me.  Why do you get on a line and then get to the counter and ask "what do you serve?"  or "what are my options?". The menu has been in front of you for the past 15 minutes!!  

        On to the food,  It was overall a decent sandwich.  The meat was just ok, borderline bland.  The Rab was fresh and well cooked.  The provolone was very tasty but a bit salty.  There was a strong taste of garlic in the sandwich which helped with the bland meat.  But if your going to sell a pork sandwich in Philly the comparisons are unavoidable.  Paesanos has juicier and more flavorful pork.  At DiNic's the cheese takes center stage but at Paesanos the pork is the star.  When I get a PORK sandwich I prefer to have the pork in the leading role.

        Atmosphere: In the loud, bustling and beautiful Reading Terminal Market. It gets super crowded during lunch hours, especially the time I went given there was a convention next door. But the line moves fast, and people are constantly leaving their seats and you can snag one.

        If the city council voted on creating a Mount Rushmore of our city's famous sandwich spots, I think they'd ask sculptors to chisel the likeness of John's Roast Pork, Pat's, Geno's (underneath which someone would probably graffiti "Bienvenidos a Filadelfia" -- god damn kids), and Tommy DiNic's. And I'm not sure why.

        To its devotees, this place's relationship with Reading Terminal is like that of Joe Paterno's to Penn State: a singular entity larger than the institution to which it belongs. And they do make a solid, if unspectacular roast pork. But the DMV inspired lines, high prices (about $10 if you add sharp prov and peppers), and overwhelming mediocrity make it baffling that some hold it in must-visit regard.

        The pork has OK flavor and is moist, but it doesn't profusely sweat the juices like I want and expect from good roast pork. Tommy and friends know what their doing with the hot peppers, though: slightly charred and with just enough embers burning to provide good heat. Find your three favorite state quarters (Ohio, Alaska, Ohio) and pay extra for this kindling. You need something to get the sandwich going.
