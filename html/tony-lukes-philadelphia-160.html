        After sampling notorious tourist magnets Pat's and Geno's, I finally got to try Tony Luke's, which I'd heard is much preferred by the locals.  Tony Luke's turns out to be quite pleasant.  It's a lot less gimmicky, it has indoor seating, the staff are incredibly friendly (Pat's: indifferent; Geno's: surly), the menu is more expansive, and most importantly of all, they make a good cheesesteak.

        I had their regular sandwich with sharp provolone, and both the meat and bread were of higher quality than either Pat's or Geno's.  My one regret is that the meat here wasn't as seasoned/flavorful as Geno's, which I feel would have definitively put it over the top.

        So, between TL and Geno's, I'm not sure yet...  More delicious taste-testing is in order!

        So the gang got together and decided to run up to Philly for the day to enjoy the weather, and we had to stop in at the cheesesteak landmarks.

        First stop was Tony Luke's because one of gang was not into beef.

        We got our order within a few minutes, and it was enjoyable and there was nothing to complain about. Better than steak'um by far, and definitely better than any of the cheesesteaks in Virginia. The frank fries were a bit salty if the hot dog slivers had been chicken I think it would have been enjoyable.

        Pat's and Geno was an assembly line of cheesesteaks ready to feed the masses.

        Pat's was chopped up ribeye like I was used to and by far had the healthiest portion of beef. Geno's was lacking in the portions department.

        For lack of speed Luke's loses out to Pat's, but beats Geno, whose cheesesteak was not living up to its hype, not to mention Luke's has an actual menu so it gets 5 stars instead of 4.
