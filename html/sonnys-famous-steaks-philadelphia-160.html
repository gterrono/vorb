    It was my first time in Philly, so I knew a cheesesteak was going to be on the menu. I had heard of Pat's and Geno's, but anywhere that's been on TV multiple times for something an entire city is known for actually made me leery. A quick Yelp check confirmed my suspicions and I decided to find something a bit off the beaten path. After spending the morning at the Liberty Bell and Independence Hall a friend and I had worked up quite the appetite. We set off to find Sonny's and it didn't take long. We went in and there was a line. It was mid-afternoon on a Saturday, so the line was promising. As you are waiting there is a guy taking whole rib eye roasts and slicing them thin, but not too thin. The guy working the register was laid back and had a welcoming attitude. Everyone seemed to be in a good mood, which makes it fun.

    I ordered the classic. The bread was good and did a great job keeping the sandwich together without getting super messy. Biting into the sandwich revealed flavorful ribeye and you could tell it was the good stuff. I wasn't a huge fan of the whiz and honestly wouldn't have ordered it with whiz except that I wanted the whole experience. Next time I'll go with provolone or, if I end up back at sonny's, blue. I found it interesting they had blue cheese as an option but I bet it's awesome. My friend went with that and I didn't get a bite, but she raved about it. They take cards, but you have to spend $10, which isn't too difficult, so not a problem. All in all, a good cheesesteak experience! I would definitely recommend Sonny's!

History

Established in 2000.

Sonny's Famous Steaks was created to bring the authentic Philadelphia Cheesesteak experience to the heart of Old City.  Using Fresh-Sliced Domestic Ribeye and Locally-Baked Italian Long Rolls, Sonny's Famous Steaks brings you the finest quality Cheesesteak Philly has to offer, without the hassle of long outdoor lines and surly, intimidating staff.
