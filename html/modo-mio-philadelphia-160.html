    I ordered the Turista option (on a weekday, not the Sunday supper), as many other Yelpers apparently have, but I thought Modo Mio was disappointing. The Turista option allows one to choose four menu items (selected from four different categories, including dessert). The items I chose were decidedly bland, over fried, and oily. But first, let me tell you what they did right:

    The service was great. My water was always full and our plates were, once emptied, never left on our table for more than 30 seconds or so. Also, their complimentary bread was delicious, as was the mushroom oil/ricotta spread with which it was served. It didn't knock my socks off, but I'd still say it was well above par. Finally, the dessert and cappuccino I had were good, with the former being both very interesting and tasty. I would give the cappuccino a 6 or 7 out of 10, not stellar but good enough to recommend. The dessert I had was a ricotta tart of some sort with raspberry topping. But here's the kicker: there were actually whole peppercorns mixed in with the ricotta! I know, when I heard the description I was skeptical too. But I decided to trust the server and so was rewarded with one of the more inventive and artful desserts I've ever had. I wish I recalled the dessert's name, but if you do go to Modo Mio I recommend asking for it by description.

    And now to the bad: everything I ordered, apart from the coffee and dessert, was over fried and excessively oily. Even the arugula salad I ordered tasted like someone had soaked the leaves in oil and only used a colander to sieve them. Gross! There were even item I couldn't finish, such as a massive, oily lamb meatball thing that tasted little better than an oil-soaked meatloaf. I'm sorry, but hearing so many great things about Modo Mio only to have my experience ruined by a terrible menu change (they overhaul the menu every 4-6 weeks to keep it "different." This one must have had "oil" as its unifying ingredient!) left me with a bitter taste in my mouth. I don't see my self returning any time, if ever. Maybe if it were free, but there's no way I'd ever pay for it again.

    I have Celiac's disease and this place goes above and beyond in terms of gluten free, from the bread to the dessert.

    When you go, just let your server know that you are gluten free and they will go over all the options with you while waiting for the kitchen to send out special gluten free bread (they grill schar bread-excellent). Most of the menu choices are either naturally gluten free or they can be altered to make it safe. In terms of pasta, they make their own gluten free gnocchi and other various pastas.

    We did the tourista menu. I started with the potato torta, which was incredible. The crab salad on top was delicious and the sweet peppers aded a nice touch.  The boar ragu with gf gnocchi was ridiculously good- nobody could tell the difference when they tasted the gf fresh pasta. For my entree, I had the filet which was incredible, and the chick pea crepe was gluten free as well! For dessert, we split the flourless chocolate cake and the pumpkin rice pudding.  The rice pudding was decent, not the best, but the cake was outstanding.

    It's BYOB and cash only. The service is also excellent.

    I woke up today still thinking about last night's meal at Modo Mio. For that reason, I'm going with five stars rather than four. Our server, Michelle, was very nice and checked in with us at all the right times even though she was quite busy with every table in the place full. We of course opted for the turista menu, a prix fixe that cannot be beat.

    To start, I opted for the mozzarella antipasti, which was like a mozzarella grilled cheese sitting in caper brown butter. It was quite rich but so delicious, especially when dipped in spicy marinara sauce from the mussels across the table.

    Next up for me was the lasagna. Thick, creamy and topped with a perfectly crisped-around-the-edges fried egg, this was one take on lasagna I'd never encountered before but definitely want to meet again. Again, it was extremely rich but so delicious. I traded plates often with my boyfriend, who at this point had a plate of hollowed spaghetti with tomato sauce flavored with pancetta and pecorino. We were warned that this dish was a little "salt forward" but I would say that most of the dishes at Modo Mio were a tad heavy on the salt. It didn't turn me off because the heaviness of the food seemed to warrant the salt, but I did drink a lot of water. Not that it mattered, because our glasses were refreshed before ever having to ask.

    Soldiering on, we greeted our entrees. For me, a red snapper filet that was crisped on the outside but moist on the inside. It sat over crushed tomatoes with green olives, capers, potatoes and onions. This is probably one of the lighter offerings on the menu and at that point I was grateful. But it didn't stop me from asking for bites of the pounded pork cutlet, blanketed in prosciutto and provolone and resting in a buttery caper sauce. I'm not even a big fan of pork but this was incredible.

    By the time we were offered dessert, I can honestly say that nothing really appealed to me because I was so full. I ordered the lightest-sounding option, honey semifreddo, which was indeed delicious although I could only take one bite. The caramel pudding, however, was devoured in a flash.

    While the restaurant was loud and bustling and not exactly romantic for our date night, we chatted with neighboring diners and even exchanged contact info with some folks from LA who make Modo Mio a part of every visit to Philly. I can't wait for my next visit to this fun, comfort food-filled BYO.

History

Established in 2007.

Peter McAndrews established Modo Mio restaurant first in a fabulous line of Italian themed restaurants.  Following Modo Mio and included in the Modo Mio restaurant group are both Paesano's Italian Style sandwich shops, Monsu restaurant, and Popolino restaurant.  Peter specializes in home cooked Italian cuisine with each restaurant representing a particular region in Italy with his interpretation attached.  From Northern Italy down through Rome and finally ending in Southern Sicily Peter evokes the essence of tradition recipes with a new wave approach.  Come in, eat, and be happy!

Meet the Business Owner

Peter M. Business Owner

Peter McAndrews has been involved in the Philadelphia Restaurant scene since before their was a Philadelphia Restaurant scene.  After working in a number of kitchens under different ownership Peter left for Italy to fine tune his skills.  He began cooking on his own with Modo Mio located in Northern Liberties.  A comfortable BYO with emphasis on Northern Italian cuisine.  He soon followed with his Paesano's sandwich shop brand focusing on flavor and ingredients with each bite.  Monsu located in the Italian Market was next which focused on Sicilian cuisine.  Finally Popolino was last which evoked a traditional Roman style Trattoria !  Each restaurant represents a different part of Italy which in turn represents a different part of Peter McAndrews
