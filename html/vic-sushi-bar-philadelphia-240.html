    We've tried to go to Vic's Sushi over and over again... Finally we capitalized on a quiet Monday to make our first trip to Vic Sushi Bar

    Food: 4.0 stars
    Wow, the ingredients are so fresh!  The rolls were delicious.  We ordered the:
    -RED DRAGON: Eel topped with Tuna
    -NARUTO: Cucumber Roll
    -OCEAN BROTHER: Shrimp Tempura and Crab
    -LOVER: Tuna topped with Salmon and Eel

    The rolls were all delicious on their own but with the exception of the NARUTO Roll lacked a 'Crunch Factor';  The cucumber in the rolls was not crisp and the lack of texture variety made the individually wonderful flavors blend together too much, the the point of making everything taste the same.  Nevertheless, I would definitely recommend the OCEAN BROTHER and NARUTO rolls if you like more raw fish.

    Service: 3.5 stars
    You are up close and personal with the chefs who do a good job and are very efficient.

    Ambiance: 1.0 star
    For such wonderful food, the restaurant is WAY TOO SMALL. It seems like they might be doing redecoration?? but still... this place need to re-evaluate.  You can be small, family oriented without making your clientele feel cramped.

    Price: 3.0 stars
    BYOB is huge. They accept credit cards.  Expect $15-25 per person.  Specialty rolls are a little pricey across the board.

    Location: 3.5 stars
    20th and Sansom by Rittenhouse Park

    Overall: 3.0 stars
    Great food but not ideal for patrons with the size of the restaurant and the cramped feel.  Its a shame that we have to try so hard just to experience Vic Sushi in its environment.

    Ultimately, Vic Sushi Bar is A GOOD OPTION FOR TAKE-OUT...

    This is my favorite sushi joint in Philadelphia. They have incredibly fresh sushi which I understand is in part a product of being a crazy busy, small outfit (i.e. you need to replenish your stock more often). Whatever the reason, the facts remain: good, good food and service.

    The specialty rolls rock (n' roll): Sansom and Vic rolls are likely the favs around my friends. They will add crunchies to anything, which my twin loves. And they make these tuna dumplings that folks go crazy over (it's avocado wrapped in tuna. it's gorgeous). They are prepared beautifully with a lot of skill, rapidity and color.

    The joint is a small BYOB so expect a wait, especially if there are more than 2 of you. The folks that work there are hard working and work ALL the time (seriously, all the time). The bright yellow walls plastered with the pictures of regulars (hey! where's mine?!) seem to actually highlight the fact that this is a total hole in the wall kinda joint. So be it! Maybe not a place you bring your parents in from out of town, unless they are my dad who gets super excited about good sushi.

    I'm lucky as I do live around the corner and can order me some Vic any day of the week (what else would I eat while watching Project Runway?).

    Let me state this fact
    Best sushi in the city
    And the best price too

    There's a ton of little sushi spots in the city, most of which offer some derivation of the 2 or 3 roll special for $9-14. Vic is right in the mix, but will always be my go-to spot for a variety of reasons.

    Reason #1: They deliver. Lots of places do too, but they're super swift about it, and will deliver anywhere you can get to by bike. Up the back wheelchair ramp of our downtown office to an unmarked door? No problem.

    Reason #2: The price. It's $10.95 for 3 rolls (the regular not the special ones). That breaks down to about $0.60 per piece. And they're pretty big pieces. One of the better prices in the city for sure.

    Reason #3: The prep.  The rolls from Vic are perfect. They're tightly wrapped, with the perfect amount of ingredients. They stay together from plate to chopstick to mouth, with no mess. As well, all the fish they use is kept at temp, not warm like some other unmentioned spots in the city.

    So yeah, you should go to Vic. It's affordable and tasty. Reason enough to go anywhere.
