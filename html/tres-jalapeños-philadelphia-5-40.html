    My lady and I decided to go to Tres Jalapenos on Friday night, as we both love Mexican, and it's half a block from her apartment. Recipe for success.

The decor was sort of kitschy but sort of not (checkered tablecloths, paper donkeys hanging from the ceiling, etc.) and I couldn't decide if I found it endearing or tacky. I'm no style guru, so I'm leaning towards the former. There was Latin music playing, which I liked, but not super loudly, which I loved.

    We split guacamole nachos, and they were great. I hate when I get a bunch of stuff on top and 90% of the nachos below are dry - not the case here. Very even distribution of deliciousness. I had the mole enchiladas. It came with rice and refried beans, and both of those were awesome. The enchiladas, however, were a bit lackluster. The chicken was a little dry and the whole thing was a little salty for my taste. My girlfriend had asked for a recommendation, and our super pleasant and attentive watiress suggested the green enchiladas. I gave them a try and thought they were really good - nice flavor, well cooked. I will say that overall, nothing I had was super spicy, which is great for me. I like the flavor but not the heat, and that's what I got.

    This place is also a BYOB, and we didn't B any B, so I just ordered a Coke. I was pleasantly surprised to find that it was a producto de Mexico, meaning glass bottle and real sugar! Nice touch.

    All in all, Tres Jalapenos had some pluses and minuses, but it's pretty new, the food was decent, and our whole dinner cost $35. I'll go back in a month or so and try something else, and I'll be sure to update my review!     Read more
