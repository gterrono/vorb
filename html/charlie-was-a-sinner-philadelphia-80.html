    I've been eager to try Charlie Was a Sinner since it opened, and I got my chance recently after catching Book of Mormon at the nearby Forrest Theater.

    Arrived around 5pm and was quickly seated.  The restaurant has a very intimate feel and a large projection of artsy photographs/film clips against one of the walls gives it a unique flair.  Considering its small space I can imagine it can get pretty packed later in the evening.

    Once sat we were quickly greeted by a very friendly and helpful server who gave a quick warning that they are an all-vegan establishment (and I overheard a server at another table remark that she's had people walk out after saying that before).  Though the beer menu was fairly limited it still featured some great selections and the server did a great job describing two that I hadn't heard of before and making a recommendation.

    The menu is tapas-style and our server recommended 2-3 plates per person.  I started with the caesar salad.  I was a bit concerned because anchovies are usually the reason I love caesars (even though I've read the original recipe doesn't call for them!), but my fears were quickly allayed when I took the first bite.  It was served with a large grilled stalk of romaine, covered in a really delicious sauce, and topped with some flavorful and crunchy croutons.  Definitely well-above average as far as caesar salads I've eaten have gone.

    Additionally I tried the chickpea fries, potato croquettes, polenta, and what they called a confit potato cup and mushroom.  All were excellent, with the confit potato cup being especially unique and delicious.  I also gave the bucatini and "meatballs" a try, and while I loved the pasta, the meatballs were not my favorite.

    Charlie was a Sinner may offer only vegan foods, but it is definitely not a restaurant for only vegans.  The great food and top notch service have me eager to return in the future.

    For staunch vegetarians, we found Charlie Was a Sinner as a welcome beacon of light in this fair city. This place takes the (vegan) cake as one of my go-to places in Center City now and I cannot recommend it enough -- to vegetarians, vegans, and carnivores alike.

I highly recommend this dish, if nothing else. To commemorate our success in finding such a great vegan restaurant, my friend and I decided to try our hand at two desserts: the Blueberry Tart and the Charlie's Chocolate Cake.     I've had Charlie Was a Sinner on my list of restaurants I've wanted to try for a couple months since I read a review of them in the City Paper. Our reservations were for 9:45 so we hit up a nearby bar beforehand. We arrived at Charlie a little before our reservation and was greeted by a friendly host who said it would just be a few minutes so we hung at their bar in the meantime. The place was packed and I quickly noticed how everyone was in a great mood. Ever walk into a restaurant and no one is talking or smiling? Kind of makes you want to leave. Well, Charlie was the opposite. It definitely made you feel like you were in for something good.

    The host found us at the bar and led us to a quaint table in the back near the video screen wall. I opened up the menu and was starry-eyed; I wanted to try everything. Side note, here's a little background: I've been vegan for 2 years, vegetarian before that for 13 years. My date eats meat, her favorites are Thai, sushi, and chicken.

    Our server, Melissa, was very friendly. She was quick to point out that they were 100% vegan. She also recommended 3-4 plates per person, as the dishes are small. I personally love tapas but as a vegan most tapas places aren't very vegan friendly so I was pleased. And they bring you a carafe of water with mint - nice touch.

    Here's what we shared (rating scale 0-5 stars):

    Charred Caesar Salad - smoky with savory dressing, crunchy croutons (4 stars)

    Peppernotta on Toast - the bread and peppers were good, a little too sweet overall for my liking and I'm not the biggest fan of capers (2 stars)

    Chickpea Fries - perfectly fried exterior with a creamy chickpea filling served with a nice garlic aioli, almost a sour cream consistency (5 stars)

    Potato Croquettes - my first bite into one was pure heaven. And the smoked paprika aioli is a perfect compliment to the saltiness of the croquettes. Let me put it this way, if I was offered a last meal on death row these potato croquettes would be on the menu (10 stars)

    Chargrilled Broccoli Rabe - perfectly charred, garlicky and spicy goodness (5 stars)

    Bucatini and Meatballs - thick pasta with a great chickpea and red sauce, soft flavorful meatballs (5 stars)

    We were also given the Heirloom Polenta compliments of the chef. The polenta had a nice texture and the tomato escabeche had a savory flavor. My date and I are not the biggest fans of mushrooms but they were good nonetheless (3 stars)

    And for dessert we ordered the Charlie's Chocolate Cake with a sweet strawberry sorbet and sprinkled with salted caramel. A perfect finishing touch.

    We can't wait to go back!
