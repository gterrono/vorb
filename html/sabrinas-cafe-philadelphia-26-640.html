    If you look at the photo I took, you will be a fan as well!

    Our Philly Day Trip began with meeting up with some friends for brunch at Sabrina's Cafe.  There are a few of them in Philly, and of course, we initially went to the wrong one.  Thankfully so because the line to get to the one on University City was off the chains!  So as we trekked to the one in the Italian Market area, our friends were waiting patiently for us, and as soon as we got there, our table was ready (they had waited 45 minutes prior for the table).  Bet!  The long lines and wait should already tell you about this cafe.

    Sabrina's is a very intimate cafe serving up delicious dishes where the portions are epic.  My buddy ordered a custom burger, his wife the French Toast, my wife the salmon burger, and my son got chicken strips.  As for me, I had to go big or go home, so I went with the "Enobaria's Big Bite ETA Special Burger" (again, see photo).  I'm trying to remember everything that came with this Angus burger since our visit here was about 2 months ago.  There was lettuce, tomato, panko-fried Japanese eggplant, slaw, shitake mushrooms, dressed with some sriracha aoli, topped with fried wontons, and accompanied with parmesan fries.  A bite of this burger resembled a Joseph Haydn symphony of flavors.  Ridiculous, and crazy good.  After a few bites, I checked with my buddy to see how his burger was, and he had already finished.  I sampled the stuffed French Toast and the Salmon burger, and both were pretty slammin'...

    One con is that they don't serve alcohol, but little did we know that this place was BYOB.  Farg!!!!  In any case, Sabrina's is a tremendous brunch spot, and next time, we'll bring some sparkling and/or vino.  I can't wait for our next Philly trip!

    Sabrina's Café is a gem of a restaurant tucked away in the Italian Market. Wait times are HUMONGOUS on the weekend due to its amazing food and BYOB nature. However, make sure you call ahead and get your name on the list- it'll take about an hour.

    When you walk into this restaurant, the seating area to wait is in a whole different section of the restaurant. Once your name is called, the host will take you outside and through a separate door into the restaurant. The morning we came for breakfast, they had a special Beyoncé themed menu- each menu item had something to do with either a Beyoncé lyric, or her life- it kept us entertained the entire meal. The décor in this restaurant is casual but quaint. There are separate rooms and it feels as if you are being taken through a maze. The crowd was definitely an eclectic group of people, from South Philly hipsters to old grandparents.

    The food is unbelievable- you see the food runners bringing plates past you and your mouth starts to water- then you second guess what you just ordered.  
    I had the special brunch- it was a piece of oatmeal cranberry basil cornbread topped with two over easy eggs, applewood bacon, arugula, broccoli and red onions. Topping the entire dish was a Texan style roasted corn-jalapeno caviar (salsa). The dish was phenomenal- I had to take the rest home. Everything was cooked to perfection, from the side of pan roasted potatoes to the broccoli that was garnishing the dish.

    My roommate opted for the Challah French Toast (which is offered everyday)- it was a monster. Stuffed with cream cheese and topped with bananas and strawberries- your mouth is assaulted by creamy sweet perfection.
    My other roommate ordered the Barking Chihuahua- a HUGE egg burrito perfect for vegetarians. She is allergic to avocado; the staff was attentive in making sure that they listened to her request and remove it from the dish.

    The fact that this restaurant is a BYOB adds to its appeal. Feel free to bring any type of breakfast drink you want. We brought champagne and ordered a pitcher of orange juice. Don't be surprised when you get your bill- the pitcher will set you back about 12 dollars. Don't worry, three of us were drinking- we finished two bottles of champagne and still had orange juice leftover.

    I would definitely recommend Sabrina's as a fun brunch spot. The weekend menu specials offer a wide variety and the everyday menu is just as creative.
