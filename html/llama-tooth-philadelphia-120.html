    With a name like Llama Tooth, and an aggressive sign out in the middle of the street pointing the way to its outdoor garden, it's hard to resist trying them out. Maybe they'll wow me more next time, but our brunch experience was just three-star-worthy - pretty good service, pretty good food, nothing exceptional.

    Between the two of us we ordered one of the burgers and the jalapeno ham omelette. I didn't try the burger, but it looked pretty good. The omelette was an omelette - nothing crazy, but there were whole jalapenos in there which is was I was going for. The waitress suggested this tropical pineapple-coconut-cinnamon-rum drink when I asked for recommendations. It sounded awesome, but tasted mostly like pineapple juice - maybe not the most experienced bartenders back there. The service was a little slow but the servers were very pleasant when they were there.

    If you are looking for a place to sit outside, relax, and enjoy the sunshine while you eat, Llama Tooth will do the trick. It was pretty empty when we went (they have kind of an odd location on Spring Garden), and from the other reviews it seems that is common.

    Llama Tooth is a fun neighborhood bar which gets most of its business from the Union Transfer crowds.  I like coming to LT because they are very dog friendly in the patio area.  They always bring a bowl of water for our dog and most of the servers will even pet him.

    LT's best feature by far is their patio.  Come here on a nice day so you can sit outside, because their interior area is disappointing, unfortunately.  It is cramped and no matter where you sit, it is too close to the kitchen.  But the patio is awesome.  They have one of the best bar patios in the city, with a tiki-style bar in the back that during busy hours is staffed by a bartender who can make some good drinks.  Their cocktail/drink menu is very good.  Try the Llamarita or the Sangria - both worthy representatives of the menu.  Their beer list is also very extensive, but my issue with LT is that they have no draft list, only bottles.  While their selection of bottles is good, I need to see some fresh, rotating drafts for a bar to really become great.

    The food at LT is just OK.  I've gone and had the same meal once and loved it, then gone back and hated it.  For example, one time my wife and I ordered their Eggplant Fries and they had a crispy crunchy thin crust with chewy delicious eggplant in the middle.  The spicy dipping sauce was a great compliment.  But the next time we went we ordered them again and they were cut huge, which meant that they were mushy and underdone and even worse, the crust was not crunchy anymore.  LT has a good burger (not great) and some descent mac and cheese varieties that they serve with french fries.  I usually avoid the mac and cheese because those coming with fries is just too much carbs and not enough veggies, but I have heard they are not bad.

    The service has always been pleasant when I've been there.  They can be a little slow at times.  I really like the man who helps out the servers quite often; I believe he is either the owner or manager.  He's always nice and cool to talk to.

    Not sure exactly what the obsession with llamas is all about - it's actually somewhat disturbing - but Llama Tooth is a pleasant brunch spot on the weekends.

    You'll have the choice of either eating on the patio, which is actually a much bigger area than the inside dining room. So if you want to stretch your legs a bit (and if it's not too hot), by all means, dine outside.

    There's a nice selection of craft beers and a small, specialized menu focused on burgers, sandwiches, and salads. One notable standout is the selection of  macaroni and cheese. They have about seven different variations, including mushroom truffle, buffalo blue, BBQ pork, and crab.

    One very pleasant surprise was the side of kimchi! How often do you see this on the menu outside of Chinatown? It's a great substitute for a salad.
