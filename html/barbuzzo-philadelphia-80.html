    Listed in Veg Friendly, Ocean Edibles, Foughettaboutit, P is for Pizza, Best Sweet Treats

    I went to Barbuzzo for restaurant week after hearing lots of great things about it. It was a lot smaller than I thought it would be, but I liked that you could see some of the dishes being made and the pizza oven going. It was pretty dimly lit and cozy it would make for a very intimate and romantic evening even if you just doing pizza and beer or wine.

    First our experience started with the crostini, that night it was Crostini, Smashed Fava-Goat Cheese Crostini and Smoky Chickpea Crostini.  As a table we agreed that the Chickpea Crostini were the best.

    The had a ton of options for restaurant week but I ended up getting the  Sheep's Milk Ricotta.  It was hands down the best Ricotta Cheese I've ever had in my life. I didn't even need the bread, I could have just eaten it with a spoon. It was so smooth and creamy, it melted in my mouth.

    For my main course I choose Pan Seared Gnocchi made vegetarian. It was made with prosciutto (w/out with me), mushrooms, and homemade truffle butter. It was good, but I would try something else next time.

    For dessert the whole table got the Salted Caramel Budino. It was the most amazing dessert I've every had. The budino is a layer of chocolate cookie crust, a thick and rich butterscotch pudding, salted caramel and whipped cream in a small (too small in my opinion) mason jar. We could have not asked for a better ending to our evening at Barbuzzo, it was so simple, but yet so flavorful. It just blew me away. I almost licked the jar when I was done.

    Overall, I loved my first experience at Barbuzzo and I cannot wait to go again.

    Public Enemy said, "Don't believe the hype".  Well in the case of Barbuzzo they are wrong!  I was pleasantly surprised!

    After a couple of failed attempts at trying Barbuzzo (Who knew it would get so popular so quickly that you need reservations, even on random days of the week?!) we finally went for Restaurant Week.

    The menu was nice, a wide selection of dishes.  I hate when the menu is so limited you are forced into an option.  This was not the case here.  We shared our dishes and enjoyed everything.  The sheep's milk ricotta was my fave started and the bread that came with it was great.  Lots of olive oil and salt but really nice.  The beet salad was nice but not enough beets!

    For our mains, the pizza/flatbread was very good.  Also the gnocchi was very nice.

    I was so stuffed by dessert I could hardly enjoy my budino but it was really good.  Some mouthfuls tasted extra salty though.  I'm not normally a tiramisu fan so I didn't order it but another person in my party did and I was surprised that this even counted as tiramisu, no chocolate or coffee to be seen!  It wasn't really to my tastes but i'm sure other people would like it.

    The restaurant is very long and narrow so the layout is a bit awkward.  Where we sat was great but I could see that at other tables I would have been too cramped.  Too people cannot pass each other along the walkway between tables towards the bathrooms.

    You take credit cards?   You have a bar?  You're not BYO?  You're entrees aren't over $20? On 13th street?  Really?

    I'll start with the negatives, which are quite minor.  Firstly, beers are completely overpriced.  I didn't even order one, but if you're coming in at $6 for inexpensive local beers that's a problem.  If you're going to sell beer in a $6-$10 range get beers that are worth it or you can't find everywhere, not super-common beers that cost $30 a case.  The wine list is actually well-priced, quartinos for $12-13 with a nice selection.

    The pigskin popcorn is tasty, but terrible if you don't feel like breaking apart greasy pork skins with your hands.  These need to be served in smaller pieces, I don't want to go out to dinner and have to cover my hands in grease.  That being said, if I were eating these at home on my couch with a beer I'd be all set.

    Onward, to the goodness.  Brussell sprouts are becoming about as trendy as PBR, but they're incredible at Barbuzzo.  Start with those.  Please.

    Uovo pizza is one of the best pizzas I've ever eaten.  The table next to us had the Margherita, which likewise looked amazing.  I can't wait to go back and try some of the other varieties.  Very much a fan of the gourmet pizza rampage in this town.  If you don't like these pizzas, the terrorists win.

    We closed with pasta, which was an admittedly odd way to go, but these 2 were both stars.  The pan seared gnocchi is something I've never seen offered, didn't know was possible to do well, and was outstanding.  We also had the dish with hen of the woods mushrooms, which was perfect.  Both pastas were incredible, and decent portions (not entree sized, but not exactly tapas)

    Salted Caramel Budino lives up to the hype.  Best dessert I've had in a restaurant.  Probably not the most refined, but it made me the happiest.  And that's what dessert is for, to send you home smiling.

    I'm usually firm in my stance that if I'm going out to eat I'm going somewhere I haven't been before, but this is one place that I really just want to go back to.  I completely disagree with other reviewers saying it's overpriced (aside from the beer).  Taking into account the high quality of food served, our food bill for 2 people was around $60, including 3 apps, a pizza, 2 pastas and a dessert.  I can't fathom how that's overpriced.

    Cut up your pigskin and step up your beer list, otherwise don't change a thing.

    Glorious. Divine. Last meal on Earth potential.

    I went out to Barbuzzo with my boyfriend and another foodie couple. We damn near ordered the entire menu, it was so difficult to choose! We started with the Mia Pera pizza, which features pears, arugala and walnut. The crust was incredible- thin and crispy but still chewy on the inside.     This place in tiny, so I wouldn't suggest going with a group larger than four. Just be prepared to eat enough food for eight.

    Every time I eat something with truffle oil, I feel sophisticated. Am I the only one? I guess this is dumb, because truffle oil is just a foodie fad, really, and it'll probably disappear off of trendy restaurant menus in a short matter of time. How will I feel grown up then?

    "It's like a cup of dirt!" said my friend Kate, referring to the childhood treat of chocolate pudding and Oreo crumbs. "Only for adults."

    I've come back from my Yelp hiatus to write a review on Barbuzzo.

    This place was good, but not as great as many reviews seem to think. There were a few highlights, but more disappointments. I'll break it down by dish:

    - Chicken Liver and Foie Gras Mousse: I'm a huge fan of both chicken liver and foie gras, but this didn't do it for me. The taste was too metallic, like the liver was underseasoned. Plus it was hard spreading it on the holey bread (crackers?).

My dinner companions say they liked it and thought it had enough truffle, so maybe it was only on one side of the pizza.

    - Chestnut Ravioli: OMG WHERE HAVE YOU BEEN MY WHOLE LIFE?! This was my favorite dish of the night. Something about the nutty chestnut flavor and tartness of the sheep's milk ricotta made me want to knock my dinner companions out so I could take their share.

    - Pan-seared gnocchi: standard, nothing special.

    - Stuffed Meatballs: I thought this had way too much flavor. It was a little overwhelming.

    Firstly, I will say that, I was excited about visiting Barbuzzo after its 3-bell review in the Inquirer and after visiting Lolita, Mercato, and Varga in the close and extended restaurant family. NONE of those wonderful places should be grouped with this review.

    Everything about my experience this evening made me say "meh" or "gross". Not a good sign.

    Upon arrival, it was 5 minutes before anyone from the restaurant greeted us. The hostess was off on another errand, apparently, but none of the servers bothered to even smile or say something like "the hostess will be right back". We were wondering for a time if she had jumped ship.

    When she did return, she took our name and gave us an estimate of the time (our table was paying their check) and shortly after offered us a seat at the counter which we took, gladly to get out of the cold doorway.

    The seats were sturdy and there was a purse hook (always appreciated by ladies, I think) underneath the counter.

    We sat a moment after the hostess took our coats then she came back with menus.

    My friend and I immediately decided that we would order two of the specials - butternut squash soup and a lardo pizza - as well as the Pig Popcorn that we had heard so much about.

    Our drink orders were taken and we sat for another 5-7 minutes before we got to order. No rush.

    The server greeted us and we answered affirmatively to his query of being first-timers. He explained that portions were small to medium plates that were meant to be shared. My friend and I were both quite hungry, so we ordered 6 dishes as suggested. The server then told us that was "on the larger side" and asked if "we were sure". I claimed that I was hungry and love leftovers. The server then proceeded to tell us that the soup, pig popcorn and goat cheese plate would come out first, then the meatballs (LaBan recommended) and Gnocchi dish, and "if we had enough room" the lardo pizza.

    Personally, I would have preferred the pizza as an appetizer, but perhaps that's because I think of meats and pasta dishes as a later, heavier course and, coincidentally, excellent to reheat. But who doesn't like day old pizza? Fine.
    The soup came out with 1 spoon, even though they claimed that everything was "meant to be shared". We asked for another which was brought out promptly. The apple slaw that topped the soup was too copious for the portion of soup and had a bitter, acidic flavor that juxtaposed the sweet squash flavor in a detracting way. (meh.)

    The cheese plate followed. It was an average cheese plate with little to rave about in the way of condiments, but it was okay. (meh.)

    The pig popcorn to which we had been looking forward never arrived. We watched order after order go out to other tables though. It looked just like the pictures, but I can't speak for the taste since we never got it.

    The stuffed meatballs came out shortly after they cleared the cheese plate. They had a little bit more of a spicy kick to them than I cared for, but the tomato sauce cut that flavor just enough for me and the accompanying bread was rich and hearty with an excellent flavor. My personal preference aside, this was the best dish of the evening.

    The meal went downhill from here. We were brought the gnocchi dish which had a one-person portion of greens that were not complementary to the other flavors. The chantrelle mushrooms were hard to find at first, and somehow the mix of gnocchi, onions, mushrooms and truffles just didn't do anything for me. I only ate about 4 gnocchi and thought to myself "why am I still eating this?". At the same time I came to this conclusion, my friend said, "This is really nothing special, right?" We were disappointed at best and nauseated at worst. (gross.)

    We set the plate aside, with about 8 gnocchi missing from the plate (about 1/8 of the plate). The server asked us how it was, and my friend replied that is was "not good". There were also mysterious shaved nuts added to the dish which were not on the menu's description. We still don't know what they were.

    Another 18 minutes went by from the gnocchi disaster before our pizza arrived - just enough time to wish I was home in bed. I even wondered if it was too late to cancel it, but the server informed us our pizza would be right out.

    Upon arrival, the pizza - about 12-14 in. diameter - was cut into 4 pieces. I know we live in America, but I prefer a more manageable slice. Again, my friend asked the runner to cut the quarters into eighths for us. It arrived back quickly. The combination of artichokes, cheese, mushrooms, and lardo was overly salty and dull. We each ate once slice and couldn't eat any more. I made my friend take the rest home, because it felt like such a waste. I suggested he feed it to his dog.

    The server did not charge us for the pig popcorn or the gnocchi.

    Sorry, Barbuzzo. 1 star because I will not be back.

    It certainly is great fun to eat at Barbuzzo - a restaurant that is fully aligned with the current Zeitgeist of small-plate, lusty flavor dishes that can be shared.  

    The popularity of the restaurant requires running a tight ship and the front desk does its job with professional efficiency (no waiting for our 8 pm table).  The service is solicitous and friendly and the menu offers a list of dishes that will please the most varied group of eaters, including vegetarians.

    No reservations for dinner?  Hit the late night happy hour at 10:30pm at this crowded, popular spot.  That's what my friend and I were forced to do when we were met with a 1 1/2 hour waiting time during the dinner crowd at 7.  The hostess also informed us that all of the open seating is posted on OpenTable.com, so early reservations are recommended for dinner.  
    On two different occasions, I enjoyed the following dishes:

    Seared Gnocchi: This entree is not available at the Happy Hour price, but is worth the experience.  The Barbuzzo pancetta and chanterelle mushrooms are blended with the smooth and subtle flavors of caramelized apple and truffle butter.  The soft, pillowy gnocchi is served seared, giving it a lightly toasted dimension.  

    Margherita Pizza: From the late night menu, this $10 dish comes on a thin crust with san marzanos, house stretched fior di latte, basil,  and cooked with extra virgin olive oil.  Pizza condiments include dried herbs and chili oil.  The cheese stretched with each bite and the olive oil gave a traditional Italian style dish a bit of Mediterranean flair.  I do prefer my crust to be crisp on the bottom, so be prepared to use some utensils with the pizzas.  

    Sangria: I like my sangrias balanced with fruit and wine, so Barbuzzo's version felt more like drinking a glass of iced red wine because the fruit flavor was so subtle.  The happy hour glass is only $4.  

    Blood Orange Sorbet: Rich and explosive with citrus flavor.  This smooth and slushy treat is refreshing and simple.    

    Dim lit and with a simple and modern design, this place doesn't need to be flashy because the food is delicious.  Highly recommended for visitors and locals alike.
