    Like many Thai places Smile Cafe's entrees are a little on the pricey side. However, they have a great and affordable lunch deal. Not the best Thai I've had but this is one of those places where price makes up for a few other things.

    Had the steamed dumplings, spring roll and the red chicken curry. The steamed dumplings were okay, a little thin which resulted in the filling coming out with any pressure applied but they still tasted good. Decent spring roll, not super memorable but not bad. Red curry was good, nice mix of vegetables, perfect amount of rice, and spicy enough to not be wimpy but not make me wish I'd brought tissues either.

    Tried one friend's yellow curry which was less impressive and the potatoes weren't cooked enough. Another friend ordered the mussels and said they were good (wouldn't know, I think they're gross), but for a lunch special they gave her a generous portion.

    The space is a little cramped so it's not really a great place for large groups (I'd say more than 4).

    I've eaten at Smile Cafe more than a dozen times. The food is consistently tasty, fresh and satisfying. One order of cabbage salad at $4.00 is a great first course to share. The restaurant is also an art gallery, displaying modern oil paintings that are changed often. We root for this kind of restaurant because it makes our city unique, diluting the saturation of chain restaurants in Philly.

And I'm not  a cheapskate by any means, I love to sit in a nice restaurant and just throw away my bills on delicious eats, but this was a rainy Friday chill-night. They did not make us feel rushed, but our server did a good job of "herding" us when necessary - it's hard to get 16 people to communally decide we are ready to order/consider dessert/etc.!
