    I went here for a belated birthday dinner.  I wasn't super excited, have to admit, since my last Philly fancy pizza experience was a little underwhelming.  But I was pleasantly surprised--even impressed!  
    To start the space is super tiny but very cute.  I could see though that there could certainly be quite a wait--so come prepared.
    Once we sat down we had a chance to look over the menu.  We decided to start with the grilled asparagus and the beets.  Both were good, but I'd particularly recommend the beets.
    We decided to split the polpettini pizza, with mini meatballs.  The crust was crispy and blistered in the right places, and held up well against the toppings (nobody likes sloppy soppy crust).  Sauce and cheese were good and in the correct proportions.  I really liked the meatballs and they stayed on the pizza, not sliding off.  I am definitely planning to go back and try something else.  Prices were also reasonable.

    Overall, great addition to the handful of wine bars in Center City and yummy pizza and wine.

    Good:
    Once you walk into the small but well decorated establishment, someone greets you promptly and seats you at a table.  The staff is knowledgeable about the menu and give great recommendations on food and drinks.  I also love that you can sample the wine prior to committing to an entire glass.

    Food is fresh and reasonably priced.  I've had several of their pizzas, and the sausage was my favorite.  The yummy smattering of delicate sausage with scattered globs of mozzarella and peppers makes me hungry just thinking about it.  (I don't think my description makes the dish sound that tasty, but that's how I see it in my head, and believe me, it's good.)

    I really liked their asparagus and chopped boiled egg combo as well.  It's pretty simple and not overpowering, but a nice prelude to a pizza.

    The wine selection, although kind of small, changes from time to time.  And the wines themselves are reasonably priced and good.  They have several beers that are available, but you're not really going there to drink beer anyway.

    And they offer sparkling water for free!

    Bad:  
    The space is a little cramped, and some of the tables are a little lopsided.  I remember we sat down at a tall table near the door (avoid this one if you can- it seats two), and it was wobbling like there was no tomorrow.  

    The place also would benefit from the use of air conditioning.  They do have fans and open all the windows/doors.  Currently, the place has the unfortunate combination of summer weather, lukewarm water (no ice), and lots of people in a little space emitting a ton of heat.

    I think the progression goes: The good, the bad, and the ugly.  But it's not really ugly.  The decor is very clean and the menu written in chalk on blackboards adds a nice little touch.

    I like this place.  If you get a chance, sit down with a couple of your friends, kick back, and eat some delicious pizza and wine.

Good dish.
