    Listed in Sweets, Treats & Desserts

    I loved everything about Kanella, from the delicious food to the wonderful service. The host greeted us with an enthusiastic "hi!" and seated us despite the fact that our entire party had not yet arrived.  Our server was so warm and friendly and seemed to really enjoy his job.  

    We started out with the Bureki: flakey, stuffed with feta & thyme and drizzled with honey - and the Saganaki: a large, flat piece of fried sheep cheese that is deliciously salty. For my entree I chose the stuffed calamari.  The calamari was so tender that I could break it apart with my fork.  It was stuffed with a flavorful mix of feta, rice & pine nuts.  For dessert, the 3 of us split 3 different items. Lemon tart: it was very tart and the berries/berry syrup complimented it nicely. The top of the tart was bruleed, an excellent dessert.  The pistachio & olive oil cake was moist and tasty. The fig & rosemary cheese cake was my favorite of the bunch.  The figs were fresh and the cheese cake  wasn't too heavy.

    We lingered a bit after dinner and never felt rushed.  This is a great place to gather with friends for dinner.

    The food at Kanella is awesome, the restaurant is cozy and the service is warm and friendly.  

    We started out with  the bureki and the saganaki.  The bureki is  flakely and delicate like a pastry and stuffed with feta and thyme and drizzled with honey.  So delicious, and could almost pass as a semi-sweet dessert.  The Saganaki is fried sheep cheese which was also amazing.  Who doesn't love some fried cheese?

    For my entree I got the baby monkfish.  I was sold on it when the server said that it was simply exquisite, a rare fish to find in a restaurant, and that there is a small window of opportunity to get it.  I don't think I've ever had monkfish before so when I got it I was shocked  that a 'baby monkfish' was quite large, and that it was SO UGLY!  It had big scary looking face with gigantic teeth, but it tasted amazing.  The fish was light and flakey and melted like butter in your mouth.  Needless to say, I ate the whole thing.  

    For dessert we decided to try the first 3 items on the menu- cheese cake with figs, pistacchio and olive oil cake, and the lemon tart.  The lemon tart was a bit too tart for my liking, the cheese cake was good but i wasn't fond of the syrupy figs on top, but the pistacchio and olive oil cake with a side scoop of ice cream was by far my favorite.  The cake was deliciously moist with a hint of pistacchio.

    This place is awesome and I can't wait to come back.  It's also BYO and they take credit cards!  I hear the brunch is also amazing.

    My girlfriend and I had a wonderful experience at Kanella recently on our way traveling back from North Carolina.

    The overall atmosphere of the restaurant is nice. It's small, without being cramped, and sparsely decorated to its benefit. The walls drip Greece (or, more accurately, Cyprus) and the menu reflects the chef's love for south east Europe.

    We shared a few appetizers which escape me at this moment because the real stars of the dinner were the entrees. I ordered the kouneli, braised rabbit with beans and yogurt. It was absolutely delicious in its light richness from the sauce and rabbit to the acidity of the yogurt. My girlfriend ordered the stuffed calamari, which she still talks about days after.

    We had the pleasure of meeting the chef and owner, Konstantinos, who was a pleasure. After talking with him for a while, he was kind enough to send a few blocks of baklava which were a sweet end to the meal.

    Our server was also excellent. He was extremely knowledgeable and familiar with the menu. His service helped contribute to the overall wonderful experience we had.

    Can't wait to get back to Philly to dine at Kanella again!

    Listed in Num Num Center City, BYOB please!, Date-pleasers, Fave Restaurants

    Delicious!  I now know that I want more home-style Cypriot food -- everything was great.

    Started with crispy fried whole red mullet fish with lusciously caramelized fig and fresh fragrant couscous with capers. Fish was fried so that you could eat the crispy fins -- very tasty.  The remarkable thing about this dish is how the flavours combine perfectly when you eat a bit of everything together in one revelatory mouthful.  That herby couscous with the fig is amazing.

    Smoked Quails are succulent and tender from the marinade, in a rich sauce with barley, pasturma, mushroom, and deliciously soft grapes.  Warm, satisfying, rich, delicious.

    Cow's Tongue Stew has tender pieces of tongue, stewed with tomato, chickpeas, pasturma.  There's a hint of spiciness.  Hearty and satisfying.

    Fig cheesecake was another revelation -- so light!

    Excellent service.  Server was helpful, knowledgeable, attentive and thoughtful.  He made sure our wine was chilled, and kept pouring more so that no glass was ever empty.  Chef comes out every so often to chat about the food, which was a nice personal touch.

    Rustic decor with wood tables, exposed brick, plaster walls.  Only thing is that it gets a bit noisy, and placement of tables felt a bit cramped.

    Comfortable and charming BYOB with great food.  So satisfying.  Can't wait to come back and try more!

Specialties

We specialise in cooking from the homeland of chef Konstantinos Pitsillides. Pitsillides hails from the island of Cyprus, an indipendant Greek island located in the very east of the Mediterranean. The Cyprus kitchen has many influences but can perhaps best be considered as part of the Levant. At home in Cyprus, as in his Philly kitchen freshness, simplicity, flavour and love are the standard ingrediants. Chef takes his French culinary training and applies it to the traditional recipes of his homeland. Homemade desserts, head to tail cooking, lemon and olive oil are all part and parcel of this neighbourhood joint.

History

Established in 2008.

Kanella is made up of a passionate bunch of characters from the chef on down. Being a local restaurant filled with the family spirit much of the staff have been there since we opened. Though Chef Pitsillides is oft misunderstood (and yes sometimes this is exaserbated by himself), the intent of the restaurant is a simple one... We have aimed to be a neighbourhood venue for good, honest and consitstant cooking. A place that you can afford to come to regularly and have a good meal, good service, a nice bottle of wine, and enjoy your company. We have been blessed with the neighborhood we are in and all our wonderfull customers, so many of which we have known for sometime now. We look forward to continued joyful cooking and eating!
