    I didn't think I liked sandwiches....but we all know that that's just not possible. Everyone loves sandwiches. And if you love sandwiches, you'll love The Corner Foodery.

    The first thing to catch your ear about this Rittenhouse gem is its unparalleled bottled beer selection to carry! We all know good beer carry-outs are a rare bird in uber-liquor-strict PA. But Corner Foodery has a bottle selection that would make any beer snob blush. The local and internationally stocked coolers that guide you from the entrance to the back order counter seem like the stairway to Heaven....and they might as well be.

    Despite their surprisingly spacious sitting area (the place is at least triple what it appears from the street), I've only had their office catering. These guys NEVER disappoint. Their ingredients are top-notch and their artisan sandwiches would rival any bready bite in the city. My faves are definitely the meatball grinder, the Milstein, and the porchetta, thought you really just can't go wrong with any of their sandwiches. This, THIS is what Philly should be known for--Gourmet, two-handed, chewy-rolled goodness. So put down your soggy cheesesteak and had over to Corner Foodery for a real sandwich (don't forget the long hots).

    The only problem you might have there is deciding whether you're more impressed by the craft beer or the craft food. I still can't decide...

    Picture a beautiful Sunday afternoon, post-workout, and all you want is some sort of sandwich item and to hydrate like a maniac. Look NO FURTHER than TCF for pretty much all of your needs, and some you didn't even know you had. Weekend deal of the century: Kegs and Eggs. Something like, 5-6 bucks for a sandwich (featuring eggs), some homefries, and a 9 oz pour of beer (when I went it was Yards Brawler, though I've heard it can vary). I grabbed a sausage egg and cheese, sipped on my beer, and basked in the warm wooden booth and the glow of soccer flags all over the place. But then, the best thing happened. I found the water fountains. That's right, the fountainS. THREE options. Kinda cool, almost room temperature water, extra cold water, and SELTZER WATER. As a chronic hydrator.... I was in paradise. Chilly, bubbly, tepid.... OPTIONS!

    When the sandwich and hashbrowns made their way to the table, I could hardly contain myself as I began to devour my sammy. The house fennel sausage was just off the chains. The eggs were fluffy and hot hot hot, and the cheese was melted to perfection. Honestly, I didn't even really need the bread, though it was tasty, and kinda just focused my dining on the other aspects of my meal. The hasbhrowns weren't anything to write home about, but there was ketchup and sriracha and THAT SELTZER WATER (not on the sandwich. just can't stop thinking about it.)

    I'm mad at myself for not eating here before, only coming for a beer now and then. Can't wait to expose more folks to the glory of Kegs and Eggs, or sammies in general, from this fine establishment.
