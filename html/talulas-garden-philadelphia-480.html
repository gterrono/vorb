    This place is pricey, well worth the first class food and service.
    They had a $100 pp menu sampler which looked great but we decided on the ala carte menu. The waitress will recommend beers and wines, I enjoyed the inversion IPA she suggested.
    The International cheese plate is awesome, served with crackers and bread- both which are homemade.
    We had the heirloom tomato salad, very very fresh and tasty and big enough too share. We tried the beef tartare, which was a good size for three to share but I would not order again. It was tasty, but looked like hash and seemed overly pickled and salty, still tasty but I would prefer to spend money on something else.
    We had the squash pasta with ricotta and it was fabulous. My son hates ricotta but loved this Italian creme sauce, and the homemade noodles were delish. We then tried the roast chicken with a olive sauce, and potatoes. The chicken was perfectly browned to a crisp but the sauce was made with green olives, so it lacked the salty olive flavor I was expecting. The chicken was prepared perfectly , I would have enjoyed it more without the olive sauce, the portion was big enough to share. Finally we had the broccoli side dish, perfectly cooked but way too much crushed red pepper, just a little less and it would be perfect. I gave 5 stars because the service , ambiance (a little loud) , creative menu were and portions were worth 5 stars!! The waitress went next door and grabbed to go cups for our coffee. The busboy separated our leftovers in neatly package bags, and both were very attentive.
    Next time I will return for dessert.

    I'm not exactly sure what series of events lead to me dining at Talula's Garden 3 times in the the past 30 days, but whatever the reasons, the fact I visited so frequently in such a short time surely suggests a high quality experience (I was not nearly as impressed the only other time I ate here in 2008, but it's really night and day - if you haven't been recently it's definitely worth another try).

    That said, my only complaint was the huge inconsistency from visit to visit (even with identical menu items).  Particularly the pastas...the hand torn ricotta pasta was delicate and delicious the first time, but super heavy and unbalanced the second time around.  The "Spaghetti and Crab" was a show-stopper upon first try, but tasted simplistic and borderline home-made on the second visit.

    But even at it's worst, TG never dropped below 3.5 stars, which is why I feel comfortable giving them a 4 star rating which is really the average between all the visits.  

    The mushroom soup, chicken under a brick, and hen (special) were consistent winners, and the cheese selection is one of the better ones in the city.  The vegetable sides stand on their own and are almost enough to go back for.

    Now back to the inconsistencies - the service quality ranged from "exceptional experience" down to "WTF is happening here?" from one visit to the next.  Our first server (I'll update the review later with his name if I remember it) was a class act, he had full command of the menu and offered great insight and recommendations (particularly on the cheeses).  

    However, by the third visit, service was just "confused." The meal progression for our 7:45 reservation was Warm Brioche (8:00), Amuse Bouche (8:10), Cheese course (8:20), Soup (8:50), Waiter asking if we're done with Soup (8:52)...2nd Warm Brioche (8:55)  - BTW it was at this point we were worried they were reseting the entire service Groundhog Day style...  

    Honestly I might not have even noticed the odd timing of the dishes, but my girlfriend who neither ordered nor had interest in the cheese or soup basically sat starved for the first hour.

    I'm mostly venting of course, it was far more weird than annoying.  But there is one critique I won't apologize for... the wine list is disappointing, both by bottle and by the glass.  How can a restaurant that is medium to high end in every other respect have such an amateur wine list?  This knocks them out of consideration for any serious special occasion.

    So that ended up being a lot more rambling and complaining than I'd intended, but seriously I do recommend Talula's.  I'm not sure I'll be back 3 times again this month, but I will be back.

    BRUNCH REVIEW

    This place along with it's sister restaurant have been on my list for a while now and we finally got to go for brunch. It's right on Washington Square and it's a beautiful space, the bar looks really nice as well and I will have to check that part of the place out in the future. We were greeted promptly by the hostess and given the choice to sit inside or outside, they have a really nice courtyard here as well and I have to say that I was tempted to sit outside.

    We sat inside though and after ordering some coffees and drinks we started off with the peach pie turnover appetizer. This is a real winner and I am glad that we caught it while in season, the basil simple syrup that came with it was so good and went perfectly with the turnovers, they were piping hot when they came out which is always a good thing and there were honeysuckle flowers on top. It didn't last long. For entrees we went with the crab salad which was good but honestly nothing too stand outish and the burger, cooked medium rare. This baby is a thing of beauty with goat cheese, bacon, a fried egg, and greens on a brioche bun. We tore into this thing and it put up a good fight but eventually we finished it off along with the fried potatoes that came with it. The burger is a short rib blend that tastes like it has some dry aged meat in there as well. I will be back just to get this burger again it was that good. The reason I went with 4 stars instead of 5 is because while the crab salad was good it didn't blow our socks off.

    All in all a fantastic brunch and I would like to return in the future for brunch or dinner.

    One of my most beloved restaurants in Philadelphia. All our visits to Aimee Olexy's gem have been memorable.

    We first went just days after they opened, to celebrate a friend's birthday. There were no "opening" issues--food service was flawless, prompt, and friendly and the food itself was mesmerizing. It was a fall menu and my friend had the chicken entree that was the most delicious chicken dish I've ever encountered. I honestly had no idea somebody could make such a simple protein taste that wonderful.

    All our visits include a cheese tray because that's Olexy's calling card. When you're in the home of a cheese bar as beautifully laden with exotic, rare, and fantastic cheeses like Talula's Garden, you'd be foolish to skip this course.

    Brunch with the family here was also an experience--everyone was besides themselves with glee at their various choices, the coffee is superb, and the breakfast pastries were divine.

    Finally, I've had some of the best cocktails here as well! The lemonade with huckleberry delight from last summer was so good, I clearly committed it to memory. Of course, their "artist" cocktail is a standard and always a fine choice.

    The attention to detail here, in both food, presentation, service, and surroundings are praiseworthy and a visit here is always worth the wait. Perhaps most impressive, is the fact that such astoundingly excellent food and service comes without an ounce of pretense. There is nothing stuffy or obnoxious about the staff, food, or environment here and I feel like I'm back with friends every time we visit.

    We bumped into Talula's Garden after a mid-summer stroll through adjacent Washington Square, one of the loveliest spots in Philly.  Instantly enchanted, especially by the lush outdoor garden and whimsical menu, we booked an anniversary dinner here.  When dinner time came, the city was beset by violent thunderstorms, almost prompting us to reschedule (thankfully we didn't), and of course the garden was off-limits.  Nonetheless, Talula's delivered one of the most memorable and exquisite meals in my memory

    The large room where we sat featured very high ceilings and an enormous, somewhat gaudy chandelier, along with scattered art and red walls.  The first morsel we sampled was a small, homemade brioche topped with a dab of sun-dried tomato butter:  hard to go wrong if that if your prelude.  Our server, though not the most warm or welcoming I've met, gave us plenty of time to deliberate over the uniformly delectable menu.

    First up were the shrimp, large, head-on, sumptuous, and seared on a plank.  The ambrosial salad of corn, bulgar and nectarines offered a unique and interesting complement.  The arugula pesto added visual appeal and a nutty undertone, and I was impressed by my first exposure to purslane, a complex-tasting green that is much maligned (considered a "weed" in the U.S., and sometimes known by the unflattering name "pigweed.")  

    Along with the shrimp came a decadent and beautiful portion of pan-fried homemade gnocchi.  The gnocchi had a deep roasted potato taste, and the outside was lightly fried until golden brown, adding some sweetness which combined well with the semi-salty shreds of ham and the halved Mission figs strewn across the plate.  The crown jewel here was the cheesy fondue the gnocchi was tossed with before serving.   A true knock-out (or should I say Gnocc-out?)

    We chose scallops, with a decidedly Thai motif, for our main course.  There was a lot going on across the plate within the circle formed by five perfectly seared, medium size scallops - a couple of fresh kaffir leaves, some seared zucchini, curry sauce with a bit of foam, sweet white corn, and a handful of cashews.  It came together like the finest symphony.

    Dessert presented a rare dilemma - six very different but nearly equally appealing options.  We continued with the well-established corn theme and ordered a blueberry upside down cake with a creamy scoop of sweet corn ice cream.  The cake was luscious and moist and warm and went seamlessly with the ice cream.  A dozen fresh blueberries and mint paired nicely as well.

    Talula's is great for a special occasion, or really any occasion.  We'll be back, hopefully on a day when we can enjoy the garden in all its glory.

    Basics:

    The tuna crudo was amazing. Simply refreshing, especially in the summer. The melons were a perfect pairing with the fish. The tartness of the lime mellowed out the sweetness and brought out the brine of the fish. Wonderful.

    Conclusion:

    Absolute joy to go to this establishment. Some of the best food in the city. Find a seat outside if possible; the package won't disappoint.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
