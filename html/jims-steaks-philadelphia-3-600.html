    It's hard to justify writing a review for a place that's already been commented on close to 650 times, but Jim's Steaks has been a part of my life for a very long time, so it's worth the couple of minutes it'll take to say what I have to say.

    To me, the allure of Jim's is the sum of three things: the fact that I've been going there since I was a kid, the fact that it's on South St., and the fact that it's open late.  I have honestly had way better cheesesteaks (we don't have to bring up the stupid debate about Pat's or Geno's - I've had better off a pushcart) - but Jim's is an institution, and it deserves respect.  

    The steaks at Jim's are extremely consistent.  The first one I had in 1989 tasted just like the last one.  If you order correctly and without dicking around, you will get your sandwiches without a side of extra surliness.  If, on the other hand, you spend your time in line obliviously chatting with your friends, then arrive at the counter with a look on your face like you have never seen a menu board or aren't sure what a cheesesteak is, you will get what you deserve.  Actually, no you won't, because they will stop short of calling you an asshole.

    Fighting for a place to sit can be difficult, but really no more so than at any restaurant in any city where the main dining area is upstairs.  I usually just go eat mine on a nearby curb or stoop to avoid the pressure.

    As far as the sandwiches themselves, my personal feeling is that there is nothing that particularly distinguishes them from their peers.  I've never actually seen how they prep the steaks, or if I have, I've never noticed, so I don't know what cut they are sliced from, but they taste like... sliced beef fried on a flattop.  And whiz is whiz the world over.  The bread is fine, but let's just say that if this were Paris instead of Philadelphia, I'm not getting up at 4 a.m. to go there and buy a couple of loaves.  I feel that bread is the difference between a good cheesesteak and a so-so one, and ultimately, why this one is nothing special.

    But like I said, it's an institution.  It really is a great place to hit late at night with a few friends, though I would council avoiding it on a balmy weekend day.  Unless you have some serious time to invest, that is.  And if you've never been, I think the more time you invest, the greater the build-up and anticipation will be, and the more likely you will feel disappointed at the end, left wondering what everyone was raving about.

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
