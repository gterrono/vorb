    Listed in Philly Phavorites

    It's university city dining days, which means I've been dreaming of the menus posted online and trying to decide whether to go for a $15, $25, or $35 meal.  When I realized Distrito was on the list (at $35), my decision was made, and we made a reservation for 6.

    The restaurant was unexpectedly empty at 6pm, but they seated us downstairs, by the door. For 2 of us, they smashed two small tables together in a fairly awkward seating arrangement.  This was my biggest complaint.  With so few folks dining, I'm not sure why they didn't seat us upstairs, or even downstairs, at a table more appropriate for food.

    Luckily, the food was too good to be worrying about the seating arrangement. I started with the salsa and guacamole. The salsa was fresh, but the guac was awesome. Huge chucks of avocado, delicious flavor. For the 2nd course, I went with Huraches de Hongo and Tacos de Mahi Mahi. Both were great. For those wondering what the heck huraches is, it's basically a flatbread. Mine was covered in yummy mushrooms. I had heard so much about the mahi mahi tacos that I was most excited for those - they were much smaller than I expected, but I devoured them quickly. To finish, I had a chocolate torte - it was just ok, wasn't a huge fan of the chili chocolate sauce.  Was disappointed both desserts were coffee flavored.

    I have to agree with Michelle C. - hard to explain, but this is just a solid 4 stars - not 5. Luckily, Distrito opened up a new location in my backyard, at the Moorestown Mall; I'm looking forward to checking it out!

    First off, I have to say that I am shocked that I have to write a negative review of this place.

    I've been to Distrito for dinner once before, a few years back when the paint was still wet...and the service was fantastic.  Jose Garces' establishments have an incredible reputation and it absolutely exceeded my expectations.

    Now, last Saturday, after many missed opportunities, I was finally able to go back for dinner for University City Dining Days. The bf and I have been lacking quality time together lately and thought Distrito would be the perfect backdrop for our romantic date night.  We made an 8:30 pm reservation and got dressed to. the. nines.  I rarely dress up, but this was a special night!

    We got to Distrito a little before 8:30 and I excitedly greeted the host.  The place was buzzing but not to the point of a frenzy and we were asked to wait a few minutes.  Another woman came and sat us down; we literally walked 4 steps and were at our table.  Huh, what?!  THIS is  the table we had to wait for?  And of course we had the pleasure of admiring other couples walking in, dressed up like us, but being escorted upstairs to the swinging chairs and other fun things.  I had a view of the staircase and my bf had a view of the slop sink.  Atmosphere!

    Now the food:  It was fantastic.  We loved the ceviche, the guac, the chicken enchilada, the mushroom flatbread, and the plaintains!  The carnitas tacos were a bit dry and the "pork rinds" weren't my favorite. The desserts were tiny yet delicious.

    Drinks:  They were just okay.  He had the cucumber house margarita that was very tasty, and I had the watermelon...but they were both watered down.  I followed up with a sangria that was very basic...I've had better sangria at Public House.  

    Maybe I'm wrong, but I thought Distrito was all about atmosphere?  For a 3 dollar sign establishment, I expect a lot more: excellent service and top-notch drinks.  .  Perhaps they are more laid back in the summer months since school's out, but I will not be coming back any time soon.  We'll have to try again elsewhere for a nice summertime romantic dinner.

    A group of 6 us of had dinner here for my birthday. We got there early for happy hour at the bar and got several orders of the guacamole which was really good. The margaritas were really great, the perfect combination of tang and sweetness.

    The service was great. The bartender returned my aunt's credit card when she left it at the bar. Our waiter was super friendly, and not (outwardly, at least) annoyed or frazzled at having a large group. Between all of us we managed to try a good portion of the menu.

    I think going with at least 4 people and trying a variety of items is the way to go here. I ordered the kale salad to start which was amazing. It came with crispy bits of bacon and deep fried egg. The saltiness from the bacon, mixed with the creamy egg yolk worked to create its own sort of dressing. I don't love kale, but I loved this salad.

    I tried the kobe beef taco which was so amazing. It came with the smallest shredded crispy potato bits I've ever seen and added crunch to the taco. This was probably my favorite thing of the night. Other items sampled: shrimp ceviche, grilled octopus, and the NY strip steak. There wasn't a thing I didn't like.

    The food was delicious, the service was great, and I would go again in a heartbeat. I'm excited to try their new location at the Moorestown Mall.

Specialties

Distrito is the new restaurant concept by Jose Garces, the chef behind the acclaimed Philadelphia restaurants Amada and Tinto. Distrito is a high-energy restaurant inspired by the spirited culture and cuisine of Mexico City. The Distrito menu features Chef Garces' fresh interpretations of classic Mexican fare and beverages include over 65 tequilas and margaritas made with fresh seasonal fruits. A striking decor includes a hot pink and bright green color scheme, a wall constructed from over 600 colorful, campy masks of lucha libre, or "free fight" professional wrestlers, and a booth crafted from a Volkswagen Beetle, a typical Mexico City taxi cab.

History

Established in 2008.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
