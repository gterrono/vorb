        My third visit here (and first visit since the rats episode). The food is still very good. But the reason for the 2 stars is the service. Each time I go here it seems to be worse than the previous visit.

        The staff is pleasant enough - but they are clueless. How else can you describe a breakfast place that seats you, and then it takes 9 minutes for the server to come over to your table. Eventually she came over to take our order, meanwhile all we wanted during those 8 minutes was someone to offer us coffee. It was just after 8am, there were only two other tables (4 people total). How do you not train your staff to offer coffee to each table within the first minute of being seating for breakfast!!  It should be the law.

        So she asks if we're ready to order and we politely tell her we'd like coffee and need more time to order since we were talking and catching up.

        Anyway, the coffee is good - it's above average, but not exceptional. Throughout the 90 minutes we were here, she only offered a coffee refill one time without us asking - otherwise we had to ask 4 times for more coffee.

        I guess I'm spoiled by Hymes Deli - they walk around with coffee all the time, and you never have to ask for a refill or top off. And they have coffee on your table within 10 seconds of being seated, every time like clockwork!

        Another issue is you can't order eggs without bacon or sausage. Yes, you can decline the meat when you order, but they won't reduce your bill. Very lame. I get that they're trying to get a higher average ticket - but they shouldn't make you pay for meat when you don't want it. It's stupid little things like this that business owners should pay attention to - instead of creating good will, they're creating bad will, which results with customers not returning.

        The last issue is the single bathroom in between the two dining rooms. It was clean, however the door lock does not work properly. I had to play with it way too long to make it actually lock. Come on, just get a locksmith and fix it properly.

        I think part of the problem is there's no manager around. The three times I've been here, it seems like it's just the hostess, servers and cooks - I've never seen anyone who looks like they're running the show. Ok, if you don't want to pay for a manager during slower weekday morning hours that's fine, but at least have a set of protocols that employees can follow and held accountable to.

        After the rats and pizza in the window this past summer, I felt they would have no trouble overcoming what would normally put a lot of places out of business, due to the quality food and the huge following they've developed. However, after my last two visits with below average service - well all I can say is if they ignore the service issues, it will eventually hurt their business.

The eggs were perfectly poached. Generously buttered toast soaked up the loose yolks and cheddar. The shrimp were average. My boyfriend insists they were slightly overcooked.

        Duck Confit - 4 out of 5
        The sweet root vegetable hash, mixed with the egg yolks, was delightful. They were too restrained with the duck, though. If an ingredient is listed first in the dish title, it should probably be more prominently included.

        Orange Juice - 2.5 out of 5
        We brought a bottle of prosecco and were offered  a carafe of orange juice. I believe it was fresh squeezed, but the taste was forgettable.

        Service -
        Our waiter was very busy and we did have to ask for water about three times and fetch our own salt, pepper, ketchup, and hot sauce. He was very friendly, had a great sense of humor, and let us enjoy our food at our own pace without rushing us out, a major plus. I was happy with the service overall.

        Overall - 3.5-4 out of 5
        For a casual breakfast joint, I was very pleased with the overall experience. I loved the two entrees, and would return just for the Shrimp & Grits Benedict.
