    Green Eggs Cafe is a welcoming restaurant with a cool vibe and a modern look with a twist. I loved the pots of plants on each table and the carefully designed decor. It is spacious yet inviting and comfy, with plenty of room between the tables to hold a private conversation( I'm a spy:~). We were greeted with a smile by the friendly hostess and  seated promptly. Menus came quickly with our professional server. Loved the variety of choices, the separation ofsweet vs savory dishes(bravo!) and the thorough description. Then came the food: not much of a wait and well presented. I had the Shrimp benedict with grits and perfectly poached eggs. The shrimp was small but well seasoned and so were the grits. So far so good, right? Unfortunately the potatoes were not. In fact those were the blandest potatoes ever( maybe I am spoiled by Sabrina's, no herb or even salt here). Another thing I wasn't impressed by were the onion skins which somehow landed on my plate. Maybe they were trying to cut corners, but those skins were just inedible( think the very thin layer under the outer skin!). I mean, common, onions are not expensive; you are charging me $11 for 4 tiny shrimps, a bit of grits , 2 eggs, a table spoon of peppers and you are gonna put onion SKIN on my plate? Seriously?
    And hey, what's up with the big TV in the dining room, showing the every day... murders on the news? Does anyone want to watch that? If you want to turn tables, the last thing you want to put in a dining room is a TV. No wonder people line up for hours on the weekend( just head to Odd Fellows Cafe nearby). Even with the sound off, to me TV is unnecessary distraction and prevents a good conversation( OK for a sports bar, of course).
    Service was fine. Our order was taken promptly, and condiments arrived immediately, but we did have to wait a long time for coffee refills, which was understandable as they were a bit busy even on that weekday. I have to say the plum and cucumber salad with quinoa was excellent: well seasoned and a great idea altogether. With a bit of work this place could become a hit in my book, so I will definately give it another try.
    All in all, a pleasant experience with a few minuses. Wish them all the best.

    Yet another breakfast option in a city of brunch fanatics. I feel like we're in an episode of Sex and the City and Philadelphia is only now getting to the table. Manhattan has made brunch a serious weekend habit for at least the last decade.

    Green Eggs Cafe is a serious contender in my book for best brunch. It's BYOB so you don't have to worry about over-paying for your mimosa since you Bring Your Own Bubbly. The portions are large enough that you'll be taking home the rest for dinner. If you're looking for something dainty and on your 1200 calorie diet, I advise staying home. The quinoa porridge, however, is a great option for those looking to avoid the damage incurred by eating french toast. There are vegan options as well - vegan french toast, vegan sausage, and more so don't forget to invite your friends with esoteric food preferences.

    I recommend sharing dishes like the creme brulee french toast or red velvet pancakes so that you can try some other things on the menu like the rosemary potatoes which are very flavorful. In this town people complain that portions are either too large or too small. I felt that $11 for a dish that feeds me twice is pretty darn reasonable.

    Of course, it will be crowded and the wait will be long on the weekends. You're not the only one who wanted to skip cereal and milk in their apartment this morning. Count on at least 15 minutes, but up to 30 depending on the size of your party. They were seating outside today but few people accepted an open table because they forgot to read the weather report before heading outside in the chill. Do your homework and hit the bank on the way as Green Eggs is cash only. They do have their own ATM with a fee of 99 cents for those who don't like to plan ahead. Do note that this spot is also popular with families and dog owners.
