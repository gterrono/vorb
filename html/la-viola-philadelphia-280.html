    Went with several friends one night for my girlfriend's birthday.  We had a 7:00 PM reservation...or so we thought.  She called to adjust the reservation number at mid-afternoon as a few friends couldn't come...and they claimed that we had never called to make a  reservation.  They misplaced/didn't record our reservation for a Saturday night (though in their eyes we never made one), but we had communicated the plan with our friends, so we chanced it and hoped for the best.

    Luckily we were able to be seated quickly (group of 7) and were promptly taken care of.  Service was quite good actually, we frequently had our water glasses refilled.  The food was ok.  I had buffalo mozzarella for an appetizer and that was alright, not worth the $9 for a few slabs of fresh mozzarella though.  For dinner, had the pollo pomodoro, which tasted fine except for the fact that it was presented like a pancake-flat piece of chicken with some tomato sauce and a slice...yes, a slice of mozzarella cheese on top.  Again, tasted fine, but looked like it had been run over.

    The place presents itself like a sophisticated Italian restaurant, but it's just not what you get on a plate.  Philly's a big city...look around some if your options are open.

    I have been to La Viola a few times in the past and have always thought of it as a place to get a solid Italian meal in Philly.  It is a little tight inside, but most of the BYOs are the same in that respect.  

    This past weekend I went with a party of 6 to celebrate a friends birthday.  We had to wait outside for about 40 minutes before we were seated, even though we had a reservation.  We noticed that there was a large party of about 20-25 people also waiting--this is a small place so we hoped that we could get get seated and get our order in first.  We were finally seated in a odd place--the front corner where they shoved a round table into a two person square table which was pretty uncomfortable.  We were then served burnt bread which we had to send back.  The rest of the food which included grilled calamari, the special salad, Barramundi and a few of the pastas were decent but nothing special to write home about.  My Barramundi was good but when I got half way through I noticed they had taken the bread which would have been nice to have with the remaining sauce.  I tried to flag down the waiter but by the time he returned it was no longer needed.

    Our real problem with the night was with the handling of the large party--after singing happy birthday at least three times before dinner was served and having the birthday girl stand up to give a speech/toast I approached the hostess to ask if they could have the party quiet down.  In response I was told that they had made the party wait two hours to sit (during which time they had obviously started on their BYO wine) so they could not ask them to do anything.  I am unsure how the restaurant's inability to manage reservations should be my table's problem, but apparently it was to the staff at La Viola.  We did not get so much as an apology for the drunken behavior of the party which really started to ruin our dinner.  La Viola should really take care to respect all of their patrons.

    I wasn't sure if I was at a restaurant or a NASCAR race.  The servers operated with the speed and precision of a pit crew trying to keep the food flying and the tables cleared.  There was nothing Italian about the decor or the waiters.  

    My review is for the basement section of this restaurant that is setup for large groups and charges a set price per person.  I had read that La Viola was a option for price conscious BYOB diners looking for Italian in the city.  A few reviews also advised that it was extremely loud and it was exactly that.

    The menu is a slimmed down version of the main menu and has a few appetizers and main courses that you can choose from.  I had a Caesar Salad and Vodka Penna for my main course.  The salad was good and I thought it was a good sized portion.  The pasta was ok.  The sauce was a bit watery and the Prosciutto was fatty, over cooked and a bit too salty.  This pasta dish is one of my favorites and I didn't think they did a great job with it.

    I had heard that the cost per person was going to be around $25.  This was,  in my opinion still more than the meal was worth but definitely not high for city standards.   We paid $38 per person.  I'm not sure where that figure came from but it was certainly highway robbery for the quality and quantity we were served.  

    I had fun because I was with good friends and downed a few Victory DirtWolf's but I wouldn't return to La Viola unless it was for another birthday party.

    This was my first BYOB experience (which at 26 is pretty pathetic but that's beside the point). I made somewhat last minute reservations (like 3 days before) for 7:30 on a Saturday night for 7 people and was surprised they had openings...I expected to be laughed at and hung up on. I was nervous because we brought a bottle of Grey Goose which I wasn't sure would be ok because a lot of BYOB's are wine and beer only but the only thing they questioned was whether or not I wanted it on ice. I started off with the mushroom appetizer which was amazing and  perfectly sauteed! For my main course I ordered the lobster ravioli (Ravioli La Viola I think?) and it was superb! You can tell everything is handmade. To top it off for dessert my friend's ordered me the chocolate cake and the whole restaurant sang happy birthday to me!! The entire experience was awesome. The service was prompt and professional and overall on point and we didn't feel rushed at all. It was a little loud I guess but nothing extreme...it's just a small restaurant and honestly it was the perfect ambiance for our group....although I probably wouldn't go on an intimate date here....at least not a first one. Just be prepared to wait a little even with a reservation...but it's worth it!!!! 10/10.

    When I graduated from Penn 5 years ago, I took my family to La Viola for some hand-made pasta for my graduation dinner. I have fond memories here and the quality of food is great for the price. Speaking of price, this place is cash only so please keep that in mind when you visit.

    This dish had grilled calamari (steaks not the rings) in lemon juice, garlic, olive oil and tomatoes. I wasn't too fond of this dish but Joe thought it was all right. To be fair, I'm not a huge fan of calamari steaks.

    Joe ordered the home-made tagliatelle pasta sauteed with salmon in a pink cognac sauce. I liked his a lot. The salmon offered a nice variety in texture.

    This was home-made tagliatelle pasta with shrimp and fish in a blush tomato creamy sauce. I preferred my dish over Joe's dish because I loved the blush sauce and the shrimp. The dishes here are simple but delicious. I love treating myself to hand-made pasta!
