    Dandelion saved the day!! On a night that looked bleak (in regards to filling our hungry bellies), a night where every single hostess in center city said to us "that will be a three hour wait", a night where even a four hour wait at village whiskey was starting to look like the answer to our prayers to the foodgods, the overlooked dandelion swooped in with a 30 minute wait and the promise of filling our empty tummies.

    Dandelion went far beyond expectations, and not just because we were so f*cking hungry we'd eat anything. They greeted us with an immediate multibeer sampler so we could forget the last hours of searching for food in the cold. We ordered immediately the classic dishes that jumped out to us on the menu - deviled eggs, caesar salad, and fish and chips.

    THE DEVILED EGGS: I loved. Not your grandmother's potluck deviled eggs that sat out in the sun at the church picnic. These deviled eggs had a kick, and we devoured them quickly.

    THE CAESAR SALAD... was a caesar salad. A very good one.  

    FISH AND CHIPS: Highlight of the evening - not too heavily battered, perfectly crispy on the outside and piping hot and tender on the inside. My boyfriend and I still talk about that fateful night where fish&chips saved us from starvation ;)

    Dandelion was adorable - clearly overlooked by the center city crowd, as it was far less packed than even mediocre restaurants down the street. The decor was homey and inviting, exactly what you'd want from a pseudopub, and next time I hope to sit by the fire chowin down on fish&chips and dark ale with my boo.

    The decor is the thing at The Dandelion, which features a dandy lion (get it?) as its mascot on menus and other stationery. The facade is painted a slick black. Indoors, the feeling is decidedly olde English, but not run down, dirty, or raucous as in the run-of-the-mill publick house.

    A trek up the worn but shined wooden steps leads to a brilliant sunlit mid-level parlor across the hall from a bar room through whose entrance you can see globe shaped lamps and a lit up sign that reads "Man's Best Friend" (booze, one assumes).

    On Easter Sunday, we shared the room only with a couple other mellow families who kept their voices to indoor levels. I was tempted to go for intense looking Scotch Honeysuckle (containing Dewar's, honey, lemon, and rose water), but contained myself given the present company of mild mannered ladies of the bachelorette weekend I'd organized. I now bitterly regret that I refrained from experiencing the drink, and will use this missed opportunity as my excuse to go back someday.

    The standard sweet and savory brunch dishes (eggs, pancakes, etc.) were selected by some of us, but standouts were one girl's aged beef burger with sharp Vermont cheddar, my beer battered fish & chips presented on basically a rustic wooden cutting board with fries sticking out into the air from a silver cup, and the $4.50 half of pink grapefruit with mint, honey, and burnt brown sugar torched onto its surface.

    My dish was basically a fish-filled donut, so I was glad to wash that down with cups of water diligently filled by the vigilant but non-imposing waitstaff. The cups in and of themselves were remarkable; ridged glass and bulbous at the top. This invariably resulted in multiple visits to the bathrooms, which was actually a good thing because I was treated to a honeycomb tiled floor, pull-flush toilets, and several instances of watercolors of ships aside cross-stitched idylls. Really felt at home here and realized again where the England in New England comes from...

    Anyway, I got increasingly anxious as the meal went on because I just really, really wanted to keep my water glass.

    So...I straight up gaffed it; just shoved it into my Coach purse after shelling out about $27 for my meal (well worth it even before the pilfering of the glass). Probably goddamn dumb of me to admit up here on such a public site, but just so you know, staff, I would have paid for it, but didn't want to risk your refusal to sell it. I'm really sorry. Yet, I'm not sorry, because any chance I go back, I'll return to the establishment and pay it back several times over with my patronage. Not that that makes theft okay...but all the same.

    This is a brunch place I'd confidently recommend right off the cuff to just about anyone looking in the Philly area. It's country-like but not coarse. It's pricey but not overpriced given the quality of the food and service. The experience can be alcoholic as a football pub in the UK or as sober as a granny's living room on the day of the Lord. The ambience is acceptable for families but not allowing of Chuck-E-Cheesy chaos. All in all, a win-win for anyone who cannot be disappointed at 10 a.m. to 3 p.m. on a Sunday after a major bender.

Specialties

London's culinary revolution comes to Philadelphia with this eclectic English pub. Cozy rooms and intimate bars create a storied, Old World aura, while updated seasonal takes on British food make up the menu. In keeping with tradition, cask and tap craft beers are always rotating, a proper afternoon tea service has a sweet and savory menu, and classic beef family-style roasts are served on Sundays.

History

Established in 2010.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
