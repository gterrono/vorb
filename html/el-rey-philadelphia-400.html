    Thankfully, I've gotten the chance to go to El Rey twice in the last few weeks, once for lunch and once for dinner, and I'm really thrilled that I did.

    My buddy and I showed up for lunch one day and got the 2 courses for 9.99 special, which was a great deal. The place doesn't look too amazing from the outside, but once you go in, the decor and ambiance is fantastic - after all, the place is attached to the Ranstead Room (another one of my Philly favorites). No idea what I got for lunch, but suffice it to say that every dish had nachos in it (whoops), but they were all still good. The notable highlights were the sauces - the salsa verde and the habanero sauces were especially awesome.

    Dinner was with my family right after graduation, and it was another awesome time. They too were skeptical from the looks on the outside, but once in, it was great. We got the black bean enchiladas (average-ish), but again, the sauce was great, as well as the chile relleno and the zucchini blossom and corn quesadilla. By and far, the quesadilla stole the show. The tortilla is freshly made, the ingredients work amazingly with each other, and you'll be left wishing there was a 3rd (it comes with 2 pieces). Portion sizes were great, and even with 4 people and a good sized meal (we got the chips & salsa again), it was a reasonable 40$.

    My one gripe is that chips and salsa are 4$ here, whilst almost always complementary at most restaurants, but even then, the service and other food more than outweigh that, still leaving El Rey at 5 stars.

    I'm from the West Coast and have to say that this is decent for East Coast Mexican-type food. It's not a place to enjoy authentic Mexican food, but it will remind you enough of Mexican food that it will suffice if you're really in the mood. I'm pretty much in the mood for Mexican food every week and live in the area, so this will be my go-to to fend off the Mexican cravings demon until I can get back back to Cali Cali (or find a better, walking-distance option).

    BEST PART of this place: Happy Hour. $1 tacos is a pretty amazing deal in my opinion. You can leave satisfied for $5 and the tacos are standard yummy. They won't blow your mind, but I don't think that's really fair to ask of $1 tacos. On one visit, the chicken taco had a mole sauce and another visit, it had a lime sauce, so this place will keep you on your cheap-o toes. This is a positive for me since I foresee many $1 taco dinners and look forward to not getting bored.

    ORDER: the brisket tacos (not available for Happy Hour). They are the best thing we've had on the menu after our 3-4 visits so far. The meat is tender and the flavors are on point.

    PRICES: aside from Happy Hour, I would say I'm slightly uncomfortable with what I'm forking over, considering the quality and lack of flavor in some of their other dishes.

    PLEASE improve: the Michelada recipe. I can't get enough of Micheladas. It's the perfect combination for me of sour, spicy, and refreshing. If I see it on a menu, I've never failed to order it. I have to say that the Micheladas here rank close to bottom (since I'm too nice to say absolute bottom). Where's the flavor? It most times tastes like watered-down beer with a few squeezes of unripe lime. Last night, it tasted the best ever, but only because I could actually taste some citrus, but still heavily lacking on that spice!

    AMBIANCE: I guess this is where the flavor has gone. They definitely went to town on the Southwest meets 70's diner theme. There's lots of eye candy on the walls for those lulls in conversation. It's only medium dim, so no need to bust out the iPhone flashlight to read the menu and you can rush your makeup since no one will tell the difference. The music can be loud, so don't bring any long-lost friends here, or anyone you genuinely hope to get to know over conversation.

Specialties

Home-style cuisine from Puebla to Veracruz and a well-worn roadside diner vibe come together to create this Mexican haven. Authentic dishes like ceviches, tortas, enchiladas and sopes, as well as tequilas, mescals and cervezas make for an upbeat meal at this local cantina.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
