    When I first saw this place under construction, I thought to myself, "Oh, look at that cool cat on the wall!" I love cats. I love everything about cats. It was only natural that I go to a place called Blue Cat.  I have been here about 5 times, and I am NEVER disappointed.  As someone who loves to cook, eat, and critique food, I'm going to discuss everything I can about this wonderful BYOB.

    When you go out to eat at a restaurant, you go somewhere based on your mood.  Classy? Go toward Rittenhouse.  Asian? Go toward Chinatown. Food made with love? You go to Blue Cat.  Too often is good food cooked with just technique and no heart; or no soul and only profit in mind. I have been searching for a place that has multiple levels in a single bite.  And that my friends, is why first and foremost, I will discuss ~~Duck with Peanut Mole~~. I have ordered this 3 times.  THREE times.  I can't get away from it, and I get giddy just thinking about assembling all of the components on my fork for the first, fifth, and last bite.  The large duck leg, most likely braised for a long time and then quick seared, falls off the bone and is topped with a beautiful mole sauce that makes you ask "... What's... in this?"  Then there is this herb sauce (cilantro I think) drizzled around the plate that offers brightness throughout your meal. My unusual guilty pleasure about this dish though is the Green Rice.  I have no idea what's in it, or what Chef Shapiro does to it, but I could eat this rice every day, week, month, and year.  The dish is also rounded out with fried plantains (best I have had to date anywhere), and is usually with green beans but I've also had it with spinach. Both are wonderfully prepared.  This is the only type of duck outside of Peking duck that I crave and would do almost anything for.  ::happy sigh:: It's some goooooood duck.

    I've also had:

    BlueCat Guacamole: a wonderful shareable of guac served on romaine lettuce with house-made potatoes, plantain, and taro chips.  Nom nom nom.

    Pati's Papas Rellenas: Texture is crispy on outside and mushy on the inside, but flavorful and balanced.  You get 3 croquettes, so it's a shareable for sure.

    Pan Seared Salmon Filet: A really classic and vibrant tasting salmon dish.  I would consider this a "safe" route for those dining in your party that happen to like seafood but are too afraid to try other items on the menu.

    Fish Taco Dinner: Amazing, flavorful components to build one of the freshest, herbacious tacos ever.  Typically made with cod, but grouper is sometimes an option too.

    Arroz con Pollo y Chorizo: Good, but I like the duck, tacos, and salmon better.

    Carne Asada: Beautifully-cooked steak topped with a mountain of fried onions and a side of nommy potatoes.  This playful dish also falls under the "sure thing" category.

    Breakfast Burrito (during Brunch): Almost the size of a football, it's loaded with eggs, peppers, potatoes, and love.  Accompanied with a watermelon and jicama slaw.  I would recommend this has a shareable item.

    Garlic Soup with Croutons and a Fried Egg: Soulful broth with underlying hints of garlic becomes tastier with every bite.  This is also a good shareable item.  

    Listen to the specials of the day: scallops and soft shell crab are among the faves that I've tried.

    DESSERT!!!!!!!!!!!
    Luli was a pastry chef at Le Bec Fin and makes all of the desserts. They're all listed on a chalkboard at the rear of the restaurant.  I want you to, if at all possible, only order the Orange Blossom Flan for yourself.  This is not a shareable item.  If shared, it would be the worst mistake of your life. Rich flan and bursts of orange makes this the best 5 dollars ever spent.  The flan also cuts really thinly and smoothly with the spoon so if you want to take small bites, it makes it easy to do so!  The Big Banana Cake with Cream Cheese frosting is also a yummy dessert, and in my opinion, shareable.  When I went last week, they offered Honey Vanilla Ice Cream and Cherry Garcia Ice Cream with shortbread cookies in the shape of a cat. Just fabulous.  I wish I could make dessert like Luli.

    I really, really, really like this restaurant.  All of my friends know it, and I'm sure their friends will know it too.  It's BYO so it definitely helps on the overall cost of the meal.  I think their pricing is very fair.  Food is always fresh, and the service is always outstanding. They pay you just enough attention without being invasive.  Ambience is no frills but fun and romantic at the same time.  Favorite BYO in the city, and favorite restaurant to date. I want to say that I'm glad I found this restaurant, but I think the restaurant found Me.

    I really believe that maybe we just  caught this restaurant on a bad day. Several of our friends have raved about it; the images of the food looked amazing; the pastry chef is a Le Bec Fin alum; the place is a BYOB  -- all the pieces were in play  for a fool-proof, amazing dining experience.

    ...but somehow, it didn't happen for us.

    I can say that the service was very nice. The staff was extremely friendly, the place looked superbly clean, the dishes came out quickly and they were presented beautifully. However, some of the food left a good bit to be desired.

    STARTERS

    The romaine salad was ridiculously overdressed, making it super greasy, and a little overboard on saltiness. We felt like it defeated the purpose of ordering a salad, because the 'good for you parts' got so deeply adulterated.

    We also tried the papa rellenas. Not feeling those. Overly dense and severely lacking in seasoning. The sauce that  came with them was not a good complement, either. It was sweet and thick, like it should've gone with a dessert, and it  just tasted  very out of place on this dish, which was meant to be savory, but never hit that mark.

    I did find the fried mussels pretty good. I enjoyed how they were fried crisply, yet still tender inside. The fried jalapenos and the  jicama watermelon salad that garnished them offered really nice flavor complements, too.

    ENTREES

    We had the Peruvian game hen special, and the oxtail stew. The stew was up there on flavor, falling-off-the-bone texture, and stewy richness. Definitely the best dish we had.

    The hen, however,  really fell far below my expectations -- let's face it; when someone says Peruvian, you expect a  HIGHLY seasoned, mouth-smacking, delicious dish, and that just wasn't what I got. I actually found the hen to be downright bland. At least it was well-cooked and not dry, so they get  one point there.  I did enjoy the julienned zucchini and the sweet potatoes that accompanied the dish.

    We added a side of rice and black beans -- which was, in a word, weird. The flavor wasn't earthy, savory or smoky like you'd expect, though the beans LOOKED like they'd been slow cooked and infused with some kind of sausage. Meh. I took two nibbles, tops, then left them alone...

    The side of plantains wasn't bad. Not too greasy, and they were nice and ripe, making them soft and sweet.

    DESSERT

    Tried the banana cake. NOT BAD!! Nice and moist, and the banana flavor was evident, without being overpowering. Good banana frosting, too.

    OVERALL

    The most unfortunate thing is that my husband and I had severely upset stomachs for the rest of the evening, after our meals. I think the let down on flavors and preparations was compounded by this nasty shock to the digestive system, so any likelihood that we'd try Blue Cat again was pretty much killed right there --  having just caught them on a bad day or not.

Specialties

Casual indie BYOB specializing in Latin-inspired dishes from Cuba, Puerto Rico, Mexico, Chile and beyond.  Chef purchases daily from local markets, bringing fresh preparation and ingredients to each dish.  Save room for house-made desserts.  Call ahead to request freshly squeezed juice mixes for margaritas, mojitos, daiquiris, sours and more!

History

Established in 2012.

Husband and wife veterans of the food industry bet the farm and opened shop in a sunny new space on the developing fringe of the neighborhood where they live.

Meet the Business Owner

Guy S. Business Owner

Following his training at the CIA, Chef-owner Guy Shapiro began his career in hotels working with classically trained European chefs.  His love of food and travel led him beyond Philadelphia to positions in New Orleans, Montauk, Vail, Memphis, France, Greece and Russia.  His love for Latin flavors has been cultivated by his many Latin co-workers over the years, as well has his own exceptional palette.
