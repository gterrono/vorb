    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge 2013!, We're not in China anymore...

    I was reluctant to return to Dim Sum Garden after my first visit to their old location, which was somewhat intimidating considering it was located in the dark, dank tunnel on 11th Street opposite rumbling commercial buses and transient folks hustling for change. The original restaurant was cramped, they didn't have many items I was looking for, etc. Now that they've relocated to Race Street and improved some of the underlying issues I had with them before, I'll be returning often!

    As Vy D. has mentioned already, the decor has significantly upgraded to a pleasant, clean setting. It's brighter, bigger, and quite frankly one of the nicest restaurants in Chinatown that I've had the pleasure of eating at. Service is still somewhat hit or miss; we spent a number of times trying to flag our waitress down for ordering, dumpling sauce, and miscellaneous things that were missing from the table.

    The biggest attraction here are by far the xiaolongbao (soup dumplings) which are obviously juicy and delicious. I was also thrilled to see that they added the fried rice cakes to their menu, a home country staple that I'd been missing for some time. That was unfortunately less flavourful than I was looking for, but the texture of the rice cakes was nicely chewy and piping hot when it arrived at the table.

    Very excited to come back with a few friends and try out more things on the menu. For a filling dinner on the cheap, it can't get any better than this.

    Dim sum garden is awesome. I recommend this place to any gringos looking for some tasty Chinese food. This place is known for their Shanghai-style dumplings (xiao long bao) -- essentially it's a dumpling with soup broth inside it.

    Ordering is simple:

    1) Shanghai Steamed Pork & CrabSoup Dumplings
    2) Shanghai Fried Pork Dumplings
    3) Scallion Pancakes
    4) Any noodle dish - Brisket noodle or Shanghai duck noodle are both good options

    All noodles and dumplings are made by hand at the restaurant.

    PS - to eat the dumplings, make a small tear with your teeth, then pour the soup into your spoon. Now you can dunk your dumpling in the soup and enjoy!

    Listed in Philly Favorites

    Dim Sum Garden is by far my favorite dim sum place in Philadelphia! You order off a menu instead of choosing from carts, which can feel less authentic, but the food is much fresher, hotter, and better.

    Firstly, they have a great selection (though I notice that they never have vegetarian steamed buns, which my vegetarian roommate would like to be able to order). While it is hard to find soup dumplings in some other restaurants, Dim Sum Garden always has them--and makes them very well. I have also had excellent fried pork dumplings and scallion pancakes, and their wonton soup is also enjoyable. My only issue is that they are not always consistent. For instance, the first time I came, the fried dumplings were phenomenal--probably the best I've ever had. Then I came back again, ordered 2 orders of fried dumplings, but they were just above average. this time, the soup dumplings were excellent! So even though each dish isn't consistent in and of itself, some dish that you order will blow your mind. The food is reasonably priced, and the food is always piping hot when you get it.

    The service is average, but better than any other dim sum place I've been to, and the waitresses are pretty competent. They usually repeat the order back to you to confirm, and many different waitresses bring your dishes to you. The place has minimal decor, but that's not really important to me for a dim sum place. The tables near the door can be cold with the draft from outside, and the tables near the kitchen can get pretty hot. However, Dim Sum Garden is usually pretty full, so you won't be able to choose where you're sitting. One time I sat by the door and wore my winter jacket the entire meal.
