    Listed in Mac N Cheese iz luv

    Another restaurant from the fantastic proprietors of the Royal Tavern in South Philly and the Cantina Tex-Mex kitchens in South Philly & NoLibs.

    The Khyber Pub Pass had been totally transformed from the total craphole that hosted dozens of wonderful punk shows in the early 90s up till last spring. It's hard to believe the same place that hosted some of my most black-out drunk nites now is home to some of the best Southern-style grub in the city of Philadelphia.

    Like A Full Plate on Liberties Walk, the menu is inspired by Southern fare with an eclectic modern feel. The center of the menu is clearly the extensive BBQ selections and Po' Boy sandwiches. There are also a number of sandwiches, many vegan options, and just a few big plates.

    I had the Sammy Po'Boy, which had ham, beef, Swiss, lettuce, tomoato and a zesty creole sauce. Served with a stack of crunchy, perfect fries, this was one helluva meal. i loved every minute. The roll especially stood out for it crusty, messy deliciousness. You simply dont get rolls like that in Philly.

    Alos had the potato salad and mac n cheese. Loved the potato salad, though the onions were a bit to noticeable. Onions work best when you dont even know they are in the dish- adding flavor, not texture. The mac n cheese was very good, crumbs on top and a mild cheddar flavor. Not overdone, with multiple cheeses or bacon, tuna, tomatoes etcetcetc.... just tasty pasta and cheese. Baked.

    The atmosphere.... While it left something to be desire, it wasnt a problem. Still, its hard to not remember all the debauchery that occurred smack where you now eats tasty gastro-fare. The floors and walls are essentially the same, with a few posters on the wall. And its a little loud, but again, its a bar so take that with a grain of salt.

    Overall, an awesome experience. I look forward to eating there many many times in the future. Old City doesnt have many places with actually serve down-to-earth fare for the city folk; normally, eateries cater to the out-of-town diner. The new Khyber is just what the doctor ordered.

    A little back story- the owner (was) a very cool guy who lived for years above the bar (grew up there really)- he was the catalyst for the bands and atmosphere long ago and was quite the party guy- then he got in trouble- and then got super religious... and then was one of the people behind the 2008 Mumbai attacks- and is now in jail forever.

    Soooo..... Here is why I felt hip while at Khyber Pass Pub which is now a gastropub with awesome beer.

    1. Beer menu is always updated and before they print new ones, they will actually stamp "kicked" on the beers that just blew (I like how I never have to hear "oooh, we're sorry, we're out of that" while it is still on the menu". I enjoyed a Victory Ale (Czech style ale) and a Great Lakes Octoberfest (Marzen- soooo good and smooth) while there and my husband delighted in the cask style ales.     3. They have a great jukebox!

    Listed in Worth Spending Your Cash

    A pub lover's dream.

    What I like most is that the Khyber Pass scores high marks in every category. Creativity, Quality, Service and Cleanliness.

    I have frequented Khyber Pass for over twenty years. I actually used to play here as a musician. While they currently do not offer live music, that should not stop you from going here. Although they do play some great recorded music.

    Lets get the easy stuff out of the way. Service is excellent. A very knowledgable staff that is friendly and attentive. Next is cleanliness. For an old property, they do a nice job of keeping it up. Even the bathrooms are clean on the busiest of nights. Then quality. Hands down fresh. They have a great beer selection (mostly micro-brew with some of the usual big names), and a nice Scotch selection as well. Unusual in the area.

    But the best is the food. The Khyber Pass sits on 3rd Street with dozens of other restaurants next door and close by. And out of all of them, even so called four star restaurants (or so they tell themselves), this place would be my first choice.

    So a few food recommendations. The fried green tomato BLT is awesome. Even more so is the duck, cashew peanut butter and pepper jelly sandwich. Just amazing. I get it every time I go there. Last is the mac and cheese and collard greens. Both authentic homestyle. So just a few standouts, but the whole menu (which is small, a plus if you read my reviews) is superb and well executed.

    If you're in the area, just go here. The Khyber Pass will please any palette with service to match.
