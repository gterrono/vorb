    As a New Yorker visiting Philly for a short trip, my boyfriend and I relied on good ole Yelp to lead us in the right direction for some good eats and it did not disappoint with Sabrina's Cafe.

    Based on the other reviews, looks like we were lucky in not having to wait at all for a table. We were seated right away and ordered July 4th Specials, a Fireworks Mexi Special and a Chicken Sandwich Special. My meal consisted of marinated pulled pork topped with scrambled eggs, a delicious hollandaise sauce, mango salsa with a side of polenta jalapeno fries and marinara dipping sauce. I ate myself into a food coma...My boyfriend's meal was a delicious chicken tender sandwich that we swore almost tasted like a peanut butter and jelly sandwich but not if that makes sense, with a side of sweet potato fried topped with POWDERED SUGAR, and it works!  A light coleslaw on the side, his meal was equally as good as mine.

    If ever in Philly again, definitely making the trek to this incredible find.

    This place is the bee's knees, a phrase I've never quite understood, but it seems fitting here. As a NYC girl, I have a deep-seated appreciation for brunch. As a visitor in Philly this weekend, my brunch-pectations were set low. It's rare to find a city so in tune with that magical weekend hangover-curing meal.     I went with 3 friends, and luckily we all ordered different things. I had a bread pudding dish with asparagus and roasted peppers topped with tomato chutney. I was skeptical of the bread pudding, but the server's insistence on everyone's love of the dish rang true for me as well. A wonderful, savory alternative to a veggie omelet, it was healthy, fresh, and unique. It came with a delightful, perfectly-dressed side salad and a baked orange with cinnamon, the perfect cap to my perfect meal.

    I took bites of my friend's burger - cooked perfectly and served with irresistible parmesan fries as well as another friend's stuffed french toast, which I must say that if you prefer a sweet breakfast, this is the ultimate way to go. It was a mountain of heavenly goodness- sweet cream cheese, bananas, fluffy challah. I'm drooling at my desk just thinking about it.

    sabrina's gets a lot of (well-deserved) hype for its brunch, but we all need to show some love for… sabrina's gets a lot of (well-deserved) hype for its brunch, but we all need to show some love for its awesome dinners, too. the same great food (yes, they now offer the full brunch menu at any time of day) without the insane crowds. bliss.

    although you can easily go full-force glutton and order the ridiculously huge and decadent stuffed french toast, i tend to stick with basic staples here. they do a really good job with simple stuff like tuna salad, fish tacos, a somewhat healthy "cali turkey" sandwich and a solid hummus plate with pita, olives, tomatoes and cukes. also, the dinner specials often include an app called calamari fra diavolo. why do most places bread and fry their calamari? it kicks much more ass when sauteed like it is in this dish.

    in general, the dinner specials are worth a try. most recently, hubs and i tried plantain-encrusted tuna and meatloaf. both were hits. note: sabrina's special meatloaf is not equal to your mom's meatloaf. theirs is stuffed with spinach, ham, salami and provolone, and i'm not convinced meatloaf is an appropriate word for it. a better word might be "yum."

    so, if you want to wait around to experience this kind of deliciousness at brunchtime, more power to you. but i suggest you grab a bottle or a sixer and make it a dinner date instead.     Read more

    An absolute must while in Philadelphia. This small, laid back and unassuming little restaurant has become a staple of regional foodies and won recognition from the local and national press. Their brunch/ breakfast has been the foundation of their popularity. Weekend wait times can exceed 2 hours but it's well worth it. Their stuffed French toast is simply amazing as is their breakfast burritos. Aside of a few items which are always offered, the majority of the items are made up of fresh seasonal ingredients from the Italian market around the corner. Often looking at the menu can be a little frightening as specials are often made up of combinations you would never of considered but somehow they always are incredible.

    The secret, even for many brunch loyalists, is their dinner. Like the breakfast menus, the dinner offerings are all made from what's fresh and seasonal. Some mainstays like their mussels, hummus, salads and meatloaf are  great. it is a BYOB so bring a couple of bottles and enjoy the evening with friends. Deserts are fantastic as well and consist of homemade stars like bread pudding, chocolate layer cake, etc.

    This restaurant is the best comfort food in the city. It has become my friends and family's "goto" place for birthdays, celebrations and just a night out.

While I always like hearing what are the servers favorites, I was already eying the Mediterranean Wrap and the Crispy Tofu.

    As we were looking over the menu, one of my friends got all excited about the crispy tofu meal, this was my chance! I quickly asked her if she would be interested in splitting the Mediterranean Wrap and the Crispy Tofu so we could each try two different meals. She was on board right away! Score!!! I was going to get to try the two meals I wanted, it's the little things in life that make me so happy.

    The Mediterranean Wrap: Hummus, eggplant, roasted red pepper, cucumbers, crumbled feta, olives, and falafel wrapped in a whole wheat wrap. This sandwich was out of this world, and I could use one as I'm writing this. I have a very similar sandwich that I always get when I go home that's my go to and thought that was the best. Well, Sabrina just took the Crown, after a few bites of this I almost wish I didn't end up splitting my meal!

    The Crispy Tofu: Served over soba noodle salad tossed with a peanut butter sauce, mango salsa and avocado. The tofu wasn't deep fried which was nice. The soba noodles took on the peanut flavor but I personally felt it could have been a little more flavorful. The mango's added a nice sweetness to the peanut sauce kicking up the flavor just a notch. I found myself more focused on the sandwich than one this dish.

    My other friend ordered The Islander: Grilled Ahi Tuna on Brichoe topped with caramelized onions, lettuce and tomatoes, finished with a wasbi mayo. She had the tuna medium rare and also had them add avocado. She opted for the Parmesan cheese fries which were quite tasty. The presentation of this dish was outstanding and it was a rather large sandwich.

    My other friend ordered the Cali Turkey: sliced roasted turkey with sprouts, avocado, and bacon served on a multigrain with lettuce, tomatoes, and finished with red pepper mayo. She opted for the Brioche roll instead as well as the Parm fries. Again, the presentation was great and what a nice sized sandwich.

    For the table we just had to get an order of the jalapeno cheese polenta fries. WOW! Talk about deliciousness. You get 5 over sized deep fried fries served with marina sauce. It's good to have as a sampler at the table as I don't think one would be able to eat these on their own ... but then again, I shouldn't speak so soon!!

    Towards the end of our meal, Robert, the owner came over to check on us. I saw him walking about prior and thought he might be the owner and of course was eager to speak with him. He asked us how we were enjoying ourselves and how the food was. I immediately jumped right in and told him that we were from NYC and in Philly for work and chose to come to Sabrina's for lunch. He was very pleased but we were more so! I told him how happy that we were to be able to try Sabrina's and that if I ever came back, I'll be back for brunch for sure.

    The service was good, we did ask for a few things which she did forget but it was fine. The waitresses were super trendy and I almost felt like I was in Williamsburg. For the four of us, the bill was $55 which I thought for the portions was great. All the meals were delish, made with love and they have you more than enough! Had we not been traveling all day I could have wrapped up that soba noodle salad to go. This place is also BYOB so what's better than that?! Huh not much!
