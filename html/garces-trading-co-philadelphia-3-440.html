    We fell into this place on a steamy Thursday night after a long drive down to Philly. I don't watch food channel and wasn't aware it was a "celebrity" place but it looked interesting. In fact had I known Sr. Garces was an Iron-whatever I'd probably have skipped the place because I haven't had great experiences in TV chef's places (I'm talking to you, Babbo, Craft, etc). However I feel like we hit the jackpot because our experiences here couldn't have been better. So good that after dinner we immediately made brunch reservations for that Saturday.

    It has a European feel, the kind of cafe you see in an upscale market over there. Lively but not frantic, pretty but not tacky or over-decorated. There's a nice selection of desserts, meats, cheeses, packaged products, sodas, and wine surrounding a busy but comfortable dining area. (Someone compared it to Eataly in that way, though I find that place as bloated and annoying as its owner.) It got busy pretty quick after we sat down but our server was patient as we asked lots of questions and, because we were hungry and stressed, had a hard time making up our minds.

    I know this is Yelp and a lot of reviewers are nuts and/or have no idea what they're talking about, but I was really surprised by the negative reviews of the staff. In each meal the servers were among the best I've encountered. Both were efficient, patient, knowledgeable, and extremely friendly without being cloying or fake. The kinds of servers where when you ask for a recommendation, they give you one, and don't patronize when you don't know a foreign name or strange ingredient. The manager came by twice to check up on us and seemed genuinely interested in our experience.

    The food was truly excellent, simple preparations of top-quality, super-fresh ingredients. I ordered a lyonnaise salad and we split a pizza and both were fantastic. We also shared a charcuterie plate and cauliflower appetizer. It seemed like smallish portions but somehow we were full when we left on just those four things. The pizzas seem small for the prices but again, the ingredients were so popping fresh and great that it was like dissing the Mona Lisa for being only 22 inches high. (Okay, I'm laying it on thick here, but I'm sick of all the places - and reviewers - who seem to think quantity outweighs quality!) Even the bread and olive oil were special, and I went home with a bottle of the stuff. We also went home with two chocolate bouchons from the bakery that were like the best brownies you've ever had. Again, these were small but three bites and we were knocked out.

    For our brunch two days later we had steak and eggs and a shurred egg special with the homemade mozzarella and potato rösti (latke where I come from) as sides. My bloody mary was the best I've had, no joke, and again, everything else was terrific, downright memorable.

    The same wonderful manager from Thursday night finished the meal off with a gratis plate of assorted macarons. (Maybe we looked more famous or important than we are, which is NOT???) I feel guilty admitting I don't particularly like macarons in general, but these were flavorful and interesting, too sweet for me but the flavors varied enough to overcome it. A nice gesture among many, such as our server holding my coffee til the end of the meal rather than hurrying to deliver all our stuff at once.

    I'd recommend going with a group so you can sample lots of things. I was frustrated was that I could only eat so much in two visits!

    On a random Sunday afternoon my boyfriend and I were wandering around Center City, deciding where we should grub for dinner.  I suggested Garces Trading Company since I have been wanting to go there for so long, plus I have been extremely pleased with all other Garces Group restaurant experiences.  There were so many enticing options on the menu, the waiter sold us on the Chef's Tasting Menu.  Once we settled on that, we decided GO BIG or GO HOME and went all out on the $55 per person version of the tasting menu.  Our waiter was very helpful in providing recommendations and also asked if there was anything on the menu that we REALLY wanted to try and see during our 4 course tasting.  

    The first course was a cheese and meat board.  Three cheeses, three dipping sauces, thinly sliced prosciutto and a nice size plate of fresh olives.  I wish I could remember which three cheeses we had, but the three accompaniments went PERFECTLY with each cheese they were meant for...Truffle Lavender Honey, Cherry Fig Marmalade, Roasted Garlic Dulce de Leche.  I don't like olives, but my boyfriend loved them and had plenty to take home to eat the next day.

    The second course was oysters, and the Funghi pizza that I had requested!. The oysters were dressed with a super light, flavorful sauce, a delicious dollop of creme fraiche and pea shoots.  I am relatively new to the oyster scene, but these were definitely the best I have had thus far!  The dressings were light but not overwhelming.  The mushroom pizza had a generous amount of wild mushrooms and just the right amount of cheese that it did not take center stage over the mushrooms.

    The third course was probably my least favorite, though still delicious.  We were served a pork chop entree, that I do not see on the menu, it must have been a special.  Though pork chops are not something I would normally order, they were cooked wonderfully and stayed tender and juicy.  The other entree was a pasta dish with Bolognese sauce.  I don't think it was the same a Bolognese on the menu though; it was good, but nothing extraordinary.  

    For dessert, we were served  the Tiramisu (Hazelnut-Espresso Sponge Cake, Mascarpone Cream, Chocolate Shell).  I am not a huge Tiramisu person, but this one was really good, and light!  

    After finishing each course, there was a slight lull, before we were presented with the next course.  I really like this, especially during a multi-course meal.  It was a relaxing experience and kept us from feeling rushed or too full.  If you plan on doing the tasting menu, give yourself 2 hours to enjoy and savor!

    I definitely plan on returning to Garces Trading Company; after I try all of JG's other establishments.  Not only was everything delicious and well presented, but Jose Garces is also a huge advocate of the farm to table movement; he even has his own farm, Luna in Bucks County where much of the produce for his restaurants are grown. 6 stars from me for locally grown, organic produce!  

    Just thinking about that Truffle Lavender Honey is making me want to stop in the cafe to grab a full jar of it to take home!

    Tiramisu: a cute little log version of the classic dessert, a roll of ladyfinger cake covered in a chocolate ganache shell, filled with sweet fluffy mascarpone cream, delish, my only minor gripe was that I could feel the espresso grinds, and it was a bit too much for my taste because of that
    Spring Verrine: my fiance was a bit hesitant, but the flavors really work together to create a delicious, fresh on the palate, light dessert. It also had a great combination of textures, with the sweet-tart blueberry jam, creamy light basil mousse, and sweet creamy cheesecake crunchy crumble on top with a white chocolate decorative disc. In one word: YUM!

    I will be back to order one (or more - very likely more) of those larger cakes.

Specialties

Garces Trading Company is a full-service bar and restaurant focused on Iron Chef Jose Garces' own favorite dishes from his travels to cafes throughout Western Europe, with a focus on Italy, Spain and France.  Our thoughtful menu of cafe staples is served throughout the day, and our bar offers a wealth of cocktails, wines and beers that pair beautifully with our menu.  Our on-site PLCB Wine Boutique, the first of its kind, makes a great resource either before sitting down to dinner (corkage fees apply) or to complement one of our decadent gift baskets, available in our newly redesigned retail section.

History

Established in 2010.

Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a refined, Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, a contemporary American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago, IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale, AZ and Palm Springs, CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the landmark Revel Resort. Today, in addition to our much-lauded restaurants around the country, Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
