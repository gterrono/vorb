    I have lived in Philadelphia for 5 years and up until this point have never made it here.

    Recently, my wife and I decided to give it a try. I must say, the experience was pleasant.

    First off, the atmosphere is really unique. The restaurant it's self is with an old four masted sailing ship, which dates back to the early 20th century. There is tons of history attached to the ship, which really makes you feel apart of something.

    When, we were seated, we were greeted by a polite, well versed server.  Started off with a glass of Chardonnay. Moshulu had a great selection of wines and various spirits. I must say, if your a scotch drinker, they had a fine list.

    My wife and I then moved on to an Asian style calamari appetizer.   At first I was a tad reluctant but wow.. Probably one of the best calamari dishes I have ever had, a must try in my book. From there, we went on to the lobster bisque, which was very tasty. Lastly, for the course we tried the diver scallops and the crab cakes. Neither my wife or I were disappointed. Servings were just the right size and the quality of the seafood was wonderful. Large scallops and nice lumps of crab.

    It's not a cheap place but its to be expected for a four diamond rated establishment.

    I must say for the amount of food and the quality of the dishes, it was worth the price of admission.

    We started with the spicy calamari, which was cooked to perfection and had the right amount of 'kick'.  Each couple at the table got soup.   My girlfriend and I shared their famous lobster bisque, which was as good as advertised.  The other couple shared the soup de jour (potato soup) which they said was excellent.  We complimentary bread at the table with 'honey butter'.  This was superb and wanted more!  We also received some beef tartar, which I didn't try, but my table loved.

    For the main course I tried the Amish Chicken Breast.  It was cooked to perfection and had great flavors overall.  The truffle gnocchi were a nice compliment.  I tried my girlfriend's crabcake and it was excellent with no filler at all.  Very tasty.  The rest of the table had the lamb chops and duck breast.  Everyone really enjoyed their entrees.

    For dessert we all shared the Coffee Chocolate cheesecake w/baileys ice cream.  As someone who isn't a fan of cheesecake, this was excellent.  The ice cream was heavenly.

Specialties

The Moshulu, the world's oldest and largest four-masted Tall Ship offers unsurpassed views for intimate to extraordinary dining aboard this Philadelphia landmark. .  Enjoy delectable, award-winning contemporary American cuisine by Chef Anthony Bonett using the freshest, local ingredients available from the land and from the sea paired with an extensive wine list hand selected by Sommelier, Dusan Vranic.

History

Established in 2003.

The Moshulu was purchased in 1968 in Naantale, Finland for restoration and conversion into a restaurant. In the fall of 1974, she was towed to Philadelphia and opened as a restaurant on Philadelphia's Penn's Landing in 1975 until she was damaged by fire. In 1994, the Moshulu was purchased by HMS Ventures, Inc and restored in the style of a turn-of-the-century luxury liner. The Moshulu was re-christened by Philadelphia Mayor Ed Rendell on July 24, 1996. In 2002, well known restauranteur, Martin Grims, purchased the ship as she is today.
