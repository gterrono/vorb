    Another reviewer mentioned that she'd heard that "the baklava isn't to be missed." Madam, you have no idea. Pity that you don't like baklava, because theirs is really rather good. From one baklava-hater to another, give it a try?
    Though not being vegan or vegetarian by any stretch of the imagination, I tend to prefer to frequent places with somewhat of a vegetarian bent. Mazza fills that preference pretty well, I'd say.
    The classic combo, which comes with humus, roasted eggplant and tabbouleh is sizeable enough to feed three. The only problem is finding someone who's willing to share. It's so fantastically good that when I order it, I can't justify giving anyone else a taste, I'm afraid.
    Mazza really shines on it's platters, though. Appetizers offer a glimmer. Sandwiches a fluorescent shimmer. But the platters, man, really glow bright on this menu.
    The chicken curry is a good introductory curry for curry virgins, but veterans will find it not up to task. Let's leave curry for curry-leaning cuisines and talk about what Lebanon does best: kafta.
    The lambiness and beefiness of Mazza's sausagelike kafta platter is only accentuated by the nice-sized serving of hummus provided with it. Screw authenticity and the frowns from purists, but the kafta is omgwtfgood with a bit of hummus on it. Fork in kafta. Cut a bite off with the knife. Spread some hummus on that bit of kafta with that same knife. Put in mouth. Repeat. A good idea is to guard your hummus with your knife hand while feeding yourself with your fork hand to keep away friends turned would-be thieves.
    The half chicken is a garlicy wonder. The potato wedges served with it came out soggy on one occasion, but have been as crisp as celery most other times. Even if soggy, the chicken definitely makes up for it.

    I'm always hesistant to give 5 stars... mostly because it's hard to compare restaurants to each other when they offer such different things at different prices.  Mazza receives 5 stars when I compare it to the other take out options in the area.

    I've driven past Mazza a million times-- it's right on my path between work and home.  I love mediterranean food and had been meaning to try it.  So one Friday night when the husband was out, I decided to order from Mazza and pick up on my way home.

    I ordered a chicken gyro and an order of the three dip sampler (I have no idea what they call this, but its 2 pitas, with hummus, tabbuleh, and baba ghanoush, sp?).  I only had to wait a couple of minutes once I arrived.  Keep in mind the word TAKE OUT-- there is one small table, but its definitely NOT an eat in joint.

    When I got my meal home, I dug right in.  And I was very very pleasantly surprised.  The food was REALLY good.  The dips were all very fresh and obviously homemade.  They had some nice garnishes (chick peas, cucumbers, olives).  The gyro was very good as well-- it was different than I expected though.  The chicken was finely chopped and the sandwich was more long and thin than I expected.  But it was super tasty.

    The total cost of my meal was about $15.  I ate the entire gyro and a few bites of dip.  The rest of the dips made a good snack over the next few days.  I would suggest asking for more pitas (or having some pitas, veggies, crackers etc at home to eat the dips with)

    Overall, I'm really glad to find a new take out spot to add to our rotation!

    I love this place. Not only is it a gem in the somewhat of a food wasteland in South Philly, but the food stands up to the best Greek/Middle Eastern places in the rest of the city. I usually get the Kafta platter, but the Chicken Kabob platter is also excellent. My wife loves the Spanikopita platter (a huge piece). The platters come with either a cucumber tomato salad, or potato wedges, or a choice of sides. The sides are killer too. Everything is FRESH. The dips (hummus, Baba Ganoush) are top notch. As good, if not better than Bitar's, although I love Bitar's too. For the kids we get the 1/2 chicken platter with potato wedges and hummus. The potato wedges come with "Dijonnaise", which is a homemade concoction that is truly addictive. Delivery is slow and the baklava is expensive and mushy (but big), but other than that, this is one of our favorite takeout places.
