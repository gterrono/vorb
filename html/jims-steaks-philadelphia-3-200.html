    My friend and I took a random trip from Rockville, MD all the way up to Philadelphia to do things: 1) eat cheesesteak and 2) visit Condom Kingdom.  Both were epic but I'll focus on the former for now.

    I was brought here as an unknowing participant.  My eyes just blew wide open when I saw this massive line outside the place.  The line snaked around three times the inside of the building, out the front door, along the front of the store, and then wrapping around half way to the back of the shop. Not realizing what greatness was stored in this tiny shack of a building, I was telling my friend "This better be the most mind blowing cheese steak ever!"  And mind blowing it was.  

    The bread was so light and fluffy, not dense like other cheese steaks I've had.  The cheese steak of it was also great.  I didn't have it "with whiz", but I did get it with provolone and banana peppers.  So yummy!  

    I really think you gotta have a strong stomach to order the cheesesteak with whiz.  For the entire hour I was in line, I was watching the cook slap on globs of cheeze whiz from this massive can and he went through two cans of that stuff by the time I came around.  The next time around, I will man up and try it with whiz.

    In short, I love this place.  There's a ton of history here and it's a good eat.  The walls are lined with a ton of autographs so there's something to do when you are waiting in line.  I'm still not sure whether this place takes credit cards or not so just bring cash just in case.  If I happen to have another 5 hours to kill, I have no issues driving up, standing in line, eat, and drive home.  It's just that good.

    OK, let's be real here guys, this is not Pat's, let's just get that out of the way. However, Jim's definitely has the #2 cheese steaks in the city.
    BUT...the hidden gem of Jim's is their hoagies.
    As a non beef eater, I was thrilled to find that not only does Jim's have hoagies, but turns out, according to my beef eating boyfriend, they have fantastic cheese steaks too! His is always ordered whiz witout. Meat is never dry or too greasy. Whiz is always just the right amount.
    Now for the hoagies, first of all, GREAT option for the non meat or non beef eaters, as opposed to Pat's or Geno's with no non meat options. Normally get the turkey with American. These hoagies are huge. These guys do not skimp. If you want a great, loaded hoagie, on a Delicious roll, Jim's is definitely the hidden hoagie gem.
    As for service, I agree with other notes, staff is almost too friendly at times!
    FYI to out of towners, like any other good cheese steak joint, line starts wrapping at peak times, so be prepared to wait (unless of course you want a hoagie, then you can skip the whole line!)

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
