    Listed in LevelUp Spots

    I'm on a mission to visit all of the businesses that use LevelUp, a pay-by-cell-phone app. This is stop #2 Circles Thai Restaurant @ 15th & Tasker

    August was a great month. So great that when I looked at my bank account, it was a little redder than I wanted. Fortunately, I had bought a coupon from Living Social for $15 to get $30 worth of food from Circles. I've ordered take-out from Circles for a while now and I ate at their restaurant once last month on a girls night out. The food has always been scrumptious. I decided to take a friend to indulge in the coupon glory.

    My coupon said to call a number which turned out to be the actual restaurant which was perfect so I could make sure they could accommodate us a half hour later. We arrived famished and immediately ordered some iced Thai coffees and an appetizer of Crab Rangoon. The Crab Rangoon showed up shortly afterwards and we dug in.

    And now for a few points that fall under my category of mysteries:
        1.Is there something in these Crab marvels that strike my taste buds more magnificently than other people or does everyone feel this way? I could eat them for the rest of my life and be content.
        2.Do they need to be deep fried in that shell? I could eat the inside and do without the fried casing and be even contenter.

    Best Crab Rangoon I've ever had though.

    I ordered their Curry Duck, my friend their Massaman Curry. The food showed up in under 10 minutes and a hush fell over the crowd. We were eating eating. It was tragically delicious and we managed to almost completely clean our plates (minus some stray carrots and rice).

    The iced Thai coffees hadn't shown up at this point, but we were satiated and didn't bother to remind our server. He did, however, mention dessert and my friend and I looked at each other and decide to continue the evening of indulgence. We split the sticky rice which is similar to tapioca on a bed of sweet rice that is, as advertised, sticky. It was pretty yum, though I'm not a huge dessert person.

    When it came time to pay, we handed over the $30 coupon and the server promptly returned with a check for $10.89. I gave him my phone to pay the balance with LevelUp and there was a long hesitation. It turns out I was the first person to use the LevelUp service with them. Now 100% of the places that I've been haven't used LevelUp yet. He took the phone and was back within minutes. I then received the confirmation text telling me that my payment had cleared. ($15 coupon + $10.89 - $3 LevelUp Discount =  $22.89 = not too shabby).

    Great food, warm atmosphere, and seemless LevelUp transaction.

    Rating:
    Circle's Restaurant: 5 Gi-Dee-Ups
    LevelUp Experience: 5 Gi-Dee-Ups

    I really, really wanted to hate this place because everybody loves it so much. I haven't eaten Thai in a really long time because there are so many less-traveled Asian options in the area. However, the other night we finally decided to go give Circles a try - the $10 for $20 yelp deal helped. There is a little takeout store on one side of the street and a very nice dining room on the other side.

    DELICIOUS. Truly a great meal. We got Thai rolls (similar to Vietnamese spring rolls), tom kha (chicken coconut soup), shrimp pad thai, and green curry with chicken. Everything was fantastic. The soup was a great sweet/sour combination, spring rolls were crispy and the ingredients tasted fresh. The shrimp were perfectly cooked on the pad thai and the green curry was creamy and spicy.

    They give you spice options for the main dishes - mild, medium, hot, or thai hot. I love spicy and can tolerate alot but the just plain hot on the pad thai was plenty. Anyone even a tiny bit wary of spicy, I would recommend go medium or mild.

    Price is average - around $33 (before yelp deal) for everything we got. BYOB - great with beer. Service is great. Portions are enormous, and everything reheats beautifully.

    Better than any other Thai food I've had in twenty years, including that which is prepared and served by Thai members of our local Buddhist Temple. Whenever there is a festival or holiday pertinent to Buddhism, the  temple has a celebration that includes members preparing authentic foods to sell in support of the temple. The majority of the temple congregation is from Thailand, as are the majority of diners at the celebrations. For Circles to pull off better food is no small feat, but they did it.

    I was back east for an extended visit and received an offer from Groupon in my e-mail, so I bought a gift certificate. I chose my nephew and his Japan born wife as my dinner guests, as we met up in Philly. Upon approaching the restaurant in our vehicle, we passed the dining room on the southeast corner, completely missing it but seeing the awning for the take-out counter on the opposite corner. My nephew lives in NYC and considers himself to be urban, but upon first glance he said to me "how much did you pay for that certificate? Can I pay you for us NOT to eat there?"  The neighborhood is South Philly, reminiscent of Rocky's digs in the first film, but it is undergoing revival and attracting young hipsters. Where else would you see a couple swinging a bottle of chardonnay while walking home in an area reminiscent of South Central?

    So, I drove around the block once, looking for parking and sizing up the area, and only on the second go around did we spy the dining room.  Something about the sight of table linens and an expresso machine calmed my nephews fears, so we parked and went in to be seated.

    Everything about the food was fabulous and it wasn't the typical Thai restaurant offerings, presentation, or preparation. We shared the tofu corn appetizer and loved it. Of course we had the Thai iced tea- call us prosaic, but there's a reason it's so popular. Entrees were the duck curry, pumpkin curry with chicken, and the drunken noodles with beef.
    The presentation of the duck curry was a feast for the eyes. The other two entrees were not quite so adorned but still appetizing upon service.
    We sampled each other's dishes and all three of us agreed that the food was exceptional.

    For dessert I wanted something more exotic, but relented to the server's opinion and my nephew's desire for the Thai doughnuts. I was expecting insipid fried dough, but they were surprisingly light, tasty, and served with a dipping sauce based in coconut cream. We ate them all, and a single serving was enough for three.

    Forgive me if the focus of this review focused more on the neighborhood than the food, but the volumes of previous reviews already had the food covered. I want to assure the white suburbanites from Delaware and other nearby counties that Circles is worth the trip and the neighborhood is stable and safe, not volatile and dangerous.
    Get out of your comfort zone if you want to eat well, you won't regret it.

    Different people have different definitions of what it means to be "spicy." Sometimes you can order "mind-blasting gastrocide death wings" that hold less heat than a loveless marriage. Because of subjectivity like this, I have my own standard of preferred spice, that which qualifies as "embarrassing to eat in public." This doesn't mean just a little sweating or some running of the nose, this means that, even alone, you'll feel a little self-conscious about your own physical response.

    That said, here's what you need to know about Circles:
    - Their Thai hot option is SPICY. I'm talking head pounding, tongue numbing goodness.
    - As a result, it is also incredibly salty. This is a turn off.
    - The red curry is superior to the pumpkin curry.
    - The tom kha soup is ridiculously sour.
    - There are TWO locations, right across the street from each other. One is the actual restaurant. The other run down shack is for carry out. Regardless of where you are sitting, all the food comes from the latter.
    - THEY DELIVER!!!...but depending on where you live, delivery fees can be high and it can take a while.

    Circles does not serve the most authentic Thai food (though really, who does), but it is certainly a place I crave from time to time when I feel like crying while eating.
