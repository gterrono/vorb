        I was very happy that Cafe La Maude lived up the reviews on yelp! This was the best brunch I have had in a long time.

        The restaurant is really very charming. There is a counter and a table out front and another seating area in the back. The place is decorated in a very nice French bistro decor that is clean, relaxing and inviting. The staff was super polite and friendly, especially Gabriele. He checked on us often and was great. We went on a Sunday around 1:00 PM and it was quite busy. We waited about 20 minutes for a table for two. They do turn the tables over quickly without being pushy.

        We had the Quiche which had herbed-carmelized onions, goat and ricotta cheese, sweet peppers, served with a side of salade russe, topped with filet steak and potato croquet, The cheese was really good and it had a little spice to it. The steak filet was awesome. This was killer. We also had the Berrylicious Pain Perdu which is a french toast stuffed brioche with mascarpone Cheese, crème Brule bananas, topped with warm berries and passion fruit sauce. This was also as fantastic as it sounds.

        Most things run in the $10-16 price range but the portions are very adequate. They serve la Colombe coffee and fresh squeezed orange juice.  They do not take reservations. They do take credit cards. There is a counter up front for coffee, pastries, and assorted macaroons. I can't wait to come back and try some other things!  Cafe La Maude  was worth the wait and lived up to the hype.

        This place has been at the top of my "to-brunch" list for quite some time, and I am so happy to have finally tried it. The menu is very unique to other places in the area, and the wait time is shorter too. For a party of 3 at prime brunch-time, we waited about 20 minutes, most of which was spent inside the flower shop next door.

        The place is very small, but decorated well. I liked the honest openness of the host. He seemed physically pained every time a new party arrived and he had to tell them that there were no tables available and they had to wait. After reading many reviews, I decided on a latte (delicious!) and I split both the foul moudamas and the chorizo benny. My boyfriend's mom got the quiche, which had great presentation. My main complaint is that a few of the flavors were weird. The veggies that come with the quiche are reminiscent of those frozen veggie variety packs at the grocery store, which isn't that appetizing. The chorizo was also not what I was expecting, but still good. The portions are very hearty, which is great.

        For the simple ingredients (hummus, beans, bread, eggs...), the prices are a bit steep. There are tons of good brunches around that cost a bit less, so this place probably won't become a regular spot. The mini hot sauce on the table was cute, but ended up just being sriracha, and you have to ask for ketchup, which is then given to you in little disposable packets. I suppose that makes it more European, but it also creates more waste...

        We left with a few macarons to-go, which was a great treat for later in the afternoon. They were way too beautiful to pass up! They didn't taste super fresh, but I will chalk that up to them sitting in the car for a few hours. Overall, it's definitely a spot to try if you love brunch.

      Specialties

      With the authentic look and feel of a true European café, this hidden gem in Philadelphia's Northern Liberties neighborhood serves up some of the most delicious food, coffee and pastries anywhere in the city. Café La Maude's specially brewed La Colombe coffee is known for its strong taste that's never bitter.  The French-Lebanese menu combines the exotic flavors of the Middle East with a distinctly Continental French accent and includes traditional favorites such as Brioche French Toast, Nicoise Salad, Taboule and Baklava along with more unique items such as the Grilled Haloumi, a baguette sandwich with olives, roasted tomatoes, mesculun greens and a basil-arugula aioli.  House specialties include the Café La Maude Salad of  arugula topped with warm seasoned chickpeas and fava beans.  Another popular dish is the traditional lebanese recipe of foul moudamas of marinated fava beans served with fresh tomatoes, chopped parsley and pita served with scrambled eggs. For meat lovers the cafe's marinated chicken sandwich and kebbe is not to be missed. Whether it's breakfast, lunch or brunch that brings you to Café La Maude, it will feel like Paris and taste like Beirut.
