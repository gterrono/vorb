        The Jones is totally rad, dude! There was a really sweet vibe to that place right upon entering the space. It's fun, spacious and quirky--from its blown-up 60s-theme images on the walls to the national landmark and state night lights at the tables. There was a lot of attention to detail paid to brand of The Jones and that is something I definitely respect and notice. You felt comfortable but not comfortable enough where you would show up in your PJs and not run a comb through your hair. The menu is simple, yet you can find something for any craving, I'm sure. I went with the Grilled Chicken sandwich with roasted tomatoes, arugula, aioli and a side of french fries. The food tasted good, but nothing special or out of the ordinary. I do have to mention that one of the people I was eating lunch with got a salad and it was HUGE. The serving size of that bowl of salad could feed a small army. The service was pretty good, our waitress had a lot of good tips/suggestions. This place is a solid 3 stars, but I gave it a 4 just because of the extra mile they went with all the design aspects of the brand.

        The Pure Tonics Launch Party brought me to Jones last night. Pure Tonics provided samples of their new product line in terms of cocktails and juices so I will be reviewing Jones from a catering/event standpoint.

        The event space included the area around the bar and the upstairs dining room. Jones provided a hummus plate and passed buf-faux-lo tofu wing bites. So let me start by saying, I'm not a huge tofu fan. I don't hate it but I'd never just order it. The tofu bites seriously tasted like chicken! I attribute the taste mostly to the sauce and the rest to the deep frying. The sauce had a slight kick and epitomized what a buffalo sauce should taste like. I'm not sure what I wouldn't eat if it was covered in that sauce. I'd go as far as saying I'd order it on my next trip to Jones! The hummus plate was pretty good too. It included hummus (duh), pita, chopped roasted red peppers, sliced cucumber and olives. Initially I thought the hummus just so so- possibly a little too garlicky. However, the more I ate, the more I liked it. My one complaint was that none of the hummus trays had any utensils. Any scooping had to be done using a cucumber and pita.

        Pure Tonics provided the cocktails and the bar was staffed by both Jones and Pure Tonics. The restaurant was open to the public so the Jones staff had the double duty of preparing drinks for the party and preparing drinks for the dining customers. All and all I thought that the bar line moved pretty quickly and it didn't seem like either the party goers or customers were neglected at any point.

        I also wanted to point out how fun the decor is. Particularly the different vintage-looking photos of Philadelphia stationed at each table.

        At no point did I feel like it was too overcrowded. I don't know the exact number of attendees at this party- maybe I'd guesstimate 40-50 at any given point. That would probably be the max I'd recommend for any event like this. The space itself and the service was pretty good for the event.

        I've been promising my husband a dinner trip to Jones for a while so I know I'll be back in the near future!

        I've only been here once so far and it was for my sister's birthday. It was our first stop of the night and she'd heard good things about it.

        The most important thing of all is that the food is good.   We had a few different appetizers and though some were pretty small when being shared between multiple people, they were all definitely good.  I'd say that the best was the Mac N' Cheese with the Calamari being good but not especially memorable.

        For my meal I had the fried chicken basket.  I'd give it about an 8.5/10.   It was moist and crispy on the outside, though perhaps in need of more seasoning.  But overall the food was definitely the best part of this place.

        The reason it didn't get a four star review was mostly because of the wait.  It was a very long wait.  I really don't think we got our food within 45 minutes.   If I found out it was an hour I wouldn't at all be surprised.   The was a wait to be seated, a long wait for food, and the place was overflowing with small loud children.

        I wasn't expecting that sort of atmosphere, but if you are then I suppose it wouldn't be as much of an issue.  Thankfully, as the sun started to set the children started to either calm down or disappear.  

        The actual decoration and atmosphere was fine, but nothing special.   I'd say try this place for a good "comfort food" meal in Philly at a moderately high, but not outrageous price.  I wouldn't be surprised if you could find better comfort food in the city, but if you want a comfortable family oriented atmosphere this is probably your best bet.

      Meet the Business Owner

      Stephen S. Business Owner

      Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
