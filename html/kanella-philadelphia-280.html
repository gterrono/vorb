    I loved Kanella. We went for dinner with friends and had a great time. The waitstaff was very attentive, knowledgeable and friendly. So friendly that when I came in for brunch a couple weeks later, I said "hey" casually to our previous waiter like he and I went way back... um, embarrassing.

    The food was fantastic. I was not expecting such interesting and innovative dishes. We had the 3 dip of the day appetizer, saganaki, and bureki to start. All were fantastic, but I almost cried when my fiance dropped his bite of bureki on the floor. it wasn't even my bite, and I was seriously distraught. It was a tasty little fillo pastry stuffed with feta and thyme and drizzled with honey. I could have seriously had 3 orders of this as an entree.

    I had the Cyprus Tortelloni for my entree. It was so different and delicious, I think I may try to replicate it at home (but probably fail miserably. I don't remember what everyone else got for entrees (I think there was a stuffed pepper and maybe lamb koftka in there) but everyone loved it.

    For dessert, we split the pistachio and olive oil cake... I got this because of the rave reviews on yelp. It was not my first choice, but I followed my fellow yelpers advice. You did NOT steer me wrong! Good god, this was delicious.

    We have also been to Kanella for brunch twice now. We've tried the Cyprus Crepe, Malohwa, spanikopita, and shrimp saganaki. All were delicious.

    I have but one complaint (which will soon be irrelevant for at least the winter months). The chairs. While not uncomfortable for anyone wearing long pants, when wearing a skirt or shorts, the wicker chairs leave rather unattractive patterns on the back of your legs. Not a serious issue, but I'm glad I wasn't on a first date or anything when we went out for dinner the first time and I was wearing a short dress.

    I think this one is going to be on the regular rotation from now on...

    We went to Kanella last Sunday for my wife's cousin birthday. I had heard and read great things about Kanella and we like Mediterranean food, so we were excited to give this restaurant a try.

    One thing I need to point out is Sunday is a Chef's tasting menu only. There are 2 options, Meat or Vegetable. The meat was $35.00 and the all Vegetable was $30.00. This is served Tapas style and the choices are completely up to the chef. Again this menu is Sunday only, the other days Kanella uses their regular menu.

    It wasn't too difficult to find on street kiosk parking nearby. It may be harder during the week with Jefferson so close by. The interior of Kanella is simple, clean, rustic and tasteful Greek decor. Our waiter was fantastic, He was very pleasant, attentive and did a nice job explaining the Chef's tasting concept and the dishes. We were a party of 4 so he recommended  2 each of the meat and vegetable only options.

    Even though it was tapas style, it was a lot of food. None of us left hungry. There was a good selection of dishes. The duck sausage for me was a stand out. The other dishes were grape leaves, salads, quail, a seafood stew, pork just to name a few. There was not one dish that was not really good. The bread is also excellent.

    We had some desert (Baklava) and the French press coffee. These were not included in the Chef's tasting menu. Kanella is BYOB which I like also. Our total bill for 4 before tip was around $160.00 including dessert, coffee and tea.

    Kanella lived up to the Yelp reviews. I will be back, but probably not on a Sunday as I would like to try the regular menu.

Specialties

We specialise in cooking from the homeland of chef Konstantinos Pitsillides. Pitsillides hails from the island of Cyprus, an indipendant Greek island located in the very east of the Mediterranean. The Cyprus kitchen has many influences but can perhaps best be considered as part of the Levant. At home in Cyprus, as in his Philly kitchen freshness, simplicity, flavour and love are the standard ingrediants. Chef takes his French culinary training and applies it to the traditional recipes of his homeland. Homemade desserts, head to tail cooking, lemon and olive oil are all part and parcel of this neighbourhood joint.

History

Established in 2008.

Kanella is made up of a passionate bunch of characters from the chef on down. Being a local restaurant filled with the family spirit much of the staff have been there since we opened. Though Chef Pitsillides is oft misunderstood (and yes sometimes this is exaserbated by himself), the intent of the restaurant is a simple one... We have aimed to be a neighbourhood venue for good, honest and consitstant cooking. A place that you can afford to come to regularly and have a good meal, good service, a nice bottle of wine, and enjoy your company. We have been blessed with the neighborhood we are in and all our wonderfull customers, so many of which we have known for sometime now. We look forward to continued joyful cooking and eating!
