    'Cozy' and 'diners' are two words that should be inseparable. Friendly waiter (the only one), great food, and a great seat (at the bar facing the street through the large front glass) make Ants Pants fantastic.

    On the bridge end of South St. Ants Pants has a great location and blends into the community perfectly. Regulars are greeted by the staff and chatted up without pretense.

    While free wi-fi is offered, this small establishment isn't the sort of place to write the great American novel, rather, to be inspired for it. On this first visit, I brought a book, and felt right at home lazily making my way through a chapter.

    The coffee, was fantastic, dark and with a bold hint of nuttiness. I had the spinach, goat cheese and caramelized onions omelette and an order of brioche toast. Let's hit the high points first. The goat cheese is creamy and wonderful. The egg is cooked perfectly (though I've always loved runnier eggs), the spinach is fresh and delicious and the caramelized onions have a smokiness that speaks of the countless meals to come before it. And the fresh fruit salad on the side was perfection.  However, the construction of the omelette itself wasn't quite what I'd hoped. Nit picky though it may be, the first fold of an omelette should not be the last, leading to toppings spilling out of the egg. Further, no effort was made to spread the toppings around as the egg cooked, the result was four bites of egg, two bites of onion and egg, one bite of spinach and two bits of cheese and egg. The sole bite that included all three was heaven, and I would've preferred that they all be so good.
    The brioche toast was exactly what I'd hoped, crispy and toasted perfectly, and delicious. My only complaint involves inclusion. The toast was a side; typically egg dishes are served with a starch inclusive.

    I seem to be ending on such nit-picky negatives but I assure you I loved the place and will frequent it often. But as good as the food is, as good as the space and the service are (never saw the bottom of my cup), those small details are what earn that fifth star. See you all there, I'll be the one at the end of the bar with the book.

    Ants pants is a great little breakfast / brunch spot on the calm end of South Street.  There will usually be a wait on the weekendsand it can get a bit crowded when the weather is bad, because there is not much room inside for a waiting area, so people are just standing around beside your table waiting to be seated.  

    The service was fast and friendly, and the decor was hip but homey.  The eggs benedict was really good, the salmon platter was large and tasty, and the dill scrambled eggs with dill and feta were pretty good too.

    They also have decent coffee and an espresso maker, so I was pretty happy to be able to order a latte to drink while we waited for our food.  

    Ants pants is cash only, and you pay diner style (at the cashier at the front) so if can be a bit of a pain to get the to cashier to pay your bill when it is crowded.

History

Established in 2004.

Inspired and conceived in Australia, our little neighborhood cafe has been serving up top notch fresh and tasty food stuffs in the Grad Hospital, SWCC Philadelphia residents.

Meet the Business Owner

Liz F. Business Owner

Jenkintown native and Graduate Hospital resident, Liz Fleming has worked at cafes, restaurants and coffee shops around Philadelphia and Boston. But it was her experience working for several years in Sydney that inspired her to open up her own cafe upon returning to her native Philly. Giving credit for the inspiration, the name represents an Aussie slang meaning 'the best' which is what she tries to bring every day. Along with business partner also from Jenkintown, Nancy Silverman they aim to create an environment that is welcoming and cozy, food that is always fresh and tasty and an experience that makes you feel pleased you came.
