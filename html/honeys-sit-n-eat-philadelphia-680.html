    Despite the so-so experience, I look forward to re-visiting, because I must be missing something if everyone is jumping and down for a seat at this joint.  I was also too full to try the deep fried banana split, so I'll have to check that off my list next time!

    After spending one night in Philly, my husband and I were scoping out breakfast/brunch spots for our Sunday. As usual, I turn to Yelp for suggestions. Found this place and after looking at the menu, reading reviews and checking out the photos, we decided to head that way!

    We arrived around 10:45 am and gave our name to the young man who was hosting and he said it would be about a 50 min wait. We were prepared to wait and it was a gorgeous day so with some benches around the corner we sat down and enjoyed watching all the other people who were coming to Honey's.

    After about 40 min our name was called. The host said there were two counter seats (we had stated we'd be fine with that) and led us to the two best seats in the house. The counter had a small "L" to it and our seats gave us a perfect view of everything going on.

    We were immediately greeted by Bob who offered us coffee and anything else to drink. Giving us a few minutes to look over the menu, he let us know he'd be back in just a few minutes to take our order. We both decided to try the Honey Cristo and we were not disappointed. I also wanted to try a potato latke and my hubs wanted a side of the bacon truffle mashed potatoes. The kitchen was obviously cranking out orders as we didn't wait too terribly long for our food. The Honey Cristo had just a hint of sweet to it which complemented the savory flavor of the ham and cheese nicely. The eggs were perfectly sunny side up and there's nothing better than a runny yolk oozing over every potential bite. We forked our way through our meal and added bites of latke and mashed potatoes along the way. The mash was rich and delicious with bites of bacon and green onion dappled through and the latke was equally as delicious yet so different. Crisp on the outside and creamy on the inside with a fabulous oniony flavor. Super!

    Bob continued to check on us and we couldn't have been more impressed with him and his service. After telling him we were from Texas, he shared that one of the owner's was also from Texas and how the restaurant came to be. Nice to have pleasant conversation even when the place was so busy but that's how good Bob was. He had everything under control. It looked like all of the waitstaff did...a well-oiled machine for sure. We also didn't feel rushed once we finished our meal. With busy places, sometimes they drop checks before you even finish your meal as to expedite turning tables over. Not here. While they certainly had every table filled and people outside waiting, they allow their customers to relax and enjoy they experience. When we finally left, we walked away feeling extremely satisfied with not only our food but service as well. Thank you Bob for the excellent service, conversation and making our virgin trip to Honey's a wonderfully memorable one!

    Being the 8th most reviewed restaurant in Philadelphia, it really doesn't require me to put in my 2 cents but I am trying to do that 100 reviews challenge even though I'll never commit to it until I know I can do it.

    I have always heard great things about this place, so I decided to finally check it out for myself. The wait is half and hour around 11, the maitre'd is a bit agitated, probably too busy to be friendly. And not given a choice, me and my friends got to seat at the bar. I guess that's the entrance fee.

    I do like the place. Dimly lit, there's a lot of painted wood furniture. The place mat was lined with drawings of nearby business promos, a notice board near the front entrance dotted with event posters and such. This is a place that makes the neighborhood community stronger. There's a very hipster retro feel to it but definitely with modern pricing. $4 for a small orange juice, really? I mean it's fresh squeezed and all, but you can get an orange for under $1.

    The place is busy and the servers cannot be super attentive all the time but they are all acceptably friendly. The food is pretty good. We ordered but saw the wall of specials at the far side, I would have appreciated it if someone had pointed that out or they had printed a copy and gave it to us along with the regular menu because they did sound really good.

    The regular menu itself is already quite large and it's really hard to decide what I want to try first. I settled on the honey cristo which is like the regular monte cristo with an abundance of thin sliced ham, melted cheese, two pieces of sweet French toast and 2 sunny side up eggs on top, and a little side salad. It's pretty well done, even though I don't really taste the cheese and the french toast can be crispier but the sweetness makes the whole sandwich pretty unique. The enfrijoladas, which is enchiladas made with frijoles (beans), is a great vegetarian dish. Tortillas filled with eggs and black beans, slathered with sour cream, sprinkled with queso fresco, smothered with salsa verde and topped with vegetables. I actually didn't miss meat at all. The dish is a little small in size for a big guy like me but a side order of vegetarian sausage will be great with it. It's like hash brown with strong sausage notes.

    Just because I was curious, I also ordered a side of green bean casserole. Awesome, thin crispy onion rings atop the buttery, mushroom gravy and the soft green beans. I used to not get green bean casseroles, but I get it now.

    I have to say I like the place, it will be a rare scene if I'm hungry and find myself in this neighborhood before noon but stranger things have happened.
