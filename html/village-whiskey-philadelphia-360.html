    "Oh God, please get it out of me."

    Probably more fitting for the "chest bursting scene from Alien, and certainly not the usual beginning to a five star Yelp review, but pertinent enough to be the headline.

    Let's moonwalk back in time to 6/16/11 around 5:30 pmish. The lads and I arrive in Rittenhouse in style in BT's pimp-mobile from the Phil's game. As we exited the vehicle I noticed a few Cheerios attached to my ass, and a stain of baby vom on my plaid shorts from the car's seat. Let's just say by that point I was feeling fine, and could have cared less. Maybe I ate one of those Cheerios (possible Yelp review in the making), maybe I didn't? What's it to you?

    Well, BT led our crew into Village Whiskey, and shockingly, there was a table available for the 5 of us, and a perpetually late straggler who shall remain nameless. The interior was not what I expected. Surprisingly, small and decked out with lots of white and black subway tile, a big bar for the space, and very few tables. Most of the group had been here before, so drinks and plates upon plates of apps were ordered from our waitress who was sporting a very "unhappy to deal with this group of morons" look on her face. I could have read her mind... "I busted my ass to get a job at a Garces restaurant so I wouldn't have to deal with A-holes like these. Look at that one there. He's showing off a permanent marker tramp stamp above his crack in the middle of the restaurant. And that one over there. He's got a half eaten Cheerio on his face while he's leering at this guy's date's cleavage. Scumbag."

    Apps arrived a plenty. Duck fat fries. Duck fat fries covered in cheese sauce and short rib meat (placed right in front of me). Tatter Tots. Popcorn shrimp. Pickled cherry tomatoes. It was gluttonous. It was soaking up the Rumple mints, and Jager shots (1996 all over again) numb nuts over there had bought us at McFadden's (another review soon to come). It was so greasy good that I just kept eating it. And eating it. And eating it. Just like that "Torture" skit from Wu Tang's Enter The 36 Chambers, but more on that to come...

    You know your food has been good when you go to take a sip of your cask pulled beer, and not one sip is gone. (One quick gripe about my beer. If I order a cask beer, I expect an Imperial pint, not a 12 oz mincy glass. At least post the size on the menu). So my stand out favorite had to be the pickled cherry tomatoes. Those little gems must have been quickly blanched, had the skins removed, and then pickled and served in a mason jar. Unbelievably bright, and full of tomato flavor. Seriously, I would come back just for them and all of the other pickled vegetables (truffled cauliflower, oh hell yeah) I didn't get to try.

    Then it came time to order a burger. Not only was I uncomfortably full just after the appetizers, and maybe had to undo the top button on my shorts, but more food. Come on. These guys I was with wouldn't let me leave with out ordering a burger, so I did. I ordered the Village Burger ( a very reasonably priced $11)  which came on a sesame roll with Tomato, Boston bibb lettuce, and
    house-made thousand island. I added raw onion. When the burger arrived it had been perfectly cooked medium rare as I had ordered it. First bite, insanely awesome. Second bite, the same. By bite six my brain overrode the awesome taste, and began too start beeping warning lights. Food Containment Full! Food containment Full!! I was stuffed beyond stuffed. I stretched out in my seat. I may have burped a few times. Whew. I am now man enough to admit, that I couldn't finish one of the best burgers I have EVER had in my life. It did me in. I'll attach a pic to my profile for all to see, so you can get a look at the burger that conquered me.
    After the straggler finally arrived, and after I watched him plow through his burger, he made the suggestion to grab a shot of espresso over at La Colombe. I ended up having a triple shot, and Lordy that was a mistake. Tectonic Plates in my gut began to shift. The ride back to Delaware was more than uncomfortable. I spent the rest of the day/night burping, and writhing in agony on the couch as my wife evil eyed me with looks that said "You know its your own fault". I couldn't even sleep that night. My head was filled with Fever Dreams of duck fat fries, and that burger.

    Well, as I write this now, three days later, let me just say that burger and I have still not parted ways yet. It is still a part of me. Kashi cereal which usually does the trick has had no effect. Coffee in the morning, nothing. There is only one hope now in these dark and desperate times. Ex-lax me, bartender!

    Despite the past few days of misery, I will go back to exact my revenge. I will order, and finish another burger only this time I will be armed with Fiber One bars.  Mark my words.

    I wish I liked burgers. I really, really do. But, alas, I'm not one to pretend I enjoy something just to fit in with the crowd (especially when it comes to food!), so I didn't order Village Whiskey's most famous menu item when I had dinner there this past weekend.

    My friends both did, however, and had very positive things to say. The burgers ($11, with additions ranging from $1-5 each) were cooked exactly as requested and looked meticulously prepared. They were also HUGE - they each left with hefty portions to have later as left-overs. We also split the truffled cauliflower "pickles" ($7), served with  black-olive tampenade, toasted sourdough bread and delicious whipped ricotta cheese. The cauliflower was very tasty, though I thought the $7 price tag was a tad high for the serving size.

    I opted to try the duck fat french fries with a side of Sly Fox cheddar cheese sauce ($7), as well as an order of the deviled eggs ($3). Both items were great, though I was hoping the eggs would have been prepared a little more uniquely (think Supper).

    Overall, we all had a very enjoyable time at dinner. The restaurant doesn't offer a ton of options for a non-hamburger lover like myself, but it definitely is worth a visit, especially for all you burger fans out there.

    I'm a fan of Jose Garces restaurants and had been itching to try Village Whiskey for quite some time, but had been daunted by the tales of 3 hour waits on weekends. So .... one dark and stormy weeknight I calculated my chances of getting seated in a timely fashion higher than normal, and off to Philly we drove.

    The "waitlist" was kept track of on an ipad, with an option of loitering around and crowding the very small establishment(I expected a much larger space, no wonder the lineups are mad!) or being texted when a table is available, to which you need to reply you are on your way. Quite modern and efficient, much better than the buzzers of yore which only allowed you to stay within a small radius.

    My dining companion and I decided to crowd around the bar given the torrential rain outside. And yes, Village Whiskey was still packed on such an evening.  We were seated soon after.

    For appetizer we ordered the Herb Roasted Oysters - juicy, flavourful, warm only second to the Parmesan baked Oysters I've had in New Orleans, I'd highly recommend these.

    I ordered the Village burger and added on the horseradish crab salad on it, while my hungrier companion ordered the $26 Whiskey Burger  (with the standard toppings which are by no means standard) - applewood bacon, fois gras, blue cheese and maple glazed cipollini onions. And a side of duck fat fries to share.

    While waiting for our dinner to arrive, I sipped on the "Debt Collector" (Apple brandy, port, lemon, and some bitters) an excellent and refreshing not-too-sugary choice,  just as i like my cocktails. I also tried a bit of the Cuba Kula (3 different kinds of rum, absinthe, lime, orange and bitters) which was also equally good.

    Our food arrived about 20 mins from when we ordered. My burger was delicious, cooked to medium rare perfection, juicy, delicious and quite messy to eat (Disclaimer: avoid on a first date unless you feel very adventurous). I was really happy with my burger till i took a bite of the Whiskey burger... and another... until it had to be pried away from me. I had a serious case of burger envy. Complex complementing flavors and textures, meaty goodness... Jose Garces is a god.

    The only negative things I can say about this place are that the fries are not all that. The waitress forgot to bring out our fries with the order and had to be reminded. On arrival they seemed to be a bit overcooked. Definitely skip the fries in favour of other sides would be my suggestion.
    Also, another minor complaint would have been from the Whiskey burger  the bottom bun was a bit soggy - perhaps due to all the toppings. The Village burger held up quite alright however.

    We decided to skip dessert in favour of more drinks. I ordered the Fishhouse punch, really good, but  quite sweet. I also had a sip of the chocolate shake my friend ordered, also top notch.

    I think the Whiskey Burger is by far the most indulgent other-worldly gourmet burger I have ever tried, and cant wait to go back so (after a day of fasting) I can order one all to myself, and try some other cocktails.

    A "MUST TRY" for any visit to Philly for the more food inclined traveller.

    A solid 4.5 stars.

Specialties

A ground-to-order Angus Beef Burger, a Veggie Burger, a Bison Burger or an Vienna All-Beef Hot Dog with all the trimmings are each served alongside duck fat French fries and Sly Fox cheddar sauce.  Burgers are topped with Village Whiskey's house-made thousand-island dressing, Bibb lettuce, beefsteak tomato and a potato-beer bun, with optional toppings including bacon, caramelized onion, truffles, artichokes and a selection of cheeses.

As one of the most comprehensive collections of its namesake spirit available in Philadelphia, Village Whiskey features a veritable library of 80 to 100 varieties of whiskey, bourbon, rye and scotch from Scotland, Canada, Ireland, United States and even a few Japanese options.

History

Established in 2009.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
