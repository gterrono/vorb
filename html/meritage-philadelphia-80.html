    I must be getting old. Meritage is much more appealing to me these days than the noisy place across the street on a Friday night. I will gladly sit at a table outside to enjoy a few drinks and conversation rather than be party to the general carousing that carries on over there. "Get off my lawn!"

    It's probably best if I shut up about all that and just write about the various dishes we've tried:
    deviled duck egg w/ truffle oil and trout roe - wife loved it, but I would prefer less truffle, more trout
    pan fried pork and chive dumpling - great, fresh taste, made in house
    calamari w/ gochujung, sesame - light batter, tender crispy calamari, nice twist w/ the red pepper paste
    pickled vegetables - refreshing and clean tasting
    kale chips - just skip them, or find out for yourself for two bucks
    braised pork cheeks, polenta and poached egg - loved the pork, wife not so much, polenta had a nice hint of lemon/vanilla
    dark chocolate, brown sugar and banana bread pudding - rich and decadent, great with the salted caramel and ice cream

    In short, lots of choices that pair well with the selection of wine, beer and spirits. And great service to boot.

    Vegan Tasting Menu Review

    Meritage offers a prix fixe vegan tasting menu on Tuesday nights.  It's $35 and not everyone at the table has to order it, like some other restaurants.  I guess that makes sense though, because if you are eating with a group of 6 people, what are the chances that all 6 would want to eat vegan?

    The menu offers 5 courses.  

    FIRST: I use the word "course" lightly since the first was tomato consomme and it was served in a shot glass.  

    SECOND: Koren taco.  Pretty much exactly what it sounds like.  It was tasty.

    THIRD: Some sort of lentil salad.  Not so good.  Not flavorful, not many ingredients, the lentils were kind of hard.

    FOURTH: Chickpea patties - these were really yummy.  Crispy, flavorful, light, soft on the inside.  Pretty much perfect.  I don't like the seaweed?? that was under it, and it could have used more of the sauce, but this was delicious.  I could have eaten another patty too.

    FIFTH: I substituted the sorbet with chocolate ice cream.  Portion was small, kind of melty already when it was served to me.  It was ok.  Note: the dessert spoons there are GIGANTIC.  Like hand shovels.  One scoop and you already ate half of your small dessert.  Maybe they should look into getting some teaspoons.

    We arrived and were seated at 7:15.  We didn't leave until right after 10:30.  This dinner was loooong.  The timing of the courses seemed off and oftentimes we were wondering if the waitstaff had abandoned us.  The place was not full at all, so I'm not sure what the issue was.  

    This place is not cheap.  It was over $50 per person with one drink each.  Only 2/6 of us got the tasting menu, so be prepared to spend a decent amount when you come here.

    My heart was set on trying their vegan tasting menu that they offer on Tuesday nights, but after perusing the rest of their menu, I would have been happy to try any of their items.  

    The tasting menu starts with an amuse, which in our case was an heirloom tomato consomme.  I personally wasn't a fan of the texture of the shooter, but it was obviously still thought out well to go with the rest of the dinner.  The first course was a Korean taco and it had a decent spice to it.  It also was a good size and I didn't feel overwhelmed by the portion.  Next, we moved on to a french lentil salad with tomoatoes, cucumbers, and a basil vinaigrette.  I literally could not stop eating it.  The lentils were very dimensional and I enjoyed the subtle curry flavor they had.  For the final dinner course, we were given a fried black olive square with chick peas, swiss chard, and a tomato chutney.  I'm not usually a fan of black olives, but everything about our last dish was outstanding.  I couldn't figure out exactly how they had prepared the olives, but they were really delicious.  We rounded out the dinner with two scoops of berry sorbet.

    Another Yelper recently remarked that she was sick of seeing sun dried tomatoes, squash ravioli, etc. on vegetarian menus and that she was dying to try something different.  Meritage certainly doesn't fall into the "overdone" category.  The vegan and vegetarian items here are very non-traditional and I think everyone would be able to find a dish here that they would enjoy.  I challenge everyone to go meatless, just for one Tuesday, and try out the tasting menu at Meritage.  I sincerely doubt you will be disappointed.

    I still can't decide if I want to go back to Meritage or not.  It's a nice looking upscale wine bar and restaurant close to Rittenhouse and Fitler with a standout menu, beautifully presented dishes, and a small but notable wine list.  But with so-so service, a rather raucous atmosphere, and middle-of-the-road food, it's not exactly screaming at me to come back again.  Plus, it's expensive.  I don't like paying a lot for food that's not worth my while.

    The BF and I came here for restaurant week last night.  We sat at a small two-top in the main dining room; behind three large noisy parties and next to the kitchen.  Even though the hostess asked if we wanted to move to the more intimate room further in, we stayed (sidenote: we should have taken her up on that).  In a reverse move mimicking the time we went to Fond, I started with the foie gras raviolis while he ordered the sweetbreads.  His appetizer was good, but nothing really notable (solidly flavored, but it didn't sing).  My foie gras was unusually tough; normally it should melt in your mouth, but this portion had a little more meat to it.  Still good, just not great.  And please, don't think that I didn't notice that when you described the dish as "raviolis", you clearly meant to say "pot-stickers" (the dough tasted like something I would buy frozen at Trader Joes or order in from Chinese takeout).

    For the main dish, we both had the duck.  It came out looking beautiful, but it tasted only so-so.  Don't get me wrong: the duck was good, as were the side dishes.  The meat was very tender and flavorful.  My only complaint is that the duck was sliced really thin.  The only piece I enjoyed on that dish was the end which was sliced noticeably thicker than the rest of the meat.  That piece had the juice and flavor I was expecting.  The rest of the duck on the plate was like eating sliced steak.  Finally, dessert (which was served rather quickly compared to the other dishes) was just ok.  My flourless chocolate torte was more like a chocolate mousse they turned upside down instead of a rich dense cake.  His butternut squash pot au creme was better, though not by much.  Not sweet enough to really finish out the meal, but definitely points for creativity to the kitchen.

    The service was ok, but some points were definitely hit or miss.  We waited longer than usual at the front of the house to be seated since the hostess wasn't there.  Our water glasses were initially dropped on our table (not thrown down, but instead of placed, just plopped).  The appetizers and entrees took a long time to come out of the kitchen; the dessert popped out just after our waiter asked us if we wanted coffee.  And it was really, really loud the entire time.  Overall, I think it tries to play up the warm, intimate cozy feel and the food looks great both on paper and on the plate.  But the taste did not match the way it looked and sounded, the crowd was too loud for a date night place, and the kitchen was stuck on two speeds: very slow and very fast.  There's an outside chance that I would return for the much raved about Korean tacos, but as for a date night or nice meal, my money will be better spent elsewhere.

    My friend and I had a 6pm resi last night for dinner.
    Upon entering the restaurant, you are greeted by a dark wooden bar, dimly lit created a nice and romantic setting for an evening out. I could do without the flat screen TV, but being in a sports town, it seems to be a rule to have one by any bar.
    The dining room was of a good size, the tables were spaced far enough away that you don't feel like you are sitting on the group next to you, although conversations tend to carry throughout the space.

    The staff was delightful and really tied the entire dining experience together for us.
    Surprisingly for RW, the portions were very generous and the presentation just as pleasing.

    I started off with the Foie Gras Ravioli, I agree with one reviewer when they stated more of pot sticker then ravioli, but the foie was the perfect temperature, packed with buttery goodness. The contrast of textures was nice, so I couldn't complain that it really wasn't a 'ravioli'

    My friend had the mussels, even though we were skeptical of the curry sauce, which is usually always done terribly and too heavy for the mussels. This rendition was light and the mussels were of a nice size. The number of mussels are usually plentiful elsewhere, but she still got a good amount.

    For our entrees, I continued my duck fest with the duck breast, again the presentation was beautiful and the meat was cooked to a nice medium rare.  I wish there were crisps of skin to add a bit more richness to the dish, the sweet potato puree was smooth and the bordelaise sauce added another dimension to the flavors, but as a whole, it was a bit bland.

    The Korean fried chicken was nice, the kim chee really brought out the spice in the chicken wings, I wish that the skin again would have been crispier, but overall not a bad dish.

    The flourless chocolate torte and peanut butter bombe packed the most flavor of the night. just enough sweetness on both plates to finish off our dinner.

    All in all, other than a couple of minor tweaks, I think Meritage is great spot for a romantic dinner and I will be stopping back here again.

    Let's be honest - I went because they had pork belly on the menu, and I wasn't disappointed. We did have to wait a bit to be seated despite our reservation but didn't mind hanging out at the bar for a bit.

    Service overall was a little harried - our server really needed to just take a deep breath and slow down - but still sweet and personable.

    Overall a very nice meal - nothing terribly edgy or surprising on the menu, but they did do a much better job than other restaurants I've been to with what they offer.

    Listed in Best of the Best, We Love Our Neighborhood!

We went once shortly after we first moved in and again for a pleasant and peaceful New Year's Eve dinner with an out of town guest. With how crazy New Year's Eve can be, it was nice to have a low-key dinner nearby. We also stopped in and sat at the bar one night to enjoy their scotch selection.

    Most recently, we went for a birthday dinner and all four of us raved about how good it was.
