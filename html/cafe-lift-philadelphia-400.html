    During our first trip to Philly, we scoped out this brunch joint and, boy, are we happy we did.  We were prepared to wait (thanks to fellow Yelpers) and that was about 45 minutes--which the host timed TO THE DOT.  Although we didn't pursue it, I believe Cafe Lift provides free coffee to those who are waiting (how friendly!).  

    Everything about our experience at Cafe Lift was absolutely fantastic--the service, the food, and the price.  We decided ahead of time to order several dishes to share--Fried Egg Panino Panini, Huevos Rancheros, and French Toast.  At the time, the Fried Egg Panino Panini was a special.  That panini, literally, changed our lives (my bf cannot stop talking about it to this day).  It was a mixture of fried eggs, sauteed broccoli rabe, asiago cheese, and sun-dried tomato pesto.  I'm positive when we're in Philly next, the bf will require we go here and request they make this panini just for him.  

    Outside of a panini love-fest, the remainder of our food was tasty; we received hearty portions to the point we couldn't finish; and the menu left us interested in other dishes if we have the opportunity to re-visit.  On another note, our food came out surprisingly fast--I would be surprised if we were sitting at our table for, maybe, 40 minutes (and, no, we weren't rushed--it was just THAT efficient!).  

    All in all, if you live in Philly, you're doing yourself a disservice if you haven't already been here.  And, if you're visiting Philly and nearby, do yourself a favor and try this place!

    **Cash only**

    Perfect 5 star Sunday brunch experience.

    We found parking on the street with ease at 9am on a Sunday.

    All breakfast dishes were $10 or below.

    First off,  I do recommend arriving as close to the opening time as possible. We arrived at 9 sharp, and the place was packed within 30 mins.

    The servers were very efficient and friendly.

    The regular coffee was superb--nice robust flavor, and very bold.

    I got the sausage spinach fritatta with a lemon ricotta pancake on the side. The fritatta was spot on--generous portion, great flavor, and the potatoes on the side were among the best I've had.  The lemon ricotta pancake was perfectly dense, and you could see the fresh lemon zest throughout the pancake.  It was so moist and light.

    Husband got the cannoli French toast. Oh my word.  The bread was fresh and most, and the marscapone cream on top was generous enough to make sure every bite had some.  The chocolate and pistachio on top brought everything together.  You probably do not need syrup. If you're getting this,  make sure your eating partner gets a savory dish so you can share with each other to ease up the sweet a little bit.

    The tables are a bit close to your neighbors,  but the noise/privacy was tolerable as we are not loud,  and neither were our neighbors.

    Highly recommended in every aspect imaginable for an unforgettable meal.

    This is my favorite brunch in the city.  A combination of consistently bold, taste-bud-quivering, sensational food, terrific service, the option to BYO (what can I say... home-infused cardamom gin with some of Lift's OJ is sublime living on a Saturday morning), and the usage of some of the most careful and thoughtful local sourcing practices I know of in town contribute to a bruncherie that, in my opinion, leads the charge of the city's best.  And for visitors, it's little more than a stone's throw from Center City, the Reading Terminal Market, and the Convention Center.

    Service and Menu
    Lift has some of the best brunch service in the city.  The hosts are welcoming and friendly, and seem to get more and more excited about your arrival with each of the six-or-so steps you have to climb once your past the front door.  The servers and folks behind the counter are always extremely knowledgable about the specials and are more than happy to offer suggestions (the menu itself is sexy as hell but the specials board also has a little something for everyone - my recommendation would be not even to crack the menu unless something(s) on the board for some ungodly reason aren't screaming your name).

    Highlights
    Delightful staff, any special frittata or french toast, an epic homemade hot sauce, perfectly runny eggs time and time again (too many places seem to struggle with this)... and perhaps the best part?  For those of us who care (should be all of us if you ask me), all beef and pork is locally-sourced and humanely raised - the owners use Philly Cow Share and source from other sustainable/humane area farmers to ensure quality, healthfulness, and high ethical standards.

    Cafe Lift is one of the better brunches in the city. Far better than Sabrina's in my opinion (which I believe to be overrated). It's got an industrial hip kind sort of vibe, and it's cash only.

    I got the Huevos Rancheros which, as many others have said, is very tasty. They do them really well. It's crispy, savory, and balanced out with their housemade hotsauce.

    The Jen's Crespelle is just okay, the eggs are done nicely as is the bacon, but the crepe could use more work. The crepe is strangely dense, and dry, you can offset the dryness density of the crepe with syrup and hotsauce, but it has an unpleasant texture in the mouth not entirely unlike eating folded up tissue paper.

    The Coffee is just average, being neither good or bad.
