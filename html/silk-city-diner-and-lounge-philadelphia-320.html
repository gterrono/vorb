    I always plan a night out with my colleagues during the last night of an annual conference I attend.  It's usally a DDD.  This year it was Silk City.  I knew it was an old school diner but was surprised when we were seated in this mini oasis of a patio which looked like it was decorated by Elvis, Elton John and Jimmy Buffet. I think the fact we were in a T-storm warning added to the nostalgia.  

    It's one of those menus which you can't decide.  It was a good thing there were 4 of us because I got to try a bit of everything....the thai ribs, empanadas, calamari and grilled sausage.   The standouts were the ribs and calamari..hands down. The calamari was deep fried with hot peppers.  The ribs were just......freaking good.

    The server killed me....she was a little annoyed  with me at first but she warmed up.  She had my heart when I asked what she would recommend for an entree. Her answer was "Well, I'm a fat girl..so I do the meatloaf and the fried chicken".  Needless to say, the chicken (complete with mashed potatoes and "baconed" collard greens) hit the table to great satisfaction.  Terrific!!!!

    I ordered the burger with hot peppers and a fried egg.  The egg was overdone so I couldn't endulge in the runny yolk but it was good nonetheless.  The fries were....ok.  I ended with a really decent bread pudding.

    They have a great local beer selection.  I was treated to some bad ass brews served  at the right temperature.      

    Despite a few flaws in execution, I had that feel good feeling when I left (even the old pull-type cigarette machine almost brought a tear to my eye).  Gonna give it a 5.

    I should preface this by saying that I only got to see Silk City as a bar/lounge on a Friday night, but if the experience I had is any indication of their diner or beer garden, then they are also spectacular.

    You literally cannot miss this place. Neon lights mixed artsy architectural design and an entire segment that looks strictly like a diner make this place stand out amongst the other establishments along Spring Garden.

    The bar area is medium sized,  has a dance floor, and is decorated with some pretty random flare along the walls. There is a cover ($7 Guys/$3 for Girls) but Silk City makes up for it with decent drink deals. We rocked the $2 PBRs, $2.50 Tecate Lights, w/a lime wedge.

    As other reviews have stated, the DJ is pretty good, though your experience may vary according to your music tastes or the night you go on. Fridays/Saturdays are prolly your best bets since there's pretty much always a big crowd.

    All in all, Silk City is a great night out within the Northern Liberties area. With essentially three different venues mixed into one, you'll be hard pressed to not find yourself having a good time.

    We had stopped by Silk City one time a few months ago when we arrived in Northern Liberties early to meet friends. We intended to just get a couple of beers but got a little hungry and ordered the calamari appetizer to share. We were impressed with the beer selection, which is small but interesting, and with the calamari, which had a very nice breading and came with mildly hot peppers that were also fried in the same breading. The staff were very friendly and welcoming. After that positive experience, we knew we wanted to go back.

    We don't get to NoLibs very often, so significant time passed between that first visit and the second, when we met the aforementioned friends at Silk City for a proper dinner. We had high expectations set after that first visit, and I can happily report we were not disappointed. I had the Grilled Chicken Sandwich with sharp provolone, prosciutto, broccoli rabe, long hots, basil pesto mayo. The chicken was tender and cooked perfectly. One of the long hots was a little tough, but the sandwich was otherwise delicious. The fries were good but nothing exceptional. My partner raved about the Buttermilk Fried Chicken, which came with mashed potatoes, bacon braised collard greens, jalapeño corn muffin, honey-chili butter. The beer selection, again, was just right. They had a nice selection ranging from a pale ale to a stout.

    The service was a bit perfunctory. Our waitress often said "you're welcome" as she set our plates or glasses down before we had a chance to thank her, and she brought our check without asking if we wanted anything else. Luckily, we were ready to move on, but it would have been nice to have been asked. Given our pleasant experience with the staff on our first visit, I'm willing to write this off as an exception.

    Our visit was additionally memorable since it was the day of Philly Naked Bike Ride and the riders went by on Spring Garden just as we finished our meal.
