    Pat's is the greatest place to purchase food on earth.

    There, that's out of the way.

    I love Pat's.  I lived in downtown Philly for six years, and I've had every variety of cheesesteak the city has to offer, from big restaurants to famous places to carts on the street.  None of them can add up to the glory that is the King of Steaks.

    The bread is perfect.  The meat is perfect.  The copious amounts of delicious, dripping cheese whiz is perfect.  Everything about this meal creates an orgasmic eating experience.  

    They also have the best cheese fries in the city.

    Oh, and by the way, come late at night and be greeted by Andre.  Andre may seem like a bum to you, but you'd be mistaken.  Andre is the man.  He will chat it up, tell jokes, get you napkins, and clean up your trash.  Better than any waiter I've ever had.  He'll even clean your windshield.

    My gripes:
    1) I miss the yellow lights.  Really, I do.
    2) The price keeps going up.  But if the people keep buying, they'll keep doing it, I don't blame them.
    3) They used to have their own sodas as well as the brand name ones, and they were fantastic.  They're gone now.
    4) Across the street from that abomination known as Genos.

    Of course, these gripes are not enough to lower my 5 star rating.  I'd rate them 10 out of 5 if only yelp would let me.

    No no no no no no no. No.

    I remember the first time I went to Pat's and Geno's during Spring Break (this is before I discovered Yelp); my friend and I were coming from Myrtle Beach,SC to Brooklyn, NY and decided to stop here for a 'midnight snack'. I ordered a cheese steak with provolone from Pat's and one with Cheese Whiz from Geno's, onions in both. After eating both of them , I decided that Geno's is the King of Steaks and doused my friend with his soda as an official declaration . Okay that didn't happen, but I was thinking it.

    Enough reminiscing, though. It has been nearly six months and Caleb P. is back in Philadelphia and it's official: the only thing keeping Pat's and Geno's alive is pure publicity.

    The simple fact is this: These two places serve the most delicious cheese steaks ever made. Delicious? Actually, a better word would be: horrendous, disgusting, grotesque, disturbing. Rather, any combination of the previously listed words would be more appropriate than 'delicious'.

    After coming here the second time, I realize that the Cheese Whiz masks how poor this cheese steak is. Order everything and have provolone instead of Cheese Whiz and you will discover how unbelievably tough, dry, and lacking the meat is. I won't say any more, rather I will let you find out yourself.

    The thing is about Geno's and Pat's is summarized in the last sentence of the previous paragraph: "....rather I will let you find out yourself." I can continue on how tough the meat is, how limp the bread is, or how overcooked the onions are. I can rant, throw a tantrum, scream to the world, but that won't stop anyone. Curiosity and skepticism will continue to bring wave upon wave of people to these joints and nothing will stop them. Their popularity has reached Paris Hilton-esque proportions where any publicity is good publicity.
