    Rembrandt's is a local favorite, but was losing the game against the newer, revamped restaurants that have popped up in fairmount.  The menu was OK, but nothing ever stood out. The burger was a burger, the flat bread was flat bread.  I can't recall in detail a single dish I ever had here .

    This new chef has completely revised the menu.  Eating here several times since his debut, some items have hit the spot, while others have fallen a little short.  What I enjoy best about this chef is that he wants critiques on his food.  The staff has been peppering customers with questions and suggestions on how to improve the dishes, taking each question and comment back to the chef.  You get a feeling that he really wants to succeed in a neighborhood that no longer accepts mediocre food.

    Additionally, I appreciate the fact that a majority of the food he serves is made in house.  The breads, buns, and hot dogs are just a few items to list on the new menu that they make themselves.  The buns were mistaken for Le Bus bread by a fellow diner of mine, which is a compliment in itself!

    I know that some of the recent reviews on yelp have produced complaints from loyal Rembrandt's customers since the Chefs arrival, but ask yourself if the owner had decided on forfeiting the name that is so well known in the neighborhood would these reviews still be negative?  Change is not easy, but restaurants are a business, and Rembrandt's needed a updating (badly).  The menu isn't perfect, the prices are a little higher, but give this new chef, who seems genuinely interested in pleasing his customers a chance.  As Fairmount has changed for the better in the past few years, so has Rembrandt's.  So give them your comments and just maybe the menu will transform into something that the masses can enjoy.

    PS, the beer selection is killer, so if you really don't enjoy the food, at least it's a great spot to relax with a good beer.

    4.5-stars for the courage to change a longstanding menu, 3.5 stars for the current food (getting dragged down by a few outliers that need some tweaks).

    I'll keep this review short and sweet:

    Rembrandt's is an awesome neighborhood bar, run by awesome people, and serves (for the most part) awesome food.  One of my best friends from grad school was a regular here, so I routinely joined him on Tuesdays for quizzo.  Of the many, many times I have been here, I have always enjoyed myself and the company.

    The beer selection is decent, and the new menu, although a bit of an adjustment from the old, still has some of the classic favorites.  The single best thing on the menu is the turkey burger, which I will order knowing that I have the option of ordering a regular burger (but I won't because the turkey burger is that much better).  Juicy turkey patty, avocado, cheddar, and burger sauce on a soft but sturdy bun.  It's terrific.  The fries aren't too shabby either (but don't order the truffle cheese fries with shredded but not melted cheese).  I am a little miffed that many of the sandwiches from the old menu are now gone, but the new menu looks more focused. I hope to try it if I ever come for a legit meal.

    It's also a great quizzo bar, and they do quizzo on Tuesdays.  A lot of regulars come around for it, and just before the final round, they pass out free Hope's Cookies (among other things, from time to time, such as snack cakes or seasonal pastries).

    Overall: highly recommended if you're in the neighborhood (or if you happen to bike across town, like me), and a great place to catch up with friends in a low-key non-pretentious atmosphere.

Specialties

A friendly neighborhood bar and gastropub with banquet and private event facilities.

Rembrandt's has one of the most inviting sidewalk cafe's in the Fairmount neighborhood.

When not hosting private events, Rembrandt's provides a variety of entertainment - Quizzo on Tuesday nights is a big draw!.  Call us to schedule your private event or use our space to show off your (or your group's) talent.  We feature a great view of the Philadelphia Skyline from our upstairs banquet room and we are fully equipped for audio and video events.

And, of course, we offer casual dining in our romantic dining room, and the same menus are available at both of our turn of the century bars.

History

Established in 1985.

The brown bars and cafes of Amsterdam, the city that was home to Rembrandt van Rijn, are well known throughout Europe for their antique charm, warmth, inviting atmosphere, and friendly but stimulating conversation. Here at Rembrandt's in Philadelphia, a seventy-five year old tavern has been renovated in a manner reminiscent of the brown bars of Amsterdam. In fact, many of the interesting fixtures you will see in the bar and restaurant were discovered in Europe by the original owners of Rembrandt's, and their history may be of interest to you. Please browse our website for photos of our neighborhood gathering spot and of the people who come to enjoy our food, drink and companionship!
