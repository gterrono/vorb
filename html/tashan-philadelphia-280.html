    And I'm back to liking Tashan.   Maybe it was a rough patch? Couples go through that right? Well, now we're back in the honeymoon stage. I came with a friend to check it out once more and give my review an update. I'm glad I came.  We did the 4 course tasting menu and the upgraded wine pairing.... do the pairing... it's just easier to be on auto pilot all night and it's so worth it.

    Here's what we got...

    Palak Chaat - AWESOME get this.  Super cool texture flavors and tastes to it.  Think kale chips meets spinach with some great creamy textures and flavors to it.

    Tandoori Shrimp - had a lovely kick to it.

    Tandoori Chaamp - I really enjoyed the lamb.  I love lamb so this should come as no suprise.

    Diver Scallops "Kangura" - this is the star of the night.  Some of the best prepared scallops I've had in recent memory.  There a nice crisp to the outside of the scallops comparable to the top of creme brulee...

    Spice Crusted Red Snapper - this was a nicely cooked fish dish.

    Naga Beef Sumi - so good, perfectly cooked. Definitely something anyone who is nervous about trying indian cuisine can order and feel comfortable with.

    Truffle Naan - I could bathe in this.  Love the truffle naan. Dream of the truffle naan.

    Spicy Brussel Sprouts - B Sprouts are all the rage these days and these definitely hold their own.  I would order these again.

    Shikaari Quail - I like the qual but it's definitely not a sharing plate and a little different vibe than the other Tashan dishes.  Don't get me wrong it's good and worth ordering it's just not a sharing friendly dish like the others.

    Tashan Crème Brulee - So good. soo soo good.

    Flaming Oolong Tea - presentation alone makes this worth ordering if you're looking to impress someone or need a jump start to get the convo going.

    I'm glad I came back.... my complaints about the service are no long apparent.  Our server was top notch and I look forward to coming back and hopefully checking out their phenomenal happy hour specials

    My fiance and I went back with a living social deal a couple weeks back, and not much was different. The food was fantastic, but the service at this place is terrible. They really need to get it together. The ambiance and food just dont match the service. As pricey as it can be, they need to step their service game up.

    The chef's tasting menu was a ton of food and it was all delicious. I definitely think they take some big risks with the fusion side of things and moving away from traditionality, but it usually works. The living social deal was for 69 dollars, when the tasting is usually 50 per person, so it was a steal, plus they had half price bottles of wine last night which was awesome. While the living social was for 4 courses, we really had like 7 different dishes and split dessert. It was a lot of food, and it was all very good. Had the service been better, it would have been a much better experience and a higher rating because the food was great.

    Indian cuisine is one of the ones I don't like as much the further it gets from traditional, but there's a good balance here and the food does shine. Standouts were the fried kale salad, tandoori salmon and shrimp, butter chicken and amazing garlic and cilantro spicy naan. Even the creme brûlée galub jamun was amazing. I'll be back to see if the service improves, but it's not worth the price at the moment.

    We came here for restaurant week and it was absolutely delicious! I love how they take a traditional indian dish and make it with a different ingredient/some kind of twist. Everything we ate was really really good. There was 3 of us so we just shared all the plates.

    First course - palak chaat, achari mushrooms, and bombay bhel
    Second course - tandoori shrimp and 2 of the kobe chopan kebob
    Third course - Xacutti Pork Tenderloin, Butter Chicken, and Kale Saag Paneer
    Fourth course - we all got the Tashan Creme Brulee

    The creme brulee with gulab jamun center was absolutely delicious. It was a great combination of two amazing desserts and not too sweet. The Kobe kebob was packed with a lot of flavor and some heat. I normally don't like chaat but the palak chaat was so crispy and very tasty. The tandoori shrimp had a delicious coconut sauce with it but it could've used more spice. The Kale Saag Paneer was one of my favorites too. Really creamy and the paneer cheese was so good.

    For people that like really spicy indian food - this place isn't for you. I think everything had really good flavor and some heat but no where near to what traditional indian food usually has.

    Growing up my best friend in the neighborhood was Indian. Milap and I would ride our big wheels, climb trees, just do little kid stuff all day long and then at night his mom would occasionally invite me over for these grand Indian dinners. Ironically Milap hated Indian food and would sneak me his dinner under the table in napkins. Then after we (I) were done eating we would sneak in his dad's food truck and Milap would gorge himself with Skittles and Crunch Bars. This was great for me because I loved the exotic flavors and although it led to childhood obesity Milap seemed to like our arrangement as well.

    As time went on we moved out of the neighborhhod and my fond memories of Indian food began to dissipitate. Life however has a way of bringing us back to the familiar and boo and I ended up moving a few blocks from Tashan. This provided me an opportunity to rediscover the flavors of my youth. I'd been hesitant to go because the reviews seem to be all over the place and it was really hard to get a read on. Boo has wanted to go here for a long time and last weekend I decided we'd check it out (half hoping it would be bad so I'd have full say over our next outing).

    We went on a perfect early fall evening, it was about 75 degrees with a light breeze for our 8 o'clock reservation. We opted to sit outside however this was quickly curbed by some misguided bros sitting on the balcony in the apartment above us, saying rude ish and actually throwing a RItz cracker down by us. Honestly I was fine with the Ritz, I love Ritz but it was kind of annoying hearing them so we went back inside. The staff couldn't have been more accomodating.

    After our host got our drink orders the waiter came over and explained the menu. There was a tasting menu with or without wine pairing which looked great but for our first time we wanted to try some staples. The menu has an explanation of the different Indian styles that the dishes are cooked in which was helpful. To start off we went with the Samosa Chaat and the Naga Beef Sumi. We took small bites in between sips of Kingfisher which was the perfect accompiniment. They were both excellent with my nod going towards the beef.

    For entrees we got the Lamb Vindaloo and the Chicken Binyari with a side the plain naan. The dishes were served over rice and they were both seasoned to perfection but the real star might have been the naan. It was so flavorful and if I wasn't worried about it killing my buzz I would have gotten 3 more orders of it.
    All in all the entire night was great. The service was some of the best I've had in the city and the food was really nostalgic for me. Don't get me wrong, the food was excellent but the experience as a whole was really what gets it into the 5 star realm. I wish I could come here with Milap so he could sneak me his order under the table.

History

Established in 2011.

Tiffin started as a web only and delivery only concept out of a small store front in the Northern Liberties area of Philadelphia and soon evolved into maybe the first multi platform restaurant in the country, offering a full ecommerce website, delivery and carry out on the phone and dine-in, all from the same location. The successful formula led to the opening of the second location in Mt. Airy followed by Elkins Park, Wynnewood and Bryn Mawr, sandwiched around the opening of Tiffin etc., an Indian street food concept. The very latest from tiffin is a 5,000 square foot theatrical ethnic restaurant. The focus of this first-class restaurant will be on sophisticated Indian fusion grills using time honored Indian grilling methods. Unique and unconventional to the east coast, this groundbreaking restaurant concept will transform the Philadelphia dining scene and thus serve as both an amenity to Philadelphians and as a destination location for people throughout the tri-state area.

Meet the Business Owner

Munish N. Business Owner

Munish Narula, a former Investment Banker and a Wharton MBA is the founder and president of Tiffin Indian cuisine. After earning a Bachelor of Science in Hotel Restaurant Management and a MS in Hospitality Administration from Johnson & Wales University, he started working for a full service chain restaurant where he quickly moved up the ladder to reach the position of Area Manager with 11 restaurants under his management. After earning a MBA degree in Finance from Wharton and a stint with bulge bracket Investment Bank, he came back to the food service business when he founded, the now nationally acclaimed, Karma restaurant in Philadelphia. It was during his time with Karma that he identified the need of providing fresh nutritious Indian food to people at affordable prices and so the idea for Tiffin.com was born.
