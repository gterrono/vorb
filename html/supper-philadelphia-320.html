    I came here for a livingsocial deal that paid for a 4 course meal and my boyfriend and I paid for the rest. I must say that this meal was quite the adventure. It was wonderful - and I really don't have many negative things to say.

    The service was great and our waitress was quite attentive. We started off the meal with rosemary bread that reminded me of a popover. Luscious and soft. Then we had their signature deviled eggs. I forget what they were but one of them was really good. Sadly, I wish I had written it down! We had fried brussel sprouts, pear salad, and cauliflower soup. The fried brussel sprouts were delicious - not because of the sprouts sadly because many of them were just burnt and charred but actually the sauce that came witth it was fantastic! It was the right amount of tang to counteract the bitterness of the sprouts that was a mixture of burnt and natural brussel sprout friedness. The cauliflower soup was so smooth and wonderful. There was some sort of oil that was dotted on top of it and the creaminess of the soup meshed so well with the cauliflower that was pureed. The salad was also very very simple but for some reason, both of us really loved it. We still have yet to pinpoint why.

    Then we had potato wedges with duck confit and waffles. We both ordered the duck confit and waffles and boy  are we glad that we did. The duck confit was cooked to perfection with a wonderfully crisp skin and the meat was just tender and divine. My boyfriend was upset with me when I didn't eat half of the skin after he made me feel bad for eating the first half...it was meant to be a joke. Sadly, the loss of the duck skin didn't make it very funny. Also, if you love potatoes, you would LOVE those potato wedges. I ended up eating most of it with that sauce which was so so good. For dessert, we had their pie of the day - YUM! I believe it was oats and raisin pie and there was also an apple crumble. By this point, we were so stuffed that I couldn't really appreciate all the desserts that came out by this point.

    Overall, we would definitely come back again without a doubt. The food was delicious and if you could catch the livingsocial deal - this meal is so well worth it!

    Listed in Best Philly Brunch

    So thoroughly impressed with my dining experience here.

    Came here for brunch this morning with 4 friends to celebrate my birthday. I've had my eye on Supper's brunch menu for a while now, so it was exciting to finally go!

    The decor of the restaurant was absolutely adorable. The slight open kitchen gave us a peak into the food preparation.

    We opted for the following:

    -Supper frittata with caramelized onions and gruyere
    -Mushroom toast
    -Red velvet waffles
    -Banana cream pie french toast
    -Butterfinger torte

    The last three dishes were phenomenal with the frittata being a standard egg dish. The mushroom toast was one of the best brunch dishes I've ever had-- the toast was thick and chewy and the flavorings creamy with the broken yolk.

    The red velvet waffles were sweet, but not TOO sweet to the point where they were overwhelming. Thick and fluffy with amazing cream cheese topping, the waffles were not dry as well.

    The french toast was one of the best french toasts I've tasted. Moist and fresh, the bread texture was on point and the sauces combined to create a wonderful burst of sweetness and banana flavoring.

    I'd definitely come back to the restaurant (especially to devour that mushroom toast again). For dessert, we opted for a butterfinger-esque dessert (multilayered peanut butter and butterfinger type of cake) with peanut butter ice cream. It was absolutely AMAZING so I'd definitely recommend a dessert.

    Not too pricey, and pretty good portion sizes as well. This restaurant is definitely a keeper!

    PARTIAL REVIEW

    Drink
    -Supper is one of Philly's many BYOB's but there are plenty of reasons not to bother bringing your own. From about 10 craft beers, cheeky original cocktails ($8-14), a dozen International wines by the glass ($8-12) and reasonably-priced bottles ($40-120), you're going to probably want to drink at Supper.
    -At brunch, there are all the usual liquid suspects including Mimosas, Bellinis, Irish Coffee, and regular coffee and tea, and they also offered about 5 or so signature cocktails.

    Sangria Blanca ($8)
    Sauvignon Blanc, Melon, Cucumber, Lemon, Limoncello, Honey, Mint Syrup with Club Soda

    Simple and refreshing- an easy daytime summer sipper.

    Just Like Heaven ($8)
    St Germain Elderflower Liqueur, Lemon Juice, Sparkling Wine

    My favourite of the bunch- I loved the floral flavour enhanced bubbles.

    Cherry Bomb ($10)
    Tequila, Cherry Bitters, Lime Juice, Sour Cherry Quinine Syrup

    A sweet yet stronger drink than the aforementioned two. I liked the mild bitter bite of the quinine syrup, but I thought the drink could have used a touch more lime to balance out the sweet.

    Food

    -Supper's menu is American comfort fare featuring a range of International inspirations that change to reflect the seasonal nature of ingredients. Most of the menu items are shareable so that tables can order a good variety of items to share.
    -On the first Sunday of every month, there is a Sunday Supper where meals are served family style beginning at 6:30 PM
    -The dinner menu is divided into snacks, charcuterie and cheese platters, veggies, grain based dishes, meats and fish, sides and desserts.
    -At brunch, when we were dining, there are more than enough items to choose. From eggs with pimento cheese or grits, to sweets like dessert-style French toast or blue corn pancakes, to lunch items like sandwiches and deep-fried hot dogs, and side dishes of breakfast meats and biscuits.  They also have a super cute "old school" cereal buffet where you enjoy as much as you want for $7. Sounds like a good option for the kids, but let's be honest, I was tempted to pay $7 just to try the cookie crisp (which as a kid, I saw commercials for but couldn't get it in Canada).

    Banana Cream Pie French Toast ($15)
    Banana Custard, Fresh Banana, Nilla Wafers, Salted Caramel

    Honestly, some of the best French toast I've ever tasted. The thick-cut bread had been generously soaked in the custard to yield a texture of moist bread pudding on the inside. After the soaking procedure, I'm not exactly sure if they used a deep-fryer or just pan-fried the heck out of that puppy, but man, did it ever make for a golden crust. Paired with sweet creamy custard, crunchy cookies, fresh banana and luscious caramel, I almost licked the plate.

    Chilled Avocado Cucumber Soup ($11)
    Pickled Jicama, Corn Truffles, Aleppo Pepper

    This was from the "lunch" area of the menu, and was a very generous portion of soup. I loved the combination of the creamy puree with the umami-rich corn truffles, sprightly jicama and mild heat of the Aleppo pepper. A nice light dish to balance out the rich French toast.

    Caramelized Onion Hash Browns ($5)

    Wicked crispy hash browns that were flawlessly seasoned. Definitely worth an order as a side.

    Lemon Meringue Doughnuts ($8)
    Lemon Curd, Toasted Meringue, Broken Pie Crust

    I will never turn down doughnuts, and these babies didn't disappoint. They were crispy on the outside, and ethereal within, with the sweetness balanced out by the bright lemon curd. Admittedly, I found the pie crust a bit redundant as there wasn't any excess curd to moisten it up. Having said that, I thought this was a very tasty treat.

    Humongous Dark Chocolate Cake ($12)
    Chocolate Sauce, Cocoa

    We ordered this as the birthday cake of the day and my goodness, was it ever rich. I'm not a chocolate lover, but even I found this deeply satisfying. One bite and you've probably got your fix. That's the way chocolate should be.

    Closing Remarks

    -I wasn't sure how much the bill came to since it also included four other people's food and drink, but I thought in general the prices were fair. This isn't a cheap eats brunch spot, and its sophistication does seem a bit out of place in the "hip" South St hood, but I thought it was a tasty choice. The food was very tasty and well prepared, the drinks were agreeable, and I liked the bright, inviting and cheerful atmosphere.  I think it made for a really good option for bringing family when you don't want to be rushed through a leisurely weekend meal.
    -Supper is open for weekend Brunch and Dinner 7 days a week, with reservations available online.

Everything was really bitter, and while the tomatoes were beautiful and delicious, everything just didnt seem to work together in the salad.

Specialties

Supper serves seasonal modern Amercian cuisine in a beautiful Urban Farmhouse setting.  Whenever and wherever possible we source local, organic and sustainable ingredients.  All charcuterie, preserves, pickles and desserts are made in house as well as the signature housebaked crackers for our All American artisan cheese selection.
Supper is open 7 nights for dinner service beginning at 5pm.  We are open for lunch Wednesdays through Saturdays from 11:30 am to 2:30 pm and for our acclaimed Sunday brunch from 11:00 am to 2:30 pm.
Supper is also available for private events.  We can custom design a menu for you through our catering company, The Global Dish or from our a la carte menu selections.  Please contact Sue Solomon so that we can help you plan an event to remember.
