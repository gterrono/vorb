    Up until the past ~6 months or so, Koreana was my go-to place; I ordered nearly 1x/week and looked forward to bringing friends to try it, etc. Their spicy dishes were great, the food was cheap, and it was close to campus. What's wrong with that?

    Unfortunately (and trust me, it took a lot for me to get to this point), the quality has been steadily declining. My favorite dish has gone from 1/20 times being a little 'off' to tasting worse every time I order it. I took a friend two weeks ago who declared her food inedible; another a month and a half ago said the meat in her dish was "verging on gross" but I ignored all these signals. My love for Koreana was so strong and deep, I kept thinking it was just an off day, but now I have to admit it's not as good as it used to be.

    Anyway, I don't think Koreana is bad for dinner with the other food options on Penn's campus...there's definitely worse out there. But in terms of lunch, there's more than a couple of food trucks I would much rather go to than Koreana again.

    I will not claim to be as well versed in the Korean food segment as I am in other areas of the gastronomic universe, however, I will say that my judgment of Koreana is based upon 4 years of solid undergraduate reflection and a fraternity of several like-minded Asian brethren...

    What's great, or not-so-great, depending on what you prefer, about Koreana is the sheer presence of International (read: Korean) Penn students that flock here daily. Sometimes I think that people come here just to feel more at "home." When's the last time you had homemade "Soondubu jigae"? Probably pre-college and likely made by a relative who was using a recipe passed down through the ages.

    They do have these great boxed meals that resemble Japanese bento boxes. They run at around $10 but you get two dumplings, steamed rice, a portion of meat (go for galbi/kalbi), some salad, and 5 pieces of kimbap (like Korean sushi).

    The food, to me, tastes pretty good (my buddies have informed me that it's the best you'll get around these parts....though we're talking about West Philadelphia... what demographic do you think we're in? haha). I enjoy the strong, spicy, savory taste of gojujang in my food and it's a great addition to a nice hot bibim bap ($7, kind of like a rice hot pot with egg, meat, and veggies). For people who've never been (read: White people), you're definitely going to feel out of place, especially when you see the menu and can't decipher or pronounce any item. And no, you can't default to fried rice and spring rolls here. If you're in doubt though, feel free to ask the owners, they speak english better than you do.

    Another great aspect about this restaurant, which I'm sure the Koreans love, is that it also sells Korean supermarket snacks like Korean ChocoPie!! :D Dunno how much it costs, but I'm sure you'd be willing to pay a premium for some Capn Crunch Berry if you found yourself hungry in China's Fujian Provence -- LOL JK!!!

    If you're in the mood for boba (bubble tea), they also serve that here. It's not my favorite (you should head to china town for that), but it certainly does the trick if you're on campus.

    Favorite Dishes:
    Beef Galbi
    Bibim Bap
    Beef Mapa Dubu

    There's enough variety on the menu that you could try something new everyday. They're usually closed on Mondays and the Kimchi is average in case you were wondering.

    Koreana makes quick food, so I'll make a quick review.

    1. The Dwen Jang Chigae is spicy and delicious. It's got rice cakes, vegetables, and mushrooms galore. It's hearty enough to be consumed as a meal...and that rice on the side is damn good. It's sticky and glutinous and probably made in a pot, not some white-folk rice cooker.

    2. The Korean Pancake is actually the best I've had in Philly. It's gooey and comes out hot and looks like it could melt off the plate. The seafood is baked in it, and there isn't any stale fishy flavor or smell emanating from its steam.

    3. The kimchi is boring and not fresh but it's spicy, so the little side cup of it is fine to munch on while you're waiting for the rest of your party to receive their food.

    I wish we had a place like this in College Park... I would be a frequent diner! We had about an hour to kill before hitting the road after an overnight trip to Philly and our Korean food cravings brought us here. There is a surprising amount of seating space--both indoor and outdoor--given the "hole in the wall" feel of the restaurant.

    They sell a ton of Korean snacks and drinks at the counter, and offer an extensive menu of authentic Korean fare (in case you haven't yet realized this is a review for a Korean(a) restaurant). There is also bubble tea available.

    The food is extremely affordable, which must be much of the reason why the place was jam-packed with college students when I came to visit on a Friday afternoon. I can certainly relate to having a pinched wallet, so I was thoroughly relieved to see prices of around $5-8 for each dish--just about what you'd pay for a Chipotle burrito bowl!

    I ordered the jajangmyun and kimbap, and paid around $12. The food came quickly and the portion size is generous, again, for the price. You also get a small SOLO container of kimchi and pickled radish. I wish I had ordered the bibimbap because my friend's came out sizzling in a hot pot and was a HUGE portion size. However, my jajangmyun was as authentic as I've had it. The kimbap was okay, but the rice was a little overcooked. It's not something I would get again, personally.

    Although I live almost 3 hours away in Maryland... if I'm ever back in this area, I won't hesitate if I am ever craving Korean food again. Spot on!
