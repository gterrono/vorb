    i wish the hours were a little later, though, especially on Fridays and Saturdays. i wouldn't hurt if they stayed open past midnight. still - i would be lost without Lazaro's.

    Let's get one thing straight:  you don't go to Lazaros for the happy atmosphere and jolly staff, you go to Lazaros for quality new-york style thin-crust pizza.

    Lazaros isn't a welcoming shop, the place looks a bit run-down and the seating area next door can sometimes carry an air of neglect; the place is almost constantly busy, so the staff have a very in and out ASAP mentality, and they can be especially short on the phone.  However, all of these seemingly negative attributes are made up for by the taste of the pizza.

    The pricing is relatively low, and the portions relatively large, a win win for me, but they only accept cash (there is an ATM on premises) so come prepared.

    About the pizza:  the sauce has a distinctive sweetness to it, and is never too liberally or conservatively spread on the crust, it's always just right.  The cheese tastes fresh, as do their meats and veggies, a breath of fresh air when compared to many of Philly's dime a dozen pizza shacks.  They have a wide variety of specialty pizzas as well, for those who want a bit of differentiation to their pie, and their Sicilian is more than adequate.

    This is the place to go for pizza lovers in Philly, and IMO one of the best in the city.
