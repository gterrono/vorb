    I picked up my food to go last week after finding out it had opened up the week before. Online ordering was a breeze and they offered delivery but I had to see it for myself. I was extremely excited that a dedicated kati roll place had opened up in Center City and was open late. Their menu is sparse, but I think they are trying to focus on getting a few things right, and I think they succeed!

    I tried all 3 paneer rolls (tikka, aachari, and aloo), and they all hit the spot just right. I do wish the tikka paneer was more tikka, but that's just me being difficult. Otherwise, great spices, nice onion and chutney kick, and their roll was not as greasy as the NYC joint makes theirs. Definite plus (even though late night, you might crave the grease)! I would probably get 2 of the tikka rolls again as the alooo paneer (potato and cheese) roll was mild and the aachari paneer (pickle and paneer) roll had a good, but overpowering flavor. I would actually recommend the aloo roll for beginners and the aachari for a more acclimated palate.

    They take a while to make them, because even coming in 30 minutes after I ordered, it wasn't ready, but you can see them making each roll through the little window in the back, so I didn't mind. Also, the mango lassi was pretty good (again, I'm just picky and wish it was colder!)

    For the uninitiated, each roll is about the size of a mini- burrito and fits perfectly in one large sized hand. That means it's not super filling, but it's good for a heavy snack, and if you get multiple it can easily be a moderately priced meal. If you do go for multiples, get the 2-for-$1-off special as long as it lasts! Each roll doesn't come with extra frills (but they do offer a great white sauce and red sauce if you want to mellow or kick up the spice, respectively), so don't expect what you are used to from a traditional Punjabi restaurant like papadam or chutneys or even rice (unless you order the platters).

    This place is going to be a hit, I just know it. Next time, I'll make sure they make my paneer extra tikka!

    Spice End attempts to recreate the New York, quasi-Indian street food classics kathi rolls and platters. They succeed probably better than anyone else in Philly, albeit at a steep cost.

    Platters come with meats layered on top of rice and are served with 2 sauces (very spicy red and creamy white), toasted pita, and an indifferent salad of iceberg lettuce and tomato. The sauces display less thickness than the iconic NYC ones but mirror the sauces' taste pretty faithfully. The fluffy rice tastes light due to a lack of excess seasoning and grease. The meats we sampled were all well-seasoned and cooked, and the Spice End platter (essentially the chef's personal rendition of classic chicken and rice) outshone the others with large, juicy chunks of aromatically spiced chicken. Though we didn't sample any kathi rolls, I've heard from numerous sources that they're decent.

    Pricing acted as the sole negative of my Spice End experience; platters clock in at around $9, a few dollars more expensive than the cart versions. Spice End offers very decent recreations of beloved street foods, and is definitely worth visiting when the insatiable craving for platters strike. If they reduced their prices a bit, this could easily become a regular destination.

    Though i have long been mourning the loss of Jojo's and their fried wontons, I was giddy with excitement when I heard an Indian street food joint decided to take over  ...

    Kati rolls have long been a street food staple of urban populous in bustling cities like Kolkata and Mumbai and have recently garnered some attention stateside, mostly in NYC. Though I admit it did take a bit long for philly to catch up on the trend, I am glad Spice End is doing it right.

    One has a variety of options for the fillings , but the concept is simple, a plethora of flavors from spices, marinated meats and dash of seasonings all bundled into one thin crispy paratha, to pack a punch all the way  to the last bite.

    We sampled the Achari chicken and paneer .. Which was well marinated and tangy cos of the Indian pickles (more of a lime or a mango base in contrast to a vinegar base). Also got a Frankie ( the egg and chicken ) or as I call it the Mumbai burrito where the Paratha is doubled up with an egg base on the griddle. The aloo (potato) paneer had a nice kick to it too.

    They all went well with the red and the white sauces provided on the side, so i guess you can't go much wrong from blindly picking one from the menu. Though if you do feel lost, the staff is nice and friendly enough to help you pick something. I will prolly try and go back and try the platters sometime soon ...

    So If a ravenous hunger pang beckons 2 of these rolls should do the trick ,  though one roll will easily satiate those late night munchies given that they are also open till late on most nights.

    P.S : They also stack ThumsUp and Limca in the fridge, some potent Indian soda if you feel inundated with spices.

    I remember what life was like in the before times, the darkest times. Philly's drunks were consistently bereft of acceptable sustenance. They starved in the streets. They wasted away. They died by the thousands.

    Spice End received its namesake because it represented the "end" of the before times. No longer were the denizens of the late night forced to trek to Gay Pizza in order to stay alive (a trip that most did not survive when dragging their emaciated bodies from Rittenhouse). Spice End saved us. It saved me.

    The Kati rolls are fantastic. The paneer tikka and achari chicken rolls are excellent. They've got a great aromatic flavor and the roll itself is delightfully doughy.

    Protip: This place has an atrocious late-night crowd. If you go in cold during a popular drinking hour, the line will be filled with loud jackasses. Not only will the line take forever, the massive number of orders will have you waiting upwards of 30 minutes for your food. This is unacceptable.

    Your best bet is to call in a take-out order and then kill time at 1 Tippling place with a drink. This will make your night progress far more efficiently.

History

Established in 2013.

Intorducing Spice End - Kati Roll and Platters
Spice End Kati Roll and platters located in Central Philly resonates the enthusiasm, flavor, authenticity and vivaciousness of the city. It represents Indian cuisine with a twist. Amidst the hustle of the city this place gives you an option of having food full of flavors at affordable prices which you can eat while walking, sitting or over a conversation with a friend, in hurry or at peace in the restaurant. Spice End redefines Indian Street food on Streets of Central Philly engulfing the modernization of its surroundings.

Kati Rolls: The essence of this place.
For those who are new to this concept, KATI Rolls are soft, warm perfectly rolled Indian flatbread, stuffed with variety of fillings such as vegetables, chicken, lamb, beef, potatoes and cottage cheese, which are perfectly marinated in mouthwatering Indian spices. Our rolls are served with special sauces which add the oomph element and complete the food.
