    I came for an amazing Philly cheese steak with the whole family, and overall, I'm leaving satisfied.

    Pros: cheese steak with brocolli rabe and provolone - yum, yum, yum!  Full of flavor and just the right level of greasiness with no aftertaste. The bread was the perfect balance between fresh and strong enough to handle the 'juice.' I ended up splitting my sandwich and it was just enough. Chicken version also got rave reviews from a family member.

    Cons: the sauce on the Buster sandwich my siblings got; really took away from the flavor of the meat. The long line wasn't too exciting either, especially when we couldn't snag a place to sit (we ate the sandwiches in the car). They also messed up the order on 2 sandwiches, which we found out after we left. If the food wasn't as tasty as it was, that would have been a deal breaker.

    The food was good enough that I'd eat it again but I'll probably try a new cheese steak joint next time I'm driving through!

    Tony Luke's was one of my backup lunch options during my visit to Philadelphia, but boy, am I happy to have ended up eating there.

    After realizing that John's Roast Pork was closed that morning, I made the 2-minute drive south to Tony Luke's. The restaurant consists of what's essentially a service window and a bunch of tables under a roof. The line was quite long, but it moved quickly.

    Rather than have Tony Luke's signature cheesesteak sandwich, I opted for what I had planned to eat that morning and ordered the Pork Italian, which included roast pork, my choice of broccoli rabe or spinach (I had spinach), and sharp provolone.

    While the bun was a little soggy from all the oil, the combination of roast pork, spinach, and sharp provolone was amazing. After eating this sandwich, which was easily a foot long, I didn't even feel an ounce of hunger until late in the the night. It was that filling. I'd definitely come back the next time I'm in Philly.

    Tip: While you wait for your order to be made, have some sliced pickles from the condiments section! They made the wait time feel a lot shorter than it really was.

    When visiting Philly, its essential to get a least one philly steak in.  I've been to most of the other famous places, but finally made it here after recommendations from my Philly friends.

    Although the line was long, it moved quickly and we found ourselves seated with drinks after about 15 minutes.  The food came out after another 10, so not bad at all.

    I went with the classic steak (I can't eat cheese), while my husband ordered the cheese steak hoagie with lettuce, tomatoes, mayo and pickles.  Both came out super hot and they were really huge.  We could have split one, easily.

    I was happy with the overall flavor and liked the steak a lot.  It was more like thinly sliced roast beef than the chopped version they have at places like Jim's.  I wasn't a huge fan of the bread though.  It became really soggy after a few bites and my sandwich fell apart.  I still ate the shit out of it, but it wasn't as tasty as some of the other philly steak places I like.  

    I did like the condiment bar, which made it easy to get my sandwich the way I wanted.  A little hot sauce, some ketchup - hellz yeah.

    Overall, it was a tasty sandwich, but not my favorite.  I liked the steak itself a lot but w/out my favorite bread, it just wasn't perfect to me.  However, my husband absolutely loved the hoagie style - so try that if you haven't had the chance yet.  I would definitely come back to try their other offerings which sounded amazing.

    Somewhere in the reviews I've read, it was talking about the lack of service from the order taker.  Well, she was working yesterday afternoon around 4p.  It wasn't so much of being rude, but just the lack of manner, or the tone of voice that could have been friendlier.  Since it was around 4p, there weren't many others, so the wait was minimum of about 5min.
