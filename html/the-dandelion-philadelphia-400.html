    REALLY GOOD STUFF!  

    THE SCENE: We've passed this place on so many occasions and never ventured in.  We'd heard great things about Dandelion - but the signage is so small, that we had no idea that this was the place on the corner of 18th & Sansom.  After walking through the massive door, I sorta felt like Alice after falling through the rabbit hole - not entirely sure why, but I did!  The hostess took us up the stairs and through room after room before finally seating us.  It was a random Wednesday evening and this place was PACKED!  

    THE LIBATIONS: Shikari - featuring jose cuervo silver, st. germain (my personal favorite), honey, lime & CAYENNE PEPPER, definitely packed a nice little punch!  The Pimms cup was also a solid choice and by the looks of the tables around us, a pretty popular choice...

    THE MEAL: We started with the chicken and duck liver parfait.  As odd as it sounds, it is that much delicious! Classic Brit style fish & chips and slow roasted pork belly for our entrees.  Large portions removed us from the "clean plate club" on this occasion, but we enjoyed every bite nonetheless.

    THE CREW: Very friendly / pleasant hostesses and wait staff.  Helpful with cruising the menu and making selections, and not at all pushy.

    We are already planning our next visit...

    Those that know me, know that I am NOT a follower of trendy food establishments.  I will usually get around to trying them, but on my own terms and not because "its all the rage".  Mr. Starr may not have hit it out of the park with each new venture, but this place is done RIGHT!

    The Dandelion is teeming with charm. It's a huge old house that's been converted to a classy restaurant with a 1920s vibe. It doesn't feel like a pub (except in the room with the long bar downstairs), even though it's clearly English-inspired. I really liked the feel, it was what I imagine visiting Teddy Roosevelt's house would be like, but with a whole bunch of revelers.
    It took my group of 3 forever to get seated, because this place is super popular. Make a reservation well in advance, learn from our mistake. When we did get a table, it was like an end table at grandma's house. An end table that is big enough to fit a little bowl of starlight mints and a small lamp. Maybe a Reader's Digest condensed book teetering on the edge. So when we ordered food, it was so cramped we had to put our water glasses on the mantle of the fireplace we were sitting next to. We managed to precariously fit our 3 plates on the table, but it was totally inadequate. There was a couple next to us that had an even worse situation than we did. They were basically on armchairs, with a coffee table to eat off. I mean, I do that most nights at home, but this place is supposed to be classier than my embarrassing Tuesday night dinner in front of Jeopardy. But, that was the only thing that wasn't fantastic about The Dandelion.
    Each food item we tried was excellent. I went with the burger because I am a simple man. It was perfect. Cooked exactly as requested, with the tastiest pickles and bacon on the planet. It's $16.50, but it's fucking fancy so deal with it or go to a crappier place and get a cheap, crappy burger. My friends loved the deviled eggs but I don't like curry so I won't weigh in on those.
    More importantly, the drinks were spot on. My friend got a gross sounding drink with egg whites, beer, rum, and apricot marmalade that tasted like the best milkshake you can imagine. So be adventurous! These bartenders are the real deal, and can make you a ridiculous cocktail. Service was great across the board. But, the bar was a thousand degrees in mid-April and I definitely would not recommend sitting in that section for your meal. Sit upstairs or in the foyer, but try to get a table that is conducive to a meal.

Specialties

London's culinary revolution comes to Philadelphia with this eclectic English pub. Cozy rooms and intimate bars create a storied, Old World aura, while updated seasonal takes on British food make up the menu. In keeping with tradition, cask and tap craft beers are always rotating, a proper afternoon tea service has a sweet and savory menu, and classic beef family-style roasts are served on Sundays.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
