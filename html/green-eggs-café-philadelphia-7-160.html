    Awful service. AWFUL SERVICE. If I could give a 2.5 I would. However, the food saves them, barely.

    My food was good, admittedly. The service ruined the majority of the experience, but my food was delicious. The red velvet pancakes that my friend had were okay, not amazing. I could probably make better so I wouldn't suggest anyone going out of their way to come here for that.

    However, the Kitchen Sink is off the hook. It was very very delicious and savory- my favorite type of breakfast. I will warn you that you will not be able to eat all of it. It's actually perfect to share with 2. I honestly was only able to eat a 1/4 of it before packing it to go. And the leftovers where just as good. Also, I ordered the Stone Ground Grits and the taste and texture of the grits, combined with the deliciously crispy scrapple was the best way to finish off a deliciously savory breakfast. Even as a leftover, as I couldn't finish that either, it was great.

    The service was the biggest turnoff for me. The server we had didn't even deserve the $1 tip we left. He only came to take our orders and didn't even deliver the food. One of the kitchen hands did. He also failed to ever refill our coffee cups, check on us and if everything was ok with our food, or comeback until we literally had to get another waiter to get him to come over to give us our bill. At one point we watched him walk past our table, though he failed to ever make eye contact though we basically went through the motions of trying to catch his attention, and had time to make himself an iced coffee, though he couldn't find time to give us any coffee refills.

    The food was their only saving grace- and I can go to other Green Eggs for that.

    I visited two separate Iron Chef flagship restaurants in Philly and enjoyed both. Immensely. That said, the singular best dish that I enjoyed in all of Philadelphia was the pork belly benedict at Green Eggs Café.

    We stayed at a boutique hotel across the street from Green Eggs and immediately noticed the crowd congregating at GEC each morning. . .and well into the afternoon. When in Rome, do as the locals do. . .so on our final morning we did exactly that. Fortunately, we went during downtime and didn't even have to wait for a table.

    The service was a bit slow - perhaps due to the post-crowd malaise, but we eventually received our coffee & menus. The menu looked brilliant; very nice selection of breakfast, brunch & lunch items - all appearing fresh and unique, but not outlandish. What caught my eye was the pork belly benedict. I ordered that, expecting a typical benedict, with a sliver of pork belly replacing the ham in a normal benedict.

    What I received was a sliver - a sliver of Heaven. In lieu of an english muffin, the base was brioche; sweet and soft, but crusted slightly on the outside enabling a good support unit for the perfectly poached/runny egg, which was complemented by pickled red onions. Most impressively, two well endowed cubes of tender, luscious pork belly were served adjacent to the two brioche halves. Tying the dish together was a dreamy maple rosemary cream (light on the rosemary) that made the dish dance with delight. Each bite was better than the last, and despite the generous portion, I was left screaming (internally) for more.

    An absolutely transcendental breakfast/brunch, and a wonderful experience. The best dish I've had in 2013, in fact. If you go there, you MUST order this dish. Wow.

    Also note: they ONLY accept cash, and though there is an ATM - make sure you come with some money. They'll make you wash dishes otherwise (just kidding - I think).

    Green Eggs and Ham? More like Clean Eggs and Pork Belly. Wow.

    This is the third Green Eggs Cafe to open in the City.  There are also locations in South Philly and Northern Liberties.  I have been to all three, and the cafes all provide a warm modern atmosphere and very friendly service.  They are by far some of the best places to get breakfast/brunch in the city.  While the atmosphere and service are great, the food is even better.  If you are looking for something savory they make an excellent steak & eggs, a delicious breakfast burrito, or the very filling "kitchen sink".  However, if you want something a little sweeter they still have it covered with options like red velvet pancakes or creme brulee french toast.  I recommend taking someone with you and sharing something savory and something sweet.  The only drawback is that they do not accept credit cards, so remember to bring cash.    If you go on the weekend, you will also need to be prepared for at least a short wait before being seated.  However, it is definitely worth it.  I continue to go back to this place and am never disappointed.
