    Restaurant week is always a fun week. This year I found myself stuck with the lady friend at Garces Trading Company. After reading reviews we walked in apprehensive about what to expect, nevertheless we were impressed, but with a few minor nitpicks that we noticed with the food.

    The atmosphere was very relaxing. The wooden decor and the ambiance was casual, yet elegant. The store front and the wine store were very nice, and also very cool.

    The service was superb. Our waiter, was very nice and provided a great dining experience worthy of his tip. Water was consistently filled, bread was consistently brought out throughout the meal, and the waiter was very polite. Overall service deserves 5/5.

    So lets go through the menu items that we got. First we ordered the cheese plate, which consisted of a semi-hard cheese similar to parm or asiago. A dipping sauce that was a citrus based honey accompanied it, which was fantastic. The cheese was one of the best cheeses I have ever tasted, and I actually bought a half pound of it upon leaving. This cheese dish was a little light on how much they gave you, but was delicious.

    The seasonal antipasti was the second dish we got in the first course, which was fairly good. The black garlic based pesto was perfect to accompany the cauliflower, that also had an herbal oil as a dressing on top of it. The dish was good, but I think they could have added a bit more than just cauliflower to accompany the strong taste of black garlic.

    The funghi pizza was a real highlight to us. The dough is cooked in duck fat which created a product that was the best tasting dough I have ever had on a pizza. The mushrooms were great along with the truffles, we just couldnt stop eating this dish. Absolutely one of the best pizza's I have ever had (wouldn't really classify it as a pizza however). The mushrooms were very fresh tasting with very earthy tones.

    The Lyonnaise salad was interesting, However I was not the biggest fan. The texture of the potatoes, were lost in the dish along side the vegetables and the duck confit. On this dish the poached egg went great with the duck confit, but the potatoes, not so much. Was not extremely impressed with this dish to be honest.

    The third course, we got the Fusilli Pasta and the Poussin. The Fusilli pasta was great. Perfectly al dente, the cheese sauce was not runny, nor too thick like a rue, but really was great. This dish however lacked the content of more meat from the guanciale, which was good, but a very small portion that lent very little to the dish. The poached egg atop this dish was not properly cooked and found it to actually be overcooked which made the texture of the egg very displeasing. Overall though, the dish was decent. The Poussin dish though, was great. The "baby chicken" of sorts was perfectly cooked, and plated very nicely. The seasoning was fantastic and tasted like it was baked/smoked. My gripe with this dish was the cold potato salad with hot chicken. The potato salad was cold and the tempertaure change between the potatoes and the meat was displeasing. However the flavors of the dish were wonderful and very enjoyable. I would order this again.

    The pumpkin cake was eh. Not a huge fan as ginger and cinnamon flavors overtook the natural flavors from the pumpkin. The portion was also very small and the addition of raspberry left us a little confused. The chocolate panna cotta though, was great. Very thick, and a little sweet in some parts, but very good.

    Coffee, is highly recommended. You get an entire french press of panama blend coffee for $4.50, which was delicious. I recommend trying that and the expresso, which was also the best expresso I've ever had. Not that I've had a lot. . . but I'm getting there.

    -Dan

    GTC reminds me of a miniature Di Bruno Bros with more of a restaurant feel. The outdoor seating is a nice perk & the inside space is open, airy and inviting.

    They have a vast selection of oils, vinegars and other accoutrements for sampling (I had a fantastic time running around collecting a number of unique flavored oils for us to enjoy alongside our crusty bread).

    And yes, yes, the rumors are true - the funghi pizza is divinely addictive, duck-fat infused dough and all. Stop wondering and TRY IT, already.

The jardinière sandwich left a bit to be desired, however, mainly due the overabundance of an oddly-flavored mayonnaisey condiment which, in my opinion, did not complement the fontina cheese.

    The pumpkin cake with cranberry gelatin was acceptable although I really just wanted that spiced mousse in the middle.

    They also have an enclosed wine store? Random yet MARVELOUS all the same.

    if you are in the area, give this place a try. it is far easier to get a table at lunch, so be prepared and make reservations if you want a spot for dinner. it's a little pricey, but if you're a foodie, you'll appreciate the quality of the dishes.

Specialties

Garces Trading Company is a full-service bar and restaurant focused on Iron Chef Jose Garces' own favorite dishes from his travels to cafes throughout Western Europe, with a focus on Italy, Spain and France.  Our thoughtful menu of cafe staples is served throughout the day, and our bar offers a wealth of cocktails, wines and beers that pair beautifully with our menu.  Our on-site PLCB Wine Boutique, the first of its kind, makes a great resource either before sitting down to dinner (corkage fees apply) or to complement one of our decadent gift baskets, available in our newly redesigned retail section.

History

Established in 2010.

Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a refined, Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, a contemporary American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago, IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale, AZ and Palm Springs, CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the landmark Revel Resort. Today, in addition to our much-lauded restaurants around the country, Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
