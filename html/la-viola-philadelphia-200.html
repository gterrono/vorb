    I'm quite surprised by the negative reviews for La Viola.  Cash only does suck but unless you live off your credit cards, then I don't see the big deal.  It's very inexpensive to eat so you can probably get by with the money in your wallet!!!  As for the wait, even with reservations, yes that does kinda suck but La Viola is not the only place in the city that is guilty of this.  

    Let me say that the "waiting issue" was not a big deal for me.  I think we ended up waiting about 10-15 minutes to be seated even though we had a reservation.  This was made up for by the outstanding service and inexpensive yet amazingly delicious meal.  When I say inexpensive, I mean it.... I think my Gnocchi was $12 and Calamari appetizer was around $7-$8.  

    We all shared each others meals and everything from red to white sauce dishes were  excellent.  The staff would help you pick out a meal and the authentic old-world accents added to the experience.  Many of the staff have a great sense of humor and are fun to interact with.  

    If you love a good BYOB, I highly recommend a visit to La Viola.  Just make sure to set your own expectations as well as your guests.  There may be a little wait even with a reservation during prime time.  Go grab a drink before hand or bring a road soda for your wait before you're seated.  

    Overall, this is a great, non-pretentious place to eat in Rittenhouse.

A friend got the pasta special, which was absolutely incredible. Had I ordered it, I would probably be rating the food a 4.5.

    4.5 stars for the service. Our waiter, Tony, was just the right amount of attentive. One of my friends has a severe gluten allergy, so Italian restaurants can be tricky. Tony was understanding, and explained in detail how things would be made, promised that "fresh" pans would be used, and helped with tailoring the list of ingredients to everything she ordered. Also, he tolerated our rowdy group and 4 bottles of wine, as well as the cat-call app I downloaded on my phone at dinner (The Wharton boys were doing their annual hazing, which meant guys kept walking by in their boxers all night. The app was absolutely necessary).

    Overall, La Viola is a great little BYO, and very reasonably priced for the Rittenhouse area. I don't really understand all the complaints about it being cash only. Do people have really pimped out rewards cards that they want to use? Anyway, stop by the ATM and the liquor store and give La Viola a try.

    Overall cute BYOB in Rittenhouse with good ambiance and friendly service.  Great food, and would likely come back again.

    APPETIZERS:
    Grigliata Di Calamari - 5 stars - My first time having grilled (versus fried) calamari.  This dish was amazing.  The calamari was extremely flavorful and worked well with the little tomato pieces.

    Zuppetta Di Cozze - 4 stars - We tried both the white wine sauce and the traditional spicy plum tomato sauce.  Both were good, although I preferred the white wine sauce.

    MAIN:
    Orecchiette Alla Romana - 4 stars - The shell shaped pasta with chicken, sausage and mushrooms actually worked quite well together.  My first time experiencing chicken and sausage in the same pasta dish.

    So for some reason this place has been distinguished as a Philadelphia staple, or so it seems.  Most people from the area have eaten here at least once before.  Overall, I wasn't overly impressed.  Maybe it's because I'm from Jersey where people REALLY know how to make Italian.  I don't know.

    So to start, we had a 7PM reservation.  If you're gonna go there, definitely get a reservation, but still, definitely expect to wait.  We waited about 30 minutes or so (there were 6 of us), but there were at least 20 people that waited longer than we did.  They have you wait in what's pretty much a hallway outside the main restaurant room.  And from what other reviews seem to note, 30 minutes wasn't bad at all.

    So when we get inside, it's crammed.  Tables for two are right on top of each other (less than a foot between tables).  We're right up against the kitchen so there are waiters and waitresses knocking into our chairs all dinner.  And it's loud.  And the entire time we felt a bit rushed (which I can see why with a ton of people always waiting for a table).

    To start, we ordered some calamari for the table.  It was grilled so it had a lot more flavor than your typical variety.  Sauce it was cooked with was pretty tasty too.

    For dinner, they have all the average Italian stuff on the menu.  Being a bit adventurous, I decided to go with the special for the evening, which was a mix of pasta, chicken, and shrimp with a tomato sauce.  Overall, the dish was kinda blah.  The sauce was very bland.  Chicken and pasta were cooked fine.  But what people say on Yelp about the seafood is absolutely true.  It was terrible.  Previously frozen, very tough consistency, and a flavor that's hard to pinpoint but a flavor I didn't really like at all.  More fishy than shrimpy.  

    It is BYOB, so that's a good thing.  But you can find a lot of other places in Philly that are BYOB.  The appetizer was good too, and some other people at my table liked their entrees (standard Italian stuff that's hard to screw up).  Bottom line: don't come here to get blown away, don't come here if you're impatient as you WILL have to wait, and don't come here to have a good conversation.  I'd recommend looking elsewhere.  Philly has plenty of other restaurants.
