    This restaurant is what it means to stumble upon a gem when you're away from home for a weekend and want a really enjoyable dining experience.

    My friend and I went for Sunday brunch, and were lucky enough to sit outside in the restaurant's back garden. Perfect atmosphere for a Sunday summer brunch. The owner (or manager) was kind enough to hoist a canopy over the tables before the called-for rain arrived.

    We ordered the stuffed french toast, the Betty Draper egg salad sandwich and a side of mac and cheese. The portions are huge. The egg salad is one of the best egg salads I've ever had - made w/capers, shallots, truffle oil and sliced cucumbers. Creative too, and much appreciated as a Mad Men fan. The sandwich came w/a pack of candy cigarettes. As an 80s kids it was absolute nostalgia remembering when it was OK to give your kids fake cigs to puff!

    This will definitely be a go-to whenever I'm traveling in Philly.

    I like places that feel like someone takes pride in offering something of good quality, that comes from the heart, and is not just about turning over tables and making a buck.  That's Wedge & Fig.  Artisanal sandwiches.  Quality ingredients.  Coffee in a French press.  Good selection of cheeses.  And a great patio that is tucked away like some secret place that someone takes you to who knows more than you do.   And the patio has real ambiance, feeling like a little hidden oasis.  It's a great place to take a little pause from an otherwise hectic day and feel like you are in a courtyard in France, and not Philadelphia.  The service, too, is not "Philadelphia" as there is a graciousness and civility about the whole experience that feels distinctly European.
    Would give it a fifth star if there was something truly amazing about the food, but I haven't been there enough to find a favorite.  I would say I have enjoyed everything, including the campy names on the menu, but not enough to make it a destination.  More of a "first choice when in the neighborhood" place for me.  Will return, however, for the overall experience.  Maybe I'll fall in love next time.

I would definitely come back and try more of the cheeses, they have an amazing selection and are glad to help you choose!

Specialties

Old City's Cheese Bistro, featuring dinner, lunch and weekend brunch.  Sample our chicken pot pie, mac & cheese and scrumptious salads in the evening.  For lunch, try  our unique sandwiches, salads and soups (take out available for most items).  Omelettes, strata, cream-cheese-stuffed French toast are some brunch favorites.  Dine under the stars in our secluded garden.  Bring a bottle of wine or ale and we will pair it with a selection of cheeses.  The perfect romantic evening!

History

Established in 2006.

We rescued Old City Cheese Shop just before it closed.  We gave it some badly needed upgrades, changed the menu, re-named the store to wedge+fig.  And we stocked the cheese case with cheese!

Kirk N. Business Owner

Cheese lover from an early age.  Lived on a sailboat for 15 years.  Used to play guitar.  Now I own a restaurant.
