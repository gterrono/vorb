    Is it a Philly sacrilege to confess that I'm not a fan of cheesesteaks?

    I just don't get what the fuss is all about.  Rolls, a bunch of tastless meat and some cheese slathered on is supposed to be the cusine calling card of this city?

    I've made efforts to try and find whether there is that all-magical traditional cheesesteak that tastes like the nectar of the gods -- and I haven't found it.  I'll keep trying though.

    Service here was tepid, no smiles or friendliness to be found.  As for the food: Cheesesteaks here weren't the best, but they weren't certainly the worst.  Sandwich was barely warm - Meat wasn't fatty or veiny gross (like the ones at Pat's)  there wasn't  enough cheese and fried onions weren't sufficiently fried. Roll was fresh. Curly fries were curly.  I gave TL an extra star (instead of the deserved two) because I got a taste  of the roast pork sandwich with sharp prov.  which was hands down better than the cheesteak. Juicy, tasty, cheezy and I bet it's heavenly with some broc. rabe.

    Ok, so I'm not by any means a cheesesteak connoiseur, so take my opinion as a Philly cheesesteak novice for what it is. I am a food snob for sure, but I enjoy a greesy meat sandwich as much as anyone else.

    My friend and I were in Philly for the day and felt like having a cheesesteak so I quickly Yelped the best places in town, we decided on Tony Luke's because apparently it had just won the Throwdown against Bobby Flay.

    My only previous Philly specific cheesesteak experience was at Geno's which was a mixed bag. I hated the way they yelled at me for not knowing how to order, but damn it was good when I got it. That was also about 10 years ago, so memory may not serve me well as my pallette has surely matured since then.

    Tony's is in a random locale and people were parked under I-95 for this place and there was a line about 30 people long, so my expectations were a bit high. I ordered mine with wiz and sauteed onions and peppers because that's how I like mine. I also usually like mine slathered in ketchup, but there was no need for that here. They use Rib Eye, which seems like too nice a cut of meat considering my understanding that a cheesesteak is typically made from a rather crappy cut of meat diced up and slathered in grease and cheese to make it taste better.  It was much better than anything I'd get up here in NYC, but it was still missing something for me. The meat didn't taste all that seasoned and it was lacking a bit of flavor for me. However, my friend got the Italian Steak which consists of sharp provolone and either spinach or broccoli rabe. She chose the rabe and boy was it good. If I ever go back, I'll definitely get that instead because it had a really nice flavor going on. We also ordered cheese fries. They only do them here with wiz, which was not my fave. I personally like mine salted and covered in provolone. They were a little soggy and I didn't eat most of them.

    The line moved fast and the people were friendly. It took about 20 minutes to get our food after we ordered because it was busy, but they seemed to be pretty efficient for the amount of people they had waiting. All-in-all I would probably go back if I was in the area, but I don't think I'd go out of my way.
