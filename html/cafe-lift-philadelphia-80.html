    This place is unassuming from the street.  I saw the sign and figured it was a tiny cafe, and I was pleasantly surprised at how spacious and modern Cafe Lift is.

    I went here with an old friend yesterday (Sunday) for brunch.  We got there around 12:45 and were told it was a half hour wait.  Close to 45 minutes later, we were finally seated.  We had to wait in the entryway in front of the stairs until the upstairs waiting area had been cleared, which kind of sucked.  

    We ended up sitting at the table of 8 near the waiting area, and sat with a group of 6 people since there were open spots.  It was kind of awkward, but we managed.  Our place settings came quickly, as did the water and coffee.

    Our waitress showed up 10 minutes later, no "Hi, How are ya?" just a "Have you decided what you want?". Hrm... ok.  So we ordered and then waited.  And waited.  And waited.  About 20-25 minutes later (my guesstimate based on the time it took to drink close to 2 cups of hot coffee and the entire pitcher of water) our food finally came.  My friend got the Huevos Rancheros, and I had ordered the Fried Egg Panino.

    I must say, their food is DELICIOUS.  My sandwich was rather large and quite tasty.  The egg wasn't over cooked and the hot chili spread was excellent. The hot chili spread was so good, I doused my home fries in it.  Their home fries were big chunks of potato smothered in spices -- very good. My friend enjoyed her Huevos Rancheros as well.

    The next time we saw the waitress was when she gave us our check.  

    I'm guessing that since I'm not the first to comment on their poor service, that this place can get away with it because their food is pretty good.  Considering the place was packed when we got there,  the lack of service and ridiculously long waits don't seem detrimental at this point, however I will bet that at some point it might catch up to them.

    Also, one last thing to note -- this place is CASH ONLY!!  If I had known that beforehand, I probably wouldnt've gone.  They do have an ATM but it charges $1.50.

    On this past Sunday morning, Elizabeth B. and I rolled up to Cafe Lift for a brunch a little closer to the "unch" part, rather than the "br", thinking we would miss the crowds. I've looked at their menu before, found it intriguing, but just never got around to trying it until this weekend. I also thought Cafe Lift would be a good choice, a spot off the beaten path (as in, a random stretch of 13th Street between Callowhill and Spring Garden), far from the queen of all brunch spots, Honey's, and everyone's OTHER favvy fav this side of Center City, Cafe Estelle. I was pretty much wrong because Cafe Lift was bumping, although closer to the degree that Cafe Estelle bumps, far from that of Honey's.

    After climbing the short flight of stairs to the dining room, the first two tables I saw were filled with pretty much everyone I know, including but not limited to guest stars of many of my reviews, Alexandra W. and Mr. & the future Mrs. Stephen S. - great minds think alike, guys. We waited around for a quick 30 minutes to be seated, but no big deal there. The food was good - after attempting to order the huevos rancheros, sans sausage, I was told that the refried beans weren't vegetarian, either. I should have inquired what about them wasn't veggie but I didn't. I went with the roma tomato fritatta instead. Compared to the most recent fritatta I had at Sam's Morning Glory, it was absolutely superior in texture, flavor, and content. My only complaint was the lack of roma tomatoes. There were (I counted) THREE IN THE WHOLE THING. THREE? Five would have been plenty, four would have been okay, but three was not enough. It was heavy on asparagus, light on the star of the show. Still very delicious. The potatoes were solid, but I'm a potato person, so that's cool, the toast had grill marks on both side, no crust, was maybe lightly buttered, but what is toast without LOTS of butter and strawberry jelly? Answer: not much, not much at all. I didn't really eat it, but whatever.

    Even though Cafe Lift is a liiiittle heavy on Ikea merchandaise, the atmosphere is pretty open and solid. All in all, a good meal, a nice little place, with a good menu. From what I heard from my homies, the breakfast burrito is tight, and the other egg dishes are solid as well. I'd like to try the french toast - I'm pretty sure one comes with pistachios. That's what I want next time. I'd probably return for the same reasons I showed up here in the first place - under-the-radar location and great menu choices. Could use more tomatoes, veggie refried beans, and could defintely do away with the whack Ikea mugs, but other than that - good times.

    Very rarely do I write a review for an establishment outside my own city, but visitors and locals alike need to hear about Café Lift.

    I visited the restaurant for breakfast on a Sunday morning and was immediately seated. The loft-style decor gives it a trendy vibe, and seating seems pretty ample. The prices are reasonable and in line with other breakfast/brunch diners. It's cash-only, but that is clearly noted on the menu. While the whole cash-only thing is usually enough to get me riled up (come on... it's 2011), they do have an ATM on-site, so I'll overlook it this time.

    This place really shines for breakfast/brunch. I had the plum tomato frittata with toast and roasted breakfast potatoes. The frittata had tomatoes, asparagus, and gorgonzola cheese - fantastic flavor and absolutely massive. The potatoes were a nice change from typical breakfast potatoes.

    The rest of my group went with sweet offerings: lemon ricotta pancakes and granola pancakes (an off-the-menu special). Both were served with honey, syrup, and whipped cream. The lemon ricotta pancakes were topped with strawberries and had a great lemon flavor without being too sweet. The granola pancakes were made with granola and oats and topped with blackberries and bananas. The granola added a fantastic texture, and the bananas were absolutely phenomenal. It was fantastic with honey and/or whipped cream or without any sugary topping at all!

    It's hard for me to give a restaurant 5-stars based on a single visit, but if you've got a morning sweet tooth, be sure to consider Café Lift. The savory options bring a lot to the table, so there is something for everyone (vegetarians included)!
