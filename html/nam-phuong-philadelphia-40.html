    Listed in Philadelphia Cheap (but Good) Eats

    This place never disappoints. It's definitely no-frills type of place but has slightly "nicer" ambiance than other no-frills type of ethnic restaurant.

    I like Vietnamese food and I eat it at least once every other week. Nam Phuong's pho broth has robust flavor, right thickness of noodles, and good mixture of meat (if you get the deluxe version). I like Nam Phuong's vermicelli noodle dishes as the rice noodles used here is thinner (which I like) than the next door's (viet cafe?). Their beef "carpaccio" salad is also very good. The thinly sliced (but not pounded...I think) meat is just slightly cooked in the lime acid in the salad and comes with good mixture of greens and robust Southeast Asian flavor of garlic chips, fish sauce, tamarind (I think), lime juice and a bit of sugar. If you haven't tried it and are not scared of uncooked beef, I suggest you try it. All their BBQ pork dishes are also very good. And their menu is so extensive that I haven't tried more than 50% of the menu. One thing I haven't really enjoyed is their appetizer section. They seem like they've been made a while before and been sitting...so the ones with rice wrapper seems to develop slightly stale taste.

    The parking lot here is pretty ridiculous: Part of the sidewalk is used as a driveway for the parking lot. (you'll see it as you're exiting the parking lot to Washington Ave.)

Welcome to Nam Phuong restaurant.
