    6/23/2012 Previous review
    If you know me, you also know that I just LOVE meat.  So I have always avoided vegetarian… If you know me, you also know that I just LOVE meat.  So I have always avoided vegetarian restaurants like the 2 week-old wrinkly tomato in the back of my fridge.  And whenever I've ignored my better judgment, I've always been sadly disappointed (with both restaurants and tomatoes).

    But the reviews of Vedge have been so mouthwateringly good, we finally broke down, and I'm very glad we did.

    In the old days, Vedge's 1890's townhouse (on the National Historic Register) was home to Deux Cheminees, one of the luminaries of Philadelphia's fine dining scene 20+ years ago.  Today, the dark wood interior, stained glass windows, charmingly cozy lounge and contemporary bar have as striking look that still preserves those past memories (although I did feel the dining rooms were a bit on the dim side).

    Upon arriving (parking is easy in one of the nearby lots) ~15 minutes early for our 7:30 PM reservation for 5 people on a Wednesday evening, we were shown to the charming, tiny lounge off of the bar.  Although we had the option to order drinks and snacks, we were eager to get on with our dinner.   However, we were not seated until almost 8PM.  Now, maybe I'm spoiled, but we never seem to have to wait at any of the other Philly restaurants we frequent, so I do admit to being a bit annoyed (no explanation was given).  But this was essentially the only damper on a fine evening.

    We opted to for the 5-course tasting menu. However, if you very carefully peruse Vedge's website, you might find the hidden small print saying that the tasting must be ordered 24 hours in advance, which we only realized that AM.  But, to the restaurant's credit, a few hours notice did secure the tasting menu for us.  We also decided to bring our own wines, despite Vedge's liquor license and interesting wine list, which resulted in a $25/bottle corkage fee.  High by Philadelphia standards, but very reasonable considering that every bottle on their list is only marked up $25 over wholesale, a true bargain.  We did order one bottle from their list, which ended up being wine of the night, and they also kindly waived the corkage on our half bottle of dessert wine.

    Tasting menu & wines:

    Amuse bouche (complementary due to the delay in seating us):

    Potato fritters with Chinese mustard--if there is one thing Vedge does well, it is fritters, as this was my second favorite dish of the evening, after the hearts of palm fritters below.  I could pop these things all evening.  Fantastic!

    1st course:

    Salad of spring greens & peas, coal-roasted carrots, shaved asparagus--a very nice, clean, simple salad, with a light dressing.  I wouldn't drive out of my way, but it was much better than your typical fancied-up lettuce salad, and it looked beautiful on its wood cutting board (see photos).

    ~2007 Cédric Bouchard (Roses de Jeanne) Champagne Blanc de Noirs Les Ursules--a big and unusual grower Champagne

    2nd course:

    Seared tofu on a bed of braised Savoy spinach & black kale, smoked mushroom stock--This was the most controversial dish of the evening.  I admired it as possibly the most delicious tofu I've ever had, obviously imitating a seared scallop, and doing a fair job of it.  But not everyone agreed.

    ~2011 Arnot-Roberts Touriga Nacional Luchsinger Vineyard (Rosé)

    3rd course:

    Green & yellow zucchinis (imitating fazzoletti, which doesn't make any sense), heirloom tomatoes, basil, dried olive, fresh hearts of palm fritters--Everyone agreed: this was the dish of the evening.  In part, the fantastic fritters (Vedge rule of thumb: order fritters), and the perfect tomatoes, but also because of the wonderful, accidental pairing of the Radikon orange wine (a white wine made with extended skin contact, so it has the tannins of a red wine and the exotic spices, orange rind and cider of a Christmas wassail).  Food/Wine pairing of the year!

    ~2005 Azienda Agricola Stanislao Radikon Collio Ribolla Gialla (500mL, $70 off list)

    4th course:

    Seared Maitake mushroom, gold & candy-striped Chioggia beets, black lentils, pickled leek salad--All I can remember about this dish were the beautiful Chioggias, and the big mushroom, crispy on one side and meaty on the other.  Unfortunately, the big fruit of the wine overpowered the mushroom.

    ~ 2006 Beaux Frères Pinot Noir Beaux Frères Vineyard

    Dessert:

    Strawberries, cheesecake, shortcake, ice cream--Being a vegan restaurant, presumably soy was used in this dish, but you'd never know it.  Perfectly ripe strawberries were a good match for the Black Muscat.

    ~2000 Philip Togni Black Muscat Ca' Togni (375mL; a wine with a fascinating back-story: bowlerwine.com/site/wine… )

    Service was outstanding, and I especially want to thank the manager, who also served as our sommelier and was very accommodating. $124/person counting tip and the Radikon off the list.

    4+ stars based on 1 visit.

    Would I go again?  Definitely, but given my carnavorian proclivities, maybe only a couple of times a year.

    Listed in Washington Square West

    I am a semi-vegetarian (I only eat birds, I know it's weird, don't ask, I have no explanation), and I generally avoid meat.  I have been dying to try Vedge forever and finally my friends were able to organize a dinner together.  Five of us went, and between us, we tried almost everything on the menu.

    Things I loved:

    Beet salad!!!!!  How is this so delicious?  I could eat four servings of this, nothing else on the menu and be completely satisfied.  Golden beets are layered with creamy cucumber, smoked tofu, and capers.  It is a perfect combination of sweet and salty.  The taste of the beets was more subtle than I expected, but it was perfect as it just brought out the other flavors.

    Black Vinegar Cucumber Pickles--I could eat a jar of plain pickles in a day, and these were great pickles.  A little bit sweet, but mostly spicy.

    U Peel Lupini Beans--The garlic really came through, the beans were well prepared.  Very tasty.

    Brussels Sprouts--these are my current favorite vegetable, so it's hard to go wrong for me here.  the sprouts were crispy and crunchy and the mustard was creamy and balanced the bitterness of the sprouts nicely.

    Spicy Grilled Tofu--the soy flavor really came through from the gochujang.  The tofu had a nice texture aided further by the crispy tofu cracklin that came with the dish.  The tofu was spicy and the edamame puree helped balance out the spice (not that I minded the heat, I like spicy dishes).

    Cauliflower Pho--this was a yummy take on a traditional pho.  Among other spices, I think I tasted some cinnamon and ginger.  It was slightly sweet, but not cloying.  The cauliflower was cooked well and not soggy.

    In addition to the above, we had several dishes that were great, but didn't rock my world in the same way.  

    Sweet Potato Pate was good, but I probably would not order it again with all of the other more inventive dishes on the menu.  The same can be said for the Fried Shishito Peppers, which are exactly what they sound like and nothing more and the Black and Green Olives which good olive preparations but I'd rather try something else I can't find elsewhere.

    For drinks, I had the Sauvignon Blanc, the Cinnamander, and the Orange wine.  The Sauvignon Blanc was a bit sweet, the cinnamander (a cinnamon-infused tequila drink) was spot-on perfect, and the orange wine tasted like a good, dry rose.

    After several hours of dinner, my group left completely stuffed and satisfied.  I will definitely be back for more of the beet salad (maybe even today).  My meat-eating friends missed the meat, but enjoyed the food.  With all the creaminess that crept into the dishes, I would say you will not miss the butter and dairy products that you would find at a regular vegetarian restaurant to make up for the lack of meat.  I was thoroughly impressed.

    The food at this restaurant is truly amazing and an experience unto itself.  I would have given it five stars had the overall experience been a little more pleasant.  But I'll start with the positives.  The food and drinks are killer.  I'm a vegetarian (pescetarian to be exact), so maybe I'm not the best judge, but you couldn't even really tell that this food was vegan.  Everything was so flavorful and rich that you didn't miss the meat (or meat based broths) or dairy (butter, cream, etc.).  The brussel sprouts were great, as everyone has mentioned.  Definitely get those.  My boyfriend (meat eater) and I shared the cauliflower pho and I was so taken aback by how perfectly it tasted like actual Vietnamese pho.  We also shared the Roasted Maitake Mushroom, which was so incredibly tasty.  I was amazed that there was no butter used in that dish and that it still had such rich, deep flavor.  We shared the acorn squash, which was buttery and toasty flavored.  Really good.  We shared the Chocolate Uber Chunk for dessert, which I highly recommend.  The fudgy peanut butter candy was to die!  For drinks I had the Elder Sage Cocktail & the Pomegranate Sangria, both really good.  The former was my favorite though - perfectly tart & very strong.   We left feeling full and satisfied, but not overly full and gross.  

    The drawback though was the overall dining experience.  They drove home the point that we had a hard two hour limit on our meal.  We were there celebrating a friend's 30th birthday, and everyone had come in from out of town to surprise her, so it was a special occasion.  I understand they have other reservations to honor, but it does take away from the experience to be pressured to leave.  They did rush us through ordering and paying the check.  The server was just a little pushy about the whole thing.  The whole placed just seemed very anal retentive.  So while the food was amazing and definitely worth the time and money, just be prepared to be rushed while you're there trying to enjoy the vegan fare.

    Listed in Vegan in the city

    Vedge is my new favorite restaurant.  As a brand new vegan and total foodie, I am thrilled I can still experience Philly fine dining at its best.  The food here is incredible.  It's as simple as that.  And the vegetables really are the star -- the menu features a lot less tofu-based, substitution-esque food than I expected.  A highlight is the salt-roasted golden beet dish.  Reminiscent of the flavors and textures of a tartare, only better.  It blew me away.  The sweet potato pate was also a stand out.  The texture was unbelievably smooth and rich.  I can't wait to go back and have them again -- not to mention every single item on the rotating dirt list.

    Only a week after our epic NYE dinner, my girlfriend and I went back for a wintery happy hour.   Happy hour is a seriously great deal, with drinks and dishes priced at $4-5.  Not only that, it's probably the most efficiently run happy hour I've ever seen.  The bar is relatively small, and, as is the case at all in-demand happy hours spots in the city, everyone waiting starts to get itchy as the clock inches toward 7.  But at Vedge, there's no need for the anxious vigilance often required to secure a coveted seat -- the host keeps track of arrivals and kindly tells anyone trying to jump the line that there's an order to be seated.  Very impressive.  

    In sum:  go to here.  I can't wait for another amazing vegan dinner with my girlfriend, and to show it off to my meat-eating father next time he's in town.
