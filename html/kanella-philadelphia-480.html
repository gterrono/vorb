    My friend (Alexis C.) accurately described this place. It is very authentic. I've been to a few Medi countries and it tasted like I was there. But usually authentic and small means cheap. This place was $20-30 for the entrees which is a little over my usual dinner budget. Wouldn't go back unless it was an occasion. Nonetheless, yummy and good service!

    I live in Baltimore MD and have been to Kanella twice so far this year. First was during a day trip in May (stumbled upon it via Trip Advisor) and then returned only to eat here in July with friends from NYC.

    The ambiance is nothing to write about but then, for me, great food and service trumps the rather 'expensive settings' one may find in fine dining restaurants. The food here is truly the best I've had in a commercial establishment (outside of home cooking that is). The lamb burger is so damn delicious that the first time I ordered it, I had two. The platter is the next best thing I remember from the menu. Everything on it was well prepared and delicious. Finally, baklava with date ice cream and the coconut-hazlenut cake with orange syrup were delicately balanced, yet explosive, flavors. Our friends from NYC were vegetarians and also loved the food.

    It's a 2 hour drive to Kanella from Baltimore and we are planning to go back again this month. Yeah, the food is that good and you don't get that level greek food near here!

Specialties

We specialise in cooking from the homeland of chef Konstantinos Pitsillides. Pitsillides hails from the island of Cyprus, an indipendant Greek island located in the very east of the Mediterranean. The Cyprus kitchen has many influences but can perhaps best be considered as part of the Levant. At home in Cyprus, as in his Philly kitchen freshness, simplicity, flavour and love are the standard ingrediants. Chef takes his French culinary training and applies it to the traditional recipes of his homeland. Homemade desserts, head to tail cooking, lemon and olive oil are all part and parcel of this neighbourhood joint.

History

Established in 2008.

Kanella is made up of a passionate bunch of characters from the chef on down. Being a local restaurant filled with the family spirit much of the staff have been there since we opened. Though Chef Pitsillides is oft misunderstood (and yes sometimes this is exaserbated by himself), the intent of the restaurant is a simple one... We have aimed to be a neighbourhood venue for good, honest and consitstant cooking. A place that you can afford to come to regularly and have a good meal, good service, a nice bottle of wine, and enjoy your company. We have been blessed with the neighborhood we are in and all our wonderfull customers, so many of which we have known for sometime now. We look forward to continued joyful cooking and eating!
