        Decent (and much needed) wood-fired pizza in an otherwise desolate food wasteland. We tried the antipasti dish of the day and were very happy with the sampling of items--marinated olives, butternut squash, beets, roasted cauliflower, and potato and leek frittata. For our pies, we ordered the margherita and mushroom--although we ate the margherita and sausage, since the hasty waiter switched our order, but we really didn't mind since we were deciding earlier between the sausage and mushroom anyway. The pies were fresh, doughy, unique, and satisfying, but also quite forgettable. Wait staff was attentive and friendly. Decor was cool. They seem to have a very vegan-friendly menu for those who subscribe to that kind of lifestyle. We went during their soft opening so the place should be getting better day by day, and will make a fine addition to the Spring Garden Street corridor. Mangia!

        I met a friend at Bufad for lunch today (Saturday), and the place was surprisingly empty, perhaps because the hours are posted incorrectly on some local sites. In any case, we had attentive, but not annoying service, and were delighted with the food and ambience. The pizza oven, which crackles at around 900 degrees, is a marvel to behold; it churns out lovely pizzas with creamy, melted cheese and light but hefty crusts. We shared the day's antipasti (roasted beets, olives, hot peppers, pieces of frittata), a pizza with tasty homemade sausage, (also homemade) biscotti with pignoli, and espressos made with La Colombe coffee. We didn't try the slices, but their display on the counter certainly tempted us and we would have ordered more after our Neapolitan pie if we hadn't already been full. Bravo to Philly for its multiplying and ambitious pizzerias, and for restaurateurs committed to sustainability and buying local products!

        Delicious and pretentious; another user used this title, and I couldn't have said it any better.  Very nice artisan/gourmet salads and pizza, including Roman style rectangular pies that are sold by the slice.  We had the margherita, the mushroom, and a fennel sopressata Roman style.  Everything was very good; I especially liked the margherita, which (in my opinion) is the benchmark for any artisan pizza shop; if you can't knock that out of the park, pack it in!  

        As another user had stated, it's pretentious, and I second that notion in spades.  When these types of things happen, I always suffer from a twinge of guilt that maybe it's just me being too picky about service.  Apparently not.  On the menu, they actually say (at the bottom) that "substitutions and modifications are politely declined."  They must have a real superstar chef...who might be good, but isn't worth the bad publicity in my opinion.  I posted to them on Facebook, and actually did speak with Jillian, the manager.  She was very nice in responding to my query about their ridiculous arrogance in not honoring the simple request of modifcations or substitutions.  She said they were a small place, and just don't have the "inventory" to honor such requests.  At the time, I was pretty incredulous to her comment, but took it at face value.  Since another user on Yelp had posted the fact that they wouldn't even give her a small side of sauce to dip, and that the chef said "the pizza was made to be eaten as-is" I can see it's total BS, and they have a prima donna chef.  While I will likely return on occasion, I was hoping to have a place in our loft neighborhood that might usurp my patronage to Osteria as a regular top-notch pizza option.  Unfortunately, I'd rather go to Osteria and deal with higher prices, and MUCH less pretention. This establishment, from the first day they were open, already thinks WAY too much of themselves; reputations are earned over time, not conjured by appearing elitist.  Our neighborhood is young and hip, and a bit on the grungy side.  This isn't Rittenhouse, and we don't need that kind of attitude.  

        With that being said...hopefully they'll change their attitude at some point; Osteria is opening a place at 20th and Callowhill when the Granary is finished, so we'll have at least two very good pizza places within easy walking distance.  Maybe the competition will help.  Very good food, but very unfortunate choice in attitude.

        In the end, I'd say worth a stop in if you're looking for something trendy and adventurous, but not really a "Friday night netflix" pizza joint.
