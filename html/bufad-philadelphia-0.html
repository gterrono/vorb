    I think I said "hmmm, that place looks cool we should try it sometime!" a thousand times before I actually stopped in randomly on Thursday night.

    This place is awesome. It's not at all very big-- lots of two-seater seats and stool seating by the window with limited outdoor seating that has heat lamps. BUT the pizza and service here is just sexy. The owner has the too-cool-for-school vibe and is easy on the eyes. Our waiter Shawn/Sean was just so friendly without being overbearing and well versed on the menu. We had the antipasti that was a plate of wild boar sausage, roasted olives, pickled fennel and celery, ricotta e bianca, peperonata, salty egg (hard boiled with anchovies on top), and the winner: pickled and fried butternut squash. Our pizza was roasted corn purée, shiitake mushrooms, pancetta, scallions, crescenza cheese. It was inventive and well balanced.

    Ok so yeah, the food is good but what kicks it up to five stars:
    1. They have a buy one pizza, get one pizza (!!! #holydeal) Sunday-Thursday 5pm-7pm take out only. The waiter told me that they have people call in and order ten pizzas at time. Love it!

    On a whim and a hunt for a good pizza, my husband and I ended up at Bufad for the first time on a Friday night. We've had sampling the pizza before at a few events but this was our first full dining experience.

    The customer service must be noted first. As I mentioned, we were dining on a whim so didn't have a reservation. We walked in a little after 7:30 and put our name on the list. I have the hostess my number for her to text me when the table was ready. The estimated wait time was about an hour but I think it was shorter. I lost track of time because we were able to head next door to Prohibition Tap Room for a drink.

    So we were initially seated outside but it was chilly and honestly a little stinky on the Spring Garden side of the restaurant. Not to the restaurant's fault but just due to the nature of the city streets. We asked to move inside and they happily accommodated with the next available table which was ready within minutes. Additionally we had the pleasure of having a polite and attentive gentleman as our server.

    We originally were planning to split one pie but based on hearing our server describe the size to a neighboring table we opted for two. Our selections of the evening included the sausage pie and the daily special, which was a white pie with a parsley pesto, summer squash and ricotta cheese. Both pies had a full crust that was light and fluffy but still managed to have a slight crisp. I didn't love the sausage pie, I think the sauce was a little too sweet and the cheese was a little sparse. That's just a personal preference thing because my husband really enjoyed it. The pesto pie on the other hand was out of this world! It was probably the best pesto pies I've ever had. The combo of pesto to squash to ricotta all fell into place perfectly as the flavors blended magically.

    The pies were a little pricy for their size but not bad in terms of gourmet pizza. Definitely worth it for a special treat. I'm hoping my pie makes a more permanent appearance on the menu as well. Next time we'll come prepared with a BYOB selection.

    I live a block and a half from Bufad and when I first read about it I was really hoping for a traditional neighborhood pizza shop. Then when I learned it was owned by the Cafe Lift / Prohibition crew I figured a traditional pizza parlor was not very likely but I was thinking maybe they would have pies similar to Pizza Brain. High quality ingredients, decently sized with the option to just get a nice big plain cheese if the occasion called for it. To my dismay that's not this.

    Bufad uses extremely high quality ingredients which I definitely appreciate. We've either eaten in or gotten take out from here 5 times and consistently boo and I have commented on the freshness and quality of the toppings and various cheeses Bufad uses. Of course there's a price that comes quality and Bufad's pies range from $11 - $15 and they're not quite large enough for a meal without a salad or soup on the side. That's for one person, boo and I typically need 2 pizzas and a large salad and we'd consider this a light dinner. I don't know if that speaks more to our appetites than to portion sizes but it is what it is.

    I classify Bufad as a gourmet pizza restaurant. The things I dislike about Bufad are the same things I dislike about other similar places like Barbuzzo, Vetri Pizza, and Stella. The pizza is a bit floppy and not quite big enough but I will say the staff here is top notch, BYOB is a plus, and the interior is really cool. I decided to go with 3 stars because the flavors are very good and just because I have a bias against these types of places anyone I've ever taken here has really enjoyed it.
