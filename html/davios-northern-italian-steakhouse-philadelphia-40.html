        We ate here for a wedding reception.  The staff were very attentive and really gracious.  There were about 80 or so people at the reception and the courses came out a little slow but that can be expected when serving so many people.

        We started with fresh mixed greens salads with a light balsamic vinaigrette.  The issue was that there was little to no dressing on our salads.  So, it was a very, very dry salad.  We kept reaching for our drinks and hoped no one would start choking.

        The entree was fabulous.  It was shrimp and a filet mignon.  The shrimp had been cooked in garlic - and I mean lots of garlic in that you can smell it when they put it in front of you.  A little too much garlic, but luckily everyone was eating the shrimp.  They were cooked perfectly and were very warm when we received them.  

        Now, the reason for the 4 stars is the Filet Mignon.  It was cooked perfectly!  And so, so tender and juicy.  This is a steakhouse, and they definitely know how to cook a steak!  One of the best steaks I have had!!   And they were serving 80 people at once.  We can not wait to go back and have a meal in their dining room.  

        If you live in Philly and like steak, consider yourselves lucky!!  What a find, we will be back!

        So the carnivore in you is screaming for red meat and your wallet is flush with cash.  It's time to go to a high end steakhouse in Philly.  You have lots of choices.  There are easily over a dozen including the big chains like Morton's and Ruth's Chris.  What to do?  What to do?  I suggest you peruse your closet for some of your nicest duds and get ready for Davio's.  Don't forget to make an Open Table reservation.  

        If you're going to pay serious $$$ you should expect a great time and that is very likely to happen at this wonderful restaurant.  Let's start with the atmosphere.  This place is seriously gorgeous.  High ceilings and beautiful décor.  

        Now for the service.  The only word I can use is superb.  Expect to be pampered.  The only restaurant I have ever experienced service this outstanding is the Four Seasons.  I mentioned this to a Davio's manager and he informed me that many of the wait staff came from the Four Seasons.  Interesting!

        If you are the kind of person that likes a drink or two you will be pleased here.  They will be well made, potent, and beautifully served.  Davio's also has an extensive wine list.

        You can expect all of your food to be impressive.  Most importantly your steak will be outstanding.  Although I have never dared to try it, I have seen their 30oz Grilled Long Bone Rib Eye.  OMG!  Davio's list of side dishes is longer than most.  As results it's extra easy to please just about everybody in your party.

        Davio's is easily a four (4) star experience.  If I could I would go for 4.5.

      Specialties

      The concept for Davio's Northern Italian Steakhouse took shape in our Philadelphia, PA location. Situated on the second floor in the historic Provident Bank Building just off fashionable Rittenhouse Row,

      Davio's Philadelphia is a jewel in the Philadelphia restaurant world,
      winning accolade after accolade, year after year. Our dramatic dining room is the perfect place for business breakfasts, lunches and dinners or special occasions. And our lounge has become a favorite of the weekday wind-down-after-work set.

      History

      Established in 1985.

      In 1985, at the young and ambitious age of 24, Steve DiFillippo purchased a locally-owned family restaurant called Davio's on Newbury Street in Boston's trendy Back Bay. As Boston's culinary tastes evolved, he transformed the location into a destination. He retooled the menu, the space, the wine list. The new Davio's soon garnered rave reviews.

      The idea behind Davio's Northern Italian Steakhouse was simple, regional Italian foods with a focus on the grill.
      Everything made by hand from the best ingredients. Serve everything from aged steaks to simple-yet-unique pasta creations. Mirror this philosophy in the kitchen with our expert and attentive staff in the dining room, seeing to your every need and taking pride in your enjoyment of the meal. Steve has since opened locations in Foxborough, MA (Patriot Place), Philadelphia, and a new location in Atlanta.

      Meet the Business Owner

      Steve D. Business Owner

      "After 25 years I feel that I am just getting started." says Davio's CEO, Steve DiFillippo. At the age of 24, Steve purchased an established restaurant called Davio's in Boston's Back Bay. And this was the first in a succession of popular restaurants. Steve is involved in the community, serving on several non-profit boards.

      In 2008, he was inducted into the Massachusetts Restaurant Hall of Fame.
      Steve lives in Wenham, MA, with his wife Pamela and their four children. He runs 4-5 miles a day, "so I can wrongfully delude myself that I can eat anything I want," he says.
