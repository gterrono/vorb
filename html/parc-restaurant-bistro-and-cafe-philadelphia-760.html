    Finally visited this restaurant, after walking by it for years whenever I visited Philadelphia.  Came here with college friends for brunch, during labor day weekend 2013.

    Food (9.0/10) = Ordered the Eggs Norwegian (Smoked salmon, hollandaise sauce, and poached eggs).  Unbelievable.  I don't think I've ever had poached eggs done as well as by the folks at Parc.  It has inspired me to repeat such perfection at home.  Was incredibly pleased with this dish.  My friends ordered the moules frites and smoked salmon tartine.  Both were pleased as well.  

    Ambiance (9.0/10) = I love places with outdoor seating.  The decor is amazing...I can't really pinpoint the time period, but it reminds me of something from an old black and white movie, with tiled floors, wood, pillars, antique cash registers, the lighting.  Maybe something from the early 1900's?  

    Service (10/10) = We sat at the bar since outdoor seating was packed.  Moreover, it was quite hot outside during our visit.  Our bartender was very accommodating to our indecisiveness on what to order (everything looked so good to us on the menu).   She was fun to talk to.  She was so attentive, that she sometimes would join in our conversation.  I forgot her name though, but she was awesome.

    Overall Opinion = One of the best places I've been to in Philly.  Wish it hadn't taken me so long to finally visit the place.  Looking forward to visiting Parc again on my next visit to Philly.  In the mean time, must learn how to poach the perfect egg for that silky velvety yolk.

    THE FOOD: Ate here today with a party of 6. We all ordered either the omelettes or the french toast with apples. Nothing was disappointing, but nothing was spectacular either... except for the side of bacon and Turkey sausage I ordered... both of which tasted homemade and were definitely the highlights of the meal. Not to say that the omelettes and french toast weren't perfectly cooked, fluffy and filling. Its just that the crispy bacon and sausage were ON POINT!!

    THE ATMOSPHERE: Parc is a French bistro and they pull it off very well. I went to Paris a few years ago and this place looks like it was literally picked up and moved into central city, Philly... brick by brick. The wooden chairs weren't that comfy for my fat tush to sit in all day, but the open feel of the place made me not want to leave. Our waitress was patient and kind although it took her a bit to long to take our order. Luckily we weren't in a rush at all so no biggie. If we were, someone probably would have complained. I love that this place has a full service coffee bar too.

    THE PRICES: Too high, probably by about 25%. Don't get me wrong, the portions are decent here . But $14 for a cheese/herb omelette with a small side of potatoes is a little nuts to me.  Or $18 for a warm shrimp appetizer? Or what about $11 for a French onion soup? Seriously, those onions better be handpicked by french farmers and flown to the states or something because I cannot justify paying $11 for a soup that consists of Gruyere cheese, bread and onions.  

    Would I drive back to Philadelphia to eat at Parc from Baltimore.... no. In that regard I may be too generous in giving this place 4 starts.

    But if I were in the neighborhood I may be inclined to go for dinner because like I said, the food was definitely solid, the service was good which it got started, and the atmosphere was great. Just a bit too pricey for what you receive in my opinion.

    I came here with a group of friends the morning after a wedding and had a lovely brunch.  I ordered a spinach and goat cheese omelet, which was a bit pricey at $15.  However, the spinach was fresh and the omelet was classic French style--foamy eggs, no browned spots to speak of.  Very yummy.  My only complaint would be that the eggs themselves were a little heavy on the salt for me and maybe a little more done than I would have liked, but that's just me.  The potatoes that came with the omelet were delicious.  We also split a basket of pastries, and all were very good.  I particularly liked the apple square, which was the feature that day.  The puff pastry was flaky, the apples were perfect, and the streusel topping was crunchy.  Yum--a symphony of flavors and textures.  The 5 other pastries were good also, though I didn't get to taste them all.

    The service was prompt and friendly, and the atmosphere is classic French bistro, as one would find all over Paris and beyond.  It is a little pricey, but I think that comes with the neighborhood.  I would definitely go back!

    I think most of us that have been to the majority of Stephen Starr establishments can agree that the man really knows how to do ambiance.  Each and every one of his restaurants creates a vibe that transports you, if only for a couple of hours, out of Philly and to some other city, country, dimension, whatever.  Parc is no different.  I really felt like I could have been at a brasserie in Paris, sipping a coffee, nibbling a croissant and trying to communicate with my waiter in horrible French.

    So along with the ambiance, I will say that the location and service is excellent at Parc.  And it's HUGE so it can easily accomodate larger groups.  And they are also open ALL THE TIME.  You'd think they were running a diner over on Rittenhouse Square with those hours.  So it's always a safe bet for a late night weekend meal or an early morning breakfast before work.

    But like with all of Starr's other establishments, the food is always somewhat disappointing to me.  Maybe I'm just more of a hole-in-the-wall, mom and pop, locals only type of a gal?  Don't get me wrong- the food is good.  I just feel like there are better places in the city for the same amount of money.

    That being said, I feel like Parc is a great place to take out-of-towners who want the Rittenhouse Square dining experience.  I would suggest everyone visit at least once, but just keep in mind that Starr is trying to create an experience, rather than blow you away with amazing food.

    Expectations for this Rittenhouse Square brasserie/French bistro were not exactly high following an absolutely outstanding meal the night before, but this rather large dining establishment certainly delivered.

    While we are not locals and lack a good baseline for dining establishments in the city of brotherly love, we hold all restaurants to extremely high standards. Not only are my husband and I foodies from nearby New York, or "the city" as we affectionately call it (as if there is no other true city in existence ), we had just dined at what is clearly a Philly hotspot in nearby Vernick!

    The outdoor seating overlooking Rittenhouse Square is likely a huge draw, but we sat inside on a fairly busy Thursday night. Everything from the wine list down to the tasty Cappucino was stellar. I had the loup de mer special, a whole fish roasted and perfectly moist served with marinated giant beans. The moules, or mussels, were a nice start to the meal and the side of sautéed spinach decent but nothing to write home about. My hubby had the roasted duck with kale which he reported as delicious.

    What I particularly appreciated about this place, despite its reputation for being a tourist destination, was the decor-signs everywhere en francais-a nice touch. Service was attentive and spot on. The chateau neuf de pape was tasty so we felt compelled to order a second bottle. While we weren't wowed by this place, it is obvious why it is still considered a reliable destination for food, drink, people watching and just good ole plain atmosphere! Would recommend and return.

Specialties

This stylish addition to Rittenhouse Square pays tribute to French cafe culture, serving breakfast, lunch and dinner seven days a week. Ideal for a quick bite or a leisurely meal, the menu is filled with classics: onion soup, escargots, steak frites and towering plateaus de fruits de mer. The baguettes are made in-house and  the wine list is expansive. Sip a Kir Royale at the zinc bar or enjoy a cafe au lait while basking in the sun.

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District.
