    While the desserts are certainly delicious and over the top (in a good way), the food is worthy on its merit!  The salmon burger tops my list - the fish always has the perfect amount of crunch, and the burger is topped with a heap of perfectly caramelized onions.  All the burgers come with a (small) side of waffle fries, which are dusted with chili and cocoa powder  and are hands down the best waffles fries I've had.  I would highly recommend ordering an extra side of them - the four or five that come with an entree are just not enough!  The veggie burger and greek salad are also favorites of mine, and everything is consistently good.  

    For dessert, I recommend the S'mores Sundae - chocolate and vanilla ice cream, marshmallow fluff, whipped cream, and milk chocolate fondue, all mixed together with graham crackers.  Heavenly!  (Minor notes - the side of white chocolate ganache seemed out of place and didn't really go with the rest of the dessert, and we didn't get the toasted marshmallow that was in the description - sad!)

    The ambiance is very date-y, but in a nice way.  The lighting is low and the decor is romantic, but not corny.  The only thing I don't like are the tiny tables - there's really not enough room for all the food!  The tables are also placed pretty darn close together so it can definitely feel like you're dining with extra companions.  If you have a party of 5 or 6, definitely go for the tall tables near the kitchen because those offer the roomiest dining experience.

    This place is nothing more than a Friendly's for Big Kids - a chain with a contrived "funky vibe."   If you are over 30 you probably want to give this a pass as your taste buds won't appreciate the extreme sugar and your ears won't appreciate the nearly club level volume of music.  We rarely patronize chains but hoped since this one had such a snooty feel and an international presence it would be good, alas this was not so.

    We both got waffle desserts - him the Apple Pie, me the Banana Split.  My slightly warm waffle tasted very stale -I guess they micro waved it for a few seconds to try to heat it up but the "warm" quickly dissipated after it reached my table and I was left with a dried out tough waffle, the edges were hard as a rock  - like when you microwave bread too long?  The tiny pitcher of melted chocolate served with it was not enough to moisten this beast so I spent a LOT of time trying to mush the small frozen solid ball of ice cream that topped it down into it to try to make it edible.  The banana was good but the milk chocolate chips were grotesquely sweet to both of us.

    I tried the apples on hubbys (also past its sell-by date) waffle and they were just a little too undercooked and kind of sour.  The caramel sauce served with the apple waffle was so sweet it literally made me feel sick.

We probably could have gotten by with a half pizza too, which was very good, but if we went again, I'd try something else.

    Overall, I was very pleased and very full of good food and chocolate when I was finished. And I left with 2 hug mugs from the gift shop, thanks to the 10% off with your dinner receipt that our waitress made sure to tell us about.

    The weird trendy plates and dishes are mostly annoying.  A friend was served a pasta meal in a dish that was so sloped, he could not rest his utensil in the bowl without it flipping out of the bowl, end over end, taking a few pieces of food with it.  That was really only cute the first couple of times.  And the mugs are unwieldy as well.  They are definitely going for a form-over-function vibe here, which just doesn't work in the restaurant world.
