    My wonderful staff gave me a nice gift certificate to Matyson's, so off to dinner we went this past Monday.  My lovely date and I decided to order the tasting menu (a tribute to the recently departed Charlie Trotter.). The first two courses, mussels with sweet & sour cucumber and the Kampachi in green tomato water were excellent. The third course of lamb with baby spinach, sun chokes and truffles was sublime, and was the one of the best lamb dishes I've ever eaten.  The duck and cornbread that following were also very good, but paled in comparison to that lamb.

    Wow!  Food was great.  I do not like mussels, but the second course was mussels.  The waitress said the tasting menu is always good, so I decided to give it a try.  I saw foie gras, which I was thinking about.  But I also saw venison as one of the courses on the tasting menu, which I had been meaning to try--since I haven't had it in years.  So I decided to do the tasting menu.  Usually on the road I need to eat a pound of beef a day, but sometimes I eat other foods.  Helps with cooking at home, to freshen up my menu.  The salad came.  Wow--very good.  Fresh clean somewhat acidic--nice mix of taste and texture, creamy bits of cheese, crunchy pomegranate seeds, scallop ceviche, clean cilantro.  Mussels came smelling like mussels :( - but I ordered them and will eat things I dont like sometimes, to see what it tastes like (you can still tell the difference between good and bad) -- this was the best mussel dish I ever had (and I have tried a lot of mussels a lot of different ways, usually gagging).  They went perfectly with the chorizo and the lobster broth was unbelievable and I don't like broth or soup, but broth and soup is the base of almost everything (so you have to taste it if you want to make just about anything good), this was as good a base for something like mussels that have shells that smell like wet dog.  I would order this dish even though I hate mussels.  Venison was also very, very good.  The sear on it was amazing, but still thin and soft.  I could have used about another 10-12oz, though--great with cranberries, endive and golden beets - very clean tasting.  The venison tasted like mating season buck (just my guess).  The fois gras, well it was good, but the venison and mussels had me not even thinking about that.  The flavors were fresh, crisp and clean.  Great use of acidity, earthiness and root tones.  With only hints of sweet.  Thank you! I dont think I ever had these flavors done this way.  That was the brightest, freshest fall combination and use of acidity possible.  It changed the way I think of fall flavors and acidity. But still kept the traditional spicy tomato base tone for the mussels, fall mushroom flavor, earthy cheese in the salad and root vegetables with the meat. I'm going to have to go back to eating things other than beef for a while. I saw someone at the next table having the pumpkin cheesecake, so I ordered it along with the apple crumb dessert that came with the tasting menu.  Both were good for a later dinner.  Not too sweet.  Definitely eat here.  Sorry about the ridiculous review, but it was that good.

    Matyson is the quintissential BYOB restaurant. Here's why it's my favorite in a city bustling with amazing BYOBs.

    It's located in one of the nicest areas of town. Cozy and intimate atmosphere, yet with ample space to create a perfect romantic setting. Although specializing in seasonal, new American cuisine, there is undoubtedly old French inspiration. And by far, the star of the show is the fois gras appetitizer, which is served with banana bread, crispy duck skin, sprinkles of fresh halzelnuts and coffee. I would dare to say that there is no better fois gras in our great foodie city.

    But what sets apart Matyson is it's $45 tasting menu served on weekdays which features themes such as lobster and pumpkin, whatever the chefs find most fresh and in season. The last time we went here we were blown away by the perfectly cooked Long Island duck breast served with delicious coconut curry, leek, and sweet potato.

    Service have always been attentive and extremely helpful. From the décor, to the well-executed food here, Matyson is the first BYOB I talk about whenever the topic is in discussion. So I will definitely be back with wine in hand to try more delightful dishes!

    OK, I'm going to be that guy that chooses 3 stars instead of 4 or 5. Let me explain... Matyson should get 4 stars. It's super-cute, super-packed, and has an amazing menu that clearly shows off the talents of the chef. Staff are talented, and it's a BYOB, which never gets old.
    However, the ribs that the server trumpeted were not 4 star ribs, whereas the duck that my wife picked was the Best of Table in a 6-person table. At a restaurant with such a great, ever-changing menu, you've gotta know what is hot and what is not.
    Furthermore, they have only ONE restroom. With capacity for ONE. This means that in a restaurant that seats at least 45-60 people, where people are swilling wine they brought, there is always going to be a 20-minute line. That takes you away from your friends and the aforementioned swilling. Fix this, and Matyson gets 4.5 stars.

    Matyson has a great idea: a weekly rotating tasting menu that is offered on Mondays through Thursdays. It's also reasonably priced at $45 for five courses.

    I went to Matyson on a Thursday evening and tried the "Lobster Around the World" tasting menu. It was generally good, but not great.

    The menu started on a high with a chilled Thailand themed red curry coconut soup with a lobster salad, pineapple, and Thai basil. It was nicely balanced with a touch of heat and sweetness. It was a light, refreshing way to start the meal.

    The next course was a French inspired lobster crepe that played on salty sweet. Unfortunately, I thought that it was a little too sweet with the cherry reduction. The Japan inspired grilled lobster yakitori was cooked well, and  I enjoyed the edamame wasabi puree, but not necessarily when eaten together. I was most disappointed with the Italian inspired poached lobster served with saffron fettuccine and uni. The promise of lobster and uni made me very excited for the dish, but it ultimately fell flat. The lobster was a bit chewy, the uni almost indistinguishable, and the saffron "fettuccine" was just not good.

    Portions were quite small so we also ordered the veal sweetbreads in addition to the tasting menu. It was quite good, but more sweetbreads should have been given at the price.

    The meal ended with a lemon cheesecake and dulce de leche napolean which were both tasty, but the fig almond tart was not as successful. None were particularly memorable.

    I overall enjoyed my meal at Matyson. They like to contrast salty and sweet in many of their dishes and they took some risks, but not all of them paid off. The tasting menu is still a great value at only $45, and the restaurant is BYOB which keeps costs further down. Due to the generally glowing reviews, I believe that either the kitchen was having a slightly off night or that the menu itself was not the best.

Specialties

One feature that sets us apart from the pack is our weekly tasting menu that showcases whatever is fresh and in season. The themes can range from lobster to vanilla. Five courses for $45 Monday through Thursday. Check the menu page for this weeks tasting menu.

History

Established in 2003.

Matyson opened its doors in the fall of 2003  and became an immediate success under the leadership of Matt & Sonjia Spector. Matt & Sonjia installed the framework for what Matyson has become today. After four great years the couple left to pursue another restaurant in the Napa Valley named Jole, but they left the restaurant in very capable hands. Younger cousin, Ben Puchowitz, whom had been working at Matyson as a line cook since its opening, assumed the role of Executive Chef, and his father, Butch Puchowitz became full owner. Ben has instilled his own personality into the food, without changing the original concepts. The staff of Matyson has continued to win awards and high acclaim for their refined cuisine and cozy atmosphere.
