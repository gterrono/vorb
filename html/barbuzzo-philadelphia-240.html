    i have held off on reviewing barbuzzo for several reasons.

    1. i wanted to make sure the awesomeness wasn't a fluke. 5 times later, i've been assured it most certainly is not.
    2. as a result of its consistent awesomeness, it's often difficult to get a table there. and the more i rave about this place, the more i hurt my chances of getting a rezzie
    3. seriously, #2 again, for emphasis.

    but the conclusion is, that barbuzzo is too good not to share with people. i can say, without exaggeration, that it's easily in my top 5 faves in philly. and i'd even go as far as to say that it's usually top 1 or 2.

    what's good, you ask? i'd answer - EVERYTHING and it seems the menu items will have slight seasonal variations. but here are some specifics - my faves from the most recent dinner there.

    sheeps milk ricotta
    fresh made and drizzled with olive oil, sea salt and usually some sort of wine reduction, it's light, creamy, with just the slightest tang from the vin cotto that keeps bringing you back in for more. spread it over the toasted slice of bread that accompanies it and conversation will stop until all is consumed.

    summer risotto: caramelized torpedo shallows + asaparagus + favas
    creamy. rich. nutty. fightworthy. the portion of this is small (not really, but it will seem small because you'll want to eat more of it), but it's rich and hearty enough that it will sate you. if there are more than 2 of you, you might want to consider getting another. the 4 of us were scraping the plate.

    asparago pizza: shaved asparagus + pancetta + secret white sauce + fior di latte (a type of mozzarella made from cow's milk) + truffle + farm egg
    people used to rave about the uovo pizza, but this asparago (which i view as the evolution of its predecessor uovo), seriously elevates pizza. the pancetta has the perfect saltiness to balance the rich truffle flavor and the egg. whereas the guanciale in the previous version maybe provided bursts of salty flavor, the pancetta distributes it evenly over the entire pizza, really helping to bring it all together. i'm not sure what's in the 'secret' white sauce, but it may as well be crack because we really had to restrain ourselves from licking that pan clean as well. (ok, maybe we have a problem with licking our food plates) but really, it's that good.

    pan seared gnocci: smoked corn, trumpet mushrooms, tomatoes, prosciutto, grana
    everything gnocci should be - soft and plump delicious with the sweetness of the corn and earthiness of the mushrooms, the semi tart bite of tomatoes, and the salt of the meat keeping it interesting.

    caciocavallo stuffed meatballs: shortrib + pork + calabrian chile
    honestly. i don't even know what to say about this other than this is stuffed with unicorn magic, happiness and enlightenment. best meatballs ever. in life. on earth. this universe.

    trecce: twisty pasta + mushrooms + arugula + lemon-walnut pesto + ricotta
    originally, we ordered this as consolation to our vegetarian friend who couldn't eat any of the meaty things. but it turned out to be such a pleasant surprise. the lemon-walnut pesto was really bright and lovely with the mushrooms and arugula. definitely a dish you could keep eating w/out getting tired of it.

    and of course... the BUDINO. yes, forget your diet. just get one. or better yet, everyone at the table get your own. worth it.

    as for the other desserts, i will say, they were a bit disappointing. but learn the lesson here - never stray from the budino!

    Does Barbuzzo need one more glowing review?  Probably not, but I'll spend five minutes writing one anyway.

    If you're trying to decide if you should eat at Barbuzzo, do it.  If you have some picky eaters in your group and they don't think they'll like anything on the menu, go anyway.

    Tonight was my second trip to Barbuzzo, and this time I brought my family.  My mom orders "the cheeseburger" no matter where we go.  Not this time!  My brothers and I shared the pig popcorn (fresh-ass pork rinds, still sizzling when they get to the table) and the roasted marrow bone.  We were enjoying the food so much that our Jewish mother ate the pig popcorn.  I don't think she regretted it.  The bone marrow will make your eyes roll back in your head, it's that good.  We ran out of crostini to spread it on, so I just slathered some all over a pork rind.  Pork-on-pork action!  I highly recommend it.

    The great thing about going to dinner with your family is that you can reach out and eat off of anyone's plate, so I got to try the octopus (with chorizo, creamy polenta, olives, and orange), short rib and pork meatballs, and the fideua.  LUCKY ME!  The octopus was cooked perfectly and I was dying to lick my plate.  Octopus is one of those things that's easy to overcook and make rubbery, but thankfully that didn't happen at Barbuzzo.  I wanted to store one of the tentacles in my cheek like a squirrel, it was that good.  The meatballs were probably the best I've ever had, and the fideua was OFF THE CHAIN.  The flavors were just so complex; you want to savor every bite just to experience the spectrum.

    Of course we got dessert.  I had the salted caramel budino which was creamy and rich as hell.  I could have had one perfect spoonful and been satisfied.  The whipped cream on top tastes so fresh, and normally I could skip the whip.  My brother's stone fruit bread pudding was actually my favorite dessert of all.  When the almond brown butter ice cream melted over top of the warm bread, it was the most decadent, comforting dessert.  Bread pudding is a great way to use up leftover bread, so it's really underrated.  ORDER IT!

    On top of all this, Barbuzzo's prices are crazy reasonable and they have an amazing beer selection.  This place has amazing food, but it's unpretentious.  It's cozy and cute--you could come here on a date, with your best friend, or with your whole family, and it would always be awesome.  Barbuzzo just makes you feel good.

    Farrotto.  Order this now!  The Farrotto at Barbuzzo is my new favorite dish of the Fall season.  Farrotto (made with Farro) basically mimics a risotto, and is equally as luxurious when made using simple ingredients.  Barbuzzo's Farrotto is no joke.  It is a comforting crock of creamy, nutty, perfectly al dente-cooked farro, laced with parmigiano reggiano and drizzled with balsamico. It was devoured in minutes.

    Now on to the rest of my review......A recent visit to Barbuzzo had us sitting at the Kitchen Counter overlooking the machine that is the Barbuzzo kitchen crew.  If you have the option to sit here, I highly recommend it.  Being so close to the fire, it only enhances your dining experience, plus it gives you the opportunity to interact with the crew that is cooking your fine food.  Extra arm room doesn't hurt either.

    We ordered about 65% of the entire menu, so I'll briefly touch on what stood out, both the Good and the Not As Good.  Pleased to see my new favorite pumpkin beer on their seasonal drink menu: Southern Tier Pumking.  The Pig Popcorn is addicting, still crackling when it's served to you, I need this at every bar in the city.  The Roasted Bone Marrow & Chicken Liver & Foie Gras Mousse tasted fine, but please serve it with the crusty grilled bread instead of the crostini.  Not a fan of trying to spread those two dishes on such fragile crostini.  The grilled bread however, I am a huge fan of.

    The Uovo Pizza was perfect, in every way. Charred & chewy crust, salty pancetta, blistered brussel sprout leaves and a creamy egg yolk in the middle.  The chili oil and fried oregano were unique side components that enhanced the flavor of the pizza.

    The Pan Seared Gnocchi and Caciocavallo Stuffed Meatballs were two of my favorite dishes.  The Gnocchi was more dense than I expected, but the flavors were spot on.  And who doesn't love some well made meatballs?

    The Fideua, Grilled Octopus & Calamari and Paccheri dishes were all just OK in my book.  The Fideua was nothing special and I wasn't sure how the garlic aioli is to be applied when eating this dish.  The Grilled Calamari wasn't charred enough.  The proportion of pork ragu to pasta was way off with the Paccheri dish.  More ragu, less pasta would make this much better.  All three of these dishes I would not order again.

    The Concord Grape & Balsamic Sorbet was a refreshing dessert.  

    Ok, I get why THE Salted Caramel Budino gets ALL the press.  It is addictively delicious. The Salted Caramel Budino is everything you want in a dessert: creamy, salty, sweet, delicious...caramel pudding perfection layered over a chocolaty crust. However, one can argue that a superior, even more delicious, yet less famous Budino dessert can be found over at 640 North Broad St.  Just saying. I will need to compare these Two Budinos (or Budini) side by side before any book is written.  You should too.

    4 Stars for Barbuzzo.
