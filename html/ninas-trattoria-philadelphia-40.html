    We were lucky enough to stumble upon this gem in the Italian market.  We had just arrived to town and wanted to grab a bite while walking through the market.  We ordered a couple of plates to share and could not have been more pleased with the food and service we received. We had the meatballs with some of the best fresh marinara I have ever had.  We also had the cured meats, cheeses, and bruschetta starter which featured some of the best, freshest cheeses from the neighboring shops in the market.  I could have died over the ricotta! Such a great experience all around!

    This is a great little BYOB, and a great find. I have been here for both lunch and dinner, and I've been very pleased both times. For lunch I had the sliders (amazing - juicy and perfectly grilled) and for dinner I had the hanger steak - which was also great, and cooked as I ordered it (medium). Great texture and nicely done!

    The bread you get while you await your meal or appetizers is wonderful. Flaky, drizzled with olive oil and more than enough for your group. The meat and cheese plate was my only minor complaint. The cheese was generous, great tasting and wonderful, and the meat...was only two pieces of each prosciutto and another type (forgetting). Maybe have 'four' pieces of each?  The bruschetta included was wonderful and fresh. Otherwise, meals are generously portioned - you'll leave feeling satiated.

    The service is attentive and fantastic. You're not ignored, they're friendly, and it's a great place to be. You always get the impression that they truly care that you are satisfied and they treat you like welcome 'guests.'

    I was glad that after having lunch there a few months ago, that the quality of the food has remained high. They use a lot of local Italian Market ingredients, and it shows. Everything always tastes fresh.

    Overall, I would definitely go there again, and recommend it to friends. The vibe is low-key, smart casual, and a great place for a date.

Since I'm in grad school and have the funds of a grad student- I jumped at the chance when I saw a Groupon for a place we've been dying to try in the neighborhood. $35 for a three course tasting menu for 2 people. Pair that with a cheap bottle of Chianti (cheap Chianti is just the same as expensive to me. lol) and you've got yourself a great, inexpensive meal.

    So why the 2 star review? Well, I read on yelp that it is necessary to make reservations for the groupon, or they will not let you use it. So last minute I called the place and the host informed me that since they were having a large party coming in that night we would have to be there within 30 minutes. Ok, not a huge problem since I live a few blocks away. We get there at 5:30, and we're the ONLY people there. I overhear the host/waiter tell the cook that the large party is coming in at 8:00...so why did we need to be there at 5:30?? And we were told they have an upstairs for plenty of space. That was a little confusing to us, but we rolled on.

    We get the first course and are told that the salad is from the Italian Market...this might be appealing to someone that doesn't usually shop at the market for bagged salad, but once you have, you'll realize that hearing that shouldn't make your mouth water. My first bite of the salad and it tasted dirty. Like actual dirt. Which is why I stopped buying salad at the market. LOL. The artichoke appetizer was...ok. Not great but not awful.
    The next course was 2 pastas, gnocchi with Gorgonzola cream and walnuts and linguine with red sauce and bacon. The gnocchi was under cooked, chewy and you could still taste the flour. The gorgonzola cream sauce didn't have that nice bite of a gorgonzola. They informed us that the cheese was from Claudio's...I've had better gorgonzola from Claudio's, so maybe they're buying the wrong one or not using enough. The linguine was good. Chunks of tomato and bacon pieces made a nice balance.
    Dessert was great- pistachio ice cream provided from across the street was really good, but Nina's can't take credit for that.
    The service was annoying and almost intrusive. The waiter was young and wanted to come across VERY mature which only made him seem more immature. The thing that upset my fiance and I the most though was when other patrons, a guy and a girl, tried to come in (~6:30) to get some dinner and the waiter turned them away saying that since they have a large party coming in they won't have space. Well this wouldn't have bothered us so much, but the waiter had already explained that there was an upstairs dining area that provided plenty of space. So if they had all of that space and about an hour and a half before their huge party, then why not take the extra business? What was even more shocking was when the waiter suggested to the 2 patrons that they should try another restaurant down the street, as far as giving them the name (and this place is CLEARLY a competitor of Nina's). The guy audibly laughed, I think out of sheer shock and they promptly left. And I can almost guarantee they won't be back. A Sunday night at the Italian Market...they were probably locals- way to lose that business forever.
    I really hope that HUGE party came in that night because they had a lot riding on it...and because of that and the so-so food we won't be back.
