    Holy crow is this place fantastic!

    Great balance of nice wines and beers on tap, with interesting and unique beer and cheese selections. I always take people here when I want to celebrate.     I'm usually off put by the frufru places, but this place somehow manages to be frufru but casually welcoming at the same time? Yeah, it's mostly bougie yuppies here, but they made me, a kinda grungy 20-something hipster and my friends, feel fairly comfortable, considering the aforementioned bougie surroundings.

    Overall a fantastic experience and, most importantly, totally and completely blown away by the food and drink.

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge, In and around Ritten-wait-for-it-HOUSE, my house, Pour me something tall and strong

    What else is there really to say about this place? Such a perfect, charming little wine/cheese/beer lets-hang-out-and-chat-awhile kinda place. Perfect for lunch, a light snack, a meet up with old friends.
    I frequent this place for before and/or after dinner drinks, but have also come here for a light lunch or dinner. Every food item is perfectly executed. If you just want snacks, order some of the rosemary potato chips or fennel roasted almonds. The toasts are also a perfect quick snack-I love the one with honey and walnuts.
    Honestly I know this place is a wine bar, but the beer is where it's at. They have really great draft selections, frequently changing, and an extensive bottle list. The prices for beer are pretty reasonable, which was surprising to me. Also to note-the happy hour specials are awesome. Definitely do happy hour here.
    This place is one of those that I'm happy to visit again and again. And everyone else in Rittenhouse feels this way, too. Expect to wait or squeeze in at the bar at peak times. It'll be worth it, though.

If you just want snacks, order some of the rosemary potato chips or fennel roasted almonds. The toasts are also a perfect quick snack-I love the one with honey and walnuts.
    Honestly I know this place is a wine bar, but the beer is where it's at. They have really great draft selections, frequently changing, and an extensive bottle list. The prices for beer are pretty reasonable, which was surprising to me. Also to note-the happy hour specials are awesome. Definitely do happy hour here.

Specialties

Welcome to Tria, Philadelphia's wine, cheese and beer cafe. We serve real wines, cheeses and beers crafted by passionate people. We relentlessly seek out the best products both locally and from around the world. We dig flavor. Our ever-changing fermentation menus are complemented by a cafe menu served from opening to late night. Don't know the difference between Cabernet and Carmignano? Don't worry. Our friendly, knowledgeable staff will help guide you through a memorable wine, cheese and beer experience.
