    Listed in All Your Beer Are Belong To Us

    One of my favorite spots in Philly.  I've probably been here a few dozen times and almost every experience was great (I don't get really upset when I have to wait in a place when it's slammed).  

    Here is a tip though, the joint isn't very large even though it's two floors.  If you're going to go from Thur-Sat evenings, do yourself a favor and make a reservation on opentable (they don't take phone reservations) otherwise you may wait a long time or not be seated.  They do get PACKED.

    First let me say that the servers are always awesome.  They have upbeat attitudes, they charge back and forth between tables and really know their stuff about beer (at least way more than me) so if you're unsure, definitely ask them - also ask if they have any special beers being offered.  They typically have a dozen beers on tap upstairs and a different dozen downstairs, these do rotate but certain staples seem to always be available - St. Bernardus Apt 12 - I love you.  If you can't find something good from that selection, they offer about 300 beers to chose from and if you can't find what you like from that, you are probably a snotty dick and I'm not writing this review for you.

    For food, it's not overly diverse.  They have a dozen different ways to get a bucket of mussels, but I never try them (they look good, I just don't like them).  There soups are good, burger is great and fish and chips are AWESOME.  If you like peel shrimp, I HIGHLY recommend getting a 1/2 or full pound to share because they are deliciously smothered in old bay.  If you stick to these staples, you'll probably enjoy your food.  But I recommend this joint for the cool experience, great beer and decent eats, not simply as a dinner spot.

    If you want to have a cool party atmosphere, reserve the coffin room upstairs - I have, it's pretty awesome.

    Don't miss this place and if you go when it's jammed, don't let that experience deter you from tying it again.

    Have been here a couple times now and if a half-star rating was possible, I'd rate this a solid three and a half stars.  I was kind and rounded it up to four.  Bottom line is Eulogy serves really, really good beer, especially if, like me, you've traveled in Europe or been to other Belgian brewpubs.  However, getting served the beer is a bigger pain in the butt here than either in Europe or at other fine, crowded Belgian pubs I've visited (i.e. Dr. Granville Moore's in DC, which fills up regularly with some of the most annoying customers ever, but has awesome, nice staff).

    On my first visit here, I was with a large party of about 10 people and we took over a big table upstairs. I got there late when everybody else had been served and there did not seem to be a waiter coming around to check on who might still need a drink.  After waiting about 10 minutes, and not wanting to hold up our party since we were all on the way to a Penn's Landing event within the hour, I went downstairs (didn't see anyone at the upstairs bar) and tried to order a beer.  At that point I discovered/ was told that (a) they were out of the beer I wanted, (b) if I was sitting upstairs I needed to order it from the upstairs bar, and (c) the waiter needed to bring it to me.  I have no idea what all this mattered - I wanted a freaking beer for heavens sake, I had been working all day, I wasn't trying to charge the beer to a tab (had a bill in my hand ready to pay for the beer right then and there) and it shouldn't have been this much of a production.  I did eventually get a beer after the waiter finally got around to bringing it to me (which took about another 10 minutes even though this was like 7 pm on a weekday, hardly peak hours) and the beer was very good and made me happy, but I wish getting it had not been such a hassle.

    On my second visit, I ended up not even getting a beer because my husband and I were looking for a place we could sit down and, although Eulogy had one table free, it was only for a party of 6 or more.  Not "reserved" through Open Table or anything, just sitting there empty waiting for 6 people to maybe show up.  I have no idea why they couldn't have filled the table with 3 couples (it was one of those tables that separates into 2 or 3 if you pull) and the waiter who shooed me from the table was some snooty guy with a Euro accent, not at all friendly.  Whatever, if they would rather have an empty table than our quick business (we were looking to drink about 2 beers apiece with one appetizer and go, and it was still early in the evening about 10 at nite) then more power to them.  We ended up going to the Khyber catty-corner from Eulogy where the beer selection is almost as good and there were plenty of tables for 2 or 4, as well as much friendlier staff.

    Eulogy still gets 4 stars because the beer is just that good, but from now on I'm only going in there very early or with a premade Open Table reservation.  Unlike all of the Belgian beer joints I frequented in Europe, Eulogy is simply not a friendly place to drop in on a whim for a beer.

Specialties

This is Beer Heaven, give a "high praise" Eulogy for your favorite beer after you consume it.  We also prepare our food in the authentic Flemish Belgian Style as opposed to other Belgian restaurants who choose the French Walloon Belgian style.  This means our fries are thick enough to truly hold a double fry technique, our fries go "crunch, goo" because they are the right thickness, the wimpy skinny fries elsewhere go "goo goo goo" because they are too skinny and the double fry technique does not work.  Also our mussels are larger than the PEIs other restaurants use, this is because our mussels are the authentic North Sea size like in Belgium.  The more meat the more flavor the mussel can hold in our ala minute pan technique (all ingredients added fresh, never ever ever any pour over sauce on mussels).  Also our handburgers are award winning based on seven secret spices and our fish and chips and other entrees are as good as the beers ! Make a reservation at www.eulogybar.com

History

Established in 2002.

My family is from Brugge, Belgium, I am passionate about Belgian beer, food and heritage, this adds an authenticity to the business.  I got the building in 2002 and built the restaurant myself using antique stain glass behind the bar from an old Philadelphia church and a chandelier from an old church as well.  The bar was constructed in Fishtown in Philadelphia by a local carpenter using Honduras Mahogany I imported and the design of the bar was based on a blueprint I drew.  
We take our beer very very very serious, our beer starts in a beer cooler and is then delivered via dual gas blenders using co2 and nitrogen blends for the proper carbonation technique.  Most of our draft beer never travels more than six feet but along that journey there are glycol filled lines keeping the beer at 40 ish degrees until it hits your glass.  We have about 200 different glasses we keep in stock because most of the taste for beer is accomplished through your smelling it, yes your nose!
