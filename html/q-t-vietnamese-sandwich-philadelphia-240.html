    Service:
      * Fast and excellent service from friendly staff.

    Ambiance and Location:
      * This is a no frills, take-out restaurant with some tables for dine in as well.
      * It's tucked away along 10th St, slightly hidden.  It's literally a hidden gem in the midst of busy Chinatown.

    Food:
      * This review is for the sandwiches.  I tried the Vietnamese coffee here and prefer the real version elsewhere.  
      * Well, for those who are familiar with the Vietnamese hoagie or "banh mi" sandwich. This delicious sandwich combines French and Vietnamese ingredients, such as baguettes, pâté and mayonnaise (French) with native Vietnamese ingredients, such as cilantro, fish sauce, and pickled carrots.
      * I have been frequenting this place, despite many others around the corner, for their extravagantly stuffed, delicious banh mi sandwiches, freshly made to order, so the baguettes are quite warm and crunchy to my expectations. Yumm.
      * A must try are the vegetarian tofu sandwiches (bánh mì chay).  I am not a tofu person and prefer everything else.  But I totally like the tofu sandwiches here, a lot.  I am genuinely surprised that such a simple shop can create a delicious tofu sandwich with such robust southeast Asian flavors.
      * My frequent orders are the lemongrass tofu sandwich or the tofu mushroom sandwiches. Both are so good.  I can't find these anywhere else in Philly, NYC, DC, Seattle, or even my hometown Cali.

    Summary and Value:
      * The Vietnamese banh mi sandwich is "the ultimate on-the-go fare".
      * Five stars for value (quality ingredients, delicious sandwiches, and reasonable price).  Also, for the different flavors (compared with other similar shops) and healthy factors, it is definitely a good value for your money.  If you are in a hurry and around Philly, definitely try this place.  Bring cash though since they don't take credit cards.
