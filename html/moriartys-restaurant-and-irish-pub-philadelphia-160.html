    Most of the time when I go to Moriarty's, I'm going for Saturday night karaoke. It's always absurdly crowded, loud, and more often than not there's a huge group of women there for a bachelorette or birthday party. I don't expect to sing much, or to be able to carry a conversation, but you can't beat the level of energy in the room.

    They have a diverse selection of both food and drink, with tons of appetizer and sandwich options. People rave about the wings, and while they are very tasty, I am not a fan. Unlike most wings, they don't trim the wingtips off, making for a difficult eating experience, as you pry apart the wing, drummette, and tip

    The waitstaff generally handles the crowds well, but can understandably get overwhelmed. They have 3 floors available, with the 1st floor often packed, the second floor with fewer TVs and hosting the karaoke night, and a private third floor for events. It seems like a popular destination for conventioneers as well.

    Moriarty's was my second stop on my self-guided gastronomical adventure last night. A local colleague recommended it earlier in the week, and when I noticed it was right across the street from Farmers Cabinet I took it as a sign. My visit was mandatory.

    Upon entering, I realized this is my kind of place. Lively, friendly crowd, ample selection of drinks, hockey playoffs on TV, and a menu featuring all of the traditional Irish fare. Harry the bartender took good care of me. When I asked his recommendation he highly recommended the wings, then delivered a plate of wings to the couple sitting beside me. Another sign.

    These are not your everyday, run-of-the-mill wing pieces. Here you get the entire wing, huge wings, covered in a delicious buffalo sauce. The portion was extremely generous, and I left two behind. I thoroughly enjoyed my evening (except for the "blue shirts" softball team or whatever that came in and totally disrupted the atmosphere for awhile) and found my wing spot for whenever I am in Philadelphia.
