    I expected a beautiful place, with plentiful food. The ambiance was a minor disappointment. The place is large and noisy, with bebop jazz that was a tad too loud for my taste.The lighting is low-beam florescent (Seems like an oxymoron!) which was not particularly to my liking. Not necessarily the place for an intimate dinner. To me, it tried a little too hard to be the hippest place in town, but maybe it's me.../  The staff was customer-focused, pleasant, and eager to please. They sat me in front of the main door, and every time it opened I got a chilling blast. When asked they willingly sat me farther away from the chilly part of the room. ./ Now to the food: I ordered the the escargot-tender, sweet, and delicious, but only about 5 snails. The sauce was divine, and they brought me an extra baggette to sop up the goodies. Yum.  I ordered the boulliabaise for the main course, which had a mixture of large, plump shrimp, tender fish (Sea bass? Bluefish?),  succulent mussels, in a silky, pale tomato sauce. There were  two sauces (one a rouille, and  one unknown) and large croutons you added yourself. Delish, but the dish was only the size of  a large appetizer. Again, more bread to dip into the sauce. The pomme frites were crisp, salty, plentiful, and skinny, just the way I like them. Then to the house-made salted caramel ice cream--
    rich, smooth, satisfying, and more caramelly than sweet- a treat to behold -an awesome finish to good meal./ Although the portions were meager and the bill was $62, the food was very tasty, and prepared with care. Would I go there again? Perhaps, but I could do with quieter atmosphere, with a little less "happening" vibe.

    When the weather outside is frightful, a trip to Parc.... delightful. "Snowed in" the other day, a buddy and I decided we couldn't be cooped up forever, and we needed to escape for a mid-day treat. When she suggested Parc, I initially scoffed, having previously enjoyed this establishment only on the sunniest of days, al fresco dining at it's best.... so I thought. Little did I know what warm, savory comfort foods lay within.

    Though, on previous trips, I'd reveled in the warm shrimp salad (an absolute MUST) and cheese plates, this visit I was craving hot, gooey, cheesy and crisp, starchy taters. And Parc, as per, delivered just that, along with a stunning few of a snow-covered Rittenhouse Wonderland. In order to maintain pre-hibernation energy, my buddy and I enjoyed the macaroni au gratin and some french fries, of course. My eyes were drawn to the steaming bowls of french onion soup and, on another day, I'm certain I'll try.... but today, I had my eyes on the prizes.

    We enjoyed a beer (for me) and a boozy cider (for her), as well as some engaging conversation with our server, who was hilarious, quick, and clever. Parc employees seem to always know what to say to make my afternoon/evening better and brighter, and this was no different.

    The macaroni gratin was unreal. I have several friends with whom I regularly discuss the idea of an "MVM" (Most Valuable Macaroni) in the city, and while the current leader is Monk's.... I think they're in need of a little day trip to Parc for re-evaluation. The pommes frites are always crisp and well salted, and the pre-meal bread.... well, we certainly got our share of carbs and starches for the day (for the week?) And we both came out as proud members of the clean plate club.

    I will say that, on another evening, I was visiting Parc on a more rambunctious day, where the pace was faster and the food was flying. It was on this earlier visit that, due to a waiter to waitress body bump, a carafe of red wine was dumped atop the chambray shirt of one of my dining partners. Not a glass, mind you. This was a butt-load of red wine. And though club soda was rushed over and portions of the meal were comped, the manager also stopped by to offer to pay for the dry cleaning. However, when she visited her dry cleaner and was immediately laughed at since the shirt was what we called "a lost cause", Parc purchased her a brand new one. No questions asked.

    The peak of customer service, the zenith of macaroni treats, and the location.... No excused not to park it at Parc.

    So, out tonight with my guy and friends for our monthly Friday Foodie gathering. It was my 1st time at Parc and I was very pleased.

    Piping through the sound system was melodic french music which sounded like a variety of contemporary jazz artists. Great tempo and volume for the overflowing crowd dining for the evening. Although there were many people filling the tables and bar area, the noise factor was surprisingly neither overpowering nor intrusive.

    The restaurant was larger than I expected but tables were spaciously distributed so you did't feel so close to the neighboring table. Dim lighting set the early evening mood with hint of romance but enough light to make it appropriate amongst friends.

    For my appetizer, I had escargot and my table-mates indulged in Macaroni au Gratin and ravioli stuffed shells with a delicate cheese. We all shared and each was an 8 out of 10 on the delicious scale. The escargot was tender in a broth containing mushrooms (& other ingredients I can't recall). They were out of their shell which was a departure from my previous escargot experiences. The Macaroni au gratin was piping hot with a white cheese. It was slightly rich and creamy with a mildly sweet taste that was so appetizing. The ravioli hors d' oeuvres of four was a appropriate size and were good. Light cheese filling and the pasta for the ravioli was simple without any fanfare.

    For my entree, I had pan-seared scallops which in one word, Divine. A mild sweet flavor, tender and juicy. Although only 4 scallops were served with dinner,  they were generous in size and very filling. A top the scallops were black trumpet mushrooms, onion suboise &  peas. I am not a mushroom fan but found the assortment of all 3 to be tasty and savory.  

    For dessert I had the coconut sorbet with rum... It was so lip and tongue swirling delicious, I decided on my next visit, I'm eating dessert 1st. Dessert & a cocktail all in one. What more needs to be written!?

    Enjoyed, happy & full = return & I mean soon...

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge

    After a NYE tasting at Twenty Manning Grill, the GF and I came to Parc for a few cocktails before heading to a NYE party with some friends. The atmosphere at this French brasserie is comfortable and cozy, decked out in a celebratory mode.

    Basilic [$10]
    Smirnoff Citrus, elderflower liqueur, basil, cucumber

    I opted for the basilic, which was easily quaffable, heady with basil and cucumber flavors. It's not overly saccharine and certainly refreshing.

    Kir Royale [$10]
    Sparkling wine, creme de cassis

    The GF went with the Kir Royale, which had some bite and effervescence from the champagne and sweetness from the creme de cassis. Classic.

    And on a recent Sunday morning, the GF and I decided to return for some brunch fare as well. So onto the food!

    Crab Galette [$14]
    Sauce gribiche  

    We started off with the crab galette. Essentially a crab cake without much filler, the accompanying gribiche brought mustard to the forefront to balance out the sweet flavor of the crab. The greens were tossed in a light vinaigrette and helped to bring some acidity to the dish.

    Bread Service

    Interestingly enough, bread service came after the initial appetizer. Even more jarring was the fact that the server asked if we still wanted it. I mean, bread? In a "French" brasserie? Why not. It came freshly baked and included a mini baguette, cranberry walnut, and some country bread. The pat of butter was thankfully room temp. Do get the bread if asked.

    Cheeseburger [$10]
    Grilled onion, raclette, pommes frites

    The GF decided to go with the burger. The raclette cheese was perfectly gooey and salty. The burger was requested medium and came out at the right temp - juicy and well-seasoned. The caramelized onions helped to add extra heft to each bite. The fries were crispy and thin - not much more you could ask for in French fries.

Specialties

This stylish addition to Rittenhouse Square pays tribute to French cafe culture, serving breakfast, lunch and dinner seven days a week. Ideal for a quick bite or a leisurely meal, the menu is filled with classics: onion soup, escargots, steak frites and towering plateaus de fruits de mer. The baguettes are made in-house and  the wine list is expansive. Sip a Kir Royale at the zinc bar or enjoy a cafe au lait while basking in the sun.

STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District.
