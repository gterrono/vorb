    The falafel fried at Mama's are golden and cripsy outside and fully fluffy and flavorful inside. A freakin delight, I tell you. The pita bread too! The pita baked here daily are super freshy and excellently soft and sturdy at the same time. Oooh if there's anything I can't stands, it's a flimsy pita. So, this particular pita problem is not something I have to worry my falafel sandwich lovin' head about here. It'll support the falafel and creamy hummus and eggplant, as well as any of the variety of pickled vegetables and tasty hot sauces that I wanna pile on them beauties from the pickle bar.

Whenever I'm in the vicinity, I make it my business to have make any excuse to stop in at this little Kosher vegetarian joint.

    Listed in The very best between two pieces of bread

    Life-changer. I had tried to come to this place at least 10 times before finally realizing that they are indeed closed every Saturday. But now, I'm totally willing to work around their schedule. After eating their falafel sandwiches, I feel like I owe them a lot more than the ~$6 I'm paying them.

    The falafel is so flavorful and crispy, and it has just enough smush factor that it melds with the other ingredients in the pita. You won't find a stale, hockey puck like chickpea patty here.

    I order the large falafel sandwich, although the small could probably do, get a little side of pickled carrots and other fix-ins, and hunker down. The veggies are always fresh tasting, and the ingredient ratios are just right. Service is fast AND friendly- they care about their customers.  I wish they had cups for tap water so I didn't have to buy a bottle, but I guess it's my fault for not remembering to bring my own water bottle each time I come.

    Finding the perfect Falafel is one of the toughest things to do.  Everyone makes them so differently.  For the four years that I was in undergrad in Manhattan I ate a falafel almost every day so I've become a bit of an expert.  I've been in the mood for a falafel so a friend recommended Mama's Veg.  It's a cute lil place.  It's cash only with a few tables if you want to eat in.  They have a salad bar for those who order sandwiches or platters, but not sides.  There are pickles, pickled beats, sauces and hot peppers there.  Be careful of that big green hot pepper...its really hottttt!  As for the falafel sandwich...all in all it was a good sandwich...very heavy.  You will not leave hungry.  You get your choice of either white pita or wheat pita and they put about 4 falafel balls into the pita with hummus and a shephard salad mix (chopped cucumbers and tomatoes) with white sauce.  I had them add a lil hot sauce too which added a nice kick.  As for the actual falafel balls.  I think they were too heavy...way too much moisture...very dense and not crispy enough!  Definitely not a perfect falafel ball.  The hummus was ok...very smooth...lacked a hint sesame flavor though.  The white sauce was average.  I prefer tahini sauce on my falafels, but yogurt sauce will do.  A positive is that the ingredients were really fresh!  Even though this wasn't the best falafel I ever had (I'm very picky about my falafels) I would come back.  Besides $6 for a healthy lunch or dinner is well worth it!  Be careful during lunch time...a line quickly forms outside. : )
