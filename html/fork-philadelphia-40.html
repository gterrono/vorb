    Amazing find while in Philly. We originally made plans to have dinner at another restaurant in the area (Chloe), but discovered that they only accepted cash. Definitely a no-go when on business trips.

    We searched places in the area and found Fork 0.3 miles away...so we made our way here. Nice, smaller, space with a boutique feel to it.  We were greeted by 2 large curtains that need to be shifted to the side to enter once past the door. We were immediately greeted by someone who gave us the option of any seat in the place - even the unique space in the "bay" of the front window. We decided not to since the seat sat 4 and it was only 2 of us. She proceeded to seat us near the back against the wall. The place was very nicely decorated; nice neutral colors and quality accents throughout.

    We immediately ordered a nice cab (not many cheap wine options here) and gave ourselves 5 minutes to order. We decided on the following:

    - Bread (free): 4 options (multigrain, wheat, white, rye)...served with a very sweet butter (brown sugar?)
    - Cauliflower Soup: We both tried the cauliflower soup...and both decided that it was definitely lacking something. Spice. Seasoning. Something...great presentation and nice touch with the chunky lobster pieces. I would pass.
    - Branzino: Unique Mediterranean fish that I had never heard of, served with head, tail, eyes, everything...tasted great. It was tough (and very annoying) to pick through all the bones though. Served with spinach and a heavy cream sauce.
    - Risotto: Great and simple dish. Great flavor.
    - Brussell Sprouts: Great; pan-seared with bacon and heavy oil. Browned to perfection.

    We were very satisfied with the meal and would highly recommend this place to anyone. I love the small feel of the restaurant; you could tell that the entrees were well thought-out. Will have this on my short list next time I'm in Philly.
