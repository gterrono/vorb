    This place is a Philadelphia staple.  Their happy hour specials are great (and gets rightfully packed), the quality of their seafood far surpasses probably any other place in the city that serves shellfish.
    The staff was friendly and pleasant despite being heckled by anxious patrons awaiting a table, the guys behind the raw bar never stopped moving.

    Our shrimp cocktail was pretty standard, the oysters were fresh and well shucked (some places leave shell and grit in the oyster, these guys know what they're doing).  Kumamotos were outstanding.

    The lobster roll is absolutely obligatory if you are going to visit this place.  I know, a $26 sandwich is outrageous.  But it's worth it, there is at least a whole lobster tail worth of meat in each sandwich.  Get it.

    I only wish the space was more bar and less dining room,  it can get rather cramped.  All the more reason to feign illness and leave work early to get a seat.

    I truly believe that the Oyster House is my favorite place in Philly because there isn't a time of day when it isn't absolutely perfect to come here. Brunch, lunch, happy hour, and dinner are all great times to visit with friends or family.

    Must haves:

    Brunch: Shrimp & Grits

    Happy Hour: Maybe the best in the city! Mon-Fri is buck a shuck ($1 an oyster) on a select variety. They also have a special mixed drink ($5), beer on draft ($3), and an oyster shooter ($3). Go for an oyster shooter if you are feeling adventurous!

    Anytime: New England clam chowder, mussels, and needless to say oysters oysters oysters! In particular my favorites are the little shemogues, moonstones, and kusshi oysters. I prefer smaller and west coast varieties!

    Look, there are shady seafood spots and then there are places that serve you quality, fresh seafood. Oyster House is definitely the ladder. You may be paying a bit more than you would at a shady South Philly seafood dive, but you sure get what you pay for. What I especially like about Oyster House is that they don't skimp on the small plate portions.     There is a lot to love about this place. Sometimes you just want to feast on seafood and being in Center City away from the water can make that difficult. Although I've now only been here once, this is my go to seafood spot from now on. FYI this is NOT the oyster house on opentable (oops) and they do not take reservations for small groups so be prepared to face the other seafood ravenous patrons.

    There is a decent sized bar space to set up shop and crush oyster crackers at while waiting for a table. Our 20 minutes on a Saturday night turned into 45 minutes but luckily we had a solid bar post up so not a big deal. This 45 minutes gave plenty of time to thoroughly examine all the menu has to offer which made for strategic ordering once our table was ready. I'm always a sucker for seafood towers and they serve a sampler with oysters, clams, shrimp and lobster. All of these were super fresh and impressive in size but the oysters and lobster shined brightest. I'm not going to pretend I can tell the difference between east coast oysters but whatever was on the plate was delicious and went great with their house white wine.

    Other items I got to try included the southern fried oysters (forgot to get the chicken salad addition) which melted in my mouth. The snapper turtle soup sounded too interesting not to sample and it was very rich, more of a winter chili-like dish but still very flavorful. Lastly the lobster roll. I could have used some more meat for $26 but there was barely any filler, how it should be. Go with the NE chilled style and the fries are the perfect complement.

    Passed on dessert but the cocktails were also very tasty from what I tried including the gin old fashioned. They do weekday and Saturday night reverse happy hour which I can see myself at in the near future.
