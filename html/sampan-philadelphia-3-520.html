    Sampan has the BEST happy hour in the city! Their food on happy hour is between $2-$4, and lemme tell you, it's a steal! I would suggest going with a couple friends and ordering several things to munch on together. I highly recommend the Kim Chee fried rice... very flavorful and a good sized portion. Other favorite items include the brussel sprouts (crispy and just the right spices), potstickers, and the korean BBQ beef satay... DELICIOUS.. but it only comes with 2 skewers. If you're here with friends make sure you get a couple orders of the beef satay because you will all want them. I've tried almost everything here, the only thing I wasn't a fan of was the edamame dumplings, everything else is so delicious. I know I used that word once already in this review but there's not a better word to describe their food! You will not be disappointed.
    I've always had great service here, everyone is very friendly. They're extremely busy during happy hour, so try not to come with a big group if you want to find seats. If the inside area is full, head out back to their graffiti bar... same menu and prices, just some extended seating! It's covered and they have heaters out back if you're worried about the cold.

    Fun and unique! Sampan offers a modern and hip atmosphere for small plates and drinks. Great spot for a girls night out, light dinner and drinks, or a date. Menu is an Asian fusion of classic dim sum style foods as well as unique twists on some classics. This restaurant is ideal for ordering a few items and sharing amongst the table. Sampan was also voted one of the best Happy Hours in Philadelphia with small bites (like edamame dumplings, spicy chicken wings, pork bao bun, kobe slider, kobe hotdog, kim chee fried rice, etc) all for $2-4 as well as drink specials. Be sure to arrive early it gets packed here during Happy Hour.
    My favorites on the menu include General Tso Soup Dumplings, "Philly Cheesesteak", Crispy Rock Shrimp, Beef Short Rib Lo Mein, and Soy Glazed Chilean Sea Bass. Chef's Tasting Menu is offered for $45 and includes 7 courses + soft serve. You should really try their original daily soft serve flavors served in mini ice cream cones (flavors like Swedish fish, chocolate covered pretzel, and cookie dough are some of my favorites).
    Make sure to also check out the cool bathrooms here that play audio from various famous movie clips!
    Sampan is a Michael Schulson Restaurant. If you like it here try visiting another Schulson Restaurant, Izakaya at The Borgata Atlantic City, NJ.

    1. THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT THING ABOUT SAMPAN: GO TO HAPPY HOUR. Happy hour food and drinks are absolutely stellar. Did I mention that the food was awesome?! The HH menu is varied and offers a great bang for the buck.

    2. Go for the short ribs. It was well braised and flavorful. The noodles in the dish are enough to feed a small country. Again family styled, meant to be shared.

    3. Skip the general tso's chicken or sesame chicken or whatever Americanized deep-fried Chicken dish. It comes out stale and a bit salty.

    4. If you have a chance to sit outside on a nice night, do it! The outside seating offers a nice contrast to the rather busy interior. Though beware, there is a slight angle on the sidewalk making the sit a bit awkward.

    Conclusion:

    The atmosphere inside is hip and colorful. HH can't be missed (though you might due to limited seating in the back). This really is a quality restaurant that does justice to Asian fusion. Remember, bring a few friends! If you each are eating your own plate, you're doing it wrong!

    Two words: HAPPY HOUR.

    There really isn't a better time to come dine at Michael Schulson's Sampan or outdoor Graffiti Bar. First of all, happy hour is EVERY SINGLE DAY. Yes, you heard correctly- from 4:30-7:00 (one of the longest happy hours in the city), you can dine on CHEAP delectable Asian fusion eats, and pair them with even cheaper alcoholic beverages.

    I will give you the Sampan/Graffiti Bar Happy Hour Breakdown:

    1. Make sure you ask what the specialty cocktail is- it changes everyday, and for just a mere $4, you could be indulging in a high end refreshing cocktail. For example: Grapefruit Margarita, Cherry Vodka Spritz, etc.
    2. Kobe Beef Slider is $2. Need I say more? Put down two of these babies, and you're on your way to red meat heaven.
    3. Edamame Dumplings $4. The most exciting and well thought out dish on the HH menu-the flavors of Edamame, Truffle Pea Shoot, and Sake broth come together to give you a new twist on an old traditional dish.
    4. Kim Chee Fried Rice $4- being the biggest dish on the HH menu, this plate is self satisfying in itself. One order of this fried rice paired with a nice glass of happy hour white or red (also $4) will leave you feeling like you just spent $50 on dinner.
    5. Last but not least the Pork Bao Bun $4. Not one, but TWO Pork "sandwiches" inhabit this dish, complete with Pork Belly, Mint and Shallot. The soft and doughy Asian bun paired with the crunchy and tender glazed pork will leave you speechless (and thankful that Happy Hour is every afternoon).
