    Good Dog is a really appropriate name for this bar. Notice that it's named "good" dog, not "great" or "outstanding" dog. Anyway, the grub they serve here is either:
    a) really good bar food or
    b) decent food for a restaurant depending on your perspective.

    We visisted on a snowy Saturday for lunch and tried the buffalo shrimp small plate (small fried shrimp in buffal wing sauce...nothing special), grilled baby octopus salad (decent but nothing earth shattering and after a while the really big pieces of watercress made me feel like a rabbit), and bigger plates including the lamb belly sandwich (which was kind of fatty but ok tasting), a grilled cheese (decent), mac and cheese (ok) blueberry cornbread (kinda dry), and a good dog burger (that was actually really, really good). The fries/sweet potato fries were decent but not as good as we remember from a previous visit. So if you want a good burger and a brew this is the place to go. Otherwise you may be a little disappointed.

    The service was attentive at first but became less so as the place filled up (I think there was only one server for all of the booths).

    I tend to think of Good Dog as the place I've gone to on a couple of awkward dates, but that was a few years ago and now it can just be a bar in the city.

    It has everything a bar should have: alcohol, noisy music, noisy people and lots of them. First impression is that it wants to be a dive. You expect to roll up to the bar for a PBR and a shot and get a greasy paper menu to order a basket of fries drowning in congealed orange sauce that's trying really hard to be cheese.

    But once you're inside and get a look at the menu, you start to think that maybe your first impression was a little off. Because the menu looks fancier than it should be. And the food, it turns out, is tastes better than you would expect out of an otherwise dumpy looking bar. I've never had the famed burger, but I can vouch for the grilled cheese (smoked gouda, slab bacon, apples). I've eaten other things back when it was an awkward date spot, and the food was more memorable than the dates. To be fair, that isn't saying much, but everything on the menu is worth trying.

    Service is fine and friendly. Last time I was here my friend and I didn't get run out by the waitress even though it was starting to get busy and we were only drinking water by then. Of course we didn't stay too long, but it was nice to hear "stay as long as you want."

    My boyfriend Sam and I saw GDB on Diners Drive-Ins and Dives and since he lives so close we figured that we needed to check it out. We were NOT disappointed. The atmosphere is sort of London pub-y, sans smoke. Dark, close, and intimate - we really liked it. We were seated almost immediately and our server automatically brought us two waters (which I love!) and the specials menu. Although I can't remember his name, he was super helpful (and cute!) and explained the specials and gave us encouragement on the things that we ordered so that we were confident in our choices.

    For our appetizer, we ordered the Truffled Cheese Steak Empanadas which were featured on Triple D. For drinks, I ordered a Caramel Apple (Jim Beam, Caramel Monin Syrup,
    Sour Mix, Cherry Bitters, Apple Slice) and my boyfriend ordered a Ginger Baker (Goslings Black Seal Rum, Crystalized Ginger, Apple Juice, Orange Bitters). The appetizer came out super fast and super hot and was out of this world good. I don't like truffle oil but I really wanted to try them due to Guy Fieri's endorsement and I was not disappointed. The drinks came up right before the appetizer came out and they were also very good. Sam liked his but thought that it needed a stronger Ginger flavor; I wasn't too crazy about mine but I am not a big alcohol person. I just gave mine to Sam and stuck with my water which was fine with me.

    For an entree, I ordered the Good Dog Burger which was 1/2 lb. of house ground sirloin with roquefort cheese and caramelized onions, on a brioche bun. It came with a mix of their potato and sweet potato fries and their house made aioli. I thought my taste buds died and went to heaven - it was that good. At first I wasn't too sure about the roquefort cheese but the more had the better I liked it. It really grew on me and by the time the burger was gone I wanted more. Also - huge portions - I had to take some fries home with me (and they were great cold out of the fridge too!). Sam ordered the Lamb Belly sandwich which consisted of thinly sliced lamb belly brined in red wine, roasted then lightly grilled, topped with oven roasted tomatoes, pickled red onions, arugula & lemon aioli on rosemary focaccia (taken right from their website), also with the same mix of fries and sweet potato fries. His was also amazing; we always trade at least one bite of whatever we have. I wasn't too sure about the pickled red onions but they really complimented the other flavors of the sandwich.

    We will definitely come back for more - next time we want to try the Duck Pot Pie and Chicken Cordon Bleu, both of which were also featured on Triple D. Can't wait until the next time - it really was THAT good!

    Ok - I love this place. It's 3 floors (bars on 1st & 2nd, pool table/hanging out room on 3rd), has black and white pics of dogs lining the walls and has awesome food. The draught beer selection is limited but Good Dog typically includes good craft beers within the small list. Cocktails are fun and festive, with warm cocktails that are sure to heat you up during the Winter and early Spring. I've eaten here numerous times and generally order the grilled cheese sandwich, it's crunchy and the bacon is a salty contrast to the tart apples.

    The mac and cheese and duck pot pie are out of this world, so good. The mac and cheese is gooey and has a crunchy topping (cornflakes!) - plus it's served with a slice of blueberry cornbread which offsets its saltiness. The duck pot pie is seriously dangerous, it is so flavorful it'll make you think you're eating in a fancy restaurant, nope - it's a dive-y bar where patrons are surrounded by canine images. My boyfriend is obsessed with the truffled cheesesteak empanadas, but I am not a fan - personally I think there is too much truffle oil which quickly becomes all I taste. But hey - that just means there are food items that'll satisfy every palate. There is typically a wait so come early to nab a booth or seats at the bar where you can order from the full menu.

    I've been to Good Dog twice in the past few weeks. I've heard about it, seen it on top Bars of Philly lists, walked by it a LOT, and have been meaning to go forever.

    I went first for the Pliny the Younger event. Not too shabby. I certainly got an 8 ounce glass of Pliny the Younger for $10, so mission accomplished. The bar staff was friendly. They gave out tickets exactly at 2, and everyone had to get in line to get one. (It would have been nice if they had "place holders" when you came into the bar and exchanged them for tickets, as some places do, but at least they HAD tickets and it wasn't a free-for-all). They also tapped Troegs Nugget Nectar while we were waiting, so it was kind of like a party.

    My husband and I also came for dinner and drinks, recently. The tap list was okay, but I've seen better. It was 1/2 off cans night, though, and they had some winners (Founders, etc in cans). 2 drinks each and 2 entrees brought our total bill to about $50, so not too bad.

    The food... is not memorable. Yet. I am totally willing to go back and try some of their more unusual menu items, but I have yet to be blown away. The first time I had the fish tacos of the day. They were typically prepared, with slaw and aioli, but not that flavorful. They really needed more acid or spice, and I only got a 1/4 lime with the two tacos. The salad that came on the side was pretty stellar though - avocado, crispy tortilla strips, pepitas, a nice dressing, and some other stuff. Second time I got the buffalo shrimp po'boy and my husband got the duck pot pie. He said the pot pie... tasted like pot pie. Nothing amazing. My po'boy was decent. The sauce was spice, but there was also a weird, underlying sweet flavor. Not bad, per se, but not really classic buffalo-style, either. It also needed more blue cheese. And the aioli that came with the fries tasted like plain mayo.

    So their seasoning needs work, but the salmon reuben looks super intriguing, and everything likes the truffled cheesesteak empanadas. I'll add it to the list of places to hit up in the neighborhood for lunch or drinks and give it another whirl, although it's not yet my favorite.

    pretty much as good as it gets.  

    I used to have this drive to try every place in the city -- from north to south, east to west, each and every fine dining, white table cloth, 3 months for a reservation kinda place to the dark dingy been around for 80 years hole-in-the-wall dives. I wanted to know the city's food scene inside and out, top to bottom. So my dining options have always been limitless.

    But in the past year or so, with a big cross-country move in the offing, i have found myself quickly drifting to a far more limited and desire driven list of potential dining establishments. And Good Dog is perhaps smack-dab on the top of that list. I have been a bit of a regular here for the last year and have never had anything but a stellar experience from food to service to atmosphere to ... did I mention the food?
