    Listed in $cammin'

    I've been to El Vez several times now, and I always leave feeling two things - stuffed to the point of sickness, and buyer's remorse.  While the food here is decent and the ambiance is light and fun, I've learned my lesson:  to get the most bang for your buck, do two things - stick with the simple margaritas, and DEFINITELY order the guacamole appetizer.  

    I forget the menu name, but it's the guac with goat cheese in it.  It is utterly heavenly, and the only thing that keeping this Yelp rating afloat.  It's served with thick, homemade tortilla chips that are perfectly warm and salted.  With the margaritas and guac appetizer, you will not be disappointed.  I've had other dishes here - various platters of tacos, enchiladas, etc.  The food's not bad, but definitely NOT worth the premium price that El Vez is charging.  

    The reason I say to stick to a simple house margarita is because I've gone for the "premium" margaritas before, with fresh-squeezed this, and special ingredient that - bottom line, they are too sweet, light on the booze, and end up leaving me with a belly ache.  They also run between $10 and $12 a pop - not worth it in my opinion.  So save yourself the disappointment and the belly ache, and keep it simple!  

    In summary, remember - simple house margarita, goat cheese guacamole, and don't forget to snap some pictures in the photo booth!  The service is always pleasant, but this is not the best of the Steven Starr restaurants.  However it is worth a visit if you order strategically.

    Solid three star experience.  I have been wanting to try El Vez for a long time, but just never managed to end up here until last night (after leaving another restaurant that did not particularly want our business, so our bar was set pretty low by that point).  

    Nothing about El Vez really blew me away.  I am a Starr fan, but i just found this to be a bit lack-luster.  We absolutely enjoyed the guac (lovely concoction of avo, goat cheese, pistachios, and tomoato -- amazing! -- just wish they had a half serving for two-tops).

    We also split the taco sampler platter.  of the 5 tacos, we really enjoyed the beef and the pork.  I also loved the mahi.  But neither of us found any flavor in the chicken, and the sea bass actually had an off flavor -- old, I think.  Rice and beans were fine.  Plantains were far too sweet and mushy (could they have subbed bananas here??)

    Margarita was just ok.  Tres leches cake had a sublime taste but lost it in an odd coconut/cottage cheese/curdled texture.  When I think tres leches, i think creamy creamy creamy.  This did not fit the bill.  

    The hit of the night was the luscious house fried tortilla chips (they rocked!) and the guac.  And the beef and pork tacos were magical.  I would go back for any of that.  I also dig the decor in a big way.  Love the bar area with the neon bike turning slowly in the middle and the very cool high-backed banquettes along the far wall.  

    One app, one entree, one drink and a soft drink, one dessert = $75 before tip.  ouch.
