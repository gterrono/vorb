    Listed in Lunch Places in Philly, Trendy Dining, Bars in Philly, Philly Happy Hour

    Valanni is one of my favorite places for a low key happy hour.  I've been here a few times-- with co-workers, friends, and my girlfriend, and I don't think I've ever been disappointed.  Their cocktails, like "Boys Gone Wild" and "Dirty & Stuffed Martini," make for interesting talking points at the least.  I haven't tried those, but I have had a few others (a margarita, black Manhattan, and caipirinha), and they were all solid, very alcohol filled cocktails.  If you come during happy hour, cocktails under $10 are half price.  Easy.

    As far as the food goes, it takes a heavier Mediterranean approach than anything else.  Offerings such as mussels, oysters, olives, hummus & baba ganouj spreads, and the like are slightly overpriced for the serving, but are executed pretty solidly.  I haven't had dinner there, but I haven't heard many, if any people, complain about it.

    My final note is on the decor.  It's a little over the top-- white vinyl/leather couches, black walls, and fluorescent accent walls make the place a little intimidating to some.  But, the drink specials are so good, and the food is enjoyable enough that you can get over the design style and enjoy what they serve.

    This is one of my favorite spots to relax after work. The bar is in the center, with a few different areas along the side with white leather couches for lounging if you don't want to sit at the bar. I love the relaxing blue lighting and glass orb lights that look like bubbles, it creates a very nice ambiance. The lounge is also in a very gay-friendly area, so you get a very nice mix of people. It is definitely a trendy place, but not intimidating or unapproachable at all.

    The martinis are delicious, try the cucumber- very refreshing. The food here is awesome (especially if you love mediterranean.) The tuna tartare is to die for. The trio of spreads is my staple here. It comes with hummus, muhammara, and tirokafteri, served with grilled pita bread. If you're looking for something more warm and hearty, try the lobster and crab mac and cheese, made with the perfect blend of fontina, parmesan, and mascarpone cheese. Ugh, I could go on and on! I've tried about half of the menu and still haven't come across something I don't like.

    My SO took me here for my birthday and it was absolutely lovely. The sleek, modern décor with all its greys and mirrors is chic, yet inviting. I was surprised that it was almost empty when we visited, but it was a Sunday night. Our waiter was extremely prompt and professional. He gave great suggestions and had wonderful taste.

    We started the meal with bacon-wrapped scallops in a truffled corn sauce. The scallops were huge and positively silky with just the right amount of salty fat to complement the sweetness of the corn. I also had a white sangria, but I preferred the mango mojito. Valanni makes theirs with mango puree, not juice, creating a richer cocktail. Next I tried the white mussels. They are prepared in a corn broth with cilantro and chorizo and a nice big crostini on top. The mussels were quite mild and a nice option for the warm weather. The tuna tartare was my final tapa and it was delectable. The balsamic soy glaze had a molasses-esque richness and lent the perfect amount of tang to the flawless tuna. The tortilla chip accompaniment is smart since it adds texture. My SO had the pork tenderloin which was cooked to perfection and had a nice fruit relish on top. We finished up with rum bananas which I would have enjoyed more had they not come with a rapidly melting scoop of vanilla ice cream. That aside, our time at Valanni was romantic and exciting. The menu is so versatile and ranges from the pedestrian to the exotic. My favorite non-Garces restaurant in Philly!

My friend is new to BCG and told me at first when he ordered it, the female bartender was very snotty when he mispronounced the name. Strike one.

Don't get me wrong, the food selection and flavors are amazing. We had lobster mac and cheese, brussel sprouts, calamari, bib and blue cheese salad, tuna tartare and SEAFOOD PAELLA!! The shrimp were not too tough, the calamari and mussels were not chewy, and the lobster was tender. Seriously, a good 4 stars for the food.

    But... Strike 2... The service. Our waiter was completely rude. He asked us if we wanted any cocktails at least 4 times. I understand that cocktails are an easy way to pad a bill but after the gasoline gimlet I wasn't taking any chances, even though I had unlocked the free mojito deal by checking in. I felt totally rushed through my meal. Each time a plate looked remotely empty, someone tried to take it from us at least once. There was a single serving left of the paella but they still tried to remove it from the table on 5 separate occasions!! Ugh I was trying to free up room by resting. We started to make jokes about how if someone asked to take it one more time we were going to growl.

    By this time, the restaurant had emptied out about half way and there were more than enough tables should another party decide to stop in. History

Established in 2000.

Valanni restaurant opened its doors in September of 2000, introducing "Medi-Latin" cuisine to Philadelphia. Situated right off of the "Avenue of the Arts," Valanni has become a pre-theater ritual and place to meet for a night-cap for many who live in and around the neighborhood. The bar at Valanni keeps up with the creative cuisine by offering an assortment of sangria, mojitos and other specialty drinks. The kitchen is open until 1 AM daily, serving Tapas and a Mediterranean inspired lounge menu.
