    I was first attracted to llama tooth while taking a mural tour of Philly. It had just opened but the outdoor area was bursting with color, vegetation, and pretty touches.

    After venturing to eat here, I think it's a great outdoors lunch or dinner venue. The mural walls surrounding you depict a Haitian Childhood of the artist (learned that one from the mural tour) exploding with flowers, color, and cheer. There is a solid beer list here, and other alcoholic beverages.The menu is medium sized, with some variety but mainly I would classify as American Fusion.

    Service, as noted in other reviews, was a little slow perhaps because of our large order, I dont know? This could show some improvement.

    As a large group, we ordered a ton of things and shared all around. The crab/lobster mac and cheeses were great, oily cheese gooey and delicious. Certainly an indulgence by most standards, but well worth it. The asian mac and cheese with the ahi and the kim chee was a bit strange and I would not order again. I had the chicken gyro, which was very tasty! The eggplant fries were huge and I personally loved them dipped in aioli, although many of my friends did not like the soft texture of the eggplant in fry form. It seems they were using american eggplants to make the fries (versus the more firm and seedless asian varieties) and perhaps this is accountable for softer texture? The ahi salad was ok, but the ahi slices were too large and chunky for seared styled ahi on salad. I would recommend they slice it more thinly in the future and less salt on the exterior-it was also quite salty.

    I would return to check it out again.

    Hit or miss is the best way to describe the Llama Tooth.  

    Doesn't quite merit 2 stars because the outdoor patio with murals, flowers, lights strung overhead, and endless soundtrack of reggae cover songs and Sublime just make this place too cute to get mad at, allowing me to overlook its flaws.

    But it also doesn't deserve 4 stars because of its inconsistent service and unavailability of menu items.

    The first time I was there was for brunch with 3 other people and it took about 45 minutes to an hour for our food to come out which is ridiculous.  Even though we had nowhere to go and the outdoor patio is a pleasant atmosphere, we all started impatiently drumming our fingers after a bit.  There were maybe 3 tables occupied outside and 1 inside so I can't see why it would take so long.

    The second time, however, was a Sunday night for dinner and we were literally the only customers in the entire place, both inside and out.  So our food came right out within 15 to 20 minutes of waiting.

    With that said, I've never been disappointed with the food but I've also never fallen head over heels in love with anything either.  The brunch the first time (burger benedict) was very good with a nice hollandaise sauce and the yogurt parfait was very satisfying and filling, served in a pint glass with a giant spoon.  Great bloody marys and mimosas, too.  

    Dinner the second time was decent (chicken gyro with really good tzatziki sauce and a Tex Mex salad on grilled romaine), but the two items we originally wanted they were out of, which seems to be a common complaint on here.  When you only have 2 sandwich options on the menu, being out of 1 of them during dinner hours kinda sucks.

    I do have to say that the "Painkiller" mixed drink was absolutely delicious with whipped cream and cinnamon on top and the beer list is very impressive.  We even tried a Ghost Pepper flavored beer called Ghostface Killah, which was pretty cool.

    Overall, I'll go back on a nice sunny afternoon for a couple of drinks and some decent food on the outdoor patio and maybe try some other menu options.

    Listed in Philly Bars, Beer Haven, Burger Time

    For better and worse, Llama Tooth is a one hell of quirky place that really manages to stand out in the Eraserhood. For every good that I've experienced there I've experienced an equal amount of the bad and the weird. So much that its one of those reviews that I can only break into 3 categories.

    THE GOOD:

    The burgers! Bacon, blue cheese, blackening spice, and angus beef rounded out the dream team roster of Bs in my B4 Burger; and caramelized onions were added on for good measure. there's also a Surf & Turf Burger with a crab cake on top, a French burger with pate and mushroom duxelles, a K Rock Burger with pulled pork and fried cilantro; and two veggie burgers (Portobello w/ Swiss, and a Spring Garden burger that sounds all kinds of awesome!).

    Another food option that's all kinds of amazing is their list of Mac & Cheeses since there's crab mac, pulled pork mac, mushroom truffle mac, and a wasabi spiced Bonsai! at 30-40 deep and rotating per the seasons, the beer list is also something that's fairly extraordinary.

    THE BAD:

    The bathrooms! Maybe not so much the bathroom inside the restaurant. But on days where its a little more packed you soon discover the disarray of the bathroom behind the restaurant. When you walk into the small cramped space a waft of stale cigarette smoke may greet you. And I've come across the plentiful assortment of butts in the paper towel filled trash bin. Really? Really!

    While I've seen some good out of the service, I've also seen a fair share of bad. I was a part of Michelle's group that experienced the fairly long wait times despite just ordering drinks on a fairly slow afternoon for them. I've also had another tables drink order brought to my table, and have seen staff sitting down and chatting up tables on a few occasions.

    There is definitely an odd disconnect going on between the outside bar and inside of the restaurant. While the outside is vibrant, colorful, quirky and inviting; the inside feels a little more desolate and fails to offer up much of an identity. So much so that I can't overly see myself going there in the winter, and as Games of Thrones has taught me (media.treehugger.com/ass…) it comes sooner than you think.

    THE WEIRD:

    The fact that the first column of their menu (on paper and online) is for salads & soups, but they don't serve any soup. Who does that!

    How often I've seen their outdoor cantina bar go unutilized! it could be a major feature on packed nights (which comes every night there's a major show at Union Transfer seeing how they're across the street), yet there have been nights that I'd have to give into a 20-30 minute wait when it was completely vacated.

    I've appreciated when they've tried to show things like Phillies games on a big screen TV. But when you're showing it on one TV that can bring viewing pleasure to a good sized living room, inside an outdoor courtyard that's the size of an entire rowhome; it really doesn't give much people the option of watching it. An area of that size however would be perfect for an outdoor projector and movie nights (especially when being across the street from a music venue doesn't make sound an issue). And if you go with that option I expect at least one drink as a thank you :)

    Bloody Marys and mimosas are available at all times of the day. While I know many people who have dubbed such a thing a blacklisted offense; as a late riser who rarely gets up for the brunching hour yet loves its enticing cocktails, I LOVE IT! And the Bloody Mary is  hand mixed blend that deliver just the right amount of fire!

    IN CONCLUSION:

    True Llama Tooth has a lot of quirks that do need some work. But much as you'd expect from a place with such a bizarre and random name, it also has its fair share of quirks that are pretty awesome. And while its also at a spot that I have already pandered to see succeed on 2 other separate occasions (yelp.com/biz/root-philad…, yelp.com/biz/thoreau-res…), I do feel that the Tooth has the right amount of quirkiness and intrigue to do exactly that.
