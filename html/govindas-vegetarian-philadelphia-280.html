    Govinda's is both a dine-in restaurant and take-out sandwich joint.  This review is for the take-out side.  I haven't dined in yet but I might check it out someday.  

    To start off, the service wasn't fantastic as the counter lady seemed wholly uninterested in serving us. The place is also dingy looking, old fixtures, crusty seating, and overall run-down looking.  That being said, I split three sandwiches yesterday: the Kofta Meatball, the Chicken Cheesesteak, and the Tofu Burrito.  By far the chicken cheesesteak was the best.  It was nicely flavored and textured and served with mozzarella on a whole wheat roll.  It would have been nice to have sauces to choose from.  the kofta meatball was pretty good and had some traditional flavors.  I also thought the tofu burrito was good.  It mainly consisted of just rice and tofu though,  so I wish it would of been a bit more dynamic.  

    I'd try this place again, especially for the chicken cheesesteak sandwich.

    If you only get the "chicken" cheesesteak, I can easily see how you would've rated this place 5 stars. I had it on wheat with mayo and mozzarella, so it definitely wasn't vegan but it was delicious. We got it to go and split this along with the tofu wrap, and got to kofta sub for a snack later.

    The tofu wrap was mostly rice, with some tofu thrown in there too. The description said the tofu was soaked in ginger, so I was expecting it to be super flavorful but I was sadly disappointed. Definitely not getting this again.

    I tried a bit of the kofta "meatball" sub while it was still hot. It was soggy and had some flavor, but again I wasn't overwhelmed with what my mouth was experiencing. After a couple hours in the fridge, and several tequila drinks later, the kofta sub was a lot better. Chilling it in the fridge kinda hardening everything up--the sogginess wasn't so bad, and the tequila pairing helped to add some more flavor. This, and the tofu wrap, are only worth three stars.

    Overall, I'd give 3 and half stars. The staff I spoke with said her favorite thing on the menu is the chicken quesedilla. Maybe I'll try that neck time I go back, but otherwise it seems like the cheesesteak is the only thing worth ordering.

    There was a time when this place was the bomb.  Their sandwiches were always on point - even if the service, cleanliness, and general ambiance were a bit in the lacking.  The food was so great, that it didn't matter if the rest of the experience sucked.  That's changed.

    The chicken cheesesteak was the no-brainer choice whenever I was lucky enough to be eating here.  Over time the sandwich quality turned for the worse.  There were a few guys who worked the grill that could cook a pretty mean sandwich, but they seem to have moved on to better things.  Now it ranges from half cooked to over-done mess.  

    If you've been going here over the years you've probably noticed the inverse relationship between size of your sandwich and its price.  My last visit I paid $9 something (including tax) for a wrap (they run out of bread everyday) the size of my fist.   When they do have bread, you'll notice they cut off the ends of the sandwich to use as the accompanying piece of bread for cup of soups.

    The cake slices used to vary from average to tiny.  They fixed that issue - they're all tiny now.  I understand the cakes (which are baked and delivered by a third party) are expensive, but you can't help but feel ripped off.   The cakes in the case look like a mess, which is in line with the general experience.  

    The service has always sucked and I can't really blame the people working there.  They're not making much and working in crappy conditions.  I'd be sour too.

    Save your cash. There's a dozen other vegan options within a five minute walk.
