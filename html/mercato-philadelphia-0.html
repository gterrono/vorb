    Have you ever been on a first date that goes so well until the kiss - like, everything is just great until your lips touch and then it's kind of "Oh, ew, that wasn't what I was expecting..." And the night ends and you're left asking yourself if it's worth a second date because you swear you really did have a connection...but ugh, that kiss...

    That's kind of how I felt about my experience at Mercato, if the date was the food and the kiss was the service.

    My man and I made reservations at Mercato for our anniversary and everyone who heard we going there said we would love it. Few things make us happier than a fab BYO, so we were feeling good. We showed up for our 8:00 Saturday night reservation and found ourselves waiting outside with five other parties who seemed to also have 8:00 reservations. The hostess was doing double, if not triple duty, as a waitress and busboy also and it was almost impossible to find her to even "check in" - she was sweet and apologetic but told us to "Hang on" and "Wait just one more minute!" about eleven times before we were seated about 20 minutes later. Call me crazy (sang to the tune of "Call Me Maybe") but doesn't that defeat the purpose of having a reservation?

    Inside, the restaurant is tiny - my BF and I felt like we had just joined the birthday party seated next to us. Despite our lovely additional dining companions, the first hour of the meal was rough.

    - My boyfriend's bottle of wine was screw top, so he was able to start drinking when we sat down, but my bottle needed a corkscrew and it was about 15 minutes before a) someone came to help me out and b) the server could find the corkscrew;
    - We waited another 15 minutes for menus because "So sorry, when we're busy, we run out of them!" (What? You run out of menus?)
    - Our meat and cheese board came out with no bread, and then when we asked for some, two different servers came and brought us 8 mini loaves (which was awesome, but just a lot. All at once.).
    - I didn't have a knife.
    - We didn't order our food until after 9:00 - over an hour since our reservation.

    However. The food. Worth it. Ugh. My boyfriend had the meatballs and the veal - I'm a veggie so I can't say how they tasted but it all looked beautiful and he loved them both. I had the full grilled artichoke which was amazing - delicious flavor and an aioli on the side. And in a world where it seems like everything is served with aioli, this was legit. I also had the special beet tagliatelle pasta, which holy balls, I could eat all the time. Beautiful beet red pasta with a little bite to the noodle, roasted mushrooms, ricotta, and served kind of room temperature, which I loved. It's rare to have a pasta dish that doesn't make you want to be rolled home, but this one of them.

    Overall my date with Mercie was really good, but I'm just not sure if I can overlook that bad kiss...ahem, service...especially when I'm dropping over a hundo on a meal and I'm expecting that old guy from the match.com commercials to set me up with my soulmate. They say a first date should be on a Tuesday or a Wednesday, not a weekend, so maybe I need to try that here. And I will - if they make me that beet pasta again.

    I have to say I really like Mercato but was torn between 3 and 4 stars.  The quality of the food and the value for price were definitely 4 star, but some there were some negatives worth mentioning.

    First, the negatives.  I can see where the room could get pretty noisy, and a number of the reviews below refer to an uncomfortable noise level.  Since it was an early summer weeknight evening, the windows were open, and the restaurant wasn't too crowded, noise wasn't and issue.  The tables inside are really close together, which made the room feel a bit cramped, as well.  There are also quite a number of tables outside, which I think would be an ideal seating location on a summer evening, to avoid the possibility of noise in the dining room.

    But the worst negative of the meal was the indifferent attitude of our server, who never broke a smile or made us feel welcome. She was very business like but there was an edge about her manner that made us feel uncomfortable almost as if we had offended her in some way. I noticed that she acted the same way with other patrons, so I don't think it was anything we did.  Perhaps she just doesn't really like her job, or people in general, or maybe feels that serving others is beneath her.  Whatever the issue, it really kept the whole dining experience from the level of satisfaction it should have.

    On to the positives. I totally loved that the crusty italian bread which was served with a compound butter flavored with herbs and chopped olives. That butter was addictively creamy, delicious and savory. My salad, a mixture of watercress, pistachio nuts and house cured bacon, was a real taste treat as well. Plus, you didn't have to look for the bacon, which came in several thick strips and made the salad actually quite filling as a first course, and could be enjoyed as a shared item if you are light eater.

    For the mains, I would recommend the pasta dishes which were creative, reasonably priced, and came in very filling portions. Unlike many of the "small plates" restaurants that seem so trendy today, Mercato served a real main dish portion and I appreciated that!

    Overall, I would say that I enjoyed the food so much that I look forward to going back to Mercato, despite issues with the cramped room and service.
