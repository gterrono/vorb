    Listed in Underdogs

    The Belgian Cafe is an excellent choice for a fun, low-key dinner out with friends. The burgers, mussels, and beer are all high-quality, tasty, and fairly reasonably priced. It's a good alternative to Monk's (less chaotic), City Tap House (less undergrad-y and much better mussels), and Village Whiksey (less exorbitant prices and better craft beer selection, although the burgers can't really compete).  That said, I've been unimpressed by a lot of their dishes other than the burgers.  They do have a lot of vegan options, though, which makes some of my friends happy to visit!

    The service is also remarkably (almost famously) bad.  The servers are rarely outright hostile (so that's a plus?), but the service is very slow and many (but not all!) of the servers tend toward the unfriendly side. This brings them down to the 3-star range.

    All in all, the Belgian Cafe is definintely worth a visit, especially if you've already found yourself in the Art Museum area. Don't expect too much in terms of service and you'll be all set for a great time.

    Came here on a Saturday night around 10:30 pm

    Food (9/10) = Sampled the Django mussels (garlic, saison, pear, bacon & bleu cheese), Van Eyke mussles (garlic sausage, herbs de provence & piraat belgian ale), and the Monk's mussels (garlic, parsley & gueuze lambic ale).  These are the best mussels I've had in a long time....a loooong assss time (I've been living in Shreveport, LA for the past 3 years.).  The mussels are incredibly soft and delicate .  The mussels came with their pomme frites (double fried, fresh cut fries served with Monk's Cafe's famous bourbon mayonnaise and our smoked ketchup for dipping).  I'm salivating again as I write this, and I just left this restaurant an hour ago.  Also sampled the Pork Schnitzel (Breaded, tender pork cutlets pan fried in a mustard cream sauce, with apple braised red cabbage and mashed potatoes), which wasn't anything special.  For beers, I sampled Chimay "blue" ale, and allagash white.  Great beers, and definitely worth a try.  Their beer selection is fantastic.

    Ambiance (9/10) = the weather was beautiful, so we sat at the tables outside.  The place is very laid back.  Just a great place to chill out and enjoy life.  

    Service (8.5/10) = Our waitress was very sweet, and attentive.  A great compliment to the chill environment of the restaurant.

    Overall Opinion = Would definitely make this place a frequent stop if I lived in Philadelphia.  If you're interested in a low-key chill place to spend an evening, stop by the Belgium Cafe.

    I still love coming to Belgian Cafe, but I do wish their dinner menu had some better vegan entrees (I've tried them all - all lackluster). The only one I've had that was awesome-worthy was a special for the night.

    But here's why I love Belgian Cafe: Treat it like a cafe.
