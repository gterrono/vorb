    I was introduced to Dandelion when I was living in DC and it has remained one of my favorite places to grab a fun cocktail and good food since moving to Philly over 2 years ago.

    The steak tartare is on par with nearby steak restaurants and is big enough to share. The cheese plate is always ordered when we're grabbing food, as are the fish and chips - yummy! One visit to Dandelion was purely for after dinner drinks and dessert, and neither disappointed. The lemon tart was awesome, perfectly balanced and went well with my favorite martini (Bombay, dirty, no olives).

    The Dandelion is home to my favorite cocktail in Philly, the Shikari - it's a blend of tequila and cayenne pepper, with enough sweet/sour notes to balance the heat and alcohol content, I love it! If the cocktails are not of your preference, the bartenders will do their best to create one specific to your palate and request. My boyfriend ordered the Pimm's DEluxe (which is awesome without substitutions) with bourbon in lieu of gin and while he would have preferred it was served in a different glass (not martini), it was very good.

    The only negative is it's sometimes crowded on Thursday evenings and hard to move around the downstairs bar, but if the upstairs "hunting lodge" is open, move on up and settle in. We took our friend from the UK there last night and he endorsed Dandelion as well, stop by and you won't be disappointed.

    I first came to Dandelion with a group of girlfriends for the afternoon tea on Sunday. What a great… I first came to Dandelion with a group of girlfriends for the afternoon tea on Sunday. What a great experience!

    First, the ambiance was lovely. Warm sunny day and open air circulating through the bar, we were seated right away and taken care of by some attentive servers even though we had such a big group.

    While we waited for two of our numbers, I had a Bramble (which was DELICIOUS--I'll get to this later, but the cocktails at Dandelion are very very very good, and worth every penny. The bartenders here know their stuff.) and some dessert--the Eton mess, which was delicious, if heavy for me (though that's the nature of the dish...)

    When my friends got there twenty minutes after I finished both dishes (and no one told us off for basically loitering!!!) we pored over the menu. Having been nostalgic for the tea culture of my home away from home (London) it was really wonderful to see the options given on the menu. Lots of the same foods I was used to seeing on English menus, but with spins on them...along with dishes we could nibble on rather than meals.

    We got the British cheese board and the tea cookie plates, along with teapigs of Earl Grey. The tea was a standard Earl Grey--nothing to complain about but also nothing to rave about. However, the cheese board and the tea cookie plates were phenomenal. The scones were perfect...good texture, great tastes. The cheeses were very interesting and the server took time to explain to us the complexities of the taste pairings.

    It was a really nice afternoon of yummy food and tea in a nice place, and for $37 dollars split 4 ways, not badly priced at all.

    More recently, I came in to kill time before karaoke at Elephant and Castle. I had planned on getting a cider or something (I love me some Magners) but the cocktail menu was intriguing. While I pored over that, my friend asked for a whiskey sour. The bartender was an amazingly friendly guy who asked a lot of questions to get the drink perfect...I was dubious about the need till I tasted the drink.

    HOLY CRAPOLY.

    Smoothest, tastiest, cleanest-feeling whiskey sour on earth. AMAZING.

    Heartened, I asked the bartender to recommend me something. I had a lot of dumb health specifications (if I don't want too much sugar why am I even drinking tbh) but he was patient and really helpful. He ended up recommending the Scotch Honeysuckle. It wasn't as good as the whiskey sour (a bit too sweet, maybe? Or actually, I probably don't much like scotch...oops...) but it smelled DIVINE bc they use rosewater to perfume the drink.

    Again, the cocktails were pretty normally priced for CC, like 8 bucks I think? Maybe even 6 or 7...all in all, VERY worth it.

    Dandelion is one of those places you need to come back to over and over again to experience differently. I visited a long while back for drinks with a friend, and I really loved its pub atmosphere, but having just come for restaurant week with a friend, it really felt like an entirely different experience. I was struck by just how attentive and careful the service was from the door to the table. A mark, to me, of good service is when the waiter preempts the dishes, preempts when you are ready for the next course and has the next step cued.

    Thankfully, despite how many restaurants might deny this, the restaurant week menu wasn't a list of throwaway dishes that The Dandelion wouldn't otherwise serve on its regular menu. I love that they encouraged us to eat from both menus as a way to enjoy the restaurant's usual hits and the restaurant week specials.  I had the following courses:

    First Course

    Winter Root Vegetable Soup
    buttermilk croutons, aged cheddar

    - So glad to have had this as a starter! Perfectly creamy for the winter and makes good use of the table bread with sea salt and butter. The croutons are a real treat for texture. Savory but not overly salty.

    Second Course

    Roasted Pork Tenderloin
    sherry braised cipollini, root vegetable mash, pork jus

    -Thank goodness for the sherry and jus combination! The pork was cooked perfectly, left pink and tender as it should be. Very large portion - certainly more than enough to share if your friend wants a bite. Could have used a little more sauce actually, but it comes together really nicely with the root mash.

    Third Course

    Cranberry Bread Pudding
    orange spice ice cream

    - I'm not normally a fan of bread pudding, but this was fantastic. I really like to see creative, house-blend ice creams, and I think the classic citrus-cranberry mix works really well here. Soaks right into the bread pudding, and really refreshing after heavier courses.

    All in all, really outstanding, and hoping to return very soon.

I was talked into a burger that I was told was one of Philly's Best, the House Blend Aged Beef Burger, with vermont sharp cheddar, brooklyn brine pickles, apple smoked bacon, and churchill sauce.

    The food is stand-out good and it's certainly a sleeper hit in a city full of great restaurants. Don't miss. This place gets my highest ranking, five stars.

Specialties

London's culinary revolution comes to Philadelphia with this eclectic English pub. Cozy rooms and intimate bars create a storied, Old World aura, while updated seasonal takes on British food make up the menu. In keeping with tradition, cask and tap craft beers are always rotating, a proper afternoon tea service has a sweet and savory menu, and classic beef family-style roasts are served on Sundays.

History

Established in 2010.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
