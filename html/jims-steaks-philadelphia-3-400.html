    Listed in Hungry Visitor in Philadelphia

    Located on the busy and funky South Street, it'll be tough to catch Jim's Steaks without a line.  The line moves at a good pace and there's plenty of entertainment to keep you preoccupied.  If you're outside, you get the open window pane where you get to watch the workers systematically saute a mountain of onions and thinly sliced red meat.  Just as you decide on ordering the classic "Philly Cheesesteak", you watch him dunk the spreading knife in a huge vat of Cheese Wiz and spread it like butter on the sandwich.  Once inside, as your mouth starts to water at the smell of sizzling beef, you can see the cheesy 80s and 90s celebrity headshots and shout-outs for Jim's.

    Because when in Philly, you order the Philly Cheesesteak, my friend and I split the cheesesteak with mushrooms and red peppers on soft chewy bread.  It was hot and hits the spot after a cold Philly evening.  So simple and quite good.  Fireworks in my mouth?  Not really.  I wish they would have sauteed the mushrooms and peppers with the meat, versus just plopping it on top from a can.  

    Note: there are dining tables and counters upstairs but your entire party must already have their order - no seat saving (and there's a large sign that reminds you, if the staff member doesn't first).

    ...was the first place my boy and I went into when we got into DT-Philly.  We came on a mission; to try our first official cheesesteak in Philly - where they were born and bred.  We couldn't remember the names of Pat's and Gino's til later; but this one seemed to be a good spot to go, got a lot of reviews(and we would later find out that our good friend from the hood likes this place the best and also another few people said the same thing, so pat on the back for us!)  

    ...once I walked in, the place looked like Philly's Best out here on the Best Coast...from the look and feel of the restaurant to the Menu style and menu list!  So what's on the menu?  Cheesesteaks baby!  Only thing I knew about getting a cheese steak in Philly (you can't call it a Philly Cheesesteak in Philly, that's like asking for Chinese food in China, there you'd just call it, "food" =p )...is that you ask for it "wiz wit," meaning that you want whiz cheese on it.  I never thought that whiz cheese would be so good on a cheesesteak samich but man oh man!  Was it good.  The West Coast twin, "Philly's Best" does have the option, but I never knew that that's what you needed to get it with.  No more american or provolone for me!  Just wiz wit always!  ha ha ha.  Of course you gotta get the onions with it too, cuz then that would just be a naked samich wiz wit on it.

    Cost - a little less than 20 for 2 cheesesteaks and sodas, just about what you'd expect.  So for under $10 you can get a samich and drink and be good and good to go.

    Service - you better know what you want and be clear with what you say; cuz they don't really mess around.  It's not exactly like the soup nazi, but if you're not clear, they'll let you know.    They're not total a-holes, but can be if you're slowing them down, he he he.  Otherwise, they're actually pretty cool.

    Crowd - BOTH times we went here(we came later that night after some good amount of imbibing), we had JUST beat the crowd...literally by a couple minutes.  So the wait can get terribly long; so come off-peak.  Seating-wise, there's seating upstairs, so don't be fooled by the small counter where you order.   Street parking is a beeeeyotch and a half, so hopefully you're walking here.

    All in all, we are so glad we hit this place up first(gonna have to compare with Phi-Best here again just to see the meat comparison), and that we got the double nod of approval from the locals!

    FYI - NO CREDIT CARDS HERE - but there's an ATM inside.

    Listed in I Got a Name, Proud to Be an American, The Greatest Thing Since Sliced Bread, Fifteen Minutes of Fame, Motown Philly

    3.5 stars.

    Two words, pescetarians: tuna sub.

    Three letters, vegetarians: S.O.L.

    So what's a non-meat eater doing writing a review of Jim's?  Well, this is for all you other non-meat eaters out there who've been asked to tag along with your carnivorous friends and are scurred you'll starve.  I've been to Jim's several times on several trips to Philadelphia -- friends love Jim's, husband loves Jim's -- doing recon for you folks.  And I actually kinda love it, too.  Why?

    1) It's on bustling, vibrant South Street.  It doesn't get much more convenient than that.

    2) Standing in a line circling the block with hundreds of others waiting for their taste of tuna (what? aren't we all in line for the tuna?) builds a fun little sense of community.  I've waited in line in the dour sleet of February, the welcome balm of July, and waltzed right into Jim's with no line.  It's all an experience.

    3) People build this place up like the workers rival the Soup Nazi's exacting expectations and precision preparation.  I find the staff who work here full of brotherly love and much-needed efficiency.

    4) I enjoy the butterflies in my stomach as the anticipation builds: "Will we get a seat upstairs or not?  Will there be space?  I'm willing to throw some elbows or take out a kid or two."  Inevitably, space always seems to open just as you stumble up the stairs.

    5) My dining companions always rave about the cheesesteaks, and I heart the bread.

    6) Tuna fish for non-meat eaters.  'Nuff said.

    [15 Minutes of Fame: "Rachael Ray's $40 a Day" and "Man v. Food" (Philadelphia Cheesesteak)]

    So next time I come back to the city of brotherly love, I'm going to Jim's Steaks.   Not to mention, the staff was friendlier, and they don't support that idiot Donald Trump.

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
