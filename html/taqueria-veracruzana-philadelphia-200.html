    You have to love a place that can put 3 awesome tacos on a plate for 6 bucks.  Personally, there is a special spot in my heart for these kinds of establishments.

    The ambiance here pretty much defines casual.  You walk in, grab a menu yourself, order from the window and sit down while your food is prepared.  The chips and salsa come out and the salsa verde has both flavor and a kick.  It's a little addictive.

    I grab the al pastor, chorizo and beef.  You simply can't find stuff this good in most other places.  Squeeze of fresh lime and I am in Mexican street food heaven.

    You leave, with part of your meal saved for breakfast the next day (everyone has tacos for breakfast right?) and your wallet is only slightly lighter.  Sounds like a win win to me.

    I've been to this place about half a dozen in times, but this review is based of my last meal there last week.

    I sat myself and had my very cute server drop of some chips with two type of salsa - I ordered Steak Milanese platter.  I ate every chip that they gave me sand saved the sauce for my entree.  My critique of the platter is that it is definitely an array of food.  Good food, for that matter - eggs, ham, rice, beans, french fries and steak.    However, I felt like they could've put a little more effort into the platter.    The fries were just so soggy from obviously being cooked WAY too far in advance.  The steak didn't have a nice crust on it and the exterior was soggy.  The flavors of the rice and beans were quite nice, but it was apparent that both were just way overcooked.  Also, in terms of appearance, the plate was a little sloppy.  I also think they could have incorporated some type of salad element on the platter for some roughage.  

    When I was younger, my father would take me to hole in the wall Mexican restaurants in Port Chester, New York and they would serve up some world class platters.  They paid attention to the finer details.  The people at Veracruzana are not serving world class platters.   I'm not even sure if it's up to Washington Avenue's standards!

    The platter came out rather quickly, but I would have gladly waited a few more minutes for the food to come out a little hotter and crispier.  Worth checking out, but there is most definitely better Mexican food in the neighborhood.
