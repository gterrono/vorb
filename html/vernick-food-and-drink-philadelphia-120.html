    I went to Vernick with my in-laws for my MIL's bday. We had a reservation for 8:30 but we arrived about ten minutes early and got a drink at the bar. I highly recommend their cocktails - I had the Old Cuban, which is Vernick's take on a mojito. It was one of the best cocktails I have had in a looong time. The bartender was great - quick on service and cheerily answered my FIL's questions about the different types of bitters they use.

    To start we had a salad- the special (a strawberry goat cheese) which was eh, seemed like a salad you could get anywhere. However, the crispy and creamy mozzarella plate was amazing. I considered snatching it away and hiding in a corner to eat it by myself - Gollum style - but I luckily wasnt quite drunk enough yet to do that. I'm a gluten free vegetarian so I didnt have the peas and bacon on toast, but I was assured it was excellent in a want-to-steal-it-away manner. We also got the grilled icerberg salad, which was great as long as you got a bite of everything on your fork (fig, cheese, etc), otherwise it was just nicely charred lettuce.

    The chef offered to make me a special plate, which I appreciated. However - and this is the reason I am withholding a star - it wasnt clear from what the waitress described that he was basically going to make me a roasted veggie plate. I was a little disappointed - no rice or anything else - just a plate of veggies. Delicious veggies, but I expected more...

    The fam loved the whole roasted chicken and fish of the day. Had an excellent dark chocolate cake for dessert (well, I just had the frosting) and without any prompting, the waitress thoughtfully included a candle for the bday. Overall, a very satisfying meal.

    I'm sorry, but I didn't love it. However, let me explain. My friend and I had been trying to go here for months, but we kept canceling on each other. Finally after 3-4 months we went. So, after this build-up I guess I was expecting to be wowed just a bit more?

    The space is lovely. If you are facing the right way or seated in the right area. First the tried to sit my friend in the cozy cave-like area in the back. She sits in an office all day...so they moved her upstairs. I didn't like this table, and they said they would move us, but my friend had a big bag and a bike helmet so it worked. Definitely push for upstairs if it's nice enough for the windows to be open.

    The cocktail list looks lovely and they made my friends Negroni spectacularly. They also have mocktails for the teetotalers.

    I started with the crab toast. Which I thoroughly enjoyed and was one of the more filling items I ate. It also had hints of peeky toe crab, which were ever so tasty. S had the avocado toast. I tried a bit. I'm not an avocado lover, but it was well done. I definitely recommend trying one or two toasts as starters.

    We also tried the string beans, which to me are a grown up take on the green beans from grace tavern. I like the ones at grace tavern better. These were a bit too peppered. You lost the crisp and clean flavor of the beans.

    Moving on we had the mozzarella dish. Amazing. Small, but mighty. I would definitely not share this next time. Get one for your damn self. Don't share.

    S then had the trout small plate as her entree, she said it was nice, but had a brunch-y feel to it. I had the ginger rice with poached egg. It was good and rich, but I wouldn't order it again.

    I was shocked by the desert prices. 16 for blueberry pie? That's a bit obnoxious. I don't care how good it is. I feel like deserts should be between 9-12. If Baked Alaska is 16 sue me. it's worth it.

    S isn't much for desert, and I wasn't keen on anything in particular, but I adore pistachio anything. They had pictachio gelato, which turned out to be green tea. Which also turns out their gelato is not home made. I thought it was. Our server graciously brought us another desert we tried the rhubarb pavlova. The pavlova was lovely, but the mix of rhubarb and basil gelato made the savoury medicinal taste in your mouth.

    I wanted to love Vernick. The service is impeccable. The space and decor is well done, depending on your view. It's trying a bit too hard, the piece of art work in the stairwell does not go well with the rest of the decor. The upstairs bathroom is all over the place? Titanic suitcase, black and white photo, reclaimed wood mirror, nautical light fixture and kitchen-y backsplash tiling?

    Would I go back again, yes, if someone really wanted to go. I would perhaps go back and sit at the bar enjoy a drink and have a toast and a small plate.

    I made a reservation here for a birthday dinner a couple of weeks ago based solely on reviews I'd seen online and on their menu.

    See, I've become a vegetarian in the past couple of years and don't eat fish, so there are not many options available to me at some of the restaurants I used to love. It seems that most places will assume you like salmon or put some grilled veggies and dry rice together and call it a day.  Both are an insult. But after seeing the Vernick menu, I had high hopes going into this dinner.

    My husband and I sat at the counter overlooking the kitchen. I started with the fromage blanc & pickled ramp toast. Cheese and pickled produce are two of my favorite things in this world (also why I can never be a vegan) and it did not disappoint. The waitress recommended that I then order two small plates  (the asparagus and the heart of romaine) and I did, but one would have been enough. And though it sounds weird, I keep fantasizing about the amazing grilled heart of romaine and fig dish. That I'm fantasizing about lettuce is odd, but it was that damn good.

    Chef Vernick asked us how everything was as he was passing through from one station to another, and I let him know that I greatly appreciated that he had more than just the aforementioned salmon and dry veggies. He said (I'm paraphrasing) that he grew up in a vegetarian household (that explains it!) and that when you buy veggies as high quality and fresh as he does, there's not a whole lot he has to do to add to it.  In any case, I appreciate that there were multiple options available to me and they were all delicious. And my carnivore husband really enjoyed his meal (the pork-blade steak).

    Every single staff member we encountered was very friendly. The cocktails were well worth the money. I'm really looking forward to returning and having more of my beloved grilled lettuce!

    After taking two steps out the door, I turned to J and asked if we could come back.

    Secretly, before dinner, I thought we might be set up for a disappointing dinner, because sometimes that happens when you read too much on Yelp before-hand. Instead, Vernick was delicious, comfortable, and a place that I definitely want to revisit. The server (and everybody else who helped out) was super friendly and nice; we felt really taken care of. It feels like I'm sitting at somebody's modern looking dining room. Tables are well-spaced so you have privacy, close-enough that you're sharing some camraderie with other diners.

    We got a complimentary shot of gazpacho to start off. It wasn't really my thing, sort of bitter. I was wary...

    but then came our Peas & Bacon Toast. Wow. THIS is what you call bacon! And peas are sweet, too! Surprising and so good. I can imagine so many ways that this wouldn't turn out well, but Vernick managed to out-do my imagination.

    I enjoyed but wasn't blown away by the Sea Bass. Really tender, and the sauce was tasty but was more watery than I expected. Still, this is akin to me complaining about a 23K gold ring.

    Pork blade steak. Every bite was like a new revelation. There were a dozen+ slices, and each one brought us the same reaction. Since when was pork so juicy and tender? I didn't get the memo til Vernick.

    Ending with Meyer lemon creme tart. Pretty and delicious. You know when you read a description and it sounds interesting, but you're not sure if it's really going to come through? This is not one of those things. This is a delicious sum of separately delicious components.

    When you go to a really popular place that everybody raves about, you arrive with the idea that you might get disappointed in the end. Vernick sure showed me up on this one.

    5 stars is "as good as it gets" then Vernick deserves all 5.  

    Since I don't yelp as often as I should, I've eaten at all of philly's and NYC's top spots.  Haute cousine and dive bars- love 'em all when done right.  

    My wife brought to Vernick for father's day and I didn't have much of an expectation going into the meal.  A chef that graduated the CIA and opens a restaurant off rittenhouse square lead me to believe this would be an average meal with an above average bill.  After all, being a CIA grad isn't much of a credential to running a restaurant.

    Boy was I surprised!   Turns out Greg Vernick is a special tallent with credentials with Jean George and Thomas Keller.  

    The most differentiating aspect of the Vernick experience is the team he has assembled.  Everyone- from the greeters, to the bartender, to the busboys, to the waitstaff, to the kitchen staff- EVERYONE executes their job with a pride and precision that is beautiful to experience.   There's a  calm confidence  that pervades the front and back of the house.  

    But do you go for just good for or a good drink?  The best spot in Phlly (or nyc) for a drink is Franklin & Mortgage.  Vernick's drinks were at least as good.  My favorite meal in Philly is Modo Mio.....was Modo Mio.  Vernick serves refined dishes without pretense.  

    So you get the best of all worlds, and served by one of the most competent staff I've every met.  

    Tip:  If you want to be near the action book a table at the bar where you can interact with the kitchen staff.

    Special note:  My vegetarian wife has gluten & dairy restrictions and they gladly accommodated.

    The best thing about this place is the ambience. Sitting upstairs on a warm summer night with the windows open really is quite gorgeous.

    The food is decent, but for some reason, perhaps it's the dishes we ordered, they all seemed to be near misses.

    Maryland crab on toast and chicken liver with red onion chutney on toast - Both of these dishes were quite delicious. There's a generous amount of crab and the red onion chutney was delicious on the chicken liver. The little hint of dill on it was nice too. The sourdough bread was good, but slightly burnt.

    Tuna poke - Very good as well, although the macadamia nuts were not needed.

    Sea urchin with warm scrambled eggs - The scrambled eggs were deliciously done, and I also love sea urchin. Together they didn't seem to harmonize as much, and everything was just... mushy.

    Grilled octopus - Again a big fan of octopus, but it came out a little mushy also. I don't like it rubbery, but at the same time... not mushy either. The smoked chili and green tomato was nice.

    Ginger rice, black trumpet mushrooms, poached egg - Seemed pretty Asian inspired, but the rice was not cooked properly. The flavors were nice though.

    Pappardelle with braised rabbit - This was a home run dish. The pasta was delicious and the rabbit melt in your mouth.

    Amish chicken - Very nicely done as well. The chicken was moist and the skin crisp. It was very smoky from the wood oven, which is also nice.

    Overall I liked the restaurant but perhaps my expectations were a bit high.

    I am not one to make reservations for a prime dinner slot 3 weeks in advance, I am usually more impromptu, so that makes me more apt to go to a fine dining establishment and sit at the bar, get excellent service, people watch, and still enjoy the chef's magical creations.  That was my story again last night.  My first time at Vernick after reading the professional reviews as well as my fellow Yelpers, and I fell in love at first bite.

    I never did make it to the dining room to scope out the decor or ambiance, but my feeling at the bar was this restaurant had a very non-pretentious, very crisp, clean and relaxed atmosphere.  The bartenders were perfect, from knowledge, to descriptions of items on the menu, to great drink concoctions (how about some tequila with a little lime juice and some liquid cayenne, with a small thin slice of lime swimming on the top, talk about flavor), to just plain old paying attention to us.  By the way, there is a real nice quaint but varied beer selection in bottle and on tap.

    For apps I started with the Hamachi (raw) with jalapeno oil and grapefruit, excellent flavor explosion, quality pieces of the fish and dish presentation.  My friend got the oysters which were sweet, tender and perfect served with a ginger and citrus mix.
