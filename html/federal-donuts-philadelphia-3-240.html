    -- fried chicken review --

    Federal Donuts on Sansom street is a very small shop with only about 6-7 seats in a row facing the kitchen. Nono its not a countertop but just stools with armrests. Luckily, the turnover is rather quick, as there is no space for hipsters to whip out their laptops. Sitting next to your friend feels like a movie date except instead of watching a movie you're looking at the menu, carefully deciding whether you should order more food until you are both too big to fit into the stools.

    We went for the butter ranch seasoned fried chicken, a half chicken with 2 breasts, a drumstick, and thigh. It will also come with a single plain donut, which was delicious.

    If you like your chicken super fried - this place is it. The skin will be fried to a crisp, with the seasoning very apparent. As a matter of fact, differently seasoned chickens are at least 100 hexidecimal of color codes apart. For a guy who has almost gave up on American Fried Chicken for Korean, Federal Donuts managed to salvage some of my faith back into 'Murica.

    Like all chicken horror stories however, half of the pieces were juicy while the other half were fiber-y and dry. Despite that, Federal Donut's fried chicken was different, delicious, and d... hmmm ... d... democratic (USA! USA!). It'll be nothing like your average KFC(both kentucky and korean) and Popeye's, so if you're a fan, definitely do give it a try.

    Oh fun fact -- the chef, Mike Solomov, is from the restaurant Zahav. Review for that to come.

    Listed in Quick Eats, Just Desserts, Coffee Meets Bagel & Teatime, Streets of Philadelphia

    I love donuts. And Federal Donuts makes some good donuts.

    While visiting Philly, I was browsing through lists of restaurants, cafes, and shops to try and narrow down what I could possibly visit in a 2-day window. I spotted Federal Donuts on several lists and immediately had it down as a must-try. 4 stars on Yelp and over 250 reviews? That's a good sign.

    Federal Donuts doesn't just make and sell donuts. They also have fried chicken. Donuts are $2 each, $12 for half a dozen, and $20 for a dozen. What a steal. I ordered a strawberry lavender donut. It was so hard not to order a dozen, but I resisted in the face of my my ever-growing stomach and appetite. I took the donut out and just walked around the streets of Philly in the bright early morning light. Eating that donut while walking was one of the best moments of my trip. A hot, delicious donut with subtle hints of lavender. The strawberry was more overpowering than the lavender, which I prefer anyways, but I loved the lavender undertones.

    What I like about Federal Donuts is that it is simple and unpretentious. Their menu offers three simple things: coffee, donuts, and fried chicken. The prices are cheap and the food is fresh. Simple and effective. The donut flavors are unique, from strawberry lavender to spicy PB&J to chocolate and sea salt.

    If you're ever in Philly, definitely make a trip to Federal Donuts.

The chicken itself is great - comes out super hot, juicy, and extra crispy on the outside. It's not exactly like Korean style fried chicken that you get at like Soho or Bonchon (in NY), but it's still got that signature double-fried crispy crunch. My only gripe was that there were only like 3 tiny, tiny slices of them. The strawberry lavender donut was great hot (not as great cold), and doused in flavored sugar. The lavender is really subtle, but I can see myself eating one of these a day for the rest of my life. Extremely yum.

    I returned around 4 PM, hoping to grab a fancy donut for myself, but alas, they were sold out. I saw that they still had some chicken left though, as well as their normal hot donuts. I'll be back, I promise.

From the business

Cake donuts in interesting flavors and Korean-style, twice-fried chicken sold by the half and whole bird. House-roasted coffee available hot or cold-brewed and iced. That's basically it.

Learn more about Federal Donuts , Opens a popup

Specialties

Cake donuts in interesting flavors and Korean-style, twice-fried chicken sold by the half and whole bird.  House-roasted coffee available hot or cold-brewed and iced. That's basically it.

History

Established in 2012.

Chef Michael Solomonov and partner Steve Cook of Zahav and Percy Street Barbecue got a wild idea to pair exotic donuts and super-crispy fried chicken. With Bodhi Coffee owners' Bob Logue and Tom Henneman's coffee expertise and professional bigmouth Felicia D'Ambrosio, the team opened the first FedNuts at 1219 S. Second St. in Pennsport, South Philly in 2011.  The Sansom Street shop followed one year later in October 2012.  We have since been cranking out fancy donuts, melt in your mouth hot fresh donuts, and truly badass chicken.  Follow us on Twitter www.Twitter.com/federaldonuts for status updates and fun contests.
