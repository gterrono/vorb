    Four stars for the food, but the service was atrocious.  

    We went as a party of four, me and three boys.  We were seated as soon as we arrived but the table seemed a bit small for four people.  The hostess/everyone we interacted with upon arrival were very nice.  

    THE FOOD:
    I ordered the rainbow ceviche because they didn't have the tuna one listen online (raw tuna is my best friend).  It was very good but a little a salty and there were one or two pieces of fish that had an off texture (a little chewy).  I also had the tuna tartare tacos (I wasn't kidding about the BFF part) and they were just ok, it would have been nice if the fish wasn't quite as blended together.  For my main dish I had the chilean sea bass which was very good.  It was my first sea bass experience and I'll definitely be adding it to my list of fish I like.  I also had some of the sweet plantains which were great and a few bites of the duck confit fried rice which I didn't love and was presented like bi bim bop which seemed rather out of place at a restaurant supposedly based on cubany food.  

    The rest of my party ordered like everything on the menu and licked their plates clean.  They said the empenadas (vegetarian) were just ok, nothing special or notable.  The smoked marlin tacos had good flavor but were a bit dry, they mentioned a hot sauce on top may have been a nice contrast.  They had the oysters which they didn't realize would be fried but they still enjoyed them.  They also ordered the 7 ceviche taster and skipped the fluke ceviche.  No one pointed out which was which (service????) but they enjoyed them all, I just can't really give specific reviews without knowing for sure which was which.  

    For their main dishes, one had the sea bass which he thought was a bit salty (mine didn't seem salty though I love salt).  Another person got the vaca frita which was well presented and tasty.  The last dish was the lechon asado which was flavorful but the portion is huge (we learned this was probably because it was a super fatty piece of meat.  

    Nothing was amazing but it was all pretty good.  

    THE SERVICE:
    It took probably 10+ minutes for us to get menus.  The waiter came to the table for drink orders. I ordered the passionfruit caipiroska which did not taste passionfruity at all but was fine.  There were two beers ordered and a diet coke.  The beers came quickly but he forgot the diet coke for about 10 minutes and then finally remembered but didn't apologize.  We put in our order and the waiter made snarky comments about our choices (especially not ordering the fluke).  Then he basically disappeared until it was time to give us our dessert menus and our bill.  It took a while for us to get the super awesome bread and when we wanted 2nds we asked one of the bus boys who got tied up in something and then our waiter, no bread appeared.  The bus boy reappeared and noticed we still didn't have bread and quickly and apologetically brought it to us.  If I had seen him on the way out, I would have tipped him more than our waiter.  

    OVERALL:
    The food was good but not worth the price.  I don't think I'll be returning when there are better options for the same or better prices.

    Was here last night with a party of 11...
    Overall, a very good time.  I guess I will go in depth because I'm waiting for my hair to finish dying.
    We arrived and were seated promptly and pleasantly at a table on the mezzanine.  We are a very rowdy company so it was good we were a bit separated from the general populous.  
    Almost immediately a server equally pleasant to the gentleman who greeted us and the young lady that sat us arrived and suggested mojitos.  Rum and lime.  Do it.  
    She was kind enough to bring us a variety of classic mojitos as well as ones with Bacardi limon for those preferring a sweeter cocktail.  I stuck with the classic and boy did it pack a wallop.  Tasty.
    The appetizers arrived and everyone was over the moon with the Gorgonzola, bacon wrapped dates on endive (meh for me though...it's such a standard appy I'm bored with it...)
    The ceviche was, in fact, exceptional as others have eluded. It was tuna and sea bass with a few shrimp and scallop.  Simply delicious - not too acidic which my heartburn prone colleague really appreciated.
    I had their Malbec with the appetizers and it was delicious.  
    The fish tacos were dry and boring.  
    For our entrees we had sea bass, salmon, hanger steak, and lamb.
    All of you can take whatever you want, but gimme the lamb.  Tender, not gamey, very rare which actually worked (lamb is the ONLY meat I eat medium-rare I think because of the gameyness of it...but this was phenomenal).
    The hanger steak was cooked to death, the salmon was dry, the bass had a strange, almost oily texture to it (man made oily...not natural oil...maybe it was poached in olive oil?  I don't know).
    I had a French 75 with my meal.  
    Oh, the rice and beans and all that jazz?  Forget it.  Don't bother.
    The desserts were lovely from what I heard...I stole a tiny bite of the flan and it was indeed tasty.  They disappeared quickly so I'm assuming you sweet toothed folks would be delighted.
    Pricey, but the ambiance and service are very, very good.  I recommend this place if you want to a) impress someone b) spend some coin and c) be treated well.

    Drinks: hibiscus margarita - awesome; passionfruit caiprioska - pretty good, not too sweet.
    Cornmeal rolls and oil dip, solely good.
    Appetizers ( this was fun): let's talk ceviche. We ordered three to contrast and compare: the shrimp, scallops and the rainbow. The rainbow was recently reworked, with shoestring potatoes now over the ceviche instead of thinly sliced as served previously. The rework was definitely worth it, as our combined scores put this ceviche as the frontrunner.
    As fas as the other two, the scallops were a clear winner over the shrimp, having too much of a sweet, clumsy and saucy overtone, where the scallops were fresher, lighter and just tastier.
    Another appetizer that knocked our socks off: chorizo sliders. As strait forward as their name, these little wonders charmed our taste buds off. I seriously contemplated the possibility of trying to recreate these on my own for a future summer barbecue. Just be sure to include the cucumbers and jalepeño garnish to achieve the full orgasm of flavor that we enjoyed.
    Since we were on an appetizer kick, we simply had to have the oysters - the Oysters Rodriguez. While I am not certain what "fufu" is, it really doesn't matter. These little fuckers were simply awesome.
    On to the main courses. We had the sugar cane tuna, a nice balance between the spinach and the tomato coulis. The beef tenderloin was almost perfect - the crabmeat could have been a little warmer, but the combination was a little mundane. The vaca frita - the skirt steak - was probably the favorite. Aside from appearing on the plate like a mexican flag - the combination of seasonings was a delight to enjoy - cinnamon, cloves, lord knows what else - it was a sensory delight.
    We're on to the desserts now, with the chocolate cigar - a tough decision over the chocolate tower - and a special dessert with some combination of avocado, white chocolate risotto and candied pineapple -  but I'm fairly cation it will be wonderful.

We told the hostess we'd like to have drinks in the lounge and then have dinner. She then asked us if we would like to check our coats and to enjoy [the lounge].. As we finished our drinks, we headed back to the host stand to request a table.

Our waitress greeted us, brought menus, more drinks, and this delicious yucca bread served with chimichurri sauce. The bread was delightful to eat and perfectly chewy and slight sweet. We've even tried to recreate it.. but had no luck finding yucca flour at Wegmans. We should just call the restaurant for the recipie.

    With our waitress's help, we started with the Smoked Marlin Tacos with the Rum-cured Marlin served in malanga boats with pickled jalapenos. This appetizer was simply amazing and I loved how all the different flavors tied together beautifully. Next, I decided to try some of the Ceviche. I had never had Ceviche before but I chose our waitress's recommendation.. the Rainbow Ceviche. It had tuna, salmon and black bass marinated in lime juice and jalapeno with roasted sweet potato slices. The acid in the juice cooks the fish ever so slightly and all the flavors just exploded in my mouth. Fantastic!

    For entrees, I had the Lechon Asado which was roasted pork, congri rice and "orange mojo" (marinade) and my girlfriend had Maine Diver Scallops with Pastel de chocolo (puff pastry) and piquillo chimichurri.

Post industrial exposed brick walls, dark red tones, bright white leather seats and benches. Clapboard ceilings with lights shining indirectly through create a more expansive space. Dim lighting, low yet comfortable chairs, three floors of beautiful artwork and absurdly attentive service combine to deliver an incredible experience.     I strolled through the cobbled streets of old Philadelphia for a while, did some shopping and found a dive to enjoy for a couple of hours before returning for dinner. I return, the hostess remembers me immediately and I am seated on the first floor at a table in the rear.

    Mojito anyone? Absolutely delightful, the crisp sparkling water, real yerba Buena and guarpo with lime. Real mojito's are hard to find. House roasted corn kernels arrives a finger food.

    I select ceviche by three. Peruvian Sea Bass with "Scallop-aji rocoto puree, key lime juice, cilantro and yucca fries." Ecuadorian Shrimp finished with "fire-roasted tomatoes, orange juice, avocado and corn nuts" and finally Long Island Fluke cooked in "Yuzu, soy, ginger, sesame and jasmine pearls." The sea bass was thick and chunky, tender and flavorful. The shrimp exuded freshness and had a wonderful kick, perfectly balanced by the roasted corn kernels. The fluke was phenomenal, tender, flaky, pure, flavored by hints of tea.

    Two appetizers for main course. Pulpo con Causa, a "grilled Spanish octopus, warm potato puree with aji amarillo, Peruvian olive sauce and sliced avocado." This dish alone is worth traveling to Philly to try.

    Next arrive the marlin tacos. Three petite specimens, unbelievably fresh fish, drizzled with olive oil, pepper. The marlin is rich and juicy, an incredible mixture. A side of fried yucca completes the meal. Glass of Syrah Navarro Coreas 2007, excellent if unusual with fish but paired well since it is relatively light on the palate.

    Alma de Cuba served up a meal I will not forget. Perfection.

    "But how was the food, MacRae, how was the foooood?" you ask?  

    Well, it seemed Top Chef Masters caliber for sure, beginning with the whole grain dinner roll/biscuits with an herbaceous oil and vinegar dip, to the plantain and taro chip basket.  Moms and I split the appetizer sampler which came with smoked marlin tacos (I wish I'd gotten a pic, because these were doll-sized and ever-so precious); oysters Rodriguez; and royal palm dates.  Moms went nuts over the bacon-wrapped, almond and cheese stuffed dates, which she said were decadently delicious.  I loved the oysters Rodriguez -- a spin on traditional Rockefeller -- with the crispy fried shellfish over fufu, sautéed spinach, horseradish, and huacatay sauce.  It was only after devouring mine that I learned fufu is mashed plantains with *bacon.*  Would have been nice to know PRE-devour.

    Moms had the Brazilian shrimp stew with greens and cashews for her entrée.  After her fifth, "Mmm, phenomenal," I made her give up a bite.  She was right: the stew was a symphony of bold flavors, smooth broth, and huge, pearly shrimp.  I had a three-ceviche sampler for dinner and was in raw fish heaven.  AdC offers eight ceviches à la carte, or a three-, five-, or seven-sampler.  I went with the Thai Mixto -- crab, shrimp, octopus, and fluke with coconut leche de tigre, mango, and tomatillo; the Peruvian Black Bass -- scallop-aji rocoto purée, key lime juice, cilantro, and yucca fries; and the Yellowtail Kingfish -- Peruvian corn and potato salad, lime juice, and aji panca glaze.  They were all fantastically fresh and loaded with flavor, but the Thai was my personal favorite.
