    Saad's...better than the world-famous Mamoun's?  Probably.  

    First off, Saad's has all of the ambiance of eating at a 1970s-era McDonalds.  The lights are bright, the seats are hard and uncomfortable, and the tables are bland in that we-took-over-a-cheap-diner-and-turned-it-into-a-­Middle-Eastern-place kind of way.  Much like eating at a 1970s-era McDonalds, Saad's has a no cell phone policy, which I couldn't care less about, but it's always nice to piss off anyone who can't put down technology long enough to eat dinner.  

    Where Saad's has McDonald's (and Mamoun's) beat, however, is that it serves as one of the only meeting places for devout Muslims and drunk college kids worldwide.  

    Oh...and the food.

    The falafel sandwich would easily rival any other highly-praised falafel sandwich found anywhere in the US, but the option to add feta really pushes it over the top.  The greek salad sandwich, which is (surprise!) just a greek salad shoved inside a pita wrap, is somehow better than it sounds and absolutely delicious.  The spinach salad sandwich is nearly identical and a little less delicious, but still very good.  The stuffed grape leaves sandwich is one of my favorite sandwiches in West Philly, and I don't even like stuffed grape leaves!  Best of all, you can always get gut-bustingly full for less than five dollars.

    My only complaint is that the sandwich-makers at Saad's are so good at tightly wrapping their sandwiches that I have a hard time unwrapping them and always end up eating aluminum foil along with my pita because I get too excited about taking the next bite and don't want to put in the extra required effort.  The alternative option is to unwrap the sandwich completely at the beginning and end up with a lap full of tahini.  I may cut the inside of my stomach to shreds one day, but it will be worth it.

    Saad's is a halal at its finest.  Don't be fooled by its mild mannered storefront because it serves up some of the best halal food around!

    I am personally a huge fan of the lamb maroosh way and get it every time, as a comfort food after a long day of work or studying.  And you can't argue with the student friendly pricing either.

    One quick tip I would caution is to keep your cell phone business to a minimum as their are signs posted everywhere that you're not to be on your phone, which I find kind of charming about the place.  It's kinda nice to see people having conversations with each other at the tables rather than yapping away on their phones.

    If I dare compare to NYC's famous chicken and rice halal food cart, this place wins by a long shot, on the plus side it avoids a 4 hour pilgrimage and like $30 in toll not to mention probably a tank of gas to get there.

    Save that money and buy more maroosh :)

Some good Arabic dishes are the chicken falafel sandwich and beef kafta platter. I personally find that their food is best eaten when fresh (as in sit-down dining) because it can get soggy if taken out. Be prepared to come on an empty stomach because the food is very filling but incredibly satisfies your hunger! My only qualm is that it can get very busy and it's hard to find seats sometimes. Service is generally quick but can be a bit slower when it's really busy. It's generally busy on Friday and Saturday nights but not always. It's a little bit pricy but the food is so good you almost can't help but eat here often. It is also halal (for Muslims) and the owner, Saad, prepares the meat himself so you know where it comes from. Saad's is a very well-known local restaurant in West Philly and if you are in the area, it is well worth a visit!
