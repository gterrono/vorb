    I like barbecue...most people do. I wouldn't say it's my favorite cuisine in the world but sometimes you just want to go back to your caveman roots and tear apart some flesh!

    That said, I lived across from Fette Sau for 4 months, literally across the street, and I always thought it was closed as the front courtyard was always empty. But finally, it dawned on me that they do have (a rather huge) interior building as well. So my bf and I stopped him after he raved about his man brunch there with his brother and father the week prior.

    We got the brisket, pulled pork, and some other juicy, meaty joy. We rounded it out with some potato salad (which was BOMB), and cole slaw (also bomb). The meat was good; the spicy sauce and vinegar definitely kicked it up a notch. We also had the bacon brownie, which was good but I wouldn't write home about it.

    So if I'm so not enthused by the food, why 4 stars? Simple--COCKTAILS!! I was not expecting a hidden BBQ joint to have my favorite drinks in Fishtown but it does! We had the Fishtown Ryatt, and whatever the one named after Columbia Ave. First, clever drink names, great homage to Fishtown. Secondly, WOW, perfectly balanced, multi-faceted bourbon cocktails! What an art form! Our bartender was less than happy to be there but gave us some good advice on an upcoming trip to New Orleans. Thirdly, love the rusty butcher knife tap handles and the killer bourbon selection!!

    Allllll I can say is that I can't wait til spring and summer, because this will be the perfect place to grab a drink outside at one of their picnic tables!

    I really enjoyed my visit last night.  Having planned my order in advance I opted for 1/2 pound each of ribs, brisket, and pork belly.  Wow.  Ribs were amazing - really didn't even require sauce.  Plenty meaty and moist.  Brisket was great too.  The pork belly was just OK.  

    I also sampled each of the three available sauces - sweet, spicy, and vinegar.  The sweet was far and away the best.  It reminded me of an amped-up version of Arby's BBQ sauce (and that's not a dig at all, btw) with a surprising touch of heat at the end.  I found the vinegar to taste like, well, vinegar.  The spicy sauce was strange in that aside from the heat it didn't really taste like anything.  Not sure if that's just the way the vinegar and spicy sauces are intended to be or if they were just underseasoned.

    You've got to try the baked beans.  Burnt ends, shards of pork throughout, and super flavorful.  Thick consistency, almost like chili.  The cold broccoli salad was a hit, too.

    Service was friendly and laid-back.  I'd give it 5 stars if it were a bit less expensive.  Will definitely be back for more ribs, brisket, and, hopefully, the beef tongue pastrami will be available.  Did you know they have flank steak, too? :)  

    A hefeweizen washed it all down nicely.  Was thinking "bacon brownie" but opted to pass on dessert.  I then waddled down Girard Avenue in a protein-induced stupor.  Surprised I was able to find my car.

    I WAS PROUD TO MAKE A FAT PIG OF MYSELF AT FETTE SAU!

    Fette Sau (German for "fat pig") is every bit as you may heard from those with enough common sense to get off their buns and get out for some real deal barbecue. Modeled after the original Fette Sau in Williamsburg, Brooklyn, this location brings all of the pork and all of the flavor. Bring your A-game and appetite or go home a sorry sucker that failed to get his fill. Get a group and gather around all the porky goodness that you are in for. This place is Vegetarian-hostile, so unless they feel like filling up on side dishes you should leave your Vegan friends for another day.

    The Crew:
    7 guys

    The Meat:
    1 lb. Black Angus Beef Brisket
    1 lb. Berkshire Pulled Pork
    1 lb. Duroc St. Louis Ribs
    1 lb. Berkshire Pork Tenderloin

    The Sides:
    1 Lg. Dante's German Potato Salad
    2 Lg. Guss' Half Sour Pickles
    2 Lg. Burnt End Baked Beans
    1 lg. Coleslaw
    7 Extra Martins Dinner Rolls

    The Drinks:
    1 gallon jug of beer

    The meat cutters at the front will recommend that you order 1/2 lb. of food per person.  We came with a group of 7, so 4 lb. of food was sound advice. No worry about buying a pig in a poke. All of the meat was proudly displayed in the showcase and then hand-sliced to order. As we arrived not long after noon, we knew that it had been sitting out there for very long. Between the Beef Brisket, Pulled Pork, St. Louis Ribs, and Pork Tenderloin it was hard to say which one was my favorite of us.  All of the meat was moist and perfectly cooked, but I could not resist the urge of using the bottles of Sweet and Spicy sauces that were on the table. Especially when I was making Pulled Pork sandwiches on Dinner Rolls. A little dab of the Sweet and a dab of the Spicy sauces was enough to keep me coming back for more.

    Even the sides are star attractions at Fette Sau. The coleslaw was neither too creamy nor has too much vinegar. My father Sam K. was present and has long been known for making a wonderful coleslaw himself, so he bears expert testimony on its quality. His three helpings of coleslaw should serve as testament as to just how good a slaw they serve.

    You'd swear that the pickles came straight from a barrel in the Lower East Side, NYC, because these are true to their word Half Sour. No bait and switch by pitching Dill Pickles and passing them off as Half Sours. My nose could smell a trick like that from a mile away.

    No pork and beans to be found at this table. Even the baked beans was flavored with pieces of flank steak instead of that bits of fat like your Mom may have served you when you were a kid. We ordered 2 large sides and I easily could have eaten another one all by myself had I not remembered the 6 other people that were eating with me.

    As we drank our gallon jug of beer from mason jars, we marveled over the two trays of food that were now bone dry. If I could have reached across the table and licked off any remaining scraps without grossing out the other patrons would be the only way that I could have done a better job. Between 4 lb. of meat and another 2 lb. of sides each one of us ate almost 1 lb. of food a piece. Rather than feeling bloated or stuffed, I felt like it may have been the first time that I had real barbecue.  It's time for me to stop putting off going to the Williamsburg location and find out what I've been missing!

    This place knows how to do BBQ, plain and simple. My Dad and I went here this past Saturday and it has now become our favorite BBQ spot in Philly. Sorry Percy's Street BBQ, while I do love your brisket this one takes the cake.

    We ordered a pound of brisket to split, with sides of baked beans with burnt ends, german potato salad, and mushrooms. The briskest was the perfect mixture of fat and meat, and the bark was crunchy and delicious. The smoke on the meat was just right, it was not overpowering but was present and let you know that the meat was certainly not baked (a cardinal sin in the BBQ world). Before trying this brisket I thought no one could top Percy's, but I was wrong. My Dad loved his baked beans with burnt ends, and I sampled a burnt end and got an explosion of flavor in my mouth. The german potato salad and mushrooms were really good as well. I'm not a big fan of cold potatoes but this side won me over.

    I liked the atmosphere of the place as well, laid back with music set at a comfortable level, and many tables both inside and outside. There are multiple heat lamps outside so when there is not enough room inside customers can eat in the yard in relative warmth and not freeze in this cold weather.  

    I never heard about this place until I read about it in the most recent issue of the Philadelphia Magazine. The author of the article said that the beef short rib he ate might be the best one he's ever had. Given my love for beef ribs, which is sometimes greater than that for brisket, I ordered a beef rib to go. I just had it with dinner tonight and I concur that the beef rib is the best I have ever had, and that was after sitting in my fridge for two days and being nuked in the microwave. I can only imagine how good it tastes eating it fresh at Fette Sau itself. I hope to experience that pleasure sometime soon, which may come quicker than I think considering my Dad loved the place just as much as I did. Both of us are definitely going to become regular customers and will frequent this place as often as we can.

    So meaty.  

    If you're in the mood for meat, and just meat, you should hit up Fette Sau.  Located next door to Frankford Hall, Fette Sau is a cafeteria style bbq joint with a rustic environment featuring wooden lengthy picnic tables and exposed brick walls.  Depending on when you go, you might not have to wait in line, or you might be in a line full of drunkards, which was the case when I visited at 7pm on a Saturday.  

    During our 30 minute queue the boo and I strategically picked out what we wanted using our notepad apps on our phones.  The meat adds up quickly, and it's impossible to try everything for less than $30 a person.  My order consisted of 1 rib, .25 lb of brisket, 1 link of sausage, a small order of roasted mushrooms, and a small order of baked beans for a total of $23.  Beers range from $3 for a half pint to $45 for a growler - which is kind of insane considering you can get a growler fill of the same Yards brew for half the price down the street at Yard's - but I guess they gotta make their money.  The staff is very helpful, and lets you know how much things weigh when you think you can house/afford 6 ribs.  

    A few 45 year old drunks were in line a row ahead of us.  After about 20 minutes or so, the queue barriers fell over and the three old men were all laying on the floor in a drunken stupor.  No idea how they fell down, but it was probably my favorite part of Fette Sau.  Drunk old men falling over in line.

    Of all the meats I tried - and I had a bite of the boo's pork belly - I'd have to say the ribs and the brisket were my faves.  The ribs are so chock full of meat.  No gristle or fat even graced the bone when I was finished and I'm not one of those slurpy chomping manly rib eaters either.  The brisket wasn't too fatty and was extra tender.  All meats are just bbq'd - so there isn't any sauce, which is why you need to use the ones on the table (sweet, spicy, vinegar) to flavor your meats.  

    Tip - don't waste your money on sides.  They're not worth it.  All the reviews I read raved over the baked beans, but I couldn't finish them...they're not a sweet baked bean...they're almost in a ragu type sauce.  In the array of sides that we tried, my favorite was the cole slaw, but even so it wasn't the best of all time.  They really should add some kind of macaroni salad or mac n cheese to the menu, as long as it doesn't step on the toes of soul food.  

    Although it's a great new place, and the meats are on point, I'd rather just get the bar food while visiting Frankford Hall, and save my bbq craving for something more satisfying.

History

Established in 2012.

Fette Sau (German for 'fat pig') is a barbecue restaurant specializing in dry-rubbed meats, sourced from
small, local farms and smoked in-house. The menu changes daily, with a rotating selection of six meats
available for purchase by the pound. The sides are kept simple and include burnt end baked beans, broccoli
salad, pickles, sauerkraut, house-made coleslaw and potato chips. The drink list offers nine beers on
tap, plus a cider, and an extensive list of North American bourbon and whiskeys. Picnic tables are the
primary form of seating, giving the restaurant a communal atmosphere. The space has an industrial feel,
with it's reclaimed wood, cement floor and exposed beam ceiling.

Meet the Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
