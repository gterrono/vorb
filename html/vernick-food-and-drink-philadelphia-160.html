    Yet another great Philly fine-dining experience!

    I'm so glad that my friend suggested this place to me a week ago.

    For an appetizer, I definitely recommend the sea urchin with warm scrambled eggs. It's an incredibly unique dish that I had never tried before, and the combination of the raw sea urchin with the warm eggs makes for a very savory and hearty starter to your meal. Don't bother with being courteous and transferring the food from the bowl to your plate first. Just go ahead and eat directly from the bowl. It doesn't look very dignified, but it really makes a difference in how the dish tastes.

    For the main meal, the pork blade steak is a good choice if you're looking for something heavier than fish. The pork is cooked medium rare and is pre-cut into thin slices of wonderfully juicy and chewy steak. I personally wasn't a fan of the mustard greens that accompanied the meat, but I didn't think they detracted from the dish's overall taste at all.

    Both the panna cotta and the blueberry pie make for solid dessert choices, but if you have to pick one, CHOOSE THE BLUEBERRY PIE. I honestly would give Vernick 5 stars on the pie alone. Incredibly crumbly, the pie crust formed a flawless container for the very fresh blueberries within. I've never had a blueberry pie that tasted so authentic (and "classy") before.

    The second floor dining room that we were seated in provided a very nice atmosphere for our meal, and the fact that we had a view of the street below made the location of our seating seem that much nicer. In regards to service, I thought that our waiter did an excellent job in contributing to our dining experience. He was incredibly knowledgeable about all of the dishes on the menu and would often offer us advice on the best way to partake in our delicious dishes. He also happened to have one of the most glorious mustaches I have ever seen on any human being. My table was just in awe at how perfectly curled the sides of his mustache were. That seems like a really random thing to point out in a review, but he (or his mustache) definitely made an impression on me.

    Long story short, Vernick has found a place in my Philly Top 5 fine-dining experiences.

    Go find out why.

    This spot is really top notch. We were visiting from New York and looking for a cool local establishment with outstanding food and memorable drinks...and we certainly found it in Vernick. Here is a summary for those who don't want to read on for all the yummy descriptions:

    Food: 4 1/2 stars
    Atmosphere: 4 stars
    Service: 5 stars

    We arrived promptly at 8:30 on a Wednesday night for our reservation and this place was packed. The bar crowd nearly monopolized the entire first floor of the restaurant and the locals seemed to be enjoying the ambiance and the warm breeze coming in from the open doors up front. The hostess station looked busy as the staff tried hard to seat everyone as quickly as possible. We weren't in a rush but the effort to seat us on time was noticeable and worth mentioning.

    Our server was a clean cut young gentleman who made ample recommendations and gave us time to sort through all of the fabulous menu choices. My husband (who is generally not a cocktail kind of guy) ordered the new-groni and I had a lovely crisp glass of white burgundy. The new groni was delicious-not sweet just very tasty. The server brought the bottle and poured me a taste upon request...as I confirmed my selection. We happened to overhear a guy (who was apparently a regular) at the next table compliment the entire menu but he complained a couple of times to his company that they needed a better wine list. We couldn't disagree more! If you're looking for your run of the mill wines, then you're better off elsewhere but if you're adventurous and want to navigate through a list of wines from lesser known regions and taste some new varietals, you will love it! The decor is modern meets industrial and suits the style of the restaurant perfectly. Several sets of doors open to the patio and upstairs terrace on both floors-the fresh air is a nice touch.

    We started with the "RAW" salmon small plate which was a tartare with the slightest kick of chili oil which gave it a beautiful spicy aftertaste. The fish was fresh and cut in perfect cubes topped with fresh dill. The whole fish preparation was a special New Zealand snapper grilled with their signature orange and fennel...wow! It elevated whole grilled fish to a new dimension, the fish was perfection, and the fennel was outrageous. The two sides we selected were the sautéed market greens (a medley of brussel sprout leaves, dandelions, chard) and the black pepper roasted cauliflower. Even if you hate cauliflower, I assure you that this will change your palate! I will be trying to replicate those for thanksgiving as they were unbelievably good.

    Stuffed to the gills, we nearly passed on dessert, but I just had to try the ice cream sandwich. Hubby had chocolate gelato which sounds ordinary, but was so rich and delicious, it is worth ordering. My ice cream sandwich was a marscapone ice cream between two crispy almond cookies. The perfect few bites...and my Cappucino was also excellent.

    If you haven't been to this restaurant, make a reservation and go. Just don't go expecting a philly cheese steak...this place is all about fresh seasonal ingredients. You won't be disappointed. Promise!

    I hate when a business rests on its laurels. And the laurels best be valid when they are high. Going into Vernick, the bar is set high, as are my expectations, so take my opinion with that in mind. Firstly, we were absolutely late for our reservation. I called twice, the hostess was sweet, and informed us that they hold your table for 15 minutes, and after that, they'll do what they can. Fair enough...we were 45 minutes late, our table was gone, there was room at the bar. I usually prefer that anyway, and if it was the same menu, no harm no foul. The thing with sitting at the bar for a meal, however, is that the bartender really does enhance or dampen the experience. Unfortunately, in this case, it was very much dampened. I wanted to like her, and smiled as I do while ordering and conversing. I think it was just in her nature to be...well, dull? Flat affect? What have you? That alone wouldn't bother me too much (just doesn't win any points for congeniality) it was how the evening played out, that detracted two stars for the experience.

    First, there was the two women who came and sat next to me. One lamented how the last bar she was in gave her a gatorade like drink when all she wanted was something "not sweet". So, she asked this bartender for a drink that wasn't sweet. I felt so bad for this lady...the bartender treated her like she was asking for a cut and color instead of a drink in the rolodex of her professional bartender mind. It was terribly uncomfortable to watch this play out, since the lady patron was the verbose-nervous type, but nice, just trying to get a drink that she likes. The bartender was not helpful or accommodating, and I could tell this poor lady wanted to crawl under a rock and finally just ordered a gimlet. Strike one on behalf of stranger lady.

    For my own part, we ordered chilled seafood salad, tuna poke, brussel sprouts with carmelized ancho and hamachi with caviar, and they arrived in that order by courteous servers. I will firstly say that all the food was very good. The sprouts were scrumptious. The seafood salad was tasty as long as you got a full bite of everything together. The tuna  poke was fresh and not overwhelmingly dressed. And the hamachi was really a nice harmony of textures and flavors, once i was able to actually eat it...

    The problem arose when I looked closely at the hamachi when it was delivered, and there was an eyebrow hair sticking out of the fish. Believe me when I say, I am the last one to want to waste perfectly good food. And hair is on all of us, it flies off, I get it...If it were just sort of laying on top, I may have just picked it off and ate it anyway. But at this kind of restaurant, I think that hair-in-food is sort of unacceptable to begin with, and this particular hair was embedded in the food.

    I didn't miss a beat and pointed it out to the bartender right away. She closely examines and says, "I don't see anything, but I believe you. Do you want a new plate?" I won't break down how every word of that response was wrong, (I'll leave that to you) but my response was to literally stand up over the bar and say, "No I want you to see this because its definitely there, and I don't want to be one of those people." She held a candle to it, and said she still couldn't see it. This was a black hair, folks. In a white fish. It was obvious, and it was there. Meanwhile, my husband, mortified, is shooting me the just-let-it-go look.

    She took the plate to the other bartender or perhaps manager, and they said, "Very sorry about that, would you like a new plate?" I said, "I really don't want to waste food, I just wanted you to know that happened, I can just scrape it off." He said, "No we'll get you a new plate." Well, okay then.
    It took about 10 minutes for them to come back with a new plate, and it was clear: They just scraped it off. I mean, the whole affected area was gone, about 2 slices of hamachi, with the cream sauce below the taken slices still there. They didn't even bother to put 2 new slices on, and then I wouldn't have even noticed.

    So to recap: Hair in food. Made to feel like a liar for pointing out hair in food. A modest apology. A returned plate with the same food, but less of it. No price modification. No offer of restitution.

    For two people to sit at the bar, order a few drinks and appetizers for $110, I simply expect it to be a great experience. The food was good, but not good enough to outweigh the failed service and uncomfortable personality of the bartender. Sorry, but 3 stars seems generous here.
