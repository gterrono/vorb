    I wanted to remark on the tremendous variety on the menu at Vedge. It's a small plate/tapas place, yet you think... "it's a full vegetarian menu, how many way's can they do brussel sprouts?"  But, you will be surprised!

    Now, you need to set your expectations, because this is not going to be a steakhouse, and if you have the attitude coming in like, "this place better serve meat and potatoes", you're going to be sorely disappointed. But on the same level, I want to stress that this place isn't geared to appease vegetarians. Their target market is a lot more sophisticated than that, and while sure, you're going to get a few bleeding-heart PETA types at tables, I really hope that omnivores and those who can appreciate the refinement of impressive food.

    Some of the vegetable dish preparations are so noteworthy that you're left a bit shocked. This place takes side dishes and makes them the star in ways you didn't realize was possible with a vegetable.  Subtle smokiness gives many dishes a complexity that makes you question what you're putting in your mouth.  The savory taste that is usually absent from vegetables is present in groves. It's really quite amazing how the chefs at Vedge can change your perception of food.

    Don't get me wrong: Vedge is not a place I would want to go regularly. I found it a bit pricey and the plates a bit small.  I also am not a gigantic fan of diet restricted menus.  However, Vedge exceeds expectations and is a must go to place to challenge your preconceived notions of the food world.  Perhaps if vegetables were embraced like this more regularly, we'd all have a very different attitude towards "eating our veggies".

    Do check it out.

    omnivores of the world - take a chance at vedge

    i have been going to vedge a lot lately and due to an influx of out of town friends, and subsequently i have found myself maybe stopping by once a week over the last few months.

    i fall more and more in LOVE with vedge every time I go.  and more importantly - i have noticed that their clientele is largely comprised of non-vegans who are experiencing a unique menu and are coming for a full on fine dining extravaganza. I overhear the staff giving expert advice and reassuring commentary to diners who are generally unaware that yes, you can make cheesecake from cashews.  

    Walking through the door you are immediately welcomed by a homey and comfortable yet opulent atmosphere.  it's the perfect mix of friendly and fashionable.  the dining area is cozy and the serving wear is interesting.  

    foodies:  you are about to experience the new frontier.  

    First off - the cocktail and wine list is second to none.  The cocktails are well crafted and well presented, plus they taste great.  but they take a second chair to the wine list!!!   The wine list changes depending on availability and wino's take note:  you will NOT find a better curated wine list in the city, i don't care WHERE  you go, this list is the best, hands down.  every glass of wine i have had a vedge has been wonderful.  you can tell that each bottle is thoughtfully chosen and of course, the presentation is impeccable.  

    I must admit - i have written down bottle names and vintage years and then gone online to find the bottles for home purchase, and it's been a challenge to track some down.  oh vedge - can you just send me cases of whatever you're ordering please?

    Now time for the food.  For starters i love the 3 for 9.  its a great way to sit down, settle in and prepare for the upcoming extravagance.  i love their black olives, which are just as good as quality olives I've had while traveling the Mediterranean, peel and eat lupini beans, and home pickled veggies.  

    Next i like to order the sweet potato pate.  its earthy and warm and served with a sweet and savory mustard and little toasted pieces of bread.  you can ask for more bread btw! (just sayin!)   The mustard works extremely well with the pate - and after eating this pate i get why fois gras is so popular.  This is a great way to have that experience without the force feeding and slaughtering of geese.

    Next up for small plates- i like to switch things up but i love the Brussels sprouts which seem to stay on the menu.  I also always try to get something new depending on what's available. I LOVE the earthy mushroom dishes, and i recently had a mushroom and kale plate that was amazing.

    For larger plates the "go to" mock meats are the tofu and seitan. While they are delicious and flavor-full i do try to focus more on larger plates like the carrot dish. The tofu is presented salmon-like and it is full of flavor.  Their seitan is hands down the BEST seitan on the planet.  

    Desserts are on a whole other level of sophistication and flavor combinations.  i don't even want to describe them as to spoil the surprise.  My favorites are the rhubarb sorbets and the stout ice cream.  everything is amazing though so you're in luck with no matter what you choose.  

    all in all this is the best place to have a meal from atmosphere, to cuisine, to service, and cost.  sure the prices can add up BUT if you compare this to some of the higher priced steak houses or pricier restaurants in philly - Vedge delivers a higher quality, a greater inspiration, and more unique experience for a competitive price.

    I seriously want to pluck Vedge up from Locust Street and drop it within walking distance of my apartment in NYC. I mean it - we desperately need a restaurant like this in the city!

    I love that Vedge doesn't aspire to be a purist vegan restaurant that goes to lengths to praise the virtues of a meatless diet. No, Vedge is not a "vegan restaurant"; it's an amazing restaurant that just happens to be 100% vegan. And 110% delicious.

    The bf and I walked in shortly before 6pm on a Saturday and were able to snatch two prime seats at the bar. We chatted up our bartender briefly as he explained the format of the menu to us and made some recommendations. He also made us some tasty cocktails including my Old Hickory that was the perfect combination of lightly oaky vermouth rojo, Manzanilla sherry, aperol and bright sweet orange. The gin-based elder sage cocktail was good as well, with the fragrant sage front and center by the addition of a hand-smacked sage leave floated on top.

    As for the food, every dish we had was flavorful, bright and beautifully executed. Standouts included the summer corn salad, a cornucopia of contrasting flavors from the deeply umami grilled trumpet mushrooms, crunchy pistachios and charred onions for acidity. The roasted maitake mushroom was also spectacular, simply roasted to showcase the mushroom's deep, slightly earthy flavor. The celery root fritter accompanying the maitake was pretty mindblowing, taking on a flavor and texture similar to a very good crab cake, especially dipped in the smoked leek remoulade. The dishes from the "dirt" menu all looked promising, and we thoroughly enjoyed the brussels sprouts that were shaved and grilled, coated in a zesty smoked mustard sauce. I appreciated that they didn't roast the shit out of the brussels, instead letting its natural cabbagey flavors to shine through. It really goes to show that you don't have to deep fry or mask vegetables to make them palatable. Another winner was the sweet potato pate, smooth and thick with a hint of savory wood flavor from the grill. The kicker for me was the jerk cashews on the side, which added unexpected nuttiness and texture sprinkled on top of the toast and pate.

    Besides the savory food, the desserts (yes, vegan too) at Vedge were also nothing short of wonderful. The flavor combinations were really innovative and refreshing, yet they also totally worked. I loved the summer corn custard with corn bread crumbs, jalapeno blackberry gastrique which highlighted the natural sweetness of the corn and actually packed a punch. I only wish the bf would have given me more than just one bite, I liked it so much that I had even contemplated going back the next night just for the corn custard. The yuzu peach tart had good flavor as well, especially the brown sugar miso ice cream that was kind of a play on salted caramel. My only qualm was that the tart dough itself was a bit raw, although I had no issue eating it since it didn't contain any eggs.

    In the end, Vedge truly deserves 5 stars for giving the bf the biggest unexpected surprise in a while. Now he can't make fun of me for having been a vegan/vegetarian for so many years...

    My husband and I decided to go to Vedge to celebrate our 3rd wedding anniversary.

    Backstory: Me: vegetarian, husband: omnivore. We moved to the Philadelphia area from SoCal in February, 2013. Our old veterinarian, actually, who attended Penn, really loved Richard Landau's old restaurant, so when we found out he had a new one, we were intrigued. Seeing his appearance on Chopped made us more eager to try it!

    The decor and atmosphere is beautiful. It's pretty large, with several dining rooms, a front bar room, several fireplaces, beautiful leaded glass, marble counters and wood floors.

    We each had a cocktail to start the night before our table was ready. I went with the Elder Sage and my husband went with the Barrel-Aged Creole Gent, both very good and well-balanced. With dinner, we each had a glass of wine, which was tasty, but their wines-by-the-glass are pricey. They had a small but quality beer list, as well.

    The concept here is small plates. We went with 4 from the Vedge Bar, which is the cold kitchen, and more appetizer-like, 2 from the hot kitchen, and 2 from the dirt list (sides). The only complaint is that about FIVE of our dishes (all of the cold dishes) came out at once, and on a small table, it was crowded. Also, we ate those in about 20 minutes, so I was afraid we'd be rushed out of the restaurant. Never fear, we spent over and hour there, but service was very good - friendly and efficient.

    Our absolute favorite dish was the portobella carpaccio. I seriously considered ordering another one. It was thinly sliced portobello, heavily peppered, and served with this wonderful mustard sauce. It tasted.... like carpaccio, I guess! Now, I haven't eaten beef in about a decade, but my husband, who eats red meat probably about once every week or two, also really, really loved it.

    Our second favorite dish was the fancy radishes. This was served shashimi-style with chop sticks and a smokey soy sauce. The presentation was BEAUTIFUL. The different components were all really tasty as well.

    Our other appetizers were the heirloom tomatoes - beautiful and delicious, but not super memorable - and the sweet potato pate - very interesting and tasty.

    Fro the dirt list, we got the fingerling fries in creamy worcestershire. They came tossed in the really tasty sauce! Nice and hearty dish. Secondly, we got the sunchoke hummus, which tasted like... hummus with sunchokes. The sunchoke chips on top were really good, but otherwise it was pretty standard.

    Our mains were the eggplant braciole and the roasted maitake mushroom. I loved the mushroom - the remoulade was really tasty, if a little salty, like fancy tartar sauce (which is kind of what remoulade is!). The braciole was hearty, but a little one-note for me. However, I'm not the biggest eggplant fan.

    For desert we went with the apple cider donuts and sticky toffee pudding. The donuts came with a sweet horseradish cream. I like horseradish, but I'm still not sure how I feel about it in a dessert. Even in the sweet cream, it seemed... savory. It's like, "what is going on? Is this real life? Is this a DONUT?" The pastry itself was really good, though. The sticky toffee pudding was excellent, especially the crunchy nut toffee on top. The ice cream wasn't super rich - I really love coconut-based vegan ice cream, which I find to be really creamy and smooth, but hey, I appreciate the effort. Also, that's the only thing of the night that I tasted and knew instantly was a vegan imitation of an animal-product dish. Not a poor imitation, mind you, and if you ARE a vegan, you'll very much enjoy it. If you've had real ice cream recently, well, it tastes not quite there.

    Despite my little criticisms, this is by far the fanciest and most interesting/innovative vegan restaurant I've ever been to. Remember: I used to live in Los Angeles and got tons of vegan food from vegan restaurants all the time (thought mostly asian-inspired, fast-casual, or "comfort" style food). This food was beautifully plated and really creative. It's definitely achieved the goal of being vegan haute-cuisine. If you're wondering if it's worth $150 for plant-based food... it totally is. Now, I wouldn't bring my traditional, meat-and-potatoes parents here. (Actually, we spent the better part of last week catering to their tastes, which involves things like roasted chicken, mac and cheese, green beans, tuna melts... you get the idea... so this dinner was a VERY nice change of pace.) But if you are up for interesting food, this is a really excellent choice. I'd certainly go back, especially with their ever-changing menu.

    Sidenote: I ended up wearing a leather jacket and leather shoes to the restaurant and I was a little ashamed of my obvious non-veganess, but the other patrons seemed to be similarly dressed. I.e. tons of "normal" looking middle class business-type people out to enjoy a really nice meal. Aside from the plant-based menu, this does NOT come off as a preachy/hippie vegan restaurant.
