    I should have stopped by this place earlier. Great service, amazing wine and beer selection topped… I should have stopped by this place earlier. Great service, amazing wine and beer selection topped off with delicious food to go with your drinks. There are many things that I love about this place, but here are a few highlights:
    - The truffled egg toast with fontina cheese... this was featured as one of the best sandwiches on Grub Street. This might be my favorite thing on the menu... there is something just so delicious about a combination of buttery toast, egg, and cheese. It's not just for breakfast!
    - Great selection of wines... the Pecorino stands out if you ask me.
    - Flourless espresso chocolate cake with mascarpone and hazelnut. I shouldn't even need to explain this one - just look at the name! This flourless cake has a wonderful richness that isn't overwhelming, and the mascarpone adds a nice light accompaniment to the chocolate.
    - If you're in the mood for a meaty dish, the Italian meat plate was pretty solid. It also comes with some sides like garlic paste and peppers which are nice garnishes too.

    Apparently there can be a little bit of a wait sometimes... it's not a huge place (but according to other reviews this location is bigger) so when I arrived with my party of 3 at 6pm on a Saturday we were given about a 15-25 min wait. But, we were a little impatient and wanted to get started since we were going to a Kimmel Center concert at 8, so we asked to get seated at the bar. The great thing about the bar is that service is good and swift - there is always someone around to take your order and they are all pretty nice :). Tria is a great place to grab a drink and a bite to eat before a Kimmel Center performance - it's only a few blocks away on Spruce!     Read more

    I find Tria simply irresistible.  One of the (many) spots in Philly that I love bringing my New York friends to, in order to show off how great Philly really is.  Thank you Tria!  Now if only I could stop dropping so much cash every time I go in there... not that this place is expensive by any means, on the contrary it's quite affordable.     Tria. Where do I start?

    Last Friday afternoon, I took the Bolt Bus from NYC to Philadelphia to meet a lovely old friend for drinks and dinner. She suggested this "wine and cheese" bar and gave me an address.  Little did I know that I'd still be talking about this little "wine and cheese" bar upon my return to NYC.  

    Stumbling into Tria, I was faced with the difficult choice of outside seating or in... I changed my mind a few times (thank you thank you patient servers... I can't stand people who do this!) and settled on one of the high tops by the door inside the bar.

    Opening the menu was like Christmas Day. First of all, I've been dying to test out a beer (really, any beer) from Russian River which I eventually saw all over Philadelphia, because what one cannot have, one immediately wants. And given the lack of Russian River Brewery in NYC/NJ, I immediately wanted a beer from this specific brewery. Upon further inspection, I found some real gems beer-wise AND wine-wise and made the difficult decision to forgo the Russian River for a Oude Gueze style from a brewery of which I cannot remember.  I've been a sucker for these lately, for some reason.

    What followed was some awesome bruschetta, a fantastic array of cheese...

    It's obvious that Tria must spend a lot of time teaching their staff about wine and cheeses.  Every time I've paid Tria a visit--and those visits have been varied, from afternoon shopping excursions to later-evening noshes to happy hour visits--the staff has known an excellent pairing for wine and cheese.  Part of the fun of visiting a place like Tria is experimenting with the pairings.  If you're not in the mood for heavy eating, but instead want to soak up the ambiance while nibbling at cheese and baguettes, Tria is your place.

    The food menu itself is rather simple, with fare like Smoked Chicken Salad and different sandwiches.  Tria seems to go with local or organic choices for some of its items.  No matter how much I've eaten at Tria or elsewhere, I always like the simple Nutella panini.  Sure, it scrapes the roof of your mouth, but chocolate can sometimes be pain.

    The interior is warmly lit and inviting.  The only downside is the seating is limited.  If you want to sit outside, you're forced to sit next to a noisy street with little area for seating.  The servers always seem annoyed when they have to serve you outside as it's an endless trek up and down the front stairs.  Otherwise, the servers are great.  

    Another thing to look out for is what Tria calls Sunday School.  Now, I don't know about you, but for me, Sunday School has an entirely different meaning.  For Tria, it means they highlight some cheese, wine, and beer choices, discount them, and give you a little lesson about them.  Obviously, they're not wine snobs, and understand that not everyone was born knowing all the different wine regions of France.  Just my kind of place, because I'm a rather no-frills gal who likes a little frill from time to time.

Specialties

Welcome to Tria, Philadelphia's wine, cheese and beer cafe. We serve real wines, cheeses and beers crafted by passionate people. Our ever-changing fermentation menus are complemented by a cafe menu served from opening to late night. Don't know the difference between Cabernet and Carmignano? Don't worry.
