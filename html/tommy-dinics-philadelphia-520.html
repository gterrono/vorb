    It's the BEST roast pork with broccoli rabe and provolone sandwich I have ever had!
    Being in Philly, everyone goes for the ubiquitous cheese steak. For those of us in the know, the Roast Pork sandwich is truly where its at! And what better place to get one of these trademark sandwiches, than Dinic's!
    I called ahead as I know this place can draw some massive lines, especially around lunchtime. I dial up Dinic's and whom else but the king of Roast Pork himself, Tommy Dinic, answers the phone and take my order. I order up half a dozen so I can bring some back to NY and share with my friends and family. Before hanging up he tells me, just come to the front of the line when you get here.
    Upon arriving at the Reading terminal Market, I see the line of at least 30 people deep waiting to order one of these heavenly creations.
    So I casually saunter to the front of the line, as if I own the place. I see Tommy. He looks up and says, "I got your sandwiches ready" I have to say I was a bit shocked and humbled that the King of Pork himself knew that I was the one whom placed this order.
    So of course I enjoy one of the delectable delights on my ride back to NY.
    The pork was tender, juicy, seasoned just right and cooked to perfection. The Broccoli rabe was soft and tender and had a slight kick of crushed red pepper. The Provolone was sharp and creamy and added just the right flavor to the pork and rabe.With all these flavors going on, you cannot forget the bread! I truly feel the roll is just about as important as what goes in it. Crusty outside that is a great complement to the warm meat and cheese, with a soft chewy middle, that absorbs all the pork and broccoli juices.  This sandwich is something that I have dreams about and it inspires me to know that someone can make something so unbelievably delicious as this magnificent creation!

    I'll see you soon Tommy!!

    And how could they? The sandwiches are sublime. I always get the same thing-- the roast pork sandwich with sharp provolone. I occasionally add in broccoli rabe, but not being a huge fan of this loudmouthed vegetable, I often forego it. The rolls are sturdy, but are not chewy, so they hold the innards of the sandwich well without making my teeth work too hard to rip chunks off. The pork itself is fantastic, roasted on premises with special spices. it's carved off of the roast right in front of you and then placed into the sandwich you are about to eat. Perfectly tasty. They add a ladle or two of the pork gravy to the top of the sandwich, which gives the pork a great juice and kind of seals the sandwich together. The cheese is not melted much at all, and it holds its shape well-- which is one of my favorite features of the roast pork sandwich. The flavor of the cheese makes the sandwich-- and it makes the sandwich pop! There is nothing better than biting into a DiNic's roast pork and getting a hunk of bread, a moist chunk of roast pork, and a biting bit of provolone cheese. My mouth waters just thinking about it. 6/5.
