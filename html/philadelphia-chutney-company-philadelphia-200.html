    There are a variety of dosas (traditional and Americana). Then, you get your choice of chutney w/ some seasonal varieties! I think I tried the strawberry one, which was too sweet and not flavorful / powerful enough for my dosas. I remember the mango one tasting really good.

    I may be a tad bit biased, but growing up with part of my family living in South India, I have a certain spice quota that needs to be met. That being said, having a South Indian place to eat Idli and Sambar for less than 4 bucks is more than I could ever ask for! Idlis are the steamed rice pattys that are mentioned above the samosas and gobi manchurian, that are a super quick and healthy snack on the go, or a meal if you have two orders! The critical component however is the quality of the Sambar, or the 'lentil soup' as its so often described. Sometimes Chutney Company is on point with their veggies//spices, but sometimes its a bit off the mark. Nevertheless, I will always re-route my path to make it to this place for a not-too-heavy taste of home!

    Mysore Masala Dosa - filled with potatoes and spicy filling - is also a nice large meal with sambar and chutney.

    I've been to the Philadelphia Chutney Company a few times, but am just getting around to writing a review. If you're looking for a quick and casual lunch, look no further.

    Food 4/5: I've had the #3 Mysore Masala Dosa, #6 Grilled Portobello Mushroom (with spinach, roasted onions and goat cheese), #A1 Samosas, and the vegetable biryani. The dosas are light, fluffy, and thin without being too soggy or too dry. They have just the right amount of filling, a variety of chutney options, and are served with sambar. The mysore masala dosa was savory, but lacked the spicy kick that I was looking for. The grilled portobello mushroom dosa, while certainly modern and different, was a departure from traditional dosas which isn't my cup of tea. However, props for fresh ingredients and a crepe-like meal. The vegetable biryani was lightly spicy (despite the request for maximum spiciness) with generous portions. The samosas were large, crispy and served warm with chutney. Overall, the food is good for the price and speed of service, but somewhat lacking in spice and full-bodied flavor that I admire in south Indian cooking.

    Atmosphere 3/5: Not particularly cozy or decorated, but clean and well kept. Oddly, I've dined here only during winter and early spring, and the draftiness of the place (from the frequent coming and going of customers through the doors) is noticeable.

    Service 4/5: Fast, friendly and energetic service. You order at the counter, pick it up when they call your order out loud (no table numbers or order numbers), bus your own table and wonder where to get a glass of water.

    Overall, great place for a quick Indian lunch, but the spice and flavor is slightly lacking.

Specialties

At the Chutney Co., we have a fun, Indian-fusion concept that we're taking to locations all around the city. Our Southern Indian fusion menu (which you'll see on our cool chalkboards in our bright blue, orange and green stores) is lighter and healthier than the heavy food most people associate with Indian cuisine. And on top of that, our food here is organically sourced, health-conscious, low fat, natural and 100% vegetarian.

History

Established in 2010.

In visionary cities like London and Paris, you can find a fast-food Indian boite bustling on every corner. But in the States, our take-outs options are restricted to only Mexican, Chinese, pizza or burger joints. The Philadelphia Chutney Company is looking to change that and bring Philly up to speed.

Meet the Business Owner

Nirav M. Business Owner

This project of passion was fueled by Nirav Mehta, a former technology entrepreneur/attorney with dreams of the dot-com era to create something special, and Baldev Singh, a former attorney whose chef dreams led him to open Aman's restaurants and eventually become the boisterous "cooking guy" behind the scenes at Chutney.
