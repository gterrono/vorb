    I have been here a couple of times.  I would highly suggest the buffet.  They have meat and vegetarian options.  The only negative would be that it's not spicy, but the staff will gladly give you spices to add.  

    The last time I was in, they didn't have the buffet, so I ordered from the regular menu. I sat at the bar, and some light crunchy bread (not naan) was brought to me right away.  My server was amazingly friendly.  He explained that the food in India is different from the north to the south and in between.  Being Indian, he told me a lot about life there and different spices that are used.  I was alone so it was cool to talk to someone while I grabbed a quick lunch.  I can tell that if you weren't up for a friendly chat, he would have left you alone.  

    I asked him what his favorite dish under vegetarian was, and he suggested an eggplant dish.  I forget the exact Indian name, but it's basically mashed up eggplant with some other veggies and sauce.  When he asked me how hot I wanted it, he suggested ordering medium because you can always add spice and you can't take it away which of course makes sense.  

    When it came out, it was good but nothing MIND BLOWING.  Again, it was good though.  I guess I prefer the buffet because how often do you find a good buffet where you get to try a little bit of everything.  

    When I wanted to add more spice, instead of some sort of sauce like I expected, he brought out actual tiny HOT peppers.  It was delicious, and added a lot to the meal.

    Overall, I will continue to go here.  The servers are so friendly, and the food is always good.  As far as price, some people talk about the prices being expensive.  I believe the buffet is about $11 for all you can eat and the entree I ordered was $12 (which included a side of rice.)  It's not a cheap spot, but it's on Chestnut, so the prices are what I expected.  I wouldn't say it's pricey.  I would suggest this place to anyone who maybe hasn't tried Indian food because they are very helpful and approachable.

    On Sunday night my lovely date and I decided to go out for some Indian as I have not had it in a while and had that urge for it. We decided on Karma as the reviews looked positive, and it afforded us a nice long walk from Washington Square to Old City.
    We weren't expecting a wait, and there was not one, but they did ask if we had a reservation anyway, a sign of professionalism in my book, especially in case we wanted to eat here again and had to make one.
    The restaurant was quite large for an Old City establishment, and had a very modern decor fused with Indian decoration. Not what I would call comfortable, but there was clearly thought that went into it and it was quite pleasing to the eye.
    We started off with an order of Samosas (my date somewhat embarrassed when I asked where the champagne and orange juice was - my idea of a bad joke) that were quite tasty - a nice battered outside that was crispy on all sides with plenty of flavor, and nice filling on the interior that was anything but bland which I find to be a problem with most Samosas.
    For our main courses we ordered Tandoori and Vindaloo with an order of Naan for dipping and scooping. The Tandoori was large in size with great visual and sound appeal - that sizzle along with the contrast of colors on the plate made for a great experience. If I could say one bad thing is that it was a tad dry, though I did not have a piece until later in the meal.
    My Vindaloo was good - had a good flavor, though if it were me, I would have had it spicier and with more meat. In retrospect I could have ordered it spicier, so I am not holding this against Karma by any means. Besides heat it did have a great variety of flavors and it was cooked quite well, not to mention it was plentiful in quantity.
    The naan came out with the meal, as asked, and definitely did a good job of being an edible scooper - flavorful, held its shape, and was nice and hot. It is a Naan I could definitely make a meal out of. The same went for the rice - it was plentiful and had its own distinct flavor; it was not there just as a filler.
    The reason I am giving this place 4 stars and not 5 is the service. The service was good; very attentive and quick, and definitely was service with a smile. I was also very impressed by their knowledge of the food being served. The negative part of it was that we had 3 people delivering our food, which made it confusing as to whose attention to attract when there was a question, or to ask for the bill. I understand this is more common especially in a busier environment, but it was not busy enough for this to be the case.
    Overall, Karma was a great experience, and I'd definitely revisit for some more delicious food and good service.

Specialties

The idea for Karma was born out of a simple premise of providing superior quality traditional Indian cuisine, great ambiance and friendly, efficient service.

Karma has worked exclusively with the local talent from Philadelphia:
- The restaurant was designed by Group G, a local architecture & design firm
- Artwork for the dining rooms was done by Shannon Presti of Doylestown
- Beth Gordon of Philadelphia painted the mural in the lounge

Great effort has gone into designing the menu that has a blend of the traditional to classic to nouvelle and has dishes from various regions of India.

History

Established in 2003.

Karma Restaurant & Bar was established in 2003 in Old City, Philadelphia with the goal of bringing a taste of Indian culture and cuisine to the steadily growing and buzzing Old City scene.
Since then, it has been a staple in Indian cuisine, drawing loyal customers from not only the Old City area, but from Center City, University City, surrounding neighborhoods in and around Philadelphia, as well as tourists from around the world.
