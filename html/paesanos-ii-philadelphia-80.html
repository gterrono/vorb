    Philadelphia was already the sandwich capital of the world. From the ubiquitous cheesesteaks to the roast pork, roast beef, Italian hoagies, bahn mi, and every other sandwich variety, you don't have to hard to find it done well. Little did I expect an upstart shop to make such an impressive.

    Paesano's first location in the Italian market didn't really work. They thought they could do a sit-down sandwich place, but it seemed many customers preferred takeout. So that building became an Italian BYO, Monsu. And Paesano's moved to the former home of Bebe's Barbecue, a short lived and much missed smokehouse.

    The new location is more what you'd expect in a sandwich shop. A high butchers table is in the center of the room, and a counter in the back. Chalkboard menus reveal the choices of fine sandwiches.

    The Arista is a brilliant evolution of a roast pork sandwich. The whole roasted pork has a different dimension than your average roast, and the rabe, long hot and sharp balance perfectly.

    The Paesano is incredible. The brisket was very tender, and the fried egg is an inspired touch, I've seen it with burgers before, but this is basically cheesesteak and eggs, taken to extremes.

    I wish they were open later, so I could pop in after work, or late night, but all the best sandwiches in Philly close early. At least they are open on the weekend so I can get there at all. Basically, if you love a good sandwich, or want a change of pace from the usual suspects, you need to go here.

    Listed in Sandwichpedia

    Bacon chocolate. Lobster donut. The spork.

    Here I had thought all of the greatest things in food had been invented. That is, until moving to Philadelphia to find that a sandwich heaven does exist, and it goes by the name of Paesano's. A trip to the Italian market is now just shy of water boarding if I can't get one of these sandwiches:

    *Arista --- A simple sandwich perfectly executed. Don't underestimate this because there is nothing secret or fancy about it, this is roast pork the right way.

    *Gustaio --- A unique experience. The lamb sausage isn't dry, the cherry mustarda provides a sweet tang to the tongue, and a rock star of cheeses - Gorgonzola - gets put to work in spread form. This is an entree on a roll.

    *Bolognese --- Without a doubt, one of the top 5 sandwiches in the city. You would expect crispy fried lasagna with an egg on a roll to be the equivalent of food porn, but it's not. It's carefully constructed and not the heavy, multi-layered mess you might expect. The thin layers of pasta are softly coated in bolognese sauce and lightly breaded and fried before getting in bed with mozzarella, sharp provolone, sweet peppers, and oh yes, a fried runny egg, OK, maybe it's soft core porn food, but the kind with the classy jazz music in the background.

    In short, my first born will be named Paesano. Sorry, future wife.

    Listed in Best Roast Pork

    I'll admit, I was skeptical when my Wife's friend said I had to try this place. Another great sandwich place in Philly, how many could there possibly be!?!?!  Why should I walk past Sarcone's and go any further down 9th for a hoagie? But wow, I am I glad I gave this place a shot, although beware, since The Food Network came to town, the lines can get long.

    I also have to admit that I much preferred the old spot. It had a warmer feeling and was more spacious, although I can tell in their new space the kitchen is a lot roomier. Now, while I still believe the Italian sandwiches at Sarcone's are better, what this place does have going for it are its awesome hot sandwiches and this is what all costumers should go for when they are here. If you are looking for roast pork, they have a great option, the Arista, delicious suckling moist roast pork that melts in your mouth with all the fixings and enough au jus to make the crusty bread extra special. I thought that the addition of long hots was a great idea. It does remind me more of an Italian style pulled pork than a more traditional sliced roast pork sandwich, and therefore it lacks the 'al dente' texture that properly cooked sliced pork can afford. So, while I am not sure its the best roast pork  in the city (see John's) it is totally delicious and the best I have had in the Italian market area.

    My favorite sandwich here is the Paesano. The moistest brisket with great flavor, salty sharp provolone, a little kick of horseradish and a touch of acidity from the pepperoncini peppers all topped with a fried egg. This must be the most well balanced sandwich I have ever tasted. Seriously, every ingredient makes the other better and the sandwich would just be missing something if it weren't there. But when it all comes together, its a flavor town explosion and makes you wonder why all sandwiches don't come topped with a fried egg.

    The lamb sausage is also good, its a little sweet with the fennel and the cherry mustrada. My wife enjoys the meatloaf sandwich (think meatball hoagie) and the next time I am going there, if I can pass up the Arista and the Paesano, I need to try the fried lasagna sandwich. The one thing I can say about this place is every sandwich tries to be about a well balanced flavor profile where each ingredient plays off the others.  I have to give this guy props for really upping the ante on Philly hoagies. These are really are gourmet sandwiches.

    TIP: Once I was there with a family and they made a pizza for the kids (not on the menu). Nice to know the place is family friendly. Also, they won't yell at you if you don't spit out your order in less then 3 seconds like other Philly institutions.

    Listed in The Highlight Reel, South Philly, Top Restaurants, The Places I Go Out, Sammiches!!!

    All hail the sandwich king!

    After years of burgers, cheesesteaks, hoagies, wraps, and then some; I can without question proclaim Paesano's to be the best place for sandwiches ever.

    When I first moved to the area, grabbing a sandwich from here was at the top of the to do list. And when I first set out to the older location (now Monsu), I made the unintentional mistake of getting there when they were minutes away from closing. Yeah, I was that guy - after years  of working in the food industry, I've grown to wish a mighty curse on that guy; and aimed to never be him. But Paesano's were perfectly accommodating with it, and made me an Italian hoagie in no time flat. The Italian is usually my least favorite of all hoagies. But when you're offered the chance in the Italian Market it's a fitting thing that you have to go with. And with the hearty bread, aged sharp provolone, mouth watering prosciutto, and then some; it managed to make me a fan.

    Since then I was able to move onto the ingredients I crave - like the lamb sausage and sun dried cherry mustard of the Gustaio; and the tender suckling pig and broccoli rabe of the Arista. Another plus is the side of Potato Arrosto; which might just be one of the best potato dishes I've ever indulged upon.

    Now that they moved further into the market it does get a lot more crowded (especially when they're on Food Network). But the area is fitting for them, and the location is a lot more suitable for a sandwich shop of its caliber. And since it still offers the same great sandwiches as always, its worth the extra wait.

Specialties

Come taste our distinctive Philly Style sandwiches with authentic Italian inspiration. Created by Chef Peter McAndrew's of his celebrated flagship Modo Mio Trattoria,  and Monsu' restaurants, Paesano's offers a robust culinary experience in a more casual setting. From house-made lamb sausage to Sicilian Style chic-pea pancakes our menu affords true Italian creation, but with Philly style gusto.

History

Established in 2010.

Tired of having to add  'gusto' to other sandwich shop offerings, Chef Peter McAndrews decided to open a shop of his own. With the help of partner Nathan Baynes, Paesano's quickly went from a hole-in-the-wall pretender to a multi-award winning contender. "It's like a dream come true," say both proud partners. "You start with this idea about how to make something better...then all the sudden boom! They say we're the Best of Philly, it's been very satisfying."
