    Listed in Best Places for Craft Beer

    Sitting at the corner of 10th and Spruce, this is one of my favorite neighborhood bars ever, I definitely make it a point to stop by here every time I come back to visit Philly.

    The kitchen is open late until 1 am, but the big draw for me is the constantly rotating draft beer list. With about 20 craft beers on tap, they range from local brews to different craft beers around the country, e.g. Oskar Blues from Colorado, Bell's from Michigan, Stone from California, etc. No folks, you won't find your ample selections of Bud Light, Miller Lite,  Keystone, MGD, and other crap beer here...well, okay, they might carry Coors Light, but at least they don't advertise it. The ultimate Cache-22 is what you have one night on tap might not be there tomorrow so be sure and drink up if you like it.

    Don't get me wrong, the food here is excellent. If in doubt, try the Duck Confit Chicken Wings, Truffled Mac 'n Cheese, Roasted Brussel Sprouts, or the Kobe Beef Sliders. Hell...bring some friends and try them all.

    Last but not least, Rich the bartender rocks!

    Listed in The Squid's Picks

    How do I love thee? Let me count the ways.....

    If you've read my other reviews, you know I've poured on the praise for other "favorite" establishments of mine. Well, Varga is the newest favorite on my list.

    Yeah, the bar's small. You just have to know when to go there, that's all. Their beer menu can easily hold its own against any other in the city.

    The one thing I love that they do with their beers is the way they divide them on the menu: Session light, strong light, session dark & strong dark. If you're a beer aficionado like me, you'll find it very cool and useful. It's the way beer should be organized. I haven't seen any other beer menu in the city like it, pure genius!

    Their outdoor seating in the summer is awesome. It's in a great location for people watching. It's on the northeast corner of 10th & Spruce, so you have a west-facing sidewalk along tenth and a southerly-facing sidewalk along Spruce. Just watch the position of the sun in the sky and sit accordingly. If it gets too hot, don't leave - go inside!

    It's clean and cute inside. They have a whole 50's pinup girl motif going on which is different and cool. The waitstaff are friendly and great. It doesn't hurt that the waitresses and barmaids are pretty attractive too.

    Did I forget the food? I love their menu. You cannot go wrong with the Kobe burger or the fish & chips. If you take nothing else away from this review: GET THE CHEESE PLATE. There are few things that make me happier than getting a selection of three cheeses paired with a delicious beer at Varga. It picks you up when you're down. It makes you a better person.

    My advice: GET THE CHEESE PLATE (and a strong dark beer).

    Varga, a restaurant led by Chef R. Evan Turney of Mercato and Valanni, is named after the 50s pinup girls that decorate its walls.  Varga is beautiful on the inside and out -- and its creative version of upscale pub fare is worth coming back for time and time again.

    Varga is not a large building but it makes good use of its space and makes its presence known at the peaceful corner spot that it sits on.  The inside is filled with a bunch of small tables and a couple larger ones and the outside is lined in small two and four top tables as well.  During the summer, the walls on both sides are completely open so that the summer breeze floats inside and the aromas of the fabulous food filter outside.  In addition to the posters of the classic pinups inside, the decor consists of a lot of black, white, and red bold prints including the black and white checkered floor, that make the atmosphere look classy yet not overdone.

    Not to mention, the menu is far from disappointing.  The portions are fair for the price that you pay although if you're anything like me, this is not a place that you could afford to feast at weekly.  If you go with a friend or a date, you could easily split three appetizer items and be fully satisfied.  There are also snack items including a $4 pot o pickles and a decent selection of cheeses.  I recommend the crab cheese fries; the steamed muscles with smoked chorizo in a fennel-garlic herb broth; and the kobe beef sliders which are served with bacon, shallots, and Vermont cheddar cheese.  Also worth trying are the grilled brussel sprouts which are topped with lemon, olive oil, and cheese.  If you are looking for a hardier entree, the smoked baby-back ribs with cornbread are delicious and filling.  Although I didn't try the dessert, they had a couple simple yet fun items that caught my eye.  Who doesn't love a rootbeer float or a bowl of ice cream with 3 different kinds of cereal sprinkled on top?

    With a classy yet fun atmosphere and such a positive vibe, good friendly service, and creative and satisfying menu items for big or small appetites, this restaurant is something that you should really try for yourself.  I don't see this restaurant going anywhere but up any time soon!

    When the veggie burger arrives at your table, you're struck with two thoughts:

    1. Wow, that looks good.

    2. Oh dear God, how am I going to eat that?

    In addition to being a spectacular feat of engineering, the burger is a Dagwoodian tower of deliciousness.

    Let's start with the most important part - the patty. Homemade and filled with corn, scallions and red bell peppers. I'm not quite sure what the base was (Rice? Oats? Does anyone know?), but it was tender, warm and toothsome.  

    The toppings lent a decidedly upscale flair to the burger - microgreens (a little awkward to eat), heirloom tomato, cheddar, cucumber slices (a little odd, but they added a brightness to the burger) and a truffle aioli that you will want to drink by the gallon (this coming from a girl who doesn't really care for mushrooms).

    The burger is messy -- truffle aioli will drip down your arms and cucumber slices and errant bits of microgreens will slip out onto your plate - but remarkably, the bun stays intact. I've said before that at most places, the bun is an afterthought but Varga Bar has the details covered.

    The burger usually comes with a salad of microgreens dressed in olive oil and white balsamic, but I opted for fries instead. Seriously, who the hell orders a burger and a salad?

    The substitution of was a great choice - the potatoes were skinny, crispy and well-seasoned. Making a good thing even better were the homemade ketchup (a sweet-spicy-smoky blend that surprisingly enough, actually tasted like tomatoes. Heinz better watch their back) and homemade beer mustard (spicy, sweet and something that should be sold on every store shelf).

    The only low point? My iced tea which was essentially hot tea with some ice cubes and a slice of lemon thrown in. I appreciate the effort to make a customer happy, but would much rather have been told they didn't have it in house.

    Service was prompt and friendly and while the spot is small, you have to appreciate any joint that festoons its walls and ceilings with images of voluptuous bombshells.

    Varga Bar is another strong contender for one of the best veggie burgers ever (in a city filled with strong contenders) and another argument against eating Boca Burgers ever again.
