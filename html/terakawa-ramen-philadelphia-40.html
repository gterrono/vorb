    Remember in school when you got a 78 on your biology exam and then it turned out that you weren't… Remember in school when you got a 78 on your biology exam and then it turned out that you weren't the only shmuck in the room who didn't study, so you ended up getting a B? Well, enter terakawa ramen. (and by the way, I'm not saying they're bad, but i would have probably given them a 3.5 but the handicap tiebreaker brings them to the magic 4).

    And you know how people refer to areas where it's hard to get access to fresh produce as food deserts? (yes, these areas unfortunately do exist in many parts of the country!). Well, Philadelphia is a ramen desert. If you've wanted ramen, the smart money said that you should grab a book (or tablet of choice), hop on a bus, and head to the big Apple. I'd say with the addition of terakawa, there is a reasonable alternative here in the city of brotherly love to satisfy a craving - but you'll probably still be making trips to new york.

    Terakawa falls in the vast no man's land between nom nom ramen and the ramen available in that other city. It's better than nom nom but some of you astute readers may wonder whether the standard is too low.

    Terakawa offers well flavored broth and pork at a reasonable price. The char sui is tender but not excessively fatty. Overall I was impressed but my small order of miso ramen came with a tiny sliver of pork. Now, they do offer a regular sized ramen bowl - and I will assume that they give more pork for a larger bowl - but given the portions for a small bowl, I am a bit skeptical of what they'd offer even for a larger serving. I ordered the pork buns too and that REALLY underwhelmed. The pork was not particularly well flavored, the buns didn't seem particularly fresh, and they added lettuce, cucumbers, and - a slice of tomato. The healthier side of the order detracted a bit from what I really wanted to taste. If there was anything to be added, I would have preferred an herb that could have complemented the pork.

    All in all, terakawa is okay. Well flavored but i hope they add more pork. Because the class is full of slackers, they get a 4 instead of a 3.5. They have nom nom (and the non existent philly ramen shops) to thank for that. The curve would definitely work against them if they were in LA.     Read more

    So it's winter and all I want to eat is a hot bowl of (usually Asian noodle) soup. We were in he neighborhood and decided to check out Terakawa because I haven't had ramen since I lived in Asia a few years ago and my friends had never tried it. We got there around 7 on a Friday night and it wasn't too busy but it filled up fast and there was constantly a wait throughout our meal.

    AMBIANCE
    The place is small, but very nicely decorated, they clearly put some thought into it, unlike a lot of the places in Chinatown. Intimate, cute, atmosphere. The menu is also small, which is nice because we didn't really know what to choose of the few options.

    SERVICE
    We ordered takoyaki,(deep fried octopus balls smothered in sauce) which came quickly and was delicious. I got the terakawa and my friends got the shoyu with chicken. I got my bowl first and waited awkwardly for my friends to get there's, then a bowl was almost put in front of my friend and then quickly whisked away. They came by to make sure they had ordered chicken, and then their bowls came out right away.

    FOOD
    I really liked the terakawa but the broth was a bit salty. The egg, pork, and crunchy veggies were really good though. The noodles were not what I expected-they tasted like thin wonton noodles, but they were good. I tasted the shoyu, and that broth was a bit salty too. Those noodles were like fancier ramen noodles that you would get in a pack at the grocery store. We were surprised at how filling it was compared to pho (I could normally eat a bowl of pho bigger than this without problems, but i struggled on the ramen). My friend's shoyu was missing an egg and she found a rogue piece of pork, leading us to believe that the earlier mixup was them taking the pork out and adding chicken. Not a big deal for her, but people who don't eat pork might have been bothered by it.

    They take credit card, but they don't take american express. They also have a customer survey, so hopefully they take customer feedback into account. Overall, I was pretty happy with my a filling noodle soup and appetizer dinner for $13 plus tax and tip.

    I will probably be back if I'm craving ramen, although I've been meaning to check out the other places, and I might be more likely to go for pho (cheaper, less salty).

    I was stationed in Japan 8 years ago near Hiroshima.  My fondest memories are having Japanese Curry (which is much different than Indian or Thai Curry) at CoCo's or one of the many curry houses in the area.  

    When I got back to the states I desperately looked to all the "japanese" restaurants in the area to satisfy my curry cravings, with most not having any idea what I was even talking about.  I ended up settling on S&B curry packages, but was never quite satisfied.

    So there I am in Yakatori Boy and finding myself once again unsatisfied at another "japanese" restaurant.  When I hop on Yelp and search "Japanese Curry Philly".  To my suprise, I see a review raving about the curry at "Terakawa Ramen"!  

    I stop eating immediately, ask for the tab and drag my girlfriend two streets down in hopes of finally satisfying my nearly decade long craving.  Sure enough, as soon as I walk in; the most delicious of aromas overwhelmed me in nostalgia and all the memories of Japan flooded back.

    The gyoza I ordered while I tried to decide on what to order was superb.  I settled on the Chicken Cutlet Curry and they even made it extra spicy for me!  I cannot begin to explain what a fulfilling experience it was once the perfectly cooked rice, potent curry and delicious chicken passed my lips.  Not to mention their authentic Japanese soda (that has the marble on top), Calpico (the original goes so well with the spicy food), and even Mochi ice cream!  

    Everything was perfect, I cannot recommend this restaurant more!  I will be returning often and dragging everyone I know there with the utmost enthusiasm!  

    Itadakimasu!

    From one business to another, Terakawa Ramen is miles better than its former self - "Ramen Boy". It's basically the same store front, same decor, even the same service staff so I have no idea what exactly happened to make this one a success and the last one a dismal failure but I can tell you that the menu has changed, the quality of the food has improved, the prices are great and there are so many more choices now and the food has taste and it tasted good.

    The menu is respectable and smart. A large variety of appetizers, ramen, curry platters and donburi, all the things that you can expect and would want to eat at a place like this. Instead of offering you different kinds of gyoza with all different kinds of sauces, they offer you one solid kind of gyoza with one solid gyoza sauce. Even if it's a bit ordinary but at least it's well executed and consistent. The pork in the pork buns here can benefit from a blow torch, the buns can be a bit smaller for a better bread to meat ratio, the tomato makes the spicy mayo a bit too runny but it's overall pretty good.

    Love the curry platters, it's what the former store missed. I also adore the gyu-don, rich taste of caramelized onion, the sweet soy dashi sauce along with the soft-boiled eggs makes the sticky rice, gooey-good. It's Asian soul food. The ramens here are much, much improved. I love their Terakawa signature ramen, the thin chewy noodles and the flavorful broth truly hits the spot. There are always special ramen which enriches the menu and for $9 a bowl, it makes me want to come back again and again to try everything they have.

    And even though I know I won't be able to eat more than one bowl, I find their kaedama option novel. For a small fee they will give you extra noodle to put in your bowl of soup. But if I'm that hungry, I would rather order another bowl of the whole thing.

    Unlike Ramen Boy, this is truly a place I can get behind.
