    The restaurant is very modern. The windows are lined with lights, and is, as very few restaurants are, Chinatown should be proud of. Tourists and locals are welcomed alike, and you do not need to speak a bit of Chinese to have a great time here.

    The food is good. It's much better than that of the Lan Zhou Hand Drawn Noodle House on Cherry and 10th (which, I think shares the same owner). The prices are about the same, maybe cheaper. The place is well lit, floors clean and tables equipped with tea (yay!) and basic Chinese sauces.

    Now, there's no point in comparing restaurants, and thus this review is not based on a conscious comparison.

    The portion sizes are decent. The rice was sticky enough. The meat was also chewy and flavorful enough. The tea, not hot enough. The noodles, however, are pretty top-notch. They have flavor within the noodle itself, and does not depend on the soup, which makes it so much more satisfying. Love it.

    Absolutely the best place in Chinatown to get hand-drawn or shaven noodles!

    My personal go-to dish here are the shaven noodles with beef tendon, but the menu offers a large enough variety of meat and noodle combinations to satisfy almost all preferences.

    Each bowl is pretty large, and you'll definitely be full after slurping down the freshly made noodles and rich broth. This is probably be the most authentic noodle dining experience you can get inside Philly Chinatown, and each bowl is only around $7!

    The restaurant itself is nothing much to talk about, but it's constantly packed with people, which should emphasize just how good this place really is. If you're very conscious about your personal space, be warned that it is pretty common to be placed at the same table as complete strangers. The servers will try to seat parties separately, but don't be surprised if you're asked to rub elbows with other parties when the restaurant is really packed.

    Cheap, authentic hand-drawn/shaven noodles. What more do you want?

    Quick tip: If you've only ever tried the traditional type of noodle, go ahead and try the shaven kind. It's a whole different kind of noodle-eating experience!

    Arguably one of my favorite meals in Philadelphia.

    Atmosphere: It's casual, loud during rush hours. Don't be surprised if you're seated at the same table as four other randoms or if you're seated 3 inches away from the stranger next to you. Service is surly, especially so if you're not speaking in Mandarin. Service is also fast and good though. One of those "you're here to eat, our job is to get you food and water" sort of wait staffs.

    Food: Love it all.

    I've almost always gotten the beef brisket noodle soup. It's pretty cheap, something like 6-7 dollars and you can expect a heaping bowl of soup meat, and noodles. I like to believe I have a huge appetite and I'm always satisfied after a bowl. Noodles are fresh and the differing textures of the sliced noodles make you never want to try the norm. The soup is salty, but flavorful. And the beef is incredibly tender.

    While I don't often get sides due to the heaping pile of noodles, soup and beef, consider ordering the spicy pig-ears. Yeah, it sounds weird. But amazing spice and flavor of these delights are great. Pig-ears also have the added bonus of being sliced - so they don't look like pig-ears.
