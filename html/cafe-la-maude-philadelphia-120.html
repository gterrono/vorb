    Me and a female friend tried to go to Honey's Sit and Eat a few doors down from here earlier today, but there was a wait so we settled for this nice looking place. I've been living in No Libs for a year now and I never even noticed this place before. I've really been missing out because La Maude is the truth.

    We were impressed from the second we walked in the door. I'm usually focused on the food rather than the decor but the whole place had a noticeably warm and friendly feel to it. Waitress was very nice. Once we saw the plates come out we knew we would be back. I got the marinated chicken sandwich with sauteed onions, arugula, tomato, pickles and a garlic aoli sauce. Sandwich was a 10/10. Great bread, fresh, high quality ingredients, and the aoli was the perfect compliment. My friend got the Ananas salad which has among other things sauteed shrimp and carmelized pineapple. She thought it was great and my samples of it were very tasty.

    All around, it was a great experience from start to finish. I thought the price of 30$ including tip was very reasonable too. I'll definitely be back to this local gem

    I went here for Sunday brunch with a couple of friends. We got there around 12:30pm. Parking around this time was really hard just because it's a residential area and there are a couple of brunch places in the area. Surprisingly, there was no wait for a table. It's an extremely small place. It's more of like a residential house turned into a café. We were seated in the back, and I asked my boyfriend to switch places with me because I was sitting directly under a fan. The waitress overheard and said she would turn down the fan.

    The menu is really small. On their website, and on yelp, I was expecting a pretty nice menu. It's a piece of paper folded in half with about 10 selections to choose from for food. I got the Genovese Omelet. My friends wanted to get some coffee, but didn't know what they wanted. The waitress asked what they were looking for. My boyfriend said he wanted something strong but not sweet. My friend said she wanted something not strong and sweet. She came back with these really cute lattes and cappuccinos. I may be mistaken about what they were, but they were pleasantly pleased with it.  

    The food came out pretty quick. The Genovese omelet was huge. I was not expecting that big of a portion. It was delicious. It was a big filled omelet with avocadoes, pesto, goat cheese, chicken sausages and tomatoes. It was so good, although I'm not a fan of goat cheese, that was the only bad part of it. It came with a skewer of fruits (watermelon, pineapple, cantaloupe) and a small side salad. I was soooo full after this meal. It was huge and totally worth the money.

    The place itself is really small, but the service is fantastic. We had 2 different waiters come up to us and ask if we needed anything or if they could do anything. Our water was always filled. The table is small though, I felt like for 3 people, the table was only meant for two.  The menu is a mix of Lebanese food and French food. They do a great job bringing the two cultures together.

    I'd come back again!

    I feel like Cafe La Maude is still relatively unknown.  How is this possible? How does it not get the attention that Honey's and Green Eggs get? I mean I LOVE the fact that there is no ridiculous hour long wait or lines of people waiting to get in for Sunday brunch, but I just don't get it.

    The food at Cafe La Maude is fantastic and unique.  Where else can you get hummus, eggs and chorizo all piled together in one dish? Where else can you get a croissant breakfast pizza-ish type thing? They also have sweeter dishes like the amazing key lime french toast. Everything on the menu looks so good that I feel like I must return many times to try it all.

    The space is a lot bigger than it looks, with a large back dining room in addition to the front room and the cute outside tables.  I love the decor, very chic.  And not cutesy-busted 'shabby chic', I mean beautifully decorated with beautiful (marble?) tiled floors. For some reason it took me years to check this place out, but I'm pretty sure I'll become a regular here.

Specialties

With the authentic look and feel of a true European café, this hidden gem in Philadelphia's Northern Liberties neighborhood serves up some of the most delicious food, coffee and pastries anywhere in the city. Café La Maude's specially brewed La Colombe coffee is known for its strong taste that's never bitter.  The French-Lebanese menu combines the exotic flavors of the Middle East with a distinctly Continental French accent and includes traditional favorites such as Brioche French Toast, Nicoise Salad, Taboule and Baklava along with more unique items such as the Grilled Haloumi, a baguette sandwich with olives, roasted tomatoes, mesculun greens and a basil-arugula aioli.  House specialties include the Café La Maude Salad of  arugula topped with warm seasoned chickpeas and fava beans.  Another popular dish is the traditional lebanese recipe of foul moudamas of marinated fava beans served with fresh tomatoes, chopped parsley and pita served with scrambled eggs. For meat lovers the cafe's marinated chicken sandwich and kebbe is not to be missed. Whether it's breakfast, lunch or brunch that brings you to Café La Maude, it will feel like Paris and taste like Beirut.
