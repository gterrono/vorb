        face it waiting for public transit can be so bad sometimes that it can really make you crave a drink while you do it. Especially when your dealing with institutions like SEPTA and Amtrack. fortunately that why Bridgewater's comes in pretty handy when your inside 30th street and your Amtrack train got delayed indefinitely for 2 hours.

        For a corner of a train station, Bridge water's manages to hold on to the traditional pub feel really well. it has the regular beers like Yuengling and Miller Lite, but you'll also find some good micro brews, as well as imports like Fullers and Belhaven. they have a good mix of liquor too.

        Tired and sick to death over the usual food options that you find in a train station - McDonalds, Taco Hell, Subway, etc? Bridgewater's changes it's menu on a monthly basis. you'll find usual entrees like Chicken wings and burgers. but you'll also find grouper sandwiches. wild boar, and Ghana stew.

        The only flaw to being in a station is that it closes on station hours (12 PM on weekdays + Sat, 9PM on Sun). but even though it's the perfect place to go to kill time while you wait, it stands enough on it's own just for the hell of it. at least you don't have to worry about driving after wards.

        No argument that this is the best food you'll find in 30th St station.  That said, I think Taco Bell lost that battle before it even began.

        The food here isn't outstanding, and is VERY meat heavy, but some of the items work pretty well and it might be as close as some people get to "adventurous eating."  One word of advice on that though: skip the gator bites.

        The beer selection is quite nice, if a little pricey.  The menu is rarely current but the chalkboard is usually not far off, and if you have a preference, the bartender can likely suggest something for you.

        The atmosphere suffers some from being in a train station and no doubt a significant portion of the people in there between 5-7pm are suffering some transit delays.  That said, it's still about the nicest environment you're going to find in 30th St.

        If you're stuck waiting on the express to *insert random East Coast destination* then you may as well pop in for a drink and a bite.  It's probably going to make your commute better if/when it finally happens.

        Like most other reviewers have said, this place is phenomenal BECAUSE it is in a damned train station. They do so much with the little space they have.  Here's the breakdown:

        FOOD: Incredible, incredible, incredible. Head chef Frederick Price is a genius. They have a good set menu, but the ever-changing specials menu is what keeps me coming back to eat at this place. The Shepherd's pie he makes around St. Patrick's day is a revelation. The man can do things to a burger I could only dream of. MUST TRY their Elk Burger. They had a Kangaroo burger once that was also incredible.

        DRINK: Extremely capable bar staff. They make great cocktails, and are all very knowledgeable about the beers they have on tap(which is ever changing), and the bottles they have in the fridge. If you want a Bud Light, go across the street to Slainte, because this place is for serious beer drinkers only. Also, don't expect to pay anything less than 4 dollars for any drink here. Don't let the prices fool you before you order though, because most of the time you get pints, not 12oz glasses. The only downside about their drink menu is that they do not have any happy hour deals for drinks, just for food.

        AMBIENCE: The hustle and bustle of the station makes for a good pub experience. This place is never quiet, and that's refreshing. Because of the small space, expect to leave here smelling like food, even if you aren't eating. The waitstaff is super-friendly, and this is actually a surprisingly nice place to bring a date.

        I wish I could give this place 6 stars.

      Specialties

      Bridgewater's is located inside 30th Street Station, and benefits from  "regulars" as well as first time travelers. The conversations overheard range from the sublime to the ridiculous.
      We offer seasonal menus and weekly specials that appeal to traditional tastes, vegetarians and the more adventurous.....have you ever tried Rocky Moutain Oysters, crocodile or ostrich?
      Our beer selections rotate every month and we offer a fine range of microbrews, German and Belgian beers.
      Come by, we'd love to meet you!

      History

      Established in 1998.

      Located inside 30th Street Station in Philadelphia, Bridgewater's Pub opened in December, 1998 and was designed by Architect Charles King of Philadelphia.  The Pub's name was taken from an1825 map of Philadelphia showing a 'Bridgewater Street' over which the train station was eventually built.  The back bar is a 1922 Brunswick style bar salvaged from a hotel in Lancaster county, PA.  It was purchased from Oley Valley Antiques, Adamstown, PA and refurbished by William Keim, Boyertown, PA.  The outer walls of the Pub are 80 year old oak and glass panels salvaged from a church in Maryland and have much of the original seeded glass.  The front bar, mahogany Irish/English style, the server station, and columns and beams were all designed and fabricated by Wallace and Heinz, Arcata, CA

      Meet the Business Owner

      Leslie S. Business Owner

      Since 1998 I've discovered that the best part of this business has two parts.  The first part are the customers whom I've come to know and love.  I've met some of the most interesting and amazing people and it all stems from just loving to hear their stories.  That's how friends are born.
      The second best part are my staff members.  They are more than employees to me, they are family.  Each one adds his or her own stamp to Bridgewater's with their personalities and talents.  They are an amazing group.
      And finally, I am Irish!  Not German.  But I have learned an incredible amount about German and Belgian beers from my German born bar manager.  Come and meet all of us next time you're in 30th Street Station.
