    Haven't been to a Continental in quit some time. I've only been to the one in Center City but the boyfriend & I wanted to try this place instead.

    The inside is much smaller but I would say they have more outdoor seating.
    We were seated inside very quickly and our waitress came and spoke with us not long after. It did not take her long to take our orders or for the food to come out but it did take some time to get the check.

    I don't remember the menu being so small and .. not interesting.

    We started off with HUMMUS and PITA: grilled pita, extra virgin olive oil and cucumbers which I thought was pretty good. They give you a lot of pita bread and they we nicely crispy and warm.

    I ordered the LOBSTER MAC n' CHEESE: orzo, gruyere and fontina. I remembered really really liking this the other times I went and would crave it sometimes but after this time, I don't think I will. Nothing against how they made it but my taste buds have probably changed. I am not a fan of orzo; but I really like the breadcrumbs they put on top.

    I don't know if we will be coming back. Their atmosphere is alright, our waitress was very friendly and I do really like their lights but right now we're not that interested; maybe if there was a new menu later on.

    This location is a little lacking. I think the midtown Continental is the ideal experience. I did enjoy the level of detail regarding the overall optics. Our server was dressed perfectly and everyone melded into the overall ambiance of the restaurant.

    In regards to the food, we got the BBQ chicken quesadilla, cheesesteak eggrolls, and crab pad Thai.

    If you've read reviews then you probably already understand that meals come out when ready. So it's not a three course environment but instead family-style of order, eat and share. This type of setup rarely works unless you are indeed a family ordering for children. Which are the sort of patrons this type of restaurant is not courting.

    Therefore we each ordered a meal we liked and one we could share. It resulted in one of us finishing a good ten minutes before the other as the pad Thai took longest to arrive.

    The pad Thai was the biggest problem of the night. It rates up there as one of the saltiest dishes I've ever ate. It was extremely difficult to enjoy. It was almost comically over salted, as if the cook resented making it.

    The cheesesteak was also on the salty side but it was slightly more expected.

    The BBQ chicken quesadillas was the best item we had, but they tasted almost exactly like a BBQ chicken pizza, and if you've had a really good version of that, then this would be nothing new.

    The Continental feels like an aging progenitor from which modern Starr restaurants, like Route 6 and Fette Sau (both of which I very much like) are descended. That said, I really had a nice experience here the other day when I stopped in for drinks, and I would definitely come back.

    I went with a cucumber mojito (which is sort of buried in the tome that is their drink list). It was really fantastic--fresh muddled cucumbers, a perfect amount of mint, etc. My wife got the gimlet, and she really enjoyed it, as well.

    I would have maybe gone with 3* here, except for the fact that the excellent service pushed it to a 4. Our bartender was so personable and pleasant, always smiling, that she made our experience above average.

    I didn't get a chance to try any of the food, but we saw a bunch of it come out of the window (which is right in front of the bar), and it looked really good, especially the lettuce wraps. The kitchen also looks pretty immaculate.

    All in all, a nice spot, even if not the most shining Starr in the family. If I find myself in the area again, I would definitely stop in for a drink and perhaps a bite to eat.

    I FINALLY made it to the Continental for actual food.  After so many missed opportunities due to nights dedicated to the liquid diet, I never made it in here.  I always had the impression that it was overly expensive and a little too yuppie for my liking... but it was neither.

    The wife and I arrived on a cold Wednesday night (free parking in Center City Philly on Wednesdays!) and the place wasn't too busy.  Unfortunately all the booths were taken so they sat us in the tables in the middle of the room.  It kinda sucked because the table was small, there wasn't much light, and people were brushing by us with their jackets.  Not a huge deal though as the food overshadowed the seating arrangement.

    Drinks.  Nice selection of specialty martinis and beers.  We both went for a mixed drink which average about $10, but that's what I come to expect for Olde City anyway.

    Food.  It's somewhat tapas style.  They have "snacks", "smalls" and "bigs."  We went with three smalls and one big to share.

    Lobster Mashed Potatoes:  I had my doubts on this one, but it was actually really delicious.  Lots of lump lobster mixed in.  Very sweet lobster.  I thought the gravy was going to ruin it, but it all worked out somehow.

    Grilled Thai Chicken Skewers : Basically grilled chicken on a stick.  Great thai peanut sauce.  It wasn't bad.

    Korean Pork Tacos:  These were great!  Fantastic flavors.  A touch of heat but not over the top.

    Seared Tuna:  This tuna was heavenly.  Served with mushroom risotto.  I didn't think those two would work together either, but it was really a nice dish.

    Overall we left full and satisfied.

    It's definitely worth checking out.  Try to get a seat in a booth or the back room away from the door.  It can get a bit drafty.

    After several visits to other Stephen Starr restaurants (both in Philly and elsewhere), I'm glad we finally made it over to his original hot spot, the Continental!  

    Everything's served up small plates-style, so my friend and I split the cheesesteak egg rolls, french onion soup dumplings, yellowfin tuna tacos, and a round of obligatory martinis.  Food was awesome but the portions were a tad small...our favorite by far was the Philly cheesesteak egg rolls, served piping hot, gooey, and with a spicy sriracha ketchup (...it shouldn't really fall under the "bigs" category, but whatever).

Specialties

This stainless steel 60's-style diner offers an exciting menu of global cuisine, including crab pad thai, cheesesteak eggrolls and lobster mashed potatoes. Famed for its sensational cocktails, the extensive bar features a wide variety of libations and over 15 types of martinis.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
