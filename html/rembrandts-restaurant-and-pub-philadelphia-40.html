    Rembrandt's is a wonderful Art Museum spot; surprised it took me so long to come here!

    This venue is versatile, sporting multiple floors. Downstairs, there's a beautiful dining room and casual bar area, plus sidewalk seating. Upstairs is as spacious as the rest of the place, with room for private parties.

    When I was here for a happy hour, the crowd was lively, and the staff welcoming. Bartender Liam worked fast, getting us whatever we needed. Shared a wee Margherita wood oven pizza (considering the price), decent, with a strong cheese. Tried their clam chowder (delicious), and munched on crackers.

    Sections of the restaurant are fancy enough for a first - or second - date, yet comfortable enough to be a regular neighborhood stop. In a nutshell: I'm a fan.

    It feels weird to give this place four stars because it's nothing spectacular on the surface.  But after living in the Art Museum district for a while, i have come to appreciate the consistency of this place.   I've eaten my way through most of the menu so here it is in no particular order:

    The Burgers:
    one of the best in the neighborhood.  Well, technically they have three burgers. Jack's has a good burger, too but overall the menu at  Rembrandt's Restaurant & Pub defeats Jack's based on variety alone.

    Rembrandt burger: with house-smoked bacon, cheddar, lettuce, tomato, carmelized onion.  the rembrandt will show you why they put their name on it. Order it with the house made aioli for a nice kick

    Lamb burger:
    It is what they say it is (feta, fennel, red onion, new jersey arugula with toasted paprika vinaigrette, kalamata olives, nine grain bread).  It's a bit dry for my taste, but I feel that way about every lamb burger.  Solid choice if that's you thing and nice that they offer it.

    The Rouge Burger: Wow...a bit heavy handed with ouse-smoked bacon, rogue smokey bleu, aioli, french fries, onion agrodulce, black pepper balsamic gastrique, fried egg.  My friend had it and devoured it.

    The Fries...great fries, fun presentation...(presentation is a theme here they really take care in how their food comes out which is one of the many pleasant surprises about this over achieving oasis)

    Tofu Bahn Mi...unless you really love these types of sandwiches i would avoid this...there;s just too many other good options.

    queso FUNdido: you can't spell spell this without spelling fun!  Yummy.  All the fun of nachos with better cheese that you control!

    Tortaa Cubano: homemade chorizo, smoked ham, swiss cheese, latin slaw, pickles, dijon mustard, pressed country roll. excellent...a lot of food.

    FLATBREADS and PIZZAS are the cornerstone of this great place...i havent snuck into the back but im sure they have some sort of fancy oven back there to chef up these delights.  perfect size to share (i'd have my eye on another app if you're sharing) or for one as a meal (with a take home box).

    The tuna, the soups the salads all fresh and well made.  Lots of specials.  GREAT STAFF on the floor and behind the bar.  The hostesses seem clueless at times, but are friendly.

    Alright Yelp until they pay me to do this I must go.

    And oh yeah, tell 'em Big Texas sent you!

Specialties

A friendly neighborhood bar and gastropub with banquet and private event facilities.

Rembrandt's has one of the most inviting sidewalk cafe's in the Fairmount neighborhood.

When not hosting private events, Rembrandt's provides a variety of entertainment - Quizzo on Tuesday nights is a big draw!.  Call us to schedule your private event or use our space to show off your (or your group's) talent.  We feature a great view of the Philadelphia Skyline from our upstairs banquet room and we are fully equipped for audio and video events.

And, of course, we offer casual dining in our romantic dining room, and the same menus are available at both of our turn of the century bars.

History

Established in 1985.

The brown bars and cafes of Amsterdam, the city that was home to Rembrandt van Rijn, are well known throughout Europe for their antique charm, warmth, inviting atmosphere, and friendly but stimulating conversation. Here at Rembrandt's in Philadelphia, a seventy-five year old tavern has been renovated in a manner reminiscent of the brown bars of Amsterdam. In fact, many of the interesting fixtures you will see in the bar and restaurant were discovered in Europe by the original owners of Rembrandt's, and their history may be of interest to you. Please browse our website for photos of our neighborhood gathering spot and of the people who come to enjoy our food, drink and companionship!
