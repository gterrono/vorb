    our food came out after a reasonable amount of time.  as is my custom, i cut the burger in half to facilitate stuffing it into my face.  unfortunately, my burger came out closer to well-done.  our server had no problem taking it back, which was just as well since it was also so slick with oil that the slab of foie gras slid off the patty seconds after it landed at our table.  my partner's burger was also way overcooked, but he was not as picky as me.  for $26, my burger better damn well be perfect.

    One of my favorite life-shortening gastropubs - so good that I generally go at least once every time I hit Philly.  

    Pros: anything your doctor wouldn't want you to go anywhere near - short rib cheddar fries, BBQ Pork Sammy, Wedge Salad, Kentucky Hot Brown, pickled Truffled Cauliflower.  

    Musts: the Whiskey King burger with seared foie is maybe my favorite burger in the country.  Bourbon glazed cipollinis, applewood bacon, blue cheese, and an absolutely ridiculous slab of seared foie on every perfectly cooked burger.  Order it with something from the extensive bourbon and rye list and you'll be joining me there on every Philly trip yourself.

    Cons: gotta go with at most 1 other person, because there are almost no three- and four-tops.  Place is tiny, but worth the wait in my estimation.  You'll be tempted to head to the Shake Shack down the street when you hear the wait - but don't do it.

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge, Bar None, Philly edition

    If there truly is a village of whiskey, please don't tell my boyfriend. He may just abandon Philadelphia and sail to that mystical locale. Hell, I might even go myself!

    Observe, in figure 1, the spectrum of medium rare:

    Fig. 1. Medium rare.
    |----------|

    In figure 2, the specificity with which the fine cooks at Village Whiskey cook a Whiskey King burger ordered to "medium rare":

    Fig. 2. Medium rare Whiskey King Burger.
    |-----*-----|

    In other words, the dudes at Village Whiskey know what the eff they're doing!

    I'm still high off the amazingness that was Village Whiskey as of this writing. Let me sing the praises of various other aspects of the Village Whiskey in list form:

    - They had Red Breast Irish whiskey, which for some reason is a rarity at other Center City venues.

    - The pickles(!) We went for the seasonal pickles, which at the time were radishes and onions. I WISH THEY SOLD THIS STUFF IN STORES. I couldn't stop munching on them!

    - The service. Excellent busboys, excellent waitresses. We were seated next to the bar, and all of the servers were so aware of the space. Don't worry about getting your drink knocked over; these guys are pros (but, y'know, don't deliberately TRY to make a mess!).

    - I know this place is called Village Whiskey, but don't let that stop you from ordering other drinks. YES, the selection of whiskey is great, but the bartenders make excellent cocktails as well, and the beer selection is full of gems. The Old Fashioned was well-balanced, yet you still got that whiskey flavor and strength you deserve. Same for the Negroni (except, with gin and Campari. Whiskey Village can proudly hold the title of "Best Negroni I have EVER had."

    Bottom line: Jose Garces's effort at an "all-American," "bar food-oriented" restaurant is not to be skipped over! Go. Gorge yourself on fat and protein for a night, and be merry!

Specialties

A ground-to-order Angus Beef Burger, a Veggie Burger, a Bison Burger or an Vienna All-Beef Hot Dog with all the trimmings are each served alongside duck fat French fries and Sly Fox cheddar sauce.  Burgers are topped with Village Whiskey's house-made thousand-island dressing, Bibb lettuce, beefsteak tomato and a potato-beer bun, with optional toppings including bacon, caramelized onion, truffles, artichokes and a selection of cheeses.

As one of the most comprehensive collections of its namesake spirit available in Philadelphia, Village Whiskey features a veritable library of 80 to 100 varieties of whiskey, bourbon, rye and scotch from Scotland, Canada, Ireland, United States and even a few Japanese options.

History

Established in 2009.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
