        Have you ever noticed there are very few restaurants in Center City where you can just walk in and get a table on a Friday or Saturday night without reservations?

        Pietros is one of those few gems where I can just walk in and get a table on any day of the week. The location can't be beat either! At 17th and Walnut, it's walking distance from pretty much everywhere in Center City.

        At Pietros, I always order the same thing: the medium pietros classic pizza, and the mista salad - basic foods, but delicious. The portions are overwhelming. At $22 the food is worth the price - especially given it's in the middle of center city. These two dishes could probably feed three or four easily. Normally I just get it for me and a date and then eat the leftovers for lunch the next day.

        I definitely recommend going here if you want a low key evening and don't have a reservation anywhere else. You can't really go wrong with Peitros pizza!

        Listed in Pizzas... Neapolitan or not...

        Pietro's is the casual place for pizza and pasta, definitely on the informal side with emphasis on family style Italian food. We called ahead for a party of 7 at 700pm and they were able to accommodate us without any problems...

        You know that this is a place for a family oriented meal when the table is a round table instead of a couple of smaller square tables combined to accommodate our party size. My wife and I were the guests of her coworker and his family who works in the Philadelphia regional office. They know this place well and recommended it...

        Since this place has family sized portions as well as individual sized portions, we started out with a large family sized portion of Insalata Mista, their mixed green salad with grape tomatoes, carrots, herbs, katamala olives, and balsamic vinegar. Along with a bottle of Napa wine, a Seven Stones Cab, we were doing just fine for starters...

        Everyone ordered their own entrees, and although this place is known for their stone fired pizzas, I preferred the Chicken Piccante that was offered as a special for the evening. It consisted of a sauteed chicken breast, rough chopped Roma tomatoes, capers, in a lemon wine sauce, dusted with chopped parsley and garnished with parsley and a lemon slice. Whereas the taste of the lemon and wine were there, the sauce was a little thinner than I like with a paccante and the chicken breast was pounded and sliced up before it was cooked when I would have preferred it whole. I'm guessing that the wine was added late over the top and not reduced down in the saute pan. In fact, you could taste the contribution of each component separately, the wine, the lemon, the capers, the tomatoes, but they didn't meld together. To this dish, there was a rather garishly large side of penne pasta in tomato sauce which was larger than the entree. I would have actually preferred to have my Chicken Piccante served over a bed of penne, I think that would have been a more elegant way to go...

        My better half ordered the Frutti di Mare, the fra Diavolo with linguine. It had a generous portion of seafood including salmon, shrimp, calamari, clams, and mussels. The marinara sauce had good character to it, was rich and hearty with a gentle kick to it, the linguine was perfectly al dente. It was dusted and garnished similarly to my chicken dish with chopped parsley, a parsley sprig and a lemon slice. I thought her dish was the object of my envy...

        Somebody else in our party ordered a stone fired signature margherita pizza pie. From where I was sitting it looked really good, the edge of the crust looked pillowy crisp just as I like it. I should have went for one of these...

        The service all evening was impeccable. The hostess handled our reservation without incident and seated us after a momentary delay to alert the wait staff. Our waiter Sokol was attentive and polite. Our water glasses were kept full and our dishes were bussed in a timely fashion...

        So aside from my own poor choice of entree, the evening went pretty well. I would go along with the 3.5 star rating and I'm comfortable with this knowing that this was probably a one time visit to this establishment, but I would encourage anyone who wants to try their pizza or family style pasta to come out and give this place a try...
