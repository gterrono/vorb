    I never write reviews, but I had such a great experience during my last brunch at Parc that I felt it was time to start. I've been there twice for brunch and a few times for a light dinner and drinks, and I have to say, it gets better every time. The food was always excellent, especially for dinner. The atmosphere for brunch looking toward the park is unrivalled in the city, in my opinion. Steven Starr's design team nailed the Parisian art nouveau look inside, right down to the tiles. To be 100% honest, you can find better french food at places like the Fountain Room, etc, but don't get me wrong, Parc's food is exemplary, and I think the originality of their atmosphere (in philly anyway) trumps any of their competition. Most importantly, however, their service is impeccable. Be advised that the pace of the food delivery is slower than your typical Steven Starr restaurant, but the staff is fantastic. They were MORE than accommodating on my last visit. If you want to impress someone with a romantic brunch, I honestly can't think of a better place in the city to do so.

    Overall A/A- and we will definitely be back.

    I had lunch with a friend here just yesterday based on her mother's recommendation.  We wanted a place where we could meet for the day (she was driving from Bethlehem and I was driving in from DC) and linger over coffee or a meal without feeling rushed by staff, much like one would at a Paris brasserie.  Parc fit our needs to a T!

    The menu selection was authentically French.  Although the Onion Soup Gratin wasn't the best I had, the flavor was hearty, and the cheese was perfectly melted and burnt and crisp... I can still hear the crunch!  The Warm Shrimp Salad was the perfect accompaniment to my soup.  Plump shrimp, shaved fennel, and buttery smooth (but not overbearing) dressing with a hint of citrus made for a refreshing combination.  My friend's Quiche Lorraine was fluffy and flavorful.  She noted that it was a huge (read: American sized) portion, and that was enough to fill her up for lunch.

    For dessert, the Apple Tarte was well prepared.  I opted for the Brown Butter Ice Cream in lieu of the usual Vanilla.  Perfect pairing!  My friend's Pear Financier sounded better on paper than it actually tasted.

    The atmosphere and decor brought back very fond memories of past trips to Paris.  It's clear to me that so much thought was put into the details - from tiles, to faucet handles, to light fixtures... it was a transporting experience.  The only thing *not* so Parisian was the attentive staff.  Instead of the laissez-faire attitude I've come to expect from French waitstaff, we were doted on by our server, the gentleman refilling our water glasses, and the busboys clearing our plates.

    The next time I find myself in Philly, I'll be sure to enjoy another meal at Parc.

Specialties

This stylish addition to Rittenhouse Square pays tribute to French cafe culture, serving breakfast, lunch and dinner seven days a week. Ideal for a quick bite or a leisurely meal, the menu is filled with classics: onion soup, escargots, steak frites and towering plateaus de fruits de mer. The baguettes are made in-house and  the wine list is expansive. Sip a Kir Royale at the zinc bar or enjoy a cafe au lait while basking in the sun.
