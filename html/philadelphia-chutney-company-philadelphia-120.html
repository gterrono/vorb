    Dosas done right. A unique selection, but definitely not complex. In a world of crazy flavor combinations, in this post Jose Andres' minibar-world in which even salmon and cotton candy can go together, dosas have the potential to be wild mixes of sweet and savory.

    It's definitely not traditional, but if there's anything American city-dwellers like more than ethnic cuisine, it's ethnic cuisine converted for their Western palates. Unless you're a purist, Philadelphia Chutney Company is the right price and the right size for a filling meal. Complementing a dosa with the sambar looked to be a good decision, and I've read overall positive reviews of that addition.

    In reality, this is a 3.5 star review. I'm giving the Chutney Company the benefit of the doubt because I don't think I chose the right dosa on my visit. On a general note, however, they could definitely afford to fill their dosa wraps with a little more food. The staff is familiar with their menu and very kind, especially to newcomers like myself who ask embarrassing questions like, "What's sambar?"

    I've been here twice now and am a big fan of the dosas. They are really unique and make quite the first impression when you see how big they are! Granted the crepe portion overhangs the actual filling but still for ~$7 they are a great value for the Rittenhouse area.

Specialties

At the Chutney Co., we have a fun, Indian-fusion concept that we're taking to locations all around the city. Our Southern Indian fusion menu (which you'll see on our cool chalkboards in our bright blue, orange and green stores) is lighter and healthier than the heavy food most people associate with Indian cuisine. And on top of that, our food here is organically sourced, health-conscious, low fat, natural and 100% vegetarian.

History

Established in 2010.

In visionary cities like London and Paris, you can find a fast-food Indian boite bustling on every corner. But in the States, our take-outs options are restricted to only Mexican, Chinese, pizza or burger joints. The Philadelphia Chutney Company is looking to change that and bring Philly up to speed.

Meet the Business Owner

Nirav M. Business Owner

This project of passion was fueled by Nirav Mehta, a former technology entrepreneur/attorney with dreams of the dot-com era to create something special, and Baldev Singh, a former attorney whose chef dreams led him to open Aman's restaurants and eventually become the boisterous "cooking guy" behind the scenes at Chutney.
