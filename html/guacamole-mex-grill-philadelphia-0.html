    Oh my god!! I had lunch from Guacamole today, a vegetarian gordita, and it was so so so good. These were two homemade corn tortillas layered with refried beans, shredded leaf lettuce, roasted vegetables, tomatoes, queso fresco, sour cream, and a raw red onions. The roasted vegetables were coated in some kind of magical orange sauce, and they were beyond incredible. Sometimes vegetarian food is just normal food without meat, and sure, that can be fine, but sometimes I feel like I'm missing out on the delicious and extra flavorful meat things that omnivores get. This gordita platter had a special touch with the marinated vegetables, and I loved it. It was so good that even though I hate eggplant, I ate a piece of the roasted eggplant unbuffered (!) by the tortilla and fillings when it slipped out while I was eating. It was delicious (what magic do they do in the kitchen?!). The other roasted vegetables were yellow squash and red onion, I think, and all of the flavors & layers worked together so well. It was one of the best things I've eaten in a while, especially for $5.

    I can't wait to go again and try more dishes. As others said, they just opened this week, so they're still getting things together (a sign on the door said cash only for now), but it was very cheerful and clean inside. The man who took my order was genuinely very friendly & courteous, and he mentioned they'd be starting delivery sometime in the future, which would be close to revolutionary for West Philly. I would never have to leave my apartment again... yummmm...

    Listed in Living the cheap life

    Until now, I had to go over the Grays Ferry bridge down to South Philly to get good Mexican food. I am so thrilled to say that's no longer the case. i went to Guacamole Mex-Grill for the first time this week with a date and we were both VERY happy with our meal. It's a new place but it seems to be doing well so far -- and after I ate there, I'm not surprised.

    First of all, the food: we both ordered the pollo tacos, and a side of guacamole, which came with free chips. You have a choice of mild or hot salsa - I chose the hot, which was incredibly flavorful and not too too hot. The chicken was delicious - perfectly spiced, perfectly seasoned, and really well cooked. Their tacos come three to an order (for $6) and the portions are *generous* - I just barely finished mine! My dining companion remarked that he was extremely full when he polished off his order, notable as he's frequently left still hungry after three tacos. The guacamole was also extremely delicious and well-made, with the perfect sharp bite of lime. The chips were great as well, your standard delicious freshly made tortilla chips. I also appreciate the gesture of the free chips.

    That's a good segue to the service at this place, which was excellent overall. It's family run and it shows - our server was sweet, thoughtful and really went out of her way to keep us happy. The other server was bantering playfully with some USP students who were eating a nearby table. There's really just a good atmosphere to the place, despite the slightly fluorescent lighting. I can't wait to go back and try the mole! I'll be sure to update you all.

    Listed in A Gringo's guide to Mexican in the Philly area

    I venture into new Mexican places with a degree of trepidation, since most people know diddly about good Mexican food. But since there hasn't been any Mexican joint worth speaking of west of the Schuykill, I was eager to try it.

    The chips and salsa were pretty decent. The salsa was more like the El Jarocho red salsa crema, flavorful and spicy without being too hot. The guacamole we ordered was also pretty decent. I was annoyed at having to pay for chips and salsa, but at least it wasn't too expensive. I would like to have gotten more than a small side sauce container full of sauce though.

    My g/f got a vegetarian torta and I got enchiladas verdes with chicken. The chicken was pretty moist and of decent quality. The verdes sauce however was pretty lifeless. I couldn't taste the tang of the tomatillos, nor much cilantro, garlic, or onion for that matter. There wasn't an aluminum  or metal tang to the sauce, so it probably wasn't made from canned tomatillos. It was probably just a sauce kept in the refrigerator a little too long. My g/f's torta was full of vegetables and queso fresco cheese. She liked it, though I wasn't that impressed from the bite or two that I had.

    The churros were hot and seemed like they had been made recently. Delicious. The sopapilla like tortilla with cinnamon and sugar was also decent, though a little overpowdered with sugar and cinnamon.

    The atmosphere was pretty warm and inviting. There were a couple of tables to sit if you wanted to to dine in, though I could imagine it getting packed r.eal quickly on a weekend night.

    The service was pretty good overall and the owner was pretty nice. He apologized for not having horchata, but said that they would have it soon.

    Overall I wasn't that impressed, but I think maybe it was just an off night for them based on the previous reviews and the smells emanating from the other peoples plates. The tacos that the two drunk guys behind me had smelled delicious. I'll probably be back to give it another go.

Specialties

Guacamole Mex Grill specializes in authentic Mexican cuisine from the Central, Northern and Gulf Coast regions of Mexico as well as a variety of Tex Mex dishes. Tacos on homemade flour tortillas, tortas, enchiladas, empanadas, burritos,taco salads, quesadillas are on the menu daily.  We also have specialty dishes Plato a la Tampiquena, Cochinita Pibil, Plato Alambre, Fajitas and Pollo con Mole.

History

Established in 2011.

The de Luna family migrated to Philadelphia from Houston TX in 2003 with dreams of one day opening a Mexican restaurant.  Beatriz de Luna's family has been in the mole business for over 50 years in Jilotepec, Mexico and it was her dream to introduce her family's mole to the US. In 2006 the de Luna family purchased the building where their future restaurant would be established and after a few years of planning, saving and renovating, Guacamole Mex Grill was born.  The restaurant is managed daily by Beatriz de Luna and her son Rudy de Luna.  It's a family business so everyone (Rafael de Luna Jr. (Husband & El Jefe), Alla de Luna (Daughter In Law) & Anna de Luna (Grand Daughter) from time to time have to pitch in.  The family restaurant was designed by her son Rudy and constructed by her two oldest sons, Rafael de Luna III & Ronaldo de Luna.

Meet the Manager

Rudy D. Manager

Rodolfo "Rudy" de Luna is the General Manager and responsible for all restaurant activities.   Rudy ensures that all activities are consistent with and supportive of the family's vision and mission, to provide great tasting quality Mexican food at affordable prices.
