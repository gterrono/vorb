    10/11/2009

    After an abhorrent experience at the parking garage next to the Walnut St. Theater, we wouldn't have made it anywhere on time.  Luckily, Jones doesn't accept reservations, and we were able to walk right in at almost 6 on a Sunday evening and sit down without any wait time.

    At first glance, the menu choices were exciting.  We were seated right in front of the fireplace, and reading the list of comfort food: mac and cheese, pierogies, meatloaf...  well it all made me long for my jammies and a good book.

    We were served quickly, and well... I mean, it was good... but it tasted a little too much like home.  There was no spin that made Jones' macaroni and cheese any different than what I might make in my own kitchen.  The pierogies tasted akin to Mrs. T's, which again, I love, but for $7.50 I could buy a couple boxes and end up with way more than the 4 pieces I was served.

    Maybe it's just me, but when I go out to eat, I want to be served something I could not make myself.  I get the idea of comfort food, but I think the food at Jones is just a little TOO familiar.  I stared longingly out the window at Chifa and couldn't help but wonder what the meatloaf might have tasted like with some kind of crazy emulsion or foam dolloped on top.

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
