    Am writing a mixed review of Silk City, not because I am nostalgic for the old Silk City which had character, yet horrible food.

    Have now been to both dinner and brunch at Silk City and had the same experience - decent food, great selection, great ambiance and vibe and awful service.

    The food is not THAT great, but it's good and the menu is diverse enough that it can suit anyone's culinary restrictions. There are many vegetarian options and the ingredients seem fresh and good, though slightly boring.

    But the service is terrible. They must hire for hipster appearance and attitude and forget that serving food is their primary responsibility. Am not really a diva about service, but on a really hot day sitting outside, my water glass sat empty for most of my meal and the waitress spilled coffee on me with no apology. At dinner, our waiter was just downright rude.

    The dance section can get quite crowded, but hearing DJ Deejay is well worth a trip there. People actually dance, which is nice, and the clientele is not too creepy.

    Bottom line, worth going to for sure, but don't expect to be blown away.

    The Silk City Diner is a spot I visited years and years ago and enjoyed because of the chocolaty deliciousness of their milkshakes. I remember it as a standard diner - nothing out of the ordinary. I was disappointed to hear reports of it's closure. I was gladdened by it's reopening. I needed to see if the place was any different. The change in decor is minimal; the change in menu is drastic.

    The interior of the diner looks the same to me. This is an awesome thing. It's like walking back into the 50s. (As far as I know. I have never actually been to the 50s.) The  booths are spacious with zany designs on the table tops. The staff is super friendly and fast both taking the order and serving the food. (Also, they are super hot. That might only matter to the pervs out there. Oh. And me.)

    You won't find a dense menu filled with traditional diner fare here. They have just a few items. Stuff like portobello burgers, seared tuna sandwiches with wasabi mayo and appetizers like edamame and blackened fish tacos.  The  edamame is  delicious . It comes with a dipping sauce that was a bit too spicy for my tastes. For my meal I skipped the fancy items and went with a more traditional burger. Their burger simply with cheese is one of the best I ever had. Dinner was followed up by warm brownie with a dollop of ice cream topped with a bit of rasberry and vanilla glazes. Amazingly good.

    I ate inside, but I peered through the gate outside into the beer garden and that looks great as well. A full bar and plenty of space to spread out. Next time I will try eating out there.

    I think this place has something for everyone. Foodies will love all the fancy stuff on the menu (and they all sound delightful.), there's the standard burger if you aren't feeling adventurous, there's the decor for the nostalgia minded, the awesome desserts for the sweet tooth, the beer garden for the alcoholics, and of course, the pervs. If you can't enjoy a place with so much to offer I think you are just too picky.

    A couple of years ago Silk City overhauled its interior and menu, and what used to be an relatively unique 50's inspired diner and alt-rock venue has become a merely ordinarily fairly cool nightclub. Different groups of friends with whom I've gone aver that it's a mixed but definitely interesting experience.

    The blacklit interior is always populated with a colorfully motley cross-section of the night's revelers. Conveniently, the  diner's open the entire night, and since it connects directly to the dance club, I've often grabbed my Friday eve dinner there if I meant to stay for the scheduled party. They sport a good variety of dishes, from vegetarian to meats, and plenty of unique domestics and imports are in stock.  

    A word of caution: check their calendar for the DJ on that eve, since the range of styles they'll schedule is pretty broad and varied, and their pick can really make or break your night. Once we caught a hip-hop DJ who barely woke us up; another night, the 80s DJ spun a very fun set. Sunday night picks are truly unique, and at times the gleefully pumped partiers transition into after-hours clubs. Overall, it's a spot that my friends and I revisit regularly.
