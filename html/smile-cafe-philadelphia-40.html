    I have to give them 3 stars because they allowed us to keep our reservation of 10 even though half of my party didn't show up until 15 minutes after the reservation and the other half about and hour after the reservation (I was on time). I really appreciated that.

    That being said. I have to say that I had the best experience here out of all my guests. It could be because the staff appreciated that I was on time so they gave me the best tasting food or it could be that I just ordered the best tasting dish out of all my guests.

    I ordered the Crispy Shrimp Roll and the Pad Sew Ewe. They give you 5 shrimp rolls for $4.95 and they are pretty good. It's basically a piece of shrimp wrapped in batter with duck sauce on the side. But I liked it. I was expecting one or two rolls, but I definitely can't complain with 5. The Pad Sew Ewe was delicious. It was very very filling so I could barely even eat half my plate. It was a good decision on my part, though I admit that I stayed away from the shrimp on my plate since the rest of my party said the shrimp tasted too fishy.

    Now, 4 of my friends got regular Pad Thai. It was way too red, and looked and tasted like it had been made with Heinz Ketchup (their words, not mine). So that was a bad choice. They all said it was disgusting.

    Another friend got the spicy fried rice. I'm glad I didn't get that, since my thinking was you can easily make fried rice (and add kimchi to make it spicy) at home, so why get it in a restaurant. Anyway, she said it was meh at best. The shrimp was too fishy and the rest wasn't memorable.

    Lastly, my friend got the Spicy Noodle. Now she is a pescatarian, so she specifically told the waiter at least 3 times to take out the chicken. He even wrote it down and repeated it twice when he repeated our order. So somehow the kitchen just didn't get the memo because the food came out with chicken. She had to go back there and make sure they just didn't take the chicken out and instead made a new plate. When she received the new plate, she found it watery and bland. She finished it, but that was because she was famished.

    They also forgot to make one of our dishes, but by that time everyone was so frustrated that they just told them to cancel the order.

    So basically everyone was disappointed but me. :)

    My food was great and they kept the water coming so overall I personally found the experience ok. Not memorable but not disastrous (for me). I wish my friends had told me it was BYOB, because I definitely would have brought wine to better the experience. Ah, well.

    I give it a 3 but everyone else probably would have given them a 1.

I'd order from here again, but really only because there isn't anywhere else that delivers for free. It just wasn't particularly memorable. But if you're in the mood for Thai delivery I wouldn't count Smile Cafe out.
