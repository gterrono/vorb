    We had such a great dinner the first time around at Rosa Blanca that we reserved ahead for dinner during restaurant week, and we had a $50 gift certificate for any Garces restaurant and were psyched to use it here. Sadly, the second time around didn't live up to the first..

    I should start by saying that we actually forgot the gift card at home, and they were nice enough to give me a free drink at the bar while my fiance ran home to get it. I wish I could rate the service, because really, it was five stars all around. But, I'm a foodie, and I'm here to rate the dinner, so..

    $35 prix fixe is no cheap deal for restaurant week, and we expected that any nice restaurant would go out of its way to make it feel like a special meal, but unfortunately that was not our experience. They did have a great selection from the regular menu, and we ordered the octopus and croquettes as appetizers. The croquettes were as delicious as remembered, and the octopus was very good - but very small. Okay, whatever, we supposedly still had a large dinner coming, right?

    For entrees we got the fish of the day, which was black sea bass (which I normally love) and a "fried pork" dish (can't remember the name). For sides we got the black beans and rice and sauteed spinach. I'm not sure if we just ordered poorly, but we were totally disappointed with the food - from the fish dish, which was overcooked and the sauce was pretty bland, to the pork dish, which turned out to be cubed pork that wasn't hot or crispy.. and then even the sauteed spinach was full of "spinach water" and yes, even the black beans and rice, which were amazing before, were just kind of bland.

    Maybe it was chef that night, or as I read other reviews.. I suspect it's restaurant week.. but we were so disappointed with the food quality AND quantity (my fiance left hungry), and then the bill came - we each had one drink, bill totaled $95. We were sad to find that we spent the same amount of money during a normal night for THREE people as we had spent on a mediocre dinner for two.

    And, of course - there was dessert. Now, we're not dessert people, but these were some of the most uninspired desserts I even tasted. Not even any chocolate!! Meh. We feel like we wasted our gift certificate and won't be rushing back anytime soon.

    As the newest addition to the Garces crew, I had reasonably high expectations for Rosa Blanca. And… As the newest addition to the Garces crew, I had reasonably high expectations for Rosa Blanca. And it lived up to every one of them. The three of us enjoyed every bit of our dinner .. we got a bunch of things and shared everything. Here's the rundown:

    We got two soups - the Guiso de Maiz and the "Lentejas" or lentil soup - I would say both were delicious but that the lentil was the big hit. It had a smoky, full flavor and was a good sized portion - big enough to be satisying, but not too big (since lentil soup can be very filling).

    Then we had a few appetizers - the veal tongue, fish ceviche, and ham croquettes. All were pretty great - I would say the veal tongue and ham croquettes were particularly awesome.

    Finally, we had the Pollo A La Brasa (the half chicken smaller size) and the Curried Lamb Stew. These were really the highlights of the meal. We all agreed that the entrees were even better than everything else we had, even though everything was really great. We got 4 sides with our entrees - black beans, fried cheese, sweet fried plantains, and "Tamal en Cazuela" - which was a pureed corn dish (this was the only thing we got that we thought was a little strange, not great).

    All in all, a great meal. The three of us were stuffed and took home a few things. With one drink each (2 of us got mojitos and the other got "La Tormenta", all amazing) the bill was only $113.

    As I told the hosts on the way out, if they keep this up they'll do great. And I'm psyched to have yet another wonderful Garces option.. especially one that's open late night!     Read more

    Disclaimer: This is a restaurant week review...

    With that caveat, Rosa Blanca's service definitely disappointed our party of five this past Friday evening. Our reservations were at 9:45p and we unhappily left our [full] drinks behind at the bar across the street in order to be at the restaurant on-time for our reservation. The hostess was very welcoming and immediately took our coats; we were then told our table was not ready as there were "campers" currently at our table. Ten minutes later and our table was still not ready and the inviting hostess wanted nothing to do with us, dodging eye contact but eventually replying to our questions with "I'm not sure." We moved from the hostess stand to the "bar area" - a glorified diner counter in order to free up space for other people waiting.

    Our friend from New York asked the bartender if we could have a round of drinks on the house to compensate for the fact we'd left full drinks in order to arrive on-time and our table was still not ready 20 minutes later. Management had to be asked but they obliged and we were treated to VERY yummy drinks (Mojito Temporado, Ah Fuego with gin and a margarita). However, while watching our drinks being made, I noticed the female bartender continuously made snide remarks about her customers to the bartender making our drinks - this is definitely off-putting, no customer wants to witness waitstaff mocking and making fun of other paying customers. What happened to "the customer is always right"?! After our drinks were served our table was finally ready, 25 minutes after our reservation time.

    We were seated at a booth, in the main dining room - really cool atmosphere but it is very loud. The acoustics in the restaurant are great for live music but I don't particularly want to listen to 4 different tables' conversations. Don't get me wrong, I love lively restaurants, but I could not hear the person seated across from me. Given it was restaurant week we had limited menu choices, but we were all happy with the selection by Garces for this special promotion.

    Food: The octopus appetizer was good but very small, as comparison Tria Taproom gives you 3x the octopus for the same price. For the main course I ordered the Churrasco (skirt steak with chimichurri), it was a decent sized steak for restaurant week but the side was fries - would have liked a more authentic side dish than fries. Also, the chimichurri sauce was very sweet, definitely not like the authentic stuff I had in South America or other Latin restaurants.

    Considering all of the above, I think Rosa Blanca warrants another trip because despite front of the house communication issues, our server was attentive and our food (given it was restaurant week) was yummy and no one left in need of "second dinner."

    When we said adios to Chifa, it was a sad day, but I was psyched to say hola to Rosa Blanca, and had the chance to do so this Restaurant Week. The ambiance in the front half of the space is cool and casual, while dining in the back seems more warm and intimate. We were happy to be seated for dinner at a table in the middle of the dining area, but the surrounding booths also looked really cozy.

    I ordered off the wine list, but my friends went for cocktails (La Tormenta & Juana Rodriguez) that were both really tasty after we all passed & shared. I started with the ceviche, because if there's ceviche on the menu, I'm going to order it. The fish was of a nice quality, and the dressing was tart & refreshing. However...corn nuts? In ceviche? Our server mentioned that they were changing up some of the ingredients a bit for restaurant week, but still! Corn nuts?!?!  As an otherwise fan of corn nuts, I have to say NOT IN MY CEVICHE. *soap box ended*

    I was debating between the roja vieja and the curried lamb for my entree, and chose the latter at our server's recommendation. (When she added "it's the spiciest thing we serve," I was totally sold.) The bowl was warm & spicy, but overly salty. The flat breads that accompanied the plate only made it saltier, but I ate the whole thing, so no major complaints. The sauteed spinach was a FESTIVAL of garlic, which was fine for me, but not so much for my table mates. ;)

    Not too much to mention about the desserts - just fine. One of my friends eats exclusively gluten-free, and our server was more than happy to talk the chef several times about what items might be suitable for her to order. Their menu is extensive, and we'll definitely be back to try more!

Specialties

Cuban comfort food, from Chef Jose Garces' dream diner. Combining the laid-back, all-day appeal of a diner with the innately comforting character of classic Cuban fare, Rosa Blanca is the best of Latin and American culinary culture from Philadelphia's top Latin-American chef: Jose Garces.

History

Established in 2005.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
