        On a Friday evening, this place is hopping.  Ambiance is all pub, lots of wood, dimly lighted, old-school, with a cool vibe.  The beers were authentic, seemingly imported, to be selected from their "Beer Bible", a veritable compendium of beers.  No doubt, this place honors its Belgium roots....

        We sat at the bar due to an estimated half-hour wait for a table, according to the hostess.  In the meantime while we waited we decided to explore some muscles.  About 10min later (not 30!-- usually a good thing) the hostess came to inform us our table was ready.  But since we already had ordered our pre-course snack, she was reluctant to shift us over to a table.  (Even though we actually had already settled the tab at the bar!)  So we decided to finish up, then move along to other venues around town.

        The Monks muscles were generously proportions and seemed pretty fresh- but flavor was not their strong suit, as they were entirely bland and the sauce was basically water.  One of my main enjoyments (and I'm sure yours too) typically associated with muscles is sopping up the sauce with bread.  In this case that became equal to dunking bread in a glass of water, except for the occasional smattering of diced garlic-- whose flavor was very tepid anyway.

        But all that said, the place did seem like it had a lot to offer, so I would give it a second chance.  Next time electing an alternative to the very ascetic Monks muscles.

        Come to Monk's if you're in the mood for a lesson on great beer.  The staff rocks and always has suggestions and is super knowledgeable.  Monk's has hundreds of beers available... the menu is literally a book.  Get the Flemmish Sour and thank me when you're done.  You're welcome.

        If you're reading this and thinking that Eulogy has a better beer list... just shut up, go to Monk's, get your beer and order some Mussels and Fries.  Then you'll be thanking me again.  They're voted one of the best fries in Philly every year for a reason.  

        I love the burger and mussels menu- lots of interesting options and flavors to play around with- and both are great.  The atmosphere is what it is- a dark bar... not too small which I like.  Just be prepared to wait if you're coming Primetime!
