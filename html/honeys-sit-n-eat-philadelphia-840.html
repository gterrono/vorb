    I don't even know where to begin my review of Honey's!  

    Everything you get here is delicious! I'll start off with all the great things about Honey's

    They serve breakfast options, portion sizes are great and your food feels like it just came right out of the kitchen (not cold or have been sitting around).  My go to favorite is the Challah French Toast with the home fries.  It's fluffy, soaks up syrup and melts in your mouth.  OH, and if they have Mac and Cheese available, I always get that as well! (So good).

    They have fresh squeezed OJ and fresh brewed coffee.  You can even watch the machine squeeze the orange juice right in front of you!  Can't get more fresh than that! They have an evolving specials of the day menu that also includes desserts of the day!

    The service is always friendly.  They come to check-in on you to make sure you have everything you need and the bathrooms are clean.

    The bad, there's always a wait.  That's how you know a place is good but the wait can leave you with hunger pains if you come during their prime hours over the weekend.  Weekday obviously is a lesser demand.  They do have specials during the weekday to entice you to come in!  

    Overall, awesome place for a casual brunch dining experience and this place is cash ONLY!

    The style of Honey's is like your Jewish grandma's country kitchen transposed into an industrial warehouse space. Floral wallpaper, muted greens, antique mixers and a hardwood bar meet with exposed I-beams and duct work. A casual vibe and friendly service makes you feel right at home.

    I've heard about this place a lot for brunch...correction, I've heard about its lines for brunch. I tried to go to the South Street location a few months ago but the line was literally out the door and the wait was over an hour. Neither location has any indoor waiting room so not wanting to turn into ice cubes, we opted to brunch else where. Fast forward a few months and about 75 degrees when we attempted to brunch again. This time we were prepared for the wait. All in all it only ended up being about 25 minutes around noon on a Saturday.

    The brunch menu options are varied, your standard brunch fare (omelettes, pancakes, etc.) usually with a little twist (challah french toast, for example.) However the most exciting options we found were on the specials board. The "Sweet Corn Cakes" - a corn pancake topped with peaches, berries, and creamed cheese - was found intriguing by so many people that they were completely out by the time we ordered.

    So instead we settled on the Fried Chicken Benedict complete with a chipotle hollandaise, two poached eggs, two pieces of fried chicken all on top of two medium sized pancakes. The pancakes were advertised as "silver-dollar-sized" but I would say they were bigger, more like CDs (if you remember what those are.) This dish was delicious. The eggs were cooked perfectly, the chipotle was a perfect spice accompaniment, and the soft spongy texture of the pancakes paired perfectly with the crunchy (boneless) fried chicken. My biggest issues with the dish was that I felt like the different elements weren't served in proportion and it was hard to get a bite of everything all at once. I ended up cutting the food into little pieces (like what your grandma does to your 2 year old cousin's hot dog) and creating a mini skewer on my fork before using it to lap up as much of chipotle hollandaise as possible. Luckily all of the items also tasted good on their own. This dish was served with a side latke - one of the few food items there that really reflected the Jewishness of the establish. (The only other hint of Jewishness I got was that the letters for "Sit n Eat" on the logo were spelled with hebrew symbols.) The latke was served as a triangle, super crispy on the outside but soft and chewy on the inside. Definitely not the latkes I'm used to, but very good.

    We knew that would be a lot of heavy food so instead of ordering a second entree for the two of us, we also ordered the watermelon-tomato gazpacho and a side salad. The gazpacho was heavily. Cold, light, with great flavor and I think chunks of watermelon. It was perfectly refreshing. The side salad was...fine. Lots of fresh veggies but nothing that really stood out. We got it with their house made maple vinaigrette which was definitely the standout of that dish.

    All in all, Honey's was a delightful experience. I philosophically have a problem giving a restaurant a perfect review, but I really couldn't find anything wrong with Honey's that would cause it a less than 5-star review. Come ready to wait, but it's well worth it!
