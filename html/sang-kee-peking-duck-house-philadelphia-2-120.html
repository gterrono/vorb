    the seating area upstairs is rather small. there's only three large tables (no lazy susans!), so if you come with a large party, be sure to come before noon when peak lunch hours start. our party of 8 was easily seated at 11:30am when the place was a ghost town.

    the menu here has some serious identity crisis issues. 1/3 of the menu is americanized "chinese", 1/3 is cantonese, and 1/3 is "shanghainese."
    we ordered the set duck dinner for four (includes soup, 2 duck dishes, chicken and eggplant, walnut shrimp, and house fried rice), a string bean dish, a stir-fried noodle dish, and a side of roast pork.

    soup: good wonton soup, although too many other random veggies
    duck: can't speak for it since i don't eat duck
    chicken/eggplant: good
    walnut shrimp: fried breaded shrimp?? so WRONG for this dish.
    fried rice: average
    string beans: good, but minced pork was mixed in with some other stuff i couldn't recognize (not meat)
    stir-fried noodles: average
    roast pork: good, a little tough and salty

    supposedly, the duck here IS amazing though. says my 7 other duck-loving friends.

    I have been to Sang Kee countless amount of times. Finally, the review has came after eating at this… I have been to Sang Kee countless amount of times. Finally, the review has came after eating at this famed Chinatown restaurant recently. Specializing in roast duck noodle soups, Sang Kee can be a place to get your quick fix of something hot and soupy. So, where does this place fail, where others deemed it amazing?

    My latest visit brought us upstairs. It may just be our table, but it was really sticky, to the point where I felt like a hopeless ant glued to those traps. The upstairs is only open to customers if it gets too packed or for special parties. I contest the cleanliness of the upstairs as well, since everything is always out there sitting there until they need to open it.

    My girlfriend and I ordered the seafood pan-fried noodle to share. This is a different route than what we are used to, but still a good decision. The noodles were crispy and had the right amount of "sauce," or whatever that clear liquid is. Had a variety of different sea creatures, but missing the orange snail/clam that I have grown to love offered at other restaurants. In the end, it was good, I just wished they gave a little more.

    Here is where the deductions come in. This place is very INCONSISTENT. You order a noodle soup one day, and it is amazing; really rich broth with excellent duck, pork, or braised beef. Noodles are cooked just right; thick egg noodle for the win! On other days, the noodle soup is just undesirable. The broth would still be a little under warm, as opposed to piping hot. One time, my friend's bowl had uncooked noodles, too. Maybe I am just hater, but consistency is really important to me, especially when I go here with friends all the time.

    I would also like to add that you should stick to what you have ordered before. I was being adventurous one day and had the eel noodle soup. I did not expect warm broth with canned eel in tomato paste to be plopped on top of my noodles. Very disappointing.

    For those who read to this point... Sang Kee is really not that bad. The cheap prices keep us coming back. Even through my rants, Sang Kee delivers good food to back up their often crowded restaurant.     Read more

    Duck: the Peking Duck was pretty good, but definitely not memorable.  The crispy duck, on the other hand, was another story...     Service: from what I remember there was nothing wrong with the service.  However the waiter did spill a pitcher of water on the floor and everyone watched as he mopped it away (as there was nothing else remotely entertaining).

Specialties

Welcome to Sang Kee Peking Duck House located in Chinatown.  We offer dining on three (3) floors.  Sang Kee serves Hong Kong cuisine in a family style setting.  Our speciality is Peking duck, dumplings, noodles, and a wide variety of other traditional Hong Kong style dishes for your enjoyment.  We've been voted "Best" by countless making us a success since 1980.  Make sure to consider our prix fixe menus, our banquet menus, and our catering menus.

History

Established in 1980.

Sang Kee Peking Duck House offers a taste of traditional and authentic Chinese dishes to customers. With a 30 year history, many have voted Sang Kee Peking Duck House in Chinatown - the best in Philadelphia. Our approach includes the use of high quality ingredients and freshness. We designed our menu specially for you that has been time tested to be the best at a reasonable price. When dining with your family and friends, you can taste the experienced authentic Asian cuisine style cooking techniques. Our interior decoration is simple and clean. We have the perfect table for your party whether its 2 or 100+. Our place is perfect for gatherings where you can pass around the dishes of exotic Asian foods over any occasion or casual dining. Sang Kee Peking Duck House in Chinatown has become an institution in the community and the Philadelphia area for good food.

Meet the Business Owner

Michael C. Business Owner

Executive Chef Michael Chow started Sang Kee in 1980 specializing in Peking style duck and Hong Kong cuisine in a modest 20-seat rented facility before moving to the current location on 9th Street. Chef Michael, originally from Hong Kong, made his transition to the Food industry in the 1970s. He studied the intricate method of making Peking Duck, a method passed through apprendiceship, which he has now perfected. Peking Duck is now reknown as a culinary masterpiece worldwide originally from China. Chef Michael is a celebrated Chef in the Philadelphia area. His current collection of restaurants continues to grow beyond Philadelphia's Chinatown.
