    I hadn't been to Audrey Claire in ages despite living down the block, and I refuse to make that mistake again. The food here is to die for and it is an absolute neighborhood gem. They only take reservations M-Th, but we were able to make one no problem. The restaurant is BYO and cash only.

    After two dinners at Audrey Claire, I'm not sure I'm willing to take another risk here. While the quality of food is solid, there's literally always something missing - either on the plates or through the service. Both times, I have chosen this restaurant based on convenience, visual appeal, and because it is supposedly the BYOB version of Twenty Manning Grill. At this point, I would rather pay $12 more for two drinks with my meal at Twenty Manning.

    I've dined here as a party of two twice, and both times we shared our dishes. We've had the antipasti for two, seared tuna, mascarpone risotto, portobello pappardelle, and the arugula/lentil/fig salad.

    The antipasti for two was exactly what you'd expect - a solid starter which you really can't mess up - but they messed it up. The brussels sprouts and artichokes were missing, and the waiter brought them separately once he realized his mistake. Regardless, I'd order it again, and it was absolutely enough for two.

    I was disappointed with the mascarpone risotto, which our waiter eagerly suggested. It legitimately tasted like macaroni and cheese and when it arrived, I felt like I had received an item off a kids' menu instead of an elegant small dish. It was certainly cheesy, but lacked any distinguishing flavor, and the consistency wasn't quite right for risotto. Cheesy rice would be a more accurate menu title.

    The arugula/lentil/fig salad looked and tasted amazing - except it was served without its lentils and with only a half of one fig. When we asked our waiter where the rest of the salad was, he promptly responded that the kitchen was out of lentils and figs, and did not offer an apology or explanation. Why weren't we told this, and why were we served an incomplete salad? The lentils are the source of protein, and the figs add the flavor. The waiter could not offer us any substitutions, charged us the full price for the salad, but offered a free half-portion of a dessert. The dessert was meh. I didn't want to ruin my meal with my family, but it was outrageous that we were charged for the salad.

    The portobello pappardelle and the tuna were the absolute winners - completely delicious. The tuna was seared just enough and beautifully presented and the portobello pappardelle was a terrific pasta dish, with lots of flavorful mushrooms. Shockingly, nothing was forgotten on these plates!

    I have higher expectations for a restaurant operated by Twenty Manning, and would hope Twenty Manning would also have higher expectations for the establishment. Kitchen mistakes, such as leaving an item off of a plate, are understandable, but the absolute lack of attention by the waiters, and unwillingness to offer a substitution, thoughtful explanation, or a refund for an item that was not as described or complete, is unacceptable for such a "nice" restaurant.

    Audrey Claire is among my favorite restaurants in Philadelphia.  I've been a regular for years.

    Perhaps it is the simplest of Audrey Claire's luxuries that I find the wisest: fresh air.  The large swinging windows are almost always open and overhead fans whisk a breeze throughout the dining area.  Whenever I enter the restaurant, I feel as if I had previously been suffocating and I was simply too numb to notice; however, I can finally breathe again once at Audrey Claire.

    Of course, the food is delicious--it's fresh, never over-prepared, and tasty.  This is the sort of eatery that caters neither to the perverse glut that many American chefs disguise as "comfort food" nor to the palate of overwrought "health food" enthusiasts.  Instead, Audrey Claire offers a cuisine of healthful, balanced nourishment that is responsive to the flavors my body truly craves: olive oil, pear, goat cheese, olives, tomato, mussels, yogurt, pork, aioli, chocolate.

    I cannot recommend this restaurant enough.  Go, it's a Philadelphia classic--and bring a bottle of wine as it's BYOB.
