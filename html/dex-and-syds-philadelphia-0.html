        I would never have found this small place - just 5 tables - in the 500 block of Washington Ave. if not for an ad I saw in South Philly Review (with a coupon). Well I finally got there today and I have to say that owners Hong and Rick are really friendly. The menu is a hybrid of cheese (or Chicken) steaks and southern seafood like gried catfish and shrimp and fresh crawfish (flown up from Texas). Platters come with fries and salad.  Though I don't do spicy cajun, Hong offered me a sample of some freshly arrived and steamed crawfish. (Thanks Hong!) I have to admit it's an acquired taste (obviously not for me). but the cheese steak that Rick made was hot and tasty. Rick tells me that they are getting in Alligator meat shortly! This is more a lunch than a dinner place but you can bring your own beer. More friendly than fancy the place is kept clean. If you are in the neighborhood it's worth stopping by to say "hi" and try it.

        Hong (Rick's wife) and Rick were originally Philadelphians and moved to Mississippi to open a restaurant. Now they are back. BTW, Dex and Syd are their son and daughter and that is the origin of the name.

        3.5 stars if Yelp allowed it.

        I grew up in the south and I'm a crawfish addict! My family and I would have weekly crawfish and crab boil and crawfish joints are plentiful in the South so believe me... I do know what GOOD crawfish taste like. It's hard to find fresh crawfish in Philly, so I was really excited to find that this place has it year round!

        I ordered 2 pounds for take out just to try it out. The crawfish smelled amazing and they were good size. It was cooked to perfection- neither under/overcooked. If only it had a bit more flavor I would be a happy, happy lady! I had to reboil it with my own seasoning to make it satisfy my taste buds. I'm hoping it was just a bad day and that next time would be better.

        Otherwise, the owner is SUPER nice and they deliver too! Seafood delivered to my door is a dream come true. I really do hope this business succeeds. I'll give this place another try as the crawfish is reasonably priced. If anything I'll just touch it up at home. The only other crawfish that I really enjoyed was Catahoula, but it's ridiculously pricey at $19.99/lb for barely 1lb of crawfish w/ some corn & sausage! Sure tasty tho! Chris Jazz's Cafe has all-you-can-eat crawfish during crawfish season, but it was way bland.

        Dex & Syd if you're reading this- please use FRESH garlic and good butter. If you're saving on cost maybe cut out the sausage that comes with it but don't skimp out on fresh especially garlic. It would make such a difference!

      Specialties

      Dex & Syd's Restaurant serves a mixture of South Philadelphia and Southern style cooking.  Our restaurant serves cheese steaks, salads, hoagies, a large selection of seafood dishes (including Snow Crab Legs and Dungeness Crabs), and fresh boiled Cajun Crawfish daily.

      Meet the Business Owner

      Hong H. Business Owner

      My husband and I grew up in the South Philadelphia but moved to Southern Mississippi for 12 years.  My family live on the east coast.  Therefore, we moved back to be closer to my family and to open our restaurant Dex & Syd's.  Many have wondered where the name Dex & Syd originated from.  To answer your question- from my two beautiful children Dexter and Sydney.  Our restaurant is a casual  and no frills kind of restaurant.  Down South a lot of the best restaurants are in a small setting and very casual.  The food is just good home cooking food served with a lot of flavor and so plenty that you can easily share a meal.  We have adapted a lot of these characteristics to our restaurant Dex & Syd's.
