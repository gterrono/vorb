    Lunch specials are a steal!  You get an appetizer or soup, salad, and an entree for only $8-$11 depending on what meat/tofu you get for the entree.

    My standard:
    Erawan salad - bed of lettuce with sliced carrots and purple cabbage with one onion ring, one cherry tomato, and one cucumber topped with a mildly spicy peanut dressing - very good
    Curry chicken dumplings - 3 fried dumplings with a sweet/spicy dipping sauce - decent
    Pork pad thai - delicious tamarind flavor with generous pieces of tofu and pork; the serving size is large and a great value

    Staff is good but can also get overwhelmed during busy hours (usually Friday lunch), so just be prepared.  

    Cash until $15 then credit cards accepted.

    I've been here on several occasions and had takeout once. I wish it were better, because I really like Thai food. But it's pretty mediocre in my book.

    The most recent meal:

    - Papaya salad: tasteless produce, overwhelming fish sauce
    - Tofu pad Thai: oops, they gave us chicken. Luckily we aren't vegetarians! And the whole thing was greasy and underwhelming
    - Rice: Too gloppy. It was all in one big wad. And I love my rice, and am not usually picky about it.
    - Curry chicken: By far the best of the bunch. Nice coconut broth. Excellent curry --  probably mild by Thai standards, but appreciated by this American.

    Bottom line: If you want decent Thai food in Philadelphia, keep looking.

    I've been eating here for the past few years and have rarely been disappointed.  My favorite dish, hands-down, is the crispy duck, superseded only in recent years by the pineapple angel duck (which is basically the crispy duck with pineapple...perfect addition!).  The duck is crispy and savory, and the basil curry sauce adds just enough heat to balance the pineapple in the dish.  For desert, the mango coconut sticky rice is a must try.  The rice is sweet, creamy, and just the right amount of sticky.  I wasn't a fan at first of serving it with unripened mango, but somehow it all works together.  Today, I tried their Pad See-Ew with pork for the first time, and it was exactly what I needed to satisfy my noodle craving.  It was warm, savory, and perfectly cooked to order (not microwaved as at some other places I've been lately.)  I paired it with the coconut soup as part of the lunch special and the whole thing came in just under $10...and the noodle portion was huge!  My only complaint about the soup is that the chicken is always overdone, but my guess is that they don't account for the additional cooking in the hot soup.  The soup itself is warm, savory, comforting, and just a tad bit sweet...also one of my favorites.  If you have room, I also highly recommend the Thai Iced Coffee...it's thick and sweet, just perfect for a summer day.

Specialties

Welcome to the First Thai Restaurant in Philadelphia, PA. With a spacious inviting interior and countless authentic Thai dishes. Enjoy Golden Bag, Satay Chicken, Green Curry, Pad Thai, Spicy Drunken Noodle, Duck Red Curry, and Crab Fried Rice.

You can expect a delicious meal for lunch or for dinner. Erawan is a great option for large parties and perfect for dates. Kid-friendly, BYOB, Delivery, Online Ordering, Lunch& Dinner Specials Offering.

We pride ourselves on providing fresh home cooked Thai food. Visit our website for menu, update, and promotion.

Owned and managed by Patt Wechthanakorn since the year 2007.
