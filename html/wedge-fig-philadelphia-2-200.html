    Not sure what I can add here that hasn't already been said, but Wedge + Fig is a great location for great food and nice ambiance. The only thing I would say right off the bat is if you're going in the summer, make sure to dine in the garden out back, just adds to the fantastic experience.

    Atmosphere - Inside is a bit like an order up smaller area with tables and chairs. A few glass cases house some of the cheese and food specials, but the real place to sit is in the garden through the red door. Charming and quiet  area with fountains and greenery compliment a perfect meal. Really can't say enough about how nice this was to have a small area to sit in the middle of Olde City.

    Waitstaff - Our waitress recommended some great cheeses from a fine selection available. Very good at bringing over water and recommending great choices from their available menu. Was very patient in waiting for our third guest to arrive and provided us with small snack items while waiting to order our meal. Prompt and efficient, our table had our dinner in about eight minutes after ordering.

    Food - We started with a mixed cheese board, which my wife and I enjoyed these very much, Especially enjoyed  a cheese made with Chimay beer which was absolutely fantastic. Went with a Mac & Cheese pie covered in lattice bacon for my entree. Let me repeat that, I ordered a Mac & Cheese pie covered in god damn lattice bacon! It was absolutely as good as it sounds, and I highly recommend it if on the menu when you go. We didn't do dessert here, but the mix of brownies, and other baked goods looked enticing if the meal doesn't fill you up.

    Intangibles - Garden area in the back is a super nice touch, but also really enjoy the simplicity of the menu. Some Olde City restaurants bombard you with so many choices, but here, they just focus on the quality of a simple two sided menu and provide you with great service to boot. Within walking distance of Franklin Fountain, Liberty Bell, and a bunch of other great spots if you're coming into the city.

    Overall - Wedge + Fig is a can't miss stop on your visit to Philadelphia. Be it as a local or a tourist, the quality of food, value, and overall dining experience should not be missed.

    I came to this BYOB for a friend's birthday party.  If you have a large group, they will let you make a reservation.  We had ten, but I imagine they reserve for less.  We sat down and were promptly given water and wine glasses for the many bottles we brought.  The tables are small, so they do not have wine buckets.  They do have wine ice packets, which they wrap around your bottle to keep the wine cool.  If you have multiple bottles, your best bet is to ask them to throw one in the cooler.

    To start we ordered a cheese plate.  You can order a pre-selected set of three or five cheeses, or make your own plate.  We went with the pre-selected Roman Holiday five cheese plate (choosing your own is overwhelming sometimes).  Everyone at the table enjoyed the cheeses, and our server brought out more bread without us even asking when we were about to run out.  It appeared that the cheese servings were in the range of 1 to 1.5 ounces range.  The cheese plate comes accompanied by honey, dried fruit, toasted baguette and nuts (so make a note if you have an allergy).  The cheese selections were served on a slate ordered from mildest to stinkiest and the slate included a chalked name for each cheese in case you forget or want to order some to take home after the meal. (There is also a cheese market in the front of the restaurant.)

    For my entree, I went with the Shackamaxon--a turkey and brie panini.  It was a little dry for me.  The sandwich could have used a bit more of the walnut-mustard aioli and a little less turkey.  The bread itself was super toasted, and thus a bit crumbly, which made eating it a bit of a challenge.  The mango was absolutely delicious, as was the brie.  The flavors were great minus the dryness of the sandwich.  

    I will definitely be back to Wedge + Fig--especially to try the build your own grilled cheese bar!

    Listed in Best and Worst of South Street-Queen Village-Old City

    I visited Wedge + Fig for Old City Restaurant Week (7/25) and I cannot understand the novelty of this place based on its Yelp! reviews.

    I was sitting in the (overrated) garden where there was no breeze therefore making it a pretty uncomfortable, humid night.  It's funny, but first and foremost, I do not "get" the point of putting room temperature tap water into an "old fashioned" bottle and leaving it on my table...Not even ice in the glass.  Is the cutesy bottle supposed to compensate for the fact that it's warm, tepid tap water?  Pointless and impractical.  Please stop doing this.

    Getting back to the garden, I do not see what is so special about it.  I would go so far as to say it's "charming," but a "must!" is just over-the-top.  Upon walking back to the garden, I was pretty alarmed.  This place is a New York City restaurant cramped.  I seriously felt like I was sitting on top of the diners to the left and right of me.  Less than a foot of space between tables.  I don't like hearing other peoples' conversations but most importantly, I do not want them hearing mine.  And while I remain unimpressed with the cramped outside seating, I would probably still choose it over the equally as cramped inside "dining room."  

    The food had it's ups and downs.  The cheese was probably the best part of the meal.  The original RW menu had given you the option of three cheeses but on this night they were offering all six cheeses for the table.  They were presented in a lovely manner with honey, walnuts, and a yummy fig cake.  All of the cheeses were delicious -- especially the brie!  I got the Porky Fig salad and I could have taken or left it.  I thought the spring mix was a bit too bitter although the figs were perfectly ripe and tasty.  We also had the prosciutto wrapped stuffed figs and the cheddar polenta -- both were excellent, tied with the cheeses for meal highlights although I would have preferred that she brought them WITH the meal instead of as appetizers on top of the cheese and salads.  As for the entrees, I got the Moyamensing because unfortunately they were out of the delicious-sounding Sassafras Street.  My entree left much to be desired.  The main protein was supposed to be chorizo although I only had one recognizable bite of chorizo.  Pretty skimpy for an $18 entree.  I did not take home my leftovers of which there were plenty.  Dessert (brownie) was alright, not mind-blowing, although my friend had the goat cheese cheesecake which WAS amazing.

    All-in-all, I think I would perhaps stop in here on a casual Saturday afternoon for a fresh sandwich but I wouldn't be paying upwards of $20 for their entrees and the space is just too small and poorly arranged.  I would advise those with claustrophobia to stay away.

    Listed in Travellin', Brunchin' Babe

    I love Philadelphia because of the historic character, the shopping, and most importantly, BYOB.  And if BYOB brunch doesn't sound fantastic, I don't know what does.  

    Wedge+Fig is an extremely cute restaurant in the Old City.  It's back patio can only be described as charming and intimate, with small tables and trellised brick walls.  Service was attentive and friendly.

    Three girls brought two bottles of champagne to brunch and we weren't sure it would be enough (but since we all like our mimosas with just a splash of orange juice, it was).  They'll chill your bottles for you, which is a nice touch.  Everything on the brunch menu sounded fantastic, but I went with something I so rarely find:  a make-your-own breakfast sandwich (brunch bar).  It comes with greens and potatoes, which is great.  And Sahar and Victoria went with the same, though someone (Sahar, I believe) ordered double cheese, and that's the way to go.  My sandwich was gouda, tomato, and bacon on white and was delicious.  Potatoes were crisp and greens were fresh.

    We ended up getting kicked out because we had a Tanqueray nip with us, and during our last round of mimosas, topped it off with the gin.  While I suppose that's our fault for not reading the rules, I wish we were not lied to and told it was because there was a wait.  There was definitely no wait.

Specialties

Old City's Cheese Bistro, featuring dinner, lunch and weekend brunch.  Sample our chicken pot pie, mac & cheese and scrumptious salads in the evening.  For lunch, try  our unique sandwiches, salads and soups (take out available for most items).  Omelettes, strata, cream-cheese-stuffed French toast are some brunch favorites.  Dine under the stars in our secluded garden.  Bring a bottle of wine or ale and we will pair it with a selection of cheeses.  The perfect romantic evening!

History

Established in 2006.

We rescued Old City Cheese Shop just before it closed.  We gave it some badly needed upgrades, changed the menu, re-named the store to wedge+fig.  And we stocked the cheese case with cheese!

Meet the Business Owner

Kirk N. Business Owner

Cheese lover from an early age.  Lived on a sailboat for 15 years.  Used to play guitar.  Now I own a restaurant.
