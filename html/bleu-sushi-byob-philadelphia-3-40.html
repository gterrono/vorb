    8/7/2014

    Really easy, very good, but simple sushi; not a lot of bells and whistles. I have gotten the sushi combo, a couple of their specialty rolls, seaweed salad and a few other things. All good. Their salad wasn't great (the dressing was a little mayo-ey instead of gingery which is a personal preference) but that seemed to change for the better on my last visit. Last visit however, everything that was to be blowtorched or run through a crisper was over-crisped, leaving the tempura crunchies slightly smoked/burnt and my taste buds all over the place. I didn't send any of it back (bc they're very nice people) but I noticed the table next to me got their tuna tataki on the house, so I think there might've been a chef-in-training issue. Speaking of which, the sushi chef looked like a teenager, and since it is a family run biz, he probably was. Our server was so young, so sweet and so confused, obviously her first rodeo, but had a really nice spirit, which can sometime seem unusual for Philly service.

    In total: last visit was a bit of a letdown from our prior experiences, but since they are new ownership, I understand they're working out the kinks. It is family run which can be a blessing or a curse, so I hope they figure out how to train/fire accordingly. A-minus, overall (on a small-business, family-owned scale).

    Oh yea: someone mentioned the pop music they play, and I agree, sensory-wise it is a little weird. Mylie Cyrus, Pitbull, and Katy Perry will all play during your meal. Lastly, the sushi combo comes with salad OR miso. Not used to that, but fair enough.
