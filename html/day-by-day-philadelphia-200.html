    I have been here several times and have been impressed every time. Service has been excellent each time that I've gone in spite of how busy it has been.

    I'm slightly obsessed with eggs benedict, and am extremely partial to lox bagels and latkes as a result of close ties to many Jewish friends. So, imagine my delight when my eyes alighted on a menu item marrying these two passions together--potato pancakes benedict! It did not disappoint. Also during that first visit, I enjoyed sharing the lemon mascarpone stuffed challah french toast with blueberry spread, although it's a little overwhelmingly large and sweet to tackle on my own. My friend visiting Philly found that the huevos rancheros perfectly satisfied her craving that day. The freshly squeezed orange juice was a wonderful addition to the meal as well.

    Other highlights since that first visit have included the quiche, the baked apple pancake, and the smoked salmon and whitefish platter, which offers great bang for your buck. The spinach, goat cheese, and sundried tomato omelette was satisfying but not anything extraordinary. In general, the options at Day by Day includes a nice combination of the classic and the innovative.

    Day by day, in my opinion, is a trendy but hipster brunch restaurant.

    First of all, they don't take reservations. And your whole party has to be present in order for you to be seated. They are only open for breakfast, brunch, and lunch.

    I really enjoyed the atmosphere of the restaurant. It's bright inside, although not very spacious. It's decorated colorfully, with a huge chalkboard wall with the menu and daily specials drawn on.

    However, I didn't get a fantastic impression from the staff, since our food arrived very slowly and they weren't too apologetic about that. However, the food was delicious. I had the challah french toast, which was delicious but heavy. We also had an order of the chorizo and eggs, bacon and scallops quiche, and breakfast potatoes. Everything was appetizing and made just right.

    In the end, though, I would definitely suggest Day By Day if another brunch opportunity came up.

    Yes, yes, a thousand times YES! to whoever suggests going here for brunch.

    Day By Day is one of those places that seems to have everything you want: homemade pastries/cakes/pies, fresh bread and muffins, pancakes, French Toast, omelettes, Benedicts...the list goes on and on. And they have delicious brunch specials too.

    My boyfriend and I have compared this place to other restaurants that serve brunch in both Hoboken and New York City...and I have to say, Day By Day beats Amanda's, Anthony David's, the Elysian Cafe, Penelope, The Brass Rail, Jane, and Joe Doe by a mile.  Their food is just that good.

    Oh yeah, speaking of food...I ordered my usual (smoked salmon Benedict over potato latkes, served with a fresh mixed green salad with balsamic vinegar), and my boyfriend ordered the potato pancakes with three different sauces. Our plates were virtually wiped clean by the time the watiress came by to pick them up. Excellent, as always. The salmon they serve is fresh, clean, and very tender, and their salads are always crisp and colorful. Unfortunately, I did not get a chance to sample any of my boyfriend's potato pancakes, since he was unwilling to share. Hmph.

    As we were paying our bill at the front register, I noticed an angel-food cake with what appeared to  be a green filling. Intrigued, I asked the cashier what that was. She said it was a type of palm oil that turned the inside green, although it had no special flavor. She then generously offered us a slice to share. It was very good, with a subtle hint of sweetness. A happy ending to a perfect meal.

    If you ever find yourself in the Rittenhouse neighborhood in the late morning/early afternoon hours on a weekend, please do yourself a favor and go here. You will not be disappointed.

    Day By Day is a highlight of my trips to Philadelphia.  I live in New York, a city inundated with brunch spots, but when I think about a memorable Saturday morning meal, Day by Day stands out.  It has a wait that's on par with any great East Coast brunch spot, but the friendly staff and lack of a pretentious aura make the wait and the subsequent experience much more bearable.  I'm also a fan of the new technology upgrade - avoiding the crowd inside and wandering Rittenhouse until they call is a nice change.

    I'm almost ashamed to admit this, but the dish that I crave most is their tuna melt.  I've had a bunch of amazing brunches there, but I found this particular melt during a weekday foray, and it is the best tuna melt I have ever eaten.  Ideal balance of ingredients, cheese melted to perfection, perfect consistency with just enough crunch, fresh taste, a sizable portion, and it comes in a croissant!  

    Despite this obsession, I like to explore on weekends.  Heading there with a few friends and a bottle of champagne is an adventure.  The reliable staples means that everyone is assured a good meal, but the highlights are Day by Day's specialty: the chunk of the menu and pastries that change weekly.  The baker's Jawns are the undiscovered yet far superior answer to the Cronut, and no matter how crazy the flavors, I'm excited every time I don't see them crossed off the chalk board.  Sitting at a table outside with one of Day by Day's goodie baskets of pastries and a coffee pretty much sums up my perfect morning.  

    They don't do anything overly fancy and I generally stick to the savory after copious amounts of pastries, but the menu is creative, sizable, reliable, and incredibly tasty.  The menu is affordable, including the more decadent items like smoked salmon benedict with crispy latkes and the pesto prosciutto and asparagus dish (I forget what this was called but it was really interesting).  I'm curious about their French toast, but I can never avoid the pastries long enough to order it.  That's about all I can think of now that I've worked up an appetite - I'm off to book my next Bolt to Philly.
