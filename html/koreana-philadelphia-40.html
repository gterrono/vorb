    Let me preface this review with a disclaimer. I have a pretty high (well, especially high) standard when it comes to Korean food. I am from Korea and once I came to the US I got spoiled by really good Korean places in LA and NY.

    Okay, with that said...

    Koreana could *for most part* be considered to be what's known in Korea as "Boon Sik", basically Korean street food...again, I emphasize "most part". They have popular street items like dduk-bbok-gi (rice cake and fish cake in hot sauce), omoo-rice (rice with omelette on the top, usually served with ketchup on the top...yes, i know--ketcup), ramen, oodong, kim-bop, etc. Koreana does have not-so-street items like dol-sot bi-bim-bap (rice with assorted vegetables and hot sauce on a hot sizzling stone-pot), dwen-jang jjigae (staple Korean soup made with Korean miso...the stinkier cousin of Japanese miso), etc.

    We ordered jjam-bbong (Korean Chinese spicy noodle soup) and dduk-mandoo-gook (rice cake and dumpling soup). The noodles in jjam-bbong were a bit too soft and dduk-mandoo-gook were a bit bland for my taste. But overall, they were good enough for me to come back to when I have craving for these types of food.

    When I was a student at a nearby university, Koreana was probably my favorite cheap-eats place on campus. Pretty much everything on the menu is under $10, and I really enjoyed the food served. The restaurant isn't very big, and the set-up is such that you order and pay for your food at the register, grab a table, and wait for your food to be brought out.

    Since I generally prefer rice dishes over noodles, I usually ordered bi bim bap and some of the other stone pot-based rice dishes served at Koreana. I was particular fond of the Dol Sot Bulgogi, which is rice, bulgogi, kimchi vegetables, and a fried egg, all served in a hot stone pot. The rice that makes contact with the stone pot becomes really nicely charred, especially if you let it sit in the pot for a minute or two after it's served.

    I guess the one downside to Koreana is that, unlike larger Korean restaurants, there is no full complement of banchan (little side dishes), which is one of my favorite aspects of dining at Korean establishments. Instead, each meal comes with a small container of kimchi.

    Koreana serves good food at reasonable prices. These qualities make it an appealing dining option for the local college crowd, particularly because there are so many Korean students and so few nearby Korean restaurants. While not life-changing, Koreana's food will certainly satisfy a Korean craving, so if you're looking for a quick, cheap, and, most importantly, good meal, than you should check out Koreana.

    I've also had the dumplings (appetizer) and glass noodles, both of which were yummy.  The boba is also good, though they're the blended, sugary kind (vs. milk tea) so it's definitely more a dessert than a drink with dinner.  They also have shaved ice, which I have yet to try, but vow to get this summer!  

    Koreana is all about the basics, and usually the service is very fast.  You order at a counter, get a number, and bus your own trays, so it's great for a meal with friends, but probably not the best place for a first date.  Definitely my to go place for Asian comfort food!

    Listed in Do UC what I see?, Holy aberration!!

    There are not a whole lot of good options for Asian food in University City, which is odd, considering there are a whole lot of Asian people in University City. Koreana is no exception to this generalized mediocrity (though I am a fan of the recent renovations).

    The pros are that the food comes out real quick. The prices are also lower than most other Korean places, but they have gone up a few dollars over the past few years (which I am a little bitter about). The con is that the food is just not particularly good. The kimchi is never fresh, and I have taken to just asking them to not give it to me so I won't end up inevitably throwing it in the trash. Many of the dishes are lacking authenticity and quality of ingredients. The dolsot bibimbap is a complete travesty - the rice around the bowl isn't even crispy! There are, however, some items that are pretty solid. I am a fan of the omurice (rice covered in an egg casing) and some of the fried rice is decent (though it is hard to mess up fried rice). The shaved ice is unexpectedly good and may be my favorite thing on the menu.

    For someone who isn't a big fan of this place, I have come here fairly often. It's not that I detest the restaurant; it's just that you have to go in with a different mindset - similar to how you wouldn't walk into Panda Express expecting amazing Chinese food. For a quick, cheap, Asian-ish lunch, I'd come here. Or maybe just stop by a food truck.

    I have never contemplated about whether to give 4 or 5 stars as much as I did for this place - it made me wish the Yelp stars were on a 1-10 scale. Normally I am very quick and good at this kind of decision-making, but Koreana makes it tough.

    On the one hand, I am grateful to them. (Needless to say that I find it strange and unusual to be 'grateful' to some place where you leave your money.) But for me, this is hands-down the best food place in all of University City - it's a no-frills restaurant, everything is rather cheap and anything I have had here came out fast and was very, very delicious. We always get one of the spicy soups (really spicy, but not Han-Dynasty-like spicy) and one rice dish, a bibimbap or a dolsot bibimbap. We would come here whenever we had a long, tiring day and do not have much more time to cook. Especially at cold winter days, they are a godsend.

    On the other hand, 5 stars are normally reserved for exceptionally good food, which Koreana cannot quite be on par with. Their miso soup is rather bland, their rice dry and almost inedible, they do not serve any banchan except for a small portion of kimchi, and while their tofu and seafood soup is very good, I know 2 places in Atlanta alone that have been better. Somehow the place is always very crowded and so loud that it's hard to have a conversation sometimes; I would not necessarily take friends here.

    I don't even remember how often we have come here... and since Philadelphia's winters are cold, we will definitely come back again and again.
