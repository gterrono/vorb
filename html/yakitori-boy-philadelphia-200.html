    Cute place.
    It's our go-to place when we can't think of anything else to eat or do. And trust me, that's very handy because we are very indecisive!

    I've been here many many many times, and after a while, I have a few favorites I always order.

    The skewers are very reasonably priced and grilled very well, but sometimes get neglected when other dishes (udon or sashimi) come to us.

    We usually get the pork belly, garbi, chicken gizzards, chicken liver, and ginkos.  Pork belly is very generic, so is the galbi.  The gizzards texture is really good and I think they changed how they were grilling it recently, so it's even better.  The chicken liver can be too watery at times, but the first 2 skewers are just great.  The ginkos.. you can't really mess up on so it's quite generic.

    Tip: when you order skewers, order a couple at a time, so that they are still hot and fresh off the grill! You can always call your waiter over for more, when you're working on it :)

    I always also order the mini udon-- and we can share that among 2 people. I think they do something with the mushroom or something, because I make udon at home but it never turns out that way that they make it! It's like this special grilled taste to it that makes me always go back and get it at yakitori!

    Another favorite of ours is the donkatsu .. the fried pork cutlet.  So I grew up eating this dish from different restaurants and even my mom cooking it for me.  I would say that this donkatsu at yaki is one of the best I've ever tasted.  The only other place that compares is Aki.  While many other restaurants make the mistake of making the donkatsu too dry or too hard and thin, yakitori does a great job of keeping the nice moistness and still cook it all the way through.  The little salad on the side is very good as well.  I usually order a bowl of rice to complement my donkatsu.

    We also get some sashimi.  We used to get the variety sorts, but after realizing we always eat the white tuna and yellowtail, we usually just order a dish of those.  

    We also get sushi when we have more people or just want something carb-y to fill us up.  The sushi is good!  And I like the smaller portions since we can taste a variety of the sushi :)

    Drinks:

    As for drinks, When we order a lot of skewers and sides, we tend to get the house sake, served hot.  Becareful! It's quite hot and you may need to pour the first glass with your napkin over the bottle!
    And  we've tried many of the other sakes, but it just reminds me of korean magulli or bokbunjah and i feel like i'm getting ripped off lol.

    We also always order the hoegaarden on tap.  Great I love hoegaarden on tap, but its weird how they give you an ORANGE with the hoegaarden... Make sure you ask your waiter to replace that orange with a lemon!

    I love yakitori and I think I go here way too often.  But it never gets old! :)

    Listed in Karaoke Joints

    I've yet to try the food here, so I can only comment on the karaoke, for which you have 2 options here - the karaoke lounge, or a private room. The lounge is a great place to watch drunk people make fools of themselves. But it's always packed on weekends, so unless you come early and/or are prepared to wait a while, your chances of actually getting to sing are somewhat slim. Plus it's so crowded that it's tough to get a drink at the bar.

    If you have a group and this is a premeditated outing, you can rent a private room. However, they're very strict about how many people are allowed in a room. For example, if it's a 6-person room, they will NOT let you have 7 people in there. That was a problem for me, because when I go out with a big group I usually don't have an idea of exactly how many people are coming until the last minute. Also, if you don't plan ahead and reserve a room ahead of time, you're probably going to be out of luck. I've come here several times with friends on a "let's all go do karaoke!" whim, only to be turned away -- as in, no rooms available AND the lounge was full.

    So all in all, I usually prefer to be a spectator at YB. Just too much of a hassle otherwise.

    If you're in the mood for karaoke and you've got a group, definitely check out Fuji Mountain.

    In short --
    1. Come here to drink, eat japas, people-watch.
    2. If want to come with a group (and you know exactly how many people are coming!), definitely reserve a room ahead of time.

    alcohol, however, is NOT cheap. house sake is decently priced, but if you get any type of top shelf alcohol here, it will be SUPER DUPER expensive. a round of 6 shots cost me roughly $100, and those weren't the only drinks i ordered that night.

    the crowd is hit or miss. sometimes you catch really good karaoke performances. other times, you'll endure some pretty horrible singing from mic hogs who are clearly tone-deaf and picking all the wrong songs to sing.

    Yakitori Boy has some awesome staff workin' for their restaurant.

    On one of my last visits, I was meeting someone before our concert at the Troc, except he forgot the tix at home and had to drive back for 'em.  So being the starving fiend that I am, I order my food first... then watch the tabletop get covered with dishes and my server come every ten minutes with a concerned, "Are you sure you're ok?"  It wasn't until my companion arrived and my server exclaimed in visible relief, "So you decided to show up!" that I realised that the staff thought I'd been sorely stood up and thereby was drowning my sorrows in delicious pork ramen, skewers of chicken liver and smelt, and sushi rolls the size of my (admittedly tiny) fist.

    Nothin' wrong with that alternate scenario, really, cuz the food here is sinfully amazing.  This place isn't your kitsch sushi bar -- Yakitori Boy means it when they say they're an izakaya-style restaurant, serving up artful plates of sushi, a wide selection of yakitori ranging from vegetarian-friendly to risk-taking, and possibly the best (only?) ramen bowls in the city.

    Girlfriends and I came here for the karaoke bar once, and though we couldn't drudge up the nerve to belt out a few tunes, we found serious enjoyment in watching the lounge patrons sing their little hearts out.  I might draw the line at "My Heart Will Go On", but give me a couple of YB's slurpalicious cocktails, and I might have a go next time.

I usually get a couple of rolls (usually 4 pieces each) and ramen (real ramen, with meat, eggs & veggies), which costs under $15!

    In addition to great soup and sushi, Yakitori Boy has lots of fried and grilled goodness on sticks, or in bowls. Their "yakitori" (or stuff-on-sticks) ranges from delicious Tsukuna (chicken meatballs), to squid legs, and even gizzards for those that like them (there's regular chicken and salmon for the less adventurous). Each yakitori usually costs about $1.50-$2.00, so you can try one or two with a Bento Box or Don Bowl, or combine a bunch to make a meal.

    The drinks here are also fantastic, including the house sake and cocktails. I can't help but eat when I'm here, but I imagine it would be a great place just to grab drinks as well. It's simply stellar.
