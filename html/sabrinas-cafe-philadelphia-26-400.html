    My girl and I have done brunch at Sabrina's on several occasions and we always walk away happy.  Saturday we decided to bring a bottle of wine and give their dinner menu a shot.  I'm so used to a long wait at this popular brunch spot but when we walked into the half empty restaurant at 7 p.m. I became a little worried that maybe dinner wasn't their thing.  I'm happy to say that the great service and food proved my worrying unnecessary.

    We started off sharing a plate of hummus and pita served with with roasted peppers, cherry tomatoes, kalamata olives and feta cheese.  Now I'm no hummus expert but I had to remark to my girlfriend that it was the best hummus dish I've ever had.  For entrees my girl went with the stuffed chicken and loved it.  I ordered salmon served over garlic mashed potatoes with steamed broccoli sprouts on the side.  In a word, delicious!

    Sabrina's caters to a no frills, hipster crowd.  The restaurant's decor is delightfully tacky and the tattooed wait staff is a pleasure to be around.  The food itself is served with a home cooked feel and I've always found it scrumptiously tasty.  This place is a Philadelphia gem and always worth the trip.

    Finally, a chance to eat at Sabrina's Cafe. Although we weren't here for the famed brunch, but for dinner, we ended up ordering off the ever-changing themed brunch menu, which is a few bucks cheaper than the dinner menu. After staring at the menu for what seemed like hours (it's a tough choice with so much to choose from!), we finally decided on the Old Bay shrimp sandwich and the fish tacos (sorry, can't remember the cute names they were given). As an appetizer, we also chose the soup du jour, which was spinach and lentil, heavy on the lentil flavor (meaning you might want to salt it up a bit).

    The sandwich: The Sarcone's roll is kind of difficult to bite through, so you have to kind of yank on it with your teeth, usually resulting in all the filling being pulled out. The fish tacos had tilapia, diced tomatoes, guacamole, chipotle sour cream (delicious!), spicy salsa (also yummy!), and double-wrapped corn tortillas (nice attention to detail, since corn tortillas tend to fall apart quite easily). The fish tacos reminded me of Baja Fresh, actually (which is not a bad thing!). On both the sandwich plate and the tacos, the same coleslaw was used. While it didn't really matter for the taco, as a separate side dish on the sandwich plate, it could have been marinated for a bit longer. Solid, delicious food, some nice attention to detail.

    It WAS Saturday night, but for a place that's famous for its brunch, it was packed! The waiters were obviously very busy, and so was the kitchen, so the slow service was understandable, although quite torturous for my hungry belly. Should have brought a bottle of wine! When the food came, we rejoiced and were merry, positively gobbling everything up. Everyone was very friendly, too! The whole ambiance of the place feels like you're eating in someone's dining room, cozy and comfortable.

    Quick tip, since parking tends to be a pain in the area: $5 parking at the Italian Market lot a couple minute walk away.

    A cute quirky place in the middle of the Italian markets on Christian St. It reminds me of a few places in Williamsburg/Carroll's Garden. Their signature item is the infamous stuffed Challah French Toast. It comes with 2 thick, sliced challah toast stuffed with gorgeous heaps of sweetened cinnamon cream cheese and bananas over vanilla bean syrup. I add blueberries to mine. And a side dish of sausage. Be warned-  the stuffed french toast alone is a 2-course meal. Hub had the huevos rancheros- blue corn tortilla topped with fried eggs and smoky chorizo sausage with lime sour cream and spicy red pepper guacamole. Served with a side of fried jalapeno peppers. He loved it so much, he really didn't want to share his plate.
    Their brunch was just excellent. My brother  had the empire strikes brunch- a panko encrusted white fish topped with spicy roasted cherry tomato red onion jalapeno-chutney.
    I don't know who designed this fantastic menu but whoever came up with the ideas
    definitely's got creative juices flowing in their blood. The dish also came with creamy, cheddar basil Gorgonzola grits and 2 eggs over easy and a side of grilled sweet potato chive cornbread.
