    Devil's Alley is all-around great.  Their ribs live up to the "soul selling" advertisiment - tender, flavorful, great charred skin.  Very good sweet St. Louis style BBQ sauce.  I've also had their BBQ chicken which is juicy and delicious, especially with the sauce glazed all over it.  The mac n cheese with the tomato jam is incredible.  And the shoestring fries mixed with the onion strings.  Need I say more?  I will.  The turkey burger is really great too.  Very fresh ground white meat topped with anything you want, even onion rings or fries (even though it defeats the purpose of eating the turkey, but honestly this is no health food store).  They have a really good brunch with some interesting dishes like a skillet of homemade chili with fried eggs on top served with hot tortillas.  Not a healthy way to start the day, but certainly filling and unique.  BBQ ain't easy to come by in Philadelphia, and for what it's worth this is a place you'll go back to.

    With a great combination of dank beer selection and good food, Devil's Alley now has a spot reserved as one of my permanent lunch options.

    I ordered the beef brisket sandwich, which I had been eagerly anticipating based upon my pre-lunch menu research (yes, I do that).  I even deflected the very tempting three-course Beer Week menu in order to try the brisket.  While I certainly enjoyed it, its not nearly the best brisket I've had--good brisket should be tender and almost-but-not-quite ready to fall apart but this was a mess.  I could also have done without all the cheese and horseradish sauce, which was not necessary with all the flavor in the meat.  It kind of reminded me of a similar sandwich I ordered on special from Continental or Jones about a year ago.  So, basically, it was good but I was expecting much more.

    The gaufrettes were nice.  Who can complain about perfectly crispy waffle chips?  The baked beans were also very good, though not what I look for in baked beans.  I apprecaited the chorizo and pieces of brisket (or pork) in there, and they were incredibly tender, but the flavor was kind of subdued.

    The half-dozen drafts offered me some choices that I had difficulty deciding amongst.  The PBC Coffee Porter was exceptional.  The Lion Winter Ale was above-average.  Next time, I'll have to order the sampler.

    The service could have been slighlty more attentive.  It was almost fifteen minutes before the server took my order.  After that, while still a bit slow, not really anything to complain about.

    Overall, great place that could use a few minor improvements.

    I visited Devil's Alley the other night for drinks and apps for a friend's 24th, and I must say: I totally dug DA's groove.

    The bar is tucked away upstairs, in a long, narrow cafe area (they don't call it an alley for nothing!) with dark paint, coat hooks scattered across the back wall, and an all-metal bar and railings, which overlook the larger downstairs dining area. Mad props for ambiance, although squeezing past random strangers while juggling drinks and a cell phone conversation felt a little awkward

    Nevertheless, this place delivered. The beer selection was outta this world (perhaps from the debaucherous land of the devil): Ithaca Apricot-Wheat, Genny Cream Ale, PBC Coffee Porter...all delicious and ON TAP. The food was well-priced for Rittenhouse and pretty decent...the chicken satay was particularly yummy.

    Overall, a solid bar, especially if you're looking to escape the droves of frat boy types at places like the Irish Pub or Drinkers, next door. I'll probably come back, if I'm ever aimless and in the neighborhood.
