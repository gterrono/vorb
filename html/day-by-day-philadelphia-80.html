    Yums. Excellent Philly brunchery (and caterering company) known for its amazing breads and pastries baked in-house, at reasonable (almost too reasonable) prices. This is the place to go if you're looking for brunch classics that are impeccably prepared using fresh, quality ingredients.  

    For pastries: the cinnamon buns here are terrific-- chewy and soft, cinnamon-y and not over-sweet.

    A brunch specialty is the indulgent Stuffed Challah French Toast, which changes its fillings each week. When I went, it was stuffed with mascarpone, lemon curd cream cheese, and blueberry sauce - and it was divine. (From what I've heard, don't bother with the regular French Toast, it's not nearly as noteworthy)  

    They don't play around with their sandwiches either: the BLT was thick with several stacks of bacon and was spread with horseradish mayo for a bit of zing. It also came with some delicious home fries.

    A bit of a wait to get a table, but only about 15 minutes for a party of 3, which is a nice alternative when you're not ready to wait a few hours at Sabrina's. Service did seem a bit scattered during the bustle of brunchtime, but we didn't have to wait too long for our food. All in all, a very satisfying Sunday brunch in a sunny space.

    the stuffing is usually a mixed fruit option and it changes weekly. whatever the choice, the result is always HUGE fluffy french toast with crispy edges, soft pillowy insides and fruit syrupy (but not too sweet) topping that is just decadent and sinfully amazing.

    this is one of the dishes that won me over on my first trip to day by day. perfect thick cut bacon, fresh lettuce and tomato, over easy fried egg, sandwiched between black bread, with a generous schmear of horseradish mayo... when you bite into this most likely dripping, crunchy, lush sandwich, you'll be in heaven. you will most definitely ignore the pleas of those sitting with you that want a bite. because it's that good that you don't want to share.

    Listed in Brunchadelphia

    Day by Day is a key player in the Philadelphia brunch scene.  It's good, but not exceptional.  It draws locals from Fitler Square and Rittenhouse with its quick table service, rich pastries, and decent offerings of brunch staples.  For a neighborhood joint, it works perfectly, especially when I'm too lazy to hop across town to better destinations.

    Of the many times I've been here, I've usually gone through the same exercise: wait in line for about 20-30 minutes, get seated at "our table" near the kitchen, and spend a few minutes perusing the menu figuring out which brunch dish I want to try that day.  With so many delicious sounding choices, I've found that the standout has always been the Stuffed French Toast.  The last time I was there it was stuffed with Pumpkin cream cheese and served with maple syrup.  It's sweet, rich, and guaranteed to fill your stomach and put you into a mild to severe food coma, but totally worth it, and way better than the other items (although I hear they make a mean Huevos Rancheros).

    But most times, I'm not going to Day by Day for the food.  It's bright interior and simple, laid back ambiance create the perfect atmosphere for recalling last night's activities and catching up over a standard serving of scrambled eggs.  Day by Day focuses on creating a setting for conversation, with food as the complement to stories being shared at the brunch table.
