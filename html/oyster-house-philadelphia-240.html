    I couldn't fly through PHL and not take at least a night and enjoy a good meal. Being that Philly Beer week was wrapping up I figured we'd head here and enjoy the Long Trail IPA Firkin.

    Sadly we did not make it here in time for the Happy Hour special =( but we were quickly seated. The ambiance is classy, but not snooty; it's got a contemporary feel to it and seems like a great place for people to meet up after work in the city.

    For our food selection we went with 2 dozen oysters (waiter's choice), the Roasted oyster sampler, and 1lb of You-Peel Shrimp.

    The raw oysters were pretty good, as I would expect. Their selection was quite nice, including one type which was humongous. I'd give a break down of each type, but unfortunately can never remember after they tell them to me.

    The roasted oysters were also a tasty treat, my favorite was the Toscana, though I'm partial to anything with garlic.

    The You-peel shrimp were deliciously coated in Old Bay spice. Most seemed to be cooked properly, though I did come across a couple that were slightly over cooked.

    As for the drinks: They had kicked the firkin before I got there, so I was very sad about that. I had the Yard's Love Oyster Stout, which I thought was excellent! The flavor was smooth and rich. It had a good after malty taste with a bit of tang. The color was beautiful and the head kept throughout my drink, as it should be for a Stout. My friend had Uncle Teddy's ESB, which was a gold amber color and smooth, but didn't have much of an aroma or taste to it; kinda unmemorable in my opinion.

    Overall, it was a good experience. I liked it. Nothing triggered in my mind that it shouldn't be me but nothing made me thing wow nice. The service was expeditious and they were kind enough to let us know which oysters were which you'd be surprised how often that doesn't happen. I'd probably swing by next time I'm in town!

    I had raw oysters for the first time a few weeks back and had been bragging about them to my wife. After getting her riled up to try some, I did a quick yelp search and lo and behold, Oyster House popped up with a lot of good reviews.

    We strolled in on a Thursday evening around 5:30PM. The bar was nearly packed for Happy Hour but my wife and I decided to grab a table for dinner. Our waitress was very helpful and made suggestions on the myriad of raw oysters they served. She recommended a sampler of a dozen oysters, two of six different types. Overall they were amazing and now my wife is a certified oyster junkie as well.

    As if that wasn't enough, we decided to order some entrees. She ordered the crab cake and I ordered the lobster roll. The crab cake was as it should be. More crab than breadcrumbs and not oily at all. She said this was the best crab she'd ever had, and she gets them quite often.

    And the lobster roll... this was the first time I'd ever had one and every bite was amazing. The lobster was full of flavor and had just the right amount of moisture (not drowning in mayonnaise). The roll was also perfectly toasted. I'm just worried that I peaked too soon and any lobster roll I have from here on out won't measure up.

    To close, the prices are higher than average here, but the point is you get what you pay for. The key word is 'value'.
