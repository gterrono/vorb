    I've been here a lot. I bring my family here. I bring my friends here. Being Taiwanese, I feel that real Taiwanese offerings are pretty scarce in Philadelphia (hint hint, someone open up a new Taiwanese joint already sheesh!). Sometimes I crave the food that you can just pick up from some random street cart in Taipei. Anyway, so I scoured the internet for Taiwanese places in Philadelphia, and if anyone has another suggestion for one, please let me know. That's how I stumbled across Empress Garden a couple years ago. I've been going fairly regularly since.

    They've recently remodeled, and the restaurant actually looks much nicer (although I was sad when they were closed for remodeling). There are a few staples I always get:

    - Scallion pancakes - Even though the ones here aren't really super authentic, they're just really good! They're deep fried, and that fish and vinegar sauce it comes with is really good. I usually get two orders, and if I'm sharing with friends I secretly hog it.

    - 3 Cups Chicken - This dish should have the bone in (for flavor), basil, ginger, and that sweet sticky soy sauce. This is really quite addicting. The flavors are perfect in combination.

    - Fried pork chop - The ones here are very juicy and have a crispy outside.

    - Jah Jang Mein - It's Taiwanese style, so for the Koreans out there, don't expect zucchini and black tarry sauce. It's savory, meaty, flavorful, and really hits the spot. I'm not sure if the noodles are house made, but they definitely have that chewy spring to them (for those who know what I'm talking about, very "Q").

    - Beef noodle soup - This also has that authentic Taiwanese developed flavor. Again, the noodles are so good here.

    They also do offer a lot of Taiwanese street foods including oyster pancake, Taiwanese meatball with glutinous rice wrap ("bah wan"), and other tasty offerings.

    Finally, if you don't speak Chinese, can't read it, or don't have a Chinese friend to bring with you to the restaurant, choose something off the above list. There are 2 menus here, the English menu and the Chinese menu (which has more of the authentic tasty food). I would hate for you to come here and end up with General Tso's chicken.

    There's two kinds of Chinese food, gourmet sit-down style and take-out/delivery; Empress Garden bends these rules and somehow falls in between.  Don't get me wrong, it's not a white tablecloth kinda place by any means, but they try to maintain a cozy dining room and the place is really clean.  The food is definitely not as greasy as typical chinese take-out, but it also lacks just a little bit in the flavor department.

    I've tried their pork fried rice, fried wontons, and the Shredded Chicken Pan Fried Noodle .  The pork fried rice was actually really good, I know it can be considered such a simple dish but I think it was better than most places I've been to.  The Shredded Chicken was awesome and served with the traditional Hard Fried Noodles that had to be soaked in broth before they softened.  Fried Wontons were nothing special and didn't taste any better or worse than typical fried wontons you'd find in any Chinese restaurant.

    This place is definitely a good spot to go for delivery/take-out but I think there are better in the area (Chinatown), so my search continues.

    A microcosm of Chinatown itself, Empress Garden is a staple for quick, quality Chinese food. Being that standard American only reaching so far within his comfort zone, I always go for the General Tso's, which is really great at Empress. The combination dinners on the back page offer this dish and many more with your choice of soup and rice for a very reasonable price (a little under $11).     Down 10th street just past the Friendship Arch sits Empress Garden, in all its simple glory, its dirty windows and plastic table covers - a recent addition that I think they should do away with. I'm sure it makes cleanup easier, but those thick plastic covers are a nuisance, in the way every time you cross your legs, sticky to the touch. Just take it from me, Empress, it's better without.

    That note aside, Chinese food - as well all know - can be hit or miss. It's like Mexican restaurants that way: you'll eat it no matter what, but sometimes you raise an eyebrow as you do so. Also, it's a BYOB, which is a nice touch if you want to grab a bottle of wine or a couple beers beforehand. If you find yourself in Chinatown, find your way to Empress Garden, one of the gems of the area and maybe, just maybe, the best General Tso's I've ever tried.
