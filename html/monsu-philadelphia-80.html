    Monsu has been on my "must try" list for over a year, and I finally made it in with my husband and another couple on a snowy Friday evening.  While I wasn't as blown away by the food as I expected from other reviews, the ease of getting a reservation and extremely good service solidified a fourth star - and also guaranteed a return visit.

    The atmosphere in the restaurant is nice - more cozy/homey than fancy.  The tables are less cramped than many similar BYOB spots.  There were many more tables open than I would have expected for a Friday evening, but the weather reports of an impending major snow (that never materialized) may have been responsible.  The noise level was lively, but not overpowering even though there was music playing and a large group dining.  The hostess was pleasant, and our waitress was a knowledgeable, super-effective sweetheart.

    Our entire party took advantage of the 4-course "Turista" menu (though, this was not obligatory), which was a great way to sample many different offerings at a reasonable price.  Everything that came to the table was, at a minimum, good food; unfortunately, many of the dishes were very rich and/or salty without those characteristics being balanced out.  The escargots and duck-gorgonzola ravioli suffered the most from this; though both were quite tasty and I would consider ordering either again, but to share with a friend and enjoy with a salad.  The mussels, snapper and a deceptively humble-sounding roasted chicken were the stars of the table.  I was also hoping for a little more wow from the desserts.  The two cannolis that came to the table were fine, a chocolate cake was a little dry, and the fourth dessert escapes memory.

    The manager (I think?) was making rounds later in the evening pouring complimentary shots of an after-dinner aperitif, which helped fortify us all for the walk home.  I'm not sure if this is standard practice, but it definitely made sure we walked out of there feeling well taken care of in addition to being well fed.  It was the little things like this that changed my take-home message from "Yeah - that was good" to "We should go back to that place!"

    This is the best restaurant in Philadelphia in my humble opinion.  Hands down.

    Forget Le Bec Fin, Morimoto's, or any of those other over-priced traps.  And forget Sabrina's Cafe for your weekend brunch; go across the street to Monsù instead.  It rocks.  

    The cuisine is Sicilian and I'm very fussy when it comes to Italian food.  I can maybe think of one or two other Italian restaurants that are worth my time, and this place meets my high standards without a doubt.

    Every appetizer and every entrée is perfect...PERFECT!  I've never had a bad experience, much less a bad meal, here.  They have a brunch menu that serves the BEST Eggs Benedict I've ever had, and I don't normally even LIKE Eggs Benedict at all -- but I LOVE theirs.  They serve scrapple ("scrappola" they call it) that is absolutely delicious -- and I'm a tough customer when it comes to scrapple; I don't like anybody "fancy-ing up" my scrapple.  Monsù does scrapple right; they make it their own without overdoing anything or diverging too much from what makes scrapple so great.

    Their arancini is amazing.  I've tried MAKING arancini, and it's hard...it's particularly hard to make stuffed arancini because the rice tends to fall apart when you're trying to stuff it.  They do it perfectly, and it's delicious.

    I can't recommend this place enough.  Do yourself a favor...stop doing whatever you're doing, and just go...right now.  Go!

    Monsu is a fabulous place to go for a special occasion, whether it's to celebrate an anniversary, graduation or birthday, it provides the perfect intimate setting to enjoy good food and conversation.  If you enjoy fresh made pasta and interesting combinations, Monsu is for you!

    I highly recommend the $40 tasting menu. My friend settled on just the pasta and missed out on the best part of the experience (even though I shared!)  You get an app, a pasta, an entree and a dessert (all slightly smaller portions than the regular plates, but still VERY satisfying!)  So bring someone with a healthy appetite - that way you can try more!  I wouldn't go here all the time - I don't really like being fussed over, but it's perfect for a big occasion.

    They started with a slice of bread per person and delicious eggplant spread.  We could have just eaten that, to be honest!  Scrumptious!  I also noticed that they served every table a tiny complimentary starter before ordering food.  We received lemony tuna on toast - very tasty.  This just started us off on the right note - it felt like we really were in a family-run restaurant.

    My friend had the ricotta gnocchi and was not disappointed.  They were obviously freshly made and served with crab and pistachio, as well as lemon cream!  The gnocchi dumplings were fluffier, creamier and lighter than potato gnocchi - a great surprise.  

    I had the A'rancia (a rice ball stuffed with tuna and peas, mozzarella and plum tomato).  It sounds weird, but picture a giant meatball, just substitute it with rice and veggies, and cover it in tomato sauce.  Yum!  I loved the interesting texture the rice gave to it, it was almost like a hand made garden burger but in the form of a meatball.  The mozzarella gave it that cheesiness that the rice needed to be a bit creamier.  You could literally pull the cheesy rice apart to see cheese strings.  So good!

    Next, I tried the Tagliatelle, which was housemade flat pasta which in itself was a star.  Very soft and perfectly cooked - It will make you wish you had time to make your own pasta!  It was served with a lighter sauce with artichoke, perfectly cooked mushrooms and lemon, olives and ricotta salata sauce.  Yum!

    For the entree, I tried the Agnello, which was lamb served with potatoes and kale.  The lamb was tender and perfectly seasoned, and the potatoes and kale were the perfect side/topping.  

    For dessert, I was so excited that they had Panna Cotta that day.  It really hit the spot - small portion of perfectly smooth panna cotta with candied orange peel.  I mean, you can't get better than that in terms of dessert.  I rest my case!

    To sum up, excellent service (although very attentive, so if you'd rather blend in unnoticed, maybe go elsewhere), a fresh take on some classics and excellent presentation.  My only con was that even though we made a reservation, we were given the choice of two angled tables, while better tables were still open.  Not sure if they were saving the window seats for regulars, but the tables we could choose from were not where I would seat people with reservations when other nice tables are open.  I'll probably request a nicer table ahead of time next time I go.

    Don't forget, it's CASH ONLY and BYOB.  :)

    Enjoyed a good meal at Monsu.  Rather than list every item our party enjoyed, and all the menu items are solid, let me say that Monsu offers a legitimate Sicilian (especially southern Sicily) interpretation of this island's unique melding of cultures in its cuisine.  How do I know this?  Well, I just came back from Sicily a month ago, hitting many of the coastal and hill towns in the center and along the southern and eastern coasts.  If you are interested in something completely different from the other Italian restaurants in south Philly, and elsewhere for that matter, by all means check out Monsu.  Lemon, anchovy, and pistachio have never been so artfully interwoven.  Service was spot on.

    So why only three stars?  First, parking in south Philly, at least for this intrepid diner, flat out sucks.  I couldn't find a parking lot nearby and all the street spots were taken.  After dropping my party off I drove around for another 10 minutes before parking a few blocks away.  Not Monsu's fault, but it might make sense to uber, cab, or train down.  Also, I found the portions small for what ended up being a $60/person BYOB evening.  This is the first time I left an Italian restaurant with room to spare, and I ordered four courses...not the chef's tasting menu mind you, but four actual courses off the menu.  Perhaps this is a good thing, as I didn't need to unbutton my pants and loosen my belt, and hey, that's a first for me afer eating Italian.  Finally, while everything is good, nothing really wowed me like what we found in Sicily.
