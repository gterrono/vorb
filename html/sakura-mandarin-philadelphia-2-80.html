    Well, after a recent evening here with a friend I have to report that things have improved, but only just a touch so.  Begrudgingly I have to improve my review, but only because this seems like the ONLY place in CC Philly that makes my beloved Shanghai fried rice cakes.

    Scallion pancakes were also decent, but not the greatest that I've had -- and we were a fan of the Szechuan string beans, but the lack of pork that's usually present in this dish really didn't help.

    Still underwhelming, but damned if rice cakes aren't my serious weakness and I'll be back for more.

    Listed in chinese food, philadelphia

    Environment

    This restaurant is one of the more modern ones in Chinese, in regard to the cleanliness and decor. The furniture isn't in bad shape, tables are glass-covered, and the walls are adorned with framed pictures and paintings, not antiquated and unnecessary car or movie posters. Clean and simple.

    Staff

    The staff were terse, came by enough times to ask to refill our water, tea, and bowls of rice. They were also uniformed. Professional, too.

    Food

    Each table comes with tea (we were served Jasmine). The teapot wasn't a cheap metal one, but rather a porcelain one. Thumbs up for that.

    We ordered the spicy bowl, which is a mandatory minimum of $7 for the base bowl, and 4 orders of $5 addons, which included shrimp, squid, tofu fish, potatoes, beef slices, beef tripe, and a couple more that I forget. That is $27 minimum for the bowl. We ordered 7, for a total of $42. The bowl was a decent portion. We ordered them with medium spiciness. It is essentially a medley of already steamed or cooked ingredients stir-fried with chili paste. Nothing special. With that being said the bowl did fill all 4 of us.

    Price

    The prices are decent to meh. The food is very comparable to that of other restaurants in Chinatown, but are made in a more presentable manner. That really helps. Xiao long baos are 8 for $10. Which is an okay price.

    A small bowl of rice is $3. Stay away.

    Overall

    This is a restaurant about which most people wouldn't have a major pet peeve or complaint. This is one of the better and cleaner ones in Chinatown (see my review on Nan Zhou Hand Drawn Noodle House). Decent lunch prices.
