        Iron Chef José Garces has another winner in Rosa Blanca, a Cuban diner restaurant serving homestyle meals and sandwiches. Aside from the Disney-like Cuba Libré, this is the only restaurant in the heart of Center City serving authentic Cuban sandwiches. I no longer have to drive 1200 miles away to get a taste of Cuba. My friend Chris W and I loved our meals. He started with a hearty bowl of Guiso de Maiz (sweet corn chowder with smoked ham, tomato, and pumpkin) and a chicken empanada and I had the Pulpo en Escabeche (grilled marinated octopus). The octopus was cooked perfectly and the sliced peppers and onions were spicy and tasty. I love the yuca chips. I would've enjoyed an entire entrée of octopus prepared like that. For our entrées, we each had the Masitas de Puerco (fried pork shoulder with tamal en cazuela) with Fufu (mashed sweet plaintains). I didn't know what tamal en cazuela was until I tried it and researched it. It's Cuban polenta! I loved its creamy consistency, a perfect contrast to the crispiness of the large nuggets of fried pork. And, oh. my. God. the Fufu was amazing! We had something similar called "mofongo" at the Rum Bar some time ago, but this is worlds better! There's more than just plaintains in the fufu. I detected corn and pieces of pork as well. Wow!!! A truly extraordinary entrée for a reasonable price.

        i came here for restaurant week earlier this year, just a month and a half after rosa blanca opened. although i came with relatively high expectations for this newest addition to the jose garces restaurant line, i left with mixed feelings about my dining experience.

        first off, i'm not sure what the concept of this restaurant was supposed to be. i loved how chifa used this space to project an intimate and romantic ambience in the past. but rosa blanca seems to throw the entire bucket at you when it comes to sensory stimulation. the front room is very diner-esque, you've got the old 50s style diner booths and bar seating. the back room is more "cuban" in decor, exuding a feeling of tropical paradise amidst dim mood lighting. then you've got the waiter service, all suited up like we're in some 3 star restaurant. way to send a mixed message -- sensory overload to the point of tacky, imo.

        the food was very hit or miss, and portion sizes were pretty small for what you're paying. none of garces's other restaurants are this off-kilter when it comes to balancing price and volume.

        GOODS:
        pulpo - great app. plump and full of flavor.
        churrasco - skirt steak grilled to medium rare perfection. accompanying fries were boring.
        ropa vieja - stewed brisket was very tender. but too much white rice and black beans for my liking.

        BADS:
        ceviche - do not try if you've ever had good ceviche before. this will make you want to cry. has corn nuts in it though, interesting.
        yuca frita - i've had MUCH better. i like the way the fries are stacked though.
        spinach - it's just cooked spinach. why is it so expensive?
        coconut custard - really tart.
        pineapple upside down cake - #mckaylaisnotimpressed.

        pretty much if we talk entrees only, i'd give rosa blanca 4 stars. everything else about the experience really drags down the overall rating though.

      Specialties

      Cuban comfort food, from Chef Jose Garces' dream diner. Combining the laid-back, all-day appeal of a diner with the innately comforting character of classic Cuban fare, Rosa Blanca is the best of Latin and American culinary culture from Philadelphia's top Latin-American chef: Jose Garces.

      History

      Established in 2005.

      Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

      Meet the Business Owner

      Jose G. Business Owner

      Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
