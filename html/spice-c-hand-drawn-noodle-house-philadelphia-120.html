    Wow-- seriously such good depth and well-balanced flavor in their noodle soup broths!

    After hearing from some friends about eating hand drawn noodles in Philly chinatown and being disappointed.. we've finally come to realize what the rave is about upon discovering Spice C for the first time yesterday. These noodles are comparable to the incredible ones in Asia! We are hardcore creatures of habit and were soo happy we ventured away from our usual routine and had a "let's just try it" moment especially after yelping it and seeing the free dumplings w/check in.

    I was nursing a horrible college-style hangover and wanted something soupy on that gloomy/rainy day (woke up too late to dine at my favorite pho spot). But wow I couldn't have imagined a more perfect meal!

    We were lucky and beat the rush so were seated quickly. Very friendly and hardworking staff! The dumplings (nice skin that wasn't too thick and good filling) were surprisingly decent especially with that amazing crack sauce. I didn't think I was up for eating it but my tastebuds wanted more. I opted for a non-spicy House Special handdrawn noodle soup while the bf had the spicy lamb hand drawn noodle soup w/a marinated tea egg. I loveee that Schezwan spicy soup broth and cursed my weak stomach for not letting me order it. The Schezwan spices were actually not too medicinal for once and super well balanced that the egg complemented perfectly. I was obsessed with the super tender braised beef and tendon in my order. It felt soo comforting as if my mom had made it especially for me to bring me back to life.

    I've never seen unlimited amounts of pickled cabbage on the table as a condiment and am sure I would have devoured the whole container if my stomach allowed it. You know it's pretty darn good when you wake up the next day craving it.. I have found another favorite noodle soup spot, yay!

    No words can describe the awesomeness that is Spice C! My son and I's favorite past time is finding little hole in the wall asian restaurants to eat at, and we nailed it here! Spice C is a small, maybe 10 table restaurant in China town. the big draw is fresh in house made noodles, shaved or hand drawn. The difference is shaved noodles are larger chunks of noodle dough, vs hand drawn are long spaghetti like noodles. Same dough, just different noodle. I went with the shaved noodle house special beef stir fry with beef and bok choy. My son had the same but went with hand drawn noodles and soup. We also started out with an order of pan fried dumplings and spring rolls. The dumplings were easily the best I've ever had. the dough was super light and airy, and you can tell the filing was just great beef. the texture on these things was almost like a mix between tempura and a regular dumpling, just insanely good. Both of our meals were just perfect. I'll for sure be back, especialy on a cold day! the only "downside" was we went to use our yelp check in for free dumplings and because we didn't tell them ahead of time weren't able to use it (not a HUGE deal). Other than that, everything else was just perfect!

    We were drawn to this noodle house for the hand drawn noodles. We tried the 3 treasures stir fry, the shaved noodle soup with duck and wontons, and Thai tea bubble tea. The best meal was the stir fry, that was definitely a tasty five star plate of noodles! The sauce had a sesame flavor to it and the noodles did not disappoint. The soup was ok, probably 3 and a half stars. I took off a star though because while the duck was flavorful and cooked well, the bones were left in. No mouthful of duck was boneless which made it clumsy to eat. I also thought the broth needed more chives and lemongrass. I ordered the spicy style soup (there was a choice of two flavors) and dropped my rating because when the bill came, I was charged $1.40 for the spicy oil that was available for free on the table. I recommend the stir fry noodles over the soup noodles, they are slightly cheaper and pack a bigger flavor punch. The bubble tea was standard bubble tea, it wasn't watered down and it was a good, sweet, dessert. Lastly, while the stir fry was delicious the portion was small and recommend an appetizer if really hungry.

History

Established in 2011.

When the owner cooked the first meal of freshly made noodle soup for her daughter after 15 years of separation, the daughter was so excited to taste the authentic Chinese food in U.S., she asked her father, what's "tasty" in English. He told her: "yummy", so the entire meal, she was keep saying: "Dad, this is 'YUMMY'!". that was the first English word the little girl ever learned, and of course, it became the name of the store when this first opened.
When the girl grown older, she went to many other countries, got to tried and learned many fine asian cuisines. When she took over the store, she rename the Store: "Spice C" (Spices by Cecilia) her passion for Freshly made, authentic Asian cuisines, expecially hand made noodle and dumplings. You can find them here to satisfy your taste bug.

My name is Cecilia, I've always had passion for food. I think a great dish makes people happy. What I want to provide to my customers are: quality food that's freshly made with best & healthy ingredients just like what parents will make for us at home :)
