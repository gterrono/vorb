    Been here three times in the past few weeks and each time it's gotten progressively worse, to the… Been here three times in the past few weeks and each time it's gotten progressively worse, to the point that I've started exploring other Mexican joints in the area.

    Visit 1: 5 Stars. Steak burrito for lunch.  It was big, the steak pieces were tender, and the chips with great salsas made this a steal for $7.  Quality comparable to what I'd been eating in the Bay Area for a few years, if not quite what I was spoiled with in Los Angeles.
    Visit 2: 3 Stars.  Got the chicken fajitas. There was hardly any chicken, certainly not enough to justify the $14.  It was chewy. The refried beans were lukewarm, and the rice was stale.  
    Visit 3: 1 Star. Hungry the next week and in the area, thought I'd go back to old faithful: the steak burrito.  This time there was probably no more than 2oz of meat in the burrito, and upon opening the thing up for inspection, I saw that the cheese wasn't even melted or sliced--looked like it belonged on a turkey sandwich, as in still cold and square.  The tomatoes were soggy.  Horrible. Worst burrito I've had in years.

    Verdict: This place is good for quick, cheap meals a la burritos and chips when I'm really hungry, but it's not a high-caliber Mexican restaurant I'll go out of my way to get to.     Read more

    At this point, writing a review for Veracruzana is totally extraneous.  It's got the coveted four… At this point, writing a review for Veracruzana is totally extraneous.  It's got the coveted four stars and tons of glowing, drooling hymns -- but I've noticed some trends in other reviews I didn't experience, so I want to, y'know, add my fifteen cents.

    The inside of Veracruzana is very clean, very bright.  I was actually surprised, since it's a little dingy-grimy on the outside.  The server (a middle-aged man, maybe the owner?) led me and my friend, C, to whatever table we wanted and promptly gave us water, chips and green and red salsa.

    Let's pause.

    The chips were fine, but the GREEN SALSA.  The red salsa has a nice tomato flavor, but the green salsa is spicy and flavorful, with a hint of something out of place (mint?).  At the end of the meal (being broke and shameless), I actually asked to bring home the green stuff, and our server agreed, laughing: "Is it good?"

    Anyhow, I was seriously considering the carnitas tortas (oh fuck I love tortas), but I went for the 7$ burrito (most tortas were also 7$ -- they offer a Cubano for 8$ which is DEEPLY INTRIGUING).  C did as well.  Oops, and I grabbed a Mexican Coke on the advice of y'all Yelpers.

    It's like if Coke tasted good!  It comes in at a pricy 1.75$, but I'd say it's worth it at least once.

    When the burritos came (again, promptly -- I know the place is small, and we weren't there when it was crowded, but our server was so attentive and friendly I have a really hard time buying the bad service complaints), the first thing I noticed was the quality of the tortilla.  This isn't your sad, pale doughy mess.  It had a different (better) texture and taste, with brown spots from actually being cooked.

    We then proceeded to make a variety of noises no one ever wants to hear in an eating establishment.

    The burrito had the perfect proportions of rice, refried beans, raw tomato and lettuce, meat and

    here's the kicker

    American cheese.

    SO GOOD.  The cheese was absolutely amazing when you get a little chunk of it -- the meat was flavorful and moist, and the rice had been cooked in some kind of sauce which added a little extra pop.  Even better, the burrito almost defeated me (I never have leftovers).  I actually had to put it down for a couple of minutes before getting the rest in my belly.

    There is nothing wrong with Veracruzana.  JUST GO.     Read more
