    Went here for dinner on Valentine's.  We had a reservation, we were a little late but still were seated IMMEDIATELY.  Kind of the point of reservations; seems I always end up waiting to be seated at other places, despite a reservation having been made.  (BTW, driving in this area is a pain if you suffer from road rage.) Anyway, the hostess was nice, and our waiter as well.  All the workers came by often, they were friendly and had good senses of humor!

    The mashed potatoes and fried banana were good sides (not sure what the 3rd was, fried dough orsomething...), I just wish there had been other sides coming to the table. I was told you could order some for an additional cost by a friend later, but still thought the guests should have the option of choosing their unlimited amount of 3 sides.  I didn't bother getting up for salad because it was so busy and crowded, that it would have been an obstacle and half to get back to the table with my whole salad on my plate and a smile still on my face.  The only other issue was that about half, of about 6 or 7 options, of the meat that came by was pork.  We don't eat pork, but were still very satisfied with the other options. They had fried cheese and it was amazing, but we only saw that guy once. We requested for some more and one of the busy workers went and got him immediately. Yay.

    Overall, I enjoyed it very much and will return.  I'd recommend to go not so late on a weekday, as opposed to dinner on a holiday or weekend.

    Fogo de Chao provides one of the best restaurant experiences possible.

    My husband is a huge fan of rodizio, so we try Brazilian Steakhouses everywhere we go. Fogo de Chao in Philadelphia is the first one we ever visited, many years ago, and it remains the gold standard for the quality of both the food and service. This review is specifically for the Philadelphia Fogo, but we've eaten at several Fogos and they're all equally fantastic.

    The side and salad bar is always fresh, and there are numerous options. Since the last time I ate here, they've added some hot sides on the salad bar. The cheese rolls are to die for, and the meats are superb. They have more cuts of meat than other Brazilian Steakhouses, and all are exquisitely cooked and seasoned. While everything is delicious, we do have our favorites - it's possible that my husband loves the Picana more than he loves me, and I could eat the lamb and Parmesan-crusted pork all day and all night.

    The service at Fogo de Chao is superb. Rather than having one server assigned to a specific table, there are multiple servers are assigned to sections of the restaurant. This means that there is always someone available to bring new plates, refill drinks, or answer questions, and they are all friendly, professional, and efficient. They're also observant - if you don't accept meat after two or three gauchos have come by, at least one manager-type will appear and ask if there's a specific meat that you're waiting for or if there are any problems. Even with their constant presence, they are not hovering or providing so much service that you feel smothered, which can be a tough balance to strike.

    Runners come 'round with a particular type of meat and it's on a spit. If you're place card is green, the runner will ask you if you want to try. If red, they'll ignore you, as you have indicated you are not trying anything at the moment. When they do come to cut you a piece, it's necessary to assist by stabbing part of the meat so that it can be cut for you. No need to remember this detail, it's covered when you get there.

    Anyhow, this will happen repeatedly until you're satisfied and turn over your red card of surrender. You can request that a certain meat be sent over and a menu will be available as a guide. Typical items include filet, top round sirloin, london broil, bacon-wrapped filet, bacon-wrapped chicken, lamb chop, and more.

    As far as decor, it's a grand dining room. Dress can be as casual - you'll see everything from hoodies to dinner jackets. All will be just fine.

    Dessert isn't mind blowing, so don't feel the need to save room.

    I'm not crazy about the concept and don't indulge often, but when I do, this is where I'd go in Philly.

Specialties

We are an authentic Churrascaria (Brazilian Steakhouse) from Rio Grande do Sul in southern Brazil which features superior service, unrivaled cuisine and a unique dining experience.

You will be pampered by our authentic Gaucho chefs who come to your tableside to carve a variety of succulent meats right off their skewers.  Our featured meats have been slow-roasted over an open flame and lightly seasoned to preserve the unique taste of each tender cut.

Adding to our unique dining experience, you will be treated to a sumptuous, gourmet salad and vegetable bar featuring over 30 items including imported cheeses, cured meats, fresh vegetables and more.  We complement your meal with traditional Brazilian side dishes such as caramelized bananas, crispy polenta, seasoned mashed potatoes and our world-famous cheese bread (pao de queijo).

No meal is complete without trying a refreshing caipirinha, something from our award-winning wine list or one of our decadent desserts.
