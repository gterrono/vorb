    Federal Donuts offer $1.25 donuts and $2 donuts. The $2 donuts are fancier than the $1.25. The $1.25 donuts seemed pretty fancy; strawberry lavender is one of the flavors for the $1.25 donuts. I went with fancier and got the blueberry mascarpone donut. It's a cake donut, as opposed to being a yeast donut. It is also not served hot. It was very visually appealing but tasted only ok. Maybe the donut was too refined for my taste and I'm used to dangerously decadent donuts. I just really couldn't appreciate the donut.

    Now, onto the chicken. I love fried chickens, especially Korean fried chicken. My favorite Korean fried chicken joint is BonChon, so you can use that to gage my taste in double fried chickens. I actually came in at around 11 because based on other yelp reviews, it seemed like the chicken goes quickly. I wouldn't have mind having fried chicken for breakfast ( actually, I never mind) but since I was on vacation, I just had a hard time getting up earlier than 9. Anyway, I guessed I arrived at a good time because they had chicken!

    You have the option of getting dry seasoning or wet glaze or naked (pft. jk!). I like saucy. I got chili garlic bc I thought it would have a kick to it. The fried chickens at Federal Donuts come with pickles and a honey donut ( which I also thought was ok). I got a half order which includes a split breast, thigh, and leg. The chicken was saucy indeed. It also came out hot and crispy. However, I didn't like the glaze. It was too vinegary for me. It was also not spicy.

    I really just couldn't get on board with the Federal Donuts following after my first visit. Perhaps I didn't pick the right donut and perhaps I picked the wrong glaze for my chicken. Regardless, I will come back and give them another shot.

    The space is tidy and has limited seating. It's not really a dine-in spot.

    I fell in love with this place when I was visiting from out of town. I went to college in Philadelphia, and would have been in serious trouble (and serious donut heaven) if this had been open when I was in school.

    These donuts are cake donuts, pretty dense, and satisfying, with interesting flavors. If you tend to like Krispy Kreme donuts that are fluffy and more airy, you may not like these. To me, getting a fresh donut straight out of the fryer is just plain amazing. I could have eaten 3 of these in a sitting, and the stomachache afterwards would have been worth it.

    If you're eating the donut in person, get one of the hot, fresh ones. I loved the cinnamon and brown sugar one - it was like a donut + churro combined in one. For $1.25, this totally blows all the chain donut shops out of the water - it isn't even a fair competition.

    I also tried 2 other flavors that my friends had bought extras of - Blackberry Walnut and Milk-Chocolate Sea Salt. Both of these were off the "Fancy Donut" menu and cost $2 each. The Blackberry Walnut was a vanilla donut with a blackberry walnut frosting / heavy glaze. It was a bit too sweet for my liking, but the more that I ate it, the more it grew on me, and had a bit of a more sour twang to it from the blackberry. It wasn't bad, but there were other flavors I liked better.

    The milk chocolate sea salt was delicious - I couldn't really tell the donut was chocolate  since it was pretty subtle, but the caramel sea salt was the perfect amount of salty and sweet. Sometimes these "sea salt caramel" things are too salty, or have salt crystals that make you cringe when you eat a chunk of salt, but this was perfect. I'd definitely get this one again.

    If you're in Philadelphia, do yourself a favor and go here. 1) Do come early - on a Friday at 3 pm they had run out of donuts completely. 2) Get more than 1 donut, share with a friend or just eat multiple. 3) Make sure to get a hot donut and eat it right then and there.

    I tried to come back here the day after I ate here, but ran out of time. I'll be back next time I visit Philadelphia!

From the business

Cake donuts in interesting flavors and Korean-style, twice-fried chicken sold by the half and whole bird. House-roasted coffee available hot or cold-brewed and iced. That's basically it.

Specialties

Cake donuts in interesting flavors and Korean-style, twice-fried chicken sold by the half and whole bird.  House-roasted coffee available hot or cold-brewed and iced. That's basically it.

History

Established in 2012.

Chef Michael Solomonov and partner Steve Cook of Zahav and Percy Street Barbecue got a wild idea to pair exotic donuts and super-crispy fried chicken. With Bodhi Coffee owners' Bob Logue and Tom Henneman's coffee expertise and professional bigmouth Felicia D'Ambrosio, the team opened the first FedNuts at 1219 S. Second St. in Pennsport, South Philly in 2011.  The Sansom Street shop followed one year later in October 2012.  We have since been cranking out fancy donuts, melt in your mouth hot fresh donuts, and truly badass chicken.  Follow us on Twitter www.Twitter.com/federaldonuts for status updates and fun contests.
