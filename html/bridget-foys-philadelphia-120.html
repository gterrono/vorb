    If you haven't tried Dining In yet - you're missing out. While Seamless and GrubHub do offer free delivery for most of their restaurants, Dining In offers better choices. Unlike the aforementioned online delivery services, Dining In is a service that picks up takeout from restaurants and brings it to you. A third-party provider if you will.

    On a recent night, the GF and I saw a deal for free delivery from Bridget Foy's on South Street. So we decided to check it out - minus the crazy crowds and drunken demeanor that's prevalent down there (not that there's necessarily anything wrong with that!).

    Head House Market Burger ($13)
    Herb mayo, swiss, mushrooms, fried egg

    The burger arrived a bit of a mess, but what do you expect when you're talking about a fried egg and delivery. It's not yet on par with Smokin' Betty's Betty Burger, which is my favorite burger in Philly (probably due to the addition of avocados and pork belly on top of the fried egg). Still, this was a monster of a burger and luckily, I was able to reconstruct it.

    The burger was cooked a bit more than the requested medium, so perhaps I should have asked for medium-rare and it would have arrived medium. While the patty itself could have been seasoned a bit more, the cheese, mushrooms, and fried egg helped to make it luxurious and coma-inducing (a good thing here). The accompanying fries were delicious and well-seasoned, though slightly limp.

    Turkey Burger ($12)
    Cranberry relish, basil mayo, gruyere, alfalfa sprouts

    The GF opted for the turkey burger and you could smell the health coming off the burger as soon as I opened the box. The alfalfa sprouts were a nice dose of freshness. More importantly, the turkey burger was moist. The cranberry relish was the tangy foil to the heft of the basil mayo and gruyere, the dash of necessary naughty to the nice - in my opinion anyway.

    Short Rib Fries ($9)

    Of course, I couldn't help but order the short rib fries when I saw it on the menu. I asked for the short rib and cheese curds to be put on the side so I wouldn't receive a resultant soggy mess by the time the meal was delivered.

    Dining In and Bridget Foy's did a great job in accommodating my request. The mozzarella curds were fresh and unsalted. However, the rich, savory short ribs made up for that - they had a great depth to them. The fries were fresh cut and well seasoned as well.

    Together, the dish was highly reminiscent of a poutine - that oft-acclaimed dish from our neighbors to the North. You've got some crunch from the fries, richness from the short ribs, and fresh clean flavors from the curds. Delicious.

    Overall, if you're ever on South Street or you feel like ordering in through Dining In, consider ordering the burger and better yet, the short rib fries at Bridget Foy's.

    My oh my how Bridget Foy's has changed since my first visit. The restaurant leaves a great first impression- updated seating & decor, quick attention from the hostess, and a neatly updated menu with big-flavor offerings (read braised short rib fries and duck tacos). It's enough to get your mouth watering.

    Unfortunately, the food didn't follow through, even with a very nice and attentive server. My girlfriend and I ordered the fried pickles and lamb meatballs for an appetizer. The pickles were fine I supposed, but lacked a quality, crispy crust. The lamb meatballs may have been from a bag, and the convoluted ingredients didn't jive-- the dish never came together to leave me with a satisfied feeling (a strange provencal sauce over pearled cous cous...I guess?).

    For entrees, we went with a burger and the crispy chicken sandwich. The burger was delicious- the fried egg made it insanely scrumptious. The crispy chicken sandwich was sad. The bread was crispier than the chicken, and it lacked decent flavor- just a big greasy chicken sandwich.

    Last, the fries are pretty terrible unless you like floppy, warm, greasy potato sticks.

    Let's give Bridget Foy's a big thumbs up for their burger, updated style, and new menu. Just be careful of the dishes you choose-- they are either a hit or a big miss.

    TIP: If you can sign-up for their insider program, they like to give $25 gift cards annually.

    Their brunch is SOLID.  One of the best things for me about Bridget's is that they open for brunch at 9 AM!  For an early riser this is great; when I have to wait until 10 or 11 to go to brunch, I might as well consider it lunch... it is no longer breakfast to me.  Brunch at Bridget's is Breakfast!  They have a nice menu, several vegetarian options, though nothing completely off the wall unique.  The portions are large but not disgustingly large.  The french fries are fresh cut, addictingly awesome and come with a spicy mayo.  I had a flat bread there once (I can' t remember the details) that was definitely 5 stars!  Eating brunch on the porch in nice weather is where it is at!  You can watch all of the characters walk by and keep your distance while enjoying your feast.

    I give this 4 stars and not 5 because the decor and bathrooms need some work and although their menu is good, there isn't anything super unique, other than whatever specials they are offering.  If you are in the area, it is a solid spot to get a good meal but if I didn't live close by, it could be a one and done (since there are SO MANY other places to try in the city!)

As we reached the end of South Street, we came across Bridget Foy's. I remember thinking to myself "Hey, I went there last year and it was great! This will be perfect!"

    I liked the whole "pick your table" concept when being seated outside. We chose a table on the middle of the deck which was a four-top only set for 2 people... Question mark? Promised anther setting immediately, we took our seats.
    Our server appeared and asked for drink orders- without a salutation or an introduction of herself. Urgh. She was tolerant with assisting my guest with choosing a beer according to his taste. I ordered a blood orange Bellini, fondly remembering how good they were on a warm summer's evening.
    But, if your menu serves a blood orange Bellini, it should have BLOOD orange. Not just regular orange substitute. If I had been told this upon ordering, I would not have bothered with it. Strike 1.
    Meanwhile, our server was standing at the neighboring table, struggling to open a bottle of wine. Eventually, those patrons took the bottle and uncorked it themselves. After that, she came over and asked my boyfriend if he wanted more soda, but completely neglected my other guest's empty beer glass. (Yes, he did want another beer and would have liked it when everyone else was apparently getting a refill). Strike 2.
    After that disappointment, we placed our dinner orders. This consisted of a turkey burger with arugula instead of alfalfa sprouts, ribs with a side salad instead of coleslaw, and wings with a side of Mac'n'cheese.
    Here is what we got: turkey burger with no greens at all, ribs a la carte, and the wings with an oversized ramekin of Mac'n'cheese. Damn. Strike 3.
    My guest immediately flagged down the hostess/assistant manager and asked for his salad. I opted to say nothing about the turkey burger. During the next 10 minutes that it apparently takes to throw some greens, onion, and dressing on a plate, we were reassured by 3 people that his salad was on its way. When it arrived, there was almost an air of "HUZZAH! ACHIEVEMENT UNLOCKED!" *facepalm*
    Remember that missing setting? yet again, we had to flag someome down in order for me to eat without getting sauce all over me. Upon commencing our meal, we realized that the coleslaw that we never wanted was oddly and unexpectedly spicy. Scrap that. Strikes 4 and 5.
    Beyond that, my turkey burger was really quite good (mmmmm basil mayo!) My companions were satisfied with their other food as well once everything seemed to be sorted out.
    Luckily, we got to use the Yelp check in deal to get a free dessert. We chose the s'mores bread pudding, which was very very good. Croissant, warmed chocolate, and marshmallow.

It seemed as if the server was paying way more attention to the table next to us instead of making sure that we were well taken care of. This really irked me as a move of both cowardice and rudeness. We were skipped on being brought more water- the only time anyone seemed to notice us next was when we had to pay for our meal.
    The food is good, and decently reasonable for location and ambience. The service is (apparently) sub-par and lazy, and leaves me with no desire to return. There are many better and more unique restaurants in this area that I would rather go to, and go to them I will.

History

Established in 1978.

Bridget Foy's restaurant has been a Philadelphia staple since 1978. The business started as East Philly Café.  Owner John Foy, met his wife at the restaurant, they had a daughter named Bridget and changed to name to Bridget Foy's.  Bridget is all grown up and is now running the business!
