    There was some spirited back-and-forth in our household over a four or five star ranking for La Fourno, and after a few days, we've come to an agreement.  

    We were seated at a great high-top table at the front of the restaurant, and our server was not only attentive, but helpful too.  He gently persuaded us to try the grilled calamari.  It was light and refreshing with a strong, acidic flavor and a little char, which was quite unique compared to the typical fried variety.  The fresh baked bread (we could watch them making it) was fantastic, but the dinner salad was not my favorite.  It was overdressed and the greens were shredded outer leaves, which gave the salad an odd texture.

    We both ordered pasta:  penne a la vodka and chicken salsiccia.  I fawned over the latter all evening.  It was an herbed pasta with big, juicy chunks of chicken, sausage, and portabella mushrooms sauteed in aged balsamic glaze and marsala wine.  Out of the countless pasta dishes I've tried, this one was truly unique and absolutely delicious.  I'd order it every time here.  The penne was no slouch either, and my date loved it (we switched plates).  The sauce had a beautiful color, and the prosciutto chunks were a nice addition.  While the "dark side of the moon" dessert is not made in-house, it is locally sourced and quite light and delicous for something that looks heavy and rich.

    When you combine fantastic food with a Groupon, BYOB on Fridays, and enough leftover to get a pesto pizza for takeout due to the deal, you can't go wrong.  Also worth mention:  the entire staff was really friendly, there were fresh flowers all over the place, and even the guy who took the reservation over the phone was super nice.

    Overall, I'm giving La Fourno 4 stars. With regards to the whole experience, our party enjoyed the service, the food, and the atmosphere. It's been awarded one of the best Italian restaurants in Philadelphia for several years, and although its the only Italian restaurant I've been to in Philly, I can say that it stacks up to many Italian restaurants I've been to around the United States.

    Service and Ambiance: La Fourno is small, with very few tables. We did not come during a very busy hour, so I had no problems with the level of noise and there was no crowding. In fact, I enjoyed the music and felt that it did not distract (and rather, complimented) the meal. Shout out to Francis, our enthusiastic and genuinely helpful server. If you go to La Fourno, I hope you get seated at one of his tables because he's very nice and actually added a lot to make our visit fun. He let us take our time, and then he kindly let us rush him as we were coming up at the end of our meal and running short on time.

    Food: The fixed price menu, with ANY one appetizer, ANY two entrees, half a carafe of wine, and dessert was impressive. Great deal for two people. I personally had the veal parmesan, which was also lauded to be the best in Philly. Glad I got it-- I really enjoyed it! The portioning was just right. The texture and taste were spot on, with regards to the veal. Not too much breading. I had a portion of lobster ravioli from my dinner mate and I enjoyed the richness and the amount of lobster in the pasta. The sauce was yummmmmmmmmm.  

    The above are what makes this restaurant four stars. There were a few setbacks, but thankfully, most were not a part of my own personal experience.

    Setbacks: While my personal entree was delicious, the rest of the food was just A-OK. For appetizer, I had a rather unmemorable calamari. Not really fried well, a little too chewy. Marinara a little too salty. The tilapia wasn't impressive, according to one of the girls, and a big bite of it didn't get me interested in it. Same thing for the seafood involtini. Maybe just not a good day for those dishes? The mousse we had for dessert was also not memorable, but perhaps this was our own fault for rushing dinner...

    If I ever have the opportunity to visit La Fourno again, I will.

    Have you ever found yourself sprawled on the sofa on a chilly, lazy evening wishing that you had a piping hot mound of steaming simple carbs adorned with delicious marinara sauce and cheesy goodness?  I have.

    While I recognize that La Fourno is primarily a semi-nice dining in spot on South Street, I know them as my go-to Italian delivery place.  Thanks to Seamless.com, a few quick minutes online brings Italian deliciousness right to my door.  The menu diversity at La Fourno works extremely well for my family.  Their pasta and entree dishes are far superior than any I have tried from other pizza-focused delivery spots, and their pizzas are truly excellent as well.  

    The prices are a little higher than an average Italian delivery, but the quality and portion sizes make up for it.  Delivery from La Fourno is really more like enjoying good restaurant food at home.  The pastas are served with a side salad and garlic bread, and the chicken dishes come with linguini.  My favorite dishes include the Baked Rigatoni and Chicken Parm.  I know that I should branch out, but these keep hitting the spot.

    A few things to note:
    They close on the early side during the week, so make sure to place your order before ~8:30.  Not everything on the full menu is available for delivery, so check the website before you get you heart set on something.  They frown upon (read: prohibit) menu substitutions for delivery.  

    Also, please note that I do do know how easy (and cheap) it is to cook good pasta at home; sometimes I don't want to.

    Otherwise, we had the much-raved about veal parmesan and the lobster ravioli. The texture of the veal was great (crispy on the outside, pleasantly tender on the inside, the overall crisp not overwhelmed by the sauce), but the sauce was tad bit too acidic/tomato-y for us. The lobster ravioli was perfect; creamy and rich, the portion may not look like much, but we had to doggy-bag it, we were so full. We also shared the tiramisu dessert, which was intensely flavored, unlike the soggy cream-and-coffee-liqueur-laden masses you sometimes run across. It really embodied the literal meaning of the word "tiramisu," or "pick-me-up."

    Again, I cannot say enough how friendly and warm the staff were. At the table behind us, one of the waiters was chatting with the family there and eventually they invited him to sit with him--it was adorable how well they all got on. At the table in front of us, the waiter was engrossed in an hours-long conversation with the three teenaged patrons, one of whom told him as they left that he was "one of the most interesting guys [he'd] ever talked to." This place seriously has the ambiance for an amazing time, the people to make you feel all warm and fuzzy inside, and the food to make your mouth water.
