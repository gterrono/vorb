    Mixto is nestled in the Old City section downtown. Not far from South Street or the historical section of Philadelphia. I went to dinner here with a previous visitor and was really satisfied wit everything bout Mixto. The music was perfectly bended with the Latin American theme of the food, drink menu and the appetizers. Definitely you can feel the touch of Afro-Cuban artistry. I am not one to go into my actual order of food and drink. I'm too drunk for all that. No, seriously... Mixto has a a flair with placing the pizazz in the simple. Their menu is not extensive, and the dishes are not exquisite. But, what they have is a blend of traditional cuisine with a "DASH" [Shout out to Kim] of contemporary fusion. The price isn't exorbitant. The Spanish Oil paintings with hanging masks and exposed interior columns give it an authentic feel. The drinks and the quiet trance of the music will delight you. There is an upstairs dining area and an open bar in the rear of the lower level. What I enjoyed most was being on the street level right in front of the windows. Under the candle lights, conversing and "mix"ing it up among the flow of the night.

    Cons: it would take 40 mins to get a cab.

    Let's talk about flavor!  You can never go wrong with Cuban, Columbian, and Caribbean Food. Mixto (just like the name says it) is a mixture of all these cousins.

    The place is located near downtown Philly.  The place inside is very nice, a very modern Caribbean feel, dimed lighting and good soft music.  And our waitress was wonderful, recommended dished and drinks, and was very friendly.  Mojitos, were definitely on the drink menu, they have over 15 flavors.  I had a coconut mojito- it was marvelous.  It was well mixed to the point where I couldn't even taste the alcohol.  It even had shredded coconut to garnish the drink.  

    The menu had an extensive selection of dished, chicken, beef, pork, seafood, and vegetarian.  I was excited to try the Ropa Vieja, which is a Cuban beef dish with tomatoes, bell peppers, onions and other ingredients.  However, everything else was very appealing.
    My dish was nothing less than what I expected, full of amazing flavors, a generous portion- especially for the price.  I feel pretty comfortable saying that my foodie friends, Pauline and Heather were also very pleased with their meal.

    I am definitely coming back to Mixto when I come back to Philly.

    The ceviche has been broken down from the previous tall glass into 3 dishes on a tray. Working from left to right, the trays contained octopus with clams, scallops, and shrimp - marinated in a nice citrusy juice with diced mango and tomatoes, all topped with nacho chip bits for that salty/sweet/crunchy/soft contrast that we all go nuts over. This was the freshest octopus I've had in a long time, and nothing like that chewy crap you sometimes get served at a sushi joint. The clams had a briny taste that came through the citrus nicely, and the scallops amazingly sweet and succulent. On the downside, the shrimp were slightly tough (too much time in the sauce?) and the tomatoes out of season - but who would dare pan such a fantastic ceviche because of mediocre tomato bits!?! Not I.
