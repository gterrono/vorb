        Listed in The Best of the Best

        I generally have a bad time in Philadelphia when I'm in town. A lot of it is from repressed memories. I'm also a NY Mets fan, so naturally I have to hate the Phillies. But they are a good team.

        Anyway, I was in Philly earlier in the week and I actually had a decent time. Sure, I was down there for work, but the times I wasn't working were pretty fun, mostly because my friend and I went to DiNics for lunch. I remember the Man vs. Food episode in Philadelphia and this was the first segment of the show. Luckily for us, we were only a block away from the Reading Terminal Market, so I knew we had to go there. The line was the longest of any line in the market; that had to be a good sign. As we were patiently waiting, we saw them preparing the meat right there and watching the guys prepare everyone's sandwiches. It was pretty hard to stay patient, though. Of course, we got the roast pork sandwich with the sharp provolone and broccoli rabe, just as Adam Richman did on MVF. We hurriedly made our way to the convention center to grab a seat outside. We just couldn't wait to dig in. The first bite was glorious. Everything in that sandwich just worked together. It was a little messy with the juices soaking through the bread, but totally worth the mess.

        Now to weigh in on the debate about which is a better sandwich: the cheesesteak or the roast pork. It's hard to say. I haven't had a cheesesteak in Philly in almost 15 years and I certainly didn't go to any of the major institutions (Pat's, Geno's, etc). While I do like a good cheesesteak, I feel like I would need to make a trip to South Philly just to make a decision. But the roast pork sandwich from DiNics is pretty damn good. Might be kind of hard to top that.

        While many herald Dinic's as one of the best sandwiches in the city, rivaling Philly's most holy temples of meat, cheese, and bread, I hesitate to crown Dinic's roast pork among the elite. I've eaten at Paesano's far too many times and made too many late night trip's to Steve's to give Dinic's roast pork sandwich a spot in the Philly sandwich pantheon. Despite this, Dinic's creation is admirable and, although not among the best, quite good and likely the best thing you can eat at Reading Terminal Market.

        Honestly, what makes the sandwiches at Dinic's sing to your tastebuds isn't the pork. I found the pork juices slathered on the sandwich to serve no other purpose than to soggy the classic Philly style roll, and the pork was rather ordinary in terms of flavor. The fact that it's simmered adds no textural enhancements to the eating experience. Rather, Dinic's topping offerings carry the sandwich to near-fame. Sauteed broccoli rabe and sharp provolone outshine the relatively lackluster pork and offer the palate a brilliant interplay between sharp, creamy cheese with bright, slightly bitter and peppery greens. Putting Dinic's version of these two toppings on a baloney sandwich would send it soaring to unforeseen heights, and these adornments are what make the sandwich truly shine. Too bad you have to shell out a little extra cash for them.

        Dinic's roast pork doesn't quite live up to the hype due to ordinary meat. But the greens and cheese are astounding, and make a visit to Dinic's worth it if you're around Reading Terminal Market.
