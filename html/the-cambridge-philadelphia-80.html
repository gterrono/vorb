    YES! A spot that's open for brunch without an hour wait, and every time is a good time. This place is open for lunch and dinner too, but I'm going to focus on brunch.

    First, it's managed by the folks who run Hawthorne's, so you know it's good. Second, it's a completely different take on brunch. Instead of the usual pancakes, french toast, eggs, eggs, and more eggs, The Cambridge focuses more on dishes that'll introduce you to some new and delicious things. What might those things be?

    Pork Belly Sliders (PBLT Sliders on menu), WITH QUAIL EGG - for anyone who likes pork belly, this is a no-brainer. Three carefully curated sandwiches, each with a pile of pork belly served with arugula and tomato, a dab of aioli, and adorned with a single, fried quail egg, all on a perfect little brioche bun. Served with home fries. Get some.

    DUCK Egg and Hash - when was the last time you had duck egg? If you haven't, let me share: it's similar to your chicken's egg, but the white is much meatier and the yolk is a bit thicker. This dish is simple... a single sunny-side up duck egg that sits atop a chorizo hash, next to charred asparagus and a piece of multi-grain toast. Simple yet satisfying.

    The Porker Burger - a nice take on Philly's famous roast pork sandwich, this burger has all the same fundamentals: broccoli rabe, sharp provolone, long hots and brined, hand-ground pork. Mouthwatering (seriously, I am salivating just thinking about it).

    There are a bunch of other great options too, but I'll stick with those three for now. The extensive beer menu is available for brunch, and unlike Hawthorne's, The Cambridge focuses more on draft beer. It's a rotating selection of 20+ crafts, so don't come here looking for Bud Light.

    As far as decor, the inside is rustic; lots of wood  - very cool. The outside, yes there's an outside in the back - is very nice and open during brunch so long as the weather cooperates.

    I'll be here at least one Saturday or Sunday morning every month - come find me!

    I've always been a little confused by the 3.5 star overall rating at Cambridge. To me it's an easy 4.5, maybe a 5. I'm rating a 5 now and will likely write an updated review in a few months to reflect whether it is indeed a perfect or an almost.

    I think sometimes yelpers don't review based on what type of spot they're at and instead compare across all places they've ever been. Personally, that's just not my style.

    For example: if I'm eating at Lacroix, I'm holding it to a higher standard. I expect the service, food, ambience and overall experience to be not only much better than the place I ate lunch at today (if you're wondering, it was a food truck) but also better than a similar place (like Vetri, perhaps). I won't give Lacroix 5 stars unless it's very good at what it does.

    Now, if I'm at Cambridge, I don't expect to receive the same level of food or service as I would at Lacroix. But I do compare my experience to one at, say, Pub & Kitchen. Aaaaand that lil diatribe brings me to my review:

    Having lived a block from Cambridge for years I was stoked to see it come in to replace Tritone. When it first opened it was definitely a little overwhelmed and not quite ready for all the people (that's the excuse that seems the most likely).

    Now, a while after it first opened, Cambridge is a fantastic neighborhood establishment. Highlights include every single beer available on tap and the bloody mary. The brunch is really fantastic. The food isn't quite perfect, but everything I've had has been either delicious or almost there.

    Long story long, I like Cambridge.

    Well now THIS is an order of French fries.  The first words that came out of my mouth, especially seeing the moderate sizes of some of the dishes around me, were, well, inappropriate to write in a Yelp review.  The bartender placed down the mound of spuds n' gravy in front of me, gave me a smile, and told me to enjoy my poutine.  And this, I sure did.  

    The French fries were so perfectly flavorful, clearly hand-cut and seasoned just enough to retain their earthy natural flavor without overpowering it.  Not a single fry was over or undercooked.  They needed NOTHING.  Not sauce, not ketchup, not cheese: NOTHING.  But oh wait, what's this?  They're slathered with heaps of sliced smoked mushrooms in brown gravy, which is melting a hefty number of fresh cheese curds right into the fries without much regard for these perfect potatoes.  

    By my tone, you may be thinking, is he actually implying that the addition of cheese and gravy is a bad thing?  The answer is no - but is a tentative "no".  I ate several forkfuls of the gravy/mushroom/curd/fry combo before having the novel idea to try each on its own and see which I preferred.  To be honest, what I preferred was a forkful of fries, healthily dipped in the gravy, and with no mushrooms.  Don't get me wrong; I LOVE a good 'shroom.  Perhaps it was the smokiness, but my taste buds were slightly overwhelmed by the fungi when mixed too liberally with the fries.  Overall, though, the curds were perfect and the gravy just salty enough and delicious.  But, though the flavors of the mushrooms worked great with the gravy - and on their own - they got cold feet when walking down that great French fry isle in the sky.  If I didn't spend half the summer '13 in Quebec, I wouldn't be such a discriminating judge; however...

    Fries alone 10/10
    Dish 8.5/10

    Overall Notes:
    Though I don't find myself on South St West too often, I sure dig me some Cambridge from time to time.  Always great beer list and friendly service.  Happy hour is reasonable too, $4 pints from 4-6.  Overall I really enjoy their food and never have been disappointed.  But this review is about fries.  And they've got some of the best I've had in Phila (fries you can always get; poutine was on special this evening).
