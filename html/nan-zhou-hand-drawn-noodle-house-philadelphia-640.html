    In my constant hunger for decent Asian food in Philly, I find myself often returning to Nan Zhou Noodle House.

    The hand drawn noodles here are authentic and flavorful. I regularly order the beef noodles, consisting of (surprise!) chunks of beef, broth, and noodles. The broth is gamey and delicious, the beef is tender, and the noodles are well-made and have a nice strength to them.

    I also like to get the sesame noodles - these are the same noodles as you'll find in the beef soup, but doused in a rich, slightly salty sesame sauce and served cold.

    Another mainstay of my occasional lunch visits is the intestine platter. I find that the boiled intestine goes very well with the picked radish it is served on - it's not too chewy, yet has good flavor.

    In summary: The food is pretty good, the waiters and waitresses are friendly and attentive, and the prices are quite reasonable. I have no doubt that I'll be back soon.

    Not that this place needs any more hype, but it really is as good as the reviews claim! This isn't one of those knock-off hand pulled noodle joints that serves packaged noodles with MSG-base soup. It's the REAL DEAL. Every time I visit Chinatown, I find myself inadvertently walking in and eating a bowl of this yummy goodness (ok...maybe it's intentional). Come hungry because they don't skimp on the noodles. Warning: it's A LOT of noodles.

    I personally prefer the hand drawn noodle soup over the stir fry. The soup broth is light and flavorful. It also comes with pickled veggies and spinach. The only downside to getting the soup is it's hard to take home leftovers. It feels like a waste when I can't finish all the noodles because it's too delicious to throw away! You don't have this problem with the stir fried noodles, but I found them to be a little on the greasy side. It doesn't have the lightness of the noodle soup. Regardless of which style you choose, the noodle is the star of the dish. Soft chewy noodle with a slightly irregular thickness and just the right denseness. All this for under $10 a bowl!! The shaved noodle is also very good for the flat-noodle lovers out there.

    In terms of appetizers--the aromatic mixed platter is probably the best bang for the buck. You get the spicy pig ears, marinated beef, and spicy beef tendons (I think).

    In the heart of Chinatown on Philly, this place specializes in hand drawn noodles (known as la mian). Their most popular dish is beef noodle soup, however what makes this place unique is their quality of fresh noodles (thick/thin hand drawn or shaved noodle) and their thinly sliced beef, not the traditional thick chunks of meat like in Taiwan cuisine. They also pride themselves in not using MSG.

    Appetizers (cold)
    Aromatic mixed meat platter (pigs ears, thinly sliced beef, tendon) $9.95

    Dumplings
    Shrimp, Pork & Chives Dumpling 8 pieces (aka san xian jiao) Steamed $4.75

    Hand Drawn Noodle Soup
    Beef Brisket Noodle Soup with Thick Hand Drawn Noodles (aka niu rou mian) $6.75
    Noodle with Pork Soy Sauce (aka zha jia mian) $6.25

    The aromatic mixed meat platter was your usual meat platter that you can get in most restaurants in SGV, and honestly, it was way overpriced here. The dumplings were nothing special, just simply as the ingredients state, shrimp, pork and chives. Okay, so down to the handrawn noodles. They were definitely good and you can taste the freshness of the handmade noodles, as well as see the differences in shape of the noodle thickness. I chose thick noodles because I prefer them over the super thin noodles for my beef soups. The broth was light and seasoned with chinese herbs, though not long enough due to its watery consistency. However, I do still applaud them for making their own noodles and the thinly sliced beef were not bad, a bit bland, but tastes just like homecooking. Overall, I'd say that this is great place for a large bowl of niu rou mian for a rasonable price for chinese food in philly. The service was good too. Parking is free on the streets on Sundays only and in certain locations (Race Street). Read the steet signs!
