    First to Review

    The entrance makes you feel like you just came to a take out place, but the further you walk to the back the more you feel like in a tavern in Greece. I believe the place is family owned and run. Last time we went with a greek friend who just moved to Philadelphia from Athens recently and he thought it was better than the average restaurant back home in Greece. So much about authenticity! Its location makes it a great place for the locals living behind the art museum and you always have the feeling there are many regulars, people know each other. Simple, straight forward, great food, you are not rushed and it is definitely good for a group of people. Sharing is easy and delicious. 4 stars only, so that no one expects the hype. If you are looking for a low key place with great, authentic greek food in a family atmosphere, add the fifth and go - but don't block my table! BYOB.

    Zorba's is by far the best Greek restaurant in the city. Being Greek myself I have tried them all - Dimitri's is still my fav restaurant in the city but I do not consider it truly Greek - more Mediterranean.

    Zorba's on the other hand is pure old school Greek. My father was born in Greece and every time we go to Zorba's he ends up in the kitchen talking 100 miles in hour in Greek with everyone working there. If you are a meat eater you have to get the char grilled platter - it is meant for 2 but I always order it myself just so I can bring a bunch home. Even the bread they bring to the table is amazing. Being that it is BYOB the checks are always delightfully lower.

I've researched on yelp & eaten at a lot of places in Philadelphia, but do I return the favor to my fellow yelpers & put a review up? No, cause I'm a bloody bastard. I'm sorry people. But, for Zorba's I'm fighting my laziness & A.D.D. to write something up.

    So last Saturday my girlfriend's cousins where coming into to town & she wanted us to take them around & bring them out for dinner. Fuck me. So immediately dreading this, I knew we had to go to a BYOB so I can drink away the awkwardness of this forced social situation. One problem solved. Next problem, where can I take a bunch of Indians out to eat? Also being Indian myself, I sure as hell did not want to go out for Indian food. Italian? Na, that's the usual alternative for Indians. Hmm, wait I know! Greek, perfect. Being, a former resident of Fairmount I immediately knew Zorba's was the place to go.

    So at 8 the 6 of us arrive at Zorba's with 2 large bottles of wine & 12 of Heineken, ready to do damage. After the wine & beer were opened & the delicious bread placed in front of us, we started working on the menu. For appetizer we got the octopus which was fresh & delicious, though the ladies at the table were a bit nervous at first to try it. Next the main course, 2 orders of Greek Pasta, 1 Shrimp Poseidon, 1 Arni Kapama (Lamb Shank), & 1 Charcoal Sampler for 2. De-fucking-licious, all of it. Honestly though. I sampled a bit of everyone's dishes and they really were all amazing. The only comment anyone made was that the food could be spicer, but someone was bound to say that when you go out eat with Indians though. But true to the quality of service at Zorba's the server brought over some spicy feta cheese for the table. I mean the food was already at 4.5 stars before the feta was brought, but after? Boom! Automatic 5.
