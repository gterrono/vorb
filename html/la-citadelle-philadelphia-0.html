    First to Review

    Simple decor and never really packed. But don't let that fool you, this place has the best (!) cappuchino in town. I believe the owner makes one of the best coffee in the city, he really knows how to use his machine. Said is from Algeria, so his coffee is like french coffee and this is not for the faint hearted amongst us... The sandwiches are rich, tasty and superfresh (if the bakery delivers the bread on time...). If this is too much for you, go for the chocolate croissant (that looks and tastes like in France...) and their homemade lemonade. That boils down to: If you look for great coffee you will not go wrong. Seriously, you can trust me on this one!

    I really love this place: I stumbled in the other day and was immediately won over. However, you have to think small-scale. The place is tiny, the food offerings are limited, and--well, mostly tiny and tiny. Much like an inspirational Disney character or comparably lame reference point, though, it makes up for its size with sheer charisma/ gumption. (That's right, gumption.)

    When I went in, the man behind the counter was carrying on several conversations with regulars. The vibe in the place was notably warm. He good-naturedly joked with me, and by the time I left, we were on a first-name basis. This is the kind of coffee place that holds regulars because of its considerable charm. I know I'll be going back, and it made enough of an impression on me for me to yelp about it.

    My small coffee was delicious, too, and my sugar cookie was good. It didn't seem pricey at all: the coffee and cookie together were $2.

    If you want your coffee place to be hip/ see-and-be-seen, then la citadelle probably isn't for you. If you enjoy coffee and small-town friendliness, get thee to 16th and pine.

    I used to love La Citadelle and then I moved to the west coast. Sayyed is a sweetheart, he makes the best Cappuccinos I have had. (A close second is this girl at Groundworks on Sunset in LA ).

    Given I used to live right down the block from here I used to stop by to pick up coffee in the morning and also used to study here. It took a while after Belly Maternity moved to become La Citadelle.

    It is very nice to stop by on a Saturday morning get a coffee and read a magazine or just chat with Sayyed, he is a great guy to talk to, he is originally from Morocco and was with La Colombe till he opened his cafe. The interiors of this place are spacious, simple and with the windows are really bright. If you want a really cozy spot sit in the back, very quiet and secluded.  I used to study in this place or meet a friend to chat, it is perfect for that.

    The coffee itself is well La Colombe coffee, so if you like that it is similar. Although I think the coffee here tastes better, is not as watery as it can be at La Colombe. There are some great Pannini sandwiches for those hungover lunches on Saturdays (or any other day ;-o). The music in this place is a great eclectic mixture of  world music. The customers here were mainly the local crew not the 'interesting' bunch frequenting La Colombe. La Citadelle is the non-pretentious version with customer service and friendliness.

    This a great cafe which has the flavor of Pine street, among the nicest streets in Philly. I miss La Citadelle :-o(

    You already know how McThrill is when he stumbles upon a coffeehouse that he hasn't been to yet so I'm not going to go that far into detail this time around. I came by 16th and Pine to scope out Omoi finally. But since it looked more than a little closed I decided to accept here and a cup of hot chocolate as next best compensation. It definitely delivered, and the old dude (owner?) working the counter was a real gent. Throw in the facts that it was very spacious, hooked up art books and copies of the metro to read through, and free wi-fi and it definitely offered one big happy package....

    So your probably asking yourself "Why the 3 stars McThrill?" and I'll gladly tell you. The only thing really giving it the Three was the La Colombe name on the coffee that can make so many indie coffeehouses in Philly un-unique. Don't get me wrong I love the coffee, but there's something about the La Colombe houses that doesn't make them stand out as much as a place like say Good Karma Cafe that hooks up the free trade. But whether it's the lack of space in the coffeehouse (Hey Loftus and Philly Java), or it has quirky issues with the service (Hey La Colombe on Rittenhouse), Most La Colombe houses have other things holding them back that La Citadelle didn't have. So while I was sitting there I totally felt that if it didn't utilize the name, and hooked up something like a free trade organic French Roast, than La Citadelle would have been the total package. Fly free little birdie, fly free!
