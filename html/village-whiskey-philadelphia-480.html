    Village Whiskey's burgers came highly recommended from friend and fellow Yelper Shakila M., and am I glad I took the plunge!

    My Village Burger with cheddar was cooked to perfection...fresh, tasty and just so darn flavorful.  Kinda wished I'd asked for extra napkins to handle its juicy goodness.  And being the lover of french fries that I am, the duck fat fries w/ sides of cheddar and ketchup for dipping were essential.  Different, yet thoroughly delicious.  

    Since I knew from other reviews just how tiny this place is, I set out early - arrived around 5:30pm on a Monday and snagged one of the last tables.  Within 20-30 minutes, all tables were occupied and the bar was full.  Service was great, no complaints at all here.  

    As expected, the whiskey drinks are hard core - but wine and beer are available as well.  I'm not much of a whiskey drinker (this may have been the first time), but I tried the Champs Elyseés which was superb.

    Truly a local favorite, but the crowd was so eclectic that I didn't feel like an out-of-towner or an outsider.  Think cozy casual - come as you are.  Don't miss it.

    As a bourbon-drinking Southerner who loves a good burger, I'd been wanting to visit Village Whiskey since I first learned about it a year ago. When I visited with my wife and some friends, we were not disappointed.

    Having eaten "famous" burgers from across the country, I can tell you that my meal was as good as other famous places. My burger with cheddar, bacon, and a fried egg was served medium, just as I requested (and trust me, this isn't always a given). The brioche bun added extra crunch and butteriness to the taste. My only complaint is that I prefer my egg yolk a bit runny when on a burger (a personal preference, really).

    And yes, the duck-fat fries are as good as everyone says.

    We also ordered pickled green beans, which came served in a mason car and were surprisingly good - less vinegary than most pickles, and even those of us who don't like pickles found them enjoyable.

    One suggestion - Village Whiskey does not take reservations. However, they do take names at the door as early as 4 PM. When we arrived at 6:30 on a Saturday, we were told the wait for a party of four was going to be three hours. Instead, we had early enough that there was plenty of room to stand and eat at the back bar. Had we shown up at 7:00 or 7:15, it would've been a different story.

    So if you plan on eating dinner here, stop by before you start your evening and give the hostess your name. You may have to wait a few hours, but at least you'll get a table. It's worth the hassle.

They take your name and cellphone number and actually send you a txt message when your table is ready, so you can leave and wander around the surrounding area while you wait for your table.     Saturday night was entirely different.

    We quickly found seats at the bar as a trio was leaving. And then we waited. Over the next 15 minutes we observed one bartender make a single cocktail. The other bartender was a bit busier but also only to happy to engage the party next to us in a lengthy discussion.

    Our only interaction with either bartender--despite sitting at the center of the bar--was when the less busy bartender threw a menu on the bar in our general direction. After several more minutes the bar back produced napkins and water. After several more minutes we concluded that we would not be getting any level of attentive service.

    During this time only one other couple arrived. So the bar was hardly overwhelmed. More to the point, at no time did either bartender so much as acknowledge we were there or make any of the usual "I'll be there in a minute" entreaties.

    Meanwhile, at one point the volume on the tunes suddenly jumped from enjoyable background ambience to noise.

    We left.

    Full disclosure: we have been ardent cocktail drinkers for the past twelve years. We have enjoyed a wide range of cocktails in some of the most notable lounges around the country. Compared to many other cocktail bars, the drink menu at Village Whiskey is limited and unimaginative.

    The owners of Village Whiskey should visit Death & Company or Silver Lining in NYC or The Varnish in LA if they want to see first hand how to run a proper saloon. There's more to a successful joint than a tin-roofed ceiling and vintage vests. You may choose to spend your money on this work in progress but we'd rather wait for them to get their act together. There's no acceptable excuse for such inconsistent service.

Specialties

A ground-to-order Angus Beef Burger, a Veggie Burger, a Bison Burger or an Vienna All-Beef Hot Dog with all the trimmings are each served alongside duck fat French fries and Sly Fox cheddar sauce.  Burgers are topped with Village Whiskey's house-made thousand-island dressing, Bibb lettuce, beefsteak tomato and a potato-beer bun, with optional toppings including bacon, caramelized onion, truffles, artichokes and a selection of cheeses.

As one of the most comprehensive collections of its namesake spirit available in Philadelphia, Village Whiskey features a veritable library of 80 to 100 varieties of whiskey, bourbon, rye and scotch from Scotland, Canada, Ireland, United States and even a few Japanese options.

History

Established in 2009.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
