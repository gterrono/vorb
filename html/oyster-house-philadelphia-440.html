    Oh Lordy, I just spent $104 on oysters & clams on the half-shell, shrimp, a half lobster, and 2 glasses of Sauvignon Blanc and it was worth every penny. I love seafood. Have I ever mentioned that? I do. So, so much.

    It all started with a unit on mollusks when I was in kindergarten at this awesome Montessori school where we also modeled clouds with shaving cream and also with marshmallow cream on cinnamon graham crackers. We studied clams, oysters, and other mollusks, culminating our unit by eating raw clams. I don't think schools, even independent ones, could get away with that anymore, and what a shame, because I developed my love of seafood singularly from that experience. But, I digress.

    I'm visiting Philadelphia for the first time and had to have seafood right away. Yelp never lets me down, leading me to this place. Just so you know, they do not take reservations unless you have a big party. It was a relatively short walk from my hotel and I was happy for the walk--there is no better way to see a new place. I sat at the bar, had nice conversation with other patrons around me. I started with a mixed green salad (great vinaigrette), then ordered a glass of Sauvignon Blanc and the sampler, which included 6 oysters, 6 clams, 4 shrimp, and half a lobster. I ordered a second glass of wine and another half dozen oysters and I am perfectly full right now. My oysters were from Cape Cod (2 kinds, salty), Chincoteague, and British Columbia (sweet and a little fishy, but in a good way--I've had these before-they are some of my favorites). One of my oysters was called the something Cowboy and was enormous (and awesome). I don't remember where my clams were from; two of them were ginormous as well (and a beautiful light pink color). You're asking too much of me to tell you what variety each oyster and clam was. I'd suggest looking at their online menu. The shrimp were large and the half lobster was awesome.  Be sure to ask for extra lemon--they are a little skimpy on the lemon.

    This was the perfect meal for me tonight. I'm so glad I went there. If you love a good raw bar, this is the place for you. Btw, I got there around 6:30 and left around 9 pm. There were seats when I first arrived, but it quickly filled up, with a sizable line inside and outside the entrance when I left.

    The Oyster House is consistently top notch.  Service, food quality, atmosphere, everything.  I've been to the Oyster House at least three or four times the past year.  We ate here last Saturday night at the oyster bar.  The staff are professional, courteous and attentive.  Their drinks are imaginative and delicious -- try the housemade rosemary lemonade for a nonalcoholic option or any one of their creative "oyster shooters."  We had a dozen fresh oysters which were phenomenal.  I had the grilled lobster with herb butter & the sauteed greens (awesome); my date had snapper soup and 6 huge cocktail shrimp.  We shared the pistachio ice cream for dessert.

One decor element is a bit kitschy and a bit fun -- oyster plates adorning the walls, artfully lit with small spotlights.
