    I am normally a big fan of Maggiano's.  I have used them in multiple cities for group dinners because they usually have banquet rooms and because their family style meals are perfect for getting people to talk with each other.  Oh yeah - and the food is usually very good for chain Italian.  

    I visited their Center City location today for a banquet event for approximately 50 people.  The mingling part of the program was good with servers walking around bring drinks to attendees.

    When we sat down, the appetizers were tasty - although the calamari was significantly saltier than I remember it being in the past.

    The entrees were also OK.  However, they brought out two of the entrees on plates that were so hot they could not be passed.  This is normally not a problem, but when your concept revolves around customers passing plates of food around the table - this is an issue!

    The big problem was during dessert, which was the same time as the program.  The staff had an awful lot of clinking of plates and glasses going on while they were trying to organize dessert and coffee - I thought I was in some sort of a china concert.  Surely, this is not the first time a presentation has been going on at the same time they are serving dessert - do your assembly work outside the room.  

    The bottom line was that this was an average experience.  I know Maggiano's can do better than this and have experienced it myself.  I look forward to writing a future review when the experience is on par with my normal experience.  

    Although there is discounted parking available after 3:30pm in the garage next door, that does not help you for lunch.  However, my lifehack for the day is that discounted parking is available if you spend $10 at a Reading Terminal Market vendor across the street - and we know that they are ALL amazing.  Today, I bought $12 of baked goods at a bakery in the market and got the equivalent of a $16 discount on my parking.  Cheaper parking AND delicious baked goods - for the win!

    My experience here is twofold. I was in the area for a conference. My research team, and other associated folks, were on the verge of starvation after 10 hours at the convention center (and only snacking on a $5 bag of chips). My advisor (gotta love him) said, "Isn't there a Maggiano's somewhere around here?" This meant he was paying. We all gave a hard "YES" in unison (the grad school struggle smh).

    So we all walked over and packed into the small waiting area (boy was it small!) that doubled as a bar area. The guys we were with are all over 6'1, so to save us from getting bumped into, they wrapped around the women and made a double circle.

    The wait. First of all, we made reservations...and still had to wait 15-20 minutes for the servers to ready the large table area. I don't know why it would take 15 minutes to push three tables together, but ok. And I say this because, there wasn't a lot of customers in the area our large group was seated.

    We were finally seated, all 10 of us, and immediately wanted to order (if it was only that easy). Remember how I said I was with my research team?? Yeah, so every decision we make is for the betterment of the group. So naturally, we decided on a communal dinner (which not so interestingly enough, is one of the variables I study...I digress), which included: Calamari fritte, mozzarella marinara and caesar salad (app); chicken piccata, beef tenderloin medallions, and grilled salmon lemon & herb; finally, apple crostada and the New York style cheesecake for dessert.  

    Appetizers: The calamari fritte and ceasar salad are always a win, but the mozzarella marinara was a complete loss. The cheese was hard, crusty and chewy.     So 3 stars for Maggiano's  for standard food and standard service, well less than standard. Our waiter went MIA for about 15 mins and apparently we were all too focused on the bread and drinks in our hands to notice smh. Academics.

Overall good experience and better than what we (my lunch dates were also part of my dinner dates the previous night) the night before. (.5 stars to make this rating 3.5 stars)
