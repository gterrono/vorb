    **My first review**
    Zavino Pizza is a home run!!

    Their pizza is probably one of the best pizzas I've ever eaten. The toppings are all high quality and the arrangements on the menu complement each very well. The Kennet and the pistachio pesto pizza are incredible. Check in to see specialty pizzas that they put together from new meats, cheeses, wines, and other tasty items.

    I am very picky and particular about what a good pie should taste like (my dad had a shop for 30 years and he had some of the best and most consistently great tasting pizza i ever had.)

    Zavino's pizza has good cheese, vegetable, meat and herb choices to add to the pie, but the REAL key is the rediculously hot (over 1000 degrees F, where most shops fire up around 600 F) ceramic oven. It's a true mediterranean brick or clay style pizza oven- not a brick facade gas stove. The heat and the ceramics work magic on the crust. It is simultaneously chewy and crispy, salty and sweet, and oh so smoky. I haven't gotten past the pizza to try the rest of the menu yet.

    The place is fun to visit. The management are more insanely obsessed with pizza than i'll ever be. Just start talking to them about BRooklyn, or Trenton or Naples... they been there.

    Happy hour is pretty awesome too. Good value.

    My second visit to Zavino came a year and a half from it's soft open and I can say this place has only gotten better with time.

    We sat outside being it was a nice summer night and I wanted to entertain out of town guests with the mix of people that frequent the corner of 13th and Sansom. Our server arrived not long after we were seated and took our drink order. He helped recommend a really great white to go along with our food selection. The rest of the table ordered from the well crafted beer menu.

    My boyfriend was so amazed at the idea of a $15 meat plate sampling we couldn't help but order it. We had some questions about the "baby jesus" cut of meat to which our server had all of the answers to. It's kind of an interesting story behind the name, so ask if you order. When the plate came out we were so astounded by the portion we thought there was a miscommunication in our order. There's no way that much quality meat is so inexpensive (insert that's what she said here).

    We ordered the polpettini with the ricotta stuffed veal meatballs - um, WOW. Our non-veal friends shared the Kennett to which created the same reaction. The crust is crisp and light, the toppings fresh and inventive. Although they are artisanal pizzas they were generously sized.

    Again the check came and we were amazed by how well priced everything came to be. The meat plate was indeed only $15 so I would recommend that to any omnivore as a starter, and the total bill for 4 people to eat and have a drink each was slightly about $80. Not bad for a nice dinner stop in Midtown Village on a Saturday night.
