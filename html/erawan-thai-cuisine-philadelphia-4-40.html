    I have been to Erawan before and was impressed with the authenticity of their food but the restaurant is a bit out of my way, so when my friend suggested that we go there for his birthday, I was pleased to have a reason to revisit Erawan. Five of us decided to go there for a late dinner on a Saturday night and the place is not packed, in fact we kinda have the place all to ourselves when we got in around 9.

    We ordered calamari, fish cakes and crunchy shrimp rolls as appetizers. The calamari for good but not a bit run of the mill, I always love their fish cakes with has thai basils inside the ground fish patties and they are as delicious as I remembered. The crunchy shrimp rolls are cute with a shrimp wrapped in wontons skins and deep fried. They are searing hot, so beware.

    For entrees, we shared the ultimate chicken pad thai, a seafood massaman curry and a red beef curry. While the chicken pad thai looked very authentic, it lacked something taste wise, but it's still pretty damn good comparing to most pad thai I've had in the city. The red beef curry was quite spicy, and I'm a bit of a wuss when it comes to spice. While my friends loved it, I find it a bit heavy on vegetables and light on meat. I think I only have a thin sliver of beef. I like vegetables but where's the beef!?! The seafood massaman curry deserves its own full page write up. This is the ultimate curry dish if I've ever had any. It was spicy, creamy, nutty, sweet. It's a bit light on the seafood but the sauce is truly amazing.

    For dessert, we ordered a fried banana roll and their sticky rice with mango. Again, any rolls you order will be piping hot, which is dangerous and it kills your taste buds. The banana rolls comes out with two dipping sauces; honey and condensed milk. If it only can cool down to a more suitable temperature I'm sure I'd like it a lot better. The sticky rice and mango is another showstopper. The sticky rice is creamy, sweet and a bit savory, like a rice pudding but with more texture. It goes perfectly with the sweet ripe mango pieces on the plate.

    The service is a little awkward, at the end when it comes to the closing time, they brought out their vacuum and turned on a harsh fluorescent light when another table of patrons are still eating. Brought out the checks without asking for desserts. Maybe it was getting late and the waitstaff wants to go home so customer service became their lesser priority but hey, the food is still good and I fully recommend this restaurant to people who likes authentic Thai food. I doubt that there's another place in the city do better Thai food than them but I'm willing to be corrected.

    I ate here several months ago, but despite my almost weekly cravings for Thai food, i just can't bring myself to go back. My boyfriend and I ordered fresh spring rolls to start- one of our favorite things to get at Thai restaurants even if they're not officially Thai. It was a hot summer night, and we wanted something cool and refreshing, but these disappointed us. Besides the rice paper being chewy and tough, the veggies and noodles inside were bland and dry. They tasted as if they had been made at lunch service...the day before. Next we ordered our Thai staples- Pad Thai and beef massaman. Now I like my Pad Thai on the drier side so that the noodles sort of stick together, and it should have a a light tangy sauce reminiscent of tamarind with a healthy dose of ground peanuts on top. It's true that I may be pickier than most about my perfect Pad Thai, but this dish couldn't even fulfill one of these requirements. it was wet and saucy, and the noodles lacked texture. Now it did have a slight peanut taste, but that was because it was more along the lines of a pile of rice noodles with a too-sweet peanut sauce dumped on top. Honestly, had my eyes been closed, i probably would not have been able to distinguish between it and another Thai noodle dish there. The beef massaman curry suffered from the same muddy flavor. It was coconutty but lacked the depth of spice that I prefer. Not to mention that it was 80% sauce and 20% meat and potatoes.

    The decor was simple yet inviting, but the food just left a lot more to be desired. I'll probably try it again some time for lunch since that seems to be where the bargain is...let's just hope the flavor is there too.
