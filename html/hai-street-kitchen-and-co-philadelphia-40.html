    Pros: new idea, fresh ingredients, tasty food
    Cons: little seating, limited beverage selection

    I came here over to the weekend to grab lunch with a friend in town from New York City, who especially loves new things that she can't necessarily get there. We weren't starving and I almost considered splitting one, but I'm glad I didn't in retrospect. They are a good portion that doesn't leave you too full or wanting more.

    I ordered the Slammin' Salmon that included: nori, brown rice, lightly seared medium-rare salmon, lettuce, cucumbers, red onions, pickled mango, fried garlic flakes, and gojuchang sauce. The mango was a little too sweet for me; I prefer slightly tart, under-ripe mango when pickled for that little crunch. They were also a little heavy handed with the sauce, which made the roll a little soggy, but the spicy and tangy flavors of it were delicious.

    The burrito concept of it comes from the rice + meat + veggies, which I really enjoy. It feels like a sushi roll on steroids. To stick a healthy image, the store only sells their own iced teas (green or hibiscus) and lemonade, along with boxed water available. I asked for a cup of iced tap water and they were fine with providing that though. The biggest downside is that there are only five or six stools available for seating. It was pretty dead on Saturday afternoon, so we sat near the window to people watch, and we were in and out quickly but this is definitely not a place for a meeting or groups. The bright side is that Rittenhouse Square is close enough that you can eat in the park.

    This place has plenty of buzz so far, so I hope that they continue to do well. I'll definitely be back to try some other rolls, like the chicken katsu one, but I might get the salmon again since it is so good.

    PROS: Fresh ingredients, interesting concept, tasty
    CONS: Pricey for lunch

    Spending close to $10 for a tasty but small salmon sushi-rito (or a large salmon sushi roll, depending on how you look at it) is not a bad deal. At a normal sushi restaurant, you'd likely order a much smaller sushi roll for around the same price.

    But burrito-fying the concept of sushi leads consumers (me) to associate lower quality to the roll on a subconscious level, I think. You get a higher quality and higher-priced NORI-wrapped Asian burrito, in contrast to the cheaper, larger, carb-intense Chipotle burrito.

    If you have some extra money to burn and a small appetite, definitely hit this place up!

    There is so much hype about this place that a lot of my friends that tried this before I did didn't really have great things to say about it because of their high expectations. I didn't lower mine, but I was still generally satisfied. This isn't a life changing meal, but it is a 'fresh' take that is great to perk up your lunch rotation.

    It's a pretty place, and it's a little reminiscent of a chipotle/qdoba set up. Clean colors and lines, and aesthetically pleasing.

    I got a hand roll filled with white rice, tataki salmon, spicy mayo, pickled mango, carrots, spring mix, cucumber, and crispy shallots. I did not taste or feel the crispy shallots at all, so I guess it didn't matter or not if I got them. I am a huge fiend for spicy mayo, so I'm glad I got the spicy mayo, rather than the gochu jang spread - which would make your roll taste more like hwedup bap than a sushi roll. I didn't really care for the pickled mango, but everything else was great. I'm glad the tataki salmon was just ever so lightly seared, so that I still got that raw texture and taste.

    I also got the mexican grilled pork - their signature version - and it was awesome. I'd get it again, most definitely.

    I laughed a bit when I saw how they got the rice onto the seaweed wrap - they use a machine! Having worked at a sushi restaurant as a server, it's just a little embarrassing to watch that happen. It's not too difficult to just spread rice on a a sheet of seaweed, but I guess the machine is part of the gimmick? Or to standardize the amount of rice being put on? I think that's the part that slowed down the process the most.

    The size was a little disappointing, but I was sufficiently filled and there's always the 'jumbo' option. I'll be back hopefully. I like this place.
