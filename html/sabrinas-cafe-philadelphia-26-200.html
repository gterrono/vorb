    For my 31st birthday, I wanted to take a trip to Sabrina's for breakfast, so we left our little burbclave and traveled down I-95, navigating the labyrinthe between the highway exit and south Philly, to perch upon a well found parking spot. We met another couple there around 8:30 or so and promptly got a table.

    The simple fact that I didn't have to wait long (probably because our friends got there first and nabbed it) was exceptional for a popular place like this. They didn't even really have to wait either. Trick is to go earlier than 9 or 9:30am (when it started filling up).

    I couldn't decide what I wanted because everything sounded delicious. The waitress we had was awesome, very friendly and helped as best she could to get my decision made. The atmosphere was quaint and artsy and I felt cozy next to the front window with the little homey potted plants and 80s music playing in the background.  I totally felt like Ali Sheedy having breakfast 25 years ago.

    So I decided to get two things...

    I ordered the huevos rancheros, thinking I had loved it when I ordered it 4 years ago, and I was very disappointed. The plate was ridiculously huge and most of it was the spiciest chili ever.  It was so spicy I couldn't even eat it. I ate the eggs sitting on top of the indedible pile of tortilla chip particle board and insanity sauce chili and the guac. The guacamole was also spicy, which I expected THAT to be, but it was not refreshing on top of the rest of the spiced food and I couldn't find a lick of the possibly cooling cilantro sour cream. I would never order this again, I don't know what happened to that delicious thing I ordered 4 or 5 years ago, but this monster certainly wasn't it. Bummer for my birthday.

    At least the other thing I ordered, a half order of the loaded challah french toast was very tasty. Although next time, if there is a next, I would order it without all the cream cheese and banana trimmings. It was just too much and I think I don't like cream cheese in my sweets anymore. I had to scrape it off. Otherwise it would have been quite good.

    Everyone else loved their meals. The men loved my french toast too and said I was crazy for not liking the cream cheese stuff until they started eating too much of it and agreed, yeah maybe they did put a little too much. lol
    My husband's firecracker chicken was freaking delicious and I wished I would have ordered that! I think it was a dish with a limited time on the menu though. :(

    On the upside, I really liked our waitress and the coffee was excellent. While we were down there, we checked out the south Philly market, got some veggies and fruits and I found a great little herb and spice shop.

    Full review with Pictures in the Blog

    Turning northward the next stop on my agenda would be a proper breakfast at Sabrina's Café.  Arriving shortly after 9am on a Wednesday I was spared the famous excessive line and was given the option of seating outdoors or inside - with 70 degree weather, a nice breeze, and the active Italian Market just to my East I decided to sit outdoors in the shade.  Greeted by an impressively attentive young female server I ordered coffee (a rather standard blend which was filled instantly and repeatedly) and browsed the menu for a few moments - identifying two items that sounded excellent I inquired if half orders were acceptable and when I was assured it was I opted for a half order of each.

    Seated, sipping my coffee, and browsing my maps I waited for approximately 20 minutes before my options would arrive - and arrive they did, excessive in both size and presentation.  Long winded in words and ingredients, my first selection would be the Killer Quasar Stuffed French Toast with Challah, Stuffed with Mascarpone, Blueberry and Vanilla Crunch, Topped with Peach-Vanilla Syrup and Seasonal Berries...and to be honest, it was Killer.  Almost a bread pudding in consistency the buttery Challah was crispy outside and custard like within while the mascarpone lent its characteristic flavor and texture.  Breaking up the smoothness were fresh strawberries and crunchy bits of blueberry granola and the whole dish was topped with a "syrup" that was more akin to a compote of pureed peaches.

    The second choice, Magnificent Magnetar Pancakes with Buttermilk Cakes Topped with Polenta-Orange Cake Crumble, Dried Apricots, Orange-Honey Berry Compote, and Walnut-Orange B utter was good, but certainly not as great as the French Toast.  Again using an amalgam of summer textures and flavors I will definitely note that the flavors were outstanding and well thought out despite the multitude of ingredients.  What was lacking in these cakes, however, was the pancakes themselves - doughy, thick, and uninspiring on their own - I actually left the lower pancake on the plate after consuming the entirety of the top pancake and all its toppings.

    Paying the modest tab and leaving a sizable tip (the service was much better than it needed to be, something uncommon for a "hip" restaurant) I made my way back to the market for more browsing before turning north en route for the Mutter museum.  An excellent start to a trip that would be full of great sites, people, and food.
