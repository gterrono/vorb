    The Dandelion is the most recent addition to Stephen Starr's restaurant empire.  Michael G. and I went on opening night which just happened to be New Year's Eve.  I was very impressed by the service and level of calm despite the circumstances - Starr Restaurants definitely has this down to a science.  

    The restaurant is many-roomed and multi-storied. The first room you enter is the bar which features some conversation-inducing decor (perhaps a good first date place?).  We made resos, but had to wait briefly at the bar while our table was readied.  I had a scotch and honeysuckle concoction (can't recall the the name) and he had a Pimm's martini of some sort . Mine was particularly refreshing.  The bar was the only place where I noticed some stress.  The two bartenders seemed to be in the weeds a bit and I don't think they ever stopped moving in their rush to create cocktails.  

    On our way up to our table we passed through a living room inspired area complete with a roaring fire place - I wanted to linger, but we were led onward and upward which may prove difficult for tipsy patrons or those wearing precarious footwear.  We sat in a dimly lit room that reminded me of an English dining room.  There was also another bar up there.  

    Our waitress was lovely and she was able to joke with us while providing excellent service.  We were given a basket of tasty bread (sourdough and honey wheat) with butter served on a slate (which made a somewhat unpleasant noise when scraped by the butter knife not unreminiscent of a nail on a chalkboard) .  Michael had a butter lettuce salad with pomegranates, walnuts, apples, Stilton cheese and a zingy dressing, yum.  I went with the Bolognese with over-easy duck egg.  A perfectly hearty winter dish, but Michael's lamb shepherd's pie was the winner.  It was rich and comforting and absolutely delicious!

    Throughout the night management stopped by to ask us how we found things and we felt very welcomed.  Overall it was an excellent experience and I would definitely go back. Enjoy!

    3.5 stars.  Went opening weekend for dinner--things seemed to be going smoothly, though, so I don't think it's wrong to review them so early on.  When we arrived, the table wasn't quite ready, so we sat at the bar.  I got a beer flip, which was pretty tasty.  The decor was pretty well-done, and as we wandered through a few other rooms on our way to the table, I liked the distinct atmosphere in each room.  We ended up in what I'll call the Dog Room--there were pictures of dogs all over, dog books, dog figurines.  I liked it.  The table was awkward to sit at, though--it took some wrangling to position my feet around the table's feet.

    I shared the root vegetable and cheddar soup, the Cumberland sausages w/ mashed potatoes (whatever they called what was essentially bangers and mash), the rabbit pie, and an order of the chips (thick-cut French fries).  The soup was rich and slightly sweet.  The sausages came with two sausages, and the rabbit pie was somewhat small.  At $19, it wasn't the greatest value.  The fries were our favorite thing.  They came with what I think was a homemade ketchup that tasted a bit more like tomato sauce than ketchup--I liked it, though.  Overall, the food was decent to good but not outstanding.

    We also shared 3 desserts, the sticky toffee pudding, the ginger Guinness cake, and the treacle apple and raisin tart.  It was probably overkill, but I've decided to work on becoming fatter this year.  The treacle tart was our least favorite, although it was perfectly respectable.  The Guinness cake was strongly spiced and a bit bitter from molasses, which might not be for everyone.  The sticky toffee pudding, though, was great.

    A few other notes--the butter with the bread and butter was salty and good, but too cold.  We had the choice of complimentary fizzy water.  Air conditioning seemed strong--my friend wondered if it might be an attempt to emulate a drafty pub.  We had to wait a bit longer than we would've liked a few times, but nothing too bad, and I'll chalk it up to getting the kinks worked out.  Our server was helpful but seemed a bit too apologetic and deferential.

    Overall impression--reasonably good food at reasonable-ish prices.  Total bill, not including drinks, for an app, 2 mains, and 3 desserts, came to about 40/per person, including tax and tip.  The novelty of the concept isn't as important to me as the quality of the food and the value of the meal, so I'd come here when I'm in the neighborhood and looking for a place to eat, but it probably wouldn't be a destination dining spot.

Specialties

London's culinary revolution comes to Philadelphia with this eclectic English pub.
