    Ravioli? Holy Cannoli!

    This place carries oives, fresh mozz, oive oil, bread, etc. but I recommend you get all that at Di Bruno's or Claudios.

    What you really come to Talluto's for is their fresh pasta. They have freezers that hold their freshly made ravioli, cavatellis, penne and sauces.   These are incredible.

    HINT: For the adventurous chefs, just ask for a sheet of pasta to bring home, and then cut/mold your own pasta at home! (It will make you feel more accomplished, trust me).

    While on previous visits to Philadelphia's 9th St Italian Market, I never had the opportunity to visit this store. I finally had the opportunity to visit today!

    The retail floor of Talluto's is not large, they pack plenty of Italian goodness into a compact space. The line to check out is rather long, but the wait is definitely worth it. It's a difficult task trying to figure out what kind of pasta you want to take home, since the selection is so wide. They also have an assortment of pre-prepared Italian food to go.

    We settled on some chicken Parmesan to go, along with a very expensive bottle of imported balsamic vinegar. Just in time for Easter, we were also able to buy a Panettone cake imported from Italy.

    So impressed with the selection and prices, I'll make it a regular visit on future visits to the Italian Market.
