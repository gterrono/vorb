        This place used to be a 4 or 5 star for me, but over the past few years it has steadily gone downhill. Don't get me wrong, I still enjoy the food for the most part.  Their mussels, while once the best mussels I had outside of Belgium, are no longer as good.  I have gotten several pots where several mussels were not opened all the way, and the general quality isn't as good.  The burgers are not as good anymore.  The other items on the dinner menu are fine, but nothing spectacular.  In addition, the service is a total crapshoot.  They tend to be extremely inattentive, even when it's not especially busy.  It can take a while to get refills of water, coffee, or even order another beer.  You really just don't know what you'll get with the waitstaff, so don't come here if you're in a rush.

        On the bright side, I happen to love their brunch items, especially the Green Eggs and Ham (scrambled eggs with ham and pesto - yum!).  On a nice day, it is a real treat to sit outside and people watch.  Their beer list is also pretty good and they always have a nice rotation of drafts.  I would gladly recommend their brunch, and I think it's a great place to go for a leisurely Saturday or Sunday.  I certainly enjoy having it in the neighborhood.  It's just not as good as it once was.

        Listed in Philly Gastropubs

        The Belgian Cafe is the sister bar to the ever-so-popular Monk's cafe in center city.  I LOVE this place, and will continuously return.  Why?  See below :)

        PROS:
        1.  Hundreds of craft bottled beers.  Not as many on tap (maybe 10?) but who cares because all the taps are pouring something rare.  You will NOT find Yeungling, Guinness, or a nasty light beer on tap here!  
        2.  Food specials.  They usually have something special cooking up that is not on the menu.  Ask your server, I usually  notice the specials written on the chalkboard outside on the sidewalk.  I had a delicious pizza once with brussels, and instead of dough crust, it was cauliflower crust!  How cool!
        3.  Atmosphere is your typical Belgian style pub.  I wish there were some high-top tables in the bar area, so you can still sit down near the bar even if the bar is full, but oh well.  There are still tables in a separate dining room.  Also, there's tons of Outside Seating during the summer months on the sidewalk.
        4.  Location is 2 blocks from Eastern State Penitentiary, which is awesome if you're showing friends or tourists one of the coolest attractions in Philly, you can take them to this yummy pub afterwards for food & drink!

        The Belgian Cafe is one of Philly's best Belgian beer bars. Along with Monks Cafe in center city, Tom Peters and Fergus Carey have been 'serving fine Belgian ales since 1985.' The food menu is simple, 'healthy and hearty' serving up eight different styles of Mussels. My favorite is the 'DeKoninck,' North Atlantic mussels served in small or large pot with caramelized leeks, apples, Swiss cheese, & DeKoninck Antwerpen Ale. Delicious!! If you had mussels at one of your local italian delivery spots and were poisoned or just discouraged by the ill prepared dish, don't turn your back on mussels forever. The Belgian Cafe will change your mind for sure. Located in the museum district (Fairmount) it's the perfect spot to sit outside on its beautifully tree lined streets or take a seat in the dining room that showcases amazing art nouveau era inspired artwork and flourishes painted by local artist, Andrew Wrigley.

        Now for the good stuff:
        The bar is what most beer loving nerds flock to this place for.  It's ornate hard wood, inviting colors and Flemish tapestries draw you straight to the stool. Now open the menu and you'll notice there's 7 extra pages. Those are for the 200 or so different Belgian beers and others from around the world. Yes, it can be intimidating trying to order a beer that you've never had before let alone can even pronounce but don't fret, the bartenders are there to serve and have a tremendous amount of beer knowledge so don't be afraid to ask questions - be adventurous! The Belgians have been brewing beer for over a thousand years, before the crusades even, so take the time to understand some of the complexities and different styles of real beer. Much like wine, beer can be just as sophisticated, complex and pretentious. You just need the right guide to introduce you to the world of real unadulterated beer and beer brewing. The Belgian Cafe is that place.

        The food:
        The food is good. I always get the same thing, the Delvaux - chicken breast sandwich with beer brined Belgian cheese and daikon radish sprouts. I wish they would warm or toast the bun and maybe give a little more mixed greens with it, but it's always good, I just think it could be better. As for entrees, there are a few very nice dishes. The Pork Schnitzel, Hanger Steak and Pan Seared Lamb Burger are three of my favorites. I would put more effort into the menu, maybe introducing some true talent in the kitchen, upping the sophistication and price point or just moving with the trends. The weekday lunch crowd is never really that busy - but it could be, I'm sure. I've never met the owners but I have a feeling they just want it to stay the same, it's working, the good is good, the beer is great, and I'm sure they're profitable. That's all that matters and they should be proud to be serving up a beer and an education for the past 28 years.

        Overall:
        I went to temple university and lived in Fairmount for a good amount of time during my stint there.  The Belgian Cafe was my spot. The bartenders know me by name and are always friendly. The serving staff is inexperienced, young, and probably just working for books or paintbrushes but they know what they're doing. I highly recommend this place for the weekday lunch or dinner. Something casual and unpretentious. A place you take your dad to catch up, drink and have some mussels. The Belgian Cafe rarely disappoints and always delivers.

        Happy eating Philly!

        k.david
