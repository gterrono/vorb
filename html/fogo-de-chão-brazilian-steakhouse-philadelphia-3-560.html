    We arrived at 7:30 PM on a Saturday night and the place was packed! There were people waiting outside front. I thought, luckily, I have a reservation. But nope, we still ended up waiting for a table.

    the cheesy bread is orgasmic here!! We may have not gotten our money's worth in meats but we definitely did in cheesy bread. The bread always came out hot and the cheese inside was still soft. Every time our bread bowl got empty, a waiter would quickly notice and replace it. Probably their ploy to get ya to fill up on bread instead of the meat, but I don't care! If I had a last meal on earth, this cheesy bread would definitely be on the table.

    oh my gosh this banana was such a warm welcomed break from the meat. The banana was sweet and warm. We had 2 other side dishes (fried polenta and mashed potatoes) which aren't worth mentioning.

    My first meat plate consisted of bacon wrapped filet mignon and beef ancho which is the prime part of the ribeye. The meat was tender and very flavorful. And what's awesome, is that they bring their meat skewers to you and ask you for your preference in degree of done-ness and they cut it for you on the spot.

    This plate had some lamb, pork loin, pork sausage and of course some more bacon wrapped filet mignon. I unfortunately did not take a picture of my other plate because it had gotten kinda unappealing-looking with all the meat juices everywhere, but I must say that the garlic steak was amazing!  All in all, this was a great dinner for a special ocassion.

    We went to try the new bar menu that's being featured on all Philly food sites.

    While we didn't have a great experience with the food, I want to say that our bartender was very friendly and accommodating. He made the best of the situation for us. I felt bad for him because it seemed like management has not fully informed their staff about the new bar menu. And with the kind of attention it's getting on the internet, they should get things sorted out with that...starting first with actually having copies of the bar menu for the customers and the bartenders.

    When we sat down and asked for a bar menu, the bartender told us that they did not have physical menus; they just had these little plastic cards on the bar that say that they offer small plates, but the cards don't even say what they offer. He told us 3 options, but he didn't mention beef skewers, which I had seen on the bar menu listed on their website. So I asked if they had the beef skewers and he said no. While he was making our drinks, I decided to pull up the bar menu from the restaurant's website and show him that it mentioned beef skewers. He said that he had never seen that menu before; nevertheless, he was kind enough to put in the order for us - beef skewers, shrimp skewers, and beef sliders.

    When the food arrives, the beef skewers were ice cold and featured three shrivelled little gray cubes of meat. They promptly removed them for replacement. The shrimp skewers were hot and cooked fine, but they were very, very spicy. I don't know if they're always like that, and maybe they're just not our taste. The beef sliders were excellent and I would definitely order those again. When the replacement beef skewers arrived, they were hot, but the meat was not very tasty. It tasted burned from the grill marks and there was no seasoning on them. The meat was not nearly as delicious as the meat on the beef sliders.

    Overall, we were a little surprised by how unprepared the restaurant seemed with respect to this bar menu that is being so heavily featured in the Philly food blogs. With the exception of the beef sliders, the quality of the bar food seemed inferior to that which you receive in the main dining room, and it also didn't seem to be prepared with much care. We got the feeling that the cooks are not too happy about having to prepare these bar plates and would rather just focus on the main event in the dining room.

    We were so excited about this bar menu. If we knew that we didn't always have to get the huge meal in the dining room, we would stop by far more often. I think a lot of people would. But the taste of the meat at the bar was really disappointing, and seemed like a major afterthought for the kitchen. The restaurant may get this sorted out, but for now, we think it's better to splurge for the main dining room.

    Fogo de Chao is the best steakhouse I've been to, hands down. As an avid red-meat-eater, I find myself constantly craving steak so a Brazilian steakhouse is perfect for me!

    I've been here a few times in the past. The first time, I did not enjoy my experience solely because of a single waiter who came by and flipped my card back to red TWICE because I told him I wasn't waiting for any particular meat (I was waiting in general for something new to come by). HOWEVER, I have not had any bad experiences since (and haven't seen that waiter again, thankfully!).

    Dinner at Fogo is amazing: the meat is perfectly seasoned, the staff is quick and friendly, and the salad bar is wonderful (though I prefer to spend most of my meal feasting on meat). My favorite cuts are the Top Sirloin, the Filet Mignon (it also comes wrapped in bacon), and the Ribeye. You can ask for whichever temperature and the staff will accommodate you.

    Furthermore, the sides are to die for! Try not to get too caught up eating these if you're looking to make good on the hefty $50 dinner price but you cannot leave without at least trying the pao de queijo (Brazilian Cheesy Bread) and the fried bananas.

    If you want a delicious (non-alcoholic) drink, try the Brazilian limeade. It's a drink made with limes and sweetened condensed milk.

    All in all, I love this restaurant and I highly recommend it!

Specialties

We are an authentic Churrascaria (Brazilian Steakhouse) from Rio Grande do Sul in southern Brazil which features superior service, unrivaled cuisine and a unique dining experience.

You will be pampered by our authentic Gaucho chefs who come to your tableside to carve a variety of succulent meats right off their skewers.  Our featured meats have been slow-roasted over an open flame and lightly seasoned to preserve the unique taste of each tender cut.

Adding to our unique dining experience, you will be treated to a sumptuous, gourmet salad and vegetable bar featuring over 30 items including imported cheeses, cured meats, fresh vegetables and more.  We complement your meal with traditional Brazilian side dishes such as caramelized bananas, crispy polenta, seasoned mashed potatoes and our world-famous cheese bread (pao de queijo).

No meal is complete without trying a refreshing caipirinha, something from our award-winning wine list or one of our decadent desserts.
