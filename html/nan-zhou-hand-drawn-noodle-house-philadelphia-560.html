    I am a fan! Yes, I really like this place ever since a friend took me in 2008 or 2009. We would refer to the place as "shaved noodles" even though we prefer hand drawn because shaved noodles is just too chewy and hard to pick up with chopsticks. The old location when we used to go was small and super crowded for most of the day. But because of it, it brings about authenticity and you somewhat travel to a location in an Asian city with good cheap food.

    What I like about this place is the freshness of the noodles and flavorful broth. The soups are just hearty and plenty for its price. My favorite to get is always sliced beef noodle soup. A friend of mine would always eat the noodle with pork soy sauce, which is also good. The menu is worth exploring considering the strange name for things like "U Toy  with Oyster Sauce." I did asked what it was but the lady had trouble explaining it to me.

    I am happy they relocated to a bigger space, but sometimes I wish they would accept credit card, especially when I'm around the area and do not have cash on hand. So I would often have to go to the other Nan Zhou noodle place next to the fire station. :/

    I wanted to eat Chinese food in Chinatown and found this place with so many reviews, many of which were very positive.
    I put the address in my phone, pretty big sign, so it wasn't too hard to find.
    Our server was not too friendly, but I wouldn't call her rude. I have never had oxtail before, so I just had to order the oxtail noodle soup. Our food came out pretty quick.

    The soup was unlike anything I ever had before. I would compare it to Pho, but had less strong broth, different noodles, and lots of bones (meat was still attached to the bone, so I had to eat around it). As long as you are aware of the bones, they are big enough to easily eat around. The meat was extremely tender and flavorful. The noodles were a wonderful consistency. I liked it without adding any condiments, but did add chili paste, just because I like extra spicy (I think that's what it was). I remember it being somewhere around $8, so a great value for the price. My friend got a hot vegetable noodle dish and said it was very good.

    I had a wonderful experience here and would definitely be back if in the Chinatown area again.

    Nan Zhou Hand Drawn Noodle House is one of my favorite restaurants in Philly. The food is very cheap for its portions, and most of it tastes amazing. To me, it really is Chinese noodle soup comfort food.

    Both hand-drawn and shaved noodles are good; it's just whichever type of noddle you prefer. As for noodle soup or noodle stir fry, I usually get the stir fry for a more filling meal whereas I get the soup if I want something more refreshing. My favorite would definitely be the seafood stir fry; there is so much seafood in there!

    Definitely grab the dumplings as well. They compete with the dumpling houses you find in New York. They are cheap, big, and SUPER GOOD!

    Now a more negative note, the service is inconsistent. When it's busy, say good bye to it and welcome fustration; the servers aren't all too proficient in English and they'll start rushing and forgetting things. When it's not, then they are very nice and they do a very good job!

    Note: When it's busy, don't expect to have your own table! This place has the traditional "share the table" policy, so if you aren't comfortable sitting with complete strangers, then don't come when it's busy!

    Pros: Cheap, Good!
    Cons: Communication with Servers might be Hard

    Recommendations: Seafood Noodle Stir Fry (Both Shaved and Hand Drawn), Dumplings (Pork and Chives, fried or steamed)

    And yet again, Yelp does not disappoint.

    Heading into Philly to check out Jeweler's Row with a girlfriend, we wanted to find amazing Asian cuisine. This was a little walk away, but the reviews sold us immediately.

    Staff and service here is prompt and friendly, and they don't steer you wrong with recommendations.

    We puffy heart this place. I've already gone back with my husband and son and nommed yet again--bonus my 3 year old loved the noodles as well. Best part is a giant dinner for the 3 of us is barely $20 and we have leftovers to take home. Love.

    I've been wanting to try Nan Zhou FOREVER and last week I finally got my chance. I was upset that it was 90 degrees out since I hear the noodles in broth is the moneymaker. My worries were quickly whisked away as we entered the heavily air conditioned interior (i'm convinced they keep it cold for that exact reason). It was pretty full on a Thursday night and I figure that's always the sign of a good restaurant, but luckily we were seated right away. They menu is fairly straightforward - appetizers, noodles in broth and stir fried noodles. We asked the waitress for recommendation and she said she preferred the hand drawn noodles (long and thin like spaghetti) over the shaved noodles (thick and chewy) but didn't really seem open to giving any more guidance after that.

    I went with the seafood noodles in broth with shaved noodles, and my friend had the seafood stir-fried with hand drawn. The dishes came out fairly quickly and let me tell you, their portions are not small. The noodles in broth were awesome, save for two things. First, the menu listed it as scallops, shrimp and crab meat, but the crab meat is actually imitation. I guess I was silly to think otherwise for a $7.50 meal, but next time I will stick with just shrimp. The other thing was that the broth was good but could have benefitted from an extra kick of flavor. A healthy dose of sriracha helped this problem. My friend ended up pouring soy sauce all over her noodles which led me to believe hers could have had more flavor as well.

    Overall,  this was an awesome, filling and cheap meal at a clean restaurant in chinatown. I will definitely be back for my noodle fix.
