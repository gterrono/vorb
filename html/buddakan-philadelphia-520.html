    Still, Buddakan gets rated not just as one of their favorite Starr Restaurants, but favorite restaurants in Philly!...I don't get it.  First off, the NYC location does a much better job with the food.  Second, there are so many better fusion or fine dining options in Philly.

    Food Review
    Crispy calamari salad.  Salad was ok, dressing is nice and light. Calamari is fried and batter was too thick and oily with a slight burnt oil aftertaste.  Continental does a better job at the same salad.

    Edamame raviolli: house speciality, it's really good with a very creamy and smooth filling, my only complaint is there's not enough on the plate and you need to order at least 2 dishes if you plan on sharing the appetizers with the table.

    Lobster fried rice: wouldn't know, it never made it to our table.  It was ordered, it was on our bill.  Chalk this one up to mediocre service.

    Szechuan braised short rib:  Rich and filling.  It is served ontop of a long roasted bone, making the meat portion look small, however you'll have a hard time finishing the whole dish,  Nice crispy outside with a fatty and moist interior that falls apart on your fork.  I can't imagine anyone not liking this dish.

    Pan seared sea bass: Average, personally I think it had a slight fishy note to it, but the rest of the table liked it.

    Service Review
    Hostesses at the front are warm and greeting and quick to take and later return your checked coat.

    Waitress was scarce and didn't check back with us after the food was delivered...otherwise she might have been told we didn't get our lobster fried rice.  Bus boys seem to be the hardest working in the restaurant as 2 minutes after finishing something, the plate was being removed from the table, and my glass of water never got below the halfway mark.  Starr should give the busboys and water boys all a raise because they're carrying the slack for the lazy waitresses.

    Restaurant Review
    The decor is getting a little dated, might be time for a slight refresh.  My date asked for a black napkin so she didn't get lint on her black dress and no one could find one.  Seriously?  I expect a higher end restaurant known for it's nightlife and scene where more than half the room is wearing black, to have black napkins onhand for these requests.  Also, the bathrooms still use the cheap papertowels you'd expect to find at a bus depot.

    Overall, it's a solid 3 star night out.  It won't be your best experience in Philadelphia, but you won't be disappointed if you are just going in for a nice dinner.  I woudn't go for a special occasion or to impress anyone, but the ravioli dish will keep us coming back every month or two.

    Listed in PhillyBestBYOB's 3-Stars

    It's hard to believe that, after almost 3 years of living in Philly, this is my very first visit to a Stephen Starr restaurant (actually, I've been to Talula's Garden, but that's more Aimee Olexy than Starr in my mind).  Perhaps I was distracted by all the amazing little BYOBs Philly has to offer?  You think???  Give me Bob's Alligator Wonderland (something with a unique viewpoint) over Disney World (cookie-cutter corporate) anytime.

    However, if you are going to try a Starr restaurant, you might as well do it on someone else's dime, which is exactly what I did thanks to a generous visiting BD director.

    Other than the 10-foot Buddha presiding over the 1st floor like Big Brother, Buddakan is rather generic--slick and modern in an unemotional way--and definitely a bit on the cavernous side.  A good place to bring someone on a 2nd date, but too noisy for a 1st date (I'd be willing to bet that at least 50% of the diners were on dates, and the other half were "girls night out" parties).  We were seated on the main floor, not far from Big Bud, but squeezed in between the metal railing surrounding a 30-person communal/group table and a huge support beam.  But what do you expect for a last-second OpenTable reservation on a busy Friday night?  However, this might have actually worked to our benefit, as the pole served to make our dinner feel a bit more private, and possibly reduced the noise somewhat.  Buddakan is definitely on the energetic+ side, but conversations are possible with only an occasional "WHAT???".

    We started off with cocktails ($11-13), which I thought were tasty, but tending towards the generic fruit/citrus persuasion with Asian flavorings like ginger and lemongrass.  Nothing really unusual.

    ~Solid: Bulleit bourbon, Thai cola, ginger, kaffir lime

    ~Strength: Ketel One vodka, cilantro, lime (quite strong on the cilantro)

    For an app:

    ~Tuna Tartare Spring Rolls (3, or 4 by request), with crispy shallot & chili ponzu--This appeared instantly and was obviously made from pre-fried crispy wrappers into which cool tartare was piped.  Tasty, but a bit one-dimensional and I thought it could do with a bit of dipping sauce to make it more interesting.

    Entrees (which are served family style for sharing, each dish was a good-sized individual portion so suitable for 2-3 to share):

    ~Miso Black Cod, bok choy, shiitake mushrooms, white radish, unagi sauce--this is obviously based on Nobu's famous dish (which, incidently, he stole!), but a very nice technical rendition with a strong miso flavor and delicate, flaky black cod, slightly burnt around the edges as it should be.  The accompanying vegetables were fine, but didn't really add much.

    ~Five Spice Duck Breast, roasted garlic, corn and scallion spoon bread, frisee salad, five spice jus--not very strong on the Chinese 5-spice, but the perfect medium-rare, thinly sliced duck was almost melt in your mouth.  The spoon bread was very interesting, a light, airy custard more than a bread.

    Dessert:
    ~Lemongrass Consommé, mango-green apple brunoises, yogurt sorbet--I lked the tart-sweet-spicy contrasts of this dish, which was quite beautiful, with two large peices of gold foil and a vertical sugar "window".  Amusingly, while my host was in the restroom, I ordered this dish "to share" and the waitress commented "just so you are aware, this is a rather intimate dish" (I think she thought we were on a 1st date).  But we managed to maintain a professional demeanor and enjoy it at the same time.

    Service was pleasant, but definitely on the slow side (we had a 6:30PM, but the restaurant was already 3/4 full), and our waitress seemed to disappear for long periods at a time.  Perhaps she couldn't find our hidden little "nook".

    Valet parking.

    At least I've now got the Starr thing checked off on my Philly restaurant "bucket list", and I can get back to those amazing BYOBs...

    Sláinte! (Or "Cilantro" if you can't pronounce Sláinte!)

    My very fabulous older brother took a few of us out to Buddakan when visiting me in Philly.  I had heard amazing things about this place and had very high hopes for this dinner.

    The atmosphere is dark, very lively, and friendly.  We were sitting right next to the kitchen, and it was pretty loud to the point where it was hard to have a good conversation across the table.  However, the service was pretty good, and the food is extremely interesting with unexpected and exciting flavors, which was sometimes amazing and sometimes disappointing.

    The fours of us split a bunch of dishes:

    Edamame Ravioli - One of my favorite dishes of the meal!  It only comes with a handful of pieces, but I could have eaten a hundred of them.  The edamame is a very creamy filling with a light, pleasant flavor.  This dish would bring me back on its own!

    Dungeness Crab Sticky Rice - Also one of my favorite dishes of the night.  The sticky rice was the perfect chewy consistency, and the flavors were bold, savory, and delicious.  It also came beautifully presented in a dungeness crab shell.

    Five Spice Duck Breast - The duck was very flavorful but also extremely fatty, which is expected with duck.  Some people at the table liked it a lot, but the amount of fat on the meat was a little unpleasant in the mouth.

    Black Pepper Beef - The dish was attractively served in a crunchy noodle shell.  Compared to black pepper beef I've had at other restaurants, this one was just average.  It was definitely tasty, but not out of this world.

    Overall, great meal that lived up to the Buddakan name and reputation.  If for nothing else, I would gladly come back for a dozen servings of edamame ravioli.

    I feel like Buddakan is one of those restaurants where I always said to myself "I gotta get over there" and just never did.  So I was super excited to finally visit a few weeks ago.

    My husband and I both had off for MLK Jr day AND it was restaurant week so we decided to go out to a nice lunch.  Of the restaurants that participate in RW, only a handful offer lunch.  After explaining to my darling husband what a bento box was, we decided to go to Buddakan and check it out.

    The ambiance, like all Steven Starr places, cannot be beat.  Awesome decor and great service.  Starr has a knack for creating a top notch dining experience.  As for the food, I thought it was very good, but not blow-my-mind amazing.  Which is why I deducted a star.

    For the RW deal, you got a bento box with three items (you choose one selection from each of three groups) and a dessert for $20.  Upon arriving we learned that every day they serve a bento for $15 where you choose one selection from each of three groups OR pay $20 to make three selections from any of the groups.  We decided to stick with the RW deal because we wanted the dessert.

    For my bento, I chose the edamame ravioli, thai chicken salad, and the wasabi tuna tataki.  I really really enjoyed this meal.  My favorite was, by far, the edamame ravioli (this coming from a person who isn't even the hugest edamame fan).  But I felt like I got a decent amount of food for the money.  For my dessert, I had a coconut flan which also was super tasty.

    I am a fairly light eater, so this meal was just the right amount of food for me.  If you are a heartier eater, I would suggest getting an appetizer to go along with your bento box.  Or just get a full sized entree instead.  

    I liked the ambiance of Buddakan and I did enjoy my meal, but I'm not sure this is an everyday type of restaurant.  Definitely more of a special occasion (or restaurant week) spot.

Specialties

Centered around a ten-and-a-half foot gold gilded Buddha, Buddakan serves family-style modern Asian cuisine. Imaginative dishes like edamame ravioli, Asian barbeque pork and lobster fried rice have made this a must-stop spot on any visit to Philly.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
