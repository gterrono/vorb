    I'm a vegetarian and find most vegan places to be rather bland but vedge was exactly the opposite. To be honestly everything was so hearty and flavorful that I didn't even realize that it was a vegan restaurant until half way through through the night when someone pointed it out. Our waitress was extremely knowledgeable about every single item on the menu and made some recommendations that were so on point.     Recommendations:
    Dirt List: Fingerling Potatoes, Brussel Sprouts, Mushrooms
    Apps: Cucumber Soup and Beet Salad (my fav)

    Listed in Places I Adore, Vegetarian Delights

    Sweeeeet jesus. For obvious reasons, I thought the best vegan food I would ever have would be in Berkeley -- and I happily stand corrected, as Vedge now reigns supreme in my heart. Rolled in at 9p on a Saturday night (such European timing, quelle horreur for my geriatric noshing ways!), and the place was bumping - not in an aggressive way, just in that way that makes you excited to put everything you see at other diners' tables in your mouth.

    The menu is made up of nibbles, starters, and plates of a variety of sizes - they rec 4ish items per diner, but I would say to err on the side of 5-6/pp, esp if you're eating w/ anyone who's usually a carnivore. Before I forget, in no way can you tell this is a vegan place -- it's just effing tasty - nothing hippied out/earthy crunch abt it - it's totally a classy joint that I'd be thrilled to take even the most ardent steak lover.

    As we eat with our eyes, to start my review, I should note that presentation of everything was beautiful - which, sadly also means that some of the portions were more petite than I would have liked - the price to portion ratio is skewed on some, accurate on others -- ex, the grilled seitan, tofu, and barciole were entree sized, the rest were pretty....dainty.

    There wasn't a single thing that we tried that I wouldn't order again - let me see if I can remember some of them - oh, the golden beets were gorgeous, as was the grilled tofu. The fancy radishes were crisp and tart and the grilled seitan was tender, smoky glory. Oh, and make sure you hail the breadboy often - it's that perfect doughy but firm texture that made me want to steal his tray and run for the door.

    Arg, I almost forgot - don't skip dessert, esp if that chocolate peanut butter thing is on the menu, or the saffron cheesecake. So. Good. Even if you're full, do a lap around the resto and brace your tummy for more.

    Thank goodness that Philadelphia is only an hour flight from Boston - it's worth the trip in my book.

    Oh, the eat well, I would say plan to throw down at least $50/pp - though...to be fair, btwn the 4 of us, we had 4 drinks, countless noshes, and 3 desserts.

    We have dined at Vedge many, many times since it opened, and at Horizons before that, so it should be clear that we are big fans of their food. However, we do have a couple of issues that have really begun to bug us.

    To begin with, it is a beautiful place-- but almost always horrendously noisy, and that really diminishes our pleasure in the dining experience. They really, really need to consider doing something to help bring down the sound.

    Second, we have noticed a drop-off in the quality of the service. When you are dining on a large selection of small plates, their service needs to be properly staggered, with attention to culinary balance as well as sheer quantity. Last night a large number of plates arrived all at once and had us scrambling to find room on the table, and also to eat them all so they didn't get cold. Then, predictably, there was a lull that was far too long before the next dishes came. The phrase "feast or famine" came to mind. We really weren't happy.

    That said, most of the food at Vedge is simply beyond compare with vegan restaurant food anywhere.

    Listed in The Yelp 100 Challenge, Let's Take a Tour...

    Yelp, you certainly do not disappoint. I was pretty hyped up to come to Vedge after reading all of the stellar reviews, and happily, all my expectations were met. Vegans, vegetarians and adventurous carnivores alike will all love this place!

    My girlfriend and I had tons of trouble deciding on what to order, but small plates are the style here, so if you like your dining partner the best way to go is to pick a bunch of things and share! From the regular menu we chose:

    Chilled Cucumber Avocado Soup
    Roasted Rutabaga Salad with Grilled Trumpet Mushrooms
    Salt Roasted Gold Beets with Smoked Tofu
    Carrots Shawarma Style with Black Lentils & Harissa
    Grilled Seitan with Swiss Chard & Pickled Turnips

    And then from tonight's "Dirt List" we picked:

    Crushed White Beets & Romesco Beans
    Wood Grilled Broccolini

    ...Now that I'm typing it out, we were maybe (possibly) a liiiiiiittle over-ambitious. Maybe. Does that explain why I'm still so full?

    Most everything we had was absolutely delicious. The highlights for me were the Gold Beets (the avocado and the smoked tofu gave the dish an incredible pop of flavor), the Rutabaga Salad (tasty, tasty mushrooms!), and the Cucumber Soup. Perfect for a hot summer day. The Broccolini and the White Beets were mostly so-so, except for the delicious Green Goddess dressing on the beets. I'd like a plate of that, please!

    We took the Saffron Cheesecake to go, and it was (are you sick of me saying it, yet?) amazing. Vedge obviously puts a lot of time and thought into creating the flavor profiles of their dishes, and for vegan food, it's a Mecca. Ambience and service also great. Don't miss this place!

    Listed in Herbivore Foodie Finds

    I think I've found the vegan Holy Grail.

    After a less than fantastic meal at Dirt Candy in New York, I was starting to lose faith that I would ever find truly great vegan food. Enter Vedge. My meal was so fantastic I literally wanted to cry it out from the rooftops. There is not a single vegan restaurant in NYC that even comes close.

    We had several small plates, and each was distinctive, delicious, and unexpected. Places like Blossom and Candle serve (usually) well seasoned, but familiar vegetable sides. Dirt Candy serves up gimmicky (but bland) vegetable concoctions like an "oreo" made out of toasted rice and wax beans. Vedge just served vegetables prepared simply, but using various herbs, spices, and cooking methods to bring out deep, sometimes surprising flavors. Often, Chef Landau captured the heart and soul of non-vegan favorites without ever feeling like imitations or placeholders. Other times, he just found ways to make vegetables taste really, really good.

    We tried so many things I don't know where to start!  My favorite, I think, was a Dirt List item that involved thin slices of wild mushroom prepared to mimic the flavor and texture of a fresh egg pasta, served in a rich, miraculously buttery sauce. My husband, who I can never get to eat brussels sprouts, went absolutely gaga over shaved brussels sprouts tossed in a smoked mustard mayonnaise. We also had a corn, potato, and mushroom stew that undeniably evoked a New English seafood boil, and a cauliflower dish had all the brightness and brininess of a Mediterranean bouillabaisse. The roasted maitake mushroom was earthy and rich, and it felt worthy of being the center piece of a meal in place of steak or roast fish.

    It just feels like Chef Landau really gets it. He understands how to make vegetable-based food multi-dimensional in a way I don't think other chefs are doing yet. I just wish Vedge were in New York!
