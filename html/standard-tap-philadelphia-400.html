    I don't know what I was thinking last time. I must have ordered the wrong thing, because I went back to Standard Tap yesterday and had a much different experience than I was expecting- I was mightily impressed by the brunch here.

    I got the goat cheese, tomato, and basil omelette, one friend got the burger, and the others got the avocado toast. Definitely make sure you ask if the dish comes with sides-- I didn't expect the hash brown (which is exciting!) or the size of the biscuit I received. Plus, my friends got an entire avocado on their plates! I was pleasantly surprised by the fries, which were light and flavorful. My omelette was really tasty.

    I thought the portions were great (even erring on the side of larger than expected). I suspect that the last time I was here, I just ordered a dish that was portioned smaller because it was a dessert. This time I had at least a four star experience.

    But the reason for the fifth star is where the food comes from. I asked the waitress (who was great with questions about beer, too) if they source locally-- not only does Standard Tap support local agriculture, but it also gets organic supplies as well. I hope that, in the future, Standard Tap can express this more clearly to customers, because it explains the pricing of the dishes better (and makes me feel good about eating it in the process!).

    Listed in Wait Free Brunches

    A recent revisit to the Standard Tap for brunch turned around my initial opinion of the place. While I admired the Tap's totally local beer list and focus on seasonal, spur of the moment cooking, I didn't really dig the food I had during my maiden voyage.

    I chalked that lackluster experience up to ordering dinner dishes during brunch, which now I understand as a no-no. Rather, sticking to breakfast food during breakfast service (who'da thunk it) resulted in a hearty, reasonably priced, and relaxing brunch experience at the Tap. Teetotalling for the day, I enjoyed the restaurant's exquisite, house-roasted coffee rather than one of the local beers on draft. The small-batch, robustly rich beans brewed here rival any of the better-known coffee spots around the city.

    The theme of the day's menu was sausage, and thus I ordered the "lamb sausage baked eggs." My vegetarian dining companion stuck with the basic breakfast and let me eat his side of scrapple (perks of dining with your parents). My dish presented a terra-cotta crock with layers of roasted eggplant, pistachio and lamb sausage, fried, runny eggs, and a bit of bechamel sauce. This lighter version of moussaka maintained a pleasant balance among the rich bechamel, sweet roasted veggies, and nutty lamb sausage.

    Biscuits (my favorites in the city), had a bit of bready chew with a significant buttermilk tang, complementing the fresh fruit jams adeptly. The star of the meal actually was the side of scrapple; the thin, crisp patty had a prominent maple note without cloying sweetness which paired well with the minerally, organy flavor of the pork.

    I do still have some gripes with this place, i.e. no menus and limited non-salad vegetarian options, but I definitely am more eager to return than prior.

Specialties

Standard Tap features locally-brewed draft beer.  Our food menu both complements the beer and expands the notion of what tavern food can be.  We use only the best available produce, poultry, meats and seafood -- much of it from area farms and local vendors - to create fresh, seasonal dishes.

Our full menu is available from 4 pm to 1 am, seven days a week.  Brunch is served Sat. & Sun. from 11 am to 3 pm.

Wine and liquor also available.

History

Established in 1999.

When Standard Tap opened its doors in December 1999, our goal was simple:  to create a neighborhood tavern that featured great beer and wholesome food.  With that in mind, we offer a draft-only beer selection that highlights the vast array of styles crafted by breweries located in Pennsylvania, New Jersey and Delaware.  Our food menu both complements the beer and expands the notion of what pub food can be.  We use only the best available produce, poultry, meats and seafood -- much of it from area farms and local vendors - to create fresh, seasonal dishes.
