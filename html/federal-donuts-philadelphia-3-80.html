    It's tough to write this review because I know how easy it is to dismiss a glowing review for a buzzed-about place as just another passenger on the bandwagon. But I really don't know what to say except this place has the best fried chicken I'd ever tasted. The coating was flavorful and so crispy it shattered, not heavy, gross, or greasy.     For a quick bite in lieu of breakfast this morning, my fiancee and I trekked down to the Center City location of Federal Donuts - we'd heard that there were tasty donuts here to sample. It's not a large shop, and seating is pretty limited, so it felt pretty packed around 10 AM or so. That said, we still managed to squeeze into one of the small seats facing the counter to try out our donuts. The prices here are decent - the donuts range from $1.25-2 for one, with a discount if you buy in bulk - and the donuts get served pretty quickly, although the hot and fresh ones require a little more time to prepare on the fly. We got the double chocolate and French toast as the 'fancy' donuts (which seem to be the ones pre-made that have a bit more frosting and other goodies on them). The double chocolate was above-average relative to a regular chocolate donut, but the extra chocolate sprinkled on top was a bit too dark/cacao-y for my tastes, and the donut mixture definitely seemed to be a bit more cake-like as well. I loved the French toast donut, as I've always had a hankering for donuts with maple frosting on them. Combined with some brown sugar sprinkled on, and this was probably my favorite donut of them all. We also had the cinnamon brown sugar donut...it's a very light donut compared to the 'fancy' ones, and the cinnamon sugar is good, if a tad on the light side. If you're used to having your sugar donuts doused with sugar, this may not be for you, but I appreciated it as a contrast to the 'fancy' donuts, which definitely are heavier and involve a lot more frosting and toppings.

    In all, the donuts here are really good. I'm sure they rotate different ones in and out on a daily basis, but on any given day, there are about 10 different donuts (more of the 'fancy' ones relative to the hot and fresh) that you can sample. I'm not a huge donut person because they aren't the healthiest things in the world, but this is definitely a place I'd come back to if I was craving a donut. I can't say much about the fried chicken because it wasn't being served when I came in...maybe next time!

    Finally - FINALLY! - after hearing so much about their fried chicken, I made the extra effort on Saturday, after my shift at work, to stop by on my way home at Federal Donuts to ensure that we could eat their chicken for dinner. I had asked someone to do it for me, but a certain midday Flyers game was APPARENTLY more important to watch.

    In the early afternoon the place was jumping, all seats taken by diners and a constant stream of patrons coming in and out. Only one type of fancy donut remained at that hour, but I only cared that the chicken was plentiful. I got two halves, one Shabazzi and one Buttermilk Ranch, and upon returning home placed them in the fridge...with reservations, but we weren't hungry yet.

    About 4 hours later I reheated them in the oven, first at 150 F for about 15 minutes and then at 350-375 F for another 5 minutes. Our apartment instantly smelled DELICIOUS. When they came out of the oven, my first bite yielded juicy, tender chicken meat beneath crispy, spiced skin. Perfectly fried! Their time in the fridge didn't make the skin soggy enough to not be revived in the oven. Some of the best fried chicken I've ever had (and my fiance agrees). Both seasonings were fantastic: the Shabazzi was earthy/spicy, and the Buttermilk Ranch was nicely tangy. This is finger-lickin' good chicken!

    Luckily enough that same day I managed to snag a French Toast donut when I went to West Elm's Etsy pop-up shop not long after getting the chicken. They had free Federal Donut donuts in pink lemonade and chocolate peanut butter for shoppers, but I spotted one French Toast hidden in a display beneath glass that the staffer was gracious enough to give me (she said it's their most popular fancy donut and sells out instantly). I made my fiance VERY happy with this little extra surprise. He loves donuts, but not usually iced/glazed ones, but he said that it was one of the best donuts ever and would be especially perfect with coffee (no wonder it sells out in the morning). The other donut that comes with the chicken was a great, classic donut that goes well with the chicken, although neither of us are the type to eat both foods at the same time. If only it was easier to get their chicken and donuts throughout the day!

From the business

Cake donuts in interesting flavors and Korean-style, twice-fried chicken sold by the half and whole bird. House-roasted coffee available hot or cold-brewed and iced. That's basically it.

Specialties

Cake donuts in interesting flavors and Korean-style, twice-fried chicken sold by the half and whole bird.  House-roasted coffee available hot or cold-brewed and iced. That's basically it.

History

Established in 2012.

Chef Michael Solomonov and partner Steve Cook of Zahav and Percy Street Barbecue got a wild idea to pair exotic donuts and super-crispy fried chicken. With Bodhi Coffee owners' Bob Logue and Tom Henneman's coffee expertise and professional bigmouth Felicia D'Ambrosio, the team opened the first FedNuts at 1219 S. Second St. in Pennsport, South Philly in 2011.  The Sansom Street shop followed one year later in October 2012.  We have since been cranking out fancy donuts, melt in your mouth hot fresh donuts, and truly badass chicken.  Follow us on Twitter www.Twitter.com/federaldonuts for status updates and fun contests.
