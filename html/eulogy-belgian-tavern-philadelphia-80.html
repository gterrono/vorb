    Monk's Cafe may be rated the #1 beer place in Philly as noted in all the travel blogs, but in my opinion...Eulogy rocks!!!
                                                                     
    This place is not as crowded nor pretentious, the beer selection is greater, & the bartender knows all about good beer since he is an aficionado himself. There is a feeling of local pride here & most patrons prefer to keep this spot a secret from tourists.
       
    At first, we were disappointed to learn they ran out of the Young's Double Chocolate Stout. The bartender suggested a far more superior alternative: The Chocarrubica Dark Ale (7% abv) brewed with carbo and cacao beans. In his words, this beer is "so f*ckin' awesome!" The obscene advertisement won us over. He is so right on that! It had a strong dark chocolate & coffee flavor. It wasn't sweet & we were impressed it came from the same makers as the Italian Perroni.

    The next beer we had was the Abbey Affigem Noel Christmas Ale (10.5% abv - aged 5 years). It was both sweet & spicy, and tasted like apple cider mixed with honey. It was expletively spectacular!
                               
    This place is too good to be true & I know some patrons will be mad at me for revealing it to the Y community.

    Eulogy is fine. Their selection is fantastic, of course, and they weren't out of anything despite being there on a busy Friday evening. They even had a few beers hiding out not on the menu--we managed to snag a Dogfish Head World Wide Stout for our table, which in hindsight may have been a mistake on the way to a nasty hangover, but at least it was delicious on the way down. The music was great (either the juke is well stocked or the bartenders have good taste. We also managed to grab a table for our party of three, which was unexpected. Because the two problems with Eulogy are its size and its clientele. On the first floor, there's room for the people on the bar stools and nothing more behind them, and some awkward clustering in the front by the door. Upstairs is a bit more spacious but still awkwardly oriented. If you get there on an off hour, great, but it's in the middle of the Old City scene and becomes packed frequently and easily. And the people that come... I mean, seriously, who orders a round of Patrón and Stella in a serious beer bar? The cognitive dissonance between the place itself and a good portion of the crowd is spectacular. Despite the hordes and terrible layout, the service on my most recent visit was great, though it's not always been that way. I've never been a fan of the food, but then again I don't eat moules, so I'll call that a push. If Eulogy were bigger and the clientele were different (i.e. if it weren't in Old City), I'd give it an easy 4, maybe 5 stars. If the beer list and the service weren't so good, I'd give it a solid 2, maybe 1 star. As is, I'll average it out to 3.

Specialties

This is Beer Heaven, give a "high praise" Eulogy for your favorite beer after you consume it.  We also prepare our food in the authentic Flemish Belgian Style as opposed to other Belgian restaurants who choose the French Walloon Belgian style.  This means our fries are thick enough to truly hold a double fry technique, our fries go "crunch, goo" because they are the right thickness, the wimpy skinny fries elsewhere go "goo goo goo" because they are too skinny and the double fry technique does not work.  Also our mussels are larger than the PEIs other restaurants use, this is because our mussels are the authentic North Sea size like in Belgium.  The more meat the more flavor the mussel can hold in our ala minute pan technique (all ingredients added fresh, never ever ever any pour over sauce on mussels).  Also our handburgers are award winning based on seven secret spices and our fish and chips and other entrees are as good as the beers ! Make a reservation at www.eulogybar.com

History

Established in 2002.

My family is from Brugge, Belgium, I am passionate about Belgian beer, food and heritage, this adds an authenticity to the business.  I got the building in 2002 and built the restaurant myself using antique stain glass behind the bar from an old Philadelphia church and a chandelier from an old church as well.  The bar was constructed in Fishtown in Philadelphia by a local carpenter using Honduras Mahogany I imported and the design of the bar was based on a blueprint I drew.  
We take our beer very very very serious, our beer starts in a beer cooler and is then delivered via dual gas blenders using co2 and nitrogen blends for the proper carbonation technique.  Most of our draft beer never travels more than six feet but along that journey there are glycol filled lines keeping the beer at 40 ish degrees until it hits your glass.  We have about 200 different glasses we keep in stock because most of the taste for beer is accomplished through your smelling it, yes your nose!
