    Sipping on organic green ice tea from HipCityVeg which just opened today!  I didn't get a chance to try anything else, since I found it on the way to Rittenhouse Square after already picking up a mediocre lunch in comparison to what is on the menu here!

    Vegans, vegetarians and mostly plant-based diet folks: rejoice!  Everything is YOU-friendly and they even have stuff for kids. I am returning tomorrow for the Ziggy Burger (smoked tempeh and "special sauce") or maybe the Philly Steak sandwich.  Definitely going to try the Green Lemonade (fresh squeezed with KALE juice and agave nectar!). I feel healthier already.

    This joint will be great for lunch and a casual dinner, but there isn't a lot of seating so it's likely take-out or picnic in the park kind of food. Prices arange from $7.50 - $9 for burgers/sandwiches/salads, with sides at $4 and drinks and sweets around the $2.50 - $5, which is reasonable to me considering its fresh and some things are organic.

    Has potential for 5 star rating, I just haven't tried everything yet. If the Ziggy Burger is remotely as good as the Tempeh Reuben at The Remedy Diner in Durham, NC, this place will be a solid 5 star joint in my book.

    Hooray! I am so glad they are here!

    I've been veggie for about seventeen years, and one of the things I miss the most is a good chicken sandwich.  I mean I've had some passable sandwiches in the past, but nothing like the Hip City Ranch.     The people are super friendly, food came out fast, and it's cute inside.  Definitely not the best place to eat in, as there's a counter facing the street, and two or three tables lining the walls.

    Word on the street is the Ziggy burger is the way to go. I'll be back.

    I'm vegan so I was thrilled to learn of a new vegan restaurant opening up in Philly. Their menu sounded great--mid-range, casual, comfort food that errs on the healthy side (lemonade with kale juice, lots of salad options, banana whips). If you've been to Foodswings in Brooklyn, this is a cleaner, better-lit, healthier alternative. Overall, I enjoyed the experience, wish the place well and plan to go back. But there are few critiques I have that I hope they will address as they get their feet wet in this new business.

    First, PROS:
    --Super friendly staff
    --Places to sit even though this is obviously a takeout joint
    --Chickn ranch sandwich was really yummy. Fried and salty and filling.
    --Caesar salad was also a hit. I love the dressing, jicama and plantain chips. A twist on the tradition.
    --Good sweet potato fries.
    --I liked the green lemonade, which wasn't too bitter or sweet and the kale juice added a nice grassy undertone that any green smoothie enthusiast will enjoy
    --The decor is clean and minimalist.

    CONS:
    --Food took a while to get to us and it wasn't super hot. They've been slammed so I'm sure they will streamline the prep process and the food was still cooked and overall delicious.
    --The cilantro bean dip that comes with the fries was low on flavor and small on size.
    --No ketchup! They need ketchup!
    --The Ziggy Burger is a veggie burger with tempeh bacon and special sauce. The burger patty was very Morningstar farms-like, which I hate. I much prefer veggie burgers made with whole veggies, beans and lots of spices and herbs. The veg burgers that try to taste like cow burgers always have a synthetic taste. Also, the bread buns are so ginormous that they are hard to chew entirely and without plates and many napkins, it makes for a very messy experience.
    --Can't stand the waste. No real plates or silverware. Ok, I know the food retail trend is to take out and go and Hip City Veg makes it very easy for you to GO. All of your food comes in a bag whether or not you stay. This might be great when you want to take your lunch to the park but not if you want to eat-in. Also, they say their cups are compostable but the evidence suggests that most "compostable" items take as long to break down as non-compostable ones. And if you don't eat in, your cup will likely end up in the trash anyway. Also, there are no trash/recycling/compost bins accessible to customers. You have to give your trash to the counter people, which is awkward when there's a line. They need bins customers can use. I think they can manage to use SOME reusable plates. So much waste.
    --No milkshakes. This is okay but several Yelp users have mentioned this. This is what we really wanted.

    OVERALL
    Hip City Veg has some food bumps to iron out and I'd really like them to address their complete reliance on disposable products but they are a welcome addition to the city and I'd definitely return to try some other menu items. Hope milkshakes are there next time! :)

    9 check-ins ROTD 10/15/2012

    Hip City Veg has been on my list since I heard about them at this year's Vegan Fest at Weaver's Way. HCV's website contains little more than a logo which left me even more intrigued. When were they coming and what was going to be on the menu? Were we talking all vegetarian or all plant-based/vegan? My questions were answered when I finally got the chance to check it out for an early dinner this past Saturday.

    At around 5pm on Saturday, after doing the city parking dance for around an hour and landing on South Street, we were in the front of the line at Hip City Veg, during one of the few lulls the new storefront has probably experienced since opening this past week. The cashier let me know that everything was indeed plant-based, which is wonderful, given the barren options for vegans in the Rittenhouse area of the city. My husband and I ordered the ziggy burger and the udon noodle salad to split, and I ordered the green lemonade.

    Sweetened with agave, ice cold and with just the right level of tartness, the green lemonade was perfect to tide me over as we waited for our meal. I don't normally drink anything but water, however, this lemonade is made with agave, so there's no sticky coating of sugar left in your mouth and no blood sugar spike. A few minutes after we sat down, our order was ready. The ziggy burger was wonderful, tasty tempeh bacon strips, a nice, if prefab, veggie patty, loads of pickles and special sauce on a soft homemade whole wheat onion bun. It was delightful and my only complaint is that I had to split it with my husband. I could have easily eaten the entire thing. The Asian udon noodle salad was good, but there was too much oily dressing on top and not enough peanuts to balance out the peppery arugala.

    If you google "hip city veg", you'll be able to find the menu online, it's just not on the official website.
