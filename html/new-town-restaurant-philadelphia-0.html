    Great place. What a gem, indeed. I was leaving today's bi-annual hipster-mania event known as the R5 Productions' Punk Rock Flea Market...and we stumbled upon this place. Great deals are to be found on your standard American fare. They have some Greek stuff and this place just looks straight out of the 70s and 80s...a place where Jerry Seinfeld would eat at (in reality Larry David, of course).

    My friend and I got two BLT's and an order of fries and it was very quick being prepared...just under $12-13 with tip.

    I will be back here, I like the cozy, non-packed places. The reason this joint doesn't get much love is because it is dead center in the middle of nowhere (No Man's Land) in the Center City area. It's pretty close to the Electric Factory and on the same block as the Starlight Ballroom. If you are planning on going to either of these venues and want to get some cheap eats beforehand...go to New Town!

    Hope this review convinces others to come here.

    This is a cash-only place, by the way. Nearest ATM is at the convenience store on 10th and Spring Garden.

    New Town is pretty much the only lunch option within walking distance of my strangely-located office, and a great sole option at that.

    Maria is the BEST. So nice, friendly and easy to talk to while waiting for pick-up. New Town is one of those places that the amount owed changes every single time you're there, even if you're getting the same exact thing, but it's always ridiculously cheap and really delicious.

    Personal recommendations are the Cheeseburger Platter ($5 for a burger with any toppings plus delicious fries and some coleslaw), the Grilled Cheese (something insanely cheap like $2) & all the subs. They have daily specials as well that are all great.

    Coming from Northern New Jersey I've missed the traditional, friendly, local diner that I grew so accustomed to. I'm so glad to have found somewhere that is exactly like what I had growing up - great food, great people and great prices.
