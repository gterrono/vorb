    I consider myself somewhat of a cheese steak connoisseur. I've been to all of the big-name places around Philadelphia multiple times and Jim's is ALWAYS my favorite. Rolls are fresh, smothered in Whiz and topped with fresh-cooked meat and onions. DELICIOUS! The meat is chopped up very fine and not fatty like some of the other places I've experienced.

    Still, for the quality of the sandwich, Jim's is the place to go!!

    Arguing about cheesesteaks in Philadelphia is like bringing up religion and politics at your girlfriends families thanksgiving dinner...everyone has an opinion, there is no swaying of opinions, and that opinion is something they'll die for (or stab you for disagreeing with)...therefor, this review is going to be totally unbiased and just a reflection of what the experience was.  There is an (arguable) quartet of choices for the "Philadelphia Cheesesteak Experience" with each having a slightly different.

    When you walk in, Jim's feels like an old school diner with the stainless steel trim and white tiled interior.  There's usually a line inside, but it moved pretty fast.  It's fairly humid downstairs from the grill being right by the front door and you're not getting out of this place without have a lingering aroma of fried steak on your jacket and hair.

    Jim's is known for their thinly shaved rib-eye, cut in house.  Compared to the rest of the quartet, Jims and Tony Luc's have a similar prepared meat (Pats/Geno's uses a few, thick sliced pieces).  The meat is cooked on a griddle and then moved over into a steaming heap of meat about the weight of a small calf.  On the other side is an equally large pile of translucent fried onions.  When you place your order, the cook uses a large spatula to slide over what seems like a pound of meat, mixes with onion if ordered "with" (unlike South Philly where it's ordered "wit", they're much more tourist friendly and open to different ways of ordering your sandwich), and then places an Amaroso roll on top (with the cheese of your choice) before flipping over and passing along to the cashier.  Also unlike the South Philly choices, there's a side cart with lettuce, tomatoes, mushrooms, peppers and even mayo standing by for custom orders.  MOST IMPORTANTLY, there's a liquor license here and beer behind the counter, so if you like to drink with your meal, this is the only one in the quartet where you don't need to bring your own brown bag.

    I've eaten her maybe 50 times in my life.  A few people I know have their picture on the wall for one reason or another and I've been here at all hours from lunch to late night drunk visits with out of town friends.  My only advice is don't eat here at the end of the night.  When you're here within the last hour of closing, the meat has sat on the grill for too long and tends to be really dry.  Dry enough that you'll consider putting some mayo on top of it just to help get it down your throat.

    Most people choose to eat upstairs, so you walk through the makeshift prep kitchen and up a very steep set of steps.  If you're a girl wearing heels, well, good luck!  The turn around here is usually pretty quick, so no need to send someone upstairs to save a table (this is where the high humidity and the almost sickenly sweet smell of meat is a bonus!  After you fill-up and head into a food coma, all you crave is the fresh air outside!).

    This is the best of Philly for some, a good option for others, while some will tell you why it sucks.  No reason to ever ask anyone in Philly where you can "get the best cheesesteak" because it's a religion here.  If you're local you've already been, and if you're a tourist you should try it if it's convenient.  Otherwise try one of the other four that's more convenient and try Jim's on your next trip.

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
