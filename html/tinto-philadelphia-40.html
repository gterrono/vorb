    1/30/2009

    A cool restaurant with great food and I can wear jeans? I'm sold!

    We had late dinner reservations with fellow yelpers, Jen R and Lisa M.  When we arrived, the place was packed. I don't know if it's because it's restaurant week or it's always this way, but I was surprised.  

    The place is very cool looking. In the hallways, framed vintage corkscrews hung. Walls were really wooden wine racks with mirrors behind it.  Tables were everywhere. To the side, up high, down low. The only small area was the bar where we squeezed against the wall until our table was ready.  

    We were seated downstairs in huge booths. There were only 4 of us and the table could have easily sat 8 people. At Tinto RW menu, you could choose 2 first courses, 2 entrees, and 1 dessert. Most of our table shared so here's what we ate:

    First course
    Basque Onion Soup - the hot broth was poured over the onions and bread at our table in the coolest bowl (it looked like dr.evil's chair!) . This was delicious without a too oniony taste.  I could have had more and I don't even like onion soup.

    Arugula salad - the only "healthy" thing we got. The orange vinaigrette was a nice compliment to the peppery taste of arugula.

    Duck Montadito - duck confit wrapped in Serrano ham with 2 black cherries on a baguette. This was delicious! I think it was my favorite of the night in fact. The cherries added such a nice sweetness to the duck and ham. Perfect balance!

    Pork Belly Montadito - I was probably looking forward to this most, but it was just OK. Nothing to talk about here, move on.

    chicken Brochette - I actually didn't eat this. It was basically chicken kabobs and grilled grapes. At least that's what we thought they were. You'll have to check out Lisa M's review of that.

    I did their 2 wine pairings with the meal. Unfortunately, I have no idea what kind it was. It was white wine and very smooth.

    Second course

    Beef Brochette - beef kabobs. The biggest disappointment. We all felt that this was missing some other flavor like a squeeze of lemon or lime, or peanut sauce on the side.  I  tried dipping it in the  green bean vinaigrette too, but it didn't help too much.

    Butifarra- their house made garlic sausage. Very good and oh, so garlicly. You could ward off Dracula's whole family with one link.

    Organic Chicken with Truffled Whipped Mash, Brussels Sprouts, Smoked Bacon.  - Okay, this was my favorite dish (I don't care what I said earlier). The mashed potatoes were fantastic. We were talking about them in the parking garage afterwards. That's how good they were. Smooth and full of flavor. I really liked the Brussels sprouts and bacon combo. The chicken was tender and juicy. The whole dish worked perfectly together.  A must have!

    Green Beans with Almonds, Dates, Orange Segments, Paprika Sherry Vinaigrette - I was surprised when Jeff ordered this, but the vinaigrette that was with them was outstanding. They need to bottle that stuff up.

    I finished my pairing with a red wine, but it was too much for me so to the hubby it went.

    Best Course

    Gate Aux Basque =Traditional Basque Cake, Pastry Cream, Black Cherries. I was the only one to order this at my table. They were basically mini tarts with a nice almond taste.

    Bananas Y Azafrán = Chocolate Cake, Caramelized Bananas, Saffron Crema. I'm really not sure how you can call this flourless cake. It was more like a fudge cake.  All 3 people eating at this table went instantly quiet with their first bite. It was rich and oh so chocolately. I love caramelized bananas so I ate Jeff's and I eyed Jen R's for awhile.  Don't judge me, it's a weakness.

    Overall, Tinto really has down the balance of ingredients.  I think I may like it more than I like Amada.

    After trying Amada earlier last year I was interested enough to try Tinto. So, for restaurant week I made the reservation. Kristine and Jeff G, and Lisa M. joined me for the experience. I must say, I was impressed with everything about Tinto.

    Upon arriving our first task was to find parking. That was very easy for us, there are a number of parking lots on the same street. We parked on Samson for $9. Already a good start to our night.

    There was a short wait once inside Tinto. The place was crowded so we found a tight spot next to the bar and tried to stay out of the way. This gave us a chance to see all the cool looking dishes flying past us so some of us had ideas of what we would order. I knew I had to have the chocolate dessert...whatever it was. The restaurant is decorated in cork screws and dim lighting. The bar was decorated with bottles of wines and corks on each shelf.

    We were seated downstairs in a booth big enough for about 8 people (there were only 4 of us). We had another booth next to us and had a wall separating the rest of the room. Unfortunately it is a small room and therefore got very loud easily. I could barely hear the waiter until a larger party was gone.

    So on to the food...

    First course
    Basque Onion Soup - The waiter placed a cool looking bowl on our table then poured in the broth. I don't normally like onion but wanted to try something different. Lucky for me, there wasn't a powerful taste of onion. The soup was really good. I was surprised I liked it so much.

    Arugula salad - I tried some of Kristine and Jeff G's. It had a great mix of taste that blended well together. This was definitely more powerful than the onion soup.

    Duck Montadito - The duck was my favorite. It was cooked perfectly and was perfectly paired with a baguette and cherries. Great blend of flavors here. I would definitely order this again.

    Pork Belly Montadito - I didn't try this one but didn't hear any complaints.

    Chicken Brochette - I thought the chicken was a little dry for my liking. It had a good grilled flavor but was probably overdone.

    Second course

    Beef Brochette - beef kabobs. The biggest disappointment. We all felt that this was missing some other flavor like a squeeze of lemon or lime, or peanut sauce on the side.

    Butifarra- I'm not a big fan of sausage but this garlic sausage was really good. I tasted some of Lisa M's. It was rich in garlic. I wonder how bad our breaths smelled after...

    Organic Chicken with Truffled Whipped Mash, Brussels Sprouts, Smoked Bacon. - This was't my favorite of the night but damn was it good! It gave the Duck a run for its money. The mashed potatoes were perfect. I don't think I've ever had Brussels Sprouts but my god they went perfectly with this dish. The chicken was tender and juicy unlike the Chicken Brochette .

    Green Beans with Almonds, Dates, Orange Segments, Paprika Sherry Vinaigrette - A great powerful mix of flavors. You could definitely taste the orange among other things. This dish was surprisingly good. I expected it to be dull but it was anything but.

    And Dessert

    Gate Aux Basque =Traditional Basque Cake, Pastry Cream, Black Cherries which Kristine G. was the only one to order.

    Bananas Y Azafrán = Chocolate Cake, Caramelized Bananas, Saffron Crema. Heaven on a plate. Ahhh, so thats what it was, rushing by me as we waited for our table. I was expecting cake but got a mountain of chocolate fudge. It was rich and delicious. The caramelized bananas were great with this dish. A must have for the chocolate fanatic.

    Overall, I loved Tinto and wouldn't mind going back on a full priced night. The service was nice and the food was great. The only disappointment was the noise level but luckily for us, it didn't last the whole night.

    I'm in serious Gourmet Brunch Heaven.  Why did I wait so long to experience this ecstasy?

    I'm almost reluctant to write this review because I don't want everyone to schlep over there and start taking advantage of the awesome valued deal Tinto offers for Sunday brunch.

    Before I delve into the meal, I have to say something about the service.  From the hostess (who was so pleasant and smile-y - I really think it goes a long way) to the runners, bus boys and of course our server (who was engaging, approachable) - everyone was kind, prompt and worked like a well-oiled machine.

    The brunch is only $25!  Twenty-five dollars, people - for Three Courses (that includes your choice of one of two desserts) and a side (I got the pork belly, no question)!  I know that might seem a bit expensive, let's say compared to what a brunch would cost you at Sabrina's or Honey's - but the meal was worth that and more!

    I'm still reeling from my second course, revueltos de hongos - deliciously shirred eggs with porcini & trumpet mushrooms, goat cheese and truffle butter with baguette slices.

    My only complaint would be that the space is VERY dimly lit, especially the dining area in the back by the bar - which might be fine and romantic for late dinners - but too dark for lunches and brunch... Seating is tight there too, and I'm not a big fan of having a full meal served at those high top tables - especially if I'm dining with older company.

    All in all, I can't wait to go back and experience the throes of their tapas dinner menu.

    Serrano ham wrapped figs: Technically my bf ate this, but I had a bite of the figs themselves and it was a morsel of caramelized goodness. My bf had to restrain himself from inhaling them to fully enjoy the flavors.

    Chicken brochettes with garbanzo puree and truffle jus: What was technically a "why not?" choice became a surprising stand out. The chicken brochettes were tender and moist, and paired with the puree and truffle jus was just fantastic.

    Heirloom tomato gazpacho with avocado and fried padron peppers: Not the chunky gazpacho style I enjoy, but delicious nonetheless. The fried peppers were a lovely addition to a basic dish.

    Sea bass with cockles in salsa verde: Absolutely incredible, the sea bass was cooked to flaky perfection. Both it and the cockles were enhanced by the salsa verde.

    Asparagus with smoked tomato compote and majon espuma: When it came out, it was NOT what I expected. Half is asparagus, the other half is the tomato compote and majon espuma, which had surprising tastes. The compote is quite smoky, and the espuma very light, airy and somewhat sweet to my taste. I didn't care for the compote too much, but the asparagus tasted great with the espuma.

    Wild Mushroom with roasted potato, shallot and parsley: Wow. This reminded me immediately of traditional Basque tapas, the mushrooms were incredibly delicious and woody-flavored. A must.

    Chocolate coffee layer cake (almond meringue, chocolate ganache, coffee buttercream layers) with hazelnut ice cream: Chosen by our waitress' recommendation, and such a good choice it was. I have a craving for hazelnut gelato now.

This is a perfect place for a special occasion - or every night if you can afford it! I definitely plan on returning soon; Garces has me hooked.

Specialties

Tinto features a mouth-watering selection of "pinxtos" (pronounced PEEN-choes), the Basque equivalent of tapas.  Along with pinxtos, Tinto offers a tempting array of charcuterie, cheeses, mariscos (shellfish), brochettes and bocadillos (sandwiches).  

Some of the inspired dishes include:  Duck Confit Montadito, with wild black cherries and bleu de Basque;  Gambas Brochettes, shrimp with grape tomato and sherry espelette chili sauce;  Roasted Rack of Lamb Au Jus, with truffled artichoke puree and candied lemon;  Marmitako de Ventresca, tuna toro, parsley oil, migas and shaved mojama;  Txanguro Montadito, Dungeness crab, avocado and espelette;  and Mousse de Queso, with goat cheese, calabaza, orange, olive oil, caramel and lavender tuille.

History

Established in 2007.

Founded by Chef-Owner Jose Garces in 2005, Garces Group has grown from one award-winning restaurant in Philadelphia,PA's historic Old City to more than a dozen nationwide. Beginning with the runaway success of Amada, an authentic Andalusian tapas bar and restaurant, Chef Garces went on to open: Tinto, a Basque tapas bar and restaurant; Distrito, a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar; Chifa, a Latin-Asian restaurant; Garces Trading Co., a European-style market-cafe and on-site wine boutique; Village Whiskey, a Prohibition-era brown liquor bar and restaurant; and JG Domestic, an American farm-to-table restaurant and bar, all in his adopted hometown of Philadelphia. Garces Group also expanded into other cities: Chicago,IL, in cooperation with Sage Restaurant Group; Scottsdale,AZ and Palm Springs,CA, in cooperation with Joie de Vivre hotels and the Sydell Group; and Atlantic City, NJ, at the Revel Resort. Garces Group also operates a thriving Catering and Private Events division.

Meet the Business Owner

Iron Chef Jose G. Business Owner

Since opening his first restaurant, Amada, in Philadelphia less than a decade ago, Chef Jose Garces has emerged as an enormous talent and one of the nation's most gifted chefs and restaurateurs.  Today, he is the owner and operator of more than a dozen wildly successful restaurants, plus a thriving catering division, in five cities:  Chicago, IL;  Scottsdale, AZ;  Palm Springs, CA;  Atlantic City, NJ;  and his flagship locations in Philadelphia, PA.  He is a 2009 winner of the James Beard Foundation's prestigious "Best Chef, Mid-Atlantic" award, and one of only a few chefs in the country to hold the coveted title of Iron Chef, appearing regularly on Food Network's hit series Iron Chef America.  Chef Garces is also the author of two cookbooks, The Latin Road Home (Lake Isle Press, October 2012), a culinary and cultural tour of Ecuador, Spain, Mexico, Cuba and Peru, and his stunning first cookbook, Latin Evolution (Lake Isle Press, Fall 2008).
