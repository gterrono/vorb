    Let me start by saying, I am not a "prissy" drinker. I like my bars as dive-y, relaxed, and un-fancy… Let me start by saying, I am not a "prissy" drinker. I like my bars as dive-y, relaxed, and un-fancy as they come; with bartenders who are a little saucy and rough around the edges. BUT -

    I don't like this place... I just don't. And yet, I've been here more times than I care to recall. It's one of those places you bring out-of-towners because it's an institution. It's really effin' old, man. And people like to see history when they're in Philadelphia - and I like to drink. So... I think you see what I'm sayin'.  

    Some of these visits have been awesome, some not so awesome. The awesome times, I attribute to the crowd I was with, the relatively inexpensive beers (pitchers of which have led to an impromptu game or two of "waterfall"). The food is "meh." I don't mind taco night though. They're cheap (2 for $1) and they go well with beer. They also go well with karaoke, which they have on Wednesday nights. I don't participate but I do heart me some laughs at the expense of bad karaokers and this place has plenty of them.  

    I have had, as someone else mentioned, hit or miss service here... mostly a miss. The bartenders are not particularly nice or attentive even when the bar isn't busy -- well, there's one. She seems to like her job and seems to understand the cause and effect relationship between service and tips. The waitresses are slightly better but watch your tab because I have, on more than one occasion, been double charged for drinks/food.

    And the smell Michelle C. mentions in her review... oh my. Let's just say, I am fairly certain I made the same "colorful' observation. I'll spare you that disgusting image and call it "Eau de Frat Party", only worse.

    So, basically, you know you're going to go here at some point. Everyone in Philly does. But it's okay not to like it.     Read more

    This is my favorite bar in Philadelphia. I can't put my finger on any one thing that I like the most about it. It has a great atmosphere, the music is done just by a touch tunes machine but the people who go there usually play some pretty good music. The pitchers are CHEAP, about 7 bucks each and they give you glass mugs to drink from which I love. There is tons of seating and you can push tables together to make long ones if you're in a big group.

    McGillans is the perfect place to start your night out. You can't get really drunk for much cheaper in the city. Once I'm hammered I usually go to bars that I can only tolerate when I'm drunk, but staying at McGillin's all night isn't bad either.

    This place gets pretty crowded Friday and Saturday nights but there has only been once or twice where I was never able to get a table. There are two floors so there is usually a good amount of space to move around. Even though this place is always very crowded the waitresses and bar tenders do a very good job at making sure you always have a full pitcher or drink. If your mug is empty you won't have to wait very long for it to be filled again.

    The food here is decent. I usually just get the Texas/Western Fries/Nachos or whatever they're called. and they are delicious. It is seriously a mountain of food and could probably feed 6 people easily.

    McGillian's is really a fine establishment and one of the best bars I have had the privilege to patronize. You can't go wrong with spending a weekend night there.

Specialties

Philadelphia's Oldest Pub, featuring 29 beers on draft focusing on local, fresh micro brews.  A pub menu loaded with homemade items, everything from our famous Nachos and French Onion Soup to the Irish Mixed Grill and Cape May Stew.

McGillin's is considered the BEST place for karaoke in the city - Sundays and Wednesdays are hopping with DJ Joe at the mic.

History

Established in 1860.

Philly's Oldest Pub!

Meet the Business Owner

Chris M. Business Owner

Christopher Mullins, Jr. runs McGillin's Olde Ale House with his parents, Mary Ellen & Chris Mullins, Sr. They purchased it from Mary Ellen's father & uncle (Christopher's grandfather & great-uncle). They're only the second family to run the historic tavern in its 150+ year history. McGillin's is the oldest continuously operating tavern in Philadelphia and one of the oldest in the country. Christopher believes that beer is the eternal equalizer - friends have been made, marriages begun, wars ended. If we can ignite camaraderie and friendship than we have done our job!
