    After much convincing (read: nagging) from my fiancee I finally gave in and decided to try Shake Shack last weekend. From the beginning let me be honest with you all, I LOVE burgers, and even the word "love" might be an understatement, so keep that in mind while reading this review. With that being said, I found the burger at Shake Shack (ShackBurger) to be small for the price, a bit under seasoned, and having a bit too many vegetables compared to the meat.     Surprisingly the favorite part of my meal was the hot dog (don't judge my gluttony), which was cooked to perfection on the griddle. My dining partner got two dogs with peppers and onions and despite being full actually ordered another one for the walk to the bar (please don't judge him either).

    Long story short, you have to try this place at least once. While it may not be like the Shack in Manhattan it is still pretty good (albeit pricey), and if they continue to improve I'm sure they'll become a fixture of the Philadelphia food scene.

    I visited Shake Shack today, having heard all the raves about the original in NYC. I was definitely not disappointed.

    I went just before noon and there was hardly any line, although by the time I actually got my burger, the place had filled up. I ordered the SmokeShack burger. My first impression was that it looked awful small, but when I bit into it, it was pure deliciousness. The patty is so juicy, flavorful and delicious, the applewood-smoked bacon was to die for, and that Shack sauce is addictive. The pickled cherry peppers added a nice hint of acidity to complement the richness of the flavors.

    It was small, but I felt perfectly satiated after eating it. Not overfull, but I wasn't hungry again for another five hours. I also had the Abita root beer, which I thought was extremely thirst-quenching and tasty. It was not overly sweet, either.

    I didn't order fries. The lady at the counter tried to push them on me ("No fries?!") but I thought that sounded like too much food, and if I had room left over, which I didn't, I would have ducked into Capogiro across the street. When I saw other patrons' pitifully small and very Ore-Ida-looking crinkle fries, I was so glad I didn't waste money or calories on them. Shake Shack really needs to step up its fry game, that is just sad!!

    All in all, I would definitely go back. No, it's no Village Whiskey, but it's definitely a solid entry in the category of moderately-priced burgers. I'd like to also echo others here in saying that the service is very friendly. Not so much at the counter, but there are staff in the dining room who greet you, offer to take your tray away, etc., and that is a super-nice touch!
