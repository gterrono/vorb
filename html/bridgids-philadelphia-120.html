    We first went to Bridgid's about 15 years ago, and back then they had the menu only on the chalkboard/verbal, and it was a seasonal rotation of surprisingly upscale items for such a humble converted row-home.  Add in the amazing Belgian beer selection, and it was a favorite.  We hadn't been recently, so had a nostalgic pull to go back last night, and boy what a surprise.  The menu is now Italian in nature, but the atmosphere and friendly vibe is still the same (as are the funky tables).  The beer selection isn't as expansive, but they have more cocktails and wine to choose from.  I had the "Judson" cocktail - vodka, lime juice, ginger - and it was one of the best darn drinks I've ever had.  Ever.  We started with a pork belly appetizer with crab, goat cheese, pine nuts and plum, and it was divine.  So interesting and for the complexity, a decent size where we didn't feel short-changed after the first wonderful bite.  We then shared the prosciutto wrapped asparagus with mushrooms, and something about the delicate frisee and tangy light vinaigrette balanced the heaviness to make it just delicious.  I had the pasta special - a bucatini with pancetta, fresh peas in a carbonara sauce - and we licked the bowl as the sauce was again balanced, with the perfect amount of garlic and salt.  He had the crispy fried quail, and the maple syrup sauce was a perfect pairing.  My only slight criticism if I reached for one, would be that the sauce was thin, and a tad too sweet for my liking, but we again finished every yummy bite.

    All in all - what a nice discovery to find Bridgid's updated menu, but with the same neighborhood charm it's always had.  We'll be back soon.

    Philadelphia is full of hidden gems, we all know this and we all have our favorite spots....however, if you're looking for a new one (or a better gem!) - I highly recommend Bridgid's!

    Almost as if you're walking in to someone's Philly home which was turned into a restaurant; Christmas lights brighten the room, a full bar, and a beer list which won't disappoint! As if the ambiance wasn't enjoyable enough, the food was incredibly delightful, portioned well, and I was definitely not expecting the plates to be so elegantly decorated!

    Although the restaurant was busy when we arrived, our server Chris was very attentive to all of his guests. There was a table next to us who felt the need to criticize and mock their waitress - those are the types of people we DON"T want to sit by - but all we could think was, "even in a crowded room, she is doing a great job keeping a nice pace!"

    Bridgid's is a great spot to bring a date, watch a game, and get a good beer! If you're in the area, it's a must try!

I won't quit you Bridgid's, but please fix this one glaring problem with your kitchen.

    This place. My husband and I moved to Fairmount from NYC in July and Bridgid's has quickly become a neighborhood favorite. Great for drinks because of a great beer selection and delicious cocktails, good for a group meal (there are two dining rooms, one upstairs that is a bit more private), or a date or late night, this place fits all occasions. The staff is super and they remember you when you come back, which we appreciate. When we first walked by the place we thought it was an Irish bar, but don't be fooled by the exterior, there is some amazing Italian food to be had here. We have had dinner here several times as well as enjoyed their M-F 11pm-12am "Shut Up and Eat," when you buy a drink and pay $5 for whatever the chef feels like cooking for you. I thought this was only at the bar but just found out it's the whole restaurant, which is the only reason I am telling you about it.

    The calamari, the porchetta cheesesteak, the fig pasta, the bolognese, the gnudi, the lamb chop, the eggplant, the caesar, the snails, the caprese salad, etc all of it delicious. If they have a special, get it. The only complaint or downside is every once in awhile the food is over seasoned and a bit salty. The portions are large so come hungry.

    It is a feat of self control that I don't eat here more.

    I went with Kathleen D to Bridgid's last week to have a late dinner after a long day at Bloktoberfest (and thus experienced the ensuing Pie Debacle of 2014). I was disbelieving of her insistence that it had Italian food (given the name and the sign), but my confusion was assuaged when I walked past the bar and into the back room, which had a totally different vibe (the front was definitely your neighborhood pub, but the back was closer in atmosphere to a dark corner trattoria).

I felt that the fries tasted and looked exactly like Five Guys fries, and the large amount of chicken breast that was on my roll overpowered the other, more delicate flavors, but I left satisfied... until I had to drive back to collect the errant slice of pie.
