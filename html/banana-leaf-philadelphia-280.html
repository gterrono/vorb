    I completely understand why some would be leery or at least apprehensive about venturing into Chinatown. Whether you're in Philadelphia, NYC, or LA - the Chinatown's and Koreatown's of these big cities are mystifying outposts filled with unfamiliar lettering. Seeing crabs littered about the sidewalks and roast ducks hanging in the windows can certainly be disconcerting. However, let loose the explorer and adventurer inside of you - and be rewarded!

    Case in point - Banana Leaf. Just on the outer boundaries of Chinatown, located at 10th and Arch Streets, this restaurant specializes in Malaysian cuisine. Banana Leaf highlights milder flavors that are not inundated with garlic (not that that's a bad thing!) and offers curries that have depth of flavor. An added bonus? It's BYO!

    Banana Leaf boasts a spacious dining area that is well designed to transplant you into a new continent of cuisines. This ain't the dilapidated streets of Philly no mo! Service is brisk, yet friendly and the dishes come out quickly and do not put in a dent on your pocket. A steal of a deal if you ask me.

    Homemade Roti Canai ($3.75)
    Pancake and Curry Chicken

    Do start your meal with the roti canai, which is an Indian-influenced flat bread often found in Malaysian cuisine. The pancake is crispy, yet chewy (trust me - you won't understand the dichotomy of textures unless you simply try it) and served as one whole gigantic circle of glutinous flat bread that may, at first glance, not seem like enough to sop up the curry. But trust me - it gets the job done.

    The accompanying curry had a nice depth - clearly from a nice stock. There were a few morsels of creamy potato chunks and chicken, which was fine, but there were also tiny bones that were somewhat annoying. It's nice to know that this was made from a proper stock, but I could do without the tiny bones.

    Prawn Mee ($7.50)
    Egg noodles, shredded pork, shrimp, vegetables, bean sprouts

    For those afraid of spicy soups - don't be alarmed by the beguiling red tinge of the broth. It's milder than it looked. Advertised as Banana Leaf's favorite - the broth had an intense flavor of the sea - briny, deep, and delicious. The bean sprouts and spinach provided a nice fresh element to the dish, which was quite savory and filling. The 'shredded' pork seemed to be more sliced and was a tad dry. The shrimp were small, but tasted fresh, not frozen. It's quite the task to finish this dish - you'll need some help!

    Curry Chicken with Rice ($7.50)

    Definitely get a curry dish at Banana Leaf. While the creamy curry sauce may initially look intense, the mild flavors of the underlying coconut milk help to assuage the spiciness. The chicken chunks were moist and mixed with the accompanying huge scoop of delicious, yet simple rice, this dish is a winner. The GF was hoping for a bit more veggies in the dish, but of course, Banana Leaf does offer other curries, which will certainly be ordered on future visits.

    Overall, Banana Leaf offers a cheap, yet satisfying meal that takes you on a journey of Malaysian proportions. I would eat anything mixed with that curry sauce and the perfectly made roti is a nice way to start your meal. The portions will keep you satisfied, but it's the tastes and flavors that will keep you coming back for more.

    One of my favorite restaurants to go to in Chinatown. For the appetizer, I always order the Indian Pancake with the chicken curry dip. It's inexpensive and comes out fairly quick, which helps to suppress my hunger for the time being before my entree arrives. I'm a fan of their Pad Thai and Chicken Basil Noodles. The Pad Thai comes with shrimp, fried tofu, egg, and bean sprouts over thin-flat noodles sprinkled with crusted peanuts. It's delish. If you're a fan of basil like me, you'll love the Chicken Basil Noodles dish. It has shrimp, chicken, and Chinese broccoli,  stir-fried with flat noodles, chili, and basil leaves. It's my favorite because you can taste the freshness of the basil, which enhances all the ingredients in the dish. Mmmm! I wouldn't recommend ordering sushi here. It's not what they're known for and it isn't very fresh. Most of their stir-fried noodle dishes are tasty, so be sure to try one of them!

    Oh, and don't expect 4 or 5 star service in Chinatown. It's rare. Most of the restaurants in Chinatown are inexpensive, which I guess, cancels out the good service. =I

    My husband and I recently hit up the Philly Auto Show and found ourselves wandering the adjacent Chinatown area, hungry and eager for some good eats. We passed Banana Leaf, which I had sworn off of for years since the one and only time we were last there, and was the subject of the very first review I ever wrote here on Yelp, and a low-star one at that. Something inside me whispered 'could it hurt to give this place one more shot?' as I looked at the sign over the entryway, and I decided, 'sure, why not?' right on the spot. A little hesitant, I suggested giving them another go to hubs, who seemed just as hesitant to my idea. Despite our doubts, we finally caved in, thinking it couldn't hurt, just one more time? Doesn't everyone deserve a second chance? So, cautiously, we entered the restaurant, swearing to each other if anything went wrong with our service again and we got rude treatment the second go around, we'd just walk out this time and take our business somewhere else. Plenty of other choices in Chinatown for food, right?

    We were seated quickly and approached just as quickly for our orders. We made our choices, ordered, then waited for our food, which didn't take long to arrive. Anxious during each step of our service, we were pleasantly surprised that it seemed to go smoothly this time, and the people serving were generally nice, helpful, and friendly. We relaxed a bit, enjoying our menu choices and savoring each bite.

    Hubs had the beef fried rice and small wonton soup, while I had the shrimp fried rice and wonton mee soup. As we usually let each other try a small sample of each others dishes, we were both wowed with the flavors of our respective choices. Especially the watermelon drink I ordered, which I let hubs have a taste test sip from. It was divine! He really liked it too, and he loves watermelon. I've tasted a few watermelon drinks before, especially of the carton supermarket variety, and they were usually overly sweetened and didn't taste natural. This drink was pure melon heaven! I'd come back just for this drink alone! Granted they continue offering the good service we encountered on this visit, of course.

    On a side note, the place seemed noisier than I remembered it the first time, as it was more packed this night than our previous visit. Still had trouble hearing ourselves talk over the din, much less each other or our waitress. A large group near us seemed particularly boisterous, but despite this, our waitress took it well in stride, listened well, and still gave us a good attitude and service during our meal, as compared to the crappy attitude we received the first time that prompted my first low-star review. Not only her, but at least two others served us or provided drink refills, and all were nice and not once showed us any 'tude during dinner service. I even timidly asked one of them in passing if they would adjust the spiciness of a curry for me, as some other places have done in the past, and the lady kindly informed me they would certainly do that upon request. Kudos to them all for improving our experience and feelings about this place. We truly appreciate it.

    Seems things have changed here over time, and we were super relieved to see that, and so have come away from our second experience with this restaurant on a very good note, prompting an update with a better rated review. This place has certainly redeemed themselves in our eyes. So long as they continue this trend of friendly service, we'll come back again. I love a good curry, and can't wait to try theirs the next time.
