    My wife and I dined here this past weekend for an early Sunday dinner.  Here's a breakdown of the experience:

    Ambiance (4.5/5) - I think we were a bit fortunate here, since we were seated in a table right next to the window on the 2nd floor.  Otherwise, I would've found this place to be too loud and a bit cramped.  

    Service (5/5 minus the awkward server) - The staff were great.  They took the time to explain things while offering recommendations (which were spot on).  I usually wouldn't comment on something like this, but this server was extremely awkward.  He came over to fill our water, and then started on conversation about how he was new to the area, and that he heard eating sushi was good to eat since it was healthy.  He later came by and hung over our table for a good 30 seconds before speaking.  He wasn't rude or anything; however, it did feel uncomfortable at times.  That said, the rest of the staff was top notch.

    Food (5/5) - I've read numerous reviews before coming here, but didn't think the food could be as good as it actually was.  If you don't like raw food, this might not be the top choice, but I think they did have enough cooked dishes to choose from.  We decided on a lot of small plates/appetizers/sides instead of a large plate.  Here is what we had:
    Cauliflower soup with gruyere biscuit: 5/5 - the soup was good, but the biscuit was phenomenal!
    Arctic char: 5/5 - a fairly large portion of deliciousness
    Diver scallop: 6/5 - wow!  This was perfectly balanced with acidity and a little heat
    Mushroom salad: 6/5 - I could go on eating this one forever.  I don't care for runny eggs, but my wife loved that part.  The mushrooms themselves are perfect.
    Beef tartare on toast: 4/5 - I liked the dish, but I think everything (toast, cheese, horshradish) took away from the taste of the beef a bit too much.  Maybe a different ratio might make it even better?
    Crispy potatoes: 5/5 - I'd love to know how they get the potato so crispy - it paired perfectly with the peppers.
    Chili-glazed octopus: 5/5 - at first bite, I thought it had too much pepper (I love spicy food). However, when you get a good amount of the apples and endive, it perfectly balances the heat of the octopus.  This is probably the second best octopus dish I've eaten (Chef's Table at Brooklyn Fare being #1) - the octopus was cooked to perfection

    Dessert
    Petite Brownie: 2.5/5 - wasn't so petite, and wasn't that great either.
    Roasted Date Cake: 5/5 - my wife likes dates, but I don't care for them so much.  However, this was yummy.  I especially loved the coconut gelato.

    Drinks
    Blue something or other (tequila with a kick of cayenne): 6/5 - I could drink these all night.
    Hanky panky: 3/5 - a bit too strong for my wife, but otherwise good.

    Overall, Vernick is everything you could ask for and then some.  The food is simple, yet perfectly prepared.  You can tell Chef Vernick takes pride in his food.  All these reviews are not wrong - this is definitely one of Philly's finest.  While I typically prefer a bit quieter of a setting, with food this good, I'd easily learn to ignore it!  Don't think, just make a reservation - you will not regret it!

Vernick's menu is basically broken down into small plates (the majority of the offerings) and large plates (just a few traditional sized main courses, most of which come out of a wood-burning oven). Everything is meant to be shared, which always makes for a fun meal. Our party of four successfully housed: the avocado and spicy radish toast, charred spinach and fontina toast, fromage blanc and pickled ramp toast, fried green tomato toast, arctic char, crispy potatoes, asparagus, egg noodles, sea bass, roasted chicken, sorbet, chocolate creme pie and berry crisp. Overall, I can't wait to go back to Vernick...albeit maybe when I'm a millionaire because if I had to pay that tab again, I might not be able to eat for the rest of the month.
