    I've been here 30+ times, at their old location and now here.

    Dim Sum Garden never disappoints and the staff is very friendly. Like everyone else I love their soup dumplings. I always get soup dumplings and sauteed vegetables, and then often try to try something new. Everything is good, including the Americanized General Tso's Chicken.

    With the upscalification of Dim Sum Garden and the very aggressive increase in new Chinatown restaurants and businesses, I think the Philadelphia Chinatown is one of the top Chinatowns in the USA. The Philadelphia Chinatown is definitely better than the DC, Boston, or Chicago Chinatowns, and I think on a per square feet basis also better than the Manhattan or San Fran Chinatown. I hear the LA and Flushing Chinatowns are better but I have not been to either of these.

    After talking with one of the waitresses, I computed that they make almost 800,000 soup dumplings a year. Don't know if this also includes the soup dumplings that they make for Jose Garces' Yubōka, at Revel in Atlantic City. In any case, that's a lot of soup dumplings.

    Of interest: From Garces website "Chef Garces has enlisted a talented team to execute the menu and has consulted with Dim Sum Garden's fifth-generation chef-owner Da Shizhou and her daughter Sally on the family's famous Xiao Long Bao, or Shanghai soup dumplings, for Yubōka."

    I'm glad I found this place thanks to my friend Justyn W.'s suggestion. As a vegetarian, a BYOB dim sum place with this many meatless options is definitely a good find. And this place provided an overall experience that was mysteriously greater than the sum of it's parts. Here's the breakdown:

    Service: 2.5/3
    Food: 3
    Atmosphere: 4
    Value: 5
    Overall Experience: 5

    I'll explain: I was the first of my group to arrive solo at Dim Sum Garden, and the waitress was initially friendly, if not a little awkward. My friends rolled up a couple minutes later with a bottle of wine and some whiskey and I could sense an immediate change in demeanor. The staff started to eye us up with apprehension, assuming we would all soon become annoying, rowdy drunks. It's a totally understandable concern, but I definitely felt prematurely judged and slightly unwelcome. It was pretty clear that they didn't want us to open the whiskey because my friend had to ask for extra cups about 3 times before they eventually brought them around.

    The food itself is pretty good, especially the scallion pancakes, vegetable buns, vegetable noodles, and fried vegetable dumplings. When I heard "dim sum", I was immediately worried that I wouldn't have enough to eat due to a lack of veggie options, but we ended up with plenty, and even some extra to take home. For some strange reason, the waitress seemed to really push the chicken on a stick, so we ordered it, but none of my meat-eating dining companions seemed to like it at all. They don't bring the carts of food around like traditional dim sum places, but there is still the fun aspect of sharing food among a table and trying new things that I really enjoy. Getting a nice wine buzz on while munching on dim sum with friends is just inherently pleasurable, and the feeling of being unwelcome was soon forgotten (or maybe just ignored). The check at the end was just an added bonus- it came out to 10 dollars a person INCLUDING tax and tip. For a veritable dim sum feast, that is truly amazing. I'd be happy to go back anytime.
