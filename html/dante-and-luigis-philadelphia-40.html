    I've been to D&L's several times, but most recently for a special occasion with someone who has never been, it didn't disappoint.  I've sat in the parlor, in the front dining room, and last night dined in the back dining room next to the fireplace; I have read reviews taking about the decor, personally I find the place to be incredibly authentic and actually quite nice, much different (in a good way) than most other south philly italian places.  Anyway, the food has always been great and priced very reasonably, we ordered an italian cabernet for $26 (a bargain), and a traditional antipasto with some very sharp and very tasty provolone cheese for $14, I had the veal catherine with shrimp and crabmeat which was perfect and my date had the veal parm, which was huge and equally as good, for dessert we split an overstuffed cannoli, the bill was $100 - an exceptional value, can't wait to go back.  Just remember to bring cash or you will be stuck with a relatively small 3.5% credit card surcharge.

    Listed in Italian in in the City, Nice dinner, serves booze

    Like dining in a historic row house, complete with beautiful molding, chandeliers, fireplaces, and deep colored wallpaper.  There are several rooms, we ate in the slightly  upstairs one with the fireplace.  It's really old and beautiful.

    The clientele included both families and couples, dressed up and dressed down.  The wine list had a lot of reasonably priced bottles, and the food menu was large.  I had the veal parmigiana, and will have it again for dinner tonight because the portions are what you'd expect from a South Philly Italian restaurant.  The antipasti appetizer was one of the better that we've had in Philly.  And for dessert, I loved the overstuffed cannoli.

    Parking is a little tough but they have a valet option.  I saw some concerns about service in other reviews.  I thought our service was fine last night though.

    Listed in Favorite Philly area Italian Restaurants

    Really enjoyed this place. Went with a large party of 11 and service and atmosphere was really enjoyable. Dining rooms are broken out between several rooms in connected town homes from the late 1800's.  Old school South Philly Italian with straightforward well prepared dishes.  Lots of appetizers were shared.  Enjoyed the long hots and the fried calamari.
    For dinner I stuck with a South Philly staple and had the Veal  Big portion and perfect. Tender veal, not greasy, and just the right amount of cheese and hearty red sauce.
    Also tried the gnocchi. Light and airy. These were very good and also recommended.
    Wine list is pretty basic but had a couple of bottles of baby amarone which was reasonably priced at about $50 a bottle.
    Only disappointment was the cannolis. The cheesecake was much better!
    Will definitely be back.

    Mamma mia! The moment I laid eyes on this corner restaurant, I knew it was a gem - it looks like Tony Soprano is inside, eating, right now. I was not surprised to read about a mob shooting that took place here a few decades back. If that doesn't add to the authenticity, I don't know what does.

    And it just got better. The room was mostly full, a small dining room with pictures on the mantle and good old tunes playing quietly beneath the pleasantly noisy conversations around us. And our meal was gorgeous, not to mention delicious - great mussels, great eggplant rollatini (I usually don't like eggplant), amazing manicotti and the most beautiful plate of seafood cioppino I've ever seen. We chose from an abundant wine list, and saved room for some of the best tiramisu I've tasted in a long time.

    But the service - I might go as far as to say that this was the best, most perfectly timed service I've ever had. Our waiter, a middle-aged, mustachioed fellow, never offered his name, but he did time our plates perfectly, left us alone long enough to digest, and even combed our crumbs away. The busboys and water servers were all spot on, too. They really earn their money at this place.

    I already know this review will be a long one.  There are just so many positive words to use when describing the oldest Italian restaurant in the city.  (Ralph's is argued to be older but no one really knows the truth).  I've gone to Dante's on multiple occasions and never had a problem with being seated or waited on in a prompt manner... this review is specifically for my last visit though.

    I went with my girlfriend and two other couples last Friday night for 8:30pm reservations.  I didn't arrive until 9, (I live close-by so I don't know how much of an issue their valet parking is) and the rest of the group had already put in for appetizers and were sipping on some pre-dinner cocktails.  As soon as I was seated the waiter came over and asked me if I'd like a cocktail and to order an appetizer.  I love being waited on like this, make me feel as if it doesn't matter at all how late I was and wasn't being rushed to order an entree.  

    For starters I decided to get the Stuffed Portabello Mushroom w/ Crabmeat.  When it came out, it wasn't what I expected at all, not that it was bad, the description was just somewhat deceiving.  It was more of a flattened portabello mushroom with a giant lump of beautiful crabmeat resting on top of it.  I quickly got over the presentation after I took a bite of it.  Everything was cooked to perfection and the crabmeat wasn't the kind that tasted like it was half-filler, it was legitimate crab from the sea.  

    For dinner, I stayed on my seafood kick (not the smartest idea, I should have changed it up a little bit) and ordered the Baked Stuffed Shrimp with Crabmeat
    With a lemon wine cream sauce.  The dish was delicious, a little on the small side with only about 6 shrimp, but I wasn't complaining at all because it gave me plenty of room for dessert.

    It was a gift in disguise dining with 6 people because we all kept trying each others dishes.  The Veal Rollantini (Medallians of veal rolled & stuffed with spinach, Prosciutto & Buffalo Mozzarella cheese with a Shitake mushroom Demi glaze) was exceptional too, but also this dish was on the small side.  It's weird because half the dishes at the table were definitely small portions and the other half (pretty much anything with pasta) were much larger plates.  Usually, restaurants sway one way or the other so just be aware of this when ordering.  The best dish I tried all night though was one I can't remember the name of... It was a pasta dish with peas and a cream sauce, and topped with sliced filet.  I've never had an Italian meat dish as good as this one.  

    SIDE NOTE:  The house Pinot Grigio went extremely well with my dishes, and is more of a sweet wine than a dry.  I love a semi-sweet wine so this was right up my alley.

    The final round included our table gorging on a variety of incredible desserts and cappuccinos.  I immediately knew I would be impressed because normally I HATE TIRA MISU and after one bite I couldn't stop eating it, it tasted like it was just made minutes before they brought it out.  The chocolate mousse cake was astounding and the LaBomba Ice Cream definitely left it's mark on my finer dessert memories.  The description of vanilla and chocolate ice cream with a hard shell does it no justice.... It's literally a fist-sized ball of hard chocolate filed with the creamiest ice cream and a chocolate treat in the middle of it.  Everyone at the table agreed it was clearly the best.  

    At the end of the night our bill was $450 w/ tip and I think that was right on par with what we all expected for the kind of meal we had (most of our cocktails were doubles too and higher end like Hendricks Gin).  I'd go back whenever I have the opportunity, and the extra cash to blow.  

    Cheers to one of the best and the oldest Italian restaurants in the country!

Specialties

Established in 1899.. Dante & Luigi's is Philadelphia"s OLDEST Italian restaurant; and one of the oldest existing Italian restaurants in the United States; specializing in all homemade Traditional Italian dishes. Specialty items such as OssoBuco, Homemade Lasagna, Crepe Manicotti , Homemade Gnocchi, a 20 oz, TBone of Veal, along with fresh seafood, veal, chicken, and ofcourse, traditional Italian Gravy and Meatballs. This is a Philadelphia landmark, and truly the most authentic and delicious restaurant in the city. Lovely "Olde World " atmosphere in their 150 yr. old townhouse setting.

History

Established in 1899.

Philadelphia's Oldest Italian restaurant. Italian Immigrents would arrive at the docks, unable to speak english, with the name of the restaurant pinned to their lapels. They would then be brought to the restaurant to live on the upper floors, which was then a boarding house, and work in the Italian Market district or in the restaurant itself. Today 111 yrs, later, the Philadelphia landmerk continues to serve only the most authentic and homemade dishes. The tradition continues.

Meet the Business Owner

Connie L. Business Owner

Dante & Luigi's is owned by Michael & Connie LaRussa. Michael, born in Sicily, learned traditional italian recipies handed down for generations,and oversees the preparation of these dishes, by his chefs , in the restaurant. Connie coordinates the running of the business and staff.
