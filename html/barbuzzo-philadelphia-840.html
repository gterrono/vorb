    Overall very impressed, Yelp writers were right on target.  I had been reading about this restaurant for several months before we booked a night out in Philly with my in-laws.  Good Yelp advice to follow: BOOK EARLY!  

    The restaurant is SMALL so you are physically very squished.  The servers are working triple time so there were some slow moments in their more or less wonderful service (delays in the check arriving and drinks).  

    Food wise you are not in your average trattoria.  Or pizza joint.  Or tapas place.  Rather, you are in a very neat little amalgam of flavors that makes it very hard to decide what to try next.  Between the 4 of us we split the gnocchi, pork ragu pasta, stuffed meatballs, arrancini, fennel and avocado salad, and the arugula pear pizza.  Everything was obviously fresh, well cooked, and intensely flavorful.  None of us went away hungry, in fact, we didn't have room for the awesome buddino that everyone keeps raving about!  Seriously, if you know portion sizes and what you are ordering you will be OK.  The arrancini is a tiny, 4 ball, portion.  The pastas were each about 1 cup.  The pizza easily gave us each 1.5 generous slices.  

    Go if you have a hankering for an imaginative menu and want to try some really good food.  I can't wait to go back to try more!

    A bit of a shaky start that almost made me say to my beau, "Let's go somewhere else..." turned out to be unfounded; I'm glad we stayed because we had one of those dinners that made us speechless on a few occasions.

    We got there to a bustling scene at about 9:30 on a Saturday night, going for a late dinner after a busy day out. Upon entering, the host asked us if we had reservations. We matter of fact told him no, to which he sighed and seemingly didn't want to pay us attention. After walking around the restaurant for a moment, he comes back to us and points at an open two-top, telling us, "There. I THINK we can accommodate you." The tone took me aback a bit; it came off to me as if he thought he was doing us the biggest favor in the world. We sat by the front window, but I almost wanted to leave. Soon after, our waiter came, who was kind and told us about the best items on the menu, and I felt more at ease.

    I knew we were going the cheesy and pizza routes for dinner, so I decided a dry Crispin Brut Hard Cider for a drink, which was the best decision. We split the Wood Oven Roasted Feta Cheese for an appetizer, which was served bubbling with mixed olives, garlic, Fresno chile, oregano, olive oil, and grilled country bread. It was a nice sour and salty mix with a thick and (I say in a good way) dry country bread with a slight hint of rosemary lingering after each bite. As for the pizza, we got the Mortadella, with secret white sauce, buffalo mozzarella, fingerling potatoes, red onion and pistachio pesto. We were surprised by also getting a layer of la quercia prosciutto rossa, which we didn't order; it was normally $4, but it didn't show up on our bill! (Lucky us!)

    By the end of the evening, I was glad we decided to stay. Also, I either one, initially misjudged the host, or two, he was a little snippy upon our arrival but changed his tune, because he came in to check on us multiple times and even poured my cider for me. He was very kind and pleasant when all was said and done. Hey, it's a busy place, to say the VERY least. Can't be perturbed at him. We all have an occasional not so awesome moment, right? No hard feelings!

    I've heard that the caramel budino is to die for! Unfortunately, we were too full to have dessert. Well, next time! (And I'd make a reservation first. Ha!)

    I have been waiting for an opportunity to check out Barbuzzo for quite a while, and finally got the chance when my office went out to celebrate administrative assistants' day (so thankful for this Hallmark holiday this year!).  We arrived in time for our reservation and were quickly seated and provided with our water options.  

    Everything on the menu looked so delicious, I had a hard time deciding--I was between the chef's plate of vegetables, the turkey sandwich and pretty much all of the pizza options.  Eventually, I decided on the Zucca, a white pizza with zucchini and roasted corn with a bit of basil pesto.  It comes fresh out of the barbuzzo oven and is served with a size of chili oil (with generous amounts of ground-up chilis) and italian oregano, dried, but still on the stem.  I added everything to my pizza and started eating.  The chili oil provided nice flavor.  However, I did not find it even the least bit spicy.  Perhaps my tastebuds have been ravaged from years of spicy foods?  The crust was crisp and cooked to perfection, though the middle of the pizza was a bit soggy (though not terribly so).  The flavor was delicious.  The zucchini is served thinly sliced lengthwise on top of the pizza with dollops of the pesto.  The corn had a tendency to fall off the pie, while eating, but I just shoved it in my mouth with my hand!

    After eating my delicious pizza, I was totally stuffed, but could not resist the salted caramel budino (which seemed to bolster quite a few 5-star Yelp reviews).  Before trying this here, the budino at Osteria was my favorite dessert in the city, so I was psyched to try this variation.  It was absolutely amazing--a creamy, salty sweet goodness with crushed up oreo like chocolate on the bottom.  It was a tough battle for first, but the salted caramel pushed this dessert over the edge and barbuzzo has surpassed Osteria as my favorite dessert destination.  

    Overall, our service was okay, if a little slow on the drink refills and check payment collection.  The biggest takeaway from this review should be that you make sure you save room for the salted caramel budino when you visit barbuzzo!

    A month before coming to Philadelphia, I was looking through an old Bon App magazine, and there was a spread featuring wonderful on trend desserts, and of course the caramel budino was pictured.  I was eager to make it at home but since i knew i would have the chance to taste it in person in a month i decided to wait and try it where it originated, at Barbuzzo of course.  I secured a reservation no problem, and was glad, because the tiny restaurant was packed.    If you have a problem sitting inches away from people, this is not the place for you.  The restaurant was buzzing with energy.  We sat down and both ordered a fabulous blood orange cocktail which i have duplicated many times at home.  I took the suggestion of yelpers, and we started with the sheeps milk and grilled bread starter.  My only complaint was the pacing of the meal.  After receiving our cocktails, the two starters immediately followed.  As great as this was the tables were small and became instantly cluttered with plates.  I applaud the kitchen staff on speed, but it we had just set down in Philly and really wanted to take in the whole experience.  The sheeps milk ricotta was excellent, so creamy and the drizzle of olive oil sent it over the edge, so good.  It came with grilled bread, a simple touch but so worth it.  The warm bread with the cold cheese was perfect.  We also had the squid salad with sopressata, potato and lemon. Everytime i see an octopus salad i have to order it, this was no exception.  It was an unusual mix of seafood and pork, and worked so well.  The addition of the lemon brightened up the plate.  Of course we had to have to Uovo pizza, with bussel leaves, guanciale, secret white sauce, parmesan, fior di latte, and truffled farm egg.  Sometimes when ordering a pizza the toppings are amazing but the crust is nothing special.  Here, each part was as good as the other.  The crust was so bready and delicious and the top was divine, and finished off with an egg, it was unbeatable.  We came to try the dessert and almost went against it because of all the food we had consumed, but decided to go with it anyway.  I was expecting it to be heavy and rich, it wasn't, so light and the caramel flavour was robust.
