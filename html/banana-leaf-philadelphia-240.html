    Solid food, solid prices, BYO!

    This place is household name among Penn students for being a very cheap, fun, delicious BYO. I have been over 15 times in the past three years and have always left happy.

    The place is pretty spacious and they take reservations for large groups all the time, which makes scheduling a little easier. They have a dinner menu as well as sushi menu, although I have never ordered sushi here. I usually order some variant of noodles and drink.

    A couple of menu highlights:

    Roti Canai: I don't think I've ever come here without ordering at least one. Although it might be a bit pricey for what it is, it's a great way to start off the meal. Toasty and crispy pancake-like bread with a delicious chicken curry dipping sauce. Tasty, a little spicy, an overall table-pleaser.

    Chow Kueh Teow: Classic fried noodle dish. The flavors are solid as are the portion. Only complaint is that they can be stingy on the shrimp.

    Chicken Rending Rice: Bang-Bang-Bang-for-your-buck. One of the cheapest items on the menu, but not cheap on portion or flavor. If you're just there to chill with friends or just want to save money, this is the dish to go with.

    Big fan, going to miss this place once I leave Philly.

    Pros: prices, flavors, seating
    Cons: sometimes servers can be snappy

    I'm from east Malaysia; my state is Sarawak. I am always on the hunt for Malaysian food because it is so hard to find. The front desk guy at the Hilton Garden Inn down the street who speaks Indonesian said this place was better than Penang, so I came here.

    I ordered the roti canai, and typically the roti is the Indian version of paratha. I was disappointed. The restaurant's version of roti arrives in a rectangular bunch (not circular pieces of roti) and is so flaky that you can't really eat the sauce with the roti. I would probably liken the flakiness to phyllo dough. The sauce wasn't true roti canai sauce. Instead, it was actually chicken curry. Although I appreciate having pieces of chicken in my sauce, the reality is that serving chicken curry as the sauce instead of true roti canai sauce signals laziness and lack of authenticity.

    I had also ordered the curry mee with tofu, and it was a disaster. At first, I thought it tasted ok, but then I kept eating. The broth kept getting greasier as I ate, judging by the amount of fat globules that rose to the top. The noodles were thicker than spaghetti, almost like udon! This is NOT the right noodle for curry mee (known as the Sarawak version of laksa); instead, mi hun or bi hun should have been used. The tofu was ok, but one of the peppers was so bitter that I spat it out. Ugh.

    The service was good, so that's why I give this place two stars. I wanted to try Penang, but I am afraid if the hotel guy says this is the better place. Perhaps this food is delicious and acceptable to a non-Malaysian, but what I ate was not true to what I get in Malaysia.

    3.5 STARS.  (I hate half stars and rarely give them but some items were amazing and the things that were bad were really bad)

    THE GOOD: Any noodle dish.  I really wish I can remember the names of the one's I tried, but they are all delicious and each dish embodies a unique flavor of it's own.  
       The Prices- I don't think a single thing on the menu is over $12.  And you're getting very generous portions.  I don't know how they make their money.
        BYOB- Not many Chinese/Malaysian/Thai etc. places are BYOB, (I know alot of sushi places are but that's a very different experience).  This place is known for BYOB, in fact I've talked to people that solely come here for the BYOB and cheap eats.  
       The Service- Host very friendly, seated immediately, servers are back and forth every few minutes and manager comes out to make sure all the tables are enjoying their meal.

    THE BAD: The bone-in duck.  It just really stands out to me.  I love duck and I've had prepared a variety of ways in a variety of different cities in this great, food-loving country.  This dish seemed like it had 2-3 neck pieces in it (probably because it's cheap) and the meat was especially tough and hard to pull off the bone.  I've never had duck meat that was as tough as this, I could be in the dark about a new way duck is prepared but I doubt it.  There was also a ridiculous abundance of bones in every piece too, I assumed "bone-in" meant I would find the main larger bones holding the succulent meat together, not every little pin-bone wrapped between the toughest duck meat I ever had.  
          The chicken wings I ordered for an appetizer were very tasty, well half of them were... the other half were bloody and undercooked.  The table next to mine had the same issue and the manager looked very embarassed and immediately recooked them and didn't charge them for it either.  At least they know about good customer service, they just have to keep a closer eye on the kitchen.

    I'll give this place another shot.  I know they have great food in that large menu they offer.  I wasn't too pleased with everything my first couple times around, but like a cult classic movie, it takes a few sittings to really appreciate it for what it's worth.
