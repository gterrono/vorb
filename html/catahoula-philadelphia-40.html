    This place is more of a vaguely Mardi Gras-themed bar serving Cajun food than a Cajun restaurant. That being said, the food they serve here is pretty good. Added bonus: there's outdoor seating in an adjoining courtyard of sorts.

    There were only 4 beers on draft, one of which was the Coronado Orange Ave Wit. For $6 the pricing was on the higher end of the average range, but it was a quite light and refreshing beer. I believe select drafts are $3 during Phillies games, but that wasn't one of them.

    As per Yelp's recommendations, I tried the My Burger. If you like goat cheese, this is a pretty delicious burger. If you don't like goat cheese, the cheese on this is a little overpowering so you might want to opt for the build-your-own Your Burger. Otherwise, this $13 burger was solid. I asked for medium and mine came out pretty well done, but it somehow still managed to be juicy. Tried a bite of the shrimp po boy; it was tasty though a little on the small side for $12. The Cajun-spiced fries were good.

    They have a $5 special on "sliders" during Phillies games: you choice of catfish, andouille sausage, or something else that I can't remember. These sliders are actually more like half sandwiches that come with a small order of fries, and if you order 2 it would be a lot of food for not a lot of cash.

    Overall, this bar serves up pretty decent Cajun food. I would like to come back at some point to try some of the real entrees, though they looked a bit expensive ($15-19) for their size and considering the fact that they're served in a bar.

    Hushpuppies ($3 during Happy Hour) were warm and thick, served over a surprisingly spicy tomato salsa and drizzled with creamy aioli.

    I had the arugula salad, served with crisp caramelized Tasso ham pieces, shaved carrots and Romano cheese and red onion pieces. It came with some very heavily spiced warm shrimp with crusted with smoked paprika -- more intense that most people would prefer, but I loved it. The advertised watermelon was conspicuously missing, though I didn't mind. Delicious and somehow walking that fine line between light and indulgent.

    As for the food, dinner entrée and lunch were okay, but I may be partial as I don't like spicy food or po boys. Desserts on the other hand were amazing. Molasses pecan pie, bread pudding, cheesecake, it's all fantastic.  Took my parents here for mother's day/wedding anniversary brunch, and they LOVED their food and the outdoor seating.

    As for the crowd, it's a lot of people from the immediate Queen Village neighborhood. More 30s to 50s. Not many young hipsters in here. Place does get filled with Phillies fans during the game. Since it's small, it does get loud inside and only has one bathroom. If you want someplace quieter, or a little more upscale, I'd recommend the Village Belle. For a low key night for a Phillies game, drinks and dessert or brunch, this is the spot!
