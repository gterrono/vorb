    Listed in Craft Beer

    Is it a little overboard to plan an entire vacation around visiting this one bar?  Well, I've done it for Eulogy Bar.

    I was lured in about 5 years ago when a coworker and I were visiting the area and just wandering around after visiting Independence Hall.  They had a sign out front that said "DRINK GOOD BEER".  Ok.  That's all I needed to read.  "Good" is an understatement.  The Belgian beer menu is massive!  Totally overwhelming to a first-timer, so I put my palate in Dave's hands and was not disappointed.  He asked me what types of beer I like generally and then offered a couple of recommendations.  What I drank that day along with the crab cake is what brought me back repeatedly over the years.  

    I've been there with my husband to see the Cardinals play the Phillies and we had the mussels prepared with two different broths (they have 9), the Moules Mariniere and Saffron.  Both superb.  Living in a land-locked state I never trust the seafood, and to have fresh mussels in Philly was really a treat.  Again Dave gave us some great recommendations and the locals in the bar were very friendly and funny considering we were wearing our Cardinals gear.  We should have stayed there instead of going to the game.  It was extremely hot that day, our seats were in the sunburn section, and the Phillies killed us.  

    I've honestly never been upstairs.  I frequently try to visit restaurants at off hours and I've never had a problem getting a table near the bar.  I saw several people peek their head in and then keep walking.  They are really missing out.  My husband and I decided that if we ever got the right job offer to live in Philly we'd live within walking distance of this place.  

    Then a couple of years later I took a coworker again and we had mussels and she had the crab cake sandwich which she could not stop moaning with pleasure over, and I tried something that I don't see on their menu anymore.   It was called something like duck and wild mushroom confit.  It was a duck and mushroom mixture in a perfectly baked phyllo dough crust.  Oh My GOD.  Heaven!  It looks like they've made a few changes to their menu.  Time to plan a return visit!

    Listed in Philadelphia, je t'aime (Or best of the best), Old City/Society Hill Hangouts, Beer me up, Scotty, Visiting Philadelphia

    Eulogy is exactly the kind of bar I want to go to--cramped and low-key with a huge selection of beers and bartenders who both a) know their beer and b) take the time to make good suggestions. I studied abroad in Brussels in college, so Eulogy has all standards I loved in Belgium along with some new favorites. With owners from Bruges and a beer list like this I really don't think you can go wrong.

    The Kwak (a Belgian specialty amber ale) and the Tripel Karmelit (full-bodied, smooth, slightly spicy) were the standouts. I had the Ommegang on special and it was crazy good, and we also tried the La Chouffe (Belgian) and the La Meule (a Swiss golden ale with sage, my least favorite).  

    Just those beers--no food--cost a pretty penny, so I don't really see this as an every Friday night type place. That being said, if you want to treat yourself to some truly delicious beers you won't find everywhere (or maybe anywhere) else, this is the spot.

    I don't know how much I could possibly add to what's been said about Eulogy. A friend and I went for the first time over the weekend, after the traditional debate that occurs every time we decide to try a restaurant together. After narrowing the neighborhood down to a still-wide swath of Old City, and eliminating Chifa (needed more beer) and Khyber Pass Pub (already been!), we finally agreed on Eulogy. I've been dying to try it for ages, and I was seriously excited. Neither of us were disappointed.

    I arrived first and was seated quickly upstairs, where I waited for my friend and tried not to get too overwhelmed by the 300+ beer list. I have a feeling the list itself was more dizzying than the actual beer, and maybe I was craving the familiar when I ordered a Weyerbacher Merry Monks for my first round. My dining partner arrived, and our server (Lindsay, I'm almost positive) walked us both through subsequent beer choices admirably. We tried several, and I fell in love with the Blanche de Bruxelles. Oh, we also ate some amazing food.

    When we eat together, especially somewhere new for us or that doesn't offer a tasting menu, we like to order a handful of things to share between us. We started with the duck confit mushroom strudel. It was a perfect amount to share, and it was delicious. The strudel was just flaky enough to stay together when it needed to, and the duck was delicious. We agreed we could have devoured at least four more. Once we'd contained our excitement over the strudel and actually looked at the rest of the menu, we went for the roasted lavender chicken, the Belgian frietjes (order all three sauces or live to regret it), and a pound of mussels with sausage, boursin and potatoes. The strudel still won out overall, but the chicken was juicy and tender and the mussels were done and presented beautifully. The frietjes on their own might not have been the most remarkable I've ever had, but the sauces were fantastic. I favored the malt vinegar aioli while my friend fell for the bourbon remoulade.

    The room was dimly lit, and populated by a mix of couples and small groups. There were servers and runners all over the place, and though it was busy, we felt neither neglected nor hounded to pay the check and move on. The check, by the way, was a very reasonable $80 total, all money well spent. I never feel totally comfortable at high top tables where there are others around, but that's my problem, not Eulogy's. Overall, the beer was fantastic, the food was delicious, and the experience was great. I'm still dreaming about that duck.

Specialties

This is Beer Heaven, give a "high praise" Eulogy for your favorite beer after you consume it.  We also prepare our food in the authentic Flemish Belgian Style as opposed to other Belgian restaurants who choose the French Walloon Belgian style.  This means our fries are thick enough to truly hold a double fry technique, our fries go "crunch, goo" because they are the right thickness, the wimpy skinny fries elsewhere go "goo goo goo" because they are too skinny and the double fry technique does not work.  Also our mussels are larger than the PEIs other restaurants use, this is because our mussels are the authentic North Sea size like in Belgium.  The more meat the more flavor the mussel can hold in our ala minute pan technique (all ingredients added fresh, never ever ever any pour over sauce on mussels).  Also our handburgers are award winning based on seven secret spices and our fish and chips and other entrees are as good as the beers ! Make a reservation at www.eulogybar.com

History

Established in 2002.

My family is from Brugge, Belgium, I am passionate about Belgian beer, food and heritage, this adds an authenticity to the business.  I got the building in 2002 and built the restaurant myself using antique stain glass behind the bar from an old Philadelphia church and a chandelier from an old church as well.  The bar was constructed in Fishtown in Philadelphia by a local carpenter using Honduras Mahogany I imported and the design of the bar was based on a blueprint I drew.  
We take our beer very very very serious, our beer starts in a beer cooler and is then delivered via dual gas blenders using co2 and nitrogen blends for the proper carbonation technique.  Most of our draft beer never travels more than six feet but along that journey there are glycol filled lines keeping the beer at 40 ish degrees until it hits your glass.  We have about 200 different glasses we keep in stock because most of the taste for beer is accomplished through your smelling it, yes your nose!
