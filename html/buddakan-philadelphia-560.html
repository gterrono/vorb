    I was treated to a belated birthday dinner with my boyfriend and his family this past weekend. Apparently the only reservation we could get for a Saturday evening was 5:30 PM which was fine by me. I'm from New York and have heard amazing things about these restaurants.
    I don't understand how anyone can rate this place with anything less than five stars. The staff was quick and frighteningly attentive. We compared our waitress stealthiness to that of the butler from Mr. Deeds.
    We started off with a pitcher of Zen-gria. The rose pedals in our glasses was a lovely touch of elegance. For appetizers we ordered/shared the edamame ravioli (INCREDIBLE - we read a yelp review that said that they were overrated, that person is an idiot, I'm sorry. They were FANTASTIC), the Asian Ceasar Salad, and the Chicken & Ginger Dumplings. Every appetizer was wonderful and was devoured in minutes.
    I had the Pan Seared Sea Bass which was cooked to perfection. Not an overly fishy taste and the perfect soft texture. My boyfriend had the Wasabi Crusted Filet and licked his plate clean. Unfortunately, I can't remember what his parents ate but everyone was raving about how delicious their entrees were.
    For dessert, we shared the Crying Chocolate and and the Dim Sum Doughnuts. The Crying Chocolate was really nice but small so if you order it, I would only get it for one or two people. The Doughnuts were more than enough for all four of us especially since it came with jam and chocolate dipping dishes. I almost forgot to mention that since we were celebrating two birthdays, they put a sparkler in each dish. No "Happy Birthday" sing-a-longs here but we were all smiles. Who doesn't love sparklers?
    All in all, it was a perfect dinner and it made the top 5 meals of my life.

    | A |
    Edamame Ravioli, Cantonese Spring Roll, Chicken Dumplings, Char Siu Pork Bun, Kobe Slider, Barbecue Ribs

    | B |
    Thai Chicken Salad, Caesar Salad, Miso Soup, Egg Drop Chicken Noodle Soup, Vegetable Rice

    Grilled Lamb Chop, Garlic Shrimp, Cashew Chicken, Wasabi Tuna Tataki

    Char Siu Pork Bun - I really like pork buns.  This one was tastey but it could use a little more sauce.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
