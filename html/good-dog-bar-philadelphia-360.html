    Maybe it's because I'm a cat person. Maybe it's because, by name alone, you'd think this place was a pet store. Or maybe it's because once I'd heard of Guy Fieri's visit I knew I'd have to let the place air out for a while (he looks like he'd smell terribly of hair gel and bronzer). In any case, I'm glad I finally took the time to give Good Dog a chance.

    A friend and I decided to meet here around 2:30pm for a late lunch. Seeing as this falls into the limbo hours between a proper lunch and dinner, we had the luxury of being able to select any seat in the house. I was surprised at how small a space the downstairs occupied. Guess I didn't know what to expect, but this place is definitely much more bar than it is restaurant. Note: I did see in pictures that the upstairs is much larger, with a pool table and such. I'll definitely have to check that space out next time around.

    The first thing you'll notice once you take your seat are the boundless pictures that cover the walls. I'll give you three guesses to their subject, and the first two don't count... From the looks of it, they're all patron-submitted photos of their own dogs. I even saw (from the back of the menu) that they have an ongoing contest where the best photographs are awarded ribbons of excellence. It's a pretty neat idea, if you ask me.

    After soaking up all the cuteness I could handle for one day, I turned my attention to the menu. And what a menu they have. This place definitely leans more in the direction of pub grub: appetizers, sandwiches, burgers, etc., but all prepared with a finesse that explains (and justifies) the price. They also offer larger plates such as duck pot pie and meatloaf, the latter rumored to be out of this world. On this day, however, I opted to go for the "Fried Tomato BLT," while my friend chose the "Buffalo Style Shrimp Po Boy."

    Our sandwiches only took a few minutes to make it to our table, and my god, after tasting them, I couldn't have waited a moment longer. Who knew that frying the tomato on a BLT would make it taste so damn good?! I sure didn't; but now that the secret's out, there's no going back for me. The Applewood-smoked bacon was cooked perfectly, the lettuce was fresh and crispy, and the mayo, oh my, the basil mayo: sublime. Not to mention all of this was packed between two healthy slices of country sourdough. My only critique, and this is purely because of personal preference: the crust on the bread was a bit much to handle and was overly chewy, compared to the softness of the bread's center.

    The single bite of my friend's sandwich that I had cemented the epic-ness of her meal as well. Fresh shrimp, perfectly spiced buffalo sauce, crumbed bleu cheese, and what I considered the crowning glory: celery leaves...Oh, and let me not forget to mention that both of these plates were served with a mix of hand-cut Yukon and sweet potato fries; just a tad bit soggy (the way I like them) and served with a delicious aioli sauce for dipping.

    We even took the time on our visit to try dessert, which startled our waitress, seeing as she said the who "did you save room for dessert" trick never works at lunch. Well it did for us: once we saw the PB&J bars on the menu. A peanut butter cookie topped with fresh strawberry jelly and peanuts, and covered in a light banana creme sauce. The cookie itself could have been a bit more peanut buttery, but all in all, it was the perfect compliment to a delicious meal.

    If you haven't already, add Good Dog to your list of places to check out. You won't be disappointed.

    Listed in Nobody Likes You, Mom, I forgot my lunch!, Burgers are Delicious, Conference Food

    When Best of Philly did a bracket-style championship for best burgers in Philadelphia, I'd tried almost all of the contenders - except for Good Dog. As the bar has also had a cameo on Diners, Drive-Ins, and Dives, my ignorance of their existence appears to be an egregious oversight. This weekend I remedied this serious problem, and while I'm late to the party I'm happy to corroborate their reputation for delicious burgers.

    I came in pretty early on a Saturday and it was relatively quiet. By the time I left, around 2p, it was starting to get busy and the bar was full. If you're coming to chat and catch up with friends, I'd recommend stopping by at an off hour - but if you're out with friends for drinks and food and are probably going to get a little loud yourselves, it's got a nice friendly vibe when it is getting full. A little hipster, but whatever, all of Philadelphia is a little hipster.

    The bartender was awesome. Shortly after taking my seat at the bar I found a spider on my shoulder - which I responded to (naturally) by screaming and flinging myself around and flailing. The bartender was extremely sympathetic and we swapped stories of giant bug encounters, and she replaced my drink because I am pretty sure I flung the giant spider into my glass. She gave me excellent recommendations for food and was on top of keeping me stocked with beverages, ketchup, etc.

    The burger absolutely lived up to my expectations. I ordered mine with gouda, caramelized onions, and bacon. It was perfectly cooked, juicy and a little greasy but not to the point that it was falling apart or making a giant mess. I am in love with their fries - I'm a fan of sweet potato fries, but about halfway through my order always start longing for some regular fries. Here, I do not have to choose! And the aioli they are served with is awesome.

    Listed in Bars for Beer Snobs

    I visited Good Dog as part of my quest to eat all of the good food and drink all of the good beer in Philly in one weekend.  (Obviously, that's unpossible, but I like to try!)

    I loved the environment. I'm an aging punk rocker and it's always great to see people with Misfits shirts and tattoos filling a place.  At the same time, since it was 2pm on a Saturday, there was a family with a small child there and it totally wasn't out of place.  

    The ambiance was cool, including a great mix of music playing.  As you might expect, there were tons of pictures of dogs on the wall.  Apparently Good Dog has a dog photo contest every year, and these appeared to be the entrants.  

    The beer list was solid, but not as extensive as I might've hoped.  I think there were about 10 beers on top and a handful of bottles and cans.  I usually expect a bit more selection from someplace when I am looking for a true beer bar, but the things they do have are solid choices.

    I kept hearing about how good the burgers were.  I was kind of disappointed to see they actually only had 2 meat burgers on the menu.  Since I'm not a fan of "stinky" cheeses, I didn't opt for the burger stuffed with Roquefort.  My friend did get it and liked it, but said it wasn't MIND BOGGLING.  I got the regular burger and had a similar reaction.  The fries are a mixture of regular fries and sweet potato fries - which is good if you like sweet potato fries, but I don't.  I did love that they brought out aioli for the fries automatically though.  

    The service was pretty good in the beginning, but our server kind of disappeared around the time we were getting ready to leave.  It wasn't a big deal, but we did wait for our check for about 10-15 minutes.  

    Overall, Good Dog didn't live up to the hype for me, but I wouldn't mind going back.

    First off, forget about Guy Fieri and the episode of Diners, Drive-ins and Dives featuring Good Dog.  Guy failed to do a Good Job of highlighting the best that Good Dog has to offer.  Good Dog is one of the better bars in the Center City area.  Completely UNpretentious, this is a place where you can drink some great brews and order up some elevated pub food.  The waitstaff and bartenders are super chill as well, even with how busy they get on most evenings.  

    I almost always order the Good Dog Burger.  It is definitely on my Top 5 Burger List for Philadelphia. A delicious concoction of a 1/2 lb. house ground sirloin stuffed with Roquefort cheese & topped with caramelized onions, served on a toasted brioche bun.  It's big and it's awesome.  Something to say about it though, it isn't for everybody.  I have introduced the Good Dog Burger to many people and to put it simply, if you don't like stinky cheese, order the regular burger instead.  Roquefort is a super funky and tangy bleu cheese, which kind of punches you in the mouth (and nose) if you are not expecting it.  Unfortunately, not everybody loves the Roquefort.  But I do.

    The fries, oh the fries.  Yes, the fries deserve their own paragraph. Because the french fries served with the burgers are the best this city has to offer.  They are some bangin' fries for sure, a signature mix of hand-cut yukon & sweet potatoes served with a side of homemade aioli.  The combination of regular potato and sweet potato provides a sweet, sweet symphony to my taste buds.     For being a bar that specializes in good beer, they have a few culinary standouts. Their Caesar salad is awesome. Like really awesome. My boyfriend declared that it was one of the best, if not the best Caesar salad he's ever eaten. I would have to agree.

    I got the chicken chili with cornbread. It's definitely enough for a full meal. The chili was flavorful with just a bit of a kick. It wasn't too brothy or greasy either- two things that make me normally shy away from restaurant chilis. The cornbread was crispy on the edges and soft in the center. It was a tad sweet, but not cake-like. I like a large crumb to my cornbread and this had it.

    My wine was pretty mediocre, but then again, I probably should've ordered wine at a bar. Or wine with chili for that matter.

    This is a little far from me to be a go-to place, but it's good bar food if you're looking for a place to have dinner with friends who love beer.
