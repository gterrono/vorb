    It usually takes me a little bit to warm up to new potential lunch spots, but it was love at first bite with Sabrina's. Here is a quick taxonomy about why you should love Sabrina's too:

    1) the space: I can't think of a restaurant that better evokes the collegiate aesthetic than Sabrina's. It's in a building/house on Drexel's campus, and is open and airy with original wood floors, etc. It feels like you should hang out here all day, drink coffee, and discuss how Breaking Bad partially adheres to the form of the classical Greek tragedy (at the expense of actually doing the reading you need to do to prepare for class).

    2) the food: the menu is pretty expansive, offered breakfast all day alongside lots of great lunch and dinner options. They also have a whimsical take on naming their specials, going for names that capture the zeitgeist (what's 'trending,' if you will), like "Gus Fring's Pollos Hermanos Chicken and Waffles."

    My favorite dish on the menu for lunch is the Cali-Turkey sandwich. It's thick-sliced turkey on an amazing toasted, whole-grain bread, with alfalfa greens and roasted red pepper mayo. It comes with bacon, but I sub out the bacon for the delicious housemade long hots. I also sub in a salad for fries, as their side salad is amazing: slices of strawberry, goat cheese, etc.

    3) the staff: I usually get the same waitress (as I usually go at the same time), and I like her very much. She's always very pleasant, is patient when I ask for substitutions (like the aforementioned one), and always gets my order right.

    Awesome place. Definitely try it out.

    I really dislike brunch, I dislike the idea of brunch and I dislike the food associated with brunch. I dislike brunch in New York, I dislike brunch everywhere. HOWEVER, the brunch at Sabrina's was actually very good. My only two complaints were the wait time to be seated [even after calling earlier to put my name down on the list] and the fact that it is literally located in a campus dormitory. I left college many years ago, as nice as my experience was, I would not like to return.

    The hostess quoted us a 30 minute wait, but it was really more like 15 so I wasn't too cranky about it. Plus we were seated by the picture window.

    One thing I absolutely loved was the special Hunger Games themed brunch menu. Every dish was named after a character from the Hunger Games. I had the "President Snow's White Rose Special Egg White Omelette" with spinach, goat cheese, celery root, and peppers [$10.95].

    I was looking for a healthy, low calorie-ish option. While in theory this sounds healthy and low calorie, the portion was HUGE, HUGE, HUGE. Massive. It was also served with bread [WITHOUT BUTTER???] and roasted potatoes. Overkill much. It was flavorful and you could taste each component. I finished it all because I was hungry but definitely felt way too full [one reason why I hate brunch].

    I have been here a couple of times in the past year and have been pleasantly surprised and pleased almost every time.

    They have a great balance of your standard American brunch dishes (french toasts, pancakes, egg dishes) and then a wonderfully innovative brunch specials menu (I'm a fan) that rotates every month or so. I usually always order from the brunch specials menu, just because it boasts dishes you would have never heard of before. They have a real knack for coming up with incredibly funky sounding combinations of food but making it all taste really great in the end.

    The one thing I would say about Sabrina's is that it gets really really crowded at peak hours on the weekends, and they don't take reservations which makes it hard. I've waited 45 minutes to almost 75 minutes for a table on Saturday or Sunday morning. However, I went Wednesday morning at around 10:30 AM today and we were seated immediately. So just be cautious and aware of the times at which you dine here. Another thing going along with the intense crowd is that the restaurant can often get really noisy and it's hard to hear the people you are dining with. However, the faux-outdoor seating area near the back of the restaurant is a perfect way to avoid this (if you can!)

    My favorites are definitely the pumpkin pancakes and the french toast dishes (the french toast dishes on the brunch specials menu are a must!). Eggs are solid as well. I tried the Huevos Rancheros this morning and was pleasantly surprised by the mix of flavors, guacamole, fried eggs, and crispy tortilla (I'm used to a different texture that isn't so crunchy or hard with my huevos rancheros, but I really liked the change). Would definitely recommend the Huevos Rancheros as well.

    Prices are standard, about $10-$12 for each dish. I will definitely be returning, seeing as I have already been back 3 or 4 times!
