    Went to this place after my girly told me how good it was.

    The atmosphere is great. The staff? Amazing and super attentive and super friendly.

I want to talk about the cranberry sauce, but it might get the review flagged for me thinking dirty things ago a food portion. Lets just say "Yum".

    We then got after dinner cocktails. I had a "Sweet Tart" cocktail...not the manliness thing, but it did get me pretty talkative after 2. Very sweet...very tart.

    Price-wise, it was very fair. I wasn't surprised at the price considering the location, quality of food, and awesome staff.

    When fiance is home in Philadelphia, this becomes a part of the steady rotation of dinner's out when I don't feel like cooking. We are huge fans of the mac and cheese, he likes mine when I make it, he loves his grandma's when she makes it, but he is beside himself when he gets that bowl of still bubbling goodness straight out the Jones oven. I don't blame him one bit, I have tried it before and really, it is some straight up good old comfort food.

    The menu being straightforward American fare leaves little room for major creativity, but I think that's why we like it so much. Everything that we have enjoyed from the meatloaf to the turkey dinner, hamburgers to grilled cheese, is simple yet tasty, worth it to not have to cook it myself with the inevitable tackling of the dirty dishes afterward. I have submitted more positive recommendation cards for waiters/waitresses here than any other Stephen Starr restaurant, I feel like they like their jobs so much better being in a casual atmosphere where they can have real conversations with their patrons instead of the stiff and stuffy demeanors of employees at Starr's other locations.

    The decor is kind of whimsical with a big mural of jumping kids on the far right wall and each table has a little plug in night light depicting natural wonders like big green fields with bright blue skies. The couches and tables in the center of the restaurant are great for groups, booths very easily fit four...tables for two are a little cramped especially when my 6'10'' fiance is along for the ride. The wait can be long on weekends, but worth it if this is your final destination after an afternoon of shopping or sightseeing.

    If you're looking for a little more upscale American dining, I would definitely recommend giving this place a chance.

    I dream about the huevos rancheros at Jones.
    I want everyone I love to taste them.
    I want them to be served at my wedding and every subsequent birthday.
    Hell, I want to be buried in a vat of them and eat my way out.

    So, yeah. I really like the huevos rancheros.

    Upon first inspection, I'll admit I was a little leery - "Dude, you just gave me a plate of beans covered in cheese with an egg on top and some guacamole in the middle. Donde esta the everything else - the tomatoes, the olives, the onions? The crispy, fried tortilla?

    Oh, how foolish I was. Good food doesn't need to be tarted up.

    The beans were smooth with an almost creamy texture.  I have no idea what seasonings they use, but I'm about to launch a campaign begging Stephen Starr to bottle and sell that spice mix. Every home in America deserves to eat something that delicious. The guacamole was fresh and chunky -- a nice contrast to the beans and the fire-roasted tomato salsa blows everything off the map with a spiciness and heat that really elevates the dish.

    My only gripe (and this is my fault for not specifying) is that I would have preferred my eggs over-easy as opposed to over-well. The thought of cracking that yolk and having it ooze all over the beans? Gives me shivers (Wylie Dufresne would be proud. According to Anthony Bourdain, that dude is a major, "egg slut.")

    I did everything but lick the plate clean and honestly, had it just been me and the fiance? I probably would have. Usually, I'm one of those people

    I didn't feel like ordering an adult beverage, but a friend ordered a Bloody Martini with Old-Bay around the rim, a blue-cheese stuffed olive and a healthy splash of both vodka and hot sauce. If that's not enough to wake you up, you might want to check for a pulse.

    So, go to Jones for brunch. Order the huevos rancheros and when you do, please tell me about it, so we can talk about how mouth-watering and life-changing they are.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
