    At least I can say I've been here!

    I checked out Shake Shack on Wednesday and thankfully didn't encounter the long wait I expected.  The customer service here is spot on.  The guru of hospitality, Danny Meyer, has taught his staff well.  One of the things I picked up on was the subtle and rarely seen customer service tactic of staff keeping eye contact with customers when taking their orders.  While most customers probably didn't notice or care, perceptive ol' me did and I appreciated it far more than the usual aloofness and fakeness which is too often the norm from servers.  When the dude ringing up my order asked me how my day was going, I actually think he meant it.  And even if he didn't, he at least pretended to.  Makes you feel like you're respected and makes you want to return.  Although, in my case, I'm not going to return.  Here's why...

    Shake - I ordered a black and white with malt.  My taste buds found no malt but they sure found the $.50 charge on top of an already expensive $5.00.  I mean, seriously,  $5.00 is preposterous for a shake that was no better than what you'd find at the golden arches.

    Burger - I got the double SmokeShack.  The dude asked me how I wanted it cooked.  I replied "medium rare".  He said they can only cook it medium or rare (WTF?) so I said, OK, I'd like it rare.  It came medium.  You charge $9.00 for a puny portion (yes, it was juicy; yes, I loved the pepper spread; yes, they give you thick applewood bacon) but then can't even show the minimum level of attention to detail?  Sad.  And they really have no excuse because they weren't that busy.  

    Cheese fries - The cheese was very sinful.  I was skeptical of crinkle-cut fries but they were fresh and well seasoned.  Very good overall.

    So, yeah, I can say I hit up the Shake Shack but call me a one/off.  500 Degrees, I'll be knocking at your door next.

    A Father's Day outing to Shake Shack got no resistance from the kids.  Good sign, because I was sure that the long line would elicit moans and groans.  But at 1pm for lunch, the line was about 25 deep and we were at the counter in 10 minutes. Bu then, we were handed the pager and used the intervening 25 minutes before we got our food to jockey for a table.  Takes a little patience, location and of course, strategy, but an appropriate table should open up.  Be ready to swoop, courteously, of course, and stake your claim.  Just don't come with the kids' friends (there were 16 of us) if you can help it.

    The burgers were very good.  Juicy (or if you have it in your heart to disparage, greasy) beyond all expectation (have lipitor at hand), good beefy Lafrieda blend (if only I had remembered to order medium rare) and bright fresh leafy lettuce and tomatoes made for a very good sandwich.  The eldest daughter got the 'Shroom Burger, which ever so slightly bested the meat sandwich.  Oozing with cheese and fried to a crisp, the moistness and deep woodsy flavor of the portobella was satisfying.  The man-cub got the best of both worlds with his Shack Stack.  

    The all important fries were ok.  Crinkle cut and crispy, but it lacked the rich potato flavor that Five guys and Nathan's fries have.  Maybe make them it bit thicker?

    The Rittenhouse Concrete was fabulous.  Chocolate flavors with depth and cocoa wattage and the crunch of La Colombe beans. Impressive.

    Service was very helpful.  Menu stated that you have to explicitly request lettuce, tomato, etc, but we didn't and we wanted.  They insisted that they make new burgers with the said toppings and delivered (with a comp order of fries) to the table.  

    Bathroom was clean and tres modern.  The paper towel dispenser was really a hand dryer.  Clever.

    New destination for the family for burgers.
