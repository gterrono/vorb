    In the summer, it was 6 a.m. on a weekday morning, and while I didn't feel like cart food in Philly, I also wasn't about to eat a healthy, part-of-this-complete breakfast. I was astonished to see this place's doors open. I went inside.

    They offer breakfast sandwiches in the a.m. and an array of hot dogs at lunch. It's a standing-room-only vibe (tall tables) with TVs. Not where you want to thank the employees at lunch... but they looked like they do serious business.

    As it turned out, the 4 dollar bacon, egg, and cheese was good... but how hard are those to screw up? Plus, for twice the price, they were half the size of a cart creation. Philly doesn't have many hot dog places, so if you need 'em, go here. But for breakfast, stick to the basics.

    On a lazy Saturday afternoon, I found a small advertisement for Moe's Hot Dog House.  A legit hot dog shop in Philly?  This piqued my interest and I immediately went to their website.  I looked at their hot dog menu and just started salivating.  Chicago Dog with yellow mustard, relish, onions, tomatoes, jalapeno, pickle & celery salt for $2.85?  The "Connie Mac", a mac-and-cheese topped hotdog?  All served on hot dog sized hoagie rolls?  I verbally spouted the menu to my friend while stuttering with my heartbeat palpitating and almost immediately we were on our way.

    The hot dog joint is slightly out of the way of everywhere, except for the graduate hospital area.  The ride there takes you through what seems to be Philadelphia's armpit; the west side of Washinton Ave and Grey's Ferry.  This is all fine because it's so worth it in the end.  The pointy building sits at an odd angle, which has the shop looking directly at a power station.  You can't really miss it because there's nothing else around and the bright yellow signs are... bright.  Inside it's spiffy clean and kinda retro looking.  

    I ordered a chicago dog and the engine #47 2-alarm (a deep fried dog that's fried quickly or longer, so it can be really dark and crispy).  So good, all-beef D&W franks.  The menu isn't just limited to wieners, there's little specials posted all over the place, including a home-made half-pound "Juicy Lucy" burger, fish cakes, hot sausages, local microbrewed sodas... I wanted it all but my puny stomach said no.  On top of it all, the staff is super friendly.  They want you to be there, as it was evident that we came right about when they were closing but graciously let us stay as they cleaned up and even made sure to check if everything was alright.  As a result, I'm going to have to make a return visit to ensure I sample everything.  How's that for repeat business.

    I'm a simple man at times - I like a good hotdog and a good breakfast sandwich. Almost everything about Moe's is wonderful, but that one qualifier has ruined quite a few lunches and breakfasts for me.

    I don't like pretentious, overpriced simple food; adding avocado doesn't make your food service frozen burger magically gourmet. What I love about Philadelphia is it's lack of pretense...we're not NYC catering to the wealthy beautiful people. We're not Chicago trying to pretend a train depot in the middle of nowhere is a world class city.We're Philadelphia - we like Vince Papale and Briawn Dawkins. That approach makes the Starr'ification of the city that much harder for me to palette. There are increasingly few places to get simple food done well. Moe's is one of them.

    Everything about being in Moe's makes me happy...the friendly, efficient service, the wonderful sandwiches, the corny plastic hotdog and the no-nonsense dining room. Moe's is a place to sit down and eat with no attitude. Conversations between guests are a common occurrence - it's like a job site cafeteria, and I dig that vibe.

    The breakfast sandwich is the best to be had in Philly - delicious, greasy, plentiful sausage. A fresh bun. Quality cheese. The hotdogs are also terrific with plenty of topping options. The food is really worth a taste for an afficianado and they do the basics right. Potatoes are crisp.

    Here's the "but" - they're never freaking open. After years I still haven't figured out their weekend hours. They're open weekday mornings and for lunch, but weekends are unpredictable. It's hard to get here when they're open!
