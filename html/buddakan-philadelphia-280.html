        A solid choice for light, tasty Asian food. I came here with a party of four for a pre-wedding dinner party. We heard about the quality of the food and service and we were not disappointed when we experienced it ourselves.

        The food is tasty but not heavy or laden with fat, which is common at some other high-end restaurants serving pan-Asian cuisine. Food is served family style with plates swapped across the table. We ordered the scallops, ginger chicken, pad Thai, and the chocolate bento box. The scallops were perfectly prepared, the ginger chicken melted in our mouths, and each of the desserts in the chocolate bento box (think banana chocolate cream, chocolate covered almonds, chocolate mousse, etc.), were a perfect book-end to the meal. The drinks were perfectly mixed: not so alcoholic that they burned going down but not watered down enough to miss the point (i.e. to get a slight buzz). Even though we felt we ordered a large amount of food, the food was light enough so our stomachs weren't splitting at the end of the meal. The service, too, was great. The water glass was always full and the server was attentive but knew when to leave us to our dinner conversation. The ambiance is fantastic. Soft lights, a gigantic glowing Buddha presiding over the dining room, and tables spaced far and wide enough to ensure privacy in Buddakan's cavernous dining room.

        But, as great as the food, service, and ambiance was at Buddakan, the restaurant's obsession with its Zen theme can be (contrary to its intended purpose) a little grating. Especially when you're guzzling down a drink called Honesty, Calm or other names you'd expect to find in the organic cosmetics aisle.

        Recently there has been some criticism of Buddakan. For example, in Philadelphia magazine's 2011 edition of the 50 best places to eat right now, Buddakan was ranked the #1 overrated restaurant. There are many restaurants in the city that are severely overrated, and to say that Buddakan is the most overrated restaurant in the city is just silly.

        First off, the decor is amazing, and every time I have been here, my party has to fight over who gets to face the giant Buddha. The service is always on point (as is usually expected from a Starr restaurant).

        The food is fantastic and since it is a family style concept, you get to try a number of dishes. The appetizers and dim sum are the best, so making a meal just out of them is a good way to go. The tea smoked spare ribs are spectacular. They are juicy and fall right off the bone. Other appetizers/dim sum to get are the duck buns, chicken and ginger dumplings, tuna pizza and shrimp toast. Also, if you are craving a salad, get the asian caesar. While you may shy away from it because you may think it is just a caesar...it is not. The dressing is great with a hint of wasabi and there are wontons and nuts instead of croutons. For an entree, get the Chilean sea bass. It is flaky and moist, and has a wonderful marinade. Finally, you must get the dip sum doughnuts. They come out fresh and hot, covered in cinnamon and sugar, with a trio of sauces for dipping.

        Make sure to get a reservation a little in advance if you are coming on a weekend. Also, come for restaurant week if you can.

        Let me start by saying that I am an incredibly harsh critic when it comes to Asian fusion or Gourmet/American Asian.  This place however had me shocked by the quality.  Everything was very fresh tasting, perfectly seasoned, and cooked to perfection.  Even something as simple as chicken fried rice was a true delight to the palate.  The rice was super flavorful and not at all greasy.  You just tasted a burst of a delicate fresh herb flavor, yum!

        The sea bass that my boyfriend got was just divine!  It was cooked perfectly--im talking the type of perfection that even great home cooks (*ahem*) are perplexed about how to achieve.  The fish was very tender inside and had a light crisp on the outside and was incredibly fresh and flavorful.  I'd definitely order this dish next time.

        The pad thai did not disappoint either.  Honestly, while looking at the menu, I expected to be disappointed by whatever I chose, but the menu descriptions really do no justice whatsoever to the delightful flavors, freshness, and quality that await at Buddakan.

        My main qualm is that the shrimp in my pad thai were seriously overcooked.  I mean, I'm funny about hard shrimp, but even my bf, who likes hard shrimp, said they were overcooked.
