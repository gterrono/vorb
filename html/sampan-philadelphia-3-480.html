    I've come here a couple times for both Graffiti Bar and for Sampan and I've had good experiences with both.

    Graffiti Bar- Graffiti Bar has an amazing happy hour. Drinks are pretty cheap and you can most anything off the Sampan menu for half price (reduced portions, but definitely worth it!). The decor is actually pretty cool. It's a "hipster looking" bar covered in graffiti. It's almost easy to miss as you have to walk through a fire-hazardy alley to get into the bar (or go through Sampan).

    Sampan- Sampan might seem a bit pricey, but it's well priced for the kind of quality and creativity it offers. It's a bit on the loud side during busy hours, but that's to be expected.

    Specific food reviews for Sampan-

    Tuna plate- Delicious! The tuna is seared to perfection and surprisingly, the creamy sauce and potatoes provide a good alternative to the classic.

    Kobe Beef Fried Rice: Also a very well made dish, but perhaps not worth the price tag. You can't really appreciate the Kobe Beef in fried rice.

    Peking Duck: Excellently done, but small portions. Honestly, a restaurant in Chinatown could do this for less and bigger portions.

    Kim Chi Fried Rice: This kind of fried rice dish is a little bit rarer and they do it well. Definitely check it out if you're a fan of kim chi.

    Korean BBQ Pork Satay: Pretty good! Very tender pork in delicious BBQ sauce.

    TLDR: This place is great if you're looking for a fairly upscale restaurant for a reasonable price.

    I've had almost everything on this menu and I have to say that the food really is amazing. Some of my favorites include Edamame Dumplings (a mutual consensus with almost everyone that's had them), Pork Bao Bun, Spring Rolls, any of the satays, Kobee fried rice, shrimp pad thai, kim chee fried rice, ok I guess I could name the whole menu.

    I never leave this place hungry. It is served family style so some of the plates are larger. If you are unsure, just ask your server - they have been helpful at recommending a good mix of dishes for the amount of people at my table. And there is always the Tasting Menu if you want to try quite a few things - 7 items + ice cream for $45. Pretty good deal for the amount of food you get.  

    The grafitti bar outside is a fun atmosphere. It can get very crowded, especially in the summer and for Sips, so get there early on a nice day.

    Sampan also has Happy Hour specials every day. Yes, even on Saturday and Sunday! You can get a lot of food on the cheap ($4 kimchee fried rice?? yes please!)

    The wait staff and bartenders have always been super friendly, and it's become one of my favorite places to go to when I'm in the mood for Asian food, or good happy hour specials!

    Went to Sampan on a business dinner without a reservation on a Monday night- it was the only place in the area we could find without a wait.  The hostess was very friendly and guided us to the bar as we waited only 10 minutes for a table to be cleared.  Service and food was prompt and attentive.

    I had the luck of being with a group large enough to try a majority of the menu items.  A few things really stood out to me.  The hamachi ceviche was one of the best I've ever had- the miso and soy paired perfectly with the citrus- absolutely delicious.  The brussel sprouts were also quite good, although a little oily for my taste.  The take on Philly cheesesteak was also delicious, with perfectly moist thinly sliced beef served on a bao bun.  

    The only item to truly disappoint was the Korean fried chicken- under-seasoned dark meat chicken with a cloyingly sweet honey glaze.  Far closer to sub par Chinese takeout than true Korean savory/spicy flavors you'd expect.  

    Overall I'd highly recommend Sampan and surely visit again- the $45 tasting menu is worth every penny and delivers more food than you can eat!

    This was our New Year's Eve restaurant of choice this year!

    Sampan is another one of those restaurants from which I lived around the corner while I was in undergrad (in other words, was too broke to eat there at the time)! So, we are so glad we finally visited for dinner, only regret is of course, not having gone sooner.

    The restaurant was packed, as it was NYE after all. We were seated upon our arrival (7pm rezzie) in a comfy corner booth. The super cool neon backdrop was predominantly purple this evening.

    The BF and I both didn't look much at the menu before our visit, didn't feel up to making decisions this NYE, so we went with the Chef's Tasting menu. No regrets! This means 7 selections & soft serve. Our server was very helpful; she collected a few facts about us so she could make good recommendations to the chef on what to cook us up!

    What we got was: edamame dumplings, lobster ceviche, chicken satay, calamari salad, crispy brussel sprouts, beef short rib lo mein, a scallop dish (on special), 4 little cones of ice cream (2 vanilla sour patch kids and 2 chocolate mint). And WOW. This sounds like a lot of food for two people and it is. This wouldn't be a problem if it weren't so perfect and delicious! Awesome, awesome, awesome. Quality of all the meat and fish were outstanding. Everything was flavorful and interesting, just how we like it.

    My favorite was absolutely the Calamari Salad. If I ever was forced to select a meal (which is hardly a punishment so I can't imagine a predicament such as this) for my lunch from a Philadelphia restaurant to eat for the rest of my life, it would be that salad.

    Don't put off going to Sampan, definitely not as long as I had...and absolutely don't skip it!
