We didn't even visit this eatery for its dessert. The brunch exceeded our expectations and the carrot cake...well, simply put...it was the icing on the cake.

    A very impressionable experience. Thank you to the owner and staff at Cafe de Maude.

    My friends and I passed by this place on our way home from brunch elsewhere, and could smell the yumminess across the street. So, we promised to check this place out next time for brunch. Within a week, we were back.. and having looked up Yelp reviews ahead of time, I was way overwhelmed, but ended up having such a great experience, I came back the next day. Service here is impeccable. Ambiance and interior design is wonderful, and there's some outdoor seating. I've seen a couple people take single tables to eat and do work. Prices are on the higher side, but like other reviewers said, I'd choose here over Honey's any day.

    Parisian Za: can't go wrong with the delicious croissant, warm goat cheese tucked into folded French ham, caramelized onions, truffle oil (!!), avocado - it basically had all my favorite things on one dish, including a small fruit salad and leafy salad.

    I tried a bite of my friend's Lyonnaise Omelet, and holy cow, it melts in your mouth. It's all egg white, but it doesn't leave you missing any yolk. Such a great combination of veggies (asparagus, broccoli, brussels sprouts, fennel) with this delicious yogurt tahini sauce.. leaves you wanting more. Unfortunately, I had to finish my own Parisian Za before I could steal any more of my friend's omelet.

    Next day, I got the Sweet Potato Benny: delightful. I loved the sweet potato hash more than I thought I would. The Lebanese sausage was YUM, but I think it was the mangoes that really tied the whole dish together.

    We shared the Gaufre: scrumptiousness.. I know that's not a word, but that's what it was. This delicious waffle with whipped ricotta and vanilla pudding (yum!), fruit that wasn't the typical selection in most restaurants, and huckleberry sauce. Again, I would have eaten all of this if I didn't already have my own dish, and was already stealing other people's foods...

    Foul Moudamas: definitely different from the foul I've had at an Ethiopian restaurant in DC (which was delicious), but still very tasty. I wouldn't necessarily get this dish again and again, over trying all the other yum-sounding dishes on the menu though.

    Halloumi Press: I. Love. Halloumi. If you do too, you'd love this panini-type sandwich. Yes.

    Thank you Cafe La Maude - I'll be back.

    First impressions are important and Café La Maude scored big with my first impression. From the greeting at the door to the food to the service...everything was excellent. Let me preface my review by saying that I am a food snob; it is not easy to impress me (most 4-star Yelp reviews, I would give 2 stars).

    I started with the best cup of cappuccino I ever had. I then had the Greek omelet. I would never thought to put hummus in an omelet but it was awesome. Add in just the right amount of bacon, feta, avocado and jalapeno...it was fantastic.

    My son had the Petite Steak that was essentially the most unique and best steak and eggs I've ever tasted. My son agreed. Steak, potatoes, mango, and apples were balanced well with the white wine cream sauce, truffle oil, and balsamic reduction. Sounds like a lot of flavors but the balance was excellent.

    My daughter had the Berrylicious Pain Perdu. What a tremendous presentation of "French toast"! She opted for Nutella over the mascarpone for the filling. I'm not a big fan of nutella but the dish was still incredible.

    I babbled on long enough. You get the point. Robert G said it the best..."You owe it to your pallet, your tummy, your soul and your self being" to eat here. Do yourself a favor...what are you waiting for...

Specialties

With the authentic look and feel of a true European café, this hidden gem in Philadelphia's Northern Liberties neighborhood serves up some of the most delicious food, coffee and pastries anywhere in the city. Café La Maude's specially brewed La Colombe coffee is known for its strong taste that's never bitter.  The French-Lebanese menu combines the exotic flavors of the Middle East with a distinctly Continental French accent and includes traditional favorites such as Brioche French Toast, Nicoise Salad, Taboule and Baklava along with more unique items such as the Grilled Haloumi, a baguette sandwich with olives, roasted tomatoes, mesculun greens and a basil-arugula aioli.  House specialties include the Café La Maude Salad of  arugula topped with warm seasoned chickpeas and fava beans.  Another popular dish is the traditional lebanese recipe of foul moudamas of marinated fava beans served with fresh tomatoes, chopped parsley and pita served with scrambled eggs. For meat lovers the cafe's marinated chicken sandwich and kebbe is not to be missed. Whether it's breakfast, lunch or brunch that brings you to Café La Maude, it will feel like Paris and taste like Beirut.
