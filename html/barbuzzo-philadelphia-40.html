    With so many restaurants in Philadelphia, finding a place you will continually go back to is impressive in my mind. This is the kind of place Barbuzzo is for me.

    The theme- Mediterranean bar and kitchen serving local produce. The menu has nothing over $20, all house made pastas, pizzas, interesting apps, cured meats, delicious vegetable platter- all things I can get down with. The outdoor seating is comfortable and cozy.  

    FOOD:

    Vegetable platter is highly recommended. Lots of work put into this platter of different, always rotating, seasonal veggies. I got roasted beets, delicious bruschetta, a really nice nice cabbage and fennel salad and an colorful array of roasted carrots with honey and thyme.

    Bone marrow with persillade, lemon and pickled onion jam
    was pretty good, very rich and fatty.  I wish there was just a wee bit more acidity from the toppings to even out the richness of the marrow and also the bread to eat with it should have been thicker and more substantial instead of the few thin crispy slices we got.  There was so much marrow we couldn't finish it!

    For my meal, I got the buscatini with pork ragu, escarole, burrata and basil.  I really think pastas are something they do very well here. The portion was substantial and hearty for the price ($13). The pasta was perfectly al dente, the pork ragu tender, meaty and the sauce was so flavorful. There was a nice balance between tangy, meaty sauce and the creamy buratta cheese that was layered throughout the huge pasta noodles. Definitely some refined comfort food goin' on.

    The goat cheese and beet ravioli, with orange zest and browned butter, and beet greens on top that some late joiners to the dinner ordered was BEST ravioli I've had in a while.

    Fideua with seafood was divine. Like paella but with broken thin noodles instead of rice.

    High quality food.

    I love the tapas style of dining, being able to try different dishes each time, and importantly a price point that allows ordering several dishes to split NOT to break the bank. Go!! You will not be disappointed!

    Decided to head over to Barbuzzo on a whim one Sunday afternoon.  Called at 5 PM to see if there was a wait, and low and behold they were booked until 9:30.  BUT - there were some seats left at the kitchen bar, so we ran right over and scooped em.

    The place is charming.  Tiny, and charming.  Totally 13th street.  Be warned though: if you sit towards the back of the kitchen bar, you aren't just actually four feet way from an industrial grade stove, you FEEL like you're four feet away from an industrial grade stove.  Me, though, I'm a lizard.  So I liked it.

    As suggested, we ordered a slew of plates and shared.  Pig Popcorn: the conclusion here is that gourmet, 24-hour-from-pig-to-plate pork rinds taste pretty much as good as dollar store pork rinds, which are pretty damn delicious to start with.  The aioli, though, you could definitely NOT get at Dollar General.  The Roasted Beets: clearly these were excellent, locally sourced beets, and the kale was perfect, crunchy and raw.  But, good beets are good beets, and I felt sort of silly paying for something I could have so easily done at home.  The Burrata: pulled in-house, and oh so tasty, but I could have used more on the plate for the price.

    Meatballs: damn good.  Our front row seats got us in on the secret behind these fantastic little meat packages: 18 iterations before *magic*.  Now that's dedication.  But keep in mind that you get TWO meatballs.  And that means each meatball costs FIVE DOLLARS.  Post meatball, I have begun to question whether or not I would have preferred to use my $5 for SIX Wawa meatballs AND a roll.... But setting aside how wildly overpriced they are, I thoroughly enjoyed every rich but delicate and creamy bite drenched in that delightfully acidic sauce.

    Rock Octopus: Aside from one night at Standard Tap when the chef temporarily harbored a god as he prepared my octopus legs, this was the tenderest octopus I've ever had.  I would have appreciated a heavier hand with the spicy chorizo factor and a bit more finesse, but the little buddies did maintain their lightly citrused sea-side charm with a slight salty kick.

    The whole menu sounded great but we settled on an ordering scheme of two boards and a pizza, then everyone picked another plate that were also pretty much shared as they came out.  This was pretty much the perfect amount of food for 5 people and we demolished everything.  The meat and vegetable boards were both fantastic, the vegetable board especially.  I expected them to be a little larger, but the quality was spot-on and the richness and flavour of everything managed to satisfy with smaller portions.  The uovo pizza was bangin, and far superior to the tartufo at Pizzeria Stella.  The truffle flavour was well-balanced and the brussels sprouts gave it texture while the guanciale and eggy cheese gave it the salty richness that would make this the perfect hangover cure.

    Plate selections around the table included (if I can remember them all): roasted squash and parsnip salad, sauteed greens, gnocchi, grilled octopus, and the fideua.  The greenery was great all around, really well-prepared vegetable dishes, and the gnocchi was rich and divine.  The disappointment was definitely the octopus.  It seemed to have been roasted in their wood oven rather than grilled, and the outside had become sort of taut and dry.  There were no char marks and the seasoning was underwhelming, so the whole dish fell flat on execution.  I don't know if maybe this was an off-night for the dish, but I wouldn't order it again.  The fideua was INCREDIBLE, one of the more delicious, satisfying comfort food dishes I've ever tasted.  It was also quite large, so don't let the higher price dissuade you.  This could easily be a whole meal for one person.  The seafood was excellent quality and everything about it was true and right and good.

    We were too stuffed for dessert but I will be back to try the budino.

    Upon inspection of both menus, we decided to go with the restaurant week menu. When in Rome, right? Since there were two of us we were able to try both the crostini starters (pretty good but nowhere near the other courses). This course really blew my mind.

    For the second (and main) course I had the pan seared gnocchi (prepared vegetarian) while my date had the grilled bronzino. I normally eat faster than anyone I know but I really restrained myself with the gnocchi. Every bite I took I didn't want to swallow it was so good - I didn't want it to end! But it did.

    We finished up with the desert, a blood orange tiramisu (wow!) and salted caramel budino, which was really good but almost overpowered by the caramel. I would have preferred greater concentration of the dark chocolate.

    The drink menu was great and although I only had beer, my date had a delicious cocktail called "the Sidecar". Amazing meal and service - would love to make it back! Props to Erin F. (& Mike) for the stellar recommendation!
