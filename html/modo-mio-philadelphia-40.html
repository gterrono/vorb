    I'm not sure who said the "omnivore is not well served."  However, i think s/he meant "herbivore."  The omnivore, on the other hand, is extremely well-served.  Modo Mio appears to be a Milanese Italian extravaganza with many interesting taste choices.  The tourista menu is really the way to go to get a sampling of this variety.  Well priced at $35/person, you choose from each menu section and receive a dessert.  For those of you with just a little appetite, don't be intimidated.  They will make the menu items fit your appetite.

    Overall, this place offers what seems like a really nice collection of traditional Italian comfort foods.  All the meals are well prepared, sometimes a little heavy-handed salt/pepper-wise, but always infinitely edible.

    Our group tried the zucca, which is an butternut squash dish which nearly melted in your mouth.  The cheese added a nice body to the squash, while the breadcrumbs added substance.  The anchovy was barely noticeable except as a hint for a bit of extra umph.

    The cannellono was a crab dish filled with prosciutto that was full of flavor, and perfectly balanced between the sea and smoke.  

    The cozze mussels in a red sauce were surprisingly well seasoned with a little bit of a bite without overwhelming the mussels.

    Maccheroni gets a new twist with a little spicy lamb (which a friend will appreciate for not "tasting cute" which usually prevents her enjoying lamb).  Very rich, and well spiced, it feels like a really good winter dish.

    The pollo umbriaco was a really great take on chicken that i had never even heard of before.  The chicken leg was literally falling off the bone, while the sauce, which was a complex rich tomato base with cognac, balanced its acidity with a milder fish broth.  Add to that the egg covered toast that sopped up the remaining sauce and it was a delicious new addition to my food knowledge.

    The report on the ribeye was satisfactory, though underwhelming.  I'm looking forward to trying their Molise style fish stew on our next visit.

    Add a vegetable to your platter:  
    the bitter broccoli is deliciously simple, sauteed with olive oil and garlic.

    Had an early dinner here a few days ago. It was the first time in. In all it was a nice dining experience and I'll definitely return to explore the menu further.

    We (two) opted for the "menu turista" tasting which consisted of four small but more than adequate plates. At $33.00 per person, I thought it was a terrific value and offered us the opportunity to sample the menu with some depth. If we had ordered the same 4 courses á la carte (with same-sized portions), I'd have thought it a bit pricey, but I think they expect most to order the tasting menu.

    Our starters were balsamic grilled octopus with oven roasted tomato and house-made sausage with poached egg in a mustard cream. Both were very good, although the octopus arrived to the table room-temperature. For a pasta course we had a spicy lamb ragu maccheroni and lasagne bolognese. Both were very good, but again they needed to arrive piping hot from the kitchen, not barely warm. For entrees, we selected the beef cheek on polenta with salsa passito and the skate in apricot brown butter and lemon. The beef cheek was fantastic. Unfortunately the skate and its sauce were not quite up to par with the rest of the meal. We also ordered a side of  italian spaetzle (according to the menu with fennel, but came with arugula(?) instead). Although we were surprised by its crunchy consistency, the flavor was good. To finish we ordered a lemon crepe and an apple-walnut tart. The tart was superb, but the lemon crepe tasted a bit flat and heavy. In all it was a very good but not great meal.

    The service was excellent, but at the time we dined, the restaurant was only a quarter full. The atmosphere is pleasant and the space fairly small. In sum we weren't wowed, but Modo Mio is a very solid 4 stars. CASH ONLY. BYOB. Resv. a must.

    Listed in Philly things that I cannot live without

    I had this placed bookmarked for what seemed like forever. When Chad and I decided it was the perfect place to celebrate both our birthdays, I called to reserve a table. Due to its small size and wonderful food, weekend reservations go fast. Definitely call two weeks in advance, just in case. Oh, and bring cash. So we called, we reserved, we drove, we parked under what I think was a police camera (oh, Girard) and then walked a block to the cozy little restaurant.

    Our server was pretty great, he was very charismatic and did not judge us for our wine selection (we don't have half a clue when it comes to wine and always choose based on attractiveness of the bottle). The menu itself seems large for such a small restaurant, but there is definitely something for everyone. We both decided the turista was the way to go, 4 courses for only $33 each is a value difficult to beat. Everything we ate popped with flavor and, despite the descriptions on the menu, even managed to surprised us at times.  

    I loved everything I ordered from beginning to end. Even the bread they served was wonderful. My starter was the gnocco, a big potato dumpling stuffed with fois gras and chicken liver, served with candied-but-not-too-candied citrus fruit. The flavors were insane, by itself the dumpling was very good, but with the fruit it was glorious. Chad's crab and prosciutto thing was good as well, but definitely suited better for him than me.

    The pasta course was what was going to make our break this meal for us, luckily it was divine. I had a slightly spicy, slightly sweet, slightly cinnamon-y lamb ragu that was to die for. This was my favorite dish, the pasta was perfect, the lamb was tender and it achieved a level of comfort equal to a mother's cooking. Chad's spicy shrimp pasta was very much his style as well, he really enjoyed it.

    For the main course I don't even remember what Chad's veal tasted like because I was almost drunk. He drove so I drank most of the wine myself, lucky me. I had the beef cheek with polenta, it was so tender and delicious. It had a nice contrast of textures and the flavors were well balanced. Also, I achieved my goal of eating as many animals as possible that night (chicken, duck or goose (not sure about the fois source), crab, pig, lamb, cow, baby cow, shrimp).

    By this point, we were almost uncomfortably full and we had another course to go. Chad had pumpkin bread pudding and I had a caramel and chocolate ganache tart, basically the most decadent thing ever created. With the cappuccinos and shots of sambuca, it was the perfect end to the meal.

    Modo Mio has to be one of the best deals in Philly. Not only is it BYOB, it also has a tasting menu that is cheaper and one course larger than restaurant week. It doesn't hurt that the food is incredible, too.

History

Established in 2007.

Peter McAndrews established Modo Mio restaurant first in a fabulous line of Italian themed restaurants.  Following Modo Mio and included in the Modo Mio restaurant group are both Paesano's Italian Style sandwich shops, Monsu restaurant, and Popolino restaurant.  Peter specializes in home cooked Italian cuisine with each restaurant representing a particular region in Italy with his interpretation attached.  From Northern Italy down through Rome and finally ending in Southern Sicily Peter evokes the essence of tradition recipes with a new wave approach.  Come in, eat, and be happy!

Meet the Business Owner

Peter M. Business Owner

Peter McAndrews has been involved in the Philadelphia Restaurant scene since before their was a Philadelphia Restaurant scene.  After working in a number of kitchens under different ownership Peter left for Italy to fine tune his skills.  He began cooking on his own with Modo Mio located in Northern Liberties.  A comfortable BYO with emphasis on Northern Italian cuisine.  He soon followed with his Paesano's sandwich shop brand focusing on flavor and ingredients with each bite.  Monsu located in the Italian Market was next which focused on Sicilian cuisine.  Finally Popolino was last which evoked a traditional Roman style Trattoria !  Each restaurant represents a different part of Italy which in turn represents a different part of Peter McAndrews
