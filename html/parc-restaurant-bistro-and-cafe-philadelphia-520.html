    Listed in Sub-par Starr

    Stephen Starr's places are either big hit or huge miss...Parc is neither, but the overall experience was still very good. After you hang out in the grass that 500 dogs have previously peed on in Rittenhouse Square, head across the street and give Parc a try. The warmth of the décor inside will magically teleport you to a French bistro somewhere in Versailles (except one that seats 250+). Once you're in, a friendly and talkative hostess will lead you to your table, and your server will be knowledgeable and attentive throughout the meal.

    Food:
    -This will be one of the few times I ever reference bread in a review...but the freshly baked bread here, especially the cranberry walnut, is pretty darn good. Crunchy on the outside, moist on the inside.
    -Croque Madame- a nice gooey fried egg, greasy grilled ham, and luxurious mornay sauce. I liked this dish, but I had two slight issues 1) they over did it on the mornay...in addition to making the whole dish a bit too rich and creamy, it also made the bread soggy 2) The croque was open faced...I was expecting more of a sandwich I could pick up and not have to eat with a fork and knife like a pansy.
    -Steak sandwich- I must admit that hair started growing on my chest after just three bites of this loaded with horseradish sandwich. It was still good though, and the ribeye was cooked the way we had asked. Definitely a must if you love horseradish.  
    -Curry chicken salad sandwich- I'm not a fan of curry, but I stole half of this sandwich from my Mom. Silky avocado, sweet green grapes, and toasted almonds...what a combo! This wasn't your ordinary chicken salad, and it had an interesting but very enjoyable flavor profile.

    That said, this place is pricey (the croquet madame, aka breakfast sandwich, was $13). Oh yeah, and check out the fanciful / awesome French music on their website, s'il te plait.

    Starr restaurants usually seem to be about a mix of elements -- solid food, good service, HEAVY on the atmosphere.  Parc is no exception, and it delivers.  The goal appears to be to create the feel of a Paris bistro, albeit one that's apparently 2000 square feet (note: I do not know how many square feet the place actually is -- I'm guesstimating).  

    So, as noted, the food is good quality, with solid execution and plenty of options on the menu.  I typically get the moules frites when I go, and they've always been delicious, although we're talking about steamed mussels, and that's kind of hard to screw up.  The wait staff blends into the background, offering polite, courteous service without standing out on its own.  The atmosphere is exactly what they go for, albeit a somewhat impersonal, constructed version thereof.  

    Really, my only criticism of Parc is one of value.  I tend to prize the quality of the food highest and while Parc delivers good food, it's clear that you're paying for the atmosphere as well.  Still, for an impromptu brunch on a weekend, it's a good place to go.

Specialties

This stylish addition to Rittenhouse Square pays tribute to French cafe culture, serving breakfast, lunch and dinner seven days a week. Ideal for a quick bite or a leisurely meal, the menu is filled with classics: onion soup, escargots, steak frites and towering plateaus de fruits de mer. The baguettes are made in-house and  the wine list is expansive. Sip a Kir Royale at the zinc bar or enjoy a cafe au lait while basking in the sun.

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011. For more information, please visit www.starr-restaurant.com.
