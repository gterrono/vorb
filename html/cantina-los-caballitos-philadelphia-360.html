    Cantina is one of my favorite go-to places when I can't think of what I feel like eating - it's just that consistently good!

    At this point, I feel like I've tried almost every flavor of margaritas available, which is a pretty good accomplishment since there are so many.  I must say that my favorite margarita on the menu is the Cadillac (Tequila, Cointreau, Guava and Fresh Lime Juice) - nothing fancy, just yummy goodness.  I highly recommend getting a pitcher of margaritas over just a glass mostly because you can't beat the price ($20 - $28 depending on the flavor/if it's a special/etc).

    There are several menu items that I recommend, but here are my top 3:
    1)  The chorizo burrito.  It's quite a handful, but isn't overwhelming.  Plenty of delicious, spicy chorizo included.

    2)  The nachos with chicken.  Enough said.

    3)  My newest favorite - the tlacoyo.  I still have no idea how to pronounce this, but it's probably the best little mexican pizza-ish dish I've ever had.  It's loaded with shrimp, sausage, jalapenos, and cheese and really hits the spot.  If you see this on menu,  definitely get it!

    Cantina - I love you, plain and simple.

    I came here with the BF last night after our 48 hour Sandy-induced lockdown.  We passed by Los Caballitos on the way to Fond last month and decided to come back this way to try it (we were both craving Mexican).  This "hipster" cantina features a down-home atmosphere with strong drinks and decent service.  However, I found the food to be so-so, and the prices, for what they are, seem a little steep for regular hours (as opposed to happy hours).

    On the suggestion of many a Foursquare tip, we split the plantains, which were great.  Sweet fried plantains covered in queso fresco and crema.  They were so good, I could have easily just had them for dinner and nothing else (and fairly priced at $6).  For dinner, I got a pork belly burrito ($12).  That was probably a mistake, considering that pork belly is probably better reserved for tacos (but the foodie in me really wanted to have a pork belly burrito).  I was really disappointed when it came out: a big, plain old sad burrito in its tin foil blanket, served on a plate with nothing else (no sides, no garnishes, no nothing).  Inside, heaps and mounds of rice and beans with little tiny pieces of pork belly and maybe a hint of the pico de gallo I asked for (50 cent surcharge).  The BF got the goat tacos ($9), with guac ($1 surcharge).  They looked and tasted infinitely better.  Dinner fail.  Also, I didn't like the fact that certain taco and burrito ingredients (such as cheese, sour cream, guac, pico de gallo, and jalapenos) must be added a la carte for an additional fee.  Guac I understand, but the other ingredients? That's ridiculous.

    I also ordered a second margarita and the drink took a long time to come out because "some christmas lights fell on the bar and we had to clean it up" the server told me, apologetically (and later comped me the drink because of the long delay, score one for service!).  In fact, the service here was much better than I had expected based on the reviews and tips.  And my drink, a base level margarita, was terrific: not too strong, but not too light either.

    Overall: a nice quasi-divey little hangout (with what looks like a killer patio come nice weather) that serves great drinks and good apps.  However, I would definitely head elsewhere for dinner.  One Foursquare tips suggests going to El Zarape across the street for better, cheaper Mexican staples.  I am definitely doing that next time.

    One of my favorite Mexican restaurants in the city!!  This quaint and adorably-decorated place is located right in the heart of South Philly.  It's within walking distance so I practically live there.

    Let's start with the margaritas - they have fruity ones and then the regular lime.  I always go with the regular lime.  You can get a pitcher or by the glass.  You can also get it frozen or on the rocks with salt.  I go with on the rocks with salt.  Yum!

    You then get an unlimited supply of chips and salsa/salsa verde.

    Since I am a vegetarian, I usually go with the bean/rice burrito.  I have also had the vegan beef burrito which is EXCELLENT!  So, yes, this place is veggie/vegan-friendly.  

    The black bean soup is scrumptious and I always start off with that.  

    The portions are big, so come hungry and don't fill up on too many chips and salsa!

    If you wind up having room in your belly for dessert, they have vegan selections as well as regular desserts.  

    Love this place!!
