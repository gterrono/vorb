    Listed in Flavours of the East, Rittenhouse Rave Reviews

    Vic Sushi is my fave little casual sushi bar in Philly.  

    Nigiri sushi is fresh and well-formed. We tried the combo which included salmon, white tuna, tuna, red snapper, whitefish, yellowtail. I enjoyed all the fish, which was very fresh.  The white tuna was especially supple and rich in texture.

    All the standard maki rolls I've tried have been good also. They have a 3 roll special for $10.95.

    The Ocean Brother roll was very tasty with a perfectly balanced set of flavours and textures.  Shrimp tempura inside the roll was crispy, and the crab on top of the roll was cool and creamy.  Black Dragon roll (topped with eel) is also good, though I liked the Ocean Brother roll more.

    Seaweed salad is solid, good but not exceptional.

    The wait can be long at rush hour, but after I'm seated, the sushi comes out very quickly after I order.

    Great sushi. Good prices. Solid service.

    If you're looking for cheap and decent sushi in Center City, Vic should definitely be on your radar.

    Here are some highlights:
    - the three roll special is so unbelievably cheap. The rolls, however, are slightly smaller than what you would pay for at a higher end sushi place. Again, it's almost criminally cheap and there are a good number of rolls that are part of this mix-and-match special. I would highly recommend this for starters.
    - the tuna dumplings seem to be the hottest item on the menu here, according to Yelp. the dish entails two large bun-shaped/sized "dumplings" that are made of tuna. The filling is something similar to avocado with some crunch. If you're not a fan of tuna and/or creamy avocado, I would pass.
    - there's only like ten seats in this entire place, so it's not the most appropriate space for groups of 5 or more. I went around 8pm on a random weekday and the wait was around 30 minutes for two.
    - very, very casual atmosphere.
    - if you want cold water, you have to buy their bottled water. they do, however, have a hot water dispenser (for tea) and you can ask for lukewarm water.
    - the seaweed salad is delicious and it's only $3.

    The place is super tiny, and it did get pretty crowded, but I was happy to have a seat and most of the people coming in seemed to be getting their orders to go. For what it lacks in ambiance and size, it definitely makes up for in freshness and taste. I had the spicy tuna (and added crunch) and it was literally the freshest sushi I've had in Philly... including the heavy hitters like Morimoto and Zama.

    The only downside was that the gyoza was nearly five dollars and it was pre-packaged and heated up in the microwave. In addition, there was no sauce to go along with it, which is my favorite part. On the other hand, it was actually fairly tasty and I could get over the lack of sauce. I doubt I'll get it again, but it's not a huge deal.

    The verdict: for great rolls at super cheap prices, Vic's is the place. Great for lunch, and super fresh fish. If you're on a date or just want ambiance, you might be a little bummed... until you get your meal!
