    This is my first negative review on Yelp.

    Looks great, great location, interesting menu and yet, there were 15 open seats while the 15 odd patrons had to wait 30 minutes.  I actually asked one of the wait staff "why are there so many open tables and yet, so many of us waiting?"  "I have no idea" was her response.  The waiting patrons were clearly getting angry as they had to wait with so many vacant tables.  They had two hosts which were glued to the podium so it couldn't have been lack of help.  I guess they like to create a line - makes for demand?  I had to wait 40 minutes and all during that time there were no less than a dozen open seats.  

    There were a list of specials on a board w/o prices.  I asked the waitress what the price of one of the 5 selections was.  "I don't know" was her response.  Wow, she had been serving specials for 5 hours and didn't know the prices.  Jumping and finding out would have been the better response.  There were other issues but what the heck why bore you.  The general attitude was we were putting these people out by asking that they take our orders and serve us.

    One of the waitresses came over and re-filled our coffee while clearly leaving a trail of coffee all over the table.  She looked down, realized she didn't have a rag, and opted to merely just walked away letting  us play clean up.  So much for professionalism.

    Cash only?  The place has upwards of 75 seats, lunch/breakfast is an easy $20 combined and only cash?  It is understandable/cute when it is a small inexpensive place.  Yet, it is a major inconvenience for the patrons in a debit card world.  They gleefully let you know there is an off brand ATM in the corner.  You can pay dollars for the transaction, three to them and two to your bank, and the restaurant even collects their percentage of the fee from the ATM owners.  Now there's a deal.  All so they can benefit from the tax benefits of only taking in cash.  

    There is a bar around 50 feet north of them with a great bike rack - you will need it b/c they have no place to lock up bikes.  Patrons should be encouraged to use green modes of transport.  They are clearly shooting for the younger hipster so.....it would be a way of saying "this is for you." It is a young and hip place so people using bikes should be both expected and supported.  Just another way of them saying "we don't care about the patron."  

    Bottom line - you are going to leave there $20 lighter per person and they seem not to give a darn about the patron.  The staff is "so over this" and the food was decent.  It could be free food and I wouldn't return for the attitude.  You can't blame the staff  - they are hired, trained and retained by the owner.  There are dozens of other like brunch places in the area, do you research and avoid this.

    With so many brunch choices in Philly, it takes something special to get me to leave my 'hood (easy walking distance to Morning Glory, Sabrinas, Kanella and so many more) and venture to the industrial no-mans-land between Callowhill and Spring Garden, but Cafe' Lift makes it happen.
    Their unobtrusive sign, a red arrow simply stating "Cafe", calls attention to the cafe's relationship to the Prohibition Tap Room across the street, which sports a similar black arrow with the word "bar".
    Once inside the decor is minimalistic and industrial,and the layout is nice, with a side area for waiting and ingenious tables that slide on a girder to accommodate different size groups, and it took me a few visits to realize the entrence stairway is located under an old steel equipment lift. Get it? The most puzzling thing about the layout is the bathroom. It's larger than most New York apartments.
    I've never had to wait very long, but I tend to brunch earlier than most, so I'm usually seated before the big rush.
    The food is excellent. They decided to focus only on brunch foods and they totally nailed it. The mushroom frittata and fried egg panino are delicious, and the french toast was so good I kept stealing bites from my friends plate when they weren't looking. The coffee is high-end diner coffee, and is exactly what you want with your brunch. Although it can be difficult to catch a servers eye for things like coffee refills and extra napkins, when you do they are so friendly it more than makes up for it.

    Listed in Yelp 100 Challenge - 2013!, Philly me up!, Bunches of Brunches!

    This is the brunch experience that New Yorkers dream about.

    Cafe Lift is a very cute, casual brunch spot with a modern, airy, lofty feel. The menu is full of deliciously creative plates with criminally low prices. The mismatched tables & chairs are almost cafeteria-style, but better. And for two people on a Sunday afternoon, we waited no more than 20 minutes to get a table.

    First off: coffee please! The old school sugar shakers on the tables are filled with cane sugar -- an early hint that this meal was going to be everything I love, but just a little bit better.

    Then, out came the food. My boyfriend ordered Huevos Rancheros, & after great debate I ordered the Lemon Ricotta Pancakes with a side of Turkey Bacon. The pancakes come with strawberries, fresh homemade whipped cream (!!!), and honey. The pancakes were so lemony & fluffy. The strawberries were just perfectly ripe. The whipped cream is so good that you'll want to pull the "Hey, look over there!" trick so your friends won't see you eat it straight from the container. And honey?! You're probably thinking it's a crazy combination. But trust me: it works.

    Everything was just perfect. A fantastic meal. And our bill was under $30! Can you even imagine? Cafe Lift, you've won the hearts of these out-of-towners.

    I wanted to dock a star because CASH ONLY, but at the end of the day -- and the start of the weekend -- I would come back here in a heartbeat. And I plan to, because have you seen their French Toast? Go look at the pictures. I had enough trouble passing that up the first time.
