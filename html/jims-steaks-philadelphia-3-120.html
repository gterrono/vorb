    Listed in Philly Cheesesteak Takedown - Philadelphia

    I've reviewed Geno's - yelp.com/biz/genos-steak…

    I've reviewed Pat's - yelp.com/biz/pats-king-o…

    And while both of these icons of Philly cheesesteak goodness were nice - Jim's won the competition for me by a landslide!!!

    We were in the Philly area for an off site training, when my coworker casually mentions that he's craving cheesesteak.  And as he's never been to Philly (?!?), he's never been to Pat's nor Geno's.  With the hunt afoot, and I whisked him away for a taste test of Geno's (2 stars) and Pat's (3 stars).

    By this point, we've each had 2 cheesesteak in about 15 minutes.  My coworker was pretty satisfied that he's had the "Philly cheesesteak experience" at this point, and from the sounds of his burps, pretty full to boot.  But me, well I've been to Philly many many times, and neither Geno's nor Pat's really hit the spot for me.

    So, with a bit of whining, and some sad puppy dog eyes (and the promise to fix the SQL bug that he's been working on), I convince him to come with me to a THIRD cheesesteak joint - Jim's.

    Please keep in mind that this is our THIRD cheesesteak in about 30 minutes at this point.  THIRD.  So I hope you understand what high praise it is, when I say that my coworker not only wolfed down his Jim's cheesesteak - but whimpered for more (we ended up ordering another cheesesteak from Jim's and split it).  Yes, we each ate 3 1/2 cheesesteaks, with the final 1 1/2 from Jim's.  Yes, it's *that* good.

    What makes Jim's better then Geno's or Pat's?  Some may say the toppings, others may point to the warmed bread.  Me?  I say it's love.  As any good glutton knows, the difference between two practically identical dishes (or in this case, 3), isn't the ingredients - after all, a cheesesteak is constrained by the bun, cheese and meat - but of the love that goes into making it.

    Jim's?  They load theirs up with an extra helping of love on each cheesesteak.  Yes, I love that they are indoors (I hate the cold), and yes, I love that they have more toppings then just "wit" (I like mine with peppers and mushrooms as well as onions).  But it's in the love that they put into the execution.

    Upon ordering, the cheesesteak-master lathers the bun with cheese, then HOLDS the bun, over the meat, letting the steam from the hot grill and the cooking meat to meld and blend with the cheese.  Then he deftly tops with whatever you desire, before briskly wrapping it up.

    Delicious.  The bread was so soft and warm.  I got the cheesewiz (again), and it was so thick and creamy and perfectly held the finely chopped meat together.  My coworker, again, got the provolone (our scoring model you understand), and the cheese was soft and gooey and stringy - like a perfect cheesesteak should.

    Of the three that we tried that night, it should come as no surprise that Jim's was BY FAR our favorite.  High marks all around in all categories - service, options, quality of bun, meltiness of cheese, and the effort that went into the deliciously chopped meat.

    There's usually a line, so expect the staff to get short with you in order to keep things moving.  For what it's worth, though, I've never had to wait more than a minute from the time I placed my order to the time the food is in my hands which is a huge plus.

Specialties

Jim's Steaks South Street has been serving the finest quality Cheese Steaks and Hoagies in Philadelphia for 37 years. Offering Cold Beer and indoor seating to accommodate your party large or small, make us unique among the other "named" Cheesesteak destinations.

History

Established in 1976.

This Philadelphia tradition started in 1939 at the original location of 431 North 62nd Street in West Philadelphia, where it still operates.

In 1976, Jim's Steaks expanded to this location at 400 South Street, which is now independently owned and operated from the original location. Our unequaled commitment to quality and customer service has made Jim's Steaks South St. world famous, and a Philadelphia landmark
