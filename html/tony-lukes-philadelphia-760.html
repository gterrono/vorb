    When I lived in Philly, I came here almost every other day. Now whenever I'm in Philly, I make the time to come here. I've had Geno's, I've had Pat's and I've had Jim's, and they're equally unremarkable and indistinguishable from cheesesteaks you'd get at IHOP.

    Tony Luke's is a big, fat pile of meat on a roll that doesn't turn into a dripping grease sponge after a couple of minutes. That should be the minimum standard for a cheesesteak, and Tony Lukes exceeds that.

    Look, this ain't the Ritz or the Moshulu-- it's a working-class place for the working class to get a damn tasty lunch fast. The staff isn't rude, they're just trying to keep things moving. Just place your order and move on-- you want the tourist experience, you can stand in line for ages at Jims or get blinded by the neon and racist garbage at Geno's.

    I would argue that this is better than the "big name" places, and I think a lot of locals would agree.  Sandwich is huge, and menu is vast.

    I ordered the Philly cheese hoagie with fries and couldn't finish them.  They had a variety of different ways to order fries, but they were out of the two I tried to order (sweet potato and salt&vinegar), so settled on plain fries.

    You walk in and order from a window, and then your name is called when ready. If you don't answer quickly, your name gets yelled- loudly; this is all part of the Philly feel.

    As far as the sandwich, the steak is not immensely flavorful, but what makes a Philly steak so good is the entirety of the sandwich. The great bread, meat, cheese all combine to make a great flavor and this places gets it right.
