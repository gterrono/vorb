    A tip of the hat to everyone at Sbraga for making my boyfriend's birthday dinner absolutely perfect. We're both big Top Chef fans and I was thrilled to finally try this place last night as I loved Season 7 and seeing Kevin win. Thankfully, my expectations were met and exceeded.

    First, the space is great. It has an industrial feel with rustic wood paneling. Very pretty. Next, the service was out of this world from the second we walked in to the second we walked out which just made the whole experience even more special.

    In the dining room, they serve a tasting menu (amazingly reasonably priced) which is filled with amazing selections that change seasonally. Additionally, they have a chef's tasting table which is different night to night and overlooks the kitchen. I will absolutely try that out the next time but for this night, we sat in the dining room.

    And now onto the fantastic dishes...
    Course 1:
    Foie gras soup: We ordered this because I've read it's Sbraga's signature dish and wow, I can see why. This soup was so complex. With every bite I tasted something new. I would essentially eat this soup every day if i could.
    Pork Belly with chili oil, sea lettuce and cucumber: I love pork belly and so does my boyfriend so we ordered this but to my surprise, it was served cold. I was fascinated by the cold pork belly but it totally worked. The combination of everything in the dish was great. It was rich from the pork but still refreshing and light.
    Course 2:
    Octopus with piri, green beans and tapenade: This dish had some heat! Thankfully, I love spicy food but I imagine it would be a problem for some. The octopus was tender and I really enjoyed the spice in this dish. It was not a typical octopus with tapenade dish at all.
    Sea Bream, calamari, ink and pork belly: This dish was well done, but I think I discovered I'm not a big sea bream fan soo, i'd likely avoid this next time. No complaints though.
    Course 3:
    Parpadelle with rabbitt and bread crumbs: Oh my god. I loved this one. The pasta was perfect. The dish had the perfect amount of richness, cheesiness and crunchiness. Every bite was better than the last.
    Venison: I'm hazy on what came accompanied with the venison but one thing stood out the most and that was how tender it was. Game can get tough fast and this was not in the slightest. It was also cooked to perfection. Yum
    Our waitress then surprised us with their take on a roast pork sandwich. We were close to ordering this but just couldn't see how it could work...we were dead wrong. This was a piece of pork belly with brocoli rabe and a provolone bread pudding. Talk about elevating a classic into something out of this world!
    Course 4: This is in our fridge at the moment. We were just too full to get to it but it absolutely looks great. Apparently I couldn't wait to write my review of this meal. :)

    To sum up, I'm already thinking about my next trip to Sbraga. I couldn't have planned a better birthday dinner for my guy thanks to this place. To cap off the night, they sent us home with a signed menu for the birthday boy. Thank you Sbraga for a fantastic meal and night!

    An epic food experience.

    Some Bravo-devotees (myself included) know Chef Kevin Sbraga as the winner of season #7 of Top Chef.  His eponymous restaurant is an absolutely sublime dining experience, featuring the sort of progressive, innovative American food that you can't taste anywhere else.

    Three of us met up here on a Thursday night with reservations made.  (And definitely make them -- Sbraga books up weeks and weeks in advance.)  I was the first to arrive and enjoyed a delicious and inexpensive Riesling at the bar.  I wish I had caught the name of the bartendress, but she was a doll.

    While waiting, I got to soak in the elegant ambiance.  The atmosphere is upscale, but not stuffy.  The highlight is the open kitchen, where Chef Sbraga is busy working the line and greeting the patrons.  Yes -- a celebrity chef who actually cooks at his own restaurant!  A miracle!

    After being seated, we were treated to some scrumptious truffle popovers while looking over the menu.  There's only one way to enjoy the breadth of the cuisine here, and that's by ordering the four-course tasting menu -- which is exactly what we all did...

    **First**
    Foie gras soup with rose petal relish.  Absolute heaven -- and one of the finest soups I've ever had.  It was rich and creamy and sweet and savory and complex and perfect.  Chef Sbraga says this is his signature dish, and I can see why.

    **Second**
    Gnocchi with escargot and tom ka gai.  Also amazing.  These gnocchi were light as air -- very different from other gnocchi I've had before.  And the escargot was delicate and practically dissolved on my tongue.  I also loved the addition of tom ka gai (coconut broth) -- it added a very unique Asian-inspired touch.

    **Third**
    Foie gras meatloaf with hazelnut crust and root vegetables.  Another "best" -- this time the best meatloaf I've ever had.  It was packed with flavor yet light as air, which was miraculous.  The vegetables were cooked beautifully too.

    **Fourth**
    Roasted red pepper pineapple with yogurt sorbet.  I wanted to taste the most unique and unusual dessert on the menu, and this one totally bowled me over!  Loved the savory and sweet combo of red peppers and pineapple.  I even noticed the reference to the Singapore Sling that brought Chef Sbraga his national acclaim.

    I had the lucky opportunity to taste a handful of other dishes that were brought out for us to taste.  My favorite was the pork belly -- and I say this as someone who normally avoids pork belly, finding it too fatty.  But this pork was crispy, elegant, and not greasy at all.  The depth of flavor was breathtaking.

    My companions both seemed to enjoy their meals as much as I did.  Lots of food porn pics were collected, and many glasses of wine, sangria, and cocktails were imbibed.  There was also much gushing over the incredibly kind and helpful staff.  It made for a Thursday evening to remember.  :-)

    All in all, I'd say the unbridled awesomeness of Sbraga deserves twenty billion trillion stars, but this is Yelp, and 5 is all I got.

    Went to Sbraga for my partner's birthday dinner.  We decided from among several restaurant options on the basis of what was an intriguing and exciting looking menu.  Because of the special occasion, we also opted for the beverage pairing with each course.  

    So first - overall experience across all courses, with both of our choices, was that each dish was a combination of too many strong flavors.  To the point where they competed rather than complimented.  Each course was "busy" with flavors.  Example: I had the octopus/piri piri/chickpeas/greenbeans.  The octopus was tender and spiced nicely with the piri piri, and the green beans made a nice slightly sweet balance to that, but the chick peas were spiced differently, the whole thing sat on a very strongly flavored smear of olive tapenade which was unnecessary, and the garnish was fresh dill, which has its own strong flavor that did not work with the rest.  Similarly, the pork main dish had pork belly stuffed with pork sausage, strongly bitter/spicy broccoli raab and provolone bread pudding.  The sausage/raab/pudding flavors worked fine, based on classic Italian comb, but there was also the plain pork sitting on some cabbagey stuff, seemingly a reference to a more German plate.  One or the other would have worked better than trying to do both on the same plate.  

    Second- the portions / proportions.  The portions are small.  More importantly, I felt that the servings were skimpy on the proteins, which to me are usually the main event.  In the octopus dish there was precious little octopus - which was the most interesting part of the dish, and it was overpowered by the rest of the dish.  The pork, which was supposed to be a "meat course" was a very small rectangle.  Some people might be ok with that, but I found it disappointing.

    Third - don't bother with the beverage pairing.  What was provided with each course was a very small (e.g. two swallows) of the paired beverage.  For $35, this was a ripoff, and frankly, I didn't think their choices paired all that well with the dishes either.  The barbacoa cocktail that was served with the steak tartar was very strong and spicy, overpowered by smoked salt on the rim, and all of it overpowered the subtle taste of the tartar.  The other beverages were less mismatched, but nothing struck me as wonderful.  And let's face it, drink prices are already hugely inflated.  Everybody knows that this is where restaurants make  a big chunk of their profits... but I expected a package deal to offer some additional value over ordering individual drinks.  That would have been true if the drink portions had been full or even 2/3 regular size.  The portions were maybe 1/2 or 1/3 of a regular drink portion - so no value here at all.

    Finally - the desserts.  Again - too busy.  I got the cherries jubilee with szechuan ice cream.  The ice cream was very interesting and good, the cherries were good, but aren't cherries jubilee supposed to be brandied and set on fire?  These weren't.  Instead, the whole thing was smothered by what appeared to be ground up maraschino cherries - an unnecessary addition that swamped an otherwise decent dessert with over-sweetness.  My partner had chocolate pot de creme... which was not too sweet, but was topped with pineapple that competed rather than complimented the chocolate.

    I will say that the service was quite good.  The staff was knowledgeable and courteous.  The popovers were so good we asked for seconds.  They provided a nice little birthday card and madelines to take home, and that was a nice touch.  

    But overall, it fell short of our expectations.  The menu looked better on paper than the execution of each dish supported.  Too many strong flavors in combinations detracted, and kept there from being a focal flavor in each course.  Portion sizes were disappointing, especially with the beverage pairing.  Fancy restaurants are a special occasion splurge for me, and I will not spend my splurge money here a second time.
