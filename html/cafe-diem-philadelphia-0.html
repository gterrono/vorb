    Cafe Diem is a small cozy place serving some of the best Bun Bo Hue that I have tried, outside of my mom's brilliant home-cooking. Even the small bowls are big and generous amounts of meat. The brisket were thinly sliced, amazing. The tendon consistency was on point, not too stretchy and no too gooey. The pigs feet, at least this time around, could have benefited from some more time simmering to soften them up a bit. They are usually really good and falls apart in your mouth.

    No complaints here really. The broth really makes a good Bun Bo Hue and they got it right. Although I suspect some MSG from the lighter coloration of broth, they managed to make it tasty with a heap amount of heat from the crushed peppers. Another small place serving great foods. I always return for the cheap prices as well ($7.50 for a small with all).

    tl;dr:

    I used to hate this place. The bun bo hue is good if you're into that sort of thing, but the broth isn't as red as you'd expect it to be. The che ba mau is good and will help you cool down after a hot bowl of spicy noodle soup. The banh cuon is delicious and the nuoc mam is bangin'. I like this place now.

    Got time to waste? Read on.

    A long time ago I swore up and down and up and down again that I would never go back to Cafe Diem. Well I'll be damned!

    Mom heard through the grapevine that the bun bo hue was good again, and, that being one of her favorite noodle soups, she just had to try it. We went with my aunt and uncle and we each had a bowl of bun bo hue. Yep, even me! I'd never had it before so I figured why not. It's Cafe Diem's claim to fame, after all.

    *chomp chomp, slurp slurp*

    If that's considered good bun bo hue, then I guess bun bo hue is just not my cup of tea. I'm not sure the 'cup of tea' expression really works in this context. I think something like "Coffee isn't exactly my cup of tea" would work well because har har get it coffee isn't tea, knowwhatimsayin?

    Anyway, as far as I can tell bun bo hue is just spicy and the rice noodles are round as opposed to flat and it's served really hot which makes it all the more spicy. All the spicier. All the more spicier. Um... it just makes it real freakin' spicy. Like stupid spicy. And I call myself a linguist... pah!

    To cool down I ordered a che ba mau, which is a delicious colorful Vietnamese dessert that you will look up if you don't know what it is but would like to find out. I took a picture of it for you. Just for you.

    Neither my aunt nor my uncle had had che ba mau at this location so they decided to let me be the guinea pig and, drum roll, it was delicious! so they shared one. I love being useful!

    Today I returned with my mom only because we were headed to the Italian market and a hot bowl of bun bo hue would be nice on a chilly day such as this. But not for me, we've already established that I wasn't a fan of bun bo hue. So mom was gonna get that and I was gonna get a banh mi and I'm sure you're now happy to know the story of my life.

    Welp, they don't do banh mi over here, so move along if that's what your heart is set on. They do have banh cuon though, and if you're resourceful enough I'm sure you'll soon know what it is.

    Hmm, mom doesn't seem too happy with her bun bo hue today; too much meat, the broth is not good enough, etc. My banh cuon, on the other hand, is amazing -- and the nuoc mam is perfect! Just the right amount of salty and spicy, gotta love it. Hard to find good nuoc mam in Viet restaurants. It figuratively blows my mind to have found a place with good banh cuon, but now my mom swears up and down and up and down again that she'd never return.

    Such is life.

    My husband and I only get 2 things here at Cafe Diem: Bun Bo Hue and Mi Kho.  And let me just say....THEY ARE BANGIN'!!!  Bun Bo Hue at Cafe Diem is one of the best I've ever had in Philadelphia (besides my momma's of course) and their Mi Kho is on point.

    For those of you who don't know these dishes, let me give you a quick run down.  Bun Bo Hue is a spicy, lemongrass, rice-noodle soup with sliced beef (I think it's beef shank, but it reminds me of skirt steak) and pork knuckles.  Mi Kho is a dry egg-noodle dish, lightly cooked with sliced BBQ roast pork, shrimp and some vegetables.  It's served with a small bowl of broth on the side which you can mix in or drink on the side.  

    Cafe Diem is definitely a hole in the wall type of place...extremely small and not the easiest to find, but if you're ever in the area, I highly recommend that stop in to fill your tummy and warm your soul :)

    First things first, everything is relative. You have to consider where you are at and what else you're dealing with.  

    In Philadelphia, there aren't many good Asian restaurants let alone good Vietnamese food.  Cafe Diem, for instance, does the job if you're craving for some Vietnamese food.

    1.  Bun bo hue
    2.  Mi bo kho

    Those of you in Philadelphia looking for some good homemade Vietnamese, I'd totally recommend Cafe Diem.
