    So last night when I arrived at Tashan and checked in, I saw that it had 3 1/2 stars.  I'm literally stunned by this.  I have been to many spots in Philly, and the reviews are usually accurate.  This place should be AT LEAST 4 stars.  

    I dined on a Monday night, so it was a pretty slow evening.  I was meeting friends from out of town who had never been.  We sat at the bar.  I arrived a bit early and ordered a drink from the bartender, Chris, who was incredibly friendly and personable.  He suggested the perfect beer.  They have an extensive wine list as well as cocktail list.  No matter what your taste may be in the alcohol department, they have you covered.  

    As far as the vibe, it's a really great feel.  They have awesome relaxing music, beautiful authentic indian decorations etc.  

    All three of us are vegetarians, so this spot was PERFECT for us.  The cauliflower dish was UNREAL.  I have never had anything like it.   We ordered a bunch of appetizers, and each seemed to get better than the last.  The bartender was laughing at us because even after we had finished our food we ordered a lot of the same thing again.  It was so good we needed a second round!

    Chris stood out as a bartender because he was on top of the service, and joined in our dining experience, and then bowed out when we were having out girly chats.  He was a good sport too as they close at 10 and he kept telling us there was no rush.  What a refreshing feeling!

    When we got the check I expected it to be much higher, but it a very affordable spot with top notch food.  It's so nice to find an Indian fusion spot.   Most Indian places in Philly are traditional, and this take is just so interesting (and addictive.)  I'm already planning to be back next week.  Don't skip this spot, you'll be quite impressed.  

    As a side note, our bartender did say that on Friday and Saturday they're packed and you'll need a reservation.

    Oh, Tashan, you try so darn hard... I held off writing a review the first time we went to Tashan because we definitely did not have a positive experience. They allegedly had a party going on upstairs, so the kitchen was backed up. We had a 6pm reservation before an 8pm show, and by the time we had to leave to run to the theatre at 7:50, we still had not received all of the food that we had paid for. But I e-mailed the manager and received a prompt phone call reply and a gift card in the mail for the missed food plus some more for our troubles. So we decided to go back to Tashan to let them try again.

    There's something to be said about the difficulty in running a kitchen with so many small plates, but Tashan just can't quite seem to get it right. Many dishes turned up at our table that were not for us, and likewise I watched out dishes go to other tables, get turned away, go back to the kitchen, and then finally to us. Took a long time both visits to get drinks, and the second time our food started showing up before our beverages. The timing at Tashan was...my friend put it this way, like Delhi at rush hour.

    The food at Tashan is hit or miss. Some are wonderful and flavorful, and others are just blah. A few highlights:
    - Palak chaat - love chaat and the inexplicable crispy balls of spinach in this dish are seriously good
    - Vindaloo boar chops - nicely cooked meat
    - Chicken makhani - moist chicken and an extra flavorful sauce
    - Ghobi manchurian - kinda like general tsao's cauliflower, really interesting
    Some to avoid (or feed to the less food-adventurous in your party):
    - Tandoori shrimp - just plain grilled shrimp
    - Spicy brussels sprouts - you could make this at home
    - Kobe kabob - pressed meat on the skewer rather than chunks of meat, not at all what we expected

    Well, we spent our gift cards at Tashan, and that was that. A nice place to try, if you're in the neighborhood.

    I've never seen an upscale Indian restaurant, but Tashan does a pretty good job. I came to Tashan recently for a formal banquet of 80+ people (for anyone planning large events: Tashan is a relatively cheap option!). Instead of each person ordering what they want, they served group portions to every 4 people. I actually ended up liking that since I got to try everything, especially since what I was originally going to get wasn't the best stuff on the menu!

    Firstly, their service is extremely nice. Someone at my table dropped a glass, a shard got stuck in my foot, and my boyfriend and I were cleaning it up in the bathroom for a while. When we came back out, we realized we had missed all the entrees. I told a waiter (and I didn't actually realize he could hear me since the party was so loud), and he promptly brought out each of the dishes meant for 4 people just for my boyfriend and me! They even asked if we wanted white rice, which they had not offered to everyone else. Hah!

    Tashan is pretty expensive for Indian food... Of course, the quality of ingredients (especially meat) is better than most Indian buffets, but it's not that much better in taste and it's not that authentic. Since I have issues with spicy food, I actually prefer their Americanized takes on Indian dishes! The butter chicken, naga beef sumi, and naan were my favorites. I think other people also enjoyed the salmon, but the chaats were meh (then again, I'm not sure I like chaats in general). Be careful, the portions aren't that big.

    The atmosphere in Tashan is pretty nice with the lighting and all. We sat next to the kitchen, and it was very cool to see them make naan in the ovens. They also have some booths, so try to reserve those--though people will have a hard time going to the bathroom and getting in and out in general.

    Tashan is definitely nice for a special occasion (again, much cheaper than upscale Center City options for those throwing large banquets and parties!), but I wouldn't come here often. I'll stick to my $11 all-you-can-eat Indian buffets for normal days.

Service - Disappointment. Then a waiter shows up at our table and tried to deliver someone else's Paneer pizza to us. We, at least, told him it wasn't ours. Overall, one tremendous disappointment.

History

Established in 2011.

Tiffin started as a web only and delivery only concept out of a small store front in the Northern Liberties area of Philadelphia and soon evolved into maybe the first multi platform restaurant in the country, offering a full ecommerce website, delivery and carry out on the phone and dine-in, all from the same location. The successful formula led to the opening of the second location in Mt. Airy followed by Elkins Park, Wynnewood and Bryn Mawr, sandwiched around the opening of Tiffin etc., an Indian street food concept. The very latest from tiffin is a 5,000 square foot theatrical ethnic restaurant. The focus of this first-class restaurant will be on sophisticated Indian fusion grills using time honored Indian grilling methods. Unique and unconventional to the east coast, this groundbreaking restaurant concept will transform the Philadelphia dining scene and thus serve as both an amenity to Philadelphians and as a destination location for people throughout the tri-state area.

Meet the Business Owner

Munish N. Business Owner

Munish Narula, a former Investment Banker and a Wharton MBA is the founder and president of Tiffin Indian cuisine. After earning a Bachelor of Science in Hotel Restaurant Management and a MS in Hospitality Administration from Johnson & Wales University, he started working for a full service chain restaurant where he quickly moved up the ladder to reach the position of Area Manager with 11 restaurants under his management. After earning a MBA degree in Finance from Wharton and a stint with bulge bracket Investment Bank, he came back to the food service business when he founded, the now nationally acclaimed, Karma restaurant in Philadelphia. It was during his time with Karma that he identified the need of providing fresh nutritious Indian food to people at affordable prices and so the idea for Tiffin.com was born.
