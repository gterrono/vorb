    New interpretations of Havana classics are on the menu in this City Center townhouse. Together with the standard empanadas, ropa vieja, and ceviche, you'll find ambitious preparations like pumpkin seed-encusted sea scallops swimming in a calabaza melon puree and sea bass on a bed of green rice with sides of plantains and stuffed peppers. The white-jacketed staff looks elegant, but service was brusque, and we felt rushed enough to ask the waiter to slow down his frenetic pace. At these high prices, we wanted to savor the meal, not gulp and run so he could turn the table.

    Appetizer I: Fire and Ice Ceviche - fluke with preserved lemon, sage, hot garlic oil and crispy lemon rings ($12)

    Appetizer II: Royal Palm Dates - almond stuffed dates wrapped in bacon with cabrales bleu cheese ($12)

    Entree I: Tempura Avocado Salad - crab and grilled asparagus with watercress tossed with preserved lemon vinaigrette ($14)

    Entree II: Sancocho De Pollo - coconut chicken broth with poached chicken slices, yuca, carrots cilantro green peas and a touch of lime juice ($9)

    Entree III: Vaca Frita "Fried Cow" - twice cooked crispy skirt steak with onions and cuban oregano with black beans, white rice and tomato escabeche ($21)

    Entree IV: Cilantro Honey Mustard Glazed Salmon - served over a banana lentil salad with fresh horseradish cream ($23)

    ---

    My friends the Marathoner v.1 (James) and the Marathoner v.2 (Paul) are leaving for Buenos Aires next week and will be living there for four months. We sat down for some much needed face time over a meal at Alma de Cuba. Alex Elkins joined us as well.

    Unfortunately, Paul and Alex weren't hungry, which made the Marathoner and I feel a bit funny for ordering up a storm. But we got over that pretty quickly.

    One of my favorite things about Alma de Cuba is their complimentary bread; it's simply incredible. The bread's texture is dense and tastes mildly sweet. The bread is served with an oil-based dipping sauce, but I find it most delicious eaten plain. My friends enjoyed the bread as well.

    The Marathoner and I ordered two appetizers. I chose the Fire and Ice ceviche because I had it once prior with Liana and loved it. The Marathoner ordered the stuffed dates because the combination of ingredients piqued his interest. The ceviche did not disappoint, it was refreshing and delicate. The fluke was tender and the crispy lemon rings were intense. Mmm. The stuffed dates were awesome as well and served atop a piece of endive with chopped red onions and a light vinaigrette. The salty bacon wrapped around the sweet date along with the pungent bleu cheese created a divine collection of flavors. Delicious!

    For our entrees, Paul ordered a salad, Alex got the soup, the Marathoner ordered the salmon, and I got the vaca frita. Paul liked his salad except for the tempura avocado, he said it tasted a bit too much like deep fried butter. Alex enjoyed his soup after he discovered that it wasn't cream-based.

    The Marathoner's salmon had definite highs and lows . The fish was fresh, the lentils were tasty (and cute), and the banana brought about an unexpected and welcomed sweetness. Unfortunately, the mustard seeds atop the fish along with the horseradish sauce were too strong for our palettes and took away from the dish overall.

    I ordered the vaca frita in order to compare it to the version I had in Little Havana this past summer. The presentation at Alma de Cuba was much more beautiful than the one in Little Havana and the meat was of higher quality. The meat was wonderfully crispy on the outside, moist on the inside, and very flavorful. The onions and tomato escabeche were a bit on the tart side, but the rice and beans were good. The sides at Alma were decent, but not as tasty as the ones in Little Havana. Fried sweet plantains should always accompany vaca frita.

    How is it this place can have only a total of 4 stars.  Maybe because Yelpers feel price and pretentious owners are reasons to demote a restaraunt by a whole star.  

    For the economically minded reader:  
    A for food
    A+ for service
    A for creativity
    A+ for ambience

    Steven Starr may be a pompous ass, and he may steal all his ideas from other dining establishments he's visited.  But he has found the formula for finding amazing chefs, not sacrificing quality (sometimes at the expense of your wallet), and giving people a little bit of what they want, whether it be a wall of candlelight (Tangerine), a giant Buddha and 10 person table (Buddakhan), or the Japanese version of the future from the 1950's (Pod).  With Alma de Cuba, he hit all the marks right.  

    People complain about the expense of the drinks, but i learned how to make Mojitoes from this place.  Muddle some mint with sugar, splash some fresh lime juice on top, add ice, fill the glass with some good high quality rum, and splash some soda water and bitters.  When i say a "splash" i mean exactly that.  For your $8 you get the equivelent liquor of a 2 drink free pour.  Also nice is the option to choose your particular spirit for your drink, and an impressive choice of scotches for a place that doesn't really specialize.  They are all about catering to a higher bar, and it's definitely for a special night out, or for those who enjoy spending their hard earned cash on food and liquor.
    Oh, and better to spend your money here than at Cuba de Libre where you are assured a very watered down drink and equal frugalessness.  

    Especially nice during the restaraunt week, where the drinks are half price, they offer some of the people's favorites on the prix fix menu.  Regardless of how much i've ever spent there, whether just a drink or a full dinner, service has been impecable.  I even ordered some coffee, had to get up to go to the bathroom, and when i didn't return to the coffee in a timely fashion, it was replaced with a fresher cup.  That's some attention detail, or someone with OCD on the wait staff.

    What it lacks in leftist politics it makes up for in great food, attention to detail and playful lighting.  Downstairs at the bar the lighting is neon from the bar and then soft and candlelit elsewhere.  They sometimes have live music here, and this is a great spot to sit for drinks in the lounge, or to grab a table for a taste of their lighter fare.  It's easy to get a table here when there's no space in the main part.  

    The restaraunt proper is upstairs, on two levels above the bar.  It's surprisingly quieter here than in the lounge/bar area and the lighting is flattering if your date has boils.  The photo projections on the wall might be our American imperialism rearing its ugly head to show we are so much better off than those poor sugar cane farmers, but then one could also say the size of the portraits could be symbolic that they are better than we are?  (sorry Nicole).  They are, however, part of the lighting and the ambience of the place.  I wouldn't trade them.

    Food:  Beautiful choices.  I have found their fish to be fresh and well seasoned (always changing), their appetizers have fun additions like a guacamole with plantain chips, or spicy modern American flare.  

    Ceviche!  This is their special gift to the northern American world (after Caiparinhas made with imported Cachaca from Brazil).  Lobster and shrimp with coconut.  Big Eyed Tuna!  Get a sampler with three five or all....If you like ceviche, you'll love the Alma de Cuba take on some classics and playing with some alternatives.  And yes, it will be acidic, as it is cooked with lime/lemon juice.  So be sure you understand ceviche before you arrive (though there are ways to make it more mellow i haven't yet mastered in my own kitchen).

    Reservations are encouraged for peak hours, especially on the weekends.  You might have a chance if you're only two, but even if you call early in the day, make the effort.  It's a fantastic place to take a date if they are there with you and not for the politics.  But if you're a person who is interested in having a nice dinner and conversation with great service, and have to worry about whether the place is politically correct enough to please them, maybe s/he's not the one?

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District.
