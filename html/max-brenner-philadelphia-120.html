        First of all, I went there for appetizers and dessert, not a full on meal. I wanted room for dessert, after all. My SO and I tried the waffle fries dusted with chili and cocoa powder. They were amazing, and the sauce that came with it was just delicious. I could have been content eating those for an entire meal. Next were the potato skins, which are easily the best ones I've ever had.

        But let's be real. The draw for this place is the dessert. We ordered the banana waffles, which came with a cute little vial of chocolate sauce, and chocolate covered rice crispies. As someone who considers herself a bit of a dessert and chocolate snob, I was wondering if this would really be worth the price tag. But my doubts were swept away with the very first bite. It was absolutely divine. My SO said it was easily one of the best desserts he'd ever had in his entire life.

        Add to a fabulous meal some excellent service, and you have a five star review. Kudos especially to the hostess at the front, who was very sweet and accommodating when we asked if we could move up our reservation at the last minute.

        First I have to say that the picture of the bald man in this restaurant sort of freaks me out. It just does... and it doesn't fit in with the rest of the otherwise nice decor in this touristy pop spot.

        Chocolate + more chocolate. That is all I came here for with 2 girl friends of mine on a Saturday night, and we were not disappointed. First, we all ordered hot chocolate. How could we not? It'd be like going to a taco stand and ordering an enchilada. All of them were pretty good... white and milk chocolates with marshmallows were pretty tasty but I thought mine, the dark chocolate cocoa puffs, was the best. First, I just think hot chocolate tastes best dark... just personal preference. Then are the cocoa puffs, which sort of float at the top of the chocolate. These taste like small balloons of liquid dark chocolate that pop in your mouth as you break open the soft wafer shell of the puff... they're pretty amazing. If you're interested in a good hot chocolate with an extra "poof" of chocolate, get the cocoa puffs. They also come in these extremely cute "Hug Mugs" which you hold up to drink by cupping your hands around the entire cup. I'm a sucker for cute and also functional design pieces, so this scored some points for me.

        More chocolate: we also got the chocolate pizza. You can add as many of the listed toppings as you want... and we added everything but the peanut butter. Chocolate (of course), bananas, chopped hazelnuts, and melted marshmallows on top of a warm nicely chewy dough. It was pretty chocotastic. But we'd each already had a cup of rich liquid chocolate, so sad to say we didn't finish the whole pizza. We didn't dare let it go to waste though. The last piece was thoroughly enjoyed for breakfast :).

        Bottom line: a must-visit for chocolate lovers... because it's something you should try at least once. No comments about the non-chocolate food... but just from glancing at the menu I can't say there was anything that jumped out at me as looking particularly special.
