    Came here one night after a fantastic meal at Chifa so it had a high bar to meet. While we couldn't point to anything in particular, we just felt our overall dining experience at Chifa was of higher quality. Having said that, we thorougly enjoyed our meal and would definitely recommend it! If we lived in the area, we would return and try some other dishes.

    We thought the Tasting menu (7 courses plus dessert) would be too much food so we opted to order off the menu. The dishes came to the table as soon as they were ready instead of all at once, so we could enjoy each dish hot and fresh. This also prevented me from stuffing my face too quickly.

    Food:
    Pork buns -- delicious! Better than Chifa's pork buns IMHO. The pork was more tender and had a crispy coating with a yummy glaze. Also a better value as you get 2 for $7. (At Chifa, it's 2 for $9).

    Lamb satay - tender and tasty! 2 small lamb chops for $13

    Korean BBQ Beef skewers - super tender, marinated steak, thickly sliced. Soooo good! 2 skewers for $10.

    Crispy rock shrimp - battered and fried shrimp with a chili mayonnaise-y sauce. It was pretty heavy and we had trouble finishing the dish between 2 people. I didn't care for the sauce on the shrimp. I think it'd be better if it was a glaze without the mayo. $14

    Crab wonton tacos - it was like a crab mayonnaise-y salad stuffed inside a fried wonton wrapper shaped like a taco. It was good but wouldn't order it again. An order of 3 for $13.

    Beef short ribs with lo mein - the braised beef was extremely tender and flavorful! The lo mein was cooked perfectly al dente and the vegetables were crisp-tender. A big bowl of noodles with three chunks of short ribs for $22.

    We were totally stuffed after this and skipped dessert.

    Service:
    Our server was very nice and attentive. I give him props for telling us to hold off on ordering the Chilean Sea Bass in addition to everything above, saying it might be too much food for 2 people. I also liked that when I couldn't decide between the shrimp and crab tacos, he told us he thought the shrimp was better. I hate it when a server says both dishes are good when I'm trying to make a decision. (We ended up ordering the tacos a few minutes later anyway).

    Ambiance:
    This place would be good for birthdays and large parties. It's really loud (on a Monday night, there were only a few tables and we could barely hear each other).

    If we're ever back in Philly, we would go back and try the Sea Bass and the pork potstickers. And the hamachi ceviche. And the General Tso Soup buns. And the kobe beef skewers.

    Sampan is like that girl who has it all - it's fabulous and beautiful inside and out.  Literally.  I've dined at Sampan (inside) and gone to happy hour at the Graffiti Bar (outside), and I can't decide which I like more.  The menu is a bit all over the place - Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Vietnamese, Thai - I would feel offended that Schulson can't make up his mind if it all weren't done so well.  The happy hour menu is a fantastic value - the banh mi is downright delicious (and a really great portion size for $4), and you can grab the famed edamame dumplings for just $4 too.  I love the creative interpretation of the Philly Cheese Steak, and I really enjoyed my Peking Duck, flavorful pulled meat served in a little jar.  I'm a savory girl, but we've had the soft serve here for dessert as well - they come up with some awesome crazy flavors, and it's fun to eat in its tiny cone.  If you're with a group (who you're not concerned about catching a communicable disease from), get a scorpion bowl - they're a grown up version of the gator bowls we used to get in college - less blue curacao and kool-aid powder, more pomegranate juice and fresh fruit but with the same fun colored straws.

    Graffiti Bar Happy Hour Review

    While I love Sampan for dinner, I love Graffiti Bar for drinking even more. A lot of people don't even notice the small sign on the by the narrow alley leading back to the bar. It is an outdoor bar with, well you guessed it, artistic graffiti everywhere. We were drinking there the night Whitney Houston died (RIP Whit) and it was snowing. Well Graffiti Bar has covers and heaters which make drinking outside a year round opportunity. It was fun to have good drinks in the snow and not be shivering cold.  

    More recently we were there on a warm summer afternoon for happy hour. Happy Hour runs from 5-7pm on weekdays. My first suggestion is going to be go for the scorpion bowl. One scorpion bowl is $23 but its packed with flavor and alcohol and lasted 2 people at least an hour and a half. For the rooks, a scorpion bowl is a fishbowl filled with, in this case either cucumber margarita or blueberry vodka lemonade, and it has about 8 straws in because it is meant for sharing. It is fun and it is worth it. Graffiti bar has NICE beer and liquor choices so don't expect to go there and get $2 Rolling Rock ponies. I think the beer special was $4 Blue Moon Summers.

    The food during Graffiti Bar happy hour is top notch! Everything is under $4 and includes bahn mhis, Kobe burgers and dogs, satay, and some sides from the indoor menu. My suggestion here would be order a few things with friends and try it all. We got a beef satay, chicken bahn mhi, Kobe hot dog, Kobe burger, and Brussels sprouts. It all added up to maybe $20?? If that? They aren't huge portions except for the bahn mhi which was large. My all time favorite dish at Sampan, although it didn't go well with alcohol, are the Brussels sprouts (mixed with fish sauce and rice cereal).

    My last bit of advice is if you are going for happy hour, let me know cause I will meet you there!
