    I had one of the best dining experiences here, ever!

    At first glance, I was a little weary of the tapas style portions, but let me tell you, I was glad the plates weren't a full portion! I was with a party of 6 and we were seated right away. Our server was friendly and very knowledgeable about the menu, explaining the portions for each section of the menu. She highly recommended that we try the Chef's selection, choosing 7 plates from the menu, 2 sides, and dessert - a great way to try a lot of things, and that is what we ended up doing. We also ordered one of each of the Scorpion bowls, large servings of cucumber margarita and blueberry lemonade. Both were phenomenal!

    We selected the following plates:
    General Tso Soup Dumpling: juicy, flavorful, with a nice kick
    Philly Cheese Steak: absolute fan favorite, this is the dish I wish we had more of!
    Pork Bao Bun: I love love love the buns and the pork was tender and delicious
    Kobe Beef Satay: served with a mustard sauce, cooked absolutely perfectly
    Crab Wonton Taco: Izakaya has a variation of crab tacos, so I was eager to try them here, and they did not disappoint!
    Pekin Duck: Tender duck with mini buns, scallops, cucumber and sweet/sour sauce - excellent!
    Shrimp & Scallop: I'm not a huge scallop fan, but these were cooked exactly how I like them and they were quite large! The shrimp was great too.
    Roasted Broccoli: Perfectly marinated, full of flavor.
    Kobe Beef Fried Rice: How can you not love kobe beef fried rice?

    The desserts for the evening was vanilla Lucky Charm and chocolate Cap'n Crunch - I loved these ice cream cones. And it appears the flavor changes daily!

    As I said, the portions ended up being perfect because by the time our final dish came out, we were all pretty full!

    I definitely want to try their happy hour one day, the menu looks very appetizing as well!

    Sampan is an upscale and trendy asian fusion restaurant.  I love this restaurant! You can get dressed up and you feel like you are in a trendy hotspot, like a scene out of sex and the city. (Note to Sushi Samba.)  Or you can dress casual and try to grab a seat at the bar. We went for happy hour and it was a fun and friendly atmosphere. The servers were very attentive and helpful.  

    Not asian fusion like pei wei... this is a true culinary treat!  Happy hour is an amazing meal and we tried a bunch of things on the menu. The Kim Chee Rice was the standout.  After trying a few of the small plates like dumplings and kobe hot dog slider, we needed something more substantial.  The Kim Chee Rice was big and filling... and really tasty! Everything was excellent. The cocktails were also top notch.  

    The bar fills up so we found two seats in the back corner overlooking the cooks preparing the food. A little hot from the stoves and ovens, but it was fun to watch them cook. We also didn't have a choice!

    The review you are about to skim/skip is for Restaurant Week, Fall 2012.

    Also, like Union Trust, Sampan boasts extremely high production values. Subtle neon backlights provide illuminating accents to nature-based wall art while choreographed clean-cut servers and line cooks zip back and forth and yuppies satiate themselves alongside their ex-yuppie parents. Aside from polish and enticement ability, Sampan and Union Trust have little in common. Of course, "little" is greater than "nothing", and that little thing that they have in common is actually the kicker here - like Union Trust, the food at Sampan is merely decent despite being well-made.

    First, the good. Whatever complaints other patrons on this page had about the small portion size is irrelevant for purposes of restaurant week. I found myself unable to put away the six courses (five dishes plus dessert) I was given, which is saying a lot because my appetite can be best described as Borderline Competitive Eater. YMMV, however; I deliberately chose the largest dishes (kobe beef fried rice, filet mignon, rock shrimp, etc) for my excursion. You can do much worse both in terms of both quality and quantity for $35. The service was quick and attentive even on the pressure cooker clusterfuck of a night when I attended, so hats off to the staff on that one. Finally, the dessert (two mini soft serve ice cream cones was great.

    As for the bad - the food was a bit less flavorful than I expected. I don't see the point to singling out particular dishes because my taste buds were asleep throughout the meal. The five courses were pretty much amounted to a chorus of "decent, had better"s. I don't think any of this is an issue of lack of care or poor execution, but rather the fact that the executed item just didn't impress me, regardless of the obvious effort put in. As I was eating, I found myself floating off into an opposite world where the meal in front of me had more spice, more heat, more depth, and more something-other-than-competent-middlebrow-fare.
