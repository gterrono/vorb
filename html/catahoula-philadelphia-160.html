    Went here for brunch last Saturday and had a surreal experience.  Party of six. We got there around 11 and it took them about a half hour to take our orders because the kitchen wasn't ready.  I ordered the eggs Benedict on fried oysters, and my friend ordered a vegetarian omelet.  My eggs came out and I almost couldn't believe it: when I bit in, instead of oysters there were oyster-sized pieces of fried chicken.  I was in denial and just ate the first one.  Half way through the second one, I had a very awkward exchange with the waiter in which I showed him the chicken.  He said he'd get the cook to make me new ones.  I was full at this point (and pretty disgusted) and said I didn't want new ones.  He came back with two fried oysters anyway and explained that the cook grabbed chicken by accident.  I can't imagine how that happens and have to conclude that they were running low on oysters and trying to skimp.

    My vegetarian friend's omelet came out with bacon on it.  When she said something the waiter took her plate (sides and all).  By the time she got her plate back we were long done eating (except my little plate of two fried oysters) and we all sat around watching her eat.  A brief apology and the full check came out despite two ruined meals.  Honestly, if he'd brought a few free Bloody Mary's out I wouldn't be complaining here but you can't screw up that royally these days and expect customers to keep quiet.

    This is my neighborhood spot when my neighborhood is Queen Village. And from time to time, pending great friendships in the area, it is. I have to be honest, it took me a little while to warm up to this place. Once I took it for what it was, I now go there lovingly with the warm and fuzzy feeling of feeling like "everyone knows your name." Too corny? Maybe..

    Anyway, solid food-especially if the food you're are looking for is Louisiana-Southern-fried-comfort food. Delicious po boys of every variety. Juicy burgers. Scrimp by the pound. FRIED PICKLES. FRIED. EVERYTHING. Really damn good. Oh, dessert-do that, too. Trust me, I rarely ever save room for this, but they have a frequently rotating board with a few options that will round out an already comforting, gluttonous experience.

    Mostly, this is a place I go to drink, though. The do not have the biggest variety of rotating drafts, but I'll admit I'm pretty spoiled/snobby on that front, and honestly they always have an option that I enjoy. This also encourages me to partake in the $5 boilermaker/city-wide special that they offer (Narraganset tall boy & heaven hill) which is just a great idea. Really, just keep drinking them.

    The place is small and I prefer to sit at the bar with my friends and chat it up with the bartenders. They are all really cool, friendly people who I enjoy talking to. Also, there is a quizzo coming soon (which apparently they're not calling quizzo-not sure of the difference?) Great, friendly, warm spot-embodies all of the things that make up the perfect neighborhood hang.
