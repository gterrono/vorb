    Service is a little sub-par. But you don't go here for the service. You go here for the ambiance and a cool place to catch up with friends and sample tons of food.

    - French onion soup dumpling

    The way this place looked just made me want to try it!  Cool hanging lamps, vinyl diner-styled seating downstairs, with even cooler hanging basket chairs on the 2nd floor that overlook the main dining area.   There is even a rooftop deck.

    If you want an outside table, grab it if you see it, we made the mistake of passing an empty table by to ask if we could take it at the front desk, by then it was already gone.  We consoled ourselves with the basket seats.  

    The menu is heavily on the seafood side, items that you would think would not be available in a diner-like setting, such as Miso Soup, Tofu-Chive Dumplings, and  Duck show up next to Sesame Crab Cakes, and Lobster Mac n' cheese  (one of the more popular dishes, and what friends had told me to try).  Then to add to the "continental" style-BBQ Chicken Quesadilla, Beef Chimichurri,  Spinach Ravioli, Vegetable Samosas, Lamb Chops, and Jamaican Jerk Chicken.  There is even Sushi on the menu.  

    We settled on Battered Calamari and Shrimp, as well as Crab Wontons and the hugest, heaping plate of shoestring fries I have ever seen.  I would suggest skipping the fries, as no matter how many you eat, the pile never seemed to get any smaller!

    This might be a great place to go if you have a few people interested in different styles of dishes.  That way most everyone should find something they like on the menu.  Also, the dishes, for the most part, are made to share.

    I definitely recommend going here - brunch (their granola is spectacular and homemade, and the bacon is thick cut and flavorful), lunch (try the lobster mac-n-cheese, made with orzo!), or dinner (the bruschetta is also to die for - mostly made with arugula and a few grape tomatoes, the seasonings and goat cheese make it fantastic).

Meet the Business Owner

Stephen S. Business Owner

Founded in 1995 by Stephen Starr, Philadelphia-based STARR Restaurants is one of the fastest growing multi-concept restaurant companies in the country. STARR Restaurants include Buddakan, Morimoto, The Continental, Barclay Prime, Continental Mid-Town, El Vez, Jones, Alma de Cuba, Pod, Parc, Butcher and Singer, Pizzeria Stella, El Rey, Ranstead Room, The Dandelion, Talula's Garden, Frankford Hall, Il Pittore, Route 6 and seasonal food-stand SquareBurger in Philadelphia, as well as, Morimoto and Buddakan in New York City's thriving Meatpacking District. STARR Restaurants expanded to Atlantic City, NJ, with Buddakan and The Continental located in the Pier Shops at Caesars in 2006, and to Florida with Steak 954 at the W Hotel Fort Lauderdale in 2009, and Makoto in the Bal Harbour Shops in 2011.
